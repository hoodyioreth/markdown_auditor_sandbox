Free Alliance of the Kron Hills
===============================

The [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) is a confederation of [Gnome](gnomes-species-1.md) clans governing the upper [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) and [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) — approximately twenty thousand gnomes organized into twelve townships under the authority of [House Tulvar](house-tulvar-organization.md). It is a geopolitical entity, a trade partner, a military ally, and a thorn in the side of every Viscount who has tried to bring the Kron Hills under direct Viscounty administration.  

For Dungeon Masters, the Free Alliance is not a minor faction to be summarized in a paragraph. It is one of the three pillars of the Verbobonc political system — alongside the [Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) and the four-layer [government](the-government-of-verbobonc-organization.md) — and in 576 CY, it is the pillar under the most stress. Every significant political crisis facing the Viscounty touches the gnome question. Every faction on the Council has a different answer to it. And the Temple of Elemental Evil is exploiting the gap between those answers with surgical precision.

 

Understanding the Free Alliance means understanding the campaign's most consequential off-screen player — the one whose decisions about whether to stay allied with Verbobonc or redirect their trade to Dyvers will determine whether the Viscounty can mount a coordinated response to the Temple's Second Rising.

 

### History and Structure

 

The Free Alliance evolved over centuries from scattered clan holdings into a structured confederation rooted in gnomish communal tradition. Its political heart is the village of [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md), where Clanlord [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md), the Eldest of Tulvar, has presided over the gnomes of the Kron Hills for more than five centuries. His family's rule is acknowledged by both nobility and commoners across the region — a stability that human governance rarely achieves.

 

*   **Power Structure:** Confederation. The twelve townships of Greenway Valley each govern themselves through family councils, but defer to House Tulvar on matters of trade policy, military coordination, and diplomacy with Verbobonc. This is not a monarchy — it is a network of obligations, and the Eldest holds it together through respect, not force.
*   **Economic System:** Market economy built on gem mining, craftsmanship, and trade caravans. The [1\. The Kron Market](1-the-kron-market-landmark.md) serves as the primary exchange — a three-tier marketplace with High Warrens (gem merchants, enchanters), Tunnel Bazaar (rare components, potions), and Back Alleys (contraband, illicit goods). The gnome economy is not simple. It is sophisticated, layered, and capable of funding a military response if the Alliance decides Verbobonc is no longer worth trading with.
*   **Military Capability:** A militia of 2,500 gnome soldiers can be mustered from Greenway Valley — veterans of the Hateful Wars and defenders of the Kron Hills against humanoid incursions. [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md), the engineering marvel guarding the Greenway Pass, maintains a permanent garrison of 100 gnomes under Thane [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md). This fortress controls the only significant pass into the Valley. An army that cannot take Kron Fortress cannot reach Greenway Valley. The gnomes know this.

 

### The Leadership: Three Voices, Three Strategies

 

The Free Alliance is not governed by a single will. Three key figures represent three different strategies for navigating the gnome crisis — and their disagreements are as important to the campaign as their agreements.

 

#### Clanlord Urthgan Tulvar — The Patriarch

[Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md), Eldest of Tulvar, is the central authority of [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and the most respected gnome in the Kron Hills. Five centuries of leadership have given him a perspective that most human politicians cannot comprehend — he has watched Viscounts come and go, alliances form and dissolve, and the Kron Hills endure through all of it. His strategy is patience: maintain the Alliance's independence, keep the trade routes functional, and wait for Verbobonc's latest political crisis to resolve itself the way every previous one has.

**DM use:** Urthgan is the voice of strategic patience. A party that earns his trust gains access to centuries of institutional memory about Verbobonc politics, Temple history, and the true value of what lies in the mines. He does not meet with outsiders easily. He meets with outsiders who bring evidence that the current crisis is different from all previous ones.

 

#### "Prince" Jimm Pithriggen — The Diplomat

[Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) — full name Faljimm Nizgafal — is the Alliance's diplomatic representative in Verbobonc, attending the [Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) as an observer with no formal vote and enormous practical leverage. He maintains stakes in several gnomish merchant houses in Verbobonc City, resides at [B6 Manor of Prince Jimm](b6-manor-of-prince-jimm-landmark.md) and the [G1 Old House of Prince Jimm](g1-old-house-of-prince-jimm-landmark.md) in the [G1-10 Gnomesberg Gnome Quarter](g1-10-gnomesberg-gnome-quarter-settlement.md), and is the gnome the Council members encounter most frequently — which is precisely his function.

Jimm is unfailingly polite. He is also keeping a ledger. Every promise made and not kept, every patrol withdrawn, every mine claim filed without the Alliance's knowledge — Pithriggen records it, and on a dozen occasions has demonstrated that his absence from future Council sessions carries more weight than his attendance.

**DM use:** Pithriggen is the party's most accessible entry point to gnome politics. He is charming, information-hungry, and will trade intelligence for intelligence. But his true motivations remain deliberately opaque — he reveals very little about his personal allegiances and is probing others for information far more often than he provides it. A party that helps Pithriggen with the gnome cause will find doors opening across the Viscounty. A party that betrays his trust will find those doors closing permanently.

 

#### "Lady Jeet" Jimbleclap — The Fortress

[Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) commands [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) — the 100-gnome garrison controlling the Greenway Pass. Elderly even by gnome standards, with thinning white hair and a white raven companion named Smoke, Jeet is a veteran warrior who personally led gnome forces to join the Righteous Host at [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md). She is a pragmatist and the Alliance leader least interested in diplomatic niceties. Where Pithriggen smooths things over, Jeet says what she means — and has no problem interrupting anyone who suggests something she deems stupid, though she does so with as much respect as possible. Where Urthgan counsels patience, Jeet demands action. Her primary concern is security: the bandit raids on gnome caravans, the withdrawal of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md), and the growing vulnerability of the trade routes. Gnome warriors call her "mother" or "grandmother." The Council of Lords calls her "Lady Jeet," which she finds amusing. She has little use for titles or honorifics, finding such things foolish and silly.

Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) has tolerated the gnomes' desire for independence, but Jeet tries his patience. The tension between the two is visible to anyone who sees them in the same room. Pithriggen intervenes and smooths things over — but the underlying disagreement is real and worsening.

**DM use:** Jeet is the Alliance's military voice — kind but tough, practical about her people's contributions, and never far from Smoke, her white raven. She defers political matters to Urthgan but will act independently on security questions. A party that brings hard evidence of organized bandit operations — especially evidence linking the raids to the Temple — will find Jeet willing to commit Kron Fortress resources to a joint operation. She is blunt, direct, and trustworthy in a way that Pithriggen deliberately is not.

 

### Territory and Resources

 

The Alliance controls some of the most strategically valuable territory in the Viscounty — and that territory is the reason every political faction in Verbobonc has an opinion about gnome autonomy.

 

#### Greenway Valley

[Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) is the Alliance's heartland: approximately 10,000 gnomes organized into twelve townships, each governed by a gnome family under the confederation. The central township of [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) serves as the political seat. The Valley's architecture is a blend of gnome ingenuity and Verbobonc Gothic influence — surface houses alongside traditional gnome "rents" (cellars) connected to the earth. Each township maintains a trading house along the Greenway, overseen by Satveegrs — agents of the Assembly responsible for tax collection and dispute mediation.

 

#### Kron Fortress

[Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) is an engineering marvel carved into a rocky outcrop guarding the Greenway Pass — the only significant passage into the Valley. Its 100-gnome garrison under Thane Jimbleclap controls who enters and who does not. Below the fortress sits the Village of Kron, the largest gnome settlement at approximately 165 gnomes, and home to the [1\. The Kron Market](1-the-kron-market-landmark.md).

 

#### The Kron Hills

The [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) contain the richest gem deposits in the region — and the ownership of those deposits is the legal and political flashpoint of the gnome crisis. The Alliance claims exclusive ancestral rights to mine these resources. The Viscounty's legal archive contains documentation challenging those claims. Who filed those documents, and whether they are genuine, is one of the most important unanswered questions in the campaign.

 

#### Gnomesberg — The Verbobonc Footprint

The [G1-10 Gnomesberg Gnome Quarter](g1-10-gnomesberg-gnome-quarter-settlement.md) is the Alliance's embassy district within Verbobonc City. Key locations include [G2 Hilewy's Gnome Palace](g2-hilewyE28099s-gnome-palace-landmark.md), the [G7 The Guild of Millers](g7-the-guild-of-millers-landmark.md), the [G9 The Glittering Grotto of the Golden Hills](g9-the-glittering-grotto-of-the-golden-hills-landmark.md) (the gnome religious center, led by High Priest Glimmer Gemheart), and the [G10 The Gnomesberg River-Piers - Nigb's Run Docks](g10-the-gnomesberg-river-piers---nigbE28099s-run-docks-landmark.md) for river trade. Gnomesberg is where Alliance interests intersect daily with Verbobonc City politics — and where the economic relationship between the two peoples is most visible.

 

### The Gnome Crisis of 576 CY

 

In 576 CY, the Free Alliance is under more pressure than at any point since the Hateful Wars. Four interlocking crises are pushing the Alliance toward a decision that will reshape the Viscounty's political landscape — and the [Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) cannot agree on how to respond to any of them.

 

#### 1\. The Mine Claim Documents

Someone has filed documentation in the Verbobonc legal archive claiming ownership of gnome mines under a 412 CY land grant — directly challenging the Alliance's ancestral rights. The documents sit unresolved. The Viscount has not acted on the filings. He is waiting for a legal determination. The legal determination has been awaited for four months. Meanwhile, the claims cast a shadow over every trade negotiation and every gnome merchant who passes through Verbobonc City.

The key question — one that the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) has not asked publicly — is who filed the documents. The answer implicates a Council member. Which one depends on what the DM wants the next six sessions to look like.

 

#### 2\. The Caravan Raids

Trade caravans traveling between Greenway Valley and Verbobonc City have been increasingly targeted by organized bandit attacks — raids that show knowledge of cargo, timing, and which roads are currently patrolled. The raids have disrupted the Valley's economy, destroyed gnome merchant goods, and produced gnomish prisoners — including at least one found at the Moathouse with an iron ring that connects to wider Temple operations.

The banditry pattern is consistent with what the Temple used before [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) to isolate settlements and prepare for resurgence. The Council is debating a logistics problem that may be a military operation.

 

#### 3\. The Mounted Borderer Withdrawal

Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) made the decision to withdraw [Mounted Borderer](mounted-borderers-organization.md) patrols from the edges of the Kron Hills — a decision that placed Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md), commander of the 3rd Company "Longriders" and the only half-elf on the Council, in an impossible position. His own men were pulled back from a region where his own people are being raided, under orders from a Viscount he technically serves and personally disagrees with.

Deleveu has not spoken about this in Council. He has also not spoken the opposite. The silence is loud.

For the gnomes, the withdrawal is the most consequential Viscounty decision of 576 CY. It told the Alliance that Verbobonc will not protect the Kron Hills — even when its own patrols are the only thing standing between gnome caravans and organized raiding. Glimmer Gemheart of the [G9 The Glittering Grotto of the Golden Hills](g9-the-glittering-grotto-of-the-golden-hills-landmark.md) has been lobbying the Viscount to reinstate the patrols. Jeet Jimbleclap has organized his own gnome scouts and mercenary escorts. Pithriggen watches the Council and records what happens.

 

#### 4\. The Dyvers Question

The unspoken threat — the one that keeps the Economic Powers on the Council awake at night — is that the Free Alliance simply redirects its trade to Dyvers. Gnome gem merchants, gnome crafters, gnome caravans — all of it flowing north instead of south, bypassing Verbobonc entirely. This would devastate [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md)'s guild revenues, damage [Roland Galans](roland-galans-person.md)'s shipping interests, and remove a critical economic ally from any coalition response to the Temple. The Alliance has not made this threat publicly. They have not needed to. Everyone on the Council knows what the numbers look like without gnome trade.

 

### Political Relationships with the Council of Lords

 

Every faction on the [Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) has a position on the gnome crisis. None of them agree.

 

*   **The Militarists** ([Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md), [Claviger Velysin](claviger-velysin-person.md)) support asserting Viscounty legal authority over the mine claims. They view gnome autonomy as a secondary concern to territorial integrity and want the Alliance to accept Viscounty administration in exchange for military protection. This is the position the gnomes reject most categorically.
*   **The Economic Powers** ([House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md), [Roland Galans](roland-galans-person.md), [Drenton Vaswell](drenton-vaswell-person.md)) want the gnome trade routes restored regardless of the legal question. Their guild revenues depend on it. They are the faction most willing to give the Alliance what it wants — if it keeps the caravans running.
*   **The Manipulators** (Lady [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md)) — Langmuir has been flagged by [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) as the Council member most likely to have information about the mine claim filings and least likely to volunteer it. Her involvement in the gnome crisis is the most dangerous unknown in the campaign.
*   **The Cultural Moderates** (Lord [Wendell Rhynehurst](wendell-rhynehurst-person.md)) support fair treatment and negotiation. Rhynehurst is the Council member the Alliance trusts most — and the one with the least institutional power to deliver what the gnomes need.
*   **The Border Interest** (Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md)) — stuck between his duty to the Viscount and his sympathy for the gnomes his own patrols used to protect. Deleveu has information about the Borderer withdrawal that he has not shared with the Council. He needs a third party to surface it.

 

### Religious and Cultural Life

 

The Alliance's religious life centers on the Gnome Pantheon, worshipped at the [G9 The Glittering Grotto of the Golden Hills](g9-the-glittering-grotto-of-the-golden-hills-landmark.md) in Gnomesberg under High Priest Glimmer Gemheart. Gemheart is not merely a spiritual leader — he is the gnome community's most visible political advocate at the [C16 The Gathering Hall of Verbobonc](c16-the-gathering-hall-of-verbobonc-landmark.md), where he fights for patrol reinstatement, mine rights, and caravan protection. His is the most politically charged delegation at the Gathering Hall — and his appeals go unanswered.

 

See [Religion in Verbobonc](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md) for how the Gnome Pantheon fits into the wider religious-political map of the Viscounty.

 

### What the Temple Exploits

 

The Temple of Elemental Evil does not need to attack the Free Alliance. It simply needs the Alliance and Verbobonc to stop cooperating — and in 576 CY, that process is already well underway.

 

*   The **caravan raids** are consistent with Temple operational patterns used before Emridy Meadows to isolate settlements. If the raids are Temple-directed, they serve a dual purpose: economic disruption and political isolation of the gnome community.
*   The **mine claim documents** may have been filed by a Temple-aligned actor — or by a Council member being manipulated by one. Either way, the legal dispute paralyzes the Viscount and prevents the Alliance from trusting Verbobonc's institutions.
*   The **Mounted Borderer withdrawal** created a security vacuum that the Alliance interprets as abandonment. Every day without patrols is another day Pithriggen's ledger grows longer.
*   The **Dyvers threat** — if the Alliance redirects trade — removes the economic incentive for the Council's Economic Powers to support military action against the Temple. Without gnome trade revenue, Haxx cannot fund mercenary patrols. Without mercenary patrols, the roads south to Hommlet and [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) stay vulnerable. The Temple wins by subtraction.

 

The party that understands this chain can disrupt it at any link. The party that treats the gnome crisis as a side quest will discover — too late — that it was the main quest all along.

 

### DM Notes — The Free Alliance as Campaign Infrastructure

 

#### How to Use the Alliance

 

*   **Pithriggen as patron and information broker:** The "Prince" is the party's most natural gnome contact. He trades intelligence, opens doors to Council members, and can commission party action on Alliance business. But every favor he provides creates an obligation — and Pithriggen always remembers what he is owed.
*   **Jeet as military resource:** If the party brings hard evidence of organized Temple involvement in the caravan raids, Jimbleclap can commit fortress resources to a joint operation. This is one of the most significant military assets available to the party outside of Verbobonc City proper.
*   **Urthgan as strategic advisor:** The Eldest has centuries of knowledge about the Kron Hills, the Temple's previous rising, and the political patterns of the Viscounty. Earning his trust is a campaign-length investment that pays dividends in intelligence no other source can provide.
*   **Greenway Valley as sanctuary:** If the party makes enemies in Verbobonc City, Greenway Valley can serve as a base of operations beyond the Viscount's direct reach — but only if the Alliance trusts them enough to extend that hospitality.
*   **The Gnome Question as political lever:** A party that helps resolve the gnome crisis in the Alliance's favor gains leverage across every Council faction simultaneously. The Militarists get a military ally. The Economic Powers get their trade routes. The Moderates get a diplomatic victory. And the party gets a reputation as the people who fixed the problem the Council couldn't.

 

#### Adventure Hooks

 

*   **The Mine Claim Documents:** Commissioned by Wilfrick, Pithriggen, or [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) — determine whether the documents are genuine and, if not, who filed them. The answer implicates a Council member. The political consequence is a Council crisis.
*   **The Caravan Guard:** Jeet Jimbleclap commissions the party to escort a high-value gem shipment from Greenway Valley to Verbobonc City. The caravan is attacked by bandits who know the route, the cargo, and the escort strength. The intelligence leak points to someone inside the Alliance — or inside the Council.
*   **The Deleveu Dilemma:** Lord Deleveu needs information about the Borderer withdrawal surfaced without his fingerprints on it. He approaches the party through an intermediary. The mission is information delivery. The political consequence is a Council crisis over the Viscount's security decisions.
*   **Pithriggen's Dinner:** The "Prince" invites the party to dinner and asks what they know about agents of Iuz and the Temple asking questions about a gnome named Lysander. What Jimm reveals depends on how much the party has earned his trust.
*   **The Trade Redirect:** The Alliance announces it is redirecting gem trade to Dyvers. The Council panics. The party is commissioned — by Haxx, by Rhynehurst, by the Viscount — to find out what it would take to reverse the decision. The answer requires resolving the mine claims, restoring the patrols, and delivering justice for the caravan raids. In that order.

##### Why This Matters for Temple of Elemental Evil

The Free Alliance of the Kron Hills is the campaign's most consequential political relationship. The Temple does not need to destroy the gnomes — it needs Verbobonc and the gnomes to stop cooperating:

*   Isolated gnomes = no military ally for Verbobonc's southern defense
*   Redirected trade = no revenue for Council military spending
*   Unresolved mine claims = paralyzed Viscount, gridlocked Council
*   Withdrawn patrols = open roads from Hommlet to Nulb

The party that fixes the gnome crisis gives Verbobonc a fighting chance against the Temple. The party that ignores it gives the Temple exactly what it needs: a city too divided to act.
