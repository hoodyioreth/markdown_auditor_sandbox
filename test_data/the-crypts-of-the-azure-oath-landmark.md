The Crypts of the Azure Oath
============================

> The Crypts of the Azure Oath — the burial complex beneath the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), where over five centuries of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) laid their dead in horizontal stone alcoves cut into the living rock of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). The tombs are built in the old northern style — long galleries of shoulder-width recesses stacked three high along both walls, each alcove sealed with a carved stone slab bearing the knight's name, rank, and date of interment. [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s divine wards sealed each tomb at the moment of consecration, and most remain sealed to this day — not through mechanical locks, but through faith made architecture. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) has breached several of the weaker tombs, stripping relics and grave goods, but the deepest and oldest burials resist their tools. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has brought a team of duergar miners from the Underdark to dig where his acolytes cannot — driving a tunnel through the bedrock toward something far older than the priory itself: a megalithic Flan carving in the deep stone, a gaping maw known in the ancient manuscripts as the Black Mouth, which seals the geomantic node that the Azure Oath was built to contain. If the Mouth opens, what rises is not the dead. It is the earth itself.

_The Crypts of the Azure Oath — Burial Complex of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) — Now Excavation Site of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)_

* * *

### The Crypts Below

The [Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) was built to bury its dead. That was its founding purpose — not a fortress, not a waystation, but a sepulchre. A house of the dead on the Long Road in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), where knights of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who fell in service could be laid to rest with the full weight of the Arch-Paladin's rites. The monastery above served the living. The crypts below served what came after.

The burial complex occupies the natural limestone shelf beneath the priory's foundations — carved into the hillside over decades by the order's own masons, expanded as each generation of knights needed more room for their honored dead. The construction follows the old northern style that the order's Oeridian founders brought from the north of Furyondy and the Shield Lands: long galleries cut into the living rock, with horizontal burial alcoves stacked three high along both walls, each recess exactly six feet deep, two feet high, and three feet wide — just enough for a body wrapped in a shroud, a sword laid along the right side, and a shield placed over the chest. Each alcove is sealed with a carved stone slab bearing the knight's name, the Azure Oath's sigil, and a single line of dedication.

At the moment of consecration, the presiding cleric spoke the Final Seal — a prayer that bound the slab to the alcove with [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s divine authority. Not a mechanical lock. Not a physical barrier. A seal of faith — the same kind of invested divine energy that protects the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) altar above. As long as the faith endured, the tombs remained sealed. The dead rested. The living walked above.

For over five centuries — from the chapter's founding circa -12 CY through its withdrawal in 562 CY — the crypts received the Azure Oath's dead. Knights, knight-commanders, senior clerics, and lay brothers who served the order in life were all interred here. Several hundred burials in total, spread across the main gallery and the branching tomb-halls that the masons carved as the complex grew. The oldest tombs lie deepest — the founding-era burials near the chamber that the original builders called the Founders' Sepulchre. The most recent lie nearest the processional staircase, where the last six guardians in **AO-22** watched over the final interments before the order left.

When the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) withdrew in 562 CY, the crypts were sealed by faith and left in the keeping of one man: **Halven Sere**, the lay brother who would not leave. His prayers — daily, unbroken, for fourteen years — maintained the connection between the priory's consecration and the wards that held the tombs shut. The dead slept. **Halven** prayed. The years passed.

Then the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) came.

### What the Earth Temple Found

When [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) descended the processional staircase from **AO-22**, they found a burial complex that should have been easy to loot. The order was gone. The only guardian was a thin old man with a broom. The crypts were dark, silent, and undefended.

But the tombs would not open.

The divine wards that sealed the burial alcoves had not expired with the order's departure. **[Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md)**'s fourteen years of prayer had kept the consecration alive — weakened, fading at the edges, but structurally intact. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s men pried at the stone slabs with iron bars and the slabs held. They struck the seals with hammers and the hammers cracked. They tried to chisel through the alcove walls and the stone resisted as though it were ten times its natural hardness. The faith that built the crypts had become the crypts.

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) understood what [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) did not. The wards were not uniform. Some alcoves — the most recent interments, those nearest the staircase — had weaker seals, their divine charge diminished by time and the gradual erosion of the order's presence. Others — the oldest tombs, the founding-era burials deep in the complex — carried wards so dense that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) could feel them through his tremorsense like walls of compressed prayer.

Over weeks of methodical work, [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s acolytes breached the weakest tombs — perhaps a dozen in total, all from the order's final decades. They stripped what they found: swords, shields, holy symbols, signet rings, and consecrated armor that could be melted down or shipped to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) for resale. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) displays the finest pieces as trophies in his quarters above. The rest was packed into tribute crates.

But [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) was not here for relics. He was here for the stone beneath the tombs — the natural bedrock that predated the priory, predated the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md), predated everything that human hands had built on this ridge. His Flan manuscripts described what lay down there: a geomantic node — a nexus of elemental earth energy sealed away by the Flan people millennia before the Oeridians arrived. The Azure Oath's founders had built their sepulchre directly on top of it, whether by design or instinct, and the accumulated faith of over five centuries of consecrated burials had reinforced the seal that the Flan had placed there first.

To reach the node, [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) needed to dig through the crypts. And to dig through stone that resisted divine wards, he needed workers who knew how to cut through consecrated ground without triggering what lay beneath it.

He needed the duergar.

* * *

### The Duergar

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) contracted a work crew of eight duergar miners through contacts in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) — gray dwarves from the Underdark warrens beneath the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), led by a foreman called **Grimlok Ashpick**. The arrangement is transactional. The duergar work for coin, for salvage rights to any non-consecrated stone and mineral deposits they encounter, and for first claim on any Underdark access routes their tunneling might open. They have no loyalty to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md), no interest in the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md), and no patience for [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s doctrine.

They are, however, exceptionally good at what they do. Duergar mining craft is millennia old — refined in the lightless depths where one wrong strike can collapse a tunnel or wake something sleeping in the stone. **Grimlok** and his crew work in shifts of four, cutting through the crypt's natural limestone with precision that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s human acolytes cannot match. They have driven an excavation tunnel from the eastern end of the crypt gallery downward and south, following the geomantic resonance that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) maps from above with his tremorsense.

The duergar despise the human cultists. They tolerate [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) because he pays and because his geomantic knowledge is genuinely impressive — **Grimlok** has grudgingly admitted that the Flan geomancer "reads stone like a deep-dwarf born." They ignore [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) entirely. They consider the human soldiers above to be loud, clumsy, and dangerously ignorant of what happens when you dig in places that do not want to be dug.

The duergar have noticed things in the deep tunnel that they have not shared with [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md). The stone changes texture near the Black Mouth — it becomes denser, darker, shot through with veins of obsidian that pulse faintly when struck. The air tastes of iron and ozone. Tools left unattended overnight are found in different positions. And twice, **Grimlok** has heard something breathing on the other side of the stone — slow, vast, and rhythmic, like the earth itself drawing breath. He has said nothing about this. Gray dwarves do not share what the deep stone tells them.

### The Architectural Character

The crypts are built in two distinct styles that collide at the excavation boundary.

**The Heironeous Crypts** are dressed limestone — precisely cut, smooth-walled, with low arched ceilings and flat flagstone floors. The burial alcoves are the defining feature: horizontal recesses cut into the walls in rows of three, stacked floor to ceiling on both sides of every gallery, each sealed with a carved stone slab. The effect is unmistakable — walking the gallery is like walking between walls of the named dead, their stone faces (where the slabs bear portraits) or their carved sigils watching from both sides. A style favored in the north of Furyondy and the Shield Lands, especially notable in the crypts and the burial galleries beneath the temples of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) in Chendl. Functional. Austere. Designed for volume — the order expected to bury its dead here for centuries.

Ceiling height is eight feet in the galleries, rising to twelve feet in the major tomb chambers (the Founders' Sepulchre, the Champions' Rest). Corridors are six feet wide — enough for pallbearers to pass but not to fight comfortably. The flagstone floors were originally polished smooth, but centuries of damp and the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s excavation traffic have worn them rough.

**The Flan Stonework** begins where the Heironeous masonry ends — at the boundary of the excavation tunnel. Here the dressed limestone gives way to natural bedrock, and carved into that bedrock are forms that predate the priory by millennia. Megalithic. Monolithic. The tool marks are different — broader, less precise, made by instruments that modern smiths do not forge. The stonework is not decorative. It is structural — shapes that reinforce the natural stone, channel the geomantic energy, and frame the opening that the Flan sealed. The scale increases as one approaches the Black Mouth. The ceiling rises. The walls widen. The stone becomes darker. The carved faces that emerge from the rock are not human. They are older.

### The Wards

The divine wards that seal the Azure Oath's tombs are not spell effects — they cannot be dispelled by _Dispel Magic_ or detected by _Detect Magic_ in the conventional sense. They are **consecrated architecture** — the accumulated weight of prayer, faith, and divine investiture built into the stone itself at the moment of each burial. The wards exist at the intersection of divine magic and physical structure, making the stone harder, the seals tighter, and the dead quieter.

The ward strength varies by age and location:

*   **Recent Tombs (450–562 CY):** Weakest wards. Most of these have been breached by the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md). The seals degraded faster because these knights served during the order's declining years, when fewer brothers attended the consecration rites. **DC 12 Strength** to force open a surviving recent tomb slab. The dead within are at rest but not protected — disturbing them carries no divine consequence
*   **Middle-Era Tombs (100–450 CY):** Moderate wards. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has not attempted these — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) judged the cost too high for the probable grave goods. **DC 18 Strength** to force (requires adamantine or silvered tools — normal iron bends). Opening triggers a **Glyph of Warding** equivalent: 4d6 radiant damage (WIS DC 14 to halve) to the opener
*   **Founding-Era Tombs (-12–100 CY):** Strongest wards. Effectively immune to physical force. The stone slabs cannot be removed, broken, or tunneled around without first disrupting the ward itself. A cleric of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who speaks the Final Seal prayer (learned from **Halven Sere** or the prayer journal in **AO-15**) can open these tombs peacefully. Anyone else attempting to open them triggers a **Divine Guardian** response — a spectral knight (equivalent to a CR 3 specter with radiant damage instead of necrotic) manifests and attacks for 1 minute or until the intruder retreats beyond the tomb chamber

**[Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md)**'s continued prayers sustain the ward network. If he is killed or removed from the priory, all wards begin degrading — recent tombs lose their seals entirely within 24 hours, middle-era wards drop to DC 14, and founding-era wards weaken to the point where the duergar could breach them within a week. This is the primary reason [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has not simply killed the old man.

### The Named Dead

The Azure Oath interred its dead in order of date, but the most honored knights received placement in the dedicated tomb chambers rather than the gallery alcoves. The following are the named burials that the party may discover, investigate, or — depending on the actions of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) — need to defend:

##### Founding Era (~-12–100 CY)

*   **Knight-Commander Theran Valegate** — First commander of the Azure Oath chapter. Led the twelve founding knights who established the priory on the Long Road when the site was dedicated in -12 CY. Died of old age and was interred with full honors in the Founders' Sepulchre. His tomb slab bears the inscription: _"He drew the line. The line held."_ Buried with a consecrated longsword that radiates faint divine magic to this day
*   **Dame Seripha Dawnshield** — One of the original twelve. Fell defending a merchant caravan from a troll war-band in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Her tomb slab reads: _"She stood where the road was darkest."_ The only founding-era knight buried with her shield still on her arm — the **Dawnshield**, a +1 shield that sheds bright light in a 10-foot radius when the wielder takes the Dodge action
*   **Sir Brannock Ironvow** — Swore an oath never to retreat from any enemy. Killed a hill giant single-handedly on the Long Road but died of the wounds three days later. His tomb slab reads: _"He did not step back."_

##### Middle Era (~100–450 CY)

*   **Sir Aldric the Steadfast** — Served the Azure Oath for forty years without missing a single dawn patrol. Died peacefully in his cell at age 68. His tomb slab reads: _"Every morning. Without fail."_ The longest-serving knight in the chapter's history
*   **Dame Koressa Stormmantle** — Led the charge against an ogre war-band that threatened [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Fell with her sword in hand. Her tomb slab reads: _"Forward. Always forward."_
*   **Sir Draven Hollowmere** — Killed by a gnoll ambush on the Long Road. His body was recovered by his squire three days later and carried back to the priory on foot. His tomb slab reads: _"Brought home."_
*   **Dame Orenthia Vael** — Fell during the Hateful Wars (~498–510 CY), fighting alongside the allied forces that drove humanoids from the Lortmils. Her tomb slab reads: _"She marched when called. She did not return."_

##### Late Era (~450–562 CY)

The last six knights interred before the order's withdrawal. Their names are carved beneath the stone guardians in **AO-22**, and their tombs occupy the alcoves nearest the processional staircase:

*   **Sir Aldren Voss** — Fell defending a gnome delegation on the Long Road
*   **Sir Tormund Kael** — Died of fever contracted in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) marshes
*   **Dame Elara Brightholm** — Killed by bandit crossbow fire on the eastern approach
*   **Sir Gerren of the Long Watch** — Collapsed during a winter patrol and was found dead at his post
*   **Sir Pendric Hale** — Fell in a skirmish with Pomarj raiders near the ridge
*   **Sir Corvan Ashenmoor** — The last knight interred. Died of wounds after a lone stand against a hobgoblin scout party. His guardian in **AO-22** was toppled by the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) to make room for the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) altar

All six late-era tombs have been breached. Their grave goods — swords, shields, holy symbols, and consecrated armor — were stripped by [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and either displayed as trophies in his quarters above (**AO-18**) or packed into tribute crates bound for [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

### The Black Mouth

Beneath the crypts, beneath the limestone shelf that the masons carved, beneath even the natural bedrock of the ridge — something was sealed. The Flan people knew about it. They called it the **Ezrach Goron** in their old tongue — the Earth's Throat. The Oeridian settlers who came after them lost the Flan name and replaced it with their own: the **Black Mouth**.

It is not a metaphor.

Carved into the deepest bedrock beneath the priory — far below the level that the Azure Oath's masons ever reached — is a megalithic face. Forty feet from chin to crown, hewn from a single slab of dark basalt that has no geological business being in the limestone shelf of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). The face is humanoid but not human — broader, flatter, with deep-set eyes that are sealed shut and a mouth that gapes wide enough for three men to walk abreast into the darkness behind its teeth. The teeth are stone. The tongue is stone. The darkness beyond is not stone. It is depth — a vertical shaft that descends into the geomantic node itself, a nexus of elemental earth energy that pulses like a heartbeat in a frequency too low for human hearing.

The Flan sealed it. They carved the face not as worship but as a **warning** — the open mouth saying: _this is what waits if the seal breaks_. Then they placed ward-stones at the cardinal points around the face and left. Centuries later, the Azure Oath's founders built their priory on the ridge above — whether they knew what was beneath them is debated in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s notes. The accumulated weight of over five centuries of consecrated burials reinforced the Flan seal from above, adding layer upon layer of divine suppression to the geomantic pressure below.

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has read the manuscripts. He has studied the resonance patterns. He has felt the pulse through his tremorsense — distant, muffled, but growing stronger as the wards above erode. He believes that Stonefang — his enchanted hammer, forged around a shard of the original Flan seal-stone — can open the Mouth. Not break it. Open it. Like a key in a lock that was never meant to be turned from the outside.

If the Mouth opens, the geomantic node activates. The consequences are beyond the scope of this dungeon — the node's awakening would send shockwaves through the elemental lattice beneath the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), destabilize the underground aquifers, trigger seismic events that would be felt as far as [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) and the Gnarley Forest, and — most critically — open a conduit between the surface and the deep elemental planes that the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) could exploit. **Hedrack** and **Senshock** at the Temple would gain a new avenue of power. [Romag](romag-person.md) would gain a promotion. And the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) would gain leverage over every other elemental faction in the Temple's internal power struggle.

This is why they are here. Not the raids. Not the relics. Not the Gift of Beauty. The Black Mouth is the objective. Everything else is logistics.

### Zones of the Crypt Level

The crypt level is organized into six zones based on current control, condition, and threat level:

##### Zone A — The Descent (Earth Temple Controlled)

The processional staircase from **AO-22** descends thirty feet to the crypt level's main entrance. This zone is fully controlled by the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) — guarded, lit by carried lanterns, and trafficked daily by duergar work crews and Earth acolytes moving between the surface and the excavation below. Two rooms:

*   **AO-C01. The Processional Landing** — Base of the staircase. Duergar checkpoint and supply cache
*   **AO-C02. The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead** — The main corridor. The central spine of the entire crypt level, with burial alcoves lining both walls. All other zones branch from here

##### Zone B — The Looted Wing (East of Gallery)

The section nearest the staircase, where the most recent (and weakest-warded) tombs were interred. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has breached most alcoves here. Stripped, ransacked, and repurposed as the duergar's base camp. Two rooms:

*   **AO-C03. The Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch** — Opened burial alcoves. Broken stone slabs. Evidence of what was taken
*   **AO-C04. The Duergar Work Camp** — Gray dwarf encampment. Eight duergar miners led by **Grimlok Ashpick**

##### Zone C — The Warded Wing (West of Gallery)

The older, deeper tombs that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has not been able to breach. The divine wards are strongest here. Three rooms:

*   **AO-C05. The Founders' Sepulchre** — Circular chamber. The oldest and most honored tombs. Knight-Commander Theran Valegate and the original twelve. Wards at maximum strength
*   **AO-C06. The Champions' Rest** — Sealed tomb-hall of the order's greatest warriors. Middle-era wards. Sacred treasures behind divine protections
*   **AO-C07. The Knight-Commander's Vault** — Deepest tomb in the warded wing. The consecration anchor stone — the original ward-stone that binds the entire network — is set into the floor here

##### Zone D — The Ossuary & Eastern Exit

The eastern arm of the crypt complex, where common soldiers and lay brothers were interred in a communal ossuary. A passage cut through the hillside's natural limestone leads to an exit on the eastern craig face. Three rooms:

*   **AO-C08. The Ossuary of the Faithful** — Communal bone repository. Some remains disturbed by the excavation vibrations. Undead stirring
*   **AO-C09. The Eastern Gallery** — Passage through natural limestone toward the hillside exit. Originally a ventilation shaft expanded into an emergency escape route
*   **AO-C10. The Craig Ledge** — The eastern exit. A narrow rock ledge on the hillside overlooking the Long Road valley. Hidden by scrub and overgrowth

##### Zone E — The Wild Depths (Far Southwest — Creature Territory)

A cave-in during the duergar excavation opened a passage to natural caves in the hillside's western face. Creatures have gotten in. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has not bothered to clear them — **Grimlok** sealed the breach with a rubble wall, but it has been breached again from the other side. Two rooms:

*   **AO-C11. The Collapsed Passage** — Cave-in zone. Rubble, unstable ceiling. The breach to the natural caves
*   **AO-C12. The Vermin Nest** — Natural cave system. Infested with carrion crawlers and a phase spider that hunts in the dark

##### Zone F — The Black Mouth (Deepest Level — Ancient Flan)

Below the Heironeous crypts, reached via the duergar's excavation tunnel. The architecture changes from dressed limestone to raw bedrock carved with Flan megalithic forms. This is [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s primary work site and the dungeon's climactic zone. Three rooms:

*   **AO-C13. The Excavation Tunnel** — Duergar-cut passage descending from the crypt gallery into the bedrock. Fresh-cut stone, support timbers, track marks from rubble carts
*   **AO-C14. The Flan Antechamber** — Pre-priory megalithic stonework. Ancient carved forms. The transition from Heironeous architecture to something far older. Flan ward-stones at the cardinal points
*   **AO-C15. The Black Mouth** — The megalithic carved face. The geomantic node. The climactic encounter location. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s ritual site

### Creature Roster

The crypt level hosts four categories of threats:

##### Earth Temple Personnel

*   **Duergar Miners (8):** Led by **Grimlok Ashpick** (duergar veteran, CR 1). The rest are standard duergar (CR 1 each). They fight if cornered but prefer to use Enlarge and retreat into tight tunnels where medium creatures struggle. They are mercenaries, not fanatics — if [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is dead and the contract is void, **Grimlok** may negotiate
*   **Earth Acolytes (3):** [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s devotees. Low-level cultists (CR 1/4 each) who assist with geomantic rituals and act as runners between the excavation and the surface. They break immediately if [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) falls
*   **[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md):** The dungeon's primary antagonist on this level. If he escapes from the main level, he retreats here and begins the awakening ritual at the Black Mouth — whether or not it is complete. See his NPC article for full stat reference

##### Divine Guardians (Heironeous Wards)

*   **Spectral Knights (variable):** Manifested by the founding-era ward system when sealed tombs are violated. Equivalent to specters (CR 3) but deal radiant damage instead of necrotic and are turned by clerics of evil deities rather than good ones. They do not attack worshipers of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) or anyone carrying a consecrated Azure Oath holy symbol
*   **The Consecration Pulse:** Not a creature. An environmental effect triggered by disruption of the consecration anchor stone in **AO-C07**. All undead in the crypt level must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom save or be destroyed outright. All living creatures feel a wave of pressure and heat. All divine wards flare to maximum strength for 1 hour

##### Disturbed Dead (Undead)

*   **Restless Bones (Skeletons, CR 1/4):** Found in the Ossuary (**AO-C08**). Not animated by necromancy — stirred by the weakening wards and the geomantic vibrations from the excavation. They are confused, not aggressive by default, but attack if touched or if they detect the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s symbol
*   **Ghoul Pack (3–4 ghouls, CR 1 each):** Drawn to the crypt level through the Collapsed Passage (**AO-C11**). Feeding on the disturbed remains in the looted tombs. They hunt in the dark spaces between the duergar camp and the ossuary. The duergar tolerate them because the ghouls eat the vermin — an uneasy ecosystem

##### Vermin & Beasts (Natural Caves)

*   **Carrion Crawlers (2, CR 2 each):** Nesting in the natural caves west of the crypt complex (**AO-C12**). Entered through the cave-in. They have laid eggs in the Vermin Nest — if the eggs are not destroyed, a new brood hatches within a week
*   **Phase Spider (1, CR 3):** Hunts in the space between the Vermin Nest and the Ossuary. Uses its Ethereal Jaunt to ambush prey from the shadows. The duergar have lost one miner to it and now refuse to work the southwest tunnels alone. **Grimlok** considers the spider a bigger problem than the party, and may offer information in exchange for killing it

### Plot Threads in the Crypts

The crypt level is the convergence point for every major thread running through the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md):

*   **Varnor Earthcaller's Excavation:** [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s obsession drives the entire crypt level. His research in **AO-17**, his excavation maps on the stone bier in **AO-22**, and the duergar work crew all point here. If he retreats from the main level, this is where he makes his stand — at the Black Mouth, with Stonefang in hand
*   **Tharvok Stonehand's Looting:** [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s interest in the crypts is practical — relics to display, grave goods to ship to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), and proof of dominion over the fallen order. The evidence trail from **AO-18** (the stolen relics on his wall) leads directly to the Desecrated Tombs (**AO-C03**)
*   **Caidax Nightshade's Side Interest:** [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) has no direct role in the excavation, but the resonance stones in **AO-13** — the same Flan stones that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) uses in his geomantic rituals — indicate that the sorcerer has been studying the node's power for potential applications in the destabilization campaign. If the node activates, [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) may attempt to siphon elemental energy for the false-flag operations
*   **Halven Sere's Knowledge:** **Halven** knows the crypt level completely — every room, every tomb, the location of the consecration anchor stone, and the existence of the eastern exit. He will guide the party if fully trusted (holy symbol from **AO-15**). He also knows the truth about the Black Mouth — that the Azure Oath's founders _chose_ this site to suppress it. The priory is a plug in a hole that the Flan drilled and could not fill
*   **The Eastern Exit:** The Craig Ledge (**AO-C10**) opens onto the eastern hillside, providing an alternate entry or escape route that bypasses the fortified main level entirely. **Halven** knows about it. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) does not — the duergar have not tunneled east. A party that discovers the Craig Ledge can stage a crypt-level assault without ever entering through **AO-22**
*   **The Gift of Beauty Connection:** The Gift of Beauty — a diplomatic treasure sent to [Celene](celene-organization.md) only weeks ago — was seized by [Romag](romag-person.md)'s raiders and brought to the priory as spoils. It is stored in [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s quarters above (**AO-18**). The Gift has no historical connection to the priory or the crypts. However, [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) suspects — but has not confirmed — that an object of such concentrated diplomatic and arcane significance may interact with the consecrated wards if brought into the Founders' Sepulchre. If placed on the bronze brazier in **AO-C05**, the Gift's latent energy reinforces the Flan seal — an unintended consequence that neither the Gift's creators nor the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) anticipated. However, if the party recovers it from AO-18 and places it on the bronze brazier here, the Gift's concentrated arcane significance interacts unexpectedly with the consecrated wards: the twelve founder alcoves glow simultaneously, the ward on the Black Mouth intensifies, and the geomantic pulse slows from once-per-minute to once-per-ten-minutes. This is an unintended resonance, not a destined return — but the effect is real and powerful.

### Environmental Conditions

*   **Light:** None. The crypt level has no natural light sources. The duergar work by darkvision. Earth acolytes carry lanterns in Zones A and B only. Zones C, D, E, and F are in total darkness
*   **Temperature:** Cool and constant — 50°F year-round. Drops to 40°F in Zone F near the Black Mouth. The cold near the Mouth is not natural — it is the geomantic node absorbing ambient heat
*   **Air Quality:** Stale but breathable in the Heironeous crypts. Fresh air circulates through the Eastern Gallery (**AO-C09/C10**). Zone F smells of iron and ozone — a sharp mineral tang that coats the back of the throat
*   **Sound:** Sound carries. The crypt galleries amplify noise — combat in any room can be heard up to 200 feet away (Perception DC varies by distance: DC 10 adjacent, DC 14 two rooms, DC 18 three rooms). The duergar work in near-silence. The party will not
*   **Vibration:** A constant low-frequency vibration permeates the crypt level, strongest near Zone F. This is the geomantic node — pulsing at roughly one beat per minute, too low for most humans to consciously hear but felt in the teeth and sternum. Characters with tremorsense perceive it clearly. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) navigates by it
*   **The Ward Glow:** Sealed alcoves emit a faint blue luminescence — visible only in total darkness, just bright enough to see the outline of the nearest slab. The glow is the divine ward. It is comforting if you worship [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). It is unsettling if you do not

### Strategic Logic — Running the Crypt Level

The crypt level is not a linear dungeon. It is a decision matrix. The party's approach — and the consequences of that approach — depend entirely on what they know, who they have rescued, and what they are willing to risk.

##### Entry Routes

*   **The Processional Staircase (AO-22 → AO-C01):** The direct route. Requires clearing or bypassing the fortified Hall of Final Vigil above. The duergar checkpoint at the bottom knows about the staircase guards — if the guards go silent, the alarm propagates downward in 1d4+1 rounds
*   **The Craig Ledge (AO-C10 → AO-C09 → AO-C08 → AO-C02):** The hidden route. Only **Halven Sere** knows about it (or the party can discover it by exploring the eastern hillside, Perception DC 16 from outside). Enters through the Ossuary — bypassing all Earth Temple checkpoints but passing through undead territory

##### Party Objectives (DM Guidance)

*   **Stop the Excavation:** Kill or neutralize [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md). Destroy Stonefang (or take it — but carrying it has consequences). Seal the Excavation Tunnel. This is the primary objective if the party understands what the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is actually doing here
*   **Recover the Relics:** The stolen grave goods from the Desecrated Tombs can be recovered from the tribute crates in the Duergar Work Camp or from [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s quarters above. Returning them to the correct alcoves restores partial ward strength
*   **Strengthen the Wards:** Restore the toppled guardian in **AO-22**. Relight the eternal flame sconces. Speak the Processional Ward. Activate the consecration anchor stone in **AO-C07**. Each action reinforces the seal on the Black Mouth
*   **Clear the Infestation:** The creatures in Zone E are a secondary threat but a genuine one — the phase spider and carrion crawlers will continue to spread if not dealt with. **Grimlok** may offer cooperation if the party kills the spider
*   **Secure the Exit:** The Craig Ledge provides a strategic evacuation route. If the party is rescuing **Halven Sere** and other prisoners, this is the safest way out — down through the crypts and out the hillside rather than back through the garrison above

##### If Varnor Reaches the Black Mouth

If [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) escapes from the main level and reaches **AO-C15** with Stonefang, he begins the awakening ritual. The ritual takes **10 rounds** (1 minute). During this time:

*   The geomantic vibration intensifies — all creatures on the crypt level feel the floor shaking. Loose stone falls from ceilings. The sealed alcoves begin cracking
*   [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) cannot be interrupted by damage alone — he must be physically pulled away from the Mouth (contested Athletics check, DC 18) or Stonefang must be taken from his hands
*   The duergar evacuate. **Grimlok** orders his crew up the Excavation Tunnel the moment the ritual begins. They know what an awakening node sounds like. They want no part of it
*   If the ritual completes, read the **Node Awakening** consequences in the DM Notes section. This is a campaign-altering event, not a dungeon-ending one — the party survives, but the world changes
