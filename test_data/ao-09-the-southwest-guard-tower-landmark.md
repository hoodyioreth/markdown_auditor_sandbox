AO-09. The Southwest Guard Tower
================================

This tower once served as the primary defensive watch post for the priory, guarding the ascent path and gatehouse approach.

From this position, defenders can:

*   Oversee the entire cobblestone ascent
*   Provide missile support to the front gate
*   Signal to the chapel roof
*   Hold the final exterior fallback point

Under Tharvok Stonehand’s command, the tower is once again operational.

It is currently:

*   Manned during daylight hours
*   Actively used at night
*   Maintained more carefully than the rest of the ruin

This is the strongest tactical position on the surface level.

##### First Sight (Exterior)

The squat stone tower rises from the southwestern corner of the courtyard wall — thicker, heavier than the surrounding ruin. Its stones are darker here, wind-scoured and iron-streaked. Narrow slits pierce the masonry, like unblinking eyes fixed upon the ascent trail below.  
  
Above, broken battlements silhouette against the sky. Even in decay, it feels alert. The tower was not built for prayer. It was built to hold.

### Level 1 – Ground Floor Armory (Storage)

The base of the southwest tower was once the disciplined heart of the priory’s defensive strength. When the monastery stood fully staffed, this chamber held the reserve arms of the Azure Oath — spears stacked in ordered ranks, shields bearing the lightning sigil of Heironeous, polished helms resting above prayer-etched weapon racks. This was not a chaotic armory. It was ritualized. Every blade blessed. Every shield accounted for.

When the knights were withdrawn during the Long War and the priory diminished to a single custodian, the armory was emptied with solemn care. Most of the sacred arms were transferred to safer strongholds in Verbobonc or Larneystoe. What remained were ceremonial pieces and damaged reserves too worn to travel. Brother-Custodian Halven Sere preserved the room respectfully, keeping it clean even as it fell silent.

Now the chamber has been reawakened — but without reverence. Tharvok Stonehand has recognized its structural value and reinstated it as a functional supply vault. Spare armor, crossbows, and crates of sorted loot are stacked where holy shields once stood. The Earth Temple does not appreciate its history; it appreciates its thickness of stone, its defensibility, and its convenience.

##### Read Aloud

You push the heavy tower door inward, and the air inside greets you cold and close, thick with the scent of old iron and oil. The chamber curves around you in a ring of stone, weapon racks bolted into the walls where shields and spears once stood in disciplined rows. Most of them are empty now, their pegs bare and splintered, though a few mismatched helms and crossbows rest against crates shoved carelessly into corners.  
  
Your footsteps echo differently here — steadier, heavier — as though the room remembers the weight of armored knights who once stood ready within it. A faded lightning sigil is still carved above one rack, half-obscured by dust and stacked plunder. The steel stored here now is not blessed. It is practical. Ready. Waiting.

### Level 2 – Guard Quarters & Firing Level

This level represents the tower’s true operational heart. Where the ground floor stores weapons, this chamber houses the men who use them. The design is deliberate: a circular interior that allows rapid movement between arrow slits, narrow sightlines that command the ascent path, and enough living space to keep guards stationed here without abandoning their post.  
  
Originally, the knights of Heironeous would have maintained strict watch rotations from this level, defending pilgrims and supply caravans traveling the Long Road below. Now, under Earth Temple control, the same vantage is used to prey upon that road. The space is functional rather than desecrated — Tharvok values this position too much to allow chaos here. The room should feel militarily efficient, tense, and ready at all times.

##### Read Aloud - Guard Quarters & Firing Level

The spiral stair opens into a round chamber lit by narrow shafts of daylight cutting through arrow slits in the stone. Thin beams of light stretch across the floor, illuminating cots pressed against the curved wall and a small table scattered with bolt heads and hardened wax drippings.  
  
A steady wind sighs through the slits, carrying with it the distant scent of pine and the faint scrape of the ascent path below. From here, you can see it clearly — the long climb toward the gates, exposed and vulnerable. Crossbows rest within arm’s reach of the firing ports. This is not a place of rest. It is a place of watchfulness.

### Roof Level – Battlements

The battlements are the tower’s ultimate advantage. From here, defenders command a full view of the ascent trail, the courtyard below, and much of the western ridge. In the days of the Azure Oath, this height symbolized watchfulness and guardianship — a promise that no pilgrim or caravan would be taken unaware.

Under Earth Temple occupation, the symbolism remains — but inverted. The same vantage now serves ambush and intimidation. This is where signal fires are lit, where horns are sounded, and where the first bolts are loosed upon approaching travelers.

Unlike the lower levels, this space is not cluttered. It is sparse, functional, and wind-scoured. The height and exposure create tension. A Dungeon Master should emphasize how vulnerable one feels standing here — visible from afar, but holding the advantage of range and elevation.

##### Read Aloud

You push up through the iron-bound trap door and emerge into open air. The wind strikes first — sharp and cold — tugging at cloak and hair as you step onto the stone roof.  
  
Broken crenellations ring the tower’s edge, their jagged silhouettes framing the world below. From here the ascent path is laid bare, winding like a scar up the ridge toward the gates. The courtyard lies within easy bowshot. Beyond, the Kron Hills roll outward in muted greens and grey stone.  
  
Nothing approaches this monastery unseen for long. And for a moment, standing in the wind, you feel the weight of that truth.

##### **Sidebar: Guard Tower Response to Intruders**

The southwestern guard tower serves as the primary overwatch position for the ascent path. From the arrow slits on the second level and the battlements above, the guards can clearly observe anyone approaching the ridge trail leading to the monastery gate. The path winds along exposed rock and sparse pine cover, offering only limited concealment. Unless the party is moving with exceptional stealth or approaching during heavy fog or darkness, the tower guards are likely to spot them long before they reach the gate.

When intruders are sighted, the tower guards react quickly but methodically. One guard immediately signals the gatehouse by horn or shouted warning while the other prepares a crossbow at the firing slit or battlement. If the approaching figures continue toward the gate, the guards begin firing from elevation, using the height and narrow approach to their advantage. Their goal is not necessarily to kill immediately, but to slow and harass the intruders while alerting the rest of the monastery defenders.

If the attackers press forward despite missile fire, the guards escalate the response. Additional cultists from the gatehouse or courtyard may be summoned, and the tower defenders maintain suppressing fire on the ascent path to prevent a coordinated rush on the gates. Only if the tower itself is directly threatened will the guards retreat downward into the courtyard, sealing the tower door behind them and joining the defense of the inner complex.

**DM Guidance:**

*   The tower provides **three-quarters cover** to defenders firing from arrow slits.
*   From the battlements, guards gain **advantage on Perception checks** to spot movement along the ascent path.
*   A horn blast or shouted alarm can alert the entire monastery within **1–2 rounds**, depending on distance and ambient noise.

This tower is not merely a watch post—it is the **first line of warning** that the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath is under threat.
