ENTERING VERBOBONC - Gates, River, Sky, and Arcane Security
===========================================================

#### A City That Learned the Hard Way

Forty years ago, Verbobonc was open. Merchants rode through the gates with wagons creaking under wool and grain. Sailors tied off along the Velverdyva with little more than a shouted greeting. Spellcasters entered freely. Adventurers were curiosities — not a constant.

Then the Temple of Elemental Evil was unearthed.

Then came treasure-seekers. Then mercenaries. Then rival cultists. Then drunken evokers testing fireballs in alleyways. Then demons in taverns. Then blades drawn in Temple Square.

Verbobonc endured its own “Wild West” era. The city did not enjoy it. Now the gates are orderly. The docks are controlled. The skies are watched. And even magic itself bends to civic law. Verbobonc remains prosperous — but it no longer trusts easily.

> “Verbobonc is prosperous — but it is never careless.”

* * *

### FROM LAND

#### The City Gates

When the party approaches by road, the difference is immediately apparent. Guards stand disciplined beneath the stone arches. Clerks record names. Tax collectors tally goods. Mounted paladins observe without speaking.

This is not a frontier town. It is a city that remembers chaos.

##### Peace-Bonding Procedures

All entrants are peace-bonded.

*   Bladed weapons tied securely to belts.
*   Quivers sealed.
*   Hafted weapons bagged in burlap sacks.
*   Holy symbols of non-good deities bagged and tied.
*   Arcane casters have two fingers loosely bound to slow somatic casting.

Removing bindings typically takes:

*   **3–5 rounds** under normal conditions.

This slows street violence and makes sudden spellcasting difficult.

##### Additional Gate Measures

The procedure is calm. Efficient. Unyielding.

*   Paladins detect for evil at entry points.
*   Collectors assess taxes on visible goods.
*   Names and descriptions recorded (especially for armed adventurers).
*   Repeat entrants are quietly tracked.

### FROM RIVER

#### The Velverdyva & Nigb’s Run

The Velverdyva carries the bulk of east-west trade across the Flanaess. Baklunish goods. Dyvers merchants. Nyr Dyv capitals. Kron Hills silver and gems.

[Nigb’s Run](nigbE28099s-run-location.md) feeds southern commerce into the city from [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) and the [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) .

The river on the current city maps reflects reality — wide and navigable. Earlier depictions showing waterfalls were corrected. No serious trade fleet could survive that.

Verbobonc depends on this river. So it controls it absolutely.

* * *

##### The Rowboat Interception Fleet

Three hundred yards upstream and downstream (and 100 yards up Nigb’s Run), a flotilla of city rowboats patrol constantly.

They move quickly and in coordination — like ants around a larger creature. Each approaching vessel is intercepted.

##### Standard River Procedure

*   Large ships: approached by **two rowboats (4 rowers total)**.
*   Smaller vessels: approached by **one rowboat**.
*   Ropes attached to guide vessels safely to docks.
*   Currents managed like tugboats in a modern harbor.
*   Preliminary cargo assessment begins immediately.

* * *

### Passing Through Without Docking

When a vessel signals that it intends only to pass through Verbobonc — neither docking nor unloading cargo — the procedure changes, but the scrutiny does not lessen.

From a distance, the ship raises a colored pennant indicating passage only. The nearest patrol rowboats respond immediately. One attaches guiding ropes to stabilize the vessel in the river’s current, while another dispatches a signal horn toward the docks.

Within moments, a specialty assessment rowboat approaches — this craft carries a merchant assessor, two armed guards, and a clerk.

*   A specialty assessment rowboat arrives.
*   Merchants and guards inspect cargo.
*   Taxes are assessed mid-river.
*   Upon payment, the vessel is escorted beyond city limits.

Large vessels require:

*   The central span of the great stone bridge to be lifted.

* * *

### Docking in Verbobonc

This system has even exposed intruders approaching from beneath the water. If a vessel intends to dock:

*   Rowboats escort it to assigned berth.
*   Two guards and one **Barzac hound** board immediately.
*   All occupants are peace-bonded.
*   Cargo assessed for:
*   Taxes
*   Contraband
*   Security risks

Two Level 1 Paladins of St. Cuthbert patrol the dock district continuously. Their sole purpose:

*   Use Detect Evil.
*   Quietly warn guards if needed.

* * *

#### The Great Bridge Lift (CRITICAL DM NOTE)

Large vessels cannot pass beneath the Great Stone Bridge without the central span being raised.

This applies to:

*   Ships docking at Verbobonc.
*   Ships merely passing through.
*   Military vessels.
*   Foreign merchant fleets.

The lifting of the bridge is:

*   A visible civic operation.
*   A timed and coordinated event.
*   Guarded by dock authority and bridge engineers.
*   Watched by citizens along the quay.

The central section rises by counterweight and chain mechanism — massive, slow, and unmistakable.

During the lift:

*   River traffic halts.
*   Dock guards increase presence.
*   Paladins patrol the span.
*   Bridge mechanisms are guarded.

#### FROM AIR

##### Aerial Surveillance

Verbobonc does not tolerate unauthorized flight over its walls. Guards along towers scan the skies constantly.

Additionally:

*   The **Ragosa Tower at** [C2 City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md) **(155 ft tall)** serves as airspace command.
*   Two city-employed wizards (5th and 3rd level) patrol magically.

They are equipped with:

*   Raven and owl familiars for scouting.
*   Eyes of the Eagle.
*   A Lantern of Revealing mounted like a lighthouse beacon.

##### Law Regarding Flight

Flying over the walls is illegal. Only one warning is given.

Procedure:

1.  Guards shout a warning.
2.  If ignored:
3.  Alarm sounded.
4.  Archers engage.
5.  Wizards respond.

#### FROM MAGIC

##### The Dimensional Funnel Defense

After the Elemental Wars, paranoia became policy.

Verbobonc commissioned Greyhawk arcanists to design a citywide magical defense combining:

*   Forbiddance
*   Dimensional Anchor

The result: **Dimensional Funnel**

* * *

##### Dimensional Funnel (Abjuration 6)

The spell seals the city against extradimensional travel.

Triggers include:

*   Teleport
*   Plane Shift
*   Gate
*   Etherealness
*   Blink
*   Dimension Door
*   Shadow Walk
*   Maze
*   Summoning spells
*   Similar psionic effects

Extradimensional perception (like basilisk gaze) is unaffected.

Summoned creatures still vanish at spell’s end.

Dispel Magic cannot remove the effect unless cast at equal or higher caster level.

* * *

##### Funnel Mechanics

Multiple overlapping castings protect the entire city perimeter.

*   Each casting creates a 10-foot-diameter spherical “Funnel.”
*   Funnels cannot be placed inside solid material.
*   All extradimensional arrivals within the warded area are redirected to a Funnel.
*   Travelers feel themselves being redirected mid-transit.
*   A successful Will save or SR allows return to origin.

Material component cost:

*   **1,500 gp per casting**

* * *

##### Funnel Locations

Signs throughout the city warn against unauthorized magical travel. Funnels are placed strategically:

*   Guarded chambers
*   Certain inns expecting travelers (notably F1)
*   Secure civic vaults
*   Dungeon holding areas

Each Funnel is:

*   Guarded
*   Monitored
*   Staffed by a tax official

* * *

#### TAXATION & CIVIC AUTHORITY

Every entry point includes taxation. Verbobonc thrives on trade. And trade pays for walls, patrols, paladins, and arcane defense.

*   Land gates assess goods.
*   River vessels taxed mid-channel or at dock.
*   Funnel arrivals assessed upon emergence.
*   Magical cargo inspected.

* * *

#### DM CAMPAIGN APPLICATION

This system allows you to:

*   Create tension at entry.
*   Enforce political control.
*   Introduce dockside corruption.
*   Use Funnels as:
*   Adventure hooks
*   Ambush points
*   Secret prisoner transfers
*   Show the city evolving after Temple chaos.
*   Reinforce the scale of Velverdyva commerce.
*   Highlight noble power structures tied to docks and gates.

#### **The Lifting of the Great Bridge**

The horns sound first.

Low. Rolling. A brassy call that trembles across the wide waters of the Velverdyva and echoes against stone towers and timbered roofs. Conversation falters along the quays. Dockworkers turn their heads. River gulls scatter in startled spirals above the masts.

Above you, the great span shudders.

Iron chains, thick as a man’s thigh, begin to move with a grinding groan that seems to rise from the bones of the city itself. Dust sifts from the mortar joints as the central arch of the bridge slowly separates, stone parting from stone with deliberate majesty. The sound is not sudden but inevitable—a deep mechanical thunder, punctuated by the rhythmic clank of gears hidden within the towers.

Water slaps against your hull as the current shifts.

Guards in tabards lean over the parapets. A banner snaps in the river wind. Far above, silhouettes move along the ramparts, watching as the immense stone roadway tilts skyward. The underside of the bridge—timbers, iron braces, and counterweights—reveals itself like the ribcage of some slumbering giant stirred awake.

The gap widens.

Sunlight pours through the opening arch, catching on the spray as the prow of the merchant vessel glides forward. The captain barks an order. Oars in the escort boats dig deep, steadying the ship as it threads the narrowing corridor of light and shadow beneath the rising span.

For a breathless moment, the city seems to hold its breath with you.

Then the vessel clears the threshold. The bells ring twice from the tower—permission granted. Behind you, the chains slow. The great arch begins its descent, returning stone to stone with a resonant finality that echoes along the riverbanks.

The bridge is closed once more. Verbobonc watches.
