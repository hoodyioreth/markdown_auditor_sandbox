Jeet Jimbleclap
===============

### Thane

There is a fortress carved into the eastern ridge of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), and in that fortress sits a gnome who has been alive longer than most human family trees can be traced. [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) guards the only significant pass into [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), and Thane Jeet Jimbleclap guards the fortress. She has held this post through the Hateful Wars, through the rise and fall of the Temple of Elemental Evil, through [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md), and through every broken promise the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md) has made to the gnome people since. She is elderly by even [gnome](gnomes-species-1.md) standards — well past three hundred years — and her vigor for defending her people has not diminished by a single degree. If anything, it has sharpened. Time has stripped away whatever patience she once possessed for diplomacy conducted in bad faith, and what remains is a commander who knows exactly what her fortress is worth, exactly what the gnomes are owed, and exactly how many human promises have been broken since the last time someone asked her to be reasonable. For a Dungeon Master running the Temple of Elemental Evil campaign, Jeet Jimbleclap is the gatekeeper — literally and politically. She controls who enters Greenway Valley. She decides whether gnome military strength supports Verbobonc or withdraws entirely. And she is running out of reasons to keep the gates open.

### EXCERPT

 

Thane **Jeet Jimbleclap** is the commander of [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) and one of the three principal leaders of the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md). Elderly even by gnome reckoning — more than three centuries of continuous service to her people — she commands the 100-gnome garrison that controls the Greenway Pass, the only significant route into [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and the gnome heartland beyond. A veteran of [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) who personally led gnome forces to join the Righteous Host against the original Temple of Elemental Evil, Jeet carries both the military credentials and the institutional memory that make her indispensable to gnome politics and infuriating to Verbobonc's human leadership. She works in uneasy coordination with "Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md), the Alliance's diplomatic voice in Verbobonc City, and answers to Clanlord [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md), the patriarch of [House Tulvar](house-tulvar-organization.md). In 576 CY, with caravan raids increasing, mine claims threatening gnome sovereignty, and the Mounted Borderers withdrawn from the hills, Jeet stands at the center of the most dangerous decision facing the Free Alliance: whether to maintain the alliance with Verbobonc or seal the gates and let the humans face the Temple alone.

#### History: Three Centuries of Service

 

Jeet Jimbleclap was born in the mid-third century CY, when the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) were still contested territory and gnome settlements faced regular incursions from humanoid raiders pushing out of the Lortmil Mountains. She grew up in a community where military service was not optional but existential — every gnome who could hold a weapon trained to hold one, and the line between civilian and soldier was drawn by necessity, not choice.

 

Her early career was shaped by the Hateful Wars, the decades-long campaign to drive humanoid forces from the Lortmils and secure the western approaches to gnome territory. Jeet served with distinction — not as a general or strategist but as a field commander who understood terrain, ambush tactics, and the brutal mathematics of defending a position with fewer soldiers than the enemy can throw at you. The Hateful Wars taught her two lessons she has never forgotten: that gnome survival depends on gnome preparedness, and that allied promises of support are worth exactly as much as the soldiers who actually show up.

 

When the **Temple of Elemental Evil** rose to power in the 560s CY and threatened to overwhelm the entire region, Jeet led a gnome contingent south to join the Righteous Host assembling against the Temple. At [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) in 569 CY, gnome forces fought alongside human and elven allies to break the Temple's armies in open battle. Jeet was there. She saw gnome blood spilled for a cause that was not exclusively gnome. And she saw what happened afterward: the human leaders celebrated victory, divided the spoils, and slowly, methodically forgot the gnomes who had bled beside them.

 

That memory — not bitterness, but clarity — defines everything Jeet does in 576 CY. She is not anti-human. She is anti-betrayal. She supported the alliance once, saw it honored in the field and broken in the peace, and now requires proof before she will commit gnome lives a second time.

 

#### The Fortress Commander

 

[Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) is a masterwork of gnome military engineering — a mountain fortress carved partly into living rock, with massive stone gates that can be closed to seal the entire Greenway Pass. The fortress controls the only significant route between [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and the eastern lowlands of the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md). Whoever holds Kron Fortress decides who enters the Valley. In 576 CY, Jeet holds it.

 

Her garrison of 100 elite gnome soldiers is a fraction of the 2,500-strong militia that [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) can muster, but these are the professionals — veteran warriors equipped with gnome-forged weapons, trained in fortress defense, ambush warfare, and the rapid deployment tactics that gnome forces have refined over centuries. The garrison patrols the Greenway Pass, mans the fortress walls, and — critically — inspects every merchant caravan, diplomatic envoy, and adventuring party that seeks entry to the Valley. Nothing passes through the gates without Jeet's knowledge. Most things pass through only with her explicit approval.

 

This authority gives Jeet enormous practical power. She can delay merchant caravans for "inspection." She can refuse entry to diplomats on "security grounds." She can close the gates entirely if she judges the threat level warrants it. These are not theoretical powers — she has exercised every one of them in the past two years, and the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) in Verbobonc has been unable to do anything about it except complain through diplomatic channels that Jeet does not control and does not particularly respect.

 

#### The Kron Market Problem

 

Below the fortress, [Kron Market](1-the-kron-market-landmark.md) has grown into a three-tier commercial complex that Jeet both oversees and struggles to control. The upper level — the High Warrens — operates legitimate gnome trade: gem merchants, tool makers, alchemists selling premium crafted goods at premium prices. The middle level — the Tunnel Bazaar — is a gray market where regulations are interpreted loosely and commerce moves faster than inspectors can follow. The lowest level — the Back Alleys — is where the problems live.

 

The Back Alleys have become a vector for smuggling, black market goods, and — Jeet suspects — Temple infiltration. With legitimate trade disrupted by caravan raids and the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md)' withdrawal, merchants are turning to illicit channels. [The Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) has begun operating in the market's margins, and some goods that pass through the Back Alleys end up in places that make Jeet deeply uncomfortable. She maintains that "order is order" and keeps the Back Alleys from becoming a haven for organized crime. But with only 100 soldiers and the Tunnel Bazaar expanding faster than she can police it, she is fighting a slow defensive battle against entropy.

 

Jeet possesses a ledger — a meticulous record linking tariffs, bribes, and "lost cargo" to a chain that ends in supplies reaching a hidden enemy. She knows this ledger is valuable. She will not hand it over to anyone. She will lease access to it — under brutal, elegant terms that ensure whoever reads it becomes indebted to the Free Alliance. This is not corruption. This is gnomish statecraft: information is currency, and Jeet has been accumulating it for three centuries.

 

#### The Three Leaders of the Free Alliance

 

Jeet does not operate alone. The [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) is governed by three distinct voices, each representing a different approach to the same fundamental question: how do the gnomes survive in a world where their most powerful neighbor wants to absorb them?

 

**Clanlord [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md)** — The Patriarch. Eldest of [House Tulvar](house-tulvar-organization.md), seated in the village of [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) at the western end of Greenway Valley, Urthgan has presided over the gnomes of the Kron Hills for more than five centuries. He is the central authority — the one to whom clan disputes, trade policies, and military decisions are ultimately referred. Urthgan favors patience, negotiation, and the long view. He has outlived every human leader who has ever tried to annex the hills, and he expects to outlive the current one. His strategy is survival through endurance. Jeet answers to Urthgan and respects his authority absolutely — but she believes his patience may outlast the Alliance's safety margin.

 

**"Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md)** — The Diplomat. Full name Faljimm Nizgafal. Jimm is the Alliance's representative in [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md), attending the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) as a diplomatic observer with no formal vote and enormous practical leverage. He resides at [B6 Manor of Prince Jimm](b6-manor-of-prince-jimm-landmark.md) in the city and maintains stakes in several gnomish merchant houses. Jimm is unfailingly polite, extravagantly dressed — handlebar mustache, splendid attire — and is keeping a ledger of every promise the Viscounty has made and not kept, every patrol withdrawn, every mine claim filed without Alliance knowledge. He has demonstrated on a dozen occasions that his absence from Council sessions carries more weight than his attendance. Jimm is the bridge between Jeet's fortress pragmatism and Urthgan's patient statecraft. When Jeet wants to close the gates, Jimm is the one who persuades her to wait one more week. When the Council tests gnome patience, Jimm is the one who makes them understand the cost of the next broken promise.

 

**Jeet Jimbleclap** — The Fortress. Of the three, Jeet is the one most likely to act. Urthgan deliberates. Jimm negotiates. Jeet controls the physical chokepoint that makes the other two relevant. Without the fortress, the Alliance's negotiating position collapses — anyone can walk into Greenway Valley. With the fortress, every conversation between gnomes and humans happens on gnome terms. Jeet understands this power instinctively because she has exercised it for longer than most human noble houses have existed. She does not seek conflict, but she does not fear it either. She has seen what happens when gnomes trust human promises without physical guarantees, and she will not permit it to happen again under her command.

 

#### Political Relationships and Tensions

 

**Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md):** The tension between Jeet and the Viscount is visible to anyone who sees them in the same room. Wilfrick wants the Kron Hills under direct Viscounty administration — not out of malice but out of a genuine belief that centralized governance produces better security. Jeet regards this as the polite version of annexation and responds accordingly. Wilfrick has tolerated the gnomes' desire for independence, but Jeet tries his patience. She speaks directly, refuses to soften her positions for diplomatic convenience, and has an institutional memory that allows her to cite, by date and clause, every agreement the Viscounty has broken. Jimm Pithriggen intervenes to smooth things over when these two are in the same room, but the underlying disagreement is real and worsening.

 

**[Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md):** Jeet's relationship with the Lord Mayor is transactional and mutually wary. [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md) depends on gnome trade flowing through Verbobonc — gem exports, crafted goods, alchemical materials. Jeet controls access to those goods at the fortress level. Haxx needs the gates open; Jeet needs the caravans protected. This creates a functional dependency that neither party particularly enjoys. Haxx's Economic Powers faction on the Council wants the gnome trade routes restored regardless of the legal mine claim disputes, because [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md) revenues depend on them. Jeet is willing to cooperate on trade — but only if Haxx uses his political weight to address the caravan raids and mine claims. She has no patience for merchants who want profits without security commitments.

 

****Roland Galans** and the Merchant Community:** Galans and other Verbobonc merchants deal with Jeet through intermediaries at [Kron Market](1-the-kron-market-landmark.md). She sets the inspection schedules, the tariff rates, and the conditions under which goods move. Merchants who comply find the process efficient. Merchants who attempt to circumvent gnome regulations find the gates closed to their next shipment. This is not petty obstruction — it is the enforcement mechanism that gives the Free Alliance economic leverage over an economy that could otherwise simply ignore gnome interests.

 

#### 576 CY: The Crisis Year

 

In 576 CY, Jeet faces the convergence of four simultaneous threats that have pushed her toward the most consequential decision of her three-century career: whether to recommend that Clanlord Tulvar seal the Greenway Pass and withdraw the Free Alliance from active cooperation with Verbobonc.

 

**The Caravan Raids:** With the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) withdrawn from the Kron Hills, bandit attacks on gnome merchant caravans have surged. Goods are being lost. Gnome merchants are being killed. The humans promised security and delivered nothing. Every destroyed caravan strengthens the argument for redirecting trade to Dyvers and Ulek — cities that may be farther away but at least don't require gnome merchants to risk death on human-controlled roads.

 

**The Mine Claims:** Human lords from Verbobonc have filed legal documentation claiming ownership of gem and silver mines traditionally held by gnome clans in [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), citing a contested 412 CY land grant that the gnomes regard as fraudulent. These claims threaten the economic foundation of the entire Free Alliance. If human legal authority can override centuries of gnome stewardship, then gnome sovereignty is fiction. Jeet regards the mine claims as an act of war conducted through paperwork, and her patience for legal niceties on this subject is exhausted.

 

**The Borderer Withdrawal:** The withdrawal of the Mounted Borderers from Greenway Valley — following an incident where gnome militia, already on edge from the mine claims, confronted and killed several Borderer soldiers — has removed the one military force that provided overland security between the Valley and Verbobonc City. The Viscount pulled the Borderers entirely rather than risk further confrontation. Jeet understands that the confrontation was a mistake, but she also understands that the Viscount chose to punish the gnomes for defending their territory rather than address the mine claims that provoked the confrontation in the first place.

 

**Temple Infiltration:** Jeet suspects — with evidence she is not sharing publicly — that the **Temple of Elemental Evil** is exploiting every one of these fractures. The caravan raids are too coordinated to be random banditry. The timing of the mine claims coincides suspiciously with Temple resurgence. The Back Alleys of Kron Market have seen new faces, new money, and new supply chains that do not connect to any legitimate merchant operation. Jeet believes the Temple's strategic planners understand that separating the gnomes from Verbobonc is the fastest way to collapse the Viscounty's western defense — and she is right.

 

#### Personality and Roleplay

 

**Voice:** Jeet speaks with the blunt precision of someone who has been talking to politicians for three centuries and has zero remaining tolerance for evasion. She does not raise her voice. She does not need to. When she speaks, it is in short, declarative sentences that leave no room for misinterpretation. She will answer a question with a question only if she intends to make the questioner uncomfortable.

 

**Humor:** Despite her severity, Jeet possesses a dry, cutting wit that emerges when she is among people she trusts. She finds genuine levity in absurd situations — the kind of humor that comes from having lived long enough to see the same mistakes repeated by successive generations of humans who never bother to read the minutes of the last meeting.

 

**Smoke:** Her white raven companion is never more than arm's reach away. Smoke watches everyone in the room, tilts its head at liars, and occasionally makes noises that Jeet interprets as commentary — though whether the raven actually communicates or Jeet simply uses it as a rhetorical device is a question no one has answered definitively. DMs should use Smoke as an atmospheric tool: the raven's behavior signals Jeet's mood to observant players before she speaks.

 

**Decision-Making:** Jeet does not deliberate publicly. She asks questions, listens to answers, and announces decisions that appear to have been made before the conversation started. This is not arrogance — it is the result of having already considered most possible arguments decades ago. She can be persuaded, but only by new information, not by rhetoric, flattery, or appeals to tradition. Anyone who tells her "the gnomes have always cooperated with Verbobonc" will receive a precise inventory of every occasion when Verbobonc did not cooperate with the gnomes.

 

#### Temple Campaign Implications: Why Jeet Matters

 

For a DM running the Temple of Elemental Evil campaign, Jeet Jimbleclap is a strategic linchpin. Her decisions determine whether Verbobonc has access to gnome military support, gnome trade goods, and the western defense line that the Free Alliance maintains. If Jeet keeps the gates open and the alliance functional, Verbobonc has 2,500 potential gnome militia to draw upon and a secure western flank. If she closes the gates, Verbobonc loses everything west of the Kron Fortress and faces the Temple with a dramatically reduced defensive perimeter.

 

**The Temple's Strategy:** The Temple of Elemental Evil does not need to attack Kron Fortress directly. It needs to convince Jeet that closing the gates is the rational choice. Every caravan raid that goes unanswered, every mine claim that goes unresolved, every Council session where Pithriggen's concerns are dismissed — these are victories for the Temple, because they push Jeet closer to the decision that isolates the gnomes and dooms Verbobonc's defense. The Temple's operatives understand that Jeet is not the target — she is the mechanism. By attacking the trust between gnomes and humans, they weaponize her integrity against the alliance.

 

**Campaign Hooks:**

*   **The Fortress Audience:** PCs seeking entry to Greenway Valley must first pass through Kron Fortress and convince Jeet to open the gates. This is not a combat encounter — it is a negotiation with a three-century-old warrior who has heard every argument before. PCs who come with evidence of Temple activity, offers of caravan protection, or news that the mine claims are being addressed will find her cooperative. PCs who come with demands or entitlement will find the gates closed.
*   **The Ledger:** Jeet possesses a ledger linking tariffs, bribes, and "lost cargo" to Temple supply chains. She will not give it to the party. She will lease access under terms that bind the party to specific obligations — protecting a gnome caravan, investigating the Back Alleys, carrying a message to Pithriggen, or confronting a specific Verbobonc lord about the mine claims. This creates a multi-session quest chain driven by gnome statecraft rather than dungeon keys.
*   **The Caravan Escort:** Jeet will hire or request PCs to escort gnome merchant caravans through the increasingly dangerous territory between Kron Fortress and Verbobonc City. Success earns her trust and access to gnome resources. Failure — or evidence that the PCs prioritized other interests over gnome lives — earns her lasting suspicion.
*   **The Back Alleys Investigation:** Jeet suspects Temple infiltration in the lower levels of Kron Market. She needs investigators who are not gnome — someone the Temple operatives won't recognize. PCs can be recruited for this, leading to encounters with smugglers, Battirovka operatives, and potentially Temple agents operating under cover in the market.
*   **The Dyvers Question:** If gnome merchants redirect their trade to Dyvers, Verbobonc's economy suffers catastrophically — and [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md)'s revenue collapses with it. PCs might be sent by Haxx, the Council, or even Pithriggen to negotiate terms that prevent the trade redirect. Jeet's position: she supports whatever keeps gnome merchants alive. If Verbobonc can guarantee security, she'll keep the trade flowing. If it can't, she won't stop her people from choosing safer markets.
*   **The Gate Decision:** In the campaign's most critical gnome-related moment, Jeet may recommend to Clanlord Tulvar that the Greenway Pass be sealed. PCs who have built trust with Jeet over multiple sessions can attempt to persuade her otherwise — but only if they can demonstrate, with evidence, that the Viscounty has addressed the caravan raids, the mine claims, and the Temple threat. This is not a single Persuasion check. It is the culmination of a campaign-long relationship.

 

#### DM Notes: Running Jeet at the Table

 

**First Impressions:** When PCs meet Jeet, describe the fortress first — the weight of the stone gates, the precision of the engineering, the silent professionalism of the gnome garrison. Then describe Jeet: small, ancient, white-haired, with a white raven on her shoulder and eyes that evaluate everything in the room before she speaks. She does not stand for visitors. They come to her. The power dynamic is established by architecture before a single word is exchanged.

 

**Respect Is Currency:** Jeet responds to competence and honesty. PCs who acknowledge gnome grievances without making promises they can't keep earn her respect faster than those who offer grand guarantees. If a PC says "I can't fix the mine claims, but I can escort your next caravan," that is worth more to Jeet than "we'll make the Council listen." She has heard the latter a hundred times. The former is actionable.

 

**Smoke as Signal:** Use the white raven as a mood indicator. When Jeet is interested, Smoke leans forward. When she's annoyed, Smoke ruffles its feathers. When she's about to end a conversation, Smoke turns away. Players who notice these cues can adjust their approach mid-negotiation. This rewards perception and social intelligence without requiring mechanical checks.

 

**Long Memory:** Jeet remembers everything. If PCs make a promise in Session 3 and fail to deliver by Session 8, she will mention it. If they succeed at a task she assigned, she will reference it positively sessions later. This makes Jeet feel like a living NPC who tracks the campaign's progress — because she does. Keep a note card of everything the PCs promise or deliver to Jeet. She has her own ledger, and the PCs are in it.

 

**The Prince Jimm Dynamic:** If PCs have already met [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) in Verbobonc City, they will find Jeet to be a dramatically different experience. Jimm is charming, diplomatic, and indirect. Jeet is blunt, direct, and uninterested in social performance. But they are not in conflict — they are complementary. Jimm works the Council room. Jeet controls the pass. Together they give the Free Alliance both a voice and a fist. PCs who try to play them against each other will discover very quickly that three centuries of shared struggle produces a bond that outsiders cannot fracture.

 

**Scaling Her Impact:** For low-level parties, Jeet is an authority figure who grants or denies access and assigns missions. For mid-level parties, she becomes a strategic ally who shares intelligence and coordinates military operations. For high-level parties, she is a peer — a fellow strategist whose fortress and garrison are assets to be deployed in the final campaign against the Temple. Scale her engagement to the party's level, but never reduce her to a quest-giver. She is always a decision-maker with her own strategic calculus.

**Why Jeet Jimbleclap Matters to Your Temple Campaign**

 

The Temple of Elemental Evil's strategic planners understand something that most DMs overlook: the fastest way to weaken Verbobonc is not to attack the city directly but to sever its alliances. The gnomes of the Kron Hills represent military capability (2,500 militia), economic value (gem exports, crafted goods, trade revenue), and strategic depth (the western defense line). Jeet Jimbleclap controls access to all of it. Her decision to keep the Greenway Pass open or to seal it will determine whether Verbobonc faces the Temple as a coalition or alone. The Temple does not need to defeat Jeet. It needs to exhaust her patience — and the humans of Verbobonc are doing that work for free.

 

**Critical Questions for DMs**

 

*   At what point does Jeet recommend sealing the gates? Define your threshold: how many unaddressed grievances tip the balance?
*   How do the PCs earn Jeet's trust? What specific actions demonstrate that the Viscounty is changing, not just making new promises?
*   What happens if the gates close? Map the economic and military cascade: gnome trade redirects to Dyvers, Haxx revenue collapses, Hardheads shrink, Council fractures further, Temple gains ground.
*   Does Jeet ever take the field again? Under what circumstances would a 338-year-old warrior leave the fortress to lead gnome forces in battle — and what would that signal to the campaign about the stakes?
