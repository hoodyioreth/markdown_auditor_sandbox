G1-10 Gnomesberg Gnome Quarter
==============================

### A Hidden Gem in the City of Verbobonc

Gnomesberg is Verbobonc’s quiet engine-room of contracts, craft, and counted coin—an enclave built to resemble the Kron Hills warrens, yet grafted into the city like a living root. Its homes are **“Rents”**: shallow burrow-complexes beneath preserved trees and terraced earthworks, forming what feels like a serene park that happens to contain a district.

And yet… the park has gaps. Roughly **three in ten warrens sit vacant**, their round doors shut, their mushroom-lamps cold—folk gone back to the Kron Hills over politics and autonomy. That absence is felt in every ledger and every unclaimed workshop bench.

#### Historical Background

*   **Establishment:** Originally designed to emulate the gnome warrens of the Kron Hills.
*   **Migration Trends:** Recent years have seen a decline in population as gnomes return to the Kron Hills.
*   **Cultural Preservation:** Despite changes, the Quarter retains its architectural and cultural heritage, reflecting the gnomes' deep connection to their traditional way of life.

#### Entering Gnomesberg

The stink and clang of the city falls away as though muffled by moss. The streets narrow into winding earthen paths, and the stone gives over to turf and root. Round doors—oak-bound, iron-hinged—peer out from green slopes beneath old trees, each lintel carved with tiny flourishes: a snail, a coin, a laughing fox, a set of scales.  
  
Somewhere below your boots, a workshop hammer taps thrice, then stops—listening. A breeze stirs the canopies, and you catch the scent of malt, oiled brass, and fresh-cut cedar. A polite hush lies over everything, the sort that says: We have heard you arrive. Proceed… and mind thy words.

#### Habitat and Lifestyle

*   **Rents:** Homes are small, comfortable dwellings carved into the hills, blending seamlessly with the natural landscape.
*   **Community Design:** The area is laid out with minimal disruption to the existing flora, featuring only small dirt paths that meander through the district.

#### Geography & Approaches

**Borders & edges (play this on the map):**

*   **Nibg’s Run** forms the **southern** edge; a stone bridge ties the quarter to the rest of the city.
*   The **Velverdyva** lies **north**, giving Gnomesberg direct river commerce leverage.
*   The **western wall** looms close—towers, stone galleries, and watch-lines that make the district feel sheltered… and watched.

**What the streets feel like:**

*   Meandering **dirt paths** and stepping-stone lanes rather than broad cobbles.
*   Terraced mounds and retaining walls; burrow-doors at odd angles; hidden service hatches leading to counting rooms and workshop tunnels.

**District travel note:  
**Gnomesberg rewards slow movement. Shortcuts exist—gnome-height arches, half-hidden stair burrows, little “public tunnels”—but they are not given freely to strangers. A guide, a favor, or the right seal can halve travel time.

* * *

#### Culture: Polite, Precise, and Not to Be Gainsaid

**Gnomesberg etiquette in three beats:**

1.  **Courtesy first.** Even insults are dressed in silk.
2.  **Precision always.** A “maybe” is a trap; a “yes” is a binding thread.
3.  **Memory matters.** Who kept faith, who broke it, who paid late—this is remembered longer than bloodlines.

**Built to preserve heritage:  
**The quarter was deliberately designed to mirror Kron Hills warrens, keeping architectural and cultural identity intact despite living inside a human city.

**The vacancy problem (and opportunity):  
**With many gnomes returning to the Kron Hills, the district has empty rents, shuttered artisan rooms, and under-used tunnels. This creates:

*   Space for **new enterprises** (and new schemes).
*   A ready-made **smuggler’s geometry** (unused passages, forgotten doors).
*   Political tension between “stay-and-endure” and “leave-and-be-free.”

* * *

#### Trade Role: Where Coin Becomes Leverage

Gnomesberg’s reputation is built on **craft and clever production**—especially jewelry, metalwork, and fine toys—though the artisan exodus has thinned output in recent years.

**Trade habits (important for roleplay):**

*   Historically, gnomes prefer dealing with **elves, halflings, and dwarves**, keeping humans at a cautious arm’s length due to old grievances and political pressure.
*   Their power is less in loud wealth than in **contracts, credit, and information**—the quiet arts.

**Use this at the table:  
**If the party needs _anything_ that touches:

*   letters of credit
*   warehousing favors
*   river freight “miscounts”
*   guild introductions
*   discreet appraisal of suspicious goods  
    …Gnomesberg is the ward that can make it happen—at a price that is usually _not_ money.

* * *

#### Social Structure & Local Power

Gnomesberg is organized around merchant-lords, a strong middle class, and artisan circles, with leadership that’s more _influence-and-consensus_ than rigid hierarchy.

**Key figures to seed early:**

*   **“Prince” Jimm Pithriggen** — respected mediator in trade and politics; a public anchor for gnome interests.
*   **Jeet Jimbleclap** — charismatic, deeply versed in gnomish lore and law; a power-broker who understands where contracts become knives.

**The central political tension:  
**The quarter seeks greater autonomy in response to past conflicts and current pressure from the Viscount’s agenda.

Factions & Pressure Points for 576 CY Play
------------------------------------------

Use these as “district dials” you can turn up or down.

##### 1) Autonomy vs. City Authority

Gnomesberg leaders push for self-determination while maintaining profitable links to Verbobonc’s broader economy. The Viscount’s agents (and human guild rivals) will test every boundary.

**How it shows up in scenes:**

*   permit delays
*   surprise inspections
*   “temporary” tariffs
*   whispered accusations that gnomes are hoarding coin or aiding foreign interests

##### 2) The Vacant Rents

Empty burrows become:

*   clandestine meeting rooms
*   dead drops for thieves
*   safehouses for political refugees
*   hidden workshops for prohibited craft (counterfeit seals, altered ledgers, disguised cargo)

##### 3) River Commerce & Quiet Intelligence

Gnomes don’t need spies when they have:

*   freight manifests
*   dock schedules
*   debt records
*   guild arbitration notes  
    Information is currency, and Gnomesberg mints it daily.

* * *

#### Temple of Elemental Evil Hooks

Tie these into your epic sandbox without forcing combat.

1.  **The Counterfeit Seal  
    **A shipment moving through the riverfront carries a seal that’s “almost right.” Gnomish scriveners notice the flaw—an artisan’s tell. They quietly seek capable outsiders to trace the forgery without triggering city politics.
2.  **The Empty Rent Choir  
    **A vacant rent begins emitting faint hymn-tones at night—pipes? wind? something stranger. Gnomes fear it is a human trick to justify an inspection… or worse, a cult sign meant to frighten more families back to the Kron Hills.
3.  **The Ledger That Shouldn’t Exist  
    **Jeet Jimbleclap possesses a ledger linking tariffs, bribes, and “lost cargo” to a chain that ends in supplies for a hidden enemy. He will not hand it over; he will lease _access_ to it under brutal, elegant terms.
4.  **The Artisan Exodus  
    **A master toymaker vanished en route to the Kron Hills. Gnomes suspect kidnapping, not migration—because the workshop keys were left behind. The trail points toward hired muscle from another district… and a patron who prefers craftsmen disappear.
5.  **Polite Arbitration, Bloody Aftermath  
    **A human guild brings a claim against a gnomish counting house. It’s all smiles and contracts—until the party notices the same guild has been quietly buying lamp oil, rope, and pickheads in bulk.
