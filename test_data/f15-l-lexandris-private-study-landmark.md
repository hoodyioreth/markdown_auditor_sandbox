F15-L — Lexandri’s Private Study
================================

Lexandri’s private study sits above the Academy Library proper, accessible only by a narrow spiral stair hidden behind a locked oak door marked with a subtle sigil of containment and order. This room is not a place of spellcasting, but of **pattern, theory, and relentless contemplation**—the quiet heart of the Unified Theory of the Arcane.

##### Entering Lexandri’s Private Study

The stair opens into a tall, narrow chamber that smells faintly of old vellum, candle wax, and dry ink. Bookshelves rise from floor to ceiling along every wall, packed so tightly that not a finger’s breadth is wasted. Sheets of parchment are pinned with care to corkboards and iron rails, each covered in dense diagrams—circles within circles, spell sigils broken apart and reassembled like fragments of shattered glass.  
  
A long writing table dominates the center of the room, strewn with neatly stacked tomes, labeled scroll tubes, and rows of ink pots arranged by hue rather than color. A single high window lets in pale daylight, illuminating a simple wooden chair worn smooth by decades of use.  
  
This is not the workspace of a magician chasing power. This is the study of a man chasing truth.

#### Security & Access

*   **Locked Entry:  
    **The stair door is always locked when Lexandri is absent. Only he and select senior faculty possess keys.
*   **Academic Wards:  
    **Non-damaging but firm arcane effects eject intruders who bypass permission.
*   **Social Enforcement:  
    **Unauthorized entry here results in **permanent loss of access** to the Wrinkle Academy grounds.

Lexandri does not threaten. He simply _remembers_.

* * *

#### Contents of Interest

*   **The Fragment Codices:  
    **Annotated spellbooks broken down by conceptual function rather than school.
*   **Comparative Scroll Sets:  
    **Divine miracles, druidic rites, and arcane spells aligned side-by-side to identify common structures.
*   **Lexandri’s Master Thesis (Incomplete):  
    **A massive, ever-growing manuscript labeled only _“Toward the Whole.”  
    _It is not magical—yet.

* * *

#### Lexandri at Work (If Present)

If the party encounters Lexandri here, he is calm, courteous, and intensely focused. He does not rush conversation, but he _controls_ it.

He will:

*   Welcome honest curiosity
*   Test assumptions with quiet questions
*   End discussions immediately if he senses deception or theft of ideas

“Knowledge shared freely multiplies. Knowledge taken rots.”

* * *

#### DM Hooks & Uses

*   Lexandri may ask the party to retrieve a lost spell fragment to test his theory.
*   His notes could hint at **Temple of Elemental Evil** rituals behaving outside expected schools of magic.
*   A rival faction may seek to steal or destroy his research before it is complete.
*   A breakthrough here could subtly alter how magic functions in future encounters.

* * *

#### Tone & Theme

This room reinforces a core truth of the **Verbobonc Campaign Guide**:

_The most dangerous ideas are not shouted from towers—  
they are whispered over desks, late at night, by patient minds._
