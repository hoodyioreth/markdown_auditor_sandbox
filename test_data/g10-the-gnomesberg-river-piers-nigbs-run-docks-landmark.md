G10 The Gnomesberg River-Piers - Nigb’s Run Docks
=================================================

_“Where the Hills pay their tithe in silver, and the City answers in grain.”_

The great human piers of Verbobonc along the Velverdyva are wide, loud, and lord-owned. These gnomish river-piers are smaller—yet sharper. Here, cargo is handled by gnome crews and recorded by gnome clerks, under gnome seals. It is the river-mouth hinge that makes Greenway Valley’s wealth flow north and Verbobonc’s necessities flow south.

Core logistical truth: Nigb’s Run is navigable only as far south as Nigb’s Rest, where barges must offload into gnome merchant wagons bound through the Kron Hills toward the Greenway Valley and ultimately Tulvar, the Alliance capital.

##### What is The Gnomesberg River-Piers

The river air is sharp with wet wood and lamp-smoke. Here at the mouth of Nigb’s Run, the water runs dark as ink beneath plank piers braced with stout timbers—each beam marked with tiny chisel-sigils that look, at first glance, like harmless dock-scratch.  
  
Lanterns hang low, hooded against wind; their light pools upon crates banded in iron and stamped with neat seals—gem-marks, pick-marks, hill-marks—each as precise as a knife-cut. Gnome crews move with practised economy: a rope tug, a nod, a tally scratch, a quiet whistle… and a whole load shifts as if the pier itself had learned to breathe.  
  
A clerk in a floppy merchant hat watches thee over a ledger as if it were a shield. He smiles—polite as a prayer—and yet thou findest thyself measuring each word ere it leaveth thy tongue.

#### **What’s Here (Physical Features)**

1.  **The River-Piers (Three Main Fingers)**: Short, thick piers of plank and oak piles, designed for barges and shallow-draft boats. Each pier ends in a low hoist-derrick and a sheltered lantern post.
2.  **The Boathouse & Winch-Shed:** A half-buried, round-doored structure of stone-and-earth with an upper plank loft. Inside: spare oars, tarred rope, pulleys, and lockable cages for “high-value” loads.
3.  **The Counting Shed (Ledger Hall):** A cramped office with two desks: one for arrivals, one for departures. The true work happens in the floor: a sliding panel hides a sealed coffer-slot where “private notes” are exchanged between factors.
4.  **The Covered Sorting Walk:** A narrow roofed walkway along the river edge where cargo is opened just enough to be verified—never enough to invite curiosity.

### Trade & Logistics (DM-Useful)

##### Northbound (Kron Hills → Verbobonc)

*   **Silver and gems** from hidden mines, carefully disguised in mundane shipments
*   Fine gnomish craftwork (tools, fittings, clever mechanisms)
*   Occasional “diplomatic parcels” under Alliance seal (do not inspect)

##### Southbound (Verbobonc → Kron Hills / Greenway)

*   Grain, flour, oil, cloth bolts, salt, iron goods—city staples that keep hill-villages comfortable and loyal Greenway Valley

#### Politics at the Waterline

These docks are a boundary ritual. The gnomes **trade** with Verbobonc, but do not **yield**. Tulvar (the political heart of the Free Alliance) pushes autonomy, even as it needs the city’s markets. Tulvar

*   The leading families—Tulvar, Jillink, Keeleene, Tillien—each have interests tied to mines, trade, and secrecy.
*   The withdrawal of protections and rising raids inflame resentment, and every missing shipment becomes a political weapon.

### Temple of Elemental Evil Hooks

##### 1) The “Honest” Manifest

A barge arrives with papers too clean—perfect handwriting, perfect seals, perfect truth. The gnomes know: perfection is a lie. Someone is inserting cargo into the route.

**Clue:** the seals match a legitimate clan mark, but the wax is mixed with an odd grit (not river sand).

##### 2) The Missing Crate from Tulvar

A sealed crate from the Alliance capital never reaches the counting shed. Tulvar politics are sensitive; the Free Assembly will not ask human guards for help.  
**The party is asked**—quietly—to find it without shaming the docks.

##### 3) Bandits With Uncanny Timing

Raiders strike wagons just after offload at Nigb’s Rest. Somebody leaks schedules. The dock factor swears innocence—truthfully—because the leak is a _note exchange_ in the counting shed floor.

##### 4) The “New Merchant” from the City

A human merchant offers “better rates” for gnome silver—too eager, too quick. The gnomes smile and stall. They want outsiders (the party) to sniff the rot.

#### Non-Combat Leverage (What Players Can Do Here)

*   **Earn Dock-Trust** by handling a dispute with courtesy (contracts, witnesses, apologies).
*   **Gain Intelligence** via the counting shed “message slot” if they prove discreet.
*   **Secure Discounted Passage** upriver or a guarded wagon seat to Nigb’s Rest.
*   **Receive a Clan Token** (social key) granting access to certain gnome businesses.

#### Immediate Encounter Seeds

1.  **River Argument:** a human porter insists on loading “one last crate.” The gnomes refuse—politely. The crate begins to sweat strange brine.
2.  **Night Lanterns:** lanterns go out one by one; a silent skiff noses in under cover.
3.  **A Sheernobb Courier:** breathless gnome rider arrives—news of trouble on the Kron road, tied to Keeleene holdings

##### Gnomesberg River-Piers

*   Type: River Piers / Boathouse & Counting Shed
*   District: Gnomesberg — Gnome Quarter (river edge)
*   Waters: Nigb’s Run (mouth) → Velverdyva Trade River
*   Owning Organization: Free Alliance of the Kron Hills (local gnome houses through their factors)
*   Trade Partners: Verbobonc markets and farms; Greenway Valley townships; Sheernobb; Tulvar Primary Exports (to Verbobonc): silver, cut gems, crafted metalworks (often discreetly packed)
*   Primary Imports (to Kron Hills/Greenway): grain, flour, smoked meats, lamp oil, textiles, iron goods
*   Political Weight: the docks are a “sovereign seam”—gnomes accept city law at the waterline, and nothing further inland without contract.

* * *

##### **The People of the Piers**

Gnomes here look like well-fed hobbits of the hills: heavy-set, bright gem-like eyes, bulbous noses, varied hair colors, and an assortment of floppy merchant hats and dock caps. Their voices are mild; their judgments are not.

##### **Dock Factor: Tamsin “Two-Thumb” Keeleene**

A cheerful, quick-counting factor with ink-stained fingers and a laugh that never quite reaches her eyes. She represents multiple clans on paper, and none of them fully in her heart.

*   Face: welcoming, helpful, endlessly “reasonable.”
*   Truth: as a valve—decides what information flows.
*   Security Presence: Hill-Hands. Not soldiers—“laborers.” Thick-armed gnomes with rope scars and cudgels that look like mallets. They do not start fights; they end them.
