C10 Guild of Architects, Masons, and Carpenters
===============================================

The Guild of Architects, Masons, and Carpenters is the beating heart of **Verbobonc’s physical city**—the stone, timber, and sweat that holds walls upright and roofs aloft. More than a trade hall, it is a political body locked in quiet (and sometimes loud) war with arcane construction, fiercely defending traditional craft against magical shortcuts that threaten livelihoods, safety, and civic control.

##### Approaching the Guildhall

The street here smells of cut stone and fresh sap. Chisel marks decorate the very walls around you—intentional, proud, and left unpolished. The guildhall rises in clean, confident lines of pale stone, every joint fitted so precisely it feels deliberate rather than decorative. Heavy oak doors stand open, and within you hear the scrape of charcoal on vellum, the low murmur of argument, and the measured cadence of professionals who know the weight of what they build.

### The Guildhall Itself

##### Structure & Atmosphere

*   **Finely Worked Stone:** The building is a living demonstration of guild standards—no illusion, no glamours, only skill.
*   **Civic Presence:** This is where city walls, bridges, vaults, and noble manors begin as lines on parchment.
*   **Constant Motion:** Apprentices move between drafting tables, workshops echo with mallet strikes, and messengers arrive daily from City Hall and private patrons.

### Internal Divisions & Facilities

##### Design Studios

Long tables strewn with vellum plans, stone samples, wood cores, and brass instruments. Every project in Verbobonc passes through here—or is quietly resented for not doing so.

##### Workshops

Separate halls for:

*   **Stone masonry**
*   **Fine carpentry**
*   **Structural repair and reinforcement**

Each supervised by veteran masters who have _rebuilt_ sections of the city after fire, riot, or siege.

##### The Archives (Restricted)

Three descending sub-basements contain:

*   Detailed plans of **city walls**, **gatehouses**, **vaults**, and **private noble structures**
*   Locked records of _who built what, when, and how_

**Security Features:**

*   Mundane and magical traps
*   Master locks
*   A pact with **six gargoyles**, who guard the deepest archive level in exchange for food and protection C10 Guild of Architects, Masons…

### Politics & Current Conflict

##### The Anti–Stone Shape Protest

The guild is actively lobbying City Hall to **ban or regulate the spell** _**stone shape**_, arguing:

*   It bypasses guild inspection
*   It weakens long-term structural integrity
*   It destroys skilled labor markets

This has created:

*   **Open friction with the Silver Consortium**
*   **Cold politeness at City Hall**
*   **Quiet support among labor guilds and common citizens**

### DM Hook

If the party uses magic to alter stonework publicly, expect:

*   Guild scrutiny
*   Formal complaints
*   Possible sabotage of permits or inspections

#### History

*   **Founding:** The guild was established several centuries ago, following Verbobonc’s expansion as a trade center.
*   **Development:** It has played a crucial role in shaping the architectural landscape of the city, contributing to both its aesthetic and functional growth.
*   **Recent Conflicts:** The introduction of magical construction methods has led to tensions with city officials and a decrease in demand for traditional craftsmanship.

#### Relationships and Politics

*   **City Hall:** Currently strained by the [C2 City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md) due to lobbying efforts against magic use in construction.
*   **Other Guilds:** Generally maintains cooperative relations, especially with the miners and woodworkers for material supplies.
*   **Magical Community:** There is notable tension with practitioners of magic who offer alternative construction methods.

#### Motivation

*   **Preservation of Craft:** The guild is motivated by a desire to preserve traditional building methods and the livelihood of its members.
*   **Advocacy for Regulations:** Pushing for regulations that favor manual craftsmanship over magical shortcuts.

#### Using This Location in Play

*   A noble manor collapses—was it sabotage or magical negligence?
*   City Hall commissions a magically-assisted project against guild advice.
*   The party must choose between arcane efficiency and political stability.
*   Thieves, cultists, or Temple agents seek access to archived plans of walls or vaults.

* * *

#### Tone for the DM

This guild is not anti-magic out of ignorance—it is anti-magic **out of experience**. They have seen shortcuts fail, walls crack, and lives lost. Their resistance is practical, proud, and politically dangerous.

##### **First Meeting with the Guildmaster**

You are ushered beyond the counting hall, past brass rails and murmuring clerks, into a quieter chamber paneled in dark oak. The air smells faintly of wax, ink, and old stone. Tall windows admit a measured civic light, illuminating ledgers stacked with obsessive care.  
  
Behind a broad desk stands Sir Alderich Feldspar, his hands folded behind his back. He does not rise at once. Instead, his eyes move over you slowly, not as one judging arms or armor, but as one weighing value. Only when the doors close behind you does he incline his head—just enough to be courteous.

* * *

##### **Dialogue – Sir Alderich Feldspar**

**Sir Alderich:  
**“You stand in the presence of the Bankers and Lapidarys Guild of Verbobonc. Words spoken here are recorded. Debts entered here are remembered. Pray choose both with care.”

_(He gestures to the chairs opposite his desk, but does not sit himself.)_

**Sir Alderich:  
**“Now then… you are not merchants, yet you come armed. You are not petitioners, yet you request audience. That suggests opportunity—or trouble.”

_(A thin, measured smile.)_

**Sir Alderich:  
**“Which do you bring me today?”

_(If the party mentions loans, vaults, or contracts)  
_“Coin may be borrowed. Trust must be earned. Collateral… is always required.”

_(If they mention cultists, bandits, or unrest)  
_“Disorder is expensive. Those who create it rarely pay the bill—but those who prevent it may yet profit.”

_(He finally takes his seat, steepling his fingers.)_

**Sir Alderich:  
**“Speak plainly. The guild has no patience for poetry—only for results.”

##### **DM Guidance (Optional)**

*   Sir Alderich never raises his voice.
*   He reacts more strongly to **economic instability** than moral outrage.
*   PCs who frame problems in terms of _trade disruption, coin flow, or risk_ gain advantage on Persuasion checks here.
*   He remembers the party’s names—and will use them later.
