AO-18. Tharvok Stonehand's Quarters
===================================

_Knight-Commander's Council Chamber of the Azure Oath — Now the War-Priest's Command Quarters_

* * *

This was the room where the knight-commander of the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) held counsel. The chapter house — where reports from the **Long Road** were heard, where patrol assignments were drawn, where the order's battle standard hung from an iron rod above a heavy oak table. It was a room of duty, not luxury. Stone walls, a weapon rack along the north face, a single shuttered window, and the chapter seal carved into the lintel above the door. The knight-commander sat here not as a lord, but as a steward — responsible for every knight who rode under the Azure Oath's banner and every pilgrim who slept under its protection.

Now the room belongs to [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md).

The oak table remains — but its surface is buried under a crude terrain model of the **Long Road** corridor, built from clay, stone chips, and bits of salvaged parchment. Carved wooden tokens mark patrol routes, ambush sites, and caravan waypoints between [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) and Nigb's Rest. The battle standard is gone — replaced by an [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) banner of brown and grey that hangs like a pall over the room. Along the north wall, stolen relics from the Lightning Kneel tombs are displayed on iron hooks: a dented shield bearing Heironeous's lightning bolt, a ceremonial sword with its edge deliberately chipped, a paladin's gauntlet crushed flat. Trophies. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) does not display them out of pride — he displays them out of doctrine. Stone outlasts steel. The [Earth](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) buries all.

Against the west wall, tucked into a recess that once held the chapter's archive chest, sits a reinforced stone reliquary — iron-banded, warded, and triple-locked. This is the most secured container in the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). Inside is the **Gift of Beauty**.

##### Entering Tharvok Stonehand's Quarters

The door is heavy oak, reinforced with iron strapping. A single guard stands outside — Black Earth tabard, crossbow slung, watching the corridor with the bored patience of a man who has stood this post many times before.  
  
Inside, the room smells of clay, lamp oil, and something metallic — old iron, old blood, old stone. A broad oak table dominates the center, its surface covered with a crude terrain model: clay hills, parchment roads, wooden tokens marking positions along what you recognize as the Long Road. A brown-grey banner hangs from the back wall — the inverted triangle of the Earth Temple, rendered in mineral pigment on rough cloth.  
  
Along the north wall, weapons and armor hang on iron hooks — but these are not the room's arsenal. They are relics. A dented shield bearing the lightning bolt of Heironeous. A gauntlet, crushed flat. A ceremonial blade with its edge deliberately notched. Each one displayed with the careful arrangement of museum pieces — or warnings.  
  
In the far corner, a stone chest sits in an alcove, banded with iron and sealed with three separate locks. Whatever is inside, someone went to considerable trouble to make sure it stays there.

### Features of the Quarters

#### The War Table

The original oak council table — where the knight-commander once reviewed reports from [AO-09](ao-09-the-southwest-guard-tower-landmark.md) patrol and coordinated pilgrim escorts — has been repurposed as [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s tactical planning surface. A terrain model of the **Long Road** corridor covers its surface, stretching from [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) in the south to Nigb's Rest in the north.

Key details on the war table:

*   **Carved wooden tokens** in three colors: brown (Earth Temple forces), white (Verbobonc patrols), green (caravan traffic). Current positions are approximate but recent
*   **Red thread** marking the route of the **Rao Delegation** — the same route flagged in **AO-16. The Scriptorium**. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has been tracking their movement personally
*   **Clay hills** marking the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) ridgeline — elevation points labeled in cramped Common script
*   A **scratched notation** near [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md): _"L.G. reports garrison doubled. Window closing."_ — **Lucius Graeme**'s intelligence

A character examining the war table:

**DC 10 Investigation**  
The token positions confirm active raiding operations along the **Long Road**. At least three ambush sites are marked between the priory and [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md).

**DC 14 Investigation**  
The red thread marks a specific route — a diplomatic convoy heading south through [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) territory. Cross-referencing with notes in the **AO-16. Scriptorium** confirms this is the **Rao Delegation route**. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) was involved in planning the interception.

**DC 17 Investigation**  
Several tokens have been **removed and replaced** recently — the clay impressions don't match. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is reorganizing his forces. The new positions concentrate forces closer to the priory, pulling back from extended patrols. He's preparing for something — either a retreat or a consolidation against an expected threat.

* * *

#### The Stolen Relics

Along the north wall, iron hooks hold weapons and armor pieces recovered from the Lightning Kneel tombs below the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). These are not looted equipment — they are **displayed**, arranged with deliberate care. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) treats them as doctrinal exhibits: proof that even the mightiest servants of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) are eventually claimed by stone.

The relics:

*   **Shield of the Third Vigil** — A battered kite shield bearing the lightning bolt of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md), dented but intact. Faint divine residue lingers (Detect Magic: abjuration). If returned to a temple of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) and reconsecrated, it functions as a **+1 shield** — but only for a character who has sworn an oath in Heironeous's name
*   **Crushed Gauntlet of Ser Aldric Venn** — Flattened deliberately, the name still legible on the wrist band. A character who carries this into the crypts and finds Ser Aldric's tomb may receive a **minor blessing** (DM's discretion — advantage on one saving throw against earth-based magic)
*   **Notched Ceremonial Blade** — A longsword with its edge deliberately chipped in three places. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) performed the notching himself as a ritual act — one chip for each century the Azure Oath held the priory. Religion DC 14: the three notches mirror an [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) desecration rite called the **Grinding**, symbolizing the erosion of faith by time
*   **Paladin's Oath-Ring** — A heavy iron ring inscribed with the Azure Oath's founding vow. Bent but not broken. A paladin of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) who wears this ring in the crypts below feels a faint warmth — the dead knights recognize one of their own

**DC 12 Religion**  
These are genuine relics of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) — not reproductions, not battlefield salvage. They were removed from consecrated tombs. A cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) recognizes this immediately and may feel anger, grief, or both.

**DC 16 History or Religion**  
The names inscribed on these relics can be cross-referenced with the burial registry in **AO-16**. These knights served between 320 and 480 CY — the Azure Oath's middle period. Their tombs have been opened and looted. The relics here represent only what [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) chose to keep as trophies; far more was stripped for raw materials or sent to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

#### The Gift of Beauty

In a stone-lined alcove against the west wall — where the chapter's archive chest once held the order's correspondence — sits a reinforced stone reliquary. Iron bands circle it at three points. Three separate locks secure the lid: one keyed to [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), one keyed to [Romag](romag-person.md) (delivered by **Lucius Graeme**), and one sealed with an [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) divine ward. This is not storage — this is a **vault**.

Inside is the **Gift of Beauty** — the sacred diplomatic artifact stolen from the Veluna Rao Delegation en route to Celene. Its theft was the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s greatest coup: an act that simultaneously disrupted a critical alliance and proved the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)'s reach extended far beyond [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

**Opening the Reliquary:**

*   **Lock 1 (Tharvok's key):** Thieves' Tools DC 18, or found on [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s person (iron key on a leather cord around his neck)
*   **Lock 2 (Romag's key):** Thieves' Tools DC 18. The key is not in the priory — it's at the Greater Temple with [Romag](romag-person.md). **Lucius Graeme** carries a wax impression but not the key itself
*   **Lock 3 (Divine ward):** Dispel Magic DC 15, or a character attuned to Earth Temple magic can touch the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) symbol and speak the opening invocation (available in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s notes in **AO-17**)
*   **Brute Force:** AC 20, HP 60, resistance to all damage except radiant. Breaking it triggers a **thunderwave** effect (Con DC 14, 2d8 thunder, pushed 10 ft) — audible throughout the north wing

**The Gift of Beauty itself:** A DM-defined artifact of extraordinary diplomatic value. Its exact nature depends on campaign needs, but its significance is clear: returning it to the Rao Delegation or Veluna could restore the stalled alliance with Celene, while its continued absence fuels suspicion and division between nations. It is the single most valuable object in the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) — and everyone in the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) hierarchy knows it.

**Faction tension over the Gift:**

*   [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) believes it should be **crushed ceremonially** — ground into dust as a demonstration of Earth Temple doctrine. Stone outlasts beauty
*   **Lucius Graeme** advises **patience** — the Gift has more value as a bargaining chip than as rubble
*   [Romag](romag-person.md) has not decided. The delay frustrates [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)
*   **Lareth the Beautiful** has been maneuvering to claim it. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) considers this the greatest present threat — not adventurers, not Verbobonc patrols, but the ambition of a rival temple operative

* * *

#### The Locked Strongbox

Beneath the war table — pushed against the table leg like an afterthought — sits a heavy iron strongbox. This is the **tribute chest**: raiding proceeds ready for shipment to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

**Thieves' Tools DC 15** to open, or [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s key (same iron ring as the reliquary key).

Contents:

*   180 gp in mixed coin (Verbobonc minted, some [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) gnome-stamps)
*   A pouch of uncut gemstones (6 gems, total value approximately 250 gp)
*   A sealed oilskin packet containing **two letters from [Romag](romag-person.md)** — tribute demands with specific quotas and deadlines
*   [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s **personal seal** — a stone stamp bearing the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) triangle over an anvil. This seal is required to authorize prisoner transfers, supply shipments, and communications sent to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) under [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s authority

**Campaign Note:** [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s personal seal can be used to forge orders — including a **prisoner release for **Halven Sere**** in **AO-20**. A character with proficiency in Forgery Kits (or Deception DC 18) can draft a convincing transfer order that the guards in **AO-20** will accept without question. This creates an alternative rescue path that doesn't require combat.

#### Tharvok's Personal Effects

The room's furnishings are sparse. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) sleeps on a stone slab — not because there isn't a bed available, but because he considers it doctrinally appropriate. A Black Earth faithful sleeps on stone.

*   **Stone sleeping slab** with a single rough wool blanket. A leather satchel beneath contains 40 gp, a whetstone, and a Flan bone talisman (50 gp) gifted by [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) as a gesture of professional respect
*   **Earth Temple prayer beads** — stone spheres on a leather cord, each carved with the inverted triangle. Hanging from a wall hook beside the banner
*   **A sealed letter** addressed to [Romag](romag-person.md), unsent. In [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s hand: a report on garrison readiness, supply levels, and a terse paragraph about **Lareth the Beautiful**'s emissary who visited three tendays ago "asking questions about the reliquary that were not his to ask." [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) requests [Romag](romag-person.md)'s guidance on how far to resist if Lareth sends a formal claim
*   **Duty roster scratched on the wall** beside the door — guard assignments for the north wing (this room, **AO-17**, **AO-20**), rotations, and standing orders. A character who studies this (Investigation DC 12) learns the guard shift schedule for the prisoner holding area (**AO-20**)

### Encounter: Tharvok Stonehand

[Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is present in his quarters during most of the day — reviewing the war table, corresponding with [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), or sitting in meditation on his stone slab with his eyes closed and his palms flat on the floor, feeling the building's vibrations. He leaves for inspection rounds (morning and evening) and for ritual observances in [AO-14. The Chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md).

One **Black Earth Guard** is posted outside the door at all times. The guard will challenge anyone approaching who is not [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md), [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md), [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md), or **Lucius Graeme**.

#### Running Tharvok

[Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is not a ranting villain. He doesn't monologue. He speaks in short, declarative sentences that land like stones dropping into still water. His presence is **oppressive** — not through threat, but through absolute certainty. He has never doubted the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s doctrine. He has never hesitated to execute [Romag](romag-person.md)'s orders. He does not see himself as evil — he sees himself as inevitable.

_"Men build. The earth buries."_

Dialogue branches:

*   **If confronted with hostility:** He is not surprised. He expected this — not today specifically, but eventually. _"You've come. I wondered when."_ He stands slowly, draws his weapon, and fights without wasted motion. He does not call for help. The guard outside will hear the combat and raise the alarm independently
*   **If the party attempts to talk:** He listens. He is patient. He will answer questions about the garrison, the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s purpose, even [Romag](romag-person.md)'s vision — but only if the party demonstrates strength worth respecting. Persuasion DC 16 to engage him in extended conversation. He does not lie. He simply does not consider the party's perspective relevant
*   **If asked about **Halven Sere**:** His jaw tightens. _"The custodian is secure. He is treated according to his station — a prisoner of war, not a slave."_ Insight DC 14: [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has a grudging respect for **Halven Sere**'s stubbornness. He will not volunteer the prisoner's location, but he won't pretend the man doesn't exist
*   **If asked about the Gift of Beauty:** His expression hardens. _"That is not your concern. It is not **my** concern. It belongs to the Temple."_ This is the one subject where [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s discipline shows cracks — he **wants** to destroy it and is frustrated that [Romag](romag-person.md) hasn't given the order. Insight DC 16 reveals the frustration
*   **If the party mentions Lareth:** The temperature in the room drops. _"That name is not welcome here."_ [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) despises **Lareth the Beautiful** with a cold, doctrinal hatred — the Air Temple's agent is everything [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) considers spiritually hollow. A clever party can exploit this rivalry
*   **If offered surrender or terms:** _"I do not surrender to wind."_ Inflexible. He will fight to the death or until incapacitated. He does not recognize the authority of anyone outside the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) hierarchy to offer terms

#### Combat: Tharvok Stonehand

[Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) fights methodically. He does not pursue. He does not overextend. He makes the ground fight for him.

**Round 1 — Cripple Movement:**  
[Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) casts **Earth Tremor** centered on the door — difficult terrain across half the room, Con DC 14 or prone for anyone within 10 feet. He uses the terrain model as improvised cover, overturning the heavy oak table (half cover).

**Round 2 — Divide:**  
**Maximilian's Earthen Grasp** on the most dangerous melee fighter — restrained, taking 2d6 bludgeoning per round. He engages the closest target with his weapon while maintaining concentration.

**Round 3 — Control:**  
If an enemy is airborne or tries to escape vertically: **Earthbind**. Otherwise, continued melee. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) uses his **Unyielding** reaction to remain standing when hit with knockback effects. He cannot be knocked prone.

**Round 4+ — Endure:**  
[Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) does not retreat. He fights until he falls. If reduced to half HP, he fights harder — his attacks become more aggressive but no less disciplined. He anchors to the reliquary alcove, forcing the party to come to him through difficult terrain.

**Tremorsense:** [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has tremorsense within stone corridors of the priory — invisible or hidden enemies who are standing on the stone floor are detected automatically within 30 feet.

**The Guard Outside:** The Black Earth Guard at the door joins combat on Round 2 (one round to assess and enter). If the party dealt with the guard first, [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) knows — he felt the vibrations through the floor.

### DM Notes

#### The Boss Room

AO-18 is the **command center** of the priory garrison. Not the spiritual heart (that's [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) in **AO-17**), not the intelligence hub (that's **AO-16** and [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md)), but the **military command**. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is the highest-ranking combatant in the building. Defeating him effectively decapitates the garrison — the remaining forces lose their coordinating authority. [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) can still direct her raiders, and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) will continue his excavation, but the unified command structure dies with [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md).

This should **feel** like a boss encounter. The room is small, the terrain becomes treacherous, and [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) fights with the grim patience of a man who has been waiting for exactly this. He is the hardest fight on the main level.

#### The Gift of Beauty — Campaign Object

The Gift of Beauty is the **campaign-level prize** of the priory. Its recovery changes the political landscape of southern Verbobonc. But getting it out presents cascading problems:

*   **Opening the reliquary takes time.** Three locks plus a ward. The party cannot grab it and run — they need tools, skills, or brute force, and brute force alerts the north wing
*   **Its absence will be noticed immediately.** Every [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) operative in the priory knows the Gift is here. If the party takes it, the pursuit begins the moment it's discovered missing
*   **Carrying it changes the campaign.** The party becomes a target for the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md), **Lareth the Beautiful**, and potentially other factions who want the Gift for their own purposes. It's a magnet for trouble
*   **Returning it is an adventure in itself.** Who do they give it to? Veluna? The Viscounty? The elves of Celene directly? Each choice has political consequences

Let the party decide. Don't push them toward taking it or leaving it. The decision — and its consequences — should be theirs.

#### Tharvok's Seal — The Quiet Key

[Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s personal seal in the strongbox may be the most tactically useful item in the room — more immediately useful than the Gift itself. With the seal, the party can:

*   Forge a prisoner transfer order to extract **Halven Sere** from **AO-20** without combat
*   Forge supply orders to redirect shipments or create confusion
*   Authenticate false communications to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), potentially buying time before the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) realizes the priory is compromised

Don't point this out. Let the party recognize the seal's value when they encounter the prisoner holding area and realize they have the authority stamp that opens doors.

#### Sacred Layer — The Chapter House

The original chapter house was where the knight-commander heard reports, assigned patrols, and made the decisions that kept the **Long Road** safe. It was the operational heart of the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) — a room where duty was administered, not celebrated.

[Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has replaced the purpose without changing the function. He administers duty from the same table, reviews reports from the same position, and commands the same building. The desecration here isn't destruction — it's **usurpation**. The room still works exactly as it was designed to work. It just serves a different master. That's the horror: the infrastructure of good is **perfectly suited** to the infrastructure of evil. The transition required almost no renovation.

#### Connecting to the Prisoner

**Halven Sere** is held in **AO-20** — close enough that [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) can have the custodian brought to his quarters for interrogation. Evidence of this is present: a second chair facing the stone slab (not the table), positioned for conversation, not comfort. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) interrogates **Halven Sere** personally — not about the **Long Road**, but about the priory's **secrets**. The altar. The crypt seals. The prayer-wards that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is trying to break. **Halven Sere** has been refusing to answer for months — and [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has not resorted to torture, because he considers torture inefficient. He simply waits. The earth is patient.

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I examine the war table."** — Investigation DC 10 (active raiding ops confirmed), DC 14 (Rao Delegation route + Tharvok involvement), DC 17 (force repositioning — consolidation or expected threat)
*   **"I look at the relics on the wall."** — Religion DC 12 (genuine Azure Oath relics from consecrated tombs), DC 16 (names match burial registry in AO-16, tombs have been looted). Each relic has individual properties
*   **"I try to open the stone reliquary."** — Three locks (DC 18, DC 18, Dispel DC 15 or Earth Temple invocation). Brute force: AC 20, HP 60, resistance except radiant — thunderwave trap on break
*   **"I open the strongbox."** — Thieves' Tools DC 15. Contains 180 gp, gems (250 gp), Romag's tribute letters, and Tharvok's personal seal
*   **"I read the letters."** — No check. Romag's tribute demands + Tharvok's unsent report on Lareth's emissary. Campaign intelligence
*   **"I take Tharvok's seal."** — No check. Its value becomes apparent in AO-20 (forged prisoner transfer) or when communicating with Nulb
*   **"I check the duty roster on the wall."** — Investigation DC 12 reveals guard schedules for AO-17, AO-18, and AO-20. Tells the party when the prisoner holding area is at minimum guard strength
*   **"I fight Tharvok."** — See Combat section. Boss encounter. Earth Tremor Rd 1, Earthen Grasp Rd 2, Earthbind Rd 3, fights to the death
*   **"I try to talk to Tharvok."** — See Running Tharvok above. Persuasion DC 16 for extended conversation. He does not lie. He is inflexible on surrender. Lareth is a pressure point
*   **"I take the relics."** — No check. Combined value approximately 350 gp if sold, but their true value is narrative: returning them to Heironeous temples or the crypts below

##### Tharvok's Unsent Letter

_Found in the sealed oilskin beneath the sleeping slab. Addressed to [Romag](romag-person.md), never sent._

**Report — Garrison Readiness, Outpost Azure**

_Master [Romag](romag-person.md),_

_Garrison strength holds. Thirty-eight under arms, adequate for current operations. [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) reports the **Long Road** corridor is productive but increasingly watched. Verbobonc patrols have doubled south of the Imeryds Run crossing. She recommends shifting operations to night ambush only. I have approved this for the current cycle._

* * *

_[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) continues his work below. He claims progress. I hear his hammers at night and feel the floor respond. I trust his devotion. I do not trust his timeline._

* * *

_The custodian remains uncooperative. He will not speak of the altar, the seals, or the crypt wards. I have not pressed the matter physically. He is more useful whole than broken, and his silence tells me as much as his words might — there is something below worth protecting. I will be patient._

* * *

_A matter requiring your guidance: three tendays ago, an emissary arrived bearing credentials from **Lareth**. He asked to inspect the reliquary. I refused. He asked to see the Gift. I refused. He asked questions about the locks and wards that were not his to ask. I escorted him from the priory under guard._

_If **Lareth** sends a formal claim under Greater Temple authority, I require instruction. I will not surrender the Gift to anyone but you. But I need to know whether resistance will be supported or punished._

_The earth holds. I hold with it._

_— [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)_
