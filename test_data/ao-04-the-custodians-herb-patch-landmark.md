AO-04. The Custodian’s Herb Patch
=================================

##### West Garden & Drying Shack

On the western side of the priory, partially shielded from the worst ridge winds by broken stonework and thorn brush, lies a small but deliberate patch of cultivated earth. This garden was not ornamental. It was practical, devotional, and necessary.

When the monastery withdrew in 562 CY, the final custodian, Halven Sere, remained behind to guard the tombs and maintain the sacred grounds. Alone upon the ridge, he tended these herbs year after year. He foraged the Kron Hills for hardy medicinal plants and replanted what would take root in thin soil. The patch served both the living and the dead — healing poultices for pilgrims, salves for minor wounds, teas for long nights of vigil.

Unlike the courtyard and gate, this place does not speak of conquest. It speaks of quiet persistence.

Since Romag’s reavers seized the site in 576 CY, the garden has not been destroyed. The Earth Temple values what is useful. Tharvok permitted it to remain, though its sanctity means nothing to him. The herbs are now used crudely for binding wounds and treating gnoll bites. The shack serves as storage for minor supplies.

This is one of the only places on the surface that still carries the faint echo of devotion rather than dominance.

##### First Discovery

As you edge west along the broken outer wall, the wind shifts and carries a different scent — sharp, green, and bitter.  
  
A small garden patch lies tucked against the stone, its beds edged with worn rocks. Some plants have been crushed beneath careless boots, yet others still grow in careful rows — low sprigs, pale flowering stems, broad leaves heavy with scent.  
  
A crooked wooden shack leans beside the beds, its slate roof sagging. Bundles of dried herbs hang from the rafters within, stirring gently in the draft. Wicker baskets sit stacked against one wall. Here, for a moment, the ridge feels less like a fortress — and more like a place someone once cared for.

### Signs of Use & Passage

This area preserves clearer evidence than the stone ascent due to soft soil and plant disturbance.

*   **Wisdom (Survival) DC 12** – General use
*   **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 13** – Organized occupation
*   **Wisdom (Survival) DC 15** – Identify species & movement patterns

* * *

##### DC 12 – Recent Use

The soil is disturbed in several places. Stems have been snapped recently, not weathered by time. This patch is still used.

**Reveals:**

*   Herbs harvested within last few days
*   Boot prints entering from courtyard
*   Ash in small fire ring behind shack (recent)

* * *

##### DC 14 – Mixed Forces

Near the shack’s doorway, you see heavy boot impressions pressed deep into the dirt. Intermixed with them are larger, clawed tracks — three-toed and wide. The gnolls come here too.

**Reveals:**

*   Black Earth Guards harvest herbs
*   Gnolls pass through frequently
*   No sign of recent prisoner movement here

* * *

##### DC 16 – Halven’s Touch

Beneath the drying rack, tucked behind a loose board, you find a smooth stone marked faintly with a lightning bolt. The carving is careful. Reverent. Someone tended this place with intention.

**Reveals:**

*   Prayer-stone garden marker
*   Evidence the custodian continued worship quietly
*   Potential hidden compartment (optional hook)

* * *

#### Foraging in the Kron Hills

A druid or ranger spending 10 minutes searching both the beds and the nearby west slope may harvest:

**Survival DC 13**

*   2 basic poultices
*   Action to stabilize without kit
*   OR advantage on next Con save vs poison/disease within 1 hour

**Survival DC 16**

*   1 bitter root infusion
*   Brewed over flame (10 minutes)
*   Grants advantage on one Wis save vs fear within 8 hours

This reinforces the martial-spiritual tone of Heironeous: endurance and resolve.

##### Earth Temple Alterations

Tharvok sees the garden as a tool. Maris sees it as useful. Caidax likely ignores it entirely.

*   Shack now stores spare rope and coarse bandages
*   Gnolls occasionally sleep here during rain
*   One herb bed partially uprooted
*   No deliberate destruction of lightning prayer-stone (likely unnoticed)

*   Provides partial cover from western wall
*   Potential stealth approach route
*   Blind spot from main gate slit (unless sentry specifically watching west side)
