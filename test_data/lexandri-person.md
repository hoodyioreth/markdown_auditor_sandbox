Lexandri
========

### Senior Archivist & Theoretical Arcanist of the Wrinkle Academy

Lexandri is not a flashy wizard, nor a battlefield mage. He is a scholar of **first principles**, a man obsessed with the idea that all magic—divine, arcane, primal, or otherwise—is merely a fractured expression of a single, unified truth. At the Wrinkle Academy of Magic, he serves as both **chief librarian** and **resident theoretician**, guarding the academy’s vast collections while quietly pursuing a lifetime theory that may one day redefine the nature of spellcasting itself.

* * *

#### Role at the Wrinkle Academy

*   **Position:** Senior Archivist & Lecturer in Arcane Theory
*   **Primary Duties:**
*   Oversees the Academy Library and Restricted Stacks
*   Curates spell scrolls, grimoires, and research treatises
*   Grants or denies outside access to magical archives
*   **Secondary Role:** Mentor to students engaged in long-form research, magical theory, and interdisciplinary study

Lexandri never formally trained at a grand magical academy. Instead, he earned his place through **sheer persistence**, having spent years poring over the Wrinkle Academy’s archives as an independent scholar before being formally hired. His appointment was controversial—yet impossible to refute—once his research began producing results that even senior professors could not ignore.

* * *

#### The Unified Theory of the Arcane

Lexandri’s life work is his **Unified Theory of the Arcane**, a belief that:

_Every spell ever cast is not a discrete creation, but a shard—  
a splintered facet—of a single, vast arcane whole._

He theorizes that:

*   Schools of magic are **artificial divisions**, created by mortal minds to categorize the incomprehensible.
*   True mastery of magic lies not in power, but in **pattern recognition** across traditions.
*   The differences between wizardry, sorcery, and even certain divine miracles are matters of **expression**, not origin.

While the theory remains unproven, several experimental correlations uncovered by Lexandri have shown enough promise to earn him cautious respect—and quiet concern—from his peers.

* * *

#### Personality & Teaching Style

Unlike many of his more severe or aloof colleagues, Lexandri is:

*   Patient and methodical
*   Soft-spoken but intensely focused
*   Genuinely welcoming to collaboration

He actively encourages students—and even visiting scholars—to assist in his research, believing that **no single mind can ever hold the whole truth**. This openness sets him apart within the academy and occasionally puts him at odds with more insular professors.

_“Knowledge hoarded is knowledge distorted,”_ he is fond of saying.

* * *

#### The Academy Library

The Wrinkle Academy Library is Lexandri’s domain.

*   **Access:**
*   Students and faculty are granted open access
*   Outside guests must apply directly to Lexandri
*   **Restrictions:**
*   Unauthorized entry is met with immediate ejection
*   Repeat offenders may be banned from the campus entirely

Lexandri enforces these rules calmly but absolutely. He does not raise his voice, nor does he negotiate once a line has been crossed. His authority here is unquestioned—even by the Academy’s most powerful spellcasters.

* * *

#### Relationships within the Academy

Lexandri maintains professional ties with:

*   Other senior professors of the Wrinkle Academy
*   The **Silver Consortium**, through academic exchange
*   Visiting researchers sponsored by the Academy

While respected, he is not universally agreed with. Some faculty see his theories as visionary; others quietly fear what might happen if he is ever proven right.

* * *

#### DM Notes & Adventure Hooks

*   Lexandri may seek the party’s help recovering lost or forbidden texts tied to pre-Cataclysm magic.
*   His research could intersect dangerously with Temple of Elemental Evil lore, Suel relics, or forgotten planar truths.
*   Characters who violate library protocol may find themselves barred from the Academy—unless they can earn Lexandri’s trust back through service or discovery.

* * *

**Independent Scholar**

Lexandri has advantage on **Intelligence (Arcana, History, or Investigation)** checks to research written magical material, identify spellwork, or trace the provenance of a scroll, sigil, or rune.

**Cautious, Not a Duelist**

Lexandri avoids violence. If threatened, he prioritizes **escape, stalling, or calling for help**. He does **not** pursue fleeing enemies.

**Ritual Savant**

Lexandri can cast **comprehend languages** and **identify** as rituals (spellbook permitting), even if not prepared.

* * *

##### Spellcasting

Lexandri is a **4th-level spellcaster (wizard)**. His spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC **13**, +5 to hit with spell attacks). He keeps magic practical and archival.

**Cantrips (at will):** _mage hand, message, prestidigitation, minor illusion_

**1st level (4 slots):** _detect magic, identify, comprehend languages, alarm_

**2nd level (3 slots):** _detect thoughts, locate object, arcane lock_

**Prepared Spells Note (DM-facing):** If you want him even “lower magic,” drop him to **wizard 2** and remove _arcane lock_ and _locate object_. Keep _detect thoughts_ as his “big” spell.

* * *

**Actions**

**Quarterstaff.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +1 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 2 (1d6 − 1) bludgeoning damage.

**Ink-Blind Toss (1/Day).** Lexandri flings a vial of thick lamp-ink. One creature within 10 feet must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or be **blinded until the end of its next turn**.

* * *

**Equipment**

Spellbook (catalogued), archive keys, sealing wax and writs, ink vials, small magnifier lens, satchel of note-slips, chalk for ward-marks.

* * *

##### DM Use Notes

*   This Lexandri is **credible**: smart, useful, and _not_ combat scary.
*   His “power” is **access and procedure**: permissions, records, hidden shelves, and knowing who asked what—when.
*   In play, he shines through: _detect thoughts_ in tense interviews, _locate object_ for missing texts, _arcane lock/alarm_ for “someone’s been in the stacks.”
