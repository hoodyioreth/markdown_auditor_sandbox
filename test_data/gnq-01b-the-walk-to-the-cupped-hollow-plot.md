GNQ-01b: The Walk to the Cupped Hollow
======================================

> Readying 11, 576 CY. Two hours before moonrise, **Sister Aelwynn Thornroot** leads the party south from [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) along the Long Road, east onto the Wild Road, and then off the road altogether — into the deep Gnarley Forest, toward a hidden ridge on the Viscounty border where the Cupped Hollow waits. On Great Moon's Glory, everything Luna's light touches glows faintly and stirs. The forest is tranquil, fey-touched, awake. This is the journey article for the GNQ-01 quest cluster — the DM prep article is GNQ-01a, the climax is GNQ-01c. Open this article when the party leaves for the Hollow. Four wonder-beats along the path, the first two phases of the Dance at the rim, and the arrival of the Howling Moon wedge. Ends on Aelwynn's cry of horror — GNQ-01c opens on Round 1.

##### How to Use This Article

This is the at-the-table journey article. Open it when the party leaves the milestone for the Hollow. Every box is meant to be read to the players as written. After each beat, a consolidated **AELWYNN'S DIALOG** reference block pulls her words out so you can glance back at them mid-roleplay without scanning prose. The register throughout is Lord Dunsany's _The King of Elfland's Daughter_ — tranquil, fey-touched, unhurried enchantment. Do not foreshadow horror. The journey is pure wonder, by design. The horror lands once, at the climax of Phase 2, when the Howling Moon wedge breaks the far rim. The contrast is the entire point.  

### The Route — DM Reference

*   **Start:** The old milestone past [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md)'s eastern fence, two hours before moonrise on Readying 11.
*   **Leg 1:** South on the Long Road, past [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) on the right and the thorp of **Kron** further south, to the junction where the **Wild Road** breaks east.
*   **Leg 2:** East along the Wild Road as the light thins, bearing toward **Hummings End** but never arriving there. The road is quiet. The party will pass one ox-cart and one shepherd, no more.
*   **Leg 3:** Short of Hummings End, Aelwynn steps off the Wild Road on its north side, into the forest. From here there is no path. The walk is cross-country, northeast, into the deep Gnarley. She casts Pass Without Trace wordlessly at the road-leaving.
*   **Leg 4:** Hours under closing canopy as dusk becomes night and Luna climbs. The Hollow is hidden on the Viscounty border, in broken ridge country, deep in the forest — no sightline touches it from any road.
*   **End:** The rim of the Cupped Hollow, full dark, moments before Phase 1 of the Dance begins.

##### Pass Without Trace — Narrative Only

Aelwynn casts it wordlessly the moment her feet leave the Wild Road. No spell-name is spoken, no gesture made. Describe the effect, not the mechanic: the forest quiets around the party's footfalls, their voices carry shorter, the undergrowth bends aside without snagging. No rolls. No DC. The spell is a tone, not a game element.

##### Pacing Reminder

The four wonder-beats are the quest's promise kept. Let each one breathe. Players who rush past a beat are telling you they want the journey to move faster — let them, but do not cut the beat itself short inside the aloud. Read it all. Then move on.  

### Beat 1 — The Road-Leaving

The old milestone past Hommlet's eastern fence is a weathered thing, knee-high, its spiral mark worn almost smooth. You reach it two hours before moonrise, as Aelwynn said you should. The sky is the color of a pressed flower. The road is empty in both directions.   She is already there. Standing, not waiting — the way she was in the grove, the way a second tree might stand.   She nods once when she sees you. Then she turns south and walks, and you walk with her.   The Long Road takes you past Sheernobb's watch-post on the right, past the quiet fields that run down to the thorp of Kron, past a farmer leading an ox off the road to let you by. He tips his hat to Aelwynn. She returns the nod without slowing. She does not speak.   At the Wild Road junction she turns east. The light has begun to thin. The Wild Road is narrower than the Long Road, packed earth and wheel-ruts, hemmed close by wall-oaks that have not been coppiced in a generation. You walk it for perhaps an hour. A shepherd passes you going the other way and gives Aelwynn a long look and says nothing. The trees begin to lean in.   Short of the point where the road would curl down toward Hummings End, Aelwynn stops. She does not point. She does not speak. She simply steps off the road on the north side, one foot, then the other, and she is in the forest.   You follow her. The last light of the road is behind you within ten paces.   She draws a breath. She lets it out. She does not turn. When she speaks, her voice is quieter than it was in the grove — not secret, not afraid, only _matched_ to where she is now.   **"From here there is no path. Walk where I walk. If I stop, stop. If I kneel, kneel. The forest will not ask anything of you tonight that costs more than your manners. But it does ask manners."**   She touches her staff once to a trunk as she passes it, as though greeting a relative by name. The berries bound near the staff's top click, quiet as a clock.   **"I have asked the forest to hold our footfalls. You will find your voices carry less than you expect. That is not magic done at you. That is the forest answering a request. It will end when I tell it to end. Until then — speak if you must, but not loudly, and not of ugly things."**   She looks back at you over her shoulder then, and the almost-smile is there for the first time since Hommlet.   **"You should have been here at dawn to see the road. But you will see better things tonight than a road at dawn. Come."**

### Beat 2 — The Fey-Touched Crossing

How long you walk, you could not say. The light has gone from pressed-flower to the deep slow blue of a stained window. Luna is not yet up, but her light is coming — you can feel the promise of it the way you feel a kettle's coming boil, a thing gathering that has not yet broken the surface.   Then it does break. The first silver slips through the high canopy in thin clean lines, and everything the light touches changes.   The moss on a fallen trunk answers the moon. Where the light lays across it, the moss glows — not brightly, only as if someone had breathed on a banked coal. A fern in a shaft of that light lifts its fronds a finger-width, slow as a sleeper turning.   You come to a stream. It is cold and dark and narrow, no wider than a long stride, running south across your path. Every stone on the bed wears a crown of that moss, and every crown pulses — faintly, patiently — in time with something you cannot hear.   A doe stands in the stream. A fawn stands beside her. They are not surprised to see you. They do not move.   Aelwynn stops. She lowers her staff until its tip rests on the water. She does not speak. She waits.   The doe walks toward her, slow, unafraid, and stops when her chest is a hand's breadth from Aelwynn's. Aelwynn does not bend. She lifts her free hand, palm upturned, and lays it gently between the doe's ears — the way one greets a cousin one has not seen in a year.   The doe breathes against her sleeve once, twice. Then she moves aside, and the fawn follows, and the stream is yours to cross.   Aelwynn speaks only when you are on the far bank.   **"The forest is awake tonight. It has been gathering its face all week, the way a woman fixes her hair for a wedding. By moonrise proper it will be wearing its true one."**   She looks at you, and her fern-green eyes are brighter than they were in the grove — not with magic, with _pleasure_.   **"This is what my order serves. Not the rules of it. Not even the old songs of it. This. The thing the forest does when it thinks no one is watching, and the thing it does when it knows someone is. Tonight it knows. I told it."**   She walks again. The moss-crowns on the streambed keep their quiet pulse behind her.

  **AELWYNN'S DIALOG — BEAT 2 (Fey-Touched Crossing)**

*   **On the forest tonight:** "The forest is awake tonight. It has been gathering its face all week, the way a woman fixes her hair for a wedding. By moonrise proper it will be wearing its true one."
*   **On her vocation:** "This is what my order serves. Not the rules of it. Not even the old songs of it. This. The thing the forest does when it thinks no one is watching, and the thing it does when it knows someone is. Tonight it knows. I told it."

 

### Beat 3 — The Deep Gnarley

The canopy closes. Whatever moon-silver had been slipping through now comes in single shafts, spaced like the pillars of a long hall. Between the shafts the dark is thick and green and alive. You have lost every bearing that a road gives. You are hours from any road. You know it the way you know an old ache.   Aelwynn walks as if the forest were laid out for her like a familiar garden. She does not look at the ground. She does not look up. She looks a middle distance — the way a person looks at a face they love.   Somewhere above, a single luminous mote drifts down through one of the moon-shafts. A spore, perhaps — a thing the wall-oaks let go of at this hour each spring, carried by no wind you can feel. It falls slow, slow, the way a feather falls in water. You watch it because there is nothing else in the shaft to watch.   It touches the leaf-litter a pace ahead of Aelwynn. Where it touches, a violet flower — tiny, no larger than a thumbnail — unfolds in the half-second before her boot passes over it. She does not step on it. She steps around it without looking.   You pass the place where the flower was. Your own boot does not crush it. You do not know whether it is still there behind you, or whether it closed up again, or whether it was ever a thing at all.   Aelwynn walks a little farther. Then she stops at a place that is no different from any other place, and she sits — not on a stump or a stone, just on the forest floor, cross-legged, her staff across her knees. She gestures for you to sit with her. You do.   She is quiet long enough that it stops being awkward. Then she speaks, and her voice is lower than it has been all night — not secret, only _given_.   **"I was seven when the forest first spoke to me. Not in words. A weight between my shoulders on a morning walk, the sense of a hand that had not been there before and was not gone when I turned. I told my mother. She was Old Faith already. She said, 'That is the beginning.' She did not say the beginning of what."**   A pause. She runs her thumb along the wire binding her staff, slow.   **"I am forty-three years old. I have walked these woods for thirty-six of them. I have never once mistaken the Gnarley's voice for my own. It does not sound like me. It does not sound like anyone. It sounds like a room that has been waiting for you to come home to it, and when you finally do, you understand why you have been tired so long."**   She looks around, not at any one thing — at the whole dark green place.   **"This is not a god. I want you to hear me say that. My order does not worship this. We serve it the way a good innkeeper serves a house — we clean what needs cleaning, we bar the door at night, we put flowers on the sill in spring. The house is not ours. It was here before us. It will be here after. We just keep it well while it is our turn."**   She lets the silence take that. Then, quieter still:   **"Hrudek does not know I have brought you this far in. He will, when the ravens tell him, and he will be angry for a quarter-hour and then he will understand. He always does. That is what I love in him. He is angry first and fair second, but he is always fair."**   She stands. She offers no hand; she trusts you to manage your own rising.   **"One more walk. Not long. You will know when we are close because the air will get kinder. Everything gets kinder near the Hollow. That is how it was made."**

### Beat 4 — The Rim of the Cupped Hollow

She was right about the air. It happens gradually, then all at once — a warmth that is not temperature, a slackening in the chest, a sense of having walked through a last door into a room that has been kept for you.   The ridge rises without warning. Aelwynn turns along its spine, walking three paces ahead of you now, and then she stops and goes down on one knee — not in reverence, only to be lower against the skyline — and she gestures for you to do the same.   You kneel beside her on the rim and look down.   The Cupped Hollow is a bowl of grass. No trees inside it, only the grass, short and silver-edged in the full moonlight — Luna has climbed fully now, and Celene is nowhere, new. The rim of trees around the bowl is a perfect dark collar. The bowl floor is perhaps sixty paces across. It is empty.   No — not empty. _Quiet_. The way a stage is quiet a breath before the curtain. Every blade of grass is perfectly still. You realize you cannot hear a single insect, a single breath of wind, a single thing.   Aelwynn's face — you glance at it — is lit from below by nothing that should be lighting it, a faint fey-blue that is not yet there in the bowl but is already there in her eyes. She is smiling. It is not the careful almost-smile of the grove. It is a child's smile.   She leans close to you and her voice is no louder than a breath.   **"Watch the trees on the far side. Not the grass. The trees. They will come out of the wood one at a time, then two, then three — like a family gathering at a table. Do not point. Do not speak until they are all in. This is their house. We were invited."**   You watch the far trees.   The first one drifts out.   It is a palm-sized candle-flame, fey-blue at its heart and silver-white at its edges, with the suggestion — only the suggestion — of wings and a small laughing face at its center. It floats at the height of a child's shoulder. It crosses the tree-line and hangs above the grass, uncertain, the way a guest hangs at a door not sure of the room's arrangement.   Then a second. Then two more. Then seven.   **"This is the Gathering,"** Aelwynn breathes. **"Phase one of three. They are finding their places."**   Within ten minutes there are perhaps a hundred of them, spaced across the bowl at the height of a tall man's head, each hanging in its own small stillness. A great loose halo of candle-flames. The grass beneath them catches the fey-blue and shines, faint, back at them. They are not yet dancing. They are breathing.   Aelwynn's hand finds yours — whichever of you is closest — and closes gently around your wrist. She is not afraid. She is _full_.   **"There are nights I have waited my whole life for. This is one of them. I did not want to watch it alone. Thank you for coming. Thank you for walking where I walked. Thank you for not being rude to the doe."**   A breath.   **"Now watch. The Dance begins."**   And it does.   The hundred candle-flames begin, all at once and without any signal any of you can perceive, to move. At first a drift — each one tracing a slow arc a hand's breadth wide, no more. Then wider. Then the arcs begin to braid, each flame finding its neighbor's path and passing through it, and passing through it again, and the pattern resolves from a thousand small drifts into a single wheeling ribbon of fey-blue divine light that rises, slowly, above the bowl's floor. Hundreds of them, packed close, spiraling and twirling around and through one another in a braided rope of light that coils upward and inward, overlapping itself in knotted vortices. You can see the individual sprites at its edges — faces, wings, tiny hands — and you can see them dissolve into pure streaming light in its heart.   It is not music but you hear music. It is not laughter but you hear laughter. It is, you understand at the back of your chest where understanding lives before words find it, _the forest itself, briefly visible_. This is the thing Aelwynn serves. This is the thing she showed you.   Aelwynn is not looking at you. She is looking at the bowl, and she is weeping, quietly, without covering her face. She lets the tears come and does not brush them.   **"This is the Dance at height,"** she whispers. **"Phase two. Ten minutes now. Only ten. Do not look away. You will not see its like again in your lives."**

### Beat 5 — The Wedge at the Far Rim

##### DM Note — Pacing the Arrival

The two aloud boxes below are the hinge of the entire quest. Read the first one and **stop**. Let the table sit in it. Watch your players' faces. Do not race into the second. The whole point is that the players do not yet know what they are looking at — they see only a black mass, and their imaginations run further than any description you could read them. Hold the silence for as long as it is working. Then read the second box, and let the recognition land.  

You are eight minutes into Phase two when the drumming starts.   It is not loud. At first you think it is your own heart, the way it has been beating in your throat since the Dance began. But your heart does not quicken and fall the way a drum does. You listen. It is a drum. Several drums. Deep and slow and far away, on the forest's far side.   Aelwynn's grip on your wrist tightens. It is the first sign of fear you have ever seen from her.   She does not speak.   On the far side of the bowl — the opposite rim, a full sixty paces of glowing air away — the trees part.   It is not figures that come out of them. It is a _shape_.   A wedge — arrow-headed, densely packed, moving as one thing — emerges from the dark of the wood and stops at the rim of the bowl. You cannot tell how many make up the shape. You cannot see limbs. You cannot see eyes. You can only see a black mass, darker than the night between the trees, hungry in the way the throat of a cave is hungry. It does not step forward. It does not retreat. It waits on the rim the way a boulder waits before it falls.   A horn sounds — one long, brazen note, from inside the mass.   The drums come up to meet it.   The Dance does not stop. The braided ribbon of fey-blue light above the bowl keeps turning, keeps laughing — it does not know it has been seen. It cannot know. It was not made to know.   But you see. And Aelwynn sees.   And beside you, in a voice you do not recognize as hers, she says the only word she can find:   **"No."**

  _— Hold here. Let the table sit in it. Do not read the next box until the silence has done its work. —_  

The black wedge moves.   It comes down off the far rim in a single disciplined motion, driving toward the bowl's center at a pace neither hurried nor slow — the pace of a thing that has decided on its meal and will not be deflected from it. Three strides. Four. Five.   On the fifth stride the light of the Dance catches the point of the wedge, and you see what it is.   It is orcs.   Orcs in iron and hide, twelve of them, wedge-formation: two great Orog lieutenants flanking, ten lesser orcs filling the body, each with a skinning knife or a cudgel or a short crescent blade, faces painted in something that runs in the moon-light like fresh blood. Their tusks show. Their eyes show — yellow-lit, hot. The drums are slung at the hips of the rearmost pair. A horn-bearer marches at the flank. Every one of them is in step.   And at the point of the wedge, one pace ahead of the Orogs, is the thing that is leading them.   He is enormous. Seven feet standing, hunched forward in the pull of his own appetite, and he is _changing_ as you watch. The orc face is still visible under what is coming out of it — a long low wolf's snout has half-emerged, ridged with black gum, and his shoulders are broadening even as his gait lengthens into something that is neither an orc's walk nor a wolf's lope but the terrible middle of both. His eyes are the hot yellow of a forge seen through smoke. His claws are out.   He is laughing.   The first sprite meets him at chest-height, floating uncertain out of the Dance — too close to the wedge's point, unable to know what it is looking at. His clawed hand reaches up for it with the slow greedy grace of a man reaching for a grape. His fingers close on it. You see its small face, laughing still, the half-second before he crushes it against his tusks and takes it into his mouth.   You see his body answer. A ripple goes through him — pleasure and power and something worse. His back arches. His shoulders grow another inch. The black fur breaks through along his spine. He throws his head back and howls, and the howl is not an orc's and not a wolf's — it is the sound of a thing being born into itself.   The orcs around him roar.   The wedge breaks into the Dance.

  **AELWYNN — HORROR CRY (end of Phase 2)**

Aelwynn is on her feet before you know she has stood. Her staff is in both hands. Her face is a ruin — tears, teeth, grief come through her mouth in a sound you did not know a human throat could make.   **"NO — NO, NO, NO, NOT HERE, NOT THEM — HRUDEK, HRUDEK, THE FOREST — SOMEONE, ANYONE, STOP THEM — THEY ARE EATING THEM, THEY ARE EATING THEM, OH GODS THEY ARE EATING THEM —"**   She takes a step forward, toward the rim, toward the bowl, as if she means to walk down into the slaughter unarmed. Her knees give. She catches herself on her staff. She does not stop weeping. She does not stop screaming.   She turns her face to you — to whichever of you is closest — and she says, raw:   **"Please. Please. I brought you here. Please."**

  **AELWYNN'S DIALOG — BEAT 5 (Horror Cry)**

*   **At first sight of the wedge:** "No." (Said in a voice the party has not heard from her — low, unrecognizing, the beginning of grief.)
*   **As the wedge breaks into the Dance:** "NO — NO, NO, NO, NOT HERE, NOT THEM — HRUDEK, HRUDEK, THE FOREST — SOMEONE, ANYONE, STOP THEM — THEY ARE EATING THEM, THEY ARE EATING THEM, OH GODS THEY ARE EATING THEM —"
*   **Her plea to the party:** "Please. Please. I brought you here. Please."

##### The King of Elfland's Daughter

_The register of this journey is modeled on Lord Dunsany's **The King of Elfland's Daughter** — slow enchanted prose, every sentence earning its wonder, peace as a tangible substance, no dread. The party is walking into the world of the fey and should feel it in the muscle of their shoulders. Do not foreshadow horror._

* * *

##### On Great Moon's Glory

_Luna is full and Celene is new. On this one night of the year, everything Luna's light touches glows faintly and stirs. Moss-crowns pulse. Ferns lift their fronds. A doe stands unafraid. A drifting spore blooms into a flower at the forest floor. This is not a spell effect and cannot be dispelled. It is what the night itself is._

* * *

##### Pass Without Trace — Narrative Tone

_Cast wordlessly at the road-leaving. No mechanic, no roll. Describe the forest quieting around the party's footfalls, voices carrying shorter, undergrowth bending aside. End it only when the article says — with the drumming at Beat 5. Until then, the spell is a tone._

* * *

##### Aelwynn's Voice Across the Night

_Her speech tightens as the journey deepens. In the grove she was formal and deliberate. On the road she is quieter. At the stream she is pleased. In the deep Gnarley she is intimate — this is the only place she speaks of her mother, of Hrudek, of being seven. At the rim she is a child. At the wedge she breaks._

* * *

##### The Hinge

_Two back-to-back aloud boxes do the work. The first — the black mass — is the players' imagination filling the silence. The second — the resolution into Gorrogh-mid-turn and twelve orcs — is the knife. The DM's single job here is to hold the silence between them for as long as it is working._

##### DM Quick Reference — Journey Handoff

**TIMING ON THE NIGHT**

*   **Departure:** 2 hours before moonrise, from Hommlet milestone
*   **Wild Road departure:** Beat 1 — dusk
*   **Fey crossing:** Beat 2 — first moon-silver
*   **Deep forest rest:** Beat 3 — Luna fully up
*   **Rim arrival:** Beat 4 — full dark, Dance begins

* * *

**REGISTER REMINDERS**

*   **Dunsany:** slow enchanted prose, peace as substance
*   **No dread** in Beats 1–4. None. The horror is earned by contrast.
*   **Luna as engine:** every wonder is her light touching something
*   **Aelwynn's arc:** formal → quiet → pleased → intimate → child → breaks

* * *

**IF THE PARTY TRIES TO SHORTCUT**

*   **Fly / teleport:** The Hollow is druid-warded; they arrive in a forest that is not the right forest. Aelwynn is not with them. They must walk back out and start again.
*   **Find the Path:** Fails. The Hollow refuses the spell. Aelwynn smiles sadly and says, _"The forest does not show its face to strangers before it shows it to them."_
*   **Scrying ahead:** Fails for the same reason. The DM should foreshadow this in GNQ-01a's first meeting so it is not a surprise at the table.

**TRANSITIONING TO GNQ-01c**

*   **End state of 01b:** Party kneeling on the near rim, Aelwynn collapsed beside them on her staff, wedge driving into the Dance, Gorrogh feeding, sprites dying

**DESIGN PHILOSOPHY — WHY THIS JOURNEY**

*   **The promise:** Aelwynn said "a sight." The journey makes good on that.
*   **The exchange:** The party gave her their manners. The forest gives them a night.
*   **The contrast:** Full reverent wonder allowed its breath, then the wedge. The knife cuts cleaner when the hand was open.
*   **The stakes:** By Beat 5, the party is not defending an abstraction. They have walked the forest. They have seen the Dance. They have heard Aelwynn speak of her mother. They will fight like it.
