AO-02. The Ridge Perimeter
==========================

The ridge itself is narrow and windswept. On the eastern edge, the land drops sharply into the valley. On the western side, jagged rock outcroppings form natural cover.

The builders chose this location intentionally — it is defensible and symbolic. The faithful of Heironeous were meant to look outward, not inward. Now the ridge serves as a forward raiding observation platform.

##### The Ridge Perimeter

The ridge narrows as you crest the ascent. To your right, the land falls sharply away into a long valley where the pale thread of the Long Road cuts through distant fields. Wagons are no more than crawling specks far below.  
  
To your left, jagged stone outcroppings claw upward from the earth, casting sharp shadows across sparse thorn brush and brittle grass. The wind carries with it a faint scent — cold stone, old ash… and something fouler beneath it.  
  
Loose gravel shifts underfoot. You hear it then — faint. A scrape of leather. The low murmur of guttural speech carried by the wind. Or perhaps it was only the wind itself. The ridge feels defensive. Chosen. Meant to be held.

### Terrain Features

*   30–50 foot drop on eastern face
*   Rocky ledges suitable for 2–3 hidden archers
*   Sparse thorn bushes
*   Old drainage channels carved into stone

### Earth Temple Additions

_Ridge Perimeter Defensive Modifications_

These additions were made within the last few months under Tharvok’s direction. They reflect Black Earth discipline, gnoll savagery, and layered defensive doctrine.

*   Shallow stone dugouts (recent)
*   Signal horn posts
*   Earthbind traps in narrow choke points
*   Gnoll refuse and bone piles along western rocks

* * *

#### 1\. Shallow Stone Dugouts (Recent)

DM Overview

Along the western ridge rocks and near the narrowest bends of the ascent, the earth has been reshaped into low defensive pits. These are not trenches — but shallow fighting positions carved directly into bedrock using Mold Earth and stone shaping techniques.

They are difficult to see from below.

They are excellent for ambush.

* * *

##### Detection

*   Passive Perception 15 (from below): notice unnatural straight edges in rock
*   Wisdom (Perception) DC 13 within 30 feet
*   Wisdom (Survival) DC 14 reveals recent earth shaping

* * *

##### Mechanics

*   Provide **Half Cover (+2 AC)** when crouched
*   Provide **Three-Quarters Cover (+5 AC)** when prone inside
*   2–3 creatures can occupy each dugout
*   Dugout edges count as difficult terrain for attackers

* * *

##### Tactical Use

*   Gnolls favor western rock dugouts
*   Black Earth Guards use central dugouts
*   Dugouts allow readied ranged attacks before initiative if ambush successful

* * *

##### Escalation Note

If the party uses dugouts against defenders later, Tharvok collapses them with Mold Earth within 24 hours.

* * *

#### 2\. Signal Horn Posts

DM Overview

At two key ridge points stand rough-hewn wooden posts fitted with hanging war horns carved from massive auroch bone. These horns carry sound down the valley and across the ridge.

This is a military early-warning system.

* * *

##### Detection

*   Obvious if visible
*   Stealth DC 15 to approach without being seen using one

* * *

##### Mechanics

*   Blowing a horn alerts:
*   Gate guards immediately
*   Courtyard mobilizes in 2 rounds
*   Tharvok alerted within 1 minute
*   Sound travels 1 mile clearly in open terrain
*   Destroying a horn requires 15 HP (AC 12)

* * *

##### Tactical Use

*   Gnolls blow horn immediately
*   Guards blow horn only if outnumbered
*   If one horn destroyed, second post increases patrol frequency

* * *

##### DM Escalation Rule

If a horn is sounded:

*   Increase encounter table by +3 next hour
*   All ridge encounters shift to 17+ range

* * *

#### 3\. Earthbind Traps (Choke Points)

DM Overview

In two narrow sections of the ridge, Tharvok has prepared the ground using ritual and spellcraft. These are not visible runes — they are areas of stone subtly infused with binding magic.

These are not permanent glyph traps — they are triggered spells prepared for quick activation.

* * *

##### Trigger Conditions

*   Activated by hidden Black Earth priest or prepared ritual focus
*   Usually triggered when intruders reach narrowest choke point

* * *

##### Mechanics (Earthbind Spell Variant)

When triggered:

*   Target creature within 60 feet must make DC 15 Strength save
*   On failure:
*   Movement reduced to 0
*   Pulled downward if flying
*   Speed halved for 1 minute
*   Repeat save at end of each turn

Area of effect can target up to 2 creatures if Tharvok present.

* * *

##### Detection

*   Arcana DC 16 to sense transmutation residue
*   Detect Magic reveals faint transmutation aura
*   Religion DC 15 identifies Black Earth ritual influence

* * *

##### Tactical Use

*   Triggered at start of ambush
*   Used to isolate front-line fighter
*   Followed by ranged fire from dugouts

* * *

##### Escalation Note

If Earthbind used:

*   Assume Tharvok is nearby or has prepared ground earlier that day

* * *

#### 4\. Gnoll Refuse & Bone Piles (Western Rocks)

DM Overview

The western rock formations are fouled with carrion scraps, gnawed bones, and filth. These are not merely trash piles — they mark territory.

They also attract scavengers.

* * *

##### Detection

*   Obvious by smell within 40 feet
*   Perception DC 12 to spot partially buried bones
*   Investigation DC 15 finds human finger bones and broken trade goods

* * *

##### Mechanics

*   Area counts as lightly obscured terrain
*   Difficult terrain within 5 feet of piles
*   Creatures moving through must succeed DC 12 Con save or become nauseated (disadvantage on next attack)

* * *

### Wandering Encounters

The ridge is actively patrolled by Black Earth forces and King Bonegnaw’s gnolls. This is not random wilderness — this is a military perimeter.

Roll once every 30 minutes if characters remain exposed on the ridge.  
Roll once upon any loud disturbance (combat, shouting, spellcasting).  
No encounter on 1–10 (d20).

#### Standard Encounters

* * *

##### 11–13: Black Earth Patrol (2 Guards)

Disciplined and methodical.

**Behavior:**

*   Walking predictable patrol route
*   Alert but not jumpy
*   Signal horn if hostiles spotted
*   Attempt capture over kill if feasible

**Read-Aloud Cue:**

You hear measured footfalls before you see them — steady, armored steps grinding against loose stone. Two figures in dark earthen cloaks move along the ridge, scanning the valley below.

* * *

##### 14–16: Gnoll Sentries (2)

Bonegnaw’s warriors posted on higher ground.

**Behavior:**

*   Watching valley approaches
*   Prefer elevated rocks
*   Aggressive if they believe they outnumber intruders
*   Howl alarm if combat lasts 2+ rounds

**Read-Aloud Cue:**

A low, wet chuckle drifts across the ridge. Yellow eyes blink between jagged stone. One gnoll crouches low, sniffing the wind.

* * *

##### 17: Mixed Patrol (1 Guard + 2 Gnolls)

Joint patrol — tense cooperation.

**Behavior:**

*   Guard commands, gnolls bristle
*   If conflict arises, gnolls attack first
*   Guard may attempt tactical withdrawal to warn gate

**Atmosphere:  
**This shows the uneasy alliance between Earth Temple and Bonegnaw.

* * *

##### 18: Rotating Guard Squad (4 Black Earth Guards)

Serious presence.

**Behavior:**

*   Reinforcement unit
*   Shields locked formation
*   Advance methodically
*   Do not flee unless outmatched

**DM Note:  
**If encountered, assume courtyard mobilization in 2 rounds if alarm sounded.

* * *

#### Rare Command Presence

* * *

##### 19: Maris, Field Agent of Romag (with 2 Guards)

Maris is not often seen openly on the ridge. When he appears, it is to inspect patrol discipline or assess intelligence.

**Behavior:**

*   Observant
*   Calculates risk
*   Prefers manipulation over open combat
*   May attempt conversation to gather information

**If forced to retreat:  
**He immediately informs Tharvok.

**DM Note:  
**Maris is rarely surprised. Consider advantage on Perception.

* * *

##### 20: Tharvok, Earth Temple Commander (with 4 Guards)

Extremely rare.

Tharvok personally inspects the ridge only if:

*   Expecting caravan movement
*   Concerned about Lareth interference
*   Intelligence suggests threat

**Behavior:**

*   Commands immediately
*   Earth spells ready
*   Attempts capture of high-value targets
*   No negotiation

**Escalation Effect:  
**If Tharvok appears, the ridge locks down.  
No further wandering checks — full alert status begins.
