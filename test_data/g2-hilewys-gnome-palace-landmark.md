G2 Hilewy’s Gnome Palace
========================

> _All the Comforts of Home… and the Sharpest Ears in the Quarter_

### A Hub of Gnome Culture and Politics

_Luxurious Underhill Splendor_

From the street, Hilewy’s looks like any other gnome mound—pleasant, modest, almost sleepy. Step inside and the place unfurls into underhill splendor: a vast, tunnel-linked hostelry of private burrows, grand communal halls, and cleverly concealed upper chambers woven into a great ipt tree and dressed in illusion.

This is where gnomes come to feel safe in a human city—where they can speak plainly (or as plainly as gnomes ever do). It is also where rival gnome factions smile over sweetbread while measuring one another’s strength… and where Prince Jimm’s invitations sometimes land like velvet manacles.

##### Approaching Hilewy’s Gnome Palace

The mound is unremarkable—rounded earth, a round door, flowers in tidy pots—no more boastful than any other burrow-home in Gnomesberg. Yet the moment thou passest the threshold, the world changes.  
  
Warm lamplight spills through carved arches. The air smells of malt, butter, cedar-oil, and ink. Tunnels branch like roots into private doors—dozens of them—each marked with tiny runes and painted tokens. Somewhere deeper, laughter rises and falls as if in a far-off hall… and above, though thou seest no stairs, a faint chiming suggests balconies and lofts that ought not fit within so humble a hill.  
  
A gnome servant bows, eyes bright as coins. “Welcome, honored guest,” they say, and the word honored lands as both comfort and measurement.

##### Lord Hilewy, the owner

is a significant political figure and represents the [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) Gnomes. The palace frequently hosts key gnome dignitaries like Clanlord Urthgan Tulvar and Prince [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md), making it a focal point for important political and social gatherings.

* * *

#### What It Looks Like (Architecture & Illusions)

Hilewy’s Palace is engineered to impress without alarming outsiders. Most of the visible mound is a mask; much of the true structure is concealed within and around a **great ipt tree**, with interiors expanded and “corrected” by **illusion-work** to feel airy and grand while still remaining scaled to gnomish comfort.

**Design notes to emphasize at the table:**

*   **Low ceilings, perfect proportions:** Humans stoop just slightly—enough to remind them they are not the intended scale.
*   **Illusory “breathing space”:** Halls seem wider than they should be; distances feel subtly shortened; flowers never wilt.
*   **Animated charm as security:** Tiny glamour-fairies drift about lanterns and buffet tables—delightful… until you realize they watch everything.

### The Palace in Play

(How the Location Functions)

##### A) A Gnomish Haven

This is the safest place in Verbobonc for a gnome traveler to arrive with coin, documents, or worries. Every need is met by gnome staff; every guest is offered a private burrow; every burrow connects into the wider palace by an intricate tunnel-web.

##### B) A Political Salon

The Palace routinely hosts gnomish elites and dignitaries—particularly when matters must be debated away from human eyes. Lord Hilewy uses “hospitality” as a way to gather competing voices under one roof and force civility through tradition.

##### C) A Pressure Valve for the Quarter

When tensions rise—mines, tariffs, caravan raids, insults from the wrong human lord—Hilewy’s becomes the place where the Free Alliance tries to keep the gnomes of Verbobonc aligned, steady, and not fractured by fear.

* * *

#### Politics & Tensions (Use as Campaign Fuel)

Hilewy’s is entangled in the great argument of 576 CY:

**Gnomish autonomy vs. human encroachment.**

Whether it’s disputed mines, heavy-handed permits, or the chilling effect of reduced border patrols, gnome merchants feel exposed. Caravan raids and banditry aren’t just “crime”—they are political pressure, whether orchestrated or merely exploited.

Hilewy keeps the gnomish factions talking instead of splintering.

And the Temple of Elemental Evil (and Iuzite cults) thrive wherever communities splinter.

* * *

### Temple of Elemental Evil & Iuz Cult Hooks

##### 1) The Buffet of False Names

A charming stranger attends a “private supper” under an assumed identity, asking after caravan schedules, gnome safehouses, and which burrows belong to which clan. The staff noticed—politely. Hilewy asks the party to identify the intruder without causing a public scandal.

**Outcome options:** expose, follow, bait, or turn the spy.

##### 2) The Bandit Ledger

A Greenway Valley caravan was hit with uncanny precision—too precise for random bandits. A ledger of supply lists and timing suggests inside knowledge. The Free Alliance wants answers without accusing the wrong human house and igniting a trade war. Hilewy offers the party a deal: solve it quietly, and earn a powerful introduction.

##### 3) The “Helpful” Priest

An Iuzite sympathizer (or Temple agent in a respectable mask) offers “protection prayers” for traveling gnomes—cheap, comforting, and subtly coercive. Several merchants took the offer. Now they’re being leaned on for “donations” and favors. The Palace becomes a staging ground for a counter-play.

##### 4) The Illusion That Shouldn’t Be There

One wing’s glamour begins behaving oddly: a flower turns black at midnight, a corridor seems longer on certain nights, a fairy-lamp flickers in a pattern like a code. The gnomes fear sabotage—Temple magic testing the Palace’s defenses, or a hidden message meant for a collaborator.

* * *

#### Non-Combat Leverage & Social Play

Hilewy’s is built for encounters where swords stay sheathed and outcomes hinge on manners, leverage, and perception.

**Ways the party can win without fighting:**

*   **Earn Guest-Right** (formal courtesy + correct conduct) to gain access to deeper rooms and better witnesses.
*   **Trade favors instead of coin** (escort a caravan, recover a stolen seal, discreetly remove a blackmailer).
*   **Use arbitration** (gnome legal custom) to force a rival into a binding agreement in front of witnesses.
*   **Turn the staff into allies** (kindness to “invisible” servants pays dividends; they know everything).

* * *

### Scenes You Can Run Immediately

##### Scene 1: The Low Door Test

The party is led through a corridor where the ceiling drops slightly. It’s not a trap—it’s a test. Do they complain? Joke? Bow their heads with grace?

**Reward:** better seating, warmer tone, more candid questions.

##### Scene 2: The Private Burrow

Each guest receives a private burrow with charming illusion décor—fresh flowers, playful lights, perfect warmth. But the party notices something: a _deliberate_ lack of places to hide contraband, and a gentle sense that the room “remembers” where you placed things.

**Tension:** they are safe… and observed.

##### Scene 3: The Salon of Balconies

A debate is underway above and behind illusion-curtains—gnome elders arguing about mines, raids, and whether to withdraw from Verbobonc entirely. Hilewy invites the party to speak once. Only once.

**Consequence:** their words become part of the district’s political memory.

### Secrets & DM Tools

##### The Palace Has Rules (Even When Unspoken)

*   Violence inside is scandal; scandal is leverage.
*   The staff will intervene with **courtesy**, not force—unless the Free Alliance authorizes otherwise.
*   Any “private meeting” is never fully private; witnesses exist for the sake of future truth.

##### The Listening Glamours

Use illusion-work as a soft security system:

*   A fairy-lamp dims when someone lies in a certain corridor.
*   A flower wilts near concealed weapons.
*   A hallway seems to “encourage” guests away from forbidden doors.

Nothing overt. Just _pressure_.

* * *

#### Rewards (What the Party Gains Here)

Not treasure—**access**.

*   A letter of introduction to a gnome merchant house or banker
*   Access to a single river manifest, caravan schedule, or tariff record
*   A “sealed favor” from Lord Hilewy (redeemable once, socially potent)
*   Quiet admission into Free Alliance confidence—enough to open doors across Gnomesberg

##### Notable People

**Lord Hilewy (Proprietor; Council Voice)**

A genial patriarch in public, a calculating civic operator in private. Hilewy collects favors the way other folk collect coins—quietly, carefully, and with a smile that makes you think you volunteered.

*   Calls everyone “friend” while never granting true familiarity.
*   Laughs softly when others grow angry—anger is useful; he prefers it unspent.
*   Offers three solutions: one costly, one embarrassing, one binding.

**Prince Jimm Pithriggen (Patron)**

Jimm’s influence here is felt more than seen—an honored guest-room always kept ready, special staff who “serve when asked,” and invitations delivered with exquisite courtesy. When Jimm wants an introduction, a meeting, or a quiet test of a newcomer’s manners, Hilewy’s Palace is the stage.

**Staff of the Palace (All Gnomes, All Trained)**

Every server is an observer. Every porter is a messenger. Nobody repeats what they heard… unless the Free Alliance decides it must be repeated.
