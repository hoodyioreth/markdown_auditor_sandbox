C11 Verbobonc Constabulary
==========================

##### _Guardians of the Viscounty_

The Verbobonc Constabulary is the _spine of law_ within the Viscounty—an ancient feudal institution where noble obligation, land stewardship, and justice intertwine. From this stern headquarters, constables administer oaths, adjudicate disputes, coordinate rural patrols, and quietly track threats to the Viscount’s peace. Unlike the city-focused Vigil Wardens, the Constabulary thinks in **fiefs, harvests, roads, and obligations**, making it a critical node for any campaign involving banditry, cult activity, noble corruption, or unrest beyond the city walls.

##### Approaching the Constabulary

The street narrows as you approach the Constabulary Hall, the stone beneath your boots worn smooth by generations of petitioners and guards alike. The building rises taller than its neighbors—solid, narrow, and uncompromising—its slate roof steep and its windows arrow-slit narrow.  
  
A carved sigil above the door depicts a rooted oak bound by a chain, its meaning unmistakable: order, endurance, and restraint. Armed constables stand watch at the entry, their tabards bearing the Viscount’s colors, eyes alert but weary—men and women accustomed to grievances more dangerous than blades. This is not a place of heroics. It is a place of records.

#### **The Constabulary Hall**

Inside, the Constabulary feels closer to a court than a barracks:

*   **Public Petition Chamber:** Long benches, iron braziers, and clerks hunched over ledgers recording fines, oaths, and warrants.
*   **Meeting Rooms:** Used by regional constables to coordinate patrols, levy obligations, and settle disputes between freeholders.
*   **Training Yard:** A narrow stone yard behind the hall where mounted tactics, riot drills, and arrest procedures are practiced.
*   **Records Vault:** A guarded archive of land claims, arrest writs, tithe obligations, and sealed investigations—some dating back centuries.

* * *

### **Law, Not Watchmen**

The Constabulary is **not** the city watch.

*   The **Vigil Wardens of Verbobonc** police streets, crowds, and markets.
*   The **Constabulary** governs **jurisdiction**—who owes whom, who has violated feudal law, and which noble or freeholder must answer for unrest.

When the Vigil Wardens arrest someone important, they deliver them _here_.

#### Historical Context

*   **Foundation and Evolution:** Established in the early days of the Viscounty to manage the sprawling lands and maintain order among the populace.
*   **Historical Milestones:** Played a crucial role in shaping the feudal landscape of Verbobonc, adapting over centuries to the changing political dynamics of the region.

#### Political and Social Relationships

*   **Integration with Nobility:** Most constables are titled nobles, integrating deep feudal traditions with the responsibilities of law enforcement.
*   **Interactions with the City:** Works closely with city officials of the [Vigil Wardens of Verbobonc](vigil-wardens-of-verbobonc-organization.md) and the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) to ensure that urban and rural policies are in harmony.

#### Operational Motivation

*   **Feudal Duties:** Ensures that each constable fulfills their feudal duties to their fiefs and senior lords, maintaining the balance of power.
*   **Law and Order:** Committed to upholding the [Legal Edicts of Verbobonc: The Codified Justice System in the Viscounty](legal-edicts-of-verbobonc-document.md) of the Viscounty, managing serfs, and protecting the rights of enfranchised peasants.

#### Notable Figures

*   **Constable Sir Reginald Morn:** A noble of high repute who often presides over the most significant meetings and is known for his fair but firm approach. **Lady Constable Elara Thorne:** Known for her progressive views on peasant rights and her efforts to reform serfdom practices within her fief.

### **Constabulary Hierarchy (Simplified)**

*   **Senior Constable (Viscounty):** Oversees all constables
*   **Landed Constables:** Nobles responsible for law in their fiefs
*   **Appointed Constables:** Professional officers assigned to roads, towns, and borderlands
*   **Sergeants & Riders:** Armed enforcers and patrol leaders

* * *

#### **Campaign Use & Plot Hooks**

*   **Temple of Elemental Evil Tie-In:**
*   Disappearances, grain thefts, and road violence are logged here—but misclassified
*   Certain constables may be compromised, misled, or politically constrained
*   **Noble Ambitions:**
*   Rival houses pressure the Constabulary to delay warrants
*   Adventurers may be deputized _unofficially_ to act where law cannot be seen acting
*   **Player Leverage:**
*   Evidence, sworn testimony, or recovered ledgers can shift political balance

* * *

#### **DM Guidance**

*   The Constabulary rewards **order**, not heroics
*   Violence without writs causes problems later
*   PCs who respect procedure gain access to deeper investigations
*   PCs who defy it may still succeed—but will be remembered

##### **Sir Reginald Morn**

Senior Constable of the Viscounty

*   Known for measured judgment and strict adherence to precedent
*   Will work with adventurers—but only if outcomes stabilize the region
*   Increasingly concerned about _organized unrest_ masquerading as banditry

##### **Lady Constable Elara Thorne**

Constable of a Mixed Freehold

*   Advocates peasant protections and clearer legal standing for free laborers
*   Quietly investigates abuses tied to noble estates
*   A potential ally for parties exposing corruption or cult influence

##### **First Meeting at the Constabulary**

You step into a hall of stone and ink. Iron braziers burn low despite the daylight, their smoke clinging to the rafters. Long benches hold farmers, messengers, and grim-faced riders, all waiting their turn. The scrape of quills on parchment never ceases.  
  
At a central desk stands a senior constable in chain and tabard, one hand resting on a ledger thick enough to break a toe. Their gaze lifts as you approach—not curious, not hostile—simply practiced. Here, justice does not hurry.

##### **Option A: Senior Constable (Authority-Focused)**

**Senior Constable:  
**“Names first. Then business. In that order.”

_(They open the ledger without looking down.)_

“Banditry, land disputes, broken oaths, unpaid levies—we hear them all. If your trouble bleeds beyond your own boots, it belongs here.”

_(A measured pause.)_

“Be advised: false testimony carries penalties. So does vigilantism. If you seek writ, warrant, or lawful leave to act… say so plainly.”

* * *

##### **Option B: Senior Clerk (Bureaucracy-Focused)**

**Senior Clerk:  
**“If you’re here to complain, take a token and sit. If you’re here to confess, speak softly. If you’re here on official business…”

_(They finally look up.)_

“…then you’ll tell me whose peace has been broken, and how far you think it reaches.”

_(A thin, tired smile.)_

“Heroes come through daily. Evidence lasts longer.”

##### **Optional Follow-Up (If PCs Mention Cultists or Unrest)**

**Constable/Clerk:  
**“We’ve heard whispers. Roads troubled. Coin moving oddly. Names that do not stay put on paper.”

_(Ledger closes with a soft thud.)_

“If you have proof, we will listen. If you have suspicion… we will remember.”

*   Reckless PCs are logged—and may face delays later
*   Mentioning **Temple of Elemental Evil**, **organized banditry**, or **noble interference** triggers private follow-up meetings
