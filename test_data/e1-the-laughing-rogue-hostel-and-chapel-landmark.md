E1 The Laughing Rogue Hostel and Chapel
=======================================

_Hostel • Courtyard-Chapel of Olidammara • Stage of Revels & Rumors_

A cheap, beloved hostel-chapel dedicated to Olidammara—where laughter is a sacrament, wine is excellent and plentiful, and the courtyard stage makes a perfect DM tool for rumors, coded messages, and spy-craft disguised as merriment.

##### Approach The Laughing Rogue

A weather-beaten wooden sign swingeth over a thick door: a grinning mask with bright eyes that seem to follow thee. Beyond, the place openeth wide—an open square courtyard beneath the sky, ringed with a covered walk, and loud with fiddles, stamping feet, and the kind of laughter that maketh a man forget his purse for half a heartbeat.  
  
At the center stands a low stage. A singer bows. A juggler maketh three knives dance like minnows. Wine is poured without ceremony—yet the very pouring hath the feel of prayer.

### A Place of Revels and Worship

The Hostel is centered around a large chapel and a squat compound, with a weather-beaten wooden sign hanging above the door. Entering through the thick wooden door reveals a large, open square courtyard surrounded by a covered walkway, filled with singing, dancing, and performances.

##### Key Features

*   **Chapel and Compound:** Large chapel with adjoining squat compound
*   **Courtyard:** Open to the sky, surrounded by a covered walkway
*   **Entertainment:** Continuous performances on a central stage

#### History

_Founding and Legacy_

The Laughing Rogue was established by a devout priest of Olidammara, serving both as a hostel and a place of worship. Over the years, it has become a favored spot for adventurers, bandits, and those seeking refuge or entertainment.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Founding:** Established by a priest of Olidammara
*   **Legacy:** Known for its vibrant atmosphere and free performances
*   **Reputation:** Favored by adventurers and disreputable characters

### What This Place Is

The Laughing Rogue is a **cross-shaped chapel joined to a squat hostel-compound**, set a little back from the road so the noise may bloom without choking traffic. It is at once shrine, inn, rumor-hub, and refuge—favored by adventurers and disreputable folk precisely because it asks few questions… and hears many answers.

**Faith in practice:** Olidammara’s worship is joy, tricks, music, and the refusal to let solemn powers own the soul. The “chapel” here is not hushed—its holiness is laughter that breaks fear’s teeth.

##### Layout & Key Areas (DM-facing)

**1) The Entry Door & Mask-Sign  
**A thick wooden door; above it, the grinning mask. Regulars touch two fingers to their lips and then the sign—half salute, half jest.

**2) The Open Courtyard  
**The heart: open to the sky, surrounded by a covered walkway. The courtyard is always busy, always watched, always useful. Performers cycle continually on a central stage.

**3) The Chapel Proper (Cross-Arm Hall)  
**A broad hall with simple benches and a small altar bearing the grinning mask. “Prayers” are often sung and improvised rather than recited from any stiff book.

**4) Lodgings Wing  
**Accommodations are plain but safe: **four private rooms** and **six large common rooms**—cheap by design, welcoming to “any soul that can laugh.”

**5) The Wine Store & Cask-Cellar  
**The wine is famously good and affordable; the cellar is the reason caravans and spies both choose this place. (Also the best spot for clandestine meetings “about inventory.”)

* * *

#### Goods & Services

**Food:** Limited—bread plates and fruit, meant to keep folk merry rather than full.  
**Drink:** Excellent, affordable wine; the “Ceremony of the Cork” is performed so often it looks like mere habit.  
**Entertainment:** Free performances—comedy, songs, pranks, and audience mischief.

* * *

##### Notable Events at The Laughing Rogue Hostel and Chapel

##### 1\. Brewfest Revelry

The hostel hosts an extravagant week-long celebration during Brewfest, featuring abundant wine, lively music, and joyous dancing. This event attracts revelers from across Verbobonc.

##### 2\. The Great Escape Play

A comedic reenactment of Olidammara's legendary escape from the archmage Zagig. The performance involves clever pranks, engaging the audience in laughter and fun.

##### 3\. New Moon Follies

On nights of the new moon, the hostel stages a three-act comedy filled with humorous antics and improvisational skits, celebrating the spirit of Olidammara.

##### 4\. Taste of a Hundred Years

An annual event where a century-old cask of wine is opened and shared among the patrons, symbolizing the hostel's commitment to tradition and joy.

##### 5\. Masquerade Ball

An enchanting evening where guests don elaborate masks and costumes, participating in dances, games, and pranks in honor of Olidammara's playful nature.

##### 6\. Bardic Competition

A lively competition where bards from near and far perform songs, tales, and tricks, vying for the title of "Laughing Bard of the Year."

##### 7\. Feast of the Doubling Dare

A post-New Year celebration involving daring challenges and feats, encouraging guests to partake in bold and entertaining activities.

**Fenthwick Merryfoot (Owner & High Priest)**

Short, round, perpetually smiling—jolly and mischievous, yet sharp as a lockpick. His stated mission is joy; his practical mission is keeping Ryemend’s travelers talking.

*   keep the hostel a haven
*   learn news before trouble ripens
*   embarrass bullies with laughter rather than steel
*   **Secret:** Fenthwick keeps a quiet tally of “who asks after rooms with only one door” and “who pays in Furyondy coin.” He never calls it spying; he calls it good hosting.

Fenthwick: _“Ho! Come in, come in—thy boots look weary, and weary boots deserve wine.”_

Fenthwick: _“Aye, we’ve beds enough—yet mind thee: the only fee I truly demand is a laugh afore the night is done.”_

Fenthwick: _“If thou must whisper, whisper on the stage. No one suspects truth when it is told as jest.”_
