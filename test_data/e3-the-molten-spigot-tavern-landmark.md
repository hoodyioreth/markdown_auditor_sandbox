E3 The Molten Spigot Tavern
===========================

A roaring hearth-tavern built around an old **smelter-room and iron flue**, famed for hot-poured ale and “hearth-broth” that chaseth river-chill from the bones. The Molten Spigot is where Ryemend’s **boatmen, drovers, Consortium clerks, and foreign listeners** all end up—because warmth makes tongues loose.

##### Approach The Molten Spigot Tavern

Heat greeteth thee before any host doth—thick and honest, like a smith’s breath. The tavern is low-roofed and dark-beamed, and at its heart standeth a great iron hood and flue, blackened with years, as though this place once swallowed smoke for a living.  
  
A long bar of scarred oak runs along one wall. Behind it, brass taps gleam red in the firelight—spigots warmed by hidden pipes—so that ale is drawn not cold, but steaming. Voices mingle with the crackle of logs and the soft hiss of hot metal cooling in water. A boatman laughs too loud. A clerk in neat boots laughs too late.

### What This Place Is

The Molten Spigot began as a small **smelter-house** attached to a riverside workshop. When the trade shifted and the workshop failed, the flue and hearth remained—and folk soon discovered that a warm hall by the river is worth more than a quiet one. The place now serves as Ryemend’s **cold-weather anchor**: if you want to find someone in Ryemend, you try the Spigot first when fog lies heavy on the Velverdyva.

**DM Use:** It is a perfect node for:

*   rumor delivery that feels natural
*   spycraft in plain sight
*   social pressure (“everyone’s watching, pretending not to”)
*   meeting “ordinary” people who carry extraordinary information

##### Layout & Key Areas

**1) The Molten Bar  
**A long oak counter, scorched at the edge where hot mugs are set down. Brass taps feed from warmed piping; the house specialty is **hot-poured ale** (safe, not boiling—served in thick clay cups).

**2) The Hearth Pit  
**A sunken circle of stone benches around the central heat. Riverfolk take the inner ring. Outsiders sit the outer ring until proven.

**3) The Flue Gallery (Upstairs Walk)  
**A narrow loft-walk overlooking the room, originally for workshop hoists. Now it’s where quiet patrons sit with a view of every door.

**4) The “Cooling Room” (Back Booths)  
**Two curtained alcoves behind the bar where deals are struck “out of the draft.” Sound carries strangely here—easy to mishear on purpose.

**5) The Yard Door  
**A side exit into a small service yard—useful for tails, escapes, and “coincidental” meetings.

#### History: A Staple of Ryemend

The Molten Spigot has been a cornerstone of the Ryemend Quarter for decades, offering a place for locals to unwind and share news about their crops and weather.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Establishment:** Founded decades ago as a gathering spot for locals
*   **Evolution:** Maintained its rustic charm and reputation for good food and drink
*   **Legacy:** Known for its welcoming atmosphere and hearty meals

### Goods and Services

_Hearty Meals and Fine Brews_

The Molten Spigot offers a wonderful selection of local brews and hearty meals that cater to both humans and gnomes. The food is simple but always good, and the beers are never watered down.

##### Key Offerings

*   **Local Brews:** Fine selection of beers and ales
*   **Hearty Meals:** Roasted pig or boar, generous portions of human and gnome dishes
*   **Specialties:** Roasted meats and piled-high plates that ensure no one leaves hun
*   **Affordable Prices:** Average costs for food and drinks

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   Warm refuge after foggy river travel
*   Meet a contact without screaming “meeting a contact”
*   Learn who is hiring guards, buying supplies, or paying for quiet riders
*   Track someone by asking after their drink, their seat, or their accent

##### If They Linger

Owain will test them with seating:

*   Put them where honest folk sit if he trusts them
*   Put them where everyone can see them if he doubts them
*   Put them under the Flue Gallery if someone is measuring them

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A fight nearly starts when a “clerk” mocks a riverhand—Owain intervenes with terrifying calm
*   A messenger collapses by the hearth, half-frozen, clutching a wet leather tube
*   Someone slips into the yard door—someone else follows two breaths later

#### Owain Pritchard

_The Affable Owner_

Owain Pritchard, the owner of The Molten Spigot, is a robust and friendly man of Oeridian descent. His warm personality and dedication to his tavern have made it a popular spot in Ryemend.

##### Character Traits

*   **Description:** Robust, friendly, and welcoming
*   **Personality:** Affable, generous, and community-oriented
*   **Motivation:** Creating a welcoming environment for locals and travelers alike

##### Character Traits

##### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (12)

1.  A “merchant’s clerk” pays in **Furyondy coin** but swears he’s never crossed the border.
2.  The Silver Consortium is about to enforce a **new seal** at the bridge—folk fear what it truly marks.
3.  A barge arrived light but sat deep—meaning hidden weight below.
4.  Owain keeps a list of patrons who ask for the Cooling Room **without ordering food**.
5.  A drover claims bandits now take folk alive “for buyers.”
6.  A man in clean boots asks after “quiet rooms” and “quiet horses” in the same hour.
7.  The hearth smoke sometimes turns greenish for a breath—old flue chemistry… or something worse.
8.  A riverhand saw lanterns hooded on the bridge at dawn—no heralds, no patrol call.
9.  A gnome runner from Gnomeburg trades messages for “one hot cup and no questions.”
10.  Sister Nerris recognizes a PC’s accent and goes suddenly stern: “Thou’rt not from where thou say’st.”
11.  A drunk sings a verse about a **temple in the south**—then apologizes too quickly.
12.  A “courier” leaves a chalk mark beneath the third spigot—someone else erases it with their sleeve.
