E8 Dieg Manor
=============

**DIEG MANOR**

_A fully rewritten, DM‑ready chapter with boxed text for every keyed area_

**Overview**

Dieg Manor is a two‑story wooden residence recently repainted a pale gray. The poor paint job leaves faint red streaks beneath the surface, giving the building a veined, unhealthy appearance. The grounds are overgrown, the walkway is weed‑choked, and the entire property radiates neglect and secrecy.

The manor serves as the hidden base of **Helthrax**, a high priest of [Iuz](iuz-person.md) impersonating Carlan Dieg. His brigands, spies, and assassins use the building as a staging ground for a coordinated strike on Verbobonc during the plague.

Night infiltration is strongly encouraged; guards and servants are less active after dark.

##### Approaching the Dieg Manor

A pale grey house standeth behind a fence that should be proud—yet the yard within is wild with weed and neglect. The paint upon the boards is new enough to stink, yet thin enough that faint red streaks bleed through like veins beneath skin. No lamp burns in the windows. No dog barks. No servant chatters. Only stillness… and the sense that the stillness is listening.

#### **What Dieg Manor Is in the Verbobonc Campaign Guide (576 CY)**

Dieg Manor is not a planar set-piece or a one-off curiosity. In this campaign, it is an Iuzite wedge driven into Ryemend—built to do three things:

*   **Control the river-throat:** Ryemend’s wharves and crossings are the easiest place to seed panic, bribe guards, and move agents.
*   **Break trust:** A “noble” face (Carlan Dieg) makes conspiracies travel faster than truth.
*   **Create a distraction war:** While the Temple’s forces and agents turn southward and inland, Iuz’s cult seeks to make Verbobonc look weak—divided between riverfront chaos and countryside threats.

The manor thus becomes a sandbox tool: **a conspiracy dungeon** that can be discovered early, revisited later, and used to explain why “random” troubles in Ryemend keep happening.

**Replace the old Fate of Istus framing:** Helthrax is not here because fate demands it—he is here because **strategy** demands it.

### Manor Description

**Entry Hall:** An Inviting Entrance

The Entry Hall is designed to project modest civic respectability, not wealth. Carlan Dieg’s family was never ostentatious, and Helthrax deliberately preserves this restraint to avoid scrutiny. The stone floor is swept clean but uneven with age. A faded runner rug leads inward, its edges worn thin. Wall sconces hold beeswax candles that are always lit, regardless of hour—an affectation of hospitality masking watchfulness. The air smells faintly of incense meant to suggest Rao or Zilchus, though a careful cleric may note the blend is wrong.

A narrow side table holds a bowl for gloves and hats, along with a ledger recording visitors—written in a careful hand that does not match Carlan Dieg’s old signatures elsewhere in the city.

This room is often unattended, but Margus or Hyrmie may observe from deeper within the manor if strangers linger too long.

##### Flavor Text: Entry Hall

The door opens upon a cool stone hall, neat and plainly appointed. A narrow rug runs down its center, the fabric worn thin by many careful steps. Candles gutter softly in iron sconces, their light steady and watchful rather than warm. The air bears the faint trace of incense—something meant to comfort, though it stirs uneasily in the nose. For a heartbeat, the house feels as though it is listening.

##### Foyer

_A Space of Quiet Sophistication_

The Foyer serves as a social filter—a place where guests are greeted, judged, and redirected. Helthrax uses this space to control movement within the manor, rarely allowing visitors to wander unescorted. Simple wooden benches line the walls. A narrow stair rises to the upper floor, its banister polished smooth by years of use. A small devotional niche stands opposite the stair, displaying a neutral, inoffensive symbol that could plausibly represent multiple lawful faiths. It is intentionally ambiguous.

This is where Helthrax (as Carlan Dieg) most often makes his first appearance—calm, courteous, and faintly distant. Conversations here are polite but shallow. Any probing questions are gently deflected toward food, drink, or “more suitable rooms.”

##### Flavor Test: Foyer

The hall opens into a broader chamber where voices seem to soften of their own accord. A stairway climbs along one wall, its rail worn smooth by careful hands. Simple benches rest beneath shuttered windows, and a small devotional alcove glimmers with candlelight. The room feels arranged for waiting—for being received. Not unwelcome, yet never entirely at ease.

##### Dining Hall

_An Elegant Gathering Place_

The Dining Hall is where Dieg Manor performs its greatest deception. A long oak table dominates the room, scarred by age but carefully maintained. High-backed chairs are arranged with deliberate symmetry. The hearth is kept burning during meals, lending warmth and civility. Meals served here are wholesome, locally sourced—bread, river fish, vegetables from the inner farmlands—meant to reinforce Dieg’s image as a pillar of the district.

Yet this is also where Iuzite clergy dine together when privacy allows. Late at night, the tone shifts: candles are reduced, voices drop, and plans are whispered beneath the crackle of the fire.

This room may host Therkle and brigands under the guise of hired guards or labor captains, especially when shipments or violence are being discussed.

##### Flavor Text: Dining Hall

A long table stretches the length of the chamber, its dark surface polished to a muted shine. The scent of hearth-smoke and fresh bread lingers in the air, comforting and familiar. High-backed chairs stand neatly in place, as though awaiting guests who are expected—and measured. The fire pops softly in the hearth, and for a moment the room feels almost honest. Almost.

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   Rumors of a noble manor gone wrong: shuttered, quiet, “new paint that bleeds.”
*   A Ryemend contact vanishes after visiting “Lord Dieg.”
*   The party follows a bribe trail from E5 House of Cards or E7 wharfside contacts to this address.

##### If They Linger

*   A servant is seen moving behind a shutter at odd hours.
*   Someone in the market square starts “helpfully” warning people away from the manor (a lookout).

##### What Goes Wrong

*   PCs are spotted fleeing: assassins disguised as entertainers are sent (your PDF’s juggler hit-squad idea is great—use it as a district consequence).

### **General Notes for Running the Manor**

*   The **upper floor is mostly empty**, with only one stairway leading down (Area 4).
*   If the PCs are spotted fleeing, Helthrax dispatches **three 7th‑level thieves disguised as jugglers** to assassinate them with poisoned daggers.

### **Overall GM Guidance**

**Tone & Atmosphere**

*   Lean into _paranoia, secrecy, and corruption_.
*   The manor should feel like a place where something is deeply wrong — not overtly haunted, but _tainted_.
*   Use sensory cues: musty air, faint rot, stale food, scuffed floors, and the eerie quiet of a house that should be lived in but isn’t.

**Player Expectations**

*   PCs should feel like investigators uncovering a conspiracy, not dungeon crawlers looting a ruin.
*   Encourage stealth and planning; reward clever infiltration.
*   Make the consequences of noise or sloppy tactics meaningful — this dungeon reacts.

### **Encounter Flow & Escalation**

This is the _intended_ flow of Dieg Manor as written — cleaned up and clarified so you can run it without flipping pages.

##### **PHASE 1 — Infiltration (Areas 1–3)**

**Goal:** Establish tension and reveal that the manor is occupied.

**1\. Door / 2. Hallway / 3. Drawing Room**: This is where PCs learn the manor is **occupied** and hostile. Therkle is your breadcrumb to Ryemend’s other vice nodes (E5 House of Cards, E3 Molten Spigot) by way of money and contact trails.

**Campaign tie:** Therkle’s “map marking the inn” is not a random note—it points to where couriers and bribes are exchanged in the district.

**Key Beats**

1.  PCs scout the exterior → discover the shutter window clue.
2.  Entry through the window or door → stealth vs. noise matters.
3.  First combat in Area 3 (Therkle + brigands).
4.  PCs realize the manor is actively used by hostile forces.

**GM Tips**

*   If they enter quietly, reward them with surprise or partial intel.
*   If they enter loudly, escalate quickly — guards mobilize.
*   Therkle is the first breadcrumb linking the inn to the manor.

##### **PHASE 2 — Exploration & Clues (Areas 4–13)**

**Goal:** Build the mystery and reveal Helthrax’s deception.

**Campaign Intergration:** Helthrax uses that symbol as a **remote anchor** for scrying and influence across the riverfront. The 25% teleport effect becomes a terrifying risk for greedy PCs—and a meaningful tool if they try to trap Helthrax by baiting him.

**Key Beats**

*   PCs explore the upper floor and living areas.
*   **8\. Servant’s Room (Hyrmie)** is the _key_ to converting this from a slaughterhouse to an investigation. He can confirm the “lord” isn’t Dieg and point them to the cellar without meta-DM nudging.
*   **13\. Master Bedroom**: the **symbol of Iuz** is the campaign’s “this is real” moment. Play its aura as nauseating wrongness; let good PCs recoil.
*   They find signs of Dieg’s disappearance (portrait spat on, unused rooms).
*   Hyrmie (Area 8) provides the first _narrative exposition_ if handled well.
*   The Master Bedroom (Area 13) reveals the **symbol of Iuz** — the biggest clue yet.

**GM Tips**

*   Hyrmie is your best tool for steering the party toward the cellar.
*   The symbol of Iuz should feel dangerous and wrong — emphasize its aura.
*   This phase should feel like peeling back layers of a conspiracy.

##### **PHASE 3 — Descent Into Danger (Areas 14–17)**

**Ryemend tie:** If the cellar fully mobilizes, you can have “help” from above arrive later—shady dockhands, paid watchmen, or a rival faction trying to contain the mess.

**Goal:** Shift from investigation to tactical dungeon crawl.

**Key Beats**

*   PCs descend into the cellar.
*   The Savage Dog encounter tests stealth and planning.
*   Area 15’s guards can trigger a **full‑dungeon alert** if the runner escapes.
*   Brigands and leaders in Areas 16–17 form the “military core” of Helthrax’s forces.

**GM Tips**

*   The runner is a _pressure valve_:
*   If he escapes → the dungeon becomes a gauntlet.
*   If he’s stopped → PCs can control the pace.
*   Use sound: barking, shouts, footsteps, metal scraping.
*   Let players feel the tension of a living, reacting enemy force.

##### **PHASE 4 — Cult Stronghold (Areas 18–22)**

**Campaign** **Integration change:** Instead of “fate,” the pools are used to watch:

*   river barges,
*   Ryemend market gossip,
*   and key individuals the cult wants blackmailed or replaced.

**Goal:** Reveal the cult of Iuz and raise the stakes.

**Key Beats**

*   Margus (Area 18) is the first major spellcaster and a cunning foe.
*   **20 Scrying Pools**: These are the manor’s true value—intel, and the brackish pool deepen the Iuz theme.
*   Underpriests (Area 22) show the fanaticism of Helthrax’s followers.

**GM Tips**

*   Margus should feel dangerous and slippery — use darkness, invisibility, and wraithform intelligently.
*   Jamboo (Area 22) is a wildcard:
*   He may betray the cult
*   He may betray the PCs
*   He may betray _everyone_
*   This phase should feel like the heart of the cult’s operations.

##### **PHASE 5 — The Elemental & the Shrine (Areas 23–26)**

**Goal:** Introduce moral choices and potential allies.

**Temple integration:** This is a great place to seed the theme: different evils compete. Iuz’s cult is not the Temple—yet both profit from chaos.

**Key Beats**

*   **24\. Earth Elemental**: Keep it as a roleplay/moral choice—free it and gain an ally against Helthrax.
*   Freeing it grants a powerful but temporary ally.
*   The Shrine to Iuz (Area 25) is a symbolic centerpiece.

**GM Tips**

*   The elemental is a roleplaying opportunity, not just a stat block.
*   If freed, it should feel like a moment of triumph and hope.
*   If left imprisoned, it becomes a future threat during Helthrax’s attack.

##### **PHASE 6 — The Gauntlet (Areas 27–32)**

**Goal:** Build toward the final confrontation.

**Key Beats**

*   Hall of Victories (Area 27) Frame this as Helthrax’s intended “display” after the coming strike—mummified officials as political terror.
*   The dracolisk (Area 28) is a major combat spike.
*   The troll keeper and undead (Areas 30–32) form a brutal attrition sequence.

**GM Tips**

*   This is where the dungeon becomes deadly.
*   Use the dracolisk’s roar to create urgency — the troll mobilizes immediately.
*   The undead should feel overwhelming unless the PCs are smart about chokepoints.

##### **PHASE 7 — Final Confrontation (Area 33)**

**Goal:** Deliver a climactic showdown with Helthrax.

**Key Beats**

*   Helthrax listens and prepares before the PCs arrive.
*   He opens with **blade barrier** to divide the party.
*   He uses curses, holds, and slay living to disable key PCs.
*   Helthrax is cunning, not suicidal. He will fight to break the party—then flee via word of recall if losing.

**GM Tips**

*   Helthrax is cunning, not suicidal.
*   He should feel like a high‑level cleric with demonic backing.
*   If he escapes, the campaign continues — this is not a failure, but a story beat.

##### **PHASE 8 — Resolution (Areas 34–35)**

**Goal:** Provide closure and rewards.

**Key Beats**

*   **Crypt (34)**: the true corpse of Carlan Dieg is your proof and your horror.
*   **Armory (35)**: tangible payoff; it also tells the PCs this was meant to arm a coordinated strike.

**GM Tips**

*   Let the players feel the weight of what they uncovered.
*   The staff of curing is a major campaign‑level reward.
*   The illusion of Iuz should feel ominous and prophetic.
