### A Sanctuary for Adventurers in Verbobonc

The Bronze Unicorn Inn is Verbobonc’s premier adventurer-centric lodging, a lively and well-guarded social hub where mercenaries, explorers, guild agents, and foreign sellswords gather to trade stories, secure contracts, and rest between expeditions. Closely affiliated with the Adventurers Guild, the inn serves as both a refuge and an informal extension of the guild’s recruitment and information network within the Foreign Quarter.

##### Arrival: The Bronze Unicorn Inn

The Bronze Unicorn announces itself before you ever touch the door — laughter, music, and the thrum of voices spilling into the street through thick stained-glass windows. The building’s stonework is warm and welcoming, its carved unicorn sigil polished smooth by countless passing hands.  
  
Inside, the air is rich with foreign spices, oiled leather, and pipe smoke. Heavy stone beams frame a packed taproom where adventurers crowd long tables, trading boasts and ballads in equal measure. Weapons hang openly, not as threats, but as trophies — and every wall bears signed relics of past deeds, each telling a story someone nearby is eager to share.

### A Stage for Every Adventurer

The Bronze Unicorn Inn caters to adventurers and mercenaries seeking relaxation and camaraderie, offering a sanctuary where they can share their tales and adventures. The inn is known for its rich history, with hundreds of signed memorabilia items decorating the walls, left behind by previous patrons. The lively taproom features foreign bards and travelers who entertain guests with stories and music, while a large, interactive map of Verbobonc on the wall guides newcomers to the city’s key attractions.

##### Legacy of the Bronze Unicorn

*   **Inn for Heroes:** The Bronze Unicorn caters to adventurers and mercenaries seeking relaxation and camaraderie.
*   **Traveler’s Memorabilia Collection:** Hundreds of signed items donated by patrons adorn the walls, showcasing the inn's rich history.

##### Entertainment for All

*   **Foreign Bards and Tales:** Foreign bards tell tales of their adventures and far-off lands on the taproom stage.
*   **Interactive Map:** A large, but crude, map of the city is marked with scribbles and additions by guests.

### **Atmosphere & Function**

The Bronze Unicorn is **never quiet** — even on slow nights. The noise is part of the charm, masking negotiations, rivalries, and whispered plans beneath song and cheer.

*   **Taproom:** Constantly full; standing room is common
*   **Stage:** Open to bards, storytellers, and returning adventurers
*   **Memorabilia Walls:** Signed weapons, shields, helms, maps, and curios
*   **Maps & Notices:** A crude but frequently updated map of Verbobonc marks routes, dangers, and guild postings
*   **Vault & Storage:** Secure lockers and vaults for valuables and recovered relics

This is a place where **reputation matters** — and where characters may be recognized before they are trusted.

#### **Relationship to the Adventurers Guild (B26)**

The Bronze Unicorn functions as the **unofficial hostel and social annex** of the Adventurers Guild.

*   Guild runners reserve rooms here for visiting companies
*   Message couriers use the inn to reach guild-affiliated parties discreetly
*   Job offers often circulate here before appearing formally at B26
*   Adventurers expelled from the guild may still drink here — but under watch

This makes the inn a **natural launch point** for:

*   New contracts
*   Rival adventuring parties
*   Rumors tied to dungeon sites, caravan routes, or political unrest**Meeting Rooms:** Separate meeting rooms attached to larger quad rooms.

#### **DM Guidance: Using the Bronze Unicorn**

Use this location to:

*   Introduce **competing adventuring parties**
*   Seed **guild politics and favoritism**
*   Allow PCs to earn reputation through storytelling or display
*   Provide safe but noisy cover for secret meetings
*   Foreshadow expeditions tied to Temple of Elemental Evil activity

The Bronze Unicorn should feel like a place where **everyone is watching everyone else** — but smiling while they do it.

### History: A Storied Inn with a Heroic Past

Founded over 20 years ago by Fallnore and Elinore Constance, the Bronze Unicorn Inn quickly established itself as a refuge for adventurers and mercenaries. The inn became renowned due to the influx of adventurers in recent years, filling to overflowing most of the year. Partnering with the Adventurers Guild, the inn provides bookings via the guild's messenger service and has collected hundreds of signed memorabilia items during its golden age.

##### Founding Era

*   **Original Establishment:** Founded by Fallnore and Elinore Constance over 20 years ago.
*   **Adventurer Refuge:** Quickly became known as a refuge for adventurers and mercenaries.

##### Golden Age of Adventurers

*   **Height of Popularity:** Gained renown due to the influx of adventurers over the past 20 years.
*   **Memorabilia Collection Growth:** Collected hundreds of signed items during this period.

##### Modern Times

*   **Adventurers Guild Affiliation:** Partnered with the [B26 Adventures Guild](b26-adventures-guild-landmark.md), allowing bookings via the guild's messenger service.
*   **Overflowing Demand:** Fills to overflowing most of the year, requiring guests to book ahead.

##### Welcomed by Tessa Freely (Head Waitress and Entertainer)

You step across the threshold of the Bronze Unicorn, and at once the air warms—woodsmoke, spiced ale, and the low murmur of many tongues mingling as one. Laughter lifts toward the rafters, chased by the bright trill of a stringed tune. From between the tables a woman turns, skirts swaying, her smile already upon you as though she had been expecting your feet upon these stones all along. Her eyes are quick, weighing you in a breath, and finding you welcome.

**Dialogue (Tessa Freely):  
**_“Ah—there you are. Gods be kind, I thought I heard the door sigh for newcomers.”  
_She dips a small, easy curtsey, half-mocking, half-sincere.  
_“Thou’rt within the Bronze Unicorn now, and I am Tessa Freely—voice of the hall, keeper of cups, and friend to all who mind their manners.”  
_Her smile widens, conspiratorial.  
_“Sit, then. Lay down thy road-dust and thy worries both. There is music enough to soften hard hearts, ale enough to loosen stiff tongues, and—”_ she glances about, lowering her voice just a touch, _“—company enough that no one need feel alone, unless they choose it.”  
_Straightening, she gestures toward the room.  
_“Now tell me, travelers… shall thy first memory of this house be song, supper, or something stronger?”_
