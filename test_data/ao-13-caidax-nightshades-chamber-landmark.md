AO-13. Caidax Nightshade’s Chamber
==================================

_Former Knight’s Cell — Now the Sorcerer’s Sanctum_

This chamber once housed one of the sworn Knights of the Azure Oath. The room was simple and austere, intended for quiet reflection and disciplined rest. Now it belongs to **Caidax Nightshade**, the monastery’s resident occultist and arcane advisor to Varnor Earthcaller.

The chamber has been transformed into a dark scholar’s den where scrolls, crystals, ritual implements, and fragments of stone runes lie scattered across tables and shelves. Caidax studies the **geomantic forces beneath the monastery**, seeking ways to harness the power of the buried altar and the whispering chasm known as the **Black Mouth**.

Where knights once prayed for courage and virtue, Caidax now listens for voices rising through the stone.

* * *

#### DM Description

This chamber once served as a simple knight’s sleeping quarters. The stone walls still bear faint carvings of lightning bolts and sacred oaths dedicated to **Heironeous**, though several have been defaced with crude triangular symbols carved into the stone.

The room has been converted into a cluttered arcane study. A narrow table stands against the wall, covered in scrolls, black candles, small stone idols, and strange crystals that faintly hum with energy. Books and parchments lie scattered across the floor beside chalk diagrams scratched directly into the stone.

A narrow cot sits in one corner, though it appears rarely used. Nearby, a travel chest stands partially open, revealing robes and ritual implements. The air smells faintly of incense, chalk dust, and damp stone. Something here is quietly resonating with the earth beneath the monastery.

##### Entering Caidax Nightshade’s Chamber

You enter what was once a knight’s chamber, but the room has been completely transformed. Scrolls and strange crystals cover a narrow table along one wall, while chalk symbols spiral across the floor in careful patterns. Several candles burn low beside a small black stone idol whose surface seems almost polished by touch. A few old carvings of lightning bolts remain in the stone walls, but someone has gouged crude triangular marks through them.  
  
A narrow cot sits in the corner beside a travel chest filled with dark robes and ritual tools. The air is quiet. Too quiet. Yet beneath the silence you feel a faint vibration in the stone beneath your feet.

### Features of the Chamber

##### Arcane Study Table

The table holds various tools used by Caidax to study the geomantic disturbances beneath the monastery.

Items present:

• several scrolls describing **earth-binding rituals  
**• chalk diagrams of ley-line intersections  
• three small crystals vibrating with faint magical energy  
• notes referencing the **Elder Elemental God**

A character studying the notes for several minutes may learn:

**DC 12 Arcana**

Caidax is attempting to **amplify the geomantic resonance beneath the monastery**.

**DC 15 Arcana**

The rituals appear designed to **strengthen Varnor Earthcaller’s communion with the buried altar**.

#### Defaced Knightly Carvings

The original carvings of the knights of Heironeous remain partially visible. Lightning symbols and fragments of sacred oaths can still be seen beneath the scratched cult symbols. A character examining the carvings notices:

**DC 13 Religion**

These carvings were once blessings invoking **Heironeous as protector of the monastery**.

Caidax has deliberately defaced them.

#### The Excavation Notes

The parchment contains rough sketches of the **crypt tunnels beneath the monastery**.

The notes reference:

• Varnor Earthcaller’s excavation orders  
• duergar mining activity  
• the location of the **buried altar chamber**

One line is written repeatedly in jagged ink:

**“The stone listens.”**

* * *

#### Encounter

Caidax may be present depending on the monastery alert level. He rarely sleeps and often works through the night.

If present, he is likely:

• studying geomantic diagrams  
• preparing ritual components  
• consulting excavation reports from duergar miners

* * *

#### Tactics

Caidax prefers **manipulation and positioning** rather than direct combat. If overwhelmed, he attempts to flee toward **Varnor Earthcaller’s sanctum**.

Typical behavior:

• attempts to disable enemies with spells  
• retreats toward his **ritual laboratory (Location 14)** if threatened  
• may attempt to summon earth-aligned magic or collapse stone debris

##### Folded Letter from Slikkina Jillink

_To Maris, Captain of the southern company,_

Our mutual acquaintances speak highly of your efficiency and your ability to command men who prefer gold over questions. Such talents are rare and therefore useful.

You and your company will find the Kron Hills an advantageous place to conduct your affairs. Nestled among the valleys and hidden roads of these hills, one may move unseen along the trade routes that run between Tulvar, the Greenway Valley, and the Viscounty of Verbobonc. From such a position, a small and capable band can shape events across a wide territory without ever revealing its hand.

Your task is simple in purpose, though delicate in execution.

Caravans will depart Tulvar as though carrying legitimate trade for the city of Verbobonc. Their appearance must remain convincing to any travelers or patrols who observe them along the road. Once they pass into the hills, your company will intercept them at locations of your choosing. The wagons themselves are of little consequence. What matters is the story that survives them.

These raids must create uncertainty and mistrust between the humans of Verbobonc and the gnomes of the Kron Hills. Let the Viscounty believe that the hill clans have grown hostile to their merchants and patrols. Let the gnomes believe that human patrols and mercenaries now threaten their roads.

The resulting confusion will serve our mutual interests.

You will also be given access to a magical device known as the **Scroll of Veil**. When used properly, it will cloak your raiding parties in illusion, making them appear to be gnomes to any who witness your attacks. With this advantage, the blame for your actions will fall naturally upon the hill clans.

Should opportunity arise to engage a patrol of the **Mounted Borderers**, you are encouraged to take it. Whether the encounter ends in blood or retreat matters little. The appearance of such an attack is enough to provoke outrage within Verbobonc.

In time, the Viscounty will withdraw its patrols from the Kron Hills, believing the region no longer safe for their riders.

Your company will operate from the valley refuge that has been arranged for you. All recruits brought into this sanctuary must enter and leave blindfolded so that none can later reveal its location. This rule is absolute.

The man called **Lak**, whom you know from the Bronze Unicorn Inn in Verbobonc City, will continue to recruit suitable fighters from the city. The half-orc soldiers among your ranks were gathered from the village of Nulb, where taverns such as the Waterside Hostel provide a steady supply of men willing to fight for coin.

Your wizard associate **Caidex Nightshade** is to assist you in organizing the raids and maintaining the discipline of your company. His talents will be useful in ensuring that your disguises and movements remain convincing.

You will receive additional instructions through the same courier channels used previously. Messages and payments will pass through trusted intermediaries so that our involvement remains distant from your operations.

Understand this clearly: the success of our arrangement depends upon silence. No prisoner, witness, or wandering merchant must ever learn the name of **Clan Jillink** or the role we play in these events.

If the humans believe the gnomes responsible, and the gnomes believe the humans responsible, then both sides will turn their anger away from the true architects of this design.

Continue your work carefully, Captain Maris. The hills are patient, and the greatest victories are those achieved without anyone realizing a war has begun.

When your efforts have achieved their intended effect, you and your company will find that the Jillink clan rewards loyalty generously.

Until then, remain unseen.

**Slikkina Jillink  
**Matriarch of Clan Jillink  
Keeper of the Greenway Vaults
