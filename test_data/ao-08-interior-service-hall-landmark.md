AO-08. Interior Service Hall
============================

Every monastery has a room like this — the unglamorous corridor where the work of keeping a holy house alive passed through on its way to the altar. In the days of the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md), lay servants carried grain from the storerooms, cleaned tack from the [stables](ao-07-monastery-stables-landmark.md), and prepared the simple meals that sustained the knight-monks between devotions. This was the hinge between the practical wing and the sacred interior — humble, necessary, and perpetually underfoot.

That humility now serves a different master. Under [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s occupation, the service hall has become a rest station for stable-hands and off-rotation guards, a checkpoint controlling movement between the [outer courtyard](ao-06-the-outer-courtyard-landmark.md) and the monastery's inner corridors, and a fallback defensive position if the perimeter is breached. Bedrolls are heaped where devotional niches once held carved saints. Muddy boots track across flagstones worn smooth by generations of barefoot prayer.

This is a choke point. If the Earth Temple becomes aware of intruders in the [stables](ao-07-monastery-stables-landmark.md) or [courtyard](ao-06-the-outer-courtyard-landmark.md), this chamber becomes a defensive hold — and the north door gets barred.

 

##### First Entry

You ease the rear stable door open, and the smell changes immediately. The heavy musk of animals fades, replaced by cold stone and old incense — faint, but still clinging stubbornly to the mortar.  
  
A narrow chamber stretches before you, its ceiling low and arched. Rusted lantern hooks hang from beams overhead. Bedrolls lie scattered against one wall, boots kicked aside, cloaks thrown carelessly over what were once devotional niches. A stone face peers out from beneath a bundled horse blanket — some forgotten saint, watching with chipped eyes.  
  
To the north, a heavy oak door stands closed — iron-banded and reinforced. To the west, a second passage opens toward the monastery's interior. The stone beneath your boots is smoother here. Worn by generations who walked these halls in silence and prayer.  
  
Now it is tracked with mud. And something darker.

 

### The Sacred Layer

In its original function, this hall served the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) as the service corridor connecting the practical east wing — stables, kitchens, and storerooms — to the processional route leading toward the [Chapel of Heironeous](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md). Lay servants passed through daily. Knight-monks crossed it between training and prayer. The room was never beautiful, but it was built with the same care as every other stone in the priory: arched ceiling, carved lightning-bolt motifs above the doorframes, and small devotional niches set into the walls at intervals where servants could pause and offer a word to the god of valor before returning to their work.

*   Arched ceiling with carved Heironeous lightning bolts above doorframes
*   Six devotional niches along the east wall (now stuffed with gear and blankets)
*   Flagstone floor worn smooth by centuries of barefoot passage
*   Faint scent of old incense still trapped in the mortar — resists the mud and sweat layered over it

 

### The Corrupted Layer

The Earth Temple has made no effort to formally desecrate this room — it is too utilitarian for ritual attention. Instead, the corruption here is casual and grinding. The devotional niches serve as gear storage. The carved saints are buried under horse blankets. Iron pegs have been hammered into the walls for hanging tack and weapons. The flagstones that knight-monks once walked barefoot are now caked with stable mud, gnoll claw-marks, and the dark stains of things dragged through on the way to somewhere worse.

*   4 bedrolls heaped against the east wall (stable-hand sleeping area)
*   Crude personal items scattered among the bedding: bone tokens, carved stones, a gnoll's fetish necklace
*   Small crate of dried meat and a waterskin near the south door
*   Iron pegs hammered into walls for hanging weapons and tack
*   Earth Temple insignia tabard hidden under one pallet (Investigation DC 14)
*   Flask of strong spirits stashed with it — contraband the guards are not supposed to have

 

### Doors & Connections

The service hall sits at a critical junction in the priory's layout. Three doors control movement between the working wing and the sacred interior, making this room a natural checkpoint — and a natural ambush point.

##### South Door — To the Stables

A weathered wooden door, unbarred, opening into the rear of the [Monastery Stables](ao-07-monastery-stables-landmark.md). The smell of animals and hay leaks through the gaps. This is the primary route from the courtyard into the interior for anyone who enters through the stable wing.

##### West Door — To the Vigil Ante-Room

A narrower passage leading to **AO-24. The Vigil Ante-Room** — the processional chamber where knights once paused before entering the chapel. Under the Earth Temple, this passage connects to the guard checkpoint controlling access between the service wing and the sacred interior. During high alert, both this door and the ante-room beyond are manned.

##### North Door — To the Interior Corridors

Heavy oak reinforced with iron straps. This is the main controlled access point between the eastern wing and the monastery's inner halls — the route toward the priests' quarters, the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md), and eventually the crypt descent.

*   Normally unbarred during the day
*   Barred at night from the interior side
*   DC 15 Strength to force when barred
*   DC 14 Thieves' Tools to bypass quietly
*   Leads to the interior hallway connecting to the chapel wing, priests' rooms, and crypt

 

### Signs of Use

The room tells its story in layers for those who know how to read it. The oldest layer is the priory itself — smooth stone, faded carvings, the ghost of incense. Over that, weeks of occupation have written a cruder text in mud, claw-marks, and spilled ale.

##### Perception DC 12

*   Muddy boot prints tracked from the stables, layered over older prints — heavy traffic
*   Gnoll claw scratches gouged into the lower stonework near the south door
*   A faint draft from the west passage carrying cold chapel air

##### Investigation DC 15

*   Defensive scarring near the north door — splintered wood from a prior forced brace
*   The iron straps on the north door are newer than the wood — reinforced after the occupation began
*   Under one bedroll: an Earth Temple insignia tabard and a flask of strong spirits

##### Religion DC 14

*   The lightning sigils carved above the doorframes are nearly invisible under grime — but one still faintly glows for a heartbeat if touched respectfully by a character who serves a good-aligned deity
*   A cleric or paladin of Heironeous feels a prickle of recognition: this room was consecrated ground, and the consecration has not been formally broken — only buried

 

### Combat Tactics

If combat erupts here, the Earth Temple guards fight to hold the north door and buy time for the interior to respond. This is not a room they expect to win in — it is a room they expect to delay in while reinforcements arrive from the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) and the inner corridors.

 

#### Earth Temple Response

*   Guards brace the north door and fight defensively to hold the choke point
*   One cultist breaks west toward **AO-24** to raise the alarm in the chapel wing
*   If gnolls are present, they use the narrow space to funnel a charge — Large creatures find the corridor difficult terrain

 

#### Environmental Advantages

*   Narrow corridor = difficult terrain for Large creatures
*   Overturned cot provides half cover
*   Oil lantern on the wall can be smashed — fire spreads slowly across the bedrolls (1d4 fire damage, 5-ft area, lasts 2 rounds)
*   The north door can be barred from either side — PCs can use it defensively too

 

#### Reinforcement Timing

*   If the alarm reaches the interior: reinforcements arrive in 3 rounds from the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) (AO-10) via the north corridor
*   If the alarm reaches the chapel wing via **AO-24**: guards from the chapel respond in 4 rounds
*   If no alarm is raised: the room can be cleared quietly without triggering wider response

 

### Halven Sere's Influence

The service hall still remembers who built it. The consecration laid by the priests of Heironeous was never formally broken — the Earth Temple simply piled their filth on top of it. But sacred ground has a long memory, and **Halven Sere**'s continued faith somewhere in this priory sustains a fading echo of divine protection that even [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has not been able to fully suppress.

##### The Whisper

There is a hairline crack in the north arch — invisible unless carefully inspected. If a character of Good alignment pauses and listens (no check required), they hear the faintest whisper:

_"Hold the line."_

This chamber still remembers its oath.

##### Hidden Cache

One stone in the eastern wall is loose. **Halven Sere** left this behind before his capture — a desperate act of preservation hidden from the Earth Temple searchers who turned the priory upside down.

**Investigation DC 17** to find the loose stone. Inside, wrapped in oilcloth:

*   Prayer beads of Heironeous (carved wood, well-worn from years of use)
*   A handwritten page of the Azure Oath's founding vows — the only surviving copy if the chapel records were destroyed
*   A rough sketch of the crypt level showing the layout **Halven Sere** knew before his capture

The crypt sketch is invaluable — it gives PCs a partial map of the lower level, though it does not show the Earth Temple's excavation changes.

 

### DM Notes

AO-08 is the pivot room between the "outdoor" phase of the dungeon (approach, perimeter, courtyard, stables) and the "indoor" phase (corridors, quarters, chapel, crypts). How the party handles this room determines whether they enter the interior with surprise or fighting a running battle.

##### Stealth Entry

If the party cleared the [stables](ao-07-monastery-stables-landmark.md) quietly and enters during the day (when only 2 guards are resting), this room can be taken without raising the alarm. The guards here are off-rotation and not expecting trouble from the stable side. A successful Group Stealth check (DC 12) catches them napping.

##### Loud Entry

If the party fought openly in the [courtyard](ao-06-the-outer-courtyard-landmark.md) or [stables](ao-07-monastery-stables-landmark.md), the guards here are already braced behind the north door. This becomes a tactical problem: the party must force the door while defenders hold the choke point and a runner heads for reinforcements.

##### The Hidden Cache

The Halven Sere cache (Investigation DC 17) is one of the most important discoveries in the priory. The crypt-level sketch gives PCs tactical intelligence for the second phase of the dungeon. The prayer beads and oath-vows are emotional weight — proof that the Azure Oath's faith endured even as the stones were defiled. If PCs later rescue **Halven Sere**, returning his prayer beads triggers a powerful roleplay moment.

 

#### Pacing

This room should feel transitional — the shift from outdoor adventure to indoor dungeon crawl. The read-aloud text emphasizes the change in atmosphere: animal smells fade, stone and incense replace them, the floor gets smoother. Let the party feel the threshold they are crossing.
