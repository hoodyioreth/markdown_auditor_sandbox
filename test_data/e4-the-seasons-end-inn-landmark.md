E4 The Season’s End Inn
=======================

_**A Restful Retreat in Ryemend**_

Ryemend’s dependable “sleep-house”—a larger inn of country charm and clean beds, prized by caravans and cautious travelers who want rest without revels. Use it as your reset button between missions… and as a place where quiet folk overhear loud secrets.

##### **Approaching The Season’s End Inn**

The inn’s lanterns burn steady—neither gaudy nor dim—casting warm squares of light upon the cobbles. A signboard shows a simple thing: a sheaf of wheat bound with twine, and beneath it the words SEASON’S END in careful hand. Within, the air smells of clean straw, soap, and wool drying by a modest hearth. No minstrel plays here. No drunken shout breaks the beams. Only the soft thump of boots, the creak of stairs, and the hush that comes when folk have paid coin for sleep and mean to get their due.

### What This Place Is

The Season’s End is one of the larger inns on the north bank, built to serve the **steady traffic** of Ryemend—caravans, merchants, and travelers who value **reliable rest** over spectacle. It grew from a modest lodging-house into a proper inn, and it keeps its reputation by doing the simple things well: clean rooms, honest locks, and a yard for mounts.

**Important constraint:** there is **no room service**, and hungry guests are pointed next door to [E3 The Molten Spigot Tavern](e3-the-molten-spigot-tavern-landmark.md). That little inconvenience is a DM gift—because it forces interaction with the Spigot’s rumor engine.

##### Exterior & Interior

**Street Face:** timber-and-stone, practical and well kept. Flower boxes in season; shuttered windows in foul weather.  
**Coach Yard:** small stable and hitching rails; stabling is **included with the room**.  
**Common Hall:** quiet tables, watered ale and tea; most folk keep voices low.  
**Stairs & Landings:** creaky but safe; a night lantern at each landing.  
**Rooms:**

*   **20 private rooms** (up to two persons each)
*   **5 small common rooms** (up to eight persons each)

**Chambermaid Service:** staff will wash clothes, change blankets, and empty chamber pots—steady, humble labor that makes this place beloved by road-weary folk.

* * *

#### History: From Humble Beginnings

The Season’s End Inn has grown from a modest lodging to one of the largest inns in Ryemend. It has maintained its reputation for providing restful accommodations to travelers and locals alike.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Establishment:** Founded as a small lodging house
*   **Growth:** Expanded to become one of the larger inns in the district
*   **Reputation:** Known for its country charm and reliable service

### How to Leverage This Inn in Play

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   A calm, safe place to sleep in a district full of watchers
*   A staging point before crossing the bridge or riding north
*   Laundry and cleanup after “unpleasant work”
*   A neutral meeting spot when the Spigot feels too loud

##### If They Linger

*   Carys mentions a “guest” who never removes their gloves (hiding a mark, a burn, or a ring)
*   Gareth quietly asks whether the party would “walk a letter” to the Spigot—because he will not be seen doing it

##### What Goes Wrong

*   Two different parties claim the same reserved room
*   A nervous courier checks in, then vanishes before dawn—leaving only wet boots and a paid bill
*   Wardens arrive asking after “a man of such-and-such description,” and Gareth’s face tightens for one breath too long

##### **Welcoming Innkeeper**

From behind a simple counter rises Gareth Llewellyn, broad of shoulder, sleeves rolled, hair touched with grey. His eyes take thy measure—mud on hem, road-dust on packs, the set of thy hands—then his face easeth into a practiced, kindly look, like a man drawing a blanket up over a child. He inclines his head, and his voice is low enough not to disturb the hall, yet clear as a bell at dusk.  
  
“Good even to thee. Thou’rt welcome at the Season’s End. Wipe thy boots there, if thou wilt—aye, just so. The road clings to a man longer than he thinks.”

##### Gareth Llewellyn (Innkeeper)

Friendly, steady-eyed, and built for long days—Gareth runs the inn like a man mending a net: patient, constant, and always watching for the one strand about to snap. E4 The Season’s End Inn

**Wants**

*   Keep the inn’s name synonymous with “safe sleep”
*   Avoid trouble that would bring Wardens nightly
*   Maintain goodwill with merchants who book rooms in bulk

**Secret (DM)  
**Gareth keeps a private “courtesy ledger” of unusual guests: folk who pay too much for quiet, folk who arrive without baggage, folk whose boots are too fine for their claimed trade. He does not call it spying—he calls it _prudence_.

**Dialogue (Gareth)**

*   “Thou’rt welcome—wipe thy boots and thou’lt find thy bed clean.”
*   “No songs, no brawls, no knives drawn. If thou desirest noise, the Spigot can oblige thee.”
*   “Pay fair, sleep sound. That is my bargain with the world.”

##### Carys Morgan (Chambermaid)

Young, cheerful, and hardworking—Carys is the inn’s bright stitch, always moving, always cleaning, always noticing. E4 The Season’s End Inn

**Secret (DM)  
**Carys overhears far more than she ought—through doors not fully latched, through whispered arguments when folk think servants are deaf. If treated kindly, she can become the party’s best source of “soft truths.”

**Dialogue (Carys)**

*   “Lay thy cloak here, ser—mist clings to wool like guilt to fools.”
*   “If thou’rt bound south, mind thy seams. I’ve washed blood from hems this week—fresh blood.”
