F10 The Pampered Polecat
========================

> The establishment goes out of its way to avoid attention from passerby—tucked between a shops. But those who know its location find that it welcomes them with suffused amber light, air tinged with perfume, and beautiful hosts of every shape, size, and gender expression imaginable. A sign at the door reads: “No touching. Look to your heart’s content.”  
>   
> This newly established business has generated lots of controversy among the local churches, guilds, and city officials. Some churches outright dismiss this type of business as abhorrent. Other religions embrace the lifestyle. Located in the Foreign District, this establishment occasionally attracts an unsavory type of clientele (rogues, adventurers, etc). But the huge influx of adventures to the city (and to this establishment) was enough for city officials to relish in the taxes it generates (and the city guards like to keep all the unsavory folk in one place). >   
> Gorgeous hosts of every shape, size, and gender expression imaginable greet you at the door with demure smiles. Soft, amber light and a curtain of perfumed smoke lends an almost magical, otherworldly glow to the spacious room, littered with comfortable seating areas and shadowed alcoves. “No touching,” your host reminds you cheerfully. “But please, feast your eyes.” A massive bar dominates one wall, its shelves stocked with every liquor imaginable, both mundane and magical. Hosts move gracefully from one table to the next, serving drinks and sharing pleasant conversation. Laughter fills the air, while in the far corner, a solitary bard strums pleasantly on a lute.

The Pampered Polecat is a narrow, well-kept tavern tucked into the Foreign Quarter’s merchant flow—comfortable, discreet, and deliberately unremarkable. It is not owned by the thieves’ guild, nor openly criminal, yet it serves as one of the Battirovka Family’s most effective **information listening posts**. Here, rumors are not extracted—they are overheard.

Travelers speak freely. Locals relax their guard. And somewhere in the room, someone is always paying attention.

_**From Greyhawk Twins to Verbobonc Magnates**_

##### Arrival at the Pampered Polecat

The smell of mulled wine and beeswax polish greets you as you step inside. The Pampered Polecat is warm without being loud, tidy without being stiff. Lanternlight glows softly off polished tables, and a low murmur of conversation fills the room—foreign accents, half-familiar names, and stories spoken as if no one important were listening. A painted polecat curls above the bar in faded gilt, its clever eyes watching the room. Somewhere behind the smiles and small talk, you get the sense that this place remembers things.

### Founding of the Polecat

Freda and Yevon Dawnhammer, twin sisters from Greyhawk, established The Pampered Polecat to create an inclusive oasis of pleasure. Initially controversial among churches and officials, it quickly gained popularity due to the influx of adventurers and the taxes it generates for the city.

*   **Dawnhammer Twins' Vision:** Freda and Yevon Dawnhammer, twin sisters from the Free City of Greyhawk, established The Pampered Polecat to create an inclusive oasis of pleasure.
*   **Controversial Beginnings:** Initially generated controversy among local churches, guilds, and city officials due to its nature.

##### Gaining Popularity

*   **Adventurer Magnet:** The influx of adventurers to Verbobonc fueled the Polecat's popularity.
*   **Tax Revenue for City Officials:** Despite controversy, the taxes generated were enough for city officials to welcome the business.

#### A Discreet Facade and a World of Luxury Inside

Tucked between shops with a discreet facade, the Polecat's interior is a spacious lounge with amber light, perfumed smoke, and comfortable seating. Gorgeous hosts welcome guests into a magical environment filled with laughter, music, and drinks, while shadowed alcoves provide privacy.

_**Exterior**_

### **The Dawnhammer Sisters’ Reputation**

Together, Freda and Yevon Dawnhammer are widely respected within the Foreign Quarter. They are known as fair hosts, careful listeners, and women who protect their establishment with quiet competence rather than force—though force is always close at hand.

Those who treat the sisters with respect find the Pampered Polecat an excellent place to hear news.  
Those who mistake kindness for weakness are rarely seen again inside its doors.

### Relationships

_Navigating Controversy and Favor Among Patrons_

The Pampered Polecat generates controversy among conservative churches like St. Cuthbert's, while faiths like Trithereon and Fharlanghn are more tolerant. City officials welcome the tax revenue, and many adventurers would defend the Dawnhammer twins due to their loyalty.

##### Religious Controversy

*   [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md): Views the establishment as abhorrent and immoral.
*   [Trithereon](trithereon-person-1.md)  and [Fharlanghn](fharlanghn-person.md) **Faiths:** More tolerant and embrace the lifestyle.

##### City Officials and Guards

#### **The Battirovka Connection (Hidden Layer)**

The Pampered Polecat is **not** controlled by the thieves’ guild, nor staffed by obvious members. Instead:

*   One or two **Friends of the Family** pass through nightly
*   Bar staff are trained—knowingly or not—to remember details
*   Information flows outward, never inward
*   No illegal business is conducted on site

The Battirovka Family prefers places like this:

_Where no one thinks they’re being watched._

* * *

##### **How the Guild Uses the Polecat**

*   Tracking **foreign caravans** entering Verbobonc
*   Monitoring talk of:
*   unusual cargo,
*   missing persons,
*   mercenary hiring,
*   cult rumors,
*   political unease
*   Identifying useful outsiders (including PCs)

If the party returns repeatedly, the guild **notices them**—quietly.

#### History — Founding of the Polecat

The Pampered Polecat began as a modest travelers’ alehouse at the edge of the Foreign Quarter, built to serve drovers and caravans who preferred a quieter cup than the riotous halls nearer the bridge. Its first owner was a cautious innkeeper who discovered an enduring truth: **people speak most freely where they believe nothing of consequence is happening.**

Over time, the alehouse earned a reputation for **clean tables, polite service, and calm voices**—a rare thing in a district full of shouting hawkers and foreign tempers. That calm drew merchants and scribes. Merchants and scribes drew **interest**.

The Battirovka Family did not seize the Polecat. They did what they prefer: they **attached** to it. A few “friends” began to drink there. A few regulars began to listen. The house remained lawful on the surface, while beneath the surface it became what it is now:

A place where information is not demanded—it is **collected**.

#### Arrival at the Polecat

Warmth rolls over you as you step inside, thick with the scent of mulled wine, beeswax polish, and faint incense. Lanternlight pools across polished tables and carved beams, casting the room in amber and shadow. Conversation hums—not loud, not hushed—foreign accents blending with half-familiar names, laughter rising and falling like a practiced tune.  
  
Behind the bar, a painted polecat curls in faded gilt, its clever eyes forever watching the room. Near the entry, an Oerdi Human woman, kept, and unremarkable—but her sharp eyes flick up the moment you enter, weighing you in a heartbeat before returning to her game.  
  
A smile creases her weathered face as she rises, tucking the cards away. She moves toward you with the easy confidence of someone who belongs here utterly, every step measured, every glance noting far more than it shows.

##### **Freda Dawnhammer — Dialogue Cues**

“Welcome, friends. First night at the Polecat, is it?”  
_(She gestures toward the room with a small, open hand.)  
_“Plenty of space, plenty of drink, and no rush to be anywhere else.”

“If you’ve a story, we like those here. If not, no shame in listening.”  
_(A quick smile, eyes already flicking to your packs, your boots, your road-dust.)_

“Sit where you please. Bar’s generous tonight—so long as cups stay full and tempers stay calm.”  
_(Lower, friendly tone.)  
_“We look after our own.”

“Oh—names?”  
_(She laughs softly.)  
_“Only if you’re keen to share. Roads have long memories, but walls here are polite.”

“If you need anything—anything at all—just say you’re a friend of the house.”  
_(A pause, just long enough to matter.)  
_“We’ll hear you.”

### Notable People

_The Dawnhammer Twins and Their Loyal Hosts_

##### **Freda Dawnhammer** (Co-Owner and Hostess)

Freda Dawnhammer is one of the twin sisters who own and operate the Pampered Polecat. Compact, sharp-eyed, and quietly industrious, Freda keeps her hands busy at all times—shuffling cards, stitching fabric, tallying coin, or idly sorting dice—while her attention never leaves the room.

*   **Race:** Oerdi Human
*   **Class/Level:** Expert 2
*   **Role:** Quiet listener; evaluates newcomers; “friend of the house”
*   **Public Face:** Cheerful regular with a fondness for stories
*   **True Use:** Notes names, routes, cargo talk, and who asks too many questions

**Tell:** She laughs half a breath late when a lie is told well.

##### **Yevon Dawnhammer**

(Co-Owner and Hostess)

Yevon Dawnhammer is Freda’s twin sister and equal partner, though her presence is more openly commanding. Where Freda blends into the background, Yevon moves through the tavern with purpose—adjusting seating, directing staff, calming disputes before they grow loud.

*   **Role:** Discreet muscle and escort; ensures the Polecat stays calm
*   **Race:** Oerdi Human
*   **Class/Level:** Expert 2
*   **Public Face:** She maintains an excellent rapport with the Polecat’s regular clientele
*   **True Use:** Yevon ensures that trouble is handled discreetly, efficiently, and without spectacle

##### **Host of the Polecat: Merrin Poleward**

(Owner/License Holder)

A local proprietor who keeps the tavern respectable and the books clean. Merrin is not a guild member; he is a man who understands that stability feeds profit.

*   **Role:** Keeps the tavern lawful, calm, and open
*   **Motivation:** Peace, steady custom, and avoiding Warden attention
*   **Secret:** He knows the Dawnhammers are “connected,” but pretends not to.

**Merrin:** _“We serve cups, not quarrels. If thou needest a fight, find a place that sells ’em.”_

##### DM Notes: How to Use the Dawnhammer Twins
