W5 The River’s Edge
===================

_**A Historic Storefront for All Your Adventuring Needs  
**_Standing just beyond the constant churn of the Velverdyva’s docks, The River’s Edge is one of the oldest surviving commercial storefronts along the Waterfront District. While the surrounding stone warehouses speak of guild power and noble coin, this timber-reinforced outfitter reflects something older — the grit and endurance of Verbobonc’s riverfolk.

Founded decades ago by former river trader Andros Kalymnos, the establishment serves as a vital provisioning point for sailors, caravan guards, explorers, and newly arrived adventurers. It is a practical shop built for survival rather than display, where rope, oil, and iron spikes are valued more than politics.

##### Arrival at The River’s Edge

The smell of tar, rope, and river salt lingers in the air as you approach a stout wooden structure standing slightly back from the pier’s edge. Unlike the stone warehouses that loom nearby, this building rises like a frontier fort — timber-framed, reinforced, and ringed by a waist-high palisade. Crates are stacked neatly along the wall. Lantern hooks hang ready over the door.  
  
Above the entrance, a carved sign reads: The River’s Edge Inside, shelves crowd every wall — coils of rope, bundles of torches, sacks of grain, iron spikes, flasks of oil, lantern glass, and tightly wrapped travel rations. The place smells of cedar, canvas, and river mud. Behind the counter stands a broad-shouldered man with scars at his jaw and one ear notched clean through. He does not smile. He sizes you up the way a river captain measures depth.  
  
_“You can’t sail far without the right gear,” he says evenly. “Step inside and be prepared.”_

### A Historic Trading Post Turned General Store

Located on the north side of the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md), across the great bridge in the bustling Waterfront District, The River’s Edge is packed tight with an assortment of general goods. The recent renovations have transformed it to look like a wooden fort, complete with a small wooden palisade surrounding the store.

### Exterior Details

The River’s Edge stands on the north bank of the Velverdyva, near the bridge approach, slightly set back from the dockline but close enough for sailors to reach it within minutes of mooring.

##### Exterior Features

*   Timber fort aesthetic with reinforced beams
*   Small wooden palisade enclosing stacked cargo
*   River-weathered planks and iron-bound shutters
*   Lantern poles for late-night trade
*   Rope hoist for lifting crates to upper loft
*   A bell pull near the door (used during pirate alerts)

This is not a decorative storefront.

It is built to withstand unrest.

* * *

#### Interior Layout

The shop is tightly packed but meticulously organized.

##### Ground Floor

*   Counter with trade ledger and scale weights
*   Walls stacked with:
*   Torches, candles, oil flasks
*   Rope (hemp, silk, treated river rope)
*   Grappling hooks, pitons, spikes
*   Bedrolls and canvas tarps
*   Waterskins and field rations
*   Locked case behind counter (special order items)

##### Upper Loft

*   Extra dry goods storage
*   Firewood kept elevated to prevent dampness
*   Emergency defensive crossbows mounted in rafters

##### Hidden Rumor

Old river hands swear the foundation predates the bridge itself.

Some claim there are sealed river tunnels beneath it.

### History

_From River Trading Post to General Store_

The River’s Edge started as one of the first general stores in the area, serving the needs of river traders and adventurers. Over time, it has evolved to meet the demands of a growing city while retaining its historic roots.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Founding:** Originally established as a trading post for river merchants.
*   **Evolution:** Transformed into a general store catering to the needs of both locals and travelers.

#### Economic Impact

_Supporting Adventurers and Travelers_

The River’s Edge plays a crucial role in the local economy by providing essential goods to adventurers, sailors, and merchants. Its strategic location near the docks makes it a convenient stop for those preparing for their journeys.

##### Key Economic Activities

*   **Sale of General Goods:** Offers a wide range of adventuring gear and dry goods.
*   **Renovations:** Recent renovations have revitalized the store, attracting more customers.

##### Goods and Services

*   **Adventuring Gear:** Basic items from the Player’s Handbook, such as torches, candles, and dry firewood.
*   **Dry Goods:** Essential supplies for long journeys.

##### Cost

*   **Pricing:** All goods are priced at average market rates.

### Economic Importance

The River’s Edge serves:

*   Newly arrived adventurers
*   River traders resupplying mid-route
*   Caravan guards moving to/from Kron Hills
*   Dockworkers
*   Independent captains

Strategically located near:

*   W2 Office of the Harbormaster
*   W3 Waterworks Superintendent Tower
*   The Great Bridge approach

It acts as the final outfitter before departure.

##### **Shopkeeper Welcome Scene (2nd Person, DM-Ready Format)**

* * *

##### Narration (Read Aloud)

The smell of tarred rope and river-mist follows you through the door. Inside, the shop is warm with lamplight and the faint tang of brine. Coils of rope hang from thick wooden pegs. Oilskins and river cloaks sway gently from ceiling beams. Barrels of pitch, crates of tackle, bundles of dried fish, and neatly stacked trade goods line the walls. A small iron stove crackles in the corner, and above it hangs a kettle whose lid trembles with a quiet hiss.  
  
Behind a scarred wooden counter stands a broad-shouldered man in a wool vest and rolled sleeves, his beard neatly trimmed but streaked with early gray. His eyes are sharp, observant—river-man’s eyes. He wipes his hands on a cloth as you enter, studying you with the quick assessment of someone who survives by knowing who carries coin… and who carries trouble. He smiles—but it is the kind of smile that measures.

* * *

##### Dialog (For the DM to Read Smoothly)

“Ah. Travelers. You’ve the look of folk who’ve seen more than tavern rafters and council halls. Welcome to the River’s Edge. Best rope in the district. Best oilskins too—unless you fancy drowning in style.”

“You’re new to the docks, I think. Word travels. It always does. Hard to miss strangers when the river’s this tense.”

“Aye, tense. You’ve not heard? Gnome caravans coming down from the Kron Hills… they’ve been harried of late. Wagons turned back. Drivers shaken. Some claim it’s bandits. Others say it’s ‘misunderstandings.’ Funny how misunderstandings always cost someone silver.”

He leans forward slightly.

“City folk mutter that the gnomes are pulling their trade inward. Gnomes mutter that human patrols look the other way when trouble comes from the hills. And merchants like me? We just watch our ledgers bleed and pray no fool decides a riot will fix the price of grain.”

“You wouldn’t happen to be heading south? Or north? Or anywhere near the Long Road? I’d pay good coin to know who’s stirring this pot… before it boils over.”

“And if you’ve business with the Gnomesberg quarter… tread carefully. Some there are tired of smiling.”

He straightens and gives a measured nod.

“Still. You’re welcome here. Coin spends the same, no matter whose blood runs hotter.”
