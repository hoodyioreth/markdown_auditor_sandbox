D4 Shrine to Fharlanghn
=======================

A sacred **bridge-shrine of living ipt wood** spanning Nigb’s Run, where travelers—elf, gnome, and man alike—seek the Dweller-on-the-Horizon’s blessing, consult an etched **map of the Viscounty** beneath their boots, and find quiet aid from the **Striders of Fharlanghn**, guardians of the open road.

##### Arrival at the Shrine to Fharlanghn

Nigb’s Run chatters below, clear and quick, and above it ariseth an arched bridge of living wood—ipt grown and shaped as if the trees themselves had chosen to become a crossing. The railings are carved with a thousand lines and sigils: roads like veins, rivers like threads of silver, and tiny marks for hamlet, ford, hill, and watch-post.  
  
As thou settest foot upon the span, thy gaze falleth to the deck—an etched map beneath thy soles, so cunningly wrought that thou canst follow the Viscounty’s lanes with a fingertip. Near the mid-arch, a simple disk-sigil is set into the wood: a horizon-line and an upturned crescent above.  
  
A priest in faded green, staff in hand, lifts two fingers in greeting. Priest: _“Fair journey, friend. Speak thy road, and I shall speak thee a blessing.”_

### What This Place Is

The Shrine to Fharlanghn is both **crossing** and **consecration**—a bridge-temple whose very body is a map and whose purpose is to keep paths open in heart and in world. Built centuries ago by elven worshipers after they found natural signs in the living wood that resembled the god’s symbol, it became a recognized holy site and then a deliberate **metaphysical crossroads** within the Enclave.

Fharlanghn is revered here not merely as “travel,” but as **the horizon’s call**—the urging to look beyond the immediate and move ever forward. His worship exalts the journey over the destination, and his servants are as often cartographers and road-keepers as they are priests.

##### Exterior & Structure

**The Living Ipt Bridge:** The shrine is made entirely of living ipt trees shaped into arch and span, famed for ornate carvings that show no age. Nigb’s Run flows beneath, splitting the quarter as the shrine binds it.

**The Map-Deck:** The bridge’s walking surface bears a detailed etched map of the region—usable as an in-world guide and an excellent prop for pointing out routes, borders, and “where the trouble lies.”

**Rest Niches:** Built-in benches and small shelters (little bower-alcoves) allow pilgrims and weary folk to rest, pray, and trade road-news without entering a tavern.

* * *

##### Interior Elements (It’s a Bridge, Yet It Has “Rooms”)

Though open-aired, the shrine contains three sacred “stations” along the span:

1.  **The Threshold Stone** (near the elven-quarter approach)  
    A carved disk-sigil set low for travelers to touch before departing.
2.  **The Mid-Span Waycross** (at the highest arch)  
    A small ceremonial space where blessings of caravans are spoken and tokens are exchanged.
3.  **The Horizon Seat** (near the far end)  
    A quiet bench beneath a lattice of ipt boughs where confessions of fear, doubt, and homesickness are heard.

### Lore: "Echoes of the Eternal Voyager"

[Fharlanghn](fharlanghn-person.md), known as The Dweller on the Horizon, is venerated here not just as a deity of travel but as a guide to spiritual and physical journeys. The Shrine, built hundreds of years ago by Elven worshipers, serves as a beacon to those who seek to find divinity in their travels. The bridge itself is a map, detailing the Viscounty and its surroundings, etched into its very structure—a testament to the union of artistry, devotion, and the spirit of exploration that Fharlanghn embodies.

##### Facilities: "Sanctuary's Offerings"

*   **Map of the Viscount**y: The bridge deck features a detailed etching of the region, usable as a geographical guide.
*   **Ceremonial Areas:** Spaces along the bridge are designated for travel blessings and other religious ceremonies.
*   **Resting Spots:** Benches and shelters built into the bridge offer places for pilgrims and travelers to rest and meditate.

#### History: "Chronicles of the Divine Pathway"

The shrine was originally recognized as a holy site when worshipers noted natural formations in the wood that resembled Fharlanghn's symbols. This spiritual significance, combined with its strategic placement over [Nigb’s Run](nigbE28099s-run-location.md), led to the development of the bridge as both a physical and metaphysical crossroads.

#### Relationships: "Converging Trails"

*   **Elven Conclave:** The Shrine maintains a close relationship with the [Elven Enclave](elven-enclave-organization.md), ensuring that Elven traditions and the sanctity of the site are preserved.
*   **Local Travelers and Merchants:** It is a point of blessing for journeys, and many local merchants seek the god's favor here before long travels.
*   **Other Religious Sites:** Although dedicated to Fharlanghn, the shrine is respected by followers of other deities, symbolizing a crossroads of faiths.

#### Politics and Motivation: "Influence of the Wanderer"

Though modest in doctrine, the shrine holds subtle influence: it encourages **peace and unity** between the enclave and the broader city by making “safe passage” a shared value. Merchants come for blessings; elves come for lore and stillness; outsiders come because it is one place in the Enclave where they are not immediately measured as intruders. It is also a whisper-hub. Road-news flows here before it reaches guildhall or court.

#### Notable People: "Guides and Guardians of the Road"

*   **Priest of the Way:** A notable elven priest, known for his wisdom on spiritual and worldly travels, oversees the shrine’s activities.
*   **The Guardians of the Road:** [Striders of Fharlanghn](striders-of-fharlanghn-organization.md) of lay worshipers who maintain the shrine and offer assistance to travelers in need.

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   Receive a formal **journey-blessing** before setting out.
*   Consult the etched **map-deck** for routes, distances, and named landmarks.
*   Seek a Strider guide, scout, or courier who knows hidden ways.
*   Ask after rumors of “closed roads,” missing caravans, or unusual patrol patterns.

##### If They Linger

*   Striders begin to treat them as either **allies** (useful road-hands) or **risks** (too loud, too hungry, too curious).
*   Elarion offers a question instead of an answer—testing whether the party’s intentions are fit for the open road.

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A nervous merchant blurts the name of a route that _should not_ be spoken aloud.
*   A disguised road-robber tries to claim Fharlanghn’s blessing as cover (Striders do not tolerate imposture).
*   Someone is attempting to “purchase” maps of restricted paths—proof that an enemy seeks to control movement.

### Optional 5e-Friendly Mechanics (DM Toolbox)

##### Blessing of the Open Road

If the party receives a proper blessing at the Mid-Span Waycross, each character gains **advantage on one Survival check** made to navigate or avoid becoming lost before the next dawn. (Alternatively, advantage on one check to resist fatigue during forced march.)

##### Strider Aid

A party that treats the shrine with courtesy may request one of the following (once per tenday):

*   A **route-hint**: +2 bonus to one overland navigation attempt.
*   A **safe-house mark**: a symbol that grants them a night’s shelter among friendly road-folk (DM’s discretion).

##### The Map-Deck as Prop

When the PCs physically point at the map-deck and describe their intended route, reward it: grant **advantage on one Investigation or History check** related to roads, borders, or regional travel lore.

##### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (10)

1.  The map-deck hath changed—one tiny road line is newly etched, though no chisel touched it.
2.  A Strider found a dead courier with a false blessing token—bandits are using holy courtesy as bait.
3.  Elarion quietly asks after strange road-signs: stacked stones in patterns that do not match any local custom.
4.  A traveler swears an oasis was seen in a place where no water should be—_Journey’s End_ is whispered of, but never promised.
5.  A polite foreigner in fine boots asks about routes that avoid watch-posts entirely.
6.  The Striders are watching for **Cagewright** influence—folk who speak of “closing roads for safety” with too much zeal.
7.  A caravan master claims an “east wind blessing” is needed—hinting at Atroa’s tie to Fharlanghn, and a coming storm-journey.
8.  A small pouch of horizon-tokens went missing from the shrine; whoever stole them wants to impersonate Striders.
9.  The shrine’s living wood formed a natural symbol again—Striders call it an omen of travel soon to be forcibly hindered.
10.  A cloaked pilgrim offers the party a “road-truth”: someone in the city profits from caravans vanishing—yet wishes travel to _seem_ free.
