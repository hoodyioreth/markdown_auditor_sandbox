Halven Sere
===========

> Halven Sere is the last custodian of the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath — a lay brother of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who stayed behind when the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) withdrew in 562 CY and maintained the priory alone for fourteen years. He tended the herb garden, fed the donkeys, carved blessings into stable posts, and prayed in every room of a building that was slowly forgetting what it was for. When the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) seized the priory, Halven was taken alive. He has been held prisoner in the guest cells ever since — questioned by [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) about the chapel's secrets, studied by [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) for the faith that sustains the priory's failing wards. He will not fight. He will not break. He will pray.

_Last Custodian of the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath — Lay Brother of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) — Prisoner of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)_

* * *

### The Man Who Stayed

In 562 CY, when the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) withdrew its knights from the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), the chapter's senior leadership decided that the building was no longer strategically viable. The Long Road was quiet. The [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) were stable. The order's resources were needed elsewhere. The knight-commander gathered his remaining brothers, packed the chapter's most valuable records, and marched south.

Halven Sere watched them go. Then he went back inside and swept the floor.

He was not a knight. He had never sworn the Oath of Vigilance or carried a sword under the Azure Oath's banner. He was a lay brother — a custodian, a caretaker, a man who maintained a building because someone had to. He had come to the priory as a young man, drawn by faith rather than martial ambition, and had spent his adult life tending the practical needs of a knightly order: the kitchens, the storerooms, the herb garden, the stables. When the knights left, nobody ordered him to stay. Nobody ordered him to leave. He simply did not go.

For fourteen years — from 562 CY to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s seizure of the priory — Halven Sere maintained the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) alone. He tended the herb garden outside the walls, foraging the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) for hardy medicinal plants. He fed the two donkeys in the stables and carved a small blessing into the stall post: _"Heironeous sees even in shadow."_ He prayed in every room of the building — the chapel, the guest cells, the corridors, the service hall — because he believed that a place of worship required worship to remain what it was. He kept the honor plaques in the oath-hall clean, though he had never known the men whose names were listed there. He patched the roof when it leaked. He replaced candles that no one else would light.

His name was added to the honor plaques himself — the last entry on the last plaque: _Halven Sere, Custodian, Appointed 571 CY_. He inscribed it himself, nine years into his solitary vigil, because he felt the priory deserved to remember that someone was still here.

He was not lonely. He had [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). He had the building. He had the donkeys. That was enough.

### The Fall

When the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) came, it came at dusk. [Romag](romag-person.md), the High Priest of the Black Earth at the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), did not send banners or trumpets. He sent reavers — men hardened by the [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) riverfront, zealots of the Black Earth, and a garrison force large enough to hold the priory indefinitely. They came up the worn cobblestone ascent in silence, pushed through the [main gate](ao-05-the-main-gate-ruin-landmark.md), and found a thin, grey-haired man in a faded blue robe standing in the courtyard with a broom in his hand.

Halven Sere did not resist. He could not have resisted. He was one man with a broom against thirty armed fighters. He was taken alive — the only person present when the priory fell. Everything after that belongs to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md).

But in the hours before the reavers arrived — in that window of time when Halven heard rumors along the road, or felt something wrong in the way the ridge wind carried sound, or simply knew in the way that a man who has lived alone for fourteen years knows when his solitude is about to end — he hid what he could. A prayer journal and his personal holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md), concealed in a hidden compartment in **AO-15. The Sacristy**. A rough sketch of the crypt level, prayer beads, and his copy of the order's oath vows, left in a loose stone in the eastern wall of **AO-08. The Service Hall**. Small things. Personal things. The kind of things a man hides when he does not know if anyone will ever come looking for them — but wants to leave a trace in case someone does.

### The Prisoner

Halven Sere has been held in **AO-20. The Guest Cells** since the occupation began. He occupies Cell 1 — closest to the corridor entrance, where the jailer can watch him. His cell is different from the others. The straw is changed weekly. He has a wool blanket. The water cup is refilled twice daily. And the prayer bench remains — facing the devotion niche, facing the small carved figure of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) never bothered to take.

This special treatment is not mercy. It is [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s pragmatism. The garrison commander keeps Halven alive for three reasons:

*   **Leverage.** If [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) officials, [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) clergy, or mounted patrols ever arrive at the ridge, Halven can be cleaned up and presented as the custodian who willingly granted shelter. A living witness who appears unharmed defuses suspicion. In reality, he speaks under threat of harm to the other prisoners
*   **Interrogation.** [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) question him regularly about the Altar of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) in [AO-14. The Chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) — its sacred geometry, the ward-stones, the hidden functions that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) priests cannot decipher through brute force. He has not broken. Sometimes they ask with courtesy. Sometimes not. He gives them nothing
*   **Spiritual suppression.** [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) suspects that Halven's continued faith is sustaining something in the crypts below — restless spirits, lingering wards, divine protections that resist the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s desecration. Killing him might unleash whatever his prayers hold in check. So they keep him alive and hope his faith erodes. It has not

### Faith as Infrastructure

Halven Sere is not a cleric. He cannot cast spells, turn undead, or channel divine power in any mechanically significant way. He is a lay brother — a man of faith without the formal investiture of the priesthood. But fourteen years of daily prayer in every room of a consecrated building have made him something the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) did not anticipate: a **relay point** in the priory's original divine architecture.

The [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) was built as a sacred space — consecrated by the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md), its walls, altars, and crypt seals invested with divine purpose. That consecration was never formally broken. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) piled filth on top of it — desecrated the chapel, looted the tombs, defaced the honor plaques — but desecration is not deconsecration. Beneath the grime, the original architecture of faith still exists. Fading, fractured, held together by threads.

Halven Sere is one of those threads. His daily prayers from the devotion niche in his cell connect — however faintly — to the wards in the chapel and the seals in the crypts below. He does not fully understand this. He prays because that is what he has always done. But the connection is real, and it is the actual reason [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has not killed him. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) suspects the truth. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) senses it through tremorsense — a faint vibration in the stone that pulses in time with the old man's prayers.

If Halven is rescued and brought to the chapel, the connection becomes visible. If he is killed, part of the priory's remaining divine protection dies with him.

### His Traces in the Priory

Halven Sere lived in this building for fourteen years. His presence left marks — small, personal, easily overlooked — scattered through every level of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). The party encounters these traces long before they meet the man himself, building a portrait of a custodian they have never seen:

*   **AO-04. The Custodian's Herb Patch:** Halven maintained this garden alone for fourteen years — medicinal plants, healing poultices, teas for vigil nights. A prayer-stone marker (DC 16) carved with [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s lightning bolt lies buried at the garden edge. If Halven is rescued and brought here, he kneels and weeps — not for himself, but because the herbs still grow
*   **AO-06. The Outer Courtyard:** A fragment of the Azure Oath's original votive plaque found in the fountain basin — a piece of the oath Halven kept alive
*   **AO-07. Monastery Stables:** A carving in the stall post — _"Heironeous sees even in shadow"_ — from Halven's years with his two donkeys. Speaking it aloud with sincerity calms horses and flickers the lantern flame blue
*   **AO-08. Interior Service Hall:** A hidden cache behind a loose stone (Investigation DC 17) — a rough sketch of the crypt level, prayer beads, and a copy of the oath vows. Left before capture. Finding these and returning the prayer beads to Halven triggers a powerful moment
*   **AO-11. Knights Access Corridor:** A faint underlying scent of incense — Halven's devotional herbs, burned long ago, still caught in the stone
*   **AO-15. The Sacristy:** A hidden compartment (Investigation DC 14) containing his prayer journal, his personal silver holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md), and a sealed vial of consecrated oil. The prayer journal's final entry ends with a desperate plea: _send someone_. The party is the someone
*   **AO-19. The Oath-Hall:** His name on the last honor plaque near the main door — _Halven Sere, Custodian, Appointed 571 CY_ — untouched amid the defacement because no one noticed or cared

By the time the party reaches **AO-20** and finds a thin man kneeling at a prayer bench behind iron bars, they should already know who he is.

### Rescue and Aftermath

Halven Sere cannot fight. He is frail from months of captivity — trembling hands, flinching at sudden noise, voice hoarse from disuse. He needs support to walk. In combat, he is a liability. But he is the single most important NPC in the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md).

**What he knows:**

*   The complete layout of the priory — every room, every corridor, every hidden corner on both the main level and the crypt level below
*   The secrets of the Altar of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) in [AO-14. The Chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) — the sacred geometry, the ward-stones, the hidden functions that [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) have been trying to extract from him
*   What [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is digging for beneath the crypts — and why the order sealed it away
*   The Dawn Invocation — a prayer that, when spoken at the chapel's east-facing window at sunrise, disrupts [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s geomantic work for 24 hours
*   He was present when the Gift of Beauty arrived at the priory. He knows where it was taken. He will not tell the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md), but he will tell rescuers

**What he will not share immediately:**

*   The crypt-level secrets. Trust must be earned — or the party must bring him to the chapel, where the connection between his faith and the wards becomes visible. He has kept these secrets through months of interrogation. He will not surrender them to strangers simply because they opened a door

**What he can do:**

*   Pray. His prayers have tangible effects in the chapel and crypts — DM's discretion on exact mechanics, but the divine architecture of the priory responds to him
*   Guide the party through the crypt level — fragile but willing, once trust is established
*   Serve as a witness. His testimony before the Viscounty, the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md), or even the Archclericy of Veluna under **Canon Hazen** can rally [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) faithful across the region and add political weight to the case against the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)

### Personality and Roleplaying

Halven Sere does not behave like a grateful quest NPC. He does not leap up, thank the party, and dispense lore. He is a man who has been imprisoned for months. His first reaction to rescue is **disbelief** — he has dreamed of this so many times that reality feels wrong. Then quiet, overwhelming gratitude that he fights to control because breaking down will cost time they do not have.

He speaks in a hoarse, careful voice. He chooses his words slowly. He has spent months in silence and has forgotten the rhythm of conversation. When he does speak, it matters — he does not waste sentences.

**Key dialogue moments:**

*   **When the cell opens:** A long silence. He stares. Then, barely above a whisper: _"Are you... you're not his."_ He stands slowly, using the prayer bench for support
*   **When asked about the priory:** _"They have taken everything. But not everything they think."_
*   **When asked about the chapel:** His eyes sharpen. _"The altar still holds. I feel it — even here. They have tried to break it. They cannot. Not while I..."_ He stops. He does not finish the sentence
*   **When asked about [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md):** _"He is... disciplined. He does not enjoy what he does. He does it because he believes it is inevitable. That is worse than cruelty. You cannot reason with inevitability."_
*   **When told [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is dead:** Another long silence. _"Then the building heard it. The stone knows."_ He believes the priory itself is aware of what happens within its walls
*   **When asked about the crypts:** _"[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is digging for something. Something the order sealed away. He asks me about it — every time. I tell him nothing."_ He withholds specifics until trust is earned
*   **When shown his prayer beads (from AO-08):** His hands close around them and do not open again. He prays in silence. After a long moment: _"You found these. Then you have been... thorough."_ This earns the party a significant measure of trust
*   **When shown his holy symbol (from AO-15):** He weeps. Quietly, without sound, tears running into his grey beard. He holds the symbol to his chest. _"I thought... I thought they had taken this too."_ This fully unlocks his willingness to share crypt-level knowledge
*   **When brought to the herb garden (AO-04):** He kneels and weeps again — not for himself, but because the herbs still grow. His care outlasted his captivity

**Core personality traits:**

*   Quiet. Patient. Stubborn beyond reason
*   Speaks rarely but means every word
*   Believes the priory is alive — that the stone remembers
*   Has never doubted [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). Not once. Not even during the worst interrogations
*   Does not hate his captors. Does not forgive them either. He simply endures
*   His faith is not performative — it is structural. He prays the way other men breathe

### Campaign Significance

Halven Sere is the moral center of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) dungeon. He is not a quest-giver, not a source of magic items, not a key to a secret door. He is a man who stayed when everyone else left, who maintained a place of worship alone for fourteen years, and who endured months of captivity without surrendering his faith or his knowledge.

Rescuing him should feel significant — not because of mechanical rewards, but because the players have spent rooms learning who he was through the traces he left behind. His name on the honor plaque. His carving in the stable. His prayer journal's last desperate entry. By the time they find him, they know him. And when that cell door opens, the story pays off.

Beyond the priory, Halven becomes a campaign asset: a witness who can testify before any authority in the Viscounty, a guide who knows the crypt level, a living symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s endurance that rallies faithful across the region. Protecting him — getting him out of the priory alive, keeping him safe on the road — becomes its own challenge. He is fragile. He cannot run. He will not leave the other prisoners behind. He is, in every way, a man worth saving and a burden to carry. That is the point.

##### Special Traits

**Consecrated Devotion.** Halven's sustained daily prayers maintain a faint connection to the priory's original divine architecture. While alive and praying within the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), the chapel wards and crypt seals remain partially active. If Halven dies or is removed from the priory for more than 24 hours, these protections begin to fail.

**Dawn Invocation (1/day).** If Halven speaks the Dawn Invocation at the chapel's east-facing window at sunrise, [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s geomantic excavation is disrupted for 24 hours — the floor vibration stops, tools crack, acolytes suffer headaches. This is not a spell; it is the priory's consecration responding to its custodian's voice.

**Priory Knowledge.** Halven knows the complete layout of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) — main level and crypt level — including secret passages, hidden compartments, and the location of every ward-stone. He can guide the party through any room and identify the original purpose of any space.

**Frail.** Months of captivity have weakened Halven. His speed is reduced to 20 ft. He cannot Dash. He has disadvantage on Strength and Constitution saving throws. If reduced to 0 hit points, he automatically fails one death saving throw.
