F15-K - Karnakia’s Lecture Hall
===============================

Karnakia’s lecture hall is a place of discipline, silence, and uncomfortable truths. Here, death is not sensationalized, feared, or glorified—it is **studied**, cataloged, and resisted. Students who enter this chamber quickly learn that necromancy, as Karnakia teaches it, is not about power over the dead, but **authority over fear**.

The hall serves a dual purpose within the academy:

*   a formal classroom for necromancy and divination, and
*   a defensive training chamber for recognizing, resisting, and countering death magic.

It is one of the most closely monitored halls on campus.

##### Entering the Karnakia’s Lecture Hall

You step into a lecture hall where the air feels still, as though even sound hesitates before entering. Stone benches rise in shallow tiers, each etched with faint sigils of warding and restraint. The walls are lined with diagrams—skeletal anatomies, arcane circles, planar cross-sections—rendered in chalk and ink with precise, unforgiving clarity. At the center of the chamber stands a broad lectern of dark wood and iron, its surface scarred by countless demonstrations.  
  
Students sit upright here. No one slouches. Some take notes with careful intensity; others watch the front of the room with fixed, nervous attention. A few whisper no more than breath allows. This is not a hall of spectacle. This is a place where mistakes are remembered.

### **Chamber Features**

#### **The Central Lectern**

A reinforced teaching dais inscribed with layered containment circles. Karnakia uses it to:

*   demonstrate necromantic theory without summoning,
*   project divinatory visions,
*   dismantle misconceptions about undeath.

Any uncontrolled magic cast within 10 feet of the lectern is dampened.

* * *

#### **Warded Stone Seating**

Each bench is subtly enchanted to:

*   prevent the rise of uncontrolled undead,
*   suppress panic-based magical surges,
*   alert faculty if forbidden necromantic rituals are attempted.

Students often describe a “pressure” while seated—subtle, but unmistakable.

* * *

#### **Instructional Diagrams**

Permanent chalk and ink illustrations cover the walls:

*   anatomical breakdowns of humanoid and monstrous undead,
*   spell flow diagrams showing necromantic corruption,
*   divination sightlines used to detect concealed death magic.

Karnakia regularly updates these diagrams herself.

* * *

#### **Observation Alcove**

A raised side alcove allows senior faculty or academy security to observe advanced lectures. It is rarely used—but its presence is well known.

* * *

#### **The Students**

Students assigned to Karnakia’s courses tend to fall into three categories:

*   **The Resolute:** future battle-mages, clerics, and ward specialists
*   **The Curious:** drawn by theory but unsettled by practice
*   **The Haunted:** those who have already encountered undeath firsthand

Attendance is mandatory. Voluntary enrollment is… uncommon.

* * *

#### **Use in Play**

*   Introduce **lore on undead, cult practices, or death magic** organically
*   Foreshadow Temple of Elemental Evil necromantic threats
*   Provide a controlled environment to **test player reactions** to dark knowledge
*   Offer research leads via Karnakia’s published works
*   Serve as a narrative counterpoint to “evil necromancer” tropes

* * *

#### **DM Note**

Karnakia’s hall works best when played **quietly**.  
Let silences linger.  
Let students watch the PCs.  
Let Karnakia speak plainly—and without apology.

This is not a place where heroes are reassured.  
It is where they are **prepared**.
