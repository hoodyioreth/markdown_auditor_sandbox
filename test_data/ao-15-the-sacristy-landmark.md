AO-15. The Sacristy
===================

_Vestment Chamber of the Azure Oath — Now Earth Temple Ritual Storage_

* * *

The sacristy once served as the **robing chamber** for the priests of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) who ministered to the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). Here, in the quiet minutes before services, the clergy donned their ceremonial vestments — blue-and-gold altar cloths embroidered with the lightning bolt of Heironeous, silver-chased prayer stoles, and the ivory-white surplices worn during funeral rites over the tombs below. A long cedar closet ran the length of the east wall, and a stone preparation table held holy oil, consecrated candles, and prayer books. The room smelled of cedar, beeswax, and old incense.

When the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) seized the priory, the sacristy was looted but not converted. The vestments were torn down and trampled. The holy oil was spilled or consumed. The prayer books were burned for kindling. But the room's original purpose — storage and preparation — proved useful enough that [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) repurposed it for his own needs. Clay jars of grave-earth line the shelves where holy oil once stood. Bundles of stone-dust incense — the resinous, mineral-sharp smell of [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) worship — have replaced the beeswax candles. Excavation tools lean against the wall beside coils of rope and miner's picks used by the duergar teams below.

The desecration here is casual, almost indifferent. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) didn't defile the sacristy with ceremony — they simply **replaced** one faith's trappings with another's, as though [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) had never mattered enough to hate.

##### Entering the Sacristy

A low wooden door opens into a narrow chamber cluttered with supplies. Clay jars and stone containers crowd rough shelves along the walls, and coils of rope sit piled beside mining picks and iron stakes. The air is thick with a sharp mineral smell — not quite incense, not quite stone dust, but something between the two that stings the back of your throat.  
  
Beneath the clutter, you catch glimpses of what this room used to be. A long cedar closet stands against the east wall, its doors hanging open and its interior gutted. Fragments of blue-and-gold cloth lie crumpled on the floor — once fine embroidery, now used as rags to wipe tools. A stone preparation table in the center holds clay bowls of dark powder and bundles of dried herbs you don't recognize.  
  
In the far corner, a torn vestment has been nailed to the wall. Someone has drawn a crude triangular symbol over the lightning bolt stitched into its chest.

### Features of the Sacristy

#### Cedar Vestment Closet

The long closet that once held the priory's ceremonial garments has been gutted. Its cedar panels are scorched where someone started a fire inside it — likely burning the prayer books. The interior still faintly smells of cedar beneath the char.

A character examining the closet finds:

**DC 10 Investigation**  
The closet is empty of anything valuable. Ash, charred leather bindings, and a few brass clasps from destroyed vestments.

**DC 14 Investigation**  
The back panel of the closet has a **hairline seam** that doesn't match the rest of the woodwork. The cedar grain runs the wrong direction — this panel was installed separately. It's a hidden compartment.

See **Halven Sere's Hidden Cache** below.

* * *

#### Stone Preparation Table

The central worktable once held holy oil and consecration tools. Now it serves [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s acolytes as a **ritual preparation station**.

Items currently on the table:

*   Three clay bowls of **grave-earth** — dark soil taken from freshly opened tombs in the crypts below
*   Bundles of **stone-dust incense** — burns with a mineral smell, used in [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) rites
*   A mortar and pestle stained with ground crystal residue
*   A copper bowl containing a **brownish liquid** — rendered tallow mixed with powdered limestone (used to anoint the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) altar in the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md))

A character studying the preparation materials may learn:

**DC 12 Religion or Arcana**  
These are ritual components for [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) worship. The grave-earth and stone-dust incense are standard offerings to the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — the faith teaches that all things return to stone.

**DC 15 Religion**  
The grave-earth isn't just ceremonial — it's taken from **consecrated tombs**. Using Heironean grave-soil in [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) rites is a deliberate blasphemy meant to **break the divine ward on the crypts below**. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is systematically desanctifying the burial ground.

**DC 18 Arcana**  
The crystal residue in the mortar is ground from **Flan resonance stones** — the same type of fragment found in [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s hidden cache ([AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md)). [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is consuming these stones as part of his geomantic amplification ritual. The supply is running low — only a few fragments remain.

* * *

#### Excavation Supplies

The south wall is stacked with tools and materials for [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation of the crypts below.

*   Four miner's picks (well-used, duergar-forged — the handles are shorter than human-standard)
*   Coils of hempen rope (200 ft total)
*   Iron pitons and hammers
*   Three shuttered lanterns with extra oil flasks
*   A canvas sack containing rubble samples tagged with chalk marks (letters in a script a DC 14 Intelligence (History) check identifies as **Flan**)

The duergar-forged picks are distinctive. A character who has encountered duergar before or succeeds on a **DC 13 Intelligence (History)** check recognizes the craftsmanship — these tools weren't looted from caravans. They were **commissioned**.

* * *

#### Defaced Vestment

A single torn vestment of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) has been nailed to the far wall like a trophy. The blue-and-gold embroidery shows a knight kneeling beneath the lightning bolt — the standard Azure Oath devotional image. Someone has drawn the [Black Earth](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) triangle in charcoal over the lightning bolt.

A character examining it closely notices:

**DC 12 Perception**  
The vestment is torn but not destroyed. The embroidery work is exceptional — silver thread on blue silk. This was a **high priest's vestment**, not a common surplice.

**DC 14 Religion (Heironeous)**  
This is a **Vestment of the Vigil** — worn only by the priory's senior officiant during funeral rites over the crypts. It would have belonged to the last high priest of the Azure Oath. The fact that it was torn but kept — displayed as a trophy rather than burned — suggests the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) wanted it **visible** as a statement of conquest.

A cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) who recovers this vestment and carries it into the crypts below gains **advantage on their first Turn Undead** attempt in the crypt level — the vestment retains a fading resonance of the funeral rites it witnessed.

* * *

#### Halven Sere's Hidden Cache

This is the sacristy's most important feature — a **hidden compartment** in the back panel of the cedar closet that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) never found.

**Finding it:**

*   **Investigation DC 14** (examining the closet interior) — notices the mismatched cedar grain
*   **Religion DC 16** (a character actively praying to [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) or invoking the god's name while searching) — feels a faint warmth emanating from behind the panel, as though the god himself is pointing the way

The compartment is shallow — barely six inches deep — and lined with faded blue silk. Inside:

*   **Halven Sere's Prayer Journal** — A small leather-bound book, well-worn, written in a careful hand. The final entries are desperate and hurried (see sidebar)
*   **A Silver Holy Symbol of Heironeous** — The custodian's personal symbol, wrapped in cloth. Not ceremonially valuable but deeply personal. Worth 25 gp as silver; priceless as a story prop
*   **A Sealed Vial of Consecrated Oil** — The last flask of holy oil from the sacristy's stores. **Halven Sere** hid it before the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) finished looting. Functions as **Holy Water** (2d6 radiant damage to undead/fiends) and can be used to re-consecrate a single defiled altar or grave

The prayer journal's final entry reads:

> _They have taken the chapel. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) speaks of an old god beneath the stones. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is worse — he listens to the earth the way our priests once listened to thunder. I have hidden what I can. The oil. My symbol. This journal. If the Lightning Lord wills it, someone will find these words and know that the Azure Oath did not surrender. We were overcome. That is different.  
>   
> I do not know how much longer they will keep me. I pray I am of use to them alive. The others — the travelers, the merchant's boy — I cannot protect them if I am dead.  
>   
> Heironeous, if you hear me through the stone, send someone. Anyone. Even one sword is enough if the hand that holds it is true._

* * *

#### Encounter

The sacristy is staffed by **1–2 [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) acolytes** during active ritual preparation. These are low-ranking cult members — not fighters, not zealots, just frightened converts performing menial work under [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s orders.

If the party encounters them:

*   **During ritual preparation (day):** 1–2 acolytes grinding components, bundling incense, filling clay jars. They are **not expecting trouble**. Surprise is automatic if the party enters quietly from **AO-24**
*   **Between rituals (night or off-hours):** Room is empty. Supplies sit unattended. This is the optimal time to search
*   **During monastery alert:** Acolytes flee toward the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) to warn [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) or toward the [courtyard](ao-06-the-outer-courtyard-landmark.md). They will not fight

**Acolyte Tactics:** These are frightened people, not soldiers. If confronted, they surrender immediately. If threatened, they babble about [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s rituals and the "voice in the stone" — genuine terror, not loyalty. They can reveal the location of [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s quarters and the crypt entrance, and they know **Halven Sere** is held prisoner but not where exactly.

### DM Notes

#### The Emotional Pivot

AO-15 is a **tone room**. Up to this point, the priory has been a tactical dungeon — guards, patrols, command structure, combat encounters. The sacristy is where the **human cost** becomes personal. **Halven Sere**'s prayer journal is the first time the party hears a victim's voice — not a villain's, not a NPC they're fighting, but a **man who was here when the faith died**.

Don't rush the journal. When a player finds it and reads the final entry, slow down. Read it aloud if the table responds to that. Let the silence after _"send someone"_ hang. The party **is** the someone Halven prayed for. They don't need you to say it. They'll feel it.

The sacred objects in the cache — the holy symbol, the oil, the journal — are mechanically useful, but their real value is emotional. If the party carries the holy symbol into **Halven Sere**'s cell (AO-20), he will recognize it instantly and weep. If they bring the vestment into the crypts, they carry the weight of the Azure Oath's last rites with them. These are not loot. They are **story**.

#### The Desecration Contrast

The sacristy's desecration is different from every other room. The [reavers](romagE28099s-reavers-occupy-the-azure-oath-article.md) in [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) scratched murals. [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) in [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md) edited the theology. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) deliberately desanctify the tombs. But here, the desecration is **indifference**. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) didn't bother to formally defile the sacristy — they just **used it**. The altar cloths are rags. The holy oil was consumed. The prayer books were kindling. The faith of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) wasn't worth the effort of a proper desecration.

This should feel worse than hate. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) didn't destroy the sacristy's sacred purpose — they **forgot it**.

#### The Hidden Cache and Pacing

The hidden compartment is designed to reward **thorough parties** — those who search past the first layer of clutter. The DC 14 Investigation check is moderate, but the key is that the closet **looks empty** on a casual glance. A party that opens the closet, sees ash and char, and moves on will miss everything.

Give the compartment a second chance if the party is invested in **Halven Sere**. If they've already met him (in AO-20), or if they've heard his name from other rooms ([AO-04](ao-04-the-custodianE28099s-herb-patch-landmark.md), [AO-08](ao-08-interior-service-hall-landmark.md)), and they're searching the sacristy specifically looking for traces of the custodian, lower the DC to 12 or grant advantage. The story works better when they find it.

The Religion DC 16 alternate path (praying while searching) exists for paladin and cleric characters. This is their room — let the god respond.

#### Grave-Earth and the Crypt Connection

The grave-earth on the preparation table is a **direct clue** that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is disturbing the tombs below. If the party hasn't yet grasped that the crypts are actively being excavated, this is where the evidence becomes unmistakable. The soil is fresh, dark, and carries the faint smell of decay. Combined with the duergar-forged tools, the picture should be clear: something is happening beneath their feet, and it involves opening graves.

The ground Flan resonance stones (DC 18 Arcana) connect to [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s research in [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md) and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s geomantic project. If the party has already found [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s hidden rune fragment, they'll recognize the crystal residue as the same material — and realize [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) has been withholding resources from [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md). Internal tension the party can exploit.

#### The Vestment as Relic

The Vestment of the Vigil isn't a magic item in the traditional sense — it's a **narrative key** that unlocks mechanical benefits through story logic. The advantage on Turn Undead in the crypts reflects the vestment's connection to the funeral rites performed over those same tombs. If no one in the party can Turn Undead, the vestment is still worth taking — it can be returned to **Halven Sere** or to the [Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) in Verbobonc as proof of what happened here.

#### Acolytes as Information Sources

The acolytes in this room are the **weakest link** in the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s information chain. They're not fanatics — they're laborers recruited from [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) or the surrounding hills, pressed into service grinding ritual components. They surrender instantly and will answer questions to save their lives. They know:

*   [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is the high priest and he's "listening to the stone" — they're terrified of him
*   [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is the war-chief — he's somewhere in the north wing
*   Duergar miners are below, working in the crypts
*   "The old man" (**Halven Sere**) is held somewhere nearby — they've seen him brought through under guard
*   The grave-earth comes from "the old graves down below" — they don't like handling it
*   They don't know about the false-flag campaign, [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s operations, or the political conspiracy. They're too low-ranking

#### Doors and Connections

The sacristy connects to:

*   **North:** [AO-14. Chapel of Heironeous](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) — heavy wooden door, usually unlocked. The primary route between sacristy and chapel
*   **East:** **AO-24. The Vigil Ante-Room** — interior door connecting to the service wing checkpoint. This is how the acolytes enter from the eastern wing

There is no exterior exit. The sacristy is an interior room — enclosed, defensible, and easy to miss if the party bypasses the chapel zone entirely. If the party enters from **AO-24** (via [AO-08](ao-08-interior-service-hall-landmark.md) and the eastern service wing), they reach the sacristy before the chapel — and the emotional payload of **Halven Sere**'s journal lands before they see what the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has done to the altar.

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I search the closet."** — DC 10 Investigation: empty, ash, char. DC 14 Investigation: hidden compartment (mismatched cedar grain). Religion DC 16 (praying while searching): warmth behind the panel
*   **"I examine the preparation table."** — Religion/Arcana DC 12 (Earth Temple ritual components), DC 15 (grave-earth blasphemy — desanctifying the crypts), Arcana DC 18 (Flan resonance stone residue — connects to Caidax's research)
*   **"I look at the tools."** — History DC 13 identifies duergar craftsmanship. The short handles confirm non-human miners
*   **"I take the vestment off the wall."** — No check needed. Religion DC 14 identifies it as a Vestment of the Vigil (high priest's funeral garment). Advantage on first Turn Undead in crypts if carried
*   **"I read the prayer journal."** — No check needed. The final entry is the emotional core of the room. If a character reads it aloud, give the moment weight
*   **"I interrogate the acolytes."** — No check needed for basic information (they surrender). Intimidation DC 10 for details about Varnor's habits. They don't know anything about the command wing or the conspiracy
*   **"I use the holy oil."** — Functions as Holy Water (2d6 radiant vs. undead/fiends). Can also re-consecrate a single defiled altar or grave. Only one vial — make it count
*   **"I try to detect magic in the room."** — The three crystals on the table radiate faint transmutation magic. The consecrated oil radiates faint evocation (divine). The Flan rune residue radiates faint conjuration. The hidden compartment does NOT radiate magic — it's mundane carpentry, which is why the Earth Temple missed it
