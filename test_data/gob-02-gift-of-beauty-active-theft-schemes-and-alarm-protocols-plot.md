GOB-02-Gift of Beauty — Active Theft Schemes and Alarm Protocols
================================================================

> Five factions are actively hunting the Gift of Beauty right now — and the Sepulchre-Priory garrison has layered alarm protocols ready to trigger the moment anyone tries to take it. This article covers Lareth the Beautiful's five parallel theft schemes (the Laughing Dog intercept, the emissary rejection, the false Viscounty agent, Gattas Void's bandit "help," and Cookie's Fire Temple intelligence pipeline), the Priory's triple-lock reliquary security (DCs 14–18), the three-stage garrison alert response, the hour-by-hour pursuit timeline after the Gift is taken, and the five safe delivery destinations where the party can end the chase. This is Part 2 of the Gift of Beauty quest reference. For the Gift itself, factions, and DM decision points, see GOB-Gift of Beauty — What It Is and Who Wants It. For NPC quick reference and table-tested scenarios, see GOB-Gift of Beauty — DM Quick Reference and Scenarios.

### Active Pursuit Schemes — Who Is Hunting the Gift of Beauty Right Now

**The Gift of Beauty is not sitting uncontested. Right now — at the moment the campaign begins — multiple factions are actively pursuing the Gift through parallel schemes.** This section consolidates every active pursuit so the DM can track who is doing what, through whom, and what happens when the party enters the picture.

#### Scheme 1: Lareth's Laughing Dog Inn Intercept (FAILED)

**Architect:** [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md)  
**Agent:** **Varek the Smiler (Smiley)** — innkeeper at the Laughing Dog Inn, 10 miles south of Sheernobb on the Long Road  
**Method:** Varek's crew was ordered to intercept the Gift during transport from [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md)'s estate to the Celene border  
**Status:** **FAILED.** The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s professional raiders under [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) reached the convoy first. Varek's crew was too slow. Lareth considers this a humiliating failure — Varek's incompetence cost him the initiative  
**Current relevance:** The Laughing Dog Inn remains a live intelligence node. Varek still works for Lareth. Any party traveling the Long Road may stop at the inn and encounter Varek — a friendly, accommodating innkeeper who reports everything to Lareth

#### Scheme 2: The Emissary to the Priory (REJECTED)

**Architect:** [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md)  
**Agent:** Unnamed emissary sent to the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md)  
**Method:** Direct approach — Lareth sent a representative to inspect the reliquary, likely claiming Greater Temple authority or [Romag](romag-person.md)'s endorsement  
**Status:** **REJECTED.** [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) refused access and escorted the emissary out under armed guard. The incident is recorded in Tharvok's unsent letter to Romag (found on the war table in AO-18)  
**Current relevance:** Tharvok is now suspicious of anyone claiming external authority over the Gift. This makes the party's task harder if they approach the priory claiming to represent anyone other than the Earth Temple chain of command. It also means Lareth knows the priory garrison is alert and the direct approach will not work again

#### Scheme 3: Using the Party as Unwitting Tools (ACTIVE)

**Architect:** [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md)  
**Agent:** Lareth himself (disguised) or an intermediary  
**Method:** Lareth approaches the party — in Hommlet, on the road, or through a contact — posing as a representative of the Viscounty or another legitimate authority. He tells the party the Gift of Beauty is being held at the Sepulchre-Priory by Earth Temple cultists. He provides _accurate intelligence_ about the priory's defenses, garrison strength, and the reliquary location. He asks the party to recover the Gift and return it to him  
**Why it works:** Everything Lareth tells the party is _true_ — the priory IS occupied by cultists, the Gift IS there, the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) IS the enemy. The only lie is who Lareth is and why he wants the Gift  
**Status:** **ACTIVE.** This is Lareth's primary scheme. He is actively looking for capable adventurers to manipulate  
**Every outcome serves Lareth:**

*   Party recovers the Gift and delivers it to "the Viscounty agent" → Lareth has the Gift
*   Party recovers the Gift but gets suspicious and keeps it → Lareth has still weakened the Earth Temple's hold on the priory without risking his own people
*   Earth Temple kills the party → Lareth has cost [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) resources and attention for free

#### Scheme 4: Bandit "Assistance" — Gattas Void's Hidden Orders (ACTIVE)

**Architect:** [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md)  
**Agent:** **Gattas Void** — bandit captain operating from the [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) region  
**Method:** Lareth offers the party "help" in the form of Gattas Void's bandit crew. The bandits fight alongside the party through the priory — clearing rooms, taking casualties, appearing to be loyal allies. Once the reliquary is opened and the Gift is in hand, Gattas Void's bandits have standing orders: **seize the Gift and return it to Lareth. The party is expendable**  
**Status:** **ACTIVE.** This scheme activates if Lareth recruits the party through Scheme 3 and the party accepts his offer of manpower  
**The question the party must answer:** Do Gattas Void's bandits serve the party's mission, or Lareth's? The answer is always Lareth's. The bandits will turn on the party the moment the Gift is secured — or attempt to slip away with it during the confusion of combat

#### Scheme 5: Cookie and the Fire Temple — Independent Pursuit (ACTIVE)

**Architect:** **Alrrem** — High Priest of the Fire Temple  
**Agent:** **Cookie** — Fire Cult priest operating undercover as the cook at the Laughing Dog Inn  
**Method:** Cookie uses carrier pigeons to send intelligence reports to **Alrrem**'s contacts. He tracks movements along the Long Road — caravans, adventurers, Earth Temple supply runs, and any party heading toward or returning from the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). Cookie does not know Lareth's true allegiance. He does not know he is reporting on a fellow Temple operative's intelligence network. He is independently feeding information to a _rival_ faction  
**Status:** **ACTIVE.** Cookie is reporting continuously. The Fire Temple has been receiving intelligence about the Gift's journey, the ambush aftermath, and any recovery attempts  
**What makes this dangerous:** If the party recovers the Gift from the priory and travels north along the Long Road, passing through or near the Laughing Dog Inn, Cookie's pigeons will have already carried the news south. **Alrrem** can dispatch a Fire Temple strike team to ambush the party on the return journey — when they think the quest is over, when they are exhausted from the priory, and when the Gift is in the open. This is the cruelest twist in the Gift of Beauty quest

##### DM Notes: Tracking Active Pursuit Schemes

Not all five schemes need to be active simultaneously. The DM should decide at campaign start which schemes are in play:

*   **Minimum complexity:** Scheme 3 alone (Lareth uses the party as tools). This is sufficient for a focused priory quest
*   **Standard complexity:** Schemes 3 + 4 (Lareth uses the party AND sends Gattas Void's bandits as a betrayal trigger). This adds a betrayal mechanic to the priory infiltration
*   **Full complexity:** All five schemes active. Schemes 1–2 are background (already failed/rejected but leave evidence). Schemes 3–4 create the priory mission. Scheme 5 creates the return-journey ambush. This is the intended sandbox experience — maximum faction interplay, maximum consequences
*   **Cookie's intelligence is the slow-burn threat.** Even if the DM does not activate a Fire Temple ambush on the return, Cookie's reports mean the Fire Temple _knows_ what happened at the priory. This intelligence can surface later — in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), at the Greater Temple, or through faction politics when the Fire Temple uses its knowledge as leverage against the Earth Temple

 

### Alarm Protocols — Sepulchre-Priory Response When the Gift Is Threatened

**The Gift of Beauty is the most valuable object at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md).** [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) knows this. His garrison defense protocols prioritize the Gift above all other assets — above prisoners, above the geomantic excavation, above the scriptorium records. If the Gift is threatened, the entire priory shifts to defense-in-depth around AO-18.

#### Reliquary Security — The Triple Lock

The Gift sits in a reinforced stone reliquary in AO-18 (Tharvok's Quarters), banded with iron and sealed with three independent locks:

*   **Lock 1 — Tharvok's Key:** Iron key on a leather cord around Tharvok's neck. Thieves' Tools DC 18. The key never leaves Tharvok's person — he sleeps with it on
*   **Lock 2 — Romag's Key:** NOT at the priory. Held by [Romag](romag-person.md) at the Greater Temple in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). Thieves' Tools DC 18. **Lucius Graeme** carries a wax impression of this key but not the key itself — the impression is useless for opening the lock, but it proves Lucius is authorized to speak for Romag
*   **Lock 3 — Earth Temple Divine Ward:** Dispel Magic DC 15, or speak the opening invocation. The invocation phrase is found in [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s notes in AO-17 — a party that searches Varnor's quarters before attempting the reliquary has a significant advantage
*   **Brute Force:** AC 20, HP 60, resistance to all damage except radiant. Breaking the reliquary triggers a thunderwave trap: Constitution DC 14, 2d8 thunder damage, pushed 10 feet — **audible throughout the entire north wing**. Any attempt to force the reliquary alerts the garrison immediately

 

#### Garrison Alert Stages

##### Stage 1 — Suspicious Activity Detected

**Trigger:** Sentries report intruders in the priory compound, or combat noise is heard from any room in the north wing.  
**Tharvok's response:** [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) rallies available guards to AO-18. He moves to a defensive position inside his quarters, places himself between the reliquary and the door, and prepares for combat. He sends a runner to [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) requesting arcane support.  
**Garrison mobilization:** All off-duty guards in the barracks (AO-06) are roused. Perimeter sentries at the wall breaches hold position — Tharvok does not strip the outer defenses. Total available response force: 6–10 guards within 3 rounds, depending on time of day.

##### Stage 2 — Direct Threat to the Gift

**Trigger:** Combat reaches AO-18, the reliquary is tampered with, or the thunderwave trap detonates.  
**Tharvok's response:** Tharvok fights to the death to protect the Gift. He does not retreat. He does not negotiate. The Gift is his charge from [Romag](romag-person.md), and losing it means losing everything — his command, his standing, and his purpose. He activates his full combat loadout and calls for [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) to provide spell support from the corridor.  
**Garrison mobilization:** Every available combatant in the priory converges on the north wing. [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) (if present) leads the counterattack from the south. Varnor may deploy earth-shaping magic to seal corridors or create barriers. Prisoners in AO-20 are ignored — all resources go to the Gift.

##### Stage 3 — Gift Taken, Pursuit Protocol

**Trigger:** The reliquary is opened and the Gift removed, OR intruders are seen fleeing with the reliquary itself.  
**Tharvok's response:** If still alive, Tharvok pursues relentlessly. He sends runners to both wall breaches and the main gate to intercept any escape route. He dispatches a rider to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) via the escape tunnel to alert [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) and [Romag](romag-person.md): _"The Gift is taken. Pursuers deployed. Seal the roads."_  
**Chain of command notification:**

*   **Hour 0:** Rider dispatched through escape tunnel to [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) at the Greater Temple
*   **Hours 2–4:** Feldrin notifies [Romag](romag-person.md). Romag is furious. He orders [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) to track the thieves and recover the Gift at any cost
*   **Hours 4–8:** **Lucius Graeme**'s courier network activates. Intelligence requests go out to agents in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), along the Long Road, and at the Laughing Dog Inn. Every road watcher and informant is now looking for the party
*   **Hours 8–12:** **Hedrack** is briefed. His response depends on scope — if the party also freed **Halven Sere** or recovered evidence from the scriptorium, the breach is catastrophic and Hedrack treats the party as a strategic threat rather than common thieves
*   **Beyond 24 hours:** [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) receives word in Verbobonc City. If the party heads to the capital with the Gift, Lakash activates his own network to track, intercept, or discredit them

##### What the Pursuit Looks Like on the Ground

A party fleeing the priory with the Gift of Beauty is not home free. The DM should track the following pursuit elements:

*   **Immediate pursuit (0–4 hours):** Surviving priory garrison on foot or horseback. If Maris is alive and free, he leads the pursuit — he is the most dangerous tracker in the priory. DC 16 Survival to evade Maris in open terrain. DC 13 in heavy forest
*   **Road network (4–12 hours):** **Lucius Graeme**'s agents are alerted. Road watchers on the Long Road, the Sheernobb trail, and the river crossing near [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) are instructed to watch for the party. Traveling by main road becomes increasingly dangerous
*   **Cookie's pigeons (8–24 hours):** If the party passes anywhere near the Laughing Dog Inn, **Cookie** sends word south. The Fire Temple now knows the Gift is loose and may dispatch its own recovery team (see Scheme 5 above)
*   **Nulb blockade (12–24 hours):** If the party's route passes through or near Nulb, [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md)'s agents are waiting. The Waterside Hostel, the Boatman's Tavern, and the river crossing are all monitored
*   **Lareth's opportunism (24–48 hours):** [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) learns the Gift has been taken — either through his own informants or through Varek at the Laughing Dog. If the party is not already working for him, he considers whether to approach them now with an offer, or to let the Earth Temple exhaust itself hunting them before he makes his move

##### Safe Delivery — Where Can the Party Take the Gift?

The pursuit does not end until the Gift reaches a faction strong enough to hold it. The party's delivery choice determines the campaign's political outcome:

*   **Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) at Deleveu Manor:** The intended handoff. Deleveu arranges transfer to the Celene Knights at the border. The party earns the Coin of Friendship, Deleveu's personal gratitude, and a quiet favor from the Viscount. Safest political outcome
*   **[Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) patrol:** If the party encounters a Borderer patrol while fleeing, the Mounted Borderers will provide escort — but this makes the Gift's recovery public knowledge. Verbobonc politics ignite
*   **Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) in the Gnarley Forest:** Hrudek blessed the Gift. He will safeguard it and arrange delivery through druidic channels. This path avoids Verbobonc's political establishment entirely
*   **[Patriarch Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) in Verbobonc City:** The Church of Rao receives the Gift as a spiritual recovery. Martiolus uses it to strengthen Veluna relations and the NSQ-05 connection. This elevates the Church of Rao's political influence
*   **Lareth's "Viscounty agent":** If the party was recruited under Scheme 3 and still believes Lareth's cover, they deliver the Gift to him. He vanishes. The party eventually learns they were duped. Recovery becomes a second quest — now tracking [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) to the [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md)

##### Lareth's Master Logic

_"Every outcome serves Lareth."_

*   Party recovers Gift, delivers to Lareth → He wins
*   Party keeps the Gift → Earth Temple weakened for free
*   Earth Temple kills the party → Tharvok loses resources for free
*   Party pretends to work for Lareth → Viable sandbox path — the party becomes a double agent

**The Cruelest Twist:** If the party recovers the Gift and travels north on the Long Road, Cookie's pigeons have already carried the news. Alrrem can dispatch a Fire Temple strike team to ambush the party on the return — when they think the quest is over.
