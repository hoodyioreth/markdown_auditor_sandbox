7\. The Weary Woodsman
======================

The **Weary Woodsman** stands as a rugged yet welcoming landmark on the **north-east road out of Twilight Falls**, serving as a vital hub for **woodsmen, merchants, and travelers** braving the **Gnarley Forest’s treacherous paths**. Established nearly **seventy years ago**, this renowned inn has long been a favored respite for those navigating the **Forest Road**, linking [Dyvers, Free Lands of](dyvers2C-free-lands-of-organization.md)**, the Wild Coast,** [Nulb](../c/nulb-category.md)**, and the** [Verbobonc, Viscounty](../c/verbobonc2C-viscounty-category.md). With a finely crafted sign depicting a **weary axeman resting on a tree stump**, the Weary Woodsman offers hearty meals, strong ale, and a warm hearth for those seeking comfort after long days of travel and labor.

Now owned by **Hidelver Nown**, a **half-elf of mixed human and Sherendyl elven heritage**, the inn continues to thrive as a key establishment in **Twilight Falls’ economy** and a **center of local gossip, trade, and political maneuvering**.

* * *

> “Rangers and merchants sit at the same table here, though only one of them trusts the other.”

### **Description**

The **Weary Woodsman** is **a large, two-story structure**, blending practical human craftsmanship with subtle **elven influences** in its design. Built from sturdy **local timber and river stones**, the inn’s **high-peaked roof and broad porch** offer a rustic charm, while its **interior boasts a spacious common room, private lodgings, and a bustling taproom**.

##### **Exterior**

*   Constructed from **heavy timber and river stones**, designed to endure the harsh forest weather.
*   **A large wooden sign** depicts an exhausted axeman resting on a tree stump.
*   A **covered stable and wagon lot** provide secure lodging for beasts and carts.
*   **Trellises adorned with ivy and climbing roses**, an addition from Hidelver’s mother’s influence.
*   A **rear courtyard** features an open firepit where meals are often roasted over an open flame.

##### **Interior**

*   **Main Taproom:** A sprawling **common hall** filled with **rough-hewn wooden tables, a roaring hearth, and the scent of roasted meat and ale**.
*   **Private Lodgings:** Eight rooms for rent, with **thick woolen blankets and sturdy beds**, catering to **merchants, rangers, and druids alike**.
*   **The Hearthside Nook:** A quieter space lined with **books, elven tapestries, and maps of the Gnarley Forest**, appealing to scholars and rangers.
*   **Cellar and Storehouse:** Stocked with preserved food, **locally brewed mead**, and casks of Dyvers-imported ale.

* * *

### **History**

Founded by **Heuon Nown**, a **seasoned woodsman and trader**, the inn began as a simple rest stop for loggers and trappers moving goods between **Twilight Falls and Dyvers**. Over time, the Weary Woodsman grew into a **well-respected inn**, frequented not just by **woodcutters and rangers**, but also by merchants and **those seeking passage through the Gnarley Forest**.

##### **Key Events**

*   **520 CY:** **Heuon Nown** establishes the inn, carving a niche for weary woodsmen needing food and shelter.
*   **544 CY:** Marries an **elven woman of Clan Sherendyl**, fostering stronger ties between the inn and the local elves.
*   **550s CY:** The inn expands, adding **a second floor and secure stables**, accommodating an increasing number of travelers.
*   **570 CY:** Heuon passes, leaving the inn to his half-elf son, **Hidelver Nown**, who modernizes and strengthens its ties with **elven traders and merchants**.
*   **Present Day:** The Weary Woodsman is **one of the most established inns in Twilight Falls**, known for its welcoming hearth and well-guarded grounds.

* * *

> “Elves bring the wine, woodsmen bring the timber, and Hidelver makes a fortune selling both.”

### **Role in Twilight Falls**

The Weary Woodsman is more than just an inn—it is a **social and political hub**, serving as neutral ground where **merchants, rangers, and even druids** can meet and exchange news.

##### **Political & Trade Influence**

*   Acts as **a meeting ground** for traders traveling between **Dyvers, the Wild Coast, and Verbobonc**.
*   Maintains **strong ties to Clan Sherendyl**, allowing rare elven goods to be traded discreetly.
*   Serves as a **neutral zone** between the **Gnarley Rangers and local merchants**, preventing disputes from escalating.
*   Hosts **visiting Mounted Borderers** from Verbobonc, who use it as a rest stop while patrolling the northern trade routes.

##### **Services Offered**

*   **Food & Drink:** Known for **roast venison, hearty stews, and a signature blackberry mead brewed from local berries**.
*   **Lodging:** Private rooms and **bunk-style dormitories** for less affluent travelers.
*   **Horse & Wagon Stables:** Secure storage and caretaking for steeds and carts.
*   **Forest Guides & Scouts:** The inn brokers connections between travelers and **local rangers or elven guides** for safe passage through the Gnarley.

> “You’ll hear tales in this taproom that’ll make you laugh, cry, or leave town faster than you planned.”

### **Staff of the Weary Woodsman**

The Weary Woodsman relies on a **small but dedicated staff** who help Hidelver keep the inn running smoothly. Each has their own reasons for working here, and all have a story to tell.

![](./images/Mara%20Thornbrook%20Senior%20Serving%20Wench.jpg "Mara Thornbrook Senior Serving Wench.jpg")

##### **Mara Thornbrook – Senior Serving Wench**

Mara is a **tough, no-nonsense woman in her late 30s**, with years of experience managing rowdy woodsmen and drunk merchants. She runs the **taproom with an iron glare and a sharp tongue**, keeping troublemakers in line with **wit, intimidation, and the occasional wooden spoon**.

*   **Role:** Oversees the serving staff, ensures customers behave, and keeps tabs on local gossip.
*   **Personality:** Sharp-witted, sarcastic, and fiercely protective of the other serving girls.
*   **Motivation:** Works hard to provide for her younger siblings back home in Twilight Falls.

* * *

##### **Lidda “Lark” Redfern – Young Serving Wench**

![](./images/Lidda%20%E2%80%9CLark%E2%80%9D%20Redfern%20Young%20Serving%20Wench.jpg "Lidda “Lark” Redfern Young Serving Wench.jpg")

Lidda “Lark” Redfern Young Serving Wench by

Lidda, nicknamed “Lark” for her **bright singing voice**, is a **cheerful, freckled young woman of 19**, who charms customers with her **lively energy and quick wit**. Unlike Mara, she **enjoys the attention** of travelers but knows how to handle herself when things get rough.

*   **Role:** Serves food and drink, entertains guests with songs, and gathers interesting rumors.
*   **Personality:** Playful, flirty, and clever, but knows when to be cautious.
*   **Motivation:** Dreams of adventure and saving up coin to travel beyond Twilight Falls.

### **Old Tegg – The Gruff Cook**

An **aging ex-mercenary turned cook**, Tegg is **bald, barrel-chested, and constantly grumbling about fools and wastrels**. He’s **ruthless in the kitchen**, tolerating no waste and **brooking no nonsense from drunkards who complain about his stew**.

*   **Role:** Prepares meals, manages food stores, and ensures the kitchen runs efficiently.
*   **Personality:** Gruff, blunt, and quick to throw a ladle at anyone causing trouble.
*   **Motivation:** Prefers the quiet life of cooking to the horrors of war, but still keeps a **dagger under his apron** just in case.

#### **Conclusion**

The **Weary Woodsman** is more than just an inn; it is **a cornerstone of Twilight Falls**, blending **practicality, diplomacy, and tradition**. Whether it’s **a ranger seeking shelter, a merchant securing safe passage, or a druid keeping an eye on the human impact on the land**, all find their way to the Woodsman. **Under Hidelver Nown’s careful stewardship, the inn remains a crucial meeting place**, ensuring that both **human industry and elven heritage** can coexist in the heart of the Gnarley Forest.

**Hidden Element – The Crimson Reapers (DM Only)  
**The Weary Woodsman has become an invaluable asset for [The Crimson Reapers](the-crimson-reapers-organization.md), a feared mercenary and criminal syndicate operating out of Nulb and Dyvers. While Hidelver is unaware of their full influence, the Reapers use the inn as a watchpoint for merchant caravans, gathering intelligence on profitable targets.  
  
**Brigand Spies in the Inn  
Jothan "The Quill" Calder (Human Rogue 5):** A scribe-for-hire, posing as a traveling chronicler, secretly tracking merchant movements and smuggling routes.  
**Kedra "Ash-Tongue" Verlan (Half-Orc Fighter 4)**: A disguised mercenary, blending in as a wandering sell-sword, ensuring the Reapers’ interests remain protected.  
  
These spies regularly relay information back to Nulb, ensuring the Crimson Reapers remain one step ahead of any opposition.
