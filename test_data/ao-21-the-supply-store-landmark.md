AO-21. The Supply Store
=======================

> The monastery larder where the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) chapter once stored grain, preserved meats, brewing supplies, and provisions for travelers on the Long Road. A practical room — cool, dry, stone-walled — built to keep a knightly order and its guests fed through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) winters. Now the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) uses it as the intake point of their theft pipeline — the room where stolen goods arrive from Long Road raids before being sorted and catalogued in **AO-19. The Oath-Hall**. Crates of looted trade goods, gnome-crafted wares from [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), weapons stripped from caravan guards, and a bin of prisoner belongings fill the shelves that once held flour sacks and jars of preserves. The room is usually unoccupied — the quietest space in the priory — and that silence makes it both a potential safe harbor for cautious parties and a damning archive of everything the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has taken.

_Monastery Larder of the Azure Oath — Now the Earth Temple Supply Depot and Raided Goods Intake_

* * *

Every monastery needs a larder. The [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) was a knightly order, not a farming community, but the priory still needed to eat — and its charter required that any traveler who reached the gate be offered a meal and a night's rest. So the builders cut this room deep into the southwest corner of the building, against the outer wall where the stone stays cool year-round, and lined it with broad oak shelves from floor to ceiling. Grain bins built into the floor. Iron hooks along the ceiling beam for hanging cured meats. A bricked alcove in the far corner where barrels of small beer and hill-country mead were kept on their sides, stoppered with waxed linen. A narrow door opened onto **AO-06. The Outer Courtyard**, where supply carts could unload directly.

**Halven Sere** maintained this room alone for fourteen years after the order withdrew. He kept the grain bins filled from forage and barter with Kron Hills hill-folk. He brewed a thin, bitter beer that nobody but him would ever drink. He put up jars of preserved herbs and root vegetables from his garden at AO-04. The larder was never full — one man cannot stock a monastery — but it was never empty, either. He left traces here, as he left traces everywhere: a shelf of neatly labeled jars in a handwriting no one in the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) can read, because the labels are in the liturgical shorthand of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md).

Now the larder holds what the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) takes.

This is the intake point of the priory's theft pipeline. Stolen goods arrive here from Long Road raids — carried up the ridge by [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s reavers, dumped in rough crates and canvas sacks, and left for the quartermasters in **AO-19. The Oath-Hall** to sort and catalogue. The room is a staging area, not a workspace. Goods accumulate between raids and are moved to the counting room in batches. Some items never leave — the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) keeps its own operational supplies here too: ropes, chains, manacles, iron spikes, camping gear, replacement weapons, and the heavy canvas hoods used to transport prisoners along the Long Road at night.

The room is usually unoccupied. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison treats it as a storeroom, not a post — no guard is assigned, no sentry watches the door. Raiders pass through when fetching supplies or dumping cargo after a raid, but between those moments the supply store sits in silence, crowded with stolen goods and the faint smell of old grain.

##### Entering the Supply Store

The door opens onto a cool, dim room. The air smells of dust, grain, oiled leather, and something underneath — old herbs, faintly sweet, like a memory of a kitchen that hasn't cooked in years.  
  
Oak shelves line every wall from floor to ceiling, packed tight. Crates are stacked on the lower shelves and across the stone floor — some nailed shut, some open, their contents visible: bolts of dyed wool, bundled tools, leather satchels, a tangle of iron chain. Sacks of grain lean against the far wall beside a row of barrels. Iron hooks hang from the ceiling beam, empty now except for a coil of rope and a canvas bag.  
  
One shelf near the door still holds a row of small clay jars, each with a paper label written in a careful, cramped hand. The labels are faded but legible — though the words aren't Common. Beside them, a wooden crate marked with a wax seal sits apart from the others, as if someone placed it there deliberately.  
  
The room is quiet. No one is here. The only sound is the faint creak of the building settling overhead.

### Features of the Supply Store

#### The Stolen Cargo

The supply store contains the raw intake of four months of Long Road raids. Unlike the sorted and catalogued goods in **AO-19**, the cargo here is unsorted — dumped by raiders and left for the quartermasters to process. This makes it messier but also more revealing, because the goods still carry their original markings, packaging, and identifying features that the counting room strips away before shipment.

**DC 10 Investigation**  
The cargo is clearly stolen. Multiple merchant marks, guild stamps, and caravan seals are visible on crates and packaging — no single source, no consistent origin. This is loot from dozens of separate raids.

**DC 13 Investigation**  
The stolen goods sort into recognizable categories:

*   **Trade goods:** Bolts of dyed Kron Hills wool, barrels of hill-country mead, bundles of tanned leather, ingots of copper and tin. Common caravan cargo worth 40–60 gp total
*   **Weapons and armor:** Short swords, crossbows, leather jerkins, a chain shirt with a [Mounted Borderer](mounted-borderers-organization.md) patrol stamp on the collar — stripped from caravan guards and road patrols. The weapons are functional but scarred
*   **Personal effects:** A wooden bin near the door holds items taken from prisoners — belts, boots, traveling cloaks, coin purses (emptied), journal books, a child's carved horse, a woman's silver hairpin. The bin is labeled in chalk: _"PERS — hold for sort"_
*   **Operational supplies:** The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s own logistics gear. Coils of hemp rope, iron manacles (three sets), replacement crossbow bolts, camp bedrolls, and six heavy canvas hoods — the kind used to hood prisoners for transport

**DC 16 Investigation**  
One of the canvas hoods still has dried blood on the inside rim and strands of blond hair caught in the drawstring. Cross-referencing the prisoner manifests in **AO-19** identifies this as likely belonging to **Tannor Greaves** — the caravan guard shipped to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) on the 11th of Readying. His partner **Berrik Holst**, currently held in **AO-20**, will recognize the hood and react with cold fury if shown it.

* * *

#### The Gnome-Crafted Goods

A separate shelf — higher up, handled with more care than the bulk cargo — holds goods taken from gnomish traders traveling the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) corridor between [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) and [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md).

**DC 12 Investigation**  
The items include:

*   A set of fine clockwork tools in a fitted leather case, stamped with the Sheernobb Tinkers' Guild mark
*   Three small gem pouches — two empty, one containing four uncut garnets (worth 25 gp each) — each pouch embroidered with a gnomish clan sigil
*   A brass astrolabe of gnomish design, engraved with Kron Hills star charts. Exquisite craftsmanship — worth 75 gp to a collector, priceless to the gnomish community as proof of caravan raids
*   A packet of correspondence in Gnomish, sealed with the stamp of [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md)'s gemcutters' association — intercepted trade letters that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has not bothered to translate

**DC 15 Investigation (or any character who reads Gnomish)**  
The intercepted correspondence includes a letter from the gemcutters' association to the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) in [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md), requesting an armed escort for high-value shipments after three consecutive losses on the Long Road. The letter names the missing traders — including **Pilnik Brassbottom**, currently held in **AO-20**. Returning these goods and this letter to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) or the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) is hard evidence that the caravan raids originate from this priory — not from gnomish clan feuds or bandit opportunism, as [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s false-flag operations are designed to suggest.

* * *

#### The Sealed Crate

Set apart from the bulk cargo — placed deliberately on its own shelf near the courtyard door — sits a reinforced wooden crate sealed with a wax stamp. The crate has not been opened by the quartermasters. It was placed here with instructions not to touch it.

**DC 10 Perception**  
The wax seal is not an [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) mark. It bears the imprint of a merchant house — a stylized balance scale wreathed in grain sheaves.

**DC 13 Investigation (or any character with merchant background)**  
The seal belongs to **House Langmuir** — a mid-tier [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) trading house with Long Road interests. **Godeleva Langmuir**, the house matriarch, sits on the Verbobonc Merchants' Council. This crate was taken from a Langmuir caravan.

**Opening the crate (no lock — pry bar or STR DC 12):**  
Inside: Six bottles of Velunese altar wine (15 gp each, 90 gp total), packed in straw. A shipping invoice lists the consignment as bound for the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md) at [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) — wine intended for a holy house, stolen in transit. The invoice bears **Godeleva Langmuir**'s personal signature.

This crate is political dynamite. The wine is minor loot, but the invoice with Langmuir's signature, combined with the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) location, proves that a Verbobonc merchant house was robbed by a cult operating from a desecrated [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) priory. Presenting this to the Viscounty, the Merchants' Council, or the Church of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) would be devastating evidence.

* * *

#### Halven's Jars

On a shelf near the inner door, pushed to the back and half-hidden behind crates of stolen goods, a row of small clay jars still sits where they were placed years ago. Each jar has a paper label written in a careful, cramped hand. The labels are in the liturgical shorthand of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) — not a secret language, but an abbreviated notation used by ordained brothers for cataloguing sacred supplies.

**DC 12 Religion (or any character who reads celestial script)**  
The labels read: _Marsh-mint, dried. Comfrey root, powdered. Feverfew, whole. Yarrow poultice, sealed._ Medicinal herbs — the last of **Halven Sere**'s stores from his years tending AO-04, the Custodian's Herb Patch. The jars are old but properly sealed. The contents are still viable.

Any character proficient in Medicine or Herbalism recognizes these as a functional healing kit. The yarrow poultice in particular functions as a _Healer's Kit_ charge (one use). The feverfew can reduce fever and ease pain. The comfrey root, applied as a compress, grants advantage on one Medicine check to stabilize a creature.

These jars are not loot. They are a trace — the same quiet presence that left prayer-bead caches in the service hall and a carved blessing in the stables. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) never found them because no one in the garrison can read the labels, and no one thought old jars of dried plants were worth investigating. The monks are gone, but the larder they kept still holds medicine for anyone who knows how to read the names.

* * *

#### The Root Cellar

In the far corner, a heavy iron ring is set into a stone trapdoor — the original root cellar access. The trapdoor is closed and has not been opened in months. Dust and grime seal the edges.

**DC 10 Perception**  
The trapdoor exists. The iron ring is cold to the touch.

**DC 14 Investigation**  
The trapdoor has not been opened since the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) took the priory. Dust patterns confirm months of neglect. Whatever is down there, the garrison ignores it.

**Opening the trapdoor (STR DC 10 — sealed by grime, not locked):**  
A narrow stone staircase descends six feet into a small, low-ceilinged root cellar — perhaps eight feet square, cold and dry. The original monastery stores are still here: three empty grain bins, a rack of oak barrels (two still sealed — the thin, bitter beer **Halven Sere** brewed years ago, now vinegar), and a shelf of clay pots containing hard-dried root vegetables that are no longer edible.

The root cellar does not connect to the crypt level. It is a dead end — a cold hole in the ground that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) found useless and forgot. But it is also the one space in the entire supply store that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has never touched. The root cellar is exactly as **Halven Sere** left it — the barrels he sealed, the bins he emptied and swept clean, the pots he stored with the care of a man who expected to come back for them. A character who has already found his traces in the herb patch, the stables, or the service hall recognizes the same hand at work.

**Tactical note:** The root cellar can hide two Medium creatures (three if they squeeze). The trapdoor can be closed from inside. A party that needs a place to rest, regroup, or hide an injured NPC during a dungeon escape could use the root cellar as a short-rest shelter — the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has no reason to check it.

* * *

#### The Courtyard Door

The narrow door on the outer wall opens directly onto **AO-06. The Outer Courtyard**, near the southwest corner. This was the original supply entrance — where carts unloaded provisions for the monastery larder. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) uses it for the same purpose in reverse: stolen goods are carried through this door from the courtyard after raids.

The door is not locked. It is not guarded. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) does not consider the supply store a security-sensitive room — it holds goods, not people or secrets.

**Stealth entry:** A party approaching from the courtyard can enter the supply store through this door without passing any guarded interior checkpoints. The door is visible from the courtyard, so a Stealth check (DC 13) is required to reach it unseen during normal garrison activity — but during a distraction, shift change, or at night, the DC drops to 10 or the check may be unnecessary entirely. This makes AO-21 one of the strongest infiltration points for parties who have already scouted the courtyard layout.

### Encounter

**The room is usually empty.** This is the quietest space in the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). No guard is posted. No worker is permanently assigned. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison treats the supply store as a storeroom — a place you visit when you need something, not a place you stay.

**25% chance:** One Earth Raider is present, fetching supplies for the [AO-10 barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md). He is alone, unarmed except for a belt knife, and not expecting trouble. He has his back to whichever door the party enters from. If surprised, he drops what he is carrying and reaches for the knife — but he would rather shout for help than fight alone.

**If the raider shouts:** Guards from [AO-10. The Barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) respond in 2d4 rounds (the barracks are adjacent). Off-duty raiders arrive drowsy and poorly equipped — leather armor, short swords, no shields.

**If the raider is silenced quickly:** No alarm. The party has time to search the room thoroughly. No one will check the supply store unless a specific errand sends someone here.

**After a raid returns (DM timing):** If the party is present when a raiding party returns to the priory, 3–4 Earth Reavers will enter through the courtyard door carrying fresh cargo. This is a much harder encounter — the reavers are armed, alert, and will fight to hold the room while one runs for reinforcements.

### DM Notes

#### The Supply Chain

AO-21 is the first link in the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s logistics chain at the priory. The full pipeline runs:

**Long Road raids → AO-21 (intake/storage) → **AO-19** (sorting/cataloguing) → waystation shipments → [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) barges**

The goods here are unsorted and still carry their original merchant marks. This makes AO-21 more useful as an evidence room than **AO-19** — the counting room strips identifying marks before shipment, but the supply store holds the raw material. A thorough party that searches both rooms can reconstruct the entire operation: who was robbed (AO-21), how it was catalogued and shipped (**AO-19**), and where it went (the manifests).

#### Connections to Other Rooms

*   **AO-19 (Counting Room):** Goods flow from here to AO-19 for processing. The manifest entries in AO-19 correspond to cargo batches that passed through this room
*   **AO-20 (Guest Cells):** **Berrik Holst**, the caravan guard held in Cell 2, will ask the party to check AO-21 for his partner **Tannor Greaves**' belongings. Finding the bloodied hood (DC 16) confirms Tannor was transported to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) and fuels Berrik's determination to pursue rescue
*   **AO-10 (Barracks):** Adjacent. Off-duty raiders are the only potential responders if an alarm is raised here. Response time: 2d4 rounds
*   **AO-25 (Knight's Armory):** The weapons stripped from caravan guards here are the mundane counterpart to the false-flag disguise gear in **AO-25**. Together, the two rooms reveal the full scope of [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s operations — real stolen weapons here, manufactured evidence there
*   **AO-06 (Courtyard):** Direct access via the courtyard door. Primary infiltration route

#### Sacred Layer

The Supply Store's desecration is entirely functional. No one defaced an altar here, no one smashed a relic. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) simply filled the shelves that once held provisions for pilgrims with goods stolen from the travelers those pilgrims would have been. The room that existed to feed anyone who came to the gate now stores the evidence of everyone who was robbed on the road outside it. The desecration is not doctrinal — it is logistical. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) does not even know this was once a place of hospitality. They just needed somewhere to put the crates.

But **Halven Sere**'s jars are still on the shelf. The root cellar is still swept clean. The larder remembers what it was, even if no one asks.

##### The Larder's Two Lives

_The Supply Store tells the priory's story in miniature — what it was and what it became._

The [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) charter required that any traveler who reached the gate be offered a meal and a night's rest. This room made that possible. The grain bins, the curing hooks, the root cellar, the brewing barrels — all of it existed to feed strangers. The [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) understood that hospitality was not charity. It was duty. A knight who would die for a stranger on the road could also cook for one.

Now the same shelves hold stolen goods taken from the travelers the priory once sheltered. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) did not desecrate this room. They simply replaced its contents. The larder that fed travelers now stores evidence of their robbery.

And somewhere on a back shelf, **Halven Sere**'s herb jars still sit with their careful labels. The larder has not entirely forgotten what it was for.

##### Infiltration Route

_For parties approaching the priory through stealth rather than force:_

*   **Entry:** Courtyard door (southwest wall of **AO-06**)
*   **Stealth DC:** 13 (normal activity), 10 (distraction/night), or automatic (during a major event)
*   **Advantage:** No guard, no sentry, unlocked door
*   **Risk:** 25% chance a raider is inside fetching supplies
*   **From here:** Interior door leads toward [AO-10 Barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) corridor — stealth becomes harder once inside the building
*   **Fallback:** Root cellar can hide 2–3 creatures if the party needs to abort

##### Common Player Actions

*   **Search the cargo:** Investigation DC 10/13/16 — escalating detail on stolen goods, merchant marks, prisoner belongings
*   **Examine gnome goods:** Investigation DC 12/15 — Sheernobb origin, intercepted correspondence naming Pilnik Brassbottom
*   **Open the sealed crate:** STR DC 12 or pry bar — Langmuir wine + signed invoice
*   **Read Halven's jars:** Religion DC 12 — medicinal herbs, Healer's Kit charge, Medicine advantage
*   **Check the root cellar:** Perception DC 10 (trapdoor), STR DC 10 (open) — hiding spot, Halven's old stores
*   **Enter via courtyard:** Stealth DC 13/10 — strongest infiltration point in the priory
*   **Silence the raider (if present):** Stealth vs. raider's passive Perception 10 — surprise round likely
*   **Gather evidence:** Merchant marks, Langmuir invoice, gnomish correspondence, Borderer chain shirt, prisoner hood — all portable
*   **Use as safe room:** Root cellar hides 2–3 creatures. Supply store itself is unmonitored between raids
