House Haxx
==========

##### A Pillar of Power and Prosperity in Verbobonc

Order and prosperity do not come cheap, nor do they come without power. House Haxx learned this lesson in the trading halls of the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md), built an empire from shipping lanes and merchant contracts, and cemented its position as the economic heart of the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md) through iron will and selective patronage. For forty years, House Haxx has controlled the flow of goods, coin, and—more quietly—information through Verbobonc City. The house commands armies dressed as tax collectors, runs intelligence networks concealed behind silk and scandal, and maintains allegiances with both merchant princes and the devout faithful. But in 576 CY, the disruption spreading from the **Temple of Elemental Evil** threatens the very trade routes that built Haxx power. Worse, the family itself fractures under the weight of corruption, ambition, and the price of legacy. This is the story of a house in motion—one that can shape the fate of Verbobonc, or crash spectacularly if the pieces fall wrong.  

### EXCERPT

 

House Haxx was founded in the latter half of the 5th century CY by merchant princes who recognized that control of the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md) meant control of commerce across the Sheldomar Valley. The house rose to prominence through [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md), a shipping enterprise that evolved into something far more: a merchant marine, an intelligence network, and a quasi-military organization. Today, under the leadership of [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md), the house commands not merely merchants but warriors, not merely traders but politicians. The house controls the collection of taxes across the entire Viscounty, operates [Haxx's Hardheads](haxx-s-hardheads-organization.md)—a force of approximately five hundred veteran soldiers—and maintains a vice-like grip on the flow of goods into and out of Verbobonc City. In 576 CY, however, House Haxx faces cascading crises: the disruption of trade by enemies of the state, internal family fractures driven by ideology and ambition, and the revelation that its wealth and power rest upon foundations far more fragile than they appear.

> _"Loyalty to our allies and the Viscount ensures our legacy endures through the ages."_

### CONTENT

 

#### History: From River Trade to Political Dominance

 

The Haxx family did not emerge from noble blood or ancient lineage. They were traders—sharp, ruthless, and visionary. In the early 540s CY, when Marakios's grandfather first established [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md), the shipping lanes of the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md) were contested ground. The house recognized that whoever controlled transportation controlled prices, politics, and power. Through a combination of competitive pricing, selective violence against rivals, and strategic marriages into established merchant families, the Haxx consolidated control of northbound trade by 560 CY.

 

The turning point came in 564 CY when the Viscount, recognizing the house's practical monopoly on trade revenue, granted Haxx the role of official tax collector for the entire Viscounty. This was not a ceremonial appointment. The Viscount needed someone who could extract taxes from far-flung settlements, enforce collection, and move large sums of coin back to the city. Haxx needed legitimacy and legal authority. [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md), already the effective leader of the house by this time, stepped into the role with the ruthlessness it demanded. The next year, he reformed and professionalized [Haxx's Hardheads](haxx-s-hardheads-organization.md), transforming what had been an irregular force of merchant guards into a standing military organization answerable directly to him, not the Viscount. By 570 CY, Haxx's Hardheads numbered close to five hundred, making them the largest organized military force in the Viscounty outside of the Viscount's own personal garrison.

 

Marakios married [Larethene Vonnia](larethene-haxx-person.md) in 570 CY, bringing into the family a woman whose skill in intrigue and intelligence work would prove as valuable as any shipment of goods. Larethene transformed the household's informal gossip networks into something far more systematic—a true spy apparatus known informally as the Velvet Net, capable of monitoring everything from dockside negotiations to private conversations in noble homes. By 574 CY, House Haxx was not merely wealthy; it was the institutional center of economic power on the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md), the Viscount's chief instrument of control, and arguably the most dangerous organization in Verbobonc City.

 

#### The Haxx Family: Assets, Heirs, and Fractures

 

**[Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md)** is a man of forty-six years whose authority is grounded in practical competence rather than personal charisma. He is first and always a merchant—calculating, patient, willing to wait for profit rather than seize it rashly. He maintains a public persona of piety and propriety, attending services at the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md) regularly and supporting the church's charitable works. This genuine faith coexists with absolute pragmatism in matters of business and politics. Marakios funds mercenary patrols to protect trade routes, not out of idealism but because disrupted commerce is unprofitable. He crushes rivals not from spite but from efficiency. He maintains alliances with both the Viscount and powerful merchant families because that is where power concentrates. In 576 CY, Marakios faces his greatest challenge: the disruption caused by the **Temple of Elemental Evil** is not merely economic but existential. If the trade routes collapse, his mercenary forces cannot be maintained. If his mercenary forces dissolve, his political power evaporates.

 

**[Larethene Haxx](larethene-haxx-person.md) (née Vonnia)**, known whisperingly throughout the city as "the Spider of Silk and Secrets," is the house's hidden power structure. Born in Veluna, she brought with her not merely aristocratic connections but a sophisticated understanding of court intrigue that she has applied ruthlessly in Verbobonc. She runs the Velvet Net, a network of informants, courtiers, and servants that reports everything from trade negotiations to the private vices of nobles. She is a patron of both the shrine of Wee Jas and the hidden shrine of Syrul within the city, which gives her access to information from both legitimate and underworld sources. In 574 CY, Larethene conducted an affair with **Rhennee Resputa**, a decision that was not passion but politics—an attempt to solidify alliances with certain merchant families. When the affair was discovered, it became public knowledge that damaged her reputation but not her power. More concerning to Marakios is her growing feud with [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md), whose family currently negotiates an arranged marriage between their son and Larethene's daughter. The two women are remarkably well-matched in intelligence and ruthlessness, and their conflict threatens to destabilize the political balance.

 

**[Savra Haxx](savra-haxx-person.md)**, the house's eldest and only child, represents the ideological break in the family's next generation. She was raised in wealth and privilege, groomed to take her place in Verbobonc society through the arranged marriage to **Ludovic Langmuir**—a union designed to cement alliance between Haxx and Langmuir economic interests. Instead, in 575 CY, she fled the family compound. Her disappearance sent shockwaves through the house, and her reappearance in the service of the Feathergale Society proved far worse. Savra has renounced her family's corruption, embraced the elemental ideology of the air cult, and positioned herself in active opposition to everything House Haxx represents. She serves under **Thurl Merosska** at the Feathergale Society, carrying weapons and faith instead of coin and secrets. For Marakios, Savra's defection is not merely a family embarrassment; it is a strategic liability. She knows the house's operations, its networks, its secrets. Worse, she appears willing to use that knowledge against her parents. The arranged marriage to Ludovic Langmuir remains nominally in force, creating a complicated diplomatic situation where the house is contractually bound to a union with a woman now actively hostile to family interests.

 

#### Structure and Assets: The Machinery of Economic Control

 

House Haxx exercises power through five primary mechanisms, each of which generates revenue, intelligence, or military force:

 

*   **The Gentle Tradewinds (Shipping Company):** The legitimate foundation of Haxx wealth. [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md) operates merchant vessels on the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md) and maintains trading posts throughout the Sheldomar Valley, including operations in [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and even far-flung connections to the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md). The company maintains a quasi-military force of ship captains, dockworkers, and sailors who serve as both merchants and soldiers. In times of peace, they transport goods. In times of conflict, they protect trade routes and enforce Haxx interests. The company is also, inevitably, a vector for smuggling, black market goods, and information transfer.
 *   **Haxx's Hardheads (Military Force):** Approximately five hundred trained soldiers, dressed officially as tax collectors and enforcement personnel, who answer directly to Marakios. They are stationed at key locations: the [Waterfront Warehouses](w6-waterfront-warehouses-landmark.md) in the Commercial Quarter, throughout the city for administrative functions, and at [City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md) where the tax collection office operates. The Hardheads are better equipped, better trained, and better paid than most militia forces, which creates both loyalty to Marakios and resentment from the Viscount, who recognizes that the Hardheads answer to Haxx before they answer to the crown. In 576 CY, the Hardheads are critical to maintaining order if Temple-related disruptions threaten the city, but their loyalty to Marakios rather than the Viscount creates a dangerous dependency.
 *   **The Tax Collection Authority:** This is the legal mechanism by which Haxx extracts value from the entire Viscounty. Every settlement from the river valley to the Kron Hills pays taxes that flow through Haxx agents before reaching the Viscount's treasury. This creates multiple opportunities for wealth extraction: official take, delayed payments that allow Haxx to profit from short-term investment, fees charged for collection services, and the simple reality that Haxx knows which settlements have surplus coin and which are struggling. The house uses this information for both political leverage and strategic trade routing.
 *   **Haxx Manor and High Quarter Properties:** The house maintains [Haxx Manor](a5-haxx-manor-landmark.md), its primary residence in the High Quarter (A5), as both home and command center. The manor is an imposing structure with extensive administrative facilities, servant quarters, and hidden passages built for security. The family also controls numerous other properties throughout the city, including [Elk Ridge Harness Shop](p11-elk-ridge-harness-shop-landmark.md) (P11), which serves as a front for lower-level operations and intelligence gathering.
 *   **The Velvet Net (Intelligence Apparatus):** Larethene's creation and pride, the Velvet Net is less a formal organization than a systematic use of servants, courtiers, merchants, and underworld contacts to gather information. The network operates through gossip, observation, blackmail, and bought loyalty. Key operatives include shrine attendants at both the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md) and hidden shrines of Wee Jas and Syrul, servants in noble households, merchants' wives, tavern keepers, and more. The network is particularly strong in the Commercial Quarter and Waterfront, but has tendrils reaching into every neighborhood. Information gathered by the Velvet Net is weaponized through careful rumor spreading, strategic revelation of secrets, and the implicit threat of further disclosure.

 

#### Political Power and the Council of Lords

 

House Haxx occupies a unique position on the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) as the institutional center of the _Economic Powers_ faction. This faction's interests align broadly with the god **Zilchus**—commerce, profit, trade, and growth—though Marakios personally worships [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md). The Economic Powers faction includes **Roland Galans** and **Drenton Vaswell** as major allies, both merchants whose interests depend on stable trade and favorable tax policy.

 

The Economic Powers faction is fundamentally opposed to any policy that disrupts commerce, which makes the **Temple of Elemental Evil** an existential threat to their interests. The Temple's agents have disrupted trade routes, created banditry, and made the Velverdyva region dangerous. More importantly, if trade redirects away from Verbobonc—for example, if gnome merchants shift their business to Dyvers—the revenue that sustains Haxx power dries up completely.

 

The Viscount uses House Haxx as a counterweight to the military ambitions of **Claviger Velysin**, who commands the city's military guard and faction oriented toward strength and martial power. By empowering Haxx and allowing the house to maintain its private military force, the Viscount ensures that no single faction can dominate the city. This is strategic genius on the Viscount's part, but it creates an inherent instability: Haxx's power depends on the Viscount's continued favor, while Haxx's military strength makes the Haxx a potential threat if favor turns to disfavor.

 

#### 576 CY: The Year of Fractures

 

In 576 CY, the confluence of external threats and internal fractures creates cascading crises for House Haxx:

 

*   **Trade Disruption and Revenue Collapse:** The Temple of Elemental Evil and its agents have made the roads north of Verbobonc increasingly dangerous. The gnomes of the Kron Hills, major trading partners, are reducing their transactions through Verbobonc City out of simple safety concerns. If this trend continues, the revenues that sustain the Hardheads will evaporate. Marakios has already begun to panic about maintaining payroll, which creates vulnerability. A house whose soldiers cannot be paid is a house in collapse.
 *   **The Savra Problem:** The arranged marriage to Ludovic Langmuir, intended to cement alliance with House Langmuir, now exists in a state of strategic ambiguity because Savra has renounced the family and joined the air cult. The marriage remains nominally valid, but the bride is actively hostile. [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md) has begun to use this situation as a weapon in her ongoing feud with Larethene. If the marriage is dissolved, it is a public humiliation and the loss of a valuable alliance. If it proceeds, Ludovic is marrying a woman of uncertain loyalty. The situation has created a permanent fracture between Haxx and Langmuir interests.
 *   **The Larethene-Godeleva Rivalry:** What began as political maneuvering has evolved into a genuine feud between two of the most intelligent and ruthless women in Verbobonc politics. Larethene's affair with Rhennee Resputa and Godeleva's exploitation of the Savra situation have escalated to open conflict. This feud destabilizes the Council of Lords, makes alliance-building difficult for the Economic Powers faction, and creates opportunities for opponents to exploit Haxx internal divisions.
 *   **The Battirovka Problem:** House Haxx's monopolistic control of trade has created exactly the economic conditions that allow [The Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) and other black market organizations to thrive. Whenever Haxx restricts the supply of legitimate goods or charges monopoly prices, they create profit opportunities for smugglers and black marketers. The Battirovka have been systematically using this dynamic to build wealth and power. Now, with trade already disrupted and merchants desperate for alternative sources, the Battirovka are positioned to expand significantly. This threatens the very economic foundations that create Haxx wealth.
 *   **Ideological Pressure:** Savra's defection and embrace of elemental ideology represents something new for Verbobonc politics: a direct challenge to the material basis of house power. If other younger nobles begin to question whether wealth and commerce should be pursued at such ruthless cost, the legitimacy of the Economic Powers faction could erode. Moreover, the Temple of Elemental Evil represents a direct and growing threat to the city itself, and the people are beginning to understand that trade stability cannot be achieved without addressing the Temple problem directly.

 

#### Temple Campaign Implications: Why House Haxx Matters to Adventurers

 

For parties engaging with the **Temple of Elemental Evil** campaign, House Haxx is a critical variable in the political and economic stability of Verbobonc City. The house's fate will determine the city's ability to support adventuring parties, maintain mercenary forces, and resist the Temple's expansion.

 

There are multiple vectors by which parties can interact with Haxx interests:

 

*   **Trade Protection Contracts:** Parties desperate for coin can contract with the Haxx to protect merchant convoys, re-establish trade routes, and hunt bandits and cultists disrupting commerce. This brings parties into direct conflict with Temple agents and creates a natural alliance between economic interests and adventuring ambitions. A party that successfully protects trade routes generates goodwill with Marakios, access to resources, and payment in coin.
 *   **The Savra Angle:** If parties encounter Savra at the Feathergale Society or elsewhere in the air cult, they face a choice: capture her for ransom/return to the family (creating debt with Marakios but alliance with Godeleva), or work with her against family interests (creating conflict with Haxx but potential advantage against the air cult). A smart party might negotiate for Savra's safe passage in exchange for intelligence on the Feathergale Society's operations.
 *   **Intelligence Markets:** The Velvet Net can be engaged as an information source. Parties seeking intelligence about city politics, criminal organizations, or Temple operations can pay the network for information—or alternatively, they can sell information to the network. This creates a dynamic where parties become participants in Verbobonc's intelligence economy.
 *   **Council of Lords Conflicts:** If parties become involved in city politics, they inevitably encounter conflicts between the Economic Powers faction (Haxx, Galans, Vaswell) and other factions. Parties might be hired by Haxx to gather compromising information on rivals, or they might be hired by rivals to undermine Haxx operations. This extends the campaign into urban intrigue.
 *   **The Hardheads as Obstacle or Ally:** The Hardheads are powerful enough to be a significant obstacle if they become hostile (450+ trained soldiers with decent equipment), but they are also potential allies if the party demonstrates that supporting Haxx interests serves their own goals. A party that repeatedly defends Haxx economic interests may find themselves with free access to the Hardheads' resources, safe houses, and information networks.
 *   **The Temple as Existential Threat:** The greatest campaign hook is this: if parties fail to address the Temple threat adequately, the continued disruption will eventually force Marakios to abandon the city (moving his operations elsewhere) or attempt a desperate military solution. Either outcome changes the political landscape significantly and creates space for other forces to move in. Conversely, if parties successfully disrupt the Temple, they become the beneficiaries of Haxx gratitude and become entangled in Marakios's political power structure.

 

#### DM Notes: Running House Haxx Intrigue

 

**Marakios as a Character:** Marakios is best portrayed as a pragmatist, not a villain. He genuinely believes that order and prosperity require strong merchants, efficient administration, and the willingness to use force when necessary. His corruption is not theatrical or sadistic; it is systematic and businesslike. He will negotiate with parties if negotiation serves his interests, will destroy parties if they become obstacles, and will reward loyalty generously because loyalty is profitable. He is not personally ambitious for power beyond economic control—he has no interest in becoming Viscount, for example. He is happy as the behind-the-scenes power because the behind-the-scenes position is more profitable and less visible. Never play Marakios as a caricature of greed; play him as a merchant who has simply extended his commercial philosophy into every aspect of life.

 

**Larethene as a Political Engine:** Larethene is the house's most dangerous asset precisely because she operates through subtlety rather than force. She will never directly order someone killed; instead, she will spread rumors that make that person a target for others. She will never directly seize property; instead, she will arrange scandals that force the owner to sell. She is an excellent character to control rival nobles, force political crises, and create intrigue without obvious causation. Players should find evidence of her manipulation only after the fact, creating the sense that everything that happens in Verbobonc politics is somehow her doing.

 

**Savra as a Symbol:** Savra represents the ideological failure of House Haxx's value system. She is proof that pure mercantilism and moral compromise create enemies within one's own family. Use her as a mirror to reflect back to parties why the house is ultimately fragile—not because of its enemies, but because it has lost the internal moral authority that made loyalty unconditional. If parties interact with Savra, treat her as genuinely conflicted: she despises her family's corruption, but she is still their daughter and still loves them. This internal conflict makes her far more interesting than a simple villain or simple victim.

 

**Scaling Haxx Resources:** The Hardheads are described as approximately 500 soldiers, but this number should scale to party strength. For low-level parties, small Hardheads detachments (10-20 soldiers) are appropriate encounters. For mid-level parties, full companies (50-100) are worth taking seriously. For high-level parties, the entire force becomes an obstacle that cannot be overcome by combat alone. This scaling encourages parties to think about negotiation, infiltration, and political leverage rather than assuming military solutions.

 

**Revenue as a Campaign Variable:** Track Haxx revenue as a campaign metric. If parties successfully protect trade routes and expand commerce, Haxx power increases, which strengthens the Viscount's position and creates more resources for city defense against the Temple. If parties fail and trade collapses, Haxx becomes desperate and may make dangerous decisions. This creates mechanical incentive for parties to care about economic systems, not merely monster-slaying.

 

**The Council Dynamic:** Do not play the Council of Lords as a single unified government. Instead, play it as a coalition that can fracture. Haxx's economic power is real, but it is not absolute. Other factions have other sources of power—military, religious, popular support, ancient legitimacy. Let parties understand that they can influence which faction dominates by changing the underlying conditions that determine each faction's power.

##### Why House Haxx Matters to Your Campaign

 

House Haxx is the institutional expression of economic power in Verbobonc. The house determines which merchants thrive, which trade routes operate, which communities receive supplies, and which suffer scarcity. The house's fate determines whether Verbobonc City can sustain the infrastructure necessary to resist the Temple of Elemental Evil.

 

More importantly, House Haxx demonstrates that the greatest threats to a city are not always monsters lurking in dungeons. They are the systems of power that concentrate wealth and authority in too few hands, the compromises with corruption that seem necessary in the moment but erode the legitimacy of authority, and the fractures within institutions that prevent coordinated response to external threats. If Marakios could trust his own daughter, if Marakios could maintain unity among economic powers, if Marakios could keep his own house from tearing itself apart with internal feuds, House Haxx would be powerful enough to solve the Temple problem independently. Instead, the house's strength masks profound fragility.

 

Use House Haxx to teach parties that politics matter, that economics are inseparable from military power, and that the most effective way to defeat an enemy is often not to fight them directly but to strengthen the legitimate authority and institutional integrity of one's allies. Give parties the tools to support House Haxx if they choose, but also let them understand the costs of alliance with a house built on monopoly, corruption, and moral compromise. The greatest lesson of House Haxx is this: order and prosperity purchased through ruthlessness are always fragile, always threatened by idealism from within and challenges from without.
