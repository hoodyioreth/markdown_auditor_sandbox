Romag’s Reavers Occupy the Azure Oath
=====================================

#### Earth Temple Forward Stronghold – Long Road Operations

##### The Seizure of the Azure Oath

When the Lightning Priory fell, it did not fall in open war. It fell in silence.

[Romag](romag-person.md) did not march banners across the ridge. He sent reavers at dusk — men hardened by the Nulb riverfront, Iron Claw bugbears from the southern Gnarley, and zealots eager to profane a shrine of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). The custodian Halven Sere was taken alive. The tomb-vaults were opened not with reverence but with iron wedges and spades.

This site was not seized for glory. It was seized for position.

From the ridge, one sees the Long Road snake through the valley — five miles distant yet perfectly observed. Any caravan between [Nigb's Rest](nigb-s-rest-settlement.md) **(10 miles north)** and [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) **(10 miles south)** must pass within the operational zone of this stronghold.

Romag understands something the other prophets do not: **geography is power.**

##### The Earth Temple Strategy

Romag’s occupation of the priory serves a larger strategic doctrine. The Earth Temple expands not through spectacle but through **slow territorial pressure**. Strong stone positions become staging grounds for raids, recruitment, and intimidation. The Sepulchre-Priory fulfills this purpose perfectly.

From here the Earth Temple can:

*   control traffic along the Long Road corridor
*   raid caravans without exposing the Temple at Nulb
*   recruit bandits and mercenaries from the Kron Hills
*   process prisoners before sending them deeper into Temple logistics networks

The whispered phrase spreading among merchants reflects this new reality:

**“Caravans pay an earth-tithe now.”**

##### Command Structure

*   Earth Temple forward base under Romag’s authority
*   Operational coordination with Commander [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md)
*   Intelligence oversight through [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md)
*   Recruitment doctrine aligned with Earth Temple strategy

A carter out of Sheernobb swore he saw a blue lamp wink thrice at dusk, then go dark. A week later, a merchant guard came limping into Hommlet, face grey as wet ash, whispering: _“They’ve set a toll where no toll should be.”_

And so the saying spread along the Long Road:

_**“Caravans pay an ‘earth-tithe’ now.”**_ _(True—Romag’s men.)_

##### Varnor Earthcaller

**High Priest of the Black Earth  
Master of the Shattered Monastery**

Varnor is a **Flan geomancer-priest**, deeply attuned to the ancient stone of the Kron Hills. Romag placed him here not merely to oversee raids but to pursue a deeper objective hidden beneath the monastery. To the Earth Temple soldiers the priory is a fortress. To Varnor, it is something else entirely.

Beneath the crypts of the fallen knights lies an **ancient Flan geomantic altar dedicated to the Elder Elemental God**. Varnor believes this altar marks a place where the stone of Oerth still carries the voice of the primordial earth. While the reavers raid the roads above, Varnor directs excavations below.

His goal is to uncover:

*   the **Flan altar of the Elder Elemental God**
*   lost geomantic runes carved into the bedrock
*   a deep chasm whispered about in ancient lore as **the Black Mouth**

If the altar can be awakened, the monastery will become far more than a raiding outpost. It will become a **holy site of the Black Earth cult**.

The monastery now serves two overlapping goals.

Romag seeks to expand Earth Temple influence across the Kron Hills and weaken regional trade routes.

The priory functions as:

*   a **raiding command center**
*   a **prisoner processing site**
*   a **recruitment funnel for bandits and mercenaries**
*   a **logistics node feeding Temple operations**

##### Varnor’s Purpose

Varnor seeks something older and more dangerous. He believes the monastery was unknowingly built atop a **geomantic wound in the earth**, a place where ancient Flan rituals once channeled the power of the Elder Elemental God. His excavations beneath the crypts attempt to awaken that power.

If successful, the Sepulchre-Priory will become a **sacred node of the Black Earth cult**, strengthening Romag’s position within the Temple of Elemental Evil and giving the Earth Temple unprecedented influence in the Kron Hills.

#### How the occupation happened (the quiet coup)

Romag did not send an army—only the right kind of men: hard reavers, a priest-lieutenant, and scouts who knew how to watch a road.

**Phase 1: The Watch**

*   Earth scouts took the ridge-lines and old cairn paths, mapping sight-lines to the Long Road and Hommlet approach trails.
*   They learned the priory’s pattern: Halven’s vigil nights, his routes, and where the seals still held.

**Phase 2: The Seizure**

*   [Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md) was **captured alive**—not slain—because the cell needed what he knew: hidden stairs, sealed doors, and the old ward-stones.
*   The reavers did not “storm” the priory. They **walked in** behind him, forced the keys from him, and took the undercroft first.

**Phase 3: The Conversion of Space  
**They did not rebuild. They **defiled and repurposed**:

*   The **Oath-Hall** became a counting-room for loot and schedules.
*   The **scriptorium** became an intelligence den: caravan marks, guild names, patrol rhythms.
*   The **crypt-mouth** became a choke-point: packed clay barricades, stonefall triggers, and sentry nests.
*   The **guest cells** became prisoner cages—hooded, gagged, and marched out at night.

Romag struck first.

The Gift of Beauty — meant to mend strained borders between Veluna, Verbobonc, and Queen Yolande — now lies beneath a profaned shrine of Heironeous. Its capture was not random. Intelligence flowed.

Romag’s seizure of the Gift accomplishes several things:

#### The Tomb Looting (what was taken, what was broken, what was left)

Romag’s cell loots with purpose. They are not grave-robbers for sport; they are logisticians with knives.

**Primary targets**

*   **Blades, helms, and seals** of the Lightning Kneel (prestige relics; morale weapons)
*   **Funerary pay-coffers** (coin, old trade bars, war rings)
*   **Journals and vow ledgers** (names of old knightly lines; leverage and blackmail)

**What they afor now)**

*   Heavy sarcophagi that require time and noise
*   Deep sealed reliquaries that risk “old wards” and collapse
*   Anything that draws attention too quickly from Verbobonc or the Abbey of the Valorous Knight

**DM Note: The “Unopened Tombs” Pressure  
**Make it clear the desecration is **ongoing**. The PCs can arrive while:

*   some tombs are freshly opened (mud, crowbar marks, torn shrouds)
*   some are marked for later (chalk lines, stacked pry-tools)
*   the deepest vaults remain sealed (still savable)

* * *

#### Relics in circulation (how the stolen holy steel spreads)

Once stolen, the relics don’t sit in one chest. Romag’s people move them like contraband.

**Circulation routes**

1.  **Local reaver use**: a few relic blades are issued as “trophies” to the harshest raiders to harden morale and terrorize guards.
2.  **Fence-and-factor trade**: lesser relics (rings, hilts, stamped seal-discs) are traded through roadside contacts and black-market peddlers.
3.  **Temple pipeline**: any “true” relic (named blade, sainted helm, oath-stone fragment) is packed and sent south-east as **high-value tribute** toward Earth Temple channels (ultimately Nulb/TOEE).

**Complications you can weaponize**

*   **Cursed in desecrator hands:** relics injure or betray reavers—blisters, rust, bad dreams, “slipping” at key moments.
*   **Blessed in rightful hands:** relics “wake” for PCs who cleanse and keep oath.
*   **Spirits react:** the Honoured Dead grow restless when their names are scattered and their steel profaned.

#### Evidence to prove it (what PCs can find)

Use these as tangible clues that link the priory → raids → larger Temple logistics:

*   **A tithe ledger** titled _Earth-Due_, listing caravan dates, guild marks, and “payments” (coin, goods, prisoners).
*   **A broken oath-ring** of a Lightning Kneel paladin used as a paperweight in the counting-room.
*   **A caravan seal press** stolen from a merchant house in Verbobonc, used to forge “toll writs.”
*   **Mud-caked crate labels** naming waystations (Laughing Dog, Ostverk road junctions, etc.) that tie into your broader web.
*   **A note about the Rao Delegation**: scouts observed their route south toward Ostverk—proof the Temple is eyeing high-profile targets.

#### Immediate consequences in the region (what changes on the Long Road)

*   Caravans travel in **larger convoys** or not at all.
*   Merchants whisper of paying “tithe” quietly to avoid bloodshed.
*   Hommlet sees more **desperate strangers** and fewer honest wagons.
*   [The Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) risks scandal if word spreads that tombs of heroes were plundered “under their watch.”

##### Power Structure Within the Outpost

The Earth Temple does not breed harmony. It breeds hierarchy.

Tharvok Stonehand commands this outpost by Romag’s decree, yet he does not stand alone. Maris Blackshale commands the raiders who give the outpost its teeth. Caidax Nightshade engineers the deception that makes their violence strategic rather than random.

Three men. One location. Uneasy balance. And above them all — Romag’s will.

* * *

##### Tharvok & Maris Blackshale

Tharvok respects strength, and Maris has proven his worth in blood and discipline. The raider camps exist because of Maris. The valley training ground was his design. The ambushes succeed because his men obey.

But Maris was not placed here by Tharvok. He answers directly to Romag. That independence unsettles the priest.

Tharvok governs doctrine, ritual authority, and artifact control. Maris governs steel, training, and execution. Their cooperation is functional — not friendly.

Tharvok sees Maris as necessary muscle. Maris sees Tharvok as necessary authority. Neither fully trusts the other.

###### Key Points

*   Professional but tense cooperation
*   Maris commands raiders and training operations
*   Tharvok commands ritual authority and final decisions
*   Maris dislikes delays for ceremony
*   Tharvok dislikes improvisation outside doctrine
*   Both remain loyal to Romag first

* * *

##### Tharvok & Caidax Nightshade

Caidax represents everything Tharvok mistrusts — illusion, manipulation, subtle destabilization. The Earth Temple crushes openly. Caidax prefers shadows.

The Scroll of Veil, the impersonation of gnomes, the engineered massacres — these are Caidax’s domain. Tharvok approved the operations because Romag approved them. But approval does not equal trust.

*   Tharvok believes destruction should be visible and final.
*   Caidax believes it should be indirect and destabilizing.

The priest watches the sorcerer carefully. And Caidax knows it.

###### Key Points

*   Low trust relationship
*   Tharvok sees Caidax as unstable and politically fragile
*   Caidax fears Tharvok’s rigid authority
*   Caidax prefers Lucius as intermediary
*   Tharvok would sacrifice Caidax if required by Romag
*   Caidax survives because Romag sees value in him

* * *

#### Maris & Caidax

Of the three, this relationship is the most practical.

*   Maris trains the men.
*   Caidax equips them with illusion and arcane disruption.

They do not share philosophy. They share utility. Maris understands that deception makes raids more effective. Caidax understands that without Maris’ disciplined fighters, his illusions mean nothing. There is no loyalty here. Only cooperation.

If forced into a crisis, Maris would side with the stronger authority. Caidax would side with survival.

###### Key Points

*   Mutually beneficial partnership
*   Maris provides muscle and discipline
*   Caidax provides illusion and destabilization
*   Neither deeply loyal to the other
*   Maris ultimately sides with Tharvok
*   Caidax ultimately sides with survival

The presence of disciplined Black Earth Guards combined with external gnoll mercenaries signals Romag’s long-term intention to hold this corridor — not merely harass it.

##### Campaign Implications of Split Gnoll Loyalty

At first glance, this seems like simple support. It is not.

King Bonegnaw is hedging his alliance. He is strengthening two rival power centers:

*   **Lareth (Greater Temple influence)**
*   **Tharvok (Romag / Earth Temple dominance)**

That creates instability in three directions:

* * *

##### 1️⃣ Earth Temple vs Greater Temple Rivalry

Romag and the Greater Temple already operate in uneasy balance.

If Lareth gains favor using gnoll brutality…

*   Romag may demand more assets.
*   Tharvok may escalate raids to compete.
*   Lucius may begin manipulating both sides.

* * *

##### 2️⃣ Gnolls as Unstable Assets

Gnolls are not Black Earth zealots. They are predators. Bonegnaw benefits if Temple factions weaken each other.

If Lareth’s gnolls and Tharvok’s gnolls encounter each other in the field:

*   They may ignore Temple hierarchy.
*   They may answer only to Bonegnaw.
*   They may demand greater spoils.
*   They may provoke conflict between outposts.

* * *

##### 3️⃣ Potential Future Crisis

There are several powerful campaign directions here:

*   Bonegnaw may demand tribute from both sides.
*   One faction may accuse the other of “poaching” gnolls.
*   Gnolls may abandon discipline and raid independently.
*   Lucius may quietly encourage rivalry to maintain Greater Temple control.
*   The PCs could exploit the divided loyalties.

* * *

##### Hidden Strategic Truth

Bonegnaw now has:

*   Eyes in both Moathouse and Azure Oath
*   Influence over two Temple commanders
*   Plausible deniability in regional raids

He is not just supplying mercenaries. He is inserting leverage.
