G3 Clotho’s Clothes
===================

_“Where Craftsmanship Meets Charisma, You Find Clotho.”_

Clotho’s Clothes is a perfumed, lantern-bright clothier packed with elegant outfits, polished gems, and glass cases that make even a poor adventurer feel underdressed. Most stock leans upper-class, but Clotho keeps a sly “low rack” for common wear—because the best disguises aren’t always rich.

Upstairs, behind a more discreet curtain of lace and velvet, Clotho keeps a small selection of magically enhanced garments—not loud “wizard robes,” but practical, wearable enchantments that let adventurers look respectable while staying alive.

##### Approaching Clotho’s Clothes

A tiny bell above the round door singeth once as thou enterest, and the scent of lavender-water and cedar-oil greets thee like a practiced bow. Racks of fine coats and gowns crowd the room in orderly splendor—silks and wools and brocades, stitched with such care they seem to have never known a sloppy hand.  
  
Beneath the glass counter, gems and jewelry catch the lamplight and fling it back in little starbursts. The gnomish proprietor—bright of eye and dressed as though every day were a festival—barely glanceth up, intent upon the glittering stones as if they were an oracle.  
  
An elderly elf nearby takes thee in with one slow, silent look—hem to collar, boot to belt—and returns to appraising a mannequin whose outfit costs more than most folk’s yearly bread.

### What’s Here

A two-floor shop dense with clothing racks, shelves, mannequins, and display cases.

##### Ground Floor — The Public Showroom

*   **Elegant displays** of high-quality garments: city fashions, travel coats, formalwear, guild-appropriate outfits
*   **Jewelry cases** near the counter: polished stones, small chains, pins, clasps, cuff-links, and gnomish “button-art”
*   **Sizing for everyone:** gnome, human, elf, halfling, dwarf—Clotho prides himself on fitting any customer without making them feel large or small

##### Upper Floor — The “Second Measure”

A quieter level for:

*   **Tailoring & alterations** (fast work if you pay; faster if you flatter)
*   **Special commissions** (custom colors, hidden pockets, reinforced seams)
*   **Magical wardrobe** (select enchanted apparel kept tasteful and discreet)

##### Elegant Displays and Diverse Offerings

*   **Store Layout:** Spanning two floors filled with clothing racks and shelves, the store features everything from high-end garments for the upper class to simpler peasant outfits.
*   **Aesthetic Appeal:** The shop is perfumed and well-lit, with display cases that shine with jewelry and racks bearing elegantly tailored outfits.

### Goods & Services

##### Clothing Inventory

Clotho carries **nearly everything from the standard clothing lists** you’d expect in a major city shop—common wear through noble styles—plus seasonal cloaks, travel gear, and a rotating selection of imported textiles.

**Tailoring Services**

*   Alterations for any folk (gnome through dwarf)
*   Color matching, discreet embroidery, and guild-acceptable “uniform looks”
*   Rush orders available (Clotho loves a deadline; it makes him feel important)

##### Special Stock for Adventurers

Clotho keeps practical pieces that make parties happy:

*   reinforced travel coats
*   boots that don’t squeak on stone
*   cloaks cut to conceal weapons without looking like you’re concealing weapons
*   “respectable” outfits for audiences with temples, nobles, and guilds

##### Upstairs: Enchanted Apparel (DM Toolkit)

Clotho sometimes has 1–3 items at any given time. Keep it rare, tasteful, and story-tied.

Examples:

*   **Cloak of Resistance** (or similar protective cloak)
*   **Enchanted clothing that functions like light armor** while remaining flexible—especially sought by monks, bards, and “I refuse to clank” types
*   **Minor wondrous accessories** related to apparel: charming brooches, warming sashes, gloves that improve grip, etc.

**How Clotho sells magic:  
**He doesn’t say “magic.” He says, “Better stitching.”

#### Why This Place Matters in Gnomesberg

Clotho’s is a **cultural bridge**—a showcase of styles across the city and beyond. Humans come here to dress like they belong among gnomes; gnomes come here to dress like they belong among humans; elves come here to quietly judge everyone.

It’s also an **information crossroads**, because:

*   the wealthy cannot resist being seen
*   the nervous cannot resist buying confidence
*   and the guilty cannot resist buying concealment

* * *

### Temple of Elemental Evil & Iuz Cult Hooks

##### 1) The Wrong Stitch

A customer commissions a travel cloak with a specific hidden seam pattern—one Clotho recognizes as a coded mark used by smugglers and “quiet couriers.” Clotho asks the party to follow the buyer… politely.

**Non-combat outcomes:** tailing, social pressure, contract leverage, turning the courier, exposing a route.

##### 2) The Coin That Stinks

Someone pays with coin that’s newly minted—or minted where it shouldn’t be. Clotho doesn’t accuse; he simply refuses service unless the party investigates who is flooding the district with suspect money.

**Temple angle:** dirty funds for logistics.  
**Iuz angle:** coercion money for threats, bribes, and “protection.”

##### 3) The Disguise Order

A frightened apprentice orders peasant clothing in five sizes, all identical, rushed by tomorrow, with hoods and gloves. Clotho suspects a snatch-and-switch—someone planning to move a captive through Gnomesberg as “just another servant.”

##### 4) The Holy Symbol Turned Inward

Clotho notices a “pious” customer wearing their symbol hidden under the collar… then buying a scarf designed to conceal the neck and jaw. Clotho doesn’t care what god they serve—he cares what they’re hiding. The party can decide whether it’s a Temple agent, an Iuzite agitator, or a frightened witness.

* * *

#### Non-Combat Leverage & Play Value

Clotho’s Clothes is perfect for parties who want options:

*   **Social upgrades:** get outfits that grant advantage in noble/guild/temple audiences (purely narrative, but impactful)
*   **Disguise play:** build identities with believable wardrobes
*   **Information gathering:** gossip is constant; judgment is casual; rumors drip off customers like rain off cloaks
*   **Soft access:** a well-dressed party gets treated differently in Verbobonc—doors open faster, guards ask fewer questions

* * *

### Scenes You Can Run Immediately

##### Scene 1: The Silent Appraisal

The elderly elf customer sizes up the party and offers a single cutting remark—true, fair, and impossible to ignore.  
Clotho reacts with delighted horror, then offers to “save” them with fashion.

##### Scene 2: The Bell and the Snub

Clotho ignores the party for an uncomfortably long moment—watching reflections in gems instead of faces.  
It’s a test: do they get angry, impatient, rude? Or do they play along?

##### Scene 3: Upstairs, Behind the Velvet

If trust is earned (or coin is convincing), Clotho leads them up with a wink and shows one enchanted garment—then asks one question in exchange:  
“Tell me, darling… what trouble followeth thee into Gnomesberg?”

* * *

#### Rewards

Not treasure. **Reputation and access.**

*   A **tailor’s chit** that gets the party treated as “respectable clients” in several gnome-run businesses
*   A **coded receipt** that serves as an introduction to a gnome merchant or courier network
*   A **single-use favor**: “I will ruin one rumor for you… or start one.”

**True Function:** A social sieve—where status, secrets, and suspicious coin all brush shoulders

* * *

##### Clotho, the Flamboyant Proprietor

Clotho is a little storm of charm and needlecraft—dramatic gestures, extravagant dress, and an infectious delight that makes people talk. He is a beloved fixture in the business life of Verbobonc and is known to own or supply multiple ventures across the city.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Calls customers “darling” in three languages, whether he knows them or not.
*   Speaks broken Common like a musician playing by ear—wrong words, perfect meaning.
*   Offers compliments like hooks: “Ah! Thy shoulders—made for a captain’s coat, yes, yes.”

**Secret talent  
**Clotho is better at reading **status and deception** than most guards are at spotting thieves. He notices:

*   poorly cleaned blood on a cuff
*   a road-stain that suggests marsh travel
*   a new coinpurse bought too quickly for an “honest” wage
*   holy symbols turned inward, as if hidden
