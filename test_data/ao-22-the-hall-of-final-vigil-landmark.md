AO-22. The Hall of Final Vigil
==============================

> The Hall of Final Vigil — the mortuary processional chamber of the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) chapter, where the order's dead were washed, anointed, shrouded in sacred vestments, and carried in solemn procession down the stone staircase to the burial crypts below. A sizable room built for the weight of ceremony — wide enough for six pallbearers abreast, tall enough for the funeral hymns to echo, and lined with carved stone guardians who watched the dead make their final descent. The eternal flame sconces that once lit the way are dark now. The stone bier where knights lay in state is stacked with excavation tools and baskets of rubble. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) has fortified the room into a choke-point — packed clay barricades, stonefall triggers, and two elite Black Earth Guards on permanent sentry duty. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation crews stage here before descending, praying to a crude altar of the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) in the same chamber where the faithful once prayed over their honored dead. The staircase still descends into darkness. What comes back up it is no longer the same.

 

_The Hall of Final Vigil — Mortuary Processional Chamber of the Azure Oath — Now the Fortified Descent and Excavation Staging Ground_

* * *

### The Hall of Final Vigil

Every knightly order buries its dead. The [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) did so with the solemnity that the Arch-Paladin demanded — not with pomp or spectacle, but with quiet, deliberate ritual that honored the life of service each knight had sworn. And the ritual began here.

When a knight of the Azure Oath fell — on the Long Road, in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), in defense of the priory itself — the body was brought first to the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md), where the presiding cleric performed the Rites of Passage at the altar. Then the body was carried through the processional door on the chapel's west wall into this room — the Hall of Final Vigil — where it was received by the custodian of the dead.

The Hall is a sizable chamber, wider than most rooms in the priory, built to accommodate the full funeral cortege: six pallbearers walking abreast, mourning brothers on either side, and the presiding cleric leading the procession with a lantern held high. The ceiling is vaulted — not as tall as the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) nave, but high enough for the funeral hymns to gather and echo in the stone ribs overhead. The floor is flagstone, worn smooth by centuries of processions, sloping gently downward toward the staircase at the far end — a subtle architectural cue that everything in this room moves in one direction: down, into the earth, into rest.

At the room's center stands the **stone bier** — a waist-high granite slab supported by four carved legs shaped as kneeling knights. Here the dead were laid in state. The custodian washed the body with consecrated water drawn from the chapel's font. He anointed the forehead, hands, and heart with sacred oil blessed by [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). He wrapped the body in a burial shroud — blue linen, embroidered with the Azure Oath's sigil — drawn from the vestment alcoves that line the north wall. And then the brothers kept vigil. Sometimes through the night. Sometimes longer, if the dead was beloved or the death was hard.

Along both long walls, **carved stone guardians** stand in recessed niches — six in total, three per side, life-sized figures of knights in full plate with their swords planted point-down before them. These are not portraits of specific men. They are idealized guardians, carved in the style of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s divine sentinels, meant to watch over the dead during the vigil and escort their spirits to the staircase. Each guardian's face is identical — a serene, helmeted visage with open eyes. Beneath each figure, a name is carved into the niche base: the name of the most recently interred knight whose procession that guardian last watched over. These names were re-carved with each new funeral.

At the far end of the hall, the floor drops away into the **processional staircase** — a wide stone descent, broad enough for pallbearers to carry a bier shoulder-to-shoulder, leading down into the burial crypts below. Iron brackets line the staircase walls where **eternal flame sconces** once burned — sacred flames that were never allowed to go out, tended by the priory's clergy, lighting the dead's path into the earth. The sconces are dark now. The sacred flames died when the last cleric left.

**Halven Sere** tended this room for fourteen years. He could not perform the Rites of Passage — he was a lay brother, not ordained. But he kept the bier clean. He polished the stone guardians. He placed fresh-cut herbs from his garden on the bier each season in memory of the dead he had never known. He tried to keep the eternal flames lit, but without consecrated oil, they guttered and failed. He wept the first time the last flame went out. Then he lit a candle instead and placed it at the top of the stairs, because someone should light the way, even if it was only tallow and faith.

* * *

### What the Earth Temple Made of It

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) understood immediately what this room was for. [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) recognized the staircase as the only controlled access point to the crypts below — and therefore the single most important defensive position in the priory. Everything that goes up or down passes through this room. Control the Crypt Mouth, control the dungeon.

The fortification was thorough:

*   **Packed clay barricades** have been built across the room in two staggered lines — waist-high walls of hardened earth and rubble that create a killing corridor. Anyone entering from the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) door or the [corridor](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) door must navigate between the barricades under crossbow fire before reaching the staircase. The barricades provide three-quarters cover for defenders behind them
*   **A stonefall trap** is rigged above the primary entrance from the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md). A concealed rope mechanism releases a section of loose masonry from the ceiling above the doorway, dropping 200 lbs of stone rubble onto anyone passing through. Perception DC 15 to spot the mechanism. Disarm DC 14 (Thieves' Tools). Triggers on a trip-wire. 3d6 bludgeoning damage (DEX DC 13 for half) in a 5-foot area
*   **Crossbow slits** have been carved into the first barricade line — narrow notches that allow a defender to fire a heavy crossbow while maintaining three-quarters cover. The slits are angled to cover both entrances
*   **The staircase** has been narrowed. Loose rubble and packed clay reduce the original six-foot-wide processional descent to a three-foot passage that only allows single-file movement. This was deliberate — if intruders breach the room, the staircase becomes a secondary choke-point

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has claimed the northern half of the room as his **excavation staging area**. Tools are piled against the wall — picks, shovels, pry bars, baskets for hauling rubble, rope coils, and iron-reinforced lanterns. The stone bier has been repurposed as a work table, its sacred surface buried under geological samples, crude maps of the crypt level, and clay jars of mineral dust that Varnor uses in his geomantic rituals. Baskets of freshly dug rubble from the excavation below are stacked beside the staircase, waiting to be carried out.

In the northeast corner — in one of the guardian niches, where a stone sentinel once watched over the dead — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has directed his acolytes to erect a **crude altar to the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)**. The guardian statue that stood here has been toppled and broken, its stone torso lying face-down on the floor. In its place: a flat stone slab stained with ash and tallow, a clay bowl of crushed obsidian, and a rough-carved triangular symbol that pulses faintly with a sickly ochre light if a candle is lit beside it. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) requires his excavation acolytes to pray here before descending — a brief invocation to the Black Earth, asking for safe passage through the tombs. The acolytes do so reluctantly. Some of them are afraid of what they have found below.

##### Entering the Hall of Final Vigil

The door from the chapel opens onto a room larger than you expected — a wide, vaulted chamber that stretches perhaps twenty-five feet across and thirty feet deep, its ceiling arching fifteen feet overhead in stone ribs that gather shadow like cobwebs. The floor is flagstone, worn smooth and sloping gently downward toward the far end of the room, where a staircase descends into darkness.  
  
The room has been fortified. Two staggered lines of packed earth barricades — waist-high, solid, reinforced with rubble — cut across the chamber, creating a narrow corridor between them. Behind the first barricade, you can see narrow slits carved into the clay — crossbow positions. The barricades are positioned to cover both doors: the one you entered and another on the north wall.  
  
Along both long walls, recessed niches hold carved stone figures of knights — life-sized, armored, swords planted before them. Most are intact, their serene helmeted faces watching the room with open stone eyes. One niche on the right has been cleared — the statue lies broken face-down on the floor, and in its place sits a crude altar: a flat stone, a clay bowl of black dust, and a rough-carved symbol that you cannot quite identify from here.  
  
At the center of the room, a heavy granite slab rests on four carved legs shaped as kneeling figures. The slab's surface is buried under tools, rocks, rope, and rolled maps. Baskets of dirt and rubble are stacked beside the staircase. A faint smell of mineral dust and damp earth rises from below.  
  
Two guards in Black Earth tabards stand behind the second barricade line, crossbows resting on the packed clay, watching both doors. They are alert. They have been expecting someone — just not you.

### Features of the Hall of Final Vigil

#### The Stone Bier

The granite preparation table at the room's center is the hall's sacred anchor. Four carved legs — kneeling knight figures, each facing outward — support a slab five feet long and three feet wide, smooth and slightly concave. A shallow channel runs along the slab's edge, designed to carry water and anointing oil away from the body during preparation. The channel feeds into a small drain in the floor, still functional.

**DC 12 Investigation**  
Beneath the pile of excavation tools and geological samples, the slab's surface is inscribed with a prayer in Old Oeridian: _"Let the faithful pass. Let the stone remember."_ The inscription is partially obscured by tool-marks and wax stains, but legible.

**DC 15 Religion (Cleric or Paladin of Heironeous)**  
The bier is **consecrated**. Despite months of desecration, the stone still carries a faint divine charge — the cumulative weight of every body that was washed and prayed over on this slab. A cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who touches the bare stone feels a pulse of warmth, then deep sorrow, then resolve. This is a functional consecrated surface — undead that touch or cross over the bier have disadvantage on saving throws against Turn Undead for 1 minute. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) does not know this because they have never cleared the tools away.

* * *

#### The Stone Guardians

Six recessed niches — three per long wall — hold life-sized carved stone knights. The figures are identical in posture and armor, differentiated only by the names carved into their niche bases. Five of the six remain intact. One has been toppled to make room for the Elder Elemental God altar.

**DC 10 Perception**  
The names carved beneath each guardian are visible: _Sir Aldren Voss. Sir Tormund Kael. Dame Elara Brightholm. Sir Gerren of the Long Watch. Sir Pendric Hale._ The sixth niche — the toppled one — reads _Sir Corvan Ashenmoor_, but the stone is cracked and the name is partially illegible.

**DC 14 History**  
These names correspond to the last six knights interred in the crypts below before the order withdrew. Cross-referencing with the honor plaques in **AO-19** or the burial registry in **AO-16. The Scriptorium** confirms their identities and dates of interment.

**DC 16 Religion**  
The guardians are not merely decorative. They are **ward-anchors** — stone sentinels invested with divine purpose during the priory's original consecration. While all six stand, they reinforce the crypt seals below, helping to keep the interred dead at rest. With one toppled, the ward is weakened. This is one reason the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s excavation has been able to open sealed tombs that should have resisted mundane force. Restoring the sixth guardian to its niche (STR DC 16 to right the statue, Religion DC 13 to re-seat it in the ward-pattern) strengthens the crypt seals and imposes disadvantage on any undead within the crypt level for 24 hours.

* * *

#### The Eternal Flame Sconces

Iron brackets line the walls flanking the staircase — eight in total, four per side — each designed to hold a consecrated oil lamp that would burn continuously, lighting the processional descent. The sacred flames represented [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s vigilance — even in the earth, even among the dead, the light does not falter.

The sconces are dark. The sacred flames died years ago when the consecrated oil ran out and **Halven Sere** could not replace it.

**DC 12 Investigation**  
One sconce near the top of the stairs still holds a stub of tallow candle — placed there by **Halven Sere** after the last sacred flame guttered out. The candle is old, the wax cracked, but it has never been lit. He placed it as a promise: someone would light the way again.

**If the party has the consecrated oil from AO-15 (the Sacristy):**  
Pouring consecrated oil into any sconce and lighting it reignites the **eternal flame** in that bracket. The flame burns blue-white and does not consume the oil — it sustains itself on divine resonance. Each lit sconce extends the consecrated light 15 feet down the staircase. If all eight are lit, the entire descent is illuminated in sacred light, and undead on the staircase have disadvantage on attack rolls. Relighting the flames is a visible, dramatic act — the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) guards below will know something has changed.

* * *

#### The Vestment Alcoves

Along the north wall, between the guardian niches, three shallow alcoves once held the burial vestments — blue linen shrouds, embroidered with the Azure Oath's sigil, used to wrap the bodies of fallen knights before interment. Oak shelves in each alcove held folded shrouds, anointing oils, and the silver instruments used in the preparation ritual: a washing basin, a censer, and a small consecrated knife used to cut the shroud to the body's measure.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has emptied most of the alcoves. The shrouds were burned or used as rags. The anointing oils were poured out. The shelves now hold excavation supplies — picks, chisels, rope.

**DC 14 Investigation**  
One alcove at the far end — partially hidden behind a stack of rubble baskets — still contains a folded burial shroud. The blue linen is faded but intact, the Azure Oath sigil still visible in tarnished silver thread. The shroud is large enough for a full-sized human body. Below it, wrapped in oilcloth, lies the **silver consecration knife** — a slender ritual blade worth 15 gp as silver, but functionally a silvered dagger that deals an extra 1d4 radiant damage to undead when wielded by a character who worships [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) or a good-aligned deity.

* * *

#### The Wall Inscriptions

Above each guardian niche, carved into the stone lintel, a line of text records a passage from the Azure Oath's funeral liturgy. The inscriptions run the length of the hall — six lines in total, one per guardian, forming a complete processional prayer when read in order from the chapel door to the staircase:

_"We who stood in the light now pass into the stone."_  
_"The road is behind us. The oath is fulfilled."_  
_"Let the shield rest. Let the sword sleep."_  
_"Heironeous remembers. The stone remembers."_  
_"We go not into darkness, but into the earth that holds us."_  
_"Rise no more. Rest at last. The vigil is kept."_

The final line — _"Rise no more. Rest at last. The vigil is kept."_ — is carved above the toppled guardian's niche, directly over the Elder Elemental God altar. The irony is not lost on anyone who reads it.

**DC 12 Religion**  
The inscriptions are not merely decorative. They are the **Processional Ward** — a spoken sequence that, when recited aloud in order from entrance to staircase, reinforces the crypt seals below. A cleric, paladin, or devout lay worshiper of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who reads all six lines aloud in sequence causes the stone guardians' eyes to briefly glow with pale blue light and the air temperature to drop. Any undead on the crypt level must make a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or be restrained for 1 round as the seals tighten. This can only be used once per day.

* * *

#### The Elder Elemental God Altar

In the northeast corner, where Sir Corvan Ashenmoor's guardian once stood, [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has erected a crude devotional altar to the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). The toppled guardian lies face-down on the floor before it — cracked across the torso, the stone sword broken into three pieces.

The altar itself: a flat stone slab balanced on stacked rubble, stained with ash and tallow drippings. A clay bowl of crushed obsidian sits at the center. A rough-carved triangular symbol — the inverted triangle of the Elder Elemental God — is scratched into the slab's surface. Beside the bowl, three stubby candles made from rendered animal fat.

**DC 14 Arcana or Religion**  
The symbol is the mark of the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — older and more dangerous than the elemental cults that serve it. The crushed obsidian is a geomantic focus: [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) uses it to attune his senses to the stone before descending. The candles have been burned recently — within the last day.

**DC 17 Arcana**  
The altar is a **resonance anchor**. It does not grant power by itself, but it creates a sympathetic vibration between this room and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation site in the crypts below. While the altar stands, [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) can sense disturbances in this room through tremorsense while he is below — he feels footsteps on the stone, hears voices as low vibrations, and knows when the guards are in trouble. Destroying the altar (AC 10, HP 5, or simply scattering the components) severs this connection and blinds [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) to events on the main level.

* * *

#### The Processional Staircase

At the far end of the hall, the flagstone floor drops into a wide stone descent — the original processional staircase to the burial crypts. The stairs were built broad and shallow, each step only four inches high and eighteen inches deep, designed so that pallbearers carrying a heavy stone bier could descend safely and with dignity. The walls of the staircase are smooth-dressed stone, the iron sconce brackets spaced evenly on both sides.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has narrowed the descent with packed clay and loose rubble, reducing the six-foot width to a three-foot passage — single file only. This serves dual purpose: it prevents armed groups from descending in formation, and it channels anyone coming UP from the crypts into the crossbow kill-zone between the barricades.

**DC 12 Perception**  
The narrowing is crude but effective. The packed clay walls that reduce the staircase width are solid — breaking through would take time and noise (10 minutes of work or STR DC 18 to force a section open). However, the clay is packed against the original stone walls, not mortared. It could be undermined from below if the party has access to the crypt side.

**DC 14 Investigation**  
A faint draft rises from below — cool, damp, carrying the smell of wet stone and something else. Old dust. Very old dust. The kind that comes from chambers that have been sealed for a long time and recently opened.

The staircase descends approximately thirty feet before reaching the crypt level. The descent is dark — the eternal flame sconces are unlit, and the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) relies on carried lanterns rather than fixed light. The darkness at the bottom of the stairs is absolute.

* * *

#### The Excavation Staging Area

The northern half of the hall — between the vestment alcoves and the Elder Elemental God altar — has been converted into [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s staging ground. Excavation crews assemble here before descending and dump their haul here when they return. The area contains:

*   Picks, shovels, and pry bars (12 sets — enough for two full work crews)
*   Baskets of rubble and stone fragments hauled up from below (currently 8 baskets, partially full)
*   Rope coils (200 ft total), iron pitons, and iron-reinforced lanterns (6 — three with oil, three empty)
*   A crude **sketch map of the crypt level** pinned to the wall with iron nails — showing which tombs have been opened (marked with X), which remain sealed (marked with a circle), and the location of [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s primary excavation site (marked with the Elder Elemental God symbol)
*   A wooden crate containing clay jars of mineral samples, geological notes in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s hand, and three sealed vials of a dark liquid labeled in Flan script (alchemical compounds used in geomantic sounding — worthless to anyone who cannot read Flan)

**DC 13 Investigation (the sketch map)**  
The map is crude but invaluable. It shows the crypt level layout: a central corridor with branching tomb chambers on each side, a sealed vault at the far end (marked _"DO NOT OPEN — ward active"_ in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s hand), and the excavation site in a chamber beneath the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) (directly below the altar). Seven of twelve tombs are marked as opened. The remaining five are sealed. The sealed vault annotation suggests even [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) respects — or fears — whatever is behind that door.

### Encounter

The Crypt Mouth is one of the most heavily defended interior positions in the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). Two **elite Black Earth Guards** are posted here on permanent sentry duty — not rotating patrols, not off-duty raiders pressed into service, but dedicated sentries chosen by [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) for discipline and loyalty. They report directly to [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), not to [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md).

Additionally, 1–2 **Earth Acolytes** may be present — excavation workers resting between shifts, sorting rubble, or praying at the Elder Elemental God altar before descending. The acolytes are not elite fighters, but they will shout for help and fight if cornered.

**Guard positions:** Both guards are behind the second barricade line (the one closest to the staircase), with heavy crossbows resting on the packed clay. They have three-quarters cover (+5 AC) from ranged attacks. One watches the chapel door. The other watches the corridor door from [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md). They have a clear field of fire through the killing corridor between the barricade lines.

**Tactics:** The guards fire crossbows as intruders enter the killing corridor. If melee is joined, they draw maces and fight from behind the barricade (half cover for melee). If one guard falls, the other blows a signal horn — a deep, droning sound that alerts [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) in **AO-18** and any forces in [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md). If both guards are overwhelmed, any acolytes present flee down the staircase to warn [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) below.

**Stonefall trap:** If the guards see the party approaching from the chapel and have time to prepare, one guard readies the stonefall trigger (a rope pull behind the second barricade). The trap fires when the first character crosses the chapel doorway threshold.

### DM Notes

#### The Transition Point

AO-22 is the gateway between the priory's two levels. Everything changes here. Above this room, the dungeon is a military occupation — reavers, guards, stolen goods, prisoners, political evidence. Below this room, the dungeon becomes something older and stranger — knight-tombs, divine wards, Varnor's geomantic excavation, and whatever the order sealed away in the vault at the crypt level's far end.

The Crypt Mouth should feel like a threshold. The read-aloud text emphasizes the downward slope, the darkness at the bottom of the stairs, the smell of old dust rising. The transition from "raided monastery" to "haunted tomb" starts here.

#### Tactical Considerations

*   **Frontal assault** is the hardest option. The killing corridor, crossbow slits, and stonefall trap make a direct charge expensive. The guards have AC 18 behind three-quarters cover (effective AC 23 against ranged attacks from the corridor)
*   **Chapel door vs. corridor door:** The stonefall trap covers the chapel door only. Entering from [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) (the corridor) avoids the trap but still enters the killing corridor. Both doors are visible to the guards
*   **Stealth:** Approaching from the corridor door (Stealth DC 16 — the guards are alert and the room echoes) could allow a surprise round. Approaching from the chapel is harder (DC 18) because the stonefall trip-wire is in the way
*   **Distraction:** If the party creates a distraction elsewhere (alarm in the barracks, fire in the courtyard), one guard may be called away, reducing the defense by half
*   **From below:** If the party accesses the crypt level through another means (unlikely but possible), they can approach the guards from behind via the staircase. The guards are positioned to watch the doors, not the stairs
*   **The altar:** Destroying the Elder Elemental God altar before engaging cuts Varnor's tremorsense connection to this room. He will not feel the fight happening above and will not send reinforcements from below

#### Sacred Layer

This is the most poignant desecration in the priory. The room built to honor the dead with washing, anointing, prayer, and solemn procession is now a military fortification and an excavation staging ground. The stone bier where knights lay in state holds shovels and rock samples. The guardian who watched over Sir Corvan Ashenmoor lies face-down beneath an altar to the god whose servants are digging up his tomb. The processional staircase — built wide so the dead could be carried with dignity — has been narrowed to a chokepoint so the living can be killed more efficiently.

And above the toppled guardian, in letters that no one in the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has bothered to read: _"Rise no more. Rest at last. The vigil is kept."_

The vigil is not being kept. Not by anyone alive. Not yet.

#### Connections

*   **AO-14 (Chapel):** Processional door on the chapel's west wall. Direct access. The stonefall trap covers this entrance
*   **AO-11 (Knights Corridor):** Access from the west wing. No trap on this door, but still enters the killing corridor
*   **AO-15 (Sacristy):** Adjacent — the vestment preparation room connects thematically (priests robed in AO-15, then entered the chapel, then the funeral cortege passed through AO-22)
*   **AO-25 (Knight's Armory):** Nearby in the west wing. The dead knight's weapons were returned to the armory after the funeral — the processional flow was chapel → AO-22 → crypts, while the arms went chapel → AO-25
*   **Crypt Level (below):** The processional staircase descends 30 feet to the burial crypts. Currently narrowed to single-file. The crypt level is not yet written

##### The Processional Flow

_How the Azure Oath buried its dead — the full funeral sequence:_

*   **1\. Chapel (AO-14):** Rites of Passage performed at the altar. The presiding cleric commends the soul to [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)
*   **2\. Hall of Final Vigil (AO-22):** Body received at the stone bier. Washed with consecrated water, anointed with sacred oil, wrapped in the blue shroud. The brothers keep vigil — sometimes through the night
*   **3\. Processional Descent:** Six pallbearers carry the bier down the staircase. The presiding cleric leads with a lantern. The brothers follow, reciting the six-line funeral prayer from the wall inscriptions. The eternal flames light the way
*   **4\. Crypts Below:** The body is placed in its designated tomb. The cleric seals the tomb with a ward-prayer. The guardian statue in AO-22 is re-inscribed with the dead knight's name
*   **5\. Return:** The cortege ascends in silence. The presiding cleric extinguishes the processional lantern at the top of the stairs. The vigil is ended. The dead rest

##### Common Player Actions

*   **Frontal assault:** Enter killing corridor. Stonefall trap (chapel door). Crossbow fire from three-quarters cover. Hardest interior fight
*   **Stealth approach:** Corridor door DC 16 / chapel door DC 18 (trip-wire). Surprise round possible
*   **Destroy altar:** AC 10, HP 5 or scatter components. Severs Varnor's tremorsense link
*   **Examine bier:** Investigation DC 12 (inscription) / Religion DC 15 (consecration — disadvantage on undead Turn Undead saves)
*   **Read wall inscriptions:** Religion DC 12 — Processional Ward. Recite all six aloud = undead on crypt level DC 13 WIS save or restrained 1 round. 1/day
*   **Restore toppled guardian:** STR DC 16 + Religion DC 13. Strengthens crypt seals. Undead disadvantage 24 hrs
*   **Relight sconces:** Requires consecrated oil from **AO-15**. Each lit sconce = 15 ft sacred light. All 8 = full staircase illuminated, undead disadvantage on attacks
*   **Find burial shroud + knife:** Investigation DC 14 in vestment alcove. Shroud prevents undead rising. Knife = silvered dagger +1d4 radiant vs. undead
*   **Study sketch map:** Investigation DC 13 — full crypt layout, tomb status, excavation site, sealed vault warning
*   **Avoid stonefall:** Perception DC 15 to spot, Disarm DC 14 with Thieves' Tools. Or enter from corridor door
