Canoness Y'dey
==============

Canoness Y'dey, an influential cleric of [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md), operates under the guise of Mother Screng in the sinister village of [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). She is a pivotal character in the fight against evil forces, balancing her faith's teachings with a pragmatic approach to her mission. The following article details her physical description, personality, background, motivations, relationships, and secret purpose in the campaign setting.

> “They all think I’m just a toothless crone with a basket of weeds. That’s why I’ll see the Temple rot from the inside out—one root at a time.”

#### Physical Description

Y'dey's physical appearance helps her blend seamlessly into Nulb's seedy backdrop, allowing her to gather information unnoticed by the town's more dangerous residents.

*   **Aged and Weathered:** Canoness Y'dey, posing as Mother Screng, appears as an old and bent crone. Her scraggly gray locks spill out from beneath an aged shawl she wears year-round. Her gnarled hands and wrinkled face suggest a lifetime of hardship and wisdom.
*   **Eccentric Attire:** Her attire is mismatched, giving her the appearance of an unkempt herbalist. Beneath this disguise, however, lies a powerful cleric equipped with plate mail +1, a shield +1, and a mace +1 when her true nature is revealed.

### Personality

Y'dey’s eccentricity endears her to the people of Nulb, allowing her to work unnoticed as she identifies potential allies and threats.

*   **Pragmatic and Compassionate**: While unconventional for a cleric of St. Cuthbert, Y'dey prioritizes common sense over strict adherence to law and order. Her focus is on redemption and protecting the innocent, even when it requires bending rules.
*   **Eccentric Healer:** In her guise as Mother Screng, Y'dey is perceived as a harmless eccentric who provides remedies for ailments. She often wanders the village muttering to herself, collecting herbs and moss while providing unsolicited advice.

### Background

Y'dey's history of opposing elemental evil makes her a vital figure in the region's ongoing struggle, despite her unassuming current role.

*   [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md): Y'dey was a key figure in the fall of the Temple of Elemental Evil and the decisive Battle of Emridy Meadows. Her tactical brilliance and clerical power earned her respect across the region.
*   **Mission in** [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md): Sent by the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md), Y'dey took up residence in Nulb under the guise of Mother Screng. She is tasked with monitoring the activities of the Temple of Elemental Evil and identifying those who can be redeemed.

#### Motivation

Y'dey's motivations combine her clerical duty with her personal quest for answers about the nature of elemental evil.

*   **Redemption of the Wicked:** Y'dey believes even Nulb’s selfish and cruel inhabitants might find redemption. She focuses on assisting those with potential for good, keeping them from falling deeper into evil.
*   **Investigating Elemental Evil:** Y'dey harbors doubts about the true origins of the Temple of Elemental Evil. Her mission involves uncovering the forces behind it and preventing their resurgence.

> _"If you’re here to make this cesspool a little less foul, I might just have a tincture or two to help. If not, don’t waste my time."_

### Relationships

_Canoness Y'dey (as Mother Screng) maintains a web of calculated relationships, all carefully orchestrated to monitor, manipulate, or undermine the Temple's growing influence in Nulb._

* * *

##### **Madame Selentis (**[Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md)**)**

*   **Publicly:** Y'dey treats Selentis with polite distance. As Nulb’s self-proclaimed lady of the manor, Selentis commands attention and deference—but Y'dey feigns subservience, claiming to be “too old to meddle in politics.”
*   **Privately:** Y'dey suspects that Selentis is the spider at the center of the Temple’s logistics web. She has traced several shipments from the [6\. Wainwright of Nulb](6-wainwright-of-nulb-landmark.md) and [1\. Waterside Hostel of Nulb](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md) directly across the bridge to the old Mayor’s Manor, confirming it as a relay station for Temple operations. She believes Selentis may be connected to [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) himself.
*   **Action Taken:** Homing pigeons have carried urgent messages from Y'dey to Veluna regarding “The Widow in Black,” her coded name for Selentis.

* * *

##### **Selentis’ Bandits and Temple Couriers**

*   **Publicly:** Y'dey offers them salves and poultices without protest, playing the harmless old woman. She often gives them "incorrect" doses that render them foggy or nauseous—just enough to be blamed on tavern drink or illness.
*   **Privately:** Y'dey maintains a careful list of names and faces. She watches for patterns—who visits Selentis, who crosses the river at odd hours, and who returns from the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) wounded. She’s recorded at least three regular courier faces she suspects answer to Lucius Graeme.

* * *

##### **Otis and Murfles (Hruda)**

*   **True Allies in Disguise:** Otis operates as a smith and observer. Hruda, disguised as Y'dey’s “daughter,” is a Velunese spy who passes messages between agents, tending the herb shop’s front while maintaining cover.
*   **Covert Operations:** Together, the three coordinate intelligence collection, sabotage efforts, and the spread of disinformation. Hruda often flirts with drunken pirates to loosen tongues, while Otis supplies damaged weapons to Temple-aligned warriors.

* * *

##### **Temple of Elemental Evil**

*   **Cautious Observer:** Y'dey has no delusions about facing the Temple openly. Her role is information and disruption. She walks the line carefully—friendly enough to avoid suspicion, passive enough to be ignored.
*   **Subversive Tactics:** When possible, she introduces purgative herbs into Temple food shipments via trusted locals, weakening guards at the Hunting Cottage or causing minor outbreaks of illness.

* * *

##### **River Pirates and the Crimson Reapers**

*   **Publicly:** Y'dey is tolerated. Her healing goods are used after drunken brawls or dockside fights.
*   **Privately:** She has bribed or tricked several low-ranking pirates into revealing smuggling routes. One pirate confessed to using the cellar of the [4: Boatman’s Tavern and Nulb Market](43A-boatmanE28099s-tavern-and-nulb-market-landmark.md) to warehouse captives before transfer to Selentis.

* * *

##### [Skole](skole-person.md)**, Wat, and** [Lodriss](lodriss-person.md)

*   **Wat (Fire Temple):** Y'dey finds him erratic and thuggish. She believes he is unworthy of command but dangerous due to his loyalty to [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md).
*   **Skole (Water Temple):** She suspects Skole is more thoughtful and fearful—possibly open to manipulation.
*   **Lodriss:** Y'dey views her as a wild card—neutral by claim, but compromised by her relationship with [Tolub](tolub-person.md), a pirate under Water Temple command. She keeps her distance but monitors the brothel through Hruda’s street contacts.

* * *

##### **Commander** [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) **and Hunting Cottage Logistics**

*   **Strategic Concern:** Y'dey is well aware of the tunnel beneath the Hunting Cottage. She has no means to access it directly but has overheard wounded brigands discussing the “dark gate below the earth.”
*   **Countermeasures:** She has begun seeding Temple supply crates (when intercepted by allies) with traceable reagents—so she can track movement and identify storage points via scent or reaction with certain herbs.

### Public Role

Y'dey’s public role as Mother Screng allows her to move freely within Nulb while maintaining her cover.

*   **Herb Shopkeeper:** Mother Screng’s Herb Shop is a vital resource for Nulb’s residents, offering remedies for illnesses and injuries. Y'dey uses this guise to build trust and gather information.
*   **Village Wanderer:** Y'dey’s eccentric habits allow her to visit homes and observe villagers without raising alarm.
*   **Healer and Advisor:** Her remedies, though mundane, earn her goodwill, making her the closest thing Nulb has to a community healer.

> _"Let them think I’m a rambling old crone. The less they notice me, the more I notice them."_

### DM Only Section

##### Secret Mission

##### Primary mission

*   **Purpose**: Y'dey’s primary mission is to monitor the resurgence of elemental evil. She reports findings to Canon Hazen of Veluna using the Sending spell or homing pigeons as a backup.
*   **Temple Connections**: Y'dey is aware of the Temple’s alliances with Iuz but suspects deeper, more ancient forces at play.

##### The Herb Shop

*   **Hidden Resources**: The shop contains a small armory of magical and mundane items, including Y'dey’s +1 mace, plate mail, and scrolls of powerful cleric spells.
*   **Pigeon Coop:** Six homing pigeons are hidden in the rear of the shop for emergency communication.

#### Roleplaying Tips

*   **Eccentric Mannerisms**: Y'dey mutters to herself, randomly collects plants, and enters homes uninvited, which endears her to some and annoys others.
*   **Strategic Helper:** She offers subtle aid to good-aligned adventurers but avoids direct involvement unless the situation is dire.

##### Potential Encounters

*   **Cultist Detection**: Y'dey identifies villagers who fall under the Temple’s sway and warns adventurers discreetly.
*   **Information Source**: She provides vital clues about the Temple and its forces, pointing adventurers toward key locations.
*   **Combat Support**: Though she avoids combat, Y'dey can serve as a powerful ally in desperate situations, using her healing abilities and magical items.

 

### If Discovered — What Happens When Mother Screng's Cover Is Blown

****Canoness Y'dey**'s cover as Mother Screng is the most fragile asset the forces of good hold in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).** She is a 6th-level Cleric of St. Cuthbert operating behind enemy lines with two allies — [Otis](otis-person.md) and the halfling [Murfles](murfles-person.md) — and no extraction plan from Veluna. If the Temple identifies her, the intelligence network collapses and the party loses their best source of information inside the village. This section covers the triggers, the Temple's response chain, Y'dey's emergency protocols, and the campaign consequences.

 

#### Discovery Triggers — What Could Blow Her Cover

Y'dey's cover can be compromised through several vectors. The DM should track which triggers are active based on party actions:

*   **The party visits the herb shop too openly.** If the party enters [Mother Screng's Herb Shop](3-mother-screngE28099s-herb-shop-landmark.md) repeatedly, asks questions about the Temple in earshot of other customers, or is seen leaving with equipment that does not match an herbalist transaction, [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md)'s informants will notice. DC 14 Stealth (group check) to visit without drawing attention during daylight. DC 10 at night, but Nulb's night patrols make after-dark movement its own risk
*   **The party is captured and interrogated.** If [Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md)'s thugs or [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md)'s bandits capture a party member and learn they have been receiving intelligence from "the old herbalist," Y'dey is burned. DC 15 Intimidation or DC 18 Persuasion to resist interrogation under duress
*   **Detect Magic or divination near the herb shop.** Y'dey casts _Nondetection_ daily on herself (she has 3rd-level slots), but her hidden armory — plate mail +1, mace +1, shield +1, Ring of Fire Resistance — radiates magic. If a Temple agent casts _Detect Magic_ within 30 feet of the shop's rear storage, the jig is up. Y'dey's _Nondetection_ covers her person, not her equipment cache
*   **[Wat](wat-person.md) overhears something.** Wat is [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md)'s double agent at the [Waterside Hostel](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md). He monitors everyone. If a party member mentions "Mother Screng helped us" or "the herbalist knows about the Temple" within the hostel's common room, Wat passes the information to the Fire Temple within 24 hours. Passive Perception 14 for Wat to overhear a careless remark
*   **Y'dey uses divine magic publicly.** If Y'dey is forced to heal someone, turn undead, or cast a spell in view of Temple-aligned NPCs, the illusion of a toothless crone herbalist disintegrates. Healing magic in Nulb is conspicuous — there are no sanctioned clerics in the village
*   **Murfles is followed.** [Murfles](murfles-person.md) moves between Y'dey and [Otis](otis-person.md) as a courier. If [Lodriss](lodriss-person.md) or [Grud Squinteye](grud-squinteye-person.md)'s wharf crew observe Murfles making regular trips between the smithy and the herb shop, the pattern alone is enough to trigger investigation. DC 16 Perception to spot Murfles on a covert run

 

#### Temple Response Chain — From Suspicion to Action

The Temple does not have a single response — it depends on _which faction_ discovers Y'dey first. Each faction has different priorities and different chains of command:

##### Scenario A: Nysera Krivaltis Discovers the Network

[Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) (Madame Selentis) is the most dangerous discovery vector. She controls Nulb's civilian informant network — children, thieves, merchants — and her Enchantment spells (_Charm Person_, _Suggestion_) mean she can interrogate suspects without them realizing it happened.

*   **Hour 0:** Selentis dispatches Krael Vortash's bandits (18–24 available, three shifts) to surround the herb shop and smithy simultaneously. She does not warn other Temple factions — this is _her_ intelligence coup
*   **Hours 0–2:** She sends a runner to [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) at the [Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) requesting armed reinforcement. Feldrin dispatches 4–6 Temple bandits via the overland trail
*   **Hours 2–6:** Selentis interrogates any captured agents using _Charm Person_ (DC 14 Wisdom save) and _Suggestion_ (DC 14 Wisdom save). She wants names — specifically, who Y'dey reports to and whether Veluna has other agents in the region
*   **Hours 6–12:** **Lucius Graeme** is notified. He escalates to **Hedrack** if the captured agents reveal connections to Canon Hazen or Veluna's church hierarchy. This transforms a local security matter into a strategic intelligence crisis
*   **Beyond 12 hours:** **Hedrack** orders [Deggum](deggum-person.md) (Master of Security and Discipline) to conduct a full counterintelligence sweep of Nulb. Deggum is paranoid, sadistic, and thorough. Every outsider in the village is investigated. The party becomes a target even if they were not connected to Y'dey

##### Scenario B: Dick Rentsch or the Waterside Hostel Discovers Her

[Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md) is an [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) operative under [Romag](romag-person.md). If his staff identifies Y'dey, the Earth Temple claims the arrest.

*   **Immediate:** Rentsch sends his manservants (crossbows from the loft, chain mail, shields) and 2–4 hostel thugs to seize Y'dey. He bars the hostel doors and detains anyone who arrived with the party
*   **Escalation:** Rentsch notifies Selentis (who controls supply logistics) and [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) (who provides enforcement). Smigmal's response is assassination, not arrest — she will attempt to kill Y'dey and Otis to eliminate witnesses
*   **Political fallout:** Romag uses the captured spy as leverage against the other cults. "My people caught the Velunese agent — what have _your_ people accomplished?" This strengthens Earth Temple's position within the [Cult of Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) hierarchy

##### Scenario C: Wat Reports to the Fire Temple

If [Wat](wat-person.md) discovers Y'dey's identity, he reports to [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md) (Fire Temple). Alrrem's response is calculated, not immediate.

*   **Alrrem's first instinct:** Do _not_ arrest Y'dey. Instead, feed her false intelligence and use her as an unwitting disinformation channel to Veluna. This is the most dangerous outcome for the party — because Y'dey appears to still be operating safely while her intelligence is now compromised
*   **Alternative:** If Alrrem decides the Fire Temple gains more by exposing Y'dey publicly, he tips off Selentis anonymously. This costs the Earth Temple a prisoner they should have caught, embarrassing Romag and Rentsch
*   **DM choice:** Alrrem's response depends on what serves the Fire Temple's inter-faction rivalry. If the Earth Temple is already ascendant, he exposes Y'dey to embarrass them. If the Fire Temple needs an intelligence advantage, he runs Y'dey as an unwitting double agent

##### Scenario D: Lareth Learns of the Network

If [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) discovers Y'dey's network — through his own informants, through Varek at the Laughing Dog, or through the party — his response is entirely self-serving.

*   **Option 1:** Lareth approaches the party and tells them Y'dey's cover is about to be blown (whether true or not). He offers to help them extract her in exchange for a future favor. This builds trust and dependency
*   **Option 2:** Lareth sells the information to whichever Temple faction offers him the best advantage. Y'dey's capture becomes currency in Lareth's power game
*   **Option 3:** Lareth does nothing and waits. He files the information away as leverage for later — knowing the forces of good have an agent in Nulb gives him a bargaining chip against both sides

#### Y'dey's Emergency Protocols — The Escape Plan

Y'dey does not have a formal extraction plan from Veluna. She was placed in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) with the understanding that she would operate until the Temple was destroyed or she was killed. But she is not suicidal. She has prepared contingencies:

##### Signal and Rally

*   **Emergency signal:** Y'dey releases her six carrier pigeons simultaneously from the herb shop's rear window. [Otis](otis-person.md) knows that six pigeons in the air means "blown — rally at the fallback point." One pigeon carries a coded message to Canon Hazen in Veluna: "Mother Screng has closed the shop"
*   **Rallying point:** The treeline on the east bank of Imeryds Run, 400 yards south of Nulb where the river bends into the Gnarley Forest. Otis, Murfles, and Y'dey know this location. The party does not — unless Y'dey has trusted them enough to share it
*   **Otis's response:** Otis arms up immediately — Chain Mail +2, Longsword +2, Cold Iron Battle Axe. He sends his two giant owls (HP 27 and 22) as aerial scouts to identify pursuit. His four sprites provide early warning in the treeline. Otis is a 10th-level Ranger — he is the most dangerous combatant in the escape party
*   **Murfles's response:** [Murfles](murfles-person.md) runs. Her job is to reach the rallying point and confirm Otis received the signal. If separated, she makes for Hommlet independently

##### Y'dey's Armory and Preparation Time

Y'dey's hidden cache is stored in the rear of the herb shop behind a false wall panel (Investigation DC 16 to find from outside; Y'dey knows its location):

*   Plate mail +1 and shield +1 — donning takes 10 minutes (requires help) or 15 minutes alone. **This is her critical vulnerability.** If the Temple strikes fast, Y'dey fights in robes (AC 11) instead of her full kit (AC 20)
*   Mace +1, Ring of Fire Resistance (worn at all times under gloves)
*   Spell scrolls: _Silence_, _Raise Dead_, _Protection from Poison_
*   4 potions of healing, 1 Potion of Flying
*   **The Potion of Flying is her panic button.** If surrounded and unable to reach the rallying point on foot, she drinks it and flies south over the river. 10-minute duration — enough to clear Nulb's perimeter and reach the treeline. The potion cannot be replaced

##### Escape Routes from Nulb

*   **Route 1 — South along Imeryds Run (preferred):** Follow the riverbank south into the Gnarley Forest. DC 14 Survival to navigate at night. DC 10 during daylight. The river provides orientation and drinking water. This is the safest route because [Tolub](tolub-person.md)'s pirates patrol _north_ of Nulb, not south
*   **Route 2 — East into the Gnarley Forest:** Direct flight into the forest. DC 12 Survival to maintain direction. Gnarley Rangers may be encountered (friendly to Otis). Risk: the forest is dense and movement is slow — pursuers on horseback can circle ahead via the road
*   **Route 3 — West via the Low Road (dangerous):** The Low Road crosses a stone bridge with a guarded gatehouse. [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md)'s men at the [Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) can block this road within 15 minutes of receiving word. DC 18 Deception to bluff through the gatehouse if guards have been alerted
*   **Route 4 — River by boat (desperate):** Steal a boat from the [wharf](43A-boatmanE28099s-tavern-and-nulb-market-landmark.md). DC 16 Stealth to reach the docks undetected. DC 14 Athletics to row against the current south. [Grud Squinteye](grud-squinteye-person.md) and the wharf crew respond within 2 rounds if the theft is spotted. Pirate vessels can pursue

#### Campaign Consequences — What Changes If the Network Burns

**The loss of Y'dey's network in Nulb is catastrophic for the forces of good and creates a cascade of consequences the DM should track:**

##### Immediate Consequences (0–48 Hours)

*   **Intelligence blackout:** Y'dey was the only source of Temple logistics intelligence inside Nulb. Without her, the party and their allies lose visibility on supply shipments, prisoner movements, and faction disputes. The Temple becomes a black box
*   **Otis's smithy is compromised:** Even if Otis escapes, he cannot return to his cover. The smithy is searched, his hidden gems (25 gems, 100 gp each) are confiscated if not recovered first, and his Nulb contacts go silent
*   **Murfles goes underground:** The half-elf spy disappears. If she escapes, she makes her way to Hommlet and can provide a debrief to the party — but her usefulness in Nulb is over
*   **[Elmo](elmo-person.md) is at risk:** If the Temple's counterintelligence sweep extends to Hommlet (which [Deggum](deggum-person.md) would push for), Otis's brother Elmo becomes a target. Elmo's cover in Hommlet is separate from Y'dey's, but family connections create a thread Deggum could pull

##### Medium-Term Consequences (Days to Weeks)

*   **Temple security hardens:** [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) increases patrols around the [Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) tunnel. Supply routes are rerouted. The [Nulb Constable](47-nulb-constable-landmark.md) is replaced or given a Temple minder. The village becomes measurably harder to operate in
*   **[Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) activates in Verbobonc:** If interrogation reveals Y'dey's Velunese connections, Lakash uses this to destabilize the Viscount's relationships — "Veluna is running covert operations on Verbobonc soil without the Viscounty's knowledge"
*   **The party's reputation in Nulb shifts:** If the party was seen visiting the herb shop, every Temple operative in Nulb now watches them. Social encounters in the [Waterside Hostel](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md) and [Boatman's Tavern](43A-boatmanE28099s-tavern-and-nulb-market-landmark.md) become hostile — Insight and Deception DCs increase by +2 for all Nulb social interactions until the heat dies down

##### Long-Term Consequences (Weeks to Months)

*   **Canon Hazen receives the pigeon:** Veluna now knows its agent is burned. The Church of St. Cuthbert may dispatch a rescue or replacement — but any new agent arriving in Nulb enters a village on high alert. The window for covert operations is closed
*   **Y'dey becomes a quest NPC:** If captured and not killed, Y'dey is held at the [Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) or transported to the Greater Temple for interrogation by Deggum. Rescuing her becomes a quest in itself — with the added urgency that every day she is held, more intelligence is extracted
*   **The "good guys" in Nulb are gone:** With Y'dey, Otis, and Murfles removed, the party has no reliable allies in Nulb. Every interaction becomes transactional or adversarial. The village's strategic value as an intelligence-gathering location drops to zero — it is now purely hostile territory

##### DM Notes: Running the Discovery

*   **Pacing:** Do not rush the discovery. Let the party make the mistake that triggers it. The most powerful version of this event is one where the players realize _they_ caused it — they visited the herb shop too often, they talked too loudly in the hostel, they let Murfles be followed. The consequence should feel earned, not arbitrary
*   **Give them a warning shot:** Before the full discovery, have Y'dey tell the party "I've noticed eyes on the shop. Be careful who sees you come here." This gives the party a chance to adjust their behavior — or to ignore the warning and face the consequences
*   **Y'dey fights to escape, not to die:** Y'dey is too valuable to waste on a heroic last stand. She will use _Hold Person_, _Command_ ("Flee!"), and the Potion of Flying to create distance. She fights only if cornered with no escape route
*   **Otis is the combat anchor:** If the escape turns into a running fight, Otis is the one who stays behind to buy time. He is a 10th-level Ranger with AC 17, 100 HP, and a +2 longsword. He can hold a corridor, a bridge, or a doorway long enough for Y'dey and Murfles to clear the village. This is a potential heroic sacrifice moment
*   **The party can prevent the discovery:** If the party is smart about operational security — meeting Y'dey at random locations outside the shop, using dead drops instead of face-to-face meetings, keeping Murfles's courier runs irregular — the network can survive indefinitely. Reward tradecraft
