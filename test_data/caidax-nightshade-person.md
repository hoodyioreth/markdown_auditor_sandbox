Caidax Nightshade
=================

> Caidax Nightshade is a patient architect of civil unrest — an Earth Temple field agent whose mission is to fracture the fragile political balance between the Kron Hills gnomes and the Viscounty of Verbobonc through a carefully constructed false-flag campaign of staged gnomish raids. Born to a minor Oeridian noble house sworn to House Mori in the Diocese of Devarnish, Veluna, his upbringing near the Verbobonc border and rational Velunan education made him a natural intriguer. Recruited personally by Romag after years of disillusionment and alcoholism, Caidax now wields the Scroll of Veil's illusion magic to disguise human raiders as gnomish war bands — shattering trust between neighbors so the Earth Temple can expand in the chaos.

Caidax Nightshade is not a battlefield brute nor a roaring zealot. He is something far more unsettling — a patient architect of civil unrest. Recently recruited into the Earth Temple by Romag himself, Caidax operates as a destabilization agent whose purpose is to fracture the fragile political balance between the Kron Hills gnomes and the Viscounty of Verbobonc.

Where others raid for loot, Caidax raids for consequence. His mission is to provoke bloodshed under false colors. It does not matter whether humans massacre gnomes or gnomes retaliate against humans. What matters is the collapse of trust, the breakdown of alliances, and the widening of suspicion along the Long Road. In this, he serves Romag faithfully and reports through Lucius Graeme, who ensures the Earth Temple’s ambitions remain aligned with the Greater Temple’s long strategy.

##### Role within the Earth Temple

*   Field agent recruited personally by [Romag](romag-person.md)
*   Political destabilizer in southern Verbobonc
*   Coordinates false-flag operations against gnome settlements
*   Works in partnership with [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)
*   Reports results and receives orders via Lucius Graeme

* * *

#### The False Gnome Campaign

Caidax’s most dangerous undertaking is the carefully constructed false-flag campaign designed to shatter relations between humans and gnomes. Rather than openly assaulting settlements as Earth cultists, his raiders impersonate gnomish war bands and strike at caravans and isolated farmsteads along the Long Road corridor. Survivors are intentionally left behind to tell the tale.

The brilliance of the strategy lies in its ambiguity. A single massacre could ignite retaliation. A retaliatory strike could escalate into border conflict. The Earth Temple need not conquer territory if it can instead turn neighbors against one another. Caidax understands that hatred spreads faster than armies.

##### Strategic Objectives

*   Provoke open conflict between Kron Hills gnomes and Verbobonc
*   Undermine regional trade stability
*   Disrupt diplomatic efforts involving Celene and Veluna
*   Increase Earth Temple influence through chaos

### Recruitment & Training Network

Caidax does not recruit personally in taverns. That task falls to intermediaries loyal to the Earth Temple. In Verbobonc City, [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) — known simply as “Lak” — recruits desperate men from [F3 The Bronze Unicorn Inn](f3-the-bronze-unicorn-inn-landmark.md). In Nulb, half-orcs are gathered from the [1\. Waterside Hostel of Nulb](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md), drawn by coin, vengeance, or cult loyalty.

These recruits are then transported — blindfolded — to a secluded valley far to the east. The valley’s precise location is known only to Maris, Caidax, and Romag. There, under Maris Blackshale’s hard discipline, they are trained in ambush tactics, coordinated retreat, and gnomish mimicry.

Caidax oversees the ideological shaping. Maris shapes the soldiers.

### Recruitment Sources

*   Humans recruited in Verbobonc City (via Lakash Quallad)
*   Half-orcs recruited in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) (Waterside Hostel)
*   Mixed raider groups assembled in hidden eastern valley
*   Blindfold protocol prevents recruits from revealing location

#### The Scroll of Veil

The deception that empowers the campaign rests on a powerful illusion device known as the Scroll of Veil. Created at Romag’s request and forged through arcane assistance from Senshock, the device allows raiders to assume the appearance of a gnome strike party.

Under its magic, height, silhouette, posture, and even voice are altered convincingly enough to fool frightened witnesses during fast-moving assaults. Caidax wields the wand focus tied to the Scroll, activating its power at the moment of engagement.

The illusion is not permanent and requires careful timing. Its limitations demand discipline — something Maris enforces ruthlessly among the raiders.

##### Capabilities

*   Illusory transformation into gnomish raiders
*   Alters physical outline and voice tone
*   Limited duration and charges
*   Requires wand focus created by [Senshock](senshock-person.md)

#### Personality

Caidax Nightshade is disarmingly articulate. His voice carries a melodic purity that has been compared to that of a nymph, yet when he speaks of civilization, nobility, or organized faith, a subtle growl creeps beneath the tone. He delights in exposing what he perceives as hypocrisy.

Curiously, he often leaves taverns cleaner than he found them and pays debts for strangers. He distributes coin to the needy and rewards loyalty generously. In his mind, he is not spreading chaos but forcing societies to confront their own fragility. He believes destruction clears the rot.

Privately, he drinks to quiet the memory of a life he abandoned.

##### Roleplaying Notes

*   Calm, eloquent, measured speech
*   Belittles institutions and aristocracy
*   Sees himself as a reformer through ruin
*   Drinks in solitude, not publicly
*   Prefers manipulation over violence

#### History

Born to a minor Oeridian noble house — a vassal family sworn to House Mori, the counts holding secular authority in the Diocese of Devarnish in the Archclericy of Veluna — Caidax's arcane potential manifested early, though not strongly enough to satisfy his ambitious parents. They forced advanced magical training upon him in hopes of elevating their status. He admired true spellcasters deeply, yet he lacked the innate brilliance he so desperately sought.

His upbringing near the Verbobonc border gave him a formative familiarity with Verboboncian politics, while his rational Velunan education made him a natural at the kind of intrigue he now thrives on. The town of [Jarl](jarl-settlement.md) ruled by Trisen Mori — the younger brother of Count Westin Mori in [Devarnish](devarnish-settlement.md) — was his point of origin: a busy border town at the intersection of two major trade routes, where the wealth of commerce mingled with the conservative religious politics of the [Diocese of Devarnish](diocese-of-devarnish-organization.md). Jarl's proximity to the Furyondian border and the [Pholtus](pholtus-person-1.md) resistance movements gave the young Caidax an early education in the fragility of political order.

Resentment grew where talent did not. Eventually he fled the expectations of his family and drifted into disillusionment and alcoholism. [Romag](romag-person.md) encountered him not as a failure, but as a man stripped of illusion and hungry for purpose. The Earth Temple offered him clarity: if civilization was flawed, it deserved to break.

##### Background Summary

#### Temple Politics

Within the Temple hierarchy, Caidax occupies a precarious but important role. He is loyal to Romag, yet watched by [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md). He is skeptical of the Air Temple, believing the Cult of Howling Hatred to be unreliable and erratic. He prefers Earth’s patience and inevitability over Air’s volatility.

If conflict between Earth and Air cultists erupts near Feathergale Spire, Caidax may quietly encourage it.

##### Political Position

*   Loyal to Romag’s command
*   Reports routed through Lucius Graeme
*   Distrustful of Air Temple
*   Competes indirectly with Lareth’s influence

### Evidence Trail — What the Party Can Find

The false-flag campaign is not airtight. Physical evidence exists across multiple locations that, when connected, exposes the entire operation. This section consolidates every recoverable piece of evidence so the DM can track what the party has found and what conclusions they can draw.

 

##### Evidence Item 1: The Slikkina Jillink Letter

**Location:** AO-13, Caidax's Chamber — folded parchment inside his travel chest  
**Discovery:** Automatic if the chest is searched; no check required  
**Physical Description:** A folded letter sealed with green wax bearing an ivy-wrapped gemstone sigil

This letter is the single most damaging piece of evidence against the false-flag operation. Written by **Slikkina Jillink**, Matriarch of Clan Jillink and Keeper of the Greenway Vaults, it is addressed to **[Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)** as "Captain of the southern company" and lays out the entire scheme in explicit detail.

What the letter reveals:

*   **The false-flag strategy:** Raiders are to impersonate gnomish war bands and strike caravans along the Long Road to fracture human-gnome relations in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md)
*   **The Scroll of Veil:** A magical disguise device that cloaks raiders in gnomish illusion — named by name in the letter
*   **The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) as targets:** The letter explicitly encourages attacks on Borderer patrols to provoke Verbobonc into withdrawing from the Kron Hills
*   **Recruitment sources:** "Lak" at the Bronze Unicorn Inn in Verbobonc City (this is [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md)) and half-orcs recruited from the Waterside Hostel in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)
*   **The hidden valley camp:** A blindfold-access training facility east of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md)
*   **"Caidex Nightshade":** Named directly as the "wizard associate" assisting the operation
*   **Clan Jillink's involvement:** The letter warns that no one must learn "the name of Clan Jillink or the role we play" — the gnomish clan is bankrolling the operation

**What the party can deduce from the letter alone:**

*   **DC 10 Intelligence (Investigation):** The false gnome raids are a deliberate deception, not genuine gnomish aggression
*   **DC 13 Intelligence (History):** Clan Jillink is a known gnomish trading clan in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) — recognizing the name connects the conspiracy to the gnomish political structure
*   **DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation):** "Lak" refers to Lakash Quallad, a known figure in Verbobonc City's underworld — this connects the recruitment pipeline to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)

**Connection to [Gimble Jillink](gimble-jillink-person.md):** Slikkina is the matriarch of Clan Jillink. Any party member who has encountered Gimble Jillink or knows of the Jillink trading network can connect Slikkina's letter to the broader gnomish political landscape. DC 12 Intelligence (History) to make this connection. This transforms a Temple evidence trail into a gnomish political scandal — the Jillinks are funding destabilization operations against their own people's alliances.

 

##### Evidence Item 2: The Scroll of Veil

**Location:** Carried by Caidax personally — recovered from his body or possessions if he is captured or killed  
**Discovery:** Automatic on Caidax's person; DC 14 Arcana to identify its function without using it  
**Physical Description:** A rolled scroll with a wand focus attached, created by **Senshock** at [Romag](romag-person.md)'s request

The Scroll of Veil is the physical mechanism behind the false-flag illusions. It cloaks a group of raiders in gnomish appearance — altering height, silhouette, posture, and voice for a limited duration. The wand focus must be activated at the moment of engagement.

**What the Scroll proves:**

*   That a magical disguise capable of impersonating gnomes exists and was actively in use
*   That the "gnomish raids" witnessed by survivors may have been humans in magical disguise
*   Combined with the Slikkina letter, this is irrefutable proof of the false-flag operation

**DC 16 Arcana:** The scroll's construction bears traces of both arcane and divine enchantment — the arcane component was crafted by Senshock (a Temple sorcerer), and the divine binding connects to Earth Temple ritual practices. This links the Scroll's creation directly to the Temple hierarchy.

 

##### Evidence Item 3: Defaced Heironeous Carvings

**Location:** AO-13, Caidax's Chamber — stone walls  
**Discovery:** Visible on entry; DC 13 Religion to identify them as [Heironeous](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md) blessings  
**Physical Description:** Faint lightning bolt carvings and fragments of sacred oaths, defaced with crude triangular Earth Temple symbols gouged into the stone

These carvings establish that Caidax is occupying a room in a former [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) monastery and has deliberately desecrated its sacred symbols. While not direct evidence of the false-flag operation, they confirm Caidax's Earth Temple allegiance and his contempt for established religious institutions.

 

##### Evidence Item 4: Excavation Notes and Geomantic Research

**Location:** AO-13, Caidax's Chamber — study table and scattered parchments  
**Discovery:** Automatic if the table is searched  
**Key Findings:**

*   Rough sketches of crypt tunnels beneath the monastery
*   References to [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation orders and duergar mining activity
*   Location of the buried altar chamber
*   Notes on earth-binding rituals and ley-line intersections
*   The repeated phrase: **"The stone listens."**

**DC 12 Arcana:** Caidax is attempting to amplify geomantic resonance beneath the monastery  
**DC 15 Arcana:** The rituals are designed to strengthen Varnor Earthcaller's communion with the buried altar

These notes connect Caidax to the deeper Earth Temple excavation operation at the Sepulchre-Priory — a separate thread from the false-flag campaign, but proof that his activities extend beyond surface-level political sabotage.

 

##### Evidence Item 5: Hidden Stone Cache

**Location:** AO-13, Caidax's Chamber — behind a loose stone near the study table  
**Discovery:** DC 14 Investigation to find the loose stone  
**Contents:** Small obsidian focus crystal (ritual component), 25 gp in mixed coin, fragment of a broken Flan stone rune

The Flan rune fragment vibrates faintly when held near deep stone. This is a minor but significant clue — it connects Caidax's geomantic research to the ancient Flan origins of the site and hints at forces older than either Heironeous or the Earth Temple.

 

##### DM Notes: Building the Evidence Chain

The evidence items above form a layered discovery path. Not every party will find everything, and the order of discovery matters for narrative impact.

**Minimum viable evidence:** The Slikkina letter alone is sufficient to expose the false-flag operation. A party that searches Caidax's chest and reads the letter has everything they need to bring the conspiracy to the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md), the gnomish Free Alliance, or the authorities in Verbobonc.

**Maximum evidence chain:** Letter + Scroll of Veil + interrogation of Caidax or [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) = irrefutable proof that implicates:

*   The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) (through [Romag](romag-person.md) and the Scroll's creation)
*   Clan Jillink (through the letter's authorship and seal)
*   [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) (through the "Lak" reference)
*   The Nulb recruitment pipeline (through the Waterside Hostel reference)

**What happens when the evidence reaches the right hands:**

*   **Mounted Borderers:** Commander [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) will dispatch patrols to verify the raiding sites described in the letter. If the party delivers the Scroll of Veil as proof, the Borderers treat the party as trusted allies — this unlocks future cooperation and intelligence sharing
*   **Gnomish Free Alliance / [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md):** Presenting the Slikkina letter to gnomish authorities triggers a political crisis within the Kron Hills. Clan Jillink faces investigation. [Gimble Jillink](gimble-jillink-person.md) may be drawn into the fallout — either as an unwitting victim of his clan's activities or as a potential ally against them
*   **Verbobonc Authorities:** Presenting evidence of the false-flag campaign to the Viscounty restores confidence in the gnomish alliance and shifts suspicion toward the Temple — but also alerts Temple spies within the city that the operation has been compromised

**If Caidax is captured alive:** He does not break easily. Caidax views himself as a reformer, not a criminal. Persuasion DC 18 or Intimidation DC 16 to extract operational details. He will confirm the false-flag campaign under sufficient pressure but will not name Romag or the Temple hierarchy — his loyalty to the cause is ideological, not mercenary. Zone of Truth (DC 14 CHA save, +6) is difficult to force; Detect Thoughts (DC 14 WIS save, +0) is the more reliable magical option and surfaces images of the hidden valley, Maris drilling raiders, and the green wax seal of Clan Jillink.

**If Caidax adapts:** If the party disrupts the false-flag campaign but Caidax escapes, he abandons the gnomish impersonation strategy and pivots to a new approach — direct provocation using Earth Temple assets disguised as [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md), shifting the blame target from gnomes to druids. The Scroll of Veil would need to be recharged or a new illusion device created, buying the party time to track him before operations resume.
