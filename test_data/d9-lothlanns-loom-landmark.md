D9 Lóthlann’s Loom
==================

_Elven Tailor • Whisper-Needle Atelier • Cloth-Sorcery of the Dawn_

An upscale elven tailor-shop where torn travel-garments are made whole with uncanny speed, and where subtle embroideries carry quiet, discoverable magics—perfect for restoring disguised PCs after Nulb, and for seeding elven mystery in a practical, DM-friendly way.

##### **Approach Lóthlann’s Loom**

A narrow frontage of pale wood and green glass gleameth beneath lantern-light. Over the door hangs no gaudy sign—only a single silver thread wound about a carved spindle, and a small bell that never ringeth twice alike. Within, the air is warm with cedar and clean linen. Bolts of cloth stand like silent soldiers: grey as moonmist, green as leaf-shadow, white as frost on bough. Needles rest in open trays as if they were jewelled daggers, and a loom in the back chamber singeth softly—thrumm, thrumm—though no hand toucheth it. A slender elf looks up from a half-finished cloak, eyes bright as candle-flame reflected in still water.

### Exterior & Approach

Lóthlann’s Loom sits where the Elven Dawn meeteth the merchant flow—close enough that travelers may find it, yet tucked off the main cobbles so that the curious do not linger long. A small vestibule screens the interior from street sight; a warded curtain of sheer gauze shifts like breath when folk pass beneath it.

**Street Impression:** Those who enter with bloodstains and torn hems are not mocked. They are simply… _seen_, and the door is shut gently behind them.

* * *

#### Interior (Key Areas)

**1) The Display Gallery  
**Mannequins of carved wood (faceless, elegant) wear seasonal elven cuts: layered silks, fitted tunics, canopy-cloaks, and travel garb meant to vanish into forest shade. Each piece bears faint embroidery—leaf veins, star knots, river-lines—subtle enough to look merely decorative.

**2) The Measuring Alcove  
**A curtained nook with a low stool and a polished mirror of dark glass. The mirror shows not only how cloth falleth, but how posture betrays disguise.

**3) The Whisper-Loom Room  
**A loom of pale ipt wood, lightning-scarred, with shuttles that slide as if guided by unseen fingers. Thread spools are labeled in elegant elvish script; some are dyed with crushed elderberry, some with powdered mica, some with ground petal-salts from Ash Horn Stream.

**4) The Stitch-Book Cabinet (Secrets)  
**A locked cabinet holds pattern-books and client ledgers. Names are written in code—metaphors and seasons rather than titles.

* * *

#### Political & Cultural Relevance

*   **Diplomatic Necessity:** Celene visitors cannot afford shabby appearances; garments are status, language, and oath.
*   **Conclave Utility:** The shop is a discreet intelligence node. Who cometh with torn sleeves and why? Who requesteth hoods meant to hide ears?
*   **Quiet Resistance:** Lóthlann’s work subtly pushes back against crude human fashion norms—reminding the Enclave who they are.

* * *

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   Repair torn/bloodstained travel clothes after Nulb (disguise recovery).
*   Commission elven disguises for city intrigue (or to pass as Enclave-affiliated).
*   Seek subtle magical garments that feel “alive” rather than “loot.”

##### If They Linger

*   Lóthlann begins asking questions that reveal the PCs’ secrets without ever sounding accusatory.
*   A Conclave messenger enters for a “routine fitting,” and the room grows watchful.

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A rival tailors’ guild (human) attempts to poach clients or spread rumor that the Loom hides “witch-thread.”
*   A noble client arrives masked and panicked—seeking repairs that hide evidence.
*   Someone tries to steal the Stitch-Book Cabinet.

#### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (8)

1.  A cloak repaired here once bore a **Temple sigil** stitched on the inside—not seen unless the lining is turned.
2.  Syllen whispers that certain thread colors are reserved for **Conclave operations**—and someone has bought them in bulk.
3.  Lóthlann keeps a box of “unclaimed scraps” from dangerous clients—proof of crimes, if pressed.
4.  A courier requests a hood that “hideth ears and conscience alike.”
5.  The ipt loom was lightning-sundered; on storm nights, it weaves **without hands**.
6.  A visiting elf from the High Forest seeks a pattern “lost since the old wars.”
7.  A Red Don steward asks for hush-linings for balcony service—so no server overhears what should not be heard.
8.  A Strider requests travel garb that resists dust and road-grit—hinting at a long, secret route.

#### Optional 5e-Friendly Mechanics (All Optional)

*   **Respectful Patronage:** If the party behaves with courtesy and discretion, grant **advantage on one Persuasion check** with Enclave NPCs within the next tenday (“They were spoken well of at the Loom.”).
*   **Ward-Thread Alarm (rare):** A commissioned cloak can be given a single-use stitch that warms when a specific symbol (Temple rune, spindle, crescent) comes within 30 feet. Costs **25 gp** and a day.

##### Lóthlann Vaelith, “Whisper-Needle”

(Master Tailor)

**Appearance:** Tall, spare, hands always clean; eyes like silvered moss under moonlight.  
**Manner:** Polite, indirect, never hurried. He asketh questions that sound like compliments and answer like needles.  
**Motivations:**

*   Preserve elven dignity in a human city
*   Keep Enclave visitors presentable (and unknowable)
*   Collect small truths through small services

**Secrets:**

*   Lóthlann sometimes weaves **ward-threads** into garments for Conclave use—subtle alarms, not battle-magic.
*   He has repaired cloaks for folk he should not have… and keeps their torn scraps in a box like evidence.

##### Dialogue (DM-friendly; keep separate)

**Lóthlann:** _“Thy cloak hath seen rough roads. Tell me—was it the road that tore it, or the hand upon the road?”_

**Lóthlann:** _“Disguise is not cloth alone. It is gait, breath, and the manner of thy looking.”_

**Lóthlann:** _“I can mend the hem. I cannot mend what thou hast done whilst wearing it.”_

**Lóthlann:** _“Speak not names in my shop. Speak seasons. I shall understand.”_

(Assistant Seamstress; Gnarley-blooded)

##### Whisper-Stitch Enchantments

(Minor, Discoverable Magics)

These are **not** loud “+1 items.” They are _fey curiosities_—perfect as soft rewards.

**Whisper-Stitches are hidden** (DCs below are optional and can be replaced by pure roleplay discovery).

##### Sample Enchanted Garments

(Pick 1–3 to stock)

1.  **Iptleaf Glow Embroidery (Tunic or sash)  
    **Leaf motifs give off a faint silvery sheen when within **100 yards of an ipt tree**.  
    _Discovery:_ faint glow noticed in dim light; Arcana/Investigation DC 12.
2.  **Dew-Thread Hem (Cloak or skirt)  
    **Cloth never truly soaks through; water beads and slides away, leaving the wearer merely cool and clean.  
    _Discovery:_ after rain, wearer stays dry at seams.
3.  **Moon-Hush Lining (Hood or scarf)  
    **Once per night, when the wearer draws the hood and holds still, their breath makes no visible mist and their voice softens to a near-whisper for one minute.  
    _Discovery:_ stealth scene; no combat advantage unless you grant it.
4.  **Elderberry Courtesy Stitch (gloves)  
    **When offering open hands in greeting, the gloves warm slightly and the wearer gains a faintly “well-bred” poise—advantage on one **Persuasion** check per tenday in elven social settings.  
    _Discovery:_ social scene; the glove warms at introductions.
5.  **Ash Horn Petal Pocket (cloak)  
    **A hidden inner pocket preserves any petal placed within, keeping it fresh for a season.  
    _Discovery:_ petals don’t wilt; ties to D7.
6.  **Thread of True North (belt sash)  
    **The embroidery subtly tugs at the waist when facing north in open air.  
    _Discovery:_ travel; Survival DC 12 to notice it’s consistent.

**DM Note:** Whisper-Stitches are meant to create _mystery and texture_. If your players love them, you can scale up later (e.g., a stitch that reacts to elemental taint, Temple sigils, or fey crossings).
