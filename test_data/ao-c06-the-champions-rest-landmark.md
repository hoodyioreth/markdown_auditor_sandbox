AO-C06. The Champions' Rest
===========================

> The Champions' Rest — the tomb-hall of the Azure Oath's most distinguished warriors, sealed behind the Seal of the Champions in the Founders' Sepulchre (**AO-C05**). A rectangular chamber forty feet long and twenty feet wide, housing twelve oversized burial alcoves — six per wall — each large enough to hold a knight in full armor with weapon, shield, and commemorative plaque. These are not founders and not rank-and-file. These are the order's exceptional — knights whose service was extraordinary enough to earn individual recognition in perpetuity. The wards here are middle-era consecrations (100–450 CY), strong but not immune: DC 18 to force, 4d6 radiant on breach, sealed with lightning-scored stone slabs that crackle with residual divine energy when touched. Four named champions anchor the hall — Sir Aldric the Steadfast, Dame Koressa Stormmantle, Sir Draven Hollowmere, and Sir Brannock Ironvow — each entombed with a signature relic that doubles as a key. The south wall holds a great stone door to **AO-C07**, the Knight-Commander's Vault, locked by the Proving of the Worthy: four relic-receptacles carved into the door, each depicting a champion's defining deed. Place the correct relic in the correct receptacle, and the door opens. Place the wrong one, and the hall reminds you that these dead earned their rest. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has never entered this room. The champions sleep undisturbed.

_AO-C06. The Champions' Rest — Tomb-Hall of the Distinguished Dead — Zone C, Warded Wing_

* * *

### The Champions' Rest

The riddle-lock door from **AO-C05** swings inward and the passage beyond is short — ten feet of warded corridor, the walls carved with alternating lightning bolts and longswords in low relief, the floor a single slab of polished limestone worn smooth by centuries of processional boots. The air is colder here than in the Founders' Sepulchre. 45°F and dropping. The ward-glow does not follow from **AO-C05** — the corridor has its own light. Every lightning bolt carved into the walls pulses with a faint blue-white radiance, each bolt firing in sequence down the corridor's length like a slow heartbeat of voltage running through stone. The effect is rhythmic, hypnotic, and deliberate. The masons built this corridor as a threshold — a transition from the sacred stillness of the founders' chamber into something more martial, more charged, more _alive_ with purpose.

The Champions' Rest is not a sepulchre. It is a barracks for the honoured dead.

The room opens at the corridor's end — rectangular, forty feet long and twenty feet wide, with a flat ceiling ten feet high. The proportions are military: clean, functional, without the dome or the circular reverence of **AO-C05**. The walls are dressed pale limestone, the same stone as the rest of the warded wing, but here the masons carved a continuous frieze running the full perimeter at shoulder height — a procession of armoured knights in various acts of service. Marching. Fighting. Praying. Falling. Being carried home. The frieze is not decorative. It is a historical record rendered in stone, depicting specific engagements from the Azure Oath's five centuries of service along the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) border.

The corridor widens into a long rectangular hall lit by its own ward-light — blue-white energy that pulses through carved lightning bolts set into every surface. The walls are lined with burial alcoves, six on each side, larger than anything you have seen in the crypts above. Each alcove is sealed with a stone slab crackling with faint arcs of static energy — tiny lightning threads that jump between carved runes on the slab surface, perpetual and restless, like the dead behind them never quite settled into stillness. A continuous stone frieze runs the walls at shoulder height: armoured knights marching, fighting, falling, being carried. Between each alcove, a bronze plaque is mounted at eye level, green with age but legible. The air smells of ozone and cold stone. At the far end of the hall, a great stone door fills the south wall — and on its surface, four empty recesses surround a carved figure of a knight-commander with arms raised, waiting.

### The Middle-Era Wards

The twelve champion alcoves are sealed with middle-era consecrations — wards laid down between 100 and 450 CY, during the Azure Oath's centuries of active service. These are not the immune, absolute barriers of the founding-era wards in **AO-C05**. They are strong, purposeful, and martial — wards designed by soldier-priests who expected that someday, someone might need to open these tombs, and who built the seals to test worthiness rather than enforce permanence.

##### Ward Appearance

Each champion's slab is carved from pale limestone and inscribed with a border of interlocking lightning bolts — the same structural motif used on the gallery doorframes, but denser, more intricate, each bolt flowing into the next in an unbroken circuit around the slab's edge. The lightning-bolt border is **active**. Faint arcs of blue-white static energy jump continuously between the carved bolts — small, sharp, visible — crackling with a sound like distant thunder compressed into a whisper. The arcs intensify when a living creature comes within five feet of the slab. They are brightest at the slab's center, where the knight's name and inscription are carved.

*   **Detect Magic:** Strong abjuration on every slab. The wards are individually powered — each slab maintains its own consecration independent of the others and independent of the Consecration Anchor Stone in **AO-C07**. Damaging or destroying one slab does not affect the others
*   **Visual ward strength:** The intensity of the lightning arcs correlates roughly to the ward's age and power. The oldest slabs (100–200 CY) crackle with dense, bright arcs. The newest (400–450 CY) have thinner, more intermittent arcs. All are functional. None have degraded to the point of failure

##### Ward Mechanics

*   **Touching a slab:** The lightning arcs jump to the hand — a sharp sting, no damage, but an unmistakable warning. The arcs intensify and the whisper-thunder grows louder. A Religion DC 12 check identifies this as a consecrated deterrent, not a lethal trap — the ward is discouraging casual contact, not punishing it
*   **Forcing a slab open:** Requires a DC 18 Strength check using silvered or adamantine tools. Standard iron or steel tools are repelled — the ward generates a magnetic-like force that pushes non-consecrated metal away from the slab surface. Bare hands cannot grip the slab at all once the ward is active. On a successful check, the slab grinds open six inches and the ward discharges: **4d6 radiant damage** to every creature within 5 feet (WIS DC 14 to halve). The discharge manifests as a burst of lightning arcs — a single violent crack of blue-white energy that leaps from the slab to every nearby surface, scorching the floor and briefly illuminating the entire hall. After the discharge, the ward is spent — the slab can be slid open fully with a DC 10 Strength check
*   **Cleric or Paladin of** [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)**:** May place both hands on the slab and speak any prayer of the Arch-Paladin. The lightning arcs part around the cleric's hands — flowing around them without contact, like water dividing at a stone. The ward recognises the faith. The slab slides open with a soft grinding sound, no damage, no resistance. The cleric feels a brief pulse of warmth — acknowledgment, not reward
*   **Halven Sere:** **Halven** is not a cleric. He is a lay custodian. But his decades of prayer and devotion have impressed themselves into his hands — the ward recognises him by accumulated faith, not by class or ordination. He opens slabs without damage but with effort: the slab resists for a moment, then yields. He breathes hard afterward. _"I am not ordained. But I have been here longer than most who were."_
*   **Halven's holy symbol (from AO-15):** A character carrying **Halven**'s holy symbol can press it to a slab. The ward recognises the custodian's authority — the slab opens without resistance or damage, exactly as if **Halven** himself had touched it. The holy symbol glows warm during the process
*   **Dispel Magic:** A _Dispel Magic_ cast at 4th level or higher suppresses a single slab's ward for 10 minutes. During suppression, the lightning arcs go dark and the slab can be opened with a DC 12 Strength check, no damage. The ward reactivates after the duration — if the slab has been opened, the ward re-seals the gap, grinding the slab shut with considerable force (DEX DC 14 to avoid 2d6 bludgeoning if a hand or arm is in the gap)

### The Four Named Champions

### The Four Named Champions

Four of the twelve champion alcoves belong to knights whose deeds were exceptional even among the exceptional — the warriors whose stories define what the Azure Oath stood for. Their alcoves are the four closest to the south wall door, two on each side, flanking the passage to **AO-C07**. Their commemorative plaques are larger than the others, and their slabs bear the strongest middle-era wards — the lightning arcs crackle with audible intensity, throwing small blue-white sparks onto the floor.

Each named champion was interred with a **signature relic** — an item so closely associated with their defining deed that it became inseparable from their story. These relics are the keys to the Proving of the Worthy, the puzzle-lock on the great door to **AO-C07**. The party must retrieve all four relics and place them in the correct receptacles on the door.

##### Sir Aldric the Steadfast (East Wall, Position 6 — Nearest to South Door)

_"Every morning. Without fail."_

Aldric served the Azure Oath for forty years — the longest continuous service of any knight in the chapter's history. He was not the strongest fighter, not the most gifted tactician, not the most inspired leader. He was the most reliable. For forty years he rose before dawn, armed himself, walked the priory walls, prayed at the chapel, and reported for duty. He never missed a day. He never asked for leave. He never complained. When younger knights questioned the point of patrolling a road that saw bandits once a season, Aldric said nothing. He was already walking the wall. He died in his sleep at seventy-three, in his cell, in his armour, with his boots on. He had not missed a morning in four decades.

*   **Commemorative plaque:** _"Sir Aldric the Steadfast — Served CY 310 to CY 350. For forty years he walked the wall before dawn, armoured and praying. He never asked why. He never faltered. He died with his boots on and his prayer half-spoken. The wall he walked still stands. — Every morning. Without fail."_
*   **Alcove contents:** Aldric's remains in plate armour (functional — his armour was never ceremonial, and the wards have preserved it). A standard round shield with the Azure Oath device. His **signature relic: a silver holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)** — not ornate, not special in material, but worn paper-thin at the edges from forty years of being gripped during morning prayers. The metal has a faint warmth that never fades. Worth 15 gp as silver. Priceless as devotion
*   **_Dawnwatch_ — +2 Longsword:** Plain, undecorated, the grip worn smooth from decades of daily use. The blade has no ornamentation — no gems, no engravings, no flourishes. It looks like a working soldier's weapon because that is exactly what it was. But the steel holds a consecration laid down by forty years of being drawn at the same hour every morning. **On the wielder's first attack each combat, _Dawnwatch_ deals an additional 1d6 radiant damage** — a single burst of stored dawn-light released on the first swing, representing Aldric's readiness. The effect resets after a short or long rest. The blade is faintly warm to the touch at dawn, cold at all other times
*   **Amulet of the Vigilant:** A flat bronze disc on a leather cord, worn beneath Aldric's gorget. One side bears the Azure Oath's sigil; the other bears a single word in Old Oeridian: _"Ready."_ The bronze is worn smooth from decades of resting against armoured chest. **While worn, the bearer has advantage on initiative rolls and cannot be surprised**. The amulet pulses with faint warmth at the moment of waking — a subtle alarm that rouses the wearer to full alertness instantly. Requires attunement
*   **Ward strength:** Middle-era, 320 CY consecration. Lightning arcs are dense and bright. 4d6 radiant on forced breach. The ward on Aldric's slab is notably stable — no flickering, no variation. Steady as the man it protects

##### Dame Koressa Stormmantle (West Wall, Position 6 — Nearest to South Door)

_"Forward. Always forward."_

Koressa led the Azure Oath's response when ogre raiders descended from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) and besieged the gnome settlement of [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) in 287 CY. The garrison at the priory was understrength — twelve knights and eight men-at-arms against a warband of thirty ogres and their goblin auxiliaries. Koressa did not wait for reinforcements. She mounted, took the Long Road south at a gallop, and hit the ogre flank at dawn with every rider she had. The charge broke the siege in forty minutes. She killed three ogres personally — the third one crushed her horse and shattered her shield arm. She fought the rest of the battle on foot, with her sword in her off-hand, refusing to withdraw until every gnome was accounted for. She survived. Her sword arm never fully healed. She continued to serve for eleven more years, retraining left-handed.

*   **Commemorative plaque:** _"Dame Koressa Stormmantle — Served CY 278 to CY 298. She rode to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) when the ogres came and did not stop riding until the siege was broken. Three ogres fell to her sword. She lost her shield arm and her horse and she did not stop. She retrained. She served. She never once said 'enough.' — Forward. Always forward."_
*   **Alcove contents:** Koressa's remains in plate armour with visible battle damage preserved by the wards — dents, scoring, one pauldron crumpled where the ogre's club struck. A standard round shield (cracked, the shield arm's last stand — mounted on the wall of the alcove, not on the body)
*   **Signature relic — _Stormbreaker_ — +2 Longsword:** Cross-guard shaped like two lightning bolts, the blade nicked and resharpened dozens of times, the grip rewrapped in leather that shows left-handed wear on the lower half. The steel carries a charge — literally. When gripped, the wielder feels a vibration in the pommel like a horse at full gallop, and the faintest crackle of static arcs between the lightning-bolt cross-guard tines. **Against Large or larger creatures, _Stormbreaker_ deals an additional 1d6 lightning damage** — the blade discharges on impact with a sharp crack and a flash of blue-white light. Koressa killed ogres with this sword. The sword remembers
*   **Gauntlets of the Charger:** Steel-and-leather riding gauntlets, articulated at the knuckles, worn thin at the reins-grip. The left gauntlet shows heavier wear — Koressa retrained left-handed after her injury, and these are the gauntlets she wore for the remaining eleven years of service. The leather is sweat-darkened and cracked. The steel knuckle-plates bear tiny lightning-bolt engravings. **While worn, when the bearer moves at least 20 feet in a straight line immediately before making a melee attack, that attack deals an additional 1d8 damage** (same damage type as the weapon). The gauntlets feel warm when the wearer is moving and cold when standing still. Koressa never stood still. Requires attunement
*   **Ward strength:** Middle-era, 298 CY consecration. Lightning arcs on this slab are erratic — bright crackling bursts followed by moments of dimness, then another surge. The ward mirrors Koressa's nature: aggressive, unpredictable, and relentless

##### Sir Draven Hollowmere (East Wall, Position 5 — Second from South Door)

_"Brought home."_

Draven was killed in a gnoll ambush on the Long Road in 389 CY — an ordinary patrol, an ordinary day, and then the treeline erupted with javelins. Two knights died instantly. Draven took a javelin through the gap between breastplate and gorget and fell from his horse. He was twenty-six years old and had served four years. He was not exceptional. His entry in the chapter rolls notes him as "competent, reliable, well-liked." What earned him a place in the Champions' Rest was what happened after he died. His squire, a boy of fifteen named Tommen Ashward, dragged Draven's body from the road under gnoll arrow fire, loaded him onto a horse, and rode twelve miles back to the priory without stopping, arriving at the gates at midnight with gnoll arrows still embedded in the saddlebags. When the Knight-Commander asked why he had not left the body and fled — which would have been faster and safer — Tommen said: _"He would have brought me home."_ The Azure Oath interred Draven among the champions not for how he fought but for what his death revealed about the brotherhood he inspired. Tommen Ashward went on to become a knight himself and served thirty years.

*   **Commemorative plaque:** _"Sir Draven Hollowmere — Served CY 385 to CY 389. He fell on the Long Road to gnoll javelins. His squire carried him twelve miles through the night because Draven would have done the same. The measure of a knight is not how he dies but what he taught the living. — Brought home."_
*   **Alcove contents:** Draven's remains in plate armour, the javelin damage still visible at the gorget — a ragged hole in the metal, preserved exactly as the wound was received. A standard longsword (functional, unexceptional)
*   **Signature relic — _Hollowmere's Aegis_ — +2 Round Shield:** Bears the Azure Oath's fist-and-lightning device on the face, the surface scarred with three gnoll javelin impacts — two deflected, one that punched through and was hammered back out, the hole patched with a rough iron disc by a field smith. The shield is heavy, dented, and has clearly saved lives. **While wielding _Hollowmere's Aegis_, when an ally within 5 feet of the bearer takes damage, the bearer may use their reaction to reduce that damage by 1d8+2**. The shield glows with faint warmth when positioned between a threat and an ally — it knows what it was made for. The iron patch over the javelin hole is always warm to the touch. Requires attunement
*   **Helm of the Faithful Return:** An open-faced steel helm with cheek-guards and a nasal bar, dented at the crown where a blow glanced off during the ambush that killed Draven. The interior is lined with padded leather bearing a small embroidered Azure Oath device — stitched by hand, likely by Draven himself. **While worn, the bearer automatically succeeds on death saving throws** — they stabilise at the end of their next turn after dropping to 0 hit points. The helm cannot prevent instant death effects or damage that exceeds the death threshold, but a bearer wearing it will always be brought back from the edge. You always get brought home. Requires attunement
*   **Ward strength:** Middle-era, 390 CY consecration. Lightning arcs on Draven's slab are soft — the gentlest of the four named champions. The arcs flow in slow, even patterns rather than crackling. The ward does not deter. It grieves

##### Sir Brannock Ironvow (West Wall, Position 5 — Second from South Door)

_"He did not step back."_

Brannock held the Long Road bridge south of the priory alone for six minutes against a hill giant in 421 CY — enough time for the refugee column behind him to cross and reach the priory gates. The giant was twelve feet tall, carrying an uprooted oak as a club. Brannock was five foot nine in plate armour, with a mace and a prayer. The bridge was eight feet wide. The giant could not get past him. Every time it swung, Brannock stepped into the blow, deflecting off-centre with his shield and striking the giant's knee with his mace. He broke the joint on the fourth strike. The giant fell. Brannock did not step back — not once, not a single inch, the entire fight. The refugees behind him watched a man who weighed less than the giant's arm refuse to give ground because ground given was lives lost. Brannock survived the fight but died two days later from internal injuries — the giant's blows had broken ribs that punctured his lungs. His last words were: _"Is the road clear?"_ It was. His spectral form still guards the gallery in **AO-C02**, replaying his stand in perpetual echo.

*   **Commemorative plaque:** _"Sir Brannock Ironvow — Served CY 410 to CY 421. He held the bridge alone against a hill giant for six minutes so that thirty-seven people could cross. The giant broke his body. It could not break his ground. He died two days later with the road clear behind him. — He did not step back."_
*   **Alcove contents:** Brannock's remains in plate armour — visibly deformed. The breastplate is caved inward on the left side where the giant's club connected. The metal is buckled, cracked along the weld lines, and dark with old blood that the wards preserved rather than cleansed. A standard round shield, splintered nearly in half (mounted on the alcove wall — it did not survive the fight)
*   **Signature relic — _Ironvow_ — +2 Mace:** Inscribed with the Azure Oath's sigil on the striking head, the weapon Brannock used to break the hill giant's knee. The head is dented at the striking face from the impact against giant bone — a depression in the consecrated steel that the chapter armourer refused to hammer out, calling it _"earned geometry."_ The mace is heavier than it looks — not magically, but as if the weight of what it did is part of the metal now. **Against Large or larger creatures, _Ironvow_ deals an additional 1d6 bludgeoning damage** — the impact lands with a bone-deep concussive force that echoes the blow that felled the giant. When gripped, the wielder feels a sensation in the soles of their feet: rootedness, solidity, the absolute refusal to move
*   **Boots of the Unyielding:** Heavy steel-toed infantry boots with reinforced soles, the leather worn through at the balls of the feet from decades of standing watch. Iron studs on the soles are ground flat — Brannock stood on stone for so long the metal conformed to it. The boots smell of road dust and old leather. **While worn, the bearer cannot be moved against their will by any effect — magical or physical — while their feet are on solid ground**. The bearer has advantage on Strength saving throws and ability checks to resist being pushed, pulled, or knocked prone. The boots feel heavier when the wearer tries to retreat and lighter when the wearer advances. Brannock's boots understood that ground held is lives saved. Requires attunement
*   **Ward strength:** Middle-era, 422 CY consecration. Lightning arcs on Brannock's slab are the most intense of the four — thick, bright, crackling with an aggression that the other wards lack. The arcs do not flow in patterns. They _strike_ — sharp, percussive, rhythmic, like a mace hitting a shield. Brannock's ward does not discourage. It challenges
*   **Spectral connection:** Brannock's spectral form in **AO-C02** is not a ghost in the traditional sense — it is a ward-echo, a repeating imprint of his final stand projected by the crypt's consecration network. If the party has already encountered the spectral knight in the gallery, a Religion DC 14 check connects the figure to this alcove. If the party opens Brannock's slab, the spectral form in the gallery flickers and dims for 1d4 hours before restabilising. The echo is tied to the ward, not the body — disturbing the remains does not destroy the echo permanently

### The Eight Unnamed Champions

The remaining eight alcoves contain knights of distinction whose service was honourable and specific but less individually storied than the four named champions. Their alcoves occupy positions 1–4 on each wall (furthest from the south door). Their wards are middle-era consecrations identical in strength to the named champions — 4d6 radiant on forced breach, DC 18 Strength with silvered or adamantine tools. Each alcove contains: consecrated plate armour (functional, preserved by wards), a +1 longsword or +1 mace, a round shield bearing the Azure Oath device, a silver holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) (15 gp), and a personal memento.

*   **East Wall, Position 1 — Dame Vessa Highkeep:** _"She held the tower when the rest had fled."_ Served CY 145–182. Defended the priory's SW tower alone for a full night during a raider assault. Memento: a hand-drawn map of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) border, annotated in her handwriting
*   **East Wall, Position 2 — Sir Colm Brightvow:** _"No debt unpaid."_ Served CY 190–221. Recovered three stolen Heironeous relics from a bandit hold near [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md). Memento: a tally-stick scratched with thirty-one marks — one for each year of service
*   **East Wall, Position 3 — Dame Auris Longwatch:** _"She saw them first."_ Served CY 256–301. The order's most gifted scout — credited with seventeen early warnings that prevented ambushes. Memento: a silver spyglass (functional, 50 gp)
*   **East Wall, Position 4 — Sir Garen Oathsworn:** _"He trained the ones who mattered."_ Served CY 340–395. Weapons master who trained three generations of Azure Oath knights, including Dame Koressa Stormmantle. Memento: a leather-bound training manual with hand-drawn fighting forms (functional — a character who studies it for 8 hours gains proficiency in one martial weapon of their choice, one-time benefit)
*   **West Wall, Position 1 — Sir Edren Halfhelm:** _"Half a face. Whole heart."_ Served CY 112–149. Lost the left side of his face to a troll's claw in his first year. Served thirty-seven more. Memento: a half-helm with the left side rebuilt in mismatched iron — his signature
*   **West Wall, Position 2 — Dame Lyssa Thornguard:** _"The children remembered."_ Served CY 200–238. Escorted seventeen refugee columns safely to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) and Verbobonc during the border conflicts. Memento: a bundle of letters from families she saved, tied with faded blue ribbon
*   **West Wall, Position 3 — Sir Morek Ashward:** _"He came back for them."_ Served CY 402–435. Grandson of Tommen Ashward — the squire who brought Sir Draven Hollowmere home. Morek repeated his grandfather's act: he recovered two fallen knights from an ogre ambush in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), carrying both across his saddle for nine miles. Memento: his grandfather Tommen's original Azure Oath commission letter, preserved in wax
*   **West Wall, Position 4 — Dame Orrith Duskbane:** _"She burned the dark out."_ Served CY 430–458. Destroyed a shadow creature infestation in the priory's cellar using nothing but consecrated torches and determination. Memento: a blackened torch-head mounted on an iron bracket — the last torch she used, burned down to a stub

### The Glyph of Warding — Central Floor

Set into the floor at the hall's exact centre — where a character walking from the north entrance to the south door would naturally step — a Glyph of Warding is inscribed into the flagstone. The glyph is carved as the Azure Oath's sigil (clenched fist and lightning bolt), three feet in diameter, inlaid with silver wire that has tarnished to near-invisibility against the grey stone.

*   **Perception DC 15:** A character actively searching the floor notices the silver inlay — faint lines in the stone that form a recognizable pattern. A character not actively searching who walks directly over the glyph does not notice it unless their passive Perception is 15 or higher
*   **Investigation DC 13:** Identifies the glyph as a defensive ward — a stored divine spell keyed to alignment. The silver inlay pattern (the Azure Oath's sigil) is the trigger-mark
*   **Trigger:** Any creature with an evil alignment that steps on the glyph or passes within 5 feet of its centre. Good and neutral-aligned creatures do not trigger the glyph. It is dormant for them — they walk over it without effect
*   **Effect:** **5d8 radiant damage** (DEX DC 15 to halve), centred on the glyph, 20-foot radius. The detonation manifests as a vertical column of lightning that erupts from the glyph to the ceiling — a ten-foot-tall pillar of blue-white energy that cracks like a thunderclap, scorches the ceiling stone, and fills the hall with ozone. The glyph is expended after one detonation and does not recharge
*   **Disarming:** _Dispel Magic_ at 5th level removes the glyph permanently. A character with thieves' tools cannot disarm a Glyph of Warding — it is a divine ward, not a mechanical trap. The silver inlay can be pried out (Thieves' Tools DC 20), which destroys the glyph but also triggers it
*   **DM note:** The glyph exists as insurance — if the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) ever breached the outer wards, this would be the last line of defence before the Knight-Commander's Vault. A party with no evil-aligned members walks through without incident. If the party includes a character serving the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) or the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — even as a double agent — the glyph fires. This can create an excellent moral dilemma if the party's rogue or warlock has been hiding their true allegiance

### The Proving of the Worthy — Relic Placement Puzzle

The south wall of the Champions' Rest holds a great stone door — ten feet tall, six feet wide, the largest and most elaborately carved door in the warded wing. The door is a single slab of white limestone, three inches thick, set into a frame of the same pale stone used throughout the crypts. The surface is carved in deep relief with a scene that fills the entire door: a **Knight-Commander** standing at the centre, arms raised above the head, palms open and facing outward, wearing full ceremonial plate with the Azure Oath's sigil on the breastplate. The figure is not a portrait of any specific commander — the face is idealised, ageless, neither male nor female. It represents the _office_, not the person.

Surrounding the central figure, four **recessed niches** are carved into the door — one top left of the commander's head, one bottom left at the feet, bottom right, one top right. Each niche is six inches deep and shaped to hold a specific type of object. Each niche is framed by its own carved scene — a small, detailed relief depicting a specific act of heroism. The niches are empty. The scenes are the clues.

##### The Four Niches

##### Puzzle Mechanics — Step by Step

The puzzle requires the party to open the four named champion alcoves, retrieve the four signature relics, read the commemorative plaques, study the four carved scenes on the door, match each relic to its corresponding niche, and place all four. The order of placement does not matter — the puzzle responds to completeness, not sequence.

*   **Placing a correct relic:** The relic slides into the shaped niche with a satisfying mechanical click — the stone grips it firmly. The carved scene surrounding the niche begins to glow with blue-white ward-light, the lightning-bolt border framing the scene igniting in sequence. A low hum joins the room's ambient sound — one note per correct relic. Each successive correct placement adds a harmonic. One relic = a single low tone. Two relics = a chord. Three relics = a resonance that vibrates in the chest. The knight figures in the corresponding carved scene come alive with light — the carved stone glows from within, each figure limned in blue-white radiance as if the ward remembers the deed
*   **Placing the fourth relic — the door opens:** When the fourth relic clicks into place, the harmonic chord resolves into a single sustained note — clear, bright, and resonant enough to be felt in the teeth. All four scenes blaze with light simultaneously. The lightning-bolt borders on all four niches arc toward the central Knight-Commander figure. The arcs converge on the figure's raised palms and race down the arms to the breastplate sigil. The sigil blazes — the clenched fist and lightning bolt burn white-hot in the stone. The Knight-Commander's carved arms slowly lower to the sides. The fist opens. The stone door splits vertically down the centre with a grinding crack, the two halves swinging inward to reveal the passage to **AO-C07**. The sustained note fades. The four relics remain in their niches, glowing steadily
*   **Duration:** The door remains open as long as all four relics are in place. Removing any relic causes the corresponding scene to go dark and that section of the door begins to swing closed — slowly, over thirty seconds, giving time to replace the relic. Removing all four relics closes the door completely and the puzzle resets

##### Wrong Placements

*   **Wrong relic in a niche:** The relic does not click into place — it slides partway in, then the niche gently pushes it back outward with a soft pulse of blue-white light. No damage. The carved scene around the niche flickers once — brighter than normal, as if showing the party the scene more clearly as a hint. The champions do not punish the ignorant. They teach
*   **Non-champion relic in a niche:** Standard weapons, holy symbols, or shields from the unnamed champion alcoves do not fit. The niches are shaped for the specific signature relics — Aldric's paper-thin holy symbol, Koressa's lightning-guard sword, Draven's javelin-scarred shield, Brannock's sigil-inscribed mace. A generic +1 longsword is the wrong dimensions for Koressa's niche. The niches reject non-matching items without activation
*   **Swapping relics between niches:** If the party places a correct relic in the wrong niche — Aldric's holy symbol in the sword niche, for example — the niche pushes it out and the scene flickers brightly. This is the key hint: the scenes flash when a wrong placement is attempted, encouraging the party to study the carvings more carefully

##### Alternative Solutions

*   **Halven Sere present:** **Halven** knows the four champions' stories and recognises the door scenes immediately. He opens the named champion alcoves without ward damage (see ward mechanics above) and places the relics himself, narrating each champion's story as he does — a private funeral oration for warriors he never met but whose deeds he memorised as custodian. This takes ten minutes. He does not rush it
*   **Valegate's prayer book (from AO-C05):** Contains a chapter titled _"The Proving"_ that describes the four champions, their relics, and the door mechanism. A party with the prayer book can solve the puzzle without reading the plaques — the book provides complete instructions
*   **Intelligence bypass:** A character who has read all four commemorative plaques can attempt an Intelligence (Religion) DC 12 or Intelligence (History) DC 14 check to match each plaque to its corresponding door scene without trial and error. Success identifies all four correct pairings. Failure identifies two correctly and requires trial and error for the remaining two
*   **Brute force:** The door is consecrated white limestone — AC 22, HP 150, immunity to non-magical damage, resistance to all magical damage except radiant. Harder to breach than the Seal of the Champions in **AO-C05** — this door protects the holiest room in the crypts. Adamantine tools could breach it in approximately 72 hours of continuous work

### The Sacred Layer

The Champions' Rest is the Azure Oath's service record carved into stone. Where the Founders' Sepulchre honours the beginning and the Knight-Commander's Vault honours the end, this hall honours the middle — the centuries of daily, unglamorous, essential work that made the order matter. Every knight here earned their place through specific acts of courage. Not destiny, not prophecy, not divine selection — just people who did the hard thing when the hard thing needed doing.

*   **The champion selection:** Not every Azure Oath knight was interred here. Most were buried in the gallery alcoves — the rank-and-file dead, honoured but not distinguished. Placement in the Champions' Rest required a formal nomination by the Knight-Commander and unanimous agreement from the senior knights. The standard was high: not just bravery, but bravery that _changed something_. Aldric's devotion set the standard. Koressa's charge saved a settlement. Draven's death inspired a lineage. Brannock's stand saved thirty-seven lives. Each act had consequences beyond the act itself
*   **The Ashward lineage:** Sir Morek Ashward (West Wall, Position 3) is the grandson of Tommen Ashward, the squire who carried Sir Draven home. The two men are buried on opposite walls of the same hall. Tommen's commission letter is in Morek's alcove. The Azure Oath's history is not a list of names — it is a web of relationships, debts, and inheritances. This is what the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is desecrating: not just bones, but memory

### DM Guidance — Running the Room

*   **Pacing:** This room is longer than **AO-C05** — there are twelve alcoves, four detailed named champions, and a multi-step puzzle. Break it into phases. Phase 1: Arrival and exploration (the read-aloud, the frieze, the ward-glow). Phase 2: The plaques (let the players read the stories — they are short and punchy, designed to hit at the table). Phase 3: The door puzzle (study the carved scenes, match to plaques, retrieve relics, place them). Phase 4: The opening
*   **The named champions:** Read the plaque inscriptions at the table. These are written to be spoken aloud. The epitaphs are deliberately short: _"He did not step back."_ Let them land
*   **Opening the alcoves:** The 4d6 radiant ward discharge is a real cost — the party is spending hit points to access the relics. A [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) cleric avoids the damage. **Halven**'s holy symbol avoids the damage. Brute-forcing four alcoves costs 4d6 × 4 = an average of 56 radiant damage distributed across whoever is opening them. For a level 4–5 party, this is significant. The puzzle has a resource cost. Make the party feel it
*   **The relic puzzle:** The puzzle is fair — the plaques tell the stories, the door scenes illustrate the stories, the niches are shaped for specific relics. Experienced players will solve it in minutes. Less experienced players may need hints: the scene-flash on wrong placements is the primary hint mechanism, and **Halven** can narrate the connections. Do not let the puzzle become frustrating — the point is the satisfaction of matching deed to relic, not stumping the players
*   **The door opening:** Play this big. The harmonic chord, the lightning arcs converging on the Knight-Commander figure, the carved arms lowering, the fist opening, the door splitting. This is the third locked door the party has opened in the warded wing (Arch of First Oath, Seal of Champions, Proving of the Worthy). Each has been harder and more elaborate. The payoff should match
*   **Loot considerations:** The four signature relics are needed for the puzzle — they stay in the door while it is open. If the party wants to keep the relics AND access **AO-C07**, they need to prop the door or find another solution. The eight unnamed champion alcoves contain +1 weapons and +1 shields that are NOT needed for the puzzle — these are free loot, gated only by the ward damage cost. A party of five with a cleric could open four unnamed alcoves without any radiant damage (cleric opens them) and gain four +1 weapons or shields. This is appropriate for level 4–5
*   **Halven Sere:** If present, **Halven** treats this room with professional respect rather than the personal grief of **AO-C05**. He did not know these knights. But he memorised their stories. He can recite every plaque from memory. He opens alcoves with the practiced care of a man who has tended graves his entire life. If the party asks who his favourite champion is, he says: _"Aldric. He understood that the work is the point."_
