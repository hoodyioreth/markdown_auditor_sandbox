41\. The Golden Griffin Inn
===========================

> This three-storey stone building accommodates Etterboak’s elite residents, invited guests and visitors who wish to stay longer than one night.   This prestigious Inn is is bustling with merchants, nobles, and well to do. From the outside this house looks nice and traditional. It has been built with tan bricks and has grey brick decorations. Tall, squared windows let in plenty of light and have been added to the house in a mostly symmetric way. The building is rectangular shaped. The house is half surrounded by overgrown wooden overhanging panels. The second floor is the same size as the first, which has been built exactly on top of the floor below it. This floor has roughly the same style as the floor below. The roof is high, triangular and layered and is covered with black roof tiles. Two large chimneys poke out the center of the roof. A few large windows let in just enough light to the rooms below the roof. The house itself is surrounded by a gorgeous garden with many hidden lights that make the garden come to life at night.   Stepping inside the air is filled with the smells of good food and the sounds of pleasant music. Marble floors and columns with large statues and paintings adorn the entrance hall. At a podium a well dressed Maitre d'hotel who obviously oversees the waitpersons and busboys, and who typically handles reservations looks up from his pages, clears his throat politely but firming and asks "Do you have a reservation lords?" Two well armored and armed guards leaning against a pillar glance your way sizing up potential trouble.

The Golden Griffin Inn is an upscale establishment located on the east side of [Etterboek](etterboek-settlement-1.md), next to Merrow Merchant House and at the foot of the Browich Bridge. This three-story stone building caters to Etterboek’s elite residents, invited guests, and visitors who wish to stay longer than one night, providing a luxurious and comfortable stay for all its patrons.

### Historical Background

_Founding and Construction_

*   **Origins:** Built over a century ago, the Golden Griffin Inn was established to accommodate the increasing number of merchants and nobles visiting Etterboek.
*   **Architectural Design:** The inn’s design reflects the traditional aesthetics of the region, with tan bricks and grey brick decorations, creating a blend of elegance and functionality.

**Expansion and Renovations**

*   **Early Renovations:** The inn underwent significant renovations 50 years ago to modernize its facilities while preserving its historical charm.
*   **Recent Upgrades:** In the past decade, the inn added a state-of-the-art alchemical heating system for private baths in each room, designed by the renowned alchemist, Sarri.

#### Key Features

**Exterior Description**

*   **Architecture:** The Golden Griffin Inn is built with tan bricks and grey brick decorations. Tall, squared windows let in plenty of light and are symmetrically placed.
*   **Garden:** The inn is surrounded by a gorgeous garden, illuminated by hidden lights that create a magical atmosphere at night.
*   **Structure:** The building is rectangular with a high, triangular roof covered in black roof tiles. Two large chimneys add to the stately appearance.

Interior Splendor

*   **Entrance Hall:** Guests are greeted by marble floors and columns adorned with large statues and paintings.
*   **Dining Area:** A grand dining hall with a podium for the maître d'hôtel, who manages reservations and oversees the staff.

#### Personality and Politics

The Golden Griffin Inn serves as a microcosm of Etterboek’s social and political dynamics. It is a place where merchants, nobles, and influential figures gather, making it a focal point for discussions and decisions that impact the village and beyond. The inn’s luxurious accommodations and high standards reflect the prosperity and importance of Etterboek within the Viscounty of Verbobonc.

#### Relationships with Lords and Politics

*   [House Langmuir](house-langmuir-organization.md): The inn enjoys a positive relationship with House Langmuir, often hosting their guests and serving as a venue for their events.
*   **Local Governance:** The inn plays a role in local politics by providing a neutral ground for discussions and negotiations among the village’s elite.

#### Conclusion

The Golden Griffin Inn stands as a beacon of luxury and hospitality in Etterboek. Its rich history, elegant accommodations, and central role in the village’s social and political life make it a landmark establishment. Whether for a short stay or an extended visit, the inn provides a welcoming and opulent environment for all its guests.

##### Notable People

*   **Gliskow:** The Maitre d'hotel who oversees the staff and ensures the inn operates smoothly. He is known for his firm but polite demeanor.
*   **Fastolph:** A male halfling entertainer with a modest appearance and a fine stiletto. He is covetous and soft-hearted.
*   **Joyce Cane:** A female human merchant with a round face, curly gray hair, and brown eyes. She is impartial and humble.

##### Services and Amenities

*   **Room Rates:** 2gp per day per person, including meals and animal care.
*   **Specialty Drinks:** The inn offers unique beverages such as the Coup de Grace, Will-o-the-Whiskey, and the Drunken Dwarf.
*   **Recreation:** The two-acre grounds offer space for horse riding, leisure activities, and outdoor games.
