The Velunan Delegation of Rao
=============================

### Overview

The loss of the Gift of Beauty is no mere bandit raid gone ill. It is a wound cut across diplomacy, faith, and honor. The gift was meant to pass southward in solemn trust toward Queen [Yolande, Her Fey Majesty](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) of Celene, borne under the protection of a Raoan delegation and tied to a fragile but meaningful effort at peace between Veluna, Verbobonc, and the elven court. Its disappearance has left too many questions unanswered, too many clerics unaccounted for, and too much room for dangerous speculation.</p>  

For Veluna, this cannot be ignored. The Archclericy does not wish to accuse Verbobonc openly, nor does it wish to alarm Celene before truth is known. Thus a second mission is formed, quieter than the first and better suited to investigation than ceremony. This new delegation is tasked not with pomp, but with discernment. They are to travel to the Viscounty of Verbobonc, establish themselves first at the Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao, gather testimony, trace the route of the lost mission, and recover the Gift if it can still be found.  

This delegation is ideal for campaign use because it carries both legitimacy and vulnerability. It has enough authority to ask hard questions, but not enough force to compel every answer. It needs guides, escorts, local allies, and—if the truth leads toward darker roads—adventurers willing to go where clerks and prelates cannot.

 

### Why This Delegation Exists

Within the Velunan political and religious framework, several powers have reason to support such a mission. The Archclericy must preserve the dignity of Rao and prove that sacred trust still means something in the world. The [The Celestial Circle - Veluna](the-celestial-circle---veluna-organization.md) must prevent the loss from becoming a greater diplomatic rupture. Lady [Elinor Asbury](elinor-asbury-person.md), as Ambassador to the Celestial Circle and one of the strongest living bridges between Veluna and Verbobonc, has every reason to encourage a controlled investigation rather than a public scandal. And in Verbobonc itself, the Viscount and his [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) can ill afford to have Veluna conclude that sacred envoys vanish upon Viscounty roads without consequence.

 

The result is a mission with unusual political weight. It arrives not as an army, nor as an accusation, but as a question that refuses to go away.

### Authority of the Mission

This delegation does not travel east as a mere group of pious envoys or concerned priests. It travels under the direct authority of the Archclericy of Veluna, and more specifically under the sanction of **His Venerable Reverence, Canon [Hazen](hazen-person.md) of Veluna**, shepherd of the faithful and supreme spiritual authority of the realm.

 

Though Canon Hazen rarely appears in public and even more rarely intervenes directly in temporal affairs, the loss of the **[The Gift of Beauty — Quest Master Reference](gob-01-gift-of-beauty-E28094-what-it-is-and-who-wants-it-plot.md)** and the disappearance of the earlier Raoan mission bound toward Queen Yolande of Celene have risen beyond the level of local embarrassment. The matter now touches upon the dignity of Rao, the credibility of Veluna, and the faith between allied powers. Because of this, Hazen and the senior machinery of the **Archclericy of Veluna** have judged that the affair cannot be left to rumor, excuses, or uncertain provincial handling.

 

The delegation has therefore been **hand-picked** by the Archclericy itself. Its members were chosen not merely for holiness, but for discretion, endurance, judgment, and the ability to move between courts, cloisters, roads, and danger. The mission is not ceremonial. It is investigative, diplomatic, and, if required, corrective.

 

This gives the delegation unusual standing. It does not outrank the Viscount within his own lands, nor may it command the clergy of Verbobonc as though they were Velunan subordinates. Yet it carries something nearly as powerful: the formal and undeniable weight of **Canon Hazen’s written concern**. In a world where Rao speaks softly, that concern is no small thing.

 

### The Official Writ of Veluna

The delegation bears a sealed **writ of ecclesiastical and diplomatic authority**, impressed with the authority of the Archclericy and countersigned through the proper channels of Veluna. This writ is intended to be presented first in **Verbobonc City**, either to **Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md)** himself or, more practically, through his chamber and trusted officials, and in parallel to **Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md)** at the **Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao**.

 

A second instruction within the same writ directs the delegation to make its concerns known, if the trail leads southward, to **[Terjon](terjon-person.md)** at the **[20\. Church of St. Cuthbert](20-church-of-st-cuthbert-landmark.md) in the Village of Hommlet**, or to whatever worthy authority there can provide testimony, shelter, or assistance in tracing the vanished mission. This clause is especially important, for it acknowledges that if the Gift and its escort were taken upon the roads, then the truth may lie not in courts but in villages, shrines, waystations, ruined roads, and frightened witnesses.

 

The writ serves several purposes at once:

*   It establishes the delegation as a lawful mission of inquiry rather than wandering suspicion
*   It gives local clergy and secular rulers cause to cooperate without public humiliation
*   It provides a visible symbol of Veluna’s seriousness should anyone attempt to delay, obstruct, or dismiss the investigation
*   It allows the delegation to request records, testimony, escorts, and safe lodging in Raoan houses and allied institutions

Should any lesser official, local noble, road captain, or suspicious reeve attempt to halt the mission, the writ is meant to answer the question before it is fully asked: _By what authority do you travel?_

### Why Canon Hazen Sent This Delegation

Canon [Hazen](hazen-person.md) did not choose this road lightly. He is no restless politician seeking to involve Veluna in every frontier disturbance. His long habit is caution, gravity, and silence until silence is no longer worthy. That is precisely why this mission matters. The loss of the Gift of Beauty is not merely a failed delivery. It is a tear in the fabric of trust between Veluna, Verbobonc, and Celene. A sacred object entrusted to diplomacy has vanished. Raoan representatives tied to that effort have been lost, broken, or scattered. And if hostile hands lie behind the affair, then those hands may already be testing how much good realms truly see of one another’s pain.  

For Hazen, three concerns stand above the rest:

*   **The honor of Rao** — sacred trust must not be seen to perish unanswered on the roads of allied lands
*   **The stability of allied diplomacy** — Celene must not conclude that Veluna and Verbobonc are careless in matters of solemn peace
*   **The deeper shadow beneath the event** — if the raid was more than banditry, then the affair may mark the movement of darker powers best faced early rather than late

Thus the mission has been formed under the quiet doctrine that truth must be recovered before blame hardens into faction, and before silence becomes permission for evil to move again.

### Orders of the Delegation

**Primary Orders**

*   Travel to Verbobonc City and establish contact with the Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao
*   Confirm the last verified movements of the original Gift of Beauty convoy
*   Identify surviving witnesses, missing clergy, escorts, and recovered property
*   Determine whether the Gift was stolen for ransom, sabotage, cult activity, or political disruption
*   Recover the Gift of Beauty if possible and secure it under Raoan authority

 

**Secondary Orders**

*   Avoid publicly humiliating the Viscount unless proof of negligence or complicity is undeniable
*   Do not provoke Celene with rumor or accusation before evidence is gathered
*   Report all findings back to Veluna through secure clerical channels
*   Seek allies in Verbobonc, but trust slowly

 

**Unspoken Orders**

*   Measure the true condition of Verbobonc’s roads, churches, and political will
*   Observe whether darker forces are again moving near Hommlet, the Moathouse, or the old Temple roads
*   Determine whether this was merely theft—or the first sign of something larger returning

### Delegation Roster

##### 1\. Father Caldrin Voss

**Role:** Delegation Leader and Lead Investigator

**Race:** Human (Oeridian)

**Class Feel:** Cleric of [Rao](rao-person.md), senior but field-capable

 

Father Caldrin is a thin, silver-haired cleric in his late fifties whose gentleness hides a hard and patient mind. He was not chosen because he is politically dazzling, but because he is difficult to mislead. He has spent years mediating disputes between diocesan authorities, trade houses, and noble estates, and he has learned that lies often sound most convincing when spoken politely.  

**Personality:** Calm, observant, unfailingly courteous, hard to rattle.

**Motivation:** To recover the Gift without letting the matter spiral into open mistrust between good realms.

**Objective:** Establish the truth, preserve Rao’s dignity, and keep the delegation unified. <p\]**Backstory:** Once served in Whitehale near Mitrik handling pilgrimage disputes and sensitive Church investigations. He has seen enough politics to know that sacred language and ambition often travel together.  

**DM Use:** He is the delegation’s moral center and chief patron figure. He can commission the party, brief them, and authorize discreet investigations.

* * *

##### 2\. Sister Maelin Harrow

**Role:** Keeper of Records, Witness Statements, and Sacred Custody

**Race:** Human (Flan)

**Class Feel:** Cleric or devout scholar-attendant of Rao  

Sister Maelin is in her early thirties, precise of dress and sharper of memory. She records everything. Names, road conditions, dates, gestures, contradictions, and omissions all find their way into her ledgers. Many mistake her for meek because she speaks softly. They often discover too late that she remembers exactly what they said three days before and can repeat it back without error.  

**Personality:** Quiet, meticulous, skeptical, patient with facts and impatient with carelessness.

**Motivation:** To build a record that no liar can overturn.

**Objective:** Create an evidentiary chain strong enough to survive scrutiny from Veluna, Verbobonc, and the Celestial Circle. <p\]**Backstory:** Former archival scribe attached to ecclesiastical courts near Veluna City. This is her first mission beyond the safer roads of the heartland.  

**DM Use:** She is the perfect NPC for clue management. She can summarize evidence, notice inconsistencies, and quietly become a target if enemies realize how much she knows.

* * *

##### 3\. Sir Dain Verrick

**Role:** Sword of the Delegation / Escort Commander

**Race:** Human (Suloise)

**Class Feel:** Paladin of [Rao](rao-person.md) or Knightly Protector  

Sir Dain is broad-shouldered, grave, and younger than most expect for a man carrying such visible discipline. He is not loud, nor theatrical, nor eager for battle, but he has the bearing of one who has stood his ground before worse things than thieves. He was assigned because the Church expects questions, and questions of this sort often draw blades.  

**Personality:** Steady, protective, suspicious of easy charm, deeply loyal once trust is earned.

**Motivation:** To ensure the delegation reaches its destination alive and returns with honor intact.

**Objective:** Protect Father Caldrin, assess threats, and determine whether the enemy is mundane or cultic.

**Backstory:** Served on dangerous road escorts between Whitehale and Falsridge before taking formal vows. He has seen what organized raiders look like and knows the difference between hunger and malice.

 

**DM Use:** He is your frontline ally, but also a quiet warning that this mission may already be more dangerous than a polite investigation should be.

* * *

##### 4\. Brother Oren Vale

**Role:** Local Liaison to Verbobonc and the Monastery of Rao

**Race:** Human (Oeridian)

**Class Feel:** Junior cleric or monk of Rao

 

Brother Oren has spent time in and around Verbobonc before and knows enough of its rhythms to keep the delegation from appearing completely green. He knows which inns are safe, which priests are reliable, and which streets in the city tell truths by their silences. He is younger than the others and still slightly idealistic, which makes him useful—and vulnerable.  

**Personality:** Earnest, diplomatic, eager to prove useful, quietly anxious about failure.

**Motivation:** To prove he is worthy of the trust placed in him.

**Objective:** Introduce the delegation to the Monastery, open doors in the city, and smooth relations with local clergy and officials. <p\]**Backstory:** Studied briefly at the Monastery of the Reverent Brothers and retained connections there. He volunteered for this mission before he understood how serious it was.  

**DM Use:** Excellent for introducing locations, rumors, and local contacts. Also a good emotional lens for showing how intimidating high politics can feel from below.

#### 5\. Optional Player Character Slot — Cleric or Paladin of Rao

**Role:** Special Envoy, Junior Investigator, or Knight-Attached Observer

 

One of the best uses of this delegation is to make one member of it a player character. A cleric or paladin of Rao from Veluna can be attached to the mission for a variety of reasons: they may be promising but untested, sent to gain experience; personally tied to the original lost delegation; recommended by Elinor Asbury or another trusted Velunan figure; or chosen precisely because they know how to move among ordinary folk rather than simply speak with bishops and nobles.  

**Why this works as a PC hook:**

*   It gives the player immediate purpose and institutional legitimacy
*   It provides a believable reason to investigate roads, ruins, churches, and nobles alike
*   It naturally leads them to recruit help from adventurers in Verbobonc or Hommlet
*   It ties personal faith to the wider politics of the campaign without overwhelming the party

 

**Recommended PC concept angles:**

*   A young paladin who believes the mission is purely righteous—until politics complicate it
*   A cleric-scribe or investigator more interested in truth than ceremony
*   A rural Raoan priest from [Diocese of Whitehale](diocese-of-whitehale-organization.md) or [Diocese of Kempton](diocese-of-kempton-organization.md) who has never seen the rougher roads of the east
*   A knightly envoy who admires Veluna but gradually sees its compromises

### How the Delegation Enters the Campaign

The delegation arrives first in the City of Verbobonc and makes formal contact at the [A1 Monastery of the Reverent Brothers Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md). From there, the investigation can widen in several natural directions depending on what kind of campaign opening you want:</p>

*   **Urban Beginning:** The delegation needs discreet local help in Verbobonc to follow witnesses, inspect records, or question suspicious merchants and teamsters.
*   **Road Investigation:** Evidence points south or east, and the party is asked to retrace the convoy’s route, identify the ambush site, or find a surviving escort.
*   **Hommlet Connection:** Rumors suggest traffic in relics, captives, or stolen valuables has moved toward Hommlet, Nulb, or the old Temple roads.
*   **Monastic Hook:** The Monastery itself becomes the safe base from which the party receives new leads, healing, and political cover.

This makes the delegation ideal as a soft opening to the campaign. It gives players a legitimate mission, a credible patron, and a reason to explore both cities and wilderness without feeling like they are simply wandering until the plot appears.

### Chain of Contact in the Viscounty

The delegation’s intended line of approach is deliberate, and a Dungeon Master should understand it as such. Veluna does not wish to insult the Viscount by treating his realm as lawless, nor does it wish to become trapped in court ceremony while the trail grows cold. Therefore the mission proceeds through **parallel channels**: secular, ecclesiastical, and practical.  

**Primary Contacts**

*   **Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md)x** — or those empowered to receive formal diplomatic concern on his behalf
*   **Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md)** — the most natural Raoan authority in Verbobonc through whom Veluna may act with legitimacy and restraint
*   **The [A1 Monastery of the Reverent Brothers Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md)** — base of inquiry, prayer, record, and recovery

 

**Secondary Contacts**

*   **[Terjon](terjon-person.md) in Hommlet** — especially if the trail of the original mission or its attackers bends south toward the old Temple roads
*   **Lady [Elinor Asbury](elinor-asbury-person.md)** — if diplomacy between Veluna and Verbobonc requires a noble intermediary already trusted by the Celestial Circle
*   **Any reliable local escort, adventuring company, or monastery contact** who can assist without turning the inquiry into public theater

This structure gives the DM flexibility. The delegation can begin formally in Verbobonc City, then broaden naturally into roads, villages, and old danger points without losing legitimacy.

### Personalities in Motion

The delegation works best when it is not a static quest-giver but a living group with internal texture:

*   Father Caldrin wants truth without scandal.
*   Sister Maelin wants proof that will hold up under scrutiny.
*   Sir Dain suspects the mission is already being watched.
*   Brother Oren wants to believe the good realms are still worthy of trust.

These differing instincts let you create meaningful table moments. Caldrin may prefer caution where Dain wants action. Maelin may insist on verification while Oren wants to trust an apparently honest witness. A player character inside the delegation can become the hinge between those temperaments and the wider adventuring party.

### Likely Allies in Verbobonc

*   **Lady Elinor Asbury:** Diplomatic ally, especially if the investigation must be handled delicately between noble and clerical channels.
*   **The Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao:** Natural base of operations, source of testimony, sanctuary, and records.
*   **Patriarch Martiolus Legeum or Raoan authorities in Verbobonc:** Religious bridge between Veluna and the Viscounty.
*   **Select Council or court officials:** Useful if the delegation must navigate Viscounty politics carefully rather than accuse openly.

### Potential Enemies or Obstacles

*   Common bandits with uncommon patrons
*   Merchants or factors who fear what the recovered truth will expose
*   Local powers who do not want Velunan investigators looking too closely at old roads and new profits
*   Temple-aligned agents who know the Gift was not stolen by chance
*   Political obstruction from those who would rather let the matter die quietly

### Player Character Option — Cleric or Paladin of Rao

One of the strongest uses of this delegation is as a **campaign start option for a player character**. If a player chooses to begin as a cleric, paladin, temple-knight, lay brother, or even a sworn investigator of Rao, that character can be written directly into the delegation as a junior but trusted member.  

This works especially well because such a character arrives in the Viscounty with three things most low-level adventurers rarely possess at the beginning of a campaign: **purpose**, **legitimacy**, and **a reason to ask questions**.   **The PC might be:**

*   A young cleric sent by Whitehale, Mitrik, or Veluna City to prove worthy in the field
*   A paladin attached to the mission as a protector and witness of sacred duty
*   A novice investigator chosen because they know the roads of Verbobonc or once studied at the Monastery of Rao
*   A devout servant personally recommended by Lady Elinor Asbury or another trusted intermediary

This not only ties the player to the setting, it also gives them a strong reason to meet and recruit other adventurers. The delegation is too small to search every road, ruin, witness, and rumor by itself. The PC therefore has a natural reason to seek help in **Verbobonc City**, at the **Monastery of Rao**, or later in the **Village of Hommlet**, where the shadow of the greater campaign grows nearer.

### DM Notes — Running This as a Campaign Hook

If a player chooses this start, treat the delegation not as a full adventuring party that overshadows the PCs, but as a **mission structure and patron body**. The senior members should have responsibilities that keep them anchored to diplomacy, records, protection, and official process, while the player characters become the practical hands that go where evidence leads.   **Best use at the table:**

*   The PC arrives in Verbobonc with the delegation and quickly learns the mission is larger than expected
*   The Monastery of Rao becomes the first safe base and information point
*   A clue or witness points toward Hommlet, the road south, or an old raid site
*   The PC is authorized—quietly—to gather capable help rather than wait for slow formal channels

  **What this gives the DM**

*   A believable reason for the party to begin together
*   A patron who can issue tasks without railroading the campaign
*   A sacred and political mystery that ties directly into Veluna, Verbobonc, and Celene
*   A bridge from city intrigue into road investigation and finally into Temple-shadowed territory

**Important tone note:** this hook works best if the delegation feels serious, restrained, and quietly worried. They should not begin by shouting accusations. They should begin by asking careful questions—questions that grow darker as more answers appear.  

### DM Notes — PC Setup Questions

If a player wants to begin as the Velunan cleric or paladin attached to this mission, ask them a few simple questions before play:

*   Who in Veluna trusted you enough to send you? A bishop, knight-commander, archivist, or noble intermediary?
*   Why were you chosen? Faith, competence, local knowledge, family duty, or because you asked for this mission?
*   What do you believe happened to the Gift of Beauty?
*   Do you trust Verbobonc, or do you arrive already wary?
*   What would recovery of the Gift mean to you personally?

These questions turn the hook from a generic assignment into a personal stake. Once that happens, the player is no longer just “on a mission.” They are already part of the world.

### DM Notes — Why This Delegation Works

This delegation is useful because it does several things at once. It gives the campaign a dignified opening, it connects Verbobonc to Veluna in a personal and political way, it makes the loss of the Gift of Beauty feel important, and it provides a natural reason for good-aligned characters to begin working together. It also lets you shift tone gradually. What begins as diplomacy and road investigation can widen into cult traces, missing clergy, corrupted escorts, stolen relic traffic, and finally the broader shadow of the Temple itself.  

If you want the players to feel that the world is larger than one village and one dungeon, this is one of the cleanest ways to do it. The mission begins with peace, trust, and ceremony already wounded. The players are simply the ones asked to walk into the wound and find out how deep it goes.
