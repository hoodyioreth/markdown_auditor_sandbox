Holy Days and Festivals of the Flanaess
=======================================

> A comprehensive calendar of holy days, sacred festivals, and divine observances across the Flanaess. The [Greyhawk Calendar](greyhawk-calendar-article.md) consists of twelve months of 28 days each, plus four seven-day festival weeks — Needfest, Growfest, Richfest, and Brewfest — totaling 364 days. Every month and festival carries its own constellation of sacred dates tied to the gods of the Flanaess, the lunar cycles of Luna and Celene, and the seasonal rhythms that govern worship from the Baklunish West to the Solnor Coast. This article catalogs them all as a GM reference for the [Religion in Verbobonc](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md) campaign and beyond.

The gods of Oerth do not mark the passage of time by mortal convention. They mark it by light, darkness, and the turning of the moons. The [Greyhawk Calendar](greyhawk-calendar-article.md) tracks two moons — Luna (the Great Moon, 28-day cycle) and Celene (the Handmaiden, 91-day cycle) — whose phases determine when temples hold their most sacred rites and when the faithful gather to honor their patrons. What follows is a working reference for the DM: every known holy day, festival, and sacred observance across the Flanaess, organized by calendar period. For a deeper look at how these faiths operate in the Viscounty, see [Religion in Verbobonc](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md).

### The Four Festival Weeks

 

##### Needfest (Year-End / New Year — 7 Days between Sunsebb and Fireseek)

Needfest marks the turning of the year, celebrated across the Flanaess with feasting, gift-giving, and reflection on the year past. The week falls during the depths of winter, and its observances blend hope for renewal with acknowledgment of darkness.

 

Day

Observance

Description

Needfest 1

**New Year's Day** — holy to [Celestian](celestian-person-1.md)

The god of Stars, Space, and Wanderers is honored at the year's first dawn. Temples of Celestian hold midnight vigils to observe the turning of the celestial sphere, and astrologers cast horoscopes for the year ahead. Travelers light lanterns and set them adrift on rivers to symbolize journeys yet to come.

Needfest 4

**Luna New / Celene Full** — holy to [Nerull](nerull-person.md), [Olidammara](olidammara-person.md), [Istus](istus-person.md), [Wee Jas](wee-jas-person.md)

A rare convergence: the Great Moon goes dark while the Handmaiden blazes full. Followers of Nerull, god of Death and Darkness, consider the darkened Luna a time when the veil between life and death thins — funerary rites and ancestor remembrances are held. Olidammara's revelers exploit the paradox of one moon dark and one bright to stage masked festivals where identities are concealed. Istus, goddess of Fate, is honored by those who cast divinations for the coming year. Wee Jas, goddess of Magic and Death, presides over ceremonies where necromancers and lawful spellcasters alike renew their arcane oaths.

Needfest 7

**Feast of Fools** — holy to **Ye'Cind**, [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md), [Vatun](vatun-person.md)

The last day of Needfest is a carnival of misrule sacred to Ye'Cind, the elven god of Music and Magical Songs. Social hierarchies are playfully inverted — servants give orders, nobles serve meals, and bards perform satirical ballads mocking the powerful. Temples of Heironeous, god of Chivalry and Valor, hold their annual Oath of Rededication, where paladins and knights renew their vows of service. In the far north, the Feast is sacred to Vatun, god of Northern Barbarians, whose followers hold contests of strength to welcome the new year.

 

##### Growfest (Spring — 7 Days between Coldeven and Planting)

Growfest heralds the arrival of spring. The snows retreat, the ground softens, and the agricultural communities of the Flanaess prepare for the planting season. The week is marked by fertility rites, spring markets, and celebrations of renewal.

 

Day

Observance

Description

Growfest 4

**Merikka's Blessing** — holy to [Merikka](merikka-person.md)

The demigoddess of Agriculture, Farming, and the Home is honored by rural communities across the Sheldomar Valley. Farmers bring seed to be blessed, homesteads are ritually cleaned, and families share communal meals. In Verbobonc's farming villages, this is one of the most important days of the year — Merikka's priests walk the fields and pronounce them ready for planting.

Growfest 6

**Atroa's Feast** — holy to [Atroa](atroa--person.md)

The goddess of Spring, the East Wind, and Renewal is celebrated with flower garlands, spring dances, and the ceremonial release of caged songbirds. Atroa's priests open temple doors to let the east wind blow through, symbolizing the sweeping away of winter's stagnation. Young couples exchange wreaths of spring blossoms as tokens of courtship.

Growfest 7

**Fool's Day** — holy to **Ye'Cind**

A second festival of mischief and music sacred to the elven god of Magical Songs. Practical jokes are encouraged, bards compete in contests of wit and improvisation, and illusionists stage elaborate magical pranks in town squares. In communities with significant elven populations, the celebration takes on a more reverent tone as Ye'Cind's sacred hymns are performed.

 

##### Richfest (Midsummer — 7 Days between Wealsun and Reaping)

Richfest is the great midsummer festival, centered on the longest day of the year. The week celebrates abundance, light, and the peak of the growing season. Midsummer's Day (Richfest 4) is the most universally observed holy day in the Flanaess.

 

Day

Observance

Description

Richfest 3

**Heironeous' Triumph** — holy to [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)

The god of Chivalry, Justice, and Valor is honored with tournaments, jousts, and martial exhibitions. Temples hold investiture ceremonies for new paladins and knights. In Verbobonc, the Mounted Borderers traditionally hold their annual review on this day, and the Viscount presides over a military parade.

Richfest 4

**Midsummer's Day** — Luna and Celene both full. Holy to [Pelor](pelor-person.md), [Wee Jas](wee-jas-person.md), [Mayaheine](mayaheine-person.md), [Olidammara](olidammara-person.md), [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md)

The most sacred day of the year for followers of Pelor, god of the Sun, Light, Strength, and Healing. Both moons hang full in the sky — an event of immense magical and spiritual significance. Pelor's temples hold daylong services from dawn to dusk. Wee Jas marks the day as a time when the boundary between arcane and divine magic blurs. Olidammara's faithful stage the year's greatest revel. Druids of the Old Faith gather at stone circles and sacred groves to perform the Midsummer Rite, celebrating the peak of nature's power.

Richfest 5

**Xerbo's Tide** — holy to [Xerbo](xerbo-person.md)

The god of the Sea, Sailing, Money, and Business is honored by coastal and merchant communities. Offerings are cast into harbors and rivers, ship blessings are renewed, and merchants settle outstanding debts. In landlocked Verbobonc, this day is observed primarily by the merchant guilds and followers of Xerbo who pray for safe passage of trade goods along the Velverdyva.

Richfest 6

**Fharlanghn's Mile** — holy to [Fharlanghn](fharlanghn-person.md)

The god of Horizons, Distance, Travel, and Roads is celebrated by travelers, merchants, and wanderers. Roadside shrines are garlanded with wildflowers, and pilgrims walk a ritual mile barefoot to honor the Dweller on the Horizon. Innkeepers traditionally offer a free meal to any traveler who invokes Fharlanghn's name.

Richfest 7

**Myhriss' Kiss** — holy to [Myhriss](myhriss-person.md)

The goddess of Love, Romance, and Beauty — the Thrice-Kissed, Maid of Light and Dark — is honored on the last day of midsummer. Weddings are considered especially blessed on this day. Lovers exchange gifts, poets recite verse, and temples of Myhriss host elaborate balls. In Verbobonc, the Business Quarter's shrine to Myhriss sees its heaviest attendance.

 

##### Brewfest (Autumn — 7 Days between Harvester and Patchwall)

Brewfest celebrates the harvest, the turning of the season, and the fruits of the year's labor. It is the most raucous of the festival weeks — a time of feasting, drinking, competitive games, and communal gratitude. In agricultural communities like those of Verbobonc, Brewfest is the social event of the year.

 

Day

Observance

Description

Brewfest 1

**Osson's Ride**

A commemoration of the legendary cavalry charge of General Donn Osson, who rode to relieve a besieged city during the Aerdi expansion. Horsemen stage mock charges and races, and veterans share war stories over the first kegs of the autumn brew. In military communities, this is a day of martial remembrance.

Brewfest 4

**Almor's Tears** — holy to [Wenta](wenta-person.md)

The goddess of Autumn, Brewing, Harvest, and the West Wind is honored at the heart of Brewfest. New ale, cider, and mead are ritually tasted, and the best brews are offered to Wenta's altar. The name "Almor's Tears" commemorates the fall of the Prelacy of Almor, but in practice the day is joyful — tears of gratitude for the harvest, not grief.

Brewfest 7

**Liberation Day** — holy to [Dalt](dalt-person.md)

The god of Portals, Doors, Enclosures, Locks, and Keys is honored on the last day of Brewfest. Prisoners may petition for release, locked doors are symbolically opened, and communities stage public ceremonies of forgiveness and reconciliation. Thieves' guilds consider this day ironically sacred — the one day when locks should _not_ be picked.

### Monthly Holy Days

 

##### Fireseek (Winter — Month 1)

The first month of the year, deep in winter's grip. The name means "seeking fire" — communities huddle around hearths while the faithful honor gods of darkness, magic, and endurance.

 

Day

Observance

Description

1

Holy to [Vecna](vecna-person.md)

The god of Destructive and Evil Secrets claims the first day of the year's first month. His scattered cults perform clandestine rites in hidden sanctums, exchanging forbidden knowledge and renewing their vows of secrecy. Most people are unaware this observance exists — which is exactly how Vecna's faithful prefer it.

11

**Luna Full** — holy to [Olidammara](olidammara-person.md)

Every full moon of Luna belongs to the god of Music, Revels, Wine, and Rogues. His followers stage moonlit parties, wine tastings, and bardic performances. The Fireseek full moon, falling in deep winter, is traditionally a night of indoor revelry — tavern keepers report their best sales of the month.

14

Holy to [Hextor](hextor-person-1.md)

The god of War, Discord, Massacres, and Tyranny demands displays of martial discipline. His temples hold sparring tournaments and his faithful perform ritualized combat exercises. In Verbobonc, where Hextor's worship is technically tolerated but socially scorned, this observance is quiet and private.

18–20

**Luna Morta** — holy to [Boccob](boccob-person.md)

The three-day period when Luna wanes to its darkest is sacred to the god of Magic, Arcane Knowledge, Balance, and Foresight. Wizards and sages use this time for deep meditation, spell research, and the crafting of arcane items. Libraries and academies hold open lectures and magical demonstrations.

25

**Luna New** — holy to [Nerull](nerull-person.md)

Every new moon belongs to the Reaper. Funerary rites are performed, cemeteries are visited, and offerings are left at crossroads to placate the dead. Sensible folk bar their doors on these nights.

Winter begins to loosen its hold. The name reflects the preparation for spring — communities "ready" themselves for the planting season. This month carries an unusually dense concentration of holy days.

The first of Pelor's quarterly holy days. The god of the Sun is honored with dawn services where priests light sacred fires and bless the growing daylight hours. As winter recedes, Pelor's faithful celebrate the Sun's slow return to strength.

The god of Power, Prestige, Money, and Business is honored by merchants who perform annual accounting, settle debts, and make offerings for a profitable year ahead. In Verbobonc, the merchant guilds hold their annual elections on this day.

The god of Athletics, Strength, and Courage is celebrated with wrestling matches, feats of strength, and outdoor physical contests — even in the winter cold. Kord's faithful believe enduring the elements builds character.

The god of Jealousy, Revenge, and Thievery is acknowledged quietly. Those who have been wronged leave offerings at Kurell's shrines and petition for justice — or vengeance. Thieves consider this a lucky day for heists.

8–14

**Rising Fest** — holy to [Beory](beory-person.md)

A week-long celebration of the Oerth Mother, greater goddess of Nature and Rain. Communities mark the first signs of spring — snowmelt, the return of migratory birds, the swelling of streams. Druids and followers of the Old Faith hold their most important winter gathering during this week.

11

**Great Moon's Glory** — Luna full, Celene new. Holy to [Olidammara](olidammara-person.md), [Celestian](celestian-person-1.md), [Atroa](atroa--person.md), [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md)

A dramatic celestial event: Luna blazes full while Celene vanishes entirely. Olidammara's revelers hold their loudest party of late winter. Celestian's faithful study the exposed stars that Celene's light normally obscures. Atroa's worshippers see the Great Moon's dominance as a herald of spring's coming. Old Faith druids consider this a powerful night for divination and communion with nature spirits.

15

**Celene New** — holy to [Lendor](lendor-person.md)

The Suel god of Time, Tedium, Patience, and Study claims the Handmaiden's disappearance as his own — a reminder that all things pass and patience outlasts all. His rare followers use this day for lengthy meditations and the keeping of precise records.

24

**Day of Lords** — holy to **Zuoken**

The Baklunish god of Physical and Mental Mastery is honored by monks and martial artists who perform elaborate katas, fasting rituals, and tests of discipline. The Day of Lords celebrates mastery over the self rather than mastery over others.

25

**Luna New** — holy to [Nerull](nerull-person.md)

The Reaper's monthly due. Funerary observances and offerings to the dead.

A folk festival of star-crossed lovers, commemorating a myth of two celestial beings separated by the sky and reunited once a year. Communities hang colored ribbons from trees and write wishes on slips of paper. The celebration has no single patron deity but is observed across many cultures.

24–25

The Suel god of Animals, Chaos, Strength, and Courage is honored with two days of competitive hunting, animal wrestling (symbolic), and tests of raw physical power. Llerg's barbarian followers consider this the best time to seek animal spirit visions.

Named for the flocking of birds and herds. Livestock are driven to summer pastures, markets swell with spring goods, and communities enjoy the last comfortable weather before summer's heat.

**Ulaa's Hunt / Harnekiah** — holy to [Ulaa](ulaa--person.md)

A week-long celebration sacred to the goddess of Hills, Mountains, and Gemstones. Dwarven and gnomish communities hold gem-cutting festivals, mining competitions, and underground feasts. The "Harnekiah" is a traditional dwarvish prospecting hunt where teams compete to find the most valuable specimen in a designated area.

Day and night stand in perfect balance. St. Cuthbert's faithful celebrate balance between wisdom and zeal — temples hold sermons on moderation and common sense. Wee Jas marks the equinox as a moment when magical energies reach equilibrium, ideal for enchantments and divinations. Druids of the Old Faith perform the Equinox Rite at stone circles, celebrating the balance of nature.

18

**Walpurgis** — Luna new. Holy to [Nerull](nerull-person.md)

One of the darkest nights of the year in folk belief. The new moon of Flocktime is called Walpurgis — a night when the dead walk, witches fly, and the boundaries between worlds thin. Communities light bonfires to ward off evil spirits, and cautious folk stay indoors. Nerull's cults consider this their second-most important observance after the new moon of Sunsebb.

22–28

Holy to [Fortubo](fortubo-person-1.md)

The god of Stone, Metals, Mountains, and Guardianship is honored during the last week of Flocktime. Dwarven communities hold their annual inspection of fortifications and defensive works. Miners and stonemasons receive blessings for the year's labor ahead.

The goddess of Malice, Caves, and Pits is honored on the last day of Wealsun. Her cults perform underground rites in natural caverns, offering sacrifices to appease the darkness below. Miners and cave-dwellers leave small offerings at cave mouths as a precaution.

##### Goodmonth (Late Summer — Month 8)

The "good month" — named for the abundance of late summer. The main grain harvest is underway, orchards are heavy with fruit, and communities begin preparing stores for winter.

The Hopping Prophet, god of Amphibians, Bigotry, and Self-Deception, is honored by his insular cult with swamp-based rituals involving live frogs, mud immersion, and xenophobic sermons. The goddess of Poetry, Prose, Literature, and Art presides over a great gathering of bards, poets, and scholars. **King's Festival**

A five-day secular celebration honoring the ruling monarch or lord of the realm. In Verbobonc, this takes the form of the Viscount's Festival — a series of public feasts, parades, and proclamations celebrating the Viscounty's prosperity and the Viscount's continued stewardship.

**Pater Larch** — holy to **Geshtai**

The most feared night of the year: both moons vanish, plunging the world into absolute darkness. Nerull's power reaches its zenith. ##### GM Notes

**Using This Calendar in Play:** This article is a reference, not a script. The DM should not announce every holy day as it passes — that bogs down the campaign. Instead, use this calendar reactively: when the party arrives in a town, check what holy day falls nearest and use it to color the scene. A market during Brewfest feels different from a market during the Days of Thought. A temple visit on a deity's holy day should feel more charged than one on an ordinary Godsday.

 

**Lunar Events as Plot Hooks:** The full moons (Olidammara's revels) and new moons (Nerull's darkness) provide natural rhythms for encounters. Werewolves and lycanthropes are more active during Luna Full. Undead and dark cultists are emboldened during Luna New. The rare dual-moon events (Midsummer's Day, Dark Night, Great Moon's Glory) are significant enough to build entire sessions around.

 

**Verbobonc Emphasis:** In the Viscounty, the most commonly observed holidays are: Merikka's Blessing (Growfest 4), Midsummer's Day (Richfest 4), Brewfest (the entire week), Lammas (Patchwall 14), and the Winter Solstice (Sunsebb 21). The Old Faith, St. Cuthbert, and Pelor dominate public observance. Darker holy days (Vecna, Nerull, Hextor, Iuz) are observed secretly or not at all.
