AO-16. The Scriptorium
======================

_Records Chamber of the Azure Oath — Now the Earth Temple Intelligence Den_

* * *

The scriptorium was the **memory of the Azure Oath**. Here, in a long chamber lined with tall writing desks and walls of scroll cubbyholes, the knight-monks of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) maintained the priory's records — every oath sworn, every knight buried in the crypts below, every letter of correspondence between the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) and the broader [Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md). The ink wells were never dry. The vellum racks were always full. A knight-monk's whole career might be spent here, copying oaths in careful script while the wind rattled the narrow windows along the north wall.

Now the scriptorium serves [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md). He recognized its value the moment the priory fell — a room built for organizing information is a room built for organizing raids. The tall writing desks now hold maps of the Long Road corridor and the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) trade routes. The scroll cubbyholes are stuffed with stolen merchant manifests, patrol schedules intercepted from Verbobonc riders, and lists of guild marks identifying which caravans carry the richest cargo. Pins and colored thread connect locations on a wall-mounted map. It is, in every practical sense, a **military intelligence center**.

The [Reavers article](romagE28099s-reavers-occupy-the-azure-oath-article.md) says it plainly: _"The scriptorium became an intelligence den: caravan marks, guild names, patrol rhythms."_

The irony is architectural. A room built to record the deeds of holy knights now records the movements of the merchants those knights once protected.

##### Entering the Scriptorium

The door opens onto a long, narrow chamber with high narrow windows along the north wall. Pale light falls across a row of tall writing desks — the kind built for standing, not sitting — each one covered in maps, parchment, and weighted markers. The walls are lined with hundreds of small scroll cubbyholes, most still containing rolled documents, though many have been pulled out and replaced with newer, rougher paper.  
  
A large map of the Long Road corridor dominates the west wall, bristling with pins and connected by threads of red and black. Names and dates are scratched in charcoal beneath each pin. At the nearest desk, an open ledger sits beside a half-eaten meal, its pages covered in neat columns of numbers, dates, and guild marks.  
  
The room smells of old ink, candle smoke, and the faintly sour tang of cheap wine. Someone was working here recently.

### Features of the Scriptorium

#### The Wall Map

The west wall holds a large parchment map — hand-drawn but detailed — showing the **High Road corridor** from [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) in the east to the Greenway Valley in the west, with the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) trade routes branching south toward [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) and Tulvar. Pins mark locations of past and planned raids. Colored thread connects ambush sites to staging points.

A character studying the map may learn:

**DC 10 Investigation**  
The map shows **seventeen raid sites** marked with red pins along the Long Road. Each pin has a date scratched below it. The most recent date is less than a tenday old.

**DC 13 Investigation**  
Black pins mark **staging areas** — points where raiding parties assemble before striking. Three staging areas are visible: one near the priory ridge, one along the Ostverk road junction, and one near the Laughing Dog waystation. Thread connects each staging point to multiple raid sites, showing the operational pattern.

**DC 16 Investigation**  
One thread — **gold, not red or black** — runs from [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) southeast to a location marked only with a small triangle. This is the route to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s supply line from the greater Temple. The gold thread has been carefully placed by a different hand — **Lucius Graeme**'s, though the party won't know that yet.

**DC 18 Investigation or History**  
A faint pencil line — almost erased — traces a route south from Verbobonc toward Ostverk. The notation reads: _"Rao del. — 3 wagons, temple escort, silk banners. South road. Watch."_ This is intelligence about the **Rao Delegation** route. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has been tracking high-profile diplomatic targets.

* * *

#### The Tithe Ledger (Earth-Due)

On the largest writing desk sits an open ledger — thick, leather-bound, with a title scratched into the cover in angular script: **_Earth-Due_**. This is the operational record of every raid, tribute payment, and "tithe" extracted by [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s [reavers](romagE28099s-reavers-occupy-the-azure-oath-article.md).

The ledger is organized in columns:

Column

Content

Date

Day and month of each raid or payment

Target

Caravan name or merchant mark (guild symbols drawn in ink)

Take

Goods seized — coin, weapons, grain, luxury items

Tithe

Portion sent to "the southern house" (the Temple at [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md))

Status

Notes: "clean," "witnesses," "patrol near," or "Lak — recruits"

A character reading the ledger carefully may learn:

**DC 10 Intelligence (no check needed for literate characters)**  
The ledger lists **dozens of raids** over several months. The operation is systematic and well-organized — this is not banditry but a **coordinated campaign**.

**DC 13 Investigation**  
Several entries in the "Status" column reference **"Lak"** — the codename for **Lakash Quallad**, the recruiter at the Bronze Unicorn Inn in Verbobonc City. Entries marked "Lak — recruits" correspond to dates when new fighters arrived at the priory. This is a direct paper trail from Verbobonc's underworld to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md).

**DC 15 Investigation**  
The "Tithe" column shows a consistent **one-third split** — [Romag](romag-person.md)'s cut, forwarded through [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). One entry is annotated in a different hand: _"L.G. confirms receipt."_ — **Lucius Graeme**'s initials, confirming the Temple's oversight of the operation.

**DC 18 Investigation**  
The final page of the ledger contains a separate list titled _"Pending."_ Three entries describe **caravans that have not yet been raided** — including dates, routes, and cargo estimates. One is scheduled for **within the next tenday**. If the party acts on this intelligence, they could intercept or warn the targeted merchants.

* * *

#### The Caravan Seal Press

On the desk beside the ledger sits a heavy bronze **seal press** — stolen from a Verbobonc merchant house. The stamp bears the crest of the **Guild of Merchantry and Brokerage** alongside a false authorization number.

[Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) uses this to forge **"toll writs"** — documents that authorize passage along sections of the Long Road. Raiders carrying these writs can pass merchant checkpoints without suspicion. The forged writs also allow the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) to identify which caravans have **not** paid the earth-tithe, marking them as targets.

**DC 12 Investigation**  
A stack of blank writs sits beside the press, pre-stamped and ready for dates and names. The forgery is good but not perfect — a merchant who deals regularly with the Guild (or a character who succeeds on a DC 14 Insight or Investigation check) would notice the authorization number doesn't match any issued series.

* * *

#### Scroll Cubbyholes — The Old Records

The walls are lined with hundreds of small cubbyholes — the original scroll storage of the Azure Oath's scriptorium. Most have been emptied or stuffed with [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) materials, but some **original monastery records** remain, pushed to the back and forgotten.

**DC 14 Investigation (searching the cubbyholes specifically)**  
Behind the newer documents, a character finds **three surviving monastery scrolls**:

*   **A burial registry** listing every knight interred in the crypts below — names, ranks, dates of death, and which tomb holds each set of remains. This is invaluable intelligence for the crypt level
*   **A correspondence fragment** from the [Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) in Verbobonc, dated months before the fall, warning of "unsettled earth" and "troubling reports from the Kron Hills." The order was aware something was wrong — but responded too late
*   **A vow ledger** recording the names and oath-dates of every knight who served at the priory. Some names match the defaced mural portraits in [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) and the crypt sarcophagi below

* * *

#### The Scribe's Desk

A smaller desk in the northeast corner belongs to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) scribe — a low-ranking intelligence clerk who maintains the ledger, copies maps, and files intercepted documents. The desk holds:

*   Ink pots and quills (some good quality, stolen from merchant supplies)
*   A half-eaten meal (bread, hard cheese, cheap wine — the scribe was here recently)
*   A **personal journal** — not operational intelligence, but complaints. The scribe writes about boredom, fear of [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), missing home, and the "noise from below" that keeps him awake. The entry about the noise references **duergar digging** at night
*   A small pouch containing **12 gp and 8 sp** — the scribe's accumulated wages

* * *

#### Encounter

The scriptorium is staffed by **one [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) scribe** — a non-combatant intelligence clerk. **[Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)** may also be present, reviewing maps or updating the ledger.

*   **Scribe alone (most common):** The scribe is a frightened man who joined the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) for coin, not conviction. If confronted, he drops his quill and surrenders. He will answer questions in exchange for his life — he knows more than most grunts because he **files the intelligence**. He can identify [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md), [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md), [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) by name and role. He knows about the **earth-tithe**, the caravan schedule, and the supply line through [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). He does **not** know about the false-flag campaign or the [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s illusion operations — that's above his clearance
*   **Maris present:** If [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) is here reviewing maps, the encounter becomes significantly more dangerous. She will not surrender. She fights from behind the heavy writing desks (half cover), kicks the ledger into the fireplace if she gets the chance, and shouts for the guard in [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) or the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md). See [AO-12](ao-12-marinE28099s-command-chamber-landmark.md) for Maris's full combat tactics
*   **Empty (off-hours):** The scribe sleeps in the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) and [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) keeps irregular hours. The room may be unoccupied during night or early morning — the optimal time for the party to search

### DM Notes

#### The Evidence Room

AO-16 is the **operational brain** of the priory's raiding campaign. Where [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md) reveals the political conspiracy (false-flag campaign, Slikkina Jillink, destabilization plot), and **AO-15** reveals the human cost (Halven Sere's journal), the scriptorium reveals the **logistics**. This is where the party learns that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is not a band of raiders but a **structured military operation** with supply lines, recruitment networks, tithe systems, and intelligence oversight.

The three intelligence rooms form a triangle of evidence:

*   **AO-13 (Caidax's Chamber):** The conspiracy — false-flag gnome raids, Slikkina Jillink, political destabilization
*   **AO-15 (Sacristy):** The human cost — Halven Sere's journal, the faith's destruction
*   **AO-16 (Scriptorium):** The operation — caravan raids, earth-tithe, supply chain, recruitment, [Romag](romag-person.md)'s cut

A party that recovers evidence from all three rooms has a complete picture of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s regional operation — enough to bring to Verbobonc's authorities and trigger a major political response.

#### Protecting the Evidence

The scriptorium's evidence is **fragile**. Paper burns. If [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) is present when the party enters, her **first instinct** is to destroy the ledger — she kicks it into the fireplace or upends a lantern onto the map wall. If the party attacks loudly in the corridor ([AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md)) and [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) hears the fighting, she has **1d4+1 rounds** to destroy evidence before the party reaches her.

This creates a tension between **stealth and speed**. The party that sneaks through the command wing and reaches the scriptorium before [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) knows they're coming gets the evidence intact. The party that fights its way through gives her time to burn the most damaging documents. Surviving evidence should scale with how quickly the party secured the room:

*   **Surprise or stealth entry:** Everything intact. Full intelligence haul
*   **Maris had 1–2 rounds:** The wall map is torn down. The ledger's final pages (the "Pending" raids) are burned. The seal press and old records survive
*   **Maris had 3+ rounds:** The ledger is in the fire. The wall map is destroyed. Only the scroll cubbyholes (old monastery records) and the seal press survive
*   **Maris had 5+ rounds:** She's gone. Fled through the north door toward **AO-18** ([Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s quarters). Most evidence is ash. The scribe, if alive, can testify but has no physical proof to show

#### The Scribe as Asset

The scribe is the **best non-combat prisoner** in the priory. Unlike the acolytes (who know almost nothing), the scribe has been **filing intelligence for months**. He knows:

*   The complete command structure: [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) (overall commander), [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) (field operations), [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) (the wizard who comes and goes), [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) (the priest who scares everyone)
*   The earth-tithe system and where payments are forwarded ([Nulb](nulb-settlement.md))
*   Caravan schedules for upcoming raids
*   That "Lak" recruits in Verbobonc and that half-orcs come from [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)
*   That **Lucius Graeme** visits periodically to collect reports — he's the **liaison** between the priory and the Greater Temple
*   The names on the burial registry (he's read the old scrolls out of boredom)
*   That the duergar dig at night and the sounds carry through the stone

He does **not** know about the false-flag gnome campaign, Clan Jillink, or the Scroll of Veil — that's [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s operation and it's compartmentalized. He also doesn't know what [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is doing in the crypts — he just knows it scares him.

#### Sacred Layer — The Memory of the Order

The scriptorium was the **institutional memory** of the Azure Oath. Every knight who swore the oath had their name recorded here. Every burial was catalogued. Every piece of correspondence was copied and filed. The scriptorium didn't just store records — it **testified** that the order existed, that the knights served, that the dead were honored.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s repurposing is a quiet atrocity. They didn't burn the scriptorium — they **wrote over it**. The cubbyholes that held oath-records now hold caravan schedules. The desks that supported prayer now support raid planning. The room still functions as a place of record-keeping — but the records now serve violence instead of devotion. The architecture remembers what the occupants have forgotten.

The surviving monastery scrolls — the burial registry, the correspondence, the vow ledger — are the last witnesses of what this room used to mean. Finding them behind the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) documents should feel like finding someone's diary behind a wall of propaganda.

#### The Burial Registry and the Crypts

The burial registry is a **campaign-critical document** for the crypt level. It lists every knight interred below with their name, rank, and tomb location. If the party carries this document into the crypts, they gain:

*   **Names for the dead** — matching the mural names from [AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) to specific tombs. This turns anonymous undead into named individuals
*   **Navigation advantage** — the registry's tomb locations function as a partial map of the crypt level
*   **Diplomatic leverage** — if spirits of the Honoured Dead can be addressed by name, interaction checks gain advantage. The dead respond to being **remembered**

#### Connecting to the Broader Campaign

The scriptorium evidence connects to multiple campaign threads outside the dungeon:

*   **The Rao Delegation:** The faint pencil notation proves the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is tracking diplomatic targets — not just merchant caravans. This elevates the threat from regional banditry to political sabotage
*   **Lakash Quallad ("Lak"):** The ledger entries create a paper trail to the Bronze Unicorn Inn in Verbobonc City. If the party presents this to Verbobonc authorities, it triggers an investigation (or a cover-up, depending on who's compromised)
*   **The Nulb Supply Line:** The gold thread on the map and the tithe column in the ledger both point to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) as the financial pipeline. This is the route that leads the party from the priory to the Temple of Elemental Evil proper
*   **Lucius Graeme's Oversight:** The "L.G. confirms receipt" annotation proves that **Lucius Graeme** — [Romag](romag-person.md)'s intelligence coordinator — personally reviews the priory's output. This is not an outpost running independently; it's a **supervised operation**

#### Doors and Connections

The scriptorium sits on the north side of the priory:

*   **South:** Interior corridor connecting to the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) zone
*   **West:** **AO-17. Varnor Earthcaller's Quarters** — a connecting door that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) sometimes uses to access the scriptorium's records
*   **East:** **AO-19. The Oath-Hall (Counting Room)** — the logistics center where stolen goods are catalogued. The scriptorium tracks what's coming in; the oath-hall tracks what's going out

The scriptorium is deeper inside the priory than most rooms — reaching it requires passing through either the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) zone or the command wing ([AO-11](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md)/**AO-18**). The party won't stumble into this room early. By the time they reach it, they've already fought through the outer garrison and should be deep in the priory's interior.

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I study the wall map."** — Investigation DC 10 (seventeen raids), DC 13 (staging areas and pattern), DC 16 (gold thread — Nulb supply line), DC 18 (Rao Delegation route). Allow 10 minutes for a thorough study
*   **"I read the ledger."** — No check for basic content (literate characters). Investigation DC 13 ("Lak" entries — Verbobonc recruitment trail), DC 15 (one-third tithe split, L.G. annotation), DC 18 ("Pending" raids — actionable time-sensitive intelligence)
*   **"I examine the seal press."** — Investigation DC 12 identifies it as forged merchant guild authorization. DC 14 Insight spots the fake authorization number
*   **"I search the cubbyholes."** — Investigation DC 14 finds the three surviving monastery scrolls (burial registry, correspondence, vow ledger). The rest is Earth Temple operational material
*   **"I search the scribe's desk."** — No check needed. Personal journal, half-eaten meal, 12 gp 8 sp. The journal mentions duergar digging at night
*   **"I interrogate the scribe."** — No check for basic cooperation (he surrenders). Intimidation DC 10 for detailed answers. He knows the command structure, earth-tithe, supply chain, Lucius Graeme's visits. Does NOT know about false-flag campaign
*   **"I try to save the ledger from the fire."** — If Maris is burning evidence: Dexterity DC 12 to grab the ledger before it catches (contested with Maris's action if same round). Partial recovery even on failure — the leather cover and earliest pages survive
*   **"I take the wall map."** — The map is large (3 ft × 4 ft) and fragile. It can be carefully rolled (1 minute) or torn from the wall (1 action, but loses edge details). It's the single best piece of campaign intelligence in the priory
*   **"I take everything."** — Smart play. The entire room's contents can be bagged in 10 minutes. The wall map, ledger, seal press, monastery scrolls, and scribe's journal together form a **comprehensive evidence package**. Verbobonc authorities will pay for this — or kill for it, depending on who's compromised
