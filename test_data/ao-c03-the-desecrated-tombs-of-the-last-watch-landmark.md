AO-C03. The Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch
==============================================

> The Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch — the most recent tomb-hall in the crypts beneath the [Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), where the final generation of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) laid their dead before the order's withdrawal in 562 CY. Twenty-eight burial alcoves cut into the walls in three tiers, each once sealed with a carved stone slab bearing the knight's name, rank, and dates of service. Every one has been forced open. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) looted this hall systematically under [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s direction — the swords stripped from beside the dead, the holy symbols pried from stiff fingers, the shield-bosses knocked free and crated for transport to [Romag](romag-person.md). What remains is worse than destruction: the outlines of bodies pressed into decades of dust, the shadows where weapons once rested, the empty shapes of lives reduced to inventory. The six named knights whose guardian statues stand watch in **AO-22** above were all buried here. Their tombs were the first to be opened. Their swords are now on [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s trophy wall.

_AO-C03. The Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch — Tomb-Hall of the Final Generation — Now Stripped and Abandoned_

* * *

### The Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch

The tomb-hall opens east from **AO-C02** through a plain stone doorway — six feet wide, seven feet tall, no carved arch, no inscription, no ward. This was a functional access corridor, not a ceremonial entrance. The last generation's dead were carried in from the gallery through this doorway, but their consecration rites were performed in **AO-22** above, not here. By the time the pallbearers reached this room, the prayers had already been spoken. The doorway was just a door.

The hall beyond is roughly forty feet long and twenty-five feet wide — the largest single tomb chamber on the crypt level, built to hold the order's dead for at least another century of service. The ceiling is flat dressed limestone, seven feet high, supported by two load-bearing pillars at the room's centerline. The pillars are square, undecorated, functional — the masons who cut this hall were craftsmen, not artists. The room was built in the order's final decades, when the brotherhood was shrinking and the stone-cutters worked without supervision from the founding-era masters who had shaped the older galleries.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has been through every inch of it.

The smell is wrong — not the cold mineral air of the gallery, but something dry and human. Dust and old linen and the faint metallic residue of tools on stone. The doorway opens into a wide, low-ceilinged chamber lit by a single duergar lantern hanging from a ceiling hook. Both walls are lined with burial alcoves stacked three high — but every one is open. Stone slabs lie broken on the floor or leaned against the pillars. The alcoves themselves are dark rectangular holes, most of them empty. In the ones that are not empty, you can see the shapes — the pressed outlines in decades of dust where a body lay, the long shadow along the right side where a sword rested, the round depression near the head where a shield once sat. The dead are still here. Their things are not.

* * *

### The Looting — What Happened Here

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) directed the operation personally. The tomb-hall was the first section of the crypt level to be breached — within days of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s occupation, before the duergar had even been contracted. Varnor's human acolytes did the work with pry bars and hammers, moving north to south through the gallery and then east into this hall. The alcoves here were the newest — consecrated during the order's declining years, when fewer brothers attended the rites and the prayers were thinner. The wards on these tombs were the weakest in the complex. They broke easily.

The operation was methodical. Each alcove was opened in sequence, working from the doorway clockwise around the room. The stone slabs were pried off — not smashed randomly, but leveraged at the seam where the slab met the wall, using the same technique throughout. The burial shrouds were pulled aside. The grave goods were catalogued and removed: swords, shields, holy symbols, signet rings, armor fragments, personal effects. The bodies themselves were not destroyed — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) ordered the acolytes to disturb the remains as little as possible. He wanted the relics, not the dead. Unnecessary disturbance risked triggering secondary wards he could not predict.

The stolen goods were sorted into three categories. Weapons and armor went to [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s quarters in **AO-18**, where they hang as trophies on his wall — a display of dominance that Tharvok considers a personal statement about the old order's weakness. Holy symbols and signet rings were packed into tribute crates in **AO-C04**, scheduled for transport to [Romag](romag-person.md) via the supply route through [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). Miscellaneous items — belt buckles, devotional tokens, prayer beads — were dumped in a pile near the south wall and forgotten.

**What the evidence tells a careful investigator:** This was not vandalism. It was asset extraction. Someone with authority decided what was valuable, directed the workforce, and maintained discipline throughout the operation. The pry-bar technique is consistent across every alcove — one team, one method, one supervisor. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) treats desecration the way a mining foreman treats ore extraction. Efficiently.

* * *

### The Twenty-Eight Alcoves

The tomb-hall contains twenty-eight burial alcoves — fourteen per long wall, stacked in three tiers (bottom, middle, top). The layout follows the same pattern as the gallery: bottom tier at knee height, middle tier at chest height, top tier at head height. Each alcove is eighteen inches tall, twenty-four inches wide, and six feet deep — sized for a single body lying in repose with weapons placed alongside.

Every alcove is open. Every slab has been removed or broken. The hall is a grid of dark rectangular mouths in pale limestone, and every mouth is empty or nearly so.

##### What Remains Inside

*   **Dust outlines:** In twenty-two of the twenty-eight alcoves, the pressed shape of a human body is visible in the accumulated dust — head, shoulders, arms folded across the chest, legs straight. The outlines are precise enough to distinguish individuals by build. Beside most outlines, the long shadow where a sword rested and the round depression where a shield sat are clearly visible. These shapes are the only record that anyone was ever here
*   **Burial shrouds:** Torn linen fragments remain in most alcoves — pulled aside during the looting, roughly bunched or trailing from the recess. The shrouds are plain white linen, now yellowed with age, bearing no embroidery or insignia. Investigation DC 10 confirms these are standard [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) funeral wrappings — identical to the shrouds described in the burial registry in **AO-16**
*   **Bone fragments:** Six alcoves contain partial skeletal remains — a femur, a ribcage, scattered finger bones. These bodies were more decayed than the others and came apart when the shrouds were pulled. The acolytes left the fragments rather than clean them out. Medicine DC 12 confirms the remains are centuries old — no recent deaths
*   **Residual ward energy:** Detect Magic reveals a faint necromantic aura suffusing the entire hall — not from the bodies, but from the stone itself. The mass violation of twenty-eight consecrated burials has left a psychic wound in the limestone. The aura is not dangerous, but it is _wrong_ — a sustained note of grief embedded in the architecture. Any worshiper of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who enters the room feels a deep, cold sorrow that has no visible source. This is not a mechanical effect — it is a roleplaying cue. The stone is mourning

### The Six Named Tombs

The six knights whose life-sized stone guardians stand watch in **AO-22** above were all buried in this hall. They were the last six interred before the order's withdrawal in 562 CY — the final generation, the ones who stayed longest. Their alcoves occupy the west wall's middle tier, a row of six in sequence from north to south, and they were the first to be opened. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) recognized that the named knights' burials would contain the most valuable relics and prioritized them accordingly.

Each alcove's slab has been broken — but the fragments are identifiable. The slabs bore longer inscriptions than the rank-and-file burials: full name, rank, years of service, manner of death, and a personal epitaph. The fragments lie on the floor beneath each alcove or in scattered pieces nearby.

##### The Six — Identifiable Inscriptions

*   **Sir Aldren Voss (Investigation DC 12):** Three slab fragments on the floor. The largest reads: _"...thirty years of the Long Road...never faltered..."_ His alcove is the first in the row. The dust outline shows a large man — broad shoulders, arms crossed high. The sword-shadow beside him is wider than the others. He carried a bastard sword
*   **Sir Tormund Kael (Investigation DC 12):** Slab cracked cleanly in two halves, both lying flat. The inscription is nearly complete: _"Tormund Kael — Knight of the Azure Oath — Fell at the Bridge of Yarrow, 558 CY."_ His alcove contains a few links of chain mail the acolytes missed — wedged in a crack at the back of the recess. Worth 2 gp as salvage, priceless as evidence
*   **Dame Elara Brightholm (Investigation DC 13):** Slab shattered into multiple pieces — the acolytes used excessive force here, possibly because the ward on this slab was slightly stronger than the others. The epitaph survives on one fragment: _"...she rode when others walked..."_ Her alcove is the only one where the dust outline shows a weapon on the _left_ side — she was left-handed
*   **Sir Gerren of the Long Watch (Investigation DC 13):** Slab pried off intact and leaned against the base of the nearest pillar. The full inscription is readable: _"Sir Gerren of the Long Watch — Knight-Sergeant — 'I will not leave the road while one still walks it.' Died on patrol, 560 CY."_ His alcove contains nothing. Stripped completely. Even the dust outline is smudged — the acolytes were less careful by this point
*   **Sir Pendric Hale (Investigation DC 12):** Slab broken into four pieces. The epitaph survives: _"...first into the breach at Deklo's Stand...last to leave..."_ His dust outline is smaller than the others — he was lean, compact. The sword-shadow is narrow. He fought with a longsword
*   **Sir Corvan Ashenmoor (Investigation DC 10):** The easiest to identify — his alcove is the last in the row, and the largest slab fragment still reads clearly: _"Last to fall. Last to rest."_ His was the final interment in the crypts. His guardian statue in **AO-22** was the one [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) toppled to make room for the Elder Elemental God altar. His sword — a finely made longsword with the Azure Oath's sigil inlaid in silver on the crossguard — is the centerpiece of [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s trophy wall in **AO-18**

**Evidence Trail — The Connection to AO-18:** A character who has visited [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s quarters and seen the trophy wall can make a **History or Investigation DC 12** check to confirm that the weapons displayed upstairs match the empty alcoves in this room. The six swords missing from these six alcoves are the six swords hanging on Tharvok's wall. The silver-inlaid longsword is unmistakable — it matches Sir Corvan Ashenmoor's inscription. This connection is the proof that the Earth Temple's leadership is directly responsible for the desecration, not just the duergar work crews.

### The Hidden Alcove

One alcove in the back row — top tier, northeast corner, partially concealed behind the support pillar — remains sealed. The slab is plain and unmarked: no name, no rank, no insignia. It is the burial of a lay brother, not a knight, and the looters passed over it because it looked unimportant. An unmarked slab in a room full of named warriors was easy to miss, especially in the top tier where the light from a hand-held lantern does not reach easily.

**Investigation DC 16** to notice the sealed slab. The DC is reduced to 12 if the party is specifically searching the top-tier alcoves or if someone has climbed a pillar for a better vantage point.

Breaking the seal requires a pry bar or similar tool and a **Strength DC 12** check. The slab cracks along its center seam and comes free in two halves. Inside:

*   **The body of Brother Emmerich** — a priory herbalist who served the order for forty-three years. His body is remarkably preserved — the consecration ward held perfectly on this small, modest burial. He lies in repose, hands folded over a leather pouch on his chest, a faint smile visible on the dried features of his face. He looks peaceful. He looks like the only person in this room who was left alone
*   **Leather pouch contents:** A **Potion of Healing (2d4+2)** in a small clay vial, stoppered with wax and still viable after decades. Brother Emmerich brewed it himself — his herbalist's mark (a tiny pressed leaf) is stamped into the wax. A handwritten note inside the pouch, in faded Old Oeridian, reads: _"For whoever needs it next."_
*   **Silver holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md):** Worn around his neck, tucked inside the burial shroud. Small, plain, well-used — the lightning bolt motif is smoothed from decades of handling. Worth 15 gp as silver. Functions as a holy symbol. If **Halven Sere** sees it, he recognizes it immediately — _"Emmerich. He grew the herbs in the garden. He made poultices for the injured and tea for the grieving. He asked to be buried with one last healing draught, in case someone came along who needed it more than he did."_

**DM Note:** Brother Emmerich's alcove is a small, quiet reward in the middle of devastation. The potion is useful but not game-changing. The holy symbol is functional. The real value is the emotional beat — one person, overlooked by the looters, still offering help from the grave. Play this gently. The note is the payoff.

### The Miscellaneous Pile

Against the south wall, near the corridor that leads to **AO-C04**, a heap of discarded items sits where the acolytes dumped anything they considered worthless. The pile is approximately three feet across and a foot high — a loose collection of the things that were not swords, shields, or holy symbols.

*   **Investigation DC 10:** The pile contains belt buckles (brass, tarnished), devotional prayer tokens (stamped tin discs with the Azure Oath's sigil), broken prayer beads (wooden, strung on rotted cord), a leather-bound journal with blank pages (the ink has faded completely), and several small carved stone tokens that the knights used as personal seals. Total salvage value: approximately 8 gp
*   **Investigation DC 14:** Among the debris, a small leather-wrapped bundle contains three **votive candles** — consecrated beeswax tapers of the same type used in the candle shelf in **AO-C01**. They are still consecrated. If lit and placed in any alcove in this room, the faint necromantic aura in that section of the wall diminishes visibly — the grief in the stone eases, just for that alcove, just for as long as the candle burns. No mechanical effect. The stone stops mourning for an hour
*   **Investigation DC 17:** Buried at the bottom of the pile, a folded piece of vellum — an inventory list written in Common in a hasty hand. The list catalogues every item removed from this room: 28 swords (type and condition), 22 shields, 31 holy symbols, 18 signet rings, and miscellaneous items. Each entry notes which alcove the item came from, identified by the occupant's name. At the bottom: _"All items logged. Weapons to Tharvok. Symbols and rings crated for Romag. Send with next Nulb shipment. — V."_ The initial is [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s. This document is a confession. It connects the looting to specific commanders by name

### The South Corridor to AO-C04

The south wall of the tomb-hall opens into a short corridor — ten feet long, five feet wide, rough-cut and unfinished — that leads to **AO-C04**, the Duergar Work Camp. The corridor was the original construction access passage between the completed tomb-hall and the unfinished secondary hall that the duergar now occupy. It was never dressed or decorated — the walls show raw chisel marks and the ceiling is low enough that a tall human must duck.

The corridor carries sound. Voices from **AO-C04** are audible from the tomb-hall entrance (Perception DC 12). The smell of the duergar cooking fire — subterranean lichen stew and dried cave fish — drifts through. If the party is approaching quietly, they can hear the off-shift duergar: low Undercommon conversation, the clink of tools being maintained, the scratch of **Grimlok Ashpick**'s chalk on his shift-planning slate.

There is no door or barrier between the two rooms. The duergar consider the tomb-hall their front porch — looted, cleared, and harmless. They do not post a guard here. Their security relies on the sentries at **AO-C01** and the alarm rope system.

### Sacred Layer

This was the order's most active tomb-hall at the time of withdrawal — the room where the brotherhood's grief was freshest, where the most recent dead were still being mourned by brothers who had known them alive. The six named knights were not distant legends when they were buried here. They were friends. Comrades. The men and women whose empty bunks in the dormitory had not yet been reassigned.

The desecration is total but clinical. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) did not smash the alcoves out of hatred. He inventoried them. He catalogued the contents. He filed an extraction report. The horror is not in the violence — it is in the efficiency. These dead were processed. Their swords weighed, their shields assessed, their holy symbols counted and crated. Five centuries of devoted service reduced to a shipping manifest signed with an initial.

And the stone remembers. The necromantic aura is not a haunting — there are no restless spirits here, no vengeance-seeking dead. It is simpler than that. The stone was consecrated to hold the faithful at rest. The faithful were taken out. The consecration is still running, still trying to protect what is no longer there, and the disconnect between purpose and reality manifests as a sustained note of loss that [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) worshipers feel in their chests. The room is grieving because it was built to grieve, and no one has told it to stop.

### DM Notes

##### Running This Room

This room is evidence, not combat. There is nothing to fight here and nothing that attacks the party. The threat is emotional — the weight of what was done, the forensic clarity of how it was done, and the connection to the trophy wall upstairs. Play it slow. Let the party investigate at their own pace. Read the alcove descriptions one at a time. Let them piece together the evidence trail.

The six named tombs are the centerpiece. If the party has already visited **AO-22** and seen the guardian statues, they will recognize the names. If they have visited **AO-18** and seen [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s trophy wall, they will recognize the swords. The moment a player says _"those are the same swords"_ is the moment this room pays off. Do not rush it.

##### Halven Sere in This Room

If **Halven Sere** accompanies the party, this room breaks something in him that the gallery did not. In the gallery, the sealed alcoves gave him hope. Here there is nothing sealed. He walks to the six named alcoves and touches each one in turn — the dust outlines, the empty spaces where the swords lay. He says the names aloud. Quietly. Not for the party. For them.

*   _"Aldren. You carried the heaviest blade and never once complained about the weight."_
*   _"Tormund. You fell at the Bridge of Yarrow and we sang for three days."_
*   _"Elara. You always led with the left and we could never break the habit."_
*   _"Gerren. You would not leave the road. We had to carry you off it."_
*   _"Pendric. First in and last out. Every time."_
*   _"Corvan."_ \[He says nothing else for this one. He touches the slab fragment that reads _"Last to fall. Last to rest."_ and then stands very still for a long time.\]

If the party shows him Brother Emmerich's alcove, the grief lifts — just slightly. _"Emmerich. Of course. Of course he left something for someone else. That was all he ever did."_ He holds the silver holy symbol for a moment, then gives it back. _"He would want you to have it. He would want it to be useful."_

##### The Emotional Architecture

This room is the deliberate counterpoint to the Founders' Sepulchre in **AO-C05**. Where **AO-C05** shows what the dead were supposed to be — twelve founders, untouched, blazing with ward-light, faith made permanent — this room shows what happens when that protection fails. Same alcoves. Same construction. Same intent. One room is luminous and sacred. This room is dark and empty. The contrast is the thesis of the entire crypt level: the wards matter. **Halven Sere**'s prayers matter. When the light goes out, this is what follows.

##### Connections

*   **AO-C02 (west):** The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead. The Looted Passage connects to the gallery's north section — plain doorway, no ward, trampled. The party's primary route in and out
*   **AO-C04 (south):** The Duergar Work Camp. Short unfinished corridor. Sound and smell carry. No barrier. The duergar are audible from here
*   **AO-18 (above, main level):** [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s Quarters. The trophy wall upstairs holds the stolen swords. The evidence trail connects these rooms across two levels
*   **AO-22 (above, main level):** The Hall of Final Vigil. The six guardian statues upstairs represent the six named knights buried here. The connection is the heart of the room's emotional impact
