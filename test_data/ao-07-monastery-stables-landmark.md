AO-07. Monastery Stables
========================

Originally constructed as a modest service stable for the priory’s small pack animals, this structure once held only two donkeys and seasonal hay storage for Brother-Custodian **Halven Sere**, the Last Oathman.

Under Tharvok Stonehand’s command, the stables now function as:

*   Mount housing for Earth Temple priests
*   Cart horses for raiding convoys
*   Temporary holding area for captured animals
*   Equipment staging for Long Road raids

This location also serves as a **secondary access point** into the monastery proper.

##### First Arrival

You step beneath the low timber overhang and into the stale warmth of animals and damp straw. The stone walls here are older than the rest — weathered smooth by centuries of wind and rain — yet the interior smells of recent occupation. Lantern light flickers along the stall rails. Iron rings have been driven into mortar joints to tether restless horses.  
  
You hear it first — the uneasy stomp of a war-trained mount shifting weight in its stall. This was once a quiet place of burden and service — a pair of donkeys for humble supply runs down the hill.  
  
Now it houses something heavier. Something ready. And as the wind dies, you catch another scent beneath the straw and hay. Gnoll.

#### The Hay Loft

Accessible via ladder (DC 10 Athletics).

Contains:

*   6 large hay bundles
*   Rope coils
*   Spare harnesses
*   Crate of confiscated trade goods (grain, salt, wool bolts)

**Hidden (Investigation DC 16):**

*   Small satchel wedged beneath hay
*   Inside: Earth Temple correspondence signed by Tharvok
*   Mentions scheduled convoy from Nigb’s Rest

* * *

#### Rear Access Door

Heavy oak, iron reinforced.

*   Barred from monastery interior side
*   DC 17 Strength to force
*   DC 15 Thieves’ Tools to bypass if unbarred
*   Leads into:  
    → Interior Service Hall (Location 8)

This door is the most tactically valuable infiltration point.

If accessed quietly:

*   Party bypasses courtyard combat entirely

#### Recently Captured Merchant Wagon

The stables now double as a temporary holding point for seized goods before they are redistributed, claimed by the Earth Temple, or quietly moved through the logistics chain toward Nulb.

Captured wagons are never stored long here. Tharvok Stonehand ensures turnover within 48 hours. Once sorted, the valuable goods are loaded onto “approved” wagons and sent north under the guise of legitimate trade.

The cover story:  
Grain merchant.  
Textile broker.  
Salt trader.

The truth:  
Black Earth logistics convoy bound for Nulb.

* * *

##### What the Party Sees

Near the eastern wall, beside the hay loft ladder, stands a heavy merchant wagon — mud-splattered, its crest roughly chiseled from the side panels.  
  
The horses are gone. One wheel bears fresh scoring from a recent repair.  
  
The canvas covering has been replaced with dark, rough cloth. The original painted merchant sigil has been scraped away but faint outlines remain beneath soot and pitch. Several crates lie open nearby, their contents half-sorted. You smell dried grain. And blood.
