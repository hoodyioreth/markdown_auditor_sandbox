GOB-QS02-Ranger-Knight-Glorandal-Palees-Mission
===============================================

> Ranger Knight Glorandal Palee's Mission is one of several Gift of Beauty quest starters — designed for parties with an elf character connected to the Gnarley Forest clans. Ranger Knight Glorandal Palee of Clan Meldarin approaches an elf PC through the bonds of kinship, clan obligation, and shared stewardship of the forest. Unlike the human ranger hook, this encounter is framed through elven cultural protocol: the Elvish greeting, the appeal to the Seldarine, Deleveu's half-elven heritage as a trust bridge, and the Gift's carvings of Corellon Larethian as a matter of sacred significance to all elvenkind. The same wooden coin, the same direction south — but the weight of the request is carried by clan honor and the ancient covenant between the People and the forest they were charged to protect.

 

### The Elven Door — A Quest Hook for Elf Characters

_"Greetings ent thar via / avia lyth..."_

 

This quest starter is designed for a DM whose party includes an **elf character** — ideally one with ties to the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) clans, the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md), or the broader [Elves of the Gnarley Forest](elves-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md). Ranger Knight **Glorandal Palee** of [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) approaches the elf PC as kin, not as a stranger recruiting a mercenary. The appeal is framed through **elven cultural obligation** — the Seldarine's interest in a sacred object bearing [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md)'s likeness, the covenant between the four clans and the forest they were charged to protect, and the half-elven heritage of the quest's patron as a bridge of trust between the People and the human world.

 

This is **one of several starter articles** for the Gift of Beauty questline. Different starters exist for different party compositions and campaign entry points. Where GOB-QS01 (Ranger Knight Wyn's Mission) appeals to a human ranger or druid through wilderness values and the common folk, this starter speaks to an elf through **kinship, sacred duty, and clan honor**. A DM running a party with both a human ranger and an elf might use both starters in sequence — Wyn first, then Glorandal confirming the mission from the elven side.

 

* * *

 

### Why This Starter Is Different

The core mission is the same: travel to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md), present the wooden coin, recover the Gift of Beauty. But the **cultural framing** changes everything about how the encounter feels at the table.

 

*   **Language:** Glorandal opens in Elvish. The greeting — _"ent thar via / avia lyth"_ — is a formal address between People of the forest. It establishes rank (Ranger Knight to Junior Ranger), shared identity, and respect. A human ranger would never hear these words. An elf PC recognizes them immediately as clan protocol
*   **Clan obligation:** Glorandal does not recruit — he _invokes_. Among the [Elves of the Gnarley Forest](elves-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md), a request from a Ranger Knight of one's own clan (or an allied clan) carries weight beyond a simple favor. The Code of the People — the oath of communal fellowship sworn by all four clans — means that a matter affecting one kindred affects all. The Gift bears [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md)'s image. Its loss is not merely political. It is an affront to the Seldarine
*   **Deleveu's blood:** Where Wyn describes Lord Deleveu as a loyal servant of the Viscount, Glorandal describes him as _"a good friend of the elves with elven blood both in race and in the manner he lives his life."_ Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) is half-elven. His estate at [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) sits on the very border of [Celene](celene-organization.md), under the watchful eye of Hestomalkor [Talarien Carewlein](talarien-carewlein-person.md) and the elven garrison of Caer Ostverk. For an elf PC, Deleveu is not a foreign lord — he is kin-by-blood, governing a settlement where elves, gnomes, and humans live side by side under the old pacts
*   **Sacred stakes:** The Gift of Beauty bears carvings of [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) and "other Fey folk" alongside scenes of the races working together to build Verbobonc. It was blessed by Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) and destined for Her Fey Majesty [Yolande](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) of [Celene](celene-organization.md). For an elf, this is not a diplomatic gift that went missing — it is a **sacred object bearing the Creator's likeness** that was stolen before it could reach the Queen of All Elvenkind. The weight is different

### Prerequisites & Timing

#### When Does This Happen?

This encounter works during the **Festival of Readying (576 CY)** or shortly after. Rumors of the Gift's disappearance reach the elven communities through different channels than the human ones — not through market gossip but through the **ranger patrol network** that connects [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md), [Clan Sherendyl](clan-sherendyl-organization.md), [Clan Fealefel](clan-fealefel-organization.md), and [Clan Enlanefel](clan-enlanefel-organization.md) with the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) and the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md). The clans know something went wrong on the road to [Celene](celene-organization.md). They do not know the details. But word has reached the Council of the Oath Oak at Alaeduril — and [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) has dispatched Glorandal to find trusted hands.

 

*   **Ideal Level:** 3–5 (party has proven themselves but is not yet famous)
*   **Prerequisite Events:** The elf PC has been active in or near the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) — traveling its trails, aiding its communities, or simply passing through territory where elven scouts could observe them. Glorandal has received reports from the patrol network
*   **Target PC:** An elf character — wood elf, high elf, or half-elf with strong elven identity. Ideally someone with clan ties, Gnarley Forest connections, or ranger training. Glorandal addresses this character _by name_ and _in Elvish_
*   **Location:** Flexible. Works best in forested terrain — a trail through the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md), a camp beneath old-growth canopy, or a woodland clearing near one of the clan territories. The original play-test placed this along a forest trail. It can also occur near [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md), **Thaymouth**, or anywhere the Meldarin patrol routes intersect the party's path

### The Encounter: Glorandal Approaches

#### Setting the Scene

The party is moving through forested country — deep enough under canopy that the light filters green and gold through the leaves, where the sounds of civilization have faded and the forest speaks in bird calls and running water. This is the domain of the [Elves of the Gnarley Forest](elves-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md). Whether the party knows it or not, they have been under observation for the last mile. Not hostile surveillance — assessment. The way a kindred clan watches travelers to determine their purpose and their worth.

 

A figure steps from behind a broad-trunked oak as if he had grown from it. Tall for a wood elf, lean and still, he wears forest leathers dyed in the muted greens and browns of the deep Gnarley — not the bright emerald of a human ranger's cloak but the layered, living colors of bark and moss and shadow. A longbow of pale ashwood rests across his back. A slim blade hangs at his hip, its pommel carved with a leaf motif. His face is angular, ageless, and grave — the face of one who measures time in seasons, not in days. His eyes find one of you — not the group, but one — and settle there with quiet recognition.

 

**Glorandal addresses the elf PC by name — in Elvish.** This is the critical difference from the Wyn encounter. He does not emerge and address the group. He speaks _to the elf_, _in the language of the People_, as one who shares blood, forest, and obligation. The rest of the party hears Elvish words they may or may not understand. The elf PC hears a formal greeting from a Ranger Knight of [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) — a greeting that carries rank, respect, and implied obligation.

 

*   **Perception DC 16:** An elf character (or one with keen senses) noticed a flicker of movement in the canopy above twenty paces back — Glorandal was tracking them from the branches before descending to ground level. Non-elves require DC 20
*   **Insight DC 12:** Glorandal is not nervous or uncertain. He has already decided this is the right person. His approach is formal — this is protocol, not improvisation
*   **Nature DC 10 (elf PCs only):** The leaf-pommel blade and ashwood bow are Meldarin craftsmanship — recognizable to any elf familiar with the Gnarley clans. The leather dyes use forest pigments that mark him as a deep-wood patrol ranger, not a border scout
*   **History DC 14:** [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) is the diplomatic confederation of nine wood-elf tribes in the western [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md), centered at Alaeduril — the Harmonious Dells. They are known for inclusive kinship, master bowcraft, and oath-bound stewardship of the forest's sacred groves

 

#### The Elvish Greeting — Clan Protocol

Glorandal's opening is spoken entirely in Elvish. The DM should deliver this in Elvish first (even if paraphrased), then translate for the table — or pass the elf PC a note with the Elvish text and let them decide what to share with the group.

 

"Greetings ent thar via / avia lyth, Junior Ranger. It seems that there would be a task that may be of interest to thee."

 

**DM Notes on the Elvish Greeting:**

*   _"Ent thar via / avia lyth"_ — a formal Elvish address between People of the forest. It acknowledges the PC as a recognized member of the elven community (not an outsider), establishes Glorandal's rank as a Ranger Knight (senior to a Junior Ranger), and opens a channel of **clan obligation**. This is not a casual hello. It is the beginning of a formal request under the Code of the People
*   If the elf PC is from [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) specifically, the weight doubles — this is a direct clan request from a superior officer. Refusal would require serious justification
*   If the elf PC is from another Gnarley clan ([Sherendyl](clan-sherendyl-organization.md), [Fealefel](clan-fealefel-organization.md), or [Enlanefel](clan-enlanefel-organization.md)), the obligation still applies through the alliance of the four kindreds — but it carries less direct authority
*   If the elf PC is not Gnarley-born (a high elf from [Celene](celene-organization.md), a wanderer, or a city elf), the greeting still signals respect and shared identity as People of the same blood. Glorandal adapts his approach — less clan obligation, more appeal to elven honor and the Seldarine

 

#### The Mission — Framed Through Elven Eyes

After the greeting, Glorandal delivers the same core intelligence as Wyn — the Gift is missing, the party should travel to Deleveu — but his framing is distinctly elven.

 

"It seems that there are rumors abound that the 'Gift of Beauty' to our Fey Majesty to the South has been... let's say removed from the control of the Viscount's power. I am not an expert in the ways of the new Viscount, but if one was to confirm this information, and if the item is found to be not in his control, shall it be returned to its rightful hands... well, you have an idea of the rewards that deeds in the name of the folk of Verbobonc can be found."

 

**DM Notes on Glorandal's Language:**

*   Like Wyn, Glorandal speaks in **careful conditionals** — "it seems," "rumors abound," "let's say." The plausible deniability is the same. But where Wyn's caution comes from ranger operational security, Glorandal's comes from **elven political instinct**. The clans do not make accusations against a human Viscount lightly. They observe, they assess, and they act through intermediaries
*   He says _"our Fey Majesty"_ — not "the Queen of Celene" or "Her Fey Majesty" in the formal human phrasing. To an elf, [Yolande](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) is _ours_. The Gift was meant for _our_ queen. Its theft is personal to the People in a way it can never be for humans
*   He appeals to "the folk of Verbobonc" — Glorandal, like all [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) members, respects the alliance between the clans and the human communities. The Gift represents the **joining of the races**. Its loss threatens that covenant

 

#### No Gold, No Orders — The Ranger's Disclaimer

Glorandal makes the same declaration of independence that Wyn does — but the subtext changes when spoken elf-to-elf.

 

"I am not hiring and have no gold or other influence to bestow. I know of no official summons to this task. I am but a servant of the woodlands and unknown to the powers that would have their way in the Viscounty. Travel to Deleveu Woods and seek Lord Deleveu. He is loyal to the Viscount and would be pleased to have assistance if it comes in the name of Wilfrick."

 

**Key information delivered:**

*   **No payment.** Among elves, this is less remarkable than among humans. The Code of the People emphasizes communal duty over individual reward. Glorandal does not need to explain why he has no gold — an elf PC understands that **this is how the People operate**
*   **No official connection.** Glorandal distances himself from the Viscounty's power structure, just as Wyn does. But the distinction matters: Glorandal is not a servant of the human Viscount. He is a servant of **the forest and the clans**. His authority comes from [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) and the Council of the Oath Oak, not from any human title
*   **The destination:** [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) in Deleveu Woods. Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) is the quest patron
*   **The name drop:** "In the name of Wilfrick" — Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix. This carries a different valence for an elf. The Viscount is a human authority. But [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) maintains careful diplomatic relations with Verbobonc. The mention tells the elf PC that this mission serves _both_ the People and the human realm — a shared interest

 

#### The Wooden Coin & Deleveu's Elven Blood

**Glorandal Palee** hands the elf PC a small carved token — a **wooden coin** with an etching of the Oak Father dyed in green on one side. But his description of Lord Deleveu is markedly different from Wyn's.

 

"Present this to Lord Deleveu, a good friend of the elves with elven blood both in race and in the manner he lives his life. He will provide you with shelter, food, and information on what must be done."

 

**DM Notes — The "Elven Blood" Line:**

*   This is the single most important difference between this starter and Wyn's. Glorandal explicitly names Deleveu's **half-elven heritage** and frames it as a qualification for trust. Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) is not just a loyal human lord — he is _kin_. His estate at [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) sits on the border of [Celene](celene-organization.md), governed alongside Hestomalkor [Talarien Carewlein](talarien-carewlein-person.md) and a permanent garrison of fifty elven warriors at Caer Ostverk
*   _"In the manner he lives his life"_ — Glorandal is saying that Deleveu does not merely carry elven blood through ancestry. He **lives by elven values**: stewardship of the land, respect for the Old Faith, cooperation between the races. This is [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md)'s highest compliment for a non-elf — that he is elven _in spirit_ as well as blood
*   For an elf PC, this transforms Deleveu from "some human lord the rangers trust" to **"one of us, in the ways that matter."** The elf is not being sent to a foreign patron — they are being sent to a **kinsman** who understands what the Gift means to the People

 

#### The Appeal to Fame

Glorandal turns to address the rest of the party — acknowledging the non-elven companions with the diplomatic grace characteristic of [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md).

 

"Rewards can be sought in many forms. Fame and the ballads of the bards will make this task a powerful tool to those seeking fame. If the rumors show true, it would serve both the common folk of Verbobonc and her Viscount. If the item is not in the hands of Her Fey Majesty or her charges, then the Viscounty will not be seen in good light for trade and other political issues. I have no knowledge on such matters, but don't think it would be well for the common folk. Deleveu Woods is an estate to the South just North of the Border of Celene."

 

**DM Notes:** Glorandal switches to Common for this speech — a deliberate shift. He is now addressing the whole party, not just the elf. His tone remains formal but becomes more accessible. He provides the geographic marker ("South just North of the Border of Celene") that Wyn does not — because Glorandal knows exactly where [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) and the Deleveu estate sit relative to the elven border. That specificity is natural to him.

 

#### The Gift Described — Through Elven Reverence

When asked about the Gift itself, Glorandal provides the physical description — but his emphasis falls on the sacred elements that matter to the People.

 

"The 'Gift to Her Fey Majesty' is a beautiful work of craftsmanship. It is a box of Ipp wood that measures nearly 2 hands tall, 3 hands wide, and 5 hands in breadth. There are carvings of Corellon Larethian and other Fey folk on the cover, as well as the joining of the races in the common bond to build and develop this land known as Verbobonc. It is a story of sorts, beautifully worked in wood. The Archdruid of [Obad-Hai](obad-hai-person.md) of the Western Gnarley blessed the gift and presented the Viscount with the gift. The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) took the gift to Deleveu Woods — then onto the border to meet the Knights of [Celene](celene-organization.md)."

 

**What Glorandal knows:**

*   The Gift's physical description — Ipp wood, carvings, dimensions
*   The carvings depict [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) and other Fey folk — **this matters deeply to Glorandal**. A box bearing the Creator's image, lost to thieves. For an elf, this borders on sacrilege
*   It was blessed by the Archdruid ([Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md)) — the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) and the clans share deep bonds. The Archdruid's blessing carries weight in both communities
*   The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) transported it toward the [Celene](celene-organization.md) border
*   It never arrived. The Knights of [Celene](celene-organization.md) were left waiting at the border

 

**What Glorandal does NOT know:**

*   Who took it (he has no intelligence on the Earth Temple or Maris Blackshale's raiders)
*   Where it is now (he does not know about the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md))
*   The faction politics surrounding it (Lareth, Romag, the inter-cult rivalry)
*   Whether the elven garrison at Caer Ostverk or Hestomalkor [Talarien Carewlein](talarien-carewlein-person.md) is involved in the search — he does not speak for [Celene](celene-organization.md)'s military

 

Like Wyn, Glorandal is a **direction arrow**, not an intelligence briefing. He points the party to Deleveu. But for an elf PC, the arrow also points toward something deeper — a sacred object of the People, bearing the Creator's face, lost in the borderlands where the clans were charged to keep watch. The failure is collective. The recovery is a matter of **honor**.

 

#### The Vanishing — An Elven Exit

Once Glorandal has delivered his message, handed over the wooden coin, and answered what questions he will, he departs — but his exit is different from Wyn's. Wyn vanishes like a human woodsman: one moment there, the next gone into the brush. Glorandal fades like a part of the forest returning to itself.

 

The Ranger Knight inclines his head — a gesture of respect between equals that carries the formality of the People. He steps backward, and the forest seems to close around him: a shaft of green light where he stood, leaves settling where there were none, and then silence. The canopy overhead sways once, as if acknowledging a friend's departure. The elf is gone. The only proof he was ever there is the wooden coin in your hand, the Oak Father's mark vivid green against the heartwood.

 

**DM Notes:** Glorandal's departure should feel **natural rather than dramatic** — less "the mysterious stranger vanishes" and more "the forest reclaimed one of its own." He does not rush away. He simply ceases to be a separate thing from the trees around him. This is how the deep-wood patrol rangers of [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) operate — they do not hide from the forest. They become it.

 

*   **Perception DC 20 (non-elves) / DC 16 (elves):** A character who immediately looks up catches a brief silhouette moving along a high branch — Glorandal is traveling by canopy, the preferred movement method of Meldarin patrol rangers in deep forest
*   **Survival DC 20 (tracking):** No ground trail. Glorandal left by the branches. Tracking him would require canopy pursuit through dense old-growth — effectively impossible without flight or similar magic

### DM Guidance: Running This Encounter

#### Tone & Pacing

Where Wyn's encounter should feel like a **moment of destiny**, Glorandal's should feel like a **moment of homecoming** — even if the elf PC has never been to [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) territory. The Elvish greeting, the formal protocol, the shared cultural references — these create an instant sense of belonging. The elf PC is being recognized not just as a capable adventurer but as **one of the People**, trusted with a matter that concerns all elvenkind.

 

The pacing should be **measured and deliberate** — more formal than Wyn's encounter. Glorandal does not hurry because elves do not hurry. But he is gravely serious. The Gift bears [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md)'s image. Its loss is not something to discuss lightly. Total encounter time: 5–10 minutes of real table time.

 

#### What If the Party Refuses?

Glorandal accepts a refusal with quiet gravity. He does not threaten or manipulate. But his response carries a different weight than Wyn's — because he is speaking to kin.

 

_"The People remember their own. As do the trees."_

 

He fades into the forest without another word. The wooden coin is not offered. The elf PC may later hear through clan channels that others were asked — and answered. The shame is not punitive. It is simply the knowledge that when the People needed them, they chose not to come. The quest remains available through other starters.

 

#### What If the Party Has No Elf?

This starter is specifically designed for parties with an elf character. If the party has no elf, use a different Gift of Beauty quest starter — GOB-QS01 (Wyn's Mission) for a ranger or druid, or a future starter for other party compositions. Glorandal does not approach humans, halflings, or gnomes. Not because they are unworthy, but because he recruits through the networks he trusts — and those networks are the **elven clans and the forest communities**.

 

#### Using Both Starters Together

If the party has both a human ranger/druid and an elf character, the DM can use **both** GOB-QS01 and GOB-QS02 in sequence. Wyn approaches first with the human ranger hook. Then, a day or two later, Glorandal appears with the elven confirmation. This creates a powerful narrative beat: two separate networks — the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) and [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) — have independently identified this party as the right people for the job. The convergence reinforces the quest's importance and tells the players that multiple factions are watching.

 

#### Connecting to the Next Phase

After Glorandal's encounter, the party's next step is to travel south to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) in Deleveu Woods, near [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) on the [Celene](celene-organization.md) border. The journey takes the party through increasingly dangerous territory — the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) border country where the Earth Temple's raiders operate.

 

For an elf PC, the approach to [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) carries additional meaning. This is the settlement where elves, gnomes, and humans live under the old pacts — where Caer Ostverk's elven garrison watches the border, where the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) hold spiritual authority, and where Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) governs with the diplomatic balance of his dual heritage. An elf arriving at Deleveu Manor is not entering foreign territory — they are entering a place where the People have influence, presence, and roots.

 

When the party arrives at [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) and presents the wooden coin, Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) recognizes it immediately. If the elf PC delivers it, Deleveu may respond with a few words of Elvish — a sign of respect and shared identity that tells the elf they have arrived among friends. Lady [Mariya Deleveu](mariya-deleveu-person.md) helps prepare them. Corporal Janic provides testimony. The quest proper begins.

### Table-Tested: The Harlan Encounter

This quest starter was play-tested during the **Cults of Orcus** campaign (Sessions 10–24). The target PC was **Junior Ranger Harlan**, an elf character traveling with companions through [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) territory.

 

Glorandal appeared along a forest trail, opened with the Elvish greeting _"Greetings ent thar via / avia lyth junior ranger Harlan,"_ and delivered the mission as described above. The key moments that played well at the table:

 

*   **The Elvish opening** immediately separated this encounter from standard quest-giver NPCs. The elf player recognized the cultural significance before the content was even delivered
*   **The "elven blood" description of Deleveu** gave the elf PC a personal stake — they were not being sent to a stranger, but to a kinsman by blood
*   **The Gift's carvings of [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md)** hit harder for an elf player than the generic "beautiful craftsmanship" line. A box with the Creator's face, stolen and lost — that is not a political problem. That is a violation of something sacred
*   **Non-elf party members** were intrigued by the Elvish exchange and asked the elf PC to translate — creating natural party roleplay as the elf decided how much to share

 

**DM takeaway:** The elven cultural framing does double work. It gives the elf PC a deeper personal stake than the generic "go find the missing thing" hook. And it creates **party dynamics** — the non-elf characters see the elf PC being approached as kin, spoken to in a language they may not share, and asked to answer a call that connects to an identity the human characters can observe but never fully access. That tension is productive. It makes the quest personal for the whole group, not just the elf.

##### Glorandal's Wooden Coin

_A small round token carved from dense heartwood, no larger than a gold piece. One face bears an etching of the Oak Father — a gnarled ancient oak with roots spreading like veins — dyed in forest green. The other face is smooth, unmarked. It weighs almost nothing. It smells faintly of pine resin and old leaves._

**Purpose:** Gnarley Ranger authentication token. When presented to Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md), it identifies the bearer as someone sent through the ranger-clan network — a trusted contact operating off-the-books on behalf of the forest and its guardians.

**Recognition:** Lord Deleveu recognizes it immediately — and for an elf-delivered coin, he may respond with Elvish words of welcome. Hestomalkor [Talarien Carewlein](talarien-carewlein-person.md) at Caer Ostverk would also recognize the coin system. The [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) and [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) scouts share this authentication method.

**Symbolism:** The Oak Father is an aspect of [Obad-Hai](obad-hai-person.md). The coin connects this mission to both the druidic network and the elven patrol network. Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) blessed the Gift of Beauty. [Ehlonna](ehlonna-person.md) and Rillifane Rallathil watch over [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md)'s sacred groves. The forest's guardians — both human and elven — want the Gift recovered.

 

##### The Four Clans of the Gnarley

_The [Elves of the Gnarley Forest](elves-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) are four kindreds bound by oath and covenant:_

**[Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md)** — "Concord of Hidden Streams." Diplomatic confederation of nine wood-elf tribes. Seat at Alaeduril (Harmonious Dells). Known for inclusive kinship, master bowcraft, and oath-law. Glorandal Palee serves this clan.

**[Clan Sherendyl](clan-sherendyl-organization.md)** — "The Hidden Fortress." Largest soldiery among the four clans. Seat at Düalarn. Disciplined, logistics-focused, leans toward formal Celene alignment.

**[Clan Fealefel](clan-fealefel-organization.md)** — "Arcane Sentinels of the Twilight Trees." Democratic magocracy guarding ley lines and planar seams. Reserved toward outsiders.

**[Clan Enlanefel](clan-enlanefel-organization.md)** — "The Quiet Flame." Smallest and most contemplative. Seat at Oakvein. Keepers of rites, relics, and long memory.

##### DM Quick Reference — Skill Checks

*   **Perception DC 16 (elves) / DC 20 (non-elves):** Noticed Glorandal tracking from canopy before he descended
*   **Insight DC 12:** Glorandal has already decided — this is formal protocol, not improvisation
*   **Nature DC 10 (elf PCs only):** Leaf-pommel blade and ashwood bow = Meldarin craftsmanship. Deep-wood patrol ranger, not a border scout
*   **History DC 14:** Clan Meldarin — diplomatic confederation of nine wood-elf tribes at Alaeduril, western Gnarley Forest
*   **Perception DC 20 (non-elves) / DC 16 (elves):** Catches Glorandal's silhouette moving along a high branch after departure
*   **Survival DC 20 (tracking):** No ground trail — Glorandal left by canopy. Impossible to follow without flight

##### Glorandal's Dialogue Beats (In Order)

*   **1\. Elvish greeting:** "Greetings ent thar via / avia lyth, Junior Ranger..." (in Elvish, to elf PC only)
*   **2\. The mission (vague):** "Rumors abound that the Gift of Beauty has been... removed..."
*   **3\. No payment:** "I am not hiring and have no gold..."
*   **4\. The direction:** "Travel to Deleveu Woods and seek Lord Deleveu"
*   **5\. The coin + elven blood:** "Present this to Lord Deleveu, a good friend of the elves with elven blood..." (hands over wooden coin)
*   **6\. Appeal to fame (in Common):** "Rewards can be sought in many forms..." (addresses full party)
*   **7\. Gift description (if asked):** Ipp wood, Corellon Larethian carvings, Archdruid blessing, Borderer transport
*   **8\. Vanish:** Steps back, forest closes around him — canopy exit

##### Key Cultural Notes for the DM

*   **Elvish Opening:** Deliver the greeting in Elvish first, then translate. Or pass the elf player a note and let them decide what to share
*   **Deleveu's Heritage:** Half-elven. Lives by elven values. Glorandal calls him kin. This transforms the patron from "foreign lord" to "one of us"
*   **Corellon's Image:** The Gift bears the Creator's face. For elves, its theft borders on sacrilege — not just politics
*   **Clan Obligation:** A request from a Meldarin Ranger Knight carries weight under the Code of the People. Refusal is possible but socially costly
*   **Dual Starter:** Can run GOB-QS01 (Wyn) + GOB-QS02 (Glorandal) in sequence for parties with both ranger/druid and elf characters

##### Road Encounters (Gnarley Forest to Deleveu)

*   **Elven patrol:** [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md) scouts observe the party from canopy — they do not interfere but an elf PC may spot them (Perception DC 18). If spotted, the scouts offer a brief nod of recognition. They know Glorandal sent this group
*   **Gnome caravan wreckage:** Dead gnomes in purple surcoats, ogre body, golden gargoyle mask on monk, stony armor warrior with triangular symbol — Earth Temple raiders, foreshadows priory
*   **Ostverk approach:** Elven sentries from Caer Ostverk challenge the party at the border. An elf PC presenting the wooden coin receives a formal greeting and passage. Non-elves are questioned more carefully
*   **Blackthorn Orc scouts:** Wilderness encounter, escalating danger near [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) territory
