AO-19. The Oath-Hall 'Counting Room'
====================================

> The formal hall where knights of the Azure Oath once swore their oaths of service beneath a modestly vaulted ceiling, surrounded by honor plaques listing every name that ever held the order's shield. A raised dais against the far wall held the chapter's altar-stone where initiates knelt. Now the hall belongs to the Earth Temple under [Romag](romag-person.md) — converted into a counting room where stolen goods are sorted, weighed, catalogued, and packed for shipment to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). The honor plaques are defaced with tally marks. A knight's broken oath-ring serves as a paperweight. Shipping manifests on the dais connect the priory's raids to waystations across the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) corridor — the [Laughing Dog Inn](the-laughing-dog-inn-landmark.md), the Ostverk road junctions, and beyond. This is the financial heart of the operation.

_Great Oath-Hall of the Azure Oath — Now the Earth Temple Counting Room and Logistics Center_

* * *

This room sits in the shadow of the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) — smaller than AO-14's soaring nave, but still a room of weight and purpose. The Oath-Hall — where every knight of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) who served the Azure Oath chapter began and ended their career. Against the far wall, a raised stone dais held the chapter's altar-stone, where initiates knelt, placed their sword-hand flat on the cold granite, and swore the Oath of Vigilance: to guard the Long Road, to shelter the pilgrim, to stand between the traveler and the dark. The ceiling is modestly vaulted — perhaps twelve feet at its peak — with stone ribs carved with the names of the order's patron saints. Along both long walls, oak-framed honor plaques listed every knight who had served — from the priory's founding in the third century CY to its last custodian, **Halven Sere**.

Now the Oath-Hall is a warehouse.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) has converted the great hall into its logistics center — what the garrison calls the counting room. Stolen goods arrive from **AO-21. The Supply Store** in rough crates and sacks; here they are sorted by type and value, weighed on a merchant's scale stolen from a Verbobonc trading house, catalogued in a leather-bound ledger, and packed into transport crates marked with Earth Temple waystation codes. The honor plaques along the walls are defaced — some scored with knife-marks, others covered in chalk tally marks tracking shipment counts. The vaulted ceiling rises overhead, but the names carved into the ribs have been chiseled away wherever a man standing on a crate could reach. The altar-stone on the dais now serves as a packing table, its surface stained with candle wax, ink, and the grime of a hundred opened crates.

Two or three [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) workers — quartermasters, not front-line fighters — manage the room at any given time. They report to [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md), who runs the raiding operations that fill this room, and to **Lucius Graeme**, who ensures the tribute quotas demanded by [Romag](romag-person.md) are met on schedule. A single Black Earth Guard is posted at the main entrance — not to protect the goods, but to ensure the quartermasters don't skim.

##### Entering the Oath-Hall

The door opens onto a room about the size of a large bedchamber, but taller — the ceiling is modestly vaulted, stone ribs arching perhaps twelve feet overhead. Whatever names were carved up there have been chiseled into illegibility — only fragments remain, half-letters and broken words that once meant something to someone.  
  
The hall is filled with cargo. Crates are stacked along both walls in rough rows, some open, some nailed shut and marked with chalk symbols you don't recognize. Sacks of grain lean against a merchant's scale — brass, good quality, clearly stolen. At the far end, a raised stone platform holds a heavy stone slab that might once have been an altar. Now it's a work surface: ledgers, ink pots, a guttering candle, and a small iron ring that someone is using to hold down a stack of parchment.  
  
Along the walls, wooden plaques hang at odd angles. Most have been scored with knife-marks or covered in chalk tallies, but a few still show fragments of engraved names — lists of some kind, partially defaced. One plaque near the door is untouched: a column of names in formal script, the last entry reading simply _Halven Sere, Custodian, 571 CY_.  
  
Two workers in brown tunics are sorting goods at a long table near the center of the room. A guard in a Black Earth tabard watches them from the doorway, arms crossed, looking bored.

### Features of the Oath-Hall

#### The Honor Plaques

The oak-framed plaques lining both long walls once held the complete roster of every knight who served the Azure Oath chapter of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md). Generations of names, ranks, and dates of service — a record of devotion spanning nearly three centuries. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has defaced most of them. Some are scored with knife-cuts, others covered in chalk tally marks tracking shipment counts or inventory figures. A few have been pulled from the wall entirely, the nails still protruding from bare stone.

But the desecration was not systematic. The quartermasters treated it as casual vandalism — a way to keep count — rather than doctrinal destruction. This means some plaques survive partially intact.

**DC 10 Perception**  
One plaque near the main door is completely untouched. The last name on the list: _Halven Sere, Custodian, Appointed 571 CY_. The quartermasters either didn't notice it or didn't care.

**DC 14 History**  
The naming conventions on the surviving fragments follow [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) military tradition: rank, name, oath-date, and the knight's chosen virtue (a word inscribed beneath each entry — _Vigilance, Valor, Endurance, Justice_). A character who cross-references these names with the burial registry in **AO-16. The Scriptorium** can identify which knights are interred in the crypts below — and which tombs [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has already opened.

**DC 17 Religion (Cleric or Paladin of Heironeous)**  
The plaques are not merely records — they are **consecrated memorials**. Each name was inscribed during a formal ceremony. The plaques themselves carry faint traces of divine investiture. A cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who touches an intact plaque feels a momentary pulse of recognition — the dead know they have been remembered. This echo is fading. Within a year, the desecration and neglect will erase it entirely.

* * *

#### The Shipping Manifests

On the raised dais — the former altar-stone — sits the counting room's most campaign-critical find: a stack of shipping manifests held down by a broken paladin's oath-ring being used as a paperweight.

The manifests are written in Common with [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) shorthand codes. They document the outbound flow of stolen goods from the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) to downstream receivers across the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) corridor and beyond. Each manifest lists a date, a cargo description (coded but decipherable), a waystation destination, and a carrier name or mark.

**DC 10 Investigation**  
The manifests confirm systematic, ongoing theft from Long Road caravans. Cargo types include trade goods, weapons, coin, and items marked _"spec"_ — special items flagged for [Romag](romag-person.md)'s personal attention at [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

**DC 14 Investigation**  
The waystation codes resolve to real locations:

*   **"LD"** — the [Laughing Dog Inn](the-laughing-dog-inn-landmark.md), the roadside inn on the Long Road south of [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md). Goods are staged here for pickup by secondary carriers
*   **"OX-3"** — a road junction three miles east of [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md), used as a dead-drop for coin shipments
*   **"NB"** — [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). Final destination for high-value tribute and special items
*   **"HM-E"** — a waystation and storage house two miles east of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) along the High Road, at the edge of the marsh. Staging area for goods moving between the priory and [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)
*   **"IR-C"** — the Wainwright house at the bridge crossing over the Imeryds Run into [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). Final transfer point where the priory's carriers hand off to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)\-side receivers

**DC 17 Investigation**  
Cross-referencing the manifests with intelligence in **AO-16. The Scriptorium** reveals the complete supply chain: [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiders intercept caravans on the Long Road → goods are brought to **AO-21. The Supply Store** → sorted and catalogued here in the Oath-Hall → packed for transport → carried by secondary couriers to waystations → final shipment to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) via the Imeryds Run barges. The entire operation runs on a roughly tenday cycle. The manifests show that the priory has been operating this supply chain for **at least four months**.

**Campaign Note:** If the party recovers these manifests and delivers them to Verbobonc authorities — the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) under **Wilfrick Revepaix**, the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md), or the Viscounty's intelligence service — it provides enough evidence to shut down the waystation network and interdict the supply line between the priory and [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). This has real consequences: without the supply line, the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) at the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) loses a significant revenue stream.

* * *

#### The Broken Oath-Ring

Sitting on the stack of manifests as a paperweight — a small, heavy object that the quartermasters treat as nothing more than a convenient weight — is a paladin's oath-ring. Heavy iron, inscribed with the founding vow of the Azure Oath chapter, bent but not broken. It is almost identical to the one recovered from [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s trophy wall in **AO-18**.

**DC 12 Religion**  
This is a genuine oath-ring of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) — the kind given to a knight upon swearing the order's vow. It would have been interred with its owner. This one was taken from the crypts below.

**DC 15 Religion (Paladin or Cleric of Heironeous)**  
The ring still holds a faint divine charge — not enough to function as a magic item, but enough to feel warm to the touch of a faithful servant of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). If carried into the crypts, the ring resonates faintly near the tomb it was taken from. A paladin who wears it feels a sense of borrowed resolve — advantage on one saving throw against fear while in the crypt level (DM's discretion, one-time use).

* * *

#### The Stolen Cargo

The crates and sacks filling the hall represent weeks of accumulated raiding along the Long Road. Not all of it is valuable — some is bulk goods (grain, cloth, raw iron) that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) uses to supply its own garrison. But scattered among the mundane freight are items that tell the story of the raids:

*   **Gnome-crafted tools and clockwork** from [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) — precision instruments, gem-cutting equipment, and a half-assembled clockwork music box. These were taken from gnome caravans on the Long Road. Returning them to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) or the Greenway Valley gnome communities would generate significant goodwill
*   **A crate of Verbobonc-stamped trade goods** — bolts of dyed wool, sealed wine casks, and a merchant's strongbox (empty, lock broken) bearing the seal of the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md). The raid that took this shipment would have been reported to the Consortium — returning the goods connects the priory to a specific theft
*   **Weapons and armor** stripped from caravan guards — mismatched, piled in a corner, most of it second-rate. One suit of chain mail, however, bears the insignia of a Viscounty road warden. That guard is either dead or imprisoned in **AO-20**
*   **A small locked coffer** (Thieves' Tools DC 13) containing 45 gp, 120 sp, and a velvet pouch holding three uncut amethysts (25 gp each). This is petty cash — skimmed tribute that the quartermasters were supposed to have packed for [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). The fact that it's still here suggests someone is stealing from the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) — or the next shipment hasn't been packed yet

* * *

#### The Tribute Ledger

On the sorting table near the center of the room — where the quartermasters work — sits a leather-bound ledger titled _Earth-Due_ in Common script. This is the counting room's master record: a running tally of everything received, everything sorted, and everything shipped. The handwriting changes between entries — the quartermasters take turns recording, and none of them writes particularly well.

**DC 10 Investigation**  
The ledger is an accounting document. Dates, cargo types, quantities, and a column marked _"Due NB"_ — tribute owed to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). [Romag](romag-person.md)'s quotas are strict.

**DC 14 Investigation**  
The ledger shows a **shortfall**. The last three tenday cycles have fallen below quota. Margin notes in a different hand — sharper, more educated — read: _"M.B. — raiding yield declining. Patrols increasing. Advise shift to night ops only."_ This is **Lucius Graeme**'s handwriting — the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s political operative, noting that [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiders are producing less because Verbobonc patrols have intensified.

**DC 17 Investigation**  
One entry near the back of the ledger is circled three times and marked _"SPEC — do not ship. T.S. holds."_ The date matches the approximate arrival of the **Gift of Beauty** at the priory. "T.S." is [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md). The Gift passed through this room before being secured in **AO-18** — it was logged like any other piece of cargo.

### Encounter: The Quartermasters

The counting room is staffed by two [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) quartermasters who manage inventory, sort cargo, and pack tribute shipments. They are not soldiers. They are clerks: men who joined the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) for steady coin, not doctrinal conviction.

*   **Tomas Vedder** — a thin, meticulous former merchant's bookkeeper from [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) who keeps the ledger in obsessively neat columns and knows every shorthand code in the system by heart
*   **Pendel Marsh** — a broad-shouldered displaced farmer from the Velverdyva bottomlands who drifted into the cult after losing his land to debt, and now moves crates and counts sacks with the resigned patience of a man who has stopped asking questions

One **Black Earth Guard** watches them — **Guardsman Relk Tarne**, a scarred, bored veteran of [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiding company who drew counting room duty as punishment for mouthing off to **Sergeant Drogath "Stonejaw" Varn**. His job is loss prevention, not room defense. He treats the quartermasters with open contempt and spends most of his time leaning against the door frame.

#### Running the Quartermasters

These are not committed enemies. If the party enters with weapons drawn, the quartermasters' first instinct is **surrender**. They are unarmed (the guard carries the only weapon in the room besides belt knives). They will lie, beg, offer information, or attempt to flee depending on how the party approaches:

*   **If threatened:** They give up immediately. _"We just count the cargo! We don't raid anyone! Please — we're not fighters!"_ This is mostly true. Intimidation DC 10 makes them talk freely about the counting room operations, the shipment schedule, and the waystation codes
*   **If bribed or offered mercy:** **Tomas Vedder** is the most useful. He knows the ledger system inside and out, can explain every shorthand code, and will draw a rough map of the supply chain on request. Persuasion DC 12. He wants to survive more than he wants to protect the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)
*   **If asked about specific people:** They know [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) by reputation (terrifying, efficient, never enters this room without a complaint about tribute quotas). They know **Lucius Graeme** (comes in twice a tenday to review the ledger, always polite, always watching). They have never spoken directly to [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and fear him
*   **If asked about prisoners:** They know prisoners are held in **AO-20** and that some were taken from caravans they catalogued the cargo from. **Tomas** can identify which shipments came from caravans that also produced prisoners — matching stolen goods to specific victims. **Pendel** has carried supplies to the prisoner cells and knows the corridor layout

#### The Guard

**Relk Tarne** is more dangerous but less informed. He knows the counting room, the guard rotation, and the corridor layout. He does not know the ledger codes, the waystation network, or the command structure above [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md). If combat starts, he fights. If overwhelmed (outnumbered 3-to-1 or reduced to half HP), he attempts to flee toward **AO-18** to alert [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md).

**Tactical Note:** The room is roughly 20 feet by 15 feet — cramped with cargo, leaving narrow aisles between crate stacks that provide partial cover throughout. The guard uses the cargo as fighting positions if pressed. The quartermasters cower behind the sorting table or attempt to slip out through the west door toward **AO-20**.

### DM Notes

#### The Financial Heart

AO-19 is not a combat room — it is an **intelligence room**. The manifests, the ledger, the waystation codes, and the quartermasters' testimony are collectively the most complete picture of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s logistical operations available anywhere outside [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). If **AO-16. The Scriptorium** is the intelligence den — where [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiders track what's coming in — then the Oath-Hall is its complement: where the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) tracks what's going out.

Together, AO-16 and AO-19 provide the party with a complete map of the supply chain from caravan interception to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) delivery. This is the kind of evidence that changes the campaign — not through combat, but through **information**. A party that hands this intelligence to the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) under **Wilfrick Revepaix**, the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md), or Verbobonc's intelligence service can cripple the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s revenue stream across the entire [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) corridor.

#### The Oath-Hall's Sacred Layer

The desecration here is different from AO-14 (the chapel) or AO-18 ([Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s quarters). In the chapel, the desecration is spiritual — the altar warped, the wards cracked, the divine presence contested. In AO-18, the desecration is doctrinal — [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) displays the relics as proof of his philosophy. Here, the desecration is **banal**. The hall where knights swore to protect travelers is now where stolen traveler's goods are counted and shipped. The honor plaques are used as tally boards. A paladin's oath-ring — a sacred object representing a lifelong vow to [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) — is used to hold down paperwork.

This is the most insulting room in the priory, not because someone intended the insult, but because **no one bothered to think about it**. The quartermasters don't know what the plaques meant. They don't know what an oath-ring is. They just needed a room with flat surfaces and a door that locks. The indifference is worse than the malice.

#### Connecting to the Supply Chain

The waystation network revealed by the manifests should feel like pulling a thread. Each code name resolves to a real location the party can visit:

*   **The [Laughing Dog Inn](the-laughing-dog-inn-landmark.md)** — already a live node in the campaign. **Varek the Smiler (Smiley)** runs the inn and has ties to **Lareth the Beautiful**. **Cookie**, the inn's cook, is a Fire Temple agent reporting to **Alrrem** via carrier pigeon. The manifests confirm the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) also uses the inn as a staging point — meaning three factions (Earth, Fire, and Lareth/Lolth) are all running operations through the same roadside inn without fully realizing it
*   **The Ostverk junctions** — dead-drops near [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) that the party can stake out or report to authorities
*   **The Wainwright house at the Imeryds Run crossing** — the bridge crossing into [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). The final handoff point. Shutting this down requires either military action or political leverage — a mission unto itself
*   **The [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) waystation** — a storage house two miles east of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) along the High Road at the marsh's edge. Someone is operating a staging area for stolen goods right under [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md)'s nose. Finding them opens a new investigation thread

Let the party decide which threads to pull. Each one leads deeper into the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)'s network.

#### The Quartermasters as NPCs

**Tomas Vedder** and **Pendel Marsh** are the most sympathetic enemies in the priory. They are not fanatics. They are not soldiers. They are low-level functionaries who ended up working for an evil organization because the pay was better than honest work and nobody asked hard questions. If the party takes them prisoner, they cooperate fully. If released, they scatter — neither will return to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md).

**Tomas** is the most useful long-term. The former [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) merchant's clerk can serve as a witness before Verbobonc authorities, decode additional [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) documents the party finds later, and identify stolen goods by their merchant marks. He is also terrified that [Romag](romag-person.md)'s people will find him. Protecting him becomes a minor campaign thread if the party keeps him alive. **Pendel** is less useful as a witness but knows the physical layout of the priory's storage areas better than anyone — he has carried crates to every room on the main level.

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I read the manifests on the dais."** — Investigation DC 10 (systematic theft confirmed), DC 14 (waystation codes decoded — Laughing Dog, Ostverk, Nulb, Hommlet marsh waystation, Wainwright house at Imeryds Run), DC 17 (complete supply chain mapped when cross-referenced with AO-16)
*   **"I check the ledger."** — Investigation DC 10 (accounting document, tribute quotas to Nulb), DC 14 (shortfall noted, Lucius Graeme's handwriting, patrols increasing), DC 17 (Gift of Beauty logged as "SPEC — T.S. holds")
*   **"I look at the plaques on the wall."** — Perception DC 10 (one intact plaque near door — Halven Sere's name), History DC 14 (Heironeous naming conventions, cross-ref with burial registry), Religion DC 17 (consecrated memorials with fading divine investiture)
*   **"I pick up the paperweight."** — Religion DC 12 (genuine oath-ring from the crypts), DC 15 for a paladin/cleric of Heironeous (faint divine charge, resonates near its tomb, one-time fear save advantage in crypts)
*   **"I search the crates."** — No check needed for general contents. The gnome-crafted goods, Silver Consortium seal, and road warden chain mail are visible. The locked coffer requires Thieves' Tools DC 13 (45 gp, 120 sp, 3 amethysts at 25 gp each)
*   **"I interrogate the quartermasters."** — Intimidation DC 10 (full cooperation on room operations), Persuasion DC 12 (bookkeeper explains entire ledger system and draws supply chain map). They know Maris by reputation, Lucius from his visits, fear Tharvok, and can connect cargo to specific prisoners in AO-20
*   **"I fight the guard."** — Single Black Earth Guard. Flees toward AO-18 if overwhelmed. Quartermasters surrender or flee toward AO-20
*   **"I take the ledger and manifests."** — No check. Campaign intelligence. Delivering them to Verbobonc authorities can shut down the supply chain
