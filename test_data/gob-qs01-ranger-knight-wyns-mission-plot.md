GOB-QS01-Ranger-Knight-Wyns-Mission
===================================

### The Wilderness Door — A Quest Hook for Rangers & Druids

_"I am not hiring and have no gold or other influence to bestow. I know of no official summons to this task. I am but a servant of the woodlands and unknown to the powers that would have their way in the Viscounty."_

 

This quest starter is designed for a DM whose party includes a **ranger, druid, or nature-aligned character** — someone with ties to the wilderness, the Old Faith, or the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md). Senior Ranger Knight [Wyn](wyn-person.md) approaches the PC personally, appeals to their shared values, and points them toward [Lord Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) at [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) — the man who lost the Gift on his watch and the quest's primary patron.

 

This is **one of several starter articles** for the Gift of Beauty questline. Different starters exist for different party compositions and campaign entry points. A DM should choose the starter that best fits their group's class makeup and current location in the campaign.

 

* * *

 

### Prerequisites & Timing

#### When Does This Happen?

This encounter works best during the **Festival of Readying (576 CY)** or shortly after — when rumors of the Gift's disappearance are circulating but not yet confirmed. The Viscount's reception has been overshadowed by street slander. Merchants at Asbury Manor and other noble houses mention that the crafted gift from the common folk has not been delivered to Her Fey Majesty, Queen Yolande of [Celene](celene-organization.md). Nothing substantial — and after all, it is merely rumor.

 

*   **Ideal Level:** 3–5 (party has proven themselves capable but is not yet famous)
*   **Prerequisite Events:** The party has been active in the southern Viscounty — clearing bandits, aiding locals, traveling the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) roads. Wyn has been watching. He does not approach strangers.
*   **Target PC:** A ranger, druid, or nature-aligned character. Ideally someone with Gnarley Forest connections, Old Faith ties, or a reputation for woodsmanship. Wyn addresses this character _by name_.
*   **Location:** Flexible. The encounter works along any riverbank, forest edge, or wilderness trail in the southern Viscounty. The original play-test placed this at **Cienega Valley** along the Nigbs Run, but it can occur at **Thaymouth**, along the High Road south of [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), or anywhere Wyn's patrol territory intersects the party's route.

 

* * *

 

### The Encounter: Wyn Approaches

#### Setting the Scene

The party is traveling, resting, or passing through settled territory near the edge of wild country. The [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) is nearby — or the Kron Hills foothills, or the vineyard country of the southern Viscounty where cultivated fields give way to untamed brush along the riverbanks. The key is proximity to wilderness. Wyn does not appear in cities or crowded markets. He emerges from the wild.

 

Stepping from the brush along the riverbank, a tall woodsman in well-worn leathers regards your group with quiet assessment. A shimmering green cloak hangs from broad shoulders, clasped at the throat with a golden acorn. His face is weather-beaten, his eyes sharp and unhurried — the gaze of a man who has spent more years reading forest trails than human faces. He carries a longbow across his back and a hand-and-a-half sword at his hip, both kept with the unconscious care of long use. He turns — not to the group, but to one of you specifically.

 

**Wyn addresses the ranger or druid PC by name.** This is critical. He has been observing this character — through his own patrols, through reports from other [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md), or through the druid network that connects the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) communities. He knows their name, their deeds, and their character. He does _not_ approach blindly.

 

*   **Perception DC 18:** A character who was watching the brush noticed a flicker of green cloak a few moments before Wyn stepped out — he was observing the group before revealing himself
*   **Insight DC 14:** Wyn is genuinely assessing whether this is the right person. His approach is deliberate but not rehearsed — he is making a judgment call
*   **Nature/Survival DC 12:** The golden acorn clasp is the insignia of a Senior Ranger Knight of the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) — a rank of significant authority

 

#### Wyn's Opening — The Personal Appeal

Wyn speaks directly to the target PC. His tone is respectful but measured — he is a superior addressing a promising junior, not a lord issuing commands. He has heard of their deeds and is satisfied they are worthy of trust.

 

"I have heard great things of your travels and mission. That you have found mighty companions and served the lords of this land well."

 

He pauses. Then, with the careful phrasing of a man who deals in deniable intelligence:

 

"I have a task of grave importance to thee. It seems that there are rumors abound that the 'Gift of Beauty' to our Fey Majesty to the South has been... let's say removed from the control of the Viscount's power. I am not an expert in the ways of the new Viscount, but if one was to confirm this information, and if the item is found to be not in his control, shall it be returned to its rightful hands... well, you have an idea of the rewards that deeds in the name of the folk of Verbobonc can be found."

 

**DM Notes on Wyn's Language:**

*   Wyn speaks in **careful conditionals** — "it seems," "rumors abound," "let's say." He is deliberately vague because he is operating outside official channels
*   He frames the Gift's theft as rumor, not confirmed intelligence. This is plausible deniability — if the party is captured or questioned, Wyn's name is not attached to an official mission
*   He appeals to **duty and the common folk**, not to gold or political ambition. This is how rangers and druids think — the Gift matters because its loss hurts the people of Verbobonc, not because some lord wants his reputation back
*   He does **not** name the Earth Temple, the Sepulchre-Priory, or any cult faction. He may not know those details. He knows the Gift is missing and he knows who is looking for it quietly

 

#### The Party Responds — Wyn's Guidance

When the party (or the target PC's companions) ask what is needed, Wyn makes his position clear:

 

"I am not hiring and have no gold or other influence to bestow. I know of no official summons to this task. I am but a servant of the woodlands and unknown to the powers that would have their way in the Viscounty. Travel to Deleveu Woods and seek Lord Corbin Deleveu. He is loyal to the Viscount and would be pleased to have assistance if it comes in the name of Wilfrick."

 

**Key information delivered:**

*   **No payment.** This is not a mercenary contract. Wyn has no gold, no bounty, no official authority to hire. He is asking for volunteers
*   **No official connection.** Wyn explicitly distances himself from the Viscounty's power structure. He is "a servant of the woodlands" — a Gnarley Ranger acting on conscience, not orders
*   **The destination:** [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) in Deleveu Woods, south of Ostverk. Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) is the quest patron — the man who was entrusted with the Gift and lost it. He has shelter, food, intelligence, and a reason to help
*   **The name drop:** "In the name of Wilfrick" — Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix. This tells the party the mission has tacit Viscounty support even though Wyn himself has no official connection

 

#### The Wooden Coin

[Wyn](wyn-person.md) hands the target PC a small carved token — a **wooden coin** with an etching of the Oak Father dyed in green on one side.

 

"Present this to Lord Deleveu. He will provide you with shelter, food, and information on what must be done."

 

The wooden coin is the quest's **key item**. It serves multiple purposes:

*   **Proof of contact.** Lord Deleveu recognizes the coin as Gnarley Ranger authentication — it tells him the bearer was sent by someone he trusts
*   **Deniable.** A wooden coin with a tree etching carries no official seal, no noble crest, no written order. If the party is searched or questioned, it is just a trinket. The system is designed to leave no paper trail
*   **Old Faith symbolism.** The Oak Father (an aspect of [Obad-Hai](obad-hai-person.md)) connects this mission to the druidic network. Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) blessed the Gift. The wooden coin says: this matter concerns the forest and its guardians
*   **Ranger tradition.** The [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) use this coin system to recruit deniable assets throughout the Viscounty. Other NPCs in the campaign may recognize it

 

#### The Appeal to Fame

Wyn turns to address the rest of the party — particularly any members motivated by reputation, glory, or political ambition:

 

"Rewards can be sought in many forms. Fame and the ballads of the bards will make this task a powerful tool to those seeking fame. If the rumors show true, it would serve both the common folk of Verbobonc and her Viscount. If the item is not in the hands of Her Fey Majesty or her charges, then the Viscounty will not be seen in good light for trade and other political issues. I have no knowledge on such matters, but don't think it would be well for the common folk."

 

**DM Notes:** This speech serves two purposes. First, it gives non-ranger party members a reason to care — fame, political favor, doing right by the common people. Second, it establishes the **political stakes** without Wyn understanding them fully. He is a woodsman, not a diplomat. He knows the Gift matters. He does not know the full web of faction politics that surrounds it. That ignorance is genuine and should be played honestly.

 

#### Wyn's Identity

If asked who he is, Wyn responds with characteristic ranger reserve:

 

"As for whom I am — I am a servant of the Woodlands and the Oaken Father. If a task must be done, I think that many would stand. The main question I have for thee is: will the names sung by the bards in fame be that of yours, or others?"

 

He does not give his name unless the target PC already knows it (through prior Gnarley Ranger contacts or a successful **History DC 16** check — Senior Ranger Knight [Wyn](wyn-person.md), who serves under Knight Ranger [Owain](owain-lynwood-terfellan-person.md) of the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md)).

 

#### The Gift Described

If the party asks what the Gift of Beauty actually is, Wyn provides a physical description — the most he would know from the ranger network:

 

"The 'Gift to Her Fey Majesty' is a beautiful work of craftsmanship. It is a box of Ipp wood that measures nearly 2 hands tall, 3 hands wide, and 5 hands in breadth. There are carvings of Corellon Larethian and other Fey folk on the cover, as well as the joining of the races in the common bond to build and develop this land known as Verbobonc. It is a story of sorts, beautifully worked in wood. The Archdruid of the Western Gnarley blessed the gift and presented the Viscount with the gift. The Borderers took the gift to Deleveu Woods — then onto the border to meet the Knights of Celene."

 

**What Wyn knows:**

*   The Gift's physical description (wood, carvings, size)
*   It was blessed by the Archdruid ([Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md), though Wyn may not name him directly)
*   The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) transported it to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md), then toward the [Celene](celene-organization.md) border
*   It never arrived

 

**What Wyn does NOT know:**

*   Who took it (he has no intelligence on the Earth Temple or Maris Blackshale's raiders)
*   Where it is now (he does not know about the Sepulchre-Priory)
*   The faction politics surrounding it (Lareth, Romag, the inter-cult rivalry)
*   The geomantic excavation, Halven Sere, or anything about the priory's interior

 

Wyn is a **direction arrow**, not an intelligence briefing. He points the party to Deleveu. Deleveu provides the operational details.

 

#### The Vanishing

Once Wyn has delivered his message, handed over the wooden coin, and answered what questions he will, he departs — abruptly and mysteriously.

 

The party looks to each other in consternation. About to ask the Ranger Knight another question, they discover that he has vanished — stepped back into the brush as silently as he emerged. The riverbank is empty. The only proof he was ever there is the wooden coin in your hand, the Oak Father's mark still fresh with green dye.

 

**DM Notes:** Wyn's disappearance is deliberate and in-character. He is a Senior Ranger Knight — Stealth is his profession. He does not linger for extended conversation because (a) he has other patrols to run, (b) he does not want to be seen with the party by anyone who might be watching, and (c) it creates a memorable dramatic beat. The party now holds a mission, a token, and a direction — but no handler. They are on their own.

 

*   **Perception DC 22:** A character who immediately scans the brush catches a flicker of green cloak moving upriver through dense willow — Wyn is already thirty yards away and gaining distance
*   **Survival DC 18 (tracking):** Wyn's trail leads upriver for a quarter mile, then crosses to the opposite bank via a submerged stone ford. After that, it merges with a game trail heading north into the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md). The party cannot catch him unless they abandon their current route entirely

 

### DM Guidance: Running This Encounter

##### Tone & Pacing

This encounter should feel like a **moment of destiny** for the target PC — a recognition from the wider world that their deeds have been noticed. Wyn's approach is personal. He chose _this_ ranger, _this_ druid, because of who they are. That matters. Play it with weight.

 

The pacing should be **unhurried but deliberate**. Wyn does not rush. He makes his case, answers a few questions, and leaves. Total encounter time: 5–10 minutes of real table time. Do not let it stretch into an extended interrogation — Wyn is not an intelligence asset and will politely decline questions he cannot answer.

##### What If the Party Refuses?

Wyn accepts a refusal without argument. He nods once, says "The forest remembers those who answer — and those who do not," and disappears into the brush. The wooden coin is not offered. The quest hook remains available through other starters (the Church of Rao, Lord Deleveu directly, the Nulb intelligence trail, etc.).

##### What If the Party Has No Ranger or Druid?

This starter is specifically designed for parties with nature-aligned characters. If the party lacks a ranger or druid, use a different Gift of Beauty quest starter instead. Wyn does not approach fighters, wizards, or rogues — not because they are unworthy, but because he recruits through the networks he trusts, and those networks are the forest communities.

##### Connecting to the Next Phase

After Wyn's encounter, the party's next step is to travel south to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) in Deleveu Woods. The journey from the central Viscounty to Deleveu's estate takes the party through increasingly dangerous territory — the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) border country where the Earth Temple's raiders operate. The DM should seed road encounters during this journey: gnome caravan wreckage (Earth Temple raids), Blackthorn Orc activity, Scarlet Moon monks inviting travelers to the Rite of the Wicker Giant, and [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) patrols who are suspicious of armed groups on the road.

 

When the party arrives at [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) and presents the wooden coin, Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) recognizes it immediately and receives them as guests. Lady [Mariya Deleveu](mariya-deleveu-person.md) helps prepare them for the journey ahead. Corporal Janic — the surviving Borderer from the ambush — provides testimony. The map to the ambush site is produced. The quest proper begins.

### Table-Tested: The Oorod Encounter

This quest starter was play-tested during the **Cults of Greyhawk Session 8, Group 2** campaign. The target PC was Junior Ranger Oorod, traveling with companions Heinrich Sonnenstrahl, Vighon Draqaro II, and Mascus Gloomleaf through Cienega Valley on Readying the 13th, 576 CY.

 

Wyn appeared along the Nigbs Run riverbank after the party had rested at the Shining Temple of Pelor and explored the village during the Festival of Readying. He addressed Oorod by name, delivered the mission exactly as described above, handed over the wooden coin, and vanished before the party could ask a follow-up question. The party proceeded south toward Hommlet, encountering a destroyed gnome caravan (Clan Tulvar ore shipment — raided by Black Earth cultists wearing golden gargoyle masks and strange stony armor) along the way — their first direct evidence of the organized raiding that connects to the Gift's theft.

 

**DM takeaway:** The gnome caravan ambush scene on the road south is a **powerful foreshadowing beat**. The party sees Earth Temple violence firsthand — dead gnomes in purple surcoats, a survivor searching for his granddaughters, a human body in monk's robes wearing a golden gargoyle mask, a warrior in black stony armor with a strange triangular symbol. They do not yet know these attackers are connected to the Gift. But when they eventually reach the Sepulchre-Priory and find that same triangular symbol on Earth Temple banners, the connection clicks. Seed early. Pay off later.

##### Wyn's Wooden Coin

_A small round token carved from dense heartwood, no larger than a gold piece. One face bears an etching of the Oak Father — a gnarled ancient oak with roots spreading like veins — dyed in forest green. The other face is smooth, unmarked. It weighs almost nothing. It smells faintly of pine resin and old leaves._

*   **Purpose:** Gnarley Ranger authentication token. Presented to Lord Deleveu, it identifies the bearer as someone sent through the ranger network — a trusted contact operating off-the-books on behalf of the forest and its guardians.
    

*   **Recognition:** Lord Corbin Deleveu recognizes it immediately. Other NPCs with Gnarley Ranger connections (Wyn, Owain, the Squirrel's Nest innkeeper) would also recognize the coin system. Casual observers see only a wooden trinket.
    

*   **Symbolism:** The Oak Father is an aspect of [Obad-Hai](obad-hai-person.md). The coin connects this mission to the druidic network — Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) blessed the Gift of Beauty. The forest's guardians want it recovered.
