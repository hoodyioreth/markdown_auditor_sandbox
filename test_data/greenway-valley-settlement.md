Greenway Valley
===============

Greenway Valley is not a pastoral idyll. It is a 35-mile-long supply line—the physical spine of gnome independence, the trade corridor that moves gems, craft goods, and political power between the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) and the wider Verbobonc economy. Every merchant caravan that moves through this valley, every tax levy that passes west from **Kron Village**, every diplomatic envoy that travels the Greenway Road—these are the sinews that hold the Free Alliance together.   In 576 CY, that supply line is under pressure. This article maps the territory as DMs need to see it: not as a historical artifact, but as a living campaign geography where the Temple of Elemental Evil's strategy unfolds in real space. Cut the Greenway, isolate the settlements, and the fragile gnome confederation fractures.  

### EXCERPT

 

**Greenway Valley** is the 35-mile east-west corridor that contains the heart of gnome power in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Five settlements stung along the Greenway Road—[Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) in the west, **Greenrest**, **Barra**, **Wilm**, and **Kron Village** anchored by [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) in the east—form the political and economic foundation of the Free Alliance. This is the territory where the Viscounty's response to the Temple of Elemental Evil must pass through, where the gnome leadership convenes, and where the campaign's deepest vulnerabilities lie.

 

A DM running this campaign must understand Greenway Valley as _geography with political weight_. The valley is only 6 miles wide north to south; the Greenway Road is the only major trade corridor. Disrupt this road, cut the flow of goods and taxes, isolate settlements from one another, and the confederation's unity dissolves. This is not the Dyvers question or the Temple threat in abstract. This is the physical threat to the valley's survival.

 

#### What Greenway Valley Is

 

Greenway Valley runs east to west along a single major trade road. It is six miles wide at its widest (north to south), constrained by rising hills and forest on all sides. The geography is not accidental: the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) form a natural fortress, a defensible pocket carved into the landscape. Within this pocket live roughly 5,000 gnomes who have declared independence from direct [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) rule and organized themselves into a confederation.

 

But geography alone does not hold a confederation together. What holds the Free Alliance together is the **Greenway Road**—the single trade corridor that connects the five settlements strung through the valley. This road is the lifeline through which gem wealth flows out, manufactured goods flow in, taxes are collected, and political authority is enforced. Cut this road in multiple places, and the five settlements stop being a confederation and become five isolated fortifications.

 

This is why understanding Greenway Valley as DM is not an exercise in geography or history. It is an exercise in _campaign vulnerability mapping_. The Temple of Elemental Evil does not need to conquer the valley. It needs only to sever the road.

 

* * *

 

#### Geography: The Greenway Road

 

The Greenway is a well-maintained trade route that cuts straight through the valley from west to east. It is the only road suitable for merchant wagons, caravans, and heavy traffic in the region. The road's condition is maintained by the confederation itself—tolls collected at each settlement fund the repairs and upkeep.

 

**Direction and Continuation:**

 

*   **West from Tulvar:** The Greenway continues deeper into the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), ascending toward passes that lead into the western Viscounty and, beyond that, toward the uncertain frontier where **Dyvers** lies.
*   **East from Kron Fortress:** The Greenway exits through the massive gates of [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) and continues eastward into the Viscounty proper, toward [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md), and ultimately [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) City itself.

 

**Settlements Along the Greenway (West to East):**

 

The valley stretches approximately 35 miles from [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) to **Kron Village**. The settlements are spaced roughly 7 miles apart, each positioned to serve as a trading hub, a defensible point, and a way station for caravans crossing the confederation.

 

*   **Tulvar** (mile 0) — Capital, House Tulvar seat, Moot Hall
*   **Greenrest** (mile ~7) — Trading house stop
*   **Barra** (mile ~14) — Trading house stop
*   **Wilm** (mile ~21) — Trading house stop
*   **Kron Village** (mile ~35) — [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) garrison town

 

Each settlement contains a gnome trading house and a _Satveegr_—an appointed tax and trade agent of the confederation. The Satveegrs report to the **Moot Hall** in Tulvar and enforce confederation law regarding commerce and taxation.

 

**Topography and Defense:**

 

The valley floor is relatively flat where the road passes, but the surrounding terrain is broken by pine forests, hills, and outcroppings of stone. To the north and south, the valley walls rise steadily. The valley narrows toward both its eastern (at Kron Fortress) and western (toward the deeper Kron Hills) ends, creating natural chokepoints.

 

Most importantly: **there is no alternate route through the valley**. Merchants cannot easily bypass the road; caravans cannot cut north or south without abandoning their wagons. This means that any force that controls the Greenway Road controls all commerce flowing through the confederation.

#### Tulvar — The Capital

 

[Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) is where gnome independence began and where it is currently defended. The settlement sits at the far western end of the valley, nestled into the rising slopes of the Kron Hills. It is the seat of [House Tulvar](house-tulvar-organization.md), the family that led the original push for Free Alliance independence, and it remains the political and military heart of the confederation.

 

**Leadership:** [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md) is the Clanlord of House Tulvar and de facto leader of the Free Alliance. He operates from the **Mayoral Residence**, though his real authority flows from the **Moot Hall** (location 2), where the confederation's assembly of clan leaders and marshals convenes. Urthgan is politically astute but not a military commander—he relies on marshals and military captains for tactical decisions.

 

**The Chief Families:**

 

Four clan houses dominate Tulvar's governance:

 

*   [House Tulvar](house-tulvar-organization.md) — Rulers, led by [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md)
*   [Clan Keeleene](clan-keeleene-organization.md) — Second family in influence
*   [Clan Jillink](clan-jillink-organization.md) — Third family in influence
*   [Clan Tillien](clan-tillien-organization.md) — Fourth family in influence

 

Each of these families has a house/estate in Tulvar and sends representatives to the Moot Hall. Political authority is shared among them, though House Tulvar's military and trading wealth gives it the decisive voice. In 576 CY, tensions between these families are rising due to the mine claims crisis (which clan gets rights to which gem deposits) and the question of whether gnome independence can survive a full Viscounty/Temple conflict.

 

**Key Locations in Tulvar:**

 

*   **Prickly Pixie Inn** (location 1) — Tavern run by **Kaleb Greenbottle**. Primary gathering place for travelers, merchants, and local gossip. This is where adventurers hear about valley affairs.
*   **Moot Hall** (location 2) — Regional meeting hall, war room, and village council chambers. This is where the confederation's assembly meets. DMs using Tulvar as a campaign location should know that the Moot Hall is _not_ open to casual visitors—this is where serious political and military decisions are made.
*   **Tulvar Wares** (location 3) — General store run by **Etune Goodbarrel**. Supplies for caravans, crafted goods, basic adventuring equipment.
*   **Tulvar Jail** (location 4) — Holding facility for prisoners. Magistrates from the Moot Hall preside over trials.
*   **Needlebriar Island** (location 5) — Small island in the Greenway containing a statue of **Snandi Tulvar**, the confederation's founder. A symbol of gnome pride and independence.
*   **House Jillink** (location 6) — Clan house of the Jillink family.
*   **Clan House Tillien** (location 7) — Clan house of the Tillien family.
*   **Mayoral Residence** (location 8) — Home and administrative center of [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md).
*   **Maara's Apothecary** (location 9) — Expert herbalist and alchemist run by **Maara Thornhill**. Known to be brilliant but impatient, possibly with wizard ambitions.
*   **Keeleene House** (location 10) — Clan house of the Keeleene family.

 

**Architecture and Design:**

 

Tulvar is built with characteristic gnome pragmatism: cellars carved into bedrock (called _rents_) serve as the foundation for surface houses, warehouses, and administrative buildings. The architecture shows strong Verbobonc Gothic influence—pointed arches, stone work, fortified corners—suggesting that the settlement was designed by a legendary architect at a time when the gnomes still imagined themselves as part of a unified Viscounty. Legend credits **Snirthiglin**, a gnome master builder, with much of Tulvar's current layout.

 

**Population and Garrison:**

 

Tulvar proper houses approximately 1,500 gnomes. In addition, the settlement serves as the confederation's military headquarters. Marshals and militia captains operate from the Moot Hall, and Tulvar can muster approximately 500 soldiers for immediate defense. The rest of the 2,500-strong confederation militia is distributed across the valley's four other settlements.

#### The Greenway Towns: Greenrest, Barra, and Wilm

 

Between Tulvar and Kron Village lie three smaller trading towns. Each follows a similar pattern: a central trading house, a Satveegr's office, a small militia garrison, and a handful of inns and merchants. These towns are not seats of power—they are _way stations_ designed to move commerce and enforce confederation law.

 

**Greenrest** (~7 miles east of Tulvar)

 

Greenrest is the most western of the interior trading towns. It marks the point where the Greenway leaves the immediate vicinity of Tulvar and enters the more exposed middle valley. The settlement is characterized by its **trading house**, a substantial stone structure that serves as merchant warehouse and embassy. A Satveegr appointed by the Moot Hall oversees taxation and dispute resolution. The town has a small militia garrison (roughly 150 gnomes) and the **Dusty Traveler Inn**, a traditional stopping point for caravans.

 

**Barra** (~14 miles east of Tulvar)

 

Barra sits roughly halfway between Tulvar and Kron Village. It is slightly larger than Greenrest and serves as a secondary administrative hub if the Greenway is cut between Tulvar and Kron Village. The trading house here is a two-story structure with underground cellars for storage. The militia garrison is stronger here—approximately 300 gnomes—reflecting the settlement's role as a midpoint stronghold. Barra's key advantage is its position: an army traveling along the Greenway cannot easily bypass it.

 

**Wilm** (~21 miles east of Tulvar)

 

Wilm is the last true settlement before Kron Village and Kron Fortress. It is the eastern anchor of the confederation's interior defenses. Like Barra and Greenrest, it has a trading house and Satveegr's office, but it also maintains a **militia barracks**—a fortified structure where soldiers can be mustered quickly. The garrison here numbers approximately 400 gnomes. Wilm's position makes it crucial: if Kron Village falls to assault or siege, Wilm becomes the confederation's last defensive line against invasion from the east.

 

**The Satveegr System:**

 

Each of the three interior towns is overseen by a _Satveegr_—a confederation official appointed by the Moot Hall. The Satveegr is responsible for:

 

*   Collecting taxes on all merchant goods passing through the settlement
*   Enforcing confederation law (disputes, crimes, contracts)
*   Maintaining the trading house and garrison
*   Reporting to the Moot Hall on caravan traffic, threats, and local conditions

 

In 576 CY, the Satveegrs are under increasing pressure. Caravan traffic is declining due to Temple-induced chaos in the Viscounty. Tax revenue is dropping. The Satveegrs have no authority to raise militia beyond their current garrison—all major military decisions flow through the Moot Hall in Tulvar.

#### Kron Village and Kron Fortress — The Eastern Gate

 

Kron Village sits at the far eastern end of Greenway Valley, at the base of [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md). Together, they form a single defensive complex that seals the valley from the east and controls all traffic flowing between the Free Alliance and the wider Viscounty.

 

**The Fortress:**

 

[Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) is a masterwork of gnome military engineering—a mountain fortress carved partly into living rock, with massive stone gates that can be closed to block all traffic. The fortress was constructed generations ago as a border defense against the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) and the eastern wilds. In the current campaign, it serves as the confederation's primary military stronghold.

 

**Commander:** [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) (female, noted warrior) commands the fortress garrison of **100 elite gnome soldiers**. This is a small garrison for a major fortress, but it reflects the Free Alliance's limited military resources. Jeet is a disciplined commander and fiercely loyal to the confederation; she will hold this fortress to the last gnome if necessary. She communicates regularly with the Moot Hall via messenger riders and messenger service.

 

**Strategic Importance:** The fortress gates are the **only significant checkpoint** through which eastbound merchant traffic passes. Jeet's garrison inspects all caravans, collects taxes, and denies passage to anyone the Moot Hall has flagged as a threat. This gives the Free Alliance enormous power over Viscounty trade flowing west and leverage over eastern powers seeking to trade with the gnome settlements. In 576 CY, with the Temple crisis escalating, Jeet is under standing orders to monitor all military movements and report any Elemental forces attempting to pass through the valley.

 

**Kron Village:**

 

The village at the fortress's base houses approximately 165 gnomes—merchants, craftspeople, military families, and support personnel for the garrison. It is substantially smaller than Tulvar but more heavily fortified. Kron Village is not an administrative center; it exists purely to support the fortress and the trade traffic passing through it.

 

**Key Locations in Kron Village:**

 

*   **Tulvar's Waypoint** (location reference) — Trade embassy and caravan way station run by **Wendellbrim "Wren" Tulvar**, a cousin of [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md). This is House Tulvar's primary trade presence in the east and a crucial link in communication between capital and fortress.
*   **Glopna's Stone Inn** — A sturdy tavern that serves both travelers and garrison soldiers.
*   **Gilded Burrow** — Merchant warehouse and trading post, smaller counterpart to Tulvar's main trading house.

 

**Kron Market:**

 

[Kron Market](1-the-kron-market-landmark.md) is a specialized commerce zone built partly within the fortress complex and partly in carved caverns beneath Kron Village. The market has three distinct tiers, each serving a different clientele:

 

*   **High Warrens** — Upper level. Legitimate merchants, licensed traders, caravans paying the fortress tariff. This is where official commerce happens.
*   **Tunnel Bazaar** — Mid-level cavern market. Semi-legal goods, independent traders, people who want to avoid the tariff. The Satveegr monitors but does not always enforce. In 576 CY, this tier is becoming more active as caravan traffic drops.
*   **Back Alleys** — Lower level. Illegal market, black market goods, weapons, stolen merchandise. Even the Kron Fortress garrison does not patrol here regularly. This is where desperate merchants and unsavory characters do business.

 

Jeet Jimbleclap maintains that "order is order" and keeps the Back Alleys from becoming a haven for Temple agents or organized crime. But with only 100 soldiers and the Tunnel Bazaar already expanding, she is fighting a slow defensive battle.

#### Governance and Law

 

The Free Alliance of the Kron Hills is a confederation of 12 townships across the greater region, but **Greenway Valley** is where its power is concentrated. The governance structure is designed to balance military efficiency with clan autonomy.

 

**The Moot Hall System:**

 

The **Moot Hall** in Tulvar is the confederation's central authority. It functions as:

 

*   **War room** — Military decisions, garrison deployments, fortress commands
*   **Assembly hall** — Where clan leaders vote on confederation policy
*   **Judicial chamber** — Where Satveegrs bring appeals and major crimes
*   **Tax bureau** — Where confederation revenue is calculated and distributed

 

Meetings are called by [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md) but require quorum from all four chief families (Tulvar, Keeleene, Jillink, Tillien). Decisions require consensus or a 3-1 majority. This system is slow but ensures that no single family can dominate.

 

In 576 CY, the Moot Hall is deeply divided over how to respond to the Temple crisis and whether the Free Alliance can afford to maintain independence from the Viscounty. See [Verbobonc Politics in 576 CY](verbobonc-politics-in-576-cy-article.md) for details.

 

**Marshals:**

 

Each settlement has a _Marshal_—an appointed military commander responsible for law enforcement and militia management in that area. The Marshals answer to the Moot Hall and coordinate with one another. In times of crisis, the Marshals can declare martial law within their settlement's territory.

 

**Satveegrs:**

 

Satveegrs are civilian administrators appointed by the Moot Hall to oversee trade, taxation, and law enforcement at the trading towns (Greenrest, Barra, Wilm) and Kron Village. They are not military but they can call on militia to enforce their decisions. They report directly to the Moot Hall and have significant autonomy in day-to-day governance.

 

**Dispute Resolution:**

 

Minor disputes are handled by Satveegrs at the settlement level (property damage, contract disputes, minor theft). Major crimes (murder, treason, organized theft) are appealed to the Moot Hall. Trials are public and witness testimony is weighted heavily. Punishment for major crimes typically involves fine, imprisonment, or exile—execution is rare and requires unanimous Moot Hall consent.

#### Economy and Trade

 

The Free Alliance's wealth flows primarily from two sources: **gem mining** in the deeper Kron Hills and **craftsmanship** in the settled valley. The **Greenway** Road is the conduit through which this wealth moves outward.

 

**Gem Mining:**

 

The deeper regions of the Kron Hills contain rich gem deposits (diamonds, rubies, sapphires). Gnome miners work these deposits and sell refined gems to merchants who sell to wider markets. In 576 CY, the mines are _the_ source of confederation wealth. However, access to mining sites is controlled by the four chief families, and disputes over mining rights are creating serious political tension at the Moot Hall.

 

**Craftsmanship:**

 

Gnomes are exceptional artisans. Tulvar and the trading towns produce:

 

*   Specialized tools and instruments
*   Fine clockwork and mechanical devices
*   Carved furniture and decorative items
*   Metalwork and enchanted items (in limited quantity)

 

These goods are sold through the trading houses and command premium prices in Verbobonc and beyond.

 

**Import/Export Flow:**

 

**Goods flowing WEST (out of the valley):** Gems (unrefined and refined), crafted goods, specialized tools, some metalwork

 

**Goods flowing EAST (into the valley):** Grain and food (the valley cannot fully feed itself), raw metals for crafting, luxuries, wine, cloth

 

The **Greenway Road** is the sole highway for this flow. Merchant caravans travel the road on a rough schedule, paying tolls at each settlement. In normal times, 15-20 caravans per month pass through. In 576 CY, that number is dropping as the Temple crisis makes the wider Viscounty increasingly unstable.

 

**Taxation and Revenue:**

 

The confederation funds itself through tariffs on all goods moving through the Greenway, taxes on mining operations, and contributions from the chief families. Total confederation revenue in a normal year is estimated at 20,000 gold pieces or more. In 576 CY, revenue is declining as caravan traffic drops.

#### The Valley Under Pressure: 576 CY

 

In the current campaign year, Greenway Valley is experiencing four simultaneous crises that threaten its stability. A DM should understand how these abstract problems manifest _geographically_ in the valley.

 

**Crisis 1: Mine Claims**

 

The four chief families are locked in an escalating dispute over mining rights in the deeper Kron Hills. This manifests in Greenway Valley as:

 

*   Reduced gem supply, as mining operations slow due to competing claims
*   Increased Moot Hall meetings as families argue over territorial boundaries
*   Private militias from each family operating in the western hills (not yet in the valley proper, but close)
*   Reduced tax revenue, as gem sales drop

 

**How DMs use this:** PCs traveling in Tulvar overhear arguments about mining claims. A caravan traveling the Greenway might encounter a scouting party from House Keeleene or Clan Jillink surveying a disputed mountain pass. Tax shortages might affect local services in the settlements.

 

**Crisis 2: Caravan Raids**

 

Bandits and Temple-affiliated forces are raiding merchant caravans on roads throughout the Viscounty. The impact on Greenway Valley is direct:

 

*   Fewer merchants are willing to risk the journey to the Kron Hills
*   Caravans are hiring mercenary escorts, driving up trading costs
*   The Satveegrs at Greenrest, Barra, and Wilm are reporting decreased traffic and lower tariff revenue
*   Attacks are beginning to happen _within_ the valley itself—on the Greenway Road between settlements

 

**How DMs use this:** A caravan escorted by PCs is ambushed on the road between Greenrest and Barra. A merchant in Tulvar hires PCs to escort a shipment of gems to Kron Village. Reports of attacks on the road create urgency for the Moot Hall to increase militia patrols—which they cannot afford to do without cutting garrison strength elsewhere.

 

**Crisis 3: Borderer Withdrawal**

 

The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md)—traditionally allies of the Free Alliance and patrollers of the northern foothills—are withdrawing their forces to concentrate on defending the wider Viscounty against Temple incursions. This affects the valley by:

 

*   Removing an important military ally from the confederation's defense network
*   Leaving the northern approaches to Greenway Valley less protected
*   Forcing the Free Alliance to increase militia patrols on its own, stretching already thin forces

 

**How DMs use this:** The Moot Hall receives a formal notice that the Borderers will no longer patrol the northern marches. This is a strategic shift that forces the confederation to either reduce garrison strength elsewhere or accept increased risk of flanking attack. PCs might be sent as messengers to negotiate with Borderer leadership, or might encounter Borderer scouts withdrawing from their posts.

 

**Crisis 4: The Dyvers Question**

 

There is uncertainty about **Dyvers**'s loyalty and intentions. Some in the Moot Hall worry that Dyvers might ally with the Temple or move to seize gnome territory in the western Kron Hills. This manifests in the valley as:

 

*   Increased military spending on western defenses
*   Scouts and patrols on the western passes
*   Diplomatic missions toward the west (PCs might be hired as escorts)
*   Paranoia about Dyvers spies and agents infiltrating the valley

 

**How DMs use this:** The Moot Hall debates whether to send military resources west to defend against potential Dyvers incursion. A merchant reports suspicious figures asking questions about western passes. PCs are hired to scout the western roads or escort a diplomatic envoy toward the Dyvers frontier.

 

**The Cumulative Effect:**

 

These four crises combine to create a single large vulnerability: the Free Alliance's forces are stretched thin, its commerce is declining, and the Moot Hall is divided. Any significant additional pressure—a Temple assault, a major caravan ambush, a forced choice between eastern and western defense—could shatter the confederation's unity.

#### What the Temple Exploits

 

The Temple of Elemental Evil does not need to conquer Greenway Valley. It needs only to **disrupt the Greenway Road**. A DM should understand the strategic logic:

 

The valley's strength is its unity—five settlements connected by a single trade corridor, governed by a confederation that moves goods and authority along that corridor. Cut the road in two or three places, and the five settlements become isolated strongpoints. Cut the road for long enough, and the confederation loses tax revenue, cannot feed itself (the valley imports grain), and fragments politically as individual settlements pursue survival rather than confederation policy.

 

**Tactical Options for Temple Strategy:**

 

*   **Raid the Road:** Small war parties attack caravans and patrols on the Greenway, creating insecurity and driving merchants away.
*   **Siege a Settlement:** A Temple force lays siege to Wilm or Barra, cutting the road at that point and isolating eastern from western settlements. The Free Alliance would be forced to choose: muster all forces for a desperate relief assault, or allow the settlement to fall.
*   **Infiltrate Kron Market:** Temple agents use the Back Alleys of Kron Market to smuggle weapons, intelligence, or agents into the valley proper. Jeet Jimbleclap's 100-gnome garrison is too small to hold all three market tiers.
*   **Incite Political Collapse:** The Temple spreads rumors, foments the mine claims dispute, or turns one of the chief families against the others. If the Moot Hall fractures, the confederation's unified response to Temple threat collapses.

 

All of these strategies depend on the same geographic reality: Greenway Valley is linear, exposed, and defended by a confederation stretched too thin to defend the entire 35-mile corridor simultaneously.

#### DM Notes: Using Greenway Valley at the Table

 

**Travel Times:**

 

Using standard travel rates (4 mph on foot, 8 mph on horseback):

 

*   Tulvar to Greenrest: ~2 hours on foot, ~1 hour on horseback
*   Greenrest to Barra: ~2 hours on foot, ~1 hour on horseback
*   Barra to Wilm: ~2 hours on foot, ~1 hour on horseback
*   Wilm to Kron Village: ~2 hours on foot, ~1 hour on horseback
*   **Tulvar to Kron Village (full journey):** ~8 hours on foot (one long day), ~4 hours on horseback (half day)

 

A caravan with wagons typically makes 10-15 miles per day, so the Tulvar-to-Kron journey takes 2-3 days.

 

**Encounter Placement:**

 

The Greenway Road provides a linear geography ideal for linear encounters:

 

*   **Ambush near Greenrest:** Bandits or Temple-aligned forces attack a caravan in the open terrain between Tulvar and Greenrest.
*   **Gate Encounter at Kron Fortress:** PCs or a caravan encounter Jeet Jimbleclap's garrison checking documents, creating a chance for interrogation, disguise, or negotiation.
*   **Market Encounter in Kron Village:** PCs explore the three tiers of Kron Market, gathering intelligence or making shady contacts.
*   **Settlement Encounter:** PCs arrive in Tulvar, Greenrest, Barra, or Wilm, where they interact with local authorities, gather information, or discover that a settlement is under threat.

 

**Campaign Integration:**

 

Greenway Valley should feature prominently in any Temple of Elemental Evil campaign that includes a Viscounty defense arc:

 

*   **Defense Phase:** The Viscounty must decide how to defend against the Temple. Coordinating that defense requires using Greenway Valley as a supply line and military staging point. PCs might be tasked with securing the road or escorting supplies through the valley.
*   **Crisis Phase:** If the valley itself comes under direct attack (siege, major raid, infiltration), the crisis forces the Moot Hall into difficult decisions. PCs can shape those decisions by their actions on the road, in negotiations, or in direct combat.
*   **Political Phase:** The mine claims dispute and the four-family tensions create opportunities for political intrigue. PCs might be hired by one family to undermine another, or might be sent to mediate disputes. Their choices can affect the confederation's unity going forward.

 

**Key NPCs for DM Use:**

 

*   [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md) — Leader, politically astute but not a warrior. Use for negotiations, Moot Hall scenes, and appeals for military support.
*   [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) — Fortress commander, loyal and disciplined. Use for frontier encounters, fortress scenes, and military intelligence about eastern threats.
*   [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) — Other key leadership figures from linked articles. Consult the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) and related articles for additional NPCs.

 

**Scaling Encounters:**

 

The Free Alliance militia is 2,500 gnomes, distributed roughly as:

 

*   Tulvar garrison: ~500 gnomes
*   Greenrest garrison: ~150 gnomes
*   Barra garrison: ~300 gnomes
*   Wilm garrison: ~400 gnomes
*   Kron Fortress garrison: ~100 gnomes (elite, commanded by Jeet)
*   Reserve/mobile forces: ~350 gnomes (available for rapid deployment)

 

A DM can scale Temple encounters knowing that the Free Alliance is limited. A Temple force of 500+ soldiers would require the confederation to commit nearly half its military to a single engagement. A prolonged campaign of 200+ soldiers raiding the road would force the Free Alliance to either negotiate with the Viscounty for military aid or face attrition.

**WHY GREENWAY VALLEY MATTERS FOR YOUR CAMPAIGN**

 

Greenway Valley is not flavor. It is _the_ most consequential geography in a Viscounty-focused Temple of Elemental Evil campaign. The valley is:

 

*   **The Viscounty's lifeline:** Gem wealth and crafted goods flow out; grain and resources flow in. Cut the flow, and Verbobonc's economy destabilizes.
*   **The gnome confederation's test:** The Free Alliance's claim to independence rests on controlling this territory. Lose it, and gnome independence collapses.
*   **The Temple's target:** A 35-mile-long trade corridor defended by 2,500 stretched-thin soldiers is an attractive target. Disrupt the road, and the Viscounty's capacity to mount a coordinated Temple response fractures.
*   **Your campaign's choke point:** The valley's linear geography makes it ideal for campaigns. Settlements can be besieged, roads can be ambushed, and every encounter has strategic weight.

 

Treat Greenway Valley as the core geography of your middle campaign acts. If the players lose the valley, the Viscounty's defensive strategy collapses. If they secure it, they unlock both economic and military resources for pushing back against the Temple.

 

This is where the campaign is won or lost. Make it matter.

 

### Random Encounters on the Greenway Road

 

Roll **2d6** once per 7-mile segment of travel along the Greenway Road (there are five segments between Tulvar and Kron Village). The bell curve ensures gnome militia patrols and merchant caravans dominate the road, while threats from the Lortmil Mountains and the Gnarley Forest fringes remain rare but present. In 576 CY, the road is under increasing pressure — encounters marked with **†** have escalated since the Mounted Borderer withdrawal.

**Lortmil Mountain Raider** (Airborne)

A lone **wyvern**, **manticore**, or **griffon** hunting from the Lortmil peaks. It circles the valley from altitude, looking for isolated prey — a straggling caravan animal, a lone traveler, or an undefended camp. It will not attack a group of 6+ unless starving. If encountered at night, it is already diving. _DM note: This is a weather event as much as a combat encounter — gnomes treat a Lortmil flyer sighting the way farmers treat a tornado warning. Trading houses bolt their shutters and militia sound horns._

**Orc War Band** †

A band of **2d4+2 orcs** from the Lortmil foothills, pushing into the valley through a northern ridge pass. They are raiding for food, weapons, and prisoners — not territory. They fight aggressively but retreat if they lose half their number. Since the Mounted Borderer withdrawal, these incursions have increased. Gnome militia will arrive in **2d6 rounds** if combat noise carries (50% chance per round). _Loot: crude weapons, 2d10 sp, possibly a stolen gnome trade token worth 25 gp to the right buyer._

**Goblin Scouts** †

A scouting party of **1d6+2 goblins** with a **hobgoblin sergeant**, watching the road from concealment in the hillside scrub. They are mapping caravan schedules and militia patrol timing — intelligence that someone is paying for. They flee rather than fight, using prepared escape routes into narrow ravines. If captured, one carries a crude map with **caravan departure times** marked in orcish. _DM note: The intelligence is flowing somewhere. The question is where — a bandit camp, a Temple agent, or a Council member's intermediary._

**5**

**Dwarf Merchants from Rockhall**

A trading party of **2d4 dwarves** with **1d3 pack mules** from Rockhall in the Lortmil Mountains. They carry **dwarven-forged tools, iron ingots, and Lortmil ale** bound for the Kron Market. They are suspicious of strangers but friendly to anyone who mentions gnome contacts by name. Their leader, a weathered merchant named **Durgrim Ironpick**, knows the mountain passes and will trade information about orc movements for news of road conditions. _They enter the valley from the western passes beyond Tulvar and travel east toward Kron Village._

**6**

**Gnome Merchant Caravan** (Small)

A caravan of **1d3 wagons** with **2d4 gnome merchants** and **1d4 hired guards** (gnome fighters, level 1-2). They are hauling **crafted goods, alchemical supplies, or raw gems** between settlements. The merchants are talkative but cautious — they share road conditions and recent sightings freely, but clam up about cargo value or destination if pressed. A Satveegr trade token hangs from the lead wagon. _If the party has helped the Alliance before, the merchants offer a 10% discount at the next trading house._

**7**

**Gnome Militia Patrol**

A patrol of **2d4+2 gnome soldiers** led by a **militia sergeant** (gnome fighter, level 3-4), marching in formation along the Greenway. They wear leather armor with the Free Alliance badge and carry short swords, crossbows, and signal horns. They will **stop and question all non-gnome travelers** — name, business, destination, and whether they carry weapons. Polite but firm. They record everything in a patrol ledger. If the party has Alliance credentials or mentions Pithriggen, the sergeant relaxes considerably and shares intelligence about recent bandit activity. _DM note: The patrol ledger is a campaign resource — if the party later needs to prove they were somewhere, this record exists._

**8**

**Gnome Merchant Caravan** (Large)

A major caravan of **2d4+2 wagons** with **3d6 gnome merchants, teamsters, and family members**, escorted by **2d4 gnome militia** and possibly **1d2 mercenary guards** (human or half-elf fighters, level 2-3). This is a high-value shipment — **cut gemstones, enchanted trinkets, or clockwork mechanisms** bound for Verbobonc City via Kron Fortress. The caravan master is a Satveegr who carries sealed trade documents. _If attacked, this caravan's loss would be reported directly to the Moot Hall and would escalate the gnome crisis by one political notch._

**9**

**Gnome Militia Checkpoint**

A semi-permanent checkpoint — **4-6 gnome soldiers** with a **Satveegr tax agent** stationed at a road narrows between two settlements. Wagons are inspected, trade tokens verified, and a **3% transit toll** collected on goods moving through the valley. Non-gnome travelers are questioned more thoroughly and may be asked to surrender weapons while in the settlement zone. The Satveegr has authority to impound suspicious cargo. _DM note: The checkpoint is also an intelligence node — the Satveegr reports to the Moot Hall and will mention unusual travelers in the next dispatch._

**10**

**Gnome Travelers**

A group of **1d6+1 gnome civilians** — crafters moving between workshops, families visiting relatives in another township, a healer making rounds, or a group of young gnomes on a coming-of-age walk along the Greenway. They are unarmed or lightly armed and will flee if threatened. They share local gossip freely: which trading house has the best prices, which road section had a sighting last week, whose daughter is marrying into Clan Keeleene. _This encounter is atmosphere — it reminds the party that the Greenway is a living road, not just a combat corridor._

**11**

**Gnarley Rangers or Elf Scouts**

A patrol of **1d4 Gnarley Rangers** (human/half-elf rangers, level 2-4) or **1d3 Sylvan Elf scouts** from the Gnarley Forest, operating at the eastern edge of the valley near Kron Fortress. They are tracking something — orc movements, unusual animal migration patterns, or rumors of unnatural activity in the forest edge. They are taciturn but will share intelligence with anyone who demonstrates knowledge of the Temple threat. _DM note: These scouts operate outside the Alliance's political structure but have informal agreements with Jeet Jimbleclap. They are a back-channel to Gnarley Forest intelligence._

**12**

**Suspicious Strangers** †

A group of **1d4+1 humans** who claim to be merchants, pilgrims, or scholars — but something is wrong. Their trade tokens are forged or outdated. Their knowledge of the road is too specific. They ask questions about **militia patrol schedules, fortress garrison strength, or mine locations**. These are **Temple agents**, **bandit intelligence gatherers**, or **agents of a Council member** collecting information for purposes that will not benefit the Alliance. They are not hostile unless confronted — they prefer to gather information and leave. If captured, they carry nothing incriminating except a **coded letter** that Pithriggen or Burne could decipher. _DM note: This encounter connects directly to the mine claim documents, the caravan raid intelligence, and the political crisis at the Council. It is the Temple's presence on the road, wearing a human face._

#### Encounter Frequency Notes for DMs

*   **When to roll:** Once per 7-mile road segment (5 segments: Tulvar → Greenrest → Barra → Wilm → Kron Village), or once per 4 hours of travel. Roll only during active travel — not while resting in a settlement.
*   **Encounter chance:** Roll 1d6 first. On a 1-3, an encounter occurs; roll 2d6 on the table. On a 4-6, the road is quiet. This gives roughly a 50% encounter rate per segment — about 2-3 encounters over the full 35-mile journey.
*   **Time of day matters:** Gnome patrols (7, 9) are daytime only. Merchant caravans (6, 8) travel dawn to dusk. Goblins (4) and orcs (3) prefer dusk and dawn. The Lortmil flyer (2) and suspicious strangers (12) can appear any time.
*   **† Escalated encounters:** Since the Mounted Borderer withdrawal, encounters marked with † have increased. If the DM wants to reflect worsening conditions, roll the encounter check on 1-4 instead of 1-3, and add +1 to any result of 3 or 4 (making orc and goblin encounters more likely to appear as the campaign progresses).
*   **Location modifiers:** Near Tulvar (segment 1), add +1 to the encounter roll — more patrols, more merchants, fewer threats. Near Kron Fortress (segment 5), subtract -1 — the fortress garrison keeps the peace. The middle segments (2-4) are the most dangerous and use the table as written.
