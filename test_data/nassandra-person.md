Nassandra
=========

### Cowled Lady

### DM Campaign Framing — READ THIS FIRST

#### CR 21 — NOT a Temple of Elemental Evil Encounter

**Nassandra is Challenge Rating 21. She is a Sorcerer 18 / Vampire. She will kill a level 4 party in a single round without effort. Do not send your Temple of Elemental Evil party here.**

This is the single most important thing a DM needs to understand about Nassandra before reading the rest of this article. She exists in the [Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576cy](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md) because she is an active threat in the Viscounty during the campaign timeframe — but she is **not connected to the Temple of Elemental Evil**, the [Cult of the Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md), the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md), or any faction the party will encounter at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) or in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). She is a completely separate plot thread.

 

#### What Nassandra IS

*   **Faction:** [Priesthood of Iuz](the-priesthood-of-iuz-organization.md) — Boneshadow agent. She serves [Iuz](iuz-person.md) the Old, not the Elder Elemental God. The Boneshadow is Iuz's elite infiltration and assassination network — separate from the Boneheart, separate from the Temple of Elemental Evil. Nassandra operates outside even the Boneshadow's formal structure, as a rogue agent with autonomous authority
*   **Threat tier:** CR 21. Sorcerer 18 with vampire template. CHA 31 (+10). AC 22. HP 117. Fly 60 ft. She has Dominate Person, Mass Suggestion, and 9th-level spell slots. This is a mid-to-high Tier 4 encounter (levels 17–20). The Temple of Elemental Evil campaign runs from Tier 1 through early Tier 3 (levels 1–12). Nassandra does not belong in any encounter the TOEE party will face during the campaign's normal arc
*   **Theater of operations:** The [Lortmil Mountains](lortmil-mountains-location.md), the southern [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), and the western [Viscounty](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md). Her attacks have targeted [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), [Twilight Falls](twilight-falls-settlement.md), and [Castle Hagthar](castle-hagthar-landmark.md) — all south and west of the TOEE campaign area. Her fortress (Midnight Keep) is in the Lortmils, far from the Gnarley Forest and the Long Road
*   **Current status:** Weakened and imprisoned by her lover, **Halmadar the Cruel**. She is not an active battlefield threat at campaign start — she is a background menace whose agents operate in the region while she recovers

 

#### What Nassandra is NOT

*   **NOT connected to the Temple of Elemental Evil.** She does not serve Zuggtmoy, the Elder Elemental God, or any elemental cult. She does not interact with **Hedrack**, Romag, Lareth, or any TOEE NPC. Her command structure runs through [Iuz](iuz-person.md) and the [Priesthood of Iuz](the-priesthood-of-iuz-organization.md), which is a rival power to the Temple
*   **NOT an encounter for the TOEE party.** A level 4–8 party investigating the Sepulchre-Priory, Nulb, or the Moathouse should never encounter Nassandra in direct combat. She would annihilate them. If the party stumbles onto rumors of the "Cowled Lady" through the Wicked Three prophecy or giant raids in the Lortmils, those rumors are _background world-building_, not quest hooks for the current tier
*   **NOT part of the TOEE evidence trail.** No evidence found at the Sepulchre-Priory, in Nulb, or at the Moathouse connects to Nassandra. The Slikkina Jillink letter, the Scroll of Veil, Lareth's diary, Tolub's journal — none of these reference her or the Iuz conspiracy. The two plot threads are completely separate

#### Why She's in the Campaign Guide

Nassandra exists in the Verbobonc Campaign Guide for three reasons:

*   **Living world backdrop:** The Viscounty is not a single-threat region. While the party fights the Temple of Elemental Evil, other threats are operating simultaneously — the Priesthood of Iuz, the giant tribes of the Lortmils, the Wicked Three prophecy. Nassandra is part of this backdrop. She makes the world feel alive and dangerous beyond the party's current scope
*   **Future campaign thread:** If the DM continues the campaign beyond the Temple of Elemental Evil (into Tier 3 and beyond), Nassandra's plot thread — the Wicked Three, the giant armies, the Boneshadow infiltration, Halmadar the Cruel — becomes relevant. She is a prepared villain for post-TOEE play
*   **Red herring potential:** Rumors of the "Cowled Lady" and giant raids on [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) may reach the party through the gnomish Free Alliance, the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) trade network, or Borderer reports. Smart players may wonder if the Cowled Lady is connected to the Temple. **She is not.** This is a deliberate red herring that rewards investigation — a party that spends time chasing the Nassandra thread discovers it is a separate conspiracy entirely, which is itself valuable intelligence for the Viscounty authorities

 

#### DM Guidelines for Using Nassandra in a TOEE Campaign

*   **Rumors only:** The party may hear tavern talk about giant raids in the south, the "Wicked Wolves" prophecy, or a vampire sorceress in the Lortmils. These are flavor. Do not present them as actionable quest hooks for Tier 1–2 parties
*   **NPC references:** Borderer patrols, gnomish traders from [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), or druids in the Gnarley Forest may mention the Cowled Lady's attacks when discussing regional threats. This shows the party that the world is bigger than the Temple
*   **Do not cross the streams:** Nassandra's agents should never appear at the Sepulchre-Priory, in Nulb, or at the Moathouse. If a DM wants to connect the Iuz thread to the TOEE thread, the natural connection point is **Lucius Graeme** — who secretly serves Iuz — but even Lucius has no operational contact with Nassandra. The connection is institutional (both serve Iuz), not personal
*   **Escalation timeline:** If the DM wants Nassandra to become a direct threat, this should happen _after_ the Temple of Elemental Evil is resolved — when the party has reached level 12+ and is looking for the next major campaign arc. The Wicked Three / Lortmil giant wars / Halmadar the Cruel thread is designed for Tier 3–4 play

Among the most terrifying and enigmatic figures to haunt the Viscounty of Verbobonc, Nassandra, known as the **Cowled Lady**, is a vampire sorceress of unmatched cunning and cruelty. She is the whispered name in shadowed alleys, the specter looming over the Lortmil Mountains, and the unseen hand guiding giant armies against the free peoples of the region. Unlike the rank-and-file agents of Iuz, Nassandra operates outside the Boneheart and Boneshadow, pursuing her own insidious designs while still serving the Old One’s greater ambitions. Her presence is felt through the devastation left in her wake—the burning villages, the enslaved tribes, and the relentless whispers of dread that spread before her approach.

* * *

The moment your eyes fall upon the creature, you cannot help but be drawn to them, inexorably. The figure is tall, lean, the curve her hips, the nape of her neck with flawless skin pale as stars and full lips red as fresh blood. Their shining, raven-black hair is slicked back, oiled and neatly tucked behind pointed ears. They move with an otherworldly grace—each arch of a sculpted brow, each gesture with a manicured hand, each careful step a measured, purposeful action. Those alluring lips part in a perfectly crooked smile, and you spy canines sharp and keen as a wolf’s.

**Description**
---------------

Nassandra is a vision of allure and menace, her flawless alabaster skin seemingly untouched by the passage of time. Her full lips, as red as freshly spilled blood, curl into an ever-present smirk, promising both pleasure and peril. Sleek, raven-black hair is oiled and combed neatly behind her pointed ears, revealing features of sculpted perfection marred only by her predatory eyes—depthless pools of malice and amusement.

Her movements are a study in predatory grace; each measured step, each elegant motion of a manicured hand speaks of confidence and control. When she speaks, her voice carries the weight of ancient power, an intoxicating melody laced with venomous intent. Cloaked in a flowing black mantle that seems to drink the very light around her, she exudes an aura of supernatural command.

The woman laughs wickedly as she falls from the rain-slick battlements of the castle. Thunder rumbles and lightning streaks across the sky as you watch her rise effortlessly, borne aloft on a fell wind. She fixes you with a predatory glare and her ruby lips part to reveal pointed, needle-like fangs as she rises higher into the starless night sky.

### **Midnight Keep: The Shadowed Fortress**

*   **A Fortress Beyond Reach:** High in the Lortmil Mountains stands **Midnight Keep**, a citadel of obsidian stone and eldritch wards, once the seat of Nassandra’s power.
*   **A Place of Dark Rituals:** Within its walls, blood sacrifices and profane magic fueled her war efforts, drawing the attention of Iuz himself.
*   **Lost and Forgotten:** Since her fall, Midnight Keep remains a desolate ruin, its halls echoing with the whispers of her former minions, waiting for their dark queen’s return.

### **Rumors and Whispers**

##### **Murmurs from the Dark**

*   **The Giant Queen:** Some claim she is the true leader of the giant tribes plaguing the Lortmils, bending their chieftains to her will through sorcery and blood rites.
*   **The Wicked Wolves Prophecy:** The sylph Elspeth prophesied three "Wicked Wolves" prowling Verbobonc—many believe the Cowled Lady to be one of them.
*   **A Mistress of Monsters:** It is said she has tamed dragons and enslaved a mountain giant of great power, using it to summon more of its kind.
*   **The Wars of Shadow:** The Cowled Lady is rumored to have led assaults on Verbobonc three times in as many years, attacking Sheernobb, Swan, Twilight Falls, and Fortress Hagthar, mustering an army in the south.

### **History of a Nightmare**

##### **The Rise of the Cowled Lady**

A mysterious figure, the Cowled Lady is reputed to be the head of the giant armies that have been plaguing Verbobonc for the past several years. It is also believed that she is one of the three “Wicked Wolves” prowling Verbobonc, as prophesied by the sylph Elspeth late last year. The Cowled Lady commands great magic. She has been able to enslave a great mountain giant, command dragons, and muster a force.

*   Reference: VER4-09 Wicked Three

##### **The Strike Against Nassandra**

When her identity was revealed, a band of adventurers launched a daring raid on her fortress, hidden within a demiplane accessible through the isolated town of Sojourn. Though she was not present, they uncovered valuable intelligence, including her personal journal, which revealed a shocking truth—her undying devotion to Halmadar, whom she considered before even her service to Iuz.

*   Reference: (in VER5-06 In the Viscount's Secret Service)

##### **The War in the Shadows**

![](./images/Nassandra%20The%20Cowled%20Lady%20bodyshot.jpg "Nassandra The Cowled Lady bodyshot.jpg")

Nassandra The Cowled Lady by

Following the revelation of her plans, the forces of Verbobonc moved quickly to disrupt her growing influence. A coalition of knights, gnomish scouts, and elven rangers launched a series of skirmishes against her minions in the Lortmil foothills. The most decisive of these was the Battle of Fellspire Pass, where her forces, including summoned elementals and enslaved giants, were routed. Though this was not enough to bring her down, it forced her into a desperate retreat, delaying her ultimate plans. More significantly, they were able to recover the Renvash Splinter, a holy relic of St. Cuthbert lost in the Lortmil Mountains many years before. These adventurers also recovered a personal journal that spoke of the Cowled Lady's devotion to “another” that she felt should come before her plots in service of Iuz— this other was Halmadar, who had become the Cowled Lady’s lover.

*   Reference: VER5-06 On the Viscount's Secret Service (3E)

##### **The Grand Assault**

With her plans exposed, Nassandra launched an all-out assault on Verbobonc. She and the infamous assassin **Obmi the Hammer of Iuz** led a combined force of giants and infiltrators, striking deep into the heartland of the Viscounty. However, her ambitions met disaster; Obmi was captured, her armies shattered, and she herself was driven from the battlefield, grievously wounded and weakened.

*   Reference: VER5-07 Crownfire

##### **A Prisoner of Love and Power**

With her plans in ruin, Nassandra fled to her lover, **Halmadar the Cruel**, only to find him consumed by his own dark machinations. Weakened beyond recovery, she became a shadow of her former self, dependent on Halmadar for survival. Trapped within his growing empire of terror, she now lingers, awaiting the day she might reclaim her strength and continue her war against the light.

*   Reference: VER8-02 Crypt Things

**Relationships and Alliances**
-------------------------------

### **Halmadar the Cruel (Lover and Captor)**

*   Though once her equal, Halmadar now holds power over Nassandra, using her as a tool in his schemes.
*   Their relationship is one of twisted devotion, fractured by ambition and betrayal.

### **Iuz the Old (Dark Patron)**

*   Though she serves the Old One, she has never been granted a formal position within his hierarchy, operating instead as a rogue agent of destruction.
*   Iuz tolerates her independence for now, but should she rise again, her loyalty may be tested.

### **The Giant Tribes of the Lortmils**

*   Many chieftains still fear her, though her direct control has waned.
*   If freed from her imprisonment, she could easily rally them once more.

* * *

### The Priesthood of Iuz

is a sinister and far-reaching organization devoted to the worship and expansion of the Old One’s dominion. Within this unholy clergy, dark magic, blood rituals, and political manipulation serve as tools to further Iuz’s plans for conquest. The priesthood extends its influence across the Flanaess, acting as both enforcers of the demigod’s will and orchestrators of chaos in lands yet to fall under his control. While the Boneheart represents Iuz’s closest circle of elite advisors and warlords, and the Boneshadow his most elite infiltrators, the Priesthood is his unyielding foundation, bringing his doctrine to conquered lands and ensuring obedience through terror.

Nassandra, the Cowled Lady, though not officially part of the Boneheart or Boneshadow, maintains a vital role within the priesthood’s network, utilizing her dark gifts to further Iuz’s objectives. Though she operates with a degree of autonomy, her connection to the priesthood ensures she remains a potent force in his growing empire.

*   **A Rogue Servant:** Though she serves Iuz, she has never been part of the Boneheart, instead aligning with the secretive **Boneshadow**, a network of spies, assassins, and enforcers who operate beyond the direct gaze of the Old One.
*   **The Dark Faith:** Nassandra was once among the most powerful independent warlords of Iuz, her faith in the Old One as much about ambition as devotion.
*   **Torn Loyalties:** While once unquestioning in her service, her growing obsession with Halmadar has caused doubt to fester, creating a potential fracture in Iuz’s dominion.

* * *

### **Nassandra’s Duties in the Priesthood**

##### **Agent of Destruction**

*   Unlike conventional priests of Iuz, Nassandra is not bound to a single temple or citadel; rather, she is deployed as an instrument of destruction.
*   She is often sent to destabilize regions, weakening them before Iuz’s armies arrive to claim dominion.
*   The giant wars she orchestrated in the Lortmil Mountains and the attacks on Verbobonc align with the priesthood’s mission to bring chaos and disorder to Iuz’s enemies.

##### **Manipulator of Mortal and Monster**

*   Nassandra is entrusted with bringing powerful beings under Iuz’s sway, either through enchantment, coercion, or force.
*   Her ability to dominate giants, manipulate dragons, and enthrall mortals makes her a valuable asset to the priesthood.
*   Some priests resent her influence, as she does not follow the strict clerical hierarchy, yet few dare to challenge her openly.

##### **Keeper of Forbidden Knowledge**

*   She is often tasked with uncovering lost relics of power that might serve Iuz’s growing might.
*   In her ruined fortress, fragments of dark knowledge from forgotten ages are rumored to lie hidden, awaiting the day she can reclaim them.
*   Her journal, recovered by adventurers, hinted at even greater ambitions—ones that may one day rival the Old One himself.

* * *

### **Adventure Hooks**

*   **A Whisper from the Shadows:** Nassandra’s minions seek artifacts of power to restore her strength. Can the players intercept them before they succeed?
*   **The Lost Mistress:** The adventurers learn of Halmadar’s secret prisoner and must decide whether to slay or free her.
*   **The Rise of the Giants:** Rumors spread that Nassandra’s agents are once again stirring the giant tribes. Can the party stop her before she wages another war on the Viscounty?
*   **Blood and Betrayal:** As she regains her power, Nassandra must choose—will she continue serving Iuz, or carve her own path of conquest?

* * *

#### **Conclusion**

Nassandra, the Cowled Lady, remains one of the most fearsome and enigmatic villains of the Viscounty of Verbobonc. A creature of dark beauty and even darker ambition, she is a foe that lingers on the edge of legend—waiting for the day when the stars align, and she may once more cast her terrible shadow over the land.

###### **Legendary Actions (3/Turn)**

Nassandra can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time, and only at the end of another creature’s turn.

*   **Blood Drain (Costs 2 Actions).** Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature grappled by Nassandra. Hit: 10 (2d6 + 4) necrotic damage, and the target's hit point maximum is reduced by the damage taken.
*   **Children of the Night.** She summons 3d6 bats, rats, or wolves under her control.
*   **Shadow Step.** Nassandra moves up to 30 feet without provoking attacks of opportunity.

* * *

###### **Actions**

**Multiattack.** Nassandra makes two melee attacks or casts a spell and makes one melee attack.

**Slam.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (1d6 + 4) bludgeoning damage plus **energy drain** (see below).

**Energy Drain.** A creature hit by Nassandra’s slam or bite attack must succeed on a DC 21 Constitution saving throw or lose **two levels** or hit dice.

**Dominate (Recharge 5-6).** Nassandra targets one creature she can see within 30 feet. The target must make a DC 21 Wisdom saving throw or be **charmed** for 24 hours or until she or her allies harm it. While charmed, the target follows her commands as if under the _Dominate Person_ spell.

**Spellcasting.** _Nassandra is an 18th-level sorcerer._ (Spell Save DC 21, +13 to hit with spell attacks).  
**Cantrips** (_at will_): _Mage Hand, Prestidigitation, Detect Magic  
_**1st Level (4 slots)**: _Charm Person, Shield, Magic Missile  
_**2nd Level (3 slots)**: _Mirror Image, Invisibility, Scorching Ray  
_**3rd Level (3 slots)**: _Fireball, Counterspell, Lightning Bolt

###### **Legendary Resistances (3/Day)**

If Nassandra fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.

###### **Shapechanger.**

She can transform into a large wolf, mist, or her humanoid form at will.

###### **Mist Escape.**

When reduced to 0 hit points, Nassandra transforms into a cloud of mist instead of falling unconscious, escaping to her lair unless prevented by radiant damage.

* * *

##### **Lair Actions (If Encountered in Midnight Keep)**

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), Nassandra can use one of her lair actions.

*   **Shadows Stir.** All creatures hostile to Nassandra must succeed on a DC 21 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened for 1 minute.
*   **Dark Illusions.** She creates an illusory copy of herself that lasts until the end of her next turn, confusing attackers.
*   **Blood Magic Surge.** Nassandra regains 20 hit points at the start of her turn while in her lair.

* * *

###### **Equipment & Magic Items**

*   **Shadowed Cloak.** Grants **+4 Charisma**, **+2 AC**, total concealment in dim light, and _Shadow Binding_ (1/day).
*   **Bracers of Armor +3.** Adds +3 to AC.
*   **Ring of Arcane Might.** Grants +1 spell DC and +1 spell attack bonus.
*   **Amulet of Proof Against Detection.** Nassandra cannot be located or divined by magical means.
*   **Cube of Force.** Can activate various protective force barriers.

* * *

##### **Tactics & Combat**

*   **Master of the Battlefield.** Nassandra prioritizes control spells like _Wall of Force_, _Globe of Invulnerability_, and _Evard’s Black Tentacles_ before unleashing _Fireball_ or _Meteor Swarm_.
*   **Evasive and Unkillable.** If reduced to 0 HP, she turns to mist and retreats to her Midnight Keep.
*   **Overwhelming Minions.** She rarely fights alone, using _Children of the Night_ to flood enemies with creatures while she strikes from afar.
