Wilfrick Revepaix
=================

### Viscount Revepaix (a.k.a. Defender of the Faith)

Viscount @Wilfrick Revepaix, lord of the [Verbobonc, Viscounty](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md), is fifty years old and still carries the frame of the fighter who helped win [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md). The gray is spreading faster than the confidence. Seven years of governing a Viscounty caught between the ambitions of Veluna, the appetite of Furyondy, the frustrations of the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md), and the internal maneuvering of his own [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) have not broken Wilfrick — but they have slowed him down. He knows it. His Court knows it. And somewhere beneath the [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) and the ruins east of Hommlet, something else has begun to know it too.

> _"We honor the legacy of those before us, like my mother, Lady Elara, while striving to secure a prosperous future for Verbobonc."_

#### Physical Description

Wilfrick is a tall Oeridian man of fifty, carrying the thinned but not collapsed frame of a man who was once genuinely formidable and has not quite finished being so.

*   **Build and Stature:** Six feet and one inch, broad through the shoulders and slightly heavier through the midsection than he was at forty. He still fills a doorway. He no longer sprints through one. He sits his throne with a residual soldier's posture — the back straight, the weight balanced — that courtiers half his age rarely match.
*   **Face:** Strong-jawed, weathered. Black hair going rapidly gray, combed back from a high forehead in the manner of Verbobonc nobility. A neatly trimmed beard that is more gray than black now, framing a face that has deep lines around the eyes — the kind that come from squinting at maps and from reading letters that don't say what you need them to say.
*   **Eyes:** Dark, insightful, and carrying the particular quality of someone who has been watching political situations develop and dissolve for three decades. Visitors who expect a feeble old man are often surprised to find that those eyes miss very little. He is not what the whispers suggest. He is simply slower to commit than he used to be.
*   **Dress:** In audience, finely tailored tunics of rich fabric with subtle embroidery, over which he wears a fur-lined cloak that is a deliberate nod to the traditional dress of his domain. Signet rings on both hands — the Viscounty seal on his right, his mother Lady Elara's ring on his left, which he has worn since her death. In private, he dresses plainly.
*   **Bearing:** He still moves like a man who was trained to fight. He does not shuffle or stoop. What has changed is the hesitation before he speaks — the consideration that his Court mistakes for weakness and that is, in fact, the calculation of a man who has learned exactly how many things can go wrong when a Viscount acts without thinking.

_

> "Navigating through conflicts and challenges requires steadfast determination and a clear vision for the future."

_

### Personality

Wilfrick is not the man his Court whispers about. He is not feeble-minded. He is not broken. He is a man who wants peace more than he wants decisiveness — and in 576 CY, that preference is being exploited by people who understand it very well.

*   **Calculated Inaction:** What looks like hesitation is frequently deliberate. Wilfrick has learned that strong action alienates allies. He has watched decisive rulers lose half their council in a season. His strategy is equilibrium: hold the balance, let the factions tire themselves against each other, and preserve the Viscounty's cohesion even if it means losing ground on individual issues. This strategy has its limits, and those limits are now being reached.
*   **Genuine Piety:** His faith in St. Cuthbert is not ceremonial. He warms instinctively to clerics and paladins and extends resources to those he deems worthy and appropriately deferential — a practical as well as spiritual reflex. He sees order and law as civilization's bedrock in a way that goes beyond political calculation.
*   **Wary of Ambition:** He has watched Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md) for twenty years. He knows what the man wants. He keeps him in reach, not out of affection but because a Haxx inside the tent is less dangerous than a Haxx outside it. He applies this logic to most of his political relationships and is occasionally wrong about which side of the tent they belong on.
*   **Grief He Does Not Show:** The disappearance of Prince Thrommel IV of Furyondy, to whom his daughter @Helena Revepaix was betrothed, struck him harder than he has admitted publicly. It closed a diplomatic door that would have secured Verbobonc's relationship with Furyondy for a generation. He misses it every time he reads a dispatch from the Furyondian court.
*   **The Weight of Legacy:** Wilfrick carries his mother's ring and his mother's expectation. Lady Elara built relationships with the gnomes of the Kron Hills over a lifetime. He is watching those relationships erode in real time and has not found a way to stop it that does not cost him somewhere else.

> "The Temple was sealed. I was there. I remember the morning after — the field, the relief, the certainty that we had bought a generation of peace. It has been seven years. I am no longer certain about the generation."

### Background

#### The Revepaix Name and Heritage

The name "Revepaix" translates from the old Oeridian court language as _Dream of Peace_ — an aspiration baked into the family's identity across generations. [House Revepaix](house-revepaix-organization.md) has governed the Viscounty of Verbobonc through a sequence of crises that would have broken weaker noble lines. The house's philosophy, instilled by its late matriarch Lady Elara, was that the Viscounty's survival depended on being indispensable to enough people simultaneously that no single faction could afford to displace it. It is an exhausting way to govern. It works, until it doesn't. Lady Elara died under circumstances that Wilfrick has never discussed in detail — "tragic" is the word the court uses, and no one presses further. She left him a viscounty, a reputation, and a set of relationships with the gnomes of the Kron Hills that she had cultivated through patient decades of fair dealing. He has tried to maintain them. It is harder than she made it look.

#### Youth, Military Formation, and Early Rule

Wilfrick received a fighter's education before a ruler's one, which shaped everything that came after. He was trained to command men in the field — to read terrain, to hold a line, to make the decision that saves the most lives when there is no decision that saves all of them. He was less trained to manage a chamber full of noble houses, each of which has been accumulating grievances and claims since before he was born. He took the seat of the Viscounty in his mid-thirties, inheriting from his father a court that was functional but not warm, and a political relationship with Veluna that was closer to supervision than alliance. He spent fifteen years before Emridy Meadows building the alliances — with the gnomes, with the Church of St. Cuthbert, with the border lords — that would make the coalition possible.

#### The Battle of Emridy Meadows — 569 CY

[The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) was the defining event of Wilfrick's reign and possibly of his life. He was forty-three years old, still physically commanding, and he led the Verbobonc forces in the allied coalition against the Temple of Elemental Evil's armies. It was not a simple victory — the Temple's humanoid forces were organized and numerous, and the allied line held by margins that Wilfrick remembers clearly, in the early morning hours of sessions that run too long. The outcome was decisive. The Temple's armies were broken. Its leadership killed or fled. The Temple itself was sealed. Wilfrick came home having accomplished what most of Verbobonc had believed impossible, and he spent the next several years managing the political consequences — including what to do with two young adventurers named Rufus and Burne who had contributed substantially and were now owed a significant debt. He sent them to Hommlet. It was the right decision, and he knows it. What he is less certain about is whether they will have enough to work with.

#### Seven Years Since Emridy

The years between 569 CY and 576 CY should have been years of consolidation. The Temple was sealed. The coalition had held. The Viscounty's position was stronger than it had been in a generation. Instead, they were years of slow erosion. The coalition partners drifted back to their own concerns. The Council of Lords resumed its factional maneuvering as though the battle had not happened. The gnomes, whose militia had fought alongside Verbobonc forces at Emridy, began to experience the gap between what the Viscounty promised and what it delivered. And Veluna and Furyondy — watching from a comfortable distance — began the slow work of measuring whether Verbobonc's independence was worth maintaining or merely an opportunity deferred._

> "My mother used to say that peace is not an absence of problems. It is the management of them. She was right. I have managed fourteen at once for seven years. I am tired. The problems have not noticed."

### Rule in 576 CY

#### The Seat of Power — Castle Greyfist

Wilfrick holds court at [V1-3 Castle Greyfist, Citadel of the Viscount](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md) in Verbobonc City — the citadel of the Viscounty and the seat of its administrative and military authority. He commands the Right of High Justice over the Viscounty, oversees its militia, and formally directs its diplomacy. He does all of this while managing a Council of Lords that is advisory in theory and politically adversarial in practice, a Lord Mayor who openly covets greater authority, and neighbors who would be delighted if the Viscounty's independent management became too costly to maintain. He works long hours. He reads every significant dispatch himself — filtered and prioritized by **[Aldric Hollend](aldric-hollend-person.md)**, his Chamberlain, who is one of perhaps three people Wilfrick trusts completely. Aldric controls access to the Viscount, manages all Council preparation and correspondence, and maintains the back-channel relationships with the gnome leadership and Gnarley Forest contacts that no other court official possesses. Beyond Aldric and his daughter Elysia, Wilfrick does not entirely trust his aides — not out of paranoia, but out of experience with what happens when a Viscount stops reading his own correspondence. His close confidant is [House Tymak](house-tymak-organization.md), whose principal figure is Verbobonc's most likely next Mayor — a relationship that is functional, mutually useful, and watched very carefully by Lord Mayor Haxx.  

#### The Mounted Borderer Decision

The most consequential decision of Wilfrick's reign since Emridy Meadows was the withdrawal of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) and [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md). He made it to de-escalate — a gnome militia had killed several Borderers in a dispute over mine claim documentation, and Wilfrick's advisors told him that maintaining the patrol would provoke a full escalation with the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md). What his advisors did not adequately model was what would happen next. The withdrawal did not de-escalate the gnome situation — it simply removed the Viscounty's visible presence at the moment when gnome caravans from [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), and [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) began to be systematically targeted by organized raiders. "Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) has since gone quiet in Council sessions — which Wilfrick's advisor reads as a cooling of tensions, and which his own instinct reads as a man assembling his arguments. He received a letter from [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) about the Borderer withdrawal. He had **Aldric Hollend**, his Chamberlain, acknowledge it. He has not reversed the decision. He is waiting to see if it becomes untenable — which is the same calculation he applied to twelve other problems this year, and the one that most often runs out of runway.

#### The Hommlet Commission and Doomwatch Keep

The commission to [Lord Rufus](lord-rufus-person.md) and [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) to hold Hommlet and construct [30\. Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md) represents Wilfrick's clearest strategic act in the post-Emridy years. He chose two men he knew personally, whose capabilities he had seen demonstrated, and placed them at the western approach to the Temple ruins — not to storm the Temple, but to ensure that whatever was coming out of it had to pass through something solid first. He has not told Rufus and Burne everything he knows. Not because he distrusts them, but because some of what he knows — about the mine claim documentation, about the advisors who recommended the Borderer withdrawal, about the names in certain Council dispatches — is not yet proven, and a Viscount who accuses his own court without proof does not remain a Viscount. He is waiting for the evidence to cohere. He is aware that waiting has a cost._

> "The Temple was sealed by coalition. It will not be unsealed by one. Every fracture in Verbobonc's alliances is a door someone is hoping to open. My job is to have fewer fractures than doors."

### The Political Pressures of 576 CY

#### External Threat — Veluna, Furyondy, and the Problem of Thrommel

Verbobonc occupies an uncomfortable strategic position: too valuable to ignore and too independent to fully control. Both Veluna and Furyondy maintain diplomatic observers in Verbobonc City whose actual function is closer to intelligence gathering than diplomacy. Wilfrick is aware of this. He manages it by giving them nothing alarming to report — a strategy that requires him to not appear alarmed, regardless of what he is actually reading. The disappearance of Prince Thrommel IV of Furyondy is the complicating factor that no correspondence can resolve. Thrommel was betrothed to [Helena Revepaix](helena-revepaix-person.md). His vanishing — somewhere in the region of Verbobonc, under circumstances no one has been able to explain to Furyondy's satisfaction — means that the Furyondian court has a standing grievance against the Viscounty that is personal as well as political. The search for Thrommel is ongoing in Furyondy. Wilfrick prays quietly and often that whatever they find does not implicate Verbobonc. His private fear, which he has shared with no one, is that Thrommel is somewhere in the vicinity of the Temple of Elemental Evil. If that is true, it changes the political calculus around the Temple entirely — because Furyondy does not negotiate over its missing crown princes.

#### The Council of Lords — A Chamber of Competing Visions

[The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) is Wilfrick's primary domestic challenge. It is an advisory body in theory. In practice it is a pressure chamber of competing interests that he must navigate without alienating any faction so completely that it costs him a working majority.

*   **The Militarists — Lords Velysin and Milinous:** They want decisive mobilization against the Temple. Wilfrick agrees with the goal. He is deeply skeptical of their proposed methods, which would burn through both his treasury and his gnome relationships simultaneously. Sir [Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md), once a reliable ally, has grown visibly less committed — which Wilfrick reads as a man positioning himself for whatever comes next rather than someone who believes in the current course.
*   **The Economic Powers — House Haxx and Its Allies:** Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md) wants the trade roads safe because disrupted commerce costs him revenue. He is not wrong about the problem. His proposed solutions tend toward guild enforcement and taxed mercenary contracts — which solves the road problem while expanding his own political footprint. Wilfrick manages this by approving the parts of Haxx's agenda that solve real problems and quietly blocking the parts that consolidate his authority.
*   **The Manipulator — Lady Godeleva Langmuir:** Lady [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md), the "Iron Lady," does not support Wilfrick and does not need him to know it. Her son Lord [Ludovic Langmuir](ludovic-langmuir-person.md) is being positioned for something, and whatever it is, it does not require a healthy Viscounty to succeed. Wilfrick watches her more carefully than anyone else in the room.
*   **The Moderate — Lord Rhynehurst:** Lord [Wendell Rhynehurst](wendell-rhynehurst-person.md) is the man Wilfrick considers most important to keep informed and closest to his actual position. A persuadable voice, a reasonable man, and a potential fulcrum if the Council's balance ever tips past the point of equilibrium.

#### The Gnome Crisis

Wilfrick's relationship with the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) is the one he worries about most in private correspondence and discusses least in Council sessions. The gnomes remember everything. His mother built that relationship one fair dealing at a time over thirty years. He is watching it unravel in a season. Human lords from within the Viscounty have filed documentation with the Verbobonc legal archive claiming ownership of gnome mines in [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) under a contested interpretation of a 412 CY land grant. Wilfrick has not yet acted on the filings. He is waiting for a legal determination. The gnomes are not waiting — they are watching to see whose side the Viscount comes down on when the determination is made. "Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) has been cordial, careful, and notably quiet. Local gnome leader [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) has been none of those things. Between the two of them, they represent the range of the Free Alliance's response: diplomatic pressure from one end, and the threat of escalation from the other. Wilfrick is trying to hold the middle. The space keeps narrowing._

> "My mother spent thirty years earning the trust of the Kron Hills. A season of bad decisions can lose what three decades built. I am trying to avoid spending the principal."

_

### House Revepaix — Family and Succession

The question of who follows Wilfrick is the most uncomfortable conversation in Verbobonc City — not because he is dying, but because none of the obvious successors are obvious at all.

##### [Helena Revepaix](helena-revepaix-person.md)

Helena, the eldest, was positioned for a political marriage that would have solved the Furyondy problem for a generation: betrothal to Prince Thrommel IV. She was poised, politically educated, and more than equal to the role. Thrommel disappeared. The betrothal ended with it. Helena remains at court, capable and largely unheld — her political value as a marriage-alliance anchor diminished by the one event she could not have prevented. She is not broken by it. She is cautious in a way she was not before.

##### [Fenward Revepaix](fenward-revepaix-person.md) — the Lefthand

Fenward is the eldest son and the expected heir. He is also, in the frank assessment of most of his father's court, poorly suited for it. He carries unexplained antipathy toward the gnomes of the Kron Hills — a liability in any Verbobonc ruler — and openly resents the Viscounty's ties to Furyondy without having any coherent alternative to offer. His nickname, "Lefthand," derives from a childhood injury and has acquired an unfortunate secondary meaning among the court wags. Wilfrick loves his son and has largely stopped expecting things of him.

##### [Elysia Revepaix](elysia-revepaix-person.md)

Elysia is Wilfrick's adopted middle child — and, in the private view of those who work closely with the Viscounty's administration, the one most likely to run it effectively. She has her father's pragmatism and a sharper political instinct than either of her siblings. She has effectively been the political mind behind Wilfrick's court for several years, drafting positions, managing strategic correspondence, and briefing the Viscount on what the Council of Lords is about to do before it does it. She works in close coordination with **Aldric Hollend**, the Chamberlain: Elysia handles political analysis, Aldric handles operational execution. Together they form a complementary engine — she sees what needs to happen, he makes it happen within the constraints of what is actually possible. If they ever genuinely disagreed on a matter of strategy, the court would fracture. So far, that has not happened. Lord Mayor Haxx opposes her at every turn — a traditionalist who views her progressive governance instincts with genuine skepticism and her adopted status as a political liability he can exploit. Wilfrick has not formally named her his successor. He watches the court watch her, and he waits.

##### [Serrol Revepaix](serrol-revepaix-person.md)

Serrol, the youngest, was not his father's favorite in court terms — he showed little interest in politics and less in military affairs. He was, however, the sibling most beloved by the common people: quietly empathetic, genuinely interested in the lives of ordinary Verbobonc citizens, and constitutionally incapable of the kind of noble performance that the court expected and that he found oppressive. He left it. He joined the @Church of St. Cuthbert under @Bishop Haufren and has served there since. Wilfrick visits him more often than court observers realize, and it is the one relationship in his life that does not feel like a negotiation.

### Relationships

##### Lord Burne and Lord Rufus

Wilfrick placed [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) and [Lord Rufus](lord-rufus-person.md) at Hommlet because he trusted them — and trust is not something he extends lightly or cheaply. He reads Burne's letters personally and generally credits them. He has not acted on every recommendation, because acting on every recommendation Burne makes would mean moving against his own court, and he is not yet certain the evidence is solid enough to survive what that would cost politically. He is aware that Burne knows this and considers it insufficient. He is aware that Rufus would find it maddening. He sends them what support he can arrange and waits for the moment when the evidence becomes undeniable.

##### Lord Mayor Marakios Willem Haxx

The relationship is functional, watchful, and occasionally useful. [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md) wants to restore the city's republic and expand his own authority. Wilfrick understands this. He has managed it by giving Haxx real authority over the things Haxx most values — trade enforcement, tax administration, guild regulation — in exchange for working loyalty on the things Wilfrick most needs. This arrangement is increasingly strained as the road disruptions cost the guild interests money and Haxx's patience with Wilfrick's measured responses shortens.

##### The Church of St. Cuthbert

Wilfrick's piety is genuine and his relationship with St. Cuthbert's clergy is among the most functional of his political relationships — partly because priests of St. Cuthbert share his fundamental commitment to law and order, and partly because they ask for less in return than his noble allies. He provides the Church with resources, access, and political cover. They provide him with moral legitimacy and institutional loyalty. He warms to adventurers affiliated with or endorsed by the Church in a way that is noticeable to anyone who has spent time in his court.

##### Aldric Hollend — The Chamberlain

Aldric Hollend is one of perhaps three people Wilfrick trusts completely — and unlike the other two (Elysia and Serrol), Aldric's trust was earned through performance, not blood. Aldric served Wilfrick's mother first. He watched Wilfrick grow into the role of Viscount. He has seen the man at his best and at his worst, and he has never leveraged either knowledge. A former Knight Captain of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md)' 3rd Company (the Longriders), Aldric took a lance wound at [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) and was recruited by Lady Elara herself to manage [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md). He has held the Chamberlain's office for thirty years across two Viscounts.   What Wilfrick values most is that Aldric does not advise. He presents facts, offers context, and lets the Viscount decide. When Wilfrick makes a decision Aldric disagrees with, Aldric executes it faithfully. This institutional loyalty — cold, dependable, and utterly professional — is exactly why Wilfrick trusts him. Aldric is also the Viscount's most valuable back-channel to the gnome leadership: his personal relationship with [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) at [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) is one of the last threads holding the gnome alliance together. Aldric's devotion to **Rao** — in a court dominated by the [Church of St. Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md) — provides Wilfrick with a theological counterweight he does not always agree with but has learned to need. Between Aldric's counsel of patience and Bishop Haufren's counsel of action, Wilfrick hears both arguments. He is grateful for this, even when the arguments make his decisions harder.

##### "Prince" Jimm Pithriggen and the Free Alliance

Wilfrick's relationship with @Jimm Pithriggen is the most important failing partnership in Verbobonc's current politics. Pithriggen is a skilled diplomat who has been waiting for the Viscounty to demonstrate that it will actually protect gnome interests when it costs something to do so. The Mounted Borderer withdrawal was the clearest test yet — and Wilfrick failed it, in Pithriggen's reading, regardless of Wilfrick's reasons. He is trying to recover the ground without making a public reversal of the withdrawal decision that would embarrass him with his own court. He has not found the path yet.

### DM Notes — Playing Viscount Wilfrick

Wilfrick is the highest civilian authority the party can access in the Viscounty. He is not a pushover and not a fool — he is a tired man managing a genuinely difficult situation with insufficient resources and too many competing interests to satisfy. He responds to demonstrated competence and hard evidence, and he has the authority to unlock resources that no other NPC in the campaign can provide.

#### In Audience

*   **He listens fully before speaking:** Wilfrick lets a party finish making their case before he responds. He is assessing not just content but reliability — whether this is information he can use and whether these are people he can trust. He will ask one or two precise follow-up questions that reveal what he already knows.
*   **He does not make promises he cannot keep:** If the party asks for the Mounted Borderers to be redeployed, he will not say no — he will say "I am considering it" and mean it, even if the consideration has been ongoing for three months. He will, however, provide what he actually can: letters of authority, access to the legal archive, introduction to the right Council member, or a quiet commission that doesn't require Council approval.
*   **He warms to clerics and paladins:** This is genuine, not performance. A party member with a St. Cuthbert affiliation or a paladin's bearing will find him slightly more forthcoming and slightly more trusting from the first meeting.
*   **He notices everything:** Wilfrick is not the passive, distracted figure of court gossip. He will notice if a party member lies to him, notice if they are holding something back, and notice which one of them is doing the actual thinking. He will address that person directly on the second meeting.

#### Adventure Hooks

*   **The Thrommel Question:** Wilfrick will never ask the party to look for Prince Thrommel directly — it would be a politically catastrophic acknowledgment that Furyondy's missing prince might be in Verbobonc's territory. He will, however, ask the party to investigate "unusual activity" near the Temple ruins, and give them a description of a signet ring they should retrieve if found. The ring is Thrommel's. He will not say this.
*   **The Archive Commission:** The mine claim documents filed against gnome holdings in Greenway Valley are in the Verbobonc legal archive. Wilfrick cannot pull them himself without tipping his hand to whoever filed them. He needs someone who can access the archive without raising questions, determine whether the documents are genuine, and report back without discussing the commission with anyone in his court.
*   **The Royal Summons:** After a significant party achievement — clearing a major Temple cell, resolving a diplomatic crisis, recovering something important — Wilfrick summons them to Castle Greyfist. He does not explain why until they arrive. When they do, he offers them a commission with real authority, real resources, and a complication he did not mention in the summons: whatever they are being asked to do will put them in opposition to a named member of his Court, and he needs it done in a way that gives him deniability.
*   **Elysia's Mission:** Lady Elysia Revepaix contacts the party directly, with a message she says is from her father. Wilfrick will neither confirm nor deny this if asked. The mission is real. Its political purpose is one Wilfrick cannot pursue through official channels without triggering exactly the Council opposition he is trying to avoid.
*   **The Spy in the Correspondence:** Someone in the Viscount's court is intercepting and copying his letters to Lord Burne. Wilfrick knows. He cannot act without evidence that will survive a High Justice proceeding. He needs someone who can catch the courier in the act — and who can do it without destroying a relationship he still needs, if the spy turns out to be who he suspects.

Personality Characteristics
---------------------------

### Motivation

Despite his fading influence, Wilfrick maintains a stern facade. His deep religious faith informs his decisions, and he presents himself as a disciplined and devoted leader, albeit one who is often consumed by his anxieties. He offers support to clerics and paladins, viewing them as extensions of his own convictions.

### Savvies & Ineptitudes

His Noble Lordship and Defender of the Faith is as serious as his titles indicate. Somber and consumed with worry over the magnitude of the threat to the people of Viscounty of Verbobonc, Wilfrick presents a dour face and a sober assessment of the situation whenever asked.   Wilfrick’s faith underlies everything he does. A devout and pious individual, he channels his convictions into his leadership decisions. He hides his anxieties behind a noble, devoted, and disciplined facade—the very image of strong but stern leadership. He cares for the soldiers under his command but does not tolerate failure. Under most circumstances, second chances are unthinkable. Wilfrick’s pride will not allow him to look foolish or weak, and he is thus prone to overcompensate when he thinks people doubt him.
