C4 The Conservatory of Lirr
===========================

The Conservatory of Lirr rises on the scholarly side of the Civic Centre, a domed and ornamented edifice facing toward the Silver Consortium and College Lane. Its façade is all carved muses, open books, and fluted pillars; stained-glass windows show scenes of poets, painters, and bards under a star-swept sky. Within, it functions as both temple and academy—Verbobonc’s foremost school for music, poetry, drama, and the fine arts, inspired by Lirr, goddess of prose, poetry, literature, and art.

Students from across the Viscounty (and beyond) study here alongside clerics of Lirr, and its recitals and exhibitions fill the Civic Centre with prestige and coin.

##### First Impression

The murmur of the square falls away as you draw near a great marble building crowned with a copper-blue dome. High above, statues of robed figures lean out over the street, books and lyres in their stone hands, while colored glass gleams between them like pages lit from within.  
  
Music drifts through the open doors—first a lone violin, then a chorus of voices, then the sudden crash of a mis-hit drum and laughter. The scent of ink, beeswax, and oiled wood follows, mixed with a hint of incense. A broad staircase leads up between carved balustrades toward tall doors of dark oak, each panel inlaid with the sigil of an open, illuminated book.  
  
Young men and women in scholar’s cloaks and bright sashes hurry past, clutching scroll-tubes or instrument cases, while a grey-robed cleric pauses at the top of the steps to gently shush an over-enthusiastic lute-player. Above them all, the symbol of Lirr—an illustrated tome circled in light—hangs engraved in the stone, watching like an ever-open eye.

#### History

Established to enrich Verbobonc’s cultural scene, the Conservatory has evolved into a pivotal institution in the city, with its graduates often rising to prominence in various artistic fields.

*   **Establishment:** Founded to foster excellence in the arts within Verbobonc, enhancing the city's cultural landscape.
*   **Development:** Over the years, it has become a cornerstone of the community, with its graduates often becoming prominent bards and artists.

#### Role in Verbobonc

*   **Cultural Heart of the Civic Centre:  
    **The Conservatory is the city’s chief institution for high art—music, drama, poetry, and illuminated manuscripts. Its concerts anchor many civic festivals, and its graduates go on to become famous bards, court performers, and master artisans.
*   **Temple of the Lorekeeper:  
    **As a major temple of Lirr, the Conservatory preserves endangered books, plays, scores, and artworks, in line with the goddess’s dogma of protecting and spreading knowledge and creative expression.
*   **Partner of the Silver Consortium:  
    **The Conservatory shares an extensive library complex with the Silver Consortium, allowing mages and artists to draw from the same shelves of lore, theory, and history. Scholars trade spells for songs, and vice versa.
*   **Bridge to the Public:  
    **Through Averna’s partnership with **Jylee’s Inn**, the Conservatory takes its art into tavern parlors and public evenings—poetry readings, chamber concerts, and plays that let common citizens and visiting merchants taste high culture.

#### Facilities & Features

**Great Performance Hall**

*   A grand auditorium under the central dome, acoustically tuned and lined with carved balconies.
*   Used for major recitals, civic ceremonies, and temple festivals honoring Lirr.C4 The Conservatory of Lirr

**Practice Auditoriums & Studios**

*   Smaller halls for student ensembles, drama rehearsals, and master classes in voice, strings, winds, and percussion.
*   Several rooms can be hired for private performances or discreet noble entertainments.

**Classrooms & Workshop Studios**

*   Rooms dedicated to poetry, rhetoric, playwriting, calligraphy, painting, and illumination.
*   Novice clerics learn to copy and embellish manuscripts here, fulfilling their charge to preserve and disseminate art and knowledge.

**The Shared Library of Lirr & the Silver Consortium**

*   A sprawling complex linked via covered walk between temple and mage-guild.
*   One wing is under Lirr’s care: shelves of music, plays, criticism, and illuminated texts; stacks of rare scores and poetic cycles; reading rooms where silence is enforced by stern, ink-fingered clerics.

**Cloisters & Garden Court**

*   A small inner court where students recite, sketch, or simply soak up inspiration among statues of Lirr and famed local artists.
*   During festivals, this space is opened to the public for outdoor performances.

**Instrument Vault & Artisan Shops**

*   Secure rooms house fine instruments—lutes, viols, harps, flutes, and foreign curiosities—many donated by patrons.
*   Craftsmen associated with the temple repair and sometimes craft bespoke pieces; students receive discounted rates on instruments and art supplies.

### Clergy, Staff & Students

##### Clergy: Roles and Responsibilities

Clerics at the Conservatory are involved in educational roles and the preservation of artistic and literary works. Novice clerics replicate important documents, while senior members teach and manage the institution’s extensive artistic resources.

*   **Novice Clerics:** Tasked with copying important archives, ensuring the preservation and dissemination of knowledge.
*   **Senior Clergy:** Engage in teaching, organizing public recitals, and overseeing the maintenance of the Conservatory’s extensive collections.

##### Clergy of Lirr

*   **Novice Clerics:**
*   Copy and illuminate archives, repair damaged manuscripts, and assist in cataloging the shared library.
*   Often serve as ushers or stage-hands during performances.
*   **Senior Clergy & Maestros:**
*   Teach classes in composition, rhetoric, drama, and sacred art.
*   Organize public recitals, festivals, and exhibitions; oversee the Conservatory’s collections and acquisitions.
*   **The Group of Twelve:**
*   A renowned ensemble of virtuoso musicians whose performances are said to “brush the edge of the divine.”
*   Their concerts are major social events, drawing nobles, guildmasters, and foreign envoys.

#### High Priestess Averna Delarosa

*   **Appearance & Presence:  
    **Averna is an elegant Oeridian woman with expressive dark-blue eyes and long auburn hair, usually robed in deep blue and silver patterned with stylized verses and motifs. Her bearing combines courtly grace with a scholar’s intensity.Averna Delarosa
*   **Role:**
*   High Priestess of Lirr and head of the Conservatory.
*   Curator of arts, overseeing vast collections, commissioning new works, and judging which pieces merit preservation.
*   **Personality & Motivation:**
*   Charismatic, inspiring, and quietly uncompromising when it comes to censorship or destruction of art.
*   Driven to preserve classic Oeridian works while championing new voices. She believes art is both prayer and weapon—capable of healing, stirring resistance, and outlasting tyrants.
*   **Allies & Relationships:**
*   Strong ties with the **Silver Consortium** and other cultural institutions, often spearheading cross-disciplinary projects.
*   Special partnership with **Master Jylee** at C3 Jylee’s Inn, where she arranges poetry nights and small ensembles, bringing Conservatory art to the general public.

#### Relationships & Politics

*   **With Civic Government:  
    **The Conservatory regularly lobbies City Hall for funding, festival space, and protections for artists. Its public events make it a visible, popular institution, and Averna is a respected voice in cultural policy.
*   **With Other Temples:**
*   Natural affinity with **Delleb** (knowledge, scholarship) and cordial relations with Pelor, Rao, and others who value learning and peace.
*   Quiet opposition to any cult or faction that burns books, silences speech, or spreads terror to stifle art—putting them philosophically at odds with the Temple of Elemental Evil’s brutal influence.
*   **With Jylee’s Inn & Civic Life:  
    **Performers from the Conservatory at Jylee’s Inn blur the line between temple stage and tavern floor, keeping the arts woven into everyday city life and making both locations natural hubs for rumors and quest hooks.

### Adventure Hooks

*   **Song of the Second Rising:  
    **Averna suspects coded propaganda and fear-mongering plays are circulating in the poorer districts, hinting at the “return of an old power in the east.” She asks the PCs to track their origin and determine if a hidden patron is tied to the Temple of Elemental Evil.
*   **The Stolen Manuscript:  
    **A newly discovered Oeridian epic—potentially containing clues relevant to the _Noble Ambitions_ arc—is stolen en route from a rural shrine. The Conservatory hires the PCs to recover it before it vanishes into some noble’s private vault… or into Temple hands.
*   **Discord in the Group of Twelve:  
    **One of the famed Twelve has disappeared, last seen drinking with rough company from the docks. Averna fears blackmail or kidnapping. Rescuing the musician could earn the party powerful allies and invitations to high-society performances.
*   **Trial by Performance:  
    **To win access to restricted sections of the library or secure Averna’s political backing, a bard or charismatic party member must compose and perform an original piece before clergy and students—success grants influence; failure still earns them a reputation.

#### Relationships and Politics

The Conservatory maintains a symbiotic relationship with the Silver Consortium, enhancing resource sharing and collaboration. It actively engages with local government to ensure the arts remain integral to city planning and community events.

*   **Connection with the Silver Consortium:** Shares resources and collaborates on academic endeavors, strengthening ties between artists and magicians.
*   **Local Government Interaction:** Regularly interacts with city officials to ensure the arts remain a key part of community planning and festivities.

#### Motivation and Goals

Driven by a commitment to preserve and celebrate the arts, the Conservatory aims to provide top-tier education in music and arts, promoting the teachings of Lirr and making artistic expression accessible to a broad audience.

*   **Cultural Preservation:** Dedicated to preserving and advancing the arts in alignment with Lirr’s teachings.
*   **Educational Excellence:** Strives to provide the highest quality education in music and the arts, making arts accessible to a wider community.

#### Notable People

The Conservatory is home to the Group of Twelve, celebrated musicians known for their divine performances, and is supported by local patrons like Jylee of Dyvers who contribute to its cultural initiatives.

*   **The Group of Twelve:** A renowned ensemble of musicians from the Conservatory, known for their heavenly performances.
*   [Averna Delarosa](averna-delarosa-person.md) serves as the High Priestess of the Conservatory of Lirr in the bustling city of Verbobonc. Renowned for her profound dedication to the arts and literature, she is a celebrated figure among the Oeridian Oerid people, embodying the virtues and teachings of Lirr, the goddess of Prose, Poetry, Literature, and Art.
*   **Jylee of Dyvers:** Although primarily known for his [C3 Jylee’s Inn](c3-jyleeE28099s-inn-landmark.md), Jylee supports the Conservatory through patronage and hosting events.

#### Summary

The Conservatory of Lirr stands as a beacon of artistic and musical excellence in Verbobonc. With its comprehensive facilities, dedicated clergy, and strong community ties, it plays a pivotal role in promoting the cultural and intellectual growth of its students and the broader community. Its connection to the deity Lirr underscores its commitment to the flourishing of arts and literature, making it a key institution in the civic center of Verbobonc.

##### **First Meeting with High Priestess Averna**

The music in the practice hall falls to a gentle murmur as a new figure steps through the side door: a tall woman in deep blue and silver robes, auburn hair braided back from an intelligent face. Quills and tiny verses are stitched into the hem of her sleeves, and a silver holy symbol of an open, illuminated book rests at her throat.

Her gaze settles on you—not unkind, but weighing, as though she were judging a stanza for rhythm and truth. The students nearby straighten instinctively. When she smiles, the hall seems to breathe again.

**Dialogue – Averna Delarosa**

“_You are not students… nor patrons I know by name._”

_She steps closer, hands folded lightly before her._

“_Welcome, all the same, to the Conservatory of Lirr. I am_ _**Averna Delarosa**__, High Priestess and mistress of this house of song and script. We keep our doors open to pilgrims, scholars… and, on occasion, those who arrive with mud on their boots and stories on their tongues._”

“_Tell me, then—what brings you here? Do you seek a book, a song, a lost name in some forgotten play… or merely a quiet place where truth may be spoken without steel?_”
