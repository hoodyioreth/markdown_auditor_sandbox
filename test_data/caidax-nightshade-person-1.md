Caidax Nightshade
=================

Caidax Nightshade is not a battlefield brute nor a roaring zealot. He is something far more unsettling — a patient architect of civil unrest. Recently recruited into the Earth Temple by Romag himself, Caidax operates as a destabilization agent whose purpose is to fracture the fragile political balance between the Kron Hills gnomes and the Viscounty of Verbobonc.

Where others raid for loot, Caidax raids for consequence. His mission is to provoke bloodshed under false colors. It does not matter whether humans massacre gnomes or gnomes retaliate against humans. What matters is the collapse of trust, the breakdown of alliances, and the widening of suspicion along the Long Road. In this, he serves Romag faithfully and reports through Lucius Graeme, who ensures the Earth Temple’s ambitions remain aligned with the Greater Temple’s long strategy.

##### Role within the Earth Temple

*   Field agent recruited personally by [Romag](romag-person.md)
*   Political destabilizer in southern Verbobonc
*   Coordinates false-flag operations against gnome settlements
*   Works in partnership with [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)
*   Reports results and receives orders via Lucius Graeme

* * *

#### The False Gnome Campaign

Caidax’s most dangerous undertaking is the carefully constructed false-flag campaign designed to shatter relations between humans and gnomes. Rather than openly assaulting settlements as Earth cultists, his raiders impersonate gnomish war bands and strike at caravans and isolated farmsteads along the Long Road corridor. Survivors are intentionally left behind to tell the tale.

The brilliance of the strategy lies in its ambiguity. A single massacre could ignite retaliation. A retaliatory strike could escalate into border conflict. The Earth Temple need not conquer territory if it can instead turn neighbors against one another. Caidax understands that hatred spreads faster than armies.

##### Strategic Objectives

*   Provoke open conflict between Kron Hills gnomes and Verbobonc
*   Undermine regional trade stability
*   Disrupt diplomatic efforts involving Celene and Veluna
*   Increase Earth Temple influence through chaos

Recruitment & Training Network
==============================

Caidax does not recruit personally in taverns. That task falls to intermediaries loyal to the Earth Temple. In Verbobonc City, [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) — known simply as “Lak” — recruits desperate men from The Bronze Unicorn Inn (F3). In Nulb, half-orcs are gathered from the Waterside Hostel, drawn by coin, vengeance, or cult loyalty.

These recruits are then transported — blindfolded — to a secluded valley far to the east. The valley’s precise location is known only to Maris, Caidax, and Romag. There, under Maris Blackshale’s hard discipline, they are trained in ambush tactics, coordinated retreat, and gnomish mimicry.

Caidax oversees the ideological shaping. Maris shapes the soldiers.

### Recruitment Sources

*   Humans recruited in Verbobonc City (via Lakash Quallad)
*   Half-orcs recruited in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) (Waterside Hostel)
*   Mixed raider groups assembled in hidden eastern valley
*   Blindfold protocol prevents recruits from revealing location

#### The Scroll of Veil

The deception that empowers the campaign rests on a powerful illusion device known as the Scroll of Veil. Created at Romag’s request and forged through arcane assistance from Senshock, the device allows raiders to assume the appearance of a gnome strike party.

Under its magic, height, silhouette, posture, and even voice are altered convincingly enough to fool frightened witnesses during fast-moving assaults. Caidax wields the wand focus tied to the Scroll, activating its power at the moment of engagement.

The illusion is not permanent and requires careful timing. Its limitations demand discipline — something Maris enforces ruthlessly among the raiders.

##### Capabilities

*   Illusory transformation into gnomish raiders
*   Alters physical outline and voice tone
*   Limited duration and charges
*   Requires wand focus created by [Senshock](senshock-person.md)

#### Personality

Caidax Nightshade is disarmingly articulate. His voice carries a melodic purity that has been compared to that of a nymph, yet when he speaks of civilization, nobility, or organized faith, a subtle growl creeps beneath the tone. He delights in exposing what he perceives as hypocrisy.

Curiously, he often leaves taverns cleaner than he found them and pays debts for strangers. He distributes coin to the needy and rewards loyalty generously. In his mind, he is not spreading chaos but forcing societies to confront their own fragility. He believes destruction clears the rot.

Privately, he drinks to quiet the memory of a life he abandoned.

##### Roleplaying Notes

*   Calm, eloquent, measured speech
*   Belittles institutions and aristocracy
*   Sees himself as a reformer through ruin
*   Drinks in solitude, not publicly
*   Prefers manipulation over violence

#### History

Born to a minor Oeridian house, Caidax’s arcane potential manifested early, though not strongly enough to satisfy his ambitious parents. They forced advanced magical training upon him in hopes of elevating their status. He admired true spellcasters deeply, yet he lacked the innate brilliance he so desperately sought.

Resentment grew where talent did not. Eventually he fled the expectations of his family and drifted into disillusionment and alcoholism. Romag encountered him not as a failure, but as a man stripped of illusion and hungry for purpose. The Earth Temple offered him clarity: if civilization was flawed, it deserved to break.

##### Background Summary

*   Minor Oeridian noble lineage
*   Trained in arcane arts beyond natural aptitude
*   Estranged from family
*   Struggles with alcoholism
*   Recruited by Romag as ideological field agent

#### Temple Politics

Within the Temple hierarchy, Caidax occupies a precarious but important role. He is loyal to Romag, yet watched by Lucius Graeme. He is skeptical of the Air Temple, believing the Cult of Howling Hatred to be unreliable and erratic. He prefers Earth’s patience and inevitability over Air’s volatility.

If conflict between Earth and Air cultists erupts near Feathergale Spire, Caidax may quietly encourage it.

##### Political Position

*   Loyal to Romag’s command
*   Reports routed through Lucius Graeme
*   Distrustful of Air Temple
*   Competes indirectly with Lareth’s influence

* * *

#### Current Objectives

At present, Caidax focuses on expanding the false-flag campaign while monitoring diplomatic movements along the Long Road. The capture of the Gift of Beauty has intensified tensions within the Temple. He sees opportunity in any delay of Veluna–Celene relations.

##### Active Goals

*   Continue staged gnome raids
*   Expand recruitment from Verbobonc and Nulb
*   Exploit Gift of Beauty political fallout
*   Maintain secrecy of hidden valley camp

##### Combat Tactics

Caidax avoids direct confrontation.

Opening round:

*   _Invisibility_ or _Expeditious Retreat_

Control:

*   _Hold Person_ on strongest melee threat
*   _Scare_ or _Cause Fear_ to scatter weaker enemies

Defense:

*   _Shield_ as reaction
*   Retreat using terrain and pre-planned escape routes

If escape seems unlikely, he deploys Quaal’s Feather Token (Bird) to flee.

He does not fight to the death.
