The Council of Lords of Verbobonc
=================================

The Council of Lords of Verbobonc is not a legislature. It has no binding vote. Its formal role is advisory — it advises Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md), shapes legislation through influence rather than law, engages in diplomacy on the Viscounty's behalf, and represents the interests of Verbobonc's noble houses against the Viscount's absolutism and against each other. In practice, it is something more volatile than its charter suggests: it is the arena where every significant decision about the Viscounty's future is actually made — not by vote, but by the slow accumulation of pressure, alliance, and leverage until a Viscount who wants to govern in peace finds that the path of least resistance leads somewhere he did not intend.

> _"Together We Lead, Together We Thrive."_

### Authority and Structure

The Council meets in the great chamber of Castle Greyfist, Verbobonc City — a hall that has been the site of every significant political crisis in the Viscounty for three generations. The stone table is long enough for all members to sit without crowding, the windows face west toward the Kron Hills, and there is a carving above the main door that reads, in Old Oeridian, _What is decided here endures._ Everyone in the room knows this is either an aspiration or a warning depending on the day.

*   **Formal Powers:** The Council advises the Viscount on military strategy, economic policy, diplomatic relations, and legislation. A Viscount who ignores his Council routinely will find his decisions undermined, his resources quietly redirected, and his allies unexpectedly unavailable when he needs them most.
*   **Limits:** The Council cannot override the Viscount's Right of High Justice or his command of the Verbobonc militia. It cannot formally remove him. What it can do is make governing so difficult that the distinction between "cannot" and "will not" becomes irrelevant.
*   **Membership:** The thirteen seats belong to the senior members of Verbobonc's noble houses by right of blood and holding. The Lord Mayor of Verbobonc City holds a fourteenth seat by office. ["Prince" Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) of the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) attends as a diplomatic observer — technically without a vote, practically with a great deal of one, because any decision that affects the gnomes without Pithriggen's knowledge tends to produce expensive consequences.
*   **The Viscount's Role:** Wilfrick chairs Council sessions but does not control them. He sets the agenda, holds the final authority, and absorbs the pressure when the chamber cannot reach working agreement. In 576 CY, the pressure is considerable.
*   **The Chamberlain:** **[Aldric Hollend](aldric-hollend-person.md)**, the Viscount's Chamberlain, is not a member of the Council — but no Council session occurs without his preparation. Before every meeting, Aldric briefs the Viscount on the current factional positions, pending disputes, and likely ambush points. He controls the agenda's sequencing, manages the flow of petitions and intelligence into [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md), and determines which correspondence reaches the Viscount's desk and when. A former Knight Captain of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) who took a lance wound at Emridy Meadows and traded the saddle for the antechamber, Aldric has served two Viscounts across three decades. He holds no vote and claims no faction. What he holds is the Viscount's schedule, the Viscount's correspondence, and the Viscount's trust — and every lord at the table knows that the man standing quietly by the door is the reason some of their letters were answered promptly and others were not.

> "The Council is not where Verbobonc is governed. It is where governance goes to be argued about until everyone is too tired to block it." — The current one, **Aldric Hollend**, has not publicly corrected the sentiment.

### The Factions of the Council — 576 CY

The Council of Lords does not have formal parties. It has interests — and interests, in Verbobonc, are the same thing as factions, just with better manners. In 576 CY, four major alignments shape the chamber's dynamics.

#### The Militarists — Velysin and Milinous

The militarist bloc believes that Verbobonc's current problems have a military solution and that the Viscount's reluctance to apply it is the primary obstacle to Viscounty security. They are not wrong about the problem. They are often wrong about the solution.

*   **Lord [Claviger Velysin](claviger-velysin-person.md) — \[[House Velysin](house-velysin-organization.md)\]:** Former Mayor of Verbobonc and current Captain of the Militia — a combination of offices that gives him more practical institutional authority than any other Council member except Haxx. His domains lie east of Verbobonc City; his connections to the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md) are genuine and go back decades. Velysin is not a subtle man. He believes the Temple's resurgence requires a preemptive military response, the gnome situation requires a firm declaration of Viscounty authority, and the banditry on the roads requires armed patrols at sufficient strength to actually deter it. He is correct about the diagnosis and consistently underestimates what his prescriptions would cost in gnome goodwill and treasury resources simultaneously.
*   **Lord [Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md) — \[[House Milinous](house-milinous-organization.md)\]:** Once among the Viscount's more reliable allies, Milinous has grown noticeably cooler in his support over the past two sessions — the kind of cooling that political observers read as a man repositioning himself for a future that doesn't look like the present one. He is hardline on military response but increasingly willing to let Velysin carry the argument while he watches the room. What he wants beyond the militarist agenda is not entirely clear yet. That ambiguity is, in itself, a strategy.

#### The Economic Powers — Haxx, Galans, and Vaswell

The economic faction cares about the roads, the trade routes, and the guild revenues — not because they are incapable of thinking beyond commerce, but because the Temple's resurgence and the gnome crisis are primarily reaching them as trade disruption, and trade disruption is the thing that costs them money and costs them allies among Verbobonc's merchant class.

*   **Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md) — \[[House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md)\]:** The most powerful single figure in Verbobonc City after the Viscount himself — and arguably before him in terms of practical daily authority. Official tax collector, guild regulator, and commander of [Haxx's Hardheads](haxx-s-hardheads-organization.md), Verbobonc's paid enforcement. He has argued openly and repeatedly for a return to more republican governance. He is, depending on one's political priors, a dangerous democrat or a disguised power-seeker using democratic language — and both readings have supporting evidence. What is not in question is that the banditry on the Long Road costs his guild interests significantly, and his patience with measured Viscounty responses is visibly shortening.
*   **Lord [Roland Galans](roland-galans-person.md) — \[[House Galans](house-galans-organization.md):** Known as "the Grand" — a nickname that refers simultaneously to his girth, his wardrobe, and the scale of his ambitions. Roland's house holds the prosperous shipping yards along the Velverdyva and the fertile fields of the river valley, which makes him Verbobonc's second-largest private employer after Haxx. He is jovial at table and precisely calculating in session. He is Haxx's ally on most trade questions, his competitor on a few key ones, and no one's friend when his shipping revenues are threatened. The disruption of the Imeryds Run and the waterway commerce through [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) has reached his ledgers. He is not pleased.
*   **Baronet [Drenton Vaswell](drenton-vaswell-person.md) — \[[House Vaswell](house-vaswell-organization.md)\]:** The Council member most routinely underestimated — partly by design. Vaswell presents as eccentric, occasionally distracted, and not entirely focused on the proceedings. His holdings along the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md) are modest in comparison to Galans but strategically placed at key crossing points. He is aware of exactly what he controls, who needs it, and how that fact can be leveraged. In Council sessions, his apparent inattention is a performance: he misses nothing, commits to nothing publicly, and tends to appear on the winning side of decisions after having contributed very little to the visible argument that produced them.

#### The Manipulators — Langmuir and Sarcina

Two Council members operate through influence rather than direct argument — positioning pieces rather than making moves, accumulating advantage in the space between other people's decisions.

*   **Lady [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md), "The Iron Lady" — \[[House Langmuir](house-langmuir-organization.md)\]:** Langmuir's estates lie in the middle of the Viscounty — strategically central, politically invaluable. She does not publicly oppose Wilfrick. She does not publicly oppose anyone. She asks questions in Council that seem neutral and are not, she arranges conversations that she does not appear in, and she is positioning her son Lord [Ludovic Langmuir](ludovic-langmuir-person.md) for something whose shape is not yet visible. Wilfrick watches her more carefully than any other member of the Council. Burne, reading the correspondence from Hommlet, has flagged her as the member most likely to have information about the mine claim filings and least likely to volunteer it.
*   **Lady [Kathryn Sarcina](kathryn-sarcina-person.md) — \[[House Sarcina](house-sarcina-organization.md)\]:** The Matriarch of House Sarcina operates through charm and apparent accessibility in the way that Langmuir operates through silence and apparent neutrality — two different entry points to the same position of influence. House Sarcina's wealth is mercantile in origin, which places them adjacent to the Haxx faction on trade issues but independent of it on everything else. Sarcina's alliances shift more fluidly than any other Council member's and have for twenty years. The key question — one that no one in the Council has fully answered — is whether she has a fixed destination or is simply optimizing for survival and advantage in whatever arrangement exists at any given moment.

#### The Border Interest — Lord Deleveu

*   **Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) — Lord of @Ostverk:** The only half-elf on the Council, and the only member who simultaneously holds a Council seat and field command of a Viscounty military force — the southern border [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md). Deleveu balances loyalties to Verbobonc and to [Celene](celene-organization.md), to the human nobility of the Viscounty and to the elven communities of the Gnarley fringe. His lordship of Ostverk places him directly in the territory most affected by the gnome caravan raids. The Viscount's decision to withdraw the Mounted Borderers from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) placed Deleveu in an impossible position: his own men pulled back from a region where his own people are being raided, under orders from a Viscount he technically serves and personally disagrees with. He has not said this in Council. He has also not said the opposite. The silence is loud.

#### The Moderate — Rhynehurst

*   **Lord [Wendell Rhynehurst](wendell-rhynehurst-person.md) — \[[House Rhynehurst](house-rhynehurst-organization.md)\]:** Beloved by Verbobonc's common folk for his genuine interest in their welfare and his support of the city's arts and local economies. His estates at Rhynehurst — held by the Saflynd family, whose Lord Alywen governs the town itself — are culturally rich and economically modest, which means he has influence without the resource base that makes influence leverage. He is the Council member Wilfrick most wants to keep informed and closest to persuaded, because Rhynehurst is the voice that can shift the chamber's mood in a way that neither the militarists nor the economic powers can. When Rhynehurst says something is wrong, people who dismiss Velysin and roll their eyes at Haxx tend to listen. Lord Burne, in his private correspondence, has identified Rhynehurst as the fulcrum — the member who, moved to the right position at the right moment, can give the Viscount the working majority he cannot currently assemble.

#### The Wildcard — Avgustin

*   **Baron [Rollof Avgustin](rollof-avgustin-person.md) — \[[House Avgustin](house-avgustin-organization.md)\]:** House Avgustin's ancestral seat at Loren's Ford gives them a significant military presence in the southern Viscounty and a complex web of trade relationships that intersect with almost every other house on the Council. Avgustin is known for complex relationships with other nobles — which in Verbobonc court language means he has debts and obligations that run in several directions and is not always free to vote his actual preference. He is influential in the trade sector and not entirely transparent about which sector of it he is most beholden to. He repays watching.

#### The Enigma — Chondell

 

*   **Lord [Kosak Chondell](kosak-chondell-person.md) — \[[House Chondell](house-chondell-organization.md)\]:** The most distant and deliberately mysterious member of the Council. Chondell's seat at [Castle Chondell](castle-chondell-landmark.md) — high on the border road between Verbobonc and [Veluna](veluna-organization.md) — is two days' ride from Verbobonc City, and his attendance at Council sessions reflects that distance. When he appears, it is an event. A tall, darkly handsome man whose gothic manner has fueled decades of vampire rumors, Chondell maintains a carefully cultivated persona of aristocratic menace that serves a practical purpose: no one bothers him uninvited, and no one knows his actual position on anything until he announces it. His house controls silver mines in the Lortmils, a trading company running routes from Dyvers to Veluna, a textile mill in the city, and the Chondell Winery in Rhynehurst — which means his economic reach is substantial even though his political profile is deliberately low. He pays his Viscounty taxes diligently and on time, takes no firm factional stance, and votes infrequently but strategically. When Chondell speaks in Council, the other twelve members pay attention — because whatever moved him to open his mouth is likely to matter. His sister Lady Elina manages the house's city interests. His cousin Sir Dorian manages the mines and pushes to expand operations. And recently, Chondell has been watching Baron Shannus of the Iron Wood, who has eyes on the town of Validia in Chondell lands — a territorial pressure that may draw the reclusive lord into more active politics whether he likes it or not.

 

#### The Loyalist — Stefania

 

*   **Baroness [Mirabella Stefania](mirabella-stefania-person.md) — \[[House Stefania](house-stefania-organization.md)\]:** The half-elf Baroness of Littleburrow is the Council member Wilfrick can count on — and in 576 CY, that makes her invaluable. She inherited her father's title and has governed with grace, warmth, and a genuine devotion to her people's welfare that has made her one of the most beloved nobles in the Viscounty. She is a staunch supporter of the Viscount, believing openly that Wilfrick is doing an excellent job leading in difficult times — a position she states without qualification in a chamber where most members hedge their endorsements with conditions and caveats. Her family maintains close ties with [House Avgustin](house-avgustin-organization.md) through the Brandenberg marriage connection, which gives her an informal alliance with Baron Rollof that occasionally provides Wilfrick with two reliable votes where he otherwise might have one. Her holdings at Visengart and Littleburrow are culturally rich and well-administered. Her bodyguard and confidante, General Bronn — a centaur who served her late father — must approve all suitors for Mirabella's hand, a detail that Council gossips find endlessly fascinating. On matters of defense and security, Stefania defers to military expertise she does not pretend to have. On matters of governance, loyalty, and community welfare, she speaks with an authority that even Velysin and Haxx cannot easily dismiss. She is not a power broker. She is something rarer: a genuine voice of principle in a room full of strategists.

 

#### The Voice of the Hills — Pithriggen

*   **"Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) — [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md):** Pithriggen attends Council as the diplomatic representative of the gnome communities of the Kron Hills. He has no formal vote. He has, on a dozen occasions, demonstrated that his absence from future sessions carries more weight than his attendance. Every significant decision the Council makes that touches gnome territory, gnome trade, or gnome mine rights produces consequences that eventually arrive at the gnome representative's seat — and Pithriggen has been at that seat long enough to know exactly which Council member filed what document, voted for which policy, and said which thing in which private conversation. He is unfailingly polite. He is also keeping a ledger.

_

> "The gnomes remember everything. Every promise made and not kept, every patrol withdrawn, every mine claim filed without their knowledge. I attend these sessions because I was asked to. I remain in this chair because what is decided here affects my people. And I will continue to remain here until the Council understands that these two sentences are not in conflict." — Jimm Pithriggen, to a junior Council aide who asked why he bothered

### The Four Debates the Council Cannot Resolve — 576 CY

In 576 CY, the Council of Lords is gridlocked on the four most consequential questions facing the Viscounty. Each faction has an answer. No two factions have the same one, and no faction has enough working votes to impose theirs.

#### The Temple of Elemental Evil — Respond or Wait?

The militarists — Velysin foremost — argue for preemptive action: reconnaissance in force toward the ruins east of Hommlet, reinforced patrols, formal declaration of a Temple Threat, and a request to [Veluna](veluna-organization.md) for auxiliary troops. The economic powers argue that the trade disruption is already costing more than a military deployment would and want security resources directed at the roads first. Langmuir asks who would command the operation and suggests that the question of command authority deserves careful consideration before resources are committed. Deleveu says nothing and watches the maps. Wilfrick has not called a formal vote. He is waiting for the intelligence from [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) to become conclusive enough to overcome the Council's inertia — and is aware that waiting for conclusive intelligence about the Temple of Elemental Evil has a historical precedent that did not end well.

#### The Gnome Crisis — Negotiate or Assert?

The mine claim documentation filed against gnome holdings in [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) sits in the Verbobonc legal archive unresolved. Velysin supports asserting Viscounty legal authority over the claims. Haxx wants the gnome trade routes restored regardless of the legal question because his guild revenues depend on them. Deleveu wants the Mounted Borderers redeployed but cannot say so without openly contradicting a direct Viscount decision. Pithriggen is watching to see what the Council does and has not yet said what the consequence of the wrong answer will be. The Viscount has not yet acted on the filings. He is waiting for a legal determination. The legal determination has been awaited for four months.

#### Road Security — Whose Problem Is It?

Banditry on the Long Road from Verbobonc south through Nigb's Rest to Hommlet and east from Nulb has escalated from opportunistic raiding to something more organized — attacks that show knowledge of cargo, timing, and which roads are currently patrolled. Haxx wants guild-funded mercenary contracts under his authority. Velysin wants a Militia response under his authority. [Lord Rufus](lord-rufus-person.md) has submitted through Burne that [Burne's Badgers](burne-s-badgers-organization.md) at thirteen men cannot cover the gap alone. The Council has authorized resources for a search of the missing stone shipment road. The resources have not yet left Verbobonc City. What no one has said in a Council session — and what Burne has noted in private correspondence — is that the banditry pattern is consistent with what the Temple used before Emridy Meadows to isolate settlements and prepare for resurgence. The Council is debating a logistics problem that may be a military operation.

#### Succession — The Question No One Asks

Wilfrick is fifty. His eldest son [Fenward Revepaix](fenward-revepaix-person.md) is broadly considered unsuitable — an opinion shared by most of the Council and, in his more candid moments, the Viscount. His adopted daughter [Elysia Revepaix](elysia-revepaix-person.md) is broadly considered the more capable heir — an opinion that Haxx contests on traditionalist grounds and that Langmuir has neither opposed nor endorsed. The question of succession is not on the formal agenda and will not be until it has to be. In the meantime, Elysia attends Council sessions as her father's briefing aide and everyone in the room knows exactly what she is doing and that she is better at it than they are._

> "This Council has debated road safety three times in six months and has not yet dispatched a single additional patrol. I have been asked to be patient. I have been patient. I am now going to be specific: which member of this body is prepared to tell their merchant constituents why the Long Road is less safe than it was a year ago, and which member is prepared to explain what happened to the gnome miners whose caravans were destroyed on the road those merchants use?" — Jimm Pithriggen, Council session, 576 CY

### DM Notes — The Council as a Campaign Engine

The Council of Lords is the campaign's political engine. It is not a place adventurers walk into — it is a place adventurers are summoned to, report back to, are deployed by, and occasionally get caught between. Every significant political development in the Viscounty either originates here or lands here eventually.

#### How to Use the Council

*   **Individual members as rival patrons:** Each Council member has their own agenda and their own resources. A party that navigates the Council's factions carefully can assemble support from multiple members simultaneously — provided they have not already made enemies of the members those patrons oppose.
*   **The information game:** What each Council member knows and doesn't know about the Temple, the gnomes, and the banditry is itself an adventure resource. Parties who bring credible intelligence to the right member at the right moment can shift Council positions in ways that produce real political movement — including the Mounted Borderer redeployment.
*   **Factional pressure:** If the party becomes publicly associated with one Council faction, the opposing factions will notice. This has consequences for access, for commissions, and for what support they can draw on when they need it.
*   **The Council as obstacle:** When Wilfrick needs something done that cannot survive a Council vote, he commissions it privately. This is how parties end up doing things the Viscount needs and cannot officially authorize — and why they sometimes find Council members actively obstructing what the Viscount has asked for.
*   **The Chamberlain as gateway:** **[Aldric Hollend](aldric-hollend-person.md)** is the party's point of entry into the Council's political machinery. Before the party sees the Viscount, they see Aldric. Before the Viscount acts on their intelligence, Aldric evaluates it. The Chamberlain is not an obstacle — he is a filter, a briefer, and the man who issues the Viscount's quiet commissions to trusted agents. A party that earns Aldric's trust gains access to political intelligence, advance warning of Council moves, and the Viscount's attention when they need it. A party that ignores or antagonizes the Chamberlain will find the doors of Castle Greyfist politely but permanently closed.

#### Adventure Hooks

*   **The Mine Claim Documents:** Someone filed false documentation in the Verbobonc legal archive claiming ownership of gnome mines under a 412 CY land grant. The party can be commissioned — by Wilfrick, by Pithriggen, or by Burne — to determine whether the documents are genuine and, if not, who filed them. The answer implicates a Council member. Which one depends on what the DM wants the next six sessions to look like.
*   **The Deleveu Dilemma:** Lord Deleveu has information about the Mounted Borderer withdrawal that he has not shared with the Council — because sharing it would require him to publicly contradict the Viscount. He needs a third party to surface that information without his fingerprints on it. He approaches the party through an intermediary. The mission is information delivery. The political consequence is a Council crisis.
*   **Langmuir's Question:** Lady Langmuir sends a private message to one party member — specifically one — asking for a meeting at her estate. She wants to know what the party found in the Temple ruins, and she will offer significant resources in exchange. She will not explain why she wants to know. The question for the party: who is she asking for, and what does she intend to do with the answer?
*   **The Hearing:** The Council convenes a formal hearing on the Temple threat — prompted by either party action or a specific incident — and summons the party as witnesses. Every faction will attempt to frame the party's testimony to support their position before the hearing begins. The party can decide whose framing to accept, whose to resist, and whether to say something no one in the room is prepared to hear.
*   **Sarcina's Alliance Offer:** Lady Kathryn Sarcina approaches the party with a proposal: she will back whatever action they need Council support for, in exchange for a service she needs done quietly. The service is not illegal. It is, however, the kind of thing that will make one of the other Council members very uncomfortable. She is charming about this. She is also quite serious.
*   **The Chamberlain's Commission:** **Aldric Hollend** summons the party to a private meeting — not in the Council chamber, not in the Viscount's study, but in the Chamberlain's own office in the lower corridors of Castle Greyfist. He has a task that cannot go through official channels. He will explain what he needs, provide what context he can, and leave the rest to the party's judgment. This is how the Viscount's quiet commissions work: Aldric is the mechanism, the party is the instrument, and the Council never learns what happened until the consequences arrive at the next session.

##### Noble Estates and Holdings

The noble houses represented on the Council have extensive estates and holdings that contribute to their power and influence.

*   [House Revepaix](house-revepaix-organization.md)**:** Viscounty of Verbobonc ruling house. Extensive lands and military installations, including the newly constructed castle at Hommlet.
*   [House Milinous](house-milinous-organization.md)**:** Expansive domains east of Verbobonc City, influential in maintaining law and order. Milinous Manor (A11) commands the High Quarter.
*   [House Sarcina](house-sarcina-organization.md)**:** Economically powerful, with significant investments and trade connections throughout the Viscounty.
*   [House Langmuir](house-langmuir-organization.md)**:** Wealthy and strategically located estates in the middle of the Viscounty's lands, though marred by internal manipulations and secret agendas.
*   [House Galans](house-galans-organization.md)**:** Prosperous shipping yards and fertile fields, bolstering their economic influence on the Velverdyva.
*   [House Vaswell](house-vaswell-organization.md)**:** Modest yet strategically placed holdings along the Velverdyva River at Eglath, vital for monitoring trade and the Imeryds Run confluence.
*   [House Avgustin](house-avgustin-organization.md)**:** Known for their ancestral home in Loren's Ford and significant military presence.
*   [House Rhynehurst](house-rhynehurst-organization.md)**:** Culturally rich estates in Rhynehurst (held by the Saflynd family) with a strong emphasis on the arts and local economy. Home to the famed Riverwalk.
*   [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md)**:** Dominates trade routes via [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md) and maintains a powerful economic and political presence from Haxx Manor (A5) and the Waterfront District.
*   [House Chondell](house-chondell-organization.md)**:** Remote but wealthy. [Castle Chondell](castle-chondell-landmark.md) on the Veluna border, silver mines in the Lortmils, textile mill and winery in Rhynehurst. Reclusive but economically significant.
*   [House Stefania](house-stefania-organization.md)**:** Well-administered holdings at Visengart and Littleburrow. Culturally vibrant, loyal to the Viscount, beloved by the common folk.
*   [House Deleveu](house-deleveu-organization-1.md)**:** Southern border estates commanding the approaches to the Viscounty's frontier, vital for defense and border security.
*   [House Velysin](house-velysin-organization.md)**:** Military estates and installations supporting the city militia and garrison. Velysin's authority extends across the city's defensive infrastructure.
