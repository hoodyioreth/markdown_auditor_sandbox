Rasskarth the Great Lizard King
===============================

Deep within the **Fens of Tor**, the name **Rasskarth** is still spoken in hushed, reverent tones among the lizardfolk of **Zassil’torr (‘Torfen’ in Common)**. Once a mighty warlord and self-proclaimed **Lizard King**, his reign of terror **threatened the rise of human civilization in Verbobonc**. Though he was defeated **centuries ago** by Count Ramsi, the first ruler of what would become the [Verbobonc, Viscounty](../c/verbobonc2C-viscounty-category.md), his **dark legacy lingers**.

> "The swamp does not forget, nor does it forgive. Beneath its waters, something ancient stirs, waiting for the day when the blood debt is repaid."

Now, in **576 CY**, whispers of **Rasskarth’s return** grow louder, as cultists of **Sess’innek**, the demon-lord of dominance and destruction, seek to **undo the ancient wards that hold his spirit at bay**. If they succeed, the Fens of Tor may once again become a **kingdom of scaled warlords, raiding and conquering in their master’s name**.

* * *

### **Ancient Origins and the Ur-Flannae Legacy**

##### **(-3000 CY to -440 CY)**

The Fens of Tor, where Rasskarth would later rise to power, were once a stronghold of **Ur-Flan necromancers and priests of Nerull**, who left behind ruins and arcane sites steeped in dark magic. These places became the focus of lizardfolk shamans, who venerated the lingering power of the Ur-Flannae. Some legends claim that Rasskarth himself was guided by dreams and visions tied to this ancient sorcery, leading him to uncover powerful relics that cemented his rule.

### **The Twin Cataclysms and Oeridian Migrations**

##### **(-440 CY to -100 CY)**

As the Suel and Baklunish empires fell, the Oeridian migrations swept into the Flanaess, bringing conflict to the region. During this time, human warbands clashed with lizardfolk tribes over control of the fertile lands and waterways. These skirmishes forced the lizardfolk into more defensive, isolated enclaves, setting the stage for their eventual unification under Rasskarth centuries later.

### **The Velondi Oeridians and the Rise of Gnome-Elf Alliances**

##### **(-100 CY to 1 CY)**

The Velondi Oeridians arrived and began establishing fortified settlements, displacing monstrous occupiers and forming alliances with gnomes and elves. This period saw the first structured human presence in the region, with settlers constructing defensive holdings that would later grow into permanent villages. The expansion of Verbobonc’s influence brought increasing tension with the lizardfolk, who viewed the growing human presence as an existential threat.

### **The Rise of the Great Lizard King**

##### **(5 CY - 80 CY)**

By 5 CY, Rasskarth had risen to power, uniting the lizardfolk of the Fens of Tor under a singular banner. His leadership combined brute force with strategic cunning, allowing his forces to push deep into human-controlled lands. His warriors, emboldened by his sorcery and ruthless leadership, expanded beyond the swamp’s murky borders. This period coincided with the consolidation of **Count Ramsi’s rule over Verbobonc**, whose forces clashed with Rasskarth’s armies in a brutal war for dominance.

Born of the **Za’rassk tribe**, Rasskarth was **larger, stronger, and far more cunning than his kin**. Legends claim he was **blessed—or cursed—by** [Sess'innek](sess-innek-person.md), the tanar’ri lord of the lizardfolk. Others whisper that he uncovered **forbidden ruins deep in the Fens**, awakening **powers no mortal should wield**.

Within a decade, **Rasskarth conquered or subjugated rival lizardfolk tribes**, forging an empire of blood and terror. He declared himself the **Great Lizard King**, and his warriors, emboldened by his sorcery and ruthless leadership, expanded beyond the swamp’s murky borders.

##### **Key Events:**

*   **5 CY** – Rasskarth rises as a warlord among the lizardfolk.
*   **10 CY - 25 CY** – Through war and dark sorcery, he unites the tribes and proclaims himself **Lizard King of the Fens**.
*   **25 CY - 80 CY** – Rasskarth expands his kingdom, raiding human settlements, clashing with gnome enclaves, and **desecrating temples in honor of Sess’innek**.
*   \*\*His followers construct the temple-fortress of **Zyssaneth**, a place of blood rituals and sacrifices.

* * *

### **The Battle of Waycombe & Rasskarth’s Fall (85 CY)**

As **Rasskarth’s empire grew**, his raids upon **the human settlements along the Velverdyva** became too bold to ignore. The newly emerging **human noble houses of Verbobonc, led by Count Ramsi**, saw the Lizard King as a **threat to their survival**.

In **85 CY**, Count Ramsi gathered an army of **Oeridian warriors, gnomish engineers, and elven rangers from Celene**. Marching deep into the **Fens of Tor**, they lured Rasskarth’s forces into the flooded lowlands near **Waycombe**, where a final battle would decide the fate of the region.

Rasskarth himself **led the charge**, wielding a **great blade infused with demonic power**. But he was met in single combat by Count Ramsi, who **wielded a sacred sword blessed by Raoan and St. Cuthbertine clerics**. In a vicious duel, Ramsi **slashed Rasskarth across the chest**, the blade burning away his unnatural vitality. The Great Lizard King **fell**, his body sinking into the mire, his warriors **scattering into the swamps**.

##### **Key Events:**

*   **85 CY – The Battle of Waycombe:** Count Ramsi defeats Rasskarth in single combat.
*   **With their leader slain, the lizardfolk empire collapses, and human rule solidifies in Verbobonc.**
*   **Rasskarth’s body is entombed in the temple of Zyssaneth**, bound by magical seals to prevent his return.

* * *

**The Legacy of Rasskarth (85 CY - 576 CY)**
--------------------------------------------

With Rasskarth’s death, his empire **crumbled**, and his followers either **assimilated into smaller lizardfolk tribes** or went into **hiding deep within the Fens**. However, not all of his people were lost. The descendants of those who followed him **still exist today** in the village of **Zassil’torr (‘Torfen’ in Common)**.

For generations, these lizardfolk have survived as **hunters and traders**, maintaining an uneasy truce with their human and gnomish neighbors. Yet within their ranks, a **fanatical sect** remains devoted to **Rasskarth’s return**—and now, in 576 CY, one of their own **seeks to break the ancient seals**.

* * *

### **The Cult of Rasskarth: A Modern Threat (576 CY)**

##### **Ssarkosh, the Swamp Seer**

Among the shamans of **Zassil’torr**, none are more feared than **Ssarkosh**, the self-proclaimed **Swamp Seer**. Once a respected elder, Ssarkosh has **turned to the worship of Sess’innek**, convinced that the Lizard King must rise again. He has gathered **fanatics and mercenaries**, promising that Rasskarth’s resurrection will **restore the dominance of the lizardfolk**.

##### **Allies in the Shadows: Nulb and the Temple of Elemental Evil**

Ssarkosh is **not alone** in his quest. **Pirate Captain** [Tolub](tolub-person.md) , a ruthless raider based in **Nulb**, has **supplied him with gold and stolen relics**, hoping to **use Rasskarth as a weapon**. Meanwhile, **Canon Belsornig, High Priest of the Water Temple in the Temple of Elemental Evil**, **watches from the shadows**, eager to turn Ssarkosh’s fanaticism into an asset for his own growing power.

If the **seals on Rasskarth’s tomb** are broken, a **new war may begin—one that could spill out of the Fens of Tor and into the lands of Verbobonc itself**.

##### **Key Events & Modern Threats:**

*   **576 CY – Ssarkosh gathers followers to break the wards on Rasskarth’s tomb.**
*   **Pirates of Nulb, led by** Tolub**, fund Ssarkosh’s efforts.**
*   **Canon Belsornig sees Rasskarth’s revival as a means to spread chaos.**
*   **High Warden Ssalissar of Zassil’torr resists Ssarkosh, but his influence is waning.**

* * *

### **Dungeon Master Plot Hooks & Adventure Ideas**

##### **The Broken Wards**

A **cleric of Rao or St. Cuthbert** summons the party to investigate **strange disturbances** near the Fens of Tor. The **seals on Rasskarth’s tomb are weakening**, and it’s up to the heroes to **prevent Ssarkosh from finishing his ritual**.

##### **Lizardfolk Civil War**

The town of **Zassil’torr is on the brink of collapse**. The **party must navigate the power struggle** between **High Warden Ssalissar, who wishes for peace, and Ssarkosh, who calls for war**.

##### **Pirates and the Lizard King**

The **party learns that Captain** Tolub has stolen **a powerful relic tied to Rasskarth**. They must **infiltrate his pirate crew** in Nulb to recover it before it’s too late.

#### **Final Thoughts: The Lizard King’s Shadow Looms**

For centuries, **Rasskarth’s name was a legend**, his bones **sealed beneath the swamp**. But **now**, in the year 576 CY, his followers seek to **undo the past**.

If the heroes fail, **a new Lizard King will rise**—and the Fens of Tor will **once again march to war.**
