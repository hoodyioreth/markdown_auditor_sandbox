Lareth
======

### (a.k.a. the Beautiful)

Lareth the Beautiful commands the Moathouse, a critical outpost for the Greater Temple of Elemental Evil. As the epitome of chaotic evil, he is young, handsome, and capable, embodying both the charm and depravity of his dark masters. His influence extends throughout the surrounding lands, serving as a linchpin in the Temple’s schemes of dominance, raiding, and recruitment.

> _"Lareth the Beautiful is the dark hope of chaotic evil: young, handsome, well endowed in abilities and aptitudes, thoroughly wicked, depraved and capricious_." - Gary Gygax.

##### The Paragon of the Temple of Elemental Evil

Lareth's appearance is striking, with a captivating beauty that masks the wickedness within.

*   **Appearance:** Youthful, with flawless skin and chiseled features, Lareth's captivating visage is both disarming and unnerving.
*   **Hair and Eyes:** His jet-black hair is always immaculately groomed, and his piercing gray eyes exude a mixture of charisma and cruelty.
*   **Clothing:** He wears ceremonial black and crimson robes adorned with the golden burning eye symbol of the Temple of Elemental Evil, often layered with a chainmail shirt beneath for battle readiness.
*   **Presence:** Lareth’s confidence and eloquence make him a figure of reverence to his followers and a dangerous manipulator to his enemies.

_

> "Do you see? Even the weak can serve a purpose—for my amusement, if nothing else."

_

#### Personality

Lareth is the embodiment of chaos and evil, blending charm, cunning, and malice into a terrifying persona.

*   **Cunning**: Lareth is a master manipulator, using his charisma and honeyed words to sway even the most skeptical of individuals.
*   **Cruelty**: He delights in the suffering of others, viewing it as both entertainment and a demonstration of his power.
*   **Ambition:** Lareth’s ultimate goal is to ascend within the ranks of the Temple and bring glory to the [Elder Elemental God](elder-elemental-god-person.md).
*   **Pragmatism:** While loyal to his masters, Lareth is willing to bend the rules or betray alliances if it serves his long-term interests.

_

> "Beauty is a weapon far sharper than any blade. How fortunate for me that I wield both."

_

### Background

Born to unknown origins, Lareth’s rise to power within the Temple hierarchy has been meteoric, driven by his natural talents and devotion to chaos.

*   **Rise in the Temple:** Selected by [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md), Supreme Commander of the Greater Temple, for his potential, Lareth quickly proved himself as an adept raider and recruiter.
*   **Connection to the Elder Elemental God:** Lareth is ostensibly loyal to the [Cult of the Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md).
*   **Outpost Command:** Lareth was assigned to rebuild and secure the Moathouse, transforming it into a staging ground for raids, recruitment, and resource distribution.

#### Motivation

Lareth’s ambitions are grand, and his actions are meticulously calculated to achieve them.

*   **Domination of the Region:** Lareth aims to consolidate power over the surrounding lands, starting with Hommlet, and expand the Temple’s influence.
*   **Accrual of Power:** Lareth constantly seeks to gain more magical and political power, eliminating any threats to his ascension.

_

> "Chaos is not the absence of order. It is the supreme expression of power."

_

### Operations at the Moathouse

The Moathouse serves as Lareth’s base of operations and a critical hub for the Temple’s activities.

##### Description of the Moathouse

The Moathouse is a decayed fortress located in the swampy lands near Hommlet.

*   **Architecture:** A crumbling stone structure with moss-covered walls and a flooded moat, the Moathouse blends into the surrounding marshland.
*   **Defenses:** Hidden traps, ambush points, and reinforced bandit patrols make it a difficult stronghold to penetrate.
*   **Supply Hub:** The Moathouse receives shipments of slaves, loot, and supplies from bandit raids, which are then funneled to the Greater Temple through the [55\. Hunting Cabin - off map](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md).

##### Lareth’s Forces

Lareth commands a well-trained and loyal group of bandits, mercenaries, and humanoids.

*   **Lieutenant** [Gattas Void](gattas-void-person.md): Lareth’s second-in-command, responsible for organizing raids and maintaining discipline among the troops.
*   **Bandits:** Eighteen guardsmen and sergeants form the core of his forces, bolstered by humanoid fighters such as gnolls and goblins.
*   **Henchmen:** Lareth employs elite lieutenants and spellcasters to enhance his tactical capabilities.

_

> "You think I am just a man? Then come closer. Let me show you how little you understand true power."

_

### Relationships

##### Temple Leadership

*   **[Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) and Senshock:** Lareth maintains regular communication with these high-ranking leaders of the Greater Temple, who view him as a rising star.
*   [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md): This Greater Temple courier frequently visits Lareth to deliver orders and assess his progress.

##### Allies and Agents

Lareth's success as the commander of the Moathouse hinges on a network of loyal allies, cunning agents, and opportunistic spies. These individuals work to support his operations, ensuring the Temple of Elemental Evil's influence continues to expand across the Viscounty of Verbobonc. Some allies are aware of Lareth's affiliation with Lolth, while others remain loyal to the Cult of the Elder Elemental God.

### Current Operations

*   Recruiting and organizing bandits to harass travelers and expand the Temple's influence through theft and intimidation.
*   Maintaining undead patrols throughout the Moathouse's lower levels to prevent exploration and guard supply caches.
*   Attempting to decipher a series of ancient Suel runes carved into the dungeon's deepest chambers — possibly a ward, a map, or a call to something older than the Moathouse itself.
*   Consolidating control over the remaining Moathouse garrison and preparing contingency plans if the Temple's outer operations are discovered.

### Adventure Hooks

1.  **The Bandit Network:** If the party questions captured bandits or recovers a recruitment ledger, they learn that Lareth has been personally vetting mercenaries and forest brigands for service. Pursuing this link forces a confrontation with Lareth as the coordinator and face of the operation.
2.  **Suel Rune Investigation:** If the party explores the Moathouse's lowest level and discovers the carved runes, they may interrupt Lareth during a translation session. He will attempt to kill intruders and has prepared the chamber as a killing ground with hidden undead allies.
3.  **The Missing Girl:** If a party member or NPC has a connection to **\[Hommlet\], they can learn that a local girl vanished weeks ago. Lareth may have imprisoned her in the Moathouse as a trophy or test. Her discovery alive triggers Lareth's obsessive rage; her death** triggers calculated revenge.
4.  **Negotiated Armistice:** If the party achieves a military stalemate and offers Lareth terms, he will negotiate — not from weakness, but to buy time for reinforcement or to extract valuables. He cannot be turned against the Temple, but he can be convinced to withdraw from the Moathouse if the price is survival and a new posting.

  **In Play** Lareth should be portrayed as dangerously charismatic—someone who can hold a conversation with genuine charm while simultaneously planning someone's death. His volatile nature means he is prone to sudden mood shifts; pleasantness can transform into cruelty in an instant if he is insulted or thwarted. His reliance on Lolth's favor and his secret devotion should remain hidden unless revealed through careful investigation or player ingenuity. The mystery of the missing Hommlet girl adds a hook for deeper investigation and potential redemption narratives, while also suggesting darker possibilities about Lareth's true intentions.

### The Gnolls: Warriors of the Bloody Maw

##### King Korvash Bonegnaw (Gnoll King of the Lortmil Fortress)

Korvash Bonegnaw, ruler of the gnolls of the Fortress of Jagged Fangs in the [Lortmil Mountains](lortmil-mountains-location.md), is a hulking gnoll of extraordinary size and ferocity. His matted fur is adorned with bone trinkets taken from his enemies, and his jagged teeth are stained crimson from years of ritualistic cannibalism. His piercing yellow eyes and a savage scar across his snout mark him as a warrior who has survived countless battles.

*   **Personality:** Korvash is ambitious, bloodthirsty, and paranoid. While he seeks to expand his influence beyond the mountains, he constantly fears betrayal from his own lieutenants. This paranoia makes him cautious in his dealings with outsiders, but he respects displays of strength and cunning.
*   **Motivation:** Korvash dreams of forging a gnoll empire stretching from the Lortmils to the Gnarley Forest. Sending gnolls to serve Lareth is both a gesture of good faith and a test of Lareth’s potential as an ally. If Lareth proves successful, Korvash hopes to use him as a pawn to weaken Verbobonc while consolidating his forces for a larger campaign.

##### Gnoll Leader: Fangmaster Gorrthak

Gorrthak is a hulking gnoll with bristling dark fur and jagged claws. He wields a massive glaive engraved with crude runes in tribute to Yeenoghu, the demon lord of gnolls. His armor is pieced together from scavenged human plate and gnoll-forged iron.

*   **Personality:** Gorrthak is ruthless and cunning, but his loyalty is conditional. He respects strength and rewards loyalty but despises being overshadowed, especially by the bugbears under Lareth's command. His growing dissatisfaction with his lot at the Moathouse makes him a potential weak link in Lareth's operations.
*   **Motivation:** Gorrthak agreed to serve Lareth to gain the favor of Korvash Bonegnaw and prove himself worthy of greater power within the Bloody Maw tribe. However, the poor loot from raids and the perceived favoritism shown to the bugbears have fueled resentment. He may betray Lareth if offered enough gold, power, or an opportunity to reclaim his pride.

##### Role and Status

Nine gnolls, led by Gorrthak, were sent by King Korvash to support Lareth. The gnolls are barracked in the lower level of the Moathouse near Lareth's chambers. They are tasked with raiding farms and merchant caravans, focusing on capturing slaves and supplies for the Temple. The gnolls are resentful due to their losses and diminished status compared to the bugbears. This dissatisfaction makes them prone to infighting and treachery.

##### Tactics

*   **Combat Role:** Ferocious melee fighters who prefer ambush tactics.
*   **Morale:** Fight to the death unless offered parley, which they may consider for a share of loot or a promise of greater status.

### The Bugbears: Clan of the Iron Claw

##### Clan and Recruitment

Lareth forged an alliance with the Iron Claw Clan, a notorious bugbear tribe dwelling deep within the Gnarley Forest. Known for their brutal efficiency and lust for gold, the Iron Claws have earned a fearsome reputation as raiders and mercenaries. Lareth’s promise of riches and dominance over the gnolls convinced them to join his cause.

##### Bugbear Leader: Gorvash the Cruel

Gorvash is a towering bugbear with a muscular build and a menacing presence. His shaggy fur is dark brown, streaked with patches of gray. His one good eye burns with malice, while the other, a milky white orb, is covered by a jagged scar. Gorvash wields a spiked morningstar and carries a shield emblazoned with the Iron Claw emblem—a red claw slashing across a black field.

*   **Personality:** Gorvash is brutal and pragmatic, valuing strength and results above all else. Unlike the gnolls, he is disciplined and methodical, though he enjoys taunting weaker foes. He relishes his clan’s elevated status within Lareth’s forces and takes every opportunity to belittle the gnolls.
*   **Motivation:** Gorvash is driven by greed and ambition. He sees Lareth as a temporary benefactor whose patronage can elevate the Iron Claws to dominance over the region. Gorvash secretly plans to use Lareth's resources to expand his clan's territory, but he is wary of Lareth’s cunning and charisma.

##### Role and Status

*   The Iron Claw bugbears are the elite enforcers of Lareth's raiding parties, often leading assaults on merchant caravans and farmsteads. They are housed in separate barracks from the gnolls, further exacerbating tensions between the two groups.
*   Gorvash personally leads the bugbears in combat and coordinates their operations with Lareth. His clan’s efficiency and brutal tactics have made them indispensable to Lareth’s plans.

##### Tactics

*   **Combat Role:** Stealth-based ambush specialists, excelling in hit-and-run tactics.
*   **Morale:** Loyal to Gorvash first, Lareth second. They will retreat if outmatched but only if Gorvash orders it.

#### Gnoll and Bugbear Tensions

The gnolls and bugbears under Lareth’s command are in constant conflict, fueled by jealousy and competition for Lareth’s favor. The gnolls resent the bugbears’ superior status and better quarters, while the bugbears view the gnolls as undisciplined and expendable.

##### Possible Outcomes

*   **Dissension**: If tensions escalate, Lareth may need to intervene to prevent open violence between the two groups.
*   **Exploitation**: Clever adventurers could exploit this animosity to sow discord within Lareth’s ranks, weakening his forces before an assault on the Moathouse.
*   **Mutual Respect**: A shared victory in battle could temporarily unite the gnolls and bugbears, making them a formidable combined force.

##### Impact on Lareth’s Plans

Lareth’s alliances with the gnolls and bugbears are critical to his success. These savage warriors provide the muscle needed to enforce his will and expand his influence. However, their volatile natures and conflicting ambitions make them a double-edged sword.

##### Key Points for the DM

*   Gorrthak and Gorvash are potential wildcards. Their dissatisfaction or ambition could derail Lareth’s operations if manipulated by the adventurers.
*   Both the gnolls and bugbears are aware of Lareth’s connection to the Temple of Elemental Evil, but only Gorvash knows the full extent of his plans.
*   If Lareth falls, Gorvash may attempt to take control of the Moathouse, while Gorrthak would likely retreat to the Lortmil Mountains.

### DM Notes: Post-Capture Escalation

This section details exactly what happens when Lareth is captured or killed at the Moathouse. The chain of command notification is automatic — Lareth does not need to send a signal. The Temple's communication network ensures word travels upward within hours, not days.

 

##### Chain of Command Notification Timeline

The moment Lareth falls or is taken, the following cascade begins:

*   **Hour 0 — Moathouse Falls:** If [Gattas Void](gattas-void-person.md) survives or escapes, he withdraws to the [55\. Hunting Cabin - off map](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) via the overland route through Demien's farm. If Gattas is also captured or killed, one of the surviving bandits or gnolls under Fangmaster Gorrthak will attempt to flee — the gnolls default to heading south toward the Lortmil Mountains, but Gorrthak knows the Hunting Cabin location. The bugbears under Gorvash scatter into the Gnarley Forest and do not report to the Temple.
*   **Hours 2–6 — Hunting Cabin Alert:** [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md) at the Hunting Cabin receives the report. Patris immediately dispatches a runner through the secret supply tunnel to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) and sends word up the supply chain toward the Greater Temple. Patris also locks down the Hunting Cabin — all shipments halt, the tunnel entrance is barricaded, and the gnoll guards go to high alert.
*   **Hours 6–12 — Lucius Graeme Notified:** [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), the Greater Temple courier and spymaster, receives word either through the Nulb network or via a scheduled check-in at the Hunting Cabin. Lucius assesses the situation and determines whether the spy network in Hommlet has been compromised. He sends an urgent report to [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) at the Temple.
*   **Hours 12–24 — Hedrack's Response:** Hedrack, Supreme Commander of the Greater Temple, receives Lucius's report. Hedrack's response depends on whether Lareth was killed or captured (see Escalation Protocols below).

 

##### Lareth's Diary — The Intelligence Bomb

Lareth's diary is the single most dangerous piece of evidence in the Moathouse. If the adventurers recover it, the following agents are directly compromised:

*   **[Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) and [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md):** Named as Temple spies operating [13\. D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile](13-d26g-dry-goods-26-general-mercantile-landmark.md) in Hommlet. The diary contains enough detail to expose their smuggling operations and intelligence reporting. DC 12 Intelligence (Investigation) to connect diary entries to specific supply shipments through D&G.
*   **[Zert](zert-person.md):** Referenced as a covert operative monitoring adventurers in Hommlet. The diary does not reveal his full capabilities — a DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check connects Zert to the sabotage of [30\. Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md).
*   **[Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md):** Referenced indirectly as a "laborer contact" providing construction intelligence. DC 18 Intelligence (Investigation) to identify Joseph by name from the diary alone — more likely the adventurers connect him through Rannos and Gremag under interrogation.

**Critical DM Note:** The diary also references the [Cult of the Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) and Lareth's correspondence with Lucius Graeme, but it does **not** reveal Lareth's secret allegiance to [Lolth](lolth-person.md) or his partnership with [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md). Those secrets are compartmented — Lareth keeps no written record of his Lolth operations.

 

##### Escalation Protocols

**If Lareth is killed:**

*   Hedrack writes off the Moathouse as a lost asset. The forward operation is abandoned.
*   Lucius Graeme is ordered to extract or silence any compromised agents in Hommlet before the adventurers can act on the diary. Lucius prioritizes extracting Rannos and Gremag (they know too much about the supply chain). Zert is considered expendable — Lucius may leave him in place as a sacrificial distraction.
*   The Greater Temple increases patrols around the Hunting Cabin and the supply tunnel to Nulb.
*   [Romag](romag-person.md), High Priest of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md), considers the adventurers a direct threat and may dispatch agents to eliminate them — particularly if they are heading toward the Temple.
*   Timeline: Lucius moves to extract Hommlet agents within 24–48 hours of confirmation. If the adventurers return to Hommlet immediately after clearing the Moathouse, they may arrive before Lucius can act — this creates a tense race against time.

**If Lareth is captured alive:**

*   Hedrack's priority shifts to silencing Lareth before he can be interrogated by Hommlet's leaders or the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md).
*   Lucius Graeme is dispatched with orders to either rescue or assassinate Lareth — rescue if feasible (Lareth knows too much about the Greater Temple hierarchy), assassination if rescue is impossible.
*   All Hommlet agents are activated for extraction. Rannos and Gremag attempt to flee Hommlet within 12 hours using pre-arranged escape routes through the Gnarley Forest. Zert goes to ground, destroying any physical evidence of his Temple affiliation.
*   The Temple may attempt a raid on wherever Lareth is being held — likely [30\. Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md) or the Church of St. Cuthbert — using a strike team of Earth Temple soldiers.

 

##### What Lareth Reveals Under Interrogation

If the adventurers or Hommlet authorities interrogate Lareth, his responses depend on the method and pressure applied:

*   **Voluntary (Persuasion DC 20 / Intimidation DC 18):** Lareth offers selective truths to bargain for his life. He names Rannos, Gremag, and Zert as Temple agents. He confirms the supply chain through the Hunting Cabin. He does **not** reveal Lucius Graeme's identity, the Greater Temple's internal structure, or anything about Lolth. Lareth's survival instinct is strong, but his loyalty to Lolth is absolute.
*   **Magical (Zone of Truth / Detect Thoughts):** Zone of Truth (DC 14 CHA save — Lareth has +4) forces factual answers but Lareth answers with misleading truths and omissions. Detect Thoughts (DC 15 WIS save — Lareth has +4) reveals surface thoughts of escape planning and deeper fears about Hedrack's retribution. A DC 20 contested Intelligence check while using Detect Thoughts surfaces fragmented images of a spider symbol and a robed figure in the Temple's lower levels — hints at Lolth and Falrinth, but not enough for identification without further investigation.
*   **Physical coercion:** Lareth breaks after sustained pressure (Constitution save DC 15, he has +3). He reveals the full Moathouse operation, names all Hommlet agents including Joseph Millar, and identifies Lucius Graeme as the courier. He still does not reveal Lolth — that secret is protected by his genuine fanatical devotion, not mere willpower.

##### Consequences for the Hommlet Spy Network

Depending on how quickly the adventurers act after capturing or killing Lareth, the Hommlet spy network faces different fates:

*   **Adventurers return to Hommlet within 6 hours:** All agents are still in place. The adventurers can confront Rannos and Gremag at D&G, find Zert at the Inn of the Welcome Wench, and locate Joseph Millar at the Doomwatch Keep construction site. None have received warning yet.
*   **Adventurers return within 12–24 hours:** Zert has heard rumors of the Moathouse raid (DC 14 Wisdom/Perception to notice his changed behavior). Rannos and Gremag are nervous but still operational. Joseph Millar is unaware.
*   **Adventurers return after 24+ hours:** Lucius Graeme has reached Hommlet. Rannos and Gremag have fled or are preparing to flee. Zert has destroyed evidence and may attempt to join the adventuring party as a double agent. Joseph Millar has been warned by Rannos and has gone to ground among the laborers.

##### Lolth’s Influence on the Temple of Elemental Evil

[Lolth](lolth-person.md), the Demon Queen of Spiders, has a vested interest in ensuring that the Temple of Elemental Evil never rises to its full potential. Her motivation is rooted in past betrayals by House Eilservs of the [Dark Elves](dark-elves-ethnicity.md) Drow, who abandoned her worship in favor of the Elder Elemental God. While she opposes the Temple's leadership, she is careful to work from the shadows, using spies and agents to manipulate events in her favor.

*   **Key Motivation:** Ensuring that the Temple remains divided and never achieves its true potential.
*   **Methodology:** Infiltration, subterfuge, and the use of key operatives such as Lareth the Beautiful and Falrinth.
*   **Artifact of Power:** Lolth possesses the key to the Elder Elemental God's prison, making her one of the few beings capable of deciding the fate of this mysterious entity.

##### Zuggtmoy’s Secret Control Over the Temple’s Clerics

The clerics and priests of the Temple believe they are praying to the Elder Elemental God, but in truth, their divine powers and spells are granted by Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi. The Abyss itself acts as a conduit for chaotic power, allowing Zuggtmoy to masquerade as the Elder Elemental God without the priests realizing the deception.

*   **Key Deception:** The Temple’s priests believe they serve the Elder Elemental God, but their power actually comes from Zuggtmoy.
*   **Reason:** Zuggtmoy is imprisoned within the Temple and needs the cult to continue worshiping her to gain strength.
*   **Implication:** If the truth were revealed, it could lead to massive schisms within the Temple's leadership.

##### Lareth the Beautiful – Lolth’s Hidden Pawn

Lareth the Beautiful is one of Lolth’s most promising agents. He controls the Moathouse, a stronghold designed to recruit new members to the Temple. However, his ultimate goal is to rise within the Temple’s ranks and convert it into a force that serves Lolth rather than Zuggtmoy.

*   **Role:** Lolth’s primary infiltrator within the Temple's ranks.
*   **Long-Term Goal:** To seize control of the Temple and redirect it toward the worship of Lolth.
*   **Secret Powerbase:** The Moathouse, where he gathers forces loyal to him while feeding selective information back to Hedrack.

##### Falrinth – The Sorcerer in Service to Lolth

[Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) is an 8th-level magic-user with a quasit familiar named Kriitch, which allows him to function as a 9th-level caster when nearby. As a secretive and ambitious figure, he serves Lolth but is ultimately self-interested, willing to betray her if it serves his purposes.

*   **Role:** A powerful sorcerer acting as a spy and potential saboteur within the Temple.
*   **True Allegiance:** Lolth, though he harbors personal ambitions that may lead to betrayal.
*   **Key Objective:** Investigating the elemental nodes of the Temple on behalf of Lolth, while keeping much of his research hidden from both the Temple’s leadership and his own goddess.

##### Final Implications

**Potential Conflict:** If Lolth’s agents gain too much power, the Temple may split into warring factions—those still loyal to Zuggtmoy and those who turn toward Lolth.

##### Adventurer Hooks

*   Discovering Falrinth’s hidden research on the elemental nodes.
*   Exposing Lareth’s double-dealings and secret connections to the drow.
*   Preventing or enabling Lolth’s ultimate plan to co-opt the Temple.

 

#### Conclusion

Lareth the Beautiful is a pivotal figure in the Temple of Elemental Evil, embodying its chaotic and depraved ambitions. As a manipulative and dangerous adversary, he presents a unique challenge to adventurers who dare to oppose the Temple’s growing influence.
