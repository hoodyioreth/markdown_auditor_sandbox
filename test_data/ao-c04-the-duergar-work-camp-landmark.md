AO-C04. The Duergar Work Camp
=============================

> The Duergar Work Camp — a half-finished tomb-hall at the eastern end of Zone B, repurposed by eight gray dwarf miners as their base of operations beneath the [Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). The original [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) masons had begun cutting burial alcoves into the east wall before the order's withdrawal in 562 CY, leaving the west wall as smooth, uncarved limestone — blank space waiting for dead that never came. The duergar have converted the unfinished alcoves into sleeping berths, set up tool racks and supply stores, and built a cooking fire over the basin of a lightly consecrated spring that still seeps clean water through the east wall. **Grimlok Ashpick**, the crew foreman, runs the camp from a curtained alcove in the deepest corner. He keeps a leather map that shows more than [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s version — the exact position of the Black Mouth and the Flan ward-stones. Three wooden tribute crates packed with stolen grave goods from the adjacent tombs sit against the north wall, scheduled for transport to [Romag](romag-person.md) via [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). The camp is the most complex social encounter on the crypt level — the duergar are not evil in the ideological sense. They are working-class miners doing a dangerous job for bad employers. The party can gain a major intelligence asset here if they play it smart.

_AO-C04. The Duergar Work Camp — Repurposed Tomb-Hall — Gray Dwarf Base of Operations_

* * *

### The Duergar Work Camp

The room was never finished. The masons of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) had started cutting alcoves into the east wall — eight recesses completed to full depth, four more roughed in but never dressed, and then nothing. The west wall is smooth limestone from floor to ceiling. No tool marks. No measurements scratched into the surface. The order withdrew before the work reached that side of the room. The ceiling is low — six and a half feet of flat stone, comfortable for duergar, uncomfortable for humans. Two iron lantern hooks have been driven into the ceiling at each end of the room, casting harsh orange light that does not reach the corners.

The room is thirty feet long and twenty feet wide — smaller than the tomb-hall in **AO-C03** to the north, and lower. The north corridor connects to the Desecrated Tombs through ten feet of rough-cut passage. The only other exit is back north through **AO-C03** and west into the gallery. There is no second way out. The duergar know this. **Grimlok Ashpick** knows this. It is the one thing about this posting that keeps him awake at night.

The cooking smell hits you first — something thick and mineral, like boiled mushrooms and wet stone. The corridor opens into a low-ceilinged chamber lit by two iron lanterns and the orange glow of a small fire pit in the center of the floor. The walls are lined with alcoves — not the sealed burial recesses of the tomb-hall, but open niches fitted with bedrolls and personal gear. Tool racks along the west wall. Wooden crates stacked against the north wall. A shallow stone basin in the east wall catches a thin stream of water seeping through the rock. Someone has hung a hide curtain across the deepest alcove in the back corner. The room smells of tallow, lichen stew, and the sharp metallic tang of recently worked stone. It smells like people live here.

* * *

### The Camp — Room Features

##### Sleeping Berths

Eight completed alcoves along the east wall have been converted to bunks — each containing a bedroll of rough-woven cave spider silk (duergar standard), a personal tool kit in a leather roll, and a small iron lockbox bolted to the stone at the foot of the berth.

*   **Lockboxes (Thieves' Tools DC 14):** Each contains the duergar's personal valuables — a mixed coin pouch holding both Underdark trade pieces (hammered iron discs stamped with clan-marks, 1d6 worth 2 gp each on the surface, double that in the Underdark) and topside Viscounty silver and copper (1d8 sp, 2d10 cp — wages skimmed from the Nulb supply runs), plus 1d4 small uncut gemstones (smoky quartz, garnet chips, and flawed amethyst, worth 5–10 gp each). Carved stone tokens used in a duergar gambling game called _kragmar_ are in every box (worthless to surface-dwellers, deeply personal to the duergar). Two of the boxes also contain small letters written in Dwarvish on thin stone tablets — from family. Wives, a brother, a mother. They are mundane, domestic, and quietly devastating if the party reads them after killing the occupants
*   **Four roughed-in alcoves:** Unfinished, used for overflow storage. Contain spare bedrolls, coiled rope, extra lanterns, and a small cask of duergar spirits (a viscous black liquor that tastes of charcoal and iron — Constitution DC 12 for surface-dwellers or be poisoned for 1 hour, -2 to Perception)

##### Grimlok's Alcove

The deepest alcove in the southeast corner is curtained with a hide flap — cured cave lizard skin, pinned to the stone with iron pitons. Behind the curtain, **Grimlok Ashpick**'s personal space is meticulously organized. The bedroll is folded with military precision. The tools are arranged by size. The floor is swept.

*   **Slate board:** Mounted on the alcove wall with iron pegs. Written in Dwarvish chalk: the current shift rotation, crew assignments, and a countdown of days remaining on the contract. The number is circled three times. The duergar are counting the days until they can leave
*   **Leather map:** Rolled in a bone tube, tucked behind the slate board. This is **Grimlok**'s personal survey of the excavation tunnel — more detailed than [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s version. It shows the exact position of the Black Mouth, the locations of three Flan ward-stones the duergar found during excavation, and notes in Dwarvish describing the stone's behavior near the node: _"Obsidian veins pulse when struck. Tools shift overnight. Stone breathes. Do not dig past the third ward-stone without the geomancer present."_ Intelligence DC 14 to recognize the significance of the ward-stone positions — they form a triangular containment pattern around the Black Mouth, suggesting the Flan deliberately sealed whatever is inside
*   **Sealed clay jar:** Contains 30 gp in Underdark trade iron and 15 gp in Viscounty silver nobles — Grimlok keeps both currencies because he trusts neither market. Beside the coin, wrapped in a strip of cave spider silk, a small obsidian holy symbol of Laduguer, the Gray Protector — a flat shield carved from a single piece of Underdark obsidian with a broken crossbow bolt motif in relief on its face. The edges are worn smooth from decades of handling. Worth 50 gp as carved obsidian to a collector, but to **Grimlok** it is priceless — it was his grandmother's, and she held it every day of her life in the deep warrens while praying to the Taskmaster for the strength to endure. He keeps it with his pay because those are the two things he will grab if the camp is overrun
*   **Grimlok's war pick —** _**Ashpick**_**:** A duergar-forged war pick of dark iron alloy, the haft wrapped in cave lizard hide, the head blackened with Underdark forge-soot. The pick is the source of his surname — duergar foremen earn their name from their primary tool. Mechanically a +1 war pick (+6 to hit, 1d8+4 piercing, +1d4 if Enlarged). The head is slightly warm to the touch — residual heat from the deep forges where it was made. Non-magical Detect Magic reveals nothing; the enhancement is metallurgical, not arcane. Worth 150 gp to a smith who understands Underdark alloys, more to a duergar buyer
*   **Belt pouch — Underdark consumables:** Tucked into **Grimlok**'s bedroll: a leather pouch containing two **Potions of Darkvision** (grants darkvision 120 ft for 8 hours to a creature without it — brewed from deep cave fungus, the liquid is black and tastes of iron and mushroom), and one **Stoneguard Salve** (a thick mineral paste in a clay tin — when applied to exposed skin as an action, grants resistance to bludgeoning damage for 10 minutes, single use). The duergar brew these in the Underdark for surface contracts where they cannot rely on familiar conditions

##### The Spring

A natural spring seeps through a fissure in the east wall, filling a shallow carved basin the original masons cut as a drinking trough during construction. The water is clean, cold, and faintly sweet — filtered through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) limestone over decades.

*   **Religion DC 14:** The basin is lightly consecrated — the masons blessed their water source as a matter of routine. The consecration is weak but persistent. Drinking from the basin removes one level of exhaustion, once per day per creature. The duergar use it constantly after shifts. They do not know it is consecrated. They think it is just very good water
*   **Detect Magic:** Faint abjuration aura on the basin. The water itself is mundane once removed from the basin — filling a waterskin from it provides no benefit. The blessing is on the stone, not the water

##### Tribute Crates

Three wooden crates against the north wall, nailed shut, branded with the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s inverted triangle. These contain the stolen grave goods from **AO-C03**, packed for transport.

*   **Crate 1 — Weapons:** Six swords (longswords and shortswords, functional but centuries old — 10 gp each), four shield-bosses removed from round shields (5 gp each). None of the named knights' weapons are here — those went to [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) in **AO-18**
*   **Crate 2 — Holy symbols and rings:** Eight silver holy symbols of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) (15 gp each, all functional), twelve signet rings bearing Azure Oath heraldic devices (5 gp each as silver, potential intelligence value — family crests may identify bloodlines still active in Verbobonc). Total value approximately 180 gp
*   **Crate 3 — Miscellaneous:** Armor fragments (corroded, 20 gp scrap total), prayer books (Old Oeridian, illegible from age), and a bundle of six burial shrouds that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) ordered preserved — he believes the consecrated linen has alchemical properties. Religion DC 15 confirms: the shrouds can be used as material components for _Protection from Evil and Good_ without being consumed

Total tribute value: approximately 350 gp. Scheduled for the next [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) supply run.

##### Tool Racks

The west wall is lined with pegged racks holding the crew's mining equipment: eight war picks (double as weapons), copper chisels (used near obsidian veins because [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) insists iron interferes with tremorsense readings), iron pitons, 200 feet of hemp rope in coils, eight hooded lanterns with oil for a week, two rubble carts (dismantled, rails stacked against the wall), and a battered set of mason's tools. Enough to outfit two full work crews.

##### The Cooking Fire

A small fire pit in the center of the room — carefully constructed with a stone ring and a crude ventilation channel that the duergar cut into the ceiling, angling the smoke through a natural fissure in the rock. The fire is always lit during off-shift hours. The duergar cook subterranean lichen stew and dried cave fish in a single iron pot. The smell is distinctive and carries into the gallery — anyone in **AO-C02** or **AO-C03** can smell it (Perception DC 10).

The fire pit sits directly over the spot where the original masons intended to cut a drainage channel. The stone here is thinner than elsewhere in the room. A character who examines the fire pit closely (Investigation DC 16) notices that the stone beneath the fire ring is discolored — not from heat, but from below. Something in the rock is leaching upward. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has not noticed this. The duergar have. **Grimlok** noted it in his personal log but has not reported it. Gray dwarves keep what the stone tells them.

* * *

### The Duergar

Eight gray dwarves. Contracted mercenaries from the Underdark warrens beneath the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). They work for coin, salvage rights to non-consecrated stone and mineral deposits, and first claim on any Underdark access routes their tunneling opens. They have no loyalty to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md), no interest in the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md), and no patience for [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s doctrine. They are here because someone is paying them to dig.

At any given time, the camp holds four to six duergar — the off-shift crew resting while the on-shift crew works the excavation tunnel at **AO-C13**. The shift rotation runs six hours on, six hours off. During rest periods, the duergar maintain tools, gamble at _kragmar_ (a stone-tossing game played in near-silence), eat, and sleep. They speak Undercommon among themselves and broken Common when forced to communicate with the human cultists. They do not socialize with the acolytes. They do not respect [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md). They tolerate [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) because he pays on time and because his geomantic knowledge is — **Grimlok** has grudgingly admitted — "like a deep-dwarf born."

##### Duergar Morale

The crew is professional but unhappy. The contract has run longer than expected. The wards in the deeper crypt have slowed progress. The stone near the Black Mouth behaves in ways that make experienced miners uncomfortable — tools shifting overnight, obsidian veins that pulse when struck, and twice, something breathing on the other side of the wall. **Grimlok** has kept discipline through routine, fair shift assignments, and the promise that the contract payout will make every one of them wealthy by Underdark standards. But the countdown on his slate board tells the story. They are ready to leave.

*   **If Varnor is alive:** The duergar follow orders, fight if cornered, and prioritize retreat over death. They will not sacrifice themselves for the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md). Standard duergar combat behavior: Enlarge, fight in tight spaces, use Invisibility to disengage and regroup
*   **If Varnor is dead:** The contract is void. **Grimlok** considers the crew's obligation fulfilled. He will negotiate if the party approaches with weapons sheathed. He will not fight alongside the party, but he will not fight against them either — provided his conditions are met
*   **If the alarm from AO-C01 has sounded:** All duergar in camp are awake, Enlarged, and behind makeshift barricades of tribute crates and tool racks. The camp becomes a fortified position. **Grimlok** commands from behind the barricade and fights only if the barricade is breached

* * *

### Grimlok Ashpick — The Negotiation

**Grimlok** is the key to the entire crypt level's social layer. He is pragmatic, professional, and utterly uninterested in ideology. He does not care about the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). He does not care about [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). He cares about his crew, his pay, and his grandmother's figurine.

**Negotiation triggers:** **Grimlok** will talk if [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is dead, incapacitated, or absent from the crypt level AND the party approaches with weapons sheathed. He speaks broken Common with a heavy Dwarvish accent. He stands with his arms crossed, war pick leaning against the wall within reach but not in hand. He does not sit. He does not offer food. He watches the party's hands the entire time.

##### What Grimlok Wants

Three things, in order of priority:

*   **1\. His crew out alive.** All of them. Non-negotiable. If any duergar has been killed by the party, **Grimlok** demands weregild — 100 gp per dead dwarf, paid before he says another word. If the party killed more than three, he will not negotiate at all. The blood debt is too high
*   **2\. Payment for work completed.** The contract specified 200 gp total for the excavation. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has paid half. **Grimlok** wants the remaining 100 gp. He considers this a valid debt — the work was done, the pay is owed. He does not care who pays it. Persuasion DC 12 to negotiate this down to 50 gp. Below 50, he walks
*   **3\. His grandmother's Laduguer symbol.** If the party has looted his alcove and taken the obsidian holy symbol — the shield with the broken crossbow bolt, the Gray Protector's sign — he wants it back. This is the only thing that makes him visibly emotional. He will give up the 100 gp demand in exchange for the symbol. If the party destroyed it or sold it, the negotiation ends. He will never forgive this

**If the party provides any two of the three:** **Grimlok** shares everything he knows. He is a generous source once the deal is struck — he has no reason to protect the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) and considerable reason to want the party to succeed (the sooner the situation resolves, the sooner his crew goes home).

##### What Grimlok Knows

*   The excavation tunnel's full layout — distances, depths, support conditions, where the stone changes
*   The Black Mouth — its exact position, the Flan ward-stones around it, what the stone sounds like near the node. _"The earth breathes there. One breath per minute. It is not sleeping. It is waiting."_
*   [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s daily routine, his obsessions, his weaknesses. _"The geomancer hears the stone better than most dwarves. But he hears what he wants to hear. He thinks the Mouth will open for him. The Mouth does not care who opens it."_
*   The ward system — he has observed it from the duergar side. He knows which wards are weakening and approximately how fast. He knows **Halven Sere**'s prayers sustain them. _"Kill the old man and the wards die. But they die unpredictably. The geomancer wants controlled access. Killing the priest gives him chaos."_
*   The creature situation in Zone E — ghouls, the carrion crawlers, the phase spider. _"We sealed the breach. Something unsealed it. We sealed it again. It unsealed again. We stopped sealing it."_
*   [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s role — **Grimlok** considers the human commander a fool. _"He collects dead men's swords and thinks it makes him strong. He has never been underground. He does not understand what underground means."_

##### What Grimlok Will Not Do

*   Fight alongside the party. His crew is leaving, not enlisting
*   Enter the Black Mouth chamber. He has seen enough. _"That place is not for the living."_
*   Betray a deal once struck. Duergar contract honor is absolute — not moral, but professional. If he shakes on it, he keeps it

### Combat — If Negotiation Fails

If the party attacks or if negotiation is not possible (Varnor alive, duergar alarmed, crew members killed), the camp becomes a defensive engagement.

##### Tactical Setup

*   **Barricades:** Tribute crates and tool racks form a waist-high barrier across the room's center. Provides half cover (+2 AC) to defenders behind it. The duergar built this on the first day — they always prepare for the contract to go wrong
*   **Chokepoint:** The north corridor from **AO-C03** is only five feet wide. Two Enlarged duergar can block it completely. The party cannot flank without climbing over the barricade
*   **Lighting:** The duergar fight in darkness if possible — they douse the lanterns immediately. All duergar have 120 ft darkvision. The party does not
*   **Retreat:** There is no second exit. If the fight turns, **Grimlok** orders an Invisibility retreat — each duergar goes invisible in sequence, slipping past the party in the confusion and fleeing north through **AO-C03** to the gallery. They regroup at **AO-C01** or flee up the staircase to the main level

##### Round-by-Round

*   **Round 1:** All duergar use Enlarge. **Grimlok** shouts orders in Undercommon. Two duergar move to block the north corridor. The rest take positions behind the barricade with war picks
*   **Round 2:** Focused fire on the first character through the chokepoint. Enlarged war pick attacks: +4 to hit, 1d8+2 plus 1d4 (Enlarged) bludgeoning damage. **Grimlok** targets spellcasters if visible
*   **Round 3+:** Hold the barricade. If two duergar fall, **Grimlok** assesses: if the party is clearly winning, he calls the retreat. If it is close, he fights on — he will not abandon his crew mid-combat
*   **If Grimlok falls:** The remaining duergar immediately use Invisibility and flee. There is no second in command. Without **Grimlok**, the crew breaks

### Sacred Layer

This was supposed to be the order's next century of burials. The east wall alcoves were cut for knights who had not yet died — space prepared in faith that the brotherhood would continue, that the Long Road would always need defenders, that there would always be faithful dead to honor. The west wall was left blank because five hundred years of history said the order would fill it eventually.

The order withdrew fourteen years later. The alcoves were never used. The west wall was never cut.

The desecration here is not violence — it is repurposing. The duergar have turned prepared graves into beds. They cook where the masons drank. They store stolen relics where the dead were meant to rest. The spring basin's consecration has been reduced to a perk — good water, no questions asked. The room has been converted from sacred to functional with the same efficiency the duergar apply to everything. They do not know what this room was for. They do not care. It is dry, defensible, and has running water. That is enough.

And in the deepest alcove, behind a hide curtain, **Grimlok Ashpick** keeps his grandmother's holy symbol of Laduguer — the Gray Protector, the Taskmaster, the Exile — beside his pay. A shield carved from Underdark obsidian with a broken crossbow bolt on its face, worn smooth by decades of prayer in the deep warrens. Even the desecrators have sacred things. They just do not look like the sacred things that were here before.

### DM Notes

##### Running This Room

This is the most complex social encounter on the crypt level. The duergar are not monsters to be killed — they are workers to be dealt with. The party has multiple approaches:

*   **Combat:** Viable but costly. Eight duergar (or four to six on-site) in a fortified room with a chokepoint. The Enlarged corridor-block tactic is brutal in tight quarters
*   **Negotiation:** The optimal approach. **Grimlok**'s intelligence is worth more than the treasure in the crates. His map of the excavation is worth more than everything in the room combined
*   **Stealth:** Possible during shift change when only two to three duergar remain. The camp can be looted quietly — but the lockboxes and Grimlok's alcove require time and noise
*   **Deception:** A character who speaks Undercommon and claims to be a Nulb contractor (Deception DC 14) can bluff their way into the camp. The duergar have seen human contractors before — it is plausible. This buys the party time to observe the room and plan

##### The Moral Weight

If the party kills the duergar and then reads the letters in the lockboxes, they should feel it. These are not cultists. They are miners. They have families. They are counting the days until they can go home. The DM should not lecture — just read the letters matter-of-factly. Let the party decide what that means.

##### Connections

*   **AO-C03 (north):** The Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch. Short unfinished corridor. Sound and smell carry. No barrier. The duergar consider this their front yard
*   **AO-C02 (northwest, via AO-C03):** The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead. The duergar transit through the gallery to reach the excavation tunnel at **AO-C13**. The shift change is their most predictable movement pattern
*   **AO-C01 (north, via gallery):** The Processional Landing. Duergar sentries rotate from this camp. Alarm rope connects directly
*   **AO-18 (above, main level):** [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s Quarters. The named knights' swords went there. The tribute crates are bound for [Romag](romag-person.md) via [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)

#### Grimlok Ashpick — Negotiation Dialogue

**NARRATION — First Contact**

> You round the corner into the low-ceilinged camp and the duergar see you immediately. Two at the fire reach for war picks. A third steps in front of the tool rack. From the curtained alcove in the back, a broad gray dwarf emerges — shorter than the others by an inch but wider across the shoulders. He does not pick up his war pick. He crosses his arms and watches your hands.

**DIALOGUE — The Challenge** _"You are not Temple. You are not Nulb runners. You are something else, and you are in my camp with your weapons put away, which means you are either very stupid or you want to talk. I will assume the second until you prove the first."_   **NARRATION — Reading the Room (Insight DC 12)** His eyes move from face to face, measuring. He is not afraid. He is calculating whether this conversation ends with coin or with blood, and he is prepared for both. The duergar behind him have not relaxed, but they have not attacked either. They are waiting for his word.   **DIALOGUE — The Terms** _"The geomancer's contract is finished. Dead, gone, or hiding — it does not matter. The work stops when the employer stops paying. So here is what happens now."_   He holds up three thick fingers. _"One. My crew walks out of this hole alive and unmolested. Every one of them. That is not a bargain — that is the price of this conversation continuing."_   _"Two. We are owed one hundred gold for work completed. The stone does not care who pays. I do not care who pays. But someone pays."_   _"Three."_ He pauses. His voice drops half a register. _"There is a small black figurine in my quarters. Obsidian. A woman with her hands folded. If any of you have touched it, you will return it now and we will not discuss what happens if you do not."_   **NARRATION — If the Party Killed Duergar** His expression does not change, but the temperature in the room drops. The duergar at the fire stand. The one at the tool rack picks up a war pick.   **DIALOGUE — Weregild** _"You killed my people. Contract workers. Miners. Men with families in the deep who will never see them come home."_ _"One hundred gold per dwarf. Paid now, before another word leaves your mouth. That is not negotiation. That is debt."_   **NARRATION — If More Than Three Dead** He uncrosses his arms. His hand moves to the wall where the war pick leans.   **DIALOGUE — No Deal** _"Three of mine are in the stone because of you. There is no price for that. There is no conversation after that. You should leave this room while I am still deciding whether to let you."_   **NARRATION — The Deal Struck** He considers for a long moment, then nods once — a single sharp movement, like a hammer strike. He extends one hand, palm up.   **DIALOGUE — The Handshake** _"Then we have terms. A duergar contract is not a promise — it is a cut in stone. I will tell you what I know about this place, and then my crew and I will walk north and you will not see us again."_   **DIALOGUE — Intel Dump (After Deal)** **On the excavation:** _"Eighty feet of tunnel, angled down at twenty degrees, timber-shored every six feet. The stone changes at forty feet — darker, denser, shot through with obsidian veins that pulse when you strike them. Your tools move overnight if you leave them near the bottom."_   **On the Black Mouth:** _"There is a face carved in the bedrock at the end of the tunnel. Old. Older than this priory, older than the people who built it. A mouth, open wide, cut from black stone. Three ward-stones around it in a triangle — Flan work, set there to keep it shut. The earth breathes behind that face. One breath per minute. Whatever is in there is not sleeping. It is waiting."_   **On Varnor:** _"The geomancer hears the stone better than most dwarves, and I do not say that to flatter the dead. But he hears what he wants to hear. He believes the Mouth will open for him because he asked it to. The Mouth does not care who opens it."_   **On the wards:** _"The old priest's prayers keep the lights burning in the gallery. Kill the priest and the wards die, but they die unpredictably — some fast, some slow, and the things behind them wake at different speeds. The geomancer wanted controlled access, not collapse. That is why the old man is alive."_   **On the creatures:** "_We sealed the breach in the southwest passage. Something unsealed it from the other side. We sealed it again. It unsealed again. We stopped sealing it. There are ghouls, crawling things with too many legs, and something that steps through walls and takes a man before anyone hears him scream."_   **On Tharvok:** _"The human commander collects dead men's swords and hangs them on his wall because he thinks that is what strength looks like. He has never been underground. He does not understand what underground means."_   **NARRATION — Departure** He turns to his crew and speaks three words in Undercommon. The duergar begin packing immediately — bedrolls rolled, lockboxes unbolted, tools racked. They move with the efficiency of men who have broken camp a hundred times in the dark. Grimlok takes the bone tube with his leather map, the clay jar, and the obsidian figurine. He leaves everything else. At the corridor he stops but does not turn around.   **DIALOGUE — Exit** _"Do not go into the Mouth. Whatever you came here to stop — stop it from the outside. Collapse the tunnel. Seal the stones. Bury it and walk away."_ A pause. _"You will not listen to that. No one ever does."_
