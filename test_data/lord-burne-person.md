Lord Burne
==========

### (a.k.a. His Most Worshipful Mage of Hommlet)

Lord Burne, _His Most Worshipful Mage of Hommlet_, is the sharpest political mind between Verbobonc City and the Temple of Elemental Evil. While his companion [Lord Rufus](lord-rufus-person.md) holds the sword, Burne holds the thread — watching the Viscount's court, reading the gnome crisis in Greenway Valley, tracking the bandit surge on the Long Road, and seeing — months before anyone in the [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) will admit it — that the Temple of Elemental Evil is not dormant. It is organizing.

> _"Adventuring isn't just about killing dragons; it's about outsmarting them and coming home with the treasure."_

#### Physical Description

Burne is a lean Oeridian man of middling height, fine-boned and precise in his movements in the way of someone who has spent decades learning to economize effort.

*   **Build and Stature:** Five feet eleven inches, slight through the shoulders but straight-backed — the posture of a man who reads standing up and has no patience for slouching. He moves quietly and does not take up more space than necessary, which fools people into underestimating him until he speaks.
*   **Face:** Angular and clean-shaven, with deep-set dark eyes that track conversations the way a strategist tracks map positions — noting everything, committing selectively. He has the particular stillness of someone who has learned not to give information away through expression.
*   **Hands:** A wizard's hands: ink-stained at the fingertips, bearing the faint discoloration of years of reagent work, one old burn scar on the back of his left hand from an early experiment he does not discuss.
*   **Dress:** In public, Burne wears good-quality Verbobonc city clothes in dark colors with a scholar's robe over them when conducting official business — the image of a man who respects his position without performing it. In his laboratory, he wears whatever is already worn out. His ring of protection is always on.
*   **Presence:** Burne is not immediately imposing. He is immediately intelligent. Most people feel it within sixty seconds of conversation — the sense that he already knows the end of the sentence you're still constructing.

_

> "The simplest explanation is usually correct. The most dangerous explanation is usually the one nobody wants to say out loud."

_

### Personality

Burne is not what people expect from a wizard who helped win a major battle. He is not grandiose, not theatrical, and not given to displays. He is precise, patient, occasionally cutting, and relentlessly pragmatic about the things that matter.

*   **Calculative:** Burne does not speak until he has assembled his thoughts. He considers the second and third order consequences of actions before he approves them. This is not hesitancy — it is the discipline of someone who has learned that impulsive magic kills people, and impulsive politics does the same.
*   **Dry Wit:** His humor is quiet and arrives without warning. He will say one precise thing in a roomful of bluster and wait for it to land. Rufus is often the only one who laughs immediately.
*   **Demanding of Fair Payment:** Burne does not provide his services — personal, magical, or political — for free. He is not mercenary, but he believes that work of value deserves compensation of value. He has turned away patrons who expected favors. He has also quietly waived his fee for people who genuinely could not pay, and told no one.
*   **Lawful to the Bone:** His faith in St. Cuthbert is not ceremonial. Burne believes in the law as a structure that holds civilization together, especially when the edges of civilization are being gnawed at by something old and malevolent. He will not break the law. He will, however, find every legitimate angle within it.
*   **Reserved, Not Cold:** Burne does not make friends quickly. The people he trusts — Rufus foremost, a handful of others — he trusts completely. He remembers every conversation. He does not forget a slight, but he also does not forget a kindness.

_

> "I don't need everyone's cooperation. I need the right people's cooperation at the right moment. The rest is noise."

_

### Background

#### Origins and Education

Burne's early history is less documented than his later career, which is partly by design — he has never found biographical details particularly interesting and does not encourage questions about them. What is known: he came to Verbobonc as a young man, already possessed of significant raw talent and enough arcane training to get himself into trouble. He completed his formal education under a master whose name he mentions rarely, acquired his spellbook through means he describes only as "earned," and was broke by the time he turned his attention to actual work. The city of Verbobonc shaped him. Its politics, its guilds, its noble houses jockeying for influence at the Viscount's court, its layered relationship with the gnomish [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) — he watched all of it and learned the map of power the way other young men learn the map of the streets. By the time he met Rufus, he already understood that the city's greatest vulnerability was not its walls but its fractures.

#### The Fellowship with Lord Rufus

Rufus and Burne met in Verbobonc — two young adventurers of complementary and contrasting talents, both more or less penniless and both unwilling to admit it. What began as a practical arrangement became the defining partnership of both their lives. Burne supplied strategy, arcane firepower, and the patience to plan. Rufus supplied execution, physical courage, and the willingness to go through the door first. For over a decade they worked the adventuring circuits of the central Flanaess, building reputation through a sequence of contracts that culminated in their most famous joint achievement.

##### The Black Dragon of the Suss Forest

The commission that made their names in Verbobonc came from Kron Hills merchants whose caravans had been devastated by a black dragon in the northern Suss. The contract was offered at a price that reflected the expected casualty rate. Burne spent three weeks on reconnaissance, spell preparation, and tactical modeling before he agreed the approach was workable. On the day of the engagement, his precisely timed blindness spell stripped the dragon's environmental advantage at the critical moment — removing its ability to use the forest canopy and giving Rufus the charge lane he needed. The dragon died. Both men nearly did not. The payment was substantial. More importantly, the story reached Verbobonc's court. The Viscount's chamberlain sent a letter. A larger commission was coming.

#### The Battle of Emridy Meadows — 569 CY

The great coalition engagement against the Temple of Elemental Evil at @The Battle of Emridy Meadows was the event that transformed two adventurers into political figures. Burne served as arcane support in the allied battle line — not front-rank combat but precisely placed evocation that collapsed two key flanking positions in the Temple's advance. The aftermath was as significant as the battle. The allies won. The Temple was sealed. And the Viscount of Verbobonc needed people he trusted, with proven records, to hold the strategic approach at @Hommlet. Burne and Rufus accepted the commission. It had been seven years. Neither of them has left._

> "The Temple was sealed, not destroyed. That distinction matters. Everyone celebrated as if it were the same thing. I did not celebrate."

_

### Role in Hommlet — 576 CY

#### His Most Worshipful Mage of Hommlet

The title is half formal designation, half private joke between Burne and Rufus — it is what the Viscount's letter called him, and it stuck. Burne does not use it himself. He is simply "Burne" to the village, "Lord Burne" to visitors who need the formality, and the person who actually runs the administrative half of Hommlet's governance while Rufus runs the military half. He handles correspondence with Verbobonc City, manages the finances of [30\. Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md), conducts negotiations with the gnomish traders passing through from [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) and [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), and maintains the official record of the Viscount's mandate over the region. He also keeps a private record that is rather more candid.

#### The Council of Hommlet

Burne and Rufus do not govern Hommlet alone. Under the Viscount's mandate, a governing council of six sits in the newly built Council Hall — timber still pale, erected beside the Inn of the Welcome Wench as the symbolic center of local authority.

*   **Lord Burne:** Presiding voice on administrative matters — correspondence, finance, law, and intelligence. In practice, the first among equals.
*   **[Lord Rufus](lord-rufus-person.md):** Military authority. Defense, patrol, enforcement. He does not chair sessions but nothing the council decides on defense happens without his approval.
*   **[Jaroo Ashstaff](jaroo-ashstaff-person.md):** Druid of the Old Faith. Speaks for the land, the Gnarley edge, and the non-human communities. His counsel is measured and difficult to argue with.
*   **Canon [Terjon](terjon-person.md):** Cleric of St. Cuthbert. The law's voice in moral matters. Burne and Terjon frequently agree in principle and occasionally argue about method.
*   **[Gundigoot](gundigoot-person-1.md):** Innkeeper. Represents the merchant and common-folk interest. Knows every rumor in Hommlet before it becomes official and feeds it to the Council with appropriate delay.
*   **[Elder Kenter Sr.](elder-kenter-sr-person.md).:** Elder of Hommlet. Eyes cloudy, voice strong, memory long. He has seen more than one crisis ebb and flow through the region and serves as the council's institutional memory.

The council meets in the plain hall with its bare stone floor and its tapestry of green field, acorns, and grain — a room that looks modest and is not. Every significant decision in Hommlet passes through this table: patrol assignments, trade disputes, emergency requisitions, and the increasingly frequent discussions about what to do with strangers who arrive carrying news that the roads are not safe.

#### Burne's Badgers

The [Burne's Badgers](burne-s-badgers-organization.md) are not a professional standing army. They are something more interesting and, in Burne's assessment, more reliable: men who were given a choice and made the right one. The unit's origin is straightforward. In the period following the [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md), Burne and Rufus encountered and captured a band of brigands operating in the region around Hommlet. Rather than hand them to Verbobonc City for adjudication — a process that would have ended most of them on a scaffold — Burne offered an alternative: serve under discipline, prove worth through conduct, earn a wage and a place. Every man accepted. The ones who couldn't manage the discipline were gone within the first season. The ones who remained became the Badgers.

*   **Composition:** In 576 CY, the unit stands at ten soldiers and three scouts — thirteen men in total, a number that sounds modest until you consider what they are asked to cover. Their uniform carries the emblem of a badger: deliberate, tenacious, not easily moved from ground it has chosen to hold.
*   **Markan of Narwell:** Lieutenant and senior veteran of the unit. A seasoned mercenary whose experience predates his time with the Badgers. He keeps order among the soldiers and serves as the operational link between Rufus's commands and the unit's daily functioning. The other Badgers respect him the way soldiers respect a man who has seen worse than what's currently in front of them and hasn't flinched.
*   **Ferkis:** Demi-elven sergeant, veteran of a dozen contracts before Hommlet. His value to the unit is twofold: combat instinct that comes from non-human reflexes and two-century memory, and a talent for training that has substantially improved the unit's baseline competency in the years since he joined. He is fiercely loyal to Rufus and holds Burne in a quieter but genuine regard.

The command arrangement is precise and functional. Rufus commands the Badgers tactically — patrol assignments, deployment decisions, the order to engage. Burne directs them strategically — which roads to prioritize, what to look for, which intelligence to bring back and how to carry it. In practice, the Badgers understand that a request from either lord carries equal weight, and they do not play them off each other. In 576 CY, the Badgers are stretched. The withdrawal of the Mounted Borderers from the Kron Hills approach has left a patrol gap that thirteen men and three scouts cannot fully cover — a fact Burne is acutely aware of and has documented in his correspondence to the Viscount without result. The Badgers are currently running extended patrol rotations on the Long Road south of Hommlet, providing security for the stone convoys supporting Doomwatch Keep Construction Site, and maintaining a watch on the High and Low roads approaches toward Nulb. The missing outrider — sent to scout the western quarry road before the stone shipment went overdue — was a Badger. Burne has not shared the full weight of what that means with the Council. He has shared it with Rufus._

> "Thirteen men. The Borderers pulled four patrols from this region. Thirteen men do not replace four patrols. I have written that sentence to the Viscount's chamberlain in five separate letters. I have varied the phrasing. The mathematics has not changed."

_

#### The Laboratory and the Work

Burne's upper floor of the [31\. Two Story Tower - Rufus and Burne](31-two-story-tower---rufus-and-burne-landmark-1.md) is his laboratory, library, and primary workspace. He has accumulated a working collection of texts on regional history, arcane theory, and the documented history of the Temple — including materials from the original T1 period that most people in Verbobonc would prefer to forget. He spends most mornings here. Visitors requiring his attention are kept waiting until he is at a stopping point. This is not discourtesy; it is the professional standard of a man who treats sloppy spell preparation as a moral failing.

#### Watching the Political Web

In 576 CY, Burne's primary concern is not the construction schedule or the budget shortfall on the Keep's eastern tower. It is the pattern. The pattern is this: the road network is deteriorating. Bandit activity on the @Long Road from Verbobonc south through Nigb's Rest to Hommlet, Sheernobb, and Ostverk has increased over eighteen months. Gnome merchant caravans from @Greenway Valley are being hit with suspicious regularity — not random robbery but targeted attacks on specific trade goods. The piracy at @Nulb is no longer casual opportunism but organized, with the Imeryds Run waterway being used to move goods that do not appear in any merchant ledger. And in the middle of all of this, Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) has _withdrawn_ the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) from Greenway Valley._

> "The Borderers are pulled from the valley at the same time caravan attacks surge. Either the Viscount knows something he is not sharing — or someone convinced him the two facts are unrelated. I find neither possibility reassuring."

_

### The Political Crisis of 576 CY

#### The Mounted Borderer Withdrawal

The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) are the Viscounty's elite patrol force — its first line of defense on the open roads and in the outlying settlements. Their withdrawal from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) and [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) region is, on paper, a redeployment decision. Burne does not believe it is merely that. The withdrawal has left the gnomish communities without Viscounty military protection at the precise moment bandit activity is accelerating. The [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md), already skeptical of Verbobonc's governance, is interpreting this as either negligence or deliberate abandonment. "Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md), the gnome representative in Verbobonc City, has been notably quiet in recent [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) sessions — which Burne reads as a man preparing to escalate rather than a man who has given up. What concerns Burne most is the incident that preceded the withdrawal: gnome militiamen attacked and killed a Mounted Borderer patrol near the Greenway Valley approach, following a dispute over mine claim documentation. The Viscount responded by pulling the Borderers out entirely. Burne does not know if this was escalation-avoidance or if someone in the court argued for it strategically. Either way, the gnomes are now defending their own caravans with their own militia — and the bandits know it.

#### The Missing Stone Shipment

The most immediate crisis on Burne's desk is not diplomatic. It is structural — literally. A scheduled delivery of dressed stone from a quarry in the western Viscounty, commissioned for the next phase of [30\. Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md)'s curtain wall, has now been overdue for a week. No messenger has arrived with explanation. The stone master in the western quarry has not sent word. The merchant contractor who arranged the shipment has not responded to the letter Burne sent three days ago. Rufus has already dispatched a Badger outrider to scout the road. The outrider has not returned. One missing stone shipment is a logistics problem. One missing stone shipment with a missing outrider, on the same road that has seen escalating banditry for eighteen months, is a pattern. Burne has raised it at the last two Council sessions. The council has authorized resources for a search. What they have not yet said — but Burne has noted in his private records — is that if the road between the western quarry and Hommlet is now effectively controlled by hostile forces, the Keep's completion timeline is not three to five years. It is indefinite._

> "The wall cannot be built without stone. The stone cannot arrive without a safe road. The road cannot be made safe without the wall. I am aware of the irony. I am also aware that someone may be counting on it."

_

#### The Caravan Raiders and the Long Road

The bandit problem is not new. What is new is the coordination. Attacks on the Long Road used to be opportunistic — small groups preying on unescorted merchants. What Burne's correspondence with Verbobonc City and with contacts in [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) describes now is something more organized: ambushes laid for specific shipments, with knowledge of cargo and timing that suggests either informants in the trading houses or a central intelligence source. [House Asbury](house-asbury-organization.md) — the Knight Banneret holdings at Penwick, north of Hommlet — has reported missing wine and agricultural shipments. The vineyards and ranches along the southern approach to Verbobonc are bleeding revenue. The guild interests in Verbobonc City, particularly those aligned with Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md), are beginning to press the Viscount for response. Burne has said privately to Rufus that the banditry pattern is consistent with what the Temple used in its resurgence prior to Emridy Meadows — systematic road disruption to isolate settlements, weaken economic confidence, and create conditions for recruitment. He has not put this in writing. Not yet.

#### Nulb — The Open Wound

[Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) has never been orderly. What it is now is something worse than disorderly: structured. The piracy on the Imeryds Run has a hierarchy — captain-crews operating with enough mutual coordination to avoid undercutting each other. The waterfront at Nulb is moving goods that Burne's Verbobonc contacts can't trace. Someone is running logistics through a town that officially doesn't answer to anyone. He monitors the reports from @Canoness Y'dey, operating in Nulb under her cover identity. He does not share this intelligence broadly. The fewer people who know what Y'dey is doing there, the safer she is._

> "Nulb exists because we permit it to exist. Every 'that's just how it is there' is a decision someone made not to spend the resources. One day that decision will have a cost."

_

### Relationships

##### Lord Rufus

The partnership with Rufus is the constant around which everything else orbits. Burne does not express this in language. He expresses it by consulting Rufus before every significant decision, deferring on anything that involves physical security, and carrying a quiet confidence that comes entirely from knowing Rufus is thirty feet away downstairs. They disagree regularly and constructively. Rufus thinks Burne over-thinks. Burne thinks Rufus under-plans. Both are occasionally right. They have not had a genuine argument in years — the last one was about the Keep's gate design, and Burne was wrong, which he acknowledged once, briefly, and has not revisited.

##### Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix

Burne respects the Viscount's authority completely and his decision-making selectively. Wilfrick Revepaix is a man shaped by the Battle of Emridy Meadows into a ruler who wants peace more than he wants decisiveness — and that instinct, while understandable, is being exploited by factions on the Council of Lords who have learned that the Viscount will choose inaction when action might alienate an ally. The Mounted Borderer withdrawal was the clearest example. Burne wrote to the Viscount's chamberlain. The letter was acknowledged. Nothing changed. He has not given up on the Viscount. He has adjusted his strategy. If the Viscount will not act, then the Council must be moved. And if the Council is gridlocked by [House Velysin](house-velysin-organization.md)'s militarists against [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md)'s mercantile interests, then Burne needs to find the fulcrum that moves it — which means he needs intelligence, patience, and the right moment.

##### The Council of Lords and Its Factions

Burne follows [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) through correspondence and through a small number of relationships he has cultivated over years. He is not a political actor in his own right — his mandate is Hommlet, not the city — but he understands the Council's internal geometry.

*   **Lord [Claviger Velysin](claviger-velysin-person.md) and Lord [Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md):** The militarist faction. They want the Viscount to mobilize against Temple resurgence aggressively. Burne broadly agrees with the goal and thinks their approach would trigger a gnome crisis and an economic panic simultaneously.
*   **Lord Mayor Marakios Willem Haxx:** The guild and economic power. He cares about trade disruption because it costs him and his allies money. Burne finds this motivation cynical and occasionally useful — a man who wants the roads safe for profit and a man who wants the roads safe for survival can sometimes work together.
*   **Lady [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md):** The manipulator. Burne watches her more carefully than anyone else on the Council. She is the kind of political actor who understands that the gap between "I did not oppose this" and "I approved this" is where real power lives.
*   **Lord [Wendell Rhynehurst](wendell-rhynehurst-person.md):** The moderate. The man who might be persuadable if the evidence becomes undeniable. Burne considers him the most important Council member to keep informed.

##### Prince Jimm Pithriggen and the Free Alliance

Burne's relationship with [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) is pragmatic and cautious on both sides. The gnome representative in Verbobonc City is a skilled diplomat who has watched human lords exploit legal ambiguity to press claims on gnome mine rights for years. He does not trust the Viscounty's institutions. He trusts individual humans who have demonstrated consistent behavior over time. Burne has not done enough to earn that trust yet. He is aware of this. The Mounted Borderer withdrawal — which he did not approve and cannot reverse — has made the situation worse. What he has done is keep his correspondence with Jimm honest. He has not promised what the Viscount will not deliver. He has not pretended the caravan attacks are random. He has been specific about what Hommlet's patrols can and cannot cover. In Burne's experience, reliable honesty is a foundation that cannot be rushed — but it is the only one worth building on._

> "The gnomes remember every promise Verbobonc ever broke. They are not wrong to. If we want their cooperation against what is coming, we need to be the faction that has not lied to them. That is a lower bar than it sounds."

_

### DM Notes — Playing Lord Burne

Burne is the party's most sophisticated patron and their most demanding ally. He will not send adventurers on fool's errands. He will also not explain everything he knows, because some of it would compromise people he is protecting and some of it he is still uncertain about.

#### When Players Seek an Audience

*   **He will ask what they already know:** Before Burne shares intelligence, he wants to understand what the party has seen. He is assessing reliability and filling gaps, not making conversation.
*   **He pays fair:** Burne believes in compensation for work of value. He will offer a specific amount for a specific task and not haggle. If players try to negotiate upward with no leverage, he will decline — if they negotiate with leverage (information, urgency, demonstrated competence) he will consider it.
*   **He speaks carefully:** When Burne says "I believe" he means he has evidence that is not yet conclusive. When he says "I know" he means he is certain. When he says nothing at all, he means he knows but cannot tell you yet.
*   **He does not like improvisation:** Players who come back from a mission having done something significantly different from the brief will face specific questions. Not anger — questions. He wants to understand the variables. If the improvisation was sound, he will acknowledge it.

#### Adventure Hooks

*   **The Informant in the Trading Houses:** Burne has identified that the bandit attacks on gnome caravans show foreknowledge of cargo manifests. Someone in the merchant network is feeding information. He needs someone to trace the leak without alerting it — quietly, in the trading houses of Verbobonc or Ostverk.
*   **The Nulb Report:** Burne has not heard from a contact in Nulb in three weeks. He needs eyes on the waterfront. He will not say who the contact is unless the party is already in Nulb. He will say what to look for.
*   **The Council Letter:** A letter Burne sent to Lord Wendell Rhynehurst through official courier has apparently not arrived. The courier route passes through settlements where bandit activity has been confirmed. He needs to know if it was intercepted, lost, or if Rhynehurst is not responding for his own reasons.
*   **The Mine Claim Documents:** Human lords have filed documentation with the Verbobonc City legal archive claiming ownership of gnome mines in Greenway Valley — citing a legal interpretation of a 412 CY land grant. Burne needs someone to access the archive and determine whether the documents are genuine or fabricated. This is the kind of provocation that, if left unresolved, will push the gnomes past the point of diplomacy.
*   **The Missing Quarry Shipment:** A week overdue. A missing outrider. A contractor who isn't answering letters. Burne needs someone to ride the western road and find out what happened to the stone — and to the Badger he sent to look for it. He will be direct about what he needs. He will not pretend the job is safe.
*   **Something Burne Won't Say:** On the second or third visit, Burne may tell the party, simply and without preamble: "We found Lareth's Diary. Some of the names in it are still alive and operating in Verbobonc. I need to know which ones." He will not elaborate further at that meeting.
