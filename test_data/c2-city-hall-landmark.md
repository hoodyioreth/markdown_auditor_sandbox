### Verbobonc's City Hall

_The Bastion of Civic Order_

City Hall is Verbobonc’s stone heart of government—a fortified civic hall where taxes are levied, laws are written, and petitions rise from the cobbled streets to the ears of Lord Mayor Marakios Haxx. From its beacon-topped Ragosa tower down to its bustling public chambers, this is where guilds, temples, and nobles collide to shape the city’s fate.

##### Approaching City Hall

The noise of Temple Street falls away as the cobbles open into a broad square of pale stone. To the south, a mass of golden limestone rises from the paving like a fortress grown from the rock itself—broad and square, two storeys high, its roof rimmed with a line of crisp crenellations.  
  
From the center of that roof, a third, taller block climbs again, half the footprint of the hall below, its narrow windows like watchful eyes. Above it all, thrusting from the north-eastern corner, a white tower spears the sky—a slender spire of stone with a parapet ring and beacon-hood at the very top. Two mailed guards stand there even now, tiny against the height, cloaks snapping in the wind.  
  
Banners of Verbobonc and House Haxx hang over the main stair, their colors stirring above a steady stream of petitioners: merchants with scroll-tubes, farmers in their best tunics, a knot of Vigil Wardens escorting a shackled smuggler, and a weary clerk shouting names from a slate. This is City Hall—the place where taxes are tallied, edicts proclaimed, disputes settled

### City Hall at a Glance

*   **Bastion of Civic Order:** The building is the physical heart of Verbobonc’s administration, where city business is recorded, argued, and enforced.
*   **Symbol of Authority:** Its crenelated silhouette and high beacon tower, the **Ragosa**, broadcast the city’s stability and watchfulness to countryside and river traffic alike.
*   **Public Face of the Law:** Ground-floor halls host town meetings, elections, proclamations, and trials; upper floors house magistrates, advocates, and the Lord Mayor’s cabinet.

### Architecture & Layout

##### Exterior

*   **Golden Limestone Massing:** City Hall is a **large square block** of warm-gold stone, two storeys high, with a crenelated parapet around the flat roof, evoking a small castle as much as a civic hall.
*   **Central Upper Keep:** A third storey, half the size of the footprint below, rises from the center; this level houses the private offices of the Lord Mayor and highest officials.
*   **The Ragosa Tower:** From the north-eastern corner rises the **Ragosa**, a 155-foot white-stone tower originally built for observation. On clear days, the view stretches for leagues, and its rooftop beacon can be seen by riverboats and hill-folk alike. Two of the Viscount’s personal guards are always on duty, day and night.

##### Interior

**Ground Floor – The Community House**

*   A broad, column-lined hall used for **town meetings**, public hearings, and major announcements. When idle, lesser magistrates and clerks use the space as an informal meeting ground, trading notes and gossip.
*   Raised dais at the far end for the Lord Mayor or his appointed presiding judge, with benches and galleries for guilds, temples, and noble observers.

**Second Floor – Magistrates’ Court & Offices**

*   Offices for **lesser magistrates, registrars, and tax clerks**, with narrow corridors snaking between record rooms and interview chambers.
*   Here, minor disputes, licenses, and trade infractions are processed away from the grand drama of the main hall.

**Third Floor – The Lord Mayor’s Suite**

*   The reduced-footprint level above houses:
*   **The Lord Mayor’s Office and Council Chamber**, paneled in dark wood with maps of the Viscounty, tariff charts, and ledgers from The Gentle Tradewinds shipping network close at hand. Marakios Willem Haxx
*   Private offices for key advisors, scribes, and the chief tax assessor.
*   Access is tightly controlled by guards loyal to both **House Haxx** and the Viscount.

**The Ragosa Interior**

*   Narrow spiral stairs climb the tower to:
*   Intermediate observation slits overlooking the Civic Centre and river.
*   The rooftop beacon platform, manned by **two of the Viscount’s personal guard**, tasked with signaling danger and greeting visiting dignitaries’ barges.

### Power Behind the Stone: Haxx & High Politics

##### Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md)

City Hall is, in many ways, an extension of **Lord Haxx** himself.

*   **Background:** Scion of [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md), a relatively new noble line, Marakios rose to power through the success of his shipping concern **The Gentle Tradewinds**, leveraging tariffs and river trade to build both wealth and political capital.
*   **Ambition & Alignment:** Lawful Neutral, ruthlessly pragmatic, he respects the law less as moral truth and more as a tool to shape society in ways that preserve order—and his advantage. Marakios Willem Haxx
*   **Public Persona:** In public, he is unfailingly courteous, never speaking down to citizens regardless of station, with a polished command of language and protocol. In private, incompetence draws sharp reprimand. Marakios Willem Haxx
*   **Faith:** A devoted follower of **St. Cuthbert**, raised as a fighting monk before turning to business and politics; he justifies his hard decisions as necessary for order and the greater good.

From his offices in the upper storey, Haxx oversees:

*   **Tax policy and enforcement**, including his elite collectors **Haxx’s Hardheads**, whose presence in City Hall corridors is a constant reminder of the price of delinquency. Marakios Willem Haxx
*   **Licenses, tariffs, and port fees**, often structured in ways that favor Gentle Tradewinds and its allies while squeezing rivals.

##### The Chamber of 13 & Advocates

While the full Chamber of 13 has its own home (C12), its judges and advocates are **constant visitors** to City Hall.

*   They administer elections, certify major civic decrees, and oversee the legal shape of Verbobonc’s justice system in partnership with the Lord Mayor and Viscount.

##### The Viscount’s Eye

Though **Viscount Wilfrick** rules from Castle Greyfist, the permanent stationing of his guards in the Ragosa and his oversight of appointments to key posts keep City Hall firmly under his gaze, preventing Haxx or any faction from seizing complete control.

### Civic Life & Public Use

##### Community House in Session

On any given day, the ground floor might host:

*   **Public hearings** over land disputes, trade disagreements, and guild quarrels.
*   **Proclamations and festivals:** tax amnesties, musters, holidays declared by the Viscount, or announcements from the Cathedral of Trithereon.
*   **Emergency assemblies** when flood, famine, or whispers of cult activity demand swift, visible leadership.

##### Bureaucratic Engine

Behind the scenes:

*   Clerks process **permits, trade charters, guild recognitions, and citizenship papers**.
*   Runners carry sealed messages between City Hall, the **Vigil Wardens**, guildhouses, and temples.
*   Scribes maintain the **Legal Edicts of Verbobonc**, the codified justice system underpinning the city’s rule of law.

* * *

#### Intrigue & Underworld Connections

Lord Haxx’s manipulation of tariffs and favoritism has **unintended consequences**:

*   By making legitimate trade more expensive and complicated for those outside his circle, he has **created space for smuggling and illicit services**, particularly those offered by the **Battirovka Family** thieves’ guild. Marakios Willem Haxx
*   While he may not openly collude with them, his policies make the Family’s “unofficial shipping” appealing to struggling merchants, giving the underworld a quiet foothold in the city’s economic heart.

In a **Temple of Elemental Evil / Noble Ambitions** campaign, this allows you to:

*   Use City Hall as the place where **Temple-tainted tariffs, missing caravans, and falsified ledgers** first show up on paper.
*   Put PCs between:
*   Haxx’s desire to preserve order and revenue,
*   The Viscount’s broader worries about cult influence,
*   And the Battirovkas’ determination to keep their own profitable peace.

### Using C2 City Hall in Your Campaign

**Roleplaying Beats**

*   **Formal Petition:** PCs climb the main stair to present a grievance or plea—about bandits, cultists, or noble corruption—and must navigate layered bureaucracy before ever seeing Haxx.
*   **Audience with the Lord Mayor:** In the third-floor chamber, Haxx receives them with polished courtesy, probing their motives while giving nothing away. He might:
*   Offer letters of passage,
*   Insist on tax or tithe,
*   Or quietly test their reliability for delicate tasks.

**Adventure Hooks**

*   **Tax Trouble:** Haxx’s Hardheads demand back taxes or tariffs related to the PCs’ loot or a friendly merchant’s cargo—unless the party agrees to undertake a **civic service** against bandits or suspected Temple agents. Marakios Willem Haxx
*   **Beacon in the Night:** Strange signals from the **Ragosa beacon** suggest someone is using City Hall’s tower to coordinate smuggling or cult movements along the Velverdyva.
*   **Trial of a Friend:** An ally of the PCs stands accused in the Community House; they must dig through City Hall’s records, sway advocates, and perhaps bargain with Haxx himself to prevent a miscarriage of justice.
*   **Edicts and Resistance:** A new set of edicts—drafted here—threatens gnomish or Kron Hills interests, tying directly into **Noble Ambitions** politics; the party can lobby, disrupt, or re-channel those laws to blunt the Temple’s schemes.

City Hall should feel like the **stone nerve-center** of Verbobonc: a place of order and ritual where, beneath the polished limestone and polite words, ambitious men like Haxx quietly decide how much chaos the city can afford.

##### **Filing a Petition at the City Hall**

You climb the broad stone steps, boots scuffing grooves worn by generations of petitioners. The banners of Verbobonc and House Haxx stir overhead, watching like painted eyes as you cross beneath them into the cool dim of City Hall.

Inside, the **Community House** is a maze of benches and desks. Harried clerks shuffle scrolls, runners dart past with armfuls of ledgers, and a dozen citizens queue before a long counter of scarred oak. Ink-stained men and women sit behind it like a cliff of parchment and disapproval.

You wait your turn. The line shuffles. Someone ahead of you is sent away to “Form Three, in triplicate.” Another is told to return with a guild witness. At last, a narrow-faced clerk with a wisp of beard and ink on his cuffs looks up at you, quill poised, eyes already tired of whatever you are about to say.

* * *

**Dialogue (Clerk)**

“Next. **Next.** Yes, you lot—forward, if you please. We’ve not all day.”

_He looks you over, taking in mud, steel, and travel-worn cloaks with a faint pinch of the mouth._

“Name of petitioner? No, not _all_ of you—one name will do for the record. Very good. Now—state your business in brief.”

“Is this a matter of unpaid tariffs, property dispute, guild complaint… or are you here about yet more ‘bandits’ and ‘dark cults’ in the countryside?”

_He sighs through his nose, already reaching for the wrong form._

“Understand this: the Lord Mayor’s time is not a tavern bench. If you would lodge a formal grievance against bandits, suspected cult activity, or noble misconduct, you must provide particulars—names, places, dates, witnesses. No rumors, no alehouse tales.”

“So then—what _precisely_ do you claim, and whom do you accuse? Speak plain, adventurers, that I may know which stack of troubles to throw you upon.”
