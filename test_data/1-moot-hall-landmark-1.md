1\. Moot Hall
=============

> In the center of this small village stands the largest hill. Wide stone steps lead to a set of large stone doors with runes carved over the door.   _"Ni te pharkalu, quo lun verra lu."_   "If you see it, it's not the right it." A phrase used to remind gnomes that first appearances can often be deceiving, and to look deeper for the truth.   The biggest building in the village is the Feasting Hall, which is usually reserved for special village gatherings. Long circular tables ring this round building so all can celebrate and enjoy as equals giving excellent view to the centers activities.

#### Front doors

Gilded double doors in the Gnome pantheon gods secured by a rope of brilliant gems open to wide stairs descending to the temple of the village.

> Stepping down into the entrance foyer of exquisite gemstones in cased on glass on pedestals along each wall. Painting or the Keeleene Family head gnomes over the centuries hang from behind the displays. They are enchanted to smile and wink at viewers. Long tables and benches line the sides of this high-ceiling chamber, leaving a wide central walkway to approach the raised table at the far end. Several high-backed chairs stand behind it, and a vast mural covers the wall. As with the tapestries that decorate the other walls, the mural depicts a wild landscape, bright beautiful gemstones, and fruitful gardens, but it also uses the height of wall to display mountains and a high circular window to serve as the sun.

The Moot Hall stands as a central pillar in the gnome village of Sheernobb. This impressive structure is not only a gathering place for the village's social and political events but also houses the Temple to the Priceless Gem, a sacred space dedicated to the gnome pantheon, led by [Garl Glittergold](garl-glittergold-person.md). The hall reflects the rich history, culture, and spirituality of Sheernobb.

### Description

In the center of Sheernobb, the largest hill boasts wide stone steps leading to grand stone doors carved with runes. These doors open into the Feasting Hall, the village's largest building, used for special gatherings. The hall's interior features long circular tables arranged in a way that allows everyone to celebrate as equals, with a clear view of the central activities.

##### Key Features

*   **Front Doors:** Gilded double doors adorned with depictions of gnome gods, secured by a rope of brilliant gems.
*   **Hall Foyer:** An entrance foyer with exquisite gemstones encased in glass on pedestals, paintings of Keeleene Family head gnomes, and long tables and benches lining the high-ceiling chamber.
*   **Interior Design:** High-backed chairs, a vast mural depicting landscapes, and a high circular window serving as the sun.

### History

_Founding  
_The Moot Hall was established by the Keeleene family as a central location for village gatherings, celebrations, and important decisions. Over the centuries, it has been a place of unity and cultural preservation.

##### Significant Events

*   **Ceremonial Gatherings:** The hall has hosted countless ceremonies, festivals, and important meetings, strengthening the village's social fabric.
*   **Defense Coordination:** During the giant raids, the hall served as a command center for organizing the village's defenses.

### Society and Culture

**Role in Community  
**The Moot Hall is a hub for social interaction, political discourse, and religious observance. It brings together the diverse population of Sheernobb, including Forest Gnomes, Rock Gnomes, and humans.

##### Cultural Significance

*   **Feasting Hall:** Reserved for special gatherings, fostering a sense of community and equality.
*   **Artistic Displays:** Enchanted paintings and murals that depict the village's history and celebrate gnomish artistry.

### Government and Politics

**Leadership  
**The Moot Hall is under the stewardship of [Glennddarc Keeleene](glennddarc-keeleene-person.md), the Headgnome of Sheernobb. His leadership ensures that the hall remains a place of unity and respect.

##### Political Tensions

The hall also plays a role in addressing political tensions with Verbobonc, especially given the recent withdrawal of the Viscounty troops and the souring relations between the gnomes and humans.

##### Political Functions

*   **Council Meetings:** The hall hosts meetings to discuss village matters, trade issues, and external relations.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** A neutral ground for mediating disputes within the village and with external parties.

##### Relationships

**Internal Relationships  
**Within Sheernobb, the Moot Hall fosters cooperation and camaraderie among the villagers, serving as a symbol of their shared heritage and values.

##### External Relationships

*   [Verbobonc, Viscounty](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md): The hall has witnessed various diplomatic efforts to improve relations with Verbobonc, despite recent political strife.
*   [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md): Acts as a liaison to address the concerns and interests of gnomes throughout the region.

### Economic Impact

**Trade and Commerce  
**The Moot Hall's gatherings often include discussions on trade, where merchant lords and traders negotiate deals that impact the village's economy.

##### Economic Functions

*   **Market Events:** Hosts market days and trade fairs that attract merchants from across the Viscounty.
*   **Crafts and Gemwork:** A showcase for the village's renowned gem cutting and craftwork, boosting Sheernobb's economic standing.

The Temple to the Priceless Gem
-------------------------------

Hidden beneath the Moot Hall, the Temple to the Priceless Gem maintained by the High Priest [Eldrin Glitterbrow](eldrin-glitterbrow-person.md) is a sacred space dedicated to [Garl Glittergold](garl-glittergold-person.md) and the gnome pantheon. It is an unassuming, magically hidden shrine that serves as the spiritual heart of Sheernobb.

*   **Entrance:** Wide stairs descend from the gilded double doors of the Moot Hall to the temple below.
*   **Interior:** Features a circular domed ceiling divided into four quadrants, with walls and ceilings adorned with gold leaf and studded with gold nuggets.
*   **Altar:** The main focus is an immense, priceless gem imbued with divine aura and magic, used for communication with other temples and deities.

##### Motivations

Glennddarc's main motivations are the prosperity and happiness of Sheernobb. He is driven by a deep sense of duty and care for his village, striving to ensure that it remains a safe and thriving community.

##### Personal Passions

*   **Magical Illusions:** Glennddarc is an accomplished illusionist, often using his talents to entertain and inspire the villagers.
*   **Gem Cutting:** His skill in gem cutting is renowned, and he takes great pride in creating intricate and beautiful works.
*   **Community Welfare:** He views the villagers as his children and is deeply committed to their well-being.

> _"Glennddarc's leadership has turned the hall into a beacon of unity and strength."_

##### High Priest [Eldrin Glitterbrow](eldrin-glitterbrow-person.md) 

*   **Personality:** Eldrin is wise, jovial, and deeply committed to his faith. His humor and wit reflect the spirit of Garl Glittergold, and he is known for his enchanting stories and illusions.
*   **Motivation:** To maintain the spiritual well-being of Sheernobb, strengthen the faith in Garl Glittergold, and ensure the village remains harmonious and prosperous.
*   **Political Relationships:** Eldrin acts as a mediator between the gnomes of Sheernobb and external political entities, leveraging his position to foster peace and understanding.
