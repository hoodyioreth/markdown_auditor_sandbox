D3 The Red Don Inn
==================

A venerable luxury inn built high upon a twisted **red-hued ipt tree**, the Red Don is the Elven Enclave’s most refined guesthouse—where diplomats from Celene, wealthy travelers, and enclave elders meet upon “neutral” boards, and where every courtesy is a veil for politics.

##### Approaching the Red Don

The lane lifteth toward the canopy, and the world below grows muffled—hoof and wheel, shout and clang, all softened to a distant murmur.  
  
Ahead, a vast ipt tree rises in a twisted grandeur, its bark carrying a strange ruddy cast, like embers banked beneath ash. Lanterns hang from bough and balcony, their light warm and honeyed, and wooden galleries wrap the trunk in old elven fashion—so seamless with living branch that thou canst scarce tell where tree endeth and carpentry beginneth.  
  
A stair of polished timber bears thee upward. At the door, a tall elf with silver hair and robes of deep green inclines his head as if thou wert a prince newly come to court.  
  
_“Welcome to the Red Don,”_ he says, voice mild as autumn wind. _“Thy weariness shall be set aside… if thou wilt be pleased to set aside thy roughness also.”_

#### What This Place Is

The Red Don Inn is a **cultural hub and prestige lodging** within the Elven Enclave—an “immersive elven experience,” famed for exquisite vegetarian fare, refined quiet, and rooms that feel like sleeping in the boughs of a living legend.  
It is also, in practice, a **soft-power embassy**: Celene’s folk and Enclave emissaries use the Red Don as a rest-stop and meeting ground precisely because it is politely “neutral.”

> “Within these screened lanterns, words are poured more carefully than wine—petals drift, ears depart, and only the moon keepeth counsel.”

* * *

#### Exterior & Approach

**The Red Ipt:** The inn is built upon a great ipt whose bark carries an unusual red hue—local whispers say it once sheltered elven royalty, and that the tree remembers.  
**Branch-Sprawl Architecture:** The structure spreads across major boughs—walkways, balconies, and small pavilions joined by graceful stairs. It feels older than the city’s common stonework, a relic of the enclave’s **ancient origins** before Verbobonc became a human dominion.

**DM Use:** Make the approach a social threshold. The Red Don is where a PC learns that “being welcomed” and “being allowed” are not the same thing.

* * *

#### Interior & Amenities

**Common Hall (Low Canopy):** Quiet conversation, harp music by night, and service that never hurryeth—guests are encouraged to speak as if each word cost a coin.

**Rooms with a View (Open to the Air):** Many chambers are **open to the elements**, shielded by subtle magical barriers that keep the temperature comfortable—likened by locals to the steadiness of a _ring of warmth_. Guests may sleep with wind and rain nearby, yet never suffer their bite.

**Carved Chambers:** Each room bears exquisite relief carvings—leaf-knot borders, moon-and-branch motifs, and scenes of elven history meant to be “read” by those who know how.

**Exclusive Pleasures:** Rainwater baths, fresh fruit feasts, and nightly performances by bardic harpists—luxury at a price, and a signal that this house expects refinement from its patrons.

* * *

#### Delon Redleaf, Sole Steward of the Red Don

Rumored to be as ancient as the inn’s namesake tree, Delon hath been the Red Don’s steward “as long as any can recall.” He treats every guest with the deference due to royalty—whether peasant-born or noble—yet that courtesy is also a tool: it coaxes folk into revealing what they are.

**Personality:** Gracious, ceremonious, and unfailingly calm.  
**Motivation:** Preserve and showcase elven culture; maintain the Red Don as a beacon of heritage.  
**Politics:** Diplomatically neutral in appearance—yet subtly promotes elven values, tradition, and long memory.

**At the Table (2nd-person dialogue):  
**Delon pours pale wine as though measuring time itself.

“Thou art far from thy hearth,” he says softly. “Tell me—dost thou travel toward something… or away from it?”

If you answer too swiftly, his smile never wavers.  
“Ah. Then we shall speak of comfort first. Truth comes better when it hath room to breathe.”

**Secret (DM):** Delon hears _everything_. Not by sorcery (though some suspect), but by old practice: he places servants, musicians, and seating with the care of a general placing scouts.

* * *

#### Politics & Cultural Relevance

**Bridge on the Low Road:** The inn’s position near the Low Road makes it a hinge between quarters—especially elven and gnomish traffic—and a natural place for “chance” meetings.

**Enclave Context:** The Elven Enclave is a deliberate bridge between elven heritage and human governance, shaped by ancient roots and renewed by refugees and revived ipt-house architecture. The Red Don benefits from this—hosting news, tradition, and the careful balancing act that keeps coexistence from breaking.

**Rift & Reconciliation:** If you wish to bring the broader Enclave tensions into play, the Red Don is ideal: it is the place where urbane enclave elves speak of harmony while quietly fretting over rifts with forest clans and the city’s expansion.

* * *

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   Seek a **Celene contact** or hear “safe” diplomatic gossip without stepping into D6’s barracks halls.
*   Rent a room to **appear respectable** while meeting someone who would not speak in a common tavern.
*   Attend a harp performance where a bard hides messages in phrasing and refrain.

##### If They Linger

*   Delon’s courtesy becomes a slow test: he offers what the PC “seems to need,” and watches whether they accept with grace—or greed.
*   The party gains a reputation: _polite guests_, _rough guests_, or _useful guests_.

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A “neutral” meeting turns sour—someone’s honor is pricked, and elven restraint becomes an icy, elegant threat.
*   A messenger arrives from outside Verbobonc with news of road trouble; the wrong ears hear it first.
*   A guest from the Foreign Quarter attempts to buy access they are not meant to have.

#### Services and Amenities

*   **Luxury at a Price:** With costs reflective of its high-quality offerings, the inn provides exquisite vegetarian cuisine, magical climate control in rooms, and serene natural surroundings.
*   **Exclusive Experiences:** Guests can enjoy special amenities like rainwater baths, fresh fruit feasts, and nightly performances by bardic harpists.

#### Relationships and Influence

*   **Elven Quarter Integration:** Positioned strategically near the Low Road, it bridges cultural exchanges between the Elven and Gnome Quarters.
*   **Role in Local Politics:** While not overtly political, the inn influences local dynamics through its status as a neutral ground for discussions among various factions.

Delon Redleaf’s mastery in creating an immersive elven experience makes the Red Don Inn a cornerstone of the Elven Quarter, embodying the spirit and heritage of its people while serving as a beacon of hospitality in Verbobonc.

### Ready-to-Run Scene: The Open-Air Chamber (Red Don Inn, D3)

### Scene Purpose (DM)

A midnight parley in a Red Don open-air chamber. The “comfort-barrier” keeps the chamber warm and dry, yet it **thins** at certain seams. A hidden listener tries to read lips, catch words, or slip a whisper-spell through the weak point. The scene delivers: **elven diplomacy, secret leverage, a clue-thread, and an immediate social hazard**.

* * *

##### Boxed Read-Aloud (Approach)

Night in the canopy is a velvet hush. Lanterns below shine like fallen stars, and the city’s distant din comes faint as surf. Your chamber’s balcony stands open to the wind, yet no chill biteth—only a clean, high air that smells of leaf and rain long gone. A delicate shimmer, nearly unseen, wavers at the edge of the archway: the inn’s gentle ward, holding weather at bay. Beyond the railing, the branches sway slow, and somewhere a harp-string trembles—whether by music or wind, thou canst not say.

* * *

#### Setup

**Location:** A Red Don “room with a view” — balcony overlooking the Enclave lanes.  
**Time:** Midnight.  
**NPC at the parley:** _Seldanna Ithilweave_ (Conclave attaché or envoy’s aide) OR substitute any refined elven negotiator.  
**Hidden listener:** _A soft-footed eavesdropper_ (Foreign Quarter agent, rival enclave youth, cult courier, or hired whisper).  
**Barrier detail:** The weather-ward is strongest at the lintel-stones and weakest near a carved leaf-motif on the balcony arch—an “ornament seam.”

* * *

#### What’s Really Happening (DM Only)

Seldanna brings a proposition: information or access in exchange for the party performing a discreet service.  
The eavesdropper is trying to confirm names, a location, or a schedule. They are not here to kill—unless caught and cornered.

* * *

##### Key Sensory Details (for you to sprinkle in)

*   The ward’s shimmer appears like heat-haze when the lanternlight hits it.
*   Wind carries words _oddly_—some syllables vanish, others arrive too clear.
*   The tree-boughs creak like old ship-timber.
*   Somewhere below, a door closes softly—too soft.

* * *

##### Cast

**Seldanna Ithilweave (Elven negotiator):** Polite, indirect, never says the “real” thing first.  
**The Listener (choose one):**

1.  **“Silk-Ear”** (human spy), cloaked, uses a thin wire “listening reed.”
2.  **“Mothkin”** (fey-touched elf youth), hidden in leaves, curious and reckless.
3.  **Temple Courier** (cult agent), tries a faint cantrip to push whisper-sound through the seam.

* * *

##### Skill Checks (Optional, 5e-friendly)

*   **Passive Perception 14+**: Notice a faint scuff on balcony rail / a leaf cluster disturbed / a shadow that holds still too well.
*   **Arcana 13+**: Notice the ward thins at the leaf-motif seam; understand sound may leak there.
*   **Insight 13+** (during talk): Seldanna is steering away from names and places—_because she suspects an ear nearby._
*   **Stealth vs Perception** (if the party tries to counter-ambush).

**If the party speaks names aloud near the seam:** The listener gains a useful clue automatically.

* * *

##### The Scene Beats

1.  **Seldanna arrives** with courtesy and veiled intent.
2.  **Negotiation**: She offers a “shape of truth,” not the truth.
3.  **The wind misbehaves**: a phrase echoes oddly—hinting at a listener.
4.  **Reveal or Catch**: The party spots the eavesdropper—or Seldanna tests them to see if they’ll protect her discretion.
5.  **Consequence**: If the listener escapes, a rival faction moves first. If captured, the party must decide: mercy, bargains, or turning them over.

* * *

### Dialogue (Keep separate for DM use)

#### Seldanna’s Entrance

**Seldanna:** “Thou hast chosen a chamber with an honest wind. I commend thee. Walls in the city have too many memories.”

**Seldanna:** “Forgive the hour. Daylight belongs to merchants. Midnight belongs to truths that would rather not be sold.”

**Seldanna:** “May I sit? Nay—better. Let us stand. Words spoken standing are harder to forget.”

* * *

#### Opening the Parley

**Seldanna:** “Thou seekest answers. Yet answers are like pearls—drawn from depth, and paid for with breath.”

**Seldanna:** “I shall not ask thy loyalty. I ask only thy _discretion_. Canst thou keep a thing unspoken even when it would profit thee to speak?”

**Seldanna:** “There is a matter—small in shape, large in consequence. A thing hath been taken. A journey was broken. And now… eyes watch the roads.”

* * *

#### The Offer (veiled, indirect)

**Seldanna:** “In the coming days, a name will be spoken in places that pretend not to know it. If thou hearest it, do not repeat it. Bring it to me.”

**Seldanna:** “I can place thee before a door that is not opened for common hands. But I will not do so for coin.”

**Seldanna:** “I will do so for a choice.”

* * *

#### The Half-Truth Wind (hint of eavesdropping)

**Seldanna:** “Speak softly. This inn is… kind. Yet kindness hath seams.”

**Seldanna:** “If I say ‘border,’ the wind carries it. If I say ‘Celene,’ the wind _keeps_ it. Curious, is it not?”

**Seldanna:** “Tell me—didst thou feel that? A shiver, though no cold is here.”

* * *

#### If the PCs press for specifics

**Seldanna:** “Thou wouldst have a map. I offer a compass.”

**Seldanna:** “Name the villain for me, and I shall disappoint thee— for villainy is seldom a single face.”

**Seldanna:** “Give me thy patience. Or give me thy refusal. Either is cleaner than hunger pretending to be courage.”

* * *

#### If the PCs notice something

(prompt them without giving it away)

**Seldanna:** “Thy eyes go yonder. Hast thou seen aught?”

**Seldanna:** “If there is an ear, let it be disappointed.”

**Seldanna:** “Say not names. Not here. Not upon that carving.”  
_(she nods subtly toward the leaf-motif seam)_

* * *

#### The Listener: Dialogue Options

##### If the listener is caught in the act

**Listener (human, tense):** “Easy—easy. I was only… making certain no one fell from the railing.”

**Listener:** “A man cannot even admire the night air now? Saints preserve us.”

**Listener:** “I heard naught. Truly. Only wind.”

##### If the listener is an elven youth (mothkin)

**Listener (whispery, ashamed):** “I meant no harm. I— I only wished to know what the great ones whisper.”

**Listener:** “Please… do not make this a matter for the elders.”

**Listener:** “I swear upon leaf and moon, I shall forget what I heard—if you let me go.”

##### If the listener is a Temple courier (cold, controlled)

**Listener:** “A fair night. A fair inn. Shame if fair folk spoke too boldly.”

**Listener:** “I need no names. I need only direction.”

**Listener:** “Move aside. The wind is mine tonight.”

* * *

#### Seldanna’s Reaction (based on party choice)

##### If the party protects discretion and catches the listener quietly

**Seldanna:** “Well done. Thou hast not merely sharp eyes… thou hast a steady soul.”

**Seldanna:** “Now we may speak a little truer. Not true enough to burn us—yet truer than before.”

##### If the party makes a scene (shouting, threats)

**Seldanna:** “Hush. Hush, I beg thee. Thou art setting lanterns in the fog.”

**Seldanna:** “If thou must swing a hammer, do it where stone may bear it—not here, where glass remembers.”

##### If the listener escapes

**Seldanna:** “So. The wind hath carried our shadow to other ears.”

**Seldanna:** “Then we must move as if we are late— for now we are.”

**Seldanna:** “If thou wouldst still be useful, meet me at dawn—beneath the turning wheel at the Basilica. Speak to no one of this.”

* * *

#### Resolution & Consequences (DM)

##### Outcomes

1.  **Listener captured quietly:** Gain a clue (a token, a note fragment, a scent, a signet). Seldanna trusts the party more.
2.  **Listener escapes:** Rival faction acts first; later scenes become harder; Seldanna grows colder, more indirect.
3.  **Party bargains with listener:** You create a recurring contact—dangerous, useful, and never fully honest.
4.  **Party turns listener over to Vigil Wardens:** The Enclave notices. “Cooperation” becomes “scrutiny.”

##### Loot/Clues (pick one)

*   A **thin wire reed** used to “feel” vibrations at the ward seam.
*   A **waxed slip** with half a route: “Deleveu—ten miles—border” (no names).
*   A **grey-blue powder** residue on gloves (trail-masking or scent-dulling).
*   A **small token** bearing a vague elemental motif (enough to worry, not enough to prove).

* * *

#### Quick DM Tagline to End the Scene

The lanterns below gutter once, as if the inn itself blinked. Seldanna’s gaze follows the dark between boughs.

**Seldanna:** “In Verbobonc, the night is full of courteous knives. Choose thine… and learn to smile while holding it.”

##### How the Red Don Serveth

(custom of the house)

Nothing is “cooked” in the common tongue. Dishes are **wakened**, **kissed by flame**, **bathed in dew**, **steeped in moon**, or **kept beneath everlight** until the proper moment. Petals are not garnish—_they are courtesy._
