55\. Hunting Cottage
====================

> To the south of and perhaps a thousand feet into the forest line is a depilated cottage and a barn. A steady stream of smoke plumes from the chimney. The cottage and barn sits by the ford of a winding stream at the base of a steep hill. The woods leading here are light and easy to navigate; in fact, a wide, often trafficked path (arguably a road given the well-worn wagon ruts) is cut through the forest here.

The Hunting cottage is a vital logistical node for the operations of the Temple of Elemental Evil. Located south of [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) and near the [Imeryd's Run](imeryd-s-run-location.md), this unassuming log structure masks a sinister purpose: it serves as a supply depot, a slave-handling station, and a gateway into the Temple itself. Hidden deep within the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md), it is a place of grim purpose and dark machinations, overseen by loyal agents of the Greater Temple and guarded by Gefreid's bandit guards.

### The Compound

##### **The Hunting Cottage**

*   **Exterior:** A log structure of aged timber, repaired with scavenged stone and iron. A thin trail of smoke curls from its chimney at all hours.
*   **Interior:** Reinforced with iron bars and bolted shutters, the cottage includes a war room (with field maps), sleeping bunks for 8–12 Temple bandits, a message alcove for encrypted missives from Lucius Graeme, and Gefreid’s command chamber.
*   **Function:** Command post and triage zone for inbound cargo, personnel, and intelligence.

##### **The Barn**

*   **Structure:** An expanded field barn, mostly intact.
*   **Contents:** Barrels, slave cages, weapon crates, haylofts, and false panels concealing Temple insignia.
*   **Use:** Temporary prisoner holding, cargo re-packing, and horse stalls for messenger couriers and inbound brigands.

##### **The Secret Tunnel**

*   **Location:** 200 feet behind the barn, camouflaged at the base of a rocky ridge.
*   **Entrance:** Natural limestone cave worked into a 6-ft-wide passage reinforced with shoring beams and stone.
*   **Length:** Approx. 300 feet descending to the **Broken Tower access point** of the Greater Temple’s lower levels.
*   **Security:**
*   Two bandit guards rotate in pairs.
*   Rune-marked stones at three locations along the tunnel are magical alarms (Temple-only magic).
*   The door at the Temple end is triple-locked and guarded by elite Greater Temple warriors.

### **Operational Logistics**

##### **Hunting Cottage Overview**

Located one mile south of the village of Nulb, the **Hunting Cottage** sits nestled in a dense pocket of the Gnarley Forest where the southern reaches of the **Kron Hills** begin to rise. The terrain is rugged and cloaked in thick underbrush and old-growth trees, making the site naturally defensible and nearly impossible to find without guidance.

Once a rustic outpost for hunters from Nulb, the cottage now serves a far darker purpose. Under **Gefreid Patris’s** command, the site has become the **primary field hub for the Greater Temple’s supply chain.**

The facility includes:

*   **The Cottage Interior** – Now reinforced with barred windows and steel bracing, it functions as a bunkhouse and command post. Gefreid and his **Temple bandit unit** (8–12 hardened men) maintain watch rotations, secure maps and orders, and rest here between supply runs.
*   **The Barn** – Used to store incoming crates, sacks, and bound captives awaiting transfer. Armaments, cult relics, and Temple-marked goods are hidden behind false walls. Some lower stalls have been converted into temporary holding pens.
*   **Hidden Tunnel Access** – Roughly **200 feet behind the cottage**, past a screen of brambles and tree cover, lies a **natural cave entrance** at the base of a short limestone ridge. This feature is **carefully disguised with forest camouflage**, but clearly guarded.

##### **The Secret Tunnel**

*   **Entrance:** A 6-foot-high limestone cave mouth, initially natural but worked over time by Temple masons to serve as a logistical artery.
*   **Tunnel Length:** Roughly **300 feet** long, sloping downward beneath the hills and toward the ruins of the Temple of Elemental Evil.
*   **Destination:** The **Greater Temple's lower levels**, where supplies, slaves, and reinforcements bypass surface detection entirely.
*   **Security Measures:**
*   **Two armed sentries** stationed at all times at the entrance.
*   **Rune-marked boulders** for signaling safe passage or danger.
*   **Logbooks of every shipment** maintained by Gefreid or his sergeant.
*   **Primary Use Cases:**
*   Receive prisoners from Madame Selentis’s bandits and Lorveth's slave contacts.
*   Collect supply crates offloaded in Nulb and carried by trusted brigands.
*   Dispatch trained recruits from Lareth’s Moathouse or surrounding regions.
*   Allow Temple operatives to move unseen between the surface and dungeon depths.

##### **Supply Chain Routing**

*   **Nulb → Madame Selentis → Hunting Cottage → Tunnel**
*   **[THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) → Gattas Void’s Raiders → Hunting Cottage → Tunnel**
*   **River Shipments → Vesrek’s Pirates → Lorveth → Selentis → Hunting Cottage → Tunnel**

### Cabin and Barn Defenses: Snares

The perimeter surrounding the **Hunting Cabin and Barn** is laced with concealed snares—crude yet deadly traps set by Gefreid’s bandits and occasionally improved by bugbear scouts. Crafted from tension-wrapped wire, camouflaged noose loops, and ground-pit toggles, these snares are designed to catch careless intruders before they ever reach the door.

They are **not visible to the casual traveler** and are meant to delay, injure, or capture trespassers for interrogation—or disposal.

> _"Think you’re clever, sneaking around my cabin? The snares will find you before I do."_

### Iron Claw Bugbear Tunnel Guard (Greater Temple Extension Force)

Recruited from the **Iron Claw Clan** of the **Southern Gnarley Forest**, this squad of bugbears serves as **Commander Feldrin’s elite subterranean security force**. They are stationed at the **secret tunnel entrance** behind the Hunting Cottage to ensure **no passage in or out** occurs without Temple approval. This unit operates autonomously from the main cottage bandits, under strict orders to enforce Temple secrecy with deadly force.

* * *

##### Position & Duties

*   **Guard Post:** Located inside the **first 60 feet of the tunnel**, where the natural cave gives way to worked stone.
*   **Barracks Niche:** A hollowed-out side chamber (approx. 20’ x 30’) with bedrolls, weapon racks, a cooking pit, and a crude idol of **Grankhul**, bugbear god of alertness and ambush.
*   **Rotations:** Always 4 on guard; 2 stationed at the entrance, 2 deeper inside near the locking gates.
*   **Command Link:** Report directly to **Commander Feldrin** via runner or Lucius Graeme.

* * *

### Monster Companions

The Iron Claw Bugbears are known for using brutal, semi-trained **beast-companions** captured from the Gnarley’s darkest hollows. Commander Feldrin has authorized their use here to supplement tunnel defenses.

##### Recommended Monster Companions:

1.  **Giant Rats**: Common in the lower tunnel edges. Swarms act as early-warning systems.
2.  **Goblins**: 5–6 **enslaved goblins** serve as scouts, errand-runners, and early-warning fodder.
3.  **Carrion Crawlers**: One or two reside in a pit chamber off the main route; released when sabotage is suspected.
4.  **Death Dogs**: Favored by bugbears for their disease-ridden bites and twin heads. Chained to posts near the tunnel entrance.

*   **Phase 1:** Tripwire alerts and rats swarm entrance; bugbears move into ambush.
*   **Phase 2:** Death dogs are unleashed and attack intruders in the main chamber.

### DM Notes & Hooks

*   **Temple Orders:** Bugbears are **not loyal to Gefreid**—they serve **Feldrin directly**. This breeds tension between the human bandits and the monstrous guards.
*   **Inter-Faction Conflict:** Selentis's men are warned never to enter the tunnel without a Temple seal. Any unsanctioned movement is met with lethal force.
*   **PC Opportunity:** PCs disguised as cultists may face a **DC 17 Deception or Religion** check to pass the bugbears unchallenged.

The Secret Tunnel Complex
-------------------------

_**Temple Access Tunnel beneath the Hunting Cottage, 579 CY**_

A critical asset of the Greater Temple, this hidden tunnel serves as the **primary supply and infiltration route** to the **lower levels of the Temple of Elemental Evil**. Located just south of Nulb within the Kron Hills-Gnarley Forest border, it allows Temple agents to move supplies, prisoners, and warbands undetected. Commanded by **Gefreid Patris**, the tunnel is guarded and maintained by a dedicated force of **Iron Claw Clan Bugbears**, their enslaved **Goblin raiders**, and monstrous cave-dwelling allies.

* * *

### Tunnel Entrances

##### 1\. **The Natural Cave Mouth (Primary Access)**

*   **Location:** 200 feet behind the Hunting Cottage, concealed by forest growth and brush along a limestone ridge.
*   **Purpose:** Main ingress/egress for wagons, prisoners, large Temple parties.
*   **Concealment:**
*   Dense screen of hanging vines and brambles (DC 15 Perception to spot from 30 ft).
*   Covered with netting camouflaged in moss and leaves (DC 17 Investigation to detect opening).
*   **Surveillance:**
*   **Two bugbear sentries** posted in a dugout blind 20 ft uphill overlooking the cave.
*   **A pair of goblin runners** posted further uptrail with a horn to alert the interior guards.

##### 2\. **The Hidden Water Well (Secondary Access)**

*   **Location:** Old, cracked stone well hidden behind the barn near the cottage.
*   **Purpose:** A narrow vertical shaft (2 ft diameter) leads down into the upper level of the tunnel.
*   **Hazards:**
*   Swarming **Giant Rats** lair in this section.
*   Slippery stones, 30-ft vertical descent (DC 14 Athletics to climb down silently).
*   **Gameplay Note:**
*   This entrance is **unguarded by bugbears**, making it ideal for stealthy PCs but dangerous due to terrain and rats.

* * *

##### Tunnel Layout and Structure

*   **Total Length:** Approx. 300 feet.
*   **Construction:** Begins as a natural cave (20 ft wide), transitioning to worked stone.
*   **Features:**
*   Iron torch sconces (magical continual flame) every 30 ft.
*   Drainage grooves cut into the floor to handle blood and runoff.
*   Two reinforced steel doors (locked, watched) leading to Temple access zone.

##### Side Caverns (Functional Rooms)

1.  **Bugbear Barracks**
2.  4 bedrolls, a stone fire pit, and war trophies on the walls.
3.  Guard rotation chart and crude temple iconography.
4.  **Goblin Slave Den**
5.  Filthy nesting space. 5–6 **enslaved goblins** sleep here, armed with shortbows and scimitars.
6.  Serve as scouts, errand-runners, and early-warning fodder.
7.  Overseen by Rult Bonegnasher, bugbear beastmaster.
8.  **Carrion Pit**
9.  A partially collapsed side tunnel sealed with a grated portcullis.
10.  Home to 1–2 **Carrion Crawlers**, fed scraps and sacrificial victims.
11.  Bugs released if invaders breach the tunnel.
12.  **Storage Grotto**
13.  Stacked crates, Temple-marked barrels, and tarpaulin-covered weapon caches.
14.  Logged by the bugbears using pictograph scratch tallies.

### Forces & Leadership

##### **Iron Claw Bugbear Guard**

*   Recruited by **Commander Feldrin** directly from Gorvash the Cruel’s tribe.
*   Loyal only to Feldrin and the Temple hierarchy—not Gefreid or Selentis.
*   Commanded by **Druk Marrgut**, a grizzled bugbear captain and brutal tactician.
*   Operate in fire teams of 2–4; rarely sleep simultaneously.

##### **Goblin Slave Raiders**

*   Captured from minor forest tribes; serve as tools and expendable scouts.
*   **Tasks:**
*   Watch trail approaches.
*   Sound alarms.
*   Assist with hauling supplies through the cave.
*   Dissuade enemy scouts via false paths and decoys.

* * *

##### Defensive Tactics

1.  **First Contact:** Goblin whistle signals, bugbear archers reposition into cave killzones.
2.  **Bugbear Intercept:** Heavy infantry blocks entrance or pushes intruders into the Carrion Pit.
3.  **Collapse Protocol:** Druk Marrgut can trigger an intentional mini cave-in using prepared supports, sealing the central passage if the tunnel is compromised.
4.  **Escape Tunnel:** Secret passage behind Carrion Pit allows 1-way escape to a distant game trail, known only to Feldrin’s command agents.

##### Encounter Suggestions for DMs

*   **Goblins Flee with Warnings:** A single goblin escapes and alerts the bugbears. The party must move fast or face reinforced defenders.
*   **Slaves Awaiting Transport:** A group of captives from Nulb are being prepped in the tunnel for final passage into the Temple.
*   **Carrion Crawler Loose:** Something has triggered the beast early—both bugbears and PCs must survive.
*   **Feldrin’s Agent Arrives:** Lucius or another courier arrives mid-scene, creating roleplay tension and chaos.

### Plot Hooks & Quest

A mile south of Nulb, a smoke-threaded “lodge” squats at the forest’s edge. On paper it’s a hunter’s stop; in truth it’s the Greater Temple’s throat—supplies, captives, and orders swallowed into the dark. Gefreid Patris runs the yard with iron routine; Commander **Feldrin** owns the tunnel; bugbears keep its teeth sharp. Everyone in Nulb feeds this place, whether they admit it or not.

#### Plot Hooks

*   **The Waterside Hand-Off (Social • Stealth)  
    **A midnight “guest” transfer leaves the **Waterside Hostel** (Dick Rentsch fronting, **Wat** pouring) for the cottage: two burlap-hooded recruits, one gagged prisoner, three crates “soap & pitch.” Follow the handcart from the **Nulb Stables** past the pouchers path. If you intercept quietly, **Madame Selentis** notices new talent—whether to hire or hurt.
*   **Bread and Blood (Investigation)  
    **Sack seams at the cottage carry **green re-stitch twine** and faint **double-dot chalk**—the same marks turning up at **Veyne’s Mill** and **Old Mavrek’s Bakery**. Track the marked sacks back to **Valegrave’s Farmstead** to prove the Earth Temple’s skimming scheme—or plant false marks to spark a feud between Earth (Romag) and Greater (Feldrin/Selentis).
*   **River to Root (Heist)  
    **A low-wake skiff offloads sealed casks at the **Wharf Pier**, then a covered wagon trundles them to the cottage barn. The labels scream “salt fish,” the weight says “weapons.” Shadow **Captain Tolub’s** crew from the **Boatman’s Tavern & Market** to the forest road and lift one crate without the tally changing.
*   **Courier in the Smoke (Ambush • Intel)  
    Lucius Graeme** rides in at dusk with waxed packets for Gefreid’s “message alcove.” Snatch one and you’ll have routes, names, and gate-phrases good for a week—maybe a note from **Smigmal Redhand** scheduling a Broken Tower audit.
*   **Gravewhisper (Occult • Social)  
    Osric Vell** at the **Nulb Graveyard** complains the dead “lean south” on storm nights. He believes soul-signs are being dragged through the tunnel. Bring him an item from the cottage pens (manacle pin, tally chip) and he’ll name a safer hour to move on the place—and what wards to expect.
*   **Constable’s Quiet Favor (Law • Recon)  
    Constable Malvaar** won’t raid—he’s not suicidal—but he’ll pay (paper, not coin) for a **snare map** of the cottage perimeter after a child lost a foot-trap to “poachers.” Hand him a rough and he’ll give you just enough legal cover to stand your ground if Temple brigands chases you through town.
*   **Selentis’ Red Line (Faction • Double-Cross)  
    **Selentis forbids her brigands to enter the tunnel proper without a seal—Feldrin’s bugbears answer to **Feldrin**, not her. Offer to “audit” Gefreid’s books for her; sell the same audit to Feldrin to prove her meddling. Either way, somebody bleeds trust.
*   **Sahl’s Mistake (Rescue)  
    **After **Sahl’s Cottage** balks on a Reaver run, **Verric Colbane** sends a message: Derrik in an iron cage, cottage barn. If you crack it, expect goblin horns echoing down the limestone and a **carrion crawler** gate going up.

### Short Quest: **“Cut the Vein”** (Levels 4–6)

**Premise.** The cottage is a 24/7 throat to the Temple. Your job isn’t to burn it; it’s to **clamp it**—sever flow for forty-eight hours without raising a general alarm. That buys Hommlet, the road, and **Bramblethorn Ranch** breathing room—and forces Temple factions to blame each other.

#### Objectives

1.  **Infiltrate** the yard or barn without alerting the bugbear tunnel guard.
2.  **Disrupt** two of three flows—people, steel, food—so logs still “look right” but nothing moves.
3.  **Exfiltrate** clean, leaving a blame trail that points at a rival faction (Earth, Water, or Selentis).

#### Approaches (choose one or chain them)

*   **The Well Drop (Stealth • Hazard):** The cracked stone **well behind the barn** drops into a rat-slick shaft (quiet climb or fight swarms). Lands near goblin dens and storage grotto; perfect for swapping crate seals, jamming gate pulleys, or doctoring tally boards.
*   **Convoy Ghosts (Deception):** Walk in as Selentis auditors with a forged writ from the **Gilded Shackle (Zilchus)** and “inspect” prisoner papers; stall all transfers pending tithe verification. Requires a decent seal and colder blood.
*   **River Tail (Skirmish • Heist):** Lift a single weapon crate at the **Wharf** and replace it with salted stones; later, at the cottage, your missing crate forces a recount and two-hour halt while Gefreid hunts ghosts.

#### Site Features to Exploit

*   **Perimeter Snares:** 30-ft ring of noose and tripwire traps. Mark or reset them behind you to carve a safe lane—and a nasty surprise for pursuit.
*   **Bugbear Rotation:** Always 4 on duty in the tunnel (2 entrance, 2 deeper). At shift change (every 4 hours), the entrance blind is weakest.
*   **Carrion Pit Gate:** A barred side-chamber holding a **carrion crawler**. A single lever jam (iron spike + hammer) prevents release, denying the defenders their panic button.
*   **Message Alcove:** Gefreid’s logs + Lucius’ packets. Swap one page: change a date, flip a sigil color (red↔green), and you can trigger a factional “audit” that freezes movement for a day.

#### Complications (roll or choose 1–2)

*   **Lieutenant Brunk, Feldrin’s Inspector Arrives:** An armored courier with **Feldrin’s seal** shows up mid-job. If he smells trouble, he orders a tunnel **mini-collapse**—escape turns into a race through dust and falling timbers.
*   **Greater Brigands on Edge:** Gefreid’s men are sober and jumpy—another crew vanished near the **Trapper’s Cottage** last tenday. Disadvantage on Deception unless you speak their slang and keep it brief.

#### Success Bands

*   **Clean Clamp (Best):** Two flows halted; logs look correct. Blame falls on **Romag’s Earth crew** (green twine planted; chalk pricks everywhere). Cottage shuts for 48 hours while factions argue. You gain: Lucius’ route cipher; Gefreid’s favor clock set to “suspicious, not hostile.”
*   **Messy Clamp (Good):** One flow halted; one loud scuffle. Cottage slows 24 hours; **Smigmal** dispatches auditors to Nulb—heat rises at **Waterside** and **Boatman’s**.
*   **Hot Exit (Barely):** Bugbears sounded the horn; you escape with a crate or prisoner, but Feldrin now tags you “assets to neutralize.” Expect night blades at the **Old Mayor’s Manor** road.

#### Rewards & Leverage

*   **Screng’s Gratitude:** **Mother Screng** trades rare salves and quiet introductions in Verbobonc.
*   **Bramblethorn Favor:** **Ormund/Rhia** grant grazing, fresh mounts, or shelter for a week.
*   **Zilchus Paper:** A counter-clause from the **Gilded Shackle** that can freeze a shipment anywhere in Nulb (once).
*   **Proof of Passage:** Marked crate seals and altered logs that tie **Selentis’ brigands** or **Romag’s agents** to specific transfers—perfect for turning one faction on another.

#### DM Notes

*   Keep clocks: **Bugbear Rotation (0–4)**, **Convoy Window (0–3)**, **Audit Spark (0–2)**.
*   Use location cross-talk: clues at **Veyne’s Mill** (green twine), **Waterside** (room assignments), **Wharf** (crate weights), **Graveyard** (ward drift), **Nulb Stables** (fresh shoeing on Temple teams).
*   Push choices: If the party spares goblin runners, they warn the bugbears; if they kill them, **Osric** feels the dead tip south and asks questions.
*   Tone: cold efficiency, low light, the sound of ledgers turning pages like knives.

### DM Notes: If the Supply Line Is Severed

The Hunting Cottage is the Temple of Elemental Evil's surface throat. Every prisoner, weapon crate, food shipment, and intelligence packet bound for the Greater Temple passes through this single chokepoint before descending through the secret tunnel. When the flow stops, the entire Temple feels it — but each faction feels it differently, blames someone different, and responds on a different timeline.

This section gives the DM a structured response framework for any scenario where the party disrupts the Hunting Cottage supply line — whether through the "Cut the Vein" quest, an ambush on the road, destruction of the cottage itself, or sabotage of the tunnel.

 

##### The Three Supply Flows

The Hunting Cottage manages three distinct flows. Disrupting any one creates consequences; disrupting two or more triggers escalation:

*   **People:** Prisoners from **Madame Selentis**'s brigands, recruits from [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) (via the Waterside Hostel), and trained raiders dispatched from the [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md). These feed the Temple's slave labor, altar sacrifices, and troop replenishment
*   **Steel:** Weapons, armor, and Temple-marked supplies arriving by river (Captain Tolub's crew → Boatman's Tavern → overland) and by road (Selentis's wagon runs from Nulb). These arm the Temple's garrison and equip new recruits
*   **Food and materiel:** Grain, salt provisions, alchemical reagents, and cult relics. The Temple cannot sustain its underground population without regular resupply — the dungeon levels produce nothing

 

##### Hour-by-Hour Disruption Timeline

**Hour 0 — Flow Stops:** A scheduled shipment fails to arrive at the cottage, or [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md) discovers sabotage. Gefreid's first response is procedural: he sends a bandit runner north to Nulb to check whether the shipment left on time. He does not alert the tunnel guard or the Temple yet — delays happen, and Gefreid does not want to report a problem that might resolve itself.

 

**Hours 2–4 — Gefreid Investigates:** The runner returns with no explanation, or the runner does not return. Gefreid dispatches a two-man patrol along the supply route (Nulb road or forest path from the Moathouse) to locate the missing shipment. He posts extra sentries on the cottage perimeter and suspends outbound transfers through the tunnel until he understands what happened.

 

**Hours 4–8 — Cottage Lockdown:** If the patrol finds evidence of an ambush (overturned wagon, dead escorts, missing cargo), Gefreid locks down the cottage compound. The barn is sealed. The tunnel entrance guard under **Druk Marrgut** is placed on high alert. Gefreid sends an encrypted message through **Lucius Graeme**'s courier network to [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) at the Greater Temple: _"Shipment intercepted. Supply flow halted. Investigating."_

 

**Hours 8–12 — Feldrin Responds:** Feldrin receives the message and makes a decision based on scope:

*   **Single missing shipment:** Feldrin orders Gefreid to resume operations within 24 hours and investigate internally. He suspects incompetence or theft by Selentis's brigands before he suspects external attack
*   **Multiple disruptions or evidence of organized assault:** Feldrin dispatches [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) to audit the cottage and identify the source of the breach. Smigmal arrives within 12–24 hours with a small escort of Greater Temple soldiers

 

**Hours 12–24 — Faction Blame Cycle:** If the supply line remains severed beyond 12 hours, the Temple's internal politics ignite. Each faction interprets the disruption through the lens of its own rivalries (see Faction Responses below).

 

**Hours 24–48 — Temple-Wide Impact:** The Greater Temple's underground levels begin to feel the shortage. Provisions for the garrison run low. Prisoner processing halts. Altar sacrifices are postponed. [Romag](romag-person.md) of the Earth Temple and the other elemental priests demand answers from Feldrin. **Hedrack** is briefed.

 

**Beyond 48 hours:** If the cottage remains non-operational for more than two days, the Temple shifts to emergency protocols. An alternate supply route is established — slower, riskier, and running through deeper Gnarley Forest paths that bypass Nulb entirely. This emergency route takes 1–2 weeks to establish and operates at roughly one-third capacity. The Temple can survive on it, but not comfortably.

 

##### Faction-by-Faction Response

Each Temple faction reacts differently to supply disruption. These responses happen in parallel and often in conflict with one another — the DM should track them as separate threads.

 

**Greater Temple (Feldrin / Hedrack):**

*   Feldrin's priority is restoring flow. He does not care who is responsible — he cares that shipments resume. His first instinct is to replace Gefreid if he suspects incompetence
*   If Smigmal's audit reveals external sabotage, Feldrin authorizes a hunt for the responsible party. He dispatches 4–6 Greater Temple soldiers to sweep the roads between Nulb and the cottage
*   **Hedrack** becomes involved only if disruption exceeds 48 hours. His response is strategic: he orders the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) garrison under [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) to increase local raiding to compensate for lost supplies. This means more bandit activity on the Long Road — which the party may encounter or hear about

 

**Earth Temple ([Romag](romag-person.md)):**

*   Romag suspects the Water Temple of sabotage — he believes Belsornig wants to control the supply chain and redirect resources to his own recruitment operations in Nulb
*   Romag pressures Feldrin to investigate the Waterside Hostel and Boatman's Tavern as the source of the leak
*   If the disruption continues, Romag orders his Earth Temple agents to establish their own supply cache independent of the cottage — stockpiling materials smuggled directly through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) route. This is unauthorized and creates friction with Feldrin

 

**Water Temple ([Belsornig](belsornig-person.md)):**

*   Belsornig is opportunistic. A supply disruption is not his problem — it is his leverage. He offers to "supplement" Temple provisions through his river contacts, but at a price: greater Water Temple representation in supply allocation
*   If Romag accuses him, Belsornig deflects by pointing at **Madame Selentis**'s brigands as unreliable middlemen who skim from every shipment

 

**Fire Temple:**

*   The Fire Temple has minimal involvement in the surface supply chain. Their response is to demand that resources already inside the Temple be redistributed — which means taking from other factions
*   This demand accelerates inter-faction tension, particularly with the Earth Temple, which controls the largest stockpiles

 

**Air Temple:**

*   The Air Temple is the most isolated faction and the least affected by surface supply disruption. However, if the disruption is blamed on adventurers, the Air Temple may dispatch Feathergale Knights to hunt them — more for glory and political credit than genuine logistical concern

 

**Nulb (Selentis's Network):**

*   **Madame Selentis** [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) treats any disruption as a threat to her commercial empire. She dispatches her own agents to investigate independently of the Temple
*   If Selentis determines that adventurers are responsible, she activates her contact network in Nulb to identify, track, and either recruit or eliminate them
*   **[Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md)**'s audit of the cottage spills over into Nulb — she inspects the Waterside Hostel, the Boatman's Tavern, and the Nulb wharf. This raises visible heat in the village that observant players will notice (more armed men, nervous tavern staff, closed doors)

 

##### What the Party Gains from Disruption

Severing the supply line — even temporarily — produces tangible campaign benefits:

*   **24-hour halt:** The party gains breathing room. Patrols on the roads thin out as Gefreid recalls his men. Prisoner transports stop, potentially saving captives currently in the pipeline
*   **48-hour halt:** Inter-faction blame erupts inside the Temple. The DM can use this to create opportunities for the party to exploit — factions distracted by internal politics are easier to infiltrate, negotiate with, or play against each other
*   **Permanent destruction of the cottage:** The Temple loses its primary surface supply route. The emergency forest route operates at one-third capacity. The Temple's garrison weakens over 2–4 weeks. [Romag](romag-person.md) becomes desperate enough to escalate the false-flag raids through [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) and [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) — generating more surface chaos to cover rebuilding efforts
*   **Intelligence harvest:** The cottage's message alcove, shipment logs, and tally boards contain route ciphers, contact names, and gate-phrases. Capturing these intact (rather than destroying the cottage outright) gives the party leverage for infiltration, forgery, or turning factions against each other

 

##### Restoring the Supply Line

If the party disrupts but does not destroy the cottage, the Temple restores full operations on the following timeline:

*   **Minor disruption (single ambushed shipment):** 24–48 hours. Gefreid reorganizes, increases escort strength on future convoys, and resumes
*   **Major disruption (cottage raided, Gefreid captured or killed):** 1–2 weeks. Feldrin appoints a replacement commander and sends additional guards. The tunnel remains operational throughout — only the surface compound is affected
*   **Catastrophic disruption (cottage destroyed, tunnel collapsed):** 3–4 weeks minimum. The Temple must establish the emergency forest route and may attempt to reopen the tunnel with duergar labor from the crypt levels. This is the maximum strategic impact the party can achieve at this location

##### **Gefreid Patris – Site Commander**

A hard-eyed enforcer of the Greater Temple’s will, Gefreid oversees the Hunting Cottage with brutal pragmatism. Appointed by Commander Feldrin, he ensures the smooth intake and transfer of prisoners, supplies, and cult recruits through the secret tunnel into the depths of the Temple. Gefreid does not seek glory—only discipline, efficiency, and control.

*   Reports directly to **Commander Feldrin** of the Greater Temple.
*   Oversees all **logistical operations**, prisoner handling, and transfer scheduling.
*   Keeps detailed shipment logs and coordinates with **Lucius Graeme** and **Madame Selentis**.
*   Avoids personal entanglement with Temple politics—focuses solely on execution of duties.

* * *

##### **Bandit Personnel (8–12)**

Hardened mercenaries and cutthroats loyal to the Greater Temple, these bandits serve Gefreid as muscle and messengers. They patrol the forest, load supply wagons, and handle the dangerous process of receiving shipments from Nulb and the surrounding region.

*   Hand-picked by **Gefreid** for loyalty, efficiency, and silence.
*   Patrol the compound, watch the trail from Nulb, and assist with prisoner transfers.
*   Report any suspicious activity, lost cargo, or irregular visits immediately.
*   Maintain the barn as a **holding and staging zone** for Temple-bound assets.

* * *

##### **Iron Claw Bugbear Tunnel Guard (6–8)**

Deeper in the tunnel lies the true muscle of the Temple’s subterranean gate—Iron Claw bugbears under **Druk Marrgut**. Sent by Feldrin, these snarling enforcers patrol the inner cave network, guard the lower access doors, and unleash brutal ambushes on any unapproved intruders.

*   Loyal only to **Commander Feldrin**, not Gefreid or human agents.
*   Guard the **main cave entrance**, tunnel interior, and final Temple gate.
*   Use enslaved **goblins** as scouts, runners, and expendable bait.
*   Deploy **Carrion Crawlers** and cave traps in emergencies.
*   Tensions run high between them and the human bandits—but both sides understand the cost of failure.

### Cabin and Barn Defenses: Snares

The perimeter surrounding the **Hunting Cabin and Barn** is laced with concealed snares—crude yet deadly traps set by Gefreid’s bandits and occasionally improved by bugbear scouts.

##### Trap Area and Layout

*   The snares are spread in a **30 ft. wide perimeter ring** around both the **cabin and barn**.
*   Most are hidden in **brush-covered depressions**, **between trees**, or beside **animal trails**.
*   Each entrance path (trail, back slope, and old game path) contains at least **1d4 snares**.
*   New snares are reset every morning by assigned bandit patrols.

* * *

##### D&D 5e Mechanics: Hunting Snares

**Snare Trap (Simple Trap)**

*   **Trigger:** Pressure (stepping into the noose loop or breaking a tripwire).
*   **Effect:** The target must succeed on a **DC 15 Dexterity saving throw** or become **restrained and lifted 3 feet off the ground** by a spring-tensioned line.
*   If the creature fails the save by 5 or more, it also takes **1d6 bludgeoning damage** from being yanked into a nearby tree or beam.
*   **Detection:** Passive Perception **DC 17** to notice the snare before stepping into it.
*   **Disarm:** Requires a successful **DC 13 Dexterity (Thieves’ Tools)** check or similar tool proficiency.
*   On failure by 5 or more, the snare triggers immediately.
*   **Escape:** A restrained creature can attempt a **DC 15 Strength (Athletics)** check to break free, or another creature can sever the rope with a slashing weapon (**AC 12**, **5 HP**).
