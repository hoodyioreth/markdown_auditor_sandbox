L1-5D. Book-Light Barracks
==========================

Pallets line the walls, most heaped with snoring orcs. Two more squat at a rough table made from battered chests, tankards in hand, arguing in grunts. The wall mosaic here is fresh and whole: a tall, robed man lifts a great book, rays of light spilling out to drive back beast-men and marsh shapes. The floor is gritty with spilled broth and ash.

Another **orc barracks** carved out of a Suel dormitory. The room’s **mosaic is pristine**: a tall robed figure holds a radiant **great tome**, its light driving back swampborn foes—classic Suel iconography of law/knowledge (likely **Lendor’s priesthood** bearing the **Book of Origins**; some scholars argue a **Wee Jas** rite of warding). **Eight orcs** billet here—six asleep on wall pallets; **two awake**, drinking and bickering at a makeshift **table** made from **two battered chests** dragged to the center. Any fight nearby draws this room on your response clock.

#### What’s here & clues

*   **The Mosaic (Religion/History DC 12):** Suel rite—**law/knowledge** mastering chaos. PCs naming [Lendor](lendor-person.md) or [Wee Jas](wee-jas-person.md) as keepers of order/arcana gain advantage on later checks to identify Suel inscriptions in this wing.
*   **Table-chests:** One **empty**, flipped as a table leg; the other **locked** (below).
*   **Arguers:** The two awake orcs slap the table and spill ale—easy to **distract** (Deception DC 12 to keep them bickering for 1 round).

* * *

#### Tactics & response

*   **First beat:** The two awake grab **axes** and bark, “Huh? A noise?” → “Found you! Now try and hide from this!”
*   **If a round passes,** one sprints to **Room 4** to blow/bray the horn while the rest form a **half-ring** around the table for **half cover** (AC +2).
*   **Response clock:** From loud combat here, **5A** sends 2 orcs in **2 rounds**, **5B** in **2–3**, **5C** peeks in **4**.
*   **Morale:** If 4+ orcs drop, survivors try to **shove** and fall back toward **5A**.

**Orc voices (sprinkle):** “Bleed! Bleed and die!” “Die, metalman!” “Stop moving, stupid human!” “You are weak! I am stroooonng!”

* * *

#### Loot & containers

*   **Locked chest (crude copper lock; DC 12 thieves’ tools / STR DC 16):  
    13 gp, 160 sp, 330 cp** in mixed coin and a knotted gut-string purse.
*   **Empty chest:** Just bones and rag-scraps; lid used as table plank.
*   **Misc:** 2 dented **tankards**, a **jawbone howl-whistle** wedged under the table (Perception DC 12; functions as horn).

* * *

#### Quick 5e plugs

*   **Orcs (8)** as MM. Optional spice: once/encounter **Moon-Frenzy**—after first time bloodied, +2 damage on next hit.
*   **Cover:** The chest-table provides **half cover** to two adjacent orcs until moved or broken (15 hp, AC 12).

* * *

#### Development

*   PCs who **praise** the “book-light keeping swamp horrors at bay” may impress any Suel-leaning NPC later (minor social edge).
*   The untouched mosaic can serve as **proof of Suel provenance** for Verbobonc officials—or as a target for Howling Moon desecration if the alarm spreads.
