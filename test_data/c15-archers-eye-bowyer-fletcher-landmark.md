C15 Archer’s Eye: Bowyer /Fletcher
==================================

The **Archer’s Eye** is both a master bowyer’s shop and a quiet shrine to [Rudd](rudd-person.md), patron of skill, chance, and daring feats. Situated on the Civic Centre’s eastern edge—where guild law yields to the open road—the Archer’s Eye serves hunters, messengers, soldiers, duelists, and adventurers who believe fortune favors the trained hand. More than a place of trade, it is a proving ground where precision, patience, and nerve are tested beneath the Laughing Goddess’s watchful gaze.

##### Approaching the Archer’s Eye

The scent of oiled yew and fresh-cut ash greets you before the door comes fully into view. A painted sign creaks softly above the lintel: a perfect bull’s-eye split cleanly by an arrow. Beneath it, chalk marks and tally notches scar the stone—records of shots taken, wagers won, and luck tested.  
  
Inside, shafts hang like organ pipes from the rafters, bows rest along felt-lined racks, and a small blue-draped niche holds a simple target marked with Rudd’s sign. Someone, not long ago, has laughed here.

#### **Interior & Features**

*   **The Shooting Lane:** A reinforced back corridor where customers may test bows. Shots taken here are often accompanied by whispered prayers—or daring boasts.
*   **The Shrine of the Unerring Shot:** A modest alcove with a bull’s-eye target, blue ribbons, and coins pinned where arrows struck true.
*   **Craft Benches:** Bow staves in various stages of carving; every weapon here is made by hand.
*   **Luck Tokens:** Arrowheads etched with sigils of chance, sold cheaply—or given freely to those who impress the keepers.

* * *

#### **Faith of Rudd at the Archer’s Eye**

The Archer’s Eye reflects **Rudd’s creed** as outlined in the _Luckbringer_ articles:

*   **Skill before Fate:** Every shot must be practiced; luck only answers effort.
*   **The Wagered Shot:** Friendly contests are encouraged—coin, dares, or pride.
*   **Cheating as Craft:** Tricks are tolerated _only_ if done cleanly and without complaint.
*   **No Turning the Dead:** In quiet homage to Rudd’s clergy, no rites against undeath are practiced here—only living skill is honored.

The bull’s-eye target, Rudd’s holy symbol, appears everywhere—subtle reminders that precision and chance share the same center.

#### Historical Background

*   **Establishment and Growth:** The shop’s establishment dates back several decades, founded by a renowned local archer whose techniques and designs have been passed down.
*   **Community Role:** Over the years, Archer’s Eye has grown from a mere shop to a community hub for archery lovers, hosting discussions and planning local archery events.

#### Products and Services

*   **Variety of Bows:** Offers a wide range of bows including shortbows, longbows, and crossbows.
*   **Specialty Items:** Available are exotic and masterwork bows, including 'mighty' bows which are customized for added strength.
*   **Ammunition:** Stocks various types of ammunition, ensuring archers have access to high-quality supplies.

#### Pricing and Quality

*   **Cost Structure:** Pricing is set at standard market rates with special items priced accordingly for their enhanced qualities.
*   **Quality Assurance:** Specializes in high-quality, durable bows with options for upgrades and customizations.

#### Community and Cultural Impact

*   **Disciples of Rudd:** The shop serves as a meeting place for followers of [Rudd](rudd-person.md), often discussing and organizing archery contests.
*   **Local Hangout:** It’s a known gathering spot where community members share their passion for archery and engage in friendly competitions.

#### Political and Social Relationships

*   **Influence on Local Policies:** The shop’s popularity and central role in archery have positioned it as a significant player in discussions about local sports and recreational policies.
*   **Partnerships:** Collaborates with local guilds and other crafters, contributing to the economic and social fabric of Verbobonc.

Archer’s Eye not only equips its patrons with essential archery supplies but also fosters a community centered around the sport, enhancing its cultural and social value within Verbobonc.

#### **Roleplaying the Archer’s Eye**

*   PCs may earn **Rudd’s favor** through contests of skill.
*   A lucky or unlucky shot may become a rumor whispered through the Civic Centre.
*   Ruddite clergy sometimes **watch silently**, evaluating potential champions.
*   A PC archer or rogue might gain reputation here—good or ill.

* * *

#### **Adventure Hooks**

1.  **The Unmissed Arrow:** A perfect shot is made here that _should not have been possible_. Rudd’s clergy want to know why.
2.  **Cheater’s Luck:** A rival archer is accused of divine cheating—was it skill, magic, or darker influence?
3.  **The Blue Ribbon:** Winning a famed contest earns a token that later opens doors among Rudd’s followers—or draws jealous foes.
4.  **Target of Fate:** An arrow fired in jest later appears embedded in a corpse miles away.

* * *

##### **Quote to Drop in Play**

_“You don’t pray to Rudd before the shot. You pray after—when you find out what she thought of it.”_
