Erevan Ilesere
==============

**Erevan Ilesere, god of Mischief, Change, Rogues and Changelings  
**_The Trickster, the Chameleon, the Green Changeling, the Evershifting Shapechanger, the Fey Jester, the Jack of the Seelie Court_     Erevan Ilesere, the deity of Mischief, Change, and Rogues, occupies a unique and somewhat nebulous position within the elven pantheon known as the Seldarine. His penchant for unpredictability and love for chaos mark him as a distinct figure among the gods, revered not for the stability he brings but for the excitement and unpredictability. This god thrives on the thrill of transformation and the art of the prank, making him a patron deity for those who live by their wits and those who seek to embrace change in all its forms.

#### Unpredictable by Nature

*   **Master of Disguise:** Erevan's ability to alter his appearance embodies his dominion over change and mischief.
*   **Symbol of the Rogue:** Erevan's followers include elves and other beings who share his love for trickery and adventure.

#### Pantheon and Partnerships

_A Place Among the Seldarine  
_Despite his capricious nature, Erevan maintains a significant role within the Seldarine, contributing to the celestial dynamics with his unique flair for mischief. His relationships with other deities, marked by mythic tales and alliances with gods of similar dispositions, underscore his ability to navigate the divine realm with the same unpredictability that he encourages among his followers.

*   **Erevan's partnerships:** particularly with the Seelie Court and Dugmaren Brightmantle, reflect shared ideals of creativity and the joy of disruption.
*   **Fellowship with Deities:** Despite his unpredictable nature, Erevan holds a place among the elven gods, contributing his unique perspective to their celestial endeavors.

#### Doctrine of Mischief

_Dogma  
_Erevan's dogma encourages followers to embrace life's inherent unpredictability, finding joy in the chaos and using mischief as a means to explore the world's complexities. This doctrine serves as a guide for his worshippers, urging them to approach life with a light heart and to recognize the value in upending convention and expectation. Erevan's teachings advocate for a life lived boldly and without undue concern for the morrow, reflecting the god's own mercurial nature.

> "Embrace change and chaos, for they are the only constants. Let mischief guide your path to uncovering the multifaceted truths of the world. Seek joy in the unexpected, and let your actions remind others not to take life too seriously."

#### The Shifting Palace

_Realm of the Trickster  
_Set within the celestial beauty of Arvandor, Erevan's realm epitomizes his essence through its ever-changing and trap-laden palace. This divine abode, located near one of Yggdrasil's roots, captures the spirit of Erevan's domain over change and mischief, offering a glimpse into the god's unpredictable character. The palace itself, with its shifting architecture, serves as a physical manifestation of Erevan's love for the unforeseen and his mastery over the art of surprise.

*   **Arvandor:** Erevan's domain is as variable as his mood, featuring a palace that defies norms and expectations, symbolizing his essence perfectly.

### The Followers of Whimsy

_Devotees of Change  
_Erevan's followers, a diverse array of beings including elves, pixies, and other creatures of whimsy, are drawn to the god's embodiment of freedom and change. These worshippers often lead lives marked by spontaneity, engaging in thievery or other rogue-like activities as expressions of their devotion. Erevan's influence encourages his followers to live independently, challenging societal norms and embracing a lifestyle defined by unpredictability.

*   **Elves and More:** While elves form the core of his worshipers, Erevan also appeals to a menagerie of creatures who embody or appreciate the spirit of mischief.
*   **Thieves and Adventurers:** Those who follow Erevan often lead lives filled with adventure, unpredictability, and sometimes, thievery.

### Clergy of Caprice

_The Unpredictable Clerics  
_The clerics of Erevan, known for their black leather attire and ever-changing places of worship, embody the god's unpredictable nature. With minimal formal duties, these clerics focus on spreading mischief and challenging the established order, delighting in the discomfort of the powerful and the disruption of the mundane. Their roles as cleric/thieves or cleric/fighter/thieves highlight the blend of divine service and adventurous spirit that characterizes Erevan's worshippers.

*   **Dress and Worship:** His clerics' attire and their nomadic worship practices mirror Erevan's ever-changing nature.
*   **Roles and Responsibilities:** Free from traditional duties, Erevan's clerics focus on spreading mischief and upsetting the societal order.

#### Festivals of the Trickster

Holy Celebrations Eclipses and spontaneous celebrations define the holy days of Erevan's faithful, who honor their deity through the sacrifice of stolen treasures and impromptu revels. These festivals, particularly the Secrets of the Heart under the full moon, embody the essence of Erevan's teachings, celebrating love, beauty, and the transformative power of affection in all its forms. The rituals and offerings made during these times reflect the followers' commitment to embodying the joy and spontaneity of their god's ethos.

*   **Eclipses:** Marked as times of heightened divine activity, eclipses serve as holy days for Erevan's followers to celebrate and make offerings of pilfered treasures.

#### Conclusion

Erevan Ilesere's place within the elven pantheon serves as a reminder of the importance of unpredictability, change, and the lighter side of life. His worshipers see in him a divine endorsement of their own unpredictable paths, making him a beloved deity among those who value freedom, flexibility, and a good prank. Through his unpredictable actions and the whimsical guidance he provides, Erevan ensures that the elven pantheon and its followers never stray too far into solemnity or predictability.
