C8 The Spruce Goose Inn
=======================

### A Luxurious Haven in Verbobonc

The Spruce Goose Inn is Verbobonc’s most refined house of hospitality—an elegant civic-square establishment catering to nobles, wealthy merchants, foreign envoys, and influential travelers. More than an inn, it is a social instrument: a place where alliances are sounded out over wine, reputations are polished or quietly ruined, and invitations to greater things are extended with a smile. Those who are seen here are assumed to matter.

##### Approaching the Spruce Goose

Facing the Civic Centre square, the Spruce Goose rises three stories of pale stone and dark timber, its tall mullioned windows glowing with lamplight at all hours. A carved goose with wings half-spread crowns the entry lintel, its surface polished by decades of passing hands. Liveried attendants stand just inside the doors, their posture relaxed but eyes keen—nothing enters unnoticed. The soundscape is restrained: low conversation, the clink of fine glass, soft music from a string quartet on certain evenings. This is not a place for shouting victories or public brawls. Here, words are chosen carefully.

#### Historical Significance

*   **Established Prestige:** The inn has long been a symbol of opulence within the city, known for its exclusive clientele and high standards of service.
*   **Founding and Evolution:** The inn's storied past reflects its growth from a high-end lodging to a landmark of luxury within Verbobonc, frequented by the most influential figures in the region.
*   **Architectural Marvel:** Constructed with a blend of stone and marble, the inn features stained glass windows and elaborate decor that speak to its rich history and cultural significance.

#### Description of Facilities

*   **Elegant Interiors:** The inn boasts a grand marble entrance hall, luxurious sitting rooms, and private suites with modern conveniences like magical lighting and indoor plumbing.
*   **Exclusive Amenities:** Features include a heated magical swimming pool, climate-controlled courtyards, and extensive security measures to ensure guest privacy and comfort.

#### Roles and Responsibilities

*   **Management Hierarchy:** Managed by a dedicated Maître D' along with a team of skilled staff who cater to every need of the guests, from room service to personal errands.
*   **Staff Expertise:** Employees are highly trained in hospitality, security, and discretion, capable of handling the unique demands of the inn's distinguished guests.

#### Notable People

*   **Distinguished Guests:** Regularly hosts royalty, dignitaries, and high-profile figures, each bringing along their retinue while relying on the inn's superior service.
*   **Elite Clientele:** The inn's reputation draws a sophisticated crowd, from visiting ambassadors to high-ranking clergy and influential merchants.

#### Goods and Services

*   **Luxurious Offerings:** The inn provides an array of exceptional services including gourmet banquets in the grand ballroom, personalized room service, and special arrangements for private functions.
*   **Exceptional Dining and Comforts:** Offers gourmet meals, luxury spa services, and opulent banquet facilities, providing everything guests might need under one roof.
*   **Additional Luxuries:** Guests can enjoy free massages, exotic entertainment upon request, and the convenience of having all their needs met without ever needing to step outside.

### Private Sitting Rooms & Discreet Luxuries

Beyond the public dining hall and marble entry lies a series of **private sitting rooms**, reserved for nobles, visiting dignitaries, wealthy merchants, and those granted Mirela Velysin’s personal favor.

These chambers are designed for **comfort, discretion, and quiet negotiation**.

##### The Sitting Rooms

Each room is appointed with:

*   Deep **plush chairs and divans** upholstered in velvet and brocade
*   Low polished tables of walnut or cherrywood
*   Thick rugs from Keoland and the Sheldomar Valley
*   Heavy curtains that mute sound from the corridor and ensure privacy

The rooms are small enough to feel intimate, yet grand enough to remind guests they are being _hosted_, not merely served.

##### Arcane Service & Comfort

Subtle magic elevates the experience beyond mundane luxury:

*   **Unseen Servant** spells quietly deliver food, wine, and fresh linens without interrupting conversation
*   Dishes arrive already plated and warmed, goblets refilled before they are noticed to be empty
*   Guests may request **free massages** performed by trained attendants, aided by minor restorative cantrips to ease fatigue from travel or council sessions

None of this magic is flashy. It is intentional, restrained, and designed so that **nothing distracts from conversation, intrigue, or influence**.

#### Relationships and Politics

*   **Strategic Location:** Situated near the city’s civic heart, the inn is intertwined with the local political and social scenes, hosting many of the city’s most significant events and meetings.
*   **Influence and Alliances:** Maintains strong relationships with local businesses and cultural institutions to ensure guests have access to the best experiences Verbobonc has to offer.

#### **Role in the Civic Centre**

The Spruce Goose functions as:

*   A **neutral ground** between rival noble houses
*   A **meeting place** for guildmasters and foreign traders
*   A **soft gateway** into political plots, including _Noble Ambitions_
*   A **staging point** for characters summoned into higher service

Lady Elinor Asbury’s early contacts, interviews, and discreet meetings are commonly arranged here, making the inn a natural starting node for ambitious adventurers.

* * *

#### **Adventurer Hooks**

*   A servant quietly slips the party a sealed invitation after they are observed behaving _competently_.
*   A rival group attempts to discredit the PCs during a shared dinner.
*   A noble patron requests discreet assistance—off the record, but well-rewarded.
*   Thieves, cult agents, or spies are rumored to watch the salon from the gallery above.

##### Special Rumor – The Nigb River Death (High Stakes Hook)

“Did you hear? The **Master Chef**—the one who catered noble tables from the Spruce Goose to Jylee’s—was found **floating face-down in the Nigb River** at dawn. No purse missing. No knife wound anyone admits to seeing.”

**Whispered Additions (choose or layer):**

*   “He cooked for _everyone_. That’s why it had to be quiet.”
*   “They say his last commission was a private supper—no servants, no guests listed.”
*   “Someone paid him to **remember a recipe** he shouldn’t have.”
*   “The watch called it an accident. The priests did not.”
*   “A wine bottle was found downstream. Wrong vintage. Wrong year.”
*   “He’d been asking odd questions—about poisons that leave no mark… and symbols burned into flesh.”

**Why This Matters**

*   The Master Chef served **noble houses, guild councils, and secret gatherings**—including invitation-only dinners tied to political maneuvering.
*   His death threatens **every faction that ate from his table**.
*   Anyone investigating risks angering **nobles, merchants, and unseen patrons** who prefer silence.

* * *

##### DM Notes – Using These Rumors

*   Any **House Asbury**, **Noble Ambitions**, or **Temple of Elemental Evil** hook can be layered into these lines.
*   Thief, bard, wizard, or cleric PCs gain advantage on gathering rumors in the Civic Centre at night.
*   Repeated rumors should **connect**, not repeat—players should feel threads tightening.

_First Sight of Mirela Velysin_
-------------------------------

The murmur of the Civic Centre seems to soften as she enters.

You notice her first by the space that opens around her—chairs subtly shifted, voices lowered by instinct rather than command. **Mirela Velysin** moves with measured grace, her presence polished rather than ostentatious: velvet trimmed in silver thread, a signet ring worn plainly, eyes sharp enough to weigh a room in a single breath.

Candlelight catches the pale green of her gown and the faint sheen of calculation behind her smile. She surveys the hall not as a guest, but as one accustomed to rooms changing shape around her intentions.

When her gaze finds you, it does not linger by accident.

She inclines her head—just enough to acknowledge equals, not supplicants—and gestures lightly for wine to be brought. The servants obey at once.

* * *

##### First-Meeting Dialogue

_Mirela Addresses the Party_

**Mirela:  
**_“You are newly come to the Goose… yet not unnoticed. That alone recommends you.”_

She studies you openly now, eyes moving from weapon to posture to expression, as though assembling a ledger only she can read.

**Mirela:  
**_“Verbobonc is a city that rewards those who arrive with purpose—and devours those who wander without it. Tell me… which are you?”_

A pause. Polite. Deliberate.

**Mirela:  
**_“You need not answer at once. Consider instead this courtesy.”  
(she gestures toward the wine)  
_“_Drink. Observe. Listen. These walls hear more truth than the council chamber ever will.”_

Her smile sharpens—pleasant, but edged.

**Mirela:  
**_“And when you decide what sort of trouble you intend to be… you may find I already have a use for you.”_

She raises her goblet slightly.

**Mirela:  
**_“Welcome to the Civic Centre. Mind your reflections here—many mirrors, few windows.”_
