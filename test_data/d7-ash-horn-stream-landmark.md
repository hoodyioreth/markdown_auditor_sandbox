D7 Ash Horn Stream
==================

In the heart of the pebblestone roads of Verbobonc's Elven Quarter lies the Ash Horn Stream religious meeting area. The stream, which empties into Nigb's Run from the sacred Pond of the Hart, serves as a spiritual hub for elves devoted to Corellon Larethian, the Creator of the Elves. A sacred **processional stream and grove-temple** where the elves of the Elven Enclave gather **thrice monthly** in gold-and-white to sing for **Corellon Larethian**, sending flower-petals down the Ash Horn from the **Pond of the Hart** to [Nigb’s Run](nigbE28099s-run-location.md) —an unbroken ribbon of blessing, memory, and political unity.

##### Approach the Ash Horn

The pebblestones soften into mossy edges, and the air changeth—cooler, cleaner, leaf-sweet. Beneath arching boughs a narrow stream slides over pale stones, whispering as if it would not wake the city. Here and there, carved stepping-stones bear old elven knots and star-forms; a low altar of green-veined stone stands by the water, strewn with petals that gleam like fallen dawn. Then thou hearest it—voices, far off, joined as one: not loud, but perfect, as if the forest itself had learned to sing.

#### What This Place Is

Ash Horn Stream is the Enclave’s **holy meeting ground**—a “temple amidst the trees” where worship is performed in motion: song, procession, and offering. It is explicitly tied to the **Pond of the Hart**, whose waters feed the stream; thus the rite binds the city’s elven quarter to the living woodland beyond the walls.

Although devoted chiefly to **Corellon Larethian** (creator and protector), the site is also a public proof of harmony between enclave elves and the **Druids of the Hart**—a relationship with real political weight in Verbobonc.

* * *

#### Exterior & Facilities

_"Temples Amidst the Trees"_

##### The Grove of Corellon

A lush grove serves as the primary “sanctuary,” deliberately unbuilt—living trees, wildflowers, and carefully tended paths rather than walls.

*   **Prayer Circles:** small ringed clearings for private vows and meditations.
*   **Hilltop Overlook:** a gentle rise giving a guarded view over the pebblestone roads—half devotional, half practical (watch the quarter without seeming to watch it).

##### Meeting Grounds

These are the “public” parts, where outsiders may be permitted during non-rite hours.

*   **Ceremonial Pathway:** the procession route from the Elven Dawn quarter down to the stream-mouth.
*   **Flower Altar:** an intricately carved stone where offerings are placed before petals are committed to the current.
*   **Singing Circle:** a sacred ring where the faithful gather and lift hymns in practiced harmony.

* * *

#### Ritual Life & Holy Observances

**Monthly Processions (thrice monthly):** The faithful don **gold and white** and sing in a measured, flawless cadence, releasing flower petals into the Ash Horn as a visible prayer carried downstream.

**Major Festivals:**

*   **Blessing of the Seldarine** (start of each season)
*   **Rites of Renewal** (spring)

**DM Use:** The rite is a _clock_ you can schedule: three times monthly means the PCs can _choose_ to arrive on a living holy day (crowds, attention, politics), or on a quiet day (secrets, meetings, surveillance).

* * *

#### History & Meaning

**Ancient Blessings:** The stream has been revered since before Verbobonc became a city—one of those places that makes the Enclave feel older than the streets around it.  
**Celene Ties:** The stream’s importance deepened as ties with Celene strengthened, making it a symbol of shared tradition and elven identity within human lands..

### Relationships & Politics

##### Elven Enclave

The Council of Elders oversees the spiritual health of the enclave and ensures observances uphold Corellon’s principles; Ash Horn is one of the quarter’s strongest tools for **cultural preservation**.

##### Druids of the Hart (Old Faith)

They maintain the stream’s sanctity alongside the Enclave, linking the rite to the **Pond of the Hart**—a place believed to be a tear of Beory and a vital ecological and strategic node.

##### Political Role (Council of Lords pressure point)

Resheph (and allied druids) represent enclave interests and the city-woodland boundary on Verbobonc’s political stage; Ash Horn is where “faith” becomes “policy” without ever saying so aloud.

**Motivations at Ash Horn**

*   Preserve elven unity and rites within a human city
*   Defend the sanctuary against threats and trespass
*   Advocate peaceful coexistence with Verbobonc’s broader society

### Player Interaction Hooks (DM Toolbox)

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   To seek elven counsel without entering the Bastion’s formal scrutiny (Ash Horn is “soft diplomacy”).
*   To witness or join the procession (earn trust through respect).
*   To gain access to druidic insight about river travel, hidden paths, or unseen threats (Resheph’s animal network).

##### If They Linger

*   They notice patrol-glances from the hilltop overlook: the grove is prayer, but also surveillance.
*   A minor rite is offered: “carry one petal” (a tiny vow that becomes a social test).

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A procession is disrupted—petals sink, or drift _upstream_ (a sign interpreted as omen or sabotage).
*   Outsiders mock the song or step into the Singing Circle unbidden; the Enclave responds with cold courtesy, not violence—yet names are remembered.

#### Optional 5e-Friendly Mechanics (All Optional)

**Sanctuary Etiquette:** If PCs behave reverently (no weapons drawn, voices lowered, no interruption of song), grant **advantage on Persuasion** checks made here with Enclave faithful for the next hour.

**The Harmony Trial:** A PC who joins the Singing Circle may attempt a **Performance (DC 13)**.

*   Success: the circle accepts them; one elf offers a discreet rumor or a small blessing (guidance once, nonmagical “social”).
*   Failure: no shame—only distance; subsequent Deception checks here are made with **disadvantage** as watchers “hear the strain.”

**Resheph’s River-Warning:** If the PCs earn Resheph’s regard, he can send an animal-runner or river-sign within a day (a nonmagical “alarm” that helps you justify timely warnings).

* * *

#### Conclusion

The Ash Horn Stream religious meeting area stands as a beacon of elven faith and unity in Verbobonc. Under the guidance of Resheph and the Council of Elders, this sacred sanctuary preserves the rich traditions of Corellon Larethian while promoting harmony and coexistence within the broader city. Through its history, facilities, and notable people, the Ash Horn Stream continues to inspire the elves of Verbobonc in their devotion to the Protector.

### People of the Stream

##### Laeroth Skyseer

(Owner in Trust; High Councilor)

Laeroth’s name is attached to D7 not as a “property claim,” but as a pledge: he guarantees the sanctity of the site, and he uses it as a quiet instrument of unity—within the Enclave, and between the Enclave and the city’s powers.

**DM Portrayal:** diplomatic, star-wise, patient—yet never naïve. He understands that holy places are also _levers_. Laeroth Skyseer

##### Resheph

(Rite-Leader; Druid of Beory; Counsel to Enclave)

Resheph is the watchman of the **Pond of the Hart** and a key steward of the Ash Horn’s sanctity. He is a human druid rarely seen in his own form, bound by rumor (and likely truth) to an accord with the Viscount: city growth without woodland harm, in exchange for his living early-warning network through beasts and river-creatures.

In Ash Horn rites, Resheph’s presence signals something important: the Enclave’s faith is not isolation—it is **custodianship**, shared.

**Dialogue  
Resheph:** “Hush, now. The water hearkeneth. Cast thy petal as thou wouldst cast thy promise—whole, and without barbs.”

##### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (12)

1.  **Petals against the current:** Once each season, a petal drifts _back_ toward the Pond of the Hart—Resheph calls it warning, not miracle.
2.  **The “quiet tally”:** Laeroth’s scribes record who attends the rites—attendance becomes a map of loyalty.
3.  **A pact whispered, not signed:** City laborers claim the Viscount’s men were turned away from woodland works after a single druidic sign; no one admits who gave the order.
4.  **A drowned token:** Something stamped with a strange sigil was found snagged beneath the Flower Altar stone (Temple courier? rival guild? bait).
5.  **The harmony test:** An elf of Celene judges “outsiders” by whether they can hold one note in the Singing Circle without forcing it.
6.  **The hilltop watcher:** A cloaked figure returns at dusk to the Overlook and marks routes on a slate—Enclave scout or enemy mapper?
7.  **Resheph’s river-eyes:** Fisherfolk say the fish “turn” when Resheph wills it—an underwater alarm that could catch smugglers or assassins.
8.  **The Hart’s tear:** Some druids insist the Pond of the Hart is more than holy—its waters _remember_ faces.
9.  **A green-glass lantern missing:** One of the Enclave’s ward-lights is gone; the next procession feels watched by the wrong eyes.
10.  **The Celene thread:** A visiting elven dignitary requests a private rite at Ash Horn, hinting at trouble in Celene politics.
11.  **Noble Ambitions seed (579 CY):** A name from later intrigue is quietly spoken here “as a prayer,” implying old knowledge of future storms.
12.  **The stream-mouth meeting:** A messenger from the Knights of the Hart arrives not to the Bastion, but to Ash Horn—because druids and elves can deny a meeting that never “officially” happened.
