The Gift of Beauty — Quest Master Reference
===========================================

#### DM Strategic Overview — Epic Sandbox Campaign Thread

> **Purpose:** This document is the DM master reference for the Gift of Beauty quest thread — the single most politically consequential event in the Verbobonc campaign. It tracks the artifact from creation through theft, current status, and all possible resolutions. It maps every faction that wants the Gift, why they want it, and what happens when the players' choices intersect with faction agendas.

### Part One: The Gift Itself

##### What Is the Gift of Beauty?

The Gift of Beauty is a diplomatic artifact — a masterwork of collaborative craftsmanship created by the common folk of Verbobonc at the request of Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix. It was intended as a gesture of peace and goodwill to Her Fey Majesty, Queen Yolande of Celene.

*   **Physical Description:** A box carved from a single piece of delicate Ipp Wood, taken from a lightning-sundered tree. It measures slightly over 2 feet by 1 foot by 1 foot. The carvings depict the elven father Corellon Larethian seated in a glade with animals and fey folk. The sides tell the stories of major events in elven history. The cover depicts the joining of the races — elves, gnomes, and humans — in the common bond to build and develop Verbobonc. It is a story of cooperation beautifully worked in wood.
    

*   **How It Was Made:** The Viscount tasked the craft guilds of Verbobonc to create a work of extraordinary craftsmanship. The Weaver Guild presented a tapestry. The Silversmiths created a chalice etched with forest animals beneath the Elven Oak Father of the Gnarley. Many independent craftsmen submitted works. An anonymous craftsman's Ipp Wood carving won supreme — chosen above all guild submissions.
    

*   **Blessing:** The winning piece was taken to Archdruid Hrudek Oakheart of the Western Gnarley (Obad-Hai) for his blessings. Once blessed, all the crafts were displayed in the court of Castle Greyfist for nearly a ten-day. Then the Gift was entrusted to Lord Waldgraf Corbin Deleveu for transport south to the border of Celene, where a patrol of Elven Knights would receive it and escort it to Queen Yolande's court at Enstad.
    

*   **Why It Matters:** The Gift is not a weapon or a magical artifact. It is a _symbol_. It represents the possibility of renewed cooperation between Verbobonc and Celene — two nations whose relationship has been strained by Celene's increasing isolationism under Queen Yolande. If delivered, it could warm relations, reopen trade, and strengthen the alliance against the rising threat of the Temple of Elemental Evil. If lost, it embarrasses the Viscount, insults Celene, and proves that Verbobonc cannot even protect a diplomatic package on its own roads.
    

 

### Part Two: The Timeline

##### The Chain of Events

*   **Festival of Readying (Winter Solstice, 576 CY)** Viscount Wilfrick commissions the Gift. Craft guilds compete. The Ipp Wood carving wins.
    

*   **Late Readying** The Archdruid of the Western Gnarley blesses the Gift. It is displayed at Castle Greyfist for a ten-day. The Viscount desires to present it on the High Day of Readying.
    

 

##### Transport Phase

The Gift is entrusted to Lord Corbin Deleveu at his estate (Deleveu Woods), south of Ostverk, near the Celene border. A patrol of Mounted Borderers — the 4th Borderers under Corporal Janic — is assigned to escort the Gift the final 10 miles from Deleveu's estate to the Celene border, where Elven Knights will take custody.

##### The Ambush

On the 10-mile journey from Deleveu's estate to the border, the Borderer patrol is ambushed. The attack force consists of Earth Cult Marauders from the Sacred Stone Monastery: 4 Black Earth Guards, 2 Black Earth Priests, 4 Black Earth Monks, 12 Gnolls, 4 Ogres, and 10 bandits — led by Maris Blackshale. Caidax Nightshade delivers the decisive blow with a lightning bolt that takes down the patrol leader, Lt. Barnes. Gimble Jillink, a gnome recruit, uses Shrink Item to reduce the Gift for transport.

##### Result

Five Borderers dead, one missing (the scout — trusted, the one who had been with the package since it was blessed). Corporal Janic survives — knocked unconscious, assumed dead, found by one of Lord Deleveu's tenant farmers. The Gift is taken west into the Kron Hills, then north to the old monastery of [The Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath - Monastery & Tomb of Heironeous](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md).

##### Current Status (576 CY, Campaign Present)

The Gift of Beauty sits in a reinforced stone reliquary in AO-18 — Tharvok Stonehand's Quarters at the Sepulchre-Priory. Triple-locked (Tharvok's key, Romag's key, divine ward). The Earth Temple hierarchy is arguing over what to do with it.

 

### Part Three: Major Factions (Full Breakdowns)

##### FACTION 1: The Earth Temple — Outpost Command

The Earth Temple garrison at the Sepulchre-Priory currently possesses the Gift. But "possession" is not the same as "agreement on what to do with it." The Gift has created a fault line within the Earth Temple command structure.

##### Tharvok Stonehand — War-Priest, Garrison Warden

*   **What he wants:** Destroy the Gift ceremonially. Grind it to dust as a doctrinal demonstration — "Stone outlasts beauty." He sees it as an idol of cooperation between races that the Earth Temple considers fundamentally flawed. Crushing it would be the purest expression of Earth Temple philosophy.
    

*   **Why he can't:** Romag hasn't given the order. Lucius Graeme counsels patience. Tharvok is obedient to the chain of command, so he waits — but his frustration is growing. He's written an unsent letter to Romag requesting guidance, particularly because Lareth the Beautiful has sent an emissary asking questions about the reliquary.
    

*   **His leverage:** He holds the Gift physically. One of the three keys is on a cord around his neck. He controls access. If the command structure fractures, Tharvok defaults to destruction.
    

##### Maris Blackshale — Raid Commander

*   **What he wants:** He doesn't care about the Gift itself. He cares about the operation's continued success. The Gift was a trophy from her greatest raid — the ambush that proved the Earth Temple could strike at will on the Long Road. He'd prefer the Gift be sent to Nulb as tribute, cementing his reputation with Romag and securing more resources for his raiders.
    

*   **His concern:** The Gift attracts attention. Every faction in the region is now looking for it. The longer it stays at the priory, the more likely someone comes looking. He's pragmatic — send it away, get it off her operational territory.
    

##### Varnor Earthcaller — High Priest, Geomancer

*   **What he wants:** He's largely indifferent to the Gift. His obsession is the geomantic node beneath the priory. The Gift is a political object; Varnor deals in cosmic forces. If pressed, he'd support whatever decision gets the argument resolved fastest so the leadership can refocus on the excavation.
    

*   **His quiet concern:** The Gift's presence draws heat. If adventurers or Verbobonc forces come to recover it, they may discover the excavation — which Varnor considers far more important than any diplomatic trinket.
    

##### Caidax Nightshade — Arcane Advisor

*   **What he wants:** The Gift has intelligence value. Caidax recognizes that possessing a diplomatic artifact meant for Celene gives the Earth Temple leverage in a game much larger than raiding caravans. He's the only person at the priory who thinks in terms of _political capital_. He'd advocate holding it as a bargaining chip — or, if the right buyer could be found, selling it for favors from a faction the Earth Temple wants to court.
    

*   **His angle:** Caidax is also running the false-flag operation (AO-25) and the intelligence network (AO-13). He sees the Gift as one piece on a much larger board. His letter to Slikkina Jillink (found in AO-13) already demonstrates he thinks in terms of multi-faction manipulation.

##### FACTION 2: The Temple of Elemental Evil — Greater Hierarchy

The outpost is a forward position. The real power sits in Nulb and the Greater Temple. The Gift's theft has sent ripples up the chain of command.

##### Romag — Earth Temple Prophet

*   **What he wants:** Prestige. The Gift's capture elevated Romag's standing among the four elemental factions. He wants to exploit it — but hasn't decided how. His instinct is to hold it as proof of Earth Temple dominance over the other cults. He may present it to Hedrack as a demonstration of the Earth Temple's operational superiority.
    

*   **His problem:** The other temple factions are maneuvering. Lareth the Beautiful has already sent an emissary to the priory. If Romag doesn't act, the Gift may be claimed by a rival — and he'll lose the prestige it represents.
    

*   **Romag's key:** The second lock on the reliquary requires Romag's key, which is NOT at the priory. It's at the Greater Temple. This means even Tharvok cannot open the reliquary unilaterally — Romag maintains control at a distance.
    

##### Lucius Graeme — Temple Agent, Spy Coordinator

*   **What he wants:** Maximum chaos, minimum exposure. Lucius serves Hedrack directly (and secretly, the Priesthood of Iuz). He's the one counseling patience to Tharvok — not because he cares about the Gift's survival, but because the _argument over the Gift_ is more useful than any resolution. As long as the Earth Temple leadership is distracted by the Gift, they're not paying attention to Lucius's real operations.
    

*   **His true agenda:** Lucius is a double agent. He reports to Hedrack, but his ultimate loyalty is to Iuz. The Gift's theft has created exactly the kind of inter-faction tension that serves Iuz's interests. If the Gift can be used to provoke conflict between the Earth Temple and other factions (particularly Lareth/the agents of Lolth), Lucius benefits.
    

*   **His role at the priory:** Lucius carries a wax impression of Romag's key but not the key itself. He delivers sealed orders, assesses operations, and monitors loyalty. He's the one who told Tharvok to wait.
    

##### Lakash Quallad — TOEE Agent in Verbobonc

*   **What he wants:** The Gift's disappearance serves his mission perfectly. Lakash Krivaltis (his true name) is operating inside Verbobonc to fracture the human-gnome alliance and destabilize the Viscounty. The missing Gift embarrasses the Viscount, strains the relationship with Celene, and fuels rumors that Verbobonc cannot protect its own roads. Lakash doesn't need the Gift recovered — he needs it to _stay missing_.
    

*   **His operations:** From his position as a wealthy merchant in Verbobonc's High Quarter, Lakash spreads rumors, sponsors gnome artisans (to build trust he can later betray), and works to infiltrate the Senate and Chamber of 13. The Gift's theft gives him ammunition: "The Viscount can't even deliver a box of wood to the elves."
    

##### Hedrack — Supreme Leader of the TOEE

*   **What he wants:** Control. Hedrack views the Gift as a minor operational success that has become a major distraction. He wants the situation resolved — preferably in a way that demonstrates the Temple's power without drawing the wrong kind of attention. He may order the Gift moved to the Greater Temple for safekeeping, or he may authorize its destruction if it becomes a liability.
    

*   **His concern:** The Gift connects the Long Road raids to the Greater Temple's operations. If the wrong people recover it from the priory, the trail leads to Nulb.

##### FACTION 3: Lareth the Beautiful — Rival Temple Operative

Lareth operates from the Moathouse as a field commander with connections to agents of Lolth and his own ambitions within the Greater Temple structure. He learned of the Gift's capture and immediately began maneuvering. Lareth is not passive — he is running multiple parallel schemes to get the Gift, and the DM should choose which (or how many) are active in their campaign.

 

**What he wants:** The Gift — as a trophy, a bargaining chip, or a demonstration that the Earth Temple cannot secure its own prizes. Lareth's rivalry with Tharvok is personal and doctrinal. Lareth considers the Earth Temple plodding and predictable. Seizing the Gift from under Tharvok's nose would be the ultimate humiliation.

 

##### _What he's done — and is still doing_:

##### Attempt 1 — The Laughing Dog Inn (Failed).

Lareth's first play was to use Varek the Smiler (Smiley) and his crew at [The Laughing Dog Inn](the-laughing-dog-inn-landmark.md) (located 10 miles south of [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), 10 miles north of Ostverk — perfectly positioned on the transport route). Varek's people were supposed to intercept the Gift. They bungled it. The Earth Temple's professional raiders got there first. Lareth considers this a humiliating failure — Varek's incompetence cost him the initiative.

*   **Critical Complication — Cookie and the Fire Temple.** The Laughing Dog's cook, known as "Cookie," is not what he seems. He is a Fire Cult priest — an agent of [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md), High Priest of the Fire Temple. Cookie has been using _carrier pigeons_ spells to tip off his contacts down south about movements along the road. This means the Fire Temple has been receiving intelligence about the Gift's journey, the ambush aftermath, and potentially about any recovery attempts. Cookie's presence at the Laughing Dog means Lareth's operation has been compromised from the inside — and Lareth doesn't know it. If the Fire Temple decides the Gift is worth pursuing, Alrrem has an agent already in position.
    

 

##### Attempt 2 — The Emissary (Rejected).

Lareth sent an emissary to the priory three tendays ago to inspect the reliquary. Tharvok refused access and escorted the emissary out under guard. Direct approach failed. Lareth hasn't escalated through official channels — yet. If he obtains Greater Temple authority (through Hedrack or other channels), he may send a formal claim.

##### Attempt 3 — Using the Players as Tools.

This is Lareth's most dangerous and most elegant scheme. Lareth approaches the party — either directly or through an intermediary — and presents himself as a representative of the Viscounty or another legitimate authority. The party has not seen his face or heard him speak (he's careful about that). He points them toward the Sepulchre-Priory, tells them the Gift is there, and asks them to recover it. He may even provide accurate intelligence about the priory's defenses.

 

*   The beauty of this scheme: **every outcome works for Lareth.** If the party succeeds and brings the Gift back to him, he has it. If the Earth Temple kills them, Lareth has cost Tharvok resources and attention without risking his own people. If the party recovers the Gift but gets suspicious and doesn't return it to Lareth, he's still weakened the Earth Temple's grip on the priory — and he can pursue the Gift through other channels later.
    

##### Attempt 4 — The Bandit "Assistance."

In another variation, Lareth tasks the players directly (after they've been recruited or manipulated into working with him) to raid the priory and recover the Gift. He offers to send [Gattas Void](gattas-void-person.md) and his bandits as "help." The bandits have their own orders — once the chest is in hand, they may decide the players are no longer necessary. The question the party must answer: do Gattas Void's men serve the players' mission, or Lareth's?The answer is always Lareth's.

##### DM Decision Points for Lareth:

*   Does Lareth approach the party before or after they've already found the priory?
*   Does the party realize they're being used? If so, do they play along to learn Lareth's secrets?
*   One tested campaign scenario: a party **pretended to befriend Lareth** and work for him, hoping to discover the TOEE's plans and leverage the factions against each other — exactly the kind of player-driven scheming the TOEE Leadership and Recruitment articles suggest is possible. This is a viable and encouraged sandbox path.
*   Does Cookie's intelligence reach Alrrem before or during the party's recovery attempt? Does the Fire Temple send its own team?

##### FACTION 3b: The Fire Temple — The Unseen Hand

The Fire Temple is not directly involved in the Gift's theft or current custody. But they are _watching_ — and they have an asset in position.

##### Alrrem — High Priest of the Fire Temple

*   **What he wants:** Anything that humiliates the Earth Temple and elevates the Fire Temple's standing with Hedrack. The Gift is an opportunity. If Alrrem can arrange for the Fire Temple to recover the Gift after the Earth Temple loses it, he proves that Romag's people cannot hold what they steal.
    

*   **His asset:** Cookie at the Laughing Dog Inn. Cookie reports via _carrier pigeons_ — short messages, limited detail, but enough to track major movements along the road. Cookie knows when adventurers pass through heading south. He knows when Earth Temple supply runs come through. He may know when the party is heading to or from the priory.
    

*   **DM Decision Point:** The Fire Temple is a slow-burn threat. They may not act during the priory quest itself — but if the party recovers the Gift and travels back north along the Long Road, passing through Laughing Dog territory, Cookie's intelligence could set up a Fire Temple ambush. The party thinks the hard part is over. It isn't.

##### FACTION 4: The Viscounty of Verbobonc — Political Establishment

The Gift's disappearance is a political crisis. The Viscount commissioned it. The common folk made it. Its loss is felt across the entire Viscounty.

##### Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix

*   **What he wants:** The Gift recovered quietly. The longer it stays missing, the more it damages his reputation and the Viscounty's standing with Celene. But he cannot mobilize openly — if word spreads that the Gift was stolen by agents of the Temple of Elemental Evil, it confirms that Verbobonc's roads are not safe and that the Temple's reach extends into the heartland. This would cause panic.
    

*   **His approach:** Use deniable assets. Lord Deleveu is the cutout — trusted, loyal, positioned near the border. Adventurers who recover the Gift are not acting in the Viscount's name; they're "merely assisting for whatever reasons they deem fit." Plausible deniability.
    

##### Patriarch Martiolus Legeum — High Priest of the Church of Rao (Verbobonc Diocese)

*   **Who he is:** Patriarch Martiolus Legeum is the Archbishop of Rao in Verbobonc, residing at the A1 Monastery of the Reverent Brothers Rao in the High Quarter. An aging Flannae cleric of considerable political influence, Martiolus maintains a strategic alliance with Viscount Wilfrick and serves as mediator within the Council of Lords. He is a declining but steadfast voice for peace and reason — a man who has spent decades cultivating a society governed by diplomacy rather than force.
    

*   **What he wants:** Peace and order. The Church of Rao has been working diplomatic channels to maintain the Celene relationship. The Gift's loss threatens those efforts. Martiolus supports recovery but counsels against violence or provocation that might escalate tensions with Celene. He may independently send agents — clerical diplomats — to make contact with Celene's court and assure them the Gift will be found.
    

 

**The NSQ-05 Connection — "On a Mission from Rao":** Martiolus is directly connected to quest NSQ-05: On a Mission from Rao. A junior priestess of Rao from Veluna named **Seranna Valeth** has arrived at the Monastery of the Reverent Brothers bearing a divine vision — Rao himself has tasked her to recover the lost Chest of Rao and find a missing priest named **Artus Kellan**. Artus was part of the Velunan diplomatic delegation that was ambushed — the same delegation that carried the Gift of Beauty. He survived the attack but was captured, tortured by [Howling Moon Clan](howling-moon-clan-organization.md) Orcs, and left with a feeblemind-like magical affliction. He is currently in the care of Lady Elinor Asbury at Asbury Manor, speaking only in chanted riddles and fragments. Artus Kellan is a living witness to the ambush — if his mind can be restored, he knows what happened to the delegation, the Gift, and the attackers' identities. Seranna's divinely guided search for Artus creates a parallel quest thread that intersects directly with the Gift of Beauty recovery.

 

**Church of Rao resources:** The Order of Rao is a significant political force in Verbobonc. They have access to divination magic, diplomatic networks, and relationships with Veluna's religious establishment. Patriarch Martiolus can mobilize the Monastery's resources — including clerical agents, healing magic for Artus Kellan's affliction, and political cover for the party's operations. If the party recovers the Gift, the Church of Rao may offer to consecrate their success as a divine act of peace.

##### The Council of Lords

**What they want:** It depends on the lord. Some want the Gift recovered to preserve the Viscounty's honor. Others see the situation as leverage against the Viscount — proof that Wilfrick's diplomatic ambitions exceed his ability to protect them. House Langmuir (the "Iron Lady" Godeleva) is particularly likely to exploit the situation politically.

##### FACTION 5: The Archclericy of Veluna — The Rao Delegation

[Veluna](veluna-organization.md), the theocratic archclericy to the west, was involved in the diplomatic effort. The Gift was part of a broader peace initiative that included Velunan ambassadors.

*   **What Veluna wants:** The Gift delivered. Veluna's relationship with Celene is critical to their own security — the elven kingdom provides a buffer against threats from the south and west. Canon Hazen and the Velunan church hierarchy see the Gift as a symbol of a multi-nation alliance against the rising darkness. Its loss damages Veluna's diplomatic credibility as much as Verbobonc's.
    

*   **The Rao Delegation:** A diplomatic convoy that traveled through Kron Hills territory. Their route was tracked by the Earth Temple (red thread on Tharvok's war table in AO-18, flagged in the AO-16 scriptorium intelligence). Romag's Reavers hit the delegation, taking the Gift of Beauty and capturing Rao priest prisoners — who were transported to the Sepulchre-Priory along with the Gift. The captured priests represent an additional diplomatic catastrophe: foreign clergy held by a cult garrison on Verbobonc soil.
    

*   **Artus Kellan — The Surviving Witness:** One priest from the delegation survived the ambush: **Artus Kellan**, a priest of Rao from Veluna. He was captured, tortured by Howling Moon Orcs, and struck by fell magic that left him with a feeblemind-like affliction. He eventually ended up in the care of Lady Elinor Asbury at Asbury Manor, where he speaks only in chanted riddles and "Rao ramblings." Artus is a living witness — if his mind can be restored (via Greater Restoration or similar magic), he can identify the attackers, describe the ambush in detail, and confirm what was taken. His existence is known to Patriarch Martiolus Legeum in Verbobonc and to priestess Seranna Valeth, who has been divinely tasked by Rao to find him (see NSQ-05: On a Mission from Rao). The party may encounter Artus through the Gift quest, through NSQ-05, or through Lady Asbury's connections — another sandbox intersection.
    

*   **The Rao Priest Prisoners:** Other captured Rao priests may be held at the priory (in AO-20 alongside [Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md) and other prisoners) or may have already been moved to Nulb. Their fate is a DM decision that shapes the campaign's urgency. If still at the priory, rescuing them alongside Halven Sere dramatically raises the stakes — and the gratitude of Veluna's church hierarchy. If moved to Nulb, their rescue becomes a separate quest thread that extends the campaign into the Greater Temple's territory.
    

 

**DM Decision Point:** Does Veluna send its own recovery team? If the party takes too long, a Velunan clerical mission — well-meaning but politically explosive — may arrive in Verbobonc and force the issue into the open. The presence of captured Rao priests adds urgency: Veluna is not just asking about a missing artifact, they're asking about missing _people_.

##### FACTION 6: Lord Corbin Deleveu & the Mounted Borderers

Corbin Deleveu is the quest giver — the man who was entrusted with the Gift and lost it on his watch.

##### Lord Corbin Deleveu — Waldgraf, Knight Captain of the Borderers

*   **What he wants:** Redemption. The Gift was lost under his protection. His honor, his family's name, and his position as Warden of the South are all at stake. He cannot mobilize the Borderers openly (the loss must be kept quiet), but he will use every resource at his disposal to recover it.
    

*   **His assets:** Deleveu Manor (the quest base), his Mounted Borderer company (the 4th), his relationships with the Gnarley Rangers and the elves, and his half-elven heritage which gives him credibility with Celene. Captain Marcones Carrio commands the Borderers stationed at the manor.
    

*   **His approach:** Deleveu recruits the party through Wyn (the ranger contact) and through the wooden coin system — deniable, off-the-books, but backed by a nobleman's resources. He provides shelter, intelligence (Corporal Janic's testimony, a map to the ambush site), and a legitimate reason to operate in dangerous territory.
    

*   **What he knows:** The ambush was professional — flanking archers, synchronized charge, magical support (lightning bolt). Not bandits. The attackers included Black Earth cultists, Sacred Stone monks, gnolls, ogres, and bandits — a mixed force that suggests a well-resourced operation. The Gift was shrunk by a gnome (Gimble Jillink) and carried away on a game trail heading west into the Lortmils.

##### Part Four: Minor Factions (Overview)

##### The Gnarley Rangers

**Interest:** The Rangers patrol the forest routes that connect Verbobonc to the southern border. Wyn, a Senior Ranger Knight, is the initial quest contact — he approaches a PC with rumors of the Gift's disappearance and points them toward Deleveu. The Rangers' concern is practical: the ambush proves organized raiding on a scale that threatens forest security. They want the raiders found and eliminated.

*   **Key NPC:** Wyn. Serves under Knight Ranger Owain. Loyal to the Viscount, trusted by Deleveu.
    

##### The Druids of the Gnarley Forest — Archdruid Hrudek Oakheart

**Interest:** Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) of the Western Gnarley personally blessed the Gift of Beauty in the name of Obad-Hai. Its theft is a desecration of that blessing. Hrudek is a weathered, imposing figure in a grey gambeson and green cloak covered in moss and vines — a mixed Oeridian-Flannae druid of dark, piercing eyes, always accompanied by his two brown bear companions Thandra and Borom. He maintains a network of scouts and informants throughout the western forest, and he is already concerned about rising Cult of the Elder Elemental Eye influence and Jaroo Ashstaff's missing reports from Hommlet. The Gift's theft by Earth Temple agents confirms his worst fears about organized cult activity on the Long Road.

 

**His resources:** Hrudek operates from an ancient ruined temple deep within the Gnarley. He commands the respect of the druid hierarchy — the Great Druid [Naimur](naimur-person.md)(a gnome elder of over 150 years) and Great Druid [Hildefer Paravis](hildefer-paravis-person.md) of the eastern region both share his concern about elemental cult incursion. The druids may offer aid (information, safe passage, healing) to parties working to recover the Gift. Hrudek's personal investment in the Gift — he blessed it — means he takes its recovery as a spiritual obligation, not merely a political favor.

*   **Organization:** The druids are guardians of the forest, few in number but deeply connected to the land. They work alongside the Rangers and the elves.
    

##### The Elves of the Gnarley Forest

**Interest:** The Gnarley elves are distinct from the elves of Celene, but they share cultural ties. The Gift was crafted with elven themes (Corellon Larethian) and was meant to honor the elven queen. The Gnarley elves view its theft as an insult to their heritage. They may provide intelligence on movements through the forest — the elves see things that other factions miss.

##### The Free Alliance of the Kron Hills

**Interest:** The gnome political body is already under strain from Lakash Quallad's destabilization efforts. The Gift's loss adds fuel to the fire — humans can't even protect a diplomatic artifact, so how can they protect gnome trade caravans? The Alliance is politically divided: some gnome leaders want closer ties with Verbobonc, others want independence. The Gift's fate may tip that balance.

*   **Key connection:** Clan Jillink is deeply entangled. Gimble Jillink (Earth Temple recruit) participated in the ambush. Slikkina Jillink (clan leader in Tulvar) is in correspondence with Caidax Nightshade. The gnome political thread runs through the Gift quest like a vein of ore through stone.
    

##### Celene — Queen Yolande's Court

**Interest:** The intended recipient. Celene's court is aware that a diplomatic gift was promised. Its non-arrival is noted — and interpreted through the lens of Celene's existing isolationism. Some at court view it as proof that human nations cannot be trusted. Others (the more moderate faction) recognize that the theft was an act of sabotage and extend grace. The Gift's recovery and delivery could strengthen the moderates. Its permanent loss empowers the isolationists.

 

**The Elven Knights:** A patrol of Celene's Knights of the Royal Court was waiting at the border to receive the Gift. They returned to Enstad empty-handed. Their report to Queen Yolande shapes Celene's response.

##### House Langmuir — The "Iron Lady"

**Interest:** Godeleva Langmuir, the Iron Lady, is a Verbobonc power player. She views the Gift crisis as a lever against Viscount Wilfrick. If the Gift is recovered, she'll claim credit for supporting the effort. If it stays lost, she'll use it as proof of the Viscount's incompetence. She is an obstacle regardless of the outcome — the party may encounter her entourage on the road (as described in the original adventure: VER4-02 A Costly Gamble encounter).

##### House Milinous

**Interest:** Lord Milinous controls territory south of Verbobonc City. His knights (the Black Griffon patrol) are hostile to gnomes and suspicious of adventurers. The party encounters his patrol on the road south — they've found burnt wagons and are investigating. They are not allies. They are not enemies. They are Verbobonc's internal political complexity made manifest.

#### Part Five: Current Status & the Priory Connection

 

##### Where Is the Gift Right Now?

The Gift of Beauty is in **AO-18 — Tharvok Stonehand's Quarters** at the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath. It is inside a reinforced stone reliquary in a wall alcove, banded with iron, sealed with three locks:

 

*   **Lock 1:** Tharvok's key (iron key on a leather cord around his neck). Thieves' Tools DC 18.
*   **Lock 2:** Romag's key (NOT in the priory — held at the Greater Temple in Nulb). Thieves' Tools DC 18.
*   **Lock 3:** Earth Temple divine ward. Dispel Magic DC 15, or speak the opening invocation (found in Varnor's notes in AO-17).
*   **Brute Force:** AC 20, HP 60, resistance to all damage except radiant. Breaking triggers thunderwave: Con DC 14, 2d8 thunder, pushed 10 ft — audible throughout the north wing.

##### The Evidence Trail

A party that thoroughly explores the priory can piece together the Gift's story from multiple rooms:

 

*   **AO-13 (Caidax's Chamber):** Slikkina Jillink letter, Caidax's notes on the operation, references to the false-flag campaign that used the ambush as cover
*   **AO-16 (Scriptorium):** The wall map showing the Rao Delegation route, the Earth-Due tithe ledger tracking the raid's proceeds, caravan seal press
*   **AO-17 (Varnor's Quarters):** Romag's letters referencing "the asset in the cells" (Halven Sere — the priory's captured custodian who may have witnessed the Gift's arrival)
*   **AO-18 (Tharvok's Quarters):** The Gift itself, Tharvok's unsent letter to Romag (detailing the Lareth emissary incident), the war table showing the ambush route, tribute strongbox with Romag's demands

 

#### Part Six: DM Decision Points & Possible Outcomes

The Gift of Beauty is a _sandbox catalyst_. The party's choices — and the DM's decisions about faction timing — determine which of many possible outcomes unfolds. Here are the major scenarios:

##### Scenario A: The Party Recovers the Gift from the Priory

The most likely outcome if the party clears or infiltrates AO-18. They now possess the most politically valuable object in southern Verbobonc.

 

**Sub-decisions:**

*   **Return to Deleveu:** The safe, intended outcome. Lord Deleveu arranges handoff to the Celene Knights. The party earns the Coin of Friendship, Deleveu's gratitude, and a political favor from the Viscount (quietly — plausible deniability).
*   **Return to Verbobonc directly:** Bigger political splash. The party is celebrated but also _exposed_ — every faction now knows who recovered the Gift and who had it. Lakash Quallad takes note.
*   **Deliver to Celene personally:** Bold. The party crosses into elven territory. If successful, they earn direct favor from Queen Yolande's court — a rare honor for non-elves. But they also bypass Verbobonc's political establishment, which some lords may resent.
*   **Keep it / sell it:** The nuclear option. The Gift is priceless in terms of political capital but has no inherent magical power. Attempting to sell it would attract every faction's attention. Keeping it makes the party a target.

##### Scenario B: The Gift Is Destroyed

Tharvok crushes it (if given enough time without orders), or the party breaks the reliquary carelessly, or combat in AO-18 damages it beyond recovery.

 

**Consequences:** The diplomatic initiative dies. Celene's isolationists gain power. Verbobonc's reputation suffers. The Viscount loses face. Lakash Quallad celebrates. The party may recover the _pieces_ — which still have symbolic value if returned ("The Gift was destroyed by the enemies of both our nations — but we brought back what remains").

##### Scenario C: Lareth Manipulates the Party

Lareth approaches the party — disguised as a Viscounty representative or other legitimate authority — and points them at the priory. The party may not realize they're being used until after they've recovered the Gift and handed it to the wrong person. See Faction 3 for the full breakdown of Lareth's multiple schemes.

 

**Sub-variations:**

*   **Party recovers Gift, gives it to "the Viscounty agent":** Lareth disappears with it. The party eventually learns they've been duped. Now they must track Lareth.
*   **Party gets suspicious, keeps the Gift:** They've still weakened the Earth Temple — Lareth considers this acceptable. He pursues the Gift through other channels.
*   **Party pretends to work for Lareth:** The bold play. They feed Lareth intelligence while actually planning to recover the Gift for themselves or the real authorities. This is a viable sandbox path — the TOEE Leadership articles explicitly support leveraging factions against each other. The party becomes a double agent operating inside the Temple's own faction war.
*   **Lareth sends bugbears as "help":** The party accepts Lareth's offer of muscle. The bugbears fight alongside the party through the priory — until the Gift is in hand. Then the bugbears' real orders activate. Who controls the chest?

##### Scenario D: Lareth's Direct Seizure

If the party takes too long, or if the DM decides to escalate, Lareth sends his own agents to the priory independently. This could happen while the party is present — creating a three-way conflict (party vs. Earth Temple vs. Lareth's agents).

 

**Consequences:** The Gift disappears into a completely different faction's hands. Recovery becomes a second quest — now the party must track Lareth's agents to the Moathouse or beyond.

##### Scenario E: The Gift Reaches Nulb

If the priory's situation resolves without party intervention, Tharvok eventually ships the Gift to the Greater Temple as tribute. Recovery now requires penetrating Nulb itself — a dramatically harder proposition.

##### Scenario F: Veluna Intervenes

If the diplomatic clock runs out, Veluna sends its own recovery mission. Well-intentioned but politically explosive — foreign clerics operating on Verbobonc soil without the Viscount's permission. The party may encounter the Velunan team and need to decide: cooperate, compete, or stay out of the way.

##### Scenario H: Fire Temple Ambush on the Return

The party recovers the Gift from the priory. They think the hard part is over. They travel north along the Long Road, pass through or near the Laughing Dog Inn. Cookie has already sent word via carrier pigeon. A Fire Temple strike team — different in composition and tactics from the Earth Temple forces the party just fought — hits them on the road.

 

**Consequences:** The party is exhausted from the priory. They're carrying the Gift in the open. The Fire Temple team is fresh. This is the cruelest twist because the party believed the quest was ending. Recovery from this requires the party to fight a faction they may not even know exists yet.

##### Scenario I: The Gnome Thread Explodes

If evidence from the priory (the false-flag trunk in AO-25, the Slikkina letter in AO-13, the Earth-Due ledger in AO-16) reaches the Free Alliance of the Kron Hills, the gnome political situation shifts dramatically. The Gift quest intersects with the destabilization campaign — recovering the Gift AND exposing the false-flag operation would be a double blow to the Temple of Elemental Evil's regional strategy.

### Part Seven-B: Table-Tested Scenarios (DM Play Reports)

The following scenarios have been tested at actual tables running this campaign. They demonstrate the sandbox's flexibility and should be considered canon-viable approaches.

##### Tested Scenario 1: Lareth the False Viscounty Agent

**Setup:** The party is in Hommlet. They've never seen Lareth's face or heard his voice. Lareth approaches them posing as a representative of the Viscounty, claiming he's been authorized to recruit adventurers to recover the Gift. He provides accurate intelligence — points them toward the Sepulchre-Priory (which he describes as a "Heironeous stronghold occupied by cultists").

 

**Why it works:** The party has no reason to doubt him. The mission sounds legitimate. Lareth's information is _true_ — the priory IS occupied by Earth Temple cultists, the Gift IS there. The lie is only about who Lareth is and why he wants the Gift recovered.

 

**Outcome branches:** Earth Temple kills the party (Lareth loses nothing), or the party recovers the Gift and returns it to "the Viscounty agent" (Lareth gets what he wants). Either way, Lareth wins. The only path Lareth loses is if the party succeeds AND gets suspicious AND refuses to hand it over.

##### Tested Scenario 2: Playing Lareth's Game from the Inside

**Setup:** A party deliberately befriends Lareth and agrees to work for him — not because they trust him, but because they want to learn the TOEE's plans and leverage the factions against each other. This is explicitly supported by the TOEE Leadership and Recruitment articles.

 

**How it plays:** Lareth tells the party about Romag's Reavers, the raid on the Rao Delegation, the Gift's location at the priory, and the Earth Temple's garrison. He tasks them to raid the outpost and bring the Gift to him. He offers bugbear support.

 

**The party's real play:** They're gathering intelligence — names, locations, faction rivalries, command structures. Every conversation with Lareth is an intelligence briefing he doesn't realize he's giving. The party plans to recover the Gift but deliver it to the real authorities — or use it as leverage themselves.

 

**DM guidance:** This is a high-wire act. The party must manage Lareth's trust (Insight vs. Deception, mutual), the bugbears' loyalty (do they report back to Lareth during the raid?), and the risk of being in too deep if Lareth catches on. Reward player creativity. The TOEE's faction war is designed to be exploited.

##### Tested Scenario 3: The Laughing Dog Complication

**Setup:** Lareth's original plan was to have Varek the Smiler (Smiley) and his crew at the Laughing Dog Inn intercept the Gift during transport. They bungled it — the Earth Temple's professional raiders were faster and more competent. But the Laughing Dog is still a live node in the intelligence web.

 

**The Cookie problem:** Cookie, the inn's cook, is a Fire Cult priest — an agent of Alrrem, High Priest of the Fire Temple. He uses _carrier pigeons_ to report movements to his contacts. This means: (a) the Fire Temple knew about the Gift's transport, (b) the Fire Temple knows about the Earth Temple's successful raid, and (c) the Fire Temple will know about any recovery attempt that passes through Laughing Dog territory.

 

**DM guidance:** Cookie is a slow-burn revelation. The party may stay at the Laughing Dog on their way south. Cookie seems harmless — a cook. But if the party recovers the Gift and passes through again on the way back, Cookie has already sent word. A Fire Temple ambush on the return journey — when the party thinks the quest is over — is devastating and narratively powerful. The danger isn't just getting the Gift out of the priory. It's getting it home.

 

### Part Eight: The Gift and the Broader Campaign

##### Why the Gift of Beauty Is the Campaign's Linchpin

The Gift of Beauty is not a side quest. It is the thread that connects every major faction in the campaign:

 

**Earth Temple:** The theft demonstrates their operational reach. The Gift's presence at the priory proves they can strike at the highest levels of Verbobonc's diplomatic apparatus.

 

**Temple of Elemental Evil:** The inter-faction rivalry over the Gift (Earth vs. Lareth/Lolth) mirrors the greater temple's internal instability. The Gift is a microcosm of the Temple's dysfunction — four factions that should be cooperating, instead competing over a single prize.

 

**Verbobonc politics:** The Gift's loss exposes the Viscounty's vulnerability. It gives Lakash Quallad ammunition. It strains the Viscount's relationship with Celene, the gnomes, and his own lords.

 

**The gnome thread:** The false-flag operation used the Gift ambush as cover for broader destabilization. Recovering the Gift AND exposing the false-flag evidence is the one-two punch that could save the human-gnome alliance.

 

**The priory itself:** The Sepulchre-Priory exists as a dungeon _because_ of the Gift. The Earth Temple occupied the Azure Oath priory to stage Long Road raids — raids that culminated in the Gift's theft. The geomantic node, the captured custodian, the desecrated chapel — all of it exists in the shadow of the Gift's strategic significance.

##### The Sandbox Promise

The beauty of the Gift of Beauty (no pun intended) is that the party can encounter this quest thread from _any direction_:

 

*   Through Wyn's approach in Verbobonc (the original adventure hook)
*   Through the priory itself (discovering the Gift while clearing the dungeon)
*   Through the gnome political thread (investigating the false-flag operation leads to the ambush evidence)
*   Through Nulb (intelligence gathered in the river town points to the priory)
*   Through Veluna/Rao channels (clerical contacts mention the missing delegation gift)
*   Through Lord Deleveu directly (if the party has connections to the southern nobility)

 

No matter which door they enter through, the Gift connects to everything else. That's what makes it the campaign's linchpin.
