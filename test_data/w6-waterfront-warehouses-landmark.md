### The Heartbeat of Commerce in Verbobonc

Along the long stone quays of the Velverdyva, behind the Mercantile Exchange and within shouting distance of the Harbormaster’s tower, stand the cavernous Waterfront Warehouses. These immense stone-and-brick storehouses form the economic lungs of Verbobonc. Goods from Dyvers, the Baklunish west, the Kron Hills, and the southern ports pass through their massive oak doors before entering the city’s markets — or vanishing into quieter ledgers.

Here, commerce is measured not in coins but in weight, volume, and influence. And House Haxx watches carefully.

### A Nexus of Trade and Activity

##### Key Features

*   **Stone and Brick Construction:** The warehouses are long cavernous buildings made of stone and brick, designed to store large quantities of goods.
*   **Barred Windows and Massive Oak Doors:** Each wall offers barred windows, and the front features a pair of massive oak doors with a crane hitch overhead.
*   **Interior Layout:** Inside, the warehouses are one big room with four windows on each wall and a big set of wagon doors on the west side. Tall stacks of crates, piled casks, bales of wool, rolls of fabric, and heaps of baskets are organized in rows running the length of the building.

#### Historical Context and Development

_From Simple Storehouses to Trade Powerhouses_

Originally serving as simple storehouses, the Waterfront Warehouses have evolved into essential structures for Verbobonc's thriving trade network. As the city expanded, so did the need for larger and more secure storage facilities.

* * *

#### **Exterior Description**

The warehouses form a continuous row behind the Mercantile Exchange along the back trade street.

Two of the largest buildings — both nearly the size of the Harbormaster’s Office — dominate the stretch.

*   Stone foundations reinforced for flood tolerance
*   Brick upper walls for expansion flexibility
*   Iron crane beams extending from upper floors
*   Wagon ramps at west and south entries
*   Gentle Tradewinds sigils discreetly marked on some door lintels

Behind them, the city wall rises further inland, reminding all merchants that commerce here exists beneath the authority of Verbobonc’s ruling powers.

* * *

#### **Interior Layout**

Each warehouse interior is a single massive hall.

The stone floor is etched with a management grid — rectangles carved directly into the slab. Each is marked with a letter and number combination.

There is a logbook.

There is always a logbook.

Goods are organized in strict rows:

*   Stacks of labeled crates
*   Casks of ale, oil, or salted fish
*   Bales of wool and dyed fabrics
*   Barrels of grain or imported spice
*   Trade baskets from Kron Hills caravans

A narrow office platform sits elevated near the rear wall where clerks oversee intake and dispatch.

At any given time, hired mercenaries or Hardheads patrol the aisles.

* * *

#### **Economic Role in the City**

These warehouses are the interim lungs of Verbobonc’s trade network.

During spring and summer:

*   River cargo moves quickly through.
*   Ships unload, wagons distribute.

During winter freeze:

*   Entire shipments remain here for months.
*   Merchants hire private guards.
*   Competition becomes territorial.

They are essential for:

*   East-West Velverdyva trade
*   Kron Hills mineral shipments
*   Nigb’s Run agricultural imports
*   Dyvers merchant redistribution

Without these warehouses, Verbobonc would choke economically within weeks.

* * *

#### **House Haxx Influence**

Several warehouses are owned outright by **The Gentle Tradewinds**, House Haxx’s merchant trade organization.

This grants Haxx:

*   Priority storage placement
*   Preferred unloading access
*   Leverage over merchant contracts
*   Embedded tax observation
*   Direct access to shipping manifests

Because Haxx’s Hardheads also collect port taxes, House Haxx sees everything that enters and leaves.

This makes W6 not merely commercial — but political.

##### Gentle Tradewinds Warehouses

_Monopoly and Authority_

[House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md), through [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md) merchant trade organization, owns several of the Waterfront Warehouses. This monopoly allows House Haxx to control a significant portion of the trade in Verbobonc, leveraging their authority to gain an advantage in the market.

##### Key Features

*   **Exclusive Use:** These warehouses are strictly used for Gentle Tradewinds operations, ensuring that House Haxx has priority access to the best storage facilities.
*   **Trade Control:** By owning these warehouses, House Haxx can dictate terms and conditions for storing goods, providing them with a competitive edge.

* * *

#### **Security & Oversight**

*   Two Hardheads posted at major doors
*   Clerks trained in cargo manifest scrutiny
*   Harbormaster liaison visits daily
*   Barzac hounds occasionally sweep interiors
*   Paladin patrols pass through riverfront zones

Merchants are responsible for:

*   Hiring private security for long-term storage
*   Guarding high-value goods overnight
*   Paying tax assessments upon intake

Winter months see tension rise sharply between rival mercenary crews.

* * *

#### **Rumors & Intrigue Hooks**

The Waterfront Warehouses are fertile ground for adventure:

*   Smugglers use false floor markings to hide crates
*   Certain Gentle Tradewinds warehouses receive “priority inspections”
*   Missing manifests suggest inventory adjustments
*   A rival merchant house suspects Haxx manipulation
*   Gnome caravans report unfair placement of their goods
*   Secret tunnels rumored beneath older foundations

These warehouses are a quiet battlefield of ledgers and influence.

* * *

#### Goods and Services

_Comprehensive Storage Solutions_

The Waterfront Warehouses offer a range of services to meet the needs of merchants and traders.

##### Key Services

*   **Bulk Storage:** The warehouses can accommodate large quantities of goods, from agricultural products to manufactured items.
*   **Security:** Each merchant is responsible for their own security, often hiring mercenaries to guard their merchandise.
*   **Management System:** A detailed logbook and etched floor plan ensure that goods are easily located and managed.
