G5 The Brass Rail
=================

### A Cozy Hearth in Gnomesberg

The Brass Rail is Gnomesberg’s communal hearth: a large natural cavern finished with wooden paneling, stone-carved benches and tables, dirt floor, low beams, and a warm haze of smoke and candlelight. It’s sized for gnomes—meaning anyone taller than about 5’5” feels pinched, stooped, and constantly aware of shoulders brushing and elbows bumping.

Most nights it’s a place to drink unknown steaming concoctions and swap rumors. Once per week it becomes a delirious riot of gnomish mischief: a practical joke competition performed on the stage, with the crowd heckling and cheering behind that tiny brass rail like it’s the edge of a battlefield.

*   For your campaign, this is the best location in the quarter to:
*   seed rumors without a lecture
*   introduce recurring gnome NPCs
*   let the party earn gnomish trust through humor and humility catch Temple agents trying (and failing) to blend in

##### Approach The Brass Rail

The passage opens into a broad cavern of warm gloom—wood-paneled walls, an unfinished dirt floor, and a ceiling so low that taller folk must stoop as though paying reverence to the beams. Smoke clings to the air, not unpleasant, but thick with hearth-breath and spiced drink. Every table bears candles; every candle fights the murk and loseth with dignity.  
  
Benches and tables are carved straight from the rock, yet softened with horsehair cushions that make the stone seem almost kind. Above, dozens of small glass globes hang on chains, each one glittering with living light—tiny fireflies, coaxed into lantern-prisons, their glow pulsing like slow laughter.  
  
At the cavern’s heart stands a little stage, fenced round by a brass rail no higher than thy knee. A poet clears their throat; a bard tunes a string. Several dour gnomes stare at thee over steaming mugs as if thou hadst tracked mud upon their grandmother’s rug.

### History and Local Significance

*   **Established Gathering Spot:** Known for being a longstanding favorite among the gnomes of Gnomesberg, The Brass Rail serves as a central hub for socializing and entertainment.
*   **Cultural Hub:** Reflecting the industrious and hospitable spirit of its gnome patrons, this tavern has become a key cultural venue where traditions and local humor flourish.

### The Space (What’s Actually Here)

##### The Main Cavern

*   **Few windows** (if any at all the whole place feels underhill and enclosed.
*   **Dim lighting** from fireplaces, candles, and the hanging firefly globes.
*   **Unfinished dirt floor** that somehow stays packed and level, even in a crowd.
*   **Low beams** everywhere—crowded for tall patrons; perfect for gnomes.
*   **Stone-carved furnishings** with horsehair cushions: comfortable in a stubborn, practical way.

##### The Brass Stage

The stage sits dead center, ringed by the short **brass rail** that gives the tavern its name. This is where:

*   nightly performers hold court (bards, poets, storytellers), and
*   once per week, gnomes host a **practical joke / trickery contest** that turns the room into a laughing storm.

##### The Hearth Line

Several fireplaces create pockets of heat. The place gets **stuffy and hot** as it fills—use that physically:

*   sweating hands
*   fogged mugs
*   flushed faces
*   cramped tempers
*   whispered deals because you must lean close to hear

### The Brass Rail’s Traditions

##### Weekly “Talent” Contest (Gnomish Mischief Night)

Once per week, gnomes attempt to outdo one another with cleverness: illusions, sleight-of-hand, spring-loaded pranks, comedic sabotage, and harmless humiliations—mostly harmless.

**Running it at the table (easy mode):**

*   Each contestant performs a “bit.”
*   The crowd votes by banging mugs and chanting.
*   The winner earns a ridiculous prize and a very real social benefit: **prestige.**

**DM twist:  
**A Temple agent tries to use the contest to smuggle a message, steal a purse, plant evidence, or embarrass a Free Alliance supporter—only to discover gnomes are _professional-grade_ at spotting trickery.

### What Players Come Here For

##### 1) Rumors That Don’t Sound Like Plot Hooks

Gnomes gossip like accountants: calmly, accurately, and with receipts.

*   caravan raids
*   tariff changes
*   suspicious new faces
*   “missing” gnomes who allegedly “moved back to the hills”
*   names that cause the room to go quiet for half a breath

##### 2) Trust Earned Through Humor

Outsiders who can laugh at themselves are welcomed. Outsiders who cannot… get eaten alive by kindness.

##### 3) A Neutral Ground

When gnomes need to meet with humans, halflings, or dwarves without formal ceremony, they do it here—under noise, smoke, and plausible deniability.

### Temple of Elemental Evil & Iuz Cult Hooks

##### 1) The Prank That Isn’t Harmless

During Mischief Night, a “joke” goes wrong: a hidden spring-knife, a poisoned pin, a collapse rig that could crush a performer. The gnomes realize someone tried to hide violence inside humor.

**Clue trail:** a buyer of odd components, a whispered phrase, or a coin minted where it shouldn’t be.

* * *

##### 2) The Dour Gnome at the Corner Table

A gnome sits alone, drinking a steaming concoction and staring at the door—watching for someone who hasn’t arrived. If approached carefully, they reveal:

*   they’ve been threatened,
*   their family was leaned on,
*   and someone wants them to “misplace” a ledger or a key.

Is it a Temple logistics squeeze? An Iuzite intimidation play? A rival guild?

* * *

##### 3) A Bard’s Song Becomes a Message

Gemmir performs a silly verse that contains a sequence of nonsense words. A Free Alliance listener reacts—too quickly. A Temple listener reacts—too slowly.

The party can:

*   catch the signal
*   follow the recipient
*   or deliberately repeat the “nonsense” later to bait a response

* * *

#### Non-Combat Play Tools (How the Brass Rail Runs Best)

*   **Lean-in conversations:** it’s hot, crowded, loud—perfect for whispered deals.
*   **Physical discomfort as pressure:** tall PCs tire, stoop, and get irritable; gnomes remain cozy.
*   **Laughter as camouflage:** a handoff can happen during roaring applause.
*   **Social consequences:** if the party starts a fight here, they don’t just anger a tavern—they anger _Gnomesberg._

* * *

### Scenes You Can Run Immediately

##### Scene 1: The Low-Beam Lesson

A tall PC cracks their head on a beam. The whole room notices. Pamphila offers an ice cloth with a grin.  
If the PC laughs: the room warms.  
If the PC snarls: the room cools.

* * *

##### Scene 2: Mischief Night Challenge

A gnome challenges the party to a “friendly contest” on the stage—nothing dangerous, just clever.

*   A riddle duel
*   A sleight-of-hand gag
*   A harmless illusion prank
*   A “tell a tale that makes the room howl” challenge

Win or lose, the party becomes _known._

* * *

##### Scene 3: The Back Table Bargain

Filibert invites the party to a quieter table near a hearth and asks what they truly want in Gnomesberg:

*   coin?
*   information?
*   protection?
*   introductions?  
    He will provide one… and ask a favor that sounds small but isn’t.

* * *

#### Rewards

*   A **local reputation bump**: advantage on first impressions with gnomes in Gnomesberg (DM-facing).
*   A **name + place + time** for a suspicious meeting connected to Temple/Iuz activity.
*   A **favor token** from Filibert (redeemable for shelter, a discreet introduction, or a warning).
*   A **stage invitation**: perform at Mischief Night and earn access to gnome social circles faster than any bribe.

##### The Brass Rail

*   Type: Under-mound Tavern / Pub / Restaurant
*   District: Gnomesberg — Gnome Quarter
*   Owner: Filibert Tinkerdabble (gnome host and community pillar)
*   Signature Feature: A small central stage with a short brass rail—home to bards, poets, and weekly gnomish “talent” prank contests
*   Vibe: Cozy, smoky, dim, crowded for tall folk, riotous with laughter when the contest begins

* * *

##### Filibert Tinkerdabble (Owner)

A gregarious gnome with deep roots in Gnomesberg and a sincere love of hospitality. Filibert remembers faces, remembers debts, and remembers who spilled ale and did not apologize.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Treats newcomers like cousins until they prove unworthy.
*   Keeps the peace with a smile… and a look that promises consequences.
*   Loves stories; hates bullies; distrusts pomp.

**Secret strength:  
**Filibert is a social switchboard. People come to him to “ask around” without admitting they’re asking around.

* * *

##### Gemmir the Joyful Bard

A staple performer with traveling-minstrel polish and a grin that can talk a room into forgiving itself. He thrives on audience participation and delights in turning suspicious strangers into the punchline—gently.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Plays the crowd like an instrument.
*   Knows _everyone’s_ embarrassing tale.
*   Will sing a song about the party within ten minutes of meeting them.

* * *

##### Pamphila Bracegirdle

(Serving Wench)

Beloved for quick service and quicker wit; she reads tables faster than most guards read trouble. If your party wants rumors, Pamphila is the faucet.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Drops plates, not secrets—unless she chooses.
*   Calls out nonsense with a smile.
*   Has the best memory in the room, and no one realizes it.
