AO-01. The Worn Cobblestone Ascent
==================================

A narrow, ancient cobbled path winds up the ridge from the southern slope. The stones are uneven, cracked, and partially swallowed by moss and hill grass.

This was once a ceremonial approach for knights and mourners. Now it is observed.

##### The Worn Cobblestone Ascent

A narrow cobbled path climbs the ridge ahead, its stones cracked and uneven, swallowed in places by moss and stubborn hill grass. The climb is exposed — no trees, no shelter — only broken retaining walls and leaning iron lantern posts long stripped of their glass.  
  
Each step grinds gravel beneath your boots. The stones are old. Worn smooth at their centers from centuries of pilgrimage. Along several blocks, you notice faint carvings — lightning bolts, nearly erased by weather and time.  
  
The path bends twice as it rises. With every turn, the valley below falls further away. Above, the ruined priory waits in silence. And though the wind howls along the ridge, you cannot shake the feeling that something watches from the broken stone above.

### Description

*   10-foot-wide stone path
*   Switchbacks twice along the ridge
*   Low retaining walls on outer edges (crumbling)
*   Broken lantern posts every 40 feet
*   Faded carved lightning bolt symbols along certain stones

The final 60 feet approach the main gate directly and offer **no natural cover**.

* * *

#### Current Earth Temple Modifications

*   Loose stones deliberately left unstable (difficult terrain)
*   Hidden observation points carved into rock face
*   Gnoll sentries frequently rotate patrol here at night
*   Mold Earth used to create shallow defensive dips off-path

* * *

#### DM Notes

*   Guards observe from upper ruin battlements
*   Any approach during daylight likely noticed automatically
*   Night approach requires Stealth vs passive perception of patrolling gnolls
*   If alarm triggered: Black Earth Guards assemble within 2 rounds

* * *

#### Sidebar: Signs Along the Ascent

##### Tracking & Evidence of Earth Temple Activity

The cobblestone path may appear ancient and wind-worn, but it is not dormant. This ridge has seen recent traffic — disciplined, repeated, and heavy.

A ranger, druid, or any character trained in wilderness tracking will immediately notice that the stones tell a story.

Though cobblestone is difficult terrain for tracking, loose gravel, moss displacement, and erosion along the edges preserve enough detail for careful observers.

* * *

##### Tracking Mechanics (5e)

**Base Check:  
**Wisdom (Survival) to track movement along stone and disturbed gravel.

**Base DC:** 14  
(Difficult surface: +2 to normal tracking DC)

Characters with favored terrain (hills) or Natural Explorer have advantage.

If searching specifically for signs of conflict or cargo movement:  
Intelligence (Investigation) DC 13

* * *

#### Success Results

* * *

##### DC 14 – General Activity

You find clear signs this road is frequently used.

**Read Aloud:**

Kneeling near the edge of the cobbles, you notice that the moss between stones has been torn free in several places. Loose gravel along the retaining wall has been recently displaced. The traffic here is not ancient. Something moves up and down this path regularly — and not lightly.

**Reveals:**

*   Repeated foot traffic within the last 2–3 weeks
*   Movement both ascending and descending
*   At least a dozen individuals in rotation

* * *

##### DC 16 – Mixed Forces Identified

Closer inspection reveals different types of prints and weight patterns.

**Read Aloud:**

Along the dirt margins beside the stone, you find impressions too wide and too clawed to be human. The stride is long. The pressure deep. Heavy bodies.  
  
Intermixed are boot prints — some disciplined, heel-to-toe and measured. Others irregular… impatient… poorly trained. This is not a simple bandit path. This is an organized force.

**Reveals:**

*   Gnoll tracks (large clawed feet, 3-toed foreprint)
*   Human boot patterns
*   At least 8–10 gnolls use this ascent
*   Several armored individuals (deep heel impressions)

* * *

##### DC 18 – Wagon & Captive Evidence

The most telling signs lie off the path.

**Read Aloud:**

Off the southern bend of the switchback, you find ruts pressed into softer soil beside the cobbles — too narrow for a farm cart, too deep for a single wagon.  
  
The ground here shows signs of struggle. A snapped leather strap. A broken horseshoe nail. Drag marks leading uphill.  
  
And here — a faint, dried stain darkened in the cracks of stone. A caravan did not pass here freely. It was brought.

**Reveals:**

*   Merchant wagon wheels forced off road
*   Horses driven uphill (likely captured)
*   Signs of prisoners dragged or forced to march
*   Blood stains at least several days old

* * *

##### DC 20+ – Tactical Use & Patrol Pattern

A highly skilled tracker notices operational structure.

**Read Aloud:**

The gnoll tracks cluster along the western rocks — always high ground. The heavier boot prints remain central on the path. Smaller, lighter prints patrol in pairs, then return to the gate.  
  
This isn’t chaos. It’s perimeter control. Whoever holds the priory uses this ascent deliberately — layered defense, rotating patrols, and forced prisoner movement uphill.

**Reveals:**

*   Rotating sentry pattern
*   Gnolls favor elevated western ridge
*   Black Earth Guards remain central and disciplined
*   Prisoners likely taken alive initially
*   Organized, not opportunistic raiding

* * *

#### Additional Optional Checks

*   **Nature DC 15:** Recognize gnoll scent trails — musk and carrion odor in thorn brush.
*   **Perception DC 15 (at dusk/night):** Spot signal horn notch carved into stone near second switchback.
*   **Arcana or Religion DC 14:** Notice faint desecration of lightning bolt carvings along path.

#### DM Guidance

This tracking moment establishes:

*   The Earth Temple is organized.
*   Gnolls are active participants.
*   Caravans are captured, not simply robbed.
*   Prisoners are taken alive.
*   The ascent is both symbolic and tactical.

If players discover wagon evidence, you may connect it to:

*   The “earth-tithe” rumor
*   Missing caravan quest hooks
*   The Gift of Beauty interception
*   A captured NPC later found in the holding cells

##### **The Worn Cobblestone Ascent**

*   **Type:** Exterior Approach Route / Monastery Access Path
*   **Original Use:** Pilgrim and supply road leading to the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath
*   **Current Use:** Raider access route used by Black Earth cultists returning from raids across the Kron Hills and Long Road
*   **Terrain:** Steep, winding cobblestone trail climbing the ridge toward the monastery gatehouse
*   **Surface Condition:** Cracked and uneven stones; many displaced by erosion and neglect
*   **Visibility:** Narrow approach with limited maneuver space; exposed in several sections to missile fire from above
*   **Overwatch Threat:** The **Southwest Guard Tower (Location 9)** has clear line of sight along much of the ascent path
*   **Defensive Advantage:** Attackers approaching the monastery must climb uphill while exposed to crossbow and arrow fire
*   **Signs of Traffic:** Wagon ruts, horse prints, and boot tracks from raiding parties and merchant wagons taken during ambushes
*   **Raid Evidence:** Broken crate boards, dropped rope bindings, and occasional bloodstains where prisoners were forced up the path
*   **Stealth Note:** Sparse cover—small boulders and scrub brush provide limited concealment
*   **Ambush Potential:** Cultists sometimes position scouts along the ridge to watch the road below
*   **Clue Value:** Tracks reveal **recent wagon traffic and multiple patrol rotations**, confirming the monastery is actively occupied
*   **Atmosphere:** The path feels old and solemn, yet defiled by the passage of raiders and prisoners dragged toward the ruin above

### Wandering Encounters

The ascent is not wilderness in the pure sense. It is a contested approach. Most encounters here should reinforce:

*   Earth Temple activity
*   Kron Hills ecology
*   The tension between civilization and wild

Roll once every hour of travel on the ascent.  
Roll with disadvantage during heavy wind or rain (visibility poor).  
No encounter on a roll of 1–14 (d20).

#### Standard Encounters

* * *

##### 15 – Kron Wolves (2d4)

Lean, grey-coated wolves native to the Kron Hills. They are cautious but hungry.

**Behavior:**

*   Observe first
*   Attack only if party appears weakened
*   Flee if one wolf drops

**Flavor Cue:**

A low growl rolls from the western rocks. Yellow eyes glint between thorns. The wind shifts… and the wolves begin to circle.

* * *

##### 16 – Goblin Scouts (1d6+1)

Small scouting party from a nearby cave network or displaced from Lortmil foothills.

**Behavior:**

*   Watching the ridge
*   Hoping to scavenge raid leftovers
*   Will flee if confronted aggressively

**Clue:  
**They fear the gnolls and mention “bone-king beasts” if captured.

* * *

##### 17 – Black Earth Patrol (2 Guards)

Disciplined patrol rotating from the gate.

**Behavior:**

*   Observe from distance
*   Signal horn if threatened
*   Attempt capture if possible

**Behavior:**

*   Attempt to drag fallen victim uphill
*   Howl to alert others if combat lasts 3+ rounds

##### Night Modifiers

At night:

*   Increase chance of gnolls by +1 result tier.
*   Wolves double in number.
*   Black Earth patrol frequency increases.
*   Owlbear chance increases by +1.

* * *

##### Campaign Escalation Logic

If the party:

*   Defeats a patrol → next patrol delayed 4 hours.
*   Captures a gnoll → Tharvok becomes cautious.
*   Leaves bodies visible → patrol numbers increase next day.
*   Retreats and returns later → encounter chance increases to 1–13 no encounter.
