4\. Grandle Farmstead
=====================

> The house and barn show that this farmer is doing well, and the stock in the fenced-in yard are very fine-looking. You see a fetching feminine face in a window.

Grandle Farmstead, located at the center of Hommlet across the crossroads from the Inn of the Welcome Wench on the Long Road, is a thriving agricultural establishment. The widow Kelagh Grandle lives here with her two grown sons, their wives, and eight children. Known for their high-quality stock and adherence to [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md), the Grandles are a prominent family in the village, although they shy away from trade and adventuring.

### Description

The Grandle Farmstead features a well-maintained house and barn, indicative of the family's prosperity. The house is a sturdy, two-story structure with a thatched roof, whitewashed walls, and flower boxes under the windows. The barn is equally impressive, made of timber and stone, housing fine-looking livestock. The farmyard is enclosed by a wooden fence, keeping the healthy animals safe.

### Lore and History

The Grandle Farmstead has been in the family for generations. Kelagh's late husband, Bryn, expanded the farm and introduced new crops such as corn, which have since become a staple. The family follows the Old Faith, maintaining a small shrine near the barn.

*   **Founding:** Established generations ago, the farm has grown under the care of the Grandle family.
*   **Expansion:** The late Bryn Grandle expanded the farm, adding new crops and improving livestock quality.
*   **Old Faith:** The family follows [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md), maintaining traditional agricultural practices and seasonal rituals.

##### Secret Inheritance

[Show spoiler](#spoiler-Kh0Vrn)

The two sons, Kelan and Kolan, have hidden a secret inheritance from their father. Buried beneath a tree in the backyard is a large iron pot containing 27 gp, 421 sp, and 150 gp worth of miscellaneous jewelry.

### Motivation and Relationships

The Grandle family is tightly knit, though tensions occasionally arise due to Kelagh's strictness and the brothers' fondness for ale. Despite the nagging, there is deep mutual respect and love among the family members.

##### Community Ties

The Grandles are well-respected in Hommlet, contributing to the local economy with their high-quality produce and livestock. Kelan and Kolan's regular visits to the Inn of the Welcome Wench have made them familiar figures among the villagers.

*   **Kelagh Grandle:** Matriarch, overseeing the farm and family with a firm hand.
*   **Kelan and Kolan:** Sons who manage the day-to-day operations, balancing their responsibilities with social activities at the inn.
*   **Wives and Children:** Supportive family members contributing to the farm's success and maintaining household harmony.

#### Motivation and Relationships

Family Unity: Despite occasional conflicts, the Grandles are united in their commitment to the farm and each other. Community Respect: Known for their hard work and quality produce, the Grandles are valued members of Hommlet.

### Current Events

##### Farm Activities

The farm is currently thriving, with the recent addition of corn fields boosting their harvest. The children help with chores, learning the family trade from a young age.

*   **Agricultural Success:** Recent harvests have been bountiful, thanks to the introduction of corn and careful management.
*   **Defensive Preparedness:** With increased bandit activity, the family stays alert, relying on their militia training to protect their home.

##### Bandit Threats

With the rise in bandit activities in the region, the Grandle family remains vigilant. Kelan and Kolan, as members of the militia, are prepared to defend their home and village if needed.

##### Farm Treasure

**Secret Inheritance:** An iron pot buried beneath a backyard tree contains valuable coins and jewelry, a hidden legacy from the past.

#### Conclusion

Grandle Farmstead stands as a testament to the resilience and hard work of the Grandle family. With its rich history, strong family ties, and commitment to the community, the farm continues to thrive in the heart of Hommlet. As the village faces new challenges, the Grandles remain a pillar of strength and stability.

### Grandle Family

##### Kelagh Grandle

A widow of 60 years, Kelagh is a stern but caring matriarch. She has a weathered face from years of working the fields, sharp blue eyes, and silver hair usually tied in a bun. Known for her strict demeanor, she often clashes with her sons.

> _"Hommlet’s fields may bend in the wind, but they will never break to brigands."_

##### Kelan Grandle

At 32, Kelan is the elder son, tall and robust, with dark hair and a beard. He is hardworking and dedicated to the farm, but often finds solace at the [7\. The Inn of the Welcome Wench](7-the-inn-of-the-welcome-wench-landmark.md).

> _"The land gives to those who protect it; we must guard it from those who would take it."_

##### Hornala Grandle

Kelan's wife, 31 years old, is a supportive and nurturing figure with a warm smile and auburn hair. She manages the household and helps with the farm work.

##### Kolan Grandle

The younger son, 27 years old, is similar in appearance to his brother but with a more easygoing attitude. He is equally diligent in the fields but enjoys relaxing at the inn.

##### Jelifin Grandle

Kolan's wife, also 27, is a cheerful and industrious woman with blonde hair and a keen eye for detail. She shares household responsibilities with Hornala.

##### Children

Meadow (12), Manda (11), Ferne (9), Grinby (10), Coinsie (7), Boadi (6), Kent (5), and Digsly (3).
