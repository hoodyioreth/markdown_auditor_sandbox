D2 The Familiar Roost Tavern
============================

> The familiar pearlescent glow of elven light cantrips glow like a beacon even in the dead of night within the common room of this inn catering to the fey elf races. Given the famous elven lack of need for sleep, patrons can be found at all hours reading tomes of ancient lore while sipping at rare wines, or debating wide-ranging subjects such as magical theory or long-term ecology over repasts of delicately seasoned venison and wild greens. ### Sanctuary of the Sylvan Song

A living tavern-inn built _around_ a massive elm, where elven nightfolk debate lore beneath pearlescent cantrip-light, druids and rangers slip in by balcony, and “familiar-bonds” are formed—making it one of the Elven Dawn Quarter’s most important social junctions for forest politics, secret meetings, and long, watchful conversations.

##### Entering the _Sanctuary of the Sylvan Song; Meeting-Tree of the Enclave_

The pebblestone lane narrows, and the scent of sap and spiced venison steals over the street. Ahead, a great elm rises—older than the mortar of Verbobonc—its trunk swallowed by timbered walls and winding stairs, as if a tavern had grown up around it like ivy round a tower. Pearlescent cantrip-light glows within the common room even at dead of night, soft as moonmilk, and through open shutters you spy folk who do not sleep: elves with books splayed wide, sipping rare wines; others leaning close, disputing the long ages of forests and the secret laws of spellcraft.  
  
A small creature—squirrel, owl, or something not quite either—watches thee from a beam, bright-eyed and solemn. Behind the bar, a stout sylvan elf woman with wild nobility in her bearing turns her jade gaze upon thee. Twin scimitars rest easy at her back, as if they were but tools for trimming briar.  
  
“Well met,” she says, and her smile is warm—yet it measureth thee. “Mind thy manners, and the Roost shall mind thy comfort.”

The Familiar Roost is an inn-tavern and meeting spot that has become a **cultural landmark** of the Elven Quarter—symbol and tool of Verbobonc’s living tie to the Gnarley Forest. It began as an elven kin-meeting place and hath grown into a hub for **Conclave talk**, druidic councils, and quiet negotiations with outsiders who “love the green.”

By custom, it is also a **druidic sanctuary**: those who guard the wild places may find shelter here—sometimes in plain sight, sometimes by stranger means.

* * *

#### Exterior & Approach

**The Treehouse Design:** The first two stories **encircle the elm’s trunk**, with beams, staircases, and corridors twisting like branches. The whole place feels like forest architecture translated into city timber—harmonious chaos, held by craft rather than symmetry.

**Loft & Balcony:** An external stair winds upward to a **secluded loft** with a balcony overlooking the city—also serving as a discreet entry for **shape-shifted druids and rangers** who prefer not to use the front door.

**The Beacon-Light:** Elven light cantrips give the Roost its constant, gentle glow; even when the street is dark, this place looks awake.

#### Interior

**Ground-Floor Common Room:** The main hall is the heart—tables set close, beams grown thick, the elm’s living trunk rising through the floor like a pillar of time. Patrons range from rural folk seeking camaraderie to fey-leaning elves poring over tomes.

**Second-Floor Rooms:** Cramped “woodsy” accommodations near the canopy—beloved by guests accustomed to forest living.

**Food & Drink:** Delicately seasoned venison and wild greens; rare wines; and peculiar favorites such as **green bark teas** and other potent, heady concoctions.

**The Familiar Corner (Shop / Roosting Nook):** A space where prospective familiars are not “sold” so much as _met_. Creatures choose their companions, and the bond begins with consent and curiosity. Donations to the “Gnarley fund” are expected, and prices for board and fare remain average to suit middle folk.

#### Facilities Description

*   **Treehouse Design:** The first two stories encircle the tree's trunk, featuring an assortment of snug rooms and corridors.
*   **Loft and Balcony:** Accessible via an external staircase winding up the tree, the loft serves as a secluded meeting space with a balcony that offers stunning city views and acts as a discreet entry point for shape-shifted druids and rangers.
*   **Second Floor Accommodations:** Very cramped rooms located high up, close to the quiet tree canopy. Ideal for guests accustomed to forest living, offering a unique, immersive experience.
*   **Ground Floor Tavern:** The main gathering space for the tavern, occupying the majority of the ground floor. Frequented by a diverse group of rural folk who enjoy the camaraderie over shared drinks.
*   **Popular beverages:** include green bark teas and other potent, heady concoctions.

#### Historical Significance

*   **Origin and Growth:** Initially a simple meeting spot for elven kin, The Familiar Roost has grown into a cultural landmark, symbolizing the city's deep connection to the Gnarley Forest and its mystical inhabitants.

* * *

#### Politics & Relevance

**Elven Conclave Ties:** The Roost hosts enclave discussions on preservation and city–forest relations—often framed as convivial debate, yet carrying real weight.

**Bridge of Folk:** Rural folk and “forest-friendly” tradesfolk gather here beside elven scholars; this makes the Roost a perfect place for PCs to gather rumor without feeling like they’ve walked into a noble court—yet the eyes are just as sharp.

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   Seek **elven lore** or druidic counsel in a social setting (less formal than D1). D2 The Familiar Roost Tavern
*   Hire a guide or earn an introduction to Enclave circles (through Gwynharwyf). Gwynharwyf Starbreeze
*   Obtain (or be chosen by) a **familiar**—or learn who in the city has recently sought one. D2 The Familiar Roost Tavern
*   Attend a **Lunar Hallowing** and gain a moonlit vision, clue, or social bond. Gwynharwyf Starbreeze

##### If They Linger

*   They are tested—gently. A stranger’s manners are observed at length.
*   Balcony traffic becomes noticeable: who comes by stair, who leaves by roofline, who never uses the front.

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A familiar chooses a PC in a way that offends another patron (jealousy, superstition, or politics). D2 The Familiar Roost Tavern
*   A druidic meeting is interrupted by a city matter—someone brings word of logging, poaching, or a “forest fire” that is not natural.
*   A quiet messenger arrives bearing _too-polite_ questions about road traffic and “missing crates” (seed your Temple-of-Elemental-Evil logistics threads without naming them yet).

#### Goods and Services

*   **Accommodations:** Offers uniquely "woodsy" rooms that, despite being compact, provide comfort akin to forest living.
*   **Tavern:** Serves as a communal area where patrons enjoy simple, wholesome fare and beverages like green bark tea.
*   **Familiar Shop:** A diverse array of familiars is available, with creatures choosing their companions, fostering a deep bond from first meeting.

##### The Roost Pricing

*   **Pricing:** Both accommodations and food are priced averagely to cater to the middle-class patron, with an emphasis on voluntary contributions towards conservation efforts.
*   **Familiar pricing:** Any manner of familiar can be ‘found’ here for free, but donations to the ‘Gnarley’ fund are usually expected.

##### Room Prices (Second-Floor Canopy Rooms)

Cramped, woodsy chambers tucked near the canopy—sleeping nooks of timber and leaf-scented linen, with the elm’s slow creak like distant surf.

*   **Common Bunk (shared loft pallet):** **5 sp** per night
*   **Canopy Nook (tiny private room, 1 bed):** **3 gp** per night
*   **Twin Nook (small private room, 2 narrow beds):** **5 gp** per night
*   **Moonlit Canopy Room (best chamber, balcony access when available):** **8 gp** per night

#### Cultural and Social Hub

*   **Seasonal Gatherings:** Hosts significant gatherings during equinoxes and solstices, aligning with druidic celebrations and enhancing community bonds.

The Familiar Roost Tavern not only serves as a unique hospitality venue but also stands as a cornerstone of Verbobonc's commitment to blending urban life with the natural world, embodying the spirit of cooperation and respect for nature that defines the Elven Quarter.

### Key NPCs

##### Gwynharwyf Starbreeze, Keeper of the Roost

A sylvan elf of deeply tanned skin and fierce presence, shorter and sturdier than most of her kind, with that unmistakable air of wild nobility. She is devout to [Sehanine Moonbow](sehanine-moonbow-person.md), and her tavern hosts **Lunar Hallowings** at the full moon—dance, reflection, and communal trance beneath moon-signs and lantern-glow.

**Motivations**

*   Keep the Roost a true sanctuary for forest-folk and their allies.
*   Strengthen bonds within the Enclave and quietly support the defense of the elven quarter.
*   Advocate for preservation and cultural respect in Verbobonc’s civic talk—she plays politics with a smile and a spine of oak.

**Manner at the Table (2nd-person dialogue prompt)**

Gwynharwyf wipes a cup, slow as tide.  
“_Whispers are like moths,” she says softly. “They fly to any flame. Tell me—art thou the candle, or the hand that shieldeth it? Earn thy questions. In my house, answers are not bait for idle hooks.”_

Secret (DM): Gwynharwyf keeps a private ledger of “trouble scents”—folk who come too often, ask too carefully, or linger in corners where the balcony stair can be watched. She is not paranoid; she is _practiced._

##### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (10)

1.  **“The elm remembers names.”** Speak thine aloud too often, and the Roost will know thee before you return.
2.  A shape-shifted ranger uses the balcony to meet a lover from the Foreign Quarter—someone is blackmailing them.
3.  Green bark tea has been “spoiled” twice this month—by accident… or by design.
4.  A familiar refused three prospective mages, then walked straight to a seemingly dull caravan guard and curled in his lap.
5.  Gwynharwyf’s full-moon Hallowing drew an uninvited presence—folk swear they heard laughter where no mouth moved.
6.  Resheph seeks discreet hands to investigate a blight that doth not smell of nature.
7.  A scholar debating “long-term ecology” insists the city’s growth is pulling something old and angry from the Gnarley’s roots.
8.  A patron paid for rare wine with coin that felt strangely cold—Gwynharwyf kept it, saying only: “Not minted here.”
9.  The Roost’s loft holds a hidden latch in the railing—used to pass small parcels without ever touching the stairs.
10.  Someone has begun asking whether a familiar might be taught to carry messages “without being seen.” Gwynharwyf is looking for the asker.
