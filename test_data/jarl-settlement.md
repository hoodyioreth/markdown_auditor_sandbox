Jarl
====

Jarl standeth where river and road are forced together by stone, law, and coin. Here the Velverdyva passeth beneath a great arch of masonry, and all who would travel east or west must be seen, counted, and taxed. It is not a town that grew by chance, but one shaped by necessity—where trade is watched, faith is questioned, and every man who entereth is already measured for profit.

* * *

#### Overview

The town lieth astride the Velverdyva, its greater part upon the Velunan bank, yet its shadow stretching across into [Furyondy](furyondy-organization.md). A great stone bridge bindeth the halves together, its arches tall enough for laden barges to pass beneath.

 

From dawn till dusk, the bridge is choked with carts, beasts, and shouting men. Beneath it, the river runneth thick with trade. And above it all stand watchful eyes—soldiers, clerks, and priests alike.

* * *

#### Trade & Commerce

Jarl prospereth because it must. The river cannot be avoided, and the road cannot be bypassed. Thus all trade is funneled here—north and south by water, east and west by land.

 

With northern routes troubled by Iuz, caravans now favor the Velverdyva and the Great Western Road. This hath swelled Jarl beyond its intended measure, filling its docks and streets with restless commerce.

*   River barges carry wool, grain, iron, and timber
*   Caravans bring finished goods from Dyvers and beyond
*   Furyondian merchants compete openly across the river
*   Velunan guild factors maintain counting houses here

 

Coin moveth swiftly, and loyalties more swiftly still.

* * *

#### Politics & Authority

Jarl is ruled in name by Trisen Mori, brother to Count Westin Mori of [Devarnish](devarnish-settlement.md). Yet his rule is thin, and oft unwilling.

 

True power is divided:

*   The Count’s authority — concerned with taxation and control
*   The Bishop’s agents — enforcing doctrine and order
*   Merchant interests — quietly controlling trade flow

 

This division breedeth tension. Orders are given twice, and obeyed only once—if at all.

* * *

#### The Pholtan Presence

Though Veluna is a realm of [Rao](rao-person.md), Jarl feeleth the heavy tread of [Pholtus](pholtus-person-1.md) more keenly than most.

*   Its wealth, distance from Devarnish, and closeness to Furyondy make it suspect. Thus inquisitors are stationed here in number, watching for signs of moral decay.
    
*   Yet the town resisteth—not openly, but in quiet ways.
    
*   Taverns remain open late. Merchants bend tithe. Priests of Rao speak softly of balance rather than judgment.
    
*   And for this, the watch is constant.
    

* * *

#### Districts

##### The Stone Docks

A forest of cranes, ropes, and shouting men. Goods flow day and night, and theft is punished swiftly—or quietly ignored.

##### The Bridge Market

Set upon the great crossing, this market dealeth in fast coin and faster bargains. One may buy anything here—if one asketh the right man.

##### The Twin Banks

Veluna on one side, Furyondy on the other. Rivalries run deep, though trade bindeth them tighter than law.

##### The Low Taverns

Dockside drinking halls where sailors, guards, and smugglers mingle. Here news arriveth before it is known elsewhere.

#### Notable NPCs

##### Trisen Mori

_Suloise_

A lord who would rather be elsewhere. He ruleth because he must, not because he desireth it. His neglect hath made Jarl a place where others seize power in quiet ways.

##### Captain Elrid Vann

_Oeridian_

Commander of the bridge guard. Stern, practical, and loyal to order above all else.

##### Sister Halvine

_Cleric of Rao_

A quiet presence in a loud town. Many confide in her, though few know what she doth with what she learneth.

*   A river barge vanisheth between Jarl and Dyvers without trace
*   A hidden sect openly resisteth Pholtan doctrine
*   Trisen Mori seeketh aid to abandon his post
*   Violence erupts between Velunan and Furyondian merchants

#### DM Notes

Jarl should feel tense, crowded, and watched. It is a place where trade moves faster than law, and where every faction seeketh advantage. Use it as a gateway for rumors, intrigue, and shifting alliances between Veluna, Furyondy, and Verbobonc.
