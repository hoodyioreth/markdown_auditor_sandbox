Karnakia
========

### Professor of Necromancy and Divination

To some students of the Wrinkle Academy, Karnakia is a figure of quiet dread—measured footsteps echoing through dim halls, eyes that seem to weigh the soul behind a question. To others, she is a rare and invaluable guide: a scholar who does not flinch from death, but studies it so that others may survive it.

Necromancy at the Wrinkle Academy is not taught for power, domination, or indulgence. It is taught for **defense**, **understanding**, and **containment**. In this, Karnakia’s role is essential. Her expertise in both necromancy and divination ensures that students learn not merely how death magic functions—but how it is recognized, anticipated, and resisted.

* * *

#### **Background**

A graduate of the **Mordant School of Magic**, Karnakia stands as proof that necromancers need not resemble the horrors they study. She is young by wizardly standards, yet her experience has been hard-earned.

Karnakia fought during [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md), where she served among forces opposing the Temple of Elemental Evil. In that brutal conflict, three of her companions—including her lover, **Armand**—were slain and raised as undead by one of the Temple’s wraith captains, a general whose name is now stricken from most official records.

That loss shaped the course of her life.

Rather than turn away from necromancy in revulsion, Karnakia pursued it with grim resolve. She swore that no student under her guidance would face undeath ignorant, unprepared, or blind to its signs. When the Wrinkle Academy sought an instructor capable of teaching necromancy _without corruption_, her credentials—and her scars—were impossible to ignore.

* * *

#### **Appearance**

Karnakia is a young human woman with long brunette hair and steady hazel eyes. Her robes are plain and functional, often accented with subtle warding stitches rather than ornamentation. She carries herself with the posture of one accustomed to battlefields as much as lecture halls.

* * *

### **Role at the Wrinkle Academy**

Karnakia teaches:

*   **Necromancy (defensive and academic application)**
*   **Divination with a focus on death magic detection**
*   Advanced seminars on **undeath containment**, **corporeal integrity**, and **ritual interruption**

Her courses are mandatory for all advanced arcane students, regardless of specialization. She firmly believes that _any wizard ignorant of necromancy is a liability—to themselves and others_.

Despite her reputation, Karnakia’s classes consistently produce some of the **highest examination scores** at the Academy. Her discipline is strict, her expectations exacting, and her lectures ruthlessly focused.

Fear, she teaches, is a tool—one best understood before it is turned against you.

* * *

#### **Published Works**

Karnakia is the author of several respected (and occasionally unsettling) academic texts, including:

*   _**Blood Ties**_ — An examination of necromantic bonds, lingering will, and emotional anchors in undeath
*   _**Anatomy for Humanoid and Monstrous Creatures**_ — A grim but indispensable reference for understanding structural weaknesses exploited by death magic
*   _**Graveyard Exploration 101 & 201**_ — Field manuals emphasizing preparation, observation, and retreat over heroics

These works are required reading for several Academy courses and are frequently referenced by campus security and the Elven Enclave.

* * *

#### **Personality**

Karnakia is:

*   **Severe, but not cruel**
*   **Unsentimental, yet deeply principled**
*   **Intolerant of recklessness**
*   **Protective of students who show discipline and honesty**

She does not raise her voice. She does not threaten.  
She simply _expects obedience to reality_.

Students who attempt bravado, humor, or moral posturing in her presence quickly learn that Karnakia has seen the consequences of ignorance—and will not indulge it.

* * *

#### **DM Notes**

*   Karnakia is an excellent **lore gateway** for undeath, necromancy, and Temple of Elemental Evil foreshadowing
*   She can recognize signs of **cult necromancy**, **wraith activity**, or **corrupted resurrection** with alarming speed
*   Her past at Emridy Meadows provides emotional weight and potential plot hooks involving lost companions, lingering undead, or unresolved vengeance

#### **Roleplaying Karnakia**

*   Speaks calmly, precisely, and without indulgence
*   Does not threaten—she **warns**
*   Treats necromancy as a **burden of knowledge**, not a fascination
*   Shows rare softness only when discussing fallen companions or Emridy Meadows

**Meeting Karnakia for the First Time**
---------------------------------------

##### **DM Narration (Read Aloud)**

You step into a chamber where the air feels cooler than the halls beyond. The stone floor is etched with faint chalk circles—old, scrubbed nearly clean but never fully erased. Shelves line the walls, filled with labeled jars, bundled bones, and thick, well-worn tomes. Nothing here is careless.  
  
At the far end of the room stands a woman in dark academic robes, her long brunette hair bound neatly behind her. Her hazel eyes lift to you at once—sharp, assessing. She does not reach for a weapon. She does not smile.  
  
You have the sudden, uncomfortable sense that she has already decided how dangerous you might be. She closes a book with deliberate care and turns fully toward you.

* * *

##### **Karnakia (Dialogue)**

“Stop there.”

_She studies each of you in turn, eyes lingering just a moment too long._

“You may approach. Slowly. If you are carrying anything recently dead, cursed, or bound to undeath, declare it now.”

_A pause. Her voice remains calm._

“Lies will only make this conversation shorter—and unpleasant.”

* * *

##### **If the party introduces themselves**

“Names are useful. Motives are essential.”

_She inclines her head a fraction._

“I am **Karnakia**. Professor of Necromancy and Divination at the Wrinkle Academy.”

“No, that does not mean what you think it means.”

* * *

##### **If asked about necromancy**

_Her gaze hardens slightly._

“Necromancy is not about death. It is about **control**—over decay, fear, and ignorance.”

“I teach my students how to recognize death magic… and how to survive it.”

_A beat._

“Those who seek power over the dead rarely survive long enough to enjoy it.”

* * *

##### **If the party mentions the Temple of Elemental Evil or undead**

_She grows very still._

“…Then you are either braver than you should be, or more foolish than you realize.”

_She studies you again, slower this time._

“We will speak further.”

“But understand this—if you dabble in death magic without discipline, I will not correct you twice.”

* * *

##### **Closing Tone**

_She steps aside, gesturing toward the chamber beyond._

“You may remain—for now.”

“Do not touch anything marked in red ink.”

“And do not mistake my patience for mercy.”

**_detect magic, protection from evil and good, false life, identify_

**_gentle repose, detect thoughts, blindness/deafness, augury_

**_animate dead_ (used sparingly and under strict doctrine),

**4th level (1 slot):  
**_blight_ **or** _blight replaced by blight-like divination at DM discretion_,
