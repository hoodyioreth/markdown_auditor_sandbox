F8 Maynard's Metal Emporium
===========================

A modest stone-front workshop in the Foreign Quarter conceals one of Verbobonc’s most quietly powerful artisans. Maynard’s Metal Emporium is renowned for producing exceptional mithral, adamantine, and cold iron works — without the roar of a forge or the stink of smoke. The truth behind this impossible craftsmanship is a closely guarded family secret tied to elemental fire, faith, and old bargains best left unspoken.

##### Approaching Maynard’s Metal Emporium

The cobblestones here are cleaner than most, swept as if dust were an insult. A modest stone-front shop sits tight between taller buildings, its windows narrow and shaded, its sign plain: MAYNARD’S METAL EMPORIUM.  
  
Yet something is strange—there is no chimney smoke, no coal stench, no ringing hammer-song. Only the faint scent of clean oil and warmed stone, as though a forge were hidden behind silence itself. In the window, a few small pieces of metalwork catch the light… and hold it. A hinge that looks too perfect. A strip of pale metal that gleams like moonlit water. A dark ingot that seems to swallow brightness rather than reflect it.

#### **Exterior Description**

Maynard’s shop presents a restrained face to the street — pale stone, narrow windows, and a carefully lettered sign hung from wrought iron. There is **no visible chimney**, no coal yard, and no clamor of hammer on anvil. This absence alone draws attention among craftsmen.

A small street-side display shows polished metal fittings, decorative hinges, and sample ingots — all flawless, all cool to the touch.

* * *

#### **Interior Description**

The interior is low-ceilinged and meticulously ordered. Shelves hold metals in various stages of refinement, each tagged with provenance marks. Display cases show finished works: blade edges that drink the light, armor plates thinner than expected, decorative pieces worked with impossible precision.

There is no forge in sight.

The air smells faintly of warm stone and clean oil — never smoke.

* * *

### **A Mysterious Family Secret (DM Only)**

##### **The Truth**

Maynard’s success is owed to **Nerath Flamewhisper**, a fire mephit bound generations ago through an oath sworn under Pholtan doctrine and elemental law.

*   **Role:** Family “pet” and metal shaper
*   **Method:** Nerath’s _controlled elemental breath_ allows mithral, adamantine, and cold iron to be shaped without traditional furnaces
*   **Limitations:** The mephit refuses mass production and reacts violently to greed or blasphemy
*   **Risk:** Discovery would invite exploitation — by cultists, mages, or worse

Maynard believes the secret is a **test of restraint**, not a gift to be abused.

#### History

_A Legacy Forged from Ancestral Roots_

##### Ancestral Background

*   **Aeridian-Baklunish Heritage:** Maynard's family boasts a mix of Aeridian (Greek) and Baklunish (Arabian) roots.
*   **Migration to Verbobonc:** Moved to Verbobonc after the Hateful Wars and opened the Metal Emporium.

##### Establishing the Emporium

*   **Family Tradition of Metalworking:** Built upon the family's long tradition of metalworking.
*   **Collaboration with Dwarves:** Collaborated with the dwarves of the Lortmil Mountains for metal imports.

##### Gaining Reputation

*   **Rare Metals Supplier:** Gained a reputation as a supplier of rare and imported metals.
*   **Mithral and Adamantine Specialist:** Known for high-quality mithral and adamantine products.

#### **Role in the Temple of Elemental Evil Campaign**

Maynard’s Emporium is a **strategic node**, not a shopfront dungeon mart.

It is a place where:

*   Temple agents might seek rare metals discreetly
*   Fire cultists could attempt infiltration or coercion
*   The party might notice _who asks too many questions_
*   Elemental imbalance could draw unwanted attention

If the Temple learns of Nerath, the consequences ripple outward — into Verbobonc’s politics, the Lortmil supply chain, and the elemental cult war beneath the city.

* * *

#### **DM Hooks**

*   A “buyer” pressures Maynard for bulk adamantine
*   Nerath senses elemental corruption in a commissioned item
*   A rival smith reports Maynard to the city council
*   A Pholtan inquisitor begins asking _uncomfortable_ questions

#### **Tone at the Table**

Maynard’s Metal Emporium should feel:

*   Quiet
*   Precise
*   Slightly _too_ perfect

A place where players realize that **not all power announces itself with fire and noise**.

#### Arrival at Maynard’s Metal Emporium (Read Aloud)

Ahead stands a stout stone workshop, its façade blackened by honest soot rather than neglect. Wide doors stand open despite the heat within, revealing workbenches crowded with tools, ingots, and half-finished pieces that gleam even in the streetlight. A painted sign swings above the entry—MAYNARD’S METAL EMPORIUM—its letters chased in brass and copper.  
  
Warm air washes over you as you draw near, tinged with coal smoke and something stranger beneath it… a dry, sulfur-kissed heat that does not belong to any common forge. Somewhere inside, metal sings as it cools, and you sense practiced hands at work—hands that know how to coax beauty from fire rather than merely endure it.  
  
A broad-shouldered man straightens from the anvil as he notices you, wiping his hands on a leather apron darkened by years of labor. His eyes are sharp, appraising, but not unkind.

##### NPC Dialogue — Maynard Sunshield (DM Reference)

**“**_**Ho there—mind your step.”  
**_He gestures casually toward a glowing trough just inside the threshold.  
_**“The floor’s hotter than it looks, and I’ve no wish to buy boots along with ore today.”**_

He studies you openly now, gaze lingering on weapons, armor, and posture alike.

**“**_**Name’s Maynard Sunshield. If you’re after honest metal, you’ve found it.”  
**_A faint smile tugs at one corner of his mouth.  
_**“If you’re after cheap tricks or flashy lies, best keep walking.”**_

He turns slightly, giving you a glimpse deeper into the shop—where embers glow brighter than they ought, and the forge breathes like something alive. He folds his arms, heat rippling around him.  
_**“So, what brings you to my door—repair, commission… or curiosity?”**_
