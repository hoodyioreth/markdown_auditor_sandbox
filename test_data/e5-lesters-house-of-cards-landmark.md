E5 Lester’s House of Cards
==========================

Ryemend’s loudest den of “lawful vice”—a weapon-checked, rule-bound gaming house where coin changeth hands fast, secrets change hands faster, and the Battirovka Family quietly skimmeth both.

##### **Enter Lester’s House of Cards**

A signboard swings above the crowd: LESTER’S HOUSE OF CARDS, painted bold as a dare. Warm light spills from leaded windows, and the very stones outside seem to breathe smoke. Within, the air is thick with pipe-haze and the sharp tang of spilled ale. Dice clatter like hail on a roof. Coins sing. Men laugh too loud. Women smile too sweet. At every table, someone is winning—yet somehow the House grows richer by the hour.  
  
At the entry desk, a stern-eyed clerk holds out a plain wooden tray. “No steel,” he says, without raising his voice. “No quarrels. No excuses.”

#### What This Place Is

Lester’s House of Cards is a bustling gambling den famed for a lively, smoky press of bodies—yet kept on a leash by **strict rules**, **weapon-check**, and **quiet removal** of rowdies.

It began with Lester Davies—an adventurer turned gambler—who knew two truths: (1) folk will always wager, and (2) a good house is not chaos, but _managed_ chaos. Over the years it hath grown in notoriety, especially for **beast-fighting nights every third night**, when the back pit fills and coin turns savage.

#### The Battirovka Arrangement (DM Truth)

Lester maintaineth a secretive, lucrative bond with **the Battirovka Family**—the city’s thieves’ guild—using the House for **fixed games**, **scores**, and most of all **information-gathering** from rich merchants and nobles.

The Family doth not want Ryemend burning; they want it **prosperous**, **predictable**, and **profitable**—and they guard their turf against independent “black sheep” thieves. Lester’s hall is one of their cleanest nets: broad, public, and full of people who talk too much when they think they’re only losing coin.

##### Underworld Access

If the PCs play with restraint and pay their debts, they may earn **Friend of the Family** contact status—granting advantage on one Investigation check per tenday to find fences, rumors, or illicit gear in Verbobonc (so long as they do not “freelance” against the Family).

### Layout & Key Areas

##### 1) The Check Desk

A plain counter with iron hooks, numbered tokens, and a tray for blades. Weapons are checked here—no argument.  
**DM detail:** the clerk’s tokens are subtly marked; the House can tell at a glance if a “returned” weapon is truly the same one.

##### 2) The Smoke Hall (Main Floor)

Card tables, dice boards, a small raised dais for the “high table,” and serving-wenches weaving like minnows through a river of elbows. The noise is constant, yet oddly contained—as if the room itself hath learned the rules.

##### 3) The Back Pit (Beast Night)

A fenced pit for **non-magical beasts**, opened **every third night**, with wagering shouted from the rail.  
**DM note:** the pit is where Lester tests new “clients.” Some folk bet; others come to see who else is betting.

##### 4) Lester’s Office (Upstairs, guarded)

Ledgers, private wagers, “debts” written polite and sharp. A second door—plain as a pantry—leads to a narrow stair used by Battirovka messengers.

#### History: Origins and Evolution

Founded by Lester Davies, a former adventurer turned gambler, the House of Cards quickly became a popular spot in Ryemend. Over the years, it has grown in fame and notoriety, especially for its beast fighting events.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Founding:** Established by Lester Davies, a former adventurer
*   **Growth:** Evolved into a well-known gambling den
*   **Notoriety:** Famous for its beast fighting nights

### Goods and Services

_High-Stakes Games and Beast Fighting_

Lester’s House of Cards offers a variety of gambling activities, from card games to dice, and hosts beast fighting events every third night. The den ensures a thrilling and engaging experience for all patrons.

##### Key Offerings

*   **Gambling:** Cards, dice, and other games of chance
*   **Beast Fighting:** Every third night, a fighting pit for non-magical beasts
*   **Drinks:** Wide selection of drinks served by efficient staff

##### Cost

*   **Games:** Varies depending on the stakes
*   **Beast Fighting:** Entry fees for participation and betting

Lester’s House of Cards remains a cornerstone of Ryemend’s vibrant nightlife, providing excitement and high-stakes entertainment for all who enter.

### The Relationship with the Thieves Guild

##### A Web of Intrigue and Deceit

Lester Davies maintains a secretive yet lucrative relationship with [The Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) , the local thieves guild. This alliance allows him to orchestrate fixed games and gather valuable information from rich merchants and nobles who frequent his establishment.

Games, Costs, and House Rules
-----------------------------

_(Use these as quick DM tools—adjust to your table’s economy.)_

**Entry:** free (the House makes coin by the bite).  
**Weapon Check:** mandatory; refusal = no entry.  
**The House’s Bite (rake):** 10% of winnings at most tables; 15% at the high table.  
**Common Stakes:** 1 sp – 2 gp per hand/throw.  
**High Table Stakes:** 10 gp – 100 gp (invitation or proven purse).

### Beast Night (every third night)

*   **Pit admission:** 5 sp
*   **Wager minimum:** 1 gp
*   **Private rail box (4 seats):** 10 gp (includes one round of strong drink)

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   To win coin fast (or _seem_ to)
*   To find a fence, a messenger, or “a cousin of the Family”
*   To eavesdrop on merchants and nobles
*   To locate someone who vanished after “one last wager”
*   To follow Temple money without ever saying “Temple”

*   A dealer offers a seat that’s _too good_—and watches who notices.
*   Megan “accidentally” repeats a name the PCs have only whispered once.
*   A Battirovka Friend of the Family sidles near with a soft warning about “black sheep” operating in Ryemend. The Battirovka Family

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A rigged game ruins the wrong man, and he blames the PCs.
*   The Wardens arrive to ask questions—someone used the House as cover for a theft. The Battirovka Family
*   On beast night, a cage latch “fails,” and panic becomes a weapon.

##### Optional 5e-Friendly Mechanics (DM Toolbox)

##### Quick Gambling Resolution (one roll)

Pick a game and roll once per “hour” of play:

*   **Cards:** Sleight of Hand (Dex) or Deception (Cha)
*   **Dice:** Sleight of Hand (Dex) or Insight (Wis)
*   **Reading the Room:** Insight (Wis)

**DC 12:** break even  
**DC 15:** win 1d6 × stake  
**DC 18:** win 2d6 × stake (and gain a rumor)  
**Fail by 5+:** lose 1d6 × stake and attract attention (a dealer, a bruiser, or a Battirovka “Friend”).

##### “Rigged Table” Tell

A PC who spends 10 minutes watching may make **Perception (Wis) DC 15** to spot a marked deck/weighted dice technique. On success, they can:

*   quietly leave without trouble, **or**
*   attempt to turn the rig against the House (**Sleight of Hand DC 18**).  
    Success wins coin; failure earns a private invitation… to a back room.
