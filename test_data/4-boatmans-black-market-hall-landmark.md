4\. Boatman's Black Market Hall
===============================

_**A Bazaar of Shadows**_

The Indoor Market Hall of Nulb is no innocent gathering of farmers and craftsmen. This is a shadowy bazaar—a place where masks are worn as often as blades, and every smile carries the scent of betrayal. Facing the village side of Nulb and operating under the unspoken blessing of the Temple of Elemental Evil, this market is **where contraband breathes, and morality withers.**

The hall connects to the Boatman’s Tavern via an internal door and includes large iron-bound oak doors facing the street for patrons. Inside, a mix of open-air stalls, curtained booths, and shelf-lined storage creates a chaotic maze of illicit commerce. Lanterns cast amber light across muddy flagstones, while incense and swamp smoke disguise the rot of desperation.

* * *

#### **General Layout and Access**

*   **Main Doors (Village Side):** Two large, iron-bound oak doors provide access for patrons. Usually guarded by hired thugs who demand a visible weapon or bribe.
*   **Tavern Door:** A connecting door leads into the Boatman’s Tavern, allowing fluid movement between drink and deal-making.
*   **Warehouse Access:** A third reinforced door leads to the warehouse section, used for loading bulk shipments or smuggled goods.

* * *

#### **Market Features**

*   **Booth Rentals:** Ramshackle booths, crates-as-counters, and curtain-shrouded tables line the hall. Merchants pay a 10% tithe to Lodriss for the privilege.
*   **General Supply Section:** A corner of tall wooden shelves holds common goods—rope, rations, flint, tallow, torches, cheap weapons—sold without questions.
*   **Artifact Alley:** A narrow row of stalls specializing in looted relics, cursed trinkets, and counterfeit holy items. Some of it is real. All of it is dangerous.
*   **The Shadow Nook:** A curtained alcove where poisons, alchemical brews, and deadly flora are sold in hushed tones.
*   **Slave Cage (Hidden):** Iron cages located in the warehouse, where a large door leads to the true holding area—beneath the floor. In the basement below lies a secret chamber, sealed with iron bars and guarded by a heavy lock lay the true prisoner chamber.

* * *

#### **Atmosphere & Activity**

*   **Smells:** Iron, mold, alchemy smoke, sweet wine, leather, and blood.
*   **Sounds:** Low bargaining, clinking chains, distant laughter, and the occasional crack of a whip.
*   **Guards:** 4–6 thugs patrol, but violence is only stopped if it interferes with trade or profit.
*   **Light:** Dim lanterns hang from rusted chains. Some corners are kept intentionally dark for illicit whispers.

* * *

### First Steps into Nulb’s Black Market

The iron-bound oak doors groan as you push them inward, coughing on the sudden shift from open air to smoke-thick gloom. The hall is dim, stinking of mold and boiled leather, its floor smeared with old mud and unspoken things. Lanterns sway from rusted chains, casting crooked shadows across rows of stalls, curtained booths, and slouching patrons who eye you like carrion birds watching fresh meat.  
  
Somewhere in the back, a hooded vendor whispers over a vial that glows blue. Another sharp-eyed figure tugs a covered crate toward a booth ringed with stolen dwarven steel. The sound of low bargaining buzzes like flies on a corpse.  
  
But before you can step further— _“Hold up.”_ Two figures peel away from a crate near the warehouse access—thugs in piecemeal leathers and stamped iron gorgets. One leans on a thick cudgel; the other drags a blade through a whetstone, slow and deliberate. They block your path, side by side, near a heavy door that reeks of rust and recent movement.  
  
The first one jerks a thumb over his shoulder and smirks through crooked teeth. _“New faces, yeah? No call to be wanderin’ back here just yet.”  
  
_His eyes slide over your weapons. He doesn’t look impressed. Just cautious. _“Lodriss don’t like surprises. This ain't a village market—you don't browse unless someone vouches.”  
  
_The other one narrows his eyes, still stroking steel across stone. _“Best find your coin and purpose fast, friend. Or go back to the tavern where folks bleed quieter.”_

##### Insight Check (DC 12):

They’re not trying to start a fight—they’re asserting control. Newcomers get one warning. Push too hard, and things escalate.

##### Dialogue Options:

*   **Polite Deference:** “We’re here to trade—no trouble.” (Disarms suspicion, they offer directions.)
*   **Name-drop Lodriss or Selentis:** “We were sent by someone upstairs.” (Roll with Advantage; may grant access.)
*   **Bluff Check (DC 15):** Claim to be meeting a vendor (e.g., Tarn, Myra).
*   **Push Past or Threaten:** Triggers combat or nearby guards call for backup.

*   A Temple relic is for sale, but cursed. Can the party break the enchantment—or use it?
*   A noble’s kidnapped heir is hidden in the slave cage.

*   Lodriss offers the party a “favor”—but it comes with binding terms.

#### **Closing Mood: Whispered Deals in the Dark**

In the Market Hall, every deal has layers. The coin buys the goods, but the price is always greater: allegiance, silence, or damnation. Those who linger too long leave poorer—or changed. And Lodriss always remembers a face.

### Featured Quest: **“Chain on the Long Road”**

_Boatman’s Black Market Hall • Investigation → Heist • Levels 3–5_

**Premise.** **Gattas Void**—a hard-eyed runner tied to **Lareth’s** road-gang—rolls into the **Boatman’s Black Market Hall** with a covered wagon after midnight. The haul? Goods lifted north of **Hommlet** on the Long Road: trade chests, dye cakes, bolts of cloth, a jeweler’s coffer. If the party follows the paper, they can map the **bandit logistics chain** from Lareth → Gattas → Nulb fences → either the **Hunting Cottage** (Temple) or a pirate skiff.

**Why it matters.** Returning named goods to Hommlet wins real allies; exposing the chain lets you turn Nulb’s factions on each other—and proves the Temple’s supply web isn’t just rumor.

* * *

#### Starting Clue (pick one to drop)

*   **On the Hall floor:** a crate branded **double-oak ring** with an **H** scratch (Hommlet cooper’s mark) and a **green re-stitch twine** seam (Earth tell).
*   **Waterside rumor (Pearl):** “Gattas Void’s wagon took the **High Road** in—Lareth’s boys did the lifting; the selling happens **here**.”
*   **Constable Malvaar:** a **lost-goods notice** from Hommlet (names, marks, partial inventory) and a promise of paper favors for proof.

**Skill nudge:** **Investigation DC 12** identifies Hommlet maker’s marks; **History DC 10** recalls that Hommlet brands goods by guild.

* * *

#### Key NPCs

*   **Gattas Void** (middleman): ex-merc with a ledger-brain; works Lareth’s road hauls for a cut.
*   **Lodriss / Skole** (the Hall): floor boss & enforcer; they don’t care where it came from if the tithe’s paid.
*   **Dick Rentsch** (Waterside): sometimes buys specific pieces for **Romag**.
*   **Gefreid Patris** (Hunting Cottage): receives “useful” cargo (steel, tools) for the tunnel.
*   **Selentis (for Feldrin):** will **interfere** if she sniffs a private sale to Earth/Fire outside her pipe.
*   **Constable Malvaar:** swaps good intel for bridge/dock papers.

* * *

#### Scenes & Beats

##### 1) Midnight Delivery (Black Market Hall)

**Read-aloud vibe:** Wagon wheels on boards, Skole’s thumb on a ledger, cloth bundles dark with rain.

*   **Perception DC 12:** note **double-oak ring + H** brand on two crates; one bolt of cloth still bears a **Hommlet dyer’s wax-stamp**.
*   **Deception DC 13** (pose as helpers) or **Bribe 5–10 gp** to get close and “weigh the lot.”
*   **Sleight of Hand DC 14:** lift a **route chit** or a torn ledger stub from Gattas’ pouch.

**What Gattas says (if pressed lightly):  
**“Wagon picked clean north of Hommlet. My cut’s earned. After the weigh, some goes along the river, some goes south to a buyer who likes tools more than talk.”

**Clue:** a chalk mark **(two dots)** on “tools; nails; lamps” crates → **Earth pipeline**. A tar swipe on “dyes & cloth” → **pirate fence**.

* * *

##### 2) Follow the Paper (choose a line)

**A) Earth Line → Hunting Cottage**

*   Gattas pays Skole, then hands “tools/ironware” to **Dick’s teamster**.
*   Tail check: **Stealth DC 13** from Hall → **Waterside → Stables → south lane**.
*   At the **Hunting Cottage**, use the **Zilchus trick** (“dual attestation on restraints / red ring vs blue chit”) to **freeze** the intake **1–2 hours** while you:
*   copy crate tallies (**Forgery Kit or Sleight of Hand DC 14**),
*   swap a crate tag to reroute it back to town, or
*   photographically memorize the lot (or just write fast).

**B) River Line → Wharf Pier**

*   “Dyes/cloth” cases pushed to a skiff; voices mention **Tolub** or a **Vesrek buyer**.
*   **Athletics DC 12** to block the cart briefly; **Thieves’ Tools DC 13** to pop one case and confirm **Hommlet mark**.
*   Optional: sell this proof to **Selentis’ clerk** to trigger a seizure of “unsanctioned pirate trade.”

* * *

##### 3) Proving the Chain

You need **two of three** to convince Hommlet/Constable (or to blackmail a faction):

1.  **Mark match:** a branded crate, bolt-stamp, or jeweler’s chiselled cipher matching the **lost-goods notice**.
2.  **Chain link:** a **Hall ledger stub** with Gattas’ mark and lot weights **plus** a **route chit** (Hall → Stables/Wharf).
3.  **Temple junction:** an **intake tally** from the **Hunting Cottage** or a signed **hold/UNVERIFIED** slip bearing your audit notes.

**Skill:** **Investigation DC 12** to collate; **Persuasion DC 13** to sell it as a packet to Malvaar or Hommlet agents.

* * *

#### Complications (pick 1–2)

*   **Selentis Oversight:** Her clerk learns Earth is buying privately; **brigands seize** Gattas’ lot mid-scene. You can ride their audit to pry goods free—or be marked as meddlers.
*   **Reaper Interest:** A **Crimson Reapers** runner claims a cut; if refused, they trail you to the Wharf for a quick knife lesson.
*   **Gattas wary:** If you telegraph too much, he shifts the route; require **two tails at once** (Stables and Wharf).

* * *

#### Resolutions

**Clean expose (best):  
**You compile marks + ledger stub + route chit. **Constable Malvaar** issues sealed papers; **Hommlet** sends a quiet agent to reclaim crates. **Rewards:** 100–200 gp in thanks, **bridge/dock passes** for a tenday, discounts with Hommlet traders.

**Faction flip (cunning):  
**You deliver proof to **Selentis**, framing it as **Earth’s unsanctioned purchase**. She **seizes** the Hall lot “for Feldrin.” **Rewards:** Greater Temple favor, a **Gilded Shackle** counter-clause (one-time shipment freeze anywhere in Nulb).

**Pirate play (messy profit):  
**You steer “dyes/cloth” into a **Tolub vs Vesrek** bidding spat and escape in the confusion with 1–2 crates. **Rewards:** Coin (150–300 gp), enemies with knives.

**Botch (hot):  
**Skole bars doors; Gattas calls men from the corral. You flee via the **Waterside → Stables secret tunnel** (if mapped) or the **Wharf**. **Fallout:** Gattas posts your description with **Lareth**; **Smigmal** hears about “curious strangers.”

* * *

#### Evidence/Trophies

*   **Waxed folio page**: Hommlet maker’s mark table (you compile it).
*   **Hall stub**: Gattas’ “GV” scratch + weights.
*   **Route chit**: “Hall→Stables (Rentsch) / Hall→Wharf (Tolub buyer).”
*   **Cottage slip**: “Hold/UNVERIFIED—dual attestation missing” (your handwriting).

* * *

#### Cross-Refs (use what you’ve got)

*   **Waterside Hostel** (Dick, Pearl/Day; Dala/Night).
*   **Nulb Stables** (Varek; red halter sign; secret tunnel link).
*   **Wharf Pier** (midnight skiffs; quiet exits).
*   **Hunting Cottage** (Gefreid; bugbears at cave mouth only; Zilchus paper freeze).
*   **Mother Screng** (sleep draughts; antidotes; rumor broker).
*   **Constable Malvaar** (pays in **paper**, not coin).

_Keep the tone lean: ledgers like knives, whispers like oars. Let the players decide whether justice is a receipt, a rescue, or a robbery._
