G7 The Guild of Millers
=======================

##### _**Heart of the Gnomesberg Quarter**_

_“Flour is Politics, and Bread is Peace.”_

The Guild of Millers is not grand, but it is weighty. Its timbered hall is where the quarter’s grain and flour contracts are argued, weighed, stamped, and bound. Rural representatives come in smelling of rain and loam; city buyers come in smelling of ink and ambition. In the middle sit the negotiating tables—scarred by years of rings, knife-points, and the hard tapping of impatient fingers.

In play, this is your economic pressure valve for the entire campaign: it ties together river trade, bread logistics, temple politics, and the Temple of Elemental Evil’s hunger for covert supply chains.

##### Approach The Guild of Millers

Approach The Guild of Millers The guildhall’s door is plain, yet the noise behind it is the sound of a city feeding itself—chairs scraping, quills scratching, coins clicking, and voices kept low only by long habit. Within, stout beams dark with age hold up a ceiling hung with drying sheaves and sample sacks.  
  
A dozen tables fill the center like a battlefield of ink—ledgers open, scales set, measures of grain poured and pinched between thumb and forefinger. A gnome in a flour-dusted apron holds a kernel to the lamplight as though it were a jewel; another stamps a contract with a seal that lands like a verdict. No one draweth steel here. They draw terms.

### Lore and Description

_A Busy Trade Center_

The Guild of Millers is a small yet vital guild hall bustling with activity. Its modest exterior belies the importance of its role in the local economy, where deals are struck and grievances aired. The hall features sturdy wooden beams and a spacious central area filled with negotiating tables.

##### Key Features

*   **Negotiation tables:** For crop and feed prices
*   **Rustic decor:** Reflecting the guild's agricultural roots
*   **Central location:** Easily accessible within the Gnome Quarter

### What’s Here

##### The Negotiation Floor

A wide central space packed with **negotiation tables**—some reserved by long-standing custom for particular mills, valleys, or caravan consortiums. Expect:

*   sacks of grain and feed as samples
*   scales, scoops, and measures
*   ledgers, receipts, and contract slips
*   “witness stools” where neutral listeners sit quietly and remember everything

##### The Rustic Decor

Everything is honest and agricultural: beams, grain-racks, feed-hooks, and carved wooden icons of field tools. It feels rural—intentionally—because the Guild exists to ensure rural voices are not swallowed by city politics.

##### The Back Office (Clerks & Seals)

A smaller chamber where agreements are copied, sealed, and archived. The true power of the Guild lives here: not the shouting—**the records**.

#### Why the Guild Matters in Verbobonc

Bread is never “just bread” in a city. When flour prices rise, tempers rise. When feed runs short, livestock prices jump, and the poor feel it first. The Guild of Millers acts as a stabilizer—until someone forces their hand.

Gnomesberg understands something the rest of Verbobonc forgets:

*   temples can preach,
*   guards can threaten,
*   nobles can decree—

…but if the mills pause, everyone listens.

* * *

#### Political Fault-Line: Magic vs. Agriculture

The Guild is a loud, persistent opponent of large-scale magical “solutions” that undercut farmers and millers—especially repeated casting that floods the market and makes honest labor feel pointless. This puts the Guild in constant tension with city clergy and do-gooders who think magic should “solve hunger” without consequences.

Use this to create debates where no one is a cartoon villain:

*   clerics want fewer hungry mouths
*   farmers want their work to remain worth doing
*   millers want stable markets
*   demagogues (and cults) want chaos

* * *

#### Free Alliance of the Kron Hills Connection

The Guild’s official alignment with the **Free Alliance of the Kron Hills** gives it teeth: it can coordinate across valleys, mills, and rural networks that Verbobonc can’t easily command. In practice, that means:

*   leverage over rural grain flow
*   collective bargaining power
*   political unity against annexation pressure
*   quiet intelligence shared through “trade talk”

This guildhall is one of the Alliance’s strongest “civil” weapons—because it looks like commerce until the day it becomes coercion.

### Temple of Elemental Evil Hooks

##### 1) The Flour That Vanishes

A certain amount of grain disappears every tenday—never enough to trigger panic, always enough to matter. The records show “spoilage.” Fimble calls it what it is: theft with a ledger.

**What it’s really for:  
**Hidden provisioning—bandits, cult cells, or shipments heading toward Temple logistics.

**Party play:  
**Audit ledgers, tail wagons, catch a handoff at the riverfront, identify the clerk being leaned on.

* * *

##### 2) The “Charity” Pressure

A well-meaning temple begins pushing for “miraculous food relief,” undermining millers and farmers. Lira fears backlash—and someone (perhaps Iuzite agitators, perhaps Temple agents) is stoking the outrage to fracture alliances.

**Party play:  
**Protect a cleric from mob anger, expose the agitator, negotiate a compromise that keeps both peace and livelihoods intact.

* * *

##### 3) Sabotage in the Measures

Tibbit finds tool-marks on the guild’s scales and measures—subtle, meant to cause disputes and accusations between rural and city buyers.

**Party play:  
**Catch the saboteur, trace the tools, uncover who benefits from a trade war inside Gnomesberg.

* * *

##### 4) The Feed-Broker’s Fear

A livestock owner bargaining for feed is pale, shaking—someone threatened their family unless they steer contracts toward a particular buyer. That buyer is a front.

**Party play:  
**Turn the victim into a witness, set a sting using a “contract meeting,” keep it quiet enough that nobody bolts.

* * *

#### Non-Combat Solutions and Leverage

The Guild is built to reward clever roleplay over violence:

*   **Arbitration:** The Guild can compel testimony by custom—if the party brings credible evidence.
*   **Witness stools:** A “neutral listener” can become the party’s strongest ally if treated with respect.
*   **Contract traps:** Let players draft clauses that corner a foe legally (delivery windows, penalties, proof-of-origin).
*   **Reputation economy:** If the party behaves honorably here, word spreads through Gnomesberg faster than any rumor table.

* * *

### Scenes You Can Run Immediately

##### Scene 1: The Sample Table

A rural rep slams down grain that’s “too perfect.” Fimble sniffs it, frowns, and mutters that it’s been treated—alchemically or magically. The room goes quiet.

**Question:** who benefits from fake abundance?

* * *

##### Scene 2: The Cleric Protest

Outside the guildhall, a small crowd argues about miracle-food magic. Inside, Lira is trying to keep deals from collapsing.

**Party choice:** mediate, pick a side, or discover who is inflaming the crowd on purpose.

* * *

##### Scene 3: The Ledger Room Whisper

A clerk slips the party a note with shaking hands: “Rear records. After bell. Come alone.”  
It’s either a witness… or a trap laid by someone who knows the party is sniffing too close.

* * *

#### Rewards

*   **Access to records** (a single manifest, supplier name, or price-change timeline)
*   **A stamped introduction** to Free Alliance contacts (economic leverage in future scenes)
*   **A guild favor** (temporary storage, discreet transport, or a protected meeting table)
*   **A new campaign clock:** “bread tension” in Verbobonc—something the party can actually influence

##### The Three Guild Leaders (Run as a Triangle)

These three gnomes run the hall together—each beloved by a different slice of the quarter.

##### Fimble Ironshanks

_The Grain-Truth Hammer_

Burly, blunt, practical. He can judge grain quality at a glance and takes pride in never being fooled by poor measures or fancy words.

*   **Description:** Burly, no-nonsense, expert in grain quality
*   **Personality:** Direct and practical
*   **Motivation:** Ensuring the highest standards for the guild’s products
*   **Politics:** Advocates for minimal magical interference in agriculture
*   **At the table:** Fimble doesn’t debate—he states. If the party tries to spin him, he stares until they stop.
*   **What he wants:** Clean standards, clean measures, and minimal magical interference.

* * *

##### Lira Greenleaf

_The Kind Hand on the Scale_

Warm, diplomatic, beloved by rural reps. Lira makes deals feel fair even when they sting.

*   **Description:** Kind-hearted, skilled negotiator
*   **Personality:** Compassionate and diplomatic
*   **Motivation:** Fair deals for farmers and millers
*   **At the table:** She asks the party questions that sound gentle—then pins them with the answer.
*   **What she wants:** Fair prices, fewer feuds, and a path that doesn’t ignite riots between temples and farmers.

* * *

##### Tibbit Copperpot

_The Clever Gear Behind the Millstone_

Inventive, mechanically inclined, always sketching improvements. Tibbit loves innovation that keeps the mills competitive without relying on magic.

*   **Description:** Inventive, mechanically inclined
*   **Personality:** Creative and resourceful
*   **Motivation:** Improving milling processes through innovation
*   **Politics:** Supports technological advancements in agriculture
*   **At the table:** He notices equipment, tools, and unusual wear marks on boots—an engineer’s eye that doubles as an investigator’s.
*   **What he wants:** Better milling, better storage, better logistics—and proof when someone is sabotaging the system.
