D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons
===============================

##### Sanctuary of the Velaeri (Atroa, Sotillion, Wenta, Telchur)

A four-winged basilica of the seasonal gods stands upon the Low Road by the South-way Gate, serving as Verbobonc’s most fashionable “neutral ground” between elven grace and gnomish merriment—where festivals, pilgrim-traffic, and whispered bargains make as much history as prayer.

  
The Basilica of the Four Seasons stands as a unique monument in Verbobonc, dedicated to the Velaeri—[Atroa](atroa--person.md), [Sotillion](sotillion-person.md), [Telchur](telchur-person.md), and [Wenta](wenta-person.md)—the [Oeridian Oerid](oeridian-oerid-ethnicity.md) gods of agriculture. This religious complex features four distinct wings, each tailored to celebrate one of the seasons with a thematic flourish. The architectural styles and decorations clash yet coalesce to reflect the diverse aspects of nature's cycle, surrounded by a deliberately unkempt garden that changes with the seasons. This basilica not only serves as a place of worship but also as a cultural hub, especially during the seasonal festivals that draw large crowds from the city.

##### Enter the Basilica

The Low Road bends, and the city’s clamour falleth away as if muffled by unseen boughs. Before thee rises a basilica white as bone-ivory, its spires and arches wrought in four manners at once—one wing soft with pastel bloom, one draped in warm splendour, one earth-rich and vine-clad, and one stark as frost with silvered ribs. A garden spreads about it, left deliberately unkempt: blossoms crowd weeds without shame; late berries hang heavy; and in a shaded corner the air is cold enough to sting thy teeth.  
  
A bell sounds—no thunderous peal, but a clear note like glass. Folk pass in and out with the wary ease of those entering a place where a stranger’s manners are weighed. An elf in layered silk, leaf-motifs stitched upon the cuff, inclines her head the barest measure.  
  
“_Choose thy season_,” she murmurs, as if ‘twere a riddle. “_And be not surprised when it chooseth thee in turn_.”

#### Strategic Location of the Basilica of the Four Seasons

Positioned strategically on the Low Road near the South-way Gate, the Basilica of the Four Seasons serves as a critical cultural and spiritual landmark between the Elven and Gnome Quarters of Verbobonc. This location not only facilitates the basilica's accessibility to diverse communities but also places it along a major thoroughfare used by travelers and traders heading southwest towards the Iron Wood and the Kingdom of Veluna.

##### What the Basilica _Is_ (and why it matters)

The Basilica of the Four Seasons is a single holy complex with **four distinct wings**, each devoted to one of the Velaeri. Its architecture “clashes yet coalesces,” intentionally—an emblem that nature’s cycle is not tidy, yet ever returning. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

It is also a **social engine**: seasonal festivals draw crowds from across the city, and alliances are forged over rites, revels, and “accidental” meetings. The autumn **Brew-fest** is the most notorious, swelling the Low Road with song, trade, and clandestine talk. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

##### Situation & Approach

**Position:** Low Road near the **South-way Gate**, set between the Elven Enclave and Gnomesberg. This makes it a **cultural bridge** and a busy stop for pilgrims, travelers, and merchants bound southwest toward Iron Wood and Veluna. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Traffic:** On ordinary days, you’ll find: elven petitioners, gnome guildfolk, farmwives from outlying steadings, caravan outriders, and quiet clergy moving like weather. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

### Exterior & Grounds

##### The Deliberately Unkempt Garden

A ring of pathways runs through “wild” beds that change by season. The disorder is _curated_—a lesson in humility for city folk who would force the world into straight lines. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Four Garden Signs (subtle, always present):**

*   **Spring:** a renewed pool and early blossoms (even out of season, you may spy a single bud that “refuseth” winter). D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons
*   **Summer:** warm stone benches, sun-tinted tiles, and scented vines. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons
*   **Autumn:** creeping vines and fruiting shrubs—sweet, heavy, tempting. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons
*   **Winter:** pale gravel, silvered branches, and a wind-bare corner that seemeth colder than the rest. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**DM Note:** The garden is where most _talk_ happens. Clergy “chance” upon folk here, and questions are asked with a smile and no ink.

* * *

#### Interior Layout (High-Level)

The basilica’s heart is a central nave where all four seasons meet—an airy crossing with a great round window of shifting hues (the “turning wheel”). From the nave, four archways lead to the wings. Each wing has:

1.  a threshold space (often used to test manners),
2.  a sanctuary,
3.  a small clergy hall,
4.  a service court (stores, offerings, and private entrances).

### The Four Wings (Each is a different social dungeon)

##### Architectural Splendor

*   **Spring Wing** **(**[Atroa](atroa--person.md)**'s Sanctuary):** Adorned with flowering plants, renewing pools, and pastel colors symbolizing rebirth and new beginnings.
*   **Summer Wing (**[Sotillion](sotillion-person.md)**'s Retreat):** Features bright colors, comfortable seating, and luxurious tapestries embodying warmth and relaxation.
*   **Autumn Wing (**[Wenta](wenta-person.md)**'s Hall)**: Rich with earthy tones, creeping vines, and a garden that yields sweet fruits, reflecting the harvest and bounty of autumn.
*   **Winter Wing (**[Telchur](telchur-person.md)**'s Domain)**: Characterized by its stark white and silver decor, often with windows open to the chill of winter to embrace the cold and stark beauty of the season.

##### Spring Wing — **Atroa’s Sanctuary**

**Theme:** Renewal, dawn, east wind, new beginnings. Atroa  
**Look & Feel:** flowering plants, renewing pools, pastel colours, soft light; the air tastes faintly of rain. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Holy Symbol Motifs:**

*   heart with an air-glyph;
*   fruitful kara tree imagery;
*   eagle-feather carvings near vents and high windows (Atroa’s herald). Atroa

**Rites & Conduct:**

*   Dawn prayers are prized; the clergy prefer supplicants who rise early and speak plain.
*   Confessions here are treated as _seeds_: the priest will ask what you shall _do_, not what you did. (Atroa’s dogma: renewal with responsibility.) Atroa

**Erolith Whisperwind (High Priest of Atroa, Clr 5)  
**Gentle, steady, and difficult to offend—yet she remembers every promise made within her hearing. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Sample play (2nd-person dialogue):  
**You kneel by the renewing pool. The water is cold, but not unkind.

Erolith’s voice comes soft as a breeze.  
“Thou wouldst be forgiven, aye. Yet forgiveness is but the gate. What road wilt thou walk thereafter?”

If you speak of vengeance, her gaze does not harden—only saddens.  
“Then plant it not here. Vengeance grows thorns, and thorns are a winter thing.” Atroa

**TOEE Thread (DM):** A wounded messenger (real or feigned) seeks spring-blessings to “recover” from bandit-work. Erolith suspects a hidden patron behind the violence and quietly tests the party with questions about roads, caravans, and “tokens carried by men who pretend to be hungry.” (Use to foreshadow the Temple’s logistics and courier-work.)

* * *

##### Summer Wing — **Sotillion’s Retreat**

**Theme:** Ease, comfort, south wind, delight. Sotillion  
**Look & Feel:** bright colours, luxurious tapestries, comfortable seating; the atmosphere inviteth lingering. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Holy Symbol Motifs:**

*   winged tiger (pure orange), carved in relief above cushioned alcoves;
*   vine and blossom tracery that looks almost lazy—until you notice the craft. Sotillion

**Rites & Conduct:**

*   Disputes are mediated here—Sotillion’s clergy are famed for smoothing tempers with charm and gentle counsel. Sotillion
*   Petitioners are offered fruit-wine and shade; only fools refuse three times.

**Maris Sunflare (High Priestess of Sotillion, Clr 4)  
**Charismatic and easygoing, she cultivates a courtly hush where secrets slip out like loose hairpins. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Sample play (2nd-person dialogue):  
**Maris reclines like a queen at a summer picnic, though her eyes miss naught.

“Thou art road-weary,” she says, and it is both kindness and assessment.  
“Sit. Take ease. For if thou canst not rest, how shall I believe thy soul is not hunted?”

When you ask for answers, she smiles—slow and bright.  
“Answers? Nay. I trade in comforts. Tell me what pain pursueth thee, and I shall see what relief may be found… for a fair consideration.” Sotillion

**TOEE Thread (DM):** This wing is the easiest place for cult agents to “blend” during festivals (ease breeds carelessness). Maris has begun to notice strangers who never sweat, never drink, and never laugh—yet always stand where they can see the doors.

* * *

##### Autumn Wing — **Wenta’s Hall**

**Theme:** Harvest, brewing, west wind, fellowship and mirth. Wenta  
**Look & Feel:** earthy tones, creeping vines, garden yields sweet fruits; warm light, the faint perfume of malt. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Holy Symbol Motifs:**

*   great mug of beer—carved in wood, embossed on offering-plates;
*   straw-braids hung as blessings;
*   wandering motifs (Wenta’s traveler’s spirit) in wind-swirl murals. Wenta

**Rites & Conduct:**

*   Sundown prayers often end in song.
*   **Brewfest** is sacred and riotous: “laughter is prayer” in Wenta’s house, and hospitality is law.

**Celia Harvestsong (High Priestess of Wenta, Clr 5)  
**Jovial and commanding, she runs Brewfest like a general runs a siege—only with barrels, not battering rams. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Sample play (2nd-person dialogue):  
**Celia thrusts a mug at you before you’ve time to protest.

“Drink, traveller! If thy tongue be true, the ale will love thee. If it be false…”  
She laughs, bright as a harvest moon.  
“…then it shall betray thee. For ale hath a way of loosening the knots men tie about their hearts.” Wenta

**TOEE Thread (DM):** Brewfest crowds are perfect cover for moving contraband—small crates marked as “yeast” or “festival spices” that are, in truth, coded messages, alchemical powders, or Temple scrip. Celia suspects _someone_ is using her feast as a mask and will enlist the party—quietly, so as not to shame the church.

* * *

##### Winter Wing — **Telchur’s Domain**

**Theme:** Cold, endurance, north wind, necessary hardship. Telchur  
**Look & Feel:** stark white and silver; windows often left open to the chill; the air is crisp and stern. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Holy Symbol Motifs:**

*   leafless tree in a field of snow (carved in pale stone
*   icicle ribs along arches;
*   wind-whistle vents that moan softly when the north gusts. Telchur

**Rites & Conduct:**

*   Observances are solemn: first frost, winter solstice, Needfest reflections. Telchur
*   Telchur’s dogma teaches preparation and respect for winter’s lesson. Telchur

**Thuron Frostbeard (High Priest of Telchur, Clr 3)  
**Stern, reserved, and utterly uncharmed by eloquence. He likes deeds that can be measured: stores laid by, roofs mended, fires stacked. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons

**Sample play (2nd-person dialogue):  
**Thuron stands by an open window; frost beads upon his beard like tiny knives.

“You seek blessing,” he says. “For what? A journey?”  
When you nod, he watches you as one might watch a candle in wind.  
“Then carry less. Speak less. Trust less. Winter slays the boastful first.” Telchur

**TOEE Thread (DM):** Telchur’s wing attracts those who fear what is coming. A contact here may whisper of “hard men in the hills” and “fires that burn without warmth.” Use Thuron as the priest most likely to speak openly of _threat_—but he will demand _preparation_ in exchange for counsel.

#### Clergy, Rivalries, and “Seasonal Etiquette”

Each wing is managed by its high priest(ess) with supporting clerics. D1 Basilica of the Four Seasons  
They cooperate publicly—yet the **seasonal temperaments** cause constant, subtle friction:

*   Spring clergy resent Summer’s indulgence when folk shirk responsibility.
*   Summer clergy mock Winter’s austerity (quietly, behind tapestries).
*   Autumn clergy believe all three need a mug and a grin. Wenta
*   Winter clergy believe all three need less talk and more wood. Telchur

**Seasonal Etiquette (simple table tool):**

*   If a PC behaves “in-season” (spring: sincere vows; summer: gracious calm; autumn: generous fellowship; winter: practical stoicism), that wing grants easier access and kinder answers.
*   If a PC violates it, the wing does not expel them—rather, it **redirects** them to the season they “deserve.”

### Player Interaction Hooks (Why PCs come here)

*   **Sanctuary & healing** (Atroa’s renewals; clerical aid). Atroa
*   **Mediation** between factions (Sotillion’s ease; elven/gnome disputes). Sotillion
*   **Information** in crowds (Brewfest, pilgrim-tales, caravan whispers).
*   **Ominous counsel** about winter roads, shortages, and hard choices (Telchur’s warnings). Telchur

##### If they linger…

They become part of the basilica’s rhythm: noticed by clergy, remembered by gardeners, and quietly measured by elven patrons who treat this place as a “safe room” for city politics.

##### What goes wrong…

*   A festival crowd becomes a **screen** for a covert meeting, a theft, or a quiet assassination attempt.
*   A traveler brings news of violence on the road; each wing interprets it differently—and the party gets tugged between them.
*   The Vigil Wardens are called to control the press of bodies, and old civic tensions flare at the gate.

##### Clergy and Rituals

*   **Roles and Responsibilities:** Each wing is managed by a high priest, supported by a cadre of clerics who maintain the sanctuaries and conduct seasonal rituals.
*   **Seasonal Celebrations:** Major festivities occur at the onset of each season, with the autumn 'Brew-fest' being the highlight, attracting throngs of celebrants from across the city.

#### Historical and Cultural Significance

*   **Foundation and Purpose:** Dedicated to the Velaeri, the church underscores the agricultural heritage and the natural cycles vital to the region.
*   **Cultural Impact:** Acts as a focal point for seasonal celebrations that reinforce community bonds and cultural heritage.

#### Political and Social Dynamics

*   **Relationships with the Community:** The basilica is a respected institution in Verbobonc, though its upkeep and the nature-focused ethos sometimes clash with urban sensibilities.
*   **Influence and Interactions:** Engages in subtle political maneuvering, mostly through alliances formed during its well-attended festivals and communal events.

Through its architectural grandeur and seasonal devotion, the Basilica of the Four Seasons stands as a testament to the deep-rooted connection between the divine, nature, and the people of Verbobonc, making it a cornerstone of both spiritual practice and community life.

##### Optional 5e-Friendly Mechanics (DM Toolbox)

##### The Turning Blessing (once per long rest, by wing)

A PC who performs a fitting act (not merely coin) may receive one small boon:

*   **Atroa (Spring):** advantage on one saving throw vs disease/poison/withering effects until next dawn (or “renewal” themed). Atroa
*   **Sotillion (Summer):** advantage on one Persuasion check made to ease tension, negotiate, or prevent violence (comfort/diplomacy). Sotillion
*   **Wenta (Autumn):** advantage on one check to gather rumors, win fellowship, or secure safe lodging among common folk (harvest fellowship). Wenta
*   **Telchur (Winter):** gain cold resistance for 1 hour, or advantage on one Con check vs exhaustion from weather (endurance). Telchur

##### Social Access: “Choose thy Season”

If the party seeks _sensitive_ aid (letters of passage, quiet introductions, hidden lore), require they first win **two wings**—proving they can bear more than one mood of the world.
