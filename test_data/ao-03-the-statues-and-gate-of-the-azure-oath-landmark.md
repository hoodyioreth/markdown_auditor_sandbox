AO-03. The Statues & Gate of the Azure Oath
===========================================

The Statues and Gate of the Azure Oath form the symbolic heart of the surface ruin. This is not merely an entrance — it is the spiritual boundary between sacred vow and profane conquest.

When the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) was constructed in -12 CY, the approach was intentionally theatrical. Pilgrims, knights, and mourners would ascend the ridge along the [Worn Cobblestone Ascent](ao-01-the-worn-cobblestone-ascent-landmark.md) under wind and sky, confronted by twelve kneeling figures — the paladins who died during what later came to be called the Lightning Kneel. These statues were erected after the Long War, commemorating the stand where twelve knights held this ridge against Keoish forces long enough for Velondi lines to regroup.

#### The Desecrated Threshold

The wind stills as you crest the final rise. Before you, twelve stone knights kneel in a half-circle upon the ridge. Each figure rests upon one knee, sword tip pressed into stone, hands folded over the pommel. Their faces are worn smooth by time, their features nearly erased.  
  
One statue lies shattered. Two others bear fresh scars — lightning symbols hacked away and replaced with a crude triangular mark carved into their bases.  
  
Beyond them stands the priory gate — a heavy stone arch set into a weathered wall. The original doors are gone. In their place stands a reinforced wooden barrier banded in iron. A narrow slit above the arch watches the ascent. The air smells of cold stone… and smoke. The kneeling knights seem to face not the valley — but you.

The statues were meant to do three things:

1.  Remind entrants that this ground was purchased with blood.
2.  Humble those who approached the tombs below.
3.  Declare that vigilance does not end with death.

The gate beyond them marked the transition from the world of conflict to the world of sacred memory. That transition no longer exists.

In 576 CY, Romag’s Earth Temple reavers assaulted this site. The original doors were burned. One statue was deliberately toppled during the desecration ritual. Lightning sigils were defaced and replaced with the triangular mark of the Black Earth. The gate was reinforced not for sanctity, but for military control.

Tharvok has deliberately left the statues standing.

This is important. The Earth Temple does not wish to erase the past here — it wishes to dominate it. The kneeling paladins now frame a gate used for raiding caravans, imprisoning captives, and transporting plunder to the crypts below.

This location functions as:

*   A choke point for all movement into the priory.
*   A defensive observation post.
*   A psychological statement of conquest.
*   A symbolic corruption of Heironeous’ militant virtue.
*   A daily reminder to the Black Earth garrison of what they have overcome.

From a campaign perspective, this threshold sets the tone for everything that follows. If your players feel the weight of history here — the sense of wrongness layered over holiness — then the rest of the site will carry emotional gravity.

#### Spirit of Sir Halbrecht Tane

_The Final Stand_

* * *

Sir Halbrecht Tane was the last of the Twelve to fall during the Lightning Kneel. When the enemy broke the line and encircled the ridge, Halbrecht remained standing even as the others knelt. Witness accounts from the Long War describe him fighting until surrounded, then kneeling deliberately, planting his blade into the stone and uttering his final vow.

His spirit has not passed.

The desecration of the priory and the defacement of the statues have disturbed his vigil. He does not rise in rage. He rises in expectation. Sir Halbrecht does not haunt the living. He tests them.

He does not manifest randomly. He appears when honor is invoked. The spirit manifests if any of the following occur:

*   A character spends 1 minute studying the statues respectfully.
*   A character attempts to repair or upright the toppled statue.
*   A character prays to Heironeous at the gate.
*   A Black Earth cultist desecrates the statues in the party’s presence.
*   The party defeats Earth Temple forces at the threshold

* * *

##### Campaign Use

Sir Halbrecht can:

*   Reappear if the altar is cleansed.
*   Aid during final battle against Tharvok.
*   Reveal the true nature of a relic blade.
*   Stand silently behind the party in a moment of near defeat.

He is not a wandering ghost. He is the line.

### DM Notes

AO-03 is the emotional gateway to the entire dungeon. This is where the campaign shifts from "traveling to a location" to "standing on desecrated ground." Everything the party feels about the priory — the sense of violated sanctity, the weight of history, the obligation to set things right — should begin here, at the feet of twelve kneeling knights who died holding this ridge.

 

#### Pacing: The Threshold Moment

Do not rush this room. The [Worn Cobblestone Ascent](ao-01-the-worn-cobblestone-ascent-landmark.md) (AO-01) and the [Ridge Perimeter](ao-02-the-ridge-perimeter-landmark.md) (AO-02) build tension through terrain and patrol avoidance. AO-03 pays it off with spectacle and meaning. Read the boxed text slowly. Let the image of twelve kneeling statues — one shattered, two defaced — settle before the party acts. If they want to investigate, let them. The tracking DCs are tiered specifically so that every party finds something, and skilled trackers find a lot.

This is also the first room where the sacred/corrupted contrast hits hard. The statues were built to honor sacrifice. The Earth Temple left them standing to mock it. That tension is the emotional engine of the entire dungeon.

 

#### When to Deploy Sir Halbrecht

The Halbrecht encounter is one of the strongest moments in the priory. Use it carefully:

*   **First visit (recommended):** If the party approaches respectfully — examining statues, praying, showing reverence — Halbrecht manifests. This sets the spiritual stakes early and gives the party a divine mandate. The Blessing of the Final Stand (+1 AC, advantage vs. fear, death save reprieve) rewards engagement with the lore and provides mechanical support for the dungeon ahead.
*   **Deferred:** If the party rushes through or comes in loud, save Halbrecht for a later return visit — after the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) is cleansed, or during a climactic battle at the gate. A spirit rising from the statues during a desperate last stand is a campaign-defining moment.
*   **Do not use both.** Halbrecht should manifest once per campaign at this location. If he appeared on the first visit, he does not reappear here — but he may manifest elsewhere (at the altar, in the crypts, or in a vision).

 

#### The Gate as Tactical Problem

If the party is approaching the priory as an assault rather than an infiltration, AO-03 is the first hard tactical decision. The gate is reinforced, manned, and observed. The signal horn behind the gate can alert the entire [courtyard](ao-06-the-outer-courtyard-landmark.md) in one round. A frontal assault here triggers a full garrison response — and the party has not yet seen what is inside.

Smart parties will recognize this and look for alternatives: the [ridge perimeter](ao-02-the-ridge-perimeter-landmark.md) may offer wall-climbing options, the stable wing has a less-guarded approach, and the [herb patch](ao-04-the-custodianE28099s-herb-patch-landmark.md) wall may be crumbling enough to breach. AO-03 should feel like the obvious way in — and the worst one.

 

#### Connecting to the Broader Campaign

The Lightning Kneel and the Twelve are not just dungeon dressing — they connect to the Azure Oath's history across the Viscounty. If the party recovers names from the statue bases (Investigation DC 13, see sidebar), those names reappear in the crypts below, on relics found in later rooms, and potentially in Heironeous temples across Verbobonc where the Twelve are still remembered. The ossuary beneath the toppled statue (DM Hook) can contain a relic tied to one of the named knights — a blade, a shield, or a holy symbol that becomes significant in the campaign's larger arc.

**Halven Sere** knew every name. If rescued later, he can tell the party the full story of the Lightning Kneel — and his grief at seeing the statues defaced is a powerful roleplay moment.

 

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I try to fix the toppled statue."** Athletics DC 18 to upright it with help (2+ characters). Triggers Halbrecht if not already manifested. The statue cannot be fully repaired without a Mending or Stone Shape spell, but uprighting it is a symbolic act that matters.
*   **"I check the toppled statue's base."** Investigation DC 15 reveals the ossuary entrance beneath (DM Hook). The ossuary is sealed with a stone plug that requires DC 16 Strength or Stone Shape to open.
*   **"I want to sneak past the gate."** The gate itself is the choke point — there is no sneaking "past" it without going around entirely. Redirect to alternative approaches: ridge wall, stable wing, or herb patch breach.
*   **"I Detect Evil/Good."** Faint holy residue from the statues. Moderate desecration from the gate and the Earth glyphs. The contrast is stark — sacred ground being consumed by something patient and heavy.

#### The Twelve Who Knelt

_The Paladins of the Lightning Kneel_

Though weather has worn their features smooth, the statues once bore names carved into their bases. Most inscriptions are faded or defaced — but fragments remain. If the players examine the statues (Investigation DC 13), they can recover partial names and symbols.

Each statue represents a knight who died in the circle defense during the Long War.

Below are the Twelve.

* * *

###### 1\. Sir Garrent Halvorn

_The First to Kneel_

Shield raised, blade planted. Garrent held the northern flank until overrun. His statue bears the deepest weathering, as if the wind favors him.

* * *

###### 2\. Dame Alinor Vexley

_The Unbroken Voice_

A battle-priest of Heironeous. Known for chanting litanies during combat. Her statue’s lips are carved slightly parted.

* * *

###### 3\. Sir Corvin Talred

_The Watchful_

Carved with head slightly turned, as if scanning the horizon. His helm crest remains faintly visible.

* * *

###### 4\. Dame Yseldra Korran

_The Lightning’s Daughter_

Youngest of the Twelve. Her blade is carved thinner than the rest. One hand rests tighter on the pommel.

* * *

###### 5\. Sir Marden Thrice-Wounded

_The Steadfast_

Legend says he took three mortal strikes before kneeling. His statue shows subtle carved gouges along the torso.

* * *

###### 6\. Dame Othrene Valis

_Bearer of the Azure Standard_

Standard-bearer of the ridge defense. Her statue once held a carved banner, now broken away.

* * *

###### 7\. Sir Pelmon Draith

_The Quiet Blade_

Known for silent discipline. His stone helm lacks ornamentation.

* * *

###### 8\. Dame Theryn Holt

_Mercy in Steel_

Healed allies before returning to combat. A faint lightning sigil remains at her base.

* * *

###### 9\. Sir Edric Vaul

_The Last Laugh_

Records say he laughed as the enemy closed in. His statue’s face retains the faintest curve at the mouth.

* * *

###### 10\. Dame Calis Arden

_Oathkeeper_

Swore to die on the ridge if ordered to retreat. She was never ordered.

* * *

###### 11\. Sir Joram Kell

_Stonewall of Veluna_

Largest of the Twelve. His statue is broader and more imposing than the others.

* * *

###### 12\. Sir Halbrecht Tane

_The Final Stand_

The last to fall. His statue is positioned at the center of the half-circle — the spiritual focal point.

#### Spirit of Sir Halbrecht Tane Manifestation

The wind stills. The air grows heavy, and the sound of the valley fades. A faint crack of thunder echoes across the ridge — though no lightning splits the sky.  
  
From the center statue, a pale blue light seeps through the cracks of weathered stone. The kneeling figure trembles, and for a heartbeat, the carved face regains sharpness. A translucent knight rises from the statue — armor unmarred, blade glowing faintly with azure light. He does not raise his weapon. He studies you.  
  
_“Who stands upon the ridge?”_

##### Roleplaying Sir Halbrecht

Halbrecht speaks in measured, restrained tones.

He does not know the year is 576 CY.

He believes the ridge is still under threat.

He asks three questions:

1.  “Do you kneel before honor, or before power?”
2.  “Is the oath yet kept?”
3.  “Do you come as conqueror, pilgrim, or thief?”

He is testing alignment of intent, not alignment category.

* * *

###### If the Party Shows Respect

Examples:

*   They speak of cleansing the temple.
*   They promise to restore the priory.
*   They reject Earth Temple corruption.
*   They upright the toppled statue.
*   They pray sincerely.

##### Halbrecht lowers his blade.

The knight’s gaze softens. _“Then the ridge is not lost.”  
  
_He lifts his sword, and faint blue lightning dances along its edge. _“Hold the line.”  
  
_The wind returns.

##### Blessing of the Final Stand (Temporary Boon)

Each respectful character gains:

*   Advantage on first saving throw against fear in this location.
*   +1 bonus to AC for one combat encounter within the priory.
*   Once, when reduced to 0 HP within the priory, may remain standing at 1 HP instead.

The boon fades after leaving the site.

* * *

##### If the Party Is Disrespectful or Desecrates

If they mock, defile, or side openly with the Earth Temple:

Halbrecht’s form grows brighter and his blade rises.

He will not pursue beyond the statue circle.

He defends the threshold only.

* * *

##### Combat (If It Occurs)

Use a modified **Specter** or **Wraith-lite** stat block appropriate to party level.

Adjust to:

*   Radiant damage instead of necrotic.
*   Immune to fear.
*   Cannot leave statue circle.
*   Attacks represent test of worth, not hatred.

If reduced to 0 HP:

He does not dissipate in rage. He kneels once more and fades silently.

* * *

##### Hidden Lore (If Questioned Respectfully)

He may reveal:

*   A concealed ossuary beneath the toppled statue.
*   That the altar below has been corrupted.
*   That the tomb of “the Azure Standard” holds something still pure.
*   That the “earth beneath now groans with false oath.”

He cannot leave the ridge. He cannot descend. His vigil is eternal.
