===================

### A Beacon for Sailors and Adventurers

The Electrum Eel is the laughter of the river made timber and stone. Where the long pier bends slightly toward the Velverdyva’s current, blue lantern-light spills across wet planks and fog-swept cobbles. Sailors swear they can see its glow before they see the walls of Verbobonc itself. Adventurers returning from the Kron Hills, smugglers slipping through the docks at night, merchants calculating their profits, and hired swords waiting for their next commission—all pass beneath its balconies. This inn is more than lodging. It is rumor, refuge, and rendezvous. And beneath its music and river songs, deals are struck.

##### Arrival at The Electrum Eel Inn

Blue light glows through the river mist like twin stars fallen to earth. Two great lanterns hang from the carved wooden balconies above, their magical flames steady even as the wind rolls in from the Velverdyva. The plastered walls are etched with intricate wooden trim—waves, fish, and curling river motifs that gleam faintly in the lanternlight.  
  
When you push through the heavy oak door, warmth hits you first—heat from the hearth and bodies pressed close at long communal tables. Laughter rises above fiddle music. Ship’s rigging drapes the rafters. Stuffed river fish stare down from mantles. Nautical charts line the walls, inked with distant ports from Dyvers to far Baklunish lands.  
  
Tankards thud. A serving girl darts between dancers. Sailors sing off-key. No one seems surprised to see armed travelers enter. This is a place where coin flows like water. And water always finds the lowest channel.

#### A Nautical Haven

The building's exterior features plastered walls and intricate wooden carvings, with darkened windows that offer a glimpse of the lively interior filled with laughter and merriment.

#### Cinematic Scene: _“Blue Lanterns and Bitter Words”_

* * *

##### Narration (Read Before Dialogue)

The music falters before you notice why. A low ripple passes through the taproom—not panic, not yet—but the subtle tightening of shoulders when men accustomed to knives sense the air turning sharp.  
  
Near the hearth, three gnomes in merchant coats sit stiff-backed at a long table. Their hats—fine Kron Hills felt—rest beside untouched mugs. Across the room, a knot of human rivermen and caravan hands lean on their elbows, tankards half-drained, eyes bright with drink and grievance.  
  
At the center of it all stands a broad-shouldered riverman with a red sash at his belt. He slams his mug down hard enough to rattle the spoons. Demetrios watches from behind the bar. No one moves yet. The blue lanterns outside sway in the wind.

* * *

##### Dialogue (Continuous for DM Roleplay)

The riverman wipes ale from his beard and grins crookedly at the gnomes across the room.

“Funny thing about caravans,” he says loudly, not looking at them but clearly speaking to them. “They seem to disappear when gnome wheels are on the road. Funny how that is.”

One of the gnomes rises slowly. His voice is calm, clipped, educated.

“Your patrols withdrew from the Long Road. We asked for escort. We were denied. Our wagons were attacked by beasts you failed to drive off. And now you accuse us?”

The riverman snorts. “Beasts? Aye. Two-legged ones with gem-cutters’ hands and hill tracks.”

A murmur of agreement rolls from the human tables. Another gnome stands now, cheeks flushed. “You tax us. Undercut our trade. Withdraw guards. And when goods fail to arrive, you blame us. That is not commerce. That is sabotage.”

The riverman leans forward, voice rising.

“Sabotage? I’ll tell you sabotage. A patrol gets ambushed last month near Nigb’s Run. Men stabbed in the dark. And what do we find nearby? Gnome cart tracks.”

The first gnome’s voice sharpens.

“Tracks are not proof. Your bishop investigates even now. Or does justice bend for humans only?”

That earns a sharp intake of breath from several patrons. A chair scrapes loudly. Another human stands.

“You lot threaten to reroute trade to Dyvers if you don’t get your way. Think we don’t hear that?”

“And perhaps we should,” the gnome replies. “Perhaps Verbobonc no longer deserves Kron silver.”

The room goes very still. Demetrios steps from behind the bar but says nothing yet. The riverman’s hand drifts to his belt.

“You think you can hold coin over us? You think your Prince Jimm can strong-arm the Viscount?”

The gnome replies coldly.

“We prefer diplomacy. But we will not be bullied.”

The riverman’s grin vanishes.

“Diplomacy’s dead.”
