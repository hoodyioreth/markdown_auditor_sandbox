C16 The Gathering Hall of Verbobonc
===================================

##### _The Forum of Verbobonc_

The **Gathering Hall of Verbobonc** is not the seat of power—but it is where power is _displayed_, _contested_, and _pressured_.

Situated in the Civic Centre, this broad civic hall serves as Verbobonc’s **public forum and committee house**, a place where guilds petition, temples testify, merchants argue precedent, and magistrates listen with carefully neutral expressions. It is here that the city’s many factions are allowed to speak, debate, and be seen—without ever stepping inside the guarded walls of Castle Greyfist.

For adventurers, the Gathering Hall is often their **first brush with legitimacy**. A complaint heard here may one day reach City Hall’s courts… or, in rare cases, echo all the way up the hill to the Viscount’s castle.

##### Approaching the Gathering Hall

The wide civic steps rise before you, worn smooth by generations of boots both humble and gilded. Tall banners bearing the green-and-gold arms of Verbobonc hang unmoving in the still air. The great doors stand open, guarded not by soldiers but by clerks and heralds—men and women whose ink-stained fingers wield as much authority as any blade.  
  
From within comes the low murmur of voices: debate measured and sharp, laughter restrained, and the occasional echo of a gavel striking oak. This is not a place for shouting. This is a place where words become law.

#### Historical Significance

The Forum of Verbobonc, established in the early days of the city’s founding, has been a cornerstone of local governance and community interaction. It has witnessed countless debates, decisions, and declarations that have shaped the course of Verbobonc's history.

#### **Interior & Layout**

**The Grand Chamber**

*   A long rectangular hall with a raised dais at the far end for the Viscount and presiding officers.
*   Tiered seating for the **Council of Lords**, arranged by precedence rather than house size.
*   Tall windows of leaded glass depict historic victories, treaties, and trials.
*   A central speaking floor where petitioners, envoys, and accused parties must stand.

**The Clerk’s Arcade**

*   Side galleries filled with scribes recording every word spoken.
*   Legal advocates confer quietly with clients while waiting to be recognized.
*   Notices of upcoming sessions, judgments, and civic decrees are posted daily.

**Private Committee Rooms**

*   Smaller chambers for closed-door negotiations.
*   Often used to settle matters before they ever reach open debate.
*   Favored by politically experienced PCs—or those with powerful sponsors.

#### **Government Function: Where Civic Power Is Heard (But Not Decided)**

The Gathering Hall occupies a critical—and deliberately limited—place in Verbobonc’s government.

It is **not** where the Viscount rules, nor where the Council of Lords formally convenes, nor where final judgments of High Law are rendered. Instead, it functions as the **civic pressure valve** of the Viscounty: a space where grievances, proposals, and testimonies are gathered, filtered, and shaped before being passed upward.

##### **Primary Uses of the Gathering Hall**

*   **Public Hearings & Delegations  
    **Guild representatives, temple advocates, foreign envoys, and civic groups present arguments, complaints, and proposals before appointed magistrates or council committees.
*   **Committee Councils  
    **Subcommittees of the **Chamber of 13** meet here to examine matters of trade, infrastructure, taxation, river law, and civic order before any ruling is issued at City Hall.
*   **Civic Arbitration  
    **Disputes too politically sensitive—or too visible—for a private chamber are aired here under controlled conditions.
*   **Announcements & Address  
    **Decrees already approved elsewhere may be proclaimed publicly from this hall, lending the appearance of transparency without surrendering authority.

* * *

#### **Relationship to Other Seats of Power**

*   [V1-3 Castle Greyfist, Citadel of the Viscount](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md)  
    The **Council of Lords** and the Viscount conduct formal Viscounty governance within Castle Greyfist. When the lords gather in force, it is _there_, under the Viscount’s roof, that decisions of war, succession, and realm policy are truly made.
*   [C2 City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md)  
    The **Chamber of 13 & Advocates** maintain their permanent courts and legal machinery at City Hall. Judges, clerks, and advocates are constant visitors to the Gathering Hall, but verdicts, indictments, and writs originate at City Hall—not here.
*   **The Gathering Hall  
    **Exists between these powers: **visible, accessible, but carefully constrained**. It allows the city to speak without allowing the city to rule.

#### Platform for Advocacy

*   Used by local leaders and activists to rally support for causes and campaigns.
*   A place where policy changes are proposed and debated openly before implementation.

#### Architecture and Layout

The design of the Forum facilitates its multifunctional use, with areas designated for different types of gatherings, from small group discussions to large public addresses.

##### Key Features

*   A central dais for speakers, prominently positioned for visibility.
*   Benches and standing areas designed to accommodate a large number of people.

#### Notable Figures

The Forum has been the proving ground for many of Verbobonc’s most influential leaders and speakers, who have used this platform to inspire, persuade, and mobilize the populace.

##### Custodians of the Forum

*   A dedicated group of city officials from [C2 City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md) and volunteers who maintain the order and schedule of events.
*   Historians who document the proceedings and ensure that the heritage of the Forum is preserved for future generations.

#### Conclusion

The Forum of Verbobonc stands as a testament to the city's commitment to open dialogue and community involvement. It remains a vital part of the civic infrastructure, encouraging active participation in governance and community life, thus fostering a sense of belonging and responsibility among the citizens of Verbobonc.

##### **A Civic Clerk**

_(For the party’s first formal appearance)_

The clerk does not rise as you approach. He adjusts his spectacles, glances at a ledger thick with seals, and sighs softly.

“Names,” he says, already dipping his quill.  
“Purpose. And tell me now—are you here to petition, to testify… or to cause inconvenience?”

He finally looks up.

“Choose carefully. The walls of this hall remember.”
