W2 Office of Harbormaster
=========================

The Office of the Harbormaster is a bustling building that functions as the central traffic control area for the entire waterfront. All river traffic, outgoing and incoming trade goods, and shipping taxes are managed here.

### The Heart of the Waterfront

The Harbormaster’s Office stands a short walk inland from the main quays — not so near the water as to smell of fish, but near enough that the cries of gulls still echo through its upper windows.

It is a broad, stone-fronted building with iron-barred lower windows and heavy oak doors reinforced with brass bands. Carved above the entry arch is a relief of crossed anchors and a sealed ledger — the symbol of lawful trade under the Viscounty of Verbobonc.

*   Dockworkers remove caps as they pass.
*   Captains straighten their coats.
*   And everyone watches the doors.

Because no ship docks, unloads, or departs without passing through this building’s authority.

Inside, the air smells of ink, wax, damp parchment, and coin. Clerks bend over tall desks. Runners carry sealed writs between tables. A rack of confiscated polearms rests against one wall — a reminder that commerce here is not merely counted, but enforced.

##### Key Features

*   **Traffic Control:** Manages all river traffic.
*   **Trade Management:** Oversees incoming and outgoing goods.
*   **Tax Collection:** Handles shipping taxes and duties.

##### Enter the Office of Harbormaster

The stone façade of the Harbormaster’s Office rises above the riverfront like a magistrate judging the tide itself. Anchors carved in relief flank the iron-bound doors, and men in tabards bearing House Haxx’s sigil stand watch beside the entrance. Merchants queue in uneasy silence, parchment clutched tight in their hands. Somewhere inside, a ledger closes with a heavy thud — and a captain’s fate is sealed with ink.

#### Structure & Atmosphere

The building is orderly, tense, and efficient — yet beneath the surface runs a current of quiet fear.

*   Long counters divide clerks from merchants.
*   Iron chandeliers burn steady lantern light.
*   A tax ledger the size of a coffin sits on a central table.
*   Two uniformed Hardheads stand near the rear doors at all times.

Behind a heavy oak partition lies the private office — where numbers become decisions.

#### Authority of the Harbormaster

The Harbormaster oversees:

*   Docking rights and pier assignments
*   Trade licenses
*   Customs duties
*   Cargo inspections
*   Maritime disputes
*   River toll coordination
*   “Emergency levies” during wartime

While technically serving the Viscount, the office operates under heavy influence from **House Haxx**, whose interests dominate river commerce. It is widely understood:

If The Gentle Tradewinds profits — the Harbormaster approves.

* * *

#### Haxx’s Hardheads Presence

A detachment of Haxx’s Hardheads is permanently stationed here.

As outlined in _Haxx’s Hardheads_ (pp. 1–3) Haxx's Hardheads:

*   They enforce tax collection across the Viscounty.
*   They conduct inventories and may “overcollect” if they suspect hidden wealth.
*   Resistance is met with confiscation and force.
*   They are loyal to Lord Marakios Willem Haxx above all.

Within the Harbormaster’s Office, they:

*   Escort merchants to “private audits.”
*   Seize undeclared cargo.
*   Impound vessels pending payment.
*   Confiscate weapons during disputes.
*   Maintain a locked strongbox for collected levies.

#### Scene: Arrival at the Waterfront

The river carries your vessel gently toward the piers of Verbobonc. Your hull rides low in the water, heavy with treasure torn from forgotten stone halls and ancient crypts. Iron-bound chests sit lashed beneath tarred canvas. A crate shifts as the current rocks you, and somewhere below deck something metallic clinks.  
  
Ahead, the Waterfront rises in tiered timber and stone. Warehouses press shoulder to shoulder along the pier. Beyond them, the great city walls rise in disciplined grey, their towers catching the late light. Dockworkers pause in their labor as your ship approaches, their eyes narrowing as they judge your draft and cargo.  
  
Before your gangplank even lowers, four armored figures step forward. Black-and-gold accents mark their cloaks. Hardheads. One man steps ahead of the others. Chainmail glints beneath his formal cloak. His posture is rigid, composed. In one hand he carries a narrow leather ledger; in the other, a sealed writ bearing the authority of the Viscounty. He waits until your ropes are secured before speaking.

_“Hold where you are. This dock operates under the authority of the Harbormaster and the Viscount of Verbobonc. I am Sergeant of the Waterfront Station.”_ **His gaze travels slowly over your deck, lingering on the heavier crates.** “_You ride low. That suggests profit.”_

**He opens the writ briefly so the seal is visible, then closes it again with deliberate calm.** _“All incoming vessels bearing undeclared cargo are subject to inspection. State your cargo. State its origin. State its declared value.”_

**His eyes lift to meet yours fully now, sharp and unreadable.** _“Adventure and looting spoils are classified as recovered assets and subject to crown levy. Failure to declare taxable goods results in seizure. Misrepresentation results in fines. Resistance results in arrest.”_

**There is no threat in his tone. Only certainty.**

_“If your accounts are accurate,” he continues evenly, “this will be brief. If they are not, this will be thorough. And I assure you — I am thorough.”_

**Behind him, the other Hardheads remain motionless. A crane creaks somewhere along the pier. The river laps softly against your hull. He waits.**

##### Subtle Bribery — A Quiet Adjustment

The Sergeant’s eyes remain steady as you step down from the gangplank. Up close, you see that the leather ledger in his hand is worn at the corners, handled often. Not the public registry — something more personal.  
  
As the inspection begins, he selects crates at measured intervals, tapping wood with the pommel of his dagger before signaling a Hardhead to open them. Each item is noted. Each weight compared against your declaration.  
  
If one of you speaks quietly — not boldly, not foolishly — and suggests that inspections can be time-consuming, that delays are costly, that appreciation can be shown for efficiency, the Sergeant does not react immediately. He closes the crate being examined and turns his attention fully upon you.

**He says calmly, loud enough that his men can hear** _“I do not accept bribes, however, expedited processing fees are within my authority to assess.”_

**His gaze lowers briefly to your purse or to whatever small token might be offered. Then he adds, more quietly,** _“A voluntary contribution to administrative burdens can reduce… unnecessary scrutiny.”_

**He opens his private ledger and makes a small mark — a symbol rather than a word.**

_“If you wish this to proceed smoothly, it must appear proper.”_

**The gold does not pass hand to hand openly. It is placed upon the inspection table beside sealed documents, described as a supplemental levy or compensatory dock assessment. One of the Hardheads records it without expression. The Sergeant closes his ledger and nods once.**

_“You will find the city accommodating to those who respect its procedures.”_

**The inspection becomes noticeably faster. One crate is weighed but not opened. Another is passed over entirely. The ship is cleared. But somewhere in that private ledger, your names now exist. And that may cost more later.**

##### Escalation — Public Confrontation

**If instead the party challenges the levy, refuses inspection, or questions his authority too loudly, the shift is immediate — though still controlled. The Sergeant straightens slightly, his voice carrying now across the pier.**

“_You are refusing lawful inspection under authority of the Viscounty?”_

**Dockworkers begin to slow their movements. A few stop entirely. The creak of ropes and scrape of crates diminishes. Eyes turn toward your vessel. The Sergeant gestures subtly, and one Hardhead steps back and blows a sharp whistle — two short bursts.**

**From beyond the warehouse row, armored figures emerge at pace. Not Hardheads this time, but city Watch — Vigil Wardens of Verbobonc, bearing the crest of the city. Their boots strike the pier in disciplined rhythm. The Sergeant does not raise his voice, but it carries cleanly.**

_“You dock at the pleasure of the Harbormaster. You unload at the pleasure of the city. If you contest that authority, you contest it before witnesses.”_

**A small crowd now gathers at a cautious distance. Dockhands whisper. A merchant nearby mutters, “Fools…” under his breath.**

**The Vigil Wardens spread into a loose half-circle behind the Sergeant, hands resting on hilts but not yet drawn.**

_“You will submit to inspection,” the Sergeant continues, calm but unmistakably resolute, “or your vessel will be impounded pending review. Cargo secured. Crew detained for inquiry.”_

**His gaze hardens just slightly.** _“You may argue with me quietly. Or you may argue with the law publicly.”_

A beat.

_“The river sees everything. And so does the city.”_

**The Hardheads step forward in measured unison. The Vigil Wardens wait for a signal.**
