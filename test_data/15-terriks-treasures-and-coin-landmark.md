15\. Terrik’s Treasures and Coin
================================

> The sign displays three yellow balls; this must be the moneychanger's. A guard in chain mail stands by the door, wearing a sword and cradling a light crossbow in his arms, with a quarrel ready. Two large dogs are sniffing in your direction.

Terrik’s Treasures and Coin serves as the primary financial institution in Hommlet, providing essential money-changing and jewelry services to villagers, travelers, and adventurers alike. Run by the unassuming but savvy Niro Terrick, this establishment is crucial to the economic stability of the village, offering a range of services with discreet efficiency.

### Location and Description

Terrik’s Treasures and Coin is situated along [Nigb’s Run](nigbE28099s-run-location.md) at the base of the hill, across the road from D&G Dry Goods & Mercantile Exchange. The shop’s proximity to Wolog’s Teamsters and the bustling marketplace makes it an ideal location for those seeking to manage their wealth.

##### Building Description

The building is new and sturdy, reflecting Niro’s investment in creating a secure and welcoming environment for his clientele. A sign above the door depicts three yellow balls, universally recognized as the symbol of a money-changer.

##### Exterior

*   **Security:** A guard stands near the door, clad in chainmail and equipped with a crossbow and arming sword. Two large mastiff-like dogs, Fang and Mutt, are always nearby, providing an additional layer of protection.
*   **Signage:** The sign depicting three yellow balls hangs prominently, signaling the establishment’s services to passersby.

##### Interior

*   **Shop Floor:** Inside, the shop is clean and orderly, with counters displaying various pieces of jewelry and currency. A strongbox and scales sit prominently on the counter, tools of Niro’s trade.
*   **Living Quarters:** Beyond the shop floor lies Niro’s modest living quarters, where he conducts his affairs and secures his wealth.

### Lore and History

Niro Terrick established his shop in Hommlet to capitalize on the growing economic activity in the region. Originally from a larger city, he recognized the potential for profit in a smaller, yet bustling village.

##### Historical Significance

Terrik’s Treasures and Coin quickly became an integral part of Hommlet’s economy, offering much-needed financial services to residents and visitors. Niro’s reputation for discretion and fairness has made his establishment a trusted cornerstone of the community.

### Motivation and Relationships

*   **Profit and Stability:** Niro is motivated by profit, seeking to maintain and grow his wealth through prudent business practices.
*   **Neutrality:** He values neutrality and avoids involvement in local conflicts, focusing instead on providing consistent and reliable services.

#### Relationships

*   **Community Trust:** Niro maintains a positive relationship with the villagers, who appreciate his services and discretion.
*   **Cautious of Traders:** While he interacts with [13\. D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile](13-d26g-dry-goods-26-general-mercantile-landmark.md), Niro remains cautious, aware of the traders’ opportunistic nature.

### Current Events

##### Business Operations

Terrik’s Treasures and Coin continues to thrive, with Niro efficiently managing transactions and crafting custom jewelry for his clients.

##### Bandit Concerns

The rising bandit activity in the region poses a threat to Niro’s business, prompting him to increase security measures and remain vigilant.

#### Conclusion

Terrik’s Treasures and Coin stands as a bastion of financial stability in Hommlet, offering essential services with discretion and efficiency. Niro Terrick’s expertise and neutrality make his establishment a trusted ally to those seeking to manage their wealth in an ever-changing landscape. As the threat of banditry looms, Niro remains committed to safeguarding his business and serving his community with integrity.

### Services Offered

##### Money-Changing

**Exchange Rates:** Niro offers currency exchange services, charging a 10% fee for transactions. He accepts various forms of currency and precious metals, providing convenient and reliable service.

##### Jewelry and Gems

 

*   **Buying and Selling:** Niro deals in gems and jewelry, purchasing items for 50% to 80% of their actual value and selling them for 110% to 120% of their value.
*   **Custom Work:** Niro also crafts jewelry, with costs reflecting the value of materials doubled. The resulting pieces are valued accordingly, providing clients with unique and personalized items.

##### Discretion and Trust

**No Questions Asked:** Niro prides himself on his discretion, asking no questions about the origins of goods or currency. His polite and obliging nature ensures clients feel comfortable conducting business.
