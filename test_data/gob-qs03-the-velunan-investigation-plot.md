GOB-QS03-The-Velunan-Investigation
==================================

> The Velunan Investigation is one of several Gift of Beauty quest starters — designed for parties with a cleric, paladin, or divine character connected to one of Verbobonc's churches or the Silver Consortium. The Diocese of Veluna has sent an investigation team to the Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao to determine why their diplomatic delegation vanished on the road south. Missing priests. A missing artifact. And a political crisis that threatens to boil over if answers are not found soon. The PC's own order — alarmed by the implications — sends their brightest and most capable acolyte to the Monastery to represent their interests and prevent this from spiraling out of control. At the Monastery, a Velunan investigator named Canon Aldric Verannis asks the PC and their companions for an escort to the ambush site. If they agree, the full weight of the Church's intelligence is shared: names, ranks, the delegation's route, what the Gift of Beauty actually is, and the terrible fate of those who carried it. This is the faith door into the Gift of Beauty questline — and it carries a warning: the last team sent south never came back.

 

### The Faith Door — A Quest Hook for Clerics, Paladins & Divine Characters

_"Reason is the greatest gift. It leads to discourse, which leads to peace, which leads to serenity... But reason alone cannot find our lost brothers. We need hands and hearts willing to walk where reason has already failed."_

 

This quest starter is designed for a DM whose party includes a **cleric, paladin, or divine character** — someone with ties to one of [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md)'s churches or the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md). The PC does not need to worship [Rao](rao-person.md) — the investigation affects all the good faiths of the Viscounty. What matters is that the PC's own order sees the Velunan investigation as a crisis that requires their best representative on the ground.

 

Unlike the wilderness starters (GOB-QS01 and GOB-QS02), this encounter takes place **inside the city of Verbobonc** — in the hallowed halls of the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md), nestled in the High Quarter between Castle Greyfist and the city's wealthiest neighborhoods. The tone is political and religious rather than wild and personal. The PC is not approached by a lone ranger in the brush — they are **summoned by their superiors** and briefed by a foreign investigator in a sanctuary of peace. The stakes are not whispered rumors of a missing artifact. They are **missing people** — priests of [Rao](rao-person.md) who vanished on a road that was supposed to be safe.

 

This is **one of several starter articles** for the Gift of Beauty questline. A DM should choose the starter that best fits their group's composition. This starter works particularly well for parties already embedded in Verbobonc's social and political fabric — characters who attend services, know their local clergy, and understand that the Church is both a spiritual institution and a political power.

### The Sending Organizations — DM Guidance

The PC is sent to the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md) by **their own order**. The specific organization depends on the PC's class, deity, and background. Choose the option that fits your player:

 

##### Option A: The Church of Rao (The [Order of Rao](the-order-of-rao-organization.md))

The most direct fit. The PC is a junior priest, acolyte, or initiate at the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md) itself — or a traveling Rao cleric who has been residing at the Monastery. Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) knows them personally. When the Velunan investigation team arrives, the Patriarch selects the PC as the Monastery's representative — the one who will stand between the Velunan investigators and whatever they find south of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md).

 

You are summoned to the Tower of Reason. Not by a fellow brother, but by the Patriarch's own hand — a folded note sealed with blue wax bearing the sigil of [Rao](rao-person.md), slipped beneath your cell door before dawn. The message is four words: "Come. The Hour Presses."   You climb the winding stair to find Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) standing at the high window, his silver hair catching the first light. He does not turn. The scent of sandalwood fills the chamber.   "I have watched you since you came to this house. You are young. You are capable. And you are — I think — the right person for a burden I would not place on anyone lightly."   He turns. His eyes — deep, thoughtful brown — are lined with fatigue you have never seen in him before.   "Veluna has sent investigators. They want to know what happened to the delegation — the priests who were carrying the Gift of Beauty south to [Celene](celene-organization.md). Our brothers, child. Priests of our own order. They left Verbobonc three tendays ago under escort of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md). They never arrived. The borderer escort was destroyed. And now Veluna wants answers that I cannot give."   He places a hand on your shoulder.   "I am asking you to be our eyes and our conscience in this matter. The Velunan investigator — Canon Aldric Verannis — wishes to travel south to find the ambush site. He needs an escort. He needs people he can trust. I am asking you to be those people. Take your companions. Protect this man. And bring me the truth of what happened to our brothers."

 

##### Option B: The Church of [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md)

The PC is a cleric or paladin of [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md) — the faith of common sense, discipline, and righteous action. St. Cuthbert's church in Verbobonc has a working relationship with the Church of Rao (Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) has publicly endorsed [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md)'s ministry). When the Velunan investigation arrives, the Cuthbertine hierarchy recognizes the danger: foreign clergy operating on Verbobonc soil, looking for missing priests, asking questions that could embarrass the Viscounty. They send their best young acolyte to represent Cuthbert's interests and keep the situation from spiraling.

 

Your superior — a grim-faced curate with arms like a blacksmith and the patience of a stone wall — finds you after morning prayers.   "The Church of Rao has a problem. Which means we have a problem. Veluna sent investigators to find out what happened to their people — priests who went missing on the road south. Patriarch Legeum has asked for our help. He needs someone with backbone, not just prayers."   The curate looks you up and down with the critical assessment of a man who has evaluated every acolyte he has ever trained.   "I am sending you. You will go to the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md) in the High Quarter. You will meet the Velunan investigator. You will represent our order with honor, discipline, and common sense. And you will make certain — absolutely certain — that this does not become a disaster for Verbobonc. St. Cuthbert's faith is built on action. Go act."

 

##### Option C: The Church of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) or [Pelor](pelor-person.md)

Paladins of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) and clerics of [Pelor](pelor-person.md) share common cause with the Church of Rao on matters of justice and light. A missing delegation of priests — possibly captured by forces of darkness — is exactly the kind of crisis that demands a holy response. The PC's temple sends them as a representative of the allied good faiths.

 

The message arrives by acolyte — breathless, wide-eyed, sent running from the High Quarter. The seal is not your temple's. It is the blue wax of the Church of [Rao](rao-person.md).   Your high priest reads it twice. Then looks at you.   "Priests of Rao are missing. An entire delegation — vanished on the road to [Celene](celene-organization.md). Veluna has sent investigators, and Patriarch Legeum is calling on the allied faiths for support. This is not just their problem. If the forces that destroyed a priestly delegation go unchecked, none of our roads are safe. None of our people are safe."   A pause. A decision made.   "You are the one I trust to see this clearly and act rightly. Go to the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md). Meet the Velunan investigator. Lend whatever aid honor demands. And remember — you carry our banner in this matter. Make us proud."

 

##### Option D: The [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) — Wizard Acolyte

The PC is a **wizard** — an acolyte, apprentice, or junior member of the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md), the prestigious mages guild and merchant-political organization that holds significant influence in [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md). The Consortium's interest is not spiritual — it is economic, arcane, and political. The Gift of Beauty's disappearance has strained the [Celene](celene-organization.md) relationship, threatened trade routes, and now drawn foreign investigators to Verbobonc's doorstep. Worse — whatever force ambushed an armed Borderer escort employed **arcane support**. A lightning bolt. Coordinated magical assault. The Consortium wants to know who these casters are, where they operate, and what kind of threat they represent to the guild's operations and the Viscounty's stability.

 

The PC is chosen specifically because they are the **brightest and most capable acolyte** the Consortium has available — young, talented, and not yet entangled in the guild's internal politics. They are expendable enough to risk on a dangerous road, but capable enough to be useful. The Consortium does not send its masters into the field. It sends its sharpest apprentices and expects results.

 

You are pulled from your morning studies by a messenger who says only two words: "Upstairs. Now."   You climb to the private offices above the guild hall — the floor the acolytes call "the Quiet" because no sound escapes the warded doors. A senior Consortium operative — one whose name you know better than to use in the corridors below — is waiting in a shuttered room. A single candle illuminates a map of the southern Viscounty pinned to the wall. The Long Road. [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md). [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md). The [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Red pins mark locations you do not yet recognize.   "Sit. Listen. Do not repeat what you hear in this room."   Your handler taps the map with a silver-ringed finger.   "The Church of [Rao](rao-person.md) has a situation. Three tendays ago, a delegation of priests and [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) was ambushed on the road south. They were carrying a diplomatic gift meant for the Queen of [Celene](celene-organization.md). The escort was destroyed. The priests are missing. The gift is gone. And now Veluna has sent investigators to find out what happened."   A pause. The handler lets that settle.   "What concerns us is the manner of the attack. Reports from the sole surviving Borderer describe a coordinated assault — flanking archers, a charge with ogres, and arcane support. A lightning bolt brought down the patrol leader. That means there are hostile casters operating on our trade routes with enough power to destroy an armed military escort. The Consortium needs to know who they are."   The handler looks at you — not with warmth, but with the cool assessment of someone selecting the right tool for a precise job.   "You are the most capable acolyte we have available. That is why you are sitting in this chair and not someone else. I am sending you to the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md) in the High Quarter. A Velunan investigator named Canon Aldric Verannis has arrived to look into the missing delegation. He needs an escort to travel south and find the ambush site. You will join that escort."   The handler leans forward.   "Your orders are simple. Three things."   A finger goes up.   "First — you represent the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) in this matter. Be professional. Be courteous. Lend whatever arcane skill you possess to the investigation. The Consortium is cooperating with the Church of Rao on this — we want to be seen as helpful, and we _are_ helpful. If these priests and the Canon need magical support, you provide it. Identification spells. Detect Magic on the ambush site. Arcane analysis of whatever weapon or spell destroyed that patrol. That is your value."   A second finger.   "Second — you report back _everything_ you learn. Every name. Every location. Every scrap of intelligence about who carried out this ambush and what arcane resources they command. I want to know if we are dealing with a rogue wizard, a cult circle, or something worse. Send reports by courier if you can. If you cannot, commit it to memory and return in person."   A third finger.   "Third — come back alive. The Consortium has invested in your training. We expect a return on that investment."   The handler slides a sealed letter across the table — the Consortium's formal introduction, bearing the silver signet.   "Present this at the Monastery. It identifies you as our representative. The Patriarch knows we are involved. He asked for our help — indirectly, through channels, the way the Church always asks. But he asked."   A thin smile.   "Not every mission the Consortium assigns comes with a spellbook reward or a promotion. But this one might. If you bring back intelligence that protects our trade routes and identifies a hostile arcane faction operating in the Viscounty — well. The masters notice results. And results open doors that talent alone cannot."

 

**DM Notes — The Wizard Acolyte Hook:**

*   This option is ideal for **wizard PCs** who need a non-religious reason to get involved. The Consortium's motivations are pragmatic: protect trade routes, identify hostile casters, and gather intelligence. The PC does not need to care about missing priests or divine artifacts — they are sent because **someone used a lightning bolt on a military escort**, and the Consortium wants to know who
*   The "three orders" structure gives the wizard PC a clear mandate: represent the guild, report everything, stay alive. This creates natural **tension with the religious NPCs** — Canon Aldric is motivated by faith and the missing priests. The wizard PC is motivated by intelligence-gathering and guild advancement. Both goals align, but the _priorities_ differ. That friction is productive roleplay
*   The sealed letter from the Consortium serves the same function as the wooden coin in the other starters — it identifies the PC as someone sent by a trusted organization. Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) accepts Consortium representatives because the Church of Rao and the Silver Consortium share a pragmatic political alliance in Verbobonc
*   The promise of "masters notice results" gives the wizard PC a **personal advancement hook** — this mission is a chance to prove themselves to the guild hierarchy. Finding hostile casters operating in the Viscounty would be a significant intelligence coup
*   At the ambush site, the wizard PC becomes the party's **arcane forensic specialist**. Detect Magic on scorched earth. Identify residual spell signatures. Analyze the type of lightning bolt used (was it arcane or divine?). Examine any magical equipment found on dead attackers. This gives the wizard a unique role in the investigation that no cleric or paladin can fill

### The Encounter: At the Monastery of Rao

#### Arrival at the Monastery

Regardless of which organization sends the PC, they arrive at the [Monastery of the Reverent Brothers of Rao](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md) in the High Quarter of [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md). The DM should read or paraphrase the following to set the scene.

 

The Monastery of the Reverent Brothers rises from the High Quarter like a sermon in stone — pale walls, arched windows, and a tower of silence that catches the morning light. The gate stands open, flanked by two members of the Viscount's High Guard in polished mail. Their presence marks this as a place under the Viscount's direct protection — not merely a church, but a site of political significance.   You pass through a central courtyard where monks in blue and white robes work at shaded tables beside a shallow reflecting pool. The scent of sandalwood and frankincense hangs in the air. Voices are low. Footsteps are measured. Even the birdsong seems quieter here.   A young brother meets you at the cloister entrance and leads you without a word through the Halls of Peace — white-plastered corridors lined with simple cells — to the Assembly Hall. The circular chamber is lit by high windows. Woven mats cover the floor. At the far end, two figures wait: one you recognize, one you do not.

 

#### The Two Men in the Assembly Hall

Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) stands to the left — the silver-haired leader of the [Monastery](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md), robed in serene blue and soft white, his brown eyes reflecting both warmth and grave concern. He is the political center of the Church of [Rao](rao-person.md) in Verbobonc — the man who invested the Viscount, who mediates the Council of Lords, who has spent a decade cultivating peace through diplomacy. Today, the weight of that peace rests visibly on his aged shoulders.

 

To the right stands a man the PC has never met: **Canon Aldric Verannis** of Mitrik, the capital of the Kingdom of Veluna.

 

The second figure is a man of middle years — perhaps forty, perhaps older — with the precise, controlled bearing of a trained investigator. He wears the blue and white vestments of a Canon of [Rao](rao-person.md), but the cut is distinctly Velunan — a narrow silver stole over squared shoulders, travel-worn boots beneath the hem, and a holy symbol that gleams with the particular polish of someone who handles it constantly. His face is lean, clean-shaven, with sharp grey eyes that assess you the moment you enter — not with suspicion, but with the careful attention of a man who needs to know quickly whether you are someone he can rely on.   He holds a leather dispatch case under one arm. It is sealed with the crimson wax of the Diocese of Veluna.

 

**DM Notes on Canon Aldric Verannis:**

*   Aldric is a **field investigator** for the Diocese of Veluna — a Canon of [Rao](rao-person.md) trained in both divine magic and forensic inquiry. He is not a diplomat. He is not a warrior. He is the man Veluna sends when they need to know _what happened_
*   He is capable but not foolish. He knows the road south is dangerous. He knows the last delegation never came back. He knows he cannot investigate the ambush site alone. He needs the party
*   His authority comes from the Diocese of Veluna — which means he represents a **foreign religious power** operating on Verbobonc soil. This is politically sensitive. Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) is cooperating, but the Viscount's office has not been formally notified. The longer this stays quiet, the better — for everyone
*   Aldric is **genuinely concerned** about the missing priests. This is not a bureaucratic exercise. He knew some of them. He has read [Artus Kellan](artus-kellan-person-1.md)'s pre-mission letters. He has been unable to sleep properly since arriving in Verbobonc

 

#### The Patriarch Introduces the Situation

Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) speaks first. His voice is calm — the practiced calm of a man who has spent decades mastering the art of delivering difficult truths without panic. The DM should read this as a man who is gravely troubled but holding himself together through force of will and faith.

 

"Thank you for coming. Please — sit."   The Patriarch gestures to a pair of cushioned mats arranged in a half-circle facing the two men. When you are settled, he speaks.   "What I am about to tell you is not common knowledge. The Viscount's office is aware of the broad situation, but the details I share now are known only to this Monastery, to the Diocese of Veluna, and — as of this morning — to you."   He folds his hands.   "Three tendays ago, a delegation left [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) bound for the court of Her Fey Majesty [Yolande](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) of [Celene](celene-organization.md). The delegation carried a diplomatic gift — the Gift of Beauty — crafted by the folk of Verbobonc and blessed by Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) of the Western [Gnarley](gnarley-forest-location.md). The delegation included an escort of [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) and — this is what concerns us most — several priests of [Rao](rao-person.md) traveling from the Kingdom of Veluna as part of the diplomatic effort."   A pause. The Patriarch's eyes close briefly.   "The delegation never reached [Celene](celene-organization.md). The Borderer escort was ambushed and destroyed on the road between [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) and the Celene border. The Gift was taken. And our priests... our brothers... have not been heard from since. We believe some were killed. We believe others may have been taken alive."   He opens his eyes.   "The Diocese of Veluna has sent Canon Verannis to investigate. I have agreed to support his work. But the Canon cannot travel south alone. The road that swallowed the first delegation will swallow a lone investigator just as easily. He needs an escort — people of faith, courage, and capable arms. I am asking you to be those people."

 

#### Canon Aldric's Briefing — The Full Intelligence

If the PC and their companions agree (or express willingness to hear more), Canon Aldric Verannis opens the sealed dispatch case and lays its contents on a low table: parchment maps, written testimonies, and a list of names in neat Velunan script. He speaks with the measured precision of a man delivering a formal report — but his hands are not entirely steady.

 

**DM Notes:** This is the information dump. David's direction was clear — if the PCs agree, the team tells them **everything**. Names, ranks, what the Gift actually is, who was in the delegation, what is known about the ambush. Aldric holds nothing back. He needs these people to understand what they are walking into. The DM should read Aldric's briefing in full — it is designed as 2nd-person read-aloud text that gives the party actionable intelligence.

 

Canon Aldric unfolds a parchment map of the southern Viscounty and smooths it across the table. His finger traces a route south from [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) to [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md).   "Let me tell you what we know. All of it."   He taps the map at [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md).   "The delegation departed the city during the Festival of Readying. Their route followed the Long Road south — through [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), into the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), and eventually to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) — the estate of Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md), Waldgraf of the southern marches. From Deleveu's estate, the final leg — ten miles to the [Celene](celene-organization.md) border — was under Borderer escort. The 4th Mounted Borderers. A full patrol."   His finger moves south. Then stops.   "They were ambushed here. Somewhere on this ten-mile stretch. A coordinated assault — flanking archers, a synchronized charge, and arcane support. A lightning bolt struck the patrol leader. The escort was overwhelmed. The Gift was taken. And our priests were either killed or captured."

 

#### The Delegation — Names and Ranks

Aldric produces a parchment from the dispatch case — a formal roster.

 

"The Rao delegation consisted of the following persons. I will name them for you because they are not abstractions. They are men and women of faith who answered a call to serve."   He reads from the parchment:   "Brother [Artus Kellan](artus-kellan-person-1.md) — priest of [Rao](rao-person.md), formerly of the Diocese of Veluna. A good man. An elderly cleric of deep conviction and long service. He was the senior priest in the delegation. We have received word — unconfirmed — that he survived the ambush but was captured by orcs and subjected to... treatment... that has left his mind broken. He is said to be alive, but no longer himself. We believe he may be in the care of a noble house somewhere in the Viscounty."   A pause. Aldric's voice tightens.   "The others — Brother Davin of Mitrik, Sister Elara Thoss, Brother Fenwick Grael — we have heard nothing. Nothing at all. They may be dead. They may be held captive. I intend to find out which."

 

**DM Notes — The Named Priests:**

*   **[Artus Kellan](artus-kellan-person-1.md)** — the surviving witness. Currently at Asbury Manor under the care of Lady Elinor Asbury, suffering from a feeblemind-like affliction from fell magic. He speaks only in chanted riddles and "Rao ramblings." His existence is known to Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) and to priestess **Seranna Valeth** (who has been divinely tasked to find him — see NSQ-05: On a Mission from Rao). If the party discovers Artus and restores his mind (Greater Restoration or similar magic), he can identify the attackers, describe the ambush in detail, and confirm what was taken
*   **Brother Davin of Mitrik, Sister Elara Thoss, Brother Fenwick Grael** — the missing priests. Their fate is a **DM Decision Point**. They may be held at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) alongside **Halven Sere** and other prisoners. They may have been moved to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). They may be dead. The DM decides — but the party should feel that **rescue is possible** if they act quickly enough

 

#### The Gift of Beauty — What It Actually Is

 

Aldric describes the artifact with the thoroughness of an investigator cataloguing evidence.

 

"Now — the Gift itself. You should know what was taken, because you may need to recognize it."   He pulls a folded sketch from the dispatch case — a charcoal rendering made from the court records at Castle Greyfist.   "The Gift of Beauty is a box carved from a single piece of Ipp wood — a rare and delicate timber. It measures nearly two hands tall, three hands wide, and five hands in breadth. The cover bears carvings of [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) — the elven Creator — seated in a glade with animals and fey folk. The sides tell stories of major events in elven history. The cover depicts the joining of the races — elves, gnomes, and humans — in the common bond to build and develop this land."   He lets that settle.   "It was crafted by the common folk of Verbobonc. Not a king's treasure — the peoples' gift. Viscount [Wilfrick](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) commissioned it as a gesture of peace to Her Fey Majesty [Yolande](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) of [Celene](celene-organization.md). The Archdruid of the Western [Gnarley](gnarley-forest-location.md) — [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) — blessed it in the name of [Obad-Hai](obad-hai-person.md). It was displayed at Castle Greyfist for nearly a ten-day. Then entrusted to Lord [Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) for transport south."   A hard exhale.   "It never arrived. The elven knights of [Celene](celene-organization.md) waited at the border. No one came. And now [Celene](celene-organization.md)'s court is asking why. Veluna is asking why. The Viscount is asking why. And no one has answers."

 

#### The Ask — Escort to the Ambush Site

After the full briefing, Aldric lays his hands flat on the table — a deliberate gesture of openness. He looks directly at the PC.

 

"I am not a soldier. I am an investigator. I find truth in evidence and testimony — in broken wagon wheels, in blood stains on stone, in the testimony of survivors. But the road south of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) is not safe for a man traveling alone with a dispatch case and a prayer."   He meets your eyes.   "I need to reach the ambush site. I need to examine the ground. I need to determine what manner of force could destroy an armed Borderer patrol and capture priests of [Rao](rao-person.md) on a road that was supposed to be under the Viscount's protection. And then I need to follow whatever trail remains — wherever it leads."   A breath.   "I am asking you — and your companions — to escort me. To protect the investigation. And to help me find our missing brothers, if any still live."   He pauses.   "I must be honest with you. The last team that traveled this road did not return. I do not know what we will find. I do not know what destroyed the delegation. But I know this: if we do not go, no one will. And if no one goes, then our brothers died in the dark for nothing. And the common folk who made that Gift — who poured their hearts into a symbol of peace — will see it vanish without anyone lifting a hand."   He straightens.   "Patriarch Legeum has assured me that any who aid this investigation will be recognized by both the Diocese of Veluna and the [Order of Rao](the-order-of-rao-organization.md). Your names will be entered in the Annals of Service. But that is not why I am asking. I am asking because people are missing. And reason demands that we act."

 

#### The Wooden Coin

If the party agrees, Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) steps forward with a familiar object — a **wooden coin** with an etching of the Oak Father dyed in green on one side.

 

The Patriarch produces a small wooden token from beneath his robes. You recognize the sigil — the Oak Father, gnarled roots spreading like veins, dyed in forest green.   "The [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) use these to identify trusted contacts. Present this at [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) when you arrive. Lord [Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) will recognize it and receive you as allies. He was the last person to hold the Gift before it was taken. He will have information that even Canon Verannis does not."   He places the coin in your palm and closes your fingers around it.   "May [Rao](rao-person.md) guide your reason. May [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md) strengthen your resolve. And may whatever gods you serve bring you safely home."

### DM Decision Point: The Velunan Recovery Team

This quest starter introduces a **ticking clock** that the previous starters (Wyn, Glorandal) do not have. The Kingdom of Veluna has invested political capital in this investigation. Canon Aldric is the first step. If the party takes too long — or if Aldric reports back that the situation is worse than expected — Veluna escalates.

 

#### The Escalation Ladder

*   **Stage 1 — Canon Aldric (current):** A single investigator with a dispatch case and the authority of the Diocese. He needs an escort. He asks politely. This is the quiet phase
*   **Stage 2 — The Velunan Clerical Mission:** If the party takes more than two tendays to produce results, Veluna sends a second team — a formal clerical mission of 6–8 priests and paladins, well-equipped and well-intentioned but **politically explosive**. Foreign clergy marching armed through Verbobonc territory, investigating a failure of the Viscounty's own security. The Viscount's office is furious. The Council of Lords is alarmed. The political damage is real — even if the Velunan team means well
*   **Stage 3 — Diplomatic Confrontation:** If the clerical mission also fails or is delayed, the Canon of Veluna (the religious head of state) sends a formal diplomatic note to Viscount [Wilfrick](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) demanding answers about the missing priests. This forces the Gift crisis into the open. Every faction in Verbobonc now knows what happened. The quiet recovery that every responsible party wanted becomes impossible

 

**The Danger:** A Velunan clerical mission traveling south past [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) faces the **same threats** that destroyed the original delegation. The Earth Temple raiders are still operating. The roads are not safe. If the Velunan team is ambushed and captured — or killed — the political crisis becomes a potential war. Veluna will not accept the loss of two delegations without a military response. The party needs to resolve this **before** the escalation reaches Stage 2.

 

**DM Notes:** The ticking clock is the strongest motivator this starter offers. Unlike Wyn or Glorandal — who present the quest as "someone should do this" — Canon Aldric presents it as "this will get worse if we don't act now." The political stakes are immediate and escalating. Use the Velunan recovery team as a background pressure: mention rumors of "more Velunan priests arriving," or have Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) send a worried message via Sending that "Veluna is losing patience." The party should feel the clock ticking.

### DM Guidance: Running This Encounter

#### Tone & Pacing

This encounter should feel like a **war council held in a temple**. The contrast is deliberate — the Monastery is a place of peace, reflection, and measured reason. And here, in its serene Assembly Hall, two men are describing an ambush, a theft, and missing prisoners. The dissonance between the setting and the content is the point. It tells the players: _things have gotten bad enough that the people of peace are asking for swords_.

 

The pacing should be **slow and heavy at the start** (the summoning, the arrival, the Patriarch's introduction) and then **gain urgency** as Aldric's briefing unfolds. By the time Aldric asks for an escort, the party should feel the weight of the situation. Total encounter time: 15–20 minutes of real table time — longer than the wilderness starters because the information is denser.

 

#### Canon Aldric on the Road

If the party accepts, Canon Aldric travels with them as an NPC companion. He is **not a combat asset**. He is a 5th-level cleric with reasonable defensive magic (Shield of Faith, Sanctuary, Healing Word) but no offensive capability beyond Sacred Flame. His role is to provide ongoing intelligence, religious context, and emotional weight. If captured, his fate becomes a powerful story beat. If killed, the Velunan escalation accelerates dramatically.

 

**Aldric's personality on the road:** Methodical, observant, quietly anxious. He catalogs everything — broken branches, old campfire sites, the condition of road markers. He prays at dawn and dusk. He asks the PC thoughtful questions about their faith, their order, and their motivations. He is a good man doing a hard job.

 

#### What If the Party Refuses?

Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md) accepts a refusal with the quiet gravity of a man who expected better.

 

_"I understand. The road is dangerous and the task uncertain. I will not compel anyone to walk into darkness."_

 

A pause.

 

_"But I would ask you to consider this: if the people of faith will not act when the people of faith are in danger — who will?"_

 

Canon Aldric says nothing. He simply gathers his dispatch case and nods once. The wooden coin is not offered. The Velunan escalation proceeds on its own timeline — and the party may hear later that Canon Aldric traveled south with a different escort, or alone, and that things did not go well.

 

#### Connecting to the Next Phase

The party's route south mirrors the delegation's route: [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) to [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md). Aldric insists on stopping at [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) to interview locals — the village sits along the road and someone may have seen or heard something. At [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md), the wooden coin gains them entry. Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md) provides Corporal Janic's testimony and a map to the ambush site. Lady [Mariya Deleveu](mariya-deleveu-person.md) helps provision the party. From there, the trail leads to the ambush site — and eventually, to the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md).

 

**The NSQ-05 Intersection:** This quest starter runs parallel to NSQ-05: On a Mission from Rao. Priestess **Seranna Valeth** — a junior Velunan priestess with a divine vision from [Rao](rao-person.md) — is also at the [Monastery](a1-monastery-of-the-reverent-brothers-rao-landmark.md), pursuing a separate but related thread: the recovery of [Artus Kellan](artus-kellan-person-1.md) and the Chest of Rao. The DM may introduce Seranna during the Monastery scene, have her appear on the road south, or save her for a later encounter. The two quests converge at Artus Kellan — the surviving priest who holds the answers both Aldric and Seranna need.

### Using Multiple Starters Together

This starter combines powerfully with GOB-QS01 (Wyn's Mission) and GOB-QS02 (Glorandal Palee's Mission). A party with a ranger, an elf, and a cleric might receive **all three hooks** — each from a different faction, each with different emphasis:

 

*   **Wyn** (wilderness) — appeals to the common folk and the wild places. No politics. Just duty
*   **Glorandal** (elven) — appeals to clan honor and the Seldarine. The Gift bears Corellon's face
*   **Aldric** (faith/political) — appeals to religious duty and political urgency. Missing priests. A ticking clock

 

The convergence of three separate faction hooks tells the party: **this is not a small problem**. The [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md), the elven clans, and the Church of [Rao](rao-person.md) — three institutions that rarely coordinate — are all pointing at the same crisis. That moment of realization, when the party sees the threads converging, is one of the most powerful beats in the Gift of Beauty questline.
