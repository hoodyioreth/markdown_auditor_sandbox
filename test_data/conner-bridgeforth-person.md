Conner Bridgeforth
==================

### Lord

Conner Bridgeforth is a cunning political operator who thrives in the noble courts of Verbobonc, cloaking his ambitions beneath a facade of charm and unwavering loyalty. A **slimy, clever, and cowardly manipulator**, Conner has ingratiated himself into the political circles of Verbobonc, gathering secrets, steering conversations, and subtly advancing the agenda of the **Cult of the Elder Elemental God**. His skill in rumor-mongering and political sabotage makes him one of the cult’s most **valuable assets** within the city.

* * *

### **Description & Personality**

##### **The Serpent in the Court**

*   **Appearance:** A **thin**, wiry man in his early forties, Conner’s features are soft and unassuming. His **dark brown hair is always neatly combed**, and his **fine clothing**, though not ostentatious, is always of excellent quality. His **hands are well-manicured**, revealing his aristocratic tastes and lack of physical labor.
*   **Demeanor:** **Soft-spoken, optimistic, and unrelentingly verbose**, Conner speaks as though he savors every word. His tone is often **soothing and persuasive**, but those who know him well recognize the carefully measured manipulation behind his words.
*   **Personality:**
*   **Deceptive and calculating**, Conner rarely says anything outright but implies much.
*   **Loyal only to himself**, using the cult as a means to power.
*   **Avoids direct confrontation**, always working through intermediaries and pawns.
*   **Expert in etiquette**, easily maneuvering through noble gatherings and courtly functions.

* * *

> 'A noble's ear is a dangerous thing to possess, but an even greater weapon to wield."

### **History & Background**

##### **Rise Through Deception**

Conner was **born into a minor noble family in Verbobonc**, where he quickly realized that **power belonged to those who controlled information, not swords**. As a young scribe in the **Council of Lords**, he learned how noble houses operated, how alliances shifted, and—most importantly—how to manipulate those around him.

His ability to **steer political gossip** into more favorable narratives earned him favors, and soon he transitioned from a mere scribe to a trusted consultant of various lords. His talent for **destroying reputations without leaving a trace** eventually brought him into contact with **Lakash Quallad**, a fellow schemer who recognized Conner’s potential and recruited him into the Cult of the Elder Elemental God.

* * *

> "Power does not belong to those who rule—it belongs to those who advise the ruler."
> 
> — Lord Bridgeforth

### **Motivations & Ambitions**

##### **Power in the Shadows**

Conner sees **loyalty as a commodity** to be traded rather than honored. His goal is not to rule but to **control those who do**. He believes that through manipulation and deception, he can **shape the politics of Verbobonc to suit his ambitions**. His motivations include:

*   **Expanding his influence in the Council of Lords** to act as an untouchable political broker.
*   **Steering noble alliances toward pro-Cult policies** by shaping their opinions through misinformation and political pressure.
*   **Maintaining his position as the Cult’s primary agent in Verbobonc** without exposing himself.
*   **Eliminating or discrediting rivals** through carefully placed rumors and forged evidence.
*   **Acquiring wealth through indirect means**, such as insider trading, bribery, and leveraging political favors.

### **Political Relationships & Affiliations**

##### [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) **– The Mastermind**

*   **Conner’s primary patron and handler within the Cult of the Elder Elemental God.**
*   The two maintain a **business-like partnership**, with Lakash using Conner’s influence to further his hidden Temple objectives.
*   Conner is aware that Lakash is dangerous, and thus, he takes great pains to **keep his own ambitions concealed** from his patron.

##### **Lady** [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) **– The Baroness of Nulb**

*   While they **do not trust each other**, Conner provides Nysera **subtle political support in Verbobonc**.
*   He uses his noble connections to **ensure Nysera’s ambitions in Nulb remain unchallenged** by the Council.
*   In return, Nysera **feeds him information** on the Cult’s dealings in Nulb.

##### [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md)

*   Conner **whispers in the ears of numerous nobles**, ensuring that **their fears, ambitions, and insecurities serve his needs**.
*   He is careful not to appear too influential, always presenting himself as a **humble advisor and friend**.
*   His ability to **steer council discussions** away from exposing the Cult makes him an invaluable asset.

##### **Sir** [Torvel Hawke](torvel-hawke-person.md) **– The Loyal Knight**

*   Sir Torvel is **deeply suspicious** of Conner and his ties to Lakash.
*   Conner considers Sir Torvel a **dangerous obstacle** and has been planting **false rumors** to discredit him in court.
*   He intends to **frame Sir Torvel** for treason at the right moment, removing him as a threat.

##### [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) **– The Whisper in the Dark**

*   **Lucius Graeme serves as Conner’s primary contact with the Temple of Elemental Evil**, acting as a courier and liaison between Conner and [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) , the cult’s high priest.
*   **Lucius, a cunning master of deception and chaos, delivers orders, intelligence, and resources** to Conner, ensuring that political influence in Verbobonc aligns with the Temple’s goals.
*   **Their relationship is one of careful collaboration**—Conner supplies Lucius with **noble gossip, merchant disputes, and council rumors**, while Lucius ensures Conner remains a well-funded and well-protected operative within the city.
*   **Under the alias “Aldren,” Lucius meets with Conner discreetly** at **Jylee’s Inn**, exchanging cryptic letters and payments under the guise of casual merchant dealings.
*   **Lucius provides Conner with fabricated scandals and misinformation**, which Conner then **weaves into courtly whispers**, subtly influencing political decisions in favor of the Temple’s greater schemes.
*   **Conner, in return, ensures that the Council of Lords never scrutinizes Lucius’ movements or disrupts the Cult’s operations in Nulb and beyond**.
*   **Neither man fully trusts the other**—Lucius sees Conner as a **useful but expendable puppet**, while Conner fears Lucius’ deep ties to the cult’s more violent enforcers.

### **Base of Operations**

Jylee's Inn, strategically located near the City Hall and the bustling [C1-16 Civic Centre](c1-16-civic-centre-settlement.md) of Verbobonc, stands as a pinnacle of luxury and comfort within the city. Known for its exquisite architecture and superior service, the inn caters to nobility, high-ranking officials, and affluent merchants, making it a notable venue for both relaxation and strategic meetings.

##### **Jylee’s Inn – The Den of Deception**

*   Conner **frequents** [C3 Jylee’s Inn](c3-jyleeE28099s-inn-landmark.md) , a well-regarded establishment in Verbobonc’s upper districts.
*   Here, he **hosts private dinners** for nobles, offering them **fine wine, exotic dishes, and carefully tailored conversation**.
*   Many of **Verbobonc’s wealthiest and most influential** citizens have, at one point, **shared a meal at Conner’s table**, unknowingly feeding his web of secrets.
*   **A hidden chamber** within his rented suite contains coded ledgers documenting his **blackmail material and cult communications**.
*   Lucius Graeme often meets Conner at Jylee’s Inn, where they conduct their most sensitive dealings under the guise of business discussions.

* * *

**Crimes & Scandals**
---------------------

Conner operates in the shadows, ensuring **his name never appears** in direct relation to any wrongdoing. His major involvements include:

*   **Framing Drinsal Burke**: One of Conner’s agents, **Vonden**, orchestrated an illusion that cast suspicion on Drinsal Burke, an innocent merchant.
*   **Blackmailing Noble Houses**: He has extensive knowledge of **noble indiscretions**, ensuring their compliance through subtle threats.
*   **Sponsoring Bandit Attacks**: He secretly **funds bandit raids on rival merchant interests**, ensuring economic power remains in the hands of Cult-friendly merchants.
*   **Misinformation Warfare**: By controlling court gossip, Conner is able to **influence legal decisions, trade agreements, and diplomatic relationships**.

A noble falsely accused of treason seeks the party’s aid in **proving Conner orchestrated the smear campaign** against him.

##### **"An Invitation to Treason"**

Conner attempts to **recruit one of the adventurers** into his political network, offering power and wealth—but at what cost?

##### **Abilities**

**Silver-Tongued Manipulator.** Conner has advantage on Deception and Persuasion checks when influencing nobility or discussing politics.

**Master of Misinformation.** As a **bonus action**, Bridgeforth can implant **a false rumor** in a conversation. If he speaks with someone for at least **1 minute**, they must succeed on a **DC 14 Insight check** or believe the rumor for **1d4 days**.

**Political Survivor.** When targeted by social manipulation (bribery, blackmail, intimidation), Bridgeforth can use his reaction to make a **DC 14 Persuasion or Deception check** to deflect suspicion or redirect blame.

##### **Actions**

**Dagger.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 4 (1d4+1) piercing damage.

**Cunning Insult.** Conner targets one creature he can see within 30 feet, forcing them to make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on their next attack roll or ability check.

##### **Reactions**

**Feign Innocence.** When an enemy attempts to expose Bridgeforth’s schemes, he can make a **Deception check (DC 15)** as a reaction. On a success, he convinces onlookers that he is innocent, shifting suspicion elsewhere.

**Parry.** The noble adds 2 to its AC against one melee attack that would hit it. To do so, the noble must see the attacker and be wielding a melee weapon.
