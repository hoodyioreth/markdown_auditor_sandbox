E7 Kabora’s Jewel of the Velerdyva
==================================

_Inn, Surveillance Hub, Political Crossroads_

Kabora’s Jewel of the Velverdyva stands **directly upon the river’s edge**, where the great trade river divides the Ryemend District from the rest of Verbobonc. Stone steps descend from its courtyard straight to the wharf, slick with spray and fish-scale sheen. Barges nose in close beneath its balconies, and skiffs are often tied off within arm’s reach of the inn’s lantern posts.

Kabora’s Jewel of the Velverdyva is among the finest inns in all of **Verbobonc**, famed for its flawless service, discreet luxury, and clientele of consequence. Wealthy merchants, river magnates, visiting nobles, and senior civic officials frequent its polished halls.

Yet despite the number of carriages, guards, and liveried servants gathered outside at all hours, the Jewel is _unnervingly quiet_.

No laughter spills from its windows. No drunken song echoes down the wharf.

Those with sharp instincts soon realize: this is not a place for merriment. It is a place to be **observed**.

Unbeknownst to most patrons, the Jewel has become the **central surveillance node** of Helthrax’s operation in Ryemend — a velvet-gloved extension of the corruption festering within **Dieg Manor (E8)**.

##### Enter Kabora’s

You smell river-water and smoke before you see the inn. Kabora’s Jewel rises at the wharf’s edge, its stone lower walls darkened by years of spray and flood, its upper timbers bright with paint and hanging lanterns. Nets dry on wooden frames nearby, and the shouts of dockhands echo between warehouses. Barges lie moored below, their crews already drinking though the sun is still high. Warm light spills from the open doors, and laughter—rough, tired, genuine—rolls out over the water.

#### **Exterior & Courtyard**

The inn’s **wharfside courtyard** is enclosed on three sides by stone and timber, and open on the fourth to the river itself. Heavy posts support hanging lanterns and wind-chimes made from old ship fittings. Long tables run parallel to the water so that most patrons can keep one eye on their vessel while they drink.

*   **Stonework:** Polished smooth by decades of boots and spilled ale.
*   **Furniture:** Thick trestle tables, scarred but sturdy; benches weighted to resist river winds.
*   **River Access:** Broad steps lead directly down to the docks; a rope rail can be raised or lowered to control traffic.
*   **Soundscape:** Water slapping stone, rigging creaking, shouted orders, the constant murmur of trade.

This space is busiest at dusk and dawn—**when crews return or prepare to cast off**.

* * *

#### **Interior: Taproom & Booths**

Inside, the taproom is warm, smoky, and dimmer than expected. The ceiling beams are low and thick, hung with charms, foreign pennants, and small shields bearing faded river-marks from upriver ports.

Booths line the walls, each fitted with **heavy canvas curtains treated with simple dweomer**—not true privacy magic, but enough to soften sound and obscure faces when drawn. Regulars know which booths are best for talk, and which are best for silence.

*   **Bar:** Long, stone-faced, designed to withstand wet boots and heavy cargo-buckles.
*   **Drink:** Ale is plentiful and strong; spiced river-wine appears when foreign ships arrive.
*   **Food:** Fish-heavy fare, often oversalted or overcooked, meant to fill bellies rather than impress.
*   **Crowd:** Sailors, dock factors, caravan guards waiting for passage, and merchants watching more than they speak.

* * *

#### **Upper Floors: Lodging**

Rooms at Kabora’s Jewel are **plain but reliable**.

Each contains:

*   A heavy bed with thick blankets (essential even in warmer months by the river),
*   A chest with a stout lock,
*   A narrow shuttered window overlooking either the wharf or the warehouses.

There are **no stables**—guests with mounts are directed inland. Those who stay here are either passing through by river… or waiting for something to arrive.

* * *

#### History

_A Pillar of Stability_

The inn has long served the community, with its courtyard stones polished by years of use. The innkeeper, Kabora Telnis, once a rivermaster herself, Kabora retired ashore after a disastrous flood season claimed her vessel. She now runs the Jewel with a sailor’s instincts and a merchant’s caution.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Founding:** Established generations ago to serve river sailors, dockmasters, and merchant captains working the Velverdyva trade routes.
*   **Evolution:** Expanded quietly over time to accommodate wealthier clientele, private dining arrangements, and discreet meetings.
*   **Modern Reputation:** Known for excellence, restraint, and a subtle hierarchy of access that favors established patrons over newcomers.

### Goods and Services

_Average Prices and Seafood Menu_

Prices are average, with a focus on seafood. While the food can be overcooked, the atmosphere and ale make up for it.

##### Key Offerings

*   **Food:** Mainly seafood, sometimes overcooked.
*   **Drink**: Abundant ale to compensate for the food’s lack of taste.
*   **Lodging:** No stables for mounts, average prices.

### Relationships

_A Hub for Diverse Clientele_

Kabora’s Jewel of the Velerdyva serves as a base for various groups, including shady merchants, adventuring parties, and spies, thanks to the bribes paid to the city guards.

#### Atmosphere & Tone

*   **Full, but subdued** — every table occupied, every voice controlled.
*   **Elite clientele** — fine clothing, restrained gestures, guarded conversations.
*   **Impeccable service** — flawless food, perfect drink, distant smiles.
*   **Quiet tension** — patrons avoid eye contact with strangers.

The Jewel feels like a place where one might discuss inheritances, trade routes, or political favors — never anything so vulgar as truth.

* * *

### The Hidden Truth (DM Information)

The Jewel is a **listening post**.

Helthrax’s network saturates the inn’s _main dining hall_ with watchers:

*   Guards posing as staff
*   Servants trained to listen, not speak
*   Street peddlers acting as message couriers
*   “Guests” who never eat, only observe

Their purpose is simple:

*   Track the movements of city officials
*   Identify shifting loyalties
*   Watch for weakness as plague fear spreads
*   Feed intelligence back to **Dieg Manor**

Notably, **Wilfrick** and other ranking figures are among those monitored here.

The **rear bar**, where the PCs are placed, is intentionally _left unwatched_ — a containment strategy. Unknown elements are isolated rather than eavesdropped upon.

### **Role in a Temple of Elemental Evil Campaign**

Kabora’s Jewel is ideal for **introducing river-based intelligence** without overt exposition.

Use it to:

*   Seed rumors of **cargo moving south that should not be**,
*   Introduce **foreign agents and middlemen** tied to Temple logistics,
*   Let players overhear half-true stories of the Kron Hills, Nulb, or river traffic near Hommlet,
*   Provide a neutral ground where factions cross paths unintentionally.

This inn works best when the party **does not realize it matters yet**.

They drink. They listen. The river carries more than goods.

* * *

### **Dungeon Master Notes**

*   Guards are present but distant—**bribed, not friendly**.
*   Fights draw attention quickly due to open wharf visibility.
*   Nighttime conversations carry over water farther than expected.
*   Anyone staying multiple nights becomes _noticed_.

### Running the Jewel at the Table (DM Cheat Sheet)

##### Purpose of the Location

*   Surveillance hub for Helthrax’s network
*   Social crossroads for Verbobonc’s elite
*   Safe-ish intel gathering point for PCs
*   A _pressure chamber_ for political tension

##### Scene Hooks

*   A disguised spy mistakes a PC for an expected contact.
*   A servant slips and mentions Dieg’s absence.
*   A city official briefly enters the back bar.
*   A peddler outside watches the party leave — and reports it.

* * *

#### Narrative Role in the Campaign

Kabora’s Jewel of the Velverdyva is not about answers.

It is about **realization**.

The party should leave feeling:

*   watched, but not threatened
*   ignored, but not forgotten
*   surrounded by power they do not yet understand

When Dieg Manor is finally confronted, the Jewel retroactively becomes obvious — the quiet eye at the center of the storm.

##### **Kabora Telnis, Proprietor**

Kabora is a broad-shouldered woman in her middle years, with iron-gray hair braided tight and hands marked by rope-burn and blade-callus. She moves through the inn with a practiced eye, missing nothing and asking little.

Once a rivermaster herself, Kabora retired ashore after a disastrous flood season claimed her vessel. She now runs the Jewel with a sailor’s instincts and a merchant’s caution.

*   **Personality:** Calm, shrewd, unsentimental—but fair.
*   **Rule:** No drawn blades inside. Disputes go outside or end at her word.
*   **Reputation:** If Kabora vouches for someone, dockhands listen.

She knows **who pays bribes**, who avoids guards, and which cargos should not be asked about aloud.

* * *

##### **Bellia Telnis, the Curious Daughter**

Kabora’s eldest daughter Bellia serves as runner and server, darting between tables with an eager smile and endless questions. She listens more than she should, remembers everything, and dreams of a life beyond the wharf.

*   **Trait:** Intensely curious about adventurers and travelers.
*   **Risk:** Will follow stories into danger if unchecked.
*   **Tension:** Kabora is fiercely protective and will blame outsiders if Bellia comes to harm.

Bellia is a **perfect vector** for rumors, accidental revelations, and complications.

* * *

##### Jessey Vestsmith — Senior House Steward (Not Owner)

**Jessey Vestsmith** is widely believed to be the _innkeeper_ by casual patrons, but in truth he serves as the **senior house steward** — the highest visible authority most guests ever interact with.

A former caravan guard who lost his taste for the road, Jessey is:

*   practical,
*   courteous,
*   deeply invested in keeping the Jewel running without incident.

He manages schedules, oversees staff discipline, and ensures the inn’s high standards are maintained — but **he does not own the establishment**, nor does he set its policies regarding guests or restricted areas. Jessey is not a villain and is not knowingly part of the cult of Iuz.

* * *

##### Gahr the Barkeep

**Role:** Unwitting Information Conduit  
**Disposition:** Jovial, friendly, genuinely likes people  
**Affiliation:** None (not part of the spy network)

Gahr is exactly what he appears to be: a hardworking barkeep who enjoys conversation and takes pride in his establishment.

However:

*   He does **not** gather information unless the PCs explicitly ask.
*   Each time he does, there is only a **20% chance per service** that he returns with something useful.
*   Even then, he never uncovers hard facts — only rumors, impressions, and oddities.

###### Portrayal Notes

*   Laughs easily, wipes the bar constantly.
*   Calls everyone “friend.”
*   Grows nervous if questioned about the main dining room.
*   Redirects conversations instinctively, not suspiciously.
