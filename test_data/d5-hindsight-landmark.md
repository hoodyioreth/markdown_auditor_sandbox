D5 Hindsight
============

Hidden beneath an ancient ipt tree at the boundary where **Elven Dawn** rubbeth shoulders with the **Gnome Quarters**, **Hindsight** is Verbobonc’s most feared and most sought-after diviner—kept by a priestess of **Istus**, who sellest glimpses of the future… and sometimes demandeth a price no mortal should offer.

##### **Arrival at the Hindsight**

The pebbled street groweth quiet here, where elf-lanterns fade and the South-way’s breath rolleth cold. An old ipt tree croucheth at the lane’s bend, its roots knotted like clenched fingers. Between two roots is a narrow door of dark wood—unmarked save for a single gilt spindle carved so thin it seemeth a scratch.  
  
Wind chimes of bone tick and clatter, though no breeze toucheth thy face. Down the steps, candles burn in hollows of the root-wall, and the air is thick with incense and old secrets. Shadows move where they should not—small, quick, listening shadows.

#### Historical Backdrop

_Echoes of the Past_

*   **Origins:** "Hindsight" was established under mysterious circumstances by its equally mysterious owner, known only as "Shady."
*   **Evolution:** Over the years, the shop has become a cornerstone for those seeking guidance from the divine or the future, embedded deeply in the local lore of Verbobonc.
*   **Reputation:** Amidst whispers of its owner's dark lineage, the shop has flourished under the cover of night, drawing in those who walk the line between light and shadow.

#### What This Place Is

“Hindsight” is a diviner’s den and a quiet shrine to **Istus**, goddess of **Fate, Destiny, Divination, Future, and Honesty**—the Lady who weaveth the world’s threads with a **golden spindle**. Within her doctrine, destiny is largely fore-ordained; divination is permitted, yet it is a dangerous courtesy—one may _glimpse_ the weave, but not seize the loom. Istus

The shop is called **Hindsight** because Shady’s readings rarely comfort. They illuminate. They reveal the choice thou _already_ madest, though thou hast not yet admitted it.

* * *

##### Exterior & Approach

**Edge of Two Peoples:** The shop’s placement matters: it draweth elven curiosity and gnomish pragmatism alike, while the South-way Gate brings in foreigners, merchants, and pilgrims who fear what lieth beyond the city walls. D5 Hindsight

**The Ipt Root-Door:** The entrance is sunk at the ipt tree’s base, leading downward into **caves woven through the roots**—an architectural marvel of living wood and hollow stone. D5 Hindsight

* * *

##### Interior & Atmosphere

**A Den of Mysteries:** Bone wind-chimes line the ceiling. The walls are scratched with mystical symbols and chalk marks half-erased. Light cometh from candles only; no hearth-fire burns here. D5 Hindsight

**The Listening Menagerie:** Bats cling in dark corners; rats move in patient lines; a pale snake sleeps coiled about a warm stone. These creatures are Shady’s eyes and ears—silent witnesses that make even bold folk lower their voices. D5 Hindsight

**The Altar of Istus:** Deeper within is a small shrine space: gauze hangings, incense, and soft, droning woodwind tones (a pipe, a breath-flute) that turn conversation into confession.

> Ask thy question once. If thou ask it twice, thou art bargaining—and I do not barter with truth.”

### Services Offered

##### Common Divinations (Standard Rates)

Hindsight offers regular divinations at standard fees, delivered in cryptic, layered speech that forces the patron to interpret—and thereby to take responsibility.

**Typical client questions:** missing persons, rival intent, best route, whether to trust a contract, what a dream “meaneth.”

##### High-Stakes Scrying (The Forbidden Tier)

For great inquiries, Shady demandeth a dreadful price: **“sacrificing what one loves most”** in exchange for unparalleled insight. This is the hook that draws desperate nobles and scheming guild leaders—and the thing that should frighten your table.

DM Note: “Sacrifice” need not be literal death. It can be _renunciation_, _betrayal_, _a sworn severing_, or _giving up a cherished future_. Make it story, not gore.

* * *

#### Political & Cultural Relevance

**A Spider at the Web’s Edge:** Shady claimeth neutrality, yet her clientele shapes Verbobonc’s decisions: guild maneuvers, noble alliances, and discreet betrayals all begin as questions whispered beneath these roots.

**Elven Enclave Tolerance:** The Enclave permits this “shadowed” shrine because it keeps dangerous curiosity contained—and because even elves, with all their long memory, fear the unknowable turn of the Wheel.

* * *

#### Connections to the Temple of Elemental Evil (DM Use)

Hindsight is a perfect conduit for the **Second Rising**: Temple agents seek prophecy without revealing themselves; frightened merchants seek warning of raids; cultists seek “permission” from fate to commit atrocity.

Shady will not ally with the Temple—yet she will sell truth to anyone who can bear it. If the party is hunting cult influence in the city, Hindsight is where a clue can be gained _at cost_.

### Player Interaction Hooks

##### Why PCs Come Here

*   They want to know **what hunts them** (or who).
*   They need a **direction** when the city’s politics grow too tangled.
*   They seek **truth-tests**: “Is this envoy lying?” “Is this relic cursed?”
*   They need an omen before crossing borders (Elven lands, gnomish tunnels, the road toward Hommlet).

##### If They Linger

*   The shop begins to _learn_ them—rats pause near their boots; a bat stirs whenever a certain name is spoken.
*   Shady offers a reading unasked… because she dislikes their silence more than their question.

##### What Goes Wrong

*   A noble’s masked servant waits outside to learn what the party asked.
*   Someone offers the party coin to steal Shady’s notes (she keepeth none—only tokens).
*   A client demands High-Stakes Scrying and tries to force the party into being the “price.”

Cleric 9 of Istus, Druid 4 of Istus

Maya Manglyeong (Enigmatic Proprietress)

Shady’s origin is a rumor with teeth: some whisper drow-elf blood; others say she is merely a woman who learned too young that fate devoureth the careless. She is devoted to Istus’s tenets—**fate and honesty**—yet her honesty is never kind. D5 Hindsight Istus

**Public Face:** Polite, quiet, controlled—she offereth certainty only in the form of questions.  
**True Motive:** Privacy and power; she keepeth distance from politics while influencing it all the same through counsel. D5 Hindsight

**Behavioral Tell:** When a choice is near, she turns a small spindle-charm between finger and thumb.

##### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (12)

1.  “Shady’s” door sometimes openeth of itself—only for those who are about to make a ruinous choice.
2.  She hath divined a coming _earth-shudder_ far south-east… and will not say where, only: “a temple that is not dead.”
3.  A guildmaster visited thrice in one tenday—each time leaving paler, as if a piece of his future was cut away.
4.  Someone is buying readings under false names, always asking about “safe roads at night.”
5.  Shady’s bats have begun flying east at dusk—toward the merchant roads, not the river.
6.  The altar-gauze sometimes shows silhouettes that are not present—three forms, crone/dame/maiden, watching.
7.  A Celene envoy refuses to enter, calling it “a human hunger for certainty.”
8.  A gnome locksmith swears the root-door cannot be picked—because it was never locked in the first place.
9.  A desperate lover offers “what I love most” and means _the wrong thing_—a child, a sibling, a promise.
10.  Shady will speak freely of fate—yet groweth cold when asked of **honesty**, as if that be the harder doctrine.
11.  A Temple-bought thug wears a tiny golden spindle as “protection.” Shady wants it back—quietly, violently, or both.
12.  A prophecy offered to the party is true… but framed to push them toward a road that benefits someone else.
