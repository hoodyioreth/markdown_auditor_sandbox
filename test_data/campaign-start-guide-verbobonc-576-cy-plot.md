CAMPAIGN START GUIDE — VERBOBONC 576 CY
=======================================

This article is the starting point for every Dungeon Master running the **Temple of Elemental Evil Epic Sandbox Campaign** using the Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576 CY. Whether you are a first-time DM picking up Greyhawk for the first time or a veteran who has run T1–4 before, this guide walks you through three decisions that will shape your entire campaign: where to begin, what classes your players should consider, and how to connect characters to the living world of the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md).

  **Short version: Yes, you absolutely can start in Greyhawk using the 2024 DMG**, and the [Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576cy](../../verbobonc-campaign-guide-576cy-stylinlp38.md) is meant to be a giant, organized DM notebook you can dip into as needed—not something you have to memorize cover-to-cover.

 

**How this guide works:** The Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576 CY is a massive, organized DM notebook — not something you need to memorize. Think of it as a buffet, not a textbook. Official D&D books (2024 PHB, DMG, MM) provide the rules. This site provides the _where_, the _who_, and the _what's going wrong_. You run rules from the books. You pull setting, NPCs, and adventures from the guide as you need them.

> Think of it like this:
> 
> **Official books = rules.  
> Verbobonc site = setting + NPCs + adventures.**
> 
> You run the _rules_ with the 2024 PHB/DMG/MM. You use my stuff for **where** you are, **who** lives there, and **what’s going wrong.**

 

### Step 1 — Choose Your Starting Location

The most important decision a DM makes before Session 1 is where the campaign begins. A sandbox campaign can start anywhere, but not every starting location demands the same amount of DM preparation. The locations below are ranked from **Difficulty 1** (least prep, most structured) to **Difficulty 5** (maximum prep, maximum freedom). Pick the one that matches your experience level and the kind of campaign you want to run.

 

**What the difficulty rating means:**

*   **Difficulty 1 — Guided:** A well-defined starting village with a clear first adventure. Minimal prep. The classic on-ramp.
*   **Difficulty 2 — Structured:** A small settlement with local problems that naturally point toward the main campaign. Moderate prep.
*   **Difficulty 3 — Open:** A city or town with multiple factions, districts, and hooks. The DM chooses which threads to pull first. Significant prep.
*   **Difficulty 4 — Advanced:** A culturally distinct starting region (elves, gnomes, druids) that requires the DM to establish non-human perspectives and politics before connecting to the main Temple plot. Heavy prep.
*   **Difficulty 5 — Expert:** A distant city or region with its own political landscape. The DM must build the bridge between the starting region and the Verbobonc/Temple storyline. Maximum prep and improvisation.

##### Difficulty 1 — The Village of Hommlet (Recommended First Campaign)

**Location:** [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md)  
**DM Experience:** Beginner — First-time DMs start here  
**Campaign Style:** Classic dungeon-crawl with a small-town mystery  
**Party Level:** 1–3  
**Prep Time:** 1–2 hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** This is the classic T1 starting point created by Gary Gygax. The village is small (fewer than 20 key NPCs), the problems are local and concrete (bandits, missing traders, strange activity at the Ruins of the Moathouse), and the adventure path is clear. Every piece of published T1–4 material assumes you begin here, which means you have the most support material available. If you are a new DM, this is the safest and most rewarding place to start.

 

**First adventure:** The party arrives in Hommlet, meets the local NPCs, hears rumors about trouble at the old Moathouse, and investigates. The Moathouse dungeon is a self-contained first adventure site that introduces combat, exploration, and the first hints of the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md).

 

**The campaign grows into:** Hommlet → [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) → the Temple of Elemental Evil. As the party gains levels and influence, [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) becomes the "big city" they travel to for serious allies, supplies, and politics.

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — straightforward martial characters thrive in Hommlet's dungeon-crawl structure
*   [Player Starter Class: Cleric and Paladins](player-starter-class3A-cleric-and-paladins-article.md) — the village temples provide immediate faction ties
*   [Player Starter Class: Rangers](player-starter-class3A-rangers-article.md) — the wilderness between Hommlet and the Temple is ranger territory

 

**DM tip:** Read the [3orcs DM Notes for T0: The Journey to Hommlet](3orcs-dm-notes-for-t03A-the-journey-to-hommlet-E28094-major-npcs-plot.md) article for guidance on the major NPCs the party will meet on the road and in the village. You do not need to read anything else before Session 1.

##### Difficulty 2 — Small Viscounty Villages

**Locations:** [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md), [Nigb's Rest](nigb-s-rest-settlement.md), [Twilight Falls](twilight-falls-settlement.md), [Penwick](penwick-settlement.md), or any small settlement within the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md)  
**DM Experience:** Beginner to Intermediate  
**Campaign Style:** Local problems that escalate into the wider Temple conspiracy  
**Party Level:** 1–3  
**Prep Time:** 2–3 hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** If your players want something slightly different from the classic Hommlet opening, any small village in the Viscounty can serve as an on-ramp. These settlements are close enough to the main campaign area that the party will naturally encounter clues pointing toward the Temple's resurgence — missing caravans, strange coin from unknown mints, cult recruiters working the roads. The DM gets a small, manageable NPC cast while still feeding the players into the main storyline.

 

**First adventure:** A local problem that has roots in the Temple's expanding influence. Bandits operating under cult direction. A merchant caravan that never arrived. A local shrine defaced with elemental symbols. The clues point toward "something brewing around Hommlet and [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)."

 

**The campaign grows into:** The local village → Hommlet (as the party follows the trail) → the Moathouse → the Temple. [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) serves as the regional hub for resupply and political connections.

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — village militia, local guards, or caravan guards are natural backgrounds
*   [Player Starter Class: Rangers](player-starter-class3A-rangers-article.md) — the rural Viscounty is prime ranger country
*   [Player Starter Class: Thief and Rogue](player-starter-class3A-thief-and-rogue-article.md) — a smuggler or trader passing through has reasons to investigate missing cargo

##### Difficulty 3 — The City of Verbobonc

**Location:** [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md)  
**DM Experience:** Intermediate  
**Campaign Style:** Political intrigue, urban adventure, and faction play leading into dungeon exploration  
**Party Level:** 1–4  
**Prep Time:** 3–5 hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** Verbobonc is the political and economic heart of the campaign region. Starting here gives the DM access to noble houses, temples, guilds, a thieves' guild, and multiple competing factions from Session 1. The trade-off is scope — Verbobonc has hundreds of NPCs, multiple districts, and layered political conflicts. The DM needs to pick _one_ district and _one_ hook to focus on for the opening sessions, then expand outward.

 

**Recommended starting points within Verbobonc:**

*   **[Jylee's Inn](c3-jyleeE28099s-inn-landmark.md)** — a natural gathering point for adventurers. The innkeeper knows everyone. Start here for a classic "you all meet in a tavern" opening with real political depth behind it.
*   **[Adventurers Guild](b26-adventures-guild-landmark.md)** — the message pole outside the Guild posts contracts, bounties, and rumors. A DM can hand the party a job notice that points them toward Hommlet, the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), or a noble patron's private request.
*   **A noble patron** — a Verbobonc noble (such as the Lady [Elinor Asbury](elinor-asbury-person.md) of the Asbury family at [Asbury Manor](asbury-manor-landmark.md)) hires the party discreetly for a mission like for example [Noble Ambitions 579cy](noble-ambitions-579cy-plot.md) that puts them on the road to Hommlet or into the political web around the Temple's agents in the city.

 

**First adventure:** A contract, a summons, or a street-level problem in one Verbobonc district. The party might investigate a missing merchant for the [Adventurers Guild](b26-adventures-guild-landmark.md), attend a noble gathering where cult agents are operating, or get drawn into the underworld of the [Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) thieves' guild.

 

**The campaign grows into:** Verbobonc (home base) → Hommlet/Nulb (where the trail leads) → the Temple (the source of the corruption). The party returns to Verbobonc between adventures for resupply, political maneuvering, and faction development.

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Thief and Rogue](player-starter-class3A-thief-and-rogue-article.md) — the [Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) and the city underworld give thieves a complete parallel storyline
*   [Player Starter Class: Cleric and Paladins](player-starter-class3A-cleric-and-paladins-article.md) — the temples and churches of Verbobonc are major political players (see [Religion in Verbobonc](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md))
*   [Player Starter Class: Wizard](player-starter-class3A-wizard-article.md) — guilds, libraries, and arcane politics are all accessible from the city
*   [Player Starter Class: PALADINS](player-starter-class3A-paladins-article.md) — the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) has a strong presence in the city and ties directly to the Temple conflict

 

**DM tip:** Do not try to read every Verbobonc article before Session 1. Pick one district, read 2–3 key location articles within it, and let the players' choices guide which articles you read next. The guide is built so you can pull in material gradually as the campaign expands.

##### Difficulty 3 — Nulb (Dark-Tone Alternate Start)

**Location:** [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)  
**DM Experience:** Intermediate  
**Campaign Style:** Gritty survival, morally grey NPCs, direct proximity to the Temple  
**Party Level:** 2–4  
**Prep Time:** 2–4 hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** Nulb is the wretched hive sitting in the shadow of the Temple itself. Starting here drops the party into immediate danger — cult operatives, river pirates, and hostile NPCs surround them from Session 1. This is not the recommended start for new DMs, but it produces a tense, atmospheric campaign for groups that want to skip the slow build and go straight into the corruption. The NPC cast is smaller than Verbobonc but every single one of them is dangerous, compromised, or desperate.

 

**First adventure:** Survival. The party arrives in Nulb (as prisoners, refugees, bounty hunters, or undercover agents) and must navigate a town where the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) is the only real authority. Their first mission might be escaping, finding an informant, or earning enough trust with one of Nulb's factions to get access to intelligence about the Temple.

 

**The campaign grows into:** Nulb → the Temple (the party is already at the doorstep) → Hommlet (as a safe haven) → [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) (for reinforcements and political backing as the stakes escalate).

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Thief and Rogue](player-starter-class3A-thief-and-rogue-article.md) — Nulb is rogue territory; every NPC is working an angle
*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — mercenaries and hired muscle fit Nulb's economy
*   [Player Starter Class: Rangers](player-starter-class3A-rangers-article.md) — a ranger tracking cult activity through the wilderness arrives at Nulb as the trail's end

##### Difficulty 4 — The Gnarley Forest and Elf Clans

**Location:** **Gnarley Forest**, [Clan Meldarin](clan-meldarin-organization.md), [Clan Sherendyl](clan-sherendyl-organization.md)  
**DM Experience:** Advanced  
**Campaign Style:** Wilderness survival, elven politics, druidic power struggles, and the slow encroachment of elemental corruption  
**Party Level:** 1–4  
**Prep Time:** 4–6 hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** The Gnarley Forest is home to wood-elf confederations, the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md), and a wilderness that is slowly being poisoned by the Temple's influence. Starting in the forest means the DM must establish elven culture, druidic politics, and the forest's own threats before connecting the party to the wider Temple conflict. This is a rewarding start for groups that want a non-human perspective on the campaign, but it requires the DM to do more worldbuilding work up front because the elven clans operate by different rules than human settlements.

 

**First adventure:** An elven patrol finds corruption spreading through the forest — blighted groves, dead animals, elemental taint in the water. The clan elders send the party (or a party including elven PCs) to trace the source. The trail leads through the **Gnarley Forest** toward the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) and eventually toward the Temple's area of influence.

 

**The campaign grows into:** Gnarley Forest → Hommlet (first contact with human settlements) → the Temple (the source of the corruption the elves detected) → [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) (as the party needs human allies to fight a human cult).

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Rangers](player-starter-class3A-rangers-article.md) — the definitive class for a Gnarley Forest campaign opening
*   [Player Starter Class: Wizard](player-starter-class3A-wizard-article.md) — elven wizards and druidic magic traditions are deeply woven into the forest clans
*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — elven warriors and clan guards protecting the borders

##### Difficulty 4 — The Kron Hills and Gnome Settlements

**Location:** [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md), [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md)  
**DM Experience:** Advanced  
**Campaign Style:** Gnome-human trade politics, hill country intrigue, and the frontier between civilized lands and cult-controlled territory  
**Party Level:** 1–4  
**Prep Time:** 4–6 hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** The Kron Hills sit between the Viscounty of Verbobonc and the territory the Temple is trying to control. Gnome settlements like [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) and [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) are feeling the pressure of cult-influenced banditry disrupting trade routes. The human–gnome trade tensions that feed the Temple's logistics network are a rich source of adventure hooks. This start works particularly well for gnome PCs or parties interested in the economic side of the Temple's war.

 

**First adventure:** A gnome trade caravan is attacked on the road between [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and Verbobonc. The goods were not stolen — they were redirected. Someone is diverting supplies to a destination in the hills. The trail leads toward the Temple's logistics network and its agents operating along the trade roads.

 

**The campaign grows into:** Kron Hills → the trade roads (where cult logistics operate) → Hommlet/Nulb (following the supply chain) → the Temple → [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) (for political resolution of the human–gnome tensions the cult has been exploiting).

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Thief and Rogue](player-starter-class3A-thief-and-rogue-article.md) — smuggling, trade fraud, and information networks are central to this start
*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — caravan guards and [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) patrolling the hill roads
*   [Player Starter Class: Wizard](player-starter-class3A-wizard-article.md) — gnome illusionists and tinkerers have their own magical traditions

##### Difficulty 5 — The City of Dyvers

**Location:** [Dyvers](dyvers2C-free-lands-of-organization.md)  
**DM Experience:** Expert  
**Campaign Style:** Mercantile intrigue, river trade, espionage, and a long approach to the Temple conflict  
**Party Level:** 1–5  
**Prep Time:** 5+ hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** Dyvers is a free city on the Velverdyva River, a major trade hub that competes economically with Verbobonc. Starting here means the party encounters the Temple's influence through trade disruption, smuggling networks, and agents operating in a city that has its own complex politics separate from the Viscounty. The DM must build the connection between Dyvers and the Temple storyline, which gives maximum creative freedom but requires significant improvisation. This is an expert-level start because the published material provides less structural support for a Dyvers campaign opening.

 

**First adventure:** A trade contract, a missing ship on the Velverdyva, or intelligence from a Dyvers merchant guild about strange activity in the Viscounty. The party follows the money or the missing goods southward toward Verbobonc and eventually toward the Temple's area of operations.

 

**The campaign grows into:** Dyvers → the river trade route → [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) (first major stop) → Hommlet/Nulb → the Temple. Dyvers can remain a secondary hub for trade and political connections throughout the campaign.

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Thief and Rogue](player-starter-class3A-thief-and-rogue-article.md) — Dyvers thrives on trade, smuggling, and information brokering
*   [Player Starter Class: Wizard](player-starter-class3A-wizard-article.md) — a port city attracts arcane scholars and traders in rare components
*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — mercenary companies and ship guards work the river

##### Difficulty 5 — The Wild Coast

**Location:** [Wild Coast](the-wild-coast-organization.md)  
**DM Experience:** Expert  
**Campaign Style:** Lawless frontier, piracy, factional chaos, and a long road to the Temple  
**Party Level:** 1–5  
**Prep Time:** 5+ hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** The Wild Coast is the anarchic southern frontier — a region of petty warlords, pirate coves, and failing city-states where the Temple's agents recruit freely because there is no central authority to stop them. More importantly, the **Slaver Lords** operate openly along the Wild Coast, and they are collaborating with the Temple's network. Prisoners taken by the Slaver Lords are transported as slaves northward by wagon through the **Gnarley Forest** in secret, delivered to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) where the Temple puts them to work as forced labor, sacrificial fodder, or broken recruits. This is the hardest starting region because the DM must invent most of the local content and build the bridge to the Verbobonc/Temple campaign arc. However, it produces a uniquely gritty, survival-heavy campaign where the party encounters the Temple's slave pipeline from the bottom up.

 

**First adventure:** The party encounters Slaver Lord agents, press gangs, or mercenary companies that are capturing people and funneling them northward toward the Temple. The party may themselves be captured and loaded onto a slave wagon heading through the Gnarley Forest — or they may intercept one. Following the pipeline through the forest leads them into the Viscounty and toward [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), where the wagons deliver their human cargo.

 

**The campaign grows into:** Wild Coast → the slave wagons through the **Gnarley Forest** → [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) (where the pipeline ends) → the Temple (the final destination for the slaves) → Hommlet or [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) (arriving as outsiders with intelligence about the Temple's southern logistics).

 

**Best class guides for this start:**

*   [Player Starter Class: Fighter](player-starter-class3A-fighter-article.md) — survival on the Wild Coast demands martial skill
*   [Player Starter Class: Thief and Rogue](player-starter-class3A-thief-and-rogue-article.md) — smugglers and pirates are the local economy
*   [Player Starter Class: Rangers](player-starter-class3A-rangers-article.md) — tracking cult supply lines through the wilderness

##### Difficulty 5 — The Kingdom of [Veluna](veluna-organization.md)

**Location:** Archclericy of Veluna  
**DM Experience:** Expert  
**Campaign Style:** Religious politics, crusade preparation, and a holy-war framework for the Temple conflict  
**Party Level:** 1–5  
**Prep Time:** 5+ hours before Session 1

 

**Why start here:** Veluna is the theocratic kingdom to the west of the Viscounty, ruled by the Archcleric of Rao. The Archclericy of Veluna fought the Temple in the original Battle of Emridy Meadows and has institutional memory of that war. Starting here connects directly to the **Gift of Beauty** quest line — the campaign's political linchpin. The Celestial Order of the Moons (or a bishop of Rao, whichever fits your table) dispatches a cleric or paladin eastward to investigate what happened to the Gift of Beauty, a sacred relic whose fate is entangled with the Temple's resurgence, the nobility of Verbobonc, and the fate of the Viscounty itself. This requires the DM to establish Velunan culture and the religious stakes before the party reaches the Viscounty, making it a high-prep start, but it produces a powerful narrative arc for divine characters because they arrive with a sacred mission that plugs directly into the campaign's central conspiracy.

 

**First adventure:** [The Celestial Circle - Veluna](the-celestial-circle---veluna-organization.md) of the Moons (or a Velunan bishop of Rao) of the Moon charges a cleric or paladin with a holy mission: travel east into the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md) and investigate what has become of the Gift of Beauty. The trail leads through Verbobonc's noble houses, church politics, and eventually toward the Temple's agents who have their own designs on the relic. See the Gift of Beauty — Quest Master Reference for the full quest line.

 

**The campaign grows into:** [Veluna](veluna-organization.md) → the western border → [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) (where the Gift of Beauty investigation begins in earnest) → the noble houses and church factions entangled with the relic → Hommlet/Nulb (as the Temple connection becomes clear) → the Temple itself. Veluna remains a powerful ally, political patron, and the ultimate authority the party reports back to throughout the campaign.

 

### Best class guides for this start

*   [Player Starter Class: Cleric and Paladins](player-starter-class3A-cleric-and-paladins-article.md) — Veluna is the definitive starting region for divine characters
*   [Player Starter Class: PALADINS](player-starter-class3A-paladins-article.md) — a paladin sent by the Archclericy is one of the strongest character hooks in the campaign
*   [Player Starter Class: Wizard](player-starter-class3A-wizard-article.md) — Velunan magical traditions and church-sponsored arcane research

##### Step 2 — Choose Your Character Class

Once the DM has chosen a starting location, players need to build characters that fit the campaign world. The Verbobonc Campaign Guide includes dedicated starter guides for each major class, explaining where that class fits in the Viscounty, which factions and organizations recruit or oppose them, and what adventure hooks are tailored to their abilities.

 

Each guide below is a standalone article a player can read without spoiling DM-only content.

*   [Player Starter Class: Cleric and Paladins](player-starter-class3A-cleric-and-paladins-article.md) — temples, churches, and the divine politics of the Viscounty. Where clerics and paladins fit in the religious hierarchy, which gods have active congregations, and how the Temple of Elemental Evil threatens the divine order.

A focused guide for players building a paladin with deep campaign roots.
*   [Player Starter Class: Rangers](player-starter-class3A-rangers-article.md) — the **Gnarley Forest**, the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), and the wilderness territories where rangers patrol, track cult activity, and defend the frontier.

##### Step 3 — Connect Your Character to the World

The strongest sandbox campaigns happen when every PC has a reason to care about the world beyond "the DM told us to go here." After choosing a starting location (Step 1) and a class (Step 2), use this section to find the hook that connects your character to the Viscounty and the Temple conflict.

 

**Class-to-Location Quick Reference:**

This table shows which starting locations offer the strongest built-in hooks for each class. A filled marker means the location has specific NPCs, factions, or storylines written for that class. An open marker means the class can work there but the DM will need to improvise the connection.

 

*   **Fighters** — Strong hooks at every location. The Viscounty is at war whether it knows it or not, and fighters are always in demand. Strongest ties: Hommlet (village militia), Verbobonc ([Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md)), Kron Hills (caravan guards).
*   **Clerics and Paladins** — Strongest at Verbobonc (temple politics, [Religion in Verbobonc](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md)), Veluna (the Archclericy sends them), and Hommlet (the village temples are ground zero for the cult infiltration). Paladins also have the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) as a dedicated faction.
*   **Rangers** — Strongest in the **Gnarley Forest**, the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), and the wilderness between Hommlet and Nulb. The [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) are natural allies.
*   **Thieves and Rogues** — Strongest in Verbobonc (the [Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md)), Nulb (every NPC is working an angle), Dyvers (trade and smuggling), and the Kron Hills (gnome trade fraud). A thief PC who is "Family" can infiltrate [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md)'s parties as staff or forged retainer and turn the Temple war into an intelligence operation.
*   **Wizards** — Strongest in Verbobonc (arcane guilds), the Gnarley Forest (elven magic), and the Kron Hills (gnome illusionists). Veluna also works for church-sponsored arcane researchers. The Temple's elemental magic is a magnet for wizards who study — or oppose — that kind of power.

##### For Experienced DMs — The Noble Ambitions Campaign Frame

If you have run T1–4 Temple of Elemental Evil before and you want to turn this campaign into a full political sandbox instead of a straight-line dungeon crawl, [Noble Ambitions 579cy](noble-ambitions-579cy-plot.md) is the campaign frame designed for you.

 

**What Noble Ambitions does:** It treats the Second Rising of the Temple as a political conspiracy, not just a dungeon. The party starts in [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) at the Civic Centre and [Jylee's Inn](c3-jyleeE28099s-inn-landmark.md), gets drawn into noble house intrigue through Lady Elinor Asbury's summons and the [Adventurers Guild](b26-adventures-guild-landmark.md) notice board, and plays through a series of investigation scenes that reveal how the Temple's agents have infiltrated the Viscounty's power structures.

 

**How to use it:**

*   **Act I (Noble Ambitions NA-01 through NA-04):** Ground the players in Verbobonc politics, noble houses, and city factions. They make contacts, take oaths, and learn that someone in the nobility is working against the Viscounty.
*   **Act II (NA-05 through NA-09):** The investigation widens. The party follows clues to [Asbury Manor](asbury-manor-landmark.md), [Penwick](penwick-settlement.md), [Anshan](anshan-settlement.md), and the [Fens of Tor](fens-of-tor-location.md). Slot the Ruins of the Moathouse in here as an early "Temple echo" between investigation beats.
*   **Act III (The Temple itself):** When the investigation into banditry, missing caravans, and cult coin points beyond the Fens and the local sites to the true resurgence of Elemental Evil, the party moves into the classic T1–4 Temple content. Noble Ambitions becomes Acts I–II of a long arc. The classic Temple is Act III and beyond. Verbobonc is the home base between all of it.

 

**Why veterans should use Noble Ambitions:** You already know the dungeon. Noble Ambitions lets you play the political campaign _around_ it — who funds the Temple, who covers for it in the nobility, who is quietly trying to stop it without collapsing the whole Viscounty. [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) at [Lakash Quallad's Manor](a12-lakash-quallad-s-manor-landmark.md) is the charming, Temple-aligned power broker using parties, trade deals, and assassins to weaken Verbobonc from inside. The [Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) thieves' guild operates as a street-level intelligence network that can either help or hinder the party depending on how they play it.

##### 1\. Where should a new DM start?

Start **small**:

*   One **home base** (a village or city district)
*   One **clear problem**
*   One **first adventure site** (dungeon/mission)

In this project, your easiest on-ramps are:

1.  **The** [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) → then Nulb → then the Temple (classic T1–4 flow).
2.  **Verbobonc City** as the home base → then out to Hommlet / Nulb / the Temple once the players care about the region.

Both are valid. If you’re very new, I’d:

*   Start in **Hommlet** (small cast, local trouble).
*   Use **Verbobonc** and all my city material as the “big city” they travel to once they need serious allies, supplies, and politics.

If you want a more political, story-driven start, you can begin in Verbobonc instead (at [Jylee’s Inn](c3-jyleeE28099s-inn-landmark.md) or the [Adventurers Guild](b26-adventures-guild-landmark.md) ) and send them toward Hommlet/Nulb via a contract or noble patron.

### For Experienced DMs / TOEE Veterans

Running the Epic Sandbox with _Noble Ambitions_

If you’ve run **T1–4 Temple of Elemental Evil** before and you’re coming back to Greyhawk with the 2024 rules, the **Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576/579 CY** and **Noble Ambitions 579 CY** are built to let you turn the whole thing into a **big, political sandbox** instead of a straight-line dungeon bash.

Here’s a clean way to do it:

##### 1\. Use _Noble Ambitions_ as your Act I spine

Treat [Noble Ambitions](noble-ambitions-579cy-plot.md) as the _campaign frame_ for the Second Rising of the Temple:

*   Start in **Verbobonc City** at the Civic Centre and **Jylee’s Inn** (NA-01, NA-02), with Lady Elinor Asbury’s discreet summons and the public notice on the message pole / Adventurers Guild.
*   Run through the early NA scenes:
*   **NA-01–NA-03**: Civic Square, Jylee’s Inn, the Oath of Asbury.
*   **NA-04**: The shadow contact and first hints that _someone_ in Verbobonc’s nobility is working against the Viscounty.

These sessions ground the players in **politics, noble houses, and city factions** before they ever see a Temple hallway.

##### 2\. Let Verbobonc City be the “living overmap”

As veterans, you already know the Temple’s bones. Use Verbobonc as the **overworld control panel**:

*   **High Quarter & Civic Center** – nobles, bishops, councils, Lakash’s parties.
*   **Business Quarter** – caravans, markets, temples, guilds, and the **Adventurers Guild message pole**.
*   **Hidden layers** – the Battirovka Thieves’ Guild under B32, Lakash Quallad’s quiet collusion with assassins like Alber Guldenstern, and the human–gnome trade tensions feeding the Temple’s logistics.

Every time the players come back from the wilds, you can adjust **prices, rumors, street mood, and faction moves** based on what they did around Hommlet/Nulb/Anshan.

##### 3\. Slot the classic TOEE content in as “where the trail leads”

_Noble Ambitions_ already points outward to:

*   [Asbury Manor](asbury-manor-landmark.md) **and** [Penwick](penwick-settlement.md) ,
*   **Anshan and the Fens of Tor**,
*   and the buried [Ancient Suel Temple](na-12-E28094-ancient-suel-temple---plot-plot.md) (NA-12) tied to deeper elemental corruption.

As an experienced DM, you can decide where to weave in:

*   **The [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md)** – as an early “Temple echo” between NA-06 and NA-09, or as a side job when clues mention old ruins and strange raiders on the roads.
*   **Nulb & the main Temple** – when the investigation into banditry, missing caravans, and cult coin points clearly beyond the Fens and Suel sites to the _true_ resurgence of Elemental Evil.

You essentially use NA as **Act I–II** of a long arc, and the classic Temple as **Act III+**, with Verbobonc as the home base between all of it.

##### 4\. Give thieves and intrigue PCs real teeth

For rogue / face-heavy groups, lean into:

*   The [Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) thieves’ guild under B32 as the street-level info network.
*   [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) (and his manor in the High Quarter) as the charming, Temple-aligned power-broker using parties, trade deals, and assassins to weaken Verbobonc from inside.

A thief PC who is “Family” can:

*   Get **invited** to Lakash’s parties as staff, “security,” or a forged noble retainer.
*   Spy in the **Ballroom of Thirteen Mirrors** and the **Green Salon**, where deals are made and cult logistics are quietly arranged.
*   Bring that intel back to the thieves’ guild or their patron temple, turning the Temple war into a **three-way game** between cult, city, and underworld.

That’s where Noble Ambitions really shines for veterans: you already know the dungeon, but now you get to **play the political campaign around it**—who funds it, who covers for it, who is quietly trying to stop it without collapsing the whole Viscounty.
