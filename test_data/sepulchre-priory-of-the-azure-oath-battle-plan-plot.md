Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath — Battle Plan
================================================

> **Purpose:** This document maps how the priory's garrison responds as a living (and flawed) system when the party enters. It covers alert states, reinforcement chains, retreat routes, magic resources, and — critically — the cracks in the organization that smart players can exploit.  
> **Design Philosophy:** This is not a military fortress. It is a raiding camp run by priests who distrust each other, staffed by mercenaries loyal to coin, and supplemented by gnolls loyal to meat. The garrison's defense has real teeth, but it also has real seams.

### **1\. THE GARRISON — WHO IS HERE AND WHY THEY DON'T GET ALONG**

##### **Command Structure (Top-Down, but Fractured)**

##### [Romag](romag-person.md) **(offsite, Greater Temple)**

gives orders but isn't present. His authority is absolute in theory, contested in practice.

##### [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) **(AO-18, NW corner)**

War-Priest, garrison warden. Romag's voice in the field. Doctrinaire, inflexible, expects obedience. Commands through discipline and fear. Does not retreat, does not negotiate, does not compromise.

_Every order from Tharvok carries religious weight — disobedience is heresy, not insubordination._

##### [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) **(AO-17, North)**

High Priest, Flan geomancer. The excavation is his project. He cares about the altar below, not the garrison above. Views the raiders as laborers and the raiding operation as Romag's concern, not his.

He will sacrifice garrison fighters to protect the excavation without hesitation. Often underground or in the chapel — unreliable as a tactical responder.

##### [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) **(AO-12, West wing)**

Field commander of the raiding operation. Professional soldier. Coordinates Long Road ambushes, manages logistics, runs the Nulb supply line.

Reports to Tharvok but considers himself the only competent tactician in the building. Dislikes Caidax. Tolerates Varnor. Respects Tharvok's discipline but not his rigidity.

##### [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) **(AO-13, West wing)**

Arcane advisor, illusionist, false-flag operator. Works alone. Reports to Varnor (geomancy) and Maris (operations).

Considers everyone else beneath him intellectually. Will not call for help unless cornered — pride forbids it. Operates the Scroll of Veil illusion device.

##### **Drogath "Stonejaw" Varn (AO-10, West side)**

Barracks sergeant, NCO. Manages the fighters. Pragmatic mercenary — not a zealot.

Respects Maris. Fears Tharvok. Doesn't understand Varnor. Has never spoken to Caidax.

#### **The Fracture Lines**

_**These are the cracks smart players can exploit:**_

**Tharvok vs. Varnor**

*   Tharvok wants to destroy the Gift of Beauty per doctrine. Varnor has not answered — because he does not care.
*   This frustrates Tharvok deeply. They coordinate logistics but do not trust each other's priorities.

**Maris vs. Caidax**

*   Maris is a soldier. Caidax is an academic playing at war.
*   Maris sees the false-flag operation as unnecessary risk.
*   Caidax sees Maris as a blunt instrument.
*   They cooperate only because Romag ordered it. Neither would risk their life for the other.

**Caidax vs. Everyone**

*   Caidax operates alone — drinks alone, studies alone, schemes alone.
*   If the priory falls, he retreats to the crypts.
*   He does not rally, does not warn, does not defend — he survives.

**Drogath and the Reavers vs. the Mission**

*   The fighters are mercenaries.
*   They signed on for coin and easy raids — not to die for doctrine**.**

**If leadership collapses (Tharvok, Maris, or command chain), morale breaks fast:**

*   Surrender
*   Flee
*   Stop fighting entirely

**Gnolls vs. Everyone**

The Bonegnaw gnolls (8–10) are loyal but opportunistic.

*   Take orders only from Tharvok
*   Granted territory: stables and ridge
*   Fight aggressively but without coordination

**If Tharvok falls:**

*   They revert to pack behavior
*   Defend their lair briefly
*   Then scatter into the hills
*   They will not die for the Temple

**Cookie (AO-23) vs. the Garrison**

The cook is not a combatant — but he knows everything.

If the party gains his trust (Persuasion DC 10–16 escalating), he reveals:

*   Meal schedules
*   Guard rotations
*   Tharvok's dinner routine (door unlocked)
*   Varnor's erratic habits
*   Prisoner conditions
*   Garrison morale (fraying)

### 2\. ALERT STATES — FROM QUIET TO CHAOS

##### State 0: Unaware

The priory operates under normal conditions. Guards follow routine patterns. No suspicion of intrusion.

**Conditions:**

*   No alarm raised
*   No suspicious activity detected
*   Routine patrols active

**Behavior:**

*   Guards remain at posts or follow patrol routes
*   Barracks relaxed
*   Command staff in normal positions
*   Gnolls in standard positions (ridge or bivouac)

**DM Notes:**

*   This is the party’s best opportunity for stealth
*   Encourage slow, deliberate movement
*   Noise and visibility matter most here

##### State 1: Suspicious

Something is wrong, but no confirmed threat. Guards are alert but not mobilized.

**Triggers:**

*   Unexplained noise
*   Missing guard
*   Strange movement or light

**Behavior:**

*   Guards investigate in pairs
*   Increased patrol frequency
*   Command staff becomes cautious but does not mobilize
*   Gnolls may begin scent tracking if nearby

**DM Notes:**

*   Tension rises, but the situation is still recoverable
*   Players can still return to stealth with careful play
*   Avoid immediate escalation unless clearly warranted

##### State 2: Partial Alert

Confirmed intrusion inside the priory, but no full alarm.

**Triggers:**

*   Combat within interior rooms
*   Guard raises alarm locally (shouting, running)
*   Body discovered

**Behavior:**

*   Nearby rooms respond to sound and movement
*   Local defenders engage
*   Courtyard remains largely unaware
*   Gnolls remain in position unless directly triggered

**DM Notes:**

*   This is the critical middle ground
*   Containment is possible if players act quickly
*   Use adjacency logic — only nearby areas respond

##### State 3: Full Alarm

The horn has sounded. The entire garrison mobilizes.

**Triggers:**

*   AO-22 signal horn is sounded
*   Clear, widespread combat detected

**Behavior:**

*   Full garrison response begins
*   Gnolls flood into the courtyard
*   Barracks deploy fighters
*   Command wing prepares defenses

**Response Timing:**

*   Round 1–2: Confusion and movement
*   Round 3–5: Organized resistance forms
*   After Round 5: Full defensive posture

**DM Notes:**

*   The courtyard becomes a kill zone quickly
*   Players must shift to objective-based play
*   This is no longer a stealth scenario

##### DM QUICK SUMMARY

*   State 0 — Silent infiltration
*   State 1 — Suspicion, recoverable
*   State 2 — Localized combat
*   State 3 — Full garrison response

**Key Rule:**

_Escalation should feel earned, not automatic. Let player actions drive the state change._

_Rooms can be isolated and cleared without alerting adjacent areas._

#### Combined Magic Threat Assessment

If all three casters are active simultaneously, the party faces:

*   **Tremorsense detection** (Tharvok) — no hiding on stone
*   **Terrain control** (Tharvok + Varnor) — difficult terrain, prone, barriers
*   **Disable effects** (Caidax) — Hold Person, Suggestion
*   **Escape mechanics** (Varnor + Caidax) — collapse, Misty Step, crypt retreat

**Reality Check:**

This scenario is unlikely.

*   Tharvok = NW (quarters)
*   Varnor = North or underground
*   Caidax = West chamber

They are not co-located. They do not coordinate.

_Each fights independently, from their own room, on their own terms._

**Tactical Truth:**

The party can defeat them sequentially if they control initiative and movement.

### 4\. THE TOWERS, WALLS & BREACHES

#### Three Towers

The priory has **three towers**, not one:

* * *

**SW Tower (AO-09) — The Main Guard Post**

The largest tower. Manned full-time.

*   Houses lookout and alarm horn
*   Overlooks main approach (AO-01/02/03)
*   Overlooks courtyard

_This is the garrison's primary observation point._

* * *

**NW Tower — Small, Vacant, with Access Door**

A small watchtower built into the northwest wall. Currently unmanned.

*   Checked irregularly by SW tower patrols
*   Not continuously occupied

**Key Feature:**

An interior access door leads:

*   Into the back of the complex
*   Into the garden (AO-04 herb patch)
*   Near the north wing (AO-18 Tharvok, AO-17 Varnor, AO-16 Scriptorium)

_This is one of the party's strongest infiltration routes if discovered._

* * *

**NE Tower — Small, Vacant**

A second small watchtower on the northeast wall.

*   Same irregular patrol checks
*   Overlooks eastern approach and stables
*   No interior access

_Observation only. No tactical value for entry._

#### Patrol Pattern

SW tower guards check the NW and NE towers on an **irregular rotation**.

*   Sometimes every hour
*   Sometimes every three hours
*   No fixed schedule

**Why this matters:**

*   The garrison relies on gnolls for perimeter warning
*   Tower patrols are lax and inconsistent
*   Security is based on assumption, not discipline

_A party observing for 1–2 hours can identify safe timing windows._

#### Wall Breaches (Two Entry Points)

**South Wall Breach — Near SW Tower**

A collapsed section of wall on the south side, adjacent to AO-09.

**Situation:**

*   Not repaired
*   Covered by tower line-of-sight (in theory)

**The Paradox:**

*   Close to strongest guard position
*   Lookout focuses on main approach, not rubble below

**Use Conditions:**

*   Night infiltration
*   During distraction

**Checks:**

*   Athletics DC 12 (climb rubble)
*   Stealth DC 14 (avoid tower notice)

_Risky, but exploitable with timing._

* * *

**East Wall Breach — Inside Stables (AO-07)**

A partially collapsed wall section within the stables.

**Situation:**

*   Covered by rubble and vegetation
*   Opens directly into stables interior
*   Bypasses courtyard and main gate

**Checks:**

*   Athletics DC 10 (climb through)
*   Stealth DC 13 (avoid noise)

**Advantages:**

*   No courtyard exposure
*   Direct access to interior via AO-08
*   Safer than all other entry points

_This is potentially the safest entry route in the entire priory._

#### DM Note

Neither breach is guarded.

*   The garrison is a raiding camp, not an engineering force
*   Structural repairs are not prioritized
*   Tharvok has reported the breaches but considers them low priority
*   Gnoll patrols are assumed to cover exterior threats

**Critical Insight:**

If the party neutralizes or bypasses the gnolls, these breaches become effectively **unguarded entrances**.

### 5\. THE GNOLL PROBLEM

#### Bonegnaw Gnoll Detachment

**Strength:** 8–10 gnolls  
**Camp:** Outside the walls near AO-04 (garden) / NW tower, in the treeline  
**Patrol:** 4–6 gnolls on ridge patrol (AO-01/02 others remain at bivouac or roam near walls

#### Why They Camp Outside

The human garrison does not allow gnolls inside the priory.

*   Drogath’s men refuse the smell
*   Horses panic if gnolls are nearby
*   Courtyard is needed for operations (wagons, prisoners, muster)

Tharvok has granted the Bonegnaw pack external territory:

*   The ridge
*   Garden perimeter
*   Treeline near NW tower

The gnolls prefer it. Their camp consists of:

*   Scraped sleeping hollows
*   Fire pit
*   Gnawed bones
*   Hide shelters

_Close enough to hear the horn. Far enough to keep the peace with the humans._

**Tactical Implication:**

*   NW tower access route passes near the gnoll camp
*   Garden approach risks direct contact
*   Scent tracking increases detection risk
*   If gnolls are on patrol, the camp may be lightly defended

#### Why Gnolls Are Dangerous

*   **Scent Tracking** — stealth alone is insufficient; wind direction matters
*   **Speed** — bivouac gnolls reach courtyard in 3–4 rounds
*   **Reinforcement** — ridge patrol returns in 2d4 minutes
*   **Aggression** — immediate charge, no coordination delay
*   **Horn-Reactive** — gnolls signal instantly without assessment

#### Why Gnolls Are Exploitable

*   **No Coordination** — fight as a pack, not a unit
*   **Territorial** — defend exterior only, not interior rooms
*   **Outside the Walls** — can be physically locked out
*   **False Alarm Vulnerability** — easily triggered by distraction
*   **Leadership Dependency** — rely on Tharvok for structure

**If Tharvok Falls:**

*   Gnolls revert to pack instinct
*   Defend camp briefly
*   Scavenge
*   Scatter into Kron Hills within hours
*   Will not defend the priory

#### Gnoll Encounter Timing

**Approach Vectors:**

*   **Ridge (AO-01/02)** — patrol gnolls are first encounter
*   **Garden (AO-04)** — bivouac is immediate obstacle
*   **East (stables breach)** — may bypass gnolls entirely

#### Best-Case Neutralization Strategies

*   Time assault when 4–6 gnolls are on ridge patrol
*   Avoid garden approach entirely
*   Use east stables breach for clean entry

Eliminate bivouac gnolls quietly:

*   Ranged attacks
*   Sleep effects
*   Ambush tactics

_Difficult, but achievable with planning._

##### ASSAULT AT A GLANCE

**Best Player Entry:**

*   East Stables Breach (Route D) — safest, stealth optimal
*   NW Tower (Route C) — high-risk decapitation route

**Biggest Weaknesses:**

*   No interior communication system
*   Broken walls (unguarded breaches)
*   Uncoordinated leadership
*   Mercenary morale (not fanatics)

**First 3 Rounds After Alarm:**

*   Total chaos — no coordination
*   Gnolls, reavers, leaders act independently
*   Best window for decisive player action

**Critical Trigger:**

*   AO-22 Signal Horn triggers full alarm
*   Tharvok becomes aware regardless of distance

**Key Chokepoints:**

*   AO-08 (service corridor)
*   AO-11 (central junction)
*   Control these to control information flow

**Leadership Targets (Priority):**

*   Drogath — breaks barracks morale
*   Maris — ends coordination
*   Tharvok — ends authority (hardest fight)
*   Varnor — stops excavation and ritual

**Morale Thresholds:**

*   30 percent casualties — wavering
*   50 percent casualties — collapse
*   Leadership deaths accelerate both

**Garrison Collapse Signs:**

*   Reavers retreat or surrender
*   Gnolls scatter into the hills
*   Barracks fall back
*   Prisoners begin breaking free

**Who Never Breaks:**

*   Tharvok — fights to the death
*   Varnor — retreats to crypt and begins ritual
*   AO-22 Guards — hold position until slain

**Best Player Exploits:**

*   Silence to isolate wings
*   Trigger gnolls to disrupt defenses
*   Strike during active raid (reduced garrison)
*   Offer surrender after leadership falls

**Golden Rule:**

_This is not a fortress — it is a brittle machine. Strike the right points, and it breaks._

##### 10\. CONFUSION FACTORS — WHAT THE PARTY CAN EXPLOIT

_The Garrison Doesn’t Drill. This is the window where decisive action wins encounters._

This is a raiding camp, not a military fortress.

*   No coordinated defense plan
*   Guards act on habit, not command
*   First 2–3 rounds after alarm = chaos
*   Gnolls, reavers, and leaders react independently

* * *

##### No Interior Communication System

*   No bells, no internal alarms
*   Only AO-22 signal horn exists
*   Communication relies on shouting and movement
*   **Exploitation:** Silence spell can sever entire alarm chain

Control choke points:

*   AO-08 (service corridor)
*   AO-11 (central junction)
*   Prevent information from spreading between wings

* * *

##### The Priests Don’t Coordinate

*   Tharvok holds position
*   Varnor retreats underground
*   Caidax flees through armory
*   Maris commands from west wing
*   They never gather to form unified command
*   They will not fight together unless forced

_Even if cornered together, they argue rather than coordinate._

* * *

##### The Mercenaries Don’t Believe

Drogath’s fighters are motivated by coin, not doctrine.

*   No loyalty to the Elder Elemental God
*   No investment in excavation
*   Professional, but not fanatical

**Exploitation:**

*   Remove leadership → morale collapse accelerates
*   Offer escape or surrender → many will take it
*   Half the garrison may disengage if given a way out

* * *

##### The Gnolls Are a Double-Edged Sword

*   Charge without orders
*   Ignore battlefield structure
*   Do not distinguish between friendly and enemy lines

**Effects:**

*   Break chokepoints unintentionally
*   Disrupt defensive formations
*   Attack prisoners if cells are opened
*   Create chaos for both sides

**Exploitation:**

*   Trigger gnoll aggression at key moments
*   Use them to fracture organized defense

* * *

##### The Raiding Party Might Be Out

*   6–8 fighters + gnolls may be absent on patrol or raid
*   Garrison rarely at full strength

**Intel Sources:**

*   Maris’s maps (AO-12)
*   Cookie (AO-23)
*   Duty roster (AO-18)

**Exploitation:**

*   Time assault for low manpower window
*   Major raid → 60–70% normal resistance

* * *

##### The Walls Are Broken

*   Two breaches exist
*   Neither guarded or repaired
*   Gnolls positioned away from stables breach
*   Tower watches main approach, not rubble gaps

_The garrison relies on threats that are not covering the weaknesses._

**Exploitation:**

*   Perimeter scouting reveals unguarded entry points
*   These are effectively open doors

NW and NE towers usually unmanned  
SW tower patrol is irregular:

*   Sometimes hourly
*   Sometimes absent for hours

**Exploitation:**

*   NW tower provides interior access
*   Timed correctly → uncontested entry
*   Direct access to command corridor

##### 7\. TACTICAL SCENARIOS — HOW IT PLAYS OUT

###### Scenario A: Clean Infiltration (No Alarm)

The party enters via stealth and moves through the interior without triggering the horn.

**What Works:**

*   Rooms can be cleared one at a time
*   No interior alarm system — adjacent rooms remain unaware
*   Cookie can be recruited for intelligence
*   Halven Sere (AO-20) can be freed unnoticed
*   False-flag trunk secured
*   Evidence gathered (AO-13, AO-16)

**What Breaks It:**

*   A shouted warning
*   Spooked horse
*   Gnoll scent detection
*   Guard discovering a body
*   Any noise reaching tower or barracks

**DM Guidance:**

*   Reward stealth heavily
*   Each silent room = meaningful progress
*   Maintain tension at all times
*   Doors = risk points
*   Corridors may contain wandering threats

_The party should feel like they are threading a needle, not clearing an empty dungeon._

###### Scenario B: Partial Alarm (Interior Only)

The party is detected inside, but the tower horn has not sounded.

**What Happens:**

*   Local response only (see Interior Breach table)
*   Courtyard garrison remains inactive
*   Gnolls remain outside
*   Tharvok may or may not respond depending on distance

**Party Advantage:**

*   Combat remains localized
*   Opportunity to contain escalation
*   Victory can reset tension if handled quickly

**Critical Decision Point:**

The AO-22 elite guards possess a **signal horn**.

*   If sounded → escalation to full alarm  
    Tharvok becomes fully aware
*   Interior response becomes coordinated

**Tactical Objective:**

*   Kill or disable guards before horn use
*   Use Silence or similar effects to prevent escalation

_This is the tipping point between control and chaos._

###### Scenario C: Full Alarm (Horn Sounded)

The guard tower horn sounds. Full garrison mobilization.

**What Happens:**

*   Response clock begins (see State 2)
*   Gnolls flood courtyard
*   Barracks deploy
*   Command wing fortifies
*   Courtyard becomes kill zone within ~5 rounds

**Party Options:**

**Hold a Choke Point**

*   AO-08 north door is defensible
*   Fight enemies piecemeal as they arrive

**Rush the Objective**

*   3–5 rounds before full deployment
*   Reach key targets before full lockdown

**Create Diversion**

*   Fire in stables splits forces
*   Noise in courtyard redirects defenders

**Divide and Conquer**

*   No unified command
*   Tharvok remains isolated
*   Varnor retreats underground
*   Maris controls west wing
*   Drogath holds barracks
*   Caidax operates alone

_The garrison cannot coordinate if pressured on multiple fronts._

###### Scenario D: Decapitation Strike

The party targets leadership before full organization occurs.

**Priority Targets:**

###### **Drogath (AO-10)**

*   Removes barracks cohesion
*   Disadvantage on next attack
*   Lose +2 initiative bonus
*   Morale collapses fastest

###### **Maris (AO-12)**

*   Removes tactical coordination
*   West wing becomes disorganized
*   Reavers lose leadership

###### **Tharvok (AO-18)**

*   Ends doctrinal authority
*   Gnolls lose patron and begin scattering
*   Varnor loses protection
*   Hardest target (tremorsense + earth magic)

###### **Varnor (AO-17)**

*   Stops excavation permanently
*   Secures Stonefang
*   Prevents escalation of crypt level
*   If he escapes → dungeon becomes significantly more dangerous

**Optimal Order:** _Drogath → Maris → Caidax → Tharvok → Varnor_

**Reality:**

*   The party will not know this order in advance
*   Discovery through play is part of the experience

##### 8\. MORALE & COLLAPSE

##### When Does the Garrison Break?

This is a raiding camp, not a fortified stronghold. The fighters are mercenaries, not zealots.

**Morale is governed by thresholds.**

###### Garrison Holds If:

*   Command structure intact (Drogath alive, Maris alive)
*   Tharvok’s authority remains unquestioned
*   Casualties below 30% (~12 fighters lost)

###### Garrison Wavers If:

*   Drogath is killed or incapacitated (barracks morale drops immediately)
*   Maris is killed or flees (west wing collapses)
*   Casualties exceed 30% with no clear leadership response
*   Gnolls break and scatter (psychological shock)

_"Even the gnolls are running…"_

###### Garrison Collapses If:

*   Both Drogath **and** Maris are removed
*   Tharvok is killed (doctrinal authority lost)
*   Casualties exceed 50% (~19 fighters lost)
*   Party demonstrates overwhelming force:
*   Area-effect devastation
*   Multiple fighters dropped in a single round

##### What Collapse Looks Like

*   Reavers stop advancing and fall back toward barracks
*   Fighters throw down weapons and attempt escape via courtyard
*   Gnolls retreat to bivouac, then scatter into the hills
*   Remaining guards barricade themselves in rooms
*   Cookie surrenders immediately
*   Prisoners in AO-20 begin shouting and attempting escape

##### Who Never Breaks

**Tharvok:** Fights to the death regardless of battlefield state

**Varnor:**

*   Retreats to crypt level
*   Refuses surrender
*   Initiates awakening ritual

###### **AO-22 Elite Guards**

*   Disciplined and unwavering
*   Hold crypt entrance until slain
