THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE
==========================

The Ruins of the Moathouse crouch in the marshland approximately two miles east of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), their jagged silhouette visible above the cattails and tamaracks as the track turns north toward the fens. The approach is slow — the path overgrown, washed out in places, and sinking into bog — and the ruin itself sits behind a stagnant moat, its drawbridge chains broken, its towers collapsed, its great hall stripped to rotting banners and cobwebs. At first glance, it is exactly what the villagers of Hommlet believe it to be: an abandoned ruin, shunned, dangerous, best left alone.   At second glance — if the party has gone down the right stairway and through the right doors — it is an active Temple of Elemental Evil forward operating base, garrisoned by fanatical guards, humanoid mercenaries, and a cleric of exceptional capability who answers directly to [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md), Supreme Commander of the Greater Temple.  

> _"They will search Hommlet, question the merchants, count the missing gnomes. And still they will not look here. Why would they? It is just ruins."_ — [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md), in a letter to Hedrack, 576 CY

 

### History: What This Place Was

  This fortress was once the Temple of Elemental Evil's advance outpost — a watchtower base for raids, looting, and the subjugation of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) and the surrounding lands. Servants of the Temple operated from here to bring the villages and farmsteads of the Viscounty into bondage, with conquered folk earmarked as slave labor for the Temple's ever-expanding construction further west.   The outpost was spared during the main Battle of [Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) in 569 CY — the armies of Furyondy and Veluna were too occupied destroying the Temple's main force to bother with this minor garrison. Afterward, they came. A detachment of horse and foot with a small siege train surrounded the Moathouse, cut it off, and battered it into ruin. Common folk from miles around came to help. The place was left broken and abandoned.   For seven years it sat in the marsh, home to nothing but vermin, a brigand band using the upper floors, and the creeping damp. The people of Hommlet avoided it. The roads slowly forgot it. And then, in 576 CY, someone went back inside, cleared the dungeon level, and began furnishing a chamber on the lowest floor.   That someone was [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md).  

### Lareth's Mission: What Hedrack Sent Him to Do

  Lareth the Beautiful is a Level 5 Cleric of exceptional charisma and tactical instinct, dispatched here by [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) with a layered mandate. He is simultaneously [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s hidden agent and the Temple's surface-level field commander — a contradiction Hedrack does not yet fully appreciate. His operational objectives in 576 CY are:  

#### 1\. Disrupt Viscounty Trade

  The merchant roads running through this region are the commercial lifelines of the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md). Lareth's forces raid caravans and travelers on all approach routes:  

*   **The Long Road** — running south from [Nigb's Rest](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) through [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) (10 miles south) and [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) (30 miles south on the border of [Celene](celene-organization.md))
*   **The eastern approaches** — the High and Low Roads running east from Hommlet to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), which the Moathouse flanks directly
*   **Farmstead and hamlet raids** — targeting the surrounding villages, isolating Hommlet and building a ring of fear

  The brigands occupying the Moathouse's upper floor (led by [Osred Burch](osred-burch-person.md), a Level 2 fighter) operate independently from Lareth's dungeon garrison — but they serve as an unwitting buffer and a plausible explanation for the bandit raids. If the party clears the upper floor and reports to Hommlet, the Council may consider the matter settled. Lareth's dungeon operation below continues untouched.  

#### 2\. Fracture Verbobonc's Political Alliances

  Hedrack has identified the political web of the Viscounty as a strategic vulnerability. The [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) — the confederation of gnome settlements anchored at [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), and [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) — is a critical pillar of Verbobonc's economic and military defense. [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md), 20 miles southwest, guards the Greenway Valley approach. [Celene](celene-organization.md), the fey elven kingdom whose northern border touches the township of [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md), extends its protection across the southern trade routes.   Lareth's raids are calibrated to blame the wrong parties. His humanoid forces — gnolls sent by King Korvash Bonegnaw of the Fortress of Jagged Fangs and bugbears freshly recruited from the same network — are used specifically for raids near gnome settlements. The political read: gnomish or near-gnomish forces are hitting Verbobonc merchant caravans. The Viscounty lords and the merchants who depend on the Long Road begin to question whether their gnome allies can be trusted, or whether the [Free Alliance](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) is harboring aggressive factions.   Simultaneously, gnome caravans and scouting parties are being hit by human brigands — a mirror of the above, designed to make the gnomes suspect Verbobonc authorities are looking the other way, or worse, profiting from the raids.   The gnome prisoner in the Moathouse's cells (see: The Prisoners) is the live evidence of this operation working exactly as intended.  

#### 3\. Recruit, Plunder, and Supply the Greater Temple

  The Moathouse is not primarily a defensive position. It is a collection point. Lareth's forces conduct raids across the surrounding region and return with:  

*   **Plundered supplies** — food, goods, weapons, and coin stripped from caravans and farmsteads
*   **Prisoners** — merchants, travelers, laborers, and gnomes captured for transport to the Temple as slave labor or for interrogation
*   **Recruits** — desperate men, criminals, humanoids, and others drawn into Temple service by promise of coin, protection, or a share of the plunder

  The black capes with the yellow eye of fire symbol — 70 of which sit in the Moathouse's sealed armory (Area 20, alongside 50 spears, 10 glaives, 6 guisarmes, 3 battle axes, and 120 arrows) — are waiting for the next wave of recruits Lareth has been promised.  

### The Logistics Chain: From Moathouse to the Temple

  Lareth does not hold his plunder at the Moathouse. The ruined fortress is a staging point, not a vault. Once a sufficient haul of supplies and prisoners is assembled, it moves east through a carefully maintained covert supply route:  

#### Stage 1: The Moathouse to [Trevil's Fens Cottage](trevilE28099s-fens-cottage-landmark.md)

  Wagons loaded at the Moathouse move west along the track toward [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), then detour north into the [Fens of Tor](fens-of-tor-location.md), where [Trevil's Fens Cottage](trevilE28099s-fens-cottage-landmark.md) sits on the western edge of the fens. [Demien Trevils](demien-trevils-person.md) maintains this waystation: a staging point for goods where the trail is too poor for heavy wagon travel and where Lareth's men can rest and rotate.   _Note for DM: This leg of the route skirts the northern edge of Hommlet's awareness zone. Characters with ranger skills or strong Hommlet contacts may intercept a supply wagon on this leg if they are actively investigating the missing caravans._  

#### Stage 2: [Trevil's Fens Cottage](trevilE28099s-fens-cottage-landmark.md) to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) via the High Road

  Goods transfer to lighter transport at the Fens Cottage and move east along the High Road through the village of [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). Nulb is a corrupt river village that serves as the Temple's civilian front — its taverns, docks, and commerce all infused with Temple operatives and collaborators.  

#### Stage 3: [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) — Madame Selentis Takes Over

  At [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) — known locally as Madame Selentis — receives the shipment. Her operation at the Waterside Hostel controls prisoner logistics for the entire region. She re-manifests the cargo as legitimate river trade and organizes final transport east under the direct command of Greater Temple men.  

#### Stage 4: [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) and the Secret Tunnel

  East of [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), approximately one mile from the ruins of the Temple of Elemental Evil site, stands [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) — a final waystation outside [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) where supplies and prisoners are prepared for underground movement. From the cottage, a concealed route leads to a secret tunnel connecting directly to Room 335 of the Greater Temple, [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md)'s domain, which connects to the escape tunnels linked to [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md)'s lair on Level Three.   _Note for DM: The full logistics chain — Moathouse → [Trevil's Fens Cottage](trevilE28099s-fens-cottage-landmark.md) → High Road → [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) → [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) → [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) → Room 335 tunnel — is the Temple's primary surface supply line. Disrupting any node of this chain creates ripple effects across the entire Temple operation. The party does not need to assault the Temple directly to hurt it. They can strangle its supply line one link at a time._  

### Geographic Context

  Understanding the Moathouse requires understanding its position in the regional map. It sits at the center of a web of roads and settlements:  

*   **[Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md)** — 1 mile west along the track. The primary adventuring base and the settlement most directly threatened by Moathouse operations. Hommlet's Council suspects bandits but does not know the Temple is active.
*   **[Nigb's Rest](nigb-s-rest-settlement.md)** — 10 miles north along the Long Road. A township on the Long Road that feeds merchant traffic south. Caravans from Nigb's Rest are primary raiding targets.
*   **Lands of [House Asbury](house-asbury-organization.md)** — 10 miles north of the Etters (a small mountain range north of Hommlet). House Asbury is a noble house with an interest in road security through this region. Raids impacting the northern Long Road approach Asbury territory.
*   **[Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md)** — 10 miles south on the Long Road. The first gnome settlement south. Gnoll raids from the Moathouse are hitting caravans between Hommlet and Sheernobb, which is the most visible and politically damaging flashpoint.
*   **[Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md)** — 30 miles south (10 miles south of Sheernobb). A township on the northern border of [Celene](celene-organization.md), the fey elven kingdom. Elven influence and Free Alliance commerce intersect here. Disrupting trade to Ostverk pulls at the Celene relationship.
*   **[Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md)** — 20 miles southwest. The military anchor of [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md). If the gnome-Verbobonc alliance fractures, Kron Fortress becomes less available as a defensive partner for the Viscounty.
*   **[Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)** — approximately 10 miles east via the High Road. The Temple's corrupt civilian hub and the next stop on the supply chain.

 

### Current Garrison: What the Party Is Walking Into

  The Moathouse operates on two levels. DMs should use the original T1 module encounter key for room-by-room details. This is the operational overview.  

#### Upper Level — The Brigands (Not Lareth's Men)

  The upper level is occupied by an independent brigand band under **Osred Burch** (Level 2 Fighter, 8 men). They are not Temple affiliates. They know someone is using the dungeons below but have no idea who. They have repaired the door to their "Black Chamber" (Area 7), avoid the lower levels entirely, and do not know about the secret stairway. For the party, clearing the brigands is a false victory — the real operation is underground. The brigands' presence is useful to Lareth: investigators who encounter and clear the upper floor brigands tend to report the problem solved.  

#### Dungeon Level — Lareth's Operation

  **Command:**

*   [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) — "The New Master." Level 5 Cleric. Located in the lavishly furnished Area 35. Commands through absolute authority and the genuine terror his capabilities inspire. Carries the Staff of Striking and wears the Phylactery of Action (immune to paralysis). If seriously threatened, he offers non-magical treasures as ransom rather than die.
*   **Gattas Void** — Lareth's lieutenant. Level 4 Fighter. Located in Area 34. Stays close to Lareth. Wealth in gems (29 gems, 100 gp each) and a platinum chain of office (1,000 gp) indicate his standing.

  **Guards (Area 33 and 34):** 18 human fighters in black scale mail with the yellow eye of fire on tunic and cloak, plus two sergeants. They are organized, disciplined, and fanatically loyal to the New Master. Their sign is the yellow eye of fire; the countersign is crossed arms before the face. Intruders who do not display the correct sign and countersign are engaged immediately.   **Humanoid Forces:**

*   **6 Bugbears (Area 27)** — recently recruited by Lareth through King Korvash Bonegnaw's network. They know only that the Master is "somewhere to the north." Dangerous on patrol. Gaining surprise on 1-3.
*   **9 Gnolls + Flind Leader (Area 29)** — sent by King Ga'awootarr of the Gnoll Fortress via King Korvash Bonegnaw. Dissatisfied with their lot (lost 6 members raiding east and south), resentful of the bugbears' preferential treatment, and willing to parley for coin or better terms. Their Flind leader wears a gnomish skull as a trophy. They can be bribed to desert for 6+ gp each.
*   **Ogre Lubash (Area 24)** — lured to Lareth's service by baubles and fresh human meat. Guards prisoners in the dungeon. Attacks anyone without the yellow eye of fire symbol or the correct arm-cross sign.

  **Undead (Lareth's sanctioned guardians):**

*   **12 Zombies (Area 21)** — positioned to intercept intruders on the main dungeon corridor
*   **4 Ghouls (Area 31)** — fed corpses from the torture chamber by Lareth

 

#### The Prisoners (Area 25)

  Lareth currently holds three prisoners in the ogre's pantry (Area 25), each with campaign significance:  

*   **Two merchants from [Dyvers, Free Lands of](dyvers2C-free-lands-of-organization.md)** — captured on the merchant road, undergoing mild torture. If rescued, they promise payment (100 sp each, delivered via caravan to Hommlet within four weeks). Their abduction represents the trade disruption Hedrack ordered.
*   **Anrew Jadethorn** — a [Tulvar](tulvar-settlement.md) gnome caught spying on gnoll movements, taken prisoner by gnolls and given directly to Lareth by King Korvash Bonegnaw. He carries an iron ring recognized by gnomes within 100 miles as a sign of alliance, granting the bearer a 25% bonus to favorable gnome reactions. His presence in a human Temple prison — and the fact that gnolls handed him over — is a live political incident. Gnomes who learn of his capture will not easily forgive whoever allowed it to happen.

 

### Key Intelligence Points

  What captured bandits or guards can reveal (under interrogation or persuasion) escalates as the party moves deeper:  

*   **Upper floor brigands** know: someone uses the cellars; they avoid it; a light sometimes shows from below at night
*   **Bugbears and gnolls** know: the "New Master" is somewhere north in the dungeon; the symbol is the yellow eye of fire; they raid east and south; they deliver prisoners to men who come to collect them
*   **Human guards** know: the New Master is Lareth; the sign and countersign; the location of the escape tunnel; that wagons run east regularly
*   **Lareth** knows: Hedrack sent him; the full logistics chain to the Temple; the location of Room 335; that [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) holds the Orb of Golden Death; Lolth's broader plan

 

### Adventure Hooks and DM Triggers

 

*   **"What Happened to the Dyvers Merchants?"** — A caravan from Dyvers is overdue. The party is hired to investigate. The trail leads east from Hommlet, and the Moathouse track is the only recent sign of wagon traffic.
*   **"The Gnome Problem"** — Sheernobb's factor in Hommlet reports that gnomish caravans south of Hommlet are being hit — by what appear to be human brigands. Someone is working very hard to make Verbobonc and the [Free Alliance](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) distrust each other.
*   **"Rescue Anrew Jadethorn"** — A gnome ally of the party's, or a contact from [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), reveals that a Free Alliance scout has gone missing. His iron ring — if the party recovers and returns it — earns permanent gnome goodwill and opens Sheernobb, Greenway Valley, and Ostverk as allies.
*   **"Follow the Wagons"** — The party intercepts a supply wagon on the High Road east of Hommlet. Surviving guards know the route. Following the wagon trail backward leads to the Moathouse; following it forward leads to [Trevil's Fens Cottage](trevilE28099s-fens-cottage-landmark.md) and then to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).
*   **"The New Master"** — Rumors in Hommlet's Inn of the Welcome Wench speak of a beautiful but terrifying priest seen once near the ruined track east of town. What cleric of such evident quality is doing in a marsh ruin?
*   **"If Lareth Dies"** — Lareth's death does not end the Moathouse operation. Hedrack sends a 10th-level assassin (Segarus) to investigate within 5-20 days. The supply chain to Nulb is not disrupted by Lareth's death. Only destroying [Trevil's Fens Cottage](trevilE28099s-fens-cottage-landmark.md) or exposing [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) breaks the chain at a meaningful scale.
