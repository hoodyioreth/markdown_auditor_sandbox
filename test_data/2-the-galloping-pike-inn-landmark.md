2\. The Galloping Pike Inn
==========================

_

> A cornerstone of Littleburrow’s charm, The Galloping Pike Inn is a warm and welcoming haven for travelers and locals alike. Known for its excellent food, fine ale, and delightful entertainment, the inn exudes halfling hospitality with every bite, sip, and smile.

_The Galloping Pike Inn is a cozy, ivy-covered building nestled near the heart of Littleburrow, close to the bustling crossroads. Its sturdy halfling-style construction combines practicality and charm.

*   **Architecture:** Built into a low hillside, the inn has round windows, a mossy slate roof, and a large round wooden door decorated with carvings of pikes and lilypads.
*   **Interior:** Inside, low ceilings and timber beams frame a snug common room with a central hearth. Tables of various heights accommodate both halfling-sized and human-sized guests. Walls are adorned with fishing trophies, local paintings, and woven tapestries.
*   **Rooms:** The Galloping Pike features ten guest rooms, each comfortably appointed with feather beds, patchwork quilts, and handwoven rugs. Two larger rooms on the second floor accommodate human-sized travelers.
*   **Courtyard**: Behind the inn lies a small courtyard with wooden picnic tables, a stone oven, and space for evening performances.

### Historical Background

The inn was founded two generations ago by the Burrows family to cater to merchants and travelers passing through the trade crossroads. Over the years, it has become a hub of social and economic activity for Littleburrow. Its reputation for hospitality and fine halfling ale has made it a popular stop for adventurers, traders, and locals alike._

> “Mind your manners at my table, or you’ll be out the door faster than you can say ‘second breakfast.’”

_

#### Culture and Community

The Galloping Pike is not just an inn; it’s a cultural hub where tales are spun, songs are sung, and strangers become friends.

*   **Halfling Hospitality**: The inn prides itself on extending a warm welcome to all who enter.
*   **Community Gatherings:** Locals often visit to celebrate festivals, share news, and listen to traveling minstrels.
*   **Entertainment:** Evening performances often include halfling bards, traveling troupes, and storytelling competitions.

_

> “A laugh and a full mug solve most of life’s problems—or at least they make ‘em more bearable.”

_

#### Notable Features and Services

*   **Exceptional Food and Ale**: Mira’s famous pies and the inn’s house ale, “Golden Pike,” are beloved by all who visit.
*   **Rumor Mill:** Travelers often share tales of far-off lands and local troubles, making the inn a valuable source of information for adventurers.
*   **Entertainment:** Nightly performances range from halfling folk songs to dramatic retellings of legendary exploits.
*   **Stables:** Pim’s care ensures that mounts and wagons are safe and well-tended.

##### Hidden Secrets

*   **Hidden Cellar**: Beneath the inn lies a hidden wine cellar, said to connect to a network of smuggler’s tunnels. Bertro is unaware of the tunnels but may unwittingly lead adventurers to their entrance.
*   **Mysterious Patron**: A cloaked figure frequently rents a room under various aliases. This individual is an agent of the Temple of Elemental Evil, using the inn to spy on travelers heading to and from Verbobonc.
*   **The Ale’s Secret:** The recipe for “Golden Pike” ale includes a rare herb found only in the Greenway Valley, making it a target for poachers and rival brewers.
*   **Mira’s Past**: Mira once served as a chef for a noble family in Verbobonc and is privy to courtly gossip that could interest the adventurers.

##### Adventurer Hooks

*   **A Missing Merchant:** A merchant traveling to Littleburrow has gone missing, and their goods were last seen at The Galloping Pike.
*   **A Stranger’s Tale:** A mysterious bard performs a song hinting at a treasure hidden in the nearby hills.
*   **An Urgent Message:** A courier staying at the inn needs adventurers to deliver an urgent message to Castle Estival.

### The Owner and Their Family

![](./images/Bertro%20Burrows.jpg "Bertro Burrows.jpg")

Bertro Burrow by

##### Bertro Burrows

Owner and head innkeeper.

*   **Description**: Bertro is a jolly, barrel-chested halfling with curly brown hair, twinkling green eyes, and a perpetual smile. He is rarely seen without his signature red waistcoat and pipe.
*   **Personality**: Warm, boisterous, and endlessly curious, Bertro loves to learn the tales and trades of every traveler who comes through his door.
*   **Motivation**: Bertro is dedicated to maintaining the inn’s reputation as a place of comfort and camaraderie.

##### Mira Burrows

Bertro’s wife and the inn’s head cook.

*   **Description**: Mira is a petite, silver-haired halfling with a no-nonsense demeanor and a knack for creating delicious meals. Her apron is often dusted with flour, and she moves through the kitchen with practiced precision.
*   **Personality**: Stern but kind, Mira ensures the inn runs like clockwork and has a soft spot for children and struggling travelers.
*   **Motivation**: Mira takes immense pride in her cooking and believes good food can heal the heart.

##### Pim Burrows

Bertro and Mira’s youngest son and the inn’s stable hand.

*   **Description**: Pim is a lanky, freckled teenager with a mop of unruly hair. He loves animals more than people and spends most of his time in the stables.
*   **Personality**: Shy but good-hearted, Pim has a quiet charm and a knack for calming even the most stubborn horses.
*   **Motivation**: Pim dreams of one day becoming a traveling animal trainer.

##### Lennin and Calie Tallowbrim

Hired staff, siblings, and servers at the inn

*   **Lennin**: A stocky halfling with a quick wit and an infectious laugh. He is often the life of the party in the common room. Outgoing, flirtatious, and always ready with a clever joke.
*   **Calie**: His more reserved sister, who has a knack for remembering customers’ preferences and a talent for handling difficult patrons. Observant, empathetic, and fiercely protective of her brother.
*   **Motivation**: Both siblings work hard to save for their dream of opening a bakery in Littleburrow.

### Clientele

The Galloping Pike serves a wide variety of guests, from locals to travelers passing through.

*   **Merchants:** Regularly stop for rest and resupply on their trade routes.
*   **Adventurers**: Seek out the inn for its warm beds and local rumors.
*   **Locals**: Often gather for meals, celebrations, or simply to hear the latest gossip.
*   **Traveling Performer**s: Attracted by the promise of an appreciative audience and a hearty meal.
