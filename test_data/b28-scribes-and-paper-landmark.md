=====================

##### Overview

**Scribes and Paper** is Verbobonc’s premier source for **durable, treated paper** and **professional scribing services**, the work of master craftswoman and widow **Alara Reed**. B28 Scribes and Paper

Her signature “Alaran paper” is **resistant to fire and water**, coated with a secret light oil that _helps_ ink bond instead of smearing it. Sheets can be fully immersed in water for hours and endure heat that would ruin common parchment—making them prized for **merchant ledgers, spellbooks, contracts, and travel-worn scrolls**. B28 Scribes and Paper

Because early experiments filled the lane with harsh odors, Alara moved the papermaking and treatment out to a small millyard **outside the city**, keeping the shop itself quiet and sweet-smelling while production chugs away beyond the walls.

##### Street Approach

The cobbles here are worn smooth by boot leather and cart wheels; this stretch of lane feels like the city’s nervous system, forever twitching with messages. Houses press close on either side, their upper stories leaning inward as if to eavesdrop.  
  
Halfway along, a small pale green cottage clings to the road’s edge so tightly that folk simply cut across its front porch as they pass. Light yellow trim frames the windows, each sill crowded with clay planters bursting with mums and bright blossoms. Hanging over the door, a painted sign reads “Scribes and Paper,” its letters twined with painted vines and flowers.  
  
The door stands propped open. Warm lamplight spills across the threshold, carrying with it the dry, pleasant smell of ink and paper overlaid with a faint, unfamiliar tang—some alchemical oil, perhaps. Inside you glimpse shelves of neatly stacked folios, racks of drying pages, and a central desk where a severe-looking woman bends over a ledger, quill scratching in precise strokes. This, you realize, is where half the city comes to make sure its promises outlive its people.

#### History and Background

The evolution of Scribes and Paper from a modest local shop to a key player in Verbobonc’s market is a testament to the innovation and resilience of its owner.

*   **Foundation and Growth:** Originally started by Alara Reed’s late husband, the business expanded significantly under her leadership after introducing the revolutionary paper.
*   **Relocation of Production:** Moved the paper production outside the city to address neighborhood concerns about odors from the treatment process.

### The Cottage Shop

##### 1\. Front Room – The Work & Sales Floor

Various ink jars and batches of parchment rest on the small desk in the center of the room. Vases full of flowers stand on top of the bookcases along the back wall and in the front corners of the desk. Large pieces of leather and thin boards rest on a workbench along the west wall. Neat stacks of paper stand on shelves beneath the workbench. A solid oak door in the back of the room leads into another part of the cottage.

The main room is both showroom and studio:

*   A **central desk** holds ink-jars, quill stands, sand shakers, and a small lockbox for coin and seals.
*   Along the back wall stand **bookcases** and pigeonhole shelves stacked with bound ledgers, blank journals, and boxed spellbooks, all bearing Alara’s discrete mark.
*   A long **workbench** against the west wall supports cutting tools, leather hides, thin wood boards, and clamps for binding—this is where she assembles her ledgers and books. Neat stacks of treated paper rest on shelves beneath.
*   A solid oak door at the rear leads into her private living area; its lock is sturdy and usually engaged.

The room is bright, orderly, and dotted with **fresh flowers in vases**—a slight defiance against the dry, ink-stained business she practices.

##### 2\. Alara’s Living Area

The tiny back room of the cottage is filled with soft, plush furniture surrounded by tall bookcases. A small bed in the northwest corner is almost completely enclosed by bookshelves, as is the unused stove in the center of the west wall. Curtains cover the few windows making the room rather dark and forbidding. Although neat and clean, the room has a sterile, empty feel to it.

Behind the shop lies a small, dim room that feels almost unused:

*   Soft, plush furniture and tall bookcases crowd the walls.
*   A small bed in the corner is almost caged by shelves; the stove stands cold, long since given over to storage.
*   Heavy curtains keep the room in perpetual dusk, neat but strangely empty, more like a tidy cell than a home.

Since her husband’s death, Alara mainly **sleeps here and little else**—she takes most meals in nearby taverns, then returns to read and collapse into bed before another long day at the desk.

### Goods – Paper

(Alaran Paper & Bound Volumes)

All products bear Alara’s subtle maker’s sigil and share the same treated-paper core.

**Alaran Paper (Sheet Goods)  
**Specially treated paper coated with a secret oil:

*   **Properties:**
*   Fire resistance equivalent to +5 vs. mundane flame and minor magical heat.
*   Can be fully submerged in water for several hours before ruin.
*   Resists insects and normal rot better than common parchment.
*   **Use:** Ideal for scrolls, contracts carried on the road, and maps expected to see hard use.
*   **Price:** About **8 sp per sheet**.

**Alaran Spellbook / Ledger**

Most buyers are merchants, but the book is built to wizardly standards:

*   **100 pages** of Alaran paper, bound to thin wooden boards and covered in leather.
*   Treated to resist insects, water immersion up to roughly eight hours, and ordinary fires (fire resistance +5).
*   Sold blank for spells, ledgers, or journals.
*   **Price:** ~25 gp per book (or adjusted by you to match the later pricing sidebar). B28 Scribes and Paper

#### Goods and Costs

The store offers its unique products and services at premium prices, justified by the superior quality and durability of the goods.

*   **Spellbooks and Ledgers:** Priced higher due to their enhanced durability and protective features.
*   **Scribing Rates:** Competitive within the industry, reflecting the high level of expertise and discretion offered.

### Scribing & Specialist Services

Alara and her staff provide **expert, discreet scribal work**:

*   **Business Transactions:** She or a trusted associate attends meetings as a neutral observer, recording terms, signatures, and witness marks, then **affixes her seal**—a mark widely respected in Verbobonc’s commercial circles.
*   **Book Copying:** Faithful reproduction of texts, from modern treatises to fragile old manuscripts. Complex or ancient scripts command higher rates. B28 Scribes and Paper
*   **Historical & Legal Recording:** Family trees, guild charters, deeds, and chronicles are painstakingly written on her treated paper for long-term preservation.

The expanded rate card in the business appendix suggests: ~10 sp/page for transactional records, 2–5 gp/page for book copying based on difficulty, and 8 sp/page for historical documents—premium prices justified by the quality and longevity of the work. B28 Scribes and Paper

Customization—embossed covers, unusual bindings, rare inks—is available for nobles, wizards, or anyone with coin and taste.

#### Relationships and Politics

Alara’s business acumen and personal history have positioned her as a respected figure in both the commercial and social circles of Verbobonc.

*   **Local Business Ties:** Works closely with various merchants and craftsmen, ensuring a steady demand for her products.
*   **Community Engagement:** Known for her impartiality and discretion, Alara has earned trust across diverse sectors, from local artisans to influential politicians.

##### Personal Motivation and Vision

Driven by a passion for her craft and the legacy of her family, Alara continues to innovate while maintaining the high standards set by her predecessors.

*   **Innovation in Papermaking:** Continues to research and develop new treatments to enhance the quality of her paper.
*   **Community Contribution:** Aims to support the local economy through job creation and by participating in community events.

### Secrets & Adventure Hooks

The original notes already seed several excellent hooks; here they are polished for table use. B28 Scribes and Paper

1.  **The Alaran Formula  
    **A local wizard, merchant prince, or even foreign agent covets Alara’s secret treatment process. They approach the PCs—overtly or through intermediaries—to **steal her notes, kidnap a worker, or spy on the out-of-town papermill**. Accepting the job risks turning Alara and a good portion of the merchant class against them; refusing may earn her gratitude and a future discount—or a rival’s enmity.
2.  **The Missing Scribe  
    **Alara leaves one morning to witness a major contract-signing and simply **never returns**. Her staff, panicked and at a loss, beg the PCs to investigate. Did someone silence her to erase a damning record? Did she vanish en route… or follow a clue related to her own past? The trail begins with her last docket of appointments.
3.  **Map on the Doorstep  
    **One dawn, Alara finds a **mysterious map** and a strange note mentioning a distant relative left on her doorstep. Unable to abandon the shop, she offers the map and a modest purse to the party, asking them to uncover who sent it and why. The map may lead to forgotten family holdings, a Temple cache, or a ruin whose secrets someone hopes only _she_ could decode.
4.  **Proof in the Ledger  
    **A noble house or guildmaster faces accusations of treason, smuggling, or Temple collaboration. The only proof lies in ledgers written on **Alaran paper**, now hidden or falsified. Because she remembers every major commission, Alara can point the PCs toward the right book… if they convince her that revealing a client’s secrets serves a higher good.

#### Alaran Paper

Alara Reed makes this special paper using a special, secret process. Thee paper is coated with a light oil that actually aids ink permanence rather than preventing it. The process of making the paper, and coating it with her special oil, makes it more resistant to water damage and somewhat resistant to fire. Alaran paper has fire resistance +5 and can be immersed completely in water for several hours before being ruined. While it is usually used for business documents, this paper is excellent for scrolls that see a great deal of travel and abuse.(8sp per sheet.)

#### Alaran Spellbook

Merchants are the main purchasers of these books, using them as ledgers for recording business deals and accounting rather than spells. Each book has 100 pages of Alaran paper bound to thin wood boards covered with leather and is resistant to insects, fire, and water. Each book has fire resistance +5, and can be immersed in water for approximately 8 hours before being permanently damaged. The wood and oil used to make the books helps repel insects making them far less susceptible to insect damage. While the books are more expensive than standard spellbooks, many traveling wizards prefer having the added protection.(25 gp per blank book)

In addition to the above products, Alara provides herself, or one of several equally trustworthy associates as a scribe on demand. She can act as a neutral observer of business dealings, make a record of the transaction, and then affix her seal to it.

##### Alara Reed – Master of Scribes

**“Crafted for the ages, not just for today.”** – Alara’s quiet motto. B28 Scribes and Paper

*   **Appearance:** A lean human woman with hair pinned up in tight knots, ink permanently staining a few fingertips. Often wears simple dark dresses, spectacles perched low on her nose, and a measuring, slightly weary gaze. B28 Scribes and Paper
*   **Demeanor:** Friendly but reserved; precise in speech, slow to offer trust, and fiercely protective of her reputation for impartiality and discretion.
*   **Background:** Took over the business from her late husband and transformed it by perfecting the Alaran treatment process and relocating the smelly papermaking out of town. The work, and his memory, are what keep her moving. B28 Scribes and Paper
*   **Motivations:**
*   Maintain the highest standard of craft in Verbobonc.
*   Keep the shop neutral ground—she will copy contracts for rivals, so long as both sides behave.
*   Continue refining her paper formula… and keep it safely out of greedy hands.

##### **Alara’s Welcome To Visitors**

The bell over the door gives a soft, silvery chime as you step inside. The street-noise falls away, replaced by the quiet scratch of a quill and the faint clink of glass on wood.

Warm lamplight spills across a tidy room of shelves and papers: ledgers and journals stacked in neat rows, pigeonholes stuffed with folios, vases of fresh flowers brightening every corner. The air smells of ink, paper, and a hint of some subtle oil—sharp at the back of your throat, but not unpleasant.

At the central desk a dark-haired woman pauses mid-stroke, quill poised above the page. Spectacles rest low on her nose; ink stains the tips of her fingers. She looks you over quickly—boots, gear, the way you carry yourselves—then sets the quill in its stand with deliberate care before rising to greet you.

**Alara’s Dialogue**

“Mind the threshold, if you please—some of these pages are older than the city’s current walls.”

_She offers a small, professional smile and inclines her head._

“Welcome to **Scribes and Paper**. I am Alara Reed, master scribe and maker of the sheets you see around you. We bind ledgers, journals, spellbooks, charters… and, on occasion, stories worth keeping.”

_Her gaze moves from face to face, sharp but not unkind._

“You look more like road-dust and steel than merchant ink, which usually means one of three things: you’ve found something written that you cannot read, you wish to put something in writing that others **must** read… or your wizard has just realized cheap parchment and fireballs are poor friends.”

_The corner of her mouth twitches, almost a smile._

“Tell me, then—what do you need of me? Blank pages that will survive the weather, a proper spellbook, copies made, or a careful hand to witness a bargain you expect to be argued over long after you’ve left the table?”
