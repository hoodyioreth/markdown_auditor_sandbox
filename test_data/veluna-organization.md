Veluna
======

Veluna is not merely the peaceful kingdom west of Verbobonc. It is a living Raoan power — a theocratic realm of dioceses, bishops, noble houses, river trade, and cultivated land — whose patience, diplomacy, and spiritual authority now bear directly upon the fate of the Viscounty. In 576 CY, Veluna's greatest immediate concern is no longer abstract foreign policy, but the disappearance of the **Gift of Beauty**, the loss of a diplomatic mission meant for Celene, and the unanswered question of what became of the Velunan Rao clergy who traveled east under sacred and political trust.  

> **Veluna** is the great Raoan kingdom west of the Viscounty of Verbobonc — a realm of bishops, noble dioceses, fertile lowlands, and old authority. It is governed through a deliberate partnership between sacred office and secular nobility, with the **Canon of Veluna**, the **College of Bishops**, and the **Celestial Order of the Moons** together shaping the life of the kingdom. For Dungeon Masters running the epic sandbox version of _Temple of Elemental Evil_, Veluna is not background geography. It is a major western power whose interests now intersect directly with Verbobonc's roads, politics, noble honor, and religious legitimacy.

 

In 576 CY, Veluna's interest in the Viscounty has sharpened. The **Gift of Beauty** — a diplomatic offering intended for Queen Yolande of Celene — has vanished. Velunan clergy tied to that mission are missing or broken. A surviving priest, **Artus Kellan**, yet lives, though his mind is shattered. What begins as diplomacy now threatens to become inquiry, and what begins as inquiry may yet become pressure. A DM should use Veluna as a realm of calm but growing consequence: bishops asking hard questions, noble houses watching for weakness, and a western kingdom deciding whether Verbobonc still merits trust.

 

#### What Veluna Is

Veluna is the western kingdom of peace, reason, and serenity — or at least that is how it presents itself, and not without cause. It is a realm deeply marked by the authority of Rao, whose doctrines do not merely adorn public worship but shape law, habit, diplomacy, and the very imagination of the state. Its fields are broad and cultivated, its towns and dioceses ordered, its political forms old and deliberate. This is not a frontier realm and not a land of restless adventuring lords. Veluna is a _settled power_, and that settled character is precisely what gives its questions weight when it turns its attention eastward toward Verbobonc.

 

Geographically, Veluna stands west of the [Verbobonc, Viscounty](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md) and north of the [Lortmil Mountains](lortmil-mountains-location.md), with fertile lowlands, reduced but still notable woodlands, and access to mineral wealth in the hills and mountain fringes. It is tied by road and river to the wider western Flanaess, and its security depends not merely on armies, but on stable corridors of diplomacy and trade. That is why disorder in Verbobonc matters to Veluna more than a distant chronicler might suppose. If the roads of the Viscounty fail, if the southern border grows lawless, if diplomacy with Celene collapses, then Veluna itself is made less secure.

### The Archclericy of Veluna

[The Archclericy of Veluna](the-archclericy-of-veluna-article.md) is a layered system of religious governance that bindeth Veluna into a unified whole. Authority floweth downward from Canon [Hazen](hazen-person.md) through a hierarchy of clergy and secular rulers, each bound by doctrine and obligation. The kingdom is ruled by the **Canon of Veluna**, the sacred and temporal head of the realm, whose office stands above the dioceses and whose authority is upheld through a complex structure of bishops, nobles, clerical councils, and political custom.

*   **Canon of Veluna** — Supreme authority, spiritual and temporal
*   **The Celestial Circle** — Advisory and doctrinal body shaping policy and interpretation
*   **Bishops of the Dioceses** — Regional religious authorities overseeing faith and governance
*   **Secular Lords (Counts, Barons)** — Administrative rulers operating within Church-sanctioned authority

Each diocese functions as both a religious jurisdiction and a political territory, ensuring that no division exists between the will of the Church and the operation of the realm.

##### The Balance of Faith and Rule

Veluna’s strength lieth in its balance between ecclesiastical authority and secular administration. Bishops guide the moral and doctrinal direction of their dioceses, while counts and barons enforce law, collect taxes, and maintain order. Neither standeth wholly above the other. This dual structure preventeth tyranny—yet it also createth constant tension, for where coin, land, and doctrine meet, agreement is seldom without contest.

##### Other Faiths Within Veluna

Though [Rao](rao-person.md) is supreme, other faiths are recognized and integrated into the structure of the realm:

*   **[St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md)** — Law, discipline, and righteous enforcement
*   **[Wee Jas](wee-jas-person.md)** — Law, contracts, and arcane order
*   **[Ehlonna](ehlonna-person.md)** — Nature, fertility, and rural life

These faiths are not rivals to Rao, but complements—each providing a necessary function within the broader harmony of Veluna.

 

Representation of these faiths is often seen within the Celestial Circle, ensuring that doctrine remaineth balanced across differing needs of the realm.

##### Doctrine as Governance

*   In Veluna, doctrine is not abstract. It is actionable.
    
*   Through sermons, rulings, and ecclesiastical guidance, the Church shapes law, trade, diplomacy, and even war. Decisions are rarely issued as commands—instead, they emerge as consensus, framed as the natural extension of Rao’s will.
    
*   Thus, Veluna is governed not by force of arms, but by the quiet certainty of belief shared across its people and leaders alike.
    

##### The Role of the Celestial Circle

*   The Celestial Circle serves as the chamber where doctrine becometh policy. It interpreteth faith in the context of governance, advising Canon Hazen and shaping the direction of the Archclericy.
    
*   Here, matters of trade, diplomacy, and internal conflict are debated—not as secular concerns alone, but as questions of moral and divine consequence.
    
*   It is within this chamber that Veluna’s future is most often decided—quietly, deliberately, and with lasting effect.
    

##### The Living Faith

*   Veluna’s religion is not distant from its people. It is present in every village shrine, every diocesan court, every road toll, and every council chamber.
    
*   To live in Veluna is to live within the structure of the Archclericy—to be guided, judged, and protected by a faith that seeketh not dominion, but harmony.
    
*   And yet, beneath that harmony, there is always tension—for where belief governs power, the question is never whether conflict existeth… but how it shall be resolved.
    

##### The Canon of Veluna

The Canon is the supreme ruler of the kingdom — equivalent in stature to a king, yet bound not to simple inheritance but to the sacred-political machinery of Velunan government. He is selected through the governing structures of the realm, advised by high councils, and paired in power by the existence of noble institutions that may check canonical overreach. This gives Veluna a tone distinct from neighboring monarchies: rule in Veluna is personal, but it is never solitary.

##### The College of Bishops

The College of Bishops is the chief spiritual and advisory council of the realm. It is composed of the bishops of the dioceses, the Archbishop of Veluna City, representatives of major religious institutions, and additional appointed members. The College exists to advise the Canon, shape religious policy, and ensure that each diocese remains bound to the greater life of the kingdom rather than drifting into purely local rule.

##### The Celestial Order of the Moons

The secular counterweight to ecclesiastical authority is the **Celestial Order of the Moons**, a body composed of representatives from the seven great noble houses of Veluna. These houses do not rule independently of the church, but neither are they decorative. They advise, influence, and restrain. They are the guardians of local power, landed wealth, and the practical governance of the dioceses. Significantly for this campaign, the **Viscounty of Verbobonc** maintains an advisory presence in this order, a quiet sign that the relationship between the two realms is not casual but structurally recognized.

##### The Diocesan Order

Veluna is divided into dioceses, and each diocese is governed through a deliberate pairing: a **Bishop of Rao** and a **ruling noble house**. This is the essential Velunan pattern and the one that should guide all future sub-articles. In some dioceses, bishop and noble cooperate closely. In others, they tolerate one another with tight courtesy. In troubled regions, they may openly work at cross-purposes while preserving a public face of order. This bishop-noble pairing is the true engine of Velunan politics, and it is where the kingdom becomes most useful at the table.

 

*   **The bishop** guards doctrine, sacred legitimacy, and the will of the Canon
*   **The noble** governs land, economy, military obligations, and local secular administration
*   **Minor noble houses** operate beneath the ruling house, complicating each diocese with local rivalries
*   **The Plar** stands as foremost among the secular great lords, a title of influence rather than simple blood inheritance

#### Mitrik and Veluna City

_The Two Hearts of the Realm_

A DM should not think of Veluna as having only one political center. It has **two different hearts**, each with a distinct function.

 

**Mitrik** is the sacred heart of Veluna — the place of deep Raoan authority, canonical memory, and spiritual legitimacy. Mitrik matters because this is where Veluna speaks in its oldest and holiest voice. When the Canon, senior bishops, or high Raoan policy are invoked, the weight behind those decisions is Mitrik's.

 

**Veluna City**, by contrast, is the realm's secular and mercantile center — the place where noble influence, administrative life, and worldly politics move more visibly. If Mitrik is where the kingdom remembers what it is, Veluna City is where the kingdom negotiates how it must live in the world.

 

This distinction is important in play. A letter from Mitrik is not the same as a delegation from Veluna City. The first bears sacred gravity. The second often bears political intention. When both move in concert, the matter is grave indeed.

#### Trade and Commerce

Veluna is not a slow inland kingdom whose markets creep from town to town by wagon alone. It is a river realm. The **Velverdyva** is the great commercial artery of the kingdom's eastern and central life, and the **Fals** stands close behind it as the western and southwestern companion waterway. Together, these rivers — and the lesser tributaries, streams, mills, landing places, and feeder roads that cling to them — make Veluna far more commercially nimble than many realms of similar size. The land may be governed by bishops and noble houses, but it moves by river.

 

This matters greatly for the Dungeon Master. In practical campaign terms, much of settled Veluna lies close enough to the Velverdyva or the Fals that goods, letters, rumors, pilgrims, and officials travel with unusual speed. A bishop's summons can move faster. A noble warning can outrun a wagon column. Grain, cloth, timber, fish, stone, wine, crafted wares, and metalwork can all be pushed along the waterways more cheaply and in greater bulk than overland roads alone would allow. Veluna's internal life is therefore more tightly stitched together than a simple map of dioceses might first suggest. :contentReference\[oaicite:3\]{index=3} :contentReference\[oaicite:4\]{index=4}

##### The Velverdyva

The Velverdyva is one of the great waterways of the Flanaess — a major trade artery and one of the most trafficked rivers in the wider region, vital for both commerce and travel. Cargo barges move bulk goods up and down its length and form the backbone of river trade, while fishing craft, Rhenee riverfolk, and other merchant traffic make the river a living road as much as a body of water. For Veluna, this means the eastern and northeastern reaches of the kingdom are never wholly isolated so long as the river remains open. News from Highfolk, northern commerce descending from the upper waters, eastern trade moving through Dyvers, and traffic touching Verbobonc all help make the Velverdyva the commercial spine of Veluna's outer-facing life. :contentReference\[oaicite:5\]{index=5} :contentReference\[oaicite:6\]{index=6}

##### The Fals and the Western Roads

If the Velverdyva is the eastern lifeline, the Fals is the western and southwestern balancing weight. Veluna's history already shows how closely trade was tied to these corridors: caravans once passed through Verbobonc and onward to Devarnish, Veluna City, and points west, while later wars and treaties made the **Fals Gap** and the **Great West Road** matters of national consequence. This is the clearest sign that trade in Veluna is not merely local market exchange, but something strategic. When river traffic or western road access is disturbed, the kingdom feels it politically as well as economically. :contentReference\[oaicite:7\]{index=7}

##### External Trade and the Four Directions

Veluna's external trade is both constrained and unusually open. It has only a limited number of overt bordering powers, yet the rivers let goods arrive from far beyond those immediate frontiers. To the **east**, commerce comes heavily through Dyvers and the Velverdyva. To the **south and west**, goods move through Bissel, Devarnish, the Fals corridor, and the western road network. To the **north**, the Velverdyva draws traffic down from the upper reaches near Highfolk and beyond, while Furyondy remains a natural commercial and political partner. A DM should therefore treat Veluna not as sealed behind diocesan lines, but as a realm through which influence, rumor, and trade circulate readily along a few powerful channels. :contentReference\[oaicite:8\]{index=8} :contentReference\[oaicite:9\]{index=9}

##### What the Rivers Carry

The rivers do more than move luxuries. They move the substance of rule. Veluna's farms, market towns, mills, bishoprics, and noble households all benefit from rapid movement of grain, fish, timber, stone, livestock, cloth, and bulk cargo. They also carry pilgrims, clerks, messengers, tax officials, priests, and diplomatic envoys. In a theocratic kingdom, this means commerce and administration are not easily separated. A busy river landing is also a place where church dues are watched, noble revenues are measured, and news from another diocese may arrive before a local lord has finished his supper. The speed of trade is part of the speed of governance. This fits the wider Velunan model in which local nobles manage the practical economies of their dioceses while bishops intervene when matters rise to church or national importance. :contentReference\[oaicite:10\]{index=10}

#### History That Still Shapes Policy

Veluna's history is not dead matter. It still shapes the instincts of its clergy and nobles in 576 CY.

 

The realm traces its sacred origins to the early union of Oeridian settlers and Flan worship in the valley protected by Rao, and to the discovery of the **Crook of Rao** at Mitrik. Later, Veluna entered the orbit of Ferrond, helped temper the western kingdoms with Raoan counsel, and eventually asserted its own sovereignty. It endured trade tension, Keoish invasion, compromise, and hard-won independence. These memories taught Veluna three lessons that still endure:

 

*   **Roads and trade corridors matter politically** — whoever controls them shapes the kingdom's freedom
*   **Spiritual legitimacy must be preserved** — Veluna does not survive by raw arms alone
*   **Allies cannot be taken for granted** — even friendly neighbors may fail when tested

 

Most relevant to this campaign is the memory of **569 CY**, when Veluna joined the forces that stood against the Horde of Elemental Evil during the crisis that threatened Verbobonc. That shared cause is one reason the present diplomatic failure cuts so deeply. The Gift of Beauty was not merely a pretty gesture. It was part of a wider architecture of trust meant to strengthen precisely the alliances that once proved necessary.

#### Veluna and the Viscounty of Verbobonc

Verbobonc and Veluna are not strangers linked by occasional emissaries. Their relationship is durable, layered, and politically significant. [The Order of Rao](the-order-of-rao-organization.md) provides the most obvious bridge, but noble, military, and mercantile ties are just as important.

##### The Viscount's Interest

For **Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md)**, Veluna is an ally whose respect must be preserved. A public rupture with so pious and established a kingdom would make the Viscounty seem weak, unstable, and unworthy of trust. Wilfrick therefore needs two things from the Velunan relationship: confidence and quiet. He needs Veluna to believe that Verbobonc remains a worthy partner, and he needs the current crisis handled before it becomes a full diplomatic scandal.

##### The Church Bridge

**Patriarch [Martiolus Legeum](martiolus-legeum-person.md)** is the chief ecclesiastical hinge between the two realms. Through him, [The Order of Rao](the-order-of-rao-organization.md) in Verbobonc can still speak in a language Veluna trusts. If Velunan bishops or investigators arrive, Martiolus is the most natural local figure through whom they may seek cooperation without forcing the matter immediately into public confrontation.

##### The Southern Lords

**Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md)** stands at the frontier where these larger relationships become painfully immediate. The Gift's failed transfer passed through the southern border region tied to his responsibility. Because of that, Deleveu is not merely a local noble in this matter. He is one of the Viscounty's key human faces before both Celene and Veluna. His honor and standing are entangled with the failure.

##### The Noble Political Problem

Not every lord in the Viscounty will greet Velunan involvement warmly. Some will desire help. Some will fear embarrassment. Some will see the matter chiefly as a means to weaken the Viscount or shift blame. In practical play, Veluna pressures [The Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) in different ways:

 

*   **Moderates and Rao-aligned figures** see Velunan involvement as necessary and legitimate
*   **Proud or suspicious nobles** may resent foreign clerics asking questions on Viscounty soil
*   **Merchant-minded factions** care chiefly whether the affair harms trade and diplomacy
*   **Manipulators** may use Velunan concern to embarrass rivals or the Viscount himself

 

**The Asbury Connection**

Lady [Elinor Asbury](elinor-asbury-person.md)'s household has become unexpectedly important to international politics because **Artus Kellan**, the broken surviving priest of Rao from the diplomatic thread, is in her care. By sheltering one damaged witness, Asbury Manor becomes a quiet crossroads between Velunan grief, Verbobonc noble responsibility, and the wider Temple conspiracy.

#### The Gift of Beauty Crisis

**[The Gift of Beauty — Quest Master Reference](gob-01-gift-of-beauty-E28094-what-it-is-and-who-wants-it-plot.md)** was commissioned in Verbobonc as a diplomatic offering for Queen [Yolande, Her Fey Majesty](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) of Celene — a symbol of friendship, peace, and the shared labor of humans, elves, and gnomes. It was not powerful because of magic, but because of meaning. It represented the possibility of renewed trust among neighboring powers at a moment when the world had good cause to seek such trust.

 

Instead, the Gift vanished. The mission tied to its delivery was struck. The roads proved vulnerable. The enemies of order reached farther than polite society wished to admit. For Verbobonc, this is humiliation. For Celene, it is insult and disappointment. For Veluna, it is both diplomatic failure _and_ ecclesiastical outrage.

 

Veluna's concern is deeper than the loss of a carved object. The missing or captured Rao clergy tied to this mission matter just as much. To lose the Gift is one thing. To lose priests under conditions of sacred trust is another. That is why the crisis cannot remain a mere local embarrassment for long.

 

**Why Veluna Takes This So Seriously**

*   The Gift formed part of a broader peace-building effort involving Veluna's interests
*   Rao clergy were present within that same diplomatic thread
*   The loss damages Veluna's standing with Celene as well as Verbobonc's
*   If the roads are unsafe for such a mission, then every future embassy is imperiled
*   The Temple of Elemental Evil's shadow is now visible in matters once thought safely political

* * *

#### Artus Kellan and the Missing Delegation

**[Artus Kellan](artus-kellan-person-1.md)** is the living wound at the center of the whole affair. He is not merely a priest in distress. He is evidence that the crisis is real, human, and unfinished. His broken state proves that the diplomatic thread did not simply fray into confusion. It was struck, and by enemies cruel enough to torture a servant of Rao and leave his mind shattered.

 

So long as Artus remains unrestored, Veluna lacks a whole witness and a whole truth. Restore him, and the affair may become clearer. Fail to restore him, and rumor, blame, and political exploitation multiply around the silence. Any DM using Veluna should understand that Artus is more than a quest hook. He is a hinge. Through him pass theology, diplomacy, noble obligation, and proof.

#### The Velunan Investigation

Veluna's most likely response in 576 CY is not open military intervention. It is **investigation with sacred authority**. The kingdom is far too civilized — and far too politically aware — to hurl troops into the Viscounty without cause. But it has ample cause to send clerics, legates, learned envoys, and perhaps a small knightly escort to inquire into the fate of its clergy and the failed embassy route.

 

**Such a delegation would likely seek four things:**

*   **Truth** — what happened to the Gift, the priests, and the route
*   **Recovery** — of survivors, relics, testimony, and sacred dignity
*   **Assurance** — that Verbobonc remains capable of keeping faith with allies
*   **Containment** — that the scandal not be allowed to destroy relations with Celene

 

For the DM, this makes the Velunan investigation a perfect pressure tool. It can arrive quietly and become urgent. It can begin with questions and end with political consequences. It can cooperate with the Viscount, move through Martiolus, clash with proud lords, or intersect directly with adventurers already entangled in the Gift of Beauty thread.

 

**At the Table, a Velunan investigation can be used as:**

*   A clerical quest thread grounded in Raoan legitimacy rather than simple adventurer rumor
*   A diplomatic timer that forces Verbobonc to act before foreign patience runs thin
*   A way to introduce new Velunan NPCs before building the separate diocese articles
*   A moral counterweight to the harsher politics of the Council of Lords
*   A quiet reminder that the Temple's reach now touches not only villages and roads, but international trust

* * *

#### Why Veluna Matters in the Sandbox

Veluna should not be used as a distant lore entry that players read once and forget. It should function in the campaign as a **source of pressure, legitimacy, and westward consequence**. When the players recover evidence, rescue clergy, restore Artus Kellan, or uncover the true fate of the Gift, Veluna is one of the realms that should care most and react most clearly.

 

Likewise, if the players fail, delay, or choose cynicism, Veluna is one of the first powers that can make that failure feel weighty without resorting to army movement. A bishop's letter. A cancelled embassy. A pained inquiry from Mitrik. A noble complaint from Veluna City. These are small-seeming things that tell the table the world is alive.

#### How to Build the Future Veluna Articles

This main article should serve as the parent foundation for all later Veluna entries. Each future diocese article should be built around the same practical structure used elsewhere in the project:

 

*   **What the diocese is** — geography, roads, settlements, resources
*   **Who rules it** — ruling noble house and Bishop of Rao
*   **How they get along** — harmony, rivalry, cold partnership, or open tension
*   **Why it matters now** — Temple threat, trade pressure, diplomacy, frontier concern, or church politics
*   **How it connects to Verbobonc** — roads, embassies, nobles, clergy, or military obligations

 

That is the proper Velunan pattern: each article should feel like a living diocese under joint rule, not merely a gazetteer entry.

#### DM Notes: Using Veluna at the Table

**Use Veluna when you want quiet power, not loud power.**

 

*   **For diplomacy:** letters, sealed requests, clerical envoys, inquiries from Mitrik
*   **For politics:** pressure on the Viscount and his lords without open war
*   **For religion:** moral authority, Raoan legitimacy, and sacred obligation
*   **For investigation:** missing clergy, broken witnesses, embassy failures, route reconstruction
*   **For future expansion:** dioceses, bishops, noble houses, Veluna City, Mitrik, Devarnish, and border regions

 

**DM shorthand:** "_Veluna does not threaten first. It asks. And when a kingdom of Rao begins asking the same question more than once, wise rulers understand that the hour of easy answers is passing._"
