The Price of a Question - D5 Hindsight
======================================

### Scene Purpose (DM)

The party enters Hindsight while a **masked noble** attempts **High-Stakes Scrying**—and Shady demands the price: _“what thou lovest most.”  
_This is a social crucible: the PCs must choose **honesty, mercy, exploitation, or leverage**, while Shady tests their hearts and a desperate patron edges toward ruin.

* * *

##### Boxed Read-Aloud (Approach)

The ipt roots knot like old fingers round a narrow door, and the steps sink downward into earth-scented dark. Bone chimes tick softly, though no wind followeth thee. Candlelight pools in hollows of root and stone, and smoke of incense hangs like pale cloth in the air.\\ Beyond a gauze curtain, a low murmur—two voices, one steady as a spindle, one trembling as a thread about to snap.

* * *

### Setup (DM)

**Location:** Hindsight’s main chamber and the deeper altar nook.  
**NPCs:**

*   **Shady** (priestess of Istus)
*   **Masked Noble** (Lord/Lady “Velaryn” — you choose gender/house)
*   **Noble’s Retainer** (silent bodyguard or anxious servant)
*   Optional: **Two “listening beasts”** (rat/bat) that react to lies.

**Mood:** Intimate, claustrophobic, candlelit. Every word feels costly.

* * *

#### The Masked Noble’s Problem (pick one)

1.  **“My heir is in another’s hand.”** (kidnapping/blackmail)
2.  **“My house will fall at council.”** (political disaster)
3.  **“I have signed a thing I should not.”** (Temple-influence contract)
4.  **“A gift was taken upon the road.”** (tie-in to Gift of Beauty chest)

The noble wants a **sure** answer: _Who? Where? When?_—and believes fate may be bought.

* * *

#### The High-Stakes Price (DM)

Shady’s stated price is always the same in form, never in detail:  
**“What thou lovest most.”  
**She will not clarify until the noble names it aloud.

**Important:** This need not be death. The “sacrifice” can be:

*   Renouncing a title/claim
*   Betraying an ally
*   Breaking a sworn vow
*   Giving up a cherished future (marriage, inheritance, dream)
*   Surrendering an irreplaceable token (ring, locket, letter) that embodies love

* * *

#### Mechanics (Optional)

**Perception 13+**: Notice the noble’s hands shaking; a bloodless bite-mark on their glove; the retainer’s hidden dagger.  
**Insight 14+**: The noble is not merely frightened—they are _guilty_.  
**Religion or Arcana 13+**: Shady’s rites are about **honesty and acceptance**, not “getting what you want.”  
**If a PC lies in this chamber:** one candle gutters; a rat pauses and stares; the air thickens. (No hard penalty—just pressure.)

* * *

### Dialogue (Keep Separate for DM Use)

##### Shady (calm, precise)

**Shady:** “Enter, if thou must. Speak not, if thou canst. The weave is thin tonight.”

**Shady:** “One question. One price. If thou wouldst haggle, go to the market.”

**Shady:** “Name it.”

**Shady:** “Not coin. Not jewels. Not the thing thou canst live without.”

**Shady:** “What thou lovest most.”

**Shady:** “Istus demandeth honesty. If thou wouldst be spared, begin there.”

* * *

#### Masked Noble (desperate, proud, cracking)

**Noble:** “I was told— I was told you _see_. That you can _tell me_ where the thread goes.”

**Noble:** “I will pay. I will double it. I will—”

**Noble:** “Do not make a sport of me.”

**Noble:** “You do not understand what is at stake.”

**Noble:** “If I speak it aloud… it becomes real.”

* * *

#### Retainer (guarded, threatening without overt threat)

**Retainer:** “My lord hath little patience for riddles.”

**Retainer:** “We came quietly. We will leave quietly. Let it stay thus.”

**Retainer:** “Mind thy tongue, strangers.”

* * *

#### Shady turns to the party (the test)

**Shady:** “Thou art witnesses now. Tell me, road-folk—why art thou here?”

**Shady:** “Do not answer with what thou _seekest_. Answer with what thou _fearest_.”

_(If they answer honestly, she softens—barely.)  
_**Shady:** “Good. Truth is a key. Few carry it.”

_(If they dodge or lie.)  
_**Shady:** “Then thou wilt fit well in Verbobonc. Sit. Watch. Learn what such fitting costs.”

* * *

#### Noble tries to substitute a false “love”

**Noble:** “Very well. Take… my signet. It is my house. My legacy.”

**Shady:** “A legacy is not love. It is appetite wearing perfume.”

**Shady:** “Speak it again. Speak it true.”

* * *

#### If the PCs intervene (three common approaches)

##### 1) PCs urge restraint / mercy

**PCs:** (your players)

**Shady:** “Mercy? Nay. Mercy is easy when it is not thine to pay.”

**Shady:** “Yet I will hear thee. What wouldst thou offer in place of another’s ruin?”

##### 2) PCs try to profit (extort the noble)

**PCs:** (your players)

**Shady:** “Ah. There it is. The little knife behind the smile.”

**Shady:** “Do as thou wilt. But know—Istus marks bargains made with a drowning soul.”

##### 3) PCs demand the truth from the noble

**PCs:** (your players)

**Noble:** “You have no right—”

**Shady:** “They have every right. In my house, truth is the toll.”

**Shady:** “If thou canst not pay it, do not demand prophecy.”

* * *

### The Moment of Naming (choose one price)

##### Option A — “My child”

**Noble:** “My… my daughter. Take her from my heart and I am hollow.”

**Shady:** “Then say the vow: _I loosen my grip._”

##### Option B — “My spouse”

**Noble:** “My husband. My wife. The only peace I have ever known.”

**Shady:** “Then choose. To protect them, or to possess them.”

##### Option C — “My honor”

**Noble:** “My honor. My good name. It is the only shield I have.”

**Shady:** “A shield can be set down. Speak the truth thou hast hidden.”

##### Option D — “My future”

**Noble:** “The future I promised myself… the life I meant to live.”

**Shady:** “Then relinquish it. Not in word— in deed.”

* * *

#### Shady’s Final Question (to the PCs)

**Shady:** “If thou couldst save them, wouldst thou… and what wouldst thou take in payment?”

**Shady:** “Answer carefully. The loom remembers.”

* * *

#### The Rite (DM Description)

Shady lifts a small golden spindle charm. The incense thickens. Candle flames lean inward, as if listening.  
She draws a single thread from a spool of grey silk—so fine it is near invisible—then lays it across a shallow bowl of dark water. The thread does not sink. It floats, trembling, as if tugged by unseen fingers.

When the noble speaks the true price, the thread tightens—straight as a road drawn on a map.

### Outcomes (Pick One)

##### Outcome 1 — The Noble Pays (and the vision comes)

**Vision (give 1–2 clear facts + 1 omen):**

*   A location (a landmark, inn, bridge, ruined milepost)
*   A symbol (spindle mark, elemental sign, unusual powder, boot pattern)
*   An omen (moonlight turns red; a thread snaps; a candle burns blue)

**Cost:** The noble must immediately perform the sacrifice (renounce, confess, sever, surrender).  
Shady insists it occur **now**, not later.

##### Outcome 2 — The Noble Refuses (and collapses into bitter shame)

Shady offers no vision. She offers a mirror:

**Shady:** “Thou camest to steal certainty. Thou art leaving with thyself.”

Hook: The noble’s enemies learn they sought prophecy—blackmail ensues.

##### Outcome 3 — The Noble Cheats (Shady exposes them)

If the noble names something they _think_ they love most but do not:

A candle gutters. The beasts stare. The thread frays.

**Shady:** “False coin. No sale.”

Hook: Shady quietly invites the PCs to remove the noble from her threshold—or she will.

##### Outcome 4 — The PCs Take the Price Instead

If the party offers to pay something they love most in exchange for the noble’s vision:  
Shady accepts—but marks them as **Thread-Tangled** (a visible omen or recurring fate complication).

*   A **spindle token** the noble drops—engraved with their house crest on one side, Istus’s spindle on the other.
*   A **sealed letter** naming a meeting at the Red Don (or D4 shrine, or a road outside the walls).
*   A smear of **grey-blue powder** (trail-masking) on the retainer’s glove.

#### Closing Line (use to end the scene)

**Shady:** “Go, then. The road is the same. Only thy eyes are changed.”

##### Scene Beats

##### Beat 1 — Arrival in the Outer Chamber

The party hears the exchange before they see it. Shady does not stop the rite for them. She simply allows their presence to become part of the test.

##### Beat 2 — The Demand

Shady asks the noble to name the price **out loud**.  
The noble hesitates—then tries to bargain, evade, or substitute coin.

##### Beat 3 — Shady’s Test of the PCs

Shady turns to the party with a calm question that forces them to reveal their nature.  
She will offer them nothing until they answer honestly.

##### Beat 4 — The Breaking Point

The noble either:

*   Agrees and names what they love most, or
*   Refuses, or
*   Tries to cheat (offering something that is not truly loved).

##### Beat 5 — Outcome & Hook

Regardless of choice, the scene yields a hook: a clue, a consequence, a new enemy, or Shady’s cold regard.
