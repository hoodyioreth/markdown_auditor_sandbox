AO-12. Marin’s Command Chamber
==============================

The knight who built this room commanded the defense of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) and the protection of every pilgrim, merchant, and traveler on the Long Road through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). His shield hung on the wall. His battle standard flew above the door. From this oak table he planned patrols, coordinated with [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) chapter houses across the Viscounty, and kept the road safe for a generation.

Those virtues are long gone.

The room now belongs to [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md), one of [Romag](romag-person.md)'s most trusted field commanders. Maris is not a zealot — he is a professional. He fights because he is good at it, follows orders because it keeps coin in his pocket, and spends that coin as fast as he earns it. From this same oak table he now plans the raids that terrorize the very road his predecessor swore to protect. Maps of ambush sites cover the walls where banners of Heironeous once hung. The irony is lost on him. He does not think about irony. He thinks about patrol routes, choke points, and the next caravan schedule.

 

##### Entering the Command Chamber

You push open the door to a chamber that once belonged to a knightly commander. The authority of the room is unmistakable even now — heavy stone walls, a high ceiling, and a large oak table dominating the center like an altar to war.  
  
But the altar has been rededicated. Rough maps cover the table's surface, pinned flat with iron daggers and stone weights. The lines of roads and hills twist across the parchment — charcoal symbols marking positions, routes, and targets. Someone has been planning with precision and patience.  
  
A cot rests against the far wall beside a heavy iron-bound chest. Weapons lean in racks nearby — spears, shields, and crossbows ready for quick deployment. Crude triangular marks are scratched into the shield faces. The mark of the Black Earth.  
  
The air smells of oil, leather, and dust. Whoever commands this place does not pray here. He plans.

 

### The Sacred Layer

This was the Knight-Commander's private quarters — the most important military room in the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). The commander of the Azure Oath's garrison lived, planned, and held council here. The heavy oak table that still dominates the room was the original: too massive to move, built into the chamber like a ship's keel. Above the doorframe, barely visible beneath scratched cult graffiti, the carved words of the Commander's Oath remain legible to anyone who thinks to look: _"The road is my charge. The traveler is my ward. The sword is my word."_

*   Heavy oak command table (original — 4 ft × 8 ft, too large to remove without demolition)
*   Stone mounting brackets on the walls where shields and battle standards once hung (now empty or holding raider gear)
*   Commander's Oath inscription above the doorframe (Religion DC 12 to notice beneath the graffiti)
*   A narrow window facing south toward the Long Road — the commander could watch the road he was sworn to protect
*   Iron weapon racks along the east wall (original, now stocked with Earth Temple equipment)

 

### The Corrupted Layer

[Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) chose this room because it suited his needs, not to make a statement. He is practical. The table was already here and it was big enough for his maps. The weapon racks were already here and they hold his gear. The narrow window lets him watch the Long Road for approaching patrols. He has not bothered to destroy the sacred carvings — he simply does not notice them.

That indifference is its own kind of desecration.

*   Rough charcoal maps spread across the table: Long Road ambush positions, [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) caravan routes, gnome mining trails near [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), patrol schedules for Viscounty guards
*   Iron daggers and stone weights pinning the maps — one dagger is driven through the spot marking the priory itself
*   Crude triangular Black Earth marks scratched into the shield faces on the weapon racks
*   Maris's cot: functional, no comfort beyond a fur bedroll and a travel blanket. He sleeps like a soldier — quick to rise, boots within reach
*   A battered war chest near the cot (see Treasure below)
*   Faint tang of weapon oil and leather polish — Maris keeps his gear in order even if he keeps nothing else

 

### The Command Table

The table is the heart of the room and the heart of the Earth Temple's raiding operations in the southern Viscounty. [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md) is meticulous about his maps — not because he cares about craft, but because sloppy planning gets people killed, and dead raiders do not bring in tribute.

##### What the Maps Show

*   Caravan choke points along the Long Road through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md)
*   Patrol routes of local Viscounty guards (with shift-change timings)
*   Gnome mining trails near [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) — marked for avoidance or for targeting, depending on orders
*   Hidden staging locations for raider patrols between the priory and the Long Road
*   A separate sketch showing the route south to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) used for tribute shipments

##### Investigation Checks

**DC 12 Investigation:** Notes in the margins describe the **"earth tithe"** — a systematic extortion levied on caravans. Amounts are tallied. The Earth Temple treats this like a tax operation, not random banditry.

**DC 15 Investigation:** A separate sheet details a planned ambush against a merchant convoy leaving Hommlet within two days. Names, cargo manifest, expected guard strength, and a recommended strike point are all noted. This is actionable intelligence — PCs can intervene.

**DC 18 Investigation:** Cross-referencing the maps with notes in Maris's journal (see sidebar) reveals that some raid targets are selected by [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) for political reasons rather than profit — specifically, gnome-affiliated caravans targeted to inflame human-gnome tensions. The evidence connects to the false-flag gear stored in **AO-25. The Knight's Armory** next door.

 

### Encounter: Maris Blackshale

[Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) may be present depending on the alert level. He is a creature of routine — he studies maps in the morning, inspects the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) at midday, leads or reviews patrols in the afternoon, and returns here at dusk to update his intelligence. If encountered, he is likely studying maps, sharpening weapons, or writing patrol assignments.

 

#### Maris in Combat

Maris fights like what he is: a disciplined battlefield commander, not a fanatic. He does not waste moves on intimidation or gloating. He assesses the tactical situation, gives clear orders to any nearby reavers, and fights to win or to withdraw — never to die gloriously.

*   Uses the command table for partial cover (half cover, AC +2)
*   Orders nearby reavers from the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) to hold choke points in the [corridor](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) (AO-11)
*   Fights defensively while organizing a coordinated response — Shield Master, Sentinel-style positioning
*   If overwhelmed (reduced below half HP or outnumbered 3:1), retreats toward [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s quarters deeper in the priory
*   Will not surrender. Not out of loyalty — out of pragmatism. He knows what the Earth Temple does to agents who get captured.

 

#### Maris's Personality in Play

If PCs attempt to negotiate or interrogate, Maris is direct, blunt, and unflinching. He does not lie — he simply says nothing useful. He is honest in his own way: he says exactly what he means, and what he means is usually "no." He has no ideology to defend and no cause to argue. He is a professional doing a job. That makes him harder to crack than a true believer.

*   **Voice:** Flat, clipped, economical. No wasted words.
*   **Attitude:** Frank with superiors, commanding with subordinates, contemptuous of amateurs
*   **Leverage:** Maris cares about survival and coin. Promises of either might shift his cooperation — but he knows the Temple's reach is long
*   **Information he knows:** Garrison strength, raid schedules, tribute routes to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), the location of prisoners, and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation — but not its purpose

 

### Secrets

##### Hidden Floor Compartment

Beneath one loose floorstone near the south wall — a holdover from the Knight-Commander's era, originally used to store the priory's emergency dispatch scrolls.

**Investigation DC 14** to locate. Contains:

*   35 gp in mixed coin (Maris's personal stash — he spends everything else immediately)
*   A small silver holy symbol of Heironeous, taken from a slain knight of the Azure Oath

Maris keeps the holy symbol as a trophy. He does not understand its significance. A cleric or paladin of Heironeous who handles it feels a surge of grief and resolve — this belonged to someone who died defending this place.

##### The Commander's Oath

Above the doorframe, barely visible beneath scratched Black Earth graffiti:

_"The road is my charge. The traveler is my ward. The sword is my word."_

**Religion DC 12** to notice. A paladin or cleric of Heironeous recognizes this as the Knight-Commander's personal oath — a binding vow that once carried divine weight. Speaking it aloud in this room causes the scratched graffiti to flake and fall away, revealing the full inscription. No mechanical effect — but it is a powerful moment.

 

### DM Notes

AO-12 is the command node of the priory's raiding operations. If the party reaches this room and has time to study the maps and journal, they gain a comprehensive picture of the Earth Temple's southern operations — enough to disrupt the caravan raids, warn potential targets, and trace the tribute pipeline back to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

##### Intelligence Value

The maps and journal together constitute actionable military intelligence. If delivered to the Mounted Borderers, the Viscounty guard, or the Council of Lords in Verbobonc, this evidence is enough to justify a military response against the priory — and it implicates the Earth Temple by name. Combined with the false-flag evidence in **AO-25** and the intelligence records in **AO-16. The Scriptorium**, the PCs can build an airtight case.

##### Maris as a Recurring NPC

If Maris escapes, he becomes a dangerous loose thread. He is professional enough to regroup, and [Romag](romag-person.md) will redeploy him. He has no grudge — but he has a job to do, and the PCs are now an obstacle. He may appear later leading a retaliatory strike, commanding a different outpost, or even switching sides if the Temple's position collapses and a better offer appears.

##### The Sacred Contrast

Emphasize the Commander's Oath inscription. The knight who carved those words above the door swore to protect travelers. The man sleeping beneath them sends raiders to rob and kill those same travelers. Every element of the room — the table, the weapon racks, the narrow window watching the Long Road — serves an inverted purpose. The tools of protection have become the tools of predation. Let the players feel that weight.
