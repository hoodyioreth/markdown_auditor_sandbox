AO-C01. The Processional Landing
================================

> The Processional Landing — the receiving chamber at the base of the processional staircase where the Azure Oath's funeral cortege paused before entering the burial galleries below. A low-ceilinged room of dressed limestone, built to compress — to force the living to bow their heads as they carried the dead from the Hall of Final Vigil above into the crypts proper. A carved stone lintel above the gallery entrance arch still reads: "The faithful descend. The stone receives." The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has converted the landing into a duergar checkpoint and supply cache — two gray dwarf sentries on rotating shift, an alarm rope strung to the Work Camp, and a stockpile of excavation tools stacked over the original consecrated candle shelf. The transition from the priory above to the crypts below starts here. The dressed stone is different — colder, closer, older. The air tastes of wet limestone and sealed chambers. The darkness at the gallery arch is not the absence of light. It is the presence of depth.

_AO-C01. The Processional Landing — Receiving Chamber at the Base of the Processional Staircase — Now Duergar Checkpoint and Supply Cache_

* * *

### The Processional Landing

The processional staircase from **AO-22** — thirty feet of broad, shallow steps descending from the Hall of Final Vigil — ends here. When the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) buried its dead, the pallbearers carrying the funeral bier descended these steps in formation, the presiding cleric leading with a lantern, the brothers reciting the final lines of the processional prayer as the eternal flames lit the walls on either side. At the bottom, they paused. The staircase opened into this chamber — a low, rectangular room of dressed limestone where the procession gathered before entering the gallery.

The pause was deliberate. The ceiling drops here — eight feet, noticeably lower than the fifteen-foot vault of **AO-22** above. The masons built the compression on purpose. After the solemn height of the Hall of Final Vigil, the landing forces the living to feel the weight of the stone above them, to bow their heads, to understand in their bodies what their minds already knew: they are underground now. They are in the earth. The dead belong here. The living are visitors.

The room is roughly twenty feet wide and fifteen feet deep — enough space for six pallbearers to set the bier down, adjust their grip, and reform the procession line before passing through the gallery entrance arch on the south wall. The floor is the same flagstone as the staircase above, but older — the stone here was laid first, during the priory's founding in -12 CY, and the gallery was carved outward from this point. The walls are smooth-dressed limestone, the joints tight, the surfaces still carrying the faint tool-marks of the original Oeridian masons. No decorative carving. No murals. The landing was built for function, not ceremony — the ceremony happened above. This room was the hinge between ritual and rest.

##### The Carved Lintel

Above the gallery entrance arch on the south wall — a wide, low arch, six feet across and seven feet high — a stone lintel bears a carved inscription in Old Oeridian:

_"The faithful descend. The stone receives."_

The inscription is the last line of the processional sequence that begins on the walls of **AO-22** above. Where those inscriptions form the spoken Processional Ward — a prayer recited aloud to reinforce the crypt seals — this final line was carved here as a threshold marker. It was not spoken. It was read. The pallbearers paused, read the lintel, and then crossed into the gallery in silence. The silence was the point. Beyond the arch, speech was reserved for the sealing prayer at the tomb itself.

**DC 10 Religion**  
The inscription is Old Oeridian, but any character proficient in Religion recognizes the liturgical form. This is a consecration boundary — the threshold between the living spaces above and the consecrated burial ground below. Crossing the arch with hostile intent toward the dead would have triggered the ward system when it was at full strength. The wards are degraded now, but the threshold still carries a faint divine charge — characters with divine senses or detect magic perceive a gossamer-thin barrier of radiant energy across the archway, barely visible, flickering like a candle flame in a draft.

**DC 14 Arcana**  
The ward on the archway is functional but critically weakened. It no longer prevents hostile passage. However, undead that cross the threshold from the gallery side into the landing take 1d4 radiant damage — a remnant of the original containment ward that kept the dead from ascending into the priory above. This is relevant if the party triggers undead in the galleries and retreats up the staircase: the landing archway provides a minor defensive advantage.

##### The Consecrated Candle Shelf

On the east wall, a stone shelf runs the full width of the room — eighteen inches deep, waist-high, its surface scored with shallow circular grooves where consecrated candles once stood in iron holders. The candle shelf was the last ritual station before the gallery. The presiding cleric placed a fresh candle here during each funeral — a votive offering to [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md), lit from the eternal flames above, carried forward into the crypts to light the dead's tomb as it was sealed. The circular grooves held the candles upright. Each groove was carved for a specific funeral — dozens of grooves mark the shelf's surface, overlapping in places where centuries of use wore the stone smooth.

The shelf is now buried under the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s supply cache. Stacked crates of excavation tools, coils of rope, and iron-reinforced lanterns cover the entire surface. The candle grooves are invisible unless the supplies are moved.

**DC 13 Investigation (requires moving supplies)**  
Clearing the shelf reveals the circular candle grooves and, in the shelf's center, a shallow depression that once held a consecrated oil reservoir — a small stone basin designed to keep votive oil ready for the funeral candles. The basin is dry and cracked, but its interior surface still carries a faint warmth when touched by a cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). This is a consecrated surface — the same divine resonance as the stone bier in **AO-22** above, weaker but present. Pouring consecrated oil from **AO-15** into the basin and lighting it produces a small, steady, blue-white flame that illuminates the entire landing in sacred light. While the votive flame burns, undead cannot enter AO-C01 from the gallery without succeeding on a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw each round.

### What the Earth Temple Made of It

The duergar claimed this room within the first week of the excavation. **Grimlok Ashpick** recognized the tactical value immediately — the landing is a natural chokepoint with a single entrance from above and a single exit below. Anyone descending the staircase enters the landing before they can reach the galleries. Anyone ascending from the galleries must pass through the landing before they can reach the staircase. Two duergar with war picks and a rope alarm can hold this room against a dozen men coming from either direction.

The conversion was minimal. The duergar are professional miners, not cultists — they fortify for efficiency, not ideology. They did not desecrate the room. They did not topple the lintel inscription or deface the walls. They stacked their supplies on the candle shelf because the shelf was flat, waist-high, and convenient. The alarm rope was strung because **Grimlok** does not trust the human guards in **AO-22** above to warn him in time if something goes wrong. The duergar view this room as a work site, not a sacred space — they do not understand what they are standing on, and they do not care.

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) understands. He feels the consecration in the stone through his tremorsense — a faint warmth, a pressure, an echo of the prayers that were spoken here over five centuries of burials. He has said nothing about it to the duergar. He has said nothing about it to [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md). The consecration is weakening on its own. It does not need his help to die.

### The Duergar Checkpoint

Two duergar sentries occupy the landing at all times — rotating on a six-hour shift from the Work Camp (**AO-C04**). They are not elite fighters. They are miners pulling guard duty because **Grimlok** does not trust anyone else to watch the staircase. They are competent, disciplined, and thoroughly bored.

##### Sentry Positions

Both sentries position themselves on the east side of the room, behind the supply cache, where they have a clear line of sight to the staircase entrance on the north wall and the gallery arch on the south wall. One watches north (staircase). One watches south (gallery). They sit on upturned rubble buckets. They play a duergar dice game called _grindstone_ — carved bone dice rolled into a shallow pit scraped into the flagstone floor. The game is quiet. Duergar do not shout when they gamble.

**Sentry Alertness**  
Under normal conditions, both sentries are **alert but not hostile**. They challenge anyone descending the staircase — the recognition signal is a fist pressed flat against the chest (the Earth Temple salute). Anyone who gives the signal is waved through. Anyone who does not is questioned in Undercommon first, then broken Common. The duergar are suspicious but not aggressive — their orders are to hold the landing and sound the alarm, not to start fights they might lose.

**If the party approaches from above (staircase):**  
The sentries hear footsteps on the stairs long before the party arrives — the staircase amplifies sound. Perception DC 12 to notice the sentries' darkvision eyes reflecting faintly at the bottom of the dark stairwell. The duergar see the party's light sources (if any) the moment they enter the staircase in **AO-22** above. There is no surprising the sentries from this direction without magical darkness or invisibility.

**If the party approaches from below (gallery):**  
This is possible only if the party entered the crypt level through the Craig Ledge (**AO-C10**) and navigated the Ossuary and gallery. Approaching from the south, the party can see the sentries' lantern-light from the gallery (Perception DC 10 — the duergar keep a single hooded lantern, turned low, as a courtesy for human acolytes who pass through). Stealth DC 14 to approach within 30 feet without alerting them. The sentries are positioned to watch the staircase primarily — they check the gallery arch less frequently.

The staircase ends. The ceiling drops — lower than the room above, close enough to feel the weight of the stone. The air is different down here. Colder. Damper. The mineral smell of old limestone fills your nose, layered with dust that tastes like it has been settling for centuries.  
  
A rectangular chamber opens before you — perhaps twenty feet across and fifteen feet deep, the walls smooth-dressed stone, the floor worn flagstone. On the east wall, a stone shelf disappears under stacked crates, coiled rope, and iron lanterns. On the far wall, a wide stone arch leads into absolute darkness — a corridor beyond, swallowed entirely by the lack of light. Above the arch, carved into a stone lintel, words in an old script you cannot quite read from here.  
  
Two figures sit behind the supply stacks on the east side — short, broad, gray-skinned, their eyes catching your light with a dull amber reflection. Duergar. One holds a war pick across his knees. The other has a hand resting on a taut rope that disappears through a narrow gap in the south wall. Neither stands. Neither speaks. They are watching you with the patient, calculating stillness of creatures who have been underground their entire lives and know exactly how sound carries in stone.

The two sentries are standard duergar — gray dwarves with darkvision, Enlarge, Invisibility, Sunlight Sensitivity, and the grim professionalism of Underdark-born miners. They are not fanatics. They are not loyal to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md). They are employees.

##### Sentry Tactics

*   **Round 0 (if alerted):** The rope sentry pulls the alarm — iron pitons rattle along the rope leading to **AO-C04**. The alarm is audible throughout Zones A and B. **Grimlok Ashpick** in the Work Camp begins mobilizing within 2 rounds
*   **Round 1:** Both sentries use Enlarge. They grow to Large size, filling the space between the supply cache and the wall. Their war picks deal an extra 1d4 damage. They block the gallery arch — no one passes south without going through them
*   **Round 2+:** They fight defensively, holding the landing. They do not pursue retreating intruders up the staircase. Their orders are to hold and alert, not chase. If one sentry falls, the other uses Invisibility to disengage and retreats south through the gallery arch toward **AO-C04** to report
*   **If overwhelmed:** A sentry reduced to 8 HP or fewer who can see an escape route will use Invisibility and flee south. Duergar do not die for human paymasters. **Grimlok** drilled this into them: "You are worth more alive than dead. Run, report, and let someone else pay for the mistake."

##### The Alarm Rope

A hempen rope, half-inch thick, strung taut from an iron piton hammered into the east wall to a second piton in the south wall, threading through a narrow gap into the corridor beyond and continuing to **AO-C04** (the Duergar Work Camp). Iron pitons are knotted onto the rope at six-inch intervals — pulling the rope rattles the pitons against the stone walls of the corridor, producing a sharp metallic clattering that carries clearly through the crypt galleries.

**Perception DC 12** to spot the rope before entering the room (it runs along the east wall at waist height, partially concealed behind the supply crates).

**Thieves' Tools DC 14** to sever the rope quietly without alerting the sentries. This requires being within 5 feet of the rope — which means being in the room. A character who severs the rope silently removes the duergar's ability to alert the camp without shouting.

**If the alarm sounds:** **Grimlok Ashpick** sends 2 armed duergar from the Work Camp within 1d4+2 rounds. They arrive through the gallery arch from the south, armed and Enlarged. If **Grimlok** judges the threat serious (based on the fleeing sentry's report), he comes himself within 2d4 rounds with the remaining crew.

### Negotiation: The Third Option

The duergar sentries can be talked to. This is not a guaranteed combat encounter. The sentries are bored, underpaid, and working for a human cult they despise. They respond to the following approaches:

**Earth Temple salute (fist flat against chest):** The sentries wave the party through without challenge. They assume anyone who knows the signal is authorized. No check required. The party can walk directly into the gallery.

**Undercommon greeting:** Any character who speaks Undercommon and addresses the sentries respectfully earns a Charisma check at advantage. The duergar are startled — surface-dwellers rarely speak their language. Persuasion DC 12 convinces them the party is here on business with **Grimlok**, not a threat. The sentries allow passage but insist on escorting the party to the Work Camp (**AO-C04**).

**Bribery:** The sentries accept coin — 10 gp per sentry to look the other way for 10 minutes. They take the gold, turn their backs to the gallery arch, and resume their dice game. They do not sound the alarm. They do not report the bribe to **Grimlok** — skimming is standard practice in duergar contract work. Persuasion DC 10 with coin in hand.

**Intimidation:** Difficult. Duergar do not frighten easily, and threatening them in their own chokepoint is strategically foolish. Intimidation DC 16, and even on a success, the sentries let the party pass but sound the alarm the moment they enter the gallery — they are not afraid, they are buying time for **Grimlok** to prepare.

**Deception:** Claiming to be reinforcements or inspectors from [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) requires Deception DC 13. The sentries have no reason to doubt it — humans come and go on [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s orders regularly. On a success, the sentries allow passage without escort. On a failure, they demand the party wait while one sentry goes to verify with **Grimlok** — a process that takes 10 minutes and results in a confrontation when **Grimlok** confirms he sent no one.

### Sacred Layer

The Processional Landing was never a grand room. It had no carvings beyond the lintel inscription, no murals, no statuary. It was a hinge — the place where the procession paused, adjusted, and passed through into the galleries. Its sacredness was functional: the consecrated candle shelf, the threshold ward on the archway, the final line of the processional prayer carved in stone. Small things. Quiet things. The accumulated residue of five centuries of funerals passing through the same twenty-by-fifteen room.

The desecration here is not violent. No one toppled a statue. No one smashed an altar. The duergar simply stacked their supplies on the candle shelf, scraped a dice pit into the consecrated flagstone, and hammered pitons into walls that were dressed by Oeridian masons six hundred years ago. The desecration is **indifference** — the failure to recognize that the room is sacred at all. The duergar have no frame of reference for surface-world faith. To them, this is a room with good sightlines and a flat shelf. The candle grooves are just marks in the stone. The lintel inscription is just old writing in a language they do not read.

That indifference is, in its own way, worse than [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s deliberate desecrations above. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) tears down statues because he understands they mean something and wants to destroy that meaning. The duergar do not even see it. The consecration erodes not because it is attacked, but because no one recognizes it enough to attack.

### DM Notes

##### Running This Room

AO-C01 is the party's first contact with the crypt level. How it plays depends entirely on how the party arrives:

*   **Loud descent from AO-22 (killed the guards above, fought through):** The sentries heard everything. They have already pulled the alarm. When the party reaches the bottom of the staircase, both sentries are Enlarged, positioned on either side of the gallery arch, war picks ready. **Grimlok**'s reinforcements arrive in 1d4+2 rounds. This is the worst-case entry
*   **Quiet descent from AO-22 (stealth, deception, or cleared the room silently):** The sentries are at their posts, playing grindstone, alert but not alarmed. The party has 1d4+1 rounds of uncertainty — the sentries expect to hear the guards above and grow suspicious when the normal sounds stop. This is the window for negotiation, deception, or a stealth approach
*   **Approach from the gallery (entered via Craig Ledge, AO-C10):** The sentries are watching the staircase, not the gallery. Stealth DC 14 from the south. The alarm rope is on the east wall, between the party and the sentries. A rogue can sever it before combat begins if positioned correctly

##### The Negotiation Angle

This is the first room where the duergar can be talked to. Push this option. The sentries are the weakest link in **Grimlok**'s operation — they are bored, underpaid, and have no personal stake in the excavation. A party that bribes or talks its way past the sentries enters the gallery without raising an alarm. A party that fights them starts a clock — **Grimlok**'s reinforcements, followed by the full camp mobilization, followed (potentially) by [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) himself if the resonance anchor in **AO-22** is intact.

The bribery DC (10) is deliberately low. The party should be rewarded for thinking before fighting. The Undercommon approach (DC 12 at advantage) rewards character backstory and language investment. The Earth Temple salute is free — and the party can learn it from **Halven Sere**, from captured cultists above, or from the letters in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s quarters (**AO-17**).

##### Connections

*   **AO-22 (above):** The processional staircase. Thirty-foot descent, narrowed to three feet by Earth Temple clay packing. Single-file only. The party's entry point if approaching from the main level
*   **AO-C02 (south, through gallery arch):** The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead — the 120-foot central spine of the crypt level. All zones branch from the gallery. The archway ward (1d4 radiant to undead crossing north) makes the landing a minor defensive fallback
