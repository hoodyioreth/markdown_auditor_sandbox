G1 Old House of Prince Jimm
===========================

_A Historical Gnome Residence of Political Intrigue  
_The Old House of Prince Jimm is a historic gnome mound-home—painted a proud red, trimmed in white railings, and watched over by two decorative suits of armor at the door—now repurposed as a secret meeting haven after Jimm relocated to his safer, higher-status city manor. It is where gnomes settle disputes that must not become public… and where outsiders may be invited—cautiously—when a problem requires blades, boldness, or deniability.

##### Approaching the Old House

The path coils through green mounds and root-work, and then the burrow rises before thee—an earthen villa dressed like a jest made solemn: red-painted woodwork, white railings, and oval windows that gleam like watchful eyes. Two suits of antique armor stand sentinel by the round door—too still to be merely ornamental, too dusty to be truly alive.  
  
A little bell chimes once, not from thy hand, but from within—as though the house itself had marked the weight of thy footfalls. The air smells of oiled hinges, sweetbread, and ink. Somewhere below, a latch clicks—then silence, patient and appraising.

#### History (Why This Place Matters)

_From Royal Residence to Secret Meeting Haven_

Once Jimm’s primary residence, the Old House became a political instrument as threats grew. Jimm ultimately moved to a grander, more secure manor elsewhere in the city—both for safety and influence—yet he **kept this old home** as a discreet node for gnomish strategy.  
This is not nostalgia; it is _positioning_. A “heritage house” draws fewer eyes than a civic hall.

#### Description

_Architectural Charm with Strategic Features  
_This is **classic gnomish architecture**: mound-like, terraced, and deliberate—built to look charming while hiding a dozen practical lines of retreat. The exterior is notable for its **red paint and white railings**; the entry is framed by **two suits of armor** that serve as symbol, warning, and (if you wish) very mundane alarm system. G1 Old House of Prince Jimm

##### Key Areas (Use these as “scenes”)

**1) Main Display Room (Public Face)  
**A tidy receiving chamber with family relics, old treaty ribbons, and curated “harmless” curios—meant to read as _heritage_, not power. Tea service is always ready. Servants here are polite and observant.

**2) The Meeting Hollow (Secret Use)  
**A hidden space for the Free Alliance’s patriots and envoys—where disagreements are reduced to ink, witness, and carefully chosen words. This is where Jimm hosts the factions that either support or contest the Viscount’s policies, and where stalemates are common because gnomish consensus is hard-won.

**3) Old Quarters (Private Echoes)  
**Rooms that still feel like a home—kept intentionally so. The emotional utility is real: guests drop their guard when they see a lived-in hearth.

**4) Escape Lines (Rumored, and likely True)  
**Whispers persist of **emergency tunnels**—the kind that exist because politics turns bitter fast. Whether you make them real, trapped, or partially collapsed is your lever for tension.

#### Faction & Politics (How to Use This in Verbobonc)

This location is a pressure valve in the city’s gnome-human relationship. The Viscounty’s interest in tighter unity strains against the Free Alliance’s isolationist autonomy; conversation here can unravel into bitter argument if mishandled.

**This house is where:**

*   disputes are arbitrated before they become riots,
*   trade grievances turn into leverage,
*   and “polite talk” becomes covert intelligence.

#### Evolving from Traditional to Influential

Prince Jimm's move from the quaint Old House in Gnomesberg to the grand  [B6 Manor of Prince Jimm](b6-manor-of-prince-jimm-landmark.md) in a bustling commercial district of Verbobonc signifies a pivotal transformation in his leadership and the role of the Kron Hills Gnomes within the city. This relocation is not just a change of residence but a strategic positioning that reflects the evolving political landscape and the prince’s aspirations for greater influence and security.

##### Reasons for the Move

_From Safety to Status_

*   **Increased Security Concerns:** As Prince Jimm's political role expanded, so did the security threats against him. The new manor, with state-of-the-art security features, including rumored magical protections and escape routes, offers much-needed safety for him and his entourage.
*   **Desire for Greater Influence:** Relocating to a commercial hub allows Prince Jimm direct access to the economic and political heartbeat of Verbobonc, enabling him to forge and maintain influential connections more effectively.

### Temple of Elemental Evil Hooks (576 CY)

##### 1) The Name That Draws Wolves — _Lysander_

Jimm privately asks the party why Temple agents (and darker powers) are asking after a gnome named **Lysander**. He can share the tale—only if trust is proven: Lysander returned from an expedition in distant ruins “not the same,” and when the Temple began searching, gnomes were kidnapped and tortured for answers. Jimm hid Lysander in Verbobonc where he could be cared for in secret. Prince Jimm  
**Use:** This becomes a long-term plot-cord into Temple intelligence-gathering, abductors, and the party’s moral choices.

##### 2) The Quiet Meeting That Must Not Be Seen

A Free Alliance delegate arrives with proof of **cheating in trade deals** or a new **mine dispute**—and the evidence points to human intermediaries tied to cult logistics. The party can choose to expose, blackmail, or redirect shipments. Free Alliance of the Kron Hills

##### 3) The Empty Tunnel Rumor

Someone has begun spreading word that the escape tunnels are “real and in use,” hoping to justify an official search of gnome property. Is it the Viscount’s hardliners, a rival guild, or Temple agents seeking Lysander’s hidden trail?

#### Non-Combat Solutions & Leverage (Gnomesberg Play)

*   **Contract Binding:** Promise service for information; Jimm respects _kept oaths_ more than coin.
*   **Witness & Seal:** Bring a neutral witness (gnome, dwarf, or halfling merchant) and gain faster trust.
*   **Courtesy Debt:** Do a small kindness in Gnomesberg first; the staff will already know.

#### DM Mechanics & Details (Drop-In)

**Security (mundane, elegant):**

*   The “armor sentries” are _not magical_ unless you decide; they may simply hide alarm cords, weighted floor plates, or whisper-tubes to the staff corridor.
*   Servants know every creak; strangers do not.

**Meeting Clock (tension tool):**

*   Every 10 minutes of loud talk, threats, or rudeness: a “polite interruption” occurs (tea, biscuits, misplaced courtesy) to test the party. Three strikes and the meeting ends—smiling.

*   A **favor owed**—sealed in wax, redeemable once.

##### “Prince” Jimm Pithriggen (Faljimm Nizgafal)

Host in Absentia

Jimm is **diplomatic, enigmatic, clever**, and often more interested in what _you_ reveal than what he says aloud. He carries himself as a bridge between cultures—splendid attire, confident charm—yet his authority over Kron Hills gnomes is **limited by consensus politics**. Prince Jimm  
When he uses this house, it is because the matter is too delicate for any public room.

**Roleplay cues:**

*   He asks questions like they are compliments.
*   He smiles when you speak plainly—because it is rare.
*   He dislikes threats; he prefers _terms_.

##### The Loyal House Staff (Unnamed by Design)

The servants remain from his earlier household and keep the place operating as though Jimm might arrive any hour.  
They are also a quiet security net: soft-footed witnesses, masters of “accidental” overhearing.

##### Visiting “Patriots” of the Free Alliance

Free Alliance figures drift in and out—merchants, clan voices, legalists, couriers—seeking autonomy and protection of Kron Hills rights. Their disputes with Verbobonc over mines, trade cheating, and caravan attacks are the daily fuel of this home’s secret heart.

### Player Handout

**A Polite Contract of Guest-Right**

_Issued from the Old House of Prince Jimm, Gnomesberg — in fair ink upon stout vellum._

* * *

**THE COURTESY OF THRESHOLD AND HEARTH  
**being a _Polite Contract of Guest-Right_, offered unto the Bearer, and unto those in their company, for the span of one visit, within the Old House and its lawful precincts.

**Let it be known** that the House extendeth **Guest-Right** in good faith, and demandeth the same, according to the ancient customs of the Kron Hills and the civil peace of Verbobonc.

**This Contract bindeth by**: spoken assent at the threshold, mark of hand, or acceptance of bread and salt.

* * *

##### I. Of Entry and Bearing

1.  **Thou shalt knock but once** and wait, neither pressing nor prying.
2.  **Thou shalt keep thy weapons sheathed** within the House, save by express invitation of the Steward.
3.  **Thou shalt not draw steel nor spell** in anger, threat, or vanity.
4.  **Thou shalt keep thy voice measured**, for loudness is the brother of quarrel.

* * *

##### II. Of Speech, Truth, and Courtesy

1.  **Thou shalt name thyself truly**, or else name thyself _consistently_; for a shifting name is a thief’s lantern.
2.  **Thou shalt not bear false witness** within earshot of the hearth.
3.  **Thou shalt not bargain in malice**, nor twist a word once it hath been fairly understood.
4.  **Thou shalt speak no oath lightly**, for oaths are nails that do not pull free without pain.

* * *

##### III. Of Seeing and Seeking

1.  **Thou shalt not open what is shut**, nor lift what is covered, nor touch what is not offered.
2.  **Thou shalt not pace the corridors unguided**, nor stray from the rooms appointed to thee.
3.  **Thou shalt not count doors, steps, lamps, or watchers** with the mind of a burglar.
4.  **Thou shalt not ask after hidden ways**, save once—and only once; a second asking is deemed ill-intent.

* * *

##### IV. Of Bread, Salt, and Safe Conduct

1.  If bread and salt be set before thee, **thou art under the House’s protection** while thou keepest this Contract.
2.  The House, in turn, shall offer **no harm, ambush, nor treachery** to any Guest who keepeth the peace.
3.  Should danger arise from without, the House shall **warn the Guest** if warning may be given without greater peril to the innocent.

* * *

##### V. Of Secrets and Quiet Matters

1.  **What is spoken in the Meeting Hollow** (or any chamber declared “Quiet”) is to be treated as _sealed talk_.
2.  A Guest may repeat sealed talk only by:
3.  consent of the Speaker, **or**
4.  order of lawful court, **and** witness thereof.
5.  To trade sealed talk for coin, favor, or malice is named **Tongue-Theft**, and is held shameful.

* * *

##### VI. Of Breach and Forfeit

1.  If a Guest breaketh this Contract, the House may:
2.  end the visit at once,
3.  escort the Guest beyond the threshold,
4.  bar the Guest henceforth,
5.  and seek lawful remedy by witness and record.
6.  **If steel be drawn or spell loosed within the House**, Guest-Right is forfeit, and the House is loosed from restraint.

* * *

##### VII. Of Tokens and Signs

This Contract is proven by the token below, which the Bearer shall present upon request:

**TOKEN MARK:** _A white-wax seal impressed with a smiling fox above a pair of scales; beneath, three dots in a row.  
(To mimic the seal: draw a fox-mask, then scales; add • • • below.)_

* * *

##### VIII. The Courtesy Clause (Read Aloud at the Threshold)

“I come in peace, and I shall keep thy peace.  
I shall take naught unoffered,  
I shall open naught unbidden,  
and I shall carry forth no spite in my mouth.  
So do I enter.”
