G6 The Rusty Nail
=================

_The Hidden Gem of Gnomesberg  
_The Rusty Nail is the gnome quarter’s most unexpected tavern: not smoky and cavernous like The Brass Rail, but vibrant, whimsical, and meticulously curated. Its walls bloom with illusionary windows that show enchanted gardens where unicorns wander and pixies flit between luminous flowers. The domed ceiling displays glowing painted vistas of the Greenway Valley and Kron Hills, turning a buried room into a living memory of home.

**This is where you bring the party when you want:**

*   a “safe” meeting place that’s too comfortable to be trusted
*   a social scene where everyone can be friendly… and still be spying
*   a neutral venue for cross-district factions to talk without admitting it’s politics
*   a perfect cover for Temple and Iuz agents to recruit, bribe, and threaten—softly

##### Approach The Rusty Nail

The door looks plain—wood, iron latch, a simple sign with a nail gone red-brown with age. Yet the moment thou enterest, the underhill gloom is gone as though swept aside by a conjurer’s hand.  
  
“Windows” line the walls—tall, arched panes opening upon impossible gardens: dew-bright leaves, flowers the color of dawn, and a white unicorn browsing as if it had never known a stable. Pixies drift through sunbeams that have no business being here. Above, a domed ceiling glows with painted hills and green valleys—so near thou mightst swear the wind stirs the grass.  
  
Polished brass pipes run behind the bar like the innards of some clockwork beast, valves gleaming, taps clicking with cheerful precision. And there, smiling wide behind it all, is a gnome with a nose like a proud prow—Snipple Bimplenose—bowing as though thy arrival were the best part of his night.

### What’s Here (Layout & Features)

##### The Garden-Wall Lounge

The main room is warm, bright, and almost outdoor-fresh—because the illusions are designed to do that. The “windows” are not merely scenic; they are _moodcraft_, meant to soothe nerves and loosen tongues.

**Illusionary Windows (signature feature):**

*   enchanted garden views
*   unicorns and pixies
*   gentle movement—leaves drifting, lights shifting, shadows passing  
    Use this as a DM tool: the illusion can _react subtly_ to mood, lies, tension, and magic.

##### The Domed Ceiling of Home

A magical-glowing panorama of the Greenway Valley and Kron Hills curves overhead. Locals will stare up when homesick, or when a hard decision is being weighed.

##### The Brass Tapworks

A showpiece system of polished brass tubes, valves, and taps. Drinks don’t just pour—they arrive with satisfying clicks, hisses, and little puffs of scented steam. Snipple loves the performance of it.

##### The Shrine Niche (Garl Glittergold)

In a small alcove sits a shrine dedicated to **Garl Glittergold**, rendered as an illusion of an arcane trickster—smiling, hand outstretched, as if about to “gift” you a joke you won’t understand until later.

#### History (Why the Rusty Nail Exists)

Snipple Bimplenose founded the Rusty Nail as a simple tavern that grew into a local landmark through **gnomish ingenuity and illusion magic**. What began humbly became a “must-visit” in Gnomesberg—beloved not for prestige, but for the feeling it gives: that you’ve stepped back into the Kron Hills for a few hours, even while the city presses close outside.

* * *

#### Politics & Social Role

The Rusty Nail maintains a **neutral stance** in Verbobonc politics and positions itself as a safe haven for diverse patrons. That neutrality is valuable—and therefore constantly tested.

**In play, neutrality means:**

*   no open faction colors
*   no public accusations
*   no bloodshed
*   no “business” that can’t be denied with a smile

Snipple will protect neutrality with charm first… and by quietly ejecting problems second.

> “Mind the nail above the door,” Snipple Bimplenose says, leaning close as though to share a jest, his vast nose near brushing thy cheek. “Rust, my darling, is only iron that hath remembered rain. Step within, and thou shalt find the sky again—painted, aye, but kinder than the stone without.” He taps the bar with a fingernail—tick, tick—and the illusion-windows bloom with garden-light, pixies sparking like drifted embers. “Drink, laugh, and speak softly. Here, even thy secrets may take a pleasant shape… ere they fly away.”

### Temple of Elemental Evil & Iuz Hooks

##### 1) The Illusion That Watches Back

A party member notices the pixies in the window illusion turning their heads when certain names are spoken—Temple names, Iuzite words, particular noble houses. Is the illusion enchanted to react to keywords? Or is someone using it as a listening device?

**Play it as:** a slow-burn paranoia seed. The party must decide whether to speak carefully… or test it.

##### 2) The Neutral Meeting

A human clerk, a gnome merchant, and a halfling river-runner all arrive separately and “coincidentally” sit within earshot. Snipple insists it’s happenstance. It isn’t. The meeting concerns a missing shipment—stolen goods moving toward Temple logistics.

**Non-combat win:** gather evidence, catch the handoff, follow the courier, or turn one participant.

##### 3) Iuzite “Protection” in a Pretty Glass

A smooth-talking stranger offers “a blessing” with a special spirit—Trickster’s Tonic or a similarly rare pour. Those who accept later find themselves pressured for favors, donations, or introductions. The party can expose the coercion without starting a brawl—if they play it smart.

##### 4) The Shrine’s Little Joke

Someone has placed a tiny token at the shrine—an offering that is _not_ gnomish. Snipple pretends not to have noticed. A Free Alliance contact quietly asks the party to identify the token’s origin: Temple sign? Iuz cult marker? Or a rival guild’s subtle threat?

* * *

#### Non-Combat Leverage & Social Play

This tavern rewards players who pay attention:

*   **Eavesdropping without sneaking:** the room is full of admirers and laughter; it’s easy to blend in.
*   **Social “cover”:** flirtation, jokes, and toasts provide excuses to move around.
*   **Illusion tells:** use the windows and ceiling as mood indicators—subtle shifts when lies are spoken or tempers rise.
*   **Neutral ground rules:** conflict becomes social and legal, not violent.

### Scenes You Can Run Immediately

##### Scene 1: The Compliment Trap

Snipple compliments the party with uncanny specificity—mud on the boot from a certain road, a bloodstain hidden under a cuff, a holy symbol worn inward. He smiles like it’s nothing.

**The party realizes:** this place is friendly… and observant.

##### Scene 2: The Garden Window Bet

An admirer wagers that the unicorn in the window will “bow” if someone tells the truth for a full minute. It’s a silly game—until it becomes a way to test a suspect.

##### Scene 3: The Tapwork Malfunction

One valve suddenly hisses and sprays—minor chaos. Snipple laughs it off… but a keen eye spots a deliberate tool mark. Someone is testing sabotage methods (Temple logistics? Iuz intimidation? guild war?).

* * *

#### Rewards

*   A quiet introduction to a Free Alliance courier or gnome merchant
*   A rumor that is _actionable_ (shipment, name, route, date)
*   A “neutral token” from Snipple: a brass nail stamped with his mark that signals “this guest is welcome” across select Gnomesberg venues
*   A secret: the Rusty Nail’s illusions can be tuned—by the right person—for surveillance or signaling

#### Snipple Bimplenose

(Owner/Barkeep)

Snipple is charisma wrapped in velvet humor—an effortless host with the confidence of a man who knows the room loves him before he speaks. His **large nose** is his pride and his brand; admirers flirt with it, joke about it, and write poems about it. He takes it all like a king receiving tribute.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Addresses folk as if they are already friends.
*   Laughs easily, but watches quietly.
*   Speaks as though every conversation is a dance—he leads, you follow, and you’re grateful.

**What Snipple really is:  
**A social engineer. The Rusty Nail is a _neutral haven_, yes… but it is also a place where information flows to him whether patrons intend it or not.

##### “Admirers” (Rotating Cast)

Snipple’s admirers are not just flavor—use them as your rumor engine and faction barometer. In a single night you can place:

*   a gnome merchant with a grievance
*   a human guild clerk with nervous eyes
*   a halfling river-runner with cargo news
*   an off-duty watchman “just enjoying the scenery”
*   a cult courier trying to look ordinary and failing

When a customer holds the menu up to the lanternlight, the ink **doesn’t sit still**. It behaves like a small, well-trained glamour—polite, flattering, and just a touch nosy.

**First glance (anyone):**

*   The parchment gives off a faint **warm garden-scent**—crushed mint and honey.
*   The border-vines **uncurl by a hair’s breadth**, like something stretching after sleep.
*   A tiny pixie-lamp in the corner **flickers once**, then steadies, as though it recognized you.

**Second glance (anyone who lingers):**

*   The illustrated **unicorn blinks**. Not a full movement—just the slow close and open of an eye, like a calm beast in sun.
*   One pixie drifts off the border, **hovering over the “Ales” section**, then zips back into the ink with a smug twinkle.
*   The “gold” in the lettering **glitters** a fraction too long—as though it enjoys being admired.

##### The “Off-the-Page” Animation (the good stuff)

If a patron stares for more than a heartbeat, the illusion _politely performs_:

*   A **pixie lifts out of the illustration** as a mote of light, circles the reader’s fingertip, and leaves behind a faint, harmless sparkle that fades in a minute.
*   The unicorn’s mane **ripples like grass in wind**, and its hoof taps once—soft as a metronome—whenever someone speaks the name of a drink.
*   When a customer points to an item, a thin ribbon of light **traces from the word to a matching tiny icon** (a tankard, a goblet, a bottle) as if the menu is helpfully confirming their choice.

##### Snipple’s “House Trick” (his favorite flourish)

When Snipple places the menu down, he murmurs—almost casually:

“Look kindly, and it looks kindly back.”

On that cue, the pixies **leave the page for a breath**—no more than a handspan above the parchment—like fireflies obeying a gentle whistle. They hover in a small halo over the menu, then settle back into the printed border, leaving the corners faintly luminous for a moment.
