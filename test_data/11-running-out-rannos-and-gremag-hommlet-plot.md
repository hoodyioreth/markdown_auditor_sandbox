11\. Running Out Rannos and Gremag - Hommlet
============================================

The PCs uncover the sinister truth behind the traders Rannos Davl and Gremag Gozlin at the D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile in Hommlet. These seemingly harmless merchants are actually spies for the Temple of Elemental Evil, funneling information and resources to their dark masters. The revelation comes through a variety of potential sources—Lareth’s diary, testimonies from prisoners in the Moathouse, or even a divination spell. The village leaders, recognizing the danger, urge the PCs to eliminate this threat before it endangers the entire village. However, Rannos, Gremag, and their loyal man-at-arms are formidable foes, requiring careful planning and strong allies to overcome.

### Quest Hook

After spending some time in Hommlet, the PCs might hear rumors or gather evidence suggesting that the traders at D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile are not what they seem. Whether through direct investigation, questioning villagers, or stumbling upon a hidden document, the truth is revealed: Rannos and Gremag are spies working for the Temple of Elemental Evil, and their presence poses a grave threat to the village.

##### 2\. Aftermath

*   **Victory:** If the PCs successfully eliminate Rannos, Gremag, and their man-at-arms, the village leaders commend them for their bravery. They are awarded an additional 1,100 XP as a milestone, and the shop is searched thoroughly for any hidden secrets or treasures. The PCs may find documents linking the traders to the Temple of Elemental Evil, as well as stolen goods from caravans.
*   **Failure or Escape:** If the traders manage to escape, they will likely attempt to regroup with other forces of the Temple of Elemental Evil. The village leaders will be deeply concerned, increasing patrols and fortifying defenses. The PCs may need to pursue the traders or prepare for a potential counterattack.

This quest serves as a crucial turning point in the PCs' efforts to protect Hommlet from the insidious influence of the Temple of Elemental Evil, while deepening their ties with the village's key figures.

#### Background on Rannos and Gremag’s Operations

Rannos Davl and Gremag Gozlin are not mere merchants, though they attempt to play that role when confronted. They are, in fact, deeply entrenched in supporting The Hangmen, a notorious bandit gang based out of Nulb, led by Naelin. The pair profit heavily from the chaos caused by banditry across the Viscounty of Verbobonc, buying stolen goods from the bandits and reselling them at a tidy profit through their Dry Goods & General Mercantile.

*   **Hangmen Connections:** Their contact with Naelin’s Hangmen allows them to move goods stolen from merchant caravans and villages, receiving a cut of the profits in exchange for providing information and goods. While Rannos and Gremag try to distance themselves from the more violent aspects of the Hangmen’s operations, they are fully aware of the gang’s activities, including slave trading and raiding merchant roads.
*   **Profiting from Banditry:** The duo has no qualms about enriching themselves through the suffering caused by bandits. However, they carefully avoid any direct mention of the Temple of Elemental Evil or their connection to its activities. Their loyalty is to profit, and they are willing to deal with any party that keeps the gold flowing.
*   **Intel on Doomwatch Keep:** The most significant piece of information Rannos and Gremag offer in their plea is their role in spying on the construction of Doomwatch Keep. They have been feeding intel to Naelin’s agents about the Keep's progress, which the Hangmen use to plan attacks or sabotage efforts that might slow its completion.
*   **Misdirection and Half-Truths:** While they are willing to confess their involvement with the Hangmen and provide details about their dealings, they are careful not to implicate themselves too deeply in the cultist activities of the Temple of Elemental Evil. Their motivation is survival, and they will say whatever they think might save their lives.

By capturing Rannos and Gremag, the adventurers can uncover a major link in the bandit activities plaguing the region, but it will take more to fully expose the network connecting them to the Temple and its nefarious goals.

### Dialog for Rannos and Gremag Apprehended

As the adventurers capture and corner Rannos Davl and Gremag Gozlin in D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile, the two traders look visibly shaken. Their usual smug demeanor is replaced with fear as they realize their game might be up. Rannos speaks first, his voice trembling slightly but attempting to hold onto his charisma:

##### Rannos Davl:

"Look, there's no need for bloodshed, alright? We're just merchants, plain and simple. Sure, we've had dealings with some questionable sorts—bandits, adventurers, you name it—but is it really a crime to trade with anyone who walks through our door? We don’t ask questions, and we pay fair coin! Who wouldn’t want a little profit in these troubled times?"   He glances over at Gremag, who is wringing his hands nervously, but then continues:

##### Rannos Davl:

"We admit it, we've bought and sold loot from the... less reputable types. But who are we to judge where it comes from, eh? It's just business, and we don’t break any laws by paying for what adventurers or—bandits—bring in! And why should we? Banditry? Pah! It’s not our concern who steals from whom."  

##### Gremag Gozlin, finally finding his voice, adds in a quick plea:

"That's right! We're merchants, not criminals! We don't ride out to pillage or plunder! We just deal in what comes to us. Sure, sometimes it’s from those like the Hangmen... but do you know who they are? Nasty lot, led by Naelin, out in Nulb. You wouldn’t want to cross them any more than we do! They're the ones raiding the roads, not us!"

##### He wipes a bead of sweat from his forehead and adds nervously:

"Look, if you let us go, we'll tell you everything we know about them. They bring in slaves from the roads, even use Nulb to smuggle goods and people. We didn’t sign up for this! We’re just here for the gold, not the bloodshed! Naelin and his lot have been making life miserable for everyone, but we had no choice but to deal with them!"   Rannos gives Gremag a quick look to silence him, but it’s too late. Sensing the adventurers' interest, Rannos continues:

##### Rannos Davl:

"You see, the Hangmen? They pay well. And there’s always something coming in from Nulb. We might’ve helped a bit, passing along info about the construction of the Keep... It was just a way to make a few extra coins! Nothing more. We don’t care who’s in charge, be it Burne and Rufus or those bandits. Whoever runs the show, we just make sure to stay in business."

##### His eyes plead as he finishes:

"We're not warriors. We don't want to die for this! Let us live, and we can help you bring down Naelin. We've dealt with them long enough to know their operations, but believe me, we’re no friends of theirs. We're just caught in the middle, trying to make a living."
