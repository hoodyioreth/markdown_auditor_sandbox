V1-3 Castle Greyfist, Citadel of the Viscount
=============================================

Greyfist Castle and its surrounding grounds serve as a symbol of power and security in Verbobonc. The intricate design and formidable defenses of the castle reflect the complex political landscape and the challenges faced by Viscount Wilfrick in maintaining order and sovereignty in the region.

### What Greyfist _is_ in your campaign

Castle Greyfist is not merely walls and banners—it is **Verbobonc’s hinge**. Every district’s trouble eventually knocks here:

*   **Merchant quarrels** (Business Quarter) become **tariffs, warrants, and levies**.
*   **Temple politics** (Civic Centre) become **alliances, public sermons, and mobilizations**.
*   **Foreign intrigues** (Foreign Quarter) become **spies, ambassadors, and “quiet arrests.”**
*   **Gnome tensions** (Gnomesberg + Kron Hills) become **border patrol orders—or the lack thereof.**

Greyfist is also the “front desk” of the Viscounty: **petitions, summons, judgments, and military directives** all pass through its gates.

##### Arrival & First Impression Castle Greyfist

The climb unto Crown Hill steals thy breath—not for steepness alone, but for the still regard of stone. Above, Greyfist rises in pale, elven lines: slender towers stitched by airy arches, windows like spear-points of glass, and gardens trimmed so neatly they seem chastened. Yet all that grace is yoked to war: a 32-foot curtain wall, round towers thrusting to 40 feet, and the faint, cruel angles of ballistae watching thee as a hawk watches field-mice.  
  
At the gatehouse, a captain in white-and-ash livery lifts a gloved hand. _“Hold, travellers. Name thy business, and speak it plain.”_  
  
A second voice—low, amused—comes from above the portcullis, where a soldier leans near the murder-holes. _“Aye, and if thy tale be thin as watered ale, we’ll send thee back down the hill with thy pride in tatters.”  
  
_Steel rings softly. A dozen eyes weigh thy purse, thy weapons, thy scars. The city’s noise fades behind thee; here there is only the discipline of stone and the patience of men who have turned away kings.

#### Getting In

Greyfist is **not** a public hall. Entry is controlled, ritualized, and political.

**You may pass the outer gate if you have:**

*   **Noble standing** (to the great hall; identification expected).
*   A **verified summons** from the Viscount or high officer.
*   **Military rank** sufficient for free access (Knight Banneret+), or clear official duty.
*   A **petitioned appointment** reviewed by the castle’s chancellor.
*   **Special event access** (rare feasts, councils, ceremonies).

**Hard line:** _No unauthorized citizens past the gate._ The guards _enjoy_ enforcing this.

### V1 — Viscount’s Outer Grounds

(the bailey & the gate discipline)

##### The Grounds

The outer grounds are meticulously kept—lush gardens, tidy paths, and sight-lines that are _too_ perfect to be innocent. Every hedge is a **channel** for bodies to move, and every statue is a **marker** for archers.

##### Walls & Towers

*   **Curtain wall:** 32 ft high
*   **Four round towers:** 40 ft high
*   **Ballistae:** mounted on towers; each tower typically holds **10 troops**
*   **Patrols:** elite warriors circulate continuously

##### Gatehouse Kill-Box

*   20-ft high gateway, ramp + drawbridge
*   Door, portcullis, and murder holes
*   Additional defenses include a sturdy door, portcullis, and murder holes for repelling invaders.
*   The gate tower is permanently guarded by 20 troops, with 6 elite warriors securing the outer gate

##### Running the gate as a scene (quick beats)

**Gate Captain (dry, polite):** _“Hast thou writ? Hast thou witness? Hast thou coin for fines, should thy tongue outrun thy leave?”_**PC reply** → **Inspection** (weapons peace-bound, names recorded, purpose questioned)  
**Complication:** a courier arrives; a noble’s carriage demands precedence; a prisoner cart rattles out. The PCs must decide whether to wait, to bribe, or to impress.

**Checks (5e-ish guidance):**

*   Persuasion (DC 13): get “waiting hall” access without appointment
*   Deception (DC 15): pass as minor functionaries (risk later exposure)
*   Insight (DC 12): notice which guards are Borderers vs militia, and who actually decides
*   Perception (DC 14): spot ballista arcs / murder-hole geometry (tactical advantage later)

### V2 Castle Grayfist: Castle of the Viscount

Castle Greyfist stands as a testament to the rich history and cultural diversity of Verbobonc. Its unique elven architectural style not only adds to the city's aesthetic appeal but also plays a significant role in the political landscape. The castle's grand halls and intricate designs serve as a backdrop for the Viscount's rule, impacting the governance and diplomatic relations within the Viscounty. The blend of human and elven elements in its construction symbolizes the unity and strength of Verbobonc, making Greyfist a revered and influential landmark in the region.

##### Historical and Architectural Overview

*   Castle Greyfist, a rectangular structure, is prominently situated on a hill in the center of Verbobonc.
*   Renowned as a remarkable example of preserved elven architecture within the city.
*   The castle's design includes a complex of buildings and gardens, meticulously arranged for aesthetic natural vistas.
*   Characterized by slender towers connected by arched bridges, a typical feature of elven construction in Verbobonc.

##### Interior Tone (what it feels like)

High ceilings and many windows drown rooms in pale light. Hallways are narrow; staircases often lack banisters—built for elves who do not fear a fall, and for humans who must learn caution quickly. Pale wood and light-grained stone make everything feel **clean… and coldly judged**.

##### Key Chambers

**The Great Hall & Throne Room  
**These rooms are designed to impress—and to remind every lord that the Viscount’s authority rests on heritage, diplomacy, and the _ability to call men to war_.

**Viscount’s Meeting Hall (older structure)  
**Predates the tower; used for official Viscounty business. Wilfrick improved it with arched windows and **gnomish-crafted stained glass**—a diplomatic gesture that the gnomes will interpret according to the week’s mood.

**Tower of the Viscount (within the bailey)  
**A 60-ft tower, circular without and square within (rounded corners), three stories:

*   Ground: storeroom
*   Middle: great hall
*   Top: Wilfrick’s private chambers  
    A **secret exit from the basement** leads beyond the city walls—an escape route, a courier line, or a betrayal waiting to happen.

#### A living court: how the PCs meet power here

##### Wilfrick Revepaix (the weary crown)

Wilfrick remains a hero in memory—Emridy Meadows still haunts every banner in this place—but age and anxiety have made his court brittle. He leans on aides, clings to dignity, and watches rivals (especially the Lord Mayor) with the tired suspicion of a man who knows the knife may already be in the room.

**Use at the table:** Wilfrick rewards deference, piety, and practical solutions. He _hates_ being made to look uncertain.

**Sample dialogue  
**Wilfrick’s voice is measured, but the words come as though weighed upon scales.

_“Thou hast walked far to trouble my threshold. Speak, then—what do thy eyes see in my city that my councillors will not name?”_

If a PC pushes too hard:  
_“Softly, friend. I am not some green lordling to be baited. I have buried brave men—and I will not bury Verbobonc for want of patience.”_

* * *

#### The Council of Lords (when Greyfist becomes a storm)

When council is called, Greyfist turns from fortress to arena: alliances, rivalries, and marriage-plots gather around Wilfrick’s chair. The Council advises on military strategy, economic policy, diplomacy, and the Temple threat—yet each lord carries their own agenda like a second dagger.

**DM lever:** If you want the party to feel “small,” let them deliver evidence to a hall where powerful people argue about whether the evidence is _useful_, not whether it is _true_.

* * *

##### Impact on Local Lords and Politics

*   The elven architecture of Greyfist symbolizes a harmonious blend of human and elven cultures in Verbobonc, enhancing the Viscount's prestige.
*   Its grandeur and historical significance command respect among the local lords and nobility.
*   The castle serves as a focal point for political meetings and decisions, influencing the political dynamics of the region.
*   The elven elements of the castle appeal to the elven population and strengthen diplomatic ties with elven communities, such as those in Celene.

In the heart of Verbobonc lies Castle Greyfist, a bastion of authority and power within the Viscounty. Gaining entry to this formidable fortress is no simple task, as access is highly regulated to ensure security and order within its walls. Below is a synopsis of the conditions under which one may be granted permission to enter Castle Greyfist:

##### Nobility Access

*   Any member of the nobility can access the castle up to the great hall without prior arrangement.
*   Nobles are expected to present themselves and any accompanying party at the gate for identification.

##### Summons

*   Individuals summoned by the Viscount or any high-ranking official within the castle are granted access.
*   Summons must be verified by the guards at the gate before entry is permitted.

##### Military Personnel

*   Military officers of a certain rank (e.g., Knight Banneret and above) have unrestricted access to the castle.
*   Lower-ranking officers may enter for official business but might be restricted to certain areas.

##### Appointment by Petition

*   Common citizens and those without noble title or military rank must petition for an appointment.
*   Petitions are submitted to the Castle's Chancellor, who reviews and schedules appointments.
*   The Chancellor's Office is responsible for vetting petitions and granting access based on the nature of the request.

##### Special Circumstances

*   On rare occasions, special circumstances may grant access to the castle, such as public events hosted by the Viscount.
*   During these events, restrictions may be temporarily lifted or modified to accommodate guests.

##### Restrictions

*   No unauthorized citizens are allowed past the gate without one of the aforementioned permissions.
*   Security measures, including identity verification and purpose of visit, are strictly enforced at the gate.

#### Castle Greyfist – Tight Encounter Flow

**Gate → Waiting Hall → Audience → Barracks Complication → Exit (or Arrest)  
**Designed for fast pacing, rising tension, and political consequence.

* * *

##### 1️⃣ THE GATE – _Measured Steel_

**Tone:** Formal, disciplined, slightly adversarial.  
**Objective:** Establish authority. Test the party. Record their presence.

###### Scene Setup

The portcullis hangs half-raised. White-and-ash cloaks stir in the wind. A Gate Captain steps forward, tablet in hand.

“Name and purpose. Speak it plain.”

Behind them: archers in murder slits, Borderers mounted within the inner yard.

###### What Happens

*   Weapons must be peace-bound.
*   Names recorded.
*   Purpose questioned twice (once politely, once pointedly).
*   A squire escorts them inward.

###### Checks (Use Sparingly)

*   **Persuasion (DC 13):** Gain expedited entry.
*   **Deception (DC 15):** Enter under false pretenses (flag for later reveal).
*   **Insight (DC 12):** Notice which guard actually makes decisions.
*   **Intimidation (DC 16):** Allowed—but earns silent hostility.

###### Escalation Trigger

If the party is evasive or arrogant:

*   A Borderer lieutenant joins.
*   Entry allowed—but marked as “watch closely.”

* * *

##### 2️⃣ THE WAITING HALL – _The Pressure of Silence_

**Tone:** Quiet scrutiny. Nobility watching from above.  
**Objective:** Let tension build. Offer information and hooks.

A vaulted chamber of pale stone and high windows. Tapestries depict Emridy Meadows. Benches line the walls.

Servants move softly. A clerk whispers names to another scribe.

###### Insert One or More:

*   A minor noble arguing over tariffs.
*   A gnome envoy waiting with visible irritation.
*   A wounded Borderer being quietly escorted past.

###### Roleplay Levers

*   PCs may gather rumors.
*   A rival may observe them.
*   A White Guard officer studies them without speaking.

**Optional Check:**

*   **Perception (DC 14):** Spot who is actually listening behind the screen.
*   **History (DC 12):** Recognize political symbols on the banners.

###### Escalation Trigger

If they draw attention (boasting, threats, loud claims):

*   A White Guard requests they lower their voices.
*   Future audience tone shifts colder.

* * *

##### 3️⃣ THE AUDIENCE – _Measured Authority_

**Tone:** Controlled. Calculated. No wasted words.  
**Objective:** Deliver information. Create political stakes.

Wilfrick seated, Alden Hawkridge standing near, Gareth Mornshield present if military matters arise.

###### Structure of the Audience

1.  PCs speak uninterrupted (briefly).
2.  Alden asks sharp clarifying questions.
3.  Gareth focuses on tactical implications.
4.  Wilfrick decides outcome.

###### Key Tension Points

*   Does this require council?
*   Does this threaten trade?
*   Does this weaken border patrols?
*   Does this embarrass the Viscount publicly?

##### Checks

*   **Persuasion (DC 15):** Secure official backing.
*   **Insight (DC 14):** Know whether Wilfrick doubts them.
*   **Performance (DC 13):** Deliver compelling testimony.
*   **Deception (DC 17):** Risky—Alden rolls Insight.

###### Possible Outcomes

*   Commissioned as agents.
*   Given limited writ.
*   Ordered to prove claims.
*   Politely dismissed.
*   Quietly investigated.

* * *

##### 4️⃣ BARRACKS COMPLICATION – _Steel Under Velvet_

This should feel organic, not random.

###### Trigger Options

Pick one that fits your campaign arc:

###### A) Patrol Recall

A messenger bursts in—Borderers needed immediately.  
The PCs’ information now affects real deployments.

**Decision:  
**Do they volunteer to ride?  
Do they remain in safety?

* * *

###### B) Accusation

A soldier recognizes one of the PCs.

“That’s the one I saw near the north road.”

False? Mistaken? Or true?

* * *

###### C) Weapons Seizure

Gareth orders inspection of confiscated goods tied to the party’s claims. Something incriminating appears.

* * *

###### D) Political Rival Intervenes

A noble claims the PCs are exaggerating or lying.

Now this is about reputation.

* * *

###### Combat?

Rare inside Greyfist.  
If blades are drawn:

*   White Guard arrive in 1 round.
*   Heavy foot in 2 rounds.
*   Escape becomes nearly impossible.

##### 5️⃣ EXIT (OR ARREST)

###### If Successful

*   Formal escort to gate.
*   Written writ or seal.
*   Soldiers treat them with measured respect.
*   Rumor begins spreading within the castle.

* * *

###### If Suspicion Lingers

*   Shadowed as they leave.
*   Borderers track movements in city.
*   Alden quietly assigns a watcher.

* * *

###### If Arrested

Move immediately to controlled escalation:

**Response Clock**

1.  Disarmed.
2.  Separated.
3.  Questioned privately.
4.  Evidence presented.
5.  Formal charge—or political negotiation.

Wilfrick rarely imprisons without reason.  
But he will protect his authority.
