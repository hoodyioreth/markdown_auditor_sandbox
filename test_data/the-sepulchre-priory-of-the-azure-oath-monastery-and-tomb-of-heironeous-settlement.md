The Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath - Monastery & Tomb of Heironeous
=======================================================================

_Monastery & Tomb of Heironeous in the Kron Hills (5 miles west of Hommlet)_

Perched high upon a weathered ridge in the **Kron Hills**, the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath was once a fortified monastery dedicated to **Heironeous**, god of valor and righteous battle. From this sanctuary the knights of the Azure Oath guarded the mountain roads and watched over ancient tombs cut deep beneath the stone.

For centuries the priory stood as a beacon of honor and protection. That age has ended.

The knights were eventually recalled to distant wars, leaving only **Brother-Custodian Halven Sere**, the last oathman charged with maintaining the sacred tombs below. Alone and aging, he could not defend the monastery when the servants of the **Earth Temple** arrived. Now the fortress serves a far darker purpose.

A companion mutters, voice small in the open air. _“If this be Heironeous’ house… why doth it look as though the god Himself turned His face?”_

Another answers, grimly: _“Gods do not depart. Men do.”_

* * *

#### What this site is in your campaign

The Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath is a **forgotten frontier tomb-priory** of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) —built early, made famous by war-burials, then **withdrawn and left to one tomb-warden**. In 576 CY, Romag’s Earth Temple cell now uses the ruins as a **forward base to raid the Long Road**, feeding supplies, prisoners, and recruitment east toward the Temple’s Earth operations. (Use this as a “hinge ruin” linking **Hommlet → Long Road raids → Nulb/TOEE**.)

##### The Earth Temple Occupation

The Sepulchre-Priory has been seized by the **Cult of the Black Earth**, servants of the Elder Elemental God operating under the authority of **Romag**, High Priest of the Earth Temple within the **Temple of Elemental Evil near the village of Nulb**.

Romag has dispatched one of his most trusted lieutenants to oversee operations in the Kron Hills.

> This history is anchored to the Verbobonc timeline: the Velondi settlement era (-100 to 1 CY), the Long War (350–355 CY), the broader post-war religious/political shifts after 438 CY, and the rise of the Temple of Elemental Evil threat in the late 550s–569 CY.

#### What the Adventurers Face

Those who approach the Sepulchre-Priory will not find a quiet ruin. Instead they will discover a **fortified cult stronghold**, guarded by patrols and watchmen and filled with raiders preparing for the next strike against the roads of Verbobonc.

The monastery’s defenders include:

• **Black Earth Reaver patrols** in the courtyard  
• guards posted in the **gatehouse and tower  
**• raiders quartered in the **barracks and stables  
**• cultists conducting rituals in the **inner shrine**

And at the center of it all waits **Varnor Earthcaller**, listening for the whispers of the deep earth beneath the stone.

* * *

### Monastery Features

Most of the **Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath** remains structurally sound despite years of abandonment and recent occupation by the **Cult of the Black Earth**. Only the **eastern wing of the monastery shows heavy structural damage**, where sections of the outer wall have collapsed into piles of broken stone and rubble. Unless otherwise noted, the following features apply throughout the monastery complex.

* * *

#### The Fallen Oath

The irony of the monastery’s fall is not lost on Varnor. Where the knights of Heironeous once vowed to defend the weak and stand against darkness, their fortress now serves the worship of a god whose creed is **inevitability, silence, and the crushing patience of stone**. The banners of Heironeous have been torn down. In their place hangs the **triangular symbol of the Black Earth**, hammered into doors and shields throughout the complex. The halls that once echoed with prayer now resound with the chanting of cultists invoking the power of the **Elder Elemental God**.

* * *

#### What the Cult Seeks

The cult’s presence in the monastery is not permanent occupation.

Varnor’s true mission is excavation.

His forces are searching for three things beneath the priory:

• the **Flan altar of the Elder Elemental God  
**• ancient geomantic runes carved into the bedrock  
• a deep chasm known in old legends as **the Black Mouth**

Varnor believes the Black Mouth is a gateway through which the voice of the Elder Elemental God may be heard.

If he succeeds in awakening the altar below the monastery, the Earth Temple will gain a powerful new foothold in the Kron Hills—and Romag’s influence within the Temple of Elemental Evil will grow accordingly.

History of the Azure Oath
=========================

##### 1) The First Thunder (-12 CY)

The Sepulchre-Priory was raised when the Velondi folk still clawed order from hill and wood. In that hard season, sanctuaries were built not for comfort, but for **discipline**—stone that taught men how to stand.

**Founder:** **Sir Aedric Vayne**, called _First Thunder  
_A paladin-captain who swore that the Long Road would not be left wholly to chance and brigandry. He chose a ridge with sight-lines to the road and set the priory’s rule:

*   shelter the lawful traveler
*   sanctify the oathbound dead
*   turn away oath-breakers unatoned

**First Prior:** **Mother Alis of the Lamp  
**She established the vigil rite: a lamp kept upon holy nights, and a roll of names spoken aloud—so the dead would not be “lost twice.”

**Consecrator:** **Curate-Bishop Jorven Pell  
**He declared the undercroft a “Sepulchre of the Honoured,” allowing interment of knightly dead who fell beyond city reach.

##### 2) The Long War Burials (350–355 CY) — The Priory becomes a legend

When Keoland invaded western Verbobonc and southern Veluna (the opening of the Long War), the priory’s purpose changed from frontier piety to **national memory**. In those years, the dead came not singly, but in honoured companies.

### The Lightning Kneel (350 CY)

_the signature legend_

The tale is told in Hommlet alehouses and Verbobonc chapels alike: twelve paladins of Heironeous held a narrow pass while refugees and wounded withdrew down-slope toward the safer valleys.

They did not die running. They knelt.

The Twelve of the Lightning Kneel formed a ring in the rain, shields planted, blades upright, each swearing the Azure Oath aloud. When the last charge broke upon them, lightning struck the stones about their circle—so the story says—and the pass was held long enough for the helpless to escape.  
  
Their bodies were borne to the Sepulchre-Priory with banners wrapped round them like shrouds. Their names were carved into the well-stone of the chapter court, and their swords were sealed beneath the Oath-Hall.

##### 3) Later Border Skirmishes & the Gate-Law (4th–5th century CY)

As borders shifted and the forest-road saw intermittent fighting and banditry, the priory became strict—less a sanctuary of warm bread, more a **test of righteousness**.

**Prior-Captain Renwald Gorse** codified the _Three Questions of Entry_ at the gatehouse:

1.  _Art thou under lawful charge?_
2.  _Hast thou broken oath unatoned?_
3.  _Dost thou come with steel for the innocent?_

Those who failed were turned away—even in winter. The rule preserved the priory’s reputation for purity… and planted the first seeds of resentment among roadfolk who preferred kinder gods.

* * *

##### 4) The Slow Drift (438–562 CY) — When institutions consolidate

After the Short War and Keoland’s withdrawal, Verbobonc’s politics and faiths shifted, and religious influence reorganized across the Viscounty. The timeline explicitly frames this era as one of **religious and political shifts** (including changing dominance among churches and renewed alliances).

###### **Two practical forces diminished the Sepulchre-Priory**

*   A. Verbobonc City’s rising temple-power: The great [The Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) in the capital drew coin, clergy, and attention. Frontier houses became “honoured,” but not funded.
*   B. Larneystoe and the Abbey of the Valorous Knight: With the Abbot’s seat in Larneystoe and strong secular protection, the Church of Heironeous centralized training, storage, and authority there. The Sepulchre-Priory became redundant—too far, too quiet, too costly to garrison.
*   C. The road “felt” safer—until it wasn’t: Guild influence, patrol expectations, and civic confidence produced a fatal assumption: _the hills no longer required a vigil-house._

* * *

##### 5) The Withdrawal Decree (562 CY)

In 562 CY, the Church issued a formal withdrawal.

**Last Prior:** **Prior Edwyne Tholl**, called _Gentle Iron  
_He ordered:

*   movable relics transferred to stronger holdings
*   ledgers copied and sent to Verbobonc and Larneystoe
*   the most sacred tombs **sealed**, not emptied

But one soul refused to abandon the honoured dead.

**Brother-Custodian Halven Sere**—_the Last Oathman_—remained as caretaker of the tombs.

* * *

#### Why it matters now (576 CY)

Verbobonc records note that the **Temple of Elemental Evil’s raids on overland trade routes intensified from the late 550s onward**, culminating in the regional unrest of **568–569 CY and the years following**. As patrols thinned and old watchposts fell silent, forgotten strongholds throughout the Kron Hills became vulnerable to reoccupation.

The Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath is exactly the kind of abandoned stone fortress that darker powers reclaim when the hills begin to stir.

##### Earth Temple Outpost

The ruined monastery is now occupied by the **Cult of the Black Earth**, operating under the authority of **Romag**, High Priest of the Earth Temple within the **Temple of Elemental Evil** near Nulb. Romag’s lieutenant **Varnor Earthcaller** commands the monastery and has transformed it into a fortified raiding post overlooking the **Long Road**.

The last caretaker, **Brother Halven Sere**, has been captured, the outer vaults have been looted, and several sacred relic-weapons once belonging to the Knights of the Azure Oath have vanished into Temple networks and black-market channels.

Among caravaners and traders a grim phrase has begun circulating in whispers: **“The earth-tithe.”** Those who refuse to pay it rarely survive the road.

* * *

##### Romag’s Strategic Purpose

Romag maintains this outpost for three primary reasons:

*   **Position:** The priory overlooks travel routes through the Kron Hills and sits close enough to the Long Road to launch rapid ambushes against merchant caravans.
*   **Stone:** The monastery’s thick walls, crypt vaults, and underground passages provide natural prison cells, storage chambers, and defensible terrain.
*   **Relics:** The tomb complex contains sacred weapons once used by the Knights of Heironeous—artifacts tied to the ancient oath known as the **Lightning Kneel**.

* * *

##### Varnor Earthcaller’s Secret Purpose

While Romag values the priory as a strategic outpost, **Varnor Earthcaller believes the site holds a far greater significance**.

Deep beneath the monastery crypts lies an **ancient Flan geomantic altar tied to the Elder Elemental God**. Varnor believes this altar marks a convergence of subterranean power—a place where the earth itself remembers forgotten rites. His excavation beneath the priory seeks to uncover:

• the **Flan altar of the Elder Elemental God  
**• geomantic stone glyphs hidden beneath the crypt floors  
• a buried chasm whispered of in ancient lore as **the Black Mouth**

Varnor believes the Black Mouth is a place where the voice of the Elder Elemental God may be heard through the living stone of Oerth.

If he succeeds in awakening the altar, the monastery will become far more than a raiding post. It will become a **holy site of the Black Earth cult**, capable of strengthening Romag’s power across the Kron Hills and the Viscounty of Verbobonc.

#### DM Toolkit

##### 1) The Relics that Remember (theme lever)

The Priory’s relic-weapons are not “loot.” They are **judgment**.

**In desecrator hands (Earth Reavers):**

*   steel pits and dulls overnight
*   hilts blister like nettle-burn
*   sleep brings oath-dreams and choking earth

**In cleansing hands (PCs who keep an oath):**

*   inscriptions clarify
*   blades ring faintly near injustice
*   once per day, a bearer may call **Vow Surge**: advantage on one roll tied to protecting the innocent, resisting fear, or standing fast

**Breaking the oath knowingly:** the relic becomes _Quiet_ until atonement in the Oath-Hall.

##### 2) The Oath of the Azure Ring (to earn Halven’s trust)

Halven Sere will not aid tomb-robbers—even heroic ones—unless they swear:

*   No relic drawn for greed
*   No tomb broken for sport
*   No dead name mocked
*   Restore honour where it is stolen

##### 3) Rumors (drop-in hooks)

1.  “A lamp burns on the hill o’ holy nights.” (True—Halven still keeps the vigil.)
2.  “Caravans pay an ‘earth-tithe’ now.” (True—Romag’s men.)
3.  “A dozen helms hang in a ring, and they turn by themselves.” (Half-true—an old alarm rite.)
4.  “The dead of the Lightning Kneel have begun to _count_ intruders.” (True—if you want haunting pressure.)
5.  “A sword taken from the vault screams when drawn.” (True—once, then only in dreams.)
6.  “The priory was abandoned because the Church feared what lay beneath.” (False… unless you want a deeper layer.)

#### Stealth & Infiltration Angles

_The Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath_

The Azure Oath is not a dungeon the party simply “enters.” It is a military outpost layered over sacred ruin. The ascent, ridge, gate, courtyard, and outbuildings form a graduated defensive shell. Earth Temple forces are disciplined, not reckless. They rely on sightlines, elevation, and intimidation.

Use this table when the party attempts to:

*   Approach unseen
*   Scale the walls
*   Slip past guards
*   Infiltrate the stables or wagon barn
*   Exploit the herb garden
*   Enter through elevation rather than frontal assault

This section assumes typical activity levels (not full alert).

If alarm is raised, increase DCs by +2 to +5 as appropriate.

##### What the Priory left behind

Most chambers of the monastery have **12-foot-high ceilings** supported by heavy stone arches.

Subterranean areas beneath the monastery (crypts, tunnels, and storage chambers) have **8-foot ceilings**.

###### Doors

**Interior Doors**

• Wooden monastery doors  
• Locked doors require **DC 10 Strength check** to force open

**Exterior Doors**

• Reinforced oak with iron bands  
• Locked exterior doors require **DC 15 Strength check** to force open

###### Locks

A typical monastery lock requires:

**DC 15 Dexterity check with thieves’ tools** to open.

###### Walls

The monastery walls rise roughly **25 feet above ground level**.

Due to weathering and collapsed stonework:

• sections contain **crumbling masonry  
**• climbing the walls requires only a **moderate Athletics check**

###### Windows

The monastery windows are **tall and narrow**, similar to arrow slits.

• Window sills are **10 feet above ground level outside  
**• Windows provide light but are difficult to climb through

##### Deception: Joining the Cult

• claiming to seek **initiation into the Black Earth cult  
**• invoking the names **Tharvok Stonehand, Maris, or Caidax Nightshade  
**• presenting something valuable to the cult

If convinced, the guards escort the party inside to **Tharvok Stonehand** for judgment.
