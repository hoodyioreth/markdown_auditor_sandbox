The Celestial Circle - Veluna
=============================

The Celestial Circle is not merely a council, nor solely a priesthood, but the quiet engine of Veluna’s will—where faith, law, and policy are wrought into one. Within its chamber, the Archclericy’s divine mandate is interpreted, refined, and set upon the world as measured influence rather than decree. Here, no crown is worn, yet kingdoms are shaped.

### Overview

The Celestial Circle stands at the heart of Veluna’s theocratic structure, serving as the body through which the Church—chiefly that of Rao—guides the realm’s temporal course. It is not a ruling council in the common sense, but a chamber of accord where doctrine becomes policy and moral authority becomes political direction.

 

Its reach extendeth beyond Veluna’s borders. Through envoys, ambassadors, and quiet influence, the Circle shapes relations with neighboring powers—none more delicately than the Viscounty of Verbobonc, whose independence is both tolerated and carefully managed.

### Structure of the Circle

##### The High Prelate

First among equals, the High Prelate convenes the Circle, frames its deliberations, and gives voice to its conclusions. Though he issueth no direct commands, his guidance setteth the path by which all others must walk.

##### The Twelve Seats of Accord

Twelve permanent seats form the Circle, each held by a senior cleric or appointed authority representing the dominant faiths and interests of Veluna.

*   Church of Rao (dominant authority)
*   Church of St. Cuthbert (law and enforcement)
*   Church of Wee Jas (law, contracts, and doctrine)
*   Church of Ehlonna (land, fertility, and rural welfare)

\[\*\]Secular delegates tied to trade and noble interests  

Each seat carrieth equal voice in form, though not in weight. Influence is determined by reputation, alliances, and necessity rather than title alone.

##### Observers and Ambassadors

Non-voting participants—such as Elinor Asbury—are granted presence but not authority. They may speak, advise, and negotiate, yet they do not shape final accord unless they sway those who do.

##### Clerks of Record

Silent and ever-present, these scribes preserve doctrine, precedent, and decision. Nothing spoken within the Circle is forgotten.

### Function and Authority

The Circle doth not rule by decree, but by consensus. Its power lies in shaping what is considered righteous, acceptable, and inevitable.

 

*   **Doctrine into Policy** — Translating divine teaching into actionable governance
*   **Diplomatic Influence** — Guiding Veluna’s relations with neighboring realms
*   **Moral Sanction** — Endorsing or condemning rulers and policies
*   **Trade Oversight** — Ensuring stability of vital economic flows

\[\*\]**Crisis Arbitration** — Mediating disputes where faith and law collide

No edict leaveth the chamber bearing the name of the Circle. Yet all who matter know whence it came.

### Relationship to Veluna

Within Veluna, the Circle acts as the unseen balance between noble authority and priestly command. The Archclericy itself is governed through dioceses and bishops, yet it is the Circle that harmonizes their will into unified direction.

 

It ensures that Veluna remaineth not fractured by competing doctrines nor weakened by noble ambition. In this, it is both stabilizer and subtle master.

### Relationship to Verbobonc

The Viscounty of Verbobonc exists in delicate equilibrium—nominally independent, yet watched closely by Veluna and Furyondy alike.

 

Through the Celestial Circle, Veluna exerteth influence without dominion. It doth not command Verbobonc—but it guideth its choices, shapes its trade, and observes its rulers.

*   Maintains diplomatic pressure through religious legitimacy
*   Influences trade agreements and grain supply
*   Advises on matters affecting regional stability
*   Seeks to prevent instability without provoking annexation

Thus Verbobonc remaineth free—but never unwatched.

### Factions Within the Circle

##### The Concord of Rao

Advocates of peace, stability, and long-term balance. They favor measured diplomacy and the preservation of existing order.

##### The Iron Doctrine (St. Cuthbert)

Champions of law and strength. They seek firmer control, stricter enforcement, and clearer authority over uncertain regions.

##### The Veiled Ledger (Wee Jas)

Guardians of contract and precedent. They favor binding agreements and legal frameworks that ensure compliance.

##### The Verdant Voice (Ehlonna)

Protectors of land and rural prosperity. They support agrarian stability and resist exploitation of the countryside.

##### The Golden Exchange

Secular and mercantile interests seeking control over trade routes, tariffs, and economic leverage.

### Current Political Tensions

##### The Grain Question

Veluna’s dependence upon external grain sources—most notably from Asbury lands—hath made trade a matter of doctrine as well as necessity. Attempts to secure exclusive supply agreements have met resistance, most notably from Elinor Asbury herself.

##### Verbobonc’s Independence

Though independent, the Viscounty remains under quiet pressure from both Veluna and Furyondy. The Circle debates whether to strengthen ties… or tighten them.

##### The Gnomish Dispute

Conflicts in the Kron Hills—legal seizures, banditry, and withdrawal of protection—have destabilized trade and trust.

 

The Circle remains divided on whether to intervene directly or allow the matter to resolve through secular authority.

### The Role of Elinor Asbury

As Ambassador of the Celestial Circle, Elinor standeth as the living bridge between Verbobonc and Veluna.

*   Represents the Viscount in matters of diplomacy
*   Advises the Circle on Verbobonc’s political realities
*   Protects the Viscounty’s independence through negotiation
*   Navigates tensions between noble authority and divine influence

 

She possesseth no vote—yet her words may sway those who do. Her presence alone marketh the importance of Verbobonc in the eyes of Veluna.

#### Political Tensions & Hidden Currents

The Circle is no sanctuary. It is a crucible.

 

As thou standest beside Elinor, thou hearest not shouting—but silence sharpened into blades.

##### The Grain Question

Veluna’s bread demandeth certainty.

 

House Asbury’s lands are vital to regional supply, and thus:

*   Veluna seeketh binding trade guarantees
*   Factor-Lord Belis pushest for exclusive contracts
*   The Circle debates whether faith may compel supply

Elinor resisteth.

 

_“My people are not tithe to be measured and taken,”_ she saith, low but firm.

##### The Independence of Verbobonc

Verbobonc standeth in a precarious balance:

*   Veluna and Furyondy both watch with interest
*   Annexation is never spoken… yet ever implied

Within the Circle:

*   Some favor closer integration (peacefully… for now)
*   Others prefer Verbobonc remain a buffer state

 

Elinor’s role is to ensure the latter—without ever naming the threat aloud.

##### The Gnomish Question

Tensions rise between Verbobonc’s lords and the gnomes of the Kron Hills:

*   Legal seizures of mines
*   Withdrawal of military protection
*   Rising banditry

The Circle is divided:

*   St. Cuthbert’s faction demandeth order
*   Rao’s clergy counsel patience
*   Merchants seek advantage in instability

 

Elinor alone presseth for fairness—knowing full well it may cost her influence.

##### The Chamber of Thirteen

Though not of Veluna, the Chamber of Thirteen in Verbobonc shadoweth all decisions:

*   Lifelong judges reviewing policy and law
*   Their rulings often align with the Viscount

Rumor whispereth that the Circle seeketh indirect sway over them—through doctrine, precedent, or… quiet persuasion.

##### Factions Within the Circle

The Circle is not united. It is divided into quiet alliances:

*   **The Concord of Rao** — Stability, diplomacy, preservation of balance
*   **The Iron Doctrine (St. Cuthbert)** — Order through strength, suspicion of autonomy
*   **The Veiled Ledger (Wee Jas)** — Law, contracts, and binding obligations
*   **The Verdant Voice (Ehlonna)** — Rural welfare, land stewardship, long peace
*   **The Golden Exchange** — Trade dominance, profit, and control of routes

Elinor walketh betwixt them all—belonging to none.

##### Enemies & Dangers

Not all threats are named openly.

##### Sir Simon Milinous

Once beloved… now changed.

 

A figure of influence among the Viscount’s circle, his loyalties are uncertain. His past connection to Elinor is known—and exploitable.

##### Lord Mayor Marakios Willem Haxx

A rising internal rival.

*   Advocates more democratic governance
*   Gains popularity within the city
*   Seeks to weaken noble and external influence alike

He views Elinor as an agent of outside power.

##### The Quiet Hand (Rumored)

Whispers tell of a faction within the Circle itself:

*   Not aligned to any god openly
*   Seeks to unify temporal and religious power
*   Prefers subtle manipulation over decree

Whether this be truth or priestly paranoia… none can say.
