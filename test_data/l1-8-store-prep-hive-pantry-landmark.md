L1-8. Store-Prep 'Hive Pantry'
==============================

Bare stone walls, a long scarred table, and dead racks tell a story of use without care. Dusty weapon racks line the sides, empty save for spider silk. Two haunches of something heavy slump on the table, fat turning gray at the edges. In one corner sits a mound of knotted, pallid roots. The air carries sour tallow, damp linen, and the cold breath of stone.

#### Suel function & motif (ancient use)

*   **Original role:** temple **storage & staging** for offerings and supplies; converted to **prep** in later centuries.
*   **Motifs to show:**
*   **Bralm (industry/hive):** faint **hex-band** borders cut into shelf uprights; tally notches on the table edge.
*   **Wee Jas (order/ledger):** a tiny **ruby-line glyph** burned into every **sixth shelf post**—an inventory cadence.
*   **Checks:** DC **12 Religion/History** reads the [Bralm](bralm-person.md)/Wee Jas marks; success gives **advantage** on one check in this room (Investigation, Medicine, or Nature) by “reading the system.”

#### Features & checks

*   **Dead racks (east & west walls).** Dust and neglect; DC **11 Investigation** finds **old iron pegs** sized for polearms and broad-head tools rather than swords, matching a work-room rather than an armory.
*   **Scarred table (center).** Grease-black cuts; DC **12 Survival** shows **fresh butchery** within a day; DC **13 Medicine** notes **spoilage** (fat sickly-sweet, mottling).
*   **Haunches (unwholesome).** If eaten without proper cooking, a creature must succeed on a **DC 12 Con save** or suffer **poisoned** until a short rest (or receive _lesser restoration_). Properly boiled/roasted for 1 hour removes the risk.
*   **Root pile (unknown tubers).** DC **14 Nature**: under-deep **sump-roots**—**edible only after triple-boil**; otherwise as above (**DC 12 Con** vs. poisoned, advantage if diluted in stew).
*   **Drag marks across the floor.** A heavy **rectangular object** was **dragged from north wall to south** recently. DC **12 Investigation** identifies the path and embedded grit (coarse crate bottom). DC **13 Survival** tracks it through the **kitchen door**—likely looted stores or a butcher’s chest. There’s **no hidden hatch** here; the marks are exactly what they look like.

#### Occupation (Howling Moon)

*   Usually **unoccupied** except at mealtimes (two orcs on kitchen duty pass through every few hours). On **noise**, a pair of orc cooks from the kitchen arrive in **1–2 rounds**; if the horn is up, add **2 more** in **2–4 rounds**.

#### Optional vermin complication

*   Disturbing the haunches may loose a **Swarm of Insects** (beetles) from beneath the table (50% chance). They scatter if shown bright flame or smoked with a torch for 1 round.

#### Loot

*   Practical gear only: two **butcher’s knives** (as daggers), a **splitting cleaver** (counts as handaxe), a **roll of rough linen** (5 sp if cleaned). No coin left here.

##### Read-aloud (short)

Empty racks; a grease-scarred table; gray meat and a mound of knotted roots. Faint hex bands and tiny tally cuts mark the wood and stone. Something heavy has scraped a path across the floor from one wall to the other and on through the kitchen door.

#### Map notes

*   Place **racks** along the long walls; **table** roughly centered; **root pile** in a corner near the kitchen door.
*   Keep a **clear 5-ft aisle** around the table for combat movement.
*   If you’re using the Bralm/[Wee Jas](wee-jas-person.md) motif, etch a **hex frieze** waist-high and add a **small ledger rune** every sixth shelf post.

#### Running tips

*   Reward players who “read” the motifs: let a successful Religion/History call out the **triple-boil** trick or the risk from spoiled meat.
*   Use the drag marks to **point traffic** into the kitchen and to sell the Howling Moon’s routine here.
*   If you need tension without a fight, let the cooks **nearly** step in—boots in the hall, a muttered complaint—giving the party a stealth beat and a choice.
