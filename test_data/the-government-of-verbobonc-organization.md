The Government of Verbobonc
===========================

#### A Practical, Layered System for a Feudal Capital

_Issued for the instruction of sworn servants, licensed agents, and those who would act lawfully within the Viscounty of Verbobonc._

The government of Verbobonc is not a single court, council, or chamber, but a **layered system of authority**, carefully divided between **fortress power**, **civic law**, and **public governance**. This separation is deliberate. It preserves noble authority, prevents mob justice, and allows the Viscount to rule a city that is wealthy, fractious, and politically active without surrendering control.

For Dungeon Masters, this structure answers a critical question quickly:  
**"Where does the party go when they want something done?"**

For a full account of how these bodies interact under political pressure — and how the Temple of Elemental Evil exploits every fault line between them — see [Verbobonc Politics in 576 CY](verbobonc-politics-in-576-cy-article.md).

 

##### 1\. The Viscount's Rule (Executive)

**Seat:** [V1-3 Castle Greyfist, Citadel of the Viscount](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md)  
**Power Type:** Absolute executive, military, and diplomatic authority

Castle Greyfist is the **true seat of power** in the Viscounty of Verbobonc. It is not merely a residence, but a fortified instrument of rule. All authority ultimately flows from [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md), Viscount of Verbobonc, whose will is enforced by oath, arms, and decree. He commands the militia, directs diplomacy, and carries the Right of High Justice. In the shadow of the Temple's Second Rising, Greyfist is becoming increasingly sealed, selective, and dangerous to approach uninvited.

**What Happens at Greyfist**

*   Issuance of **Viscounty decrees**
*   **Military command**, musters, and border defense
*   **Diplomatic receptions** and foreign envoys
*   Appointments of high offices, wardens, and magistrates
*   Emergency judgments where the Viscount intervenes directly

**DM shorthand:** "_If it touches war, borders, titles, or the Viscount's will — Greyfist."_

##### 2\. The Council of Lords (Aristocratic Legislature + High Council)

**Seat:** [V1-3 Castle Greyfist, Citadel of the Viscount](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md) (primary)  
**Power Type:** Advisory, feudal coordination, noble arbitration

The [Council of Lords of Verbobonc](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) is not a public senate. It is a **feudal council**, composed of the Viscount's great lords, banner-holders, and landed peers. They advise, pressure, bargain, and posture — but always **under the Viscount's roof**. The Council holds at Greyfist. This is not symbolic.

*   Lords rule **by grant**, not by right
*   Their power exists **within** the Viscount's domain
*   Disloyalty is remembered — and punishable

 

##### What the Council Actually Does

*   Advises on taxation, levies, and military obligations
*   Coordinates feudal responsibilities between houses
*   Resolves inter-lord disputes **when the Viscount commands it**
*   Acts as a political pressure chamber, not a court of law

Lords may appear in public ceremonies at City Hall or the Civic Centre, but **decisions are made at Greyfist**.

 

##### The Council's Internal Fault Lines

The Council of 576 CY is not a unified body. It is a battlefield of competing interests, and the Temple's resurgence has sharpened every existing division. Four factions define its internal politics:

*   **Militarists** — [Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md) and [Claviger Velysin](claviger-velysin-person.md) advocate aggressive military response and expanded border authority. They want the hammer brought down on the Temple now, at full force.
*   **Economic Powers** — [Roland Galans](roland-galans-person.md), [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md), and Baron [Drenton Vaswell](drenton-vaswell-person.md) prioritize trade route stability and resist any military action that disrupts commerce or raises their tax burden.
*   **Manipulators** — Lady [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md) operates through hidden agendas, secret alliances, and carefully timed betrayals. Her true position on the Temple is unknown, which is precisely the point.
*   **Cultural Moderates** — Lord [Wendell Rhynehurst](wendell-rhynehurst-person.md) counsels patience and negotiation. He is the faction most likely to seek diplomatic solutions — and most likely to be outmaneuvered.

**DM shorthand:** "_Lords can talk anywhere. Lords decide at Greyfist."_

##### 3\. The Chamber of 13 & Advocates (Judicial + Civic Law Machine)

**Seat:** [C2 City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md)  
**Power Type:** Judicial authority, civic law, administration

City Hall is the **beating heart of law** in Verbobonc. While the Viscount rules the realm, the **Chamber of Thirteen** governs the city's legal reality day to day.

 

##### What Happens at City Hall

*   Criminal and civil courts
*   Issuance of writs, summons, indictments, and warrants
*   Registration of contracts, charters, guild privileges
*   Licensing, fines, and civic penalties
*   Oversight of city enforcement and clerks

Advocates — many trained or sanctioned by the Church of Pholtus — ensure procedure is followed precisely. Justice here is not swift, but it is **relentless**.

**DM shorthand:** "_If it's law, it's City Hall."_

* * *

 

##### 4\. C16 The Gathering Hall (Civic Forum + Committees + Public Business) — **NOT where the Lords hold court**

**Seat:** [C16 The Gathering Hall of Verbobonc](c16-the-gathering-hall-of-verbobonc-landmark.md)  
**Power Type:** Public governance, committees, controlled access politics

The Gathering Hall is **not** where the Council of Lords holds court. Instead, it is where the city is allowed to be _heard_ — without being allowed to _rule_.

This distinction is crucial. The Gathering Hall exists to manage political pressure, not to exercise political authority.

*   Public hearings and formal petitions
*   Guild disputes and merchant grievances
*   Delegations from temples, guilds, gnomes, and wards
*   Subcommittees of the Chamber of Thirteen
*   Controlled civic debate, carefully observed

This is where the powerful **perform listening**, where politics is visible, and where pressure is applied indirectly.

**DM shorthand:** "_If it's politics the city is allowed to watch, it's the Gathering Hall."_

#### How It Plays at the Table (Quick, Usable Routing)

When the party wants…

*   **Justice / a warrant / an indictment / to clear their name** → [C2 City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md)
*   **A public hearing that stirs factions** (guilds, temples, merchants, gnomes) → [C16 The Gathering Hall of Verbobonc](c16-the-gathering-hall-of-verbobonc-landmark.md)
*   **A lord's patronage / noble arbitration / Viscount's judgment / military action** → [V1-3 Castle Greyfist, Citadel of the Viscount](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md) (via appointment, summons, or the right introduction)
*   **To understand the full political landscape driving all of it** → [Verbobonc Politics in 576 CY](verbobonc-politics-in-576-cy-article.md)

##### Why This Matters for Temple of Elemental Evil

As the Second Rising unfolds, the four layers of Verbobonc's government do not hold together — they fracture along exactly the lines the Temple was designed to exploit:

*   **City Hall** becomes clogged with accusations, writs, and fear — law weaponized by competing interests
*   **The Gathering Hall** fills with merchants, temples, gnomes, and guilds demanding action the Viscount cannot or will not take
*   **The Council of Lords** fractures: the **Militarists** demand war, the **Economic Powers** resist it, the **Manipulators** exploit the confusion, and the **Cultural Moderates** are drowned out
*   **Greyfist** becomes increasingly sealed — [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) is still in control, but his circle narrows, his decisions slow, and access to him becomes the most valuable commodity in the Viscounty

Player characters can:

*   Exploit jurisdictional gaps between the four bodies
*   Carry writs that grant terrifying authority
*   Be blocked by procedure — or weaponize it
*   Choose whether to act _within_ the law or _above_ it
*   Become the thing that holds the government together — or the spark that breaks it open

This is not just governance.  
It is **gameplay infrastructure**.
