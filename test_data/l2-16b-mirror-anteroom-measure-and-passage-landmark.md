L2-16b. Mirror Anteroom — Measure & Passage
===========================================

A compact chamber of black ashlar and bronze trim opens off the hall. In the center, a 5-foot round sigil of the Ruby Skull-Star is set into the floor. A square stone coffer stands along the north wall; to the south, neat racks of stacked bones wait for rites. The east wall holds a low altar-table with twin bronze candle-holders and a lidded incense vessel, all square and true. In the north-west corner, an elaborate Suel mirror—full-length, framed in geometric bronze with tiny ruby chips—leans in dignified silence. At the center of the north wall, a finely carved mural of Lendor—bars of the Six-Measure and a calm patriarch’s profile—looks merely decorative… until breath fogs the stone and faint ruby seams glow.

### The Ruby Anterite

(_quick boon; player-skill, no checks_)

Perform as in 16a (identical rite):

1.  Place/Light candles **left → right** while **counting to six**.
2.  On **six**, waft the incense over the **floor sigil**; set the vessel between the candles.
3.  **Ring the bell once**.

**Success:** Up to **two creatures** gain **Ruby Composure** for **1 hour** (advantage on the next **Wis** save vs. fear/charm or undead aura). The first **Attention** earned here or in the adjoining hall is **ignored**.  
**Fumble:** Candle sputters; **+1 Attention**. Retry after **1 minute**.

* * *

#### Secret door — **Mural of Lendor**

_**(“Measure & Passage”)**_

**Clue path (no rolls required):**

*   **Fog the mirror** with breath or incense: its ghost-script reads _“Set your face; keep the count.”_
*   Stand upon the **floor sigil** facing the **mirror** (set your face), then perform a simple six-count touching sequence at the mural.

**Opening steps (simple, thematic):**

1.  One creature stands centered on the **floor sigil** facing the **mirror**.
2.  A second creature places a palm on the mural’s **Six-Measure bars**, tapping **each bar once**, **top → bottom**, in a **steady six-count** (one, two… six).
3.  On the **sixth beat**, touch the tiny **ruby pip** at the mural’s lower right corner.  
    **Result:** The **mural panel clicks and swings inward**, revealing a **5-ft-wide, 10-ft passage**.

**Fail-forward (soft):** Wrong order or off-count: the bars give a thin **buzz**, the door **locks for 1 minute**, and a **hair-thin Stone Bite** nicks the tapper (DEX 13, **1d4 slashing**, **+1 Attention**). Try again after the minute.

**From the far side:** The short passage ends at the **backside of a second secret door** with a simple **six-stud push plate** (opens outward; destination keyed in a later room).

* * *

#### Hazards & pressure

*   **Noise in here** (bone scatter, forcing panels) adds **+1 Attention**.
*   **Attention ≥ 2:** A **Ruby Ghoul** with **1 Ruby Zombie** investigates from the Great Hall within **1d4 rounds**.

* * *

#### Treasure (light, thematic)

*   **Incense sticks (6)**; **ruby chalk (1 use)**; **brace-nails (6)**; **prayer bell** (5 gp as art).
*   Optional: a **Lendor count-cord** (braided red/white cord with six knots; once/day, grant **+1** to an ally’s next ability/tool check after you speak a six-count).

### Boxed outcomes

*   **Boon rite succeeds:** Candlelight steadies; incense curls over the ruby disc. The bell’s single tone hangs like held breath, and the room settles around you.
*   **Mural opens:** Tap… tap… tap… tap… tap… tap—on the last touch the ruby flickers and stone unlatches. The mural eases inward, revealing a narrow, dust-sweet passage.
*   **Off-count fumble:** The bars hum with disapproval; a hairline seam snaps at your fingertip, sharp as a paper cut, and the panel goes still.

##### At-a-glance (DM quick)

*   **Where:** East wall of **L2-16. Great Crypt Hall of the Six**, the **second door** (≈ two-thirds up the hall).
*   **Size/shape:** **15′ × 15′**; intact (no collapse).
*   **Function:** Second **processional prep cell**. Adds a **Suel full-length mirror** (NW corner) and a **hidden mural-door** centered on the **north wall** that leads to a short **service slip** (10′, 5′-wide) and the **backside of another secret door** (keyed elsewhere).
*   **Tone:** Composed, ritual, museum-quiet; ruby chips thrum on a six-count.

##### Features

*   **Center floor sigil (5′ disc).** Ruby Skull-Star of **Wee Jas** with ring text in Old Suloise: _“Measure holds; Law witnesses.”_
*   **North coffer (unlocked).** Two sets of **funerary vestments**, a **prayer bell**, **wax tablets** (blank), **ruby chalk** (1 use), **brace-nails (6)**.
*   **South bone stacks.** Cleaned femurs/tibiae in cradle racks for symbolic handling; loud scattering is **+1 Attention**.
*   **East altar-table.** Basalt slab, **two candle-holders**, **incense canister** (sticks within), and six tiny **ruby pips** along the rim.
*   **NW full-length mirror.** Suel geometric frame (bronze & ruby chips). **Breath or incense smoke** reveals ghost-script around the edge: _“Set your face; keep the count.”_
*   **North mural (secret door).** Lendor’s profile and six horizontal bars (the Measure). Hairline ruby seams encircle the panel.

##### Exits

*   **West:** back to **L2-16. Great Crypt Hall of the Six**.
*   **North secret mural:** opens to the **10′ service slip** leading to the **backside of another secret door** (destination TBD).
