17: Maizy’s Pots
================

Maizy’s Pots, located next to Terrik’s Treasures and Coin along Nigb's Run, is a bustling pottery shop renowned for its diverse array of earthenware. Run by the industrious Maizy Potter and her children, this establishment supplies villagers and traders with high-quality pottery and ceramic goods, serving as a vital component of Hommlet's economy.

### Location and Description

Maizy’s Pots is strategically situated next to Pilar’s Cabinetmaker and Terrik’s Treasures and Coin, creating a synergy between craftspeople. The shop's position along Nigb's Run offers easy access for merchants and travelers, enhancing its role as a key supplier in the village.

##### Building Description

The pottery shop is a quaint, rustic building with a thatched roof and clay walls, reflecting the artisanal craft within. Its exterior is adorned with shelves displaying finished wares, inviting passersby to explore the shop's offerings.

##### Exterior

*   **Display Shelves:** Outside, shelves showcase a variety of pots, jugs, and decorative items, giving a glimpse of Maizy’s craftsmanship.
*   **Signage:** A wooden sign hangs above the door, etched with the image of a potter’s wheel, signaling the shop’s purpose.

##### Interior

*   **Workshop:** Inside, the shop is filled with pottery wheels, shelves of drying clay pieces, and kilns for firing the earthenware.
*   **Showroom:** The front of the shop serves as a showroom, displaying a wide range of pottery from functional dishes to decorative art pieces.

### Lore and History

Maizy Potter inherited the shop from her late husband, continuing the family tradition of pottery-making. Under her leadership, the business has flourished, becoming a staple in Hommlet’s economy.

##### Historical Significance

*   **Community Contribution:** Maizy’s Pots is well-regarded for its quality craftsmanship, supplying the village with essential ceramics and decorative pieces.
*   **Cultural Influence:** The shop’s designs often reflect the beliefs and values of the Old Faith and Saint Cuthbert, bridging the family’s traditional and newly embraced faiths.

### Motivation and Relationships

*   **Family Legacy:** Maizy is driven by the desire to uphold her family’s legacy, ensuring the shop’s success for future generations.
*   **Community Support:** She is committed to supporting the village by providing high-quality pottery and engaging in local events and traditions.

##### Relationships

*   **Community Ties:** Maizy maintains strong ties with fellow artisans, particularly Pilar, fostering a supportive network among local craftspeople.
*   **Faith and Tradition:** As recent converts to [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md), the family respects both their new faith and [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md), reflecting a harmonious blend of beliefs.

### Current Events

##### Business Operations

Maizy’s Pots continues to thrive, with steady demand from villagers, traders, and travelers. The shop’s reputation for quality and creativity ensures a loyal customer base.

##### Bandit Concerns

The increasing threat of banditry has prompted Maizy to enhance security measures at her shop, with her children serving as guards to protect their livelihood.

#### Conclusion

Maizy’s Pots stands as a symbol of craftsmanship and resilience in Hommlet, offering finely crafted pottery that serves both functional and aesthetic purposes. Maizy Potter’s dedication to her family and community, along with her embrace of both old and new faiths, makes her shop a vital part of the village’s cultural and economic landscape. As bandit threats loom, Maizy and her family continue to support their community through their craft and vigilance, contributing to Hommlet’s strength and prosperity.
