Marakios Willem Haxx
====================

### Lord Mayor Haxx

T

he Lord Mayor of Verbobonc cuts an impressive figure in the high halls of power: a broad-shouldered man of six feet one inch, blonde hair neatly trimmed above a face that seems almost boyish despite the keen intelligence burning behind his brown eyes. Marakios Willem Haxx carries himself with the composed confidence of someone who has spent decades bending others to his will not through coercion, but through the irresistible logic of commerce and shared interest. When he speaks, even his harshest critics pause to listen. When he moves through the city streets, merchants and common folk alike track his progress with the wariness of prey aware that a apex predator shares their space. Yet perhaps his greatest gift is this: he makes people believe that his ambitions serve a higher cause than mere self-enrichment. Whether that belief is justified is a question that will define the future of Verbobonc in the post-Temple era.

 

Haxx is not the most powerful figure in the Viscounty—the Viscount himself holds that distinction—but he is arguably the most consequential. A formidable merchant lord commanding The Gentle Tradewinds shipping company, a practicing warrior with monastic training in the disciplines of St. Cuthbert, a skilled manipulator of tariffs and guild politics, and the commander of approximately five hundred veteran warriors loyal to him rather than to the Viscounty proper, Marakios Willem Haxx represents the consolidation of wealth, martial power, and institutional control into a single pair of hands. The question that animates the Council of Lords and the shadowed corridors of power is not whether he will act, but when—and whether his "Order and Prosperity" will serve Verbobonc or merely Marakios Willem Haxx.

Born in 530 CY to a lesser noble family of Flan heritage, Haxx was raised as a fighting monk in the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md) before abandoning the contemplative life for the pursuit of commerce and political power. ### CONTENT

 

#### Origins and Early Life

 

Marakios Willem Haxx was born in 530 CY to a minor noble family of Flan heritage with modest holdings and modest prospects. His parents recognized early that their son possessed an unusual combination of intellectual acuity and martial ambition. Rather than raising him for a typical noble career of land-holding and inheritance, they made an unconventional choice: they presented young Marakios to the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md) as a novice fighting monk. This decision was ostensibly spiritual but pragmatically calculated. The Church of St. Cuthbert provided elite martial training, rigorous discipline, and access to the upper echelons of Verbobonc's religious and political hierarchy.

 

In the monastery, Haxx distinguished himself as an exceptional student of the monastic disciplines. He mastered the martial arts of the unarmed fighting monks, studied theology and rhetoric, and developed the leadership qualities that would later define his political career. More importantly, he cultivated relationships with senior clergy and powerful merchants who frequented the Church for counsel and blessing. By his mid-twenties, Haxx had achieved the rank of senior novice—a position of significant responsibility within the monastery.

 

Yet something in the austere environment of monastic life proved insufficient for Haxx's ambitions. At age 26, he departed the Church and renounced his vows, shocking the senior clergy but not violating any formal prohibition. His mentors understood what Marakios himself had come to believe: that his destiny lay not in prayer but in power, not in renunciation but in accumulation. He would carry the teachings of St. Cuthbert—discipline, order, righteousness of purpose—into the secular world as the guiding philosophy of a commercial and political empire.

 

#### The Rise of House Haxx

 

Between 556 CY and 570 CY, Haxx undertook the patient work of building a commercial dynasty. He inherited modest family holdings and, through a combination of shrewd negotiation, strategic marriages, partnership agreements, and the careful cultivation of relationships with established merchant houses, he gradually expanded his influence across the city's trade networks. By 560 CY, he had secured a significant partnership stake in what would become [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md), a merchant vessel company with multiple ships trading along the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md) and coastal routes to the south.

 

Haxx's management of The Gentle Tradewinds demonstrated a talent for extracting maximum value from commercial operations while maintaining just enough propriety to avoid open accusations of corruption. He negotiated favorable terms with the Viscounty for docking privileges, negotiated advantageous tariff structures with the various merchant councils, and ensured that his vessels received preferential routes and cargo assignments. By 565 CY, The Gentle Tradewinds represented perhaps the single most profitable merchant enterprise in Verbobonc. Haxx's personal wealth grew accordingly.

 

Equally important as commercial success was military power. Around 562 CY, Haxx began recruiting and training a private military force drawn primarily from veteran sellswords, retired soldiers from the Viscounty's service, and mercenary companies seeking permanent employ. This force, which would become known as [Haxx's Hardheads](haxx-s-hardheads-organization.md), started as a modest garrison of perhaps one hundred and fifty warriors. By 576 CY, it had grown to approximately five hundred individuals—a formidable military asset that rivaled the Mounted Borderers in size and potentially exceeded them in cohesion and loyalty. Critically, the Hardheads answered to Marakios Haxx personally, not to the Viscount or the Viscounty government.

 

In 570 CY, at age 40, Haxx married [Larethene Haxx](larethene-haxx-person.md), née Vonnia—a woman of minor rank from the lesser nobility of Veluna. The marriage was, by Haxx's own pragmatic calculation, mutually beneficial. Larethene brought a connection to Veluna's court (useful for establishing trade relationships with Greyhawk's northern ally) and the social legitimacy of her minor noble status. She, in turn, married into a rapidly ascending house with substantial wealth and growing political power. By all accounts, the marriage was cordial if not passionate, and Larethene proved a capable partner in managing the social expectations of an increasingly prominent noble household.

 

#### Political Power and the Council of Lords

 

Haxx's appointment as Lord Mayor of Verbobonc represented the culmination of his systematic accumulation of power and the official recognition of his status as Verbobonc's most influential economic authority. As Lord Mayor, he holds the official position of tax collector for the Viscounty—a role that grants him legitimate access to the detailed financial records of virtually every significant commercial transaction in the city. It is a position that, in less skillful hands, might be seen as obviously corrupt. In Haxx's hands, it provides a mask of legitimacy for his financial manipulations and a constant source of intelligence about the economic activities of his rivals and allies.

 

On the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md), Haxx represents the Economic Powers faction—the alliance of merchant lords who believe that trade, mercantile expansion, and commercial prosperity represent the truest path to Verbobonc's security and stability. His principal allies within this faction are **Roland Galans**, a wealthy merchant of Oeridian heritage, and **Drenton Vaswell**, a veteran of the trading wars and a pragmatic manipulator of market forces. Together, these three wield considerable influence over the Council's economic policies and, by extension, over many of the political decisions that flow from economic considerations.

 

Haxx's faction aligns institutionally with the worship and philosophy of Zilchus, god of commerce, though Haxx himself maintains personal devotion to St. Cuthbert. This distinction is important: Haxx's Lawful Neutral alignment and his monastic training make him fundamentally committed to order, discipline, and—crucially—personal moral codes that he believes transcend mere legality. He genuinely believes that his accumulation of power and wealth serves a greater good. He is not a mere mercenary seeking profit. He is a true believer in "Order and Prosperity" as a framework that will, ultimately, benefit the Viscounty as a whole.

 

His greatest rival on the Council is **Claviger Velysin**, the senior military commander and representative of the militarist faction. Velysin advocates for martial law, expanded Mounted Borderer patrols, and a militaristic approach to the challenges facing the Viscounty. Haxx argues, not unreasonably, that such measures are economically ruinous and that trade, not swords, will ultimately restore order and stability. These two powerful figures have not yet come into open conflict, but the tension between their visions for Verbobonc's future is palpable. The Viscount himself has deliberately maintained their equilibrium, using each as a counterweight to the other's ambitions.

 

#### The Savra Problem

 

No discussion of Marakios Willem Haxx can ignore the elephant in the great hall: his daughter, [Savra Haxx](savra-haxx-person.md). Born around 558 CY, Savra was raised in privilege as the daughter of one of Verbobonc's most powerful and ambitious families. However, by her late adolescence, she began to demonstrate tendencies that horrified her father: a fascination with exotic philosophy, a distrust of traditional authority, and a susceptibility to charismatic individuals who promised enlightenment and transcendence.

 

At some point between 574 and 575 CY, Savra fell under the influence of the Feathergale Society—an air-cult organization that later proved to have connections to the Temple of Elemental Evil. She fled Verbobonc to join this group, an act that was both a personal betrayal and a catastrophic embarrassment to her father's political standing. That the Lord Mayor of Verbobonc could not control his own daughter's behavior cast serious doubt on his fitness for command and his ability to project authority over others.

 

Haxx has responded to this crisis with characteristic pragmatism. Rather than publicly disavowing Savra, he has pursued a strategy of damage control and recovery. An arranged marriage has been negotiated between Savra and Ludovic Langmuir, scion of the wealthy and influential Langmuir family and kin of the formidable [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md). The marriage represents a sophisticated political solution: it will integrate Savra back into legitimate noble society, forge a family alliance with one of Verbobonc's most powerful merchant houses, and provide the veneer of legitimacy that Haxx believes will restore his reputation.

 

However, this solution has also created a secondary problem. The marriage arrangement has brought Haxx into closer proximity with Godeleva Langmuir, a manipulator of considerable skill who views all relationships as opportunities for leverage and advantage. Whether Haxx recognizes the full extent of the risk inherent in this alliance remains unclear to observers, though those close to him believe he is not naïve about Godeleva's nature—merely willing to accept a degree of risk as the cost of rehabilitating his family's honor.

 

#### The Temple Crisis and Trade Disruption

 

By 576 CY, the rising activity of the Temple of Elemental Evil and its affiliated cults has emerged as the single greatest threat to Haxx's power base. His entire political and economic system is predicated on the stability of trade routes, the security of merchant vessels traveling the [Velverdyva River](velverdyva-river-location.md), and the consistent flow of commerce through Verbobonc's docks and markets. Caravan raids by cultist mercenaries, the disappearance of merchant vessels, and the increasing dangers to traders traveling the roads have begun to disrupt this system.

 

Even more concerning, rumors suggest that gnome traders—historically a significant source of commerce—are considering redirecting their trade routes to [Dyvers](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) rather than Verbobonc. If this occurs, the loss of revenue would be catastrophic for both The Gentle Tradewinds and for Haxx's personal ability to fund the Hardheads and maintain his faction's influence on the Council of Lords.

 

Compounding this problem is the withdrawal of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) from active patrol and defense of trade routes. This leaves merchant protection to private mercenaries and guild-hired guards—most of whom are loyal to Haxx and his allies. This creates an opportunity: Haxx could position his Hardheads as the guardians of trade and claim credit for any restoration of security. It also creates a problem: if the Mounted Borderers' withdrawal is seen as a consequence of his political pressure (which it partially is), and if the Hardheads prove insufficient to protect the trade routes, the blame will fall on him.

 

Haxx's response to the Temple crisis has been measured but increasingly assertive. He has advocated on the Council of Lords for expanded mercenary contracts and has funded his own military expeditions to investigate disruptions. He has also been remarkably quiet about any knowledge of the Temple's specific location or organization, suggesting that he is gathering intelligence before committing to a major action. Those who know him well suspect that Haxx is waiting to see how the situation develops, positioning himself to emerge as the patron of whatever solution ultimately saves Verbobonc's trade infrastructure.

 

#### Personality and Approach

 

Marakios Willem Haxx is a man of studied courtesies and impeccable manners. He never talks down to anyone in public, never raises his voice, and maintains a calm composure even in situations of significant tension or crisis. His charisma is not the flamboyant variety of a bard or entertainer but rather the subtle magnetism of a man who appears to have achieved mastery over himself and over circumstances. People believe what he says not because he is glib but because he appears to genuinely believe it.

 

Privately, however, Haxx is capable of sharp, cutting criticism and ruthless judgment of those he perceives as incompetent, disloyal, or frivolous. His subordinates at The Gentle Tradewinds and within the Hardheads know him as a demanding but fair employer who expects excellence and does not tolerate failure. He is willing to reward loyalty and competence generously, but betrayal or negligence results in swift and comprehensive punishment.

 

His monastic training remains evident in his approach to decision-making. Haxx takes time to deliberate, consults advisors and intelligence sources, and commits to courses of action only after he believes all relevant factors have been considered. This can make him appear cautious to the point of indecision to those expecting faster action. However, once committed to a course, he prosecutes it with single-minded determination and does not easily second-guess himself.

 

His faith in St. Cuthbert is genuine and serves as a moral framework for his decision-making. He genuinely believes in the virtues of order, discipline, and righteousness—though his definition of "righteousness" is pragmatic enough to encompass tariff manipulation, guild monopolization, and the maintenance of private military forces. He would likely argue that such measures are righteous insofar as they serve the greater order and stability of Verbobonc, even if they involve moral compromises by more rigid standards.

### DM's Strategic Assessment: Why Marakios Matters to Your Campaign

 

##### The Political Fulcrum

 

Marakios Willem Haxx is perhaps the single most important NPC for managing the political layer of a Temple of Elemental Evil campaign. Unlike other NPCs who represent single factions or organizations, Haxx simultaneously represents the economic power of Verbobonc, the voice of institutional pragmatism, and the moral framework of St. Cuthbert-influenced rule. This makes him uniquely positioned to either accelerate or impede the party's ability to address the Temple crisis through political means.

 

The key insight for running Haxx effectively is understanding that he is not the villain of the campaign. He is not corrupt in a simple, obvious sense. He is a legitimate power-holder with reasonable concerns about trade security and institutional stability. However, his methods—tariff manipulation, private military forces, selective application of law—create the black market opportunities and institutional resentments that make the Temple's agents so effective at manipulating Verbobonc's political landscape.

 

A well-run Haxx can force your party to confront the moral complexity inherent in the campaign's political layer. They cannot simply defeat Haxx or expose him as corrupt, because he is genuinely trying to maintain order and is not obviously acting against the public good. Instead, they must either work with him, manipulate him, or find ways to redirect his ambitions toward more directly confronting the Temple threat.

 

##### Mechanical Leverage Points

*   **Trade Route Intelligence:** Haxx has extensive contacts throughout Verbobonc's merchant community. Any significant disruption to trade will generate reports that flow to him. Use this to have Haxx provide intelligence leads that point the party toward larger threats—not out of pure generosity, but because trade restoration serves his interests. This gives the party actionable information while maintaining the fiction that Haxx is acting in character rather than as a quest-dispensing NPC.
    

*   **Mercenary Contracting:** The party can negotiate directly with Haxx to fund specific military expeditions in exchange for intelligence or action against threats to trade. This creates a business-like relationship that feels more authentic than outright charity and makes the party feel like they are genuinely solving problems in an economically coherent world.
    

*   **Savra Leverage:** The party may discover knowledge of Savra's location, her status, or her involvement with elemental cults. Haxx will be intensely motivated to recover or rehabilitate his daughter, creating opportunities for negotiation, manipulation, or moral complexity depending on what the party learns about her actual involvement with the Temple forces.
    

*   **The Hardheads as Opposition:** If the party comes into conflict with Haxx's interests, the Hardheads represent a credible military threat. However, they are not mindless soldiers—they answer to Haxx, and a sufficiently convincing argument that the party serves Verbobonc's (and thus Haxx's) interests can redirect them. This creates opportunities for negotiation and political maneuvering rather than simple combat.
    

 

##### Suggested Encounter Approaches:

*   **The Formal Audience:** Arrange a meeting in Haxx's office at [City Hall](c2-city-hall-landmark.md) or in his private solar at [Haxx Manor](a5-haxx-manor-landmark.md). Haxx will be courteous, attentive, and genuinely interested in the party's perspectives. However, he will also be evaluating them as potential assets or liabilities. If the party impresses him, they can gain substantial access and resources. If they fail to impress, they will be treated with cool professional courtesy but no real support.
    

*   **The Warehouse Negotiation:** Have the party encounter Haxx conducting business at the [Waterfront Warehouses](w6-waterfront-warehouses-landmark.md). This allows them to see Haxx in his commercial element—negotiating with merchants, reviewing cargo, managing logistics. A conversation in this setting will be more candid and reveal Haxx's genuine concerns about trade disruption in a way that formal political meetings might not.
    

*   **The Hardheads Patrol:** The party may encounter Haxx's private militia conducting patrol or investigation of trade route threats. These soldiers are professional and disciplined but will report to their superiors about any party interaction. How the party conducts themselves in this encounter will shape Haxx's initial impression before any formal meeting.
    

*   **The Family Crisis:** If the party learns significant information about Savra Haxx, they can approach Haxx privately with this intelligence. This creates a moment where Haxx's political persona may crack, revealing genuine parental concern and creating an emotional hook for future relationships. A Haxx who feels that the party helped save his daughter's honor is a Haxx who will offer genuine support and alliance.
    

 

##### Tone and Voice for Roleplay:

 

Haxx speaks with the measured cadence of someone accustomed to being listened to. He does not interrupt and is comfortable with silences. He often responds to questions with follow-up questions rather than immediate statements, seeking to understand the questioner's motivations and perspectives. When making a point, he builds it methodically, like constructing a legal argument. He smiles rarely but genuinely, and when he does, it reaches his eyes. His hands gesture economically—no unnecessary flourishes, every movement purposeful.

 

In conversation, Haxx often references St. Cuthbert's teachings about order and discipline, but he does so without sanctimony or preaching. It is simply his framework for understanding the world. He believes deeply that civilization depends on law, commerce, and shared commitment to stability—and he believes equally deeply that his role is to ensure these things exist. Whether other people agree with his methods is, from his perspective, somewhat beside the point.

 

##### Why He Cannot Be Ignored

 

A common temptation in running sandbox campaigns is to treat powerful NPCs as background color—people who exist in the world but don't really affect the party's story. Haxx must not be treated this way. His control of trade, his command of the Hardheads, his position on the Council, and his access to intelligence make him someone whose actions will tangibly affect what the party can accomplish.

 

If the party ignores Haxx or treats him with contempt, the consequences should be concrete. Cargo they were expecting may be delayed due to "security concerns." The Hardheads may become less cooperative with party plans. Information that Haxx possesses—critical information the party needs—may become unavailable. Conversely, a party that respects Haxx and works with him should find that doors open, resources become available, and their efforts against the Temple are substantially aided by Verbobonc's most powerful economic authority.

 

Most importantly, Haxx must be understood as a genuine constituent of power in Verbobonc. The party cannot solve the Temple crisis without dealing with him in some way, whether as an ally, a neutral party, or a complication. His vision of "Order and Prosperity" and the party's vision of defeating an apocalyptic temple may ultimately be compatible—or they may be fundamentally at odds. Either way, the party will know they are in the presence of a genuine player of the game, not a mere functionary.

As of 576 CY, Haxx is preoccupied with several concurrent challenges that will likely define his actions throughout a campaign based on the Temple of Elemental Evil:

*   **Trade Route Disruption:** The recent increase in merchant vessel disappearances and caravan raids has begun to threaten Haxx's economic base. He is conducting his own investigations through the Hardheads and the intelligence networks of The Gentle Tradewinds, seeking to identify the sources of these disruptions and determine whether they represent organized attacks or random banditry.
    

*   **Gnome Trade Concerns:** Haxx is aware of rumors that gnome traders may redirect their commerce to Dyvers. *   **Mounted Borderers Withdrawal:** While Haxx has advocated for the withdrawal of the Mounted Borderers from aggressive patrol (believing such activities stimulate banditry and disrupt commerce), he may be beginning to regret the consequences of this success. The resulting vacuum in security is forcing him to rely on his own Hardheads to maintain order, an expensive proposition that further strains his resources.
    

*   **Savra's Recovery:** Haxx is actively engaged in negotiating the marriage between Savra and Ludovic Langmuir, working through intermediaries to ensure that the arrangement serves his family's honor and political interests. He is also quietly attempting to gather intelligence about Savra's current location and status, likely without Savra's knowledge or consent.
    

*   **Council of Lords Maneuvering:** Haxx continues to advocate for expanded mercenary contracts and commercial support measures while opposing Velysin's militaristic proposals. He is also positioning himself as a potential broker of peace or mediator in various conflicts, seeking to expand his influence as a problem-solver.

### RUNNING HAXX IN YOUR CAMPAIGN

 

**Key Principles**

 

When running Marakios Willem Haxx, keep these principles in mind:

*   **He Is Genuinely Trying to Help:** Haxx is not a hidden villain or a secret ally of the Temple. He genuinely believes that maintaining trade, enforcing order, and preserving institutional stability serve the greater good. His methods may be morally questionable, but his intent is not corrupt.
    

*   **He Plays the Long Game:** Haxx does not make impulsive decisions. He gathers information, consults advisors, and waits for circumstances to clarify. This can make him seem frustratingly cautious, but it also means that once he commits to action, it is backed by careful preparation.
    

*   **He Respects Competence:** If the party demonstrates intelligence, courage, and practical effectiveness, Haxx will respect them. He may not like them personally, but respect translates to cooperation, resource access, and genuine support.
    

*   **He Judges by Results:** Haxx cares less about the party's methods than about their effectiveness. A party that solves problems in pragmatic ways will earn his admiration far more readily than a party that insists on moral purity while failing to accomplish anything concrete.
    

*   **He Has Genuine Limits:** Despite his ambition, Haxx will not openly betray the Viscount or attempt to overthrow the legitimate government. His ambitions are for expanded power and influence within the existing system, not for revolution. This sets boundaries on how far he will go in his maneuvering.
    

 

**Integration with Temple Campaign Themes**

 

The Temple of Elemental Evil fundamentally disrupts the stable order that Haxx has spent his career building. Cultists raid caravans, merchant vessels disappear, and the trade routes that fuel his power become increasingly dangerous. This creates a narrative in which the party, by fighting the Temple, is simultaneously serving Haxx's interests.

 

However, the party might also discover that some of Haxx's trade manipulations—his tariff schemes, his monopolization of certain goods, his control of guild revenues—create the desperation and resentment that make Verbobonc's lower classes vulnerable to cult recruitment. A sophisticated party might realize that fighting the Temple requires not just defeating its military forces but also addressing the economic injustices that the Temple's agents exploit for recruitment.

 

This tension creates space for complex moral negotiation. The party must decide whether to work within Haxx's framework (accepting his methods while fighting the Temple together), to try to reform his methods (a long-term project that distracts from the immediate Temple threat), or to maneuver him toward cooperation with broader reforms (the most subtle approach, requiring significant political skill).
