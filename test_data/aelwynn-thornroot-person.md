Aelwynn Thornroot
=================

> First Encounter:   She is already standing in the grove behind the Church when you arrive — not waiting, exactly, but standing as a tree stands. Auburn braid streaked grey, falling over her shoulder. Cloak the color of bark. A staff of pale wood bound with silver wire and hung with red berries. She does not look at you when you enter. She looks at you when you stop walking. Her eyes are the green of old ferns, and they are quietly delighted.

 

##### Summary

**Sister Aelwynn Thornroot** is a human female druid in her mid-forties, junior to [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) — Archdruid and Guardian of the Western Gnarley. She has served the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) for twenty years and is custodian of the western verge that runs from the [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) east into the deep [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md), bordered to the south by the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Hrudek considers her his most trusted junior and is quietly grooming her as a potential successor for the western verge if she chooses to claim it. She is one of the few druids of the Gnarley granted authority by Hrudek to host outsiders at sacred rites of the Old Faith — a privilege she exercises rarely and with great care.  

##### Motivation & Personality

**Primary Drive:** Steward the western Gnarley as her mother and grandmother did — preserve the rites of the Old Faith intact for the next generation. **Secondary Drive:** Earn Hrudek's full confidence by proving she can act as the verge's custodian without his oversight. **Hard Limit:** She will not bring outsiders to a sacred site again if her trust is betrayed. Her invitations are finite — once a party violates the sanctity of a rite she has shared, she will report the failure to Hrudek and never extend the privilege to another stranger. The forest, she believes, watched her choose them; if her choice was wrong, the cost is hers to bear and never to repeat.  

##### Background

Aelwynn was born in the western Gnarley to a generational druidic family. Her mother, **Vellanys Thornroot**, served the verge before her and died of a winter illness in 568 CY. Her grandmother, **Old Mirenneth**, was a wisewoman of considerable repute among the Old Faith and is buried beneath a marker oak two days' walk from the Cupped Hollow. Aelwynn took her novitiate at twelve, completed her vows at twenty, and has served Hrudek as junior since 556 CY — twenty years and one season, by the reckoning of the spring she now keeps. She has never lived outside the forest. She has never wished to.   She is not a warrior. She is not a politician. She is a _custodian_ — a word she would use about herself before any other.

##### Current Operations (576 CY, Readying)

1.  **Hosting the Dance of Great Moon's Glory** at the Cupped Hollow on Readying 11 — the first time she has been granted authority to host the rite alone. The outcome of **GNQ-01** determines whether Hrudek extends or restricts her authority going forward.
2.  **Tending the warding stones** of the western verge — the four Silver Candle Stones at the Cupped Hollow rim and three others scattered along the Long Road. She walks the stones once each season.
3.  **Watching for Pomarj sign** — humanoid incursions from the south have grown bolder. She reports to [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) and shares low-tier intelligence with the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) when their patrols cross her path.
4.  **Mentoring two novices** — a Hommlet farm-girl named **Brenna** (16) and an orphaned half-elf boy named **Calenwyr** (14). Both live with her at her grove-house. Neither is yet ready for vows.

 

### Adventure Hooks

1.  **The Invitation to Banas-Nimoram (GNQ-01).** On Readying 11, Aelwynn arrives at [Jaroo Ashstaff](jaroo-ashstaff-person.md)'s grove in [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) to seek strangers worthy of witnessing the Dance. If the party accepts her invitation and the Dance survives, she becomes a lifelong contact for druidic intelligence across the western Gnarley. See **GNQ-01: The Dance of Great Moon's Glory**.
2.  **The Stolen Novice.** One of her two novices — **Brenna** or **Calenwyr** — vanishes during a foraging trip near the Long Road. Aelwynn cannot leave the verge to search alone (her concentration on the seasonal warding cannot be broken safely). She asks the party to find the missing novice. The trail leads either to slavers from the Pomarj or to a cult cell operating from the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md).
3.  **The Dying Marker Oak.** Old Mirenneth's grave-marker, an ancient oak two days' walk from the Cupped Hollow, has begun to wither out of season. Aelwynn suspects desecration — a buried Earth Temple token, a corrupted geomantic line, or worse. She asks the party to investigate while she keeps vigil at the grave. Whatever they find connects to the Earth Temple's interest in the verge.

##### GM Notes

**In play:** Aelwynn speaks rarely and listens hard. When she does speak, she does so with the slow weight of someone who has spent decades alone with old trees. She does not waste words. She does not lecture. She rewards quiet attention and forgives ignorance, but she withdraws coldly from arrogance, cruelty, or contempt for the forest. Her warmth, when offered, is deep — but she gives it sparingly.   **Tactical priorities:** In combat, she opens with _Pass Without Trace_ for the party (if not already active), then _Entangle_ on grouped enemies, then falls back on _Shillelagh_ and her quarterstaff. She uses _Call Lightning_ only against creatures that profane the forest itself — orc raiders, lycanthropes, Earth Temple cultists. She will Wild Shape into a wolf only if the party is in serious danger and she needs to close a distance fast.   **Negotiation pressure points:** Three things move her — appeals to the Old Faith, appeals to Hrudek's authority, and concrete evidence that an outsider has acted in the forest's interest before. Three things will not move her — gold, titles, and any invocation of the Cuthbertine or Heironeous churches as moral authority.   **What breaks her:** Betrayal at a sacred site. If the party attends Banas-Nimoram and behaves with reverence, she is theirs for life. If they desecrate the Hollow — through theft, violence, mockery, or recklessness — she will not raise a hand against them, but she will report the failure to Hrudek and her invitation will never be extended again. The cost of her trust is its finite character.   **If she dies:** Hrudek's grief is private but absolute. Her grove passes provisionally to her senior novice **Calenwyr**, who is too young for the full duty. The western verge becomes measurably weaker until Hrudek can install a successor. Any party responsible for her death — by negligence as much as by intent — will find the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) closed to them across all groves for at least a year.

##### On the Forest

_"My grandmother said the forest does not love us. It tolerates us, the way a deep river tolerates a leaf riding its current. I think she was wrong about half of it. The river does not love the leaf, but the leaf loves the river, and the river is made of leaves. Make of that what you will."_

* * *

##### On Strangers

_"I have brought one outsider to a rite in twenty years. He kept his feet quiet. He did not speak. He wept at the right moment and did not weep at the wrong one. I was right about him. He is dead now, of an ordinary winter, and I have not brought another. Until you. Do not make me wrong twice."_

* * *

##### On Hrudek

_"He has been my teacher since I was twenty. He will be my teacher when I am ninety, if either of us lives so long. He does not need me to be perfect. He needs me to be honest. The first is impossible. The second is harder than the first."_

* * *

##### On Her Limit

_"I do not extend my trust twice. Not because I am proud — because the forest watches my choosing, and I will not waste its patience on a second mistake. If you fail me at the Hollow, you will not see me again. You will not need to. I will be a story the western verge tells about the year the strangers were wrong."_
