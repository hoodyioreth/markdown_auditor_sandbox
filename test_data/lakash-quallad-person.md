Lakash Quallad
==============

Lakash Quallad is the false name of Lakash Krivaltis, a Temple of Elemental Evil agent operating inside Verbobonc under Hedrack's direct authority. Posing as a charming Oeridian merchant, he works to fracture the human-gnome alliance, infiltrate the Senate and Chamber of 13, and destabilize the Viscounty from within. His sister, Madame Selentis, runs the Temple's operations in Nulb.

### Temple Agent in a Merchant's Mask

Lakash Quallad is a lie. The man who hosts glittering balls in the High Quarter, sponsors gnome artisans, and speaks of civic duty at every merchant gathering is Lakash Krivaltis — youngest son of the traitor Lord Eldris Krivaltis, whose house was destroyed for supplying the first Temple of Elemental Evil with food, coin, and intelligence. Lakash survived because [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), the Temple's courier and spymaster, saw potential in the boy. Today, Lakash serves [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md), the Temple's Supreme Commander, as one of his most dangerous assets inside Verbobonc's walls.

*   **True Identity:** Lakash Krivaltis, youngest child of the executed Lord Eldris Krivaltis of [House Krivaltis](house-krivaltis-organization.md).
*   **Cover Identity:** "Lakash Quallad," an [Oeridian](oeridian-oerid-ethnicity.md) merchant from Dyvers, relocated to Verbobonc. The surname is his mother's maiden name.
*   **True Allegiance:** Temple of Elemental Evil, reporting to Hedrack through Lucius Graeme.
*   **Mission:** Fracture the human-gnome alliance, infiltrate Verbobonc's political institutions, and destabilize the Viscounty to prepare for the Temple's resurgence.
*   **Sister:** [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md), who operates in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) as "Madame Selentis," Hedrack's political shadow and the Temple's local overseer.
*   **Key Agent:** [Conner Bridgeforth](conner-bridgeforth-person.md), a political operative who lobbies inside [The Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) on Lakash's behalf.
*   **Class:** Wizard 12, Enchanter specialist. His preferred weapons are charm, suggestion, and dominate person — not steel.
*   **Base:** [Lakash Quallad's Manor](a12-lakash-quallad-s-manor-landmark.md) in the High Quarter, which serves as both a luxury residence and a covert command post for Temple operations.

* * *

 

### The Cover — Public Persona

To the people of Verbobonc, Lakash Quallad is everything a successful newcomer should be: generous, cultured, and politically engaged. He sponsors festivals, funds gnome artisan workshops, donates to churches, and hosts the most sought-after social events in the High Quarter. His [manor](a12-lakash-quallad-s-manor-landmark.md) rivals Lady [Kathryn Sarcina](kathryn-sarcina-person.md)'s estate in opulence, and his "Ballroom of Thirteen Mirrors" draws senators, nobles, visiting dignitaries, and gnome clan leaders under the same roof. The cover is extraordinarily effective because Lakash genuinely enjoys the performance — he likes being admired, he likes the game, and he is very, very good at it.

*   **Public Reputation:** Generous businessman, patron of the arts, champion of human-gnome cooperation. Quoted for saying, "In the intricate dance of commerce, one must lead with grace and follow with guile."
*   **Business Front:** Trade partnerships with gnome families from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), giving him access to gnome commercial networks and political figures.
*   **Social Access:** His events bring together nobles, merchants, and gnome leaders in an environment he controls completely. Every conversation, every deal, every argument that happens under his roof feeds intelligence back to the Temple.
*   **Magical Edge:** As a 12th-level Enchanter, Lakash routinely uses subtle magic — charm person, suggestion, detect thoughts — during social gatherings. Guests leave his parties having agreed to things they didn't quite intend, without remembering why.

### History — The Fall of House Krivaltis

[House Krivaltis](house-krivaltis-organization.md) was a minor noble family that held farmlands west of [Mol](mol-settlement.md) in the Viscounty. During the first Temple uprising, Lord Eldris Krivaltis secretly provided the Temple of Elemental Evil with grain, coin, and intelligence from the Verbobonc court. After [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md), Eldris was exposed and executed for treason. The family lands were seized, the name was struck from the noble registers, and the surviving children — Nysera and Lakash — fled under the protection of Sir Torvel Hawkes.

*   **Father's Example:** Lakash was young during the fall, but he watched and learned. While his sister Nysera was horrified by their father's treason, Lakash quietly admired the audacity — and especially admired [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), the Temple courier who visited their estate. He saw cunning and power where Nysera saw shame.
*   **Diverging Paths:** Nysera sought to restore the family honor. Lakash abandoned the Krivaltis name entirely, adopted his mother's maiden name "Quallad," and chose to build something new — on the Temple's terms.
*   **Recruitment:** After the house's fall, Lucius recognized Lakash's intelligence and ruthlessness. He brought the young man into the Temple's network, training him as an informant and political operative. Lakash proved so capable that Hedrack himself approved his long-term placement inside Verbobonc.
*   **Resentment:** Lakash harbors no loyalty to his father's memory. He resented Eldris for getting caught — for being weak — but admired his willingness to reach beyond the constraints of petty nobility. Lakash intends to succeed where his father failed.

### The Temple Connection — Hedrack's Long Game

Lakash does not operate independently. He is a directed asset under the authority of [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md), the Supreme Commander of the Temple of Elemental Evil. His chain of command runs through [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), who recruited him, trained him, and continues to serve as his primary handler and communications channel. Lakash provides intelligence on Verbobonc's political landscape, advances Temple interests through political sabotage, and acts as a financial conduit for Temple resources flowing through the city. This is not freelance ambition — it is a deliberate operation ordered from the Greater Temple.

*   **Handler:** Lucius Graeme. Lakash receives instructions and delivers intelligence through Lucius, who coordinates the Temple's network of agents, couriers, and blackmailers across the region.
*   **Authority:** Hedrack. All major operations — the anti-gnome campaign, the Senate infiltration, the coordination with Nysera — are sanctioned by Hedrack's direct approval.
*   **Intelligence Provided:** Lakash feeds the Temple detailed reports on Verbobonc's political factions, military readiness, noble rivalries, and the state of the human-gnome alliance. His social events are intelligence-gathering operations.
*   **Resources Provided:** Lakash launders Temple funds through his merchant operations and uses his trade partnerships to move supplies and personnel. His gnome business contacts are unwitting cover for the movement of Temple resources.
*   **Field Agent:** [Conner Bridgeforth](conner-bridgeforth-person.md), a cunning political operator who has infiltrated the noble courts of Verbobonc. Bridgeforth lobbies for political positions favorable to the Temple inside the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md), acting on Lakash's direction.
*   **Sister Operation:** [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) (Madame Selentis) runs the Temple's covert operations in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) — safeguarding supply routes, managing recruitment, and stabilizing that lawless village as a critical node in the Temple's network. The siblings coordinate, but Lakash keeps Nysera unaware of the full scope of his direct ties to the Temple's leadership.

### The Three-Phase Operation

Lakash's mission in Verbobonc is not improvised — it is a structured, multi-phase campaign designed by the Temple to weaken the Viscounty from within. Each phase builds on the last, escalating from whispers to political rupture. The brilliance of the plan is that no single incident looks like Temple work. Every provocation appears to be a natural consequence of existing tensions between humans and gnomes.

 

#### Phase 1 — Inciting Subtle Discord

Lakash seeds small, seemingly isolated incidents that highlight and worsen existing cultural tensions between humans and gnomes.

*   **Staged Misunderstandings:** Agents deployed to markets and public gatherings instigate disputes between human and gnome traders, exploiting historical prejudices.
*   **Propaganda:** Rumors and misinformation spread through taverns and merchant circles questioning the loyalty and intentions of gnome partners — or painting human officials as exploiting gnome labor.
*   **Provocation Events:** At social gatherings (including Lakash's own), staged "insults" escalate into public confrontations. Lakash then publicly smooths things over, reinforcing his image as a peacemaker while the damage is done.

 

#### Phase 2 — Economic Sabotage

With mistrust sown, Lakash escalates to disrupting the trade routes that bind the human-gnome alliance together economically.

*   **Hired Brigands:** Brigands attack caravans traveling between the [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and Verbobonc, specifically targeting gnome caravans.
*   **False Flags:** Evidence is planted to implicate Verbobonc patrols — gnome coinage left on defeated brigands, patrol route schedules found in bandit camps. Each side comes to suspect the other.
*   **Targeted Disruption:** Only shipments that would cause maximum suspicion are hit. High-value gnome goods vanish while human merchants are left untouched, or vice versa, depending on which narrative Lakash needs that week.

 

#### Phase 3 — The Breakdown

The final phase aims to force a political break between Verbobonc and the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) gnomes — a rupture that would shatter the alliance protecting the region from the Temple's ambitions.

*   **Engineered Crisis:** A major incident — an assassination, a public betrayal, or a staged act of gnome "aggression" — forces the Council of Lords to take drastic action against gnome interests in Verbobonc.
*   **Legal Pressure:** Lakash leverages his political allies and Conner Bridgeforth's Council influence to push harsh measures through the Senate and Chamber of 13.
*   **Severed Alliance:** If successful, the gnomes withdraw from Verbobonc's trade networks and political structures. The security vacuum this creates — particularly in the [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and the Kron Hills border — is exactly what the Temple needs to expand operations unchecked.

### Political Ambitions — The Senate and Chamber of 13

Lakash's personal ambition runs in lockstep with his Temple mission. He is actively maneuvering for a seat on the Senate, which would give him legislative power and direct access to the city's inner workings. Beyond that, his long-term goal is the Chamber of 13, the judicial body that reviews legal claims and policy issues under the oversight of Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md). A seat there would let him manipulate legal outcomes, shield Temple operations from investigation, and influence the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) directly.

*   **Senate Campaign:** Lakash is securing endorsements from key merchant families and nobles, deploying his wealth to outmaneuver competitors, and using enchantment magic to nudge undecided votes.
*   **Chamber of 13:** The ultimate prize. Membership would give Lakash the power to review judicial decisions, influence policy, and create a legal environment that protects Temple activity while weakening its opponents.
*   **Strategic Alliances:** Forms partnerships with powerful merchant families and nobility through lucrative business deals. These allies do not know they are advancing the Temple's agenda — they simply see a wealthy, well-connected partner with shared interests.
*   **Cover Reinforcement:** Every political move also reinforces his public persona. Running for Senate? A sign of civic commitment. Hosting nobles? A generous patron. Funding gnome artisans? A bridge-builder. The Temple's saboteur hides in plain sight as Verbobonc's model citizen.

### Key Relationships

 

#### Hedrack — The Supreme Commander

Lakash serves at Hedrack's pleasure. The Supreme Commander of the Temple personally approved Lakash's placement inside Verbobonc and sanctions all major operations. Lakash has never met Hedrack face to face — all communication flows through Lucius Graeme — but he understands that his value depends on results. If the three-phase operation succeeds, Lakash becomes one of the Temple's most important assets. If it fails, he is expendable.

*   **Authority:** Hedrack gives strategic direction; Lakash has operational autonomy in how he executes.
*   **Accountability:** Lakash reports through Lucius. Failure or exposure would result in Lucius cutting him loose — or worse.

 

#### Lucius Graeme — The Handler

[Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) is the man who made Lakash what he is. After the fall of House Krivaltis, Lucius recognized the boy's intelligence and recruited him into the Temple's service. He trained Lakash in espionage tradecraft, connected him to Temple resources, and positioned him in Verbobonc as a long-term infiltrator. Their relationship is part mentor-student, part handler-agent. Lakash respects Lucius's cunning but harbors no illusions — Lucius serves only the Temple, and Lakash is a tool to be used.

*   **Role:** Primary handler, communications channel, and intelligence coordinator.
*   **History:** Lucius visited House Krivaltis during Lord Eldris's secret dealings. Young Lakash eavesdropped on those conversations and came to admire Lucius's resourcefulness.
*   **Current:** Lucius coordinates between Lakash in Verbobonc, Nysera in Nulb, and Hedrack in the Greater Temple. He is the connective tissue of the entire operation.

 

#### Nysera Krivaltis — Madame Selentis of Nulb

[Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md) is Lakash's elder sister. Where Lakash infiltrated the Verbobonc elite, Nysera was placed in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) under the alias "Madame Selentis" by Hedrack's order, with Lucius Graeme arranging her extraction, false death, and new identity. She serves as Hedrack's political shadow in Nulb — stabilizing the village's chaotic factions, protecting Temple supply routes, and managing cult recruitment. The siblings maintain a pragmatic alliance, but their methods and loyalties pull in different directions.

*   **Nysera's Role:** Temple agent in Nulb. Manages supply routes, recruitment channels, and cult infiltration. Reports to Hedrack through Lucius.
*   **Lakash's View:** He supports Nysera's position because her success in Nulb strengthens his own network and provides resources. But he sees her lingering attachment to family honor as naive and is prepared to sacrifice her if it protects his operation.
*   **Nysera's View:** She sees Lakash as a valuable but dangerous ally. She respects his cunning but does not fully trust him, sensing that his ambitions often supersede family bonds.
*   **Information Gap:** Lakash deliberately keeps Nysera unaware of the full extent of his direct ties to the Temple's leadership. She knows he has connections; she does not know he reports directly to Hedrack through Lucius.
*   **Shared Vulnerability:** If either sibling is exposed, it threatens the other. The Krivaltis name is still listed among "Notable Traitorous Offspring" in Verbobonc's records, with a bounty on surviving family members.

 

#### Conner Bridgeforth — The Council Operative

[Conner Bridgeforth](conner-bridgeforth-person.md) is Lakash's most valuable field agent inside Verbobonc's political institutions. A minor noble by birth, Bridgeforth is a slimy, clever, and cowardly manipulator who has ingratiated himself into the noble courts, gathering secrets and steering conversations to advance the agenda of the cult. He operates inside [The Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) on Lakash's direction, lobbying for positions and policies favorable to the Temple.

*   **Role:** Political lobbyist and rumor-monger in the noble courts. Bridgeforth's specialty is controlling what people believe about each other.
*   **Relationship:** Bridgeforth reports to Lakash. He is loyal only to himself and views the cult as a vehicle for personal power, making him useful but unreliable if pressured.
*   **Campaign Use:** Bridgeforth is a perfect NPC for PCs to encounter at Council functions, noble gatherings, and political events — always whispering, always smiling, always steering.

 

#### The Viscount and the Viscounty

Lakash has no personal relationship with Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md), nor does he seek one — the Viscount is too well-guarded and too suspicious for direct manipulation. Instead, Lakash works around Wilfrick by targeting the institutions and people the Viscount depends on: the Senate, the Chamber of 13, the noble houses, and the gnome alliances. If the three-phase operation succeeds, the Viscount will find his government fractured, his alliances broken, and his authority undermined — all without Lakash ever having spoken to him directly.

*   **Strategy:** Indirect. Weaken the foundations; the castle falls on its own.
*   **Wilfrick's Awareness:** None. The Viscount does not know Lakash Quallad exists as a threat. He sees a popular merchant, nothing more.

### DM Notes — How to Use Lakash Quallad

Lakash is one of the most dangerous NPCs in the campaign precisely because he does not look dangerous. He is not a dungeon boss or a battlefield general — he is a political weapon, deployed to destroy alliances and corrupt institutions from the inside. His value to the DM is as a slow-burn antagonist whose actions explain why the political world of Verbobonc keeps getting worse.

*   **The Hidden Hand:** Lakash is the answer to the question "why are the gnomes pulling back?" and "why is the Council so divided?" His three-phase operation creates the political instability that forms the backdrop of the entire campaign. DMs should let his effects be felt long before his identity is revealed.
*   **Not a Combat Encounter:** Lakash avoids direct confrontation at all costs. He fights with information, enchantment, and deniability. If PCs challenge him publicly, he feigns outrage and deploys his network of alibis, friendly senators, and character witnesses. If cornered physically, he uses dimension door, invisibility, and charm to escape — not fireball.
*   **The Brother-Sister Thread:** The Krivaltis sibling connection is one of the campaign's strongest investigative threads. Clues found in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) about Madame Selentis can eventually lead to Lakash in Verbobonc, and vice versa. The DM can run this as a two-city mystery where each end of the thread reveals the other.
*   **Conner Bridgeforth as the Weak Link:** Bridgeforth is cowardly and self-interested. If the PCs lean on him — through intimidation, blackmail, or offering him a better deal — he may crack and reveal Lakash's operation. This is the most likely path to exposing the network.
*   **The Manor as a Set Piece:** The [manor](a12-lakash-quallad-s-manor-landmark.md) article contains the full location description, the guest list, room details, and location-specific hooks. Use the person article for who Lakash is; use the manor article for where and how the PCs encounter him.
*   **Timing the Reveal:** Lakash works best when the PCs spend sessions dealing with the consequences of his operations — failed trade negotiations, gnome tensions, political gridlock — before they ever learn his name. When the trail finally leads to the charming merchant in the High Quarter, the betrayal hits harder because the PCs have been to his parties.

### Campaign Hooks

*   **The Merchant's Masquerade:** Lakash invites the party (or their patron) to a masked ball at his manor. During the festivities, a staged "gnome insult" erupts into a brawl. PCs can spot the planted agitators and choose whether to foil Lakash's script or allow tensions to rise.
*   **Ledger of the Krivaltis:** A contact reveals that proof of House Krivaltis's surviving heirs lies hidden in a secret room within the manor. Recovering it could expose both Lakash and Nysera, triggering turmoil in the Viscount's court and Nulb alike.
*   **Council Seat for Sale:** As Lakash maneuvers for a Senate position, a rival noble or gnome councilor hires the PCs to investigate his background. They must dig through layers of false records, bribed witnesses, and subtle enchantments before the vote is cast.
*   **Brother and Sister at Odds:** News reaches the party that a mysterious "merchant patron" in Verbobonc and a "noble widow" in Nulb are coordinating bandit raids and political agitation. Tracing both ends of the thread forces the PCs into the deadly rivalry — and uneasy alliance — between Lakash and Madame Selentis.
*   **The Greenway Gambit:** Gnome caravans from the [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) are being ambushed, and evidence points to Verbobonc patrols. The Mounted Borderers are furious, the gnomes are withdrawing, and someone is profiting from the chaos. Investigating the false flag operation leads eventually to Lakash's network of hired brigands.
*   **The Enchanter's Guests:** A noble ally of the PCs attends one of Lakash's parties and returns having agreed to a trade deal that makes no sense and a political position they never held before. Detect magic and careful investigation reveal the work of an enchanter operating inside the High Quarter's most prestigious address.

##### Roleplaying Lakash

The man who greets you is everything you expected and nothing you suspected. His smile is warm. His handshake is firm. His wine is excellent. He asks about your journey with genuine interest, remembers your companion's name after hearing it once, and offers a toast to "the bonds that make Verbobonc great." By the end of the evening, you have agreed to attend his next gathering, accepted his offer to introduce you to a senator, and cannot quite remember whose idea any of it was.

*   **Voice:** Warm, measured, slightly amused. Never raises his voice. Speaks as if sharing a private joke that only the listener is clever enough to understand.
*   **Body Language:** Open, confident, unhurried. Makes eye contact. Touches shoulders and arms in the way powerful people do to signal trust. Never fidgets. Never looks nervous.
*   **Tell:** The only crack in the performance is his eyes. When he thinks no one is watching, the warmth drains out completely. For a heartbeat, you see a man calculating odds, weighing threats, and filing away everything he has learned. Then the smile returns, and the moment is gone.
*   **If Threatened:** Lakash does not panic. He expresses confusion, then concern, then hurt. He asks what he could possibly have done to deserve suspicion. He has witnesses. He has friends. He has a senator who will vouch for him. If all else fails, he has a 6th-level spell list and a dimension door prepared.
