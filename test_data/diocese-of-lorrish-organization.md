Diocese of Lorrish
==================

#### Overview

The Diocese of Lorrish lieth west of Kempton and south of Falsridge, where the ordered fields of Veluna give way to broken stone, winding passes, and stubborn folk. Here the land itself resisteth mastery, rising in the tangled ridges known as the Lorridges, where valleys end in silence and roads lose themselves in stone.

This is no soft diocese of grain and doctrine. It is a place of endurance. Of memory. Of old bargains between folk and hill.

 

And above it all ruleth a gnome—patient as the earth, and twice as enduring.

 

#### Geography — The Lorridges

The Lorridges define all things within the diocese. Jagged hills, box canyons, and narrow defiles confound the unlearned traveler. Paths twist, vanish, and reappear, known only to those born to them.

*   Stone outcrops rise like broken teeth, and scattered spurs of the Lortmil Mountains pierce the southern reaches. Water runneth in hidden courses, and whole settlements lie concealed within hillsides.
    
*   No army may march here swiftly. No lord may command it fully. Only those who belong to the land may truly move within it.
    

#### Government & Rule

Lord Corbin Jamorrie hath ruled Lorrish for more than sixty years—and may yet rule sixty more.

*   Unlike other dioceses where bishop and noble contend for primacy, here authority resteth firmly in the hands of the gnomish lord. The Church of Rao is honored, respected, and supported—but it doth not command.
    
*   Corbin’s rule is marked by patience, long memory, and quiet strength. Where others issue decrees, he waiteth. Where others act swiftly, he endureth.
    
*   The Bishop adviseth. The Lord decideth. And the hills remember.
    

#### The Church of Rao in Lorrish

The Church’s presence in Lorrish is… tempered.

*   Bishop Tarnath Pendale was not sent here to rule—but to be removed. His youthful ambition findeth little purchase in a land where decisions are measured in decades.
    
*   He speaketh of reform, of structure, of expanding Raoan authority. Lord Corbin listeneth, noddeth, and doth as he will regardless.
    
*   Thus the Church endureth here not as master, but as guest—welcomed, respected, and gently ignored.
    

#### The Gnomish Compact

The hills are not ruled by decree alone.

 

Gnomish and halfling clans dwell throughout the Lorridges, bound to Lord Corbin by ancient oath. In exchange for protection and representation, they offer loyalty—and knowledge of the land that no outsider may possess.

These clans control hidden paths, secret stores, and the quiet wealth of the hills. Without them, Lorrish would be blind.

#### Economy & Trade

Lorrish produceth little in the way of grand export—yet what it yieldeth is rare, valued, and hard-won.

*   Refined sugars and specialty crops from isolated valleys
*   Rare ores and metals from abandoned or dangerous workings
*   Craft goods traded through gnomish and halfling networks

Goods flow outward through Kempton and into Veluna City, where merchants prize their scarcity.

Trade here is not abundant—but it is profitable.

 

#### Relationship to Veluna & the Celestial Circle

Though distant in terrain, Lorrish standeth close to the heart of Veluna’s true power.

*   Lord Corbin was among the earliest and strongest supporters of Canon Hazen, and their alliance endureth still.
    
*   Through his granddaughter, Sharis Jamorrie—leader within the Celestial Circle—Lorrish exerts influence far beyond its rugged borders.
    
*   Where other dioceses contend openly, Lorrish moveth quietly. And oft, more effectively.
    

#### Military & Defense

There is no standing army in the manner of other dioceses.

Instead, Lorrish is defended by knowledge, terrain, and small, highly capable bands.

*   Clan militias familiar with every ridge and pass
*   Hidden watchposts overlooking key routes
*   Corbin’s Brigade—rangers and adventurers operating far beyond Veluna’s borders

Invaders would find no grand battle here—only confusion, isolation, and quiet death among the stones.

#### Notable Locations

**Lorrish (Town)**

Half-carved into the hillside, the town is as much beneath the earth as above it. Homes open outward, yet stretch deep within stone, offering protection from both weather and war.

  **Sugarberg**

A strange and fearful village, bound by ancient superstition. Its people dread the “little men,” not knowing their own lord is of that very kind.

  **Mines of Ri’tin**

A place of ruin and unanswered questions. Once rich beyond measure, now burned, collapsed, and resistant to all attempts at reopening.
