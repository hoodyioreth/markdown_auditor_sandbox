AO-17. Varnor Earthcaller's Quarters
====================================

_Senior Priest's Cell of the Azure Oath — Now the Geomancer's Sanctum_

* * *

This chamber once belonged to the **senior priest** of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) — the highest-ranking cleric of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) stationed at the monastery. It was a room of quiet dignity: a narrow bed, a shelf of theological texts, a stone prayer niche carved with the lightning bolt, and a single window facing east — toward the dawn, symbolic of Heironeous's eternal vigilance. Each morning, the senior priest would rise, face the window, and speak the Dawn Invocation: _"Light comes. I stand ready."_

Now the room belongs to [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md).

The theological texts are gone — replaced with stacks of **Flan geomantic manuscripts** written in a spiraling script that predates Common by centuries. The prayer niche has been recarved: the lightning bolt chipped away and replaced with the **inverted triangle of the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)**, outlined in mineral pigment that catches the light. Stone samples from the excavation below cover every flat surface — tagged, catalogued, and arranged in patterns that only [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) understands. The room smells of mineral dust, burned incense, and deep stone — the same sharp smell that rises from freshly broken rock.

The east window remains. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) faces it at dawn, too — but he is not greeting the light. He is **listening to what the stone says before the day drowns it out**.

##### Entering Varnor Earthcaller's Quarters

The door opens onto a chamber that feels older than the monastery around it. Every surface is covered — stone fragments, rolled manuscripts, and clay tablets are stacked on shelves, piled on the bed, and laid out across the floor in careful arrangements. A narrow window on the east wall lets in a shaft of pale light that falls across a prayer niche carved into the stone — but the symbol inside is wrong. Where a lightning bolt should be, an inverted triangle has been chiseled into the stone, its edges outlined in dark pigment.  
  
The air smells of mineral dust and something deeper — a burnt, earthy incense that coats the back of your throat. Stone powder dusts every surface. A heavy weapon — a stone hammer inscribed with spiral runes — leans against the wall beside the niche like a pilgrim's walking staff.  
  
The room is still. But beneath the stillness, you feel something — a faint vibration in the floor, so subtle you're not sure it's real. As though the stone beneath your feet is breathing.

### Features of the Quarters

#### The Recarved Prayer Niche

The original prayer niche — a recessed alcove in the east wall where the senior priest knelt each morning — has been **fundamentally altered**. The lightning bolt of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) has been chipped away with careful, deliberate strokes and replaced with the inverted triangle of the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). Unlike the crude charcoal defacement elsewhere in the priory, this work is **precise** — almost reverent. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) carved it himself.

Inside the niche:

*   A small bowl of powdered stone mixed with mineral oil — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s anointing medium, applied to forehead and palms before geomantic prayer
*   Three **Flan resonance stones** — dark, smooth pebbles that hum faintly when touched. These are the same type of stones [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) has been studying in [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md)
*   A carved stone pendant depicting the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) triangle — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s personal holy symbol

A character examining the niche:

**DC 12 Religion**  
The inverted triangle is the symbol of the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — specifically the **Earth aspect**. The carving has been consecrated; this is a functioning shrine, not a decoration.

**DC 15 Religion or Arcana**  
The niche radiates faint divine magic. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has been praying here daily for months, and the accumulated devotion has created a **minor locus of elemental energy**. A cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) who touches the niche feels a cold, grinding resistance — the stone pushing back against their divine presence.

**DC 18 Religion**  
Beneath [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s carving, the **original lightning bolt is still there** — not destroyed, just covered. The old symbol's divine resonance hasn't been fully extinguished. A cleric of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) who spends 10 minutes praying at this niche could attempt to **reclaim it** — contested Religion check against [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s months of accumulated devotion (DC 20). Success restores the lightning bolt and disrupts the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)'s minor influence in this room.

* * *

#### Stonefang

Leaning against the wall beside the prayer niche — placed there with the care of a sacred object, not a weapon — is [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s personal weapon: **Stonefang**.

Stonefang resembles a heavy stone hammer inscribed with spiral runes that **shift slightly** when exposed to geomantic energy. The head is forged around a jagged shard of dark stone — believed to originate from the **Flan altar** buried beneath the priory. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) carries it like a pilgrim's staff rather than a weapon.

**DC 13 Arcana or Religion**  
Stonefang radiates moderate transmutation and divination magic. The spiral runes are Flan in origin — older than anything in the monastery's Azure Oath carvings.

**DC 16 Arcana**  
Stonefang is not merely a weapon — it is a **key**. The altar shard embedded in its head resonates with the buried altar below. When brought near the altar, the shard amplifies the altar's power, allowing the wielder to hear whispers from the deep earth. Without Stonefang, the altar cannot be fully activated.

**If [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is not present:** Stonefang is here, unguarded. The party can take it — but carrying it produces an unsettling effect. The wielder feels faint vibrations in their palms, and at night, they hear a low humming just below the threshold of comprehension. The stone wants to go home — **downward**.

* * *

#### The Geomantic Research Table

A broad table — originally the priest's writing desk — is buried under [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s research materials. Stone samples, manuscript pages, and chalk diagrams compete for space.

Key items on the table:

*   **Flan geomantic manuscripts** — written in a spiraling script on treated animal hide. These describe the theory of geomantic nodes: places where the earth's energy concentrates, forming natural conduits to deeper powers. The manuscripts are genuine ancient Flan scholarship, not [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) propaganda
*   **Excavation progress map** — a hand-drawn diagram of the **crypt level** below the priory, showing which tombs have been opened, which corridors have been cleared, and where the **buried altar chamber** lies. Annotations in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s hand track daily progress. The map is more detailed than [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s rough sketches in [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md)
*   **Stone samples** — dozens of labeled rock fragments arranged in concentric patterns. Each is tagged with depth, location, and resonance notes. Some samples vibrate faintly when placed near others
*   **A journal** — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s personal excavation diary (see sidebar)

A character studying the research materials:

**DC 12 Arcana or History**  
The manuscripts describe a **geomantic node** — a natural concentration of earth energy — located directly beneath the priory. The Flan people knew about it centuries ago. The Azure Oath's founders may have built the priory _on top of it_ deliberately, either to protect it or to contain it.

**DC 15 Arcana**  
[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s notes describe a ritual sequence for **awakening the node** — a multi-day process requiring Stonefang, the resonance stones, grave-earth from the tombs (being prepared in **AO-15**), and the **opening of the Black Mouth**. The ritual is **incomplete** — [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is still working out the final stage

**DC 18 Arcana or Religion**  
One annotation, underlined three times in [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s hand: _"The node was sealed — not by decay, but by divine intervention. The priests of the lightning god built their house of worship here specifically to suppress the voice beneath. Their faith was the ward. When the faith died, the ward began to crack."_ This means the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md)'s fall didn't just leave the priory undefended — it **weakened the seal on the geomantic node itself**. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s occupation isn't just opportunistic. It was **necessary**.

* * *

#### The East Window

The narrow window faces east — the only natural light source in this interior room. In the Azure Oath's time, the senior priest faced this window at dawn to speak the Dawn Invocation.

[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has not covered or defaced the window. He uses it — but at dusk, not dawn. He stands facing east as the light dies, listening to the deep earth as the sun's noise fades. His evening ritual is a deliberate inversion of the Azure Oath's morning prayer: where the priest greeted the light, [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) greets the dark.

**DC 14 Perception (at dusk only):** A character standing at the window during the transition from light to dark feels the floor vibration **intensify** — as though the stone responds to the setting sun. This is not magical in the traditional sense. It is **geomantic** — the node below pulses with the earth's natural rhythm, and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has learned to read it.

* * *

#### The Narrow Bed

The original priest's cot remains, though [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) rarely uses it. Stone samples and manuscripts have colonized most of the sleeping surface. When he does sleep, he sleeps on the floor — directly on stone, with his palm pressed flat against it. He claims the dreams are clearer that way.

Beneath the cot:

*   A battered leather satchel containing **60 gp in mixed coin** and a carved bone talisman of Flan origin (worth 75 gp to a collector of antiquities)
*   A sealed clay jar of **mineral oil** — used in geomantic anointing rituals
*   A bundle of letters from [Romag](romag-person.md), sealed with the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s wax triangle (see sidebar)

* * *

#### Encounter

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) may or may not be present. He divides his time between this room, the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) (where he conducts rites), and the excavation below (where he oversees the duergar teams). He is **alone** in his quarters — no guards, no servants. He considers himself beyond the need for protection. The stone protects him.

*   **Varnor present — studying:** He sits at the research table, cross-referencing manuscripts and stone samples. He does not startle. If the door opens, he looks up slowly and assesses. He may speak first: _"You are not stone. The stone would have warned me."_
*   **Varnor present — praying:** He kneels at the recarved prayer niche, palms pressed to the floor, eyes closed, murmuring in Flan. He does not respond to noise until he finishes — 1d4 rounds. Interrupting him mid-prayer provokes genuine anger, which for [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) means a single cold sentence before combat: _"You have broken the listening."_
*   **Varnor absent:** The room is empty. Stonefang leans against the wall. All research materials are accessible. This is the **optimal outcome** — the party gets the excavation map, the research notes, and Stonefang itself without fighting a major NPC

##### Varnor's Tactics (if combat begins)

[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is **not a brawler**. He is a priest who commands earth and stone. See the [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) NPC article for full stat reference.

*   **Round 1:** Raises stone barriers from the floor (half cover, difficult terrain between him and the party)
*   **Round 2:** Calls on the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — the floor vibration intensifies, every character must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution save or be knocked prone
*   **Round 3+:** Retreats toward the door to **AO-22 (Crypt Mouth)** or the corridor to [AO-14](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md). He fights with Stonefang only if cornered. If overwhelmed, he collapses a section of ceiling (Dexterity DC 15, 3d6 bludgeoning, 10-ft area) to cover his escape
*   **If he escapes to the crypts:** He descends to the altar chamber and begins the awakening ritual early — whether or not it's complete. The crypt level becomes significantly more dangerous

### DM Notes

#### The Quiet Horror

AO-17 is the room that explains **why the Earth Temple is here**. Not for the caravan loot. Not for the military outpost. Not even for the political destabilization. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) came to this specific monastery because there is something **beneath it** — and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is the man who can hear it speaking.

The horror here isn't visceral. It's conceptual. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) isn't a ranting cultist — he's a **scholar** who has found something real beneath the stone, and his calm certainty should be more unsettling than any theatrical villain. When he says the earth is alive and patient, he is not speaking metaphorically. The floor vibration is real. The resonance stones hum. Stonefang pulls downward. Everything in this room confirms that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is not delusional — and that's the problem.

#### Running Varnor as an Encounter

[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is the **most important NPC in the priory after [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)**. He's also the most likely to **talk** — not out of fear or negotiation, but because he genuinely believes in what he's doing and finds the idea of explaining it to outsiders intellectually interesting.

*   **If the party threatens violence:** He responds with quiet disappointment, not fear. _"You came all this way to swing swords? The earth has waited centuries. You could have listened too."_ Then he fights
*   **If the party asks about the excavation:** He explains willingly. He describes the geomantic node, the Black Mouth, the Flan altar, and the ward the Azure Oath built on top of it. He speaks with the calm passion of a lecturer, not a madman. Insight DC 16: he's telling the truth — at least, truth as he understands it
*   **If the party mentions Heironeous or the Azure Oath:** He pauses. A flicker of something — respect? regret? — crosses his face. _"They built well. Their faith was strong enough to silence the earth for three centuries. That is worthy of remembrance."_ He genuinely admires the Azure Oath's builders, even as he dismantles their work
*   **If the party mentions Halven Sere:** He goes quiet. This is the one subject that costs him composure. _"The custodian is... necessary. He knows the priory's secrets. I take no pleasure in his confinement."_ Insight DC 14: this is true. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is uncomfortable with **Halven Sere**'s imprisonment but considers it essential
*   **If the party offers to help or convert:** He considers it seriously. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is not a recruiter, but if a character shows genuine interest in geomancy or the deep earth, he responds with warmth. This can be exploited — a Deception DC 16 can buy time or extract critical information while he "teaches"

#### Stonefang as Campaign Object

Stonefang is not just loot — it's a **campaign-level artifact key**. Without it, the altar beneath the priory **cannot be fully activated**. This creates a critical decision point:

*   **Party takes Stonefang:** [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) cannot complete the awakening ritual. The crypt level remains partially dormant. But the weapon unsettles whoever carries it — the humming, the pull downward, the dreams of stone
*   **Party leaves Stonefang:** If [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) escapes, he retrieves it and begins the ritual. The crypt level becomes actively hostile
*   **Party destroys Stonefang:** Extremely difficult (AC 19, HP 50, resistance to all damage except radiant). If broken, the altar shard shatters and the geomantic node destabilizes — tremors throughout the priory, cracks in the crypt walls, potential collapse. This is the nuclear option

#### The Node Revelation

The DC 18 annotation — that the Azure Oath's faith was the ward suppressing the node — is the most important piece of lore in the priory. It reframes the entire story:

*   The monastery wasn't just built **near** a holy site — it was built **on top of a sealed evil**
*   The Azure Oath's daily prayers weren't just devotion — they were **maintenance of a divine seal**
*   The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s conquest wasn't just territorial — it **broke the ward**
*   Restoring the priory to [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) isn't just justice — it may be **necessary to reseal the node**

Don't reveal this all at once. Let the party piece it together from [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s notes, **Halven Sere**'s testimony, and the crypts below. The full picture should only become clear when they stand in the altar chamber and realize what they're standing above.

#### Sacred Layer — The Dawn Invocation

The original prayer practice — the Dawn Invocation spoken at the east window — was not ceremonial decoration. In a priory built to contain a geomantic node, the senior priest's daily prayer was a **renewal of the divine ward**. Every morning, for three centuries, the priest of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) stood at that window and spoke the same words: _"Light comes. I stand ready."_ And every morning, the ward held.

[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s inversion — facing the same window at dusk, greeting the dark — is not just blasphemy. It's **counter-ritual**. He's systematically unwinding the ward the Azure Oath spent centuries maintaining. The vibration in the floor grows stronger each day because [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s evening prayers are **working**.

If a cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) learns the Dawn Invocation (from **Halven Sere**, the prayer journal in **AO-15**, or the surviving monastery records in **AO-16**) and speaks it at this window at dawn, the floor vibration **stops** for 24 hours. The ward reasserts itself, briefly. This is a clue that the faith of [Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) is the **key to resealing the node** — not swords, not magic, but sustained prayer.

*   **East:** **AO-16. The Scriptorium** — connecting door. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) occasionally accesses the records next door
*   **West:** **AO-18. Tharvok Stonehand's Quarters** — adjacent. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) confer regularly
*   **South:** Interior corridor toward [AO-14. Chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) and **AO-20 (Guest Cells / Prison)**

[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s quarters sit in the north wing between the scriptorium and [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s command chamber. The north wing is the **leadership spine** of the priory — reaching it means the party has penetrated deep into enemy territory. By the time they find this room, they should understand that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s operation has layers they hadn't anticipated.

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I examine the prayer niche."** — Religion DC 12 (EEG shrine), DC 15 (minor elemental locus, divine pushback for Heironeous faithful), DC 18 (original lightning bolt survives beneath — niche reclaimable with DC 20 Religion)
*   **"I pick up Stonefang."** — No check needed. Arcana DC 13 (transmutation/divination magic, Flan runes), DC 16 (it's a key to the buried altar). Carrying it produces unsettling vibrations and dreams
*   **"I study the research table."** — Arcana/History DC 12 (geomantic node), DC 15 (awakening ritual details — incomplete), DC 18 (the ward revelation — Azure Oath's faith suppressed the node). The excavation map provides a detailed layout of the crypt level
*   **"I search under the bed."** — No check needed. Satchel with 60 gp, Flan bone talisman (75 gp), mineral oil, Romag's letters
*   **"I read Romag's letters."** — No check (they're in Common). Orders, progress demands, and veiled threats. Confirms Varnor reports to Romag. One letter references "the asset in the cells" (Halven Sere)
*   **"I try to talk to Varnor."** — He engages. See Running Varnor above. He tells the truth about the excavation but does not reveal the awakening ritual's final stage. Insight DC 16 catches omission
*   **"I try to reclaim the prayer niche."** — Requires Heironeous cleric/paladin. Religion DC 20 (contested against accumulated devotion). Success restores lightning bolt, disrupts elemental influence. Failure: the stone pushes back — 2d6 psychic damage
*   **"I speak the Dawn Invocation at the window."** — Must know it (from Halven Sere, AO-15 journal, or AO-16 records). Speaking at dawn: floor vibration stops for 24 hours. This is the clue for resealing the node
*   **"I take everything."** — Excavation map, manuscripts, stone samples, Romag's letters, journal, Stonefang. 15 minutes for a thorough collection. The excavation map alone is worth the risk of entering this room
