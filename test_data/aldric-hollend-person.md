Aldric Hollend
==============

### Lord Steward

The most powerful person in **Castle Greyfist** is not the Viscount. The Viscount makes the decisions. But the man who decides which decisions reach the Viscount's desk — who walks through the study door unannounced, who schedules the audiences and routes the dispatches and knows which Council lord is lying about what — that man is Aldric Hollend. He carries no noble title. He commands no soldiers. He holds no seat on the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md). What he holds is the Viscount's schedule, the Viscount's correspondence, and the Viscount's trust — and in the politics of the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md), those three things are worth more than any title. A former Knight Captain of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) who took a lance wound at [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) and traded the saddle for the antechamber, Aldric has served two Viscounts across three decades. He is the institutional memory of Castle Greyfist — the one person who remembers how things worked before Wilfrick, who knows where every promise was made and which ones were kept, and who understands that the greatest threat to the Viscounty in 576 CY is not the **Temple of Elemental Evil** but the possibility that the people who should be fighting it cannot stop fighting each other long enough to organize a response.  

### EXCERPT

**Aldric Hollend** is the Viscount's Chamberlain of Verbobonc — chief of household, gatekeeper to the Viscount's presence, and the operational center through which the executive machinery of the Viscounty flows. A former Knight Captain of the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md)' 3rd Company (the Longriders), Aldric served twenty-five years in the field — patrolling the **Gnarley Forest** frontier, liaising with elven clans and ranger networks, and building the kind of cross-border relationships that most diplomats only read about. A lance wound at the Battle of Emridy Meadows in 569 CY ended his field career. Lady Elara Revepaix, recognizing that his instincts for people were sharper than his instincts for cavalry charges, appointed him Chamberlain of Castle Greyfist in 571 CY. He has held the post ever since — surviving the transition from Lady Elara's rule to Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md)'s, navigating three decades of Council politics, and becoming the quiet hinge upon which the doors of power open or remain firmly closed. In 576 CY, Aldric is sixty-two years old, walks with a slight limp he has learned to use strategically, worships **Rao** in a court dominated by [St. Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md), and is the only person alive who can walk into the Viscount's private study without knocking.

 

#### History: The Longrider Who Learned to Listen

Aldric Hollend was born in 514 CY to a family of minor freeholders in the southern reaches of the Viscounty — close enough to the **Gnarley Forest** that he grew up hearing ranger stories and close enough to the frontier that military service felt less like a career choice and more like a fact of life. He enlisted in the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) at seventeen, assigned to the 3rd Company — the Longriders — whose patrol territory covered the eastern Viscounty, the Gnarley Forest borderlands, and the southeastern reaches of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md).

 

The Longriders were not garrison soldiers. They were a small-unit force that operated in deep terrain for weeks at a time, maintaining relationships with wood elf communities along the Gnarley fringe, coordinating with ranger networks, and keeping the peace between human settlements and the forest peoples who had lived there centuries longer. This work required something more than martial skill: it required listening, negotiation, the ability to sit in an elf elder's home for three hours drinking tea before broaching the subject you came to discuss, and the discipline to keep promises even when your commanding officer did not understand why those promises mattered. Aldric excelled at all of it.

 

By 555 CY, he had risen to Knight Captain — commanding the 3rd Company's eastern patrols and serving as the Borderers' primary liaison with the elf clans and the Woodsmen of the Gnarley Forest. He was respected by his soldiers for never ordering a patrol he wouldn't ride himself, and respected by the forest peoples for being one of the rare humans who understood that respect is demonstrated through behavior, not speech. He maintained contact with gnome traders and militia in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), occasionally coordinating shared patrols that protected caravan routes beneficial to both gnome and human merchants.

 

When the **Temple of Elemental Evil** rose to power in the 560s and the call went out for forces to join the Righteous Host, Aldric led a Longrider contingent south. At [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) in 569 CY, his company fought on the Host's right flank — alongside gnome militia from the Free Alliance and elven archers from the Gnarley's edge. It was there that a Temple cavalryman put a lance through Aldric's left thigh, shattering the bone and ending his career in the saddle. He was forty-five years old and had spent twenty-eight of those years on horseback.

 

He spent a year recovering. During that time, Lady Elara Revepaix — the Viscount's mother and the true political mind behind the Viscounty at the time — visited him three times. Not out of charity, but out of recruitment. She had watched Aldric for years: his ability to navigate elf politics, his gnome connections, his quiet competence, his instinct for reading people's real positions beneath their stated ones. She needed someone to manage Castle Greyfist who understood that a court is a frontier — a place where different peoples with different interests meet, negotiate, and occasionally draw weapons. In 571 CY, Aldric accepted the appointment as Chamberlain. He has never left the post.

 

#### The Chamberlain's Office: What Aldric Actually Does

The Chamberlain of Verbobonc is not a ceremonial title. It is the operational center through which the Viscount's executive authority is administered daily. Aldric's responsibilities fall into six interconnected domains, each of which generates intelligence, influence, and the capacity to shape events without appearing to do so.

 

**Access Control:** No one sees the Viscount without passing through Aldric. Every audience request, every petition, every "urgent" message from a Council lord is filtered through the Chamberlain's office. Aldric decides who gets fifteen minutes and who gets an hour. Who sees the Viscount today and who waits until next week. Who gets a private audience and who is received in the public chamber with witnesses. This is not bureaucracy — it is power in its purest form. A lord who cannot reach the Viscount cannot influence the Viscount. Aldric understands this and wields it with precision.

 

**Correspondence and Intelligence:** Every significant dispatch entering or leaving **Castle Greyfist** passes through Aldric's hands. He reads everything. He knows what the Veluna observers are reporting before they report it. He knows which Council lords are writing to each other and which are writing to contacts outside the Viscounty. He does not spy in the traditional sense — he does not employ agents or break seals. He simply pays attention with the methodical thoroughness of a man who has been reading dispatches for thirty years and can identify a lie by the way it is phrased on parchment.

 

**Council Preparation:** Before every [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) session, Aldric briefs Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) on the current factional positions, pending disputes, and likely ambush points. He does not advise the Viscount on what to decide — that is not his role, and he would refuse if asked. What he provides is the situational awareness that allows Wilfrick to walk into the chamber knowing what each lord actually wants, as opposed to what each lord will say they want. This intelligence function is arguably the most valuable service the Chamberlain provides.

 

**Diplomatic Liaison:** Aldric's Longrider background gives him relationships that no other court official possesses. He maintains back-channel contact with elf clan leaders in the **Gnarley Forest** — people he patrolled alongside for decades and who trust him personally. He coordinates with the Woodsmen of the Gnarley through former Borderer colleagues who still ride those patrols. He communicates with Veluna's diplomatic representatives through protocols he helped establish under Lady Elara. And critically, he maintains a quiet relationship with [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) at [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) — one of the few human-gnome back-channels that still functions in 576 CY. Jeet remembers the gnome forces at Emridy Meadows. She remembers who fought beside them. Aldric was there. That shared history is the foundation of a trust that no diplomat appointed last year could replicate.

 

**Household Administration:** Castle Greyfist is a working fortress, not a palace. Aldric manages its staff of approximately sixty servants, guards, cooks, grooms, and administrative workers. He ensures the castle functions smoothly, that supplies are maintained, that security protocols are observed, and that the small army of people required to keep the Viscount's household operational are paid, housed, and — critically — loyal. A compromised servant is an intelligence breach. Aldric interviews every new hire personally.

 

**The Quiet Commissions:** There are tasks the Viscount needs accomplished that he cannot officially authorize. Investigations that would alarm the Council if conducted through official channels. Messages that cannot be sent through normal diplomatic pouches. Operations that require plausible deniability. These are the Chamberlain's quiet commissions — tasks assigned verbally, funded from discretionary accounts, and executed through networks that Aldric has built over three decades. He does not enjoy this part of the work. He does it because someone must, and because the alternative is allowing problems to metastasize until they require public and politically costly intervention.

 

#### Key Relationships

**Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md):** Aldric is one of perhaps three people Wilfrick trusts completely — and unlike the other two (his daughter Elysia and his son Serrol), Aldric's trust was earned through performance, not blood. Aldric served Wilfrick's mother first. He watched Wilfrick grow into the role of Viscount. He has seen the man at his best — decisive, courageous, principled — and at his worst — paralyzed by competing pressures, second-guessing decisions, exhausted beyond the capacity for clear judgment. Aldric does not flatter. He presents facts, offers context, and lets the Viscount decide. When Wilfrick makes a decision Aldric disagrees with, Aldric executes it faithfully and records his objection in a private journal that no one else has read. He is loyal to the office of Viscount because the office is larger than any one man. If Wilfrick were ever removed or abdicated, Aldric would serve the successor with the same precision. This institutional loyalty — cold, dependable, and utterly professional — is exactly why Wilfrick trusts him.

 

**Elysia Revepaix:** The Viscount's adopted daughter is the political mind behind the court — she drafts positions, manages strategic correspondence, and briefs the Viscount before Council moves happen. Aldric and Elysia work in close coordination: she handles political analysis, he handles operational execution. Their relationship is one of deep mutual respect with an undertone of generational tension. Elysia is brilliant but impatient. Aldric is methodical but sometimes slow to adapt. Together they form a complementary engine: she sees what needs to happen, he makes it happen within the constraints of what is actually possible. If they ever genuinely disagreed on a matter of strategy, the court would fracture. So far, that has not happened.

 

**Bishop Haufren ([Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md)):** The Viscount's primary confessor and closest clerical advisor. Aldric and Haufren share professional respect and theological disagreement. Haufren believes the Temple crisis demands decisive force — marshal the Viscounty's resources, confront the threat directly, accept the costs. Aldric, a devotee of **Rao**, believes that premature military action against the Temple will shatter the fragile alliances the Viscounty needs to survive. This disagreement plays out quietly but continuously: in the advice each gives the Viscount, in the framing of intelligence reports, in the emphasis placed on different threats. Neither man dislikes the other. They simply see the world through fundamentally different lenses — Cuthbert's demand for righteous action versus Rao's counsel of reason, patience, and peace achieved through understanding rather than force. The Viscount needs both voices. Aldric knows this. He suspects Haufren knows it too.

 

**[Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md):** This is Aldric's most strategically valuable relationship and the one he guards most carefully. Jeet does not trust humans as a category. She trusts specific humans who have earned it through specific actions over specific periods of time. Aldric earned that trust at Emridy Meadows, where he fought beside gnome militia and afterward did not participate in the celebration that excluded gnome commanders. Thirty years later, he remains one of the few humans who can send a message to Kron Fortress and receive a reply. This back-channel is gold in 576 CY: with the official gnome-Viscounty relationship deteriorating over mine claims and the Borderer withdrawal, Aldric's personal line to Jeet is one of the last threads holding the alliance together. He uses it sparingly and honestly. If he ever lied to Jeet through this channel — even once — the thread would snap permanently.

 

**"Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md):** Aldric and Pithriggen have known each other for decades — their paths crossed regularly when Aldric's Longrider patrols coordinated with gnome merchant escorts. Pithriggen understands that Aldric is one of the Viscount's people who actually takes gnome concerns seriously, and he uses this understanding strategically. When Pithriggen needs something to reach the Viscount's attention urgently, he goes to Aldric. When Aldric needs to gauge the gnome reaction to a proposed Council action, he asks Pithriggen. This is not friendship — it is professional mutual utility, conducted with the courtesy of two men who have been doing this long enough to know that courtesy is cheaper than conflict.

 

**[Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md):** The Lord Mayor regards Aldric as an obstacle — not because Aldric opposes Haxx's interests, but because Aldric controls access to the one person whose decisions determine whether those interests succeed or fail. Haxx has attempted, on multiple occasions, to establish a direct line to the Viscount that bypasses the Chamberlain's office. Each attempt has been politely but firmly redirected. Aldric does not dislike Haxx. He understands that Haxx's economic machinery is essential to the Viscounty's survival. But he also understands that allowing any single Council lord unfiltered access to the Viscount would destroy the balance of power that keeps all thirteen lords manageable. Haxx resents this. Aldric considers the resentment an acceptable cost of doing his job.

 

****Claviger Velysin**:** Velysin is one of the few Council members who genuinely respects Aldric — not because they agree on policy, but because Velysin recognizes a fellow soldier. Both men were shaped by military service. Both understand command, discipline, and the weight of decisions that cost lives. Their disagreement is strategic, not personal: Velysin wants the Viscounty to project military strength, while Aldric argues that military posture without sufficient diplomatic preparation creates exactly the kind of provocation that triggers the conflicts military strength is supposed to prevent. When Velysin needs the Viscount's ear on a military matter, he approaches through Aldric with professional courtesy — and Aldric ensures the request is heard promptly. This mutual respect is rare in a court where most relationships are transactional.

 

****Corbin Deleveu**:** The half-elf lord of the southern border and current commander of the Longriders — Aldric's old company. Aldric served under Deleveu's predecessor and knows the 3rd Company's territory, its patrol routes, and its relationships with the forest peoples better than anyone alive who no longer rides those routes. Deleveu consults Aldric informally on matters of Gnarley Forest diplomacy, particularly regarding elf clan sensitivities and ranger network contacts that Aldric built decades ago. These informal consultations constitute an unofficial intelligence channel between the Borderers and the Viscount that bypasses the Council entirely. Neither man advertises this arrangement.

 

**The Gnarley Forest Contacts:** Aldric's decades of Longrider service left him with a network of relationships in the **Gnarley Forest** that no other court official possesses. He knows elder rangers by name. He has shared camps and councils with wood elf leaders. He understands the forest peoples' concerns — about Verbobonc's expanding logging operations, about human settlements encroaching on traditional forest boundaries, about the growing darkness emanating from the south. These contacts do not report to him formally. They simply talk to him when he asks, because he spent twenty-five years earning the right to ask.

 

#### 576 CY: The Chamberlain Under Pressure

Aldric has managed crises before. He has navigated Council disputes, diplomatic incidents, border skirmishes, and the death of a Viscount's mother. But 576 CY is different. The convergence of threats — the Temple's resurgence, the gnome alliance fracturing, the Council's internal paralysis, the Mounted Borderer withdrawal, the trade disruptions — creates a cascading pressure that exceeds anything his office was designed to handle. He is managing more correspondence, scheduling more audiences, routing more intelligence, and sleeping less than at any point in thirty years of service.

 

**The Gnome Back-Channel:** Aldric's personal relationship with [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) is one of the Viscounty's most valuable assets — and one of its most fragile. The official relationship between the Viscounty and the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) is deteriorating rapidly. Mine claims, the Borderer withdrawal, caravan raids — all of it pushes the gnomes toward closing the [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and redirecting trade to Dyvers. Aldric knows that if Jeet recommends sealing the pass, Clanlord [Urthgan Tulvar](urthgan-tulvar-person.md) will likely agree. He is working every back-channel he possesses to prevent this — but he can only slow the erosion. He cannot stop it without the Council making real concessions on the mine claims, and the Council cannot agree on what those concessions should be.

 

**The Rao-Cuthbert Tension:** Aldric's devotion to Rao puts him increasingly at odds with the dominant religious voice in the court. Bishop Haufren and **Tanner BrightBlade** both counsel action — confront the Temple, mobilize forces, assert Viscounty authority. Aldric counsels preparation before action — secure alliances first, stabilize the economy, ensure that when the hammer falls, it falls on the Temple and not on a fractured coalition. He is not wrong. Neither are they. But the theological dimension makes the disagreement harder to resolve, because Rao's counsel of peace through understanding and Cuthbert's demand for order through righteous action are genuinely different philosophies, not merely different tactics. Wilfrick listens to both. Aldric worries that the Viscount's natural tendency toward caution is being mistaken for indecision by Council members who want decisive leadership — and that the longer the decision is delayed, the fewer options remain.

 

**The Temple Intelligence Problem:** Aldric suspects that Temple agents have penetrated the Viscounty's intelligence infrastructure to some degree. Not the Chamberlain's office — he is confident of his staff. But the wider network of informants, merchant contacts, and military intelligence that feeds into Castle Greyfist may be compromised. Information that should reach him quickly is arriving late or not at all. Reports from the southern approaches contain gaps that feel deliberate. The caravan raids on gnome merchants are too well-coordinated to be opportunistic banditry. Someone is directing these operations with knowledge of patrol schedules and trade routes — information that should not be available outside the Borderer command structure. Aldric has not shared these suspicions widely. He is investigating quietly, through his own channels, and he needs help.

 

#### Personality and Roleplay

**Voice:** Aldric speaks in the measured, precise cadences of a man who learned to choose his words in rooms where the wrong word could start a war. He never raises his voice. He finishes every sentence. He does not interrupt. When he is angry, his voice becomes quieter and more deliberate — not louder. PCs who are paying attention will notice the drop in volume and understand its significance. Those who are not paying attention will learn.

 

**The Limp:** Aldric walks with a slight limp in his left leg — the legacy of the Emridy Meadows lance wound. He has carried this limp for thirty years and uses it strategically. People underestimate the old soldier with the bad leg. They assume he is slower than he is, weaker than he is, less dangerous than he is. Aldric does not correct these assumptions. The limp is real; the limitation it implies is not. He can still draw a sword, still move with purpose when necessary, and still command a room through presence alone. The limp is a reminder that he paid for his position with blood, not patronage.

 

**Observation:** Aldric watches everything. When a Council lord is speaking to the Viscount, Aldric is watching the other lords' reactions. When a diplomat presents credentials, Aldric is watching their hands, their eyes, their posture. When PCs enter Castle Greyfist, Aldric evaluates them in the first thirty seconds: who leads, who follows, who is nervous, who is armed, and who is lying about why they are here. He will not share these observations unless asked — and even then, selectively. DMs should describe Aldric's eyes tracking the room when PCs are present. Observant players will notice. Good players will wonder what he has noticed about them.

 

**Humor:** Dry, infrequent, and devastating when deployed. Aldric's humor emerges only among people he trusts — Wilfrick, Elysia, occasionally Deleveu. In public, he is courteous and unreadable. In private, he is capable of observations so precisely targeted that the Viscount has been known to put down his wine to avoid spilling it. This humor is the remnant of the Longrider who spent weeks in the field with a small company of soldiers and learned that laughter is a survival skill.

 

**Motivations:** Aldric is motivated by institutional loyalty, not personal ambition. He does not want power — he wants the machinery to work. He does not want fame — he wants the Viscounty to survive. He does not want legacy — he wants to hand the Chamberlain's office to a successor with the tools and relationships necessary to serve the next Viscount effectively. This lack of personal ambition makes him both trustworthy and, to some nobles, incomprehensible. They cannot find his lever because he does not have one in the conventional sense. His lever is competence. Threaten the Viscounty's ability to function, and Aldric becomes an obstacle. Support it, and he becomes the most useful ally in Verbobonc.

 

#### Temple Campaign Implications: Why Aldric Matters

For a DM running the Temple of Elemental Evil campaign, Aldric Hollend is the operational bridge between the party and the Viscounty's political machinery. He is the NPC who translates political reality into actionable missions, who provides context that transforms a simple job into a strategic operation, and who makes the party feel like participants in a living political system rather than mercenaries executing disconnected tasks.

 

**Campaign Hooks:**

*   **The Quiet Commission:** This is Aldric's primary function for the party. The Viscount needs something investigated, protected, or accomplished — but cannot officially authorize the operation without alerting the Council or provoking a political crisis. Aldric approaches the party, explains the situation with as much candor as security allows, and offers payment, access, or future consideration. These commissions create the campaign's core mission structure: jobs that matter politically, funded quietly, with consequences that ripple through the Council chamber.
*   **The Gnome Negotiation:** Aldric's back-channel to [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) is deteriorating. He may ask the party to carry a message to Kron Fortress — one that he cannot send through official channels because the Council would interpret it as concession on the mine claims. Or he may ask them to investigate the caravan raids to provide Jeet with evidence that the Viscounty is taking gnome security seriously. These missions directly affect whether the gnome alliance survives.
*   **The Intelligence Leak:** Aldric suspects Temple infiltration of the Viscounty's intelligence network. He needs outside investigators — people who are not part of the existing system and therefore cannot be compromised by it. PCs recruited for this task would investigate Borderer patrol schedules, merchant intelligence networks, and potentially the households of Council members. This is dangerous work that makes powerful enemies regardless of the outcome.
*   **The Gnarley Forest Channel:** Aldric's old Longrider contacts in the **Gnarley Forest** have sent word of unusual activity — movements that suggest Temple operations extending into the forest. He needs someone to investigate without alerting the Council (which would trigger a military response that might provoke the forest peoples) or the Temple (which would cause the agents to scatter). PCs with ranger or forest connections are ideal for this mission.
*   **The Diplomatic Reception:** Veluna or Furyondy envoys are arriving for discussions the Viscount does not want the Council to know about. Aldric needs the party to provide discreet security, serve as unofficial escorts, or handle complications that arise during sensitive negotiations. This introduces the party to the wider geopolitical context beyond Verbobonc's borders.
*   **The Gatekeeper:** Before the party can see the Viscount for any reason, they must convince Aldric that the meeting is warranted. This is not an obstacle — it is a roleplay opportunity. Aldric is evaluating the party: their competence, their honesty, their potential utility. A party that impresses Aldric gains access not just to the Viscount but to the entire network of quiet commissions, intelligence sharing, and political protection that the Chamberlain's office provides.

 

#### DM Notes: Running Aldric at the Table

**First Encounter:** The party should meet Aldric before they meet the Viscount — preferably as the person who politely but firmly explains that the Viscount is not available, evaluates whether the party's business is genuine, and either schedules an audience or offers to handle the matter himself. This establishes the power dynamic immediately: the Chamberlain is not a servant. He is a filter. PCs who treat him as an obstacle will find the Viscount perpetually busy. PCs who treat him as a person with authority and judgment will find doors opening.

 

**Information Delivery:** Aldric is the ideal NPC for delivering complex political information to the party in digestible form. He does not lecture. He briefs. Short, precise statements of fact followed by a question that invites the party to draw their own conclusions. "Lord Haxx's revenue is down twelve percent. The Hardheads missed their last payroll cycle by a week. What do you think happens when five hundred mercenaries go unpaid?" This Socratic approach makes the party feel like they are figuring things out rather than being told, which is more engaging and more memorable.

 

**Trust Escalation:** Aldric does not trust the party initially. He trusts competence, honesty, and results. Track the party's reliability as a campaign variable: each quiet commission successfully completed, each honest report delivered, each promise kept moves Aldric one step closer to full trust. At maximum trust, he shares intelligence freely, warns the party about dangers the Viscount doesn't even know about, and personally intervenes to protect the party from political consequences. At zero trust, he is polite, distant, and offers nothing beyond what the party could learn from a tavern rumor.

 

**The Rao Dimension:** Aldric's devotion to Rao is genuine but quiet. He does not proselytize. He does not argue theology. He simply acts according to Rao's principles: seek understanding before action, prefer peace to conflict, and recognize that the most effective solutions are usually the ones that leave all parties able to live with the outcome. PCs with religious connections may recognize Rao's influence in Aldric's counsel and either align with it or push back against it. A paladin of Heironeous might find Aldric too cautious. A cleric of St. Cuthbert might find him too accommodating. These are productive tensions that create roleplay depth.

 

**Vulnerability:** Aldric is sixty-two, walks with a limp, and is under more stress than at any point in thirty years of service. He is not invulnerable. He gets tired. He makes errors of judgment when sleep-deprived. He is carrying the weight of the Viscounty's survival on shoulders that are strong but not infinite. If the party earns his trust, they should occasionally see the cracks — a moment of exhaustion, a rare admission of uncertainty, a flash of the young Longrider who signed up to ride patrol and somehow ended up holding a government together. These moments make him human. And they remind the party that even the most capable NPC in the Viscounty is running out of time.

**Why the Chamberlain Matters to Your Temple Campaign**

Aldric Hollend is the connective tissue of your Verbobonc campaign. He links the party to the Viscount's authority, to the Council's politics, to the gnome alliance, to the Gnarley Forest's intelligence, and to the military structure of the Mounted Borderers — all through a single NPC whose trust must be earned through demonstrated competence. He is the mechanism by which the Viscount's quiet commissions reach the party, the filter through which political intelligence is delivered, and the relationship that determines how much of Verbobonc's institutional support the party can access when the Temple crisis reaches its peak. Without Aldric, the party is disconnected from the machinery of governance. With him, every mission has political context, every success has political consequences, and the campaign operates as the interconnected strategic sandbox it is designed to be.

 

**Critical Questions for DMs**

*   How quickly do the PCs earn Aldric's trust? Define specific milestones: the first quiet commission, the first honest report, the first time they deliver bad news without embellishment.
