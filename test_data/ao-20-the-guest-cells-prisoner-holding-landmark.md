AO-20. The Guest Cells 'Prisoner Holding'
=========================================

> The austere guest quarters where visiting pilgrims, traveling clerics, and knights-errant once found shelter beneath the Azure Oath's protection. Simple stone cells, each with a narrow bed and a prayer bench, arranged along a short corridor off the north wing. Now the guest cells are a prison. Iron bars have been bolted across the doorframes. The prayer benches are overturned or broken. The window slits are stuffed with rags. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) holds its captives here — caravan drivers, merchants, a gnome gem-trader, and one prisoner who matters more than all the others combined: **Halven Sere**, last custodian of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), kept alive for reasons that go far deeper than leverage.

_Guest Quarters of the Azure Oath — Now the Earth Temple Prisoner Holding Cells_

* * *

The guest cells occupy a short corridor off the north wing of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), tucked between **AO-19. The Oath-Hall** to the east and **AO-17. Varnor Earthcaller's Quarters** to the west. The corridor runs perhaps twenty-five feet, with four small stone cells opening off the right side — each no larger than a monk's chamber, maybe eight feet by six. The left wall is unbroken stone, cold and damp, the only light a single oil lamp hanging from an iron bracket at the corridor's midpoint.

When the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) held the priory, these cells welcomed anyone who traveled the Long Road in need of shelter. Pilgrims heading south to holy sites. Clerics of allied faiths passing through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Knights-errant with nowhere else to sleep. Each cell had a narrow straw bed, a prayer bench facing a small niche in the wall (for private devotion to whatever god the guest served), and a window slit barely wide enough for a hand — open to the air, letting in the sound of wind across the ridge. Hospitality was a sacred duty. The Azure Oath fed its guests, gave them a place to pray, and sent them on their way with a blessing.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) has converted every cell into a cage. Iron bars — crudely forged, bolted with heavy brackets into the ancient stone — block each doorframe. The prayer benches are overturned or splintered for kindling. The window slits are stuffed with rags to muffle sound. The straw beds remain, but they are filthy, unchanged for weeks. The corridor smells of unwashed bodies, stale food, and the sharp mineral tang of the stone walls. A wooden stool sits at the corridor entrance where the jailer keeps his post.

##### Entering the Guest Cells

The corridor is narrow — barely five feet across — and dim. A single oil lamp hangs from the ceiling halfway down, its flame guttering in a draft that comes from nowhere you can see. The air is thick with the smell of sweat, old straw, and something sharper beneath it — fear, maybe, or just stone that has been too long enclosed.  
  
Four cell doors line the right wall, each fitted with iron bars bolted crudely into the stone frame. The bars are rough work — not a locksmith's craft, but a blacksmith's hurried job, functional and ugly. Through the first set of bars you can see a figure huddled on a straw pallet, knees drawn up, face turned to the wall.  
  
At the corridor entrance, a guard sits on a wooden stool, a ring of heavy iron keys hanging from his belt. He looks up when you appear — not alarmed, but watchful. Beside him, a clay jug of water and a half-eaten loaf of bread sit on the floor. Provisions for the prisoners, or for himself. Probably both.

### The Prisoners

The cells hold the human cost of [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiding operations along the Long Road. These are not enemy combatants — they are travelers, merchants, and working people who were in the wrong place when the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s raiders came through. Each was evaluated on capture: recruit, labor, ransom, or sacrifice. Their fates depend on how useful the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) finds them.

#### Cell 1 — Halven Sere (Special Prisoner)

The first cell — closest to the corridor entrance, where the guard can watch him — holds \[b[Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md), last custodian of the [Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). He is the only prisoner who was here before the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) arrived. He was here when they came. He has been here ever since.

**Halven Sere** is a thin man in his late fifties, grey-haired, with the deep-set eyes and weathered hands of someone who has spent decades maintaining a building that was always too large for one person. He wears a faded blue robe — the vestment of a lay brother of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) — now threadbare and stained. His cell is different from the others. It is the cleanest. The straw is changed more often. He has a blanket. There is a wooden cup of water that is refilled regularly. And the prayer bench in his cell has not been removed — it still faces the devotion niche in the wall, and the niche still holds a small carved figure of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) never bothered to take.

He is not chained. He is not gagged. He is not broken.

But he has been here for months, and it shows. His hands tremble slightly. He flinches at sudden noises. When he speaks, his voice is hoarse from disuse — he has no one to talk to except the guard, who doesn't talk back, and [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), who comes to ask questions **Halven Sere** refuses to answer.

**Why the Earth Temple keeps him alive:**

*   **Leverage.** If [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) officials, [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) clergy, or mounted patrols ever arrive at the ridge, **Halven Sere** can be brought out — cleaned up, dressed, and presented as "the custodian who willingly granted us shelter." A living witness who appears unharmed defuses suspicion. In reality, he speaks under threat of harm to the other prisoners
*   **Interrogation.** [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) are still trying to solve the riddle of the Altar of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) in [AO-14. The Chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md). **Halven Sere** knows things about the sacred geometry, the ward-stones, and the chapel's hidden functions that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) priests cannot decipher through brute force. They question him regularly — sometimes with courtesy, sometimes not. He has not broken
*   **Spiritual suppression.** [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) suspects that **Halven Sere**'s continued faith may be sustaining something in the crypts below — restless spirits, lingering wards, or divine protections that resist the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s desecration. Keeping him alive and worn down may be safer than killing him and unleashing whatever his faith still holds in check

#### Cell 2 — Berrik Holst (Caravan Guard)

**Berrik Holst** is a stocky, dark-haired man in his early thirties — a professional caravan guard who worked the [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md)\-to-[Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) run for the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md). He was captured alongside a fellow guard, Tannor Greaves, on the 7th of Readying. Greaves was shipped to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) as forced labor. **Berrik** was kept — evaluated as a potential recruit for [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiding company.

He has refused. Twice. The bruises on his ribs are the result. **Sergeant Drogath "Stonejaw" Varn** handles recruitment persuasion, and he is not gentle about it.

**Berrik** is angry, scared, and looking for any way out. If freed, he is a competent fighter (equivalent to a **Guard** stat block) who will join the party for the duration of their time in the priory. He knows the Long Road, the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md)'s trade routes, and can identify stolen Consortium goods in **AO-19** and **AO-21** by their merchant marks. He wants to find Tannor Greaves, who he believes was sent downriver to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md). This becomes a future campaign hook.

#### Cell 3 — Pilnik Brassbottom (Gnome Gem-Trader)

**Pilnik Brassbottom** is a gnome merchant from the [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) gem-trading community — small even for a gnome, with oil-stained fingers and quick, nervous eyes. He was captured on the 14th of Readying while traveling the Long Road south of [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) with a small cargo of uncut amethysts and gem-cutting tools. His initial evaluation was "ransom or sacrifice," but [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) intervened — **Pilnik** knows the gem routes through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), and [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) wants that knowledge for the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s mineral operations.

**Pilnik** has been interrogated twice by [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) personally. He gave up some information — surface-level trade route knowledge that any Sheernobb merchant would know — but is holding back the real prize: the locations of unregistered gnome gem caches hidden in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) for emergency use. He is terrified and will cooperate with anyone who frees him.

If rescued, **Pilnik** is not a fighter, but he is deeply connected to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md)'s gnome community. Returning him safely generates significant political goodwill — particularly valuable given the ongoing human-gnome tensions that [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) is working to inflame. He can also identify every piece of gnome-crafted goods in the priory as stolen property.

#### Cell 4 — Davven Millwright (Merchant)

**Davven Millwright** is a middle-aged cloth merchant from the Hendel Marsh road — a broad, soft-spoken man with ink-stained fingers and a merchant's careful way of watching everything. He was captured on the 22nd of Readying during what the counting room ledger calls a "thin take" — a low-yield raid when [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiders were desperate for any cargo. His evaluation: ransom. The quartermasters in **AO-19** noted that he has guild connections — possibly the [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md).

**Davven** is the calmest prisoner. He has been held the shortest time and has not been beaten or interrogated. He spends his hours sitting quietly, watching the guard's patterns, counting the steps between sounds, and building a mental map of the priory's routine. He is more observant than he looks.

If freed, **Davven** can tell the party things none of the other prisoners know: the guard shift timing (he has counted every cycle), the direction sounds come from (he can describe the priory's layout by sound alone), and the names of three raiders who escorted him from the road — useful intelligence for identifying [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) operatives later. His guild connections in [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) can open doors for the party if they return him safely.

### The Jailer

**Guardsman Orvic Denn** is a lean, quiet man with a scar running from his left ear to his jaw — not a talker, not a bully, and not particularly cruel. He is the most professional guard in the garrison, which is exactly why [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) assigned him to the prisoner cells. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) does not want the prisoners abused — not out of mercy, but because damaged prisoners are less useful. **Orvic** follows orders precisely. He feeds the prisoners on schedule, changes **Halven Sere**'s straw when told, and does not engage in conversation with any of them.

He carries the cell keys on an iron ring at his belt — four keys, one per cell, plus a master that opens all four. He is armed with a short sword and a hand crossbow (loaded, resting on his knee when seated). He does not leave the corridor except during shift changes, when he is relieved by a rotating Black Earth Guard from the barracks at [AO-10](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md).

#### Running Orvic Denn

*   **If confronted with force:** **Orvic** fights. He is disciplined — he does not panic, does not flee, and does not call for help (the corridor is too far from other posts for shouting to carry). He fires the crossbow first, then draws his sword and uses the narrow corridor to his advantage, forcing attackers to come at him one at a time. He fights to incapacitation — not to the death. If reduced to a quarter HP, he drops his sword and surrenders. He is a professional, not a zealot
*   **If presented with Tharvok's seal and a forged transfer order:** He inspects the seal carefully. Forgery Kit check or Deception DC 18. If the forgery passes, **Orvic** complies without question — he unlocks whichever cell the order specifies and steps aside. He does not ask why. Orders are orders. This is the **non-combat rescue path** established in **AO-18**
*   **If approached with deception (disguise, false authority):** **Orvic** recognizes every member of the command staff — [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md), [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md), [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md), **Lucius Graeme**. Strangers claiming authority are challenged: "Name and authorization." Deception DC 16 to bluff past him. If the party is wearing looted Earth Temple tabards (from **AO-10** or **AO-25**), he accepts them as garrison members but still requires authorization for prisoner access
*   **If bribed:** No. **Orvic** does not take bribes. He is loyal to [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) specifically — not the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) doctrine, but the man. [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) earned his loyalty through competence and consistency. Insight DC 14 reveals this personal loyalty — which means if the party has already killed [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), informing **Orvic** of this fact (with proof) may break his resolve. Persuasion DC 14 _after_ he knows [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is dead. Without that leverage, he is unmovable
*   **If the party has already cleared the north wing:** **Orvic** is aware that something is wrong — he has heard the sounds of combat, or noticed that his relief has not arrived. He is on high alert, crossbow aimed at the corridor entrance. Stealth DC 16 to approach undetected in this state

### Features of the Guest Cells

#### The Cells Themselves

Each cell is roughly eight feet by six — barely room for a straw pallet, a prayer bench (if it hasn't been broken), and a person. The walls are bare stone, cold and damp. A narrow window slit in the exterior wall provides ventilation but no useful light — most are stuffed with rags to prevent prisoners from signaling or shouting.

**Cell locks:** The iron bar doors are secured with heavy padlocks — crude but effective. Thieves' Tools DC 14 to pick, or the keys from **Orvic**'s belt. The bars themselves can be bent with a combined Strength DC 22 (two characters working together can attempt this).

**Prayer niches:** Each cell has a small recessed niche in the wall — the original devotion alcove for guests. Most are empty. **Halven Sere**'s still holds a small carved figure of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). **Pilnik Brassbottom**'s holds nothing, but the gnome has scratched a tiny symbol of **Garl Glittergold** into the stone with his thumbnail — barely visible unless you know to look (Perception DC 15).

* * *

#### Halven Sere's Cell — The Sacred Detail

**[Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md)/b\]'s cell is different. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) treats him differently because [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) ordered it — and [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s orders are absolute. The straw is changed weekly. The blanket is wool, not rags. The water cup is refilled twice daily. And the prayer bench remains — facing the niche, facing the carved [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md), undisturbed.**

**

**DC 12 Religion**  
The devotion niche in **Halven Sere**'s cell still holds a faint consecration — a whisper of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s presence sustained by the custodian's daily prayers. A cleric or paladin of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) who enters this cell feels it immediately: warmth in the stone, a sense of being watched over, the faintest vibration of a prayer-ward that refuses to die.

**DC 16 Religion**  
This is not just faith — this is **active divine maintenance**. **Halven Sere** has been praying in this cell every day for months, and his prayers are doing something. The consecration in this niche is connected — however faintly — to the wards in the chapel ([AO-14](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md)) and the seals in the crypts below. He is a relay point. His faith is holding part of the priory's original divine architecture together. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) suspects this but cannot prove it — and it is the real reason [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) has not killed him.

* * *

#### The Guard's Stool

**Orvic Denn**'s guard position at the corridor entrance is spartan: a wooden stool, a clay jug of water, a half-loaf of bread, and a leather satchel containing his personal effects — 12 gp, a bone-handled knife (sentimental, not combat), and a folded piece of parchment: his original recruitment letter from [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md), promising steady pay and "honorable work protecting the temple's interests." The irony is lost on him.

The key ring on his belt holds five keys — one per cell plus a master. The master key is distinguishable by a small triangle filed into its bow.

### Encounter: Rescuing the Prisoners

This is the room the campaign has been building toward — or one of them. Rescuing **Halven Sere** is the emotional payoff of the priory dungeon. The other prisoners are the moral weight: real people suffering because of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s operations. How the party handles the rescue defines their approach.

#### Rescue Paths

*   **Combat:** Kill or incapacitate **Orvic Denn**, take the keys. Straightforward but noisy — the corridor amplifies sound. If the party has not yet cleared **AO-18** and **AO-17**, combat here alerts the north wing
*   **Forged Transfer Order:** Present [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s seal (from **AO-18** strongbox) with a forged prisoner transfer document. Forgery Kit check or Deception DC 18. **Orvic** complies without question. This path is established in the **AO-18** article and is the intended reward for players who explored the command quarters thoroughly
*   **Tharvok Is Dead:** If the party killed [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) and can prove it (his holy symbol, his weapon, or simply the blood on their hands), **Orvic**'s personal loyalty collapses. Persuasion DC 14 to convince him to lay down his keys and walk away. He does not join the party. He does not fight. He simply leaves — walks out of the priory and disappears into the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md)
*   **Stealth:** The corridor is narrow and **Orvic** is attentive. Stealth DC 14 under normal conditions (DC 16 if he is on alert). A successful approach allows a surprise round or a chance to pickpocket the keys (Sleight of Hand DC 16). The prisoners will stay silent if they see armed strangers — they have learned not to draw attention
*   **Distraction:** Creating a commotion elsewhere in the north wing (explosion in **AO-17**, fire in **AO-19**) draws **Orvic** away from his post for 1d4+1 rounds to investigate. The prisoners can be freed in his absence, but **Halven Sere** cannot move quickly — he needs support to walk

#### After the Rescue

Freeing the prisoners creates immediate complications:

*   **Halven Sere cannot fight.** He is frail, exhausted, and spiritually drained. He can walk with support, but combat is beyond him. Protecting him during an escape is a tactical challenge. He will, however, pray — and his prayers may have tangible effects in the chapel and crypts (DM's discretion)
*   **Berrik Holst wants a weapon.** Give him one and he fights alongside the party — Guard stat block, motivated and furious. He will not leave without checking **AO-19** and **AO-21** for his partner Tannor Greaves' belongings
*   **Pilnik Brassbottom wants to run.** He will bolt for the nearest exit unless the party convinces him to stay (Persuasion DC 10 — not hard, he just needs to believe they can protect him). If he runs alone, there is a 50% chance he is spotted by a patrol and recaptured
*   **Davven Millwright is calm.** He follows the party's lead, does not panic, and provides tactical intelligence about guard patterns he has memorized. He is the most useful non-combatant companion in the priory
*   **Moving a group through the priory is harder than moving alone.** Four freed prisoners plus the party makes stealth nearly impossible (group Stealth check at disadvantage). The party must choose: escort everyone out now, stash prisoners somewhere safe while they finish clearing the priory, or split the group

### DM Notes

#### The Moral Hinge

**Halven Sere** is the campaign's moral center in this dungeon. He is not a quest-giver, not a source of magic items, not a key to a secret door. He is a man who stayed when everyone else fled, who maintained a place of worship alone for years after the order dissolved, and who has endured months of captivity without surrendering his faith or his knowledge. Rescuing him should **feel** significant — not because of mechanical rewards, but because the players have spent several rooms learning who he was and what was done to the place he protected.

His name is on the honor plaque in **AO-19**. His prayer journal is hidden in **AO-15**. His captors argue about him in **AO-18**. His faith sustains the wards that [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is trying to break. By the time the party reaches this cell, they should know who he is — even if they have never met him.

#### Running Halven Sere

**Halven Sere** does not behave like a grateful NPC from a video game. He does not leap up, thank the party, and dispense lore. He is a man who has been imprisoned for months. His first reaction is **disbelief** — he has dreamed of rescue so many times that reality feels wrong. Then quiet, overwhelming gratitude that he fights to control because breaking down will cost time they do not have.

Dialogue when freed:

*   **First words:** A long silence. He stares. Then, barely above a whisper: _"Are you... you're not his."_ (Meaning: not [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s people.) He stands slowly, using the prayer bench for support
*   **If asked about the priory:** _"They have taken everything. But not everything they think."_ He knows the layout intimately — every room, every corridor, every hidden corner. He can guide the party anywhere in the priory and the crypt level below
*   **If asked about the chapel (AO-14):** His eyes sharpen. _"The altar still holds. I feel it — even here. They have tried to break it. They cannot. Not while I..."_ He stops. He does not finish the sentence. Religion DC 14 to understand: his prayers are sustaining the chapel's wards from this cell
*   **If asked about the crypts:** _"[Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is digging for something. Something the order sealed away. He asks me about it — every time. I tell him nothing."_ He knows what [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is looking for but will not reveal it immediately. Trust must be earned — or the party must bring him to the chapel, where the connection between his faith and the wards becomes visible
*   **If asked about [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md):** _"He is... disciplined. He does not enjoy what he does. He does it because he believes it is inevitable. That is worse than cruelty. You cannot reason with inevitability."_
*   **If told [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) is dead:** Another long silence. Then: _"Then the building heard it. The stone knows."_ This is not metaphor. **Halven Sere** believes the priory itself is aware of what happens within its walls

#### The Other Prisoners — Campaign Threads

Each prisoner is a seed for future content:

*   **Berrik Holst** — His partner Tannor Greaves was shipped to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) as forced labor. Finding and freeing Greaves is a hook for the [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) arc. Berrik also has [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) connections that can pay rewards for recovered cargo and provide intelligence about other [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) raids
*   **Pilnik Brassbottom** — Returning him to [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) counteracts [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s destabilization campaign by proving that humans rescued a gnome from the very people causing the conflict. Pilnik also knows the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) gem routes — a valuable resource for future operations
*   **Davven Millwright** — His merchant guild connections in [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) can open political doors. He can serve as a witness before the Viscounty's courts. He also owes the party a life-debt — and merchants with resources and connections are powerful allies

#### Common Player Actions

*   **"I attack the guard."** — Combat in a 5-foot-wide corridor. Orvic fires crossbow first (if not surprised), then draws short sword. Fights to quarter HP, then surrenders. Noise carries to AO-17 and AO-19 if those rooms are still occupied
*   **"I show the guard the forged transfer order."** — Forgery Kit or Deception DC 18. Success: Orvic unlocks specified cell(s), no questions. Failure: Orvic draws weapon, demands the party identify themselves. Combat or fast-talk (Deception DC 20 to recover)
*   **"I tell the guard Tharvok is dead."** — Requires proof. With proof + Persuasion DC 14: Orvic drops keys and leaves. Without proof: Orvic does not believe them. Insight DC 14 from a PC reveals that Orvic's loyalty is personal to Tharvok, not doctrinal
*   **"I sneak up on the guard."** — Stealth DC 14 (DC 16 if alert). Success: surprise round or pickpocket attempt (Sleight of Hand DC 16 for keys)
*   **"I pick the cell locks."** — Thieves' Tools DC 14 per cell. Or keys from Orvic. Or Strength DC 22 to bend bars (two characters together)
*   **"I talk to Halven Sere."** — See Running Halven Sere above. He is cautious, then grateful, then informative. He knows the entire priory and crypt level
*   **"I ask about the crypts."** — Halven knows but does not share immediately. Trust-building required, or bring him to the chapel
*   **"I free everyone and head for the exit."** — Group Stealth at disadvantage. Halven needs support to walk. Berrik fights. Pilnik panics unless calmed. Davven follows orders. Moving 4+ prisoners through the priory is a tactical puzzle
*   **"I leave the prisoners and come back later."** — Viable. But if the alarm has been raised, the Earth Temple may move or kill the prisoners before the party returns. Clock is ticking

##### The Guest Cells (Prisoner Holding)

**Original Purpose**  
Guest quarters for traveling pilgrims, clerics, and knights-errant — [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md). Hospitality was a sacred duty.

**Current Use**  
Prison cells for the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison under [Romag](romag-person.md). Captured travelers held for labor, recruitment, ransom, or sacrifice. **Halven Sere** held as special prisoner.

**Allegiance**  
[Romag](romag-person.md) — Earth Temple, [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) at [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)

**Connected Areas**

*   **AO-19. The Oath-Hall** (east) — counting room
*   **AO-17. Varnor's Quarters** (west) — geomancer's cell
*   **AO-18. Tharvok's Quarters** (northwest) — via corridor

##### [Halven Sere](halven-sere-person.md) — The Custodian

_Last custodian of the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath. Lay brother of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). Prisoner since the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) occupation._

* * *

**Appearance:** Thin man, late fifties, grey-haired. Deep-set eyes, weathered hands. Faded blue robe (Heironeous vestment), threadbare and stained.

**Condition:** Frail from months of captivity. Trembling hands. Flinches at sudden noise. Voice hoarse from disuse. Cannot fight. Needs support to walk.

**Faith:** Unbroken. Prays daily at the devotion niche in his cell. His prayers sustain the priory's original divine wards — connecting his cell to the chapel and the crypt seals. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) suspects this but cannot prove it.

**Knowledge:** Knows every room in the priory. Knows the crypt level layout. Knows what [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is searching for. Knows the chapel altar's secrets. Will not share crypt knowledge immediately — trust must be earned.

**Personality:** Quiet. Patient. Stubborn beyond reason. Has endured months of interrogation by [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) without breaking. First reaction to rescue: disbelief, then controlled gratitude.

##### Room Reference

*   **Room ID:** AO-20
*   **Zone:** North-Center — Prisoner Wing
*   **Type:** Prison Cells / Rescue Encounter
*   **Dimensions:** Corridor ~25 ft × 5 ft. Four cells, each ~8 ft × 6 ft
*   **Occupants:** **Guardsman Orvic Denn** (jailer). Prisoners: **Halven Sere** (custodian), **Berrik Holst** (caravan guard), **Pilnik Brassbottom** (gnome trader), **Davven Millwright** (merchant)
*   **Lighting:** Single oil lamp at corridor midpoint (dim). Cells are near-dark
*   **Smell:** Unwashed bodies, stale straw, old food, damp stone
*   **Sound:** Quiet. Occasional cough or shuffle from cells. Guard's breathing
*   **Access:** East to **AO-19** (Oath-Hall). West to **AO-17** (Varnor). Northwest corridor to **AO-18** (Tharvok). South corridor to [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) zone
*   **Sacred Layer:** Guest quarters — hospitality as sacred duty. Prayer niches in each cell. Halven Sere's niche still consecrated
*   **Desecration:** Inversion — rooms built to shelter travelers now cage them. Hospitality replaced by imprisonment. The cruelest irony in the priory
*   **Critical Finds:** Halven Sere (campaign's moral hinge), prisoner witnesses, Orvic's keys, divine ward connection (Halven's cell)

##### DM Quick Reference

*   **Orvic Denn — Combat:** Crossbow first, then short sword. Narrow corridor forces 1-on-1. Surrenders at quarter HP. Professional, not zealot
*   **Orvic — Forged Order:** Forgery Kit or Deception DC 18 with Tharvok's seal. Success: unlocks cells, no questions
*   **Orvic — Tharvok Dead:** Requires proof + Persuasion DC 14. Drops keys and leaves forever
*   **Orvic — Disguise/Bluff:** Deception DC 16 to pass as garrison. Still requires authorization for prisoner access
*   **Orvic — Bribe:** No. Loyal to Tharvok personally, not doctrine. Insight DC 14 reveals this
*   **Orvic — Alert State:** Stealth DC 16 to approach (DC 14 normally). Crossbow aimed at entrance
*   **Cell Locks:** Thieves' Tools DC 14 per cell. Master key (triangle notch) opens all four. Bars: Strength DC 22 to bend (two characters)
*   **Halven Sere — Condition:** Frail, cannot fight, needs support to walk. Will pray (tangible effects in chapel/crypts, DM discretion)
*   **Halven — Niche:** Religion DC 12 (faint consecration, Heironeous presence). DC 16 (active divine maintenance — his prayers sustain chapel wards and crypt seals)
*   **Halven — Knowledge:** Full priory layout. Crypt level guide. Chapel altar secrets. Withholds crypt info until trust earned
*   **Berrik Holst:** Guard stat block if armed. Wants to find Tannor Greaves (shipped to Nulb). Silver Consortium connections
*   **Pilnik Brassbottom:** Non-combatant. Panics unless calmed (Persuasion DC 10). Gnome community connections in Sheernobb. Counteracts Caidax destabilization
*   **Pilnik — Hidden:** Garl Glittergold symbol scratched in niche (Perception DC 15)
*   **Davven Millwright:** Non-combatant. Calm, observant. Knows guard shift timing by ear. Can describe priory layout by sound. Merchant guild connections in Verbobonc City
*   **Group Escape:** Group Stealth at disadvantage with prisoners. Halven needs support. Berrik fights. Pilnik bolts (50% recaptured if alone). Davven follows lead
*   **Left Behind:** If alarm raised and party leaves prisoners, Earth Temple may move or kill them before party returns
*   **Treasure:** Orvic: 12 gp, bone knife (sentimental), recruitment letter. Keys (5). No other treasure — this room's value is people, not loot
