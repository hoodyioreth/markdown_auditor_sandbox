Mounted Borderers
=================

#### Guardians of the Viscounty of Verbobonc

The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) are the Viscount's sword arm, his eyes on the frontier, and the closest thing the [Viscounty of Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md) possesses to a professional military force. Four companies of mounted soldiers — lancers, scouts, forest fighters, and the Viscount's own household guard — patrol the borders from the **Gnarley Forest** to the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), enforce the Viscount's law in places no magistrate can reach, and carry the authority to arrest anyone in the Viscounty — including nobles. Founded over a century and a half ago by veterans and adventurers who swore fealty directly to the Viscount, the Borderers have been the backbone of every military campaign the Viscounty has fought, including the decisive victory at the [Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md). In 576 CY, they are stretched thin, pulled from the Kron Hills by a political decision that has left gnome caravans unprotected, and watching Temple-linked activity resurface along routes they used to patrol. They are the best soldiers Verbobonc has. There are not enough of them.

#### Mounted Borderers of Verbobonc

The Mounted Borderers swear fealty to the Viscount — not to the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md), not to the Lord Mayor, not to any noble house. That single fact defines everything about what they are and why they matter. In a Viscounty where political loyalty is negotiated, conditional, and frequently for sale, the Borderers represent the one military institution that answers to the executive authority alone. When a Council lord defies the Viscount's writ, it is a Borderer who rides out with the warrant. When a frontier community is threatened and no one else is coming, it is a Borderer patrol that arrives. And when the Viscounty needs something done that cannot be debated in committee, argued in the Council chamber, or filtered through the political machinery of thirteen noble houses — it is the Mounted Borderers who do it.   This is why Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md) has spent twenty years trying to dilute their authority. This is why the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) watches their deployments with more attention than they give to any other military matter. And this is why, in 576 CY, the decision to withdraw them from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) has consequences that extend far beyond a few empty patrol routes.  

#### History and Founding

  The Mounted Borderers were established approximately one hundred and fifty years before 576 CY by a coalition of veterans and adventurers who had served previous Viscounts in the field and recognized that the Viscounty needed a permanent, professional mounted force rather than relying on levied militia and noble retinues whose loyalty ran to their own lords first and the Viscount second. The founding charter bound the Borderers directly to the Viscount's person through an oath of fealty that bypasses the feudal hierarchy entirely — a legal arrangement that has been challenged by ambitious nobles in every generation since and has survived every challenge.   The order grew from a single company of frontier riders into the four-company structure that exists today, each company adapted to the terrain and threats of its assigned territory. The culture that developed over those fifteen decades is distinctive: the Borderers see themselves not as an elite class but as instruments of the Viscount's justice in places where that justice would otherwise be theoretical. They are professional soldiers who live in the saddle, marry within their own ranks when they marry at all, and maintain an institutional identity that runs deeper than any individual commander's personality.  

*   **Founded:** Approximately 426 CY (~150 years before 576 CY) by veterans and adventurers swearing direct fealty to the Viscount.
*   **Founding Charter:** Binds Borderers to the Viscount's person, bypassing the feudal hierarchy. Renewed by oath of fealty at each Viscount's accession.
*   **Institutional Identity:** Not a noble order — a professional military force. No hereditary membership. Entry by merit and demonstrated capability only.
*   **Culture:** Rigorous equestrian and combat training. Small-unit tactics. Extended frontier patrols lasting weeks. Members often marry within the ranks. Veterans establish businesses serving active Borderers — most notably the Grim Wolf Inn.
*   **Legacy:** Every significant military operation the Viscounty has conducted in the last century and a half has been led or supported by Mounted Borderers, including the decisive engagement at the [Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) in 569 CY.

 

#### Authority and Mandate

  The Mounted Borderers carry the Viscount's direct authority into the field — a legal mandate that makes them unique among the Viscounty's institutions. While the [Vigil Wardens](vigil-wardens-of-verbobonc-organization.md) enforce the law within [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) and local sheriffs handle village matters, the Borderers operate in the spaces between: the frontier, the contested borders, the roads where trade moves and bandits hunt, and the noble estates where the Viscount's writ is sometimes treated as a suggestion rather than a command.   What makes them politically significant — and politically dangerous — is the scope of their judicial authority. A Knight Captain or Knight Commander of the Borderers can arrest a noble, seize evidence from a lord's estate, and bring charges before the Viscount under the Right of High Justice. No other institution in the Viscounty has this power. It is exercised rarely and carefully, because every time a Borderer arrests a lord, the Council chamber feels the tremor. But the authority exists, and every noble who sits in the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md) knows it.  

*   **Patrol Duties:** Frontier defense, border surveillance, and route protection across all Viscounty territories — from the Lortmils to the Gnarley Forest to the Kron Hills.
*   **Justice Administration:** Empowered to investigate, arrest, and detain in remote thorps and villages beyond the reach of local magistrates and sheriffs.
*   **Noble Jurisdiction:** Authority to investigate disputes involving nobles or their retainers, arrest violators of the Viscount's law, and compel testimony regardless of rank.
*   **Right of High Justice:** Officers can bring any citizen — including nobles and their vassals — before the Viscount to answer charges. This is the Borderers' most politically powerful and most carefully guarded authority.
*   **Viscount's Protection:** The 1st Company maintains a dedicated garrison at [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md), directly responsible for the Viscount's personal security.
*   **Coordination:** Formal cooperation with the [Vigil Wardens](vigil-wardens-of-verbobonc-organization.md) (city law enforcement), the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) (forest patrols), and the [Woodsmen of the Gnarley Forest](woodsmen-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) (shared values, common frontier). The Woodsmen relationship is the closest external alliance — built on decades of shared patrols and mutual respect.

 

#### The Four Companies

  The Borderers are organized into four companies, each assigned to a specific territory and each shaped by the terrain, threats, and relationships its region demands. The companies operate with significant independence — a Knight Captain in the field makes tactical decisions without waiting for orders from [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md) — but all four answer to the Viscount and coordinate through his Chamberlain's office when operations cross territorial boundaries.  

##### 1st Company — High Guard of the Viscount

  The 1st Company is the Viscount's household guard and the most politically visible of the four companies. Stationed at [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md), they are responsible for the Viscount's personal security, the safety of [Verbobonc City](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md), and the execution of special assignments that come directly from the Viscount's desk. Selection for the 1st Company is the highest honor in the Borderer ranks — reserved for soldiers who have demonstrated exceptional courage, resourcefulness, and absolute loyalty.  

*   **Territory:** Verbobonc City and the immediate approaches to Castle Greyfist.
*   **HQ:** [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md).
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Ryiis el Lendreer**, Knight Bannerette.
*   **Function:** Personal security detail, special assignments from the Viscount, crisis response within the capital.
*   **Political Note:** The 1st Company answers to the Viscount alone. Their presence at Castle Greyfist means that any attempt to move against the Viscount militarily must first reckon with the best soldiers in the Viscounty stationed in the same building.

 

##### 2nd Company — "The Spears of Hagthar"

  The 2nd Company guards the western Viscounty, the Iron Wood, and the southwestern reaches of the Lortmil Mountains. They specialize in mountain warfare and the containment of threats emerging from the west — lycanthropes, humanoid raiding parties, and whatever else the Lortmils produce. Under Knight Captain **Egret Rhynehurst** — a relation of Lord [Wendell Rhynehurst](wendell-rhynehurst-person.md) — the Spears have earned a reputation as the most disciplined and protocol-adherent of the four companies. Where the Longriders are lone wolves and the 4th Company is still finding its identity, the Spears of Hagthar are textbook soldiers who fight by the manual and win by precision.  

*   **Territory:** Western Viscounty, Iron Wood, Southwestern Lortmils.
*   **HQ:** Fortress Hagthar.
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Egret Rhynehurst**, Knight Captain.
*   **Specialization:** Mountain warfare, anti-lycanthrope operations, western frontier defense.
*   **Political Note:** Egret's connection to Lord Rhynehurst gives the 2nd Company an indirect political link to the moderate faction on the Council. This is not a formal alliance — Egret is a soldier, not a politician — but his family connection means the Rhynehurst voice in Council is informed by frontline intelligence that most lords never see.

 

##### 3rd Company — "The Longriders"

  The Longriders are the Borderers' deep-terrain specialists — the company that operates in the eastern Viscounty, the **Gnarley Forest** borderlands, and the southeastern [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). They are guerrilla fighters, forest scouts, and — critically — the Viscounty's primary liaison with the elf clans and ranger networks of the Gnarley. Their work requires something more than martial skill: the ability to sit in an elf elder's home for three hours drinking tea before broaching the subject you came to discuss, and the discipline to keep promises even when your commanding officer does not understand why those promises matter.   The Longriders produced one of the most consequential figures in Verbobonc's current government: **Aldric Hollend**, the Viscount's Chamberlain, served as Knight Captain of the 3rd Company for over a decade before a lance wound at the [Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) ended his field career. Aldric's Longrider instincts — for reading people, for cross-cultural negotiation, for maintaining relationships across species and political lines — became the foundation of his thirty-year tenure as the operational center of Castle Greyfist. The current commander, Knight Captain **Corbin Deleveu**, continues this tradition: a half-elf lord honored with the fey title "Hrōvacán" (Wild Commander) by the [Elves of the Gnarley Forest](elves-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md), Deleveu is both a noble house lord and a Borderer officer — a dual role that gives him unique reach across military and political spheres.  

*   **Territory:** Eastern Viscounty, Gnarley Forest borderlands, Southeastern Kron Hills.
*   **HQ:** [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md).
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Corbin Deleveu**, Knight Captain. Half-elf, Lord of House Deleveu, "Hrōvacán" of the Gnarley elves.
*   **Specialization:** Forest and hill warfare, guerrilla tactics, elf and ranger liaison, deep-terrain reconnaissance.
*   **The Longrider-to-Chamberlain Pipeline:** **Aldric Hollend** is not the only Longrider who transitioned from field service to governance. The 3rd Company's emphasis on diplomacy, cross-cultural communication, and intelligence gathering produces soldiers whose skills translate directly into court and administrative roles. Aldric remains informally connected to the Longriders through Deleveu — an unofficial intelligence channel between the Borderers and the Viscount that bypasses the Council entirely. Neither man advertises this arrangement.
*   **Political Note:** Deleveu's position as both a Council lord (House Deleveu holds the southern border) and a Borderer commander creates a structural tension: he is simultaneously subject to the Viscount's military authority and a participant in the Council that debates how that authority is used. He navigates this by keeping his military and political roles scrupulously separate in public — and less separate in practice.

 

##### 4th Company

  The 4th Company is the newest addition to the Borderer order — created by a previous Viscount in response to growing threats from the south and the strategic importance of the northern [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Under Knight Captain **Roland Galans**, the 4th Company patrols the southern Viscounty and the northern Kron approaches — territory that puts them directly in the middle of the gnome crisis, the mine claim disputes, and the caravan raids that have destabilized the region since the Borderer withdrawal.   Galans himself is a complicated figure: a wealthy noble whose family fortune comes from agriculture and Velverdyva River trade, who commands soldiers in terrain where his family's commercial interests are directly at stake. He is jovial, shrewd, and fiercely independent — he does not align strictly with any Council faction, which makes him useful to the Viscount and unpredictable to everyone else.  

*   **Territory:** Southern Viscounty, Northern Kron Hills.
*   **HQ:** De Chute's Ford.
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Roland Galans**, Knight Captain. Lord of House Galans, known as "the Grand."
*   **Specialization:** Southern border defense, caravan protection, Kron Hills approaches.
*   **Political Note:** Galans's independent streak means the 4th Company does not automatically follow Council directives that come through military channels. When Lord Mayor Haxx attempted to redirect 4th Company patrols to cover trade routes benefiting [Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md) shipping, Galans politely declined — citing his orders from the Viscount. This did not improve his relationship with Haxx. It did improve his standing with the Viscount considerably.

#### The Kron Hills Withdrawal — 575 CY

  The most consequential decision affecting the Mounted Borderers in a generation was not made by a Borderer commander. It was made by Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) in 575 CY, when he ordered the withdrawal of Borderer patrols from the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) and [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md).   The proximate cause was a violent incident: gnome militia under the [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) killed several Borderers in a dispute over mine claim documentation. Wilfrick's advisors told him that maintaining the patrols would provoke a full military escalation with the Free Alliance. The withdrawal was a de-escalation measure — remove the Viscounty's visible presence and let tensions cool.   What actually happened was the opposite. The withdrawal created a security vacuum that organized raiders — some likely Temple-linked — exploited immediately. Gnome caravans from [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md), [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md), and [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) began to be systematically targeted. The gnomes, who had killed Borderers partly because they resented the human military presence, now found themselves unprotected by the very force they had driven away. "Prince" [Jimm Pithriggen](jimm-pithriggen-person.md) went quiet in Council sessions — not a sign of cooling tensions, but of a diplomat assembling his arguments. Commander [Jeet Jimbleclap](jeet-jimbleclap-person.md) at [Kron Fortress](kron-fortress-landmark.md) began reinforcing gnome patrols and guard outposts. The alliance is not cooling. It is hardening.  

*   **Date:** 575 CY, approximately one year before the current campaign date.
*   **Cause:** Gnome militia killed several Borderers in a mine claim dispute. Wilfrick ordered withdrawal to prevent escalation.
*   **Immediate Effect:** Security vacuum in the Kron Hills and Greenway Valley. Organized raiders exploited the gap within months.
*   **Gnome Response:** Commander Jeet Jimbleclap expanded gnome patrols, built guard outposts, and began treating the withdrawal as a permanent condition rather than a temporary measure.
*   **Borderer Morale Impact:** The Longriders (3rd Company) and 4th Company felt the withdrawal most acutely — both companies had built relationships with gnome communities over years of shared patrols. Being pulled out felt like abandoning allies. Deleveu and Galans obeyed the order. Neither forgave the advisors who recommended it.
*   **Political Fallout:** The withdrawal handed the militarist faction — Lords **Claviger Velysin** and [Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md) — their strongest argument: the Viscount's caution is creating the very insecurity it was supposed to prevent.
*   **The Chamberlain's View:** **Aldric Hollend**, himself a former Longrider Knight Captain, has not publicly commented on the withdrawal. Privately, he uses his back-channel to Jeet Jimbleclap to slow the deterioration of the gnome alliance — one of the last threads holding it together.
*   **[Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md)'s Response:** Burne wrote to the Viscount about the withdrawal. The Chamberlain acknowledged the letter. The decision has not been reversed.

 

#### Political Connections

  The Mounted Borderers do not operate in a political vacuum. Their commanders hold noble titles, their deployments affect trade routes that fund the [Council of Lords](the-council-of-lords-of-verbobonc-organization.md), and their authority to arrest nobles makes every lord in the Viscounty pay attention to where Borderer patrols are riding and — more importantly — where they are not.  

##### The Viscount

  Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) is the Borderers' supreme commander and their sole source of legitimate authority. The relationship is personal as well as institutional: Wilfrick fought alongside Borderers at the [Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md), he knows the commanders by name, and he understands what it costs to ride a frontier patrol for three weeks without resupply. The ceremony of fealty — where Knight Captains and Knight Commanders swear loyalty to a new Viscount — is not ceremonial performance. It is the renewal of a bond that predates every noble house on the Council.  

*   **Direct Authority:** Borderers answer to the Viscount alone. No Council lord, no Lord Mayor, and no noble house can legally redirect Borderer deployments.
*   **The Chamberlain Connection:** **Aldric Hollend** — former Knight Captain of the 3rd Company — now serves as the Viscount's Chamberlain, creating an institutional bridge between the Borderer command structure and the executive household of Castle Greyfist. Deleveu consults Aldric informally on Gnarley Forest diplomacy. Aldric uses his Borderer contacts to maintain intelligence channels the Council cannot access.
*   **Current Tension:** The Kron Hills withdrawal strained the relationship. Borderer commanders obeyed the order but consider the decision a strategic error. Wilfrick knows this. He has not reversed the decision because the political cost of reversal — appearing to capitulate to gnome pressure — is, in his calculation, higher than the military cost of absence. The Borderers consider this calculation incorrect.

 

##### The Militarist Faction — Velysin and Milinous

  Lords **Claviger Velysin** and [Simon Milinous](simon-milinous-person.md) are the Council's strongest voices for military mobilization — and the Borderers' natural political allies. Both men are soldiers themselves. Velysin is a former Mayor and current Captain of the Militia who fought beside the Viscount at Emridy Meadows. Milinous is a Knight Captain of the Borderers in his own right — a [House Milinous](house-milinous-organization.md) lord whose Emridy experience hardened him from a hopeful young knight into a stern, disciplined commander.   The militarists want the Borderers redeployed to the Kron Hills, the caravan routes secured, and the Viscounty's military posture strengthened against the Temple's resurgence. They are not wrong about the threats. What they lack is a plan that does not simultaneously bankrupt the treasury and shatter the gnome alliance — two consequences that the Viscount's advisors have told him are likely if he authorizes the mobilization the militarists demand.  

*   **Velysin:** Captain of the Militia. Shares professional respect with Borderer commanders as a fellow soldier. Advocates for projecting military strength. His relationship with the Borderers is founded on mutual recognition of service.
*   **Milinous:** Knight Captain of the Mounted Borderers and Lord of [House Milinous](house-milinous-organization.md). Dual role as noble lord and Borderer officer. Once a reliable Viscount ally, now visibly less committed — a man positioning himself for whatever comes next rather than investing in the current course. His Borderer rank gives him inside knowledge of deployment gaps that makes his Council arguments particularly effective.
*   **The Tension:** The militarists want action. The Viscount wants preparation. The Borderers are caught between a command structure that says wait and a strategic reality that says the waiting is getting people killed.

 

##### Lord Mayor Haxx and the Economic Interests

  Lord Mayor [Marakios Willem Haxx](marakios-willem-haxx-person.md) wants the trade roads safe — not because he cares about the Borderers as an institution, but because disrupted commerce costs his guild network revenue. His proposed solutions tend toward guild-funded enforcement and taxed mercenary contracts through organizations like [The Gentle Tradewinds](the-gentle-tradewinds-organization.md) — which solve the road problem while expanding his political footprint at the Borderers' expense.  

*   **Haxx's Goal:** Replace Borderer functions on trade routes with guild-controlled security forces answerable to the Lord Mayor's office rather than the Viscount.
*   **The Borderer Response:** Quiet institutional resistance. Galans refused to redirect 4th Company patrols to cover Gentle Tradewinds shipping. Deleveu maintains Longrider patrols on routes Haxx considers secondary. The Borderers see Haxx's proposals as an attempt to privatize their mission.
*   **The Danger:** If the Borderers' recruitment crisis deepens and their strength continues to decline, Haxx's alternative — guild mercenaries — becomes the practical reality whether anyone approves it or not. The Lord Mayor does not need to defeat the Borderers politically. He only needs them to become insufficient.

 

##### Lady Langmuir and the Opposition

  Lady [Godeleva Langmuir](godeleva-langmuir-person.md), the "Iron Lady," is no friend of the Viscount or his institutions. Her opposition to the Borderers is indirect but structural: she supports anything that weakens the Viscount's independent power base, and the Borderers are the clearest expression of that power. She does not attack the Borderers openly — that would be politically expensive. She simply ensures that Council discussions about Borderer funding, recruitment, and deployment are more difficult than they need to be.  

*   **Method:** Procedural obstruction in Council. Questioning Borderer budgets. Raising jurisdictional disputes when Borderers operate near Langmuir territory.
*   **Goal:** Weaken the Viscount by constraining his most loyal institution. Whatever Langmuir is positioning her son Ludovic for, it requires a Viscounty with less executive authority, not more.

 

#### Operations and Culture

  Life as a Mounted Borderer is not garrison duty. It is weeks in the saddle, sleeping in way stations and forest camps, maintaining relationships with communities that range from welcoming to hostile, and carrying the Viscount's authority into places where authority is a concept rather than a fact. The Borderers' distinctive culture — part cavalry regiment, part frontier ranger, part traveling court — reflects the reality of their mission.  

*   **Small Group Tactics:** Borderers operate in patrols of twenty to thirty soldiers led by a Knight Bachelor or Knight Bannerette and supported by Sergeants at Arms. Each patrol is a self-sufficient unit capable of sustained independent operations.
*   **Equipment:** Light warhorses (gnome patrols ride ponies by comparison). Shortbows cased on saddles, longswords sheathed. Enlisted ranks wear studded leather with steel caps. Officers in chain or plate with personal weapons.
*   **Special Missions:** Select soldiers are detached for high-stakes assignments requiring diplomacy, espionage, or covert capability. These missions are authorized by the Viscount or his Chamberlain and are not discussed in Council.
*   **Intelligence Function:** Every patrol generates intelligence — observations about road conditions, settlement status, suspicious activity, merchant traffic, and the movements of people who should not be where they are. This intelligence feeds into Castle Greyfist through command channels and, informally, through the Chamberlain's back-channel relationships with former Borderers.
*   **Community Bonds:** Borderers marry within the ranks. Veterans establish businesses serving active soldiers. The Grim Wolf Inn is the most famous example — a Borderer haven that functions as an unofficial message drop, reunion point, and intelligence exchange.
*   **Allied Relationships:** Formal cooperation with the [Woodsmen of the Gnarley Forest](woodsmen-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) (shared frontier, common values), the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) (joint patrols, intelligence sharing), and historically with gnome militia of the [Free Alliance](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md) (now strained since the withdrawal).

 

#### 576 CY: The Borderers Under Pressure

  The Mounted Borderers face a convergence of crises in 576 CY that threatens to overwhelm an institution that was already stretched before any single crisis arrived. Each pressure compounds the others, and the Borderers' traditional response — ride harder, patrol longer, hold the line — is running into the limits of what finite soldiers can accomplish.  

*   **Recruitment Crisis:** The ranks are thinning. Qualified candidates are scarce. The demanding lifestyle, the extended deployments, and the political uncertainty around the Borderers' future are discouraging enlistment. Every patrol that rides out is smaller than the one before it.
*   **Kron Hills Vacuum:** The withdrawal left two companies' worth of patrol routes unridden. Raiders have filled the gap. Gnome caravans are being hit. The roads the Borderers used to protect are becoming dangerous — and the danger is being attributed to Borderer absence, not to the raiders causing it.
*   **Temple Resurgence:** Activity consistent with Temple of Elemental Evil operations is surfacing along routes the Borderers used to patrol. Coordinated caravan raids. Intelligence gaps that feel deliberate. Someone is directing operations with knowledge of patrol schedules and trade routes — information that should not be available outside the Borderer command structure.
*   **Trade Route Disruption:** Disrupted commerce costs the guild network revenue, which empowers Haxx's argument for private security alternatives. Every week the roads are unsafe is a week the Borderers lose ground to the Lord Mayor's proposals.
*   **Cult Activity:** Heightened vigilance against cult infiltration is stretching already thin resources. The Borderers are now fighting on multiple fronts — frontier defense, caravan protection, intelligence gathering, and internal security — with a force designed for the first two.
*   **The Intelligence Leak:** **Aldric Hollend** suspects Temple agents have penetrated the Viscounty's intelligence infrastructure. Patrol schedules, trade routes, and deployment information are reaching people who should not have it. The Chamberlain is investigating quietly, through his own Longrider channels, because if the leak is inside the Borderer command structure itself, official inquiries would alert the source.

 

#### Campaign Hooks

  The Mounted Borderers are one of the most versatile campaign tools in the Verbobonc sandbox. They provide the party with missions, contacts, opponents, and a living institution whose health directly reflects the Viscounty's ability to respond to the Temple crisis.  

*   **The Borderer Patrol:** The party encounters a Longrider patrol in the **Gnarley Forest** or along a Kron Hills road. The patrol is undermanned, overextended, and carrying information they need to deliver to Castle Greyfist. They need escort, reinforcement, or someone to investigate what they found but cannot pursue.
*   **The Recruitment Drive:** A Borderer commander approaches the party with an offer: serve as temporary auxiliaries on a specific mission. This is a test. Perform well, and the Borderers become a reliable patron. Perform poorly, and the Borderers remember — they always remember.
*   **The Intelligence Leak:** **Aldric Hollend** or Deleveu needs outside investigators — people not embedded in the Borderer command structure — to determine how Temple agents are obtaining patrol schedules. This investigation leads into the Borderer ranks themselves, where the party must identify a compromised soldier without destroying unit cohesion.
*   **The Withdrawn Route:** A gnome merchant hires the party to escort a caravan through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) — a route the Borderers used to patrol. The raiders who hit gnome caravans are too well-organized for ordinary bandits. Following the intelligence chain leads to Temple connections and potentially to the mine claim conspiracy.
*   **The Noble Arrest:** A Borderer officer asks the party to assist with an arrest — a noble lord whose estate is generating evidence of Temple collaboration. The Right of High Justice authorizes the arrest. The political consequences of executing it may be worse than the crime. The party must decide how far the Viscount's law extends when enforcing it could fracture the Council.
*   **The Borderer Deserter:** A former Borderer — discharged or deserted — approaches the party with information about what he saw on his last patrol. The information is valuable. The source is unreliable. Verifying the intelligence requires riding the same route that got the deserter discharged.

 

#### DM Notes: Running the Borderers

  The Mounted Borderers are not a static institution the party reads about — they are a living organization the party interacts with, relies on, and potentially transforms through their actions.  

*   **The Borderers as Barometer:** The health of the Mounted Borderers reflects the health of the Viscounty. Track their status as a campaign variable: every successful mission that supports the Borderers — recovering intelligence, protecting a patrol, solving the recruitment problem — strengthens Verbobonc's ability to respond to the Temple. Every failure weakens it. Let the party see the consequences.
*   **Commanders as NPCs:** Deleveu, Galans, Milinous, and Ryiis el Lendreer are fully realized characters with their own agendas, loyalties, and blind spots. Use them as quest givers, allies, obstacles, and sources of conflicting information. A mission from Deleveu may put the party at odds with Galans's territory. An order from the 1st Company may conflict with what the Longriders need.
*   **The Authority Problem:** When the party rides with a Borderer warrant, they carry the Viscount's authority. This opens doors — but it also makes them targets. Nobles who have something to hide will notice a Borderer-backed investigation before the investigators arrive. Use this to create tension: the party's authority is real, but so is the political cost of using it.
*   **The Withdrawal as Leverage:** The Kron Hills withdrawal is the single most useful political pressure point in the campaign. Gnome NPCs blame the Borderers' absence. Borderer NPCs blame the Viscount's advisors. The Viscount blames the situation. Everyone has a version of the story. The party can influence whether the Borderers return to the Kron Hills — and the political consequences of that decision ripple through the Council, the Free Alliance, and the Temple's strategic calculations.
*   **Aldric as Bridge:** The Chamberlain's Longrider past makes him the natural liaison between the party and the Borderer command structure. If the party has earned Aldric's trust, he can open Borderer doors that would otherwise remain closed. If they have not, the Borderers are polite, professional, and unhelpful.

### Mounted Borderers Organizational Chart

The Mounted Borderers are divided into four distinct companies, each with its own specific duties, headquarters, and leadership. Here's an overview of each company:  

#### 1st Company - High Guard of the Viscount

*   **Duty:** Directly responsible for the safety of Verbobonc City and the Viscount.
*   **Headquarters:** [Castle Greyfist](v1-3-castle-greyfist2C-citadel-of-the-viscount-landmark.md)
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Ryiis el Lendreer**, Knight Bannerette.

**Description:** This elite unit is comprised of Mounted Borderers who have demonstrated exceptional courage, resourcefulness, and honor. Their primary duties include ensuring the day-to-day safety of the Viscount, addressing potential threats, and executing special assignments directly from the Viscount. They are pivotal in averting many potential crises through their bravery and intelligence.  

#### 2nd Company – "The Spears of Hagthar"

*   **Duty:** Guarding the Western Viscounty, Ironwood, and the Southwestern Lortmils.
*   **Headquarters:** Fortress Hagthar.
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Egret Rhynehurst**, Knight Captain.

**Description:** Specializing in mountain warfare and combating the mysterious creatures of the west, the 2nd Company is highly skilled in various terrains and climates. Led by the disciplined and honorable Knight Captain Rhynehurst, the "Spears of Hagthar" are renowned for their organization and adherence to military protocol, earning them a reputation as the most honorable among the Mounted Borderers.  

#### 3rd Company – "The Longriders"

*   **Duty:** Operating in the Eastern Viscounty, the Gnarley Forest, and Southeastern Kron.
*   **Headquarters:** [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md).
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Corbin Deleveu**, Knight Captain.

**Description:** Known for their guerrilla tactics and strategic timing, the "Longriders" excel in forest and hill warfare. Their expertise in using the terrain to their advantage makes them exceptionally effective in their region. They are described as lone wolves, highly valued for their ability to outmaneuver and unsettle opponents.  

#### 4th Company

*   **Duty:** Protecting the Southern Viscounty and Northern Kron.
*   **Headquarters:** De Chute's Ford.
*   **Commanding Officer:** **Roland Galans**, Knight Captain.

**Description:** The newest addition to the Mounted Borderers, created by a previous Viscount in response to growing threats from the south. This company's formation underscores the Viscount's commitment to safeguarding the southern borders and the strategic Kron Hills. Their presence serves as a strong deterrent to potential aggressors and reinforces the Viscount's resolve to protect his realm.   Each company of the Mounted Borderers plays a crucial role in maintaining the security and law of the Viscounty of Verbobonc, showcasing the diversity of their responsibilities and the specialized skills they bring to their respective areas.
