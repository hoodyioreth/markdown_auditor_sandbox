Falrinth
========

#### TRUE ALLEGIANCE: Lolth

Falrinth is a rogue wizard of immense cunning, secret allegiances, and layered agendas. Though not officially part of the Greater Temple's hierarchy, he is a linchpin in the Temple's web of manipulation. He holds the **Orb of Golden Death**, a powerful relic tied to the Elder Elemental God — but serves Lolth, the Demon Queen of Spiders. His true ambition: to **undermine the elemental cults**, **release Zuggtmoy**, and **seize control of the Temple's arcane core**.

> “They fight over fire and stone while I hold the key to gods. Fools, every one of them.”

#### Rogue Wizard and Keeper of the Orb of Golden Death

Though not an official member of the Greater Temple, Falrinth is an important figure in the Temple's web of intrigue. A skilled and ambitious 8th-level magic-user, Falrinth operates independently, serving as both an ally and a potential liability to the Greater Temple.

#### Location

Falrinth resides in Area 337 of the Temple of Elemental Evil's third level. This location connects to the Escape Tunnel, a hidden route used by bandits allied with Falrinth and Smigmal Redhand that leads to the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md).

### TRUE ALLEGIANCE: Lolth, Demon Queen of Spiders

**Falrinth does not serve the Elder Elemental God. Falrinth does not serve Zuggtmoy. Falrinth serves [Lolth](lolth-person.md), the Demon Queen of Spiders.** This is the single most important fact about him, and the one that every other faction in the Temple — including Hedrack — does not know.

His work with the Orb of Golden Death, his experiments on the Elemental Nodes, his manipulation of [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) and exploitation of [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md)'s bandit network — all of it serves Lolth's strategic objective: ensure the Temple of Elemental Evil remains divided, weakened, and ultimately fails. If Zuggtmoy is freed in the process, Lolth gains leverage over a rival demon queen. If Zuggtmoy remains imprisoned, the Temple collapses without its patron. Either outcome serves the Spider Queen.

No one in the Temple's hierarchy — not [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md), not [Senshock](senshock-person.md), not [Romag](romag-person.md), not [Belsornig](belsornig-person.md) — knows Falrinth's true allegiance. Only three beings share this secret: Lolth herself, [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) (Lolth's other infiltrator, commanding the [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md)), and **Kriitch**, Falrinth's quasit familiar.

 

### The Lolth Connection — Falrinth and Lareth's Parallel Operation

Falrinth and [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) are Lolth's two agents inside the Temple of Elemental Evil, operating a coordinated but compartmented infiltration. They know of each other. They share intelligence when opportunity allows. But they operate independently to limit exposure — if one falls, the other continues.

 

##### How the Partnership Works

*   **Lareth (Surface):** Commands the Moathouse as the Temple's forward raiding base. Publicly serves Hedrack and the [Cult of the Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). Privately gathers intelligence on Temple operations, recruits agents loyal to him personally, and positions himself to seize power when Lolth gives the signal. Lareth's diary does **not** mention his Lolth allegiance — he keeps no written record of the partnership
*   **Falrinth (Dungeon):** Operates on the Temple's third level with no official title or faction allegiance. Studies the Elemental Nodes, holds the Orb of Golden Death, and manipulates Temple logistics through Smigmal and Feldrin's networks. His isolation is deliberate — he answers to no one inside the Temple, which means no one monitors his true activities
*   **Communication:** Falrinth and Lareth communicate through **Lucius Graeme**'s courier network without Lucius knowing the messages carry Lolth intelligence. They also use Kriitch as an invisible messenger — the quasit can travel between the third level and the surface undetected

 

##### The Orb of Golden Death — Lolth's Prize

The **Orb of Golden Death** is the most powerful artifact in the Temple and Falrinth has it. He keeps it hidden in his lair (Area 337), using it to:

*   **Experiment on the Elemental Nodes:** Mapping their boundaries, testing their stability, and determining how they can be repurposed — or collapsed — to serve Lolth's interests
*   **Disrupt Zuggtmoy's prison seals:** Falrinth is actively working to unlock the seals. This appears to serve Zuggtmoy, but Lolth's plan accounts for both outcomes. A freed Zuggtmoy is weakened and indebted. A still-imprisoned Zuggtmoy leaves the Temple without its patron
*   **Scry and manipulate Temple rituals:** The Orb gives Falrinth surveillance capability that no one in the Temple suspects. He uses this to stay ahead of factional politics and avoid detection

The Orb requires **four Elemental Power Gems** — one from each Elemental Node — to reach full power. Falrinth is actively hunting these gems. If he recovers all four, the Orb becomes capable of breaking Zuggtmoy's final prison seal. Only Lolth, Lareth, and Kriitch know Falrinth possesses the Orb.

 

##### The Underdark Escape Plan

Falrinth never fights to the death. He has prepared a layered escape plan that he will execute the moment his position in the Temple becomes untenable:

*   **Primary escape:** _Teleportation Circle_ to a pre-prepared destination in the Underdark — a Lolth-aligned drow outpost in the deep tunnels beneath the [Lortmil Mountains](lortmil-mountains-location.md). The circle is inscribed in his lair and can be activated in one round
*   **Secondary escape:** _Dimension Door_ into the Escape Tunnel (connecting to the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md)), then overland through the Gnarley Forest. This route is slower but does not require the Teleportation Circle
*   **Tertiary escape:** Flee into the Elemental Nodes themselves using the Orb. This is a last resort — the Nodes are dangerous even for Falrinth, but they are effectively unreachable by pursuit
*   **What he takes:** The Orb of Golden Death (always), his three ivory scroll tubes containing backup spellbooks, the portable shrine of Lolth, and Kriitch. Everything else is expendable
*   **What he destroys:** Before fleeing, Falrinth triggers glyph of warding traps in Areas 337 and 338 to destroy any remaining research notes and ward inscriptions. He leaves nothing useful for the Temple or the party

 

##### Evidence the Party Can Find Connecting Falrinth to Lolth

Falrinth is careful, but not perfect. The following evidence can connect him to Lolth if the party knows what to look for:

*   **Portable Shrine of Lolth (Area 337):** Hidden among his possessions. DC 14 Investigation to find it during a thorough search of his lair. DC 12 Religion to identify it as a Lolth devotional object. This is the most direct proof of his allegiance
*   **Kriitch's behavior:** The quasit familiar occasionally whispers in Abyssal dialect associated with Lolth's domain, not the Elder Elemental God's. DC 16 Arcana or DC 14 Religion (with knowledge of fiend lore) to notice the distinction
*   **Spider motifs in ward inscriptions:** Falrinth's magical wards in Areas 337–338 incorporate subtle spider-web geometric patterns into the standard elemental warding symbols. DC 18 Arcana to detect. A character who has previously encountered Lolth-aligned magic (drow temples, Underdark encounters) recognizes the pattern on a DC 14 Arcana check instead
*   **Lareth's diary (cross-reference):** Lareth's diary at the Moathouse references correspondence with an unnamed "ally within the Temple's depths." It does not name Falrinth or Lolth, but a party that has found both the diary and the portable shrine can connect the two with a DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check
*   **Falrinth's journal (Area 337):** His journal discusses the Orb, the Elemental Nodes, and Zuggtmoy's prison in detail — but never mentions Lolth directly. However, DC 17 Insight while reading the journal reveals that Falrinth writes about Zuggtmoy's freedom as a _means_, not an _end_ — he does not serve her, he is using her. This realization reframes everything else the party has learned

##### DM Notes: Why the Lolth Connection Matters

The Falrinth-Lareth-Lolth axis is the deepest conspiracy in the Temple of Elemental Evil. It transforms the campaign from a dungeon crawl against elemental cultists into a three-way power struggle between Zuggtmoy (imprisoned, trying to break free), the [Cult of the Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) (unknowingly serving Zuggtmoy), and Lolth (manipulating both sides through Falrinth and Lareth).

For the DM, this means:

*   **Falrinth is never a simple enemy.** He can be negotiated with if the party offers something Lolth values. He can be turned against the Temple if the party threatens to expose him. He can even become a temporary ally against Zuggtmoy — though he will betray them the moment the Orb is within reach
*   **Discovering the Lolth connection changes the party's understanding of everything.** Every faction in the Temple is fighting a war they think they understand. Only Falrinth and Lareth know there is a third player at the table
*   **If Falrinth escapes to the Underdark with the Orb, the campaign has a sequel hook.** Lolth now possesses the most powerful artifact from the Temple, and Falrinth becomes an ongoing threat operating from drow territory

#### Role in the Temple of Elemental Evil

Falrinth lives in isolation on the third level of the Temple, his lair guarded by traps, illusions, and his quasit familiar **Kriitch**. He holds **no official title**, yet his knowledge of the Temple's deepest secrets—especially the Elemental Nodes—makes him one of its most dangerous figures.

*   **Holds the Orb of Golden Death**, kept secret from all but Lolth and Lareth
*   **Guides** [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) from the shadows as her “arcane benefactor”
*   **Exploits Commander Feldrin’s bandit network** for secret rituals and rare components
*   **Co-leads Lolth’s infiltration efforts** with [Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful, undermining both Iuz and Zuggtmoy

> _"They say the Elder Elemental God whispers in the dark. I’ve heard those whispers... and one day, I will whisper back."_

> _"Every move I make is a step closer to freedom—from the Temple, from the Eye, from all of it. I’ll see them all burn before I’m bound again."_

### Possession of the Orb of Golden Death

Falrinth secretly possesses the Orb of Golden Death, an artifact tied to the Elder Elemental God and central to the Temple’s power. He keeps the Orb hidden from his allies and superiors, viewing it as his ultimate bargaining chip or tool for personal ascension. Falrinth keeps the **Orb of Golden Death** hidden in his lair, using it to:

*   **Experiment on the Elemental Nodes**
*   **Disrupt the magical seals imprisoning Zuggtmoy**
*   **Scry, ward, and manipulate Temple rituals without detection**

Only Lolth, Lareth, and Kriitch are aware of his possession of the Orb.

> _"The Orb holds secrets even the gods fear to reveal. And now those secrets are mine to command—or destroy."_ \- Falrinth

##### Integrating Lolth, Lareth, and Falrinth into the Temple of Elemental Evil's Structure

##### Lolth’s Influence on the Temple of Elemental Evil

[Lolth](lolth-person.md), the Demon Queen of Spiders, has a vested interest in ensuring that the Temple of Elemental Evil never rises to its full potential. Her motivation is rooted in past betrayals by House Eilservs of the [Dark Elves](dark-elves-ethnicity.md) Drow, who abandoned her worship in favor of the Elder Elemental God. While she opposes the Temple's leadership, she is careful to work from the shadows, using spies and agents to manipulate events in her favor.

*   **Key Motivation:** Ensuring that the Temple remains divided and never achieves its true potential.
*   **Methodology:** Infiltration, subterfuge, and the use of key operatives such as Lareth the Beautiful and Falrinth.
*   **Artifact of Power:** Lolth possesses the key to the Elder Elemental God's prison, making her one of the few beings capable of deciding the fate of this mysterious entity.

##### Zuggtmoy’s Secret Control Over the Temple’s Clerics

The clerics and priests of the Temple believe they are praying to the Elder Elemental God, but in truth, their divine powers and spells are granted by Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi. The Abyss itself acts as a conduit for chaotic power, allowing Zuggtmoy to masquerade as the Elder Elemental God without the priests realizing the deception.

*   **Key Deception:** The Temple’s priests believe they serve the Elder Elemental God, but their power actually comes from Zuggtmoy.
*   **Reason:** Zuggtmoy is imprisoned within the Temple and needs the cult to continue worshiping her to gain strength.
*   **Implication:** If the truth were revealed, it could lead to massive schisms within the Temple's leadership.

##### Lareth the Beautiful – Lolth’s Hidden Pawn

[Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful is one of Lolth’s most promising agents. He controls the [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md), a stronghold designed to recruit new members to the Temple. However, his ultimate goal is to rise within the Temple’s ranks and convert it into a force that serves Lolth rather than Zuggtmoy.

*   **Role:** Lolth’s primary infiltrator within the Temple's ranks.
*   **Long-Term Goal:** To seize control of the Temple and redirect it toward the worship of Lolth.
*   **Secret Powerbase:** The Moathouse, where he gathers forces loyal to him while feeding selective information back to Hedrack.

##### Falrinth – The Sorcerer in Service to Lolth

[Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) is an 8th-level magic-user with a quasit familiar named Kriitch, which allows him to function as a 9th-level caster when nearby. As a secretive and ambitious figure, he serves Lolth but is ultimately self-interested, willing to betray her if it serves his purposes.

*   **Role:** A powerful sorcerer acting as a spy and potential saboteur within the Temple.
*   **True Allegiance:** Lolth, though he harbors personal ambitions that may lead to betrayal.
*   **Key Objective:** Investigating the elemental nodes of the Temple on behalf of Lolth, while keeping much of his research hidden from both the Temple’s leadership and his own goddess.

##### Final Implications

**Potential Conflict:** If Lolth’s agents gain too much power, the Temple may split into warring factions—those still loyal to Zuggtmoy and those who turn toward Lolth.  

##### Adventurer Hooks

*   Discovering Falrinth’s hidden research on the elemental nodes.
*   Exposing Lareth’s double-dealings and secret connections to the drow.
*   Preventing or enabling Lolth’s ultimate plan to co-opt the Temple.
