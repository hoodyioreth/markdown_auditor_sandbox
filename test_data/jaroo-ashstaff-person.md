Jaroo Ashstaff
==============

Jaroo Ashstaff is a venerable druid residing in the village of Hommlet, where he serves as the spiritual leader for those who follow the Old Faith. Despite his frail appearance, Jaroo commands deep respect and wields significant influence in the community. His primary role is to maintain the natural balance and protect the village from any rising threats, particularly those stemming from the Temple of Elemental Evil.

> Jaroo Ashstaff is a Level 7 druid of [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md) stationed in the village of Hommlet by Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) to monitor for any resurgence of the Temple of Elemental Evil. A veteran of [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) and a quiet authority among Hommlet's leadership, Jaroo operates from [24\. The Druid's Grove](24-the-druidE28099s-grove-landmark.md) where he maintains the natural balance, tends to Old Faith rituals, and has recently grown alarmed by escalating bandit activity and missing communications with his superiors in the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md)

##### Summary

Jaroo Ashstaff is a Level 7 druid of [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md), stationed in the village of Hommlet for over a decade as the eyes and ears of the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md). He reports directly to Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md), who oversees the western Gnarley and has grown concerned that Jaroo's reports have stopped arriving. Jaroo is a veteran of [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) (569 CY), where he fought alongside Robilar and Prince Thrommel IV to destroy the Temple of Elemental Evil. Now 62 years old, he serves on the Hommlet Council alongside [Elder Kenter Sr.](elder-kenter-sr-person.md), Captain [Zeb Fletcher](zeb-fletcher-person.md), [Gundigoot](gundigoot-person-1.md), and Canon [Terjon](terjon-person.md) — though his primary loyalty is to the forest, not the village's political structure.

> First Encounter:   The grove smells of wet bark and crushed sage. An old Flan man kneels at a rock cairn, whispering something to a heap of acorns and wildflowers. A staff carved with spiraling runes leans against an oak wide enough to hide a cart. The bear sees you first — a black shape shifting in the undergrowth behind the cottage — but the old man speaks without turning. "You've come a long way. Sit. The forest already told me."

##### Motivation & Personality

**Primary Drive:** Protect the balance of nature in the Hommlet region and prevent any resurgence of the Temple of Elemental Evil — the mission he was sent here to fulfill. **Secondary Drive:** Preserve the Old Faith's influence in a community that is steadily converting to the worship of [St. Cuthbert](st-cuthbert-person.md), not out of jealousy but because he fears the loss of respect for the natural order. **Hard Limit:** Jaroo will not leave Hommlet to accompany adventurers into danger. His mission is to watch, report, and guide — not to fight another man's war. The one exception: if the Grove itself is threatened, he will defend it with lethal force.

### Appearance and Demeanor

Jaroo is an elderly Flan man — weathered skin, long grey hair falling in untamed locks, dark eyes that seem to evaluate everything they rest on. He wears simple robes marked with Old Faith symbols and carries a wooden staff carved with spiraling druidic runes. Despite his frailty, he moves with deliberate precision. He speaks rarely and in measured sentences. When he does offer advice, it sounds like a warning. He keeps his own counsel, shares information only when he judges the recipient worthy of it, and never volunteers more than is asked. **Notable Physical Details:**

*   Height 5'11", thin build, but straight-backed and alert
*   Dark eyes that seem to look through people rather than at them
*   Hands stained with herb paste and earth — always working
*   A faint scent of sage and wet stone clings to his robes

### Background and History

Jaroo was born in 514 CY among the Flannae Flan people. He was recruited into the Old Faith as a young man and trained in the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) under the tutelage of the druidic circles. When the Temple of Elemental Evil rose to power, Jaroo fought at [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) in 569 CY alongside Robilar, Prince Thrommel IV, and other forces of good. That victory broke the Temple — but Jaroo knows from experience that evil of that scale is never truly destroyed, only suppressed.   After the battle, the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) assigned Jaroo to Hommlet as a permanent watcher. His orders from Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) are clear: monitor the region around the [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) and the old Temple site for any signs of cult resurgence, report regularly to Hrudek via animal messenger, and maintain a foothold for the Old Faith in a community increasingly dominated by the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md).   He has been in Hommlet for over a decade. In that time, he has built [24\. The Druid's Grove](24-the-druidE28099s-grove-landmark.md) into a functioning sanctuary, earned a seat on the village council, trained an apprentice (**Ono**, a half-elven initiate), and accumulated a modest hoard of gemstones — an eccentric habit he does not explain.

### The Chain of Command: Jaroo and the Gnarley Druids

Understanding Jaroo's position requires understanding the hierarchy he answers to. The [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) operate under a clear command structure within [The Old Faith](the-old-faith-organization.md):   **Great Druid Naimur** — Spiritual fulcrum of the entire Gnarley Forest, the highest-ranking druid in the region. Naimur served briefly as Grand Druid of the Flanaess before returning to the Gnarley. He sets broad policy but delegates operational matters to his two Archdruids.   **Archdruid Hildefer Paravis** — Oversees the eastern Gnarley (Dominion of Greyhawk side). Not Jaroo's direct superior, but aware of his assignment.   **Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md)** — Jaroo's direct superior. Oversees the western Gnarley bordering the Viscounty of Verbobonc. A pragmatic leader who maintains a network of scouts and informants including woodsmen, Gnarley Rangers, and friendly elves. Hrudek lives in an ancient ruined temple deep in the forest, guarding Ur-Flannae catacombs from tomb raiders. He lists Jaroo and **Kella** as his most loyal and capable field agents.   **Jaroo Ashstaff** — Field operative in Hommlet. His job: observe, report, and maintain the Old Faith's local presence. He is not a policy-maker — he is an intelligence asset stationed at the border between civilization and the Temple's sphere of influence.

##### The Missing Reports

**This is a live campaign problem.** Hrudek has grown concerned because Jaroo's expected reports have stopped arriving. The Archdruid's article states this explicitly: _"Missing Reports from Jaroo Ashstaff: Hrudek has grown concerned about Jaroo Ashstaff, a druid stationed in the village of Hommlet, who has failed to send his expected reports."_   The DM should decide **why** the reports have stopped. Options:

*   **Interception:** Jaroo's animal messengers (birds) are being intercepted by agents of the [Cult of Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — possibly **Lareth the Beautiful's** network or the Hommlet spy ring run through **Rannos Davl** and **Gremag**. DC 14 Perception check by the party while near the Grove might spot a dead hawk with a tiny scroll case, signs of a hunting falcon, or a magical snare (DC 16 Arcana to identify as a glyph trap targeting small beasts).
*   **Caution:** Jaroo has deliberately held back reports because he suspects he is being watched and does not want to reveal the druidic communication channel. This makes him appear more paranoid and secretive to the party but is strategically sound.
*   **Disruption:** The corruption spreading from the Moathouse area has disturbed the natural pathways — animals behave erratically near the old Temple roads, and Jaroo's messengers refuse to fly east. A Nature check DC 13 near the fens reveals birds and small mammals avoiding the area entirely.

**Campaign Impact:** If the reports remain missing, Hrudek will eventually send someone to check on Jaroo. This could be an adventuring party hired by the Gnarley druids (an alternate campaign hook), a ranger named **Eluvithyl** who arrives in Hommlet asking questions, or Hrudek himself — which would be a significant event given his CR 13 stat block and his two brown bear companions.

##### Current Operations

**1\. Monitoring the Moathouse and Bandit Activity:** Jaroo suspects the recent surge in banditry around Hommlet is connected to something larger. His animal scouts (hawks, crows, forest creatures) have reported unusual activity near the [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md), including groups of armed men moving at night and the smell of worked metal from the ruins. He suspects the bandits have taken refuge "in hidden areas of the forest, potentially near places where the Old Faith holds sway." He has shared this concern with the Hommlet Council.   **2\. Investigating the Darkwood Grove Corruption:** The Darkwood Grove, once a sacred site of the Old Faith near Hommlet, has become infested with sinister creatures. Jaroo needs the grove cleansed before whatever is corrupting it spreads further. This is his primary side quest for adventurers — see [1\. Clearing Out the Darkwood Grove](1-clearing-out-the-darkwood-grove---hommlet-plot.md).   **3\. Recovering Virgil Tamen's Ale:** The hijacking of **Virgil Tamen's** ale shipment by bandits is both a personal matter (Tamen is Old Faith) and an intelligence indicator — the bandits are growing bold enough to hit targets they previously would have avoided. See [3\. Tamen's Hijacked Ale](3-tamenE28099s-hijacked-ale---hommlet-plot.md).   **4\. Restoring Communication with Hrudek:** Jaroo knows his reports have not been reaching the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md). He is quietly trying to determine why — sending test messages via different animal species, varying the routes, and watching for interception. He has not told anyone in Hommlet about this problem.  

### Adventure Hooks

**1\. The Druid's Request (Pre-Moathouse):** If the party visits the Grove and shows respect for the Old Faith (DC 10 Religion check to recognize the altar, or simply asking about druidic matters), Jaroo will ask them to investigate the Darkwood Grove corruption. Reward: A blessing from Jaroo (advantage on one saving throw against poison or disease within the next 7 days) and a deeper understanding of the local druidic network.   **2\. The Stolen Ale (Pre-Moathouse):** Jaroo mentions that **Virgil Tamen**, a faithful brewer, has lost a shipment to bandits. If the party recovers the ale, Jaroo offers healing services (up to 3 castings of Cure Wounds at no charge) and shares his suspicion that the bandits are operating from somewhere to the northeast — pointing toward the Moathouse without naming it directly.   **3\. The Silent Messenger (Any Time):** A dead hawk is found near the Grove with a tiny scroll case strapped to its leg. Inside: a coded message in Druidic script. If the party brings this to Jaroo, he recognizes it as one of his intercepted reports to Hrudek. His face goes pale. He will not explain the full significance but asks the party — urgently — to investigate who is killing his birds. DC 14 Survival to track the kill pattern reveals the hawks are being taken by a trained hunting falcon, suggesting a deliberate counterintelligence operation. This trail leads to **Rannos Davl** and **Gremag's** trading post, where a falcon roost is hidden on the upper floor (DC 16 Investigation to find).   **4\. Hrudek's Emissary (Mid-Campaign):** If the party has not restored Jaroo's communication line within 2-3 sessions, Hrudek sends **Eluvithyl**, a half-elven ranger-druid, to Hommlet to check on Jaroo. Eluvithyl arrives asking pointed questions at the Welcome Wench and may mistake the party for Temple agents if they have been seen near the Moathouse. This creates an encounter that can go friendly or hostile depending on the party's reputation.   **5\. The Apostate's Farm (Post-Bandit Investigation):** If the party discovers that **Demien Trevil** — a former druid of the Old Faith — has been harboring bandits and routing loot to the Moathouse, Jaroo will ask to be told everything. His reaction: cold, controlled fury. He declares Trevil an apostate and asks the party to bring him back alive for druidic judgment. If the party complies, Jaroo sends Trevil to Hrudek in the Gnarley under escort — a significant narrative beat that strengthens the party's relationship with the druidic hierarchy.  

### After the Moathouse: Jaroo's Response to the Lareth Discovery

**This section covers what happens when the party returns from the [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) after driving off [Lareth](lareth-person.md) and his forces.**  

##### Immediate Reaction

When the party reports to the council (or directly to Jaroo) that [Lareth](lareth-person.md) — a cleric of the [Cult of Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) — was commanding cultists and bandits from beneath the Moathouse, Jaroo's reaction proceeds in three stages:   **Stage 1 — Grim Confirmation (Immediate):** Jaroo does not look surprised. He looks vindicated and afraid. He has suspected Temple activity for months but lacked proof. Now he has it. He will say something like: _"Then the rot runs deeper than I feared. If the Moathouse held a cleric of the Elder Elemental Eye, he did not work alone. This is a network, not a lone wolf."_   **Stage 2 — Intelligence Debrief (Within the Hour):** Jaroo will ask the party detailed questions — not about the combat, but about the evidence:

*   Did they find letters, orders, or correspondence? (He wants to know who Lareth reported to.)
*   Did they find cult symbols — specifically the Elder Elemental Eye? (This confirms the Temple connection rather than a splinter group.)
*   Did they find evidence of other agents in Hommlet? (He suspects **Rannos Davl** and **Gremag** but needs proof.)
*   Was Lareth killed or did he escape? (If escaped, Jaroo considers this the worst possible outcome — a compromised operative who knows the region and will seek revenge or reinforcements.)

  **Stage 3 — Emergency Report to Hrudek (Same Day):** Jaroo will attempt to send an emergency report to Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) immediately. If his bird messengers are still being intercepted, he will ask the party to physically carry a sealed message into the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) and deliver it to Hrudek's compound. This creates a significant side quest: a journey into the western Gnarley to meet the Archdruid himself.  

##### What Jaroo Knows (and Shares) After the Moathouse

Once the Moathouse is cleared, Jaroo becomes significantly more forthcoming with information. He now considers the party proven allies. The DM can use Jaroo to deliver the following intelligence:   **On the Temple's Structure:** _"Seven years ago, I watched the Temple burn. But fire does not kill an idea. The Elder Elemental Eye is a god of corruption — it works through cells, through agents, through the ambitious and the desperate. If Lareth was running a cell from the Moathouse, there are others. The Temple was divided into factions — Earth, Fire, Water, Air. Each jealous of the others. That division was their weakness before. Pray it is again."_   **On the Spy Network in Hommlet:** Jaroo has long suspected that someone in Hommlet is funneling intelligence to the cult, but he has no hard proof. He will name **Rannos Davl** and **Gremag** as his primary suspects: _"Those traders arrived in Hommlet too conveniently, and their prices are too good. Honest merchants do not undercut the competition in a village this small unless they are subsidized. Someone is paying their losses."_ If the party has already found evidence linking the traders to the cult, Jaroo will push the council to act immediately.   **On Nulb:** _"If you wish to find where Lareth's supply chain leads, follow the road north to the river village they call Nulb. It is a nest of pirates, smugglers, and worse. The cult has roots there — deep ones. Be careful. Nulb is not Hommlet. There is no council, no law, and no one to hear you scream."_   **On the Gnarley Druids' Concern:** Jaroo will finally reveal his chain of command to the party: _"I am not merely a village holy man. I was placed here by the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) to watch for exactly this. My superior, Archdruid Hrudek, has been waiting for my reports — reports that have not reached him. If the cult was intercepting my messages, they know about me. Which means they may also know about you."_  

##### Mechanical Consequences (Post-Moathouse)

*   **Jaroo unlocks as a full quest-giver:** He will now offer healing services freely (up to his daily spell slots), provide Nature and Survival consultation (effective DC bonus of +3 on checks related to the Kron Hills and Gnarley edge), and serve as a safe house for the party to rest and plan.
*   **Council support solidifies:** Jaroo argues forcefully at the next council meeting that the party should be granted official backing — supplies, militia escort to the forest edge, and a letter of introduction to Borderer commanders.
*   **Hrudek connection becomes available:** The party can now travel to meet [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) in the western Gnarley. Hrudek is a CR 13 Archdruid with legendary actions, two brown bear companions, and a network of allies. He can provide: mission direction toward the Temple, druidic magic items (scrolls, potions of Barkskin or Neutralize Poison), and intelligence about cult movements in the forest border region.
*   **Reputation shift:** If the party cleared the Moathouse and reported to Jaroo, their reputation with Old Faith NPCs across the campaign improves by one tier. Gnarley Rangers encountered in the wilderness will have heard of them. This matters when the party eventually enters the deeper Gnarley or seeks druidic allies.

 

##### Jaroo at the Council — Post-Moathouse Session

When the [Elder Kenter Sr.](elder-kenter-sr-person.md) convenes the council to hear the party's report (see the Summons to the Council of Hommlet quest), Jaroo plays a specific role:   **Position:** He argues that the bandit problem and the Moathouse cult cell are two faces of the same threat. He pushes for immediate action against suspected Temple agents in the village (**Rannos Davl**, **Gremag**) and for the party to be resourced for a longer campaign against the cult.   **Tension with Terjon:** Canon [Terjon](terjon-person.md) will want to handle the spiritual dimension through the [Church of St Cuthbert](church-of-st-cuthbert-organization.md). Jaroo sees this as jurisdictional overreach — the Temple is an Old Faith problem first, because it was the druids who fought at Emridy Meadows and the druids who have been watching the ruins ever since. This creates a tense but nonviolent political subplot for the DM to exploit.   **DC Guidance for the Council Scene:**

*   DC 12 Insight to notice Jaroo is holding back information he does not want Terjon to hear
*   DC 14 Persuasion (by the party) to get Jaroo to reveal his druidic chain of command publicly — he would prefer to keep this private
*   DC 10 Persuasion to get Jaroo to support the party's preferred course of action at the council, provided it does not conflict with the druidic mission

##### GM Notes

**Combat Priority:** Jaroo will not fight unless the Grove is directly threatened or the party is defending Hommlet against a cult assault. If forced to fight, he leads with Entangle to control the battlefield, activates the Staff of the Serpent to create a grapple threat, then supports with Conjure Animals (8 wolves or 2 brown bears) while staying at range behind Barkskin. If losing, he goes invisible and retreats into the forest.   **Negotiation Pressure Points:** Jaroo responds to respect for the Old Faith, evidence of Temple activity, and appeals to the natural balance. He shuts down when pressed for information he considers above the party's clearance. The best way to earn his trust is to complete a quest for him — actions speak louder than words.   **What Breaks Jaroo:** News that **Demien Trevil** has betrayed the Old Faith hits him harder than anything else. He trained with Trevil decades ago. If the party delivers this news, Jaroo will be visibly shaken — the only time in the campaign he drops his composure. Exploit this moment for roleplay.   **Jaroo as a Long-Term Campaign Asset:** After the Moathouse, Jaroo becomes the party's primary connection to the druidic power structure. Through him, they can access [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md), the Gnarley Rangers, and eventually **Great Druid Naimur** himself. This chain matters in the mid-to-late campaign when the party needs allies against the Temple's full strength. Do not waste Jaroo as a one-and-done quest giver — he is a slow-burn NPC whose value increases with every session.

##### Campaign Role Reference

**Function:** Intelligence asset, quest giver, druidic liaison **Council Seat:** Hommlet Village Council (Old Faith representative) **Reports To:** Archdruid [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) **Mission:** Monitor for Temple of Elemental Evil resurgence **Status:** Reports to Hrudek have stopped (cause TBD by DM)

##### DM Quick Reference

**All DCs and Checks:**

*   DC 10 Religion — Recognize the Grove's altar as Old Faith
*   DC 12 Persuasion — Befriend Ono for intel on Jaroo's behavior
*   DC 12 Insight — Notice Jaroo holding back info at council
*   DC 13 Nature — Detect animal avoidance near Moathouse fens
*   DC 14 Perception — Spot dead hawk with scroll case near Grove
*   DC 14 Persuasion — Get Jaroo to reveal chain of command publicly
*   DC 14 Survival — Track hawk kill pattern to trained falcon
*   DC 15 Spell Save DC — All Jaroo's offensive/control spells
*   DC 16 Arcana — Identify magical snare targeting animal messengers
*   DC 16 Investigation — Find falcon roost at Rannos/Gremag's trading post
*   DC 18 Investigation — Find Jaroo's gemstone hoard under altar

* * *

**Key Combat Stats:**

*   Staff of the Serpent: Animates into giant constrictor snake (HP 60, grapple DC 16)
*   Conjure Animals: 8 wolves OR 2 brown bears
*   Ring of Invisibility: Emergency escape
*   Tanak (bear): HP 25, +4 to hit, bear hug DC 12

**Dialogue Triggers:**

*   Mention "Old Faith" or "Obad-Hai" → Jaroo warms noticeably
*   Mention "Temple" or "cultists" → Jaroo becomes alert, asks probing questions
*   Mention "Demien Trevil" + betrayal → Jaroo visibly shaken (only time he breaks composure)
*   Show cult symbols from Moathouse → Triggers Stage 1 reaction (grim confirmation)
*   Show golden monk mask → Triggers Sacred Stone Monastery info dump
