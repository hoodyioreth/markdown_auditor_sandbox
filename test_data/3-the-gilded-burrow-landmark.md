3\. The Gilded Burrow
=====================

_A Lavish Inn for the Wealthiest of Travelers in Kron_

#### **Introduction**

Tucked into the heart of Kron, **The Gilded Burrow** is the most extravagant inn and tavern in the village. Designed to cater to noble merchants, scholars, and adventurers with deep pockets, this **opulent establishment boasts fine wines, rare delicacies, and an ambiance of excessive luxury** rarely found in gnome settlements. Unlike the more rustic and charming burrows of Kron, The Gilded Burrow is a **testament to excess**, featuring **polished oak paneling, golden fixtures, and silk-draped walls.**

While its clientele enjoys the indulgence of **plush furnishings, enchanted chandeliers, and overpriced vintage spirits**, The Gilded Burrow is not without its reputation. **Coin flows freely here, but only in one direction—into the pockets of its owner.**

> "The wine is rich, the food is divine, and the regret comes the morning after."
> 
> — A regretful merchant from Verbobonc

* * *

### **Exterior and Structure**

##### **An Ornate Jewel Amongst Stone**

_Unlike the unassuming burrow homes of Kron, The Gilded Burrow stands as a beacon of excess._

*   Built **into a natural rise in the landscape**, but its **grand arched entrance** of lacquered wood and polished brass stands out.
*   A sign of **carved gold filigree** depicting a wine goblet and a heap of coins hangs above the doorway.
*   **A magical ever-burning lantern** bathes the entrance in warm golden light at all hours.
*   Large, tinted glass windows (a rarity in gnome architecture) allow passersby to glimpse **velvet curtains and flickering chandeliers.**
*   The **scent of exotic spices, aged wines, and smoked meats** wafts enticingly from within.

> "Drink up, my friend! I promise, the bill is the only thing that will hurt you in the morning."
> 
> — Hostren Velbrindle, laughing at a new customer

* * *

### **Interior and Atmosphere**

##### **A Burrow of Extravagance**

_The inside of The Gilded Burrow is designed to impress and intimidate in equal measure._

*   **Polished mahogany walls**, draped with **deep crimson silk**, give the interior an almost theatrical feel.
*   **Crystal chandeliers glow with soft magical light,** illuminating the room in a rich golden hue.
*   A grand **circular bar of carved ebony** dominates the main hall, attended by well-dressed servers pouring exotic wines.
*   **Fluffy velvet cushions and elaborately embroidered rugs** cover every seat and floor.
*   Subtle enchantments play **soft instrumental music**, adding to the luxurious atmosphere.

##### **The Rooms and Accommodations**

*   **Private chambers** offer **king-sized feather beds, silk sheets, and personal vanity desks.**
*   A **magical heating system** keeps every room warm, regardless of the season.
*   **Arcane books and alchemical tools** are available for rent (at an absurd price).
*   The more elite suites include **personal arcane illumination, heated baths, and self-replenishing wine decanters.**

### **Politics and Intrigue**

##### **The Hub of the Wealthy and the Ambitious**

*   Many **merchants, visiting diplomats, and nobles from Verbobonc** frequent The Gilded Burrow.
*   Deals are made, alliances are formed, and **fortunes are lost** over a bottle of wine.
*   It is said that Hostren has **connections with smugglers and illicit traders**, using the inn to conduct shady business.

##### **The Price of Indulgence**

*   **Running out of coin in The Gilded Burrow is a grave mistake.**
*   Guests who fail to pay their debts often **wake up in a different part of Kron—without their valuables.**
*   Some claim Hostren has **secret passages leading from the inn’s basement to the darker parts of the village.**

> "A bottle of Silverberry Wine and a warm bath? That’ll be five gold. No, I don’t care if that’s robbery."
> 
> — Hostren Velbrindle, unimpressed by complaints

##### **The Missing Merchant’s Ledger**

_A wealthy merchant left behind an important ledger before disappearing from The Gilded Burrow. Who took it—and why?_

##### **The Vanishing Debtors**

_Several travelers who couldn’t pay their tab have simply vanished overnight. Where did they go?_

##### **Fel’s Secret Past**

_Miss Felrietta has a bounty on her head. Someone from Verbobonc has come looking for her._

##### **The Gilded Betrayal**

_Hostren has been overheard speaking with a suspicious Verbobonc noble about a secret deal that could affect all of Kron._

**Conclusion**
--------------

The Gilded Burrow is the pinnacle of wealth and indulgence in Kron, but beneath its golden exterior lurks **greed, deception, and hidden dealings.** A perfect setting for adventurers who seek to **unravel intrigues, recover stolen fortunes, or simply enjoy a glass of the finest (and most expensive) wine in the Greenway Valley.**

##### **Hostren Velbrindle**

**The Lavish Proprietor**

*   A **portly, smug gnome in an embroidered velvet coat**, Hostren is the shrewd mastermind behind The Gilded Burrow.
*   He **never drinks his own wine**, preferring cheap ale from the back room while charging customers exorbitantly.
*   Maintains a **small army of well-trained servants** who ensure that patrons never question the pricing.
*   If a guest **can’t pay their bill, their belongings become property of the inn.**

##### **Miss Felrietta "Fel" Swiftrill**

**The Enchanting Bartender**

*   A **charming and silver-tongued halfling** who expertly convinces customers to **buy another round.**
*   Rumored to be a **former confidence trickster** who fled to Kron after conning a noble lord.
*   **Knows everything that happens in the inn** and sells secrets for the right price.

##### **Jivben "Jibs" Tumblesprock**

**The Silent Enforcer**

*   A **hulking gnome with an unsettling stare**, Jibs ensures **unruly guests are escorted out**—forcefully, if necessary.
*   Never speaks more than a few words, but **everyone knows better than to cross him.**
*   **Loyal to Hostren**, but has been seen secretly meeting with unknown figures in the Back Alleys of Kron.
