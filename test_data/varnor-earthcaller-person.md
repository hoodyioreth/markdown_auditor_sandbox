Varnor Earthcaller
==================

_High Priest of the Black Earth – Master of the Shattered Monastery_

Varnor Earthcaller is the **second-ranking priest of the Black Earth cult serving Romag**, High Priest of the Earth Temple within the **Temple of Elemental Evil** near the village of Nulb. Acting under Romag’s authority, Varnor commands the **Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath**, a ruined monastery hidden within the Kron Hills that now serves as a forward operating base for the cult’s raids and excavations.

Unlike many of Romag’s brutal lieutenants, Varnor is a **geomancer, scholar, and mystic of the deep stone**. Where others see the monastery as a convenient fortress, Varnor recognizes it as something far more important—a place where the earth itself whispers.

The ruins conceal a **forgotten Flan altar dedicated to the Elder Elemental God**, buried beneath centuries of stone and ritual warding. Varnor’s purpose at the monastery is not merely to command raiders or control territory; he seeks to awaken the ancient altar and listen to the voice that waits beneath the stone.

To Varnor, the monastery is not a base of operations—it is a **mouth through which the earth may finally speak again**.

* * *

#### Background

Varnor was born among the **Flan tribes of the Kron Hills**, descendants of the ancient peoples who once revered the spirits of earth and stone. Even as a child he displayed an uncanny sensitivity to tremors and vibrations within the ground, claiming he could hear “songs beneath the soil.”

These abilities eventually drew the attention of **Romag**, the powerful Earth Temple prophet serving the Temple of Elemental Evil. Recognizing Varnor’s natural affinity for geomantic forces, Romag brought him into the cult’s priesthood and began shaping him into something greater than a mere cleric.

Under Romag’s guidance, Varnor studied:

*   Flan geomantic rituals
*   ancient stone glyphs
*   the theology of the **Elder Elemental God**

When scouts discovered the ruined monastery in the Kron Hills—along with evidence of a buried altar to the Elder Elemental God—Romag appointed Varnor to lead the expedition.

The mission was simple:

Seize the monastery.  
Excavate the altar.  
Awaken what sleeps beneath the stone.

* * *

#### Personality

Varnor Earthcaller is quiet, contemplative, and unsettlingly calm. Unlike many cult leaders who rant and threaten, Varnor speaks softly and slowly, as though every word must pass through the weight of the earth before being spoken.

He often pauses mid-conversation to listen—tilting his head slightly as though hearing distant echoes beneath the ground.

To his followers, this behavior is a sign of divine communion. To outsiders, it is deeply unnerving.

Varnor genuinely believes the earth is alive, ancient, and patient. In his view, the world will eventually return to the dominion of stone and silence.

The Elder Elemental God is simply the voice that reminds the world of this inevitable truth.

* * *

#### Goals and Motivations

Varnor serves Romag with absolute loyalty, but his ambitions extend beyond the immediate success of the monastery.

His primary objectives are:

**Awaken the Altar of the Elder Elemental God  
**The broken altar beneath the monastery must be restored and activated.

**Open Communion with the Black Mouth  
**Deep beneath the ruins lies a sealed chasm whispered about in ancient Flan myths. Varnor believes this “Black Mouth” is a conduit to the Elder Elemental God.

**Expand the Cult’s Influence in the Kron Hills  
**Raids against merchants and settlements weaken the region while supplying resources for excavation.

**Recover Lost Flan Geomantic Knowledge  
**Varnor believes the ancient Flan once understood truths about the earth that modern civilizations have forgotten.

Ultimately, he seeks to become the **voice through which the earth speaks to the mortal world**.

#### Relationship to Romag

Romag is the undisputed **High Priest of the Earth Temple** within the Temple of Elemental Evil.

Varnor does not challenge this authority. Instead, he sees himself as **Romag’s instrument**, entrusted with the sacred task of awakening ancient powers.

Romag values Varnor for several reasons:

*   his deep knowledge of Flan geomancy
*   his loyalty and discipline
*   his ability to command both priests and raiders

While Romag focuses on the **political struggles within the Temple of Elemental Evil**, Varnor oversees the **field operations in the Kron Hills**.

If Varnor succeeds in awakening the altar beneath the monastery, Romag’s power within the Temple will grow dramatically.

#### Artifact: Stonefang

Varnor wields a strange weapon known as **Stonefang**, forged around a jagged shard believed to originate from the altar itself.

The weapon resembles a heavy stone hammer inscribed with spiral runes that shift slightly when exposed to geomantic energy.

Stonefang is not merely a weapon—it is a **key**.

When brought near the altar beneath the monastery, the shard resonates, amplifying the altar’s power and allowing Varnor to hear whispers from the deep earth.

These whispers grow stronger each day.

* * *

#### Appearance

Varnor is a tall Flan man with weathered skin and dark hair streaked with gray. His eyes are pale and reflective, like polished stone.

He dresses in layered robes of brown and black leather reinforced with stone fragments and metal studs. Around his neck hangs a carved pendant depicting the triangular symbol of the Earth Temple.

His hands are often dusted with fine stone powder, the result of constant ritual work and excavation.

When he walks, he carries **Stonefang** like a pilgrim’s staff rather than a weapon.

* * *

#### Combat Style

Varnor prefers to let others fight for him.

He commands cultists and raiders while using spells that manipulate earth and stone to control the battlefield.

Typical tactics include:

*   raising stone barriers
*   collapsing ceilings or tunnels
*   summoning elemental servants
*   striking with Stonefang only when necessary

If cornered, he fights with terrifying determination, believing death in service to the Elder Elemental God will only deepen his communion with the earth.

* * *

#### Rumors About Varnor

Local rumors circulating through the Kron Hills include:

*   “The priest in the monastery can hear the mountains breathing.”
*   “Miners say the ground trembles when he prays.”
*   “Some say he talks to something buried beneath the hill.”

Whether these rumors are exaggerations—or warnings—remains to be seen.

##### DM Insight

Varnor’s authority comes from **Romag’s appointment**, but his personal obsession lies deeper than cult politics. While Tharvok and Maris see the monastery as a strategic fortress and raiding base, Varnor believes the site sits atop a **living geomantic wound in the earth**.

If he succeeds in awakening the buried altar, Varnor may gain influence rivaling even Romag’s position within the Earth Temple hierarchy.

The cultists kneel upon the cold stone floor, their voices rising together in a slow, rhythmic chant that echoes through the chamber.

_“Stone beneath us, eternal and unbroken, hear the voices of those who serve the deep earth. As the towers of the faithless crumble and their bones sink into the dust, so too shall all things return to the dominion of earth. We kneel before the strength that endures when all else fades.
