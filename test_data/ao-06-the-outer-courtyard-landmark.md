AO-06. The Outer Courtyard
==========================

This courtyard is the transitional space between sacred past and profane present.

Once a ceremonial ground of procession for the Knights of the Azure Oath, this open-air courtyard now serves as Romag’s forward operating yard. Raiders gather here before departing for Long Road ambushes. Captured wagons are processed. Prisoners are sorted. Gnolls lair here when not hunting.

The Earth Temple does not attempt to restore the courtyard’s former glory — they defile it. Their presence is practical, brutal, and strategic.

This area should feel:

*   Militarized
*   Desecrated
*   Functional
*   Watchful

It is not chaos. It is organized occupation.

##### First Entry into the Courtyard

The heavy gate groans behind you as it shuts. Beyond it lies a broad courtyard open to the grey sky above. Broken cloister arches frame the perimeter, their columns fractured and leaning like old warriors refusing to fall. Rubble lies scattered across cracked flagstones.  
  
At the center, the dry basin of a once-grand fountain sits split by a jagged seam, its carved lightning motif worn nearly smooth.  
  
To the right, the roofline of old stables rises above the inner wall — lantern light flickers within.  
  
To the left, a shadowed doorway stands half-barricaded, and in the far corner a narrow guard door leads into the crumbling tower that watches the ascent trail below.  
  
Bone fragments and dark stains mark the stone near the western wall. Bedrolls and stacked crates occupy what was once ceremonial ground. The scent reaches you last — gnoll musk, damp straw, and old blood. The wind stills. Somewhere deeper within the ruin, a low savage laugh echoes.

### **Courtyard Layout & Key Features**

##### 6A. The Central Fountain (Desecrated)

Once dedicated to ceremonial purification, this stone basin now stands dry and cracked.

*   Lightning carvings (Heironeous symbol) worn by time
*   Basin split by impact or structural failure
*   Used now as refuse pit for broken gear and bones
*   Bloodstains frequently washed into its cracks

**Hidden Detail (DC 15 Investigation):  
**Under rubble in the basin lies a fragment of an original votive plaque bearing the Azure Oath.

* * *

##### 6B. Knights’ Stables (Right Side Wall – Interior Structure)

These were once honorable mounts’ quarters for the paladins of the Azure Oath.

Now:

*   Used by Black Earth Guards and gnoll riders
*   Holds 4–6 horses (stolen or seized from raids)
*   Crude black banners hang over old holy carvings
*   Gnoll nesting area in rear stall section

**Sounds:**

*   Hoof stamping
*   Low growls
*   Leather creaking
*   Occasional snapping of bone

**Tactical Note:  
**Gnolls use the stable interior as fallback position if courtyard breached.

* * *

##### 6C. Wagon Barn (Near Right Rear Wall)

A roofed stone addition built into the inner wall.

Purpose:

*   Storage for captured merchant wagons
*   Loot sorting
*   Prisoner holding before transfer

Inside:

*   1–3 confiscated merchant wagons
*   Trade goods awaiting transport to Nulb
*   Shackles mounted on inner posts

**Smell:** damp canvas, blood, sweat, animal musk

* * *

##### 6D. Processional Steps (Directly Opposite Main Gate)

Large stone stair ascending toward the main chapel level.

These steps were once ceremonial.

Now:

*   Used for prisoner display
*   Used for discipline
*   Used for sacrificial staging before crypt descent

Black Earth sigils scratched into stair faces.

* * *

##### 6E. West Wall – Bone Field

Near the western wall:

*   Gnoll defilement pit
*   Animal carcasses
*   Broken humanoid bones
*   Signs of ritual humiliation

This is intentional psychological warfare.

* * *

##### 6F. Left Side Access Door (Secondary Entry)

Leads deeper into monastery interior.

Currently:

*   Reinforced from inside
*   Guard rotation checks it frequently
*   Quick access for cultist priests

* * *

##### 6G. Guard Tower Door (Near Left Corner)

Small iron-bound door leading into the crumbling tower.

Tower Function:

*   Overlooks ascent trail
*   Signal position
*   Crossbow nest

Typically 1 guard stationed above during daylight.

* * *

#### **Faction & Political Context**

This courtyard is not random brutality.

It serves:

*   Romag’s recruitment machine
*   Gnoll alliance reinforcement
*   Caravan suppression campaign
*   Supply consolidation for Earth Temple

The Bonegnaw gnolls (10 assigned here) are loyal but opportunistic.  
Tension simmers between human cultists and gnolls.

##### **The Outer Courtyard (Surface Ruin)**

*   **Type:** Surface Courtyard / Staging Ground
*   **Original Use:** Processional yard of the Azure Oath; ceremonial gathering space for knights of Heironeous
*   **Current Use:** Black Earth garrison muster point; sorting area for raid spoils and prisoners
*   **Primary Occupants:** Black Earth Reavers (typical 3 on patrol; more respond on alarm)
*   **Adjacent Areas:**
*   **Main Gates** behind (entry from ascent)
*   **Processional Steps** ahead (6 steps) to the main monastery doors
*   **Stables** to the right (inside the walls; roofline visible)
*   **Left Access Door** to inner halls (secondary entry)
*   **Guard Tower Door** in near-left corner (to Southwest Guard Tower)
*   **Landmarks:** Broken cloister arches, fractured columns, rubble-strewn flagstones, dry split fountain basin at center
*   **Signs of Desecration:** Bone fragments and dark stains near the western wall; gnoll musk and old blood in the air
*   **Stealth Note:** Limited cover—best concealment is behind rubble piles, cloister arches, and fountain ruin
*   **Tactical Note:** Courtyard is the **primary reinforcement hub**—alarm here pulls barracks response quickly
*   **Clue Value:** Evidence of recent raids—crates, bedrolls, broken merchant goods, wagon tracks into stables area
*   **Escalation:** Loud fight triggers **tower overwatch** and **barracks mobilization** within 2–4 rounds
*   **Mood Beat:** A place once meant for honor now feels like a violated parade ground—laughter and cruelty echo from within
