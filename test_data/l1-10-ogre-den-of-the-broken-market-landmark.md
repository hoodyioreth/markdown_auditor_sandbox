L1-10. Ogre Den of the Broken Market
====================================

A single, smeared bloody handprint mars the door—too small for an ogre, the span of a Medium creature. Inside, a chill hangs over battered furnishings: a bedframe collapsed beneath a heap of matted furs, a large chair with grass stuffing bulging from splits, and a blanket-draped chest shoved near the wall. The far wall is a mural of a bustling market… until you look closer: slaves with animal features bend under loads, heavily armed guards watch from balconies, and white-robed priests torment a prisoner at a pillory.

Suel features & lore keys
-------------------------

*   **Market Mural (north wall):** From a distance, serene trade life; up close, the **anomalies** sharpen—sub-human porters, priests dispensing “justice,” archers on the colonnades.
*   **DC 12 Investigation:** Notice the corrections and over-painting; parts were **re-worked** to hide cruelty.
*   **DC 13 History/Religion:** Reads as a **Suel civic scene** turned authoritarian—ledger lines and trade glyphs (Xerbo/Osprem) buried under the later hand of **law-as-punishment** (Wee Jas) and spite (Beltar). Players who call this out gain **advantage on the next Insight** against Temple loyalists in this dungeon (they’ve “seen through the pageantry”).
*   **Defacement:** Scraped spirals in one corner hint at later **forbidden icon** cleansing; a smear of newer soot covers it.

A single **bloody handprint** stains the door. Inside, broken furniture crowds a den of furs and stink. A **market mural** bright with color masks something uglier—slaves bend and priests torment a prisoner while guards level spears from balconies. A blanket slouches over a chest; a giant chair’s stuffing shows through its wounds.

#### Features & checks

*   **Bloody handprint (door):** **DC 10 Medicine/Survival**—fresh within a day; **orc-sized**; blood mixed with ash (from nearby halls).
*   **Collapsed bed + furs:** **DC 11 Perception** reveals a **vermin scuttle**; disturbing the pile has a 1-in-6 chance to release a **Swarm of Insects** (flees if set alight or doused with oil smoke).
*   **Blanket-draped chest:** The **simple grey blanket** hides the lid; **DC 12 Investigation** notes it’s a **working chest**, not trapped. Inside are the ogre’s **hide sketches** (see Items).
*   **Large chair (broken):** Counts as **half cover** if tipped; the ogre can wrench a leg free as an **improvised club** (d6 bludgeoning).

#### Tactics & response

*   **If no prior alarm:** **Diirag** lounges in the furs, scratching and drinking. On seeing intruders he **rises and charges** with a laugh.
*   **Fighting style:** He tries to **bull rush** the largest foe, using the bed heap to block others, then **hurls** anything handy before closing with the greatclub. If bloodied and a doorway is blocked, he **grabs and throws** the chair to clear space (improvised 1d6 bludgeoning, 10/30).
*   **Response clock:** Any combat or shout here draws aid as follows:
*   **Rooms 11 & 12**: arrive **next round**.
*   **Chief + bodyguard**: **3 rounds**.
*   **Barracks warriors**: **6 rounds**.

#### Items (as found)

*   **Crude charcoal drawings on hide** inside the chest and tacked to the wall—**victims and kill scenes**, with **real blood** rubbed into wounds and splashes. (If shown to thinking foes, **Intimidation** checks against orcs who fear Diirag gain **advantage** once.)
*   Odds & ends: cracked mug, bone dice (worthless), bent nose ring.

#### Map notes

*   **Single entrance.** Place the **mural** on the wall opposite the door to command the eye.
*   **Bed + furs** along one long wall; **chair** near the mural; **chest** near the door.
*   Keep **10 ft** clear in the center so the ogre can build speed for a charge.

##### Running tips

*   Let the mural be a **moral mirror**; players who call out what they see earn social leverage later.
*   Use the response timing to create **pressure**—the den should feel like a lid about to blow once the fight starts.
*   If the party is quiet and clever, the **blanket** can be flung over Diirag’s head (contested **Athletics** on success, he fights **blinded** until he tears free (action).
