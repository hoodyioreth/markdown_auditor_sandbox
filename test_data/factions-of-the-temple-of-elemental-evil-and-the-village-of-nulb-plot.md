Factions of the Temple of Elemental Evil and the Village of Nulb
================================================================

##### Summary

This article maps every faction in play across the Temple of Elemental Evil sandbox campaign — not just the Temple itself, but the full web of powers competing for control of the region. At the highest level, three cosmic forces drive events: [Iuz](iuz-person.md) the Old One orchestrating the Temple's second rising as a precursor to the Greyhawk Wars, [Lolth](lolth-person.md) the Spider Queen infiltrating the Temple to subvert it from within, and Zuggtmoy the Demon Queen imprisoned beneath it all. Below them, the Greater Temple command, four elemental cults, the Crimson Reapers and their Orcus cult, river pirates, political agents in Verbobonc City, the Cowled Lady's giant armies in the Kron Hills, and a handful of undercover good-aligned agents all pursue their own agendas. For the DM, this means the campaign is a pressure system the players can exploit: factions can be turned against each other, agents can be flipped, supply lines can be severed, and political alliances can be shattered. This article provides the complete searchable reference — every faction, its key personnel, operational bases, inter-faction tensions, recruitment pipelines, and adventure hooks.

 

### The Greater Temple — Supreme Command

##### Overview

The Greater Temple sits above the four elemental cults and controls the Temple of Elemental Evil's true strategic direction. While the elemental cults believe they serve the [Elder Elemental God](elder-elemental-god-person.md), only the Greater Temple leadership knows the truth: the Temple ultimately serves Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi, who remains imprisoned beneath the dungeon. [Iuz](iuz-person.md) placed [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) in command to prepare the Temple as a staging ground for an invasion of Furyondy and Veluna, while secretly pursuing a direct connection between the Temple and Dorakaa, Iuz's capital.

##### Key Leaders

*   **[Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) — Supreme Commander, Mouth of Iuz** | Absolute ruler of the Temple. Ruthless, manipulative, fanatically devoted to Iuz. Must walk a fine line — maintaining the illusion that he serves the [Cult of the Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) while advancing Iuz's agenda. Enforces rivalry between elemental temples to keep them too distracted to unite against him.
*   **[Barkinar](barkinar-person.md) — Commander of Greater Temple Troops** | Military leader and Hedrack's heir apparent. Calculating and disciplined. Works with Deggum to secure the Temple but watches for opportunities to succeed Hedrack. Recruits monstrous warbands for the Temple's wilderness presence.
*   **[Senshock](senshock-person.md) — Lord Wizard of the Temple** | Arcane adviser and intelligence overseer. Cunning, power-hungry. Feigns loyalty to Hedrack while secretly working with [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md). His ultimate goal is to seize power and reshape the Temple to his own vision.
*   **[Deggum](deggum-person.md) — Master of Security and Discipline** | Head of counterintelligence and internal security. Paranoid, sadistic, calculating. Works with Barkinar but distrusts Senshock and Smigmal. Recruited the Blackthorn Tribe (ogres and monstrous humanoids from the Eastern Gnarley Forest).
*   **[Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) — Commander of Temple Guards and Supply Chains** | Supreme logistical officer overseeing recruitment, movement of resources, and all supply chain operations. Architect of the Nulb–[55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md)–Temple tunnel route. Uses [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md) as his trusted field lieutenant. Recruited the Iron Claw Bugbears from the Southern Gnarley Forest.
*   **[Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) — Keeper of the Orb of Golden Death** | Scholar of forbidden lore and artifact guardian. Paranoid and obsessive. Romantically linked to Smigmal Redhand but secretly serves drow patrons loyal to [Lolth](lolth-person.md).
*   **[Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) — Commander of Outer Defenses** | Assassin and surface operations commander. Directs bandit groups including [The Crimson Reapers](the-crimson-reapers-organization.md). Places trusted lieutenants under Madame Selentis' command in Nulb. Uses an elven disguise to eavesdrop in taverns and gauge threats.

##### Greater Temple Nulb Operations

The Greater Temple's hand in Nulb is Madame Selentis ([Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md)), who operates from the Old Mayor's Manor. She coordinates all surface activity — the movement of supplies, loot, prisoners, and recruits through Nulb and into the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md). Her Temple-appointed guards, hardened men once loyal to [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md), hold the manor as their base. The illusion of fragmented criminal control in Nulb is intentional — keeping outside eyes blind to the Temple's hand.

*   **Old Mayor's Manor (33):** Selentis' base. Temple guards, logistics coordination, prisoner processing before transfer to the Hunting Cottage tunnel.
*   **[55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md):** Primary supply hub south of Nulb under [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md). Contains a 200-foot hidden tunnel connecting to the Temple. Guarded by Iron Claw Bugbears. All materials from every faction must pass through this chokepoint.
*   **Wainwright Depot (6):** Staging ground for covert supply and prisoner logistics. Operated by Ivar "Two-Fingers" Maddok under [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md)'s oversight. Receives loot, prisoners, and supplies from regional bandit groups. Guarded by [The Crimson Reapers](the-crimson-reapers-organization.md).
*   **[Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) — Intelligence Handler & Courier:** Hedrack's primary courier and intelligence handler. Travels between the Temple, Nulb, Hommlet, and outer regions. Directly manages four embedded agents in Hommlet: Rannos Davl and Gremag at D&G Dry Goods (intelligence and smuggling), [Zert](zert-person.md) at the Inn of the Welcome Wench (enforcer and adventurer surveillance), and [Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) at the Doomwatch Keep construction site (saboteur). Also oversees field coordination among Temple cells and recruits informants across the Viscounty. All Hommlet intelligence flows up through Lucius to [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md).

##### Greater Temple Strategic Goals

*   Restore the Temple to full power and prepare it as a staging ground for invasion of Furyondy and Veluna.
*   Pursue the release of Zuggtmoy while keeping the elemental cults oblivious to her existence.
*   Establish a direct connection between the Temple and Dorakaa (Iuz's capital).
*   Maintain inter-faction rivalry among elemental temples — their infighting keeps them distracted and dependent on Greater Temple arbitration.
*   Ensure no rumor of the Temple's second rising escapes Nulb.

 

### Greater Temple Forward Operation — [The Ruins of the Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md)

##### Overview

The Greater Temple's first move in the second rising was re-establishing the Moathouse — a partially collapsed fortress in the marshlands northeast of Hommlet, two miles off the Long Road. During the first Temple War, this ruin served as the Temple's advance staging point for subjugation of the Hommlet region. Now it operates again under [Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful, a 5th-level cleric handpicked by [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) for his charisma, tactical cunning, and ability to command disparate humanoid forces. Lareth is the "New Master" — his mission is banditry, recruitment, and intelligence-gathering across the Hommlet corridor. What Hedrack does not know is that Lareth's true allegiance is to [Lolth](lolth-person.md), making the Moathouse both a Greater Temple asset and a Lolth infiltration platform.

##### Command Structure

*   **[Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) — Strategic Authority:** Appointed Lareth and oversees the Moathouse through [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), who serves as courier and intelligence handler between the Temple and the Moathouse. All orders flow down through Lucius.
*   **[Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful — Field Commander:** Cleric 5 (AC 20, HP 44, Str 18, Wis 18, Cha 18). Commands the entire Moathouse garrison from the dungeon-level master's chamber (Area 35). Carries Plate Mail +1, Staff of Striking, Minor Mace of Smiting, Ring of Freedom of Action, and a Phylactery of Action. His diary documents the full Temple operation and implicates his Hommlet spies — a critical piece of evidence if recovered.
*   **[Gattas Void](gattas-void-person.md) — Lieutenant:** Fighter 4. Organizes raids, manages prisoner transport, trains new recruits. Coordinates directly with [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md) at the Hunting Cottage for prisoner and loot transfer. Returns from prisoner runs alone — the implication is deliberate.
*   **Sergeant Kapral — Guard Commander:** Fighter 2. Commands the 18-man guard force (6 crossbowmen and spearmen in the guardroom, 12 in the barracks).

##### Garrison Forces

*   **Human garrison:** 18 bandits, guardsmen, and sergeants across the dungeon-level guardroom (Area 33) and barracks (Area 34). Equipped with crossbows, spears, and light warhorses.
*   **Iron Claw Bugbears (6):** Elite force from the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md), commanded by **Gorvash the Cruel**. Elevated status — separate barracks (Area 27). Gorvash is loyal to his own ambitions first and secretly plans to leverage Lareth's resources for Iron Claw expansion. Will only retreat on his own order.
*   **Jagged Fang Gnolls (9 + 1 Flind leader):** Dispatched from the Gnoll Fortress by King Korvash Bonegnaw as a test of Lareth's potential. Commander **Fangmaster Gorrthak** is resentful — his gnolls lost 6 warriors in raids but receive smaller shares than the newly recruited bugbears. Gnolls are 75% likely to accept bribes (6+ gp per soldier, triple for Gorrthak). At double the bribe, they will desert and guide intruders to Lareth's chamber.
*   **Lubash (Ogre):** Oversees prisoner holding in Area 24–25. Feeds on captives. Key chokepoint for prisoner extraction or rescue.
*   **Undead and Beasts:** 12 zombies (Area 21), 4 ghouls (Area 31), giant crayfish (Area 30), and assorted dungeon hazards provide passive defense layers.

##### Hommlet Spy Network

Lareth's operation extends directly into the village of Hommlet through four embedded agents. This network is the Greater Temple's primary intelligence source on Hommlet's defenses, adventurer movements, and the construction of Doomwatch Keep.

*   **Rannos Davl & Gremag — D&G Dry Goods:** Merchants operating a commercial front. Funnel intelligence to the Temple and facilitate smuggling operations between Hommlet and the Moathouse. Primary recruitment contacts for civilians sympathetic to the cause.
*   **[Zert](zert-person.md) — Mercenary Agent:** Monitors adventurers and sabotages Doomwatch Keep construction. Initially not known to Lareth directly, but recruitable as an assassin once discovered. Destabilizes Hommlet from within.
*   **[Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) — Cultist Infiltrator:** Laborer recruited by [Romag](romag-person.md) (Earth Temple). Monitors and sabotages Doomwatch Keep. Acts as courier between the Earth Temple and the Moathouse — a rare link between Romag's and Lareth's operations.

##### Supply Chain — Moathouse to Temple Pipeline

Raided goods and prisoners flow from the Moathouse through an established logistics corridor. Gattas Void manages prisoner transport to the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) south of Nulb, run by [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md). From the Hunting Cottage, prisoners and materials enter the Temple's main logistics pipeline — the same funnel used by the Azure Oath operation, the Crimson Reapers, and the River Pirates. The Moathouse also stages supplies through a secondary transfer point at Trevil's Fens Cottage. [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) uses the secret tunnel from the Hunting Cottage for covert assessment visits, carrying orders from Hedrack and evaluating Lareth's progress.

##### The Lolth Factor — Lareth's True Allegiance

Lareth's secret devotion to [Lolth](lolth-person.md) makes the Moathouse a uniquely dangerous node in the campaign. While he builds the Greater Temple's raiding and recruitment operations to Hedrack's satisfaction, he simultaneously advances Lolth's agenda: undermining the [Elder Elemental God](elder-elemental-god-person.md) cult from within, cultivating a covert partnership with [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) (fellow Lolth agent, keeper of the Orb of Golden Death), and positioning himself to seize control of the elemental nodes. If the party captures Lareth's diary, it implicates Rannos Davl, Gremag, and [Zert](zert-person.md) — blowing the Hommlet spy network wide open. Lareth serves two masters. The Moathouse serves three factions. This is the most dangerous forward position in the campaign precisely because no one — not Hedrack, not the elemental cults, not even Lolth's other agents — has a complete picture of what it actually is.

 

### Cult of the Black Earth — Earth Temple

##### Overview

The Earth Temple is the most entrenched faction in Nulb, operating through a mature network of agents centered on the [1\. Waterside Hostel of Nulb](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md). Led by [Romag](romag-person.md), the pragmatic and calculating High Priest known as the Prophet of Earth, this cult values stability, secrecy, and patience. Romag believes the Elder Elemental God represents the bedrock of existence — through decay and destruction, new foundations are laid for eternal empires.

##### Leadership

*   **[Romag](romag-person.md) — Prophet of Earth, High Priest** | Pragmatic, calculating. Operates through agents rather than direct action. Sees Belsornig and the Water Temple as his principal adversary. Seeks favor with Hedrack through calculated loyalty.

##### Nulb Operations

*   **[1\. Waterside Hostel of Nulb](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md) — Primary Base:** The hostel is the Earth Temple's recruitment center, information hub, and logistical coordination point. [Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md) operates the hostel as a laundering and staging house for the Temple's land-based criminal operations. He coordinates with [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) and [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) to shelter Temple agents, smuggle contraband, and launder coin. [Wat](wat-person.md) serves as barkeeper and observer, screening new arrivals, though Wat secretly supports the Fire Temple (see below).

##### Key Agents in Nulb

*   **[Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md) — Innkeeper, Waterside Hostel:** Plays the fool to deflect suspicion. Recruits land-based thugs and mercenaries, hosts Temple agents, facilitates prisoner holding prior to transport. All revenue flows back to the Temple through backdoor deals and false ledgers.
*   **[Wat](wat-person.md) — Barkeeper, Waterside Hostel:** Monitors deals, gossip, and troublemakers. Passes intelligence along Temple channels. Secretly a Fire Temple agent — reports to [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md) and keeps tabs on rival factions. This dual loyalty is a critical vulnerability for Romag.

##### Recruitment

The Earth Temple recruits heavily from bandits, mercenaries, and smugglers using intimidation, coercion, and religious fervor. Romag relies on field operations in Nulb through the Waterside Hostel. Prisoners and resources are delivered to the Temple via [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md) at the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) using the hidden tunnel. Romag also coordinates with monstrous allies — the Iron Claw Bugbears and the Jagged Fang Gnolls.

##### Inter-Faction Relationships

*   **Romag → Belsornig (Water):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Deep rivalry. Romag views Belsornig as his principal adversary — conflicting operational strategies, shared ambitions in Nulb's logistics.
*   **Romag → Kelno (Air):** Attitude: 3/5 | Trust: 2/5 | Tense neutrality. Kelno attempts diplomatic overtures to leverage Earth Temple stability and resources.
*   **Romag → Alrrem (Fire):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 1/5 | Deep rivalry. Alrrem harbors profound animosity toward Romag. Compounded by Wat's double-agent status.
*   **Romag → Hedrack:** Attitude: 3/5 | Trust: 2/5 | Seeks favor through calculated loyalty and strategic defense, but unaware Hedrack serves Iuz.

##### Misdirection Tactics

When a Paladin or Cleric of Good arrives in Nulb, Romag plays the genial outlaw innkeeper role through Dick Rentsch — offering safe rooms and helpful gossip. Wat plants false stories about small cult groups in the swamps, distracting from real logistics hubs. Romag avoids open hostility; killing a holy envoy would invite trouble from Verbobonc's knights. His preferred misdirection target is the Shattered Monastery — a real ruin with enough danger to waste an investigation team's time.

 

### Earth Temple Forward Stronghold — [The Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md)

##### Overview

The Earth Temple's most significant operation outside the Temple proper is the seizure of the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath — a Heironeous monastery located on the Long Road in the Kron Hills, directly across from the village of Hommlet. This is not a raiding camp. It is a fully occupied forward stronghold with a garrison of 38 personnel under arms, a functioning command structure, and a dual strategic mission: systematic caravan extortion on the Long Road and a deep excavation of an ancient geomantic node beneath the priory's crypts. (See: [Romag's Reavers Occupy the Azure Oath](romagE28099s-reavers-occupy-the-azure-oath-article.md))

##### Command Hierarchy

*   **[Romag](romag-person.md) — Strategic Authority:** Commands the entire operation from the Temple. Withholds decisions on sensitive matters — including the disposition of the Gift of Beauty. All tribute and intelligence flow through his chain.
*   **[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) — High Priest, Priest-Governor:** Flan geomancer and Master of the Shattered Monastery. Manages day-to-day operations and leads the excavation beneath the crypts. His true objective is awakening a buried Flan geomantic altar dedicated to the Elder Elemental God. Reports directly to Romag.
*   **[Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) — War-Priest, Garrison Warden:** Highest-ranking combatant in the priory. Commands the garrison's defensive operations from AO-18 (the former Knight-Commander's council chamber). Controls the Gift of Beauty in a triple-locked stone reliquary. Carries the personal seal required to authorize prisoner transfers. Reports to Varnor and Romag.
*   **[Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) — Field Commander, Long Road Raids:** One of Romag's most trusted field commanders. A professional soldier — not ideological — who runs raiding schedules, intelligence analysis, and patrol coordination from AO-12. Maintains an "earth-tithe" ledger tracking systematic caravan extortion. Reports to Tharvok and Varnor.
*   **[Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) — Arcane Operative:** Newly recruited cult member running false-flag operations to fracture the Kron Hills alliances. Conducts disguised attacks meant to look like gnome clan rivalries or bandit activity, destabilizing the political landscape around [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md). Collects Flan-origin resonance stones needed for Varnor's awakening ritual.
*   ****Lucius Graeme** — Intelligence Oversight:** Monitors faction movements and courier channels from Nulb. Carries Romag's key to the Gift of Beauty reliquary. Manages internal Temple political intelligence related to the priory.

##### Garrison Forces

*   **38 personnel under arms** (per Tharvok's report to Romag).
*   Earth fanatics rotated from Nulb, human mercenaries, Nulb half-orcs, and hardened Reavers.
*   Iron Claw Bugbear patrols from the Southern [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) supporting Long Road operations.
*   Duergar mining crews underground conducting the excavation.
*   Black Earth Guards posted at command quarters and prisoner cells.

##### Dual Strategic Mission

**Mission 1 — The Earth-Tithe (Surface Operations):** The priory commands a 20-mile raiding corridor along the Long Road (10 miles north to Nigb's Rest, 10 miles south to Sheernobb). From the ridge, the Long Road can be observed for 5 miles in each direction. Maris Blackshale runs systematic caravan extortion — forced tribute collected through ambush and intimidation. Prisoners are evaluated for recruit, slave, or sacrifice and funneled into the Temple logistics pipeline. An intelligence den in AO-16 (the former scriptorium) tracks caravan schedules, guild marks, patrol rhythms, and Long Road traffic. The "earth-tithe" ledger and stolen caravan seal press in this room are hard evidence linking the priory raids to the Temple's broader operations.

 

**Mission 2 — The Geomantic Awakening (Underground):** Beneath the crypts lies the true strategic objective. Varnor Earthcaller is excavating a buried Flan geomantic altar dedicated to the Elder Elemental God, sealed above a deep chasm called "the Black Mouth" — prayer-sealed with divine engineering by the original Heironeous monks. Duergar crews are opening tomb galleries toward the altar. The awakening ritual requires Varnor's artifact weapon Stonefang, Flan-origin resonance stones collected by Caidax, grave-earth from the tombs, and the opening of the Black Mouth itself. If completed, this converts the priory into a consecrated holy site of the Black Earth cult — massively strengthening Romag's position in the Temple hierarchy and establishing a permanent geomantic power source for the Earth Temple.

##### Key Assets at the Priory

*   **The Gift of Beauty:** A diplomatic artifact of campaign-level significance, stolen from a Rao Delegation. Stored in Tharvok's quarters (AO-18) in a triple-locked stone reliquary. Romag has not authorized its destruction. [Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful sent an emissary three tendays ago seeking to inspect it — a Lolth move to steal or subvert this asset.
*   **Halven Sere:** The captured custodian and knight-priest of Heironeous, held in AO-20. Interrogated for relic locations and kept alive as leverage. He remains uncooperative but unbroken.
*   **Lightning Kneel Relics:** Stolen paladin artifacts displayed as trophies in Tharvok's quarters — the Shield of Third Vigil, the Crushed Gauntlet of Ser Aldric Venn, a Notched Ceremonial Blade, and an Oath-Ring.
*   **Shipping Manifests:** In the Oath-Hall counting room (AO-19), manifests with waystation names (Laughing Dog, Ostverk road junctions) that connect to the broader Temple logistics web.

##### Material Flow — Priory to Temple Pipeline

Raided goods are consolidated at the priory, packed in the Oath-Hall counting room (AO-19), and shipped south to Nulb via established logistics routes. Prisoners are marched out at night from the guest cells (AO-20), hooded and gagged. All materials enter the same Nulb pipeline described in the Supply Chain section — through Madame Selentis, the Wainwright, and the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) tunnel. The priory is the Earth Temple's primary revenue source and its strongest bid for dominance within the Temple hierarchy.

 

### Cult of the Crushing Wave — Water Temple

##### Overview

The Water Temple is the Earth Temple's mirror image and chief rival in Nulb. Led by Canon [Belsornig](belsornig-person.md), this faction employs extensive espionage networks extending throughout Nulb and beyond. Belsornig believes the Elder Elemental God lies dormant beneath endless seas, waiting to drown the unworthy. His ambition extends beyond the cult — he aims to achieve dominance over the entire Temple network, even if it means betraying the Elder Elemental God himself.

##### Leadership

*   **Canon [Belsornig](belsornig-person.md) — Prophet of Water, High Priest** | Cunning, strategic. Employs espionage over force. Maintains covert connections with river pirates and smuggler networks. Views Romag as both a pawn and an obstacle.

##### Nulb Operations

*   **[4: Boatman's Tavern and Nulb Market](43A-boatmanE28099s-tavern-and-nulb-market-landmark.md) — Primary Base:** The entire Boatman's complex — tavern mainroom, warehouse, black market hall, brothel, and basement — serves as the Water Temple's central hub for espionage, smuggling, and cult recruitment. Controlled through proxies [Lodriss](lodriss-person.md) and Captain [Tolub](tolub-person.md).

##### Key Agents in Nulb

*   **[Lodriss](lodriss-person.md) — Power Broker, Boatman's Tavern:** The true power behind the tavern. Uses her relationship with Captain Tolub and her wits to maintain control. Collects a 10% tithe from all market booth rentals.
*   **[Skole](skole-person.md) — Proprietor, Boatman's Tavern and Nulb Market:** Ex-riverman turned smuggler. Controls the tavern and market. Outwardly neutral, but his dealings with pirates and bandits serve Belsornig's interests.
*   **Captain [Tolub](tolub-person.md) — Pirate Leader, Iron Dagger:** Supplies the Temple with slaves and goods through the Boatman's complex. Commands respect and fear in equal measure. Dependent on Selentis' approval to move anything to the Greater Temple — a chokepoint that limits his autonomy.
*   **[Grud Squinteye](grud-squinteye-person.md) — Lieutenant of the Iron Dagger:** Tolub's brutal enforcer. Absolute loyalty to his captain.
*   **Captain [Marad Vesrek](marad-vesrek-person.md) — Operator of the Crimson Trader:** Traffics in slaves, weapons, and black-market goods. Sells slaves to [Lorveth Thrask](lorveth-thrask-person.md) "The Broker" at the Boatman's for redistribution to the Temple.
*   **[Lorveth Thrask](lorveth-thrask-person.md) — "The Broker":** Middleman for slave redistribution from pirate crews to the Temple. Operates within the Boatman's complex.
*   **Elvanna Thorne — Madam of the Brothel:** An acolyte of the Elder Elemental God and a direct agent of [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md). Her presence in the Boatman's complex means the Greater Temple has its own eyes inside Belsornig's territory. Maintains a hidden shrine to the Elder Elemental Eye in her private chamber and a concealed escape hatch to the basement warehouses.

##### The Boatman's Complex — Five Interconnected Operations

The Boatman's Tavern is not one building but five interlocking operations under a shared roof, making it Nulb's most dangerous and commercially active location:

*   **Tavern Mainroom:** Unofficial town hall for Nulb's underworld. Pirates arrive, mercenaries are hired, Temple envoys meet with bandit lords.
*   **Warehouse:** Occupies nearly a third of the structure. Crates of stolen goods, smuggled arms, and Temple-bound supplies. Basement level contains contraband storage and a secret prisoner room with five reinforced slave cages. A false wall leads to this hidden holding area.
*   **Black Market Hall:** Indoor bazaar facing the village side. Booth rentals, general supplies, an "Artifact Alley" of looted relics and cursed trinkets, a "Shadow Nook" for poisons and alchemical brews. Guards patrol to protect trade, not people.
*   **Brothel (Elvanna's Loft):** Second floor above the tavern. Velvet-draped corruption and Temple recruitment. Contains Elvanna's private shrine to the Elder Elemental Eye, a hidden prison room for breaking new recruits, and a crawlspace connecting to the basement warehouses.
*   **Basement and Secret Dungeon:** Processes slaves and Temple recruits. Guarded by brutal cultists. Connected to the warehouse via stone steps and to the brothel via crawlspace.

##### Recruitment

The Water Temple recruits smugglers, pirates, and mercenaries through bribes and influence. Belsornig's key relationships are with Captain [Tolub](tolub-person.md) and [Lodriss](lodriss-person.md). Tolub supplies the Temple via river access, but all materials must pass through Madame Selentis' coordination and the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) under Greater Temple control — a dependency Belsornig resents.

##### Inter-Faction Relationships

*   **Belsornig → Romag (Earth):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Open rivalry. Conflicting operational strategies, shared ambitions. Belsornig would gladly destroy Romag's network.
*   **Belsornig → Kelno (Air):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Intense distrust. Seeks to manipulate and weaken Kelno's few remaining forces.
*   **Belsornig → Alrrem (Fire):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Open antagonism. Both factions would destroy the other given the chance.
*   **Belsornig → Hedrack:** Attitude: 2/5 | Trust: 1/5 | Serves out of necessity. His ambition to dominate the entire Temple means eventual conflict is inevitable.

##### Misdirection Tactics

When a holy envoy arrives, Belsornig views the situation as both a liability and an opportunity to throw suspicion onto Romag. Pirate crews may drunkenly pick fights, but under orders to lose believably — making the envoy feel they are winning while learning nothing. Belsornig may leak minor smuggling route intel that leads to dead ends. His preferred misdirection target is the River Fort — a real pirate holdout that wastes investigative time without threatening the Temple.

 

### Cult of the Eternal Flame — Fire Temple

##### Overview

The Fire Temple is the most aggressive and volatile faction, but its presence in Nulb is minimal. Led by [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md), this cult believes the Elder Elemental God is the all-consuming fire that purges weakness, making way for rebirth through flames. Alrrem commands through brutality and terror, frequently burning entire villages to assert dominance.

##### Leadership

*   **[Alrrem](alrrem-person.md) — Prophet of Fire, High Priest** | Aggressive, volatile. Commands a temple known for its destructive power. Recruits fire giants and militant fanatics. Harbors profound animosity toward every rival faction. Relies solely on brute strength.

##### Nulb Operations

The Fire Temple has no dedicated base in Nulb. Its influence operates through a single, well-placed spy:

*   **[Wat](wat-person.md) — Barkeeper at the Waterside Hostel:** Though he works in the Earth Temple's stronghold under [Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md), Wat secretly reports to Alrrem. He uses bar talk to plant false stories, stir chaos with firebrand zealots, screen new arrivals, and keep tabs on rival Temple factions. This makes Wat the most dangerous double agent in Nulb — positioned inside the enemy's own base.

##### Recruitment

The Fire Temple focuses on chaos-loving humanoids and fire-worshiping fanatics. Recruitment relies on messengers and emissaries traveling from the Temple proper to the surrounding wildlands to attract werewolves, bugbears, and trolls. Minimal recruitment activity occurs in Nulb itself — Alrrem's agents work in the wilderness. Their preferred misdirection target is Etter's Watchtower — close enough to seem plausible as a cult hideout.

##### Inter-Faction Relationships

*   **Alrrem → Romag (Earth):** Attitude: 0/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Profound animosity. The Wat situation compounds the hatred.
*   **Alrrem → Belsornig (Water):** Attitude: 0/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Seeks outright destruction of Water Temple operations.
*   **Alrrem → Kelno (Air):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Actively targets Kelno's weakened temple.
*   **Alrrem → All:** Hostile to virtually all rivals. The Fire Temple is the faction most likely to provoke open intra-Temple violence.

 

### Cult of the Howling Hatred — Air Temple

##### Overview

The Air Temple is the weakest faction, its influence waning as rivals grow stronger. Led by [Kelno](kelno-person.md), a fanatical and arrogant zealot who sees himself as the true prophet of elemental chaos, the Air Temple survives through stealth, sabotage, and carefully targeted strikes. Kelno believes the Elder Elemental God is the eternal storm — invisible yet ever-present, able to tear down civilizations with whispering winds of madness.

##### Leadership

*   **[Kelno](kelno-person.md) — Prophet of Air, High Priest** | Fanatical, arrogant. Struggles to maintain relevance. Actively sabotages both Fire and Water Temples using covert methods and mercenary bugbear allies. Strategic focus on intelligence gathering and targeted strikes.

##### Nulb Operations

The Air Temple has no fixed base in Nulb. Kelno relies on covert mercenary operations and temporary hideouts to orchestrate clandestine sabotage. His cultists are few but deadly — often seeded quietly into bandit or merchant caravans. Falcon operatives conduct secretive field operations in and around Nulb.

##### Recruitment

The Air Temple appeals to stealth-based operatives — assassins, scouts, and spies. Recruitment involves infiltration, manipulation, and surveillance missions spanning Nulb, the Temple, and the broader region. Air Temple cultists captured at the outskirts of Nulb among ruined stilted houses have been known to target magical creatures like sprites for intelligence purposes.

##### Inter-Faction Relationships

*   **Kelno → Romag (Earth):** Attitude: 3/5 | Trust: 2/5 | Attempts careful diplomatic overtures to leverage Earth Temple stability and resources.
*   **Kelno → Belsornig (Water):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Intense distrust. Fears being absorbed or eliminated by the Water Temple's growing influence.
*   **Kelno → Alrrem (Fire):** Attitude: 1/5 | Trust: 0/5 | Fears the aggression of the Fire Temple. Vulnerable target.

 

### The [River Pirates](the-river-pirates-organization.md) — Independent Criminal Power

##### Overview

The river pirates operate as a semi-independent criminal force in Nulb, aligned primarily with the Water Temple but maintaining enough autonomy to be a wild card. They control Nulb's waterways and dominate the river trade, making them indispensable to the Temple's logistics yet dangerous to its secrecy.

##### Key Personnel

*   **Captain [Tolub](tolub-person.md) — Captain of the Iron Dagger:** Water Temple agent and pirate leader. Dominates Nulb's trade routes. His partnership with Belsornig funds his operations, but Greater Temple control of the Hunting Cottage tunnel means he cannot operate independently.
*   **[Grud Squinteye](grud-squinteye-person.md) — Lieutenant of the Iron Dagger:** Brutal enforcer with absolute loyalty to Tolub.
*   **Captain [Marad Vesrek](marad-vesrek-person.md) — Captain of the Crimson Trader:** Traffics slaves, weapons, and black-market goods. Operates more independently than Tolub but sells through the Boatman's complex.

##### Operations

Pirate crews disrupt river trade and supply the Temple through the Boatman's Tavern pier-side access. The Boatman's Warehouse pier doors accept nighttime deliveries of stolen goods and captives. Tolub's crews squabble publicly, creating the appearance of fragmented criminal control — the exact illusion Madame Selentis cultivates to mask the Temple's hand.

 

### [The Crimson Reapers](the-crimson-reapers-organization.md) — Bandit Company and Cult of Orcus

##### Overview

The Crimson Reapers are far more than common bandits. This hardened company of ex-soldiers, deserters, and professional killers operates out of the second floor of the [1\. Waterside Hostel of Nulb](1-waterside-hostel-of-nulb-landmark.md), where their leadership maintains a fortified regional operations base behind a locked and guarded door. Originally from [Dyvers, Free Lands of](dyvers2C-free-lands-of-organization.md), the Reapers are allied with the Cult of Orcus — members bear a branded glyph of Orcus carved into their chests through arcane ritual, granting resistance to mind-affecting spells but binding their souls to the Demon Prince of Undeath. [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) permits them to operate with her blessing, and [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) uses the Waterside Hostel as a command post via [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md).

##### Leadership — Waterside Hostel, Second Floor

*   **Zelt the Bat — Master of the Crimson Reapers:** Rarely present in Nulb. Moves between Nulb and Dyvers via magical means. Commands the gang's overall strategy and maintains the Orcus alliance. His authority is absolute, but his absences leave operational control to his lieutenants.
*   **[Maldrith the Hexed](maldrith-person.md) — Second-in-Command, Priest of Orcus:** The gang's dark spiritual enforcer. Conducts unholy rites and blood sacrifices to maintain Orcus' favor. Brands members with infernal glyphs preventing desertion. Summons lesser demons and undead minions. Oversees the Hexblades — warlocks sworn to Orcus who specialize in demonic possession. His long-term ambition is to transform Nulb into a necropolis and carve dark ley lines to weaken the Material Plane–Abyss boundary. Mistrusted by [Romag](romag-person.md) and [Belsornig](belsornig-person.md), but recognized as a potential ally by [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) and [Senshock](senshock-person.md).
*   **[Rom Khavernus](rom-khavernus-person.md) — Battle-Mage and Strategist ("Hollow Jack"):** Zelt's right-hand enchanter. Deploys fireballs, lightning bolts, and conjured blades on the battlefield. Crafts magical items (wands, scrolls, potions) for the gang. Also acts as the gang's "Whip" — delivering warnings, punishments, and final blows. Charming, clever, and self-assured. Respects Zelt but not above replacing him if the opportunity arises. Distrusts Maldrith's necromancy.
*   **[Felix Fahnor](felix-fahnor-person.md) — Chief Enforcer and Master Assassin ("Silent Blade"):** Former agent of the Horned Society. Personal executioner of Zelt the Bat. Oversees assassinations, kidnappings, and disappearances. Creates and harvests deadly poisons. A master of stealth, illusion magic, and disguise. Though stationed at the Waterside Hostel, Felix is mostly in the field — raiding with his band along the merchant roads of the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) and the [Verbobonc, Viscounty](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md). His absolute loyalty is to Zelt alone.

##### Gang Structure

*   **The Reapers:** Elite warriors and field commanders. Marked by the crimson Orcus glyph.
*   **The Hexblades:** Sorcerers and warlocks sworn to Maldrith. Demonic possession specialists.
*   **The Marauders:** Standard brigands and cutthroats executing raids on merchant caravans.
*   **The Skinners:** Slave capture and interrogation specialists.

##### Nulb Deployments

*   **Waterside Hostel, Second Floor:** Regional operations base. Fortified chamber behind locked and guarded door. Leadership (Maldrith, Rom, Felix when present) directs operations from here.
*   **Old Mayor's Manor (33):** Temple-appointed guards hold the manor for Selentis.
*   **Wainwright Depot (6):** Brigand outpost across the road from Maddok's workshop. Ensure depot security and operational secrecy.
*   **Convoy Escorts:** Guard wagons moving between Nulb, the Wainwright, and the Hunting Cottage.
*   **Forest Roads:** Felix's raiding bands operate along the Forest Road from Dyvers to the Wild Coast, and merchant routes connecting [Narwell](narwell-settlement.md), Verbobonc, and Dyvers.

##### Income Sources

*   Raiding merchant caravans on Forest Road and Wild Coast trade routes.
*   Smuggling contraband through the Hunting Cottage tunnel into the Temple.
*   Selling slaves to the Boatman's Brothel or directly to the Temple.
*   Extorting protection money from Nulb traders and dockworkers.

 

### Madame Selentis — The Greater Temple's Hand in Nulb

##### Overview

Madame Selentis is the alias of Lady [Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md), formerly heir to [House Krivaltis](house-krivaltis-organization.md). Now serving as [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md)'s protector and high-level field commander, she is the single most powerful individual in Nulb. She coordinates all surface activity on behalf of the Greater Temple and manages prisoner and supply deliveries to the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) for tunnel transfer.

##### Strategic Function

Selentis is the chokepoint through which every faction's Nulb operations must pass. Tolub's pirate deliveries, Romag's hostel recruits, and Maddok's wagon shipments all ultimately require her coordination to reach the Temple. This gives Hedrack leverage over every cult leader — none can move material to the Temple without the Greater Temple's approval. Selentis enforces this dependency ruthlessly while maintaining the public illusion that she is simply a wealthy newcomer establishing herself in Nulb's criminal economy.

##### Lakash Quallad — Temple Agent in Verbobonc City

[Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) is Nysera Krivaltis's brother — born Lakash Krivaltis, youngest son of the executed Lord Eldris Krivaltis. He adopted his mother's maiden name "Quallad" to escape his family's disgraced identity. Saved as a youth by [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), who recognized his potential, Lakash now operates as a Temple of Elemental Evil agent inside Verbobonc City under [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md)'s direct authority.

*   **Cover Identity:** "Lakash Quallad," a wealthy Oeridian merchant from the city of [Dyvers, Free Lands of](dyvers2C-free-lands-of-organization.md). Maintains a manor at [A12, High Quarter, Verbobonc](a12-lakash-quallad-s-manor-landmark.md). Hosts glittering balls as intelligence-gathering operations.
*   **True Mission:** Fracture the human-gnome alliance through subtle discord, economic sabotage, and false flag operations. Infiltrate the Verbobonc Senate and Chamber of 13. Destabilize the Viscounty from within.
*   **Methods:** A 12th-level Enchanter wizard. Uses enchantment magic to influence political outcomes. Maneuvers for Senate and Chamber of 13 seats. Employs field agent Conner Bridgeforth as a political operative in the Council of Lords.
*   **Handler:** Reports to Hedrack via [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md), who serves as both handler and mentor.
*   **Significance:** Lakash extends the Temple's reach beyond Nulb into the political heart of the Viscounty. While Selentis controls the logistics pipeline in Nulb, her brother wages a parallel war of political subversion in the capital. Together, the Krivaltis siblings form the Temple's two-pronged approach to destabilizing the region — one through covert supply chains, the other through courtly manipulation.

##### Misdirection Protocol

When holy agents arrive in Nulb, Selentis receives orders directly from Hedrack: observe, mislead, and isolate. She assigns spies to track the party and steer them toward red herrings — petty pirate feuds, false leads on splinter cults, and staged contraband busts. Her goal is to waste their time, wear them down, and make them report back that there is no Temple presence.

 

### The Supply Chain — Nulb to Temple Pipeline

##### Overview

The Temple's logistics system moves supplies, prisoners, loot, and recruits from Nulb through a structured pipeline controlled by the Greater Temple. Every faction feeds into this system, but the Greater Temple controls the bottleneck.

##### The Route

*   **Step 1 — Collection Points:** Waterside Hostel (Earth Temple), Boatman's Tavern complex (Water Temple), and Old Mayor's Manor (Greater Temple) collect recruits, prisoners, supplies, and loot.
*   **Step 2 — Staging:** Materials are transferred to the Wainwright Depot (6) for sorting, loading, and false manifesting. Wagons are prepared for transport across [Imeryd's Run](imeryd-s-run-location.md).
*   **Step 3 — Transfer:** Covered wagons move to the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) (1 mile south of Nulb) under [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md)'s command. Iron Claw Bugbears guard the perimeter. A 30-foot ring of hunting snares protects the approaches (1d4 snares per path).
*   **Step 4 — Tunnel:** A 200-foot hidden tunnel behind the cottage connects to a natural limestone cave and ultimately to the Greater Temple interior. Iron Claw Bugbears guard the tunnel entrance. Fallback defenses include trained death dogs, enslaved goblins, and a carrion crawler emergency release.

##### Disruption Opportunities

Cutting the supply chain at the Hunting Cottage is the single highest-impact action players can take short of assaulting the Temple directly. The "Cut the Vein" quest (Levels 4–6) targets this node specifically — disrupting supply flow for 48 hours causes cascading shortages in the Temple. Every approach option (frontal assault, stealth, sabotage, faction manipulation) is viable.

 

### Forces of Good — Undercover in Nulb

##### Overview

The forces of good have not abandoned Nulb entirely. Two undercover agents operate within the village, investigating and resisting the Temple in secret, waiting for the right moment — or the right allies — to act openly. However, the major good-aligned powers (Gnarley Rangers, Druids of the Gnarley, Sylvan Elf Clans, agents of the [Verbobonc, Viscounty](verbobonc2C-viscounty-organization.md), Veluna, Furyondy, and the Fey Court of Celene) do not enter Nulb due to political inertia, internal prioritization, or suspicion that the problem is just another bandit ring.

##### Key Agents

*   **[Canoness Y'dey](canoness-y-dey-person.md) (as "Mother Screng") — Herb Shop (3):** Disguised as an eccentric herbalist, secretly a cleric of St. Cuthbert investigating the Temple's influence and nurturing redemption. Her shop is a quaint establishment at the heart of Nulb, brimming with medicinal herbs and serving as a front for clandestine intelligence operations. Her "daughter" Hruda Murfles (sharp-tongued and disagreeable) tends the counter. The tavern keepers regard Mother Screng as a harmless crone — a misjudgment that serves Y'dey's purposes perfectly.
*   **[Otis](otis-person.md) — Blacksmith, Otis's Smithy and Stable (2):** A quiet but formidable former adventurer. Secretly aids Y'dey and watches the Temple's activities from his forge. He maintains contacts with sprite allies in the area and, if the party earns his trust, can provide critical intelligence on the Temple's interior — including the hidden tunnel to Level 2 in the Broken Tower and a window into the Main Temple's East Vestry.

##### The Constable

*   **Constable Edrick Malvaar — Nulb Constable (47):** Not an agent of good in any meaningful sense — but not a villain either. A broken, disillusioned lawman whose calls for help from the Council of Lords of Verbobonc have gone unanswered. He is a puppet figurehead, carefully manipulated by Temple factions to point troublemakers toward rival faction targets. When a Paladin arrives, Edrick becomes a cynical information source whose misdirection serves whichever faction last pressured him. His hopelessness is genuine, making him a tragic potential ally if the party can offer real hope of change.

 

### Agents of [Iuz](iuz-person.md) — The Old One's Network

##### Overview

The entire second rising of the Temple of Elemental Evil is orchestrated by [Iuz](iuz-person.md), the demigod of deceit, pain, and wickedness. This is not a side plot — it is Iuz's strategic move to destabilize the Viscounty of Verbobonc, weaken Furyondy and Veluna, and position himself for the Greyhawk Wars that will engulf the Flanaess in a few years' time. The Temple is his weapon, and every agent listed here serves that larger ambition whether they know it or not. Iuz operates through the [Priesthood of Iuz](the-priesthood-of-iuz-organization.md), an organization divided into the Boneheart (Greater and Lesser — his inner circle of advisers) and the Boneshadow (elite infiltrators and spies). The Temple of Elemental Evil falls under this network as Iuz's primary forward operation in the southern Flanaess.

##### The Wicked Three — Iuz's Primary Agents in Verbobonc

Iuz dispatched three independent agents to the Viscounty of Verbobonc, each operating outside the normal Temple chain of command. Together they form a strategic trident designed to destabilize the region from multiple angles simultaneously:

 

*   **[Nassandra](nassandra-person.md) — "The Cowled Lady," Vampire Sorceress:** One of Iuz's most dangerous independent operatives. Commands giant armies from the Midnight Keep in the Lortmil Mountains. Orchestrates destabilization campaigns, enslaves giants, and manipulates dragons. She conducts devastating raids with hill giants on the gnome settlements of the Kron Hills, creating a secondary crisis that diverts Verbobonc's military attention away from the Temple's resurgence. Formerly unquestioning in her devotion to Iuz, she is now torn by her relationship with the undead warlord Halmadar — a vulnerability that could be exploited.
*   **[Ryjenis](ryjenis-person.md) — Master of Disguise:** A direct agent of Iuz who has impersonated Lord Simon Milinous for eight years. Through this stolen identity, Ryjenis infiltrates Verbobonc's nobility, expands the House Guard as a personal power base, forces marriages for land acquisition, and corrupts the political establishment from within. His deception predates the Temple's second rising — Iuz planted him as a long-term sleeper agent.
*   **Obmi — "The Hammer of Iuz":** Dwarven assassin and military commander who operates within Nassandra's forces. A direct agent of Iuz specializing in targeted killings and military leadership. His capture during campaign events is a significant blow to Iuz's network.

##### Temple-Based Agents of Iuz

*   **[Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) — Supreme Commander, 14th-Level Cleric of Iuz:** The Mouth of Iuz and his primary emissary in the Temple. Hedrack publicly proclaims allegiance to the [Elder Elemental God](elder-elemental-god-person.md) while secretly advancing Iuz's agenda. His grand strategy is to prepare the Temple as a staging ground for invasion of Furyondy and Veluna, while pursuing a direct connection between the Temple and Dorakaa. Every operation in the Temple — recruitment, supply chains, cult management — ultimately serves Hedrack's mandate from Iuz.
*   **[Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) — Courier, Spy Master, 9th-Level Priest of Iuz (Boneshadow):** A member of the Boneshadow — Iuz's elite espionage and infiltration corps. Sent by Iuz to serve Hedrack directly. Manages all Temple communications, controls smuggling routes, blackmails key figures, and coordinates regional agents across Verbobonc, Hommlet, and Nulb. His agent network includes [Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md), [Wat](wat-person.md), Gremag in Hommlet, Joseph Millar (saboteur), Zert (enforcer), and Madame Selentis. He also mentored [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md), making him the architect of the Krivaltis siblings' placement in both Nulb and Verbobonc. Note: Lucius is also suspected of Lolth allegiance — see the Agents of Lolth section below.
*   **[Beryn Talkin](beryn-talkin-person.md) — Boneshadow Operative, 9th-Level Priest of Iuz:** A Boneshadow member who travels on special assignments. Wields authority over local cult officials and can commandeer cult resources. Motivated by fear of Iuz's torture and a desire for adventure and power. His presence in the region indicates Iuz's direct investment in the Temple's success.

##### Regional Agents in the Iuzian Network

*   **In Nulb:** Madame Selentis ([Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md)), [Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md), [Wat](wat-person.md), Captain [Marad Vesrek](marad-vesrek-person.md), Captain [Tolub](tolub-person.md).
*   **In Hommlet:** Gremag (Traders' Shop merchant), Joseph Millar (saboteur at Doomwatch), Zert (mercenary enforcer at Inn of Welcome Wench).
*   **In Verbobonc City:** [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) (political infiltrator), [Ryjenis](ryjenis-person.md) (Lord Milinous imposter).
*   **In the Lortmil Mountains / Kron Hills:** [Nassandra](nassandra-person.md) (Cowled Lady, giant army commander), Obmi (dwarven assassin/military commander).
*   **Temple Command:** [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md), [Barkinar](barkinar-person.md), [Deggum](deggum-person.md), [Gefreid Patris](gefreid-patris-person.md) — all Greater Temple commanders who execute Hedrack's orders and thus serve Iuz's strategy indirectly.

##### Strategic Goals

Iuz's plan operates on multiple timelines. The Temple's second rising is the immediate objective — rebuild military strength, secure supply chains, and establish a forward operating base. The Cowled Lady's giant raids destabilize the Kron Hills and divert Verbobonc's military response. Ryjenis's political infiltration erodes the nobility's ability to mount a coordinated response. Lakash's Senate manipulation fractures the human-gnome alliance. When the Greyhawk Wars begin, Iuz intends the Viscounty to be too weakened, too divided, and too distracted to resist.

 

### Agents of [Lolth](lolth-person.md) — The Spider Queen's Infiltration

##### Overview

[Lolth](lolth-person.md), the Demon Queen of Spiders, has a deeply personal stake in the Temple of Elemental Evil. The drow House Eilservs originally abandoned Lolth to serve the Elder Elemental God — a betrayal she has never forgiven. Lolth possesses the key to the Elder Elemental God's prison, giving her unique leverage over the Temple's core theology. Her strategy is not to destroy the Temple but to subvert it from within: keep the factions divided, weaken Iuz's hold, and ultimately convert the Temple's power to serve her own purposes. She operates through a network of agents placed at critical nodes within the Temple hierarchy.

##### Confirmed Agents of Lolth

*   **[Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful — Lolth's Primary Infiltrator:** Commander of the Moathouse, 5th-level Cleric. Ostensibly serves the Elder Elemental God but his true allegiance is to Lolth. His ultimate goal is to convert the Temple to serve the Spider Queen rather than Zuggtmoy. He builds forces loyal to him personally (Jagged Fang Gnolls from the Lortmil Mountains, bugbears), works covertly with [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) on joint infiltration efforts, and plans an eventual takeover of Temple leadership. Lareth also runs parallel schemes to steal the Gift of Beauty from the Earth Temple. He commands Lieutenant Gattas Void and maintained spies in Hommlet (Rannos Davl, Gremag, Zert, Joseph Millar) before his capture at the Moathouse.
*   **[Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) — Rogue Wizard, Keeper of the Orb of Golden Death:** An 8th-level magic-user (9th with his quasit familiar Kriitch) who serves Lolth above all others. Secretly possesses the Orb of Golden Death and experiments with it on the Elemental Nodes — advancing Lolth's goal of destabilization. He seeks to unlock Zuggtmoy's prison (another Lolth objective), manipulates Temple factions through espionage and sabotage, and co-leads Lolth's infiltration alongside Lareth. His lover [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) aids his schemes, making her an indirect Lolth asset.
*   **[Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) — Suspected Triple Agent:** While serving as a Boneshadow member of Iuz's priesthood and Hedrack's spy master, Lucius has a documented allegiance to Lolth. This makes him potentially the most dangerous figure in the entire Temple — a triple agent who serves Iuz publicly, coordinates with Lolth's agents privately, and controls the information flow that both sides depend on. His direct connection to Lareth (fellow Lolth agent) and his role as the Temple's intelligence hub means Lolth has access to virtually everything Hedrack knows.

##### Indirect and Suspected Lolth Connections

*   **[Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) — Indirectly Connected via Falrinth:** Smigmal is Falrinth's lover and supports his Lolth-aligned schemes. She commands the Temple's outer defenses and bandit operations, giving Lolth's network eyes on every surface approach to the Temple. Her elven disguise (via Hat of Disguise) allows her to conduct intelligence operations in Nulb's taverns. [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) suspects her of divided loyalties between himself and Falrinth.
*   **[Deggum](deggum-person.md) — Rumored Connections:** The Temple's security commander has rumored connections to Lolth that make him a liability to Hedrack. His half-elf heritage and personal ambitions make him potentially useful to Lolth as a future asset, though no direct evidence of active collaboration has been confirmed.

##### Hedrack's Counter-Intelligence

[Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) suspects both Lareth and Falrinth of siding with Lolth and plots their removal. However, he has not yet moved against them openly — Lareth commands the Moathouse (a critical forward position), and Falrinth holds the Orb of Golden Death (an irreplaceable artifact). Eliminating either without a replacement ready would weaken the Temple more than tolerating the infiltration. This creates a window of opportunity for the players: Hedrack may actually provide intelligence against Lareth or Falrinth if he believes the party will do his dirty work for him.

##### Lolth's Strategic Goals

*   **Prevent the Temple from reaching full potential:** A unified Temple under Iuz's control threatens Lolth's own interests. Internal chaos serves her.
*   **Control the Orb of Golden Death:** Falrinth's possession of this artifact is central to Lolth's strategy. His experiments threaten to destabilize the Temple's magical foundations in ways Lolth can exploit.
*   **Unlock Zuggtmoy's prison:** Paradoxically, Lolth may want Zuggtmoy freed — a released demon queen would create chaos that Lolth can manipulate, while also weakening Iuz's control over the Temple.
*   **Convert or destroy the Temple:** The endgame is either turning the Temple's power to Lolth's service or ensuring it never serves Iuz's invasion plans.

 

### Zuggtmoy — The Imprisoned Queen

##### Overview

Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi, remains imprisoned beneath the Temple of Elemental Evil. Only the Greater Temple leadership knows she exists. The elemental cults worship the [Elder Elemental God](elder-elemental-god-person.md) without realizing Zuggtmoy is the true power they are inadvertently serving. Her release is one of [Iuz](iuz-person.md)'s long-term objectives — and paradoxically, it may also serve [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s goals of sowing chaos. Zuggtmoy's continued imprisonment is the one thing both Iuz and Lolth may agree on — for entirely different reasons and with entirely different plans for what happens next.

### Adventure Hooks by Faction

##### Greater Temple Hooks

*   **Cut the Vein (Levels 4–6):** Disrupt the Hunting Cottage supply route. Multiple approach options — frontal assault, stealth, sabotage, or turning the Iron Claw Bugbears against the Temple. Success cuts Temple supply for 48 hours and causes cascading shortages.
*   **The Courier:** Intercept [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) moving between Nulb and the Temple. His dispatches contain intelligence on faction operations and Hedrack's strategic orders.
*   **Manor Investigation:** Investigate the Old Mayor's Manor and Selentis' operations. Requires surviving her guard detail and the Crimson Reapers stationed nearby.

##### Earth Temple Hooks

*   **Rentsch Makes an Offer (Level 4):** If the party has kept a low profile, Dick Rentsch approaches them on behalf of [Romag](romag-person.md) — hire the party to ambush gnolls from the Gnoll Fortress. Accepting draws them deeper into Earth Temple operations. Refusing flags them as a threat.
*   **Dick Pays the Price (Level 5):** Wat poisons Dick Rentsch — a Fire Temple move against the Earth Temple's Nulb base. The party can investigate, choose sides, or exploit the chaos.

##### Water Temple Hooks

*   **The Elf at Knifepoint (Level 3):** Captain Tolub's pirates hold an elven scout on the wharf — an agent of Celene searching for the missing Countess Tilahi. Rescuing the elf earns intelligence and a quest reward from Tilahi's estate.
*   **Tolub's Journal (Level 3–4):** Heist to steal the pirate captain's diary from the Boatman's Tavern. Contains records of slave shipments, smuggling routes, and Water Temple contacts.
*   **An Argument Overheard (Level 5):** Espionage encounter — overhear Water Temple recruitment in progress. Leads to identification of Belsornig's agent network in Nulb.

##### Fire Temple Hooks

*   **Mother Screng and the Cultists (Level 6):** A troll and Fire Temple handlers attack the herb shop. Defending Mother Screng may reveal her true identity as [Canoness Y'dey](canoness-y-dey-person.md) — or at minimum earns the gratitude of Nulb's only good-aligned power broker.

##### Air Temple Hooks

*   **Rescuing Sprites (Level 5):** Air Temple cultists have captured [Otis](otis-person.md)'s sprite allies at the outskirts of Nulb. Freeing them earns Otis's trust and critical intelligence about the Temple interior — including the hidden tunnel to Level 2 and a window into the East Vestry.

##### Cross-Faction Hooks

*   **The Drunken Sailor (Level 3):** Gather intelligence from a drunk pirate at the Boatman's. Reveals connections between river pirates, the Temple, and the broader smuggling network.
*   **A Roof Overhead (Level 4):** Discover and claim an old cultist's abandoned house with an elemental binding. Factions react based on which cult the house was associated with.
*   **Falrinth Gets a Drink (Level 7):** The Orb-carrying sorcerer [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) appears in a Nulb tavern. An independent operator carrying one of the Temple's most powerful artifacts — and serving Lolth's interests rather than Hedrack's. High-risk, high-reward encounter.

##### GM Notes: Using This Article

**The Pressure System:** Every faction in this article is a lever the players can pull. The Earth and Water Temples actively hate each other — information planted with one will be weaponized against the other. The Fire Temple's spy (Wat) inside the Earth Temple's base is a ticking bomb the party can trigger or exploit. The Air Temple's desperation makes Kelno a potential, if untrustworthy, source of intelligence. And every faction's materials must flow through Selentis and the Hunting Cottage — a chokepoint the party can sever.

 

**Escalation Logic:** If the party disrupts one faction, the others rush to fill the vacuum. Destroying the Waterside Hostel strengthens the Boatman's Tavern. Sinking Tolub's ships forces Belsornig to recruit overland, increasing traffic through the Wainwright. Every action creates a reaction. Plan faction responses to party actions as chain reactions, not isolated events.

 

**The Secrecy Imperative:** Hedrack's standing order — no provocations, no martyrs, no attention — governs every faction's behavior toward outsiders. This means the Temple will not attack the party openly in Nulb unless the players force a confrontation. Instead, factions misdirect, manipulate, and attempt to co-opt. The party's greatest danger in Nulb is not combat but being played.

 

**Undercover Allies:** Y'dey and Otis will not reveal themselves unless the party earns significant trust through actions — not words. Defending the herb shop, freeing the sprites, or demonstrating genuine opposition to the Temple can trigger these reveals. Once revealed, these two become the party's most valuable intelligence sources in Nulb, but their exposure also means the Temple factions will escalate their response.

##### Article at a Glance

*   **Three cosmic powers** drive every event: [Iuz](iuz-person.md) (orchestrating the 2nd Rising to launch the Greyhawk Wars), [Lolth](lolth-person.md) (infiltrating the Temple to subvert it), and Zuggtmoy (imprisoned beneath it all).
*   **The Greater Temple** controls the Temple through [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) (Mouth of Iuz), with commanders Barkinar, Senshock, Deggum, Feldrin, Falrinth, and Smigmal Redhand.
*   **Four elemental cults** — Earth, Water, Fire, and Air — compete for dominance while unknowingly serving Zuggtmoy through the [Elder Elemental God](elder-elemental-god-person.md) theology.
*   **Earth Temple** controls the Waterside Hostel (ground floor) via [Dick Rentsch](dick-rentsch-person.md). **Water Temple** controls the Boatman's Tavern complex via [Lodriss](lodriss-person.md) and Captain [Tolub](tolub-person.md).
*   **[Wat](wat-person.md)** is a Fire Temple spy embedded in the Earth Temple's own tavern — the most dangerous double agent in Nulb.
*   **[The Crimson Reapers](the-crimson-reapers-organization.md)** (Cult of Orcus) occupy the Waterside Hostel's second floor: [Maldrith the Hexed](maldrith-person.md) (priest of Orcus), [Rom Khavernus](rom-khavernus-person.md) (battle-mage), [Felix Fahnor](felix-fahnor-person.md) (assassin, mostly in the field raiding).
*   **Madame Selentis** ([Nysera Krivaltis](nysera-krivaltis-person.md)) is Hedrack's hand in Nulb — logistics chokepoint at the Old Mayor's Manor. Her brother [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) runs political subversion in Verbobonc City.
*   **The Wicked Three** — Iuz's independent agents: [Nassandra](nassandra-person.md) (Cowled Lady, giant raids in Kron Hills), [Ryjenis](ryjenis-person.md) (Lord Milinous imposter), Obmi (dwarven assassin).
*   **Lolth's network**: [Lareth](lareth-person.md) (Moathouse), [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) (Orb of Golden Death), [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md) (suspected triple agent controlling all Temple intelligence).
*   **Two forward strongholds** extend the Temple's reach: [Lareth](lareth-person.md) commands the [Moathouse Ruins](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) (Greater Temple, dual allegiance to Lolth) near Hommlet; Romag's Reavers garrison the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) (Earth Temple) on the Long Road.
*   **Forces of Good** are limited to two undercover agents: [Canoness Y'dey](canoness-y-dey-person.md) (as Mother Screng) and [Otis](otis-person.md) (blacksmith).
*   **Every supply line** funnels through the [55\. Hunting Cottage](55-hunting-cottage-landmark.md) tunnel — sever it and the entire Temple starves.
