AO-C11. The Collapsed Passage
=============================

> The Collapsed Passage — a storage corridor turned death trap in the mid-section of the crypt level beneath the [Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). Originally a dead-end supply corridor where the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) masons stored surplus construction materials for future crypt expansion — dressed stone blocks, iron tools, barrels of mortar. The corridor was never intended for burial and has no alcoves, no inscriptions, and no divine wards. The duergar excavation vibrations destabilized the ceiling and brought down thirty feet of passage, opening a breach through the western rock face into natural limestone caves in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). **Grimlok Ashpick** sealed the breach with a rubble wall. Something on the other side pushed through it. He sealed it again. It was pushed through again. He stopped sealing it. Cool, damp air flows through the gap carrying the smell of rot and ammonia — the signature of the carrion crawler nest in **AO-C12** beyond. The duergar have marked the passage entrance with a single Dwarvish rune on a strip of hide: _"Death."_ They do not enter. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has not explored it. The passage belongs to whatever lives on the other side.

_AO-C11. The Collapsed Passage — Storage Corridor and Creature Breach — Zone E Entrance_

* * *

### The Collapsed Passage

The passage entrance is easy to miss. Sixty feet south of the gallery entrance — in the middle section of **AO-C02** where the ward-glow transitions from flickering to steady — a narrow doorway opens in the west wall, set back in an alcove between two sealed burial recesses. The doorway is four feet wide and six feet tall, framed in plain dressed stone with no arch, no inscription, and no carved ornamentation. This was a utility corridor, not a ceremonial space, and the builders treated it accordingly. The doorway is partially concealed by its position — the alcove creates a shadowed recess that a party focused on the gallery's central corridor could walk past without noticing.

**Perception DC 14** to spot the doorway while walking the gallery. The DC drops to 10 if the party is specifically searching the west wall or if they have noticed the draft of cool air flowing from the recess. The smell of rot and ammonia is detectable within ten feet of the doorway (Perception DC 12 by smell alone).

An iron piton has been hammered into the wall at shoulder height beside the doorway. A strip of cured hide is tied to it, bearing a single Dwarvish rune: _"Death."_ Any character who reads Dwarvish understands the warning immediately. The piton is duergar work — the same type used throughout the camp in **AO-C04**. **Grimlok Ashpick** placed it himself.

The doorway is half-hidden in a shadowed recess between two glowing burial slabs, easy to miss if you are watching the corridor ahead. But the smell finds you first — something wet and organic cutting through the clean mineral air of the gallery, carried on a draft that should not be here. Cold air, flowing from a gap in the west wall that opens into darkness. A strip of hide nailed beside the doorway bears a single rune. Beyond it, the passage is choked with fallen stone. The ceiling has come down. Somewhere past the rubble, in the dark, something is breathing.

* * *

### The Corridor — Before the Collapse

The original passage ran forty feet southwest from the gallery doorway, terminating in a blank wall. It was five feet wide and seven feet tall — functional dimensions for transporting stone blocks and barrels by hand-cart. The [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) masons used it as construction overflow storage during the crypt's expansion periods. The corridor was supported by timber shoring — oak beams set at eight-foot intervals, anchored in the limestone walls and ceiling.

The timbers have rotted. Five centuries of damp air, no maintenance, and no traffic left the supports as brittle husks. When [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation work sent vibrations through the crypt level's stone, the weakened timbers failed. The ceiling came down in a section roughly thirty feet long, filling the corridor with broken limestone, shattered beams, and dust. The collapse punched through the thin western wall of the passage and into a natural limestone cavity beyond — a cave system in the hillside's rock face that the priory's builders never knew existed.

* * *

### The Rubble Field

The first thirty feet of passage beyond the doorway are choked with cave-in debris. Broken limestone slabs, shattered timber, stone dust, and crushed mortar barrels fill the corridor from wall to wall, rising in places to within two feet of the remaining ceiling. The footing is treacherous — loose stone shifts underfoot, broken timber splinters catch on boots and armor, and the dust is thick enough to muffle sound and reduce visibility.

*   **Difficult terrain** throughout the rubble field. Movement at half speed. Dashing requires an Athletics DC 12 check or the character stumbles and falls prone
*   **Ceiling instability:** The ceiling above the rubble field is cracked and sagging. Loud noise or significant vibration — combat, thunder damage, explosions, a character falling prone on the rubble — risks a secondary collapse. Each round of combat in the rubble field, roll 1d6. On a 1, a section of ceiling gives way: **3d6 bludgeoning damage, DEX DC 14 half, 10-foot area**. The collapse creates additional difficult terrain in the affected area and raises a cloud of choking dust (heavily obscured for 1 round, then lightly obscured for 2 rounds)
*   **Stealth through the rubble:** Possible but difficult. Stealth DC 15 to move through without dislodging loose stone. Failure does not trigger a collapse but produces noise audible from **AO-C12** (Perception DC 10 for creatures beyond the breach)

##### Salvageable Materials

The original construction supplies are still here, buried in the rubble. A party that spends 30 minutes searching (or 10 minutes with Investigation DC 14) can recover:

*   **20 dressed stone blocks:** Each weighs approximately 40 pounds. Intact, cleanly cut limestone — the same material used throughout the crypt level. These blocks can be used to **re-seal the breach** if the party wants to permanently block creature access from the natural caves. Sealing the breach requires 10 blocks, 1 hour of work, and a character proficient in mason's tools (or Athletics DC 14 to stack without mortar)
*   **6 iron chisels:** Old but functional. The mason's marks on the hafts match the marks on the dressed stone throughout the crypt — the same workshop, the same hands
*   **1 barrel of morite mortar:** A speciality compound used in Oeridian ecclesiastical construction —iteite can resist moisture, vermin, and moderate force. Still viable after five centuries due to the sealed barrel and dry storage conditions. Worth 25 gp to a mason who recognizes the material. If used with the stone blocks to seal the breach, the seal becomes permanent — nothing short of a siege engine or sustained magical force will breach it again

* * *

### The Breach

At the far end of the rubble field, where the passage's western wall once met the blank terminus, the cave-in has torn a ragged gap through the limestone into the natural caves beyond. The breach is approximately four feet wide and five feet tall — large enough for a Medium creature to pass through by ducking, large enough for a carrion crawler to squeeze its segmented body through without difficulty.

**Grimlok Ashpick** sealed this gap twice. The first time, a rubble wall of stacked stone blocks and packed clay. The ghouls pushed through within a week. The second time, heavier blocks wedged into the gap with iron pitons. The carrion crawlers dissolved the clay with their digestive secretions and shouldered the blocks aside. **Grimlok** did not attempt a third seal. He hammered the death-rune at the entrance and told his crew to stay out.

The breach is currently open. Cool, damp cave air flows through — significantly colder than the crypt level's ambient temperature, carrying the unmistakable smell of rot, ammonia, and the sweet-sick undertone of carrion crawler digestive acid. The stone around the breach is stained with a slick residue where the crawlers have squeezed through — Investigation DC 12 identifies it as organic secretion, not mineral seepage.

*   **Looking through the breach:** Darkvision or a light source reveals the first ten feet of natural cave beyond — unworked limestone, irregular walls and ceiling, a floor that slopes downward to the southwest. The cave is larger than the passage — at least fifteen feet wide and ten feet tall at the nearest point. Stalactites are visible overhead. The air moves, suggesting the cave system extends further
*   **Listening at the breach (Perception DC 14):** A wet, rhythmic scraping sound — like something large dragging itself across stone. The carrion crawlers moving in the nest chamber beyond. Occasionally, the clicking of mandibles
*   **Detect Magic:** Nothing. The breach, the caves, and the passage have no magical properties. This is purely physical territory — no wards, no consecration, no divine architecture. The faith of the [Holy Order](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) never reached this stone

### The Tracks

The dust in the passage and the rubble field preserves a record of everything that has passed through. A character who examines the ground finds overlapping tracks from multiple creatures:

*   **Investigation DC 10:** Heavy boot-prints — duergar work boots, wide-soled, deep impressions. These lead from the gallery doorway to the breach and back. They are old — weeks, possibly months. The duergar have not been here recently
*   **Investigation DC 12:** Clawed humanoid prints — narrow, three-toed, with deep gouge marks from extended claws. Ghouls. Multiple sets, overlapping, moving in both directions through the breach. These tracks are fresh — within the last day or two. The ghouls pass through regularly
*   **Investigation DC 14:** Wide, segmented impressions — multiple parallel tracks, like the treads of a living conveyor belt. Carrion crawlers. The tracks are concentrated near the breach, where the crawlers squeeze through, and fade within fifteen feet as the hard stone of the crypt passage gives way to the natural cave floor. A slick residue accompanies the tracks — the crawlers' digestive mucus
*   **Investigation DC 16:** Faint web-traces on the ceiling — gossamer-thin strands, nearly invisible, running along the upper corners of the passage from the gallery doorway to the breach and beyond. The phase spider uses this corridor as a hunting route, traveling along the ceiling in the Ethereal Plane and phasing into the material world when prey presents itself. Survival DC 14 confirms: the web-traces are anchor points, not a full web. The spider does not lair here. It passes through

### The Phase Spider — Hunting Behavior

The phase spider that lairs in **AO-C12** uses the Collapsed Passage as its primary hunting corridor. It does not nest here — its web is in the upper reaches of the Vermin Nest cave. But it phases through the breach regularly, stalking along the ceiling of the passage in the Ethereal Plane, watching for prey in the material world through the shimmering boundary between planes.

If the party enters the passage, there is a **30% chance** (d10: 1–3) that the phase spider is currently in the Ethereal Plane within the passage, watching. It does not attack immediately. It waits for the party to commit to the rubble field — difficult terrain, restricted movement, unstable ceiling — and then phases in behind the last character in the marching order.

*   **Ambush attack:** The spider phases into the Material Plane within 5 feet of the rearmost character. It makes a bite attack (+4 to hit, 1d10+3 piercing plus 4d8 poison on a failed CON DC 11 save) and then uses Ethereal Jaunt to phase back to the Ethereal Plane on the same turn if it hits. If it misses, it remains in the Material Plane until its next turn
*   **Repeat pattern:** The spider stalks from the Ethereal Plane for 1d4 rounds, then phases in for another bite. It targets injured or isolated characters. It does not engage in sustained melee — it is a hit-and-run predator, not a brawler
*   **Retreat:** If reduced to half HP (16 or fewer), the spider phases to the Ethereal Plane and retreats through the breach to its web in **AO-C12**. It does not return to the passage for 24 hours. If the party follows it to the nest, it fights to the death defending its territory
*   **Ceiling combat:** The spider clings to the ceiling when in the Material Plane. Melee attackers on the ground have disadvantage unless using reach weapons. The passage ceiling is seven feet high (pre-collapse) or as low as four feet in the rubble field — in the rubble field, the spider is within melee reach but has less room to maneuver

**DM Note:** The phase spider ambush is the passage's real threat — more dangerous than the rubble, more dangerous than the ceiling collapse. The first time the spider phases in and bites the rearmost character, the party learns that something hunts here. The second time, they learn it hunts from a place they cannot reach. The third time, they should be setting traps, guarding the rear, or pushing through to the nest to deal with the problem at its source.

### Sacred Layer

There is none. This corridor was never consecrated. It held stone blocks and mortar barrels, not the dead. The [Holy Order](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) did not pray here, did not carve inscriptions, did not light sacred flames. The passage exists in a gap between the divine architecture of the gallery and the raw natural stone of the hillside — unclaimed ground, belonging to neither the faith nor the earth.

That absence is what makes it dangerous. The wards in **AO-C02** do not extend here. The consecrated doorframes that block undead from crossing thresholds elsewhere in the crypt do not protect this doorway. The ghouls that enter the crypt level through this passage do so because it is the one corridor where the dead god's light does not reach. The phase spider phases through here because the Ethereal Plane boundary is thinner where the stone is not consecrated — divine architecture compresses the planar boundary, but ordinary stone does not. The builders left this passage unblessed because it did not need blessing. They were wrong.

### DM Notes

##### Running This Room

The Collapsed Passage is a gauntlet — a transition space between the managed threat of the crypt level and the wild threat of Zone E. It should feel different from the gallery. The gallery is architecture, history, and divine light. This passage is rubble, darkness, and things that hunt in the dark. The shift in tone should be immediate and physical — the moment the party steps through the doorway, the ward-glow from the gallery vanishes behind them. There is no blue light here. There is no warmth in the stone. There is dust and broken timber and the smell of something rotting in the dark ahead.

Pace the passage in three beats: the rubble field (difficult terrain, ceiling risk), the tracks (information gathering, building dread), and the breach (the decision point — go through or turn back). If the phase spider attacks during the crossing, it disrupts the rhythm and forces the party to fight in the worst possible terrain. That is the point.

##### The Strategic Choice

The party has three options when they find this passage:

*   **Ignore it.** The passage is optional. The party can continue exploring the gallery and the other zones without entering Zone E. But the creature threats persist — ghouls and the phase spider will continue appearing on the random encounter tables for **AO-C02** and **AO-C03** until the nest in **AO-C12** is cleared
*   **Clear the nest and seal the breach.** The optimal solution. Kill or drive off the creatures in **AO-C12**, then use the salvaged stone blocks and morite mortar to permanently seal the breach. This eliminates Zone E from the random encounter tables entirely and earns **Grimlok**'s grudging respect (the duergar lost a crew member to the phase spider)
*   **Seal the breach without clearing the nest.** Possible but temporary. The creatures inside will eventually breach the seal again — 1d6 days with a mortar seal, 2d6 days without. This buys time but does not solve the problem

##### Halven Sere in This Passage

If **Halven Sere** accompanies the party, he stops at the doorway and looks at the passage with an expression of confusion, not fear. _"This was a store-room. Stone and mortar. The brothers kept their extra blocks here for when the south gallery needed expanding."_ He has never been inside — the passage was sealed by the cave-in before his custodianship began. He does not know what is beyond the breach. He does know that the passage was never consecrated, and he says so with visible discomfort: _"We should have blessed every stone. We thought there was time."_

##### Connections

*   **AO-C02 (northeast):** The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead. The passage entrance is in the gallery's middle section, west wall, sixty feet south of the entrance arch. Ward-glow from the gallery is visible from the doorway but does not extend into the passage
*   **AO-C12 (southwest, through breach):** The Vermin Nest. Natural cave system beyond the breach. Carrion crawler nest and phase spider lair. The creature source

##### Durnik — The Missing Miner

_The duergar the phase spider killed. His body is in AO-C12._

**Grimlok** does not speak Durnik's name unprompted. If the party asks about the death-rune or why the duergar avoid this passage, he says only: _"We lost one. The thing that walks through walls took him. His body is in there somewhere. If you find his clan-token — a flat stone with my mark on it — bring it back. I would consider that a debt worth repaying."_

Returning Durnik's clan-token to **Grimlok** is worth more than gold — it shifts his disposition from transactional to cooperative. He offers the party his leather map freely and shares intelligence about the Black Mouth that he would otherwise withhold.
