GNQ-01a: The Dance of Great Moon's Glory
========================================

> On Readying 11, 576 CY — Great Moon's Glory, when Luna is full and Celene is new — Sister Aelwynn Thornroot, junior druid of the Old Faith, invites the party to witness a rite few outsiders in a generation are permitted to see: the Banas-Nimoram, the Dance of the Sprites of the Old Faith, at a hidden bowl in the western Gnarley called the Cupped Hollow. What begins as wonder becomes horror. A twelve-orc wedge from the Howling Moon tribe, led by the werewolf-chief Gorrogh Nightmaw, has learned that devouring sprites kindles a fey-blooded battle-rage — and arrives at the climax of the Dance to consume it. The Dance cannot be halted. It must complete its cycle or die in place. This is the DM overview and prep article for the GNQ-01 quest cluster — the journey and climax are detailed in GNQ-01b and GNQ-01c. Standalone Gnarley side quest, party level 3–4, approximately one session.

GNQ-01a: The Dance of Great Moon's Glory — Quest Overview
---------------------------------------------------------

##### How to Use This Article

This is the DM prep article. Read it before the session. It covers what the quest is, why it matters to the campaign, how Sister Aelwynn finds the party, what she will and will not reveal, and what changes in the world depending on outcome. Nothing in this article is read-aloud. The two run-at-the-table articles are:

*   **GNQ-01b: The Walk to the Cupped Hollow** — the journey phase, with read-aloud text, Pass Without Trace mechanics, and the three wonder-beats of the path east.
*   **GNQ-01c: The Banas-Nimoram at the Cupped Hollow** — the climax, with the Dance read-aloud, the Howling Moon's arrival, the sprite-consumption clock, the full combat encounter, and the three-branch aftermath.

Open 01a for prep. Open 01b when the party leaves for the Hollow. Open 01c when the party reaches the rim.

### Summary — What This Quest Is

The Cupped Hollow — called _Ael-Niath-Hollow_ (Moon's Drinking Bowl) by the druids of the Old Faith — is a hidden grassed depression set into the ridge country east of the [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), between the Long Road and Twilight Falls, near the northern verge of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). It is a sacred site of the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) and appears on no published map. Once in a generation, on the night of Great Moon's Glory when Luna is full and Celene is new, the Hollow hosts the **Banas-Nimoram** — the Dance of the Sprites of the Old Faith. The ambient spirit of the Gnarley manifests as a wheeling pattern of hundreds of palm-sized candle-flames, each alive, each a fragment of the forest's own localized fey essence made briefly visible.

**Sister Aelwynn Thornroot** — junior druid of the Old Faith under [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md), Archdruid and Guardian of the Western Gnarley — has for weeks prepared to attend the Dance alone. In 576 CY, she has been granted leave to bring witnesses. She has chosen the party. It is an honor few outsiders in a generation are offered.

The rite is threatened. A twelve-orc wedge from the Pomarj tribe called the Howling Moon, driven north from their village within the Fens of Tor by the planar thinning of the festival night, has followed their lycanthropic chieftain — **Gorrogh Nightmaw** — who has learned that each sprite he consumes kindles a stacking fey-blooded rage that thickens his hide, charges his teeth with force, and eventually makes him immune to the bindings fey-kind use on their enemies. Aelwynn does not yet know this. She believes she has given the party a gift. What she has actually done is brought witnesses to what may be a slaughter.

### Why This Quest Matters to the Campaign

**If the Dance survives:** The Gnarley's ambient fey magic renews for another generation. Aelwynn becomes a lifelong ally of the party, and [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) — rarely accessible to outsiders — grants the party an introduction. The party gains a foothold in the western Gnarley druidic intelligence network. The [Earth Temple Cultist](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) cells at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) never learn the Hollow exists.

**If the Dance is consumed:** The sprites do not return for a generation. Gorrogh, if he survives, carries his accumulated Fey-Blooded Rage stacks forward as a permanent condition and becomes a recurring threat to druids across the verge. The [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) close all groves to strangers for a year. [Celene](celene-organization.md) takes diplomatic notice. Most damagingly, the Earth Temple hears of the weakened site within ten days — Varnor Earthcaller marks the Hollow as a future extraction target, opening a direct thread into the main campaign's geomantic subplot.

**If Aelwynn dies:** The western verge loses its most trusted junior custodian. Hrudek's grief is private but he does not forget. Any party responsible for her death, by negligence or intent, finds the Druids closed to them for at least a year.

This quest is the party's first opportunity to earn standing with the Old Faith faction — a standing that will matter significantly when the Gift of Beauty politics of the main campaign begin to touch the Druids.

### Key Cast

*   **Sister Aelwynn Thornroot** — quest-giver, junior druid, custodian of the western verge. Human female, mid-forties, quietly fierce. Her full profile, stat block, and hard limits are in her own article.
*   [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) — offscreen authority. Aelwynn's mentor. Archdruid and Guardian of the Western Gnarley. Does not appear in person unless the quest's outcome requires him.
*   [Jaroo Ashstaff](jaroo-ashstaff-person.md) — bridge NPC. The Hommlet druid who makes the introduction in Hook A. Not a participant beyond that.
*   **Gorrogh Nightmaw** — antagonist. Werewolf-orc chief of the Howling Moon tribe. CR 4. Full stat block in GNQ-01c. Clever, patient, came for the sprites rather than the kills. Empowered by Fey-Blooded Rage — each sprite consumed grants him +2 temporary hit points and one stack of a stacking fey-tinted battle-fury.
*   **Howling Moon Wedge** — twelve orcs in formation: Gorrogh at the point, two Orogs (MM p. 247) flanking as lieutenants, and ten Orcs (MM p. 246) filling the body of the wedge. The formation mirrors the ur-vile wedge of the tonal source — disciplined, arrow-shaped, punching toward the bowl's center rather than fanning out to skirmish.
*   The Sprites of the Old Faith — what is being defended. Not creatures in the conventional sense. Localized fey essence of the Gnarley, condensed and made visible. They cannot flee the pattern. They cannot defend themselves.

### The Three-Act Shape

1.  **Act One — The Invitation.** Covered in this article. Aelwynn finds the party (see Plot Hooks below). She explains what she will explain, withholds what she withholds, and extends her invitation to the Dance on Readying 11. The party accepts, negotiates, or declines.
2.  **Act Two — The Journey.** Covered in **GNQ-01b**. Aelwynn casts Pass Without Trace and leads the party east from the last milestone on the Long Road to the rim of the Cupped Hollow. Three wonder-beats along the path. Two foreshadowing-beats (the wrong-step scars). Approximately five hours of in-game travel.

*   **Phase 1 — The Gathering.** The sprites drift in from the surrounding trees, one and two at a time, and settle in a slow loose spiral above the bowl floor. The party watches from the rim beside Aelwynn. This is the slow breath. No combat. No threat yet audible.  
    **Phase 2 — The Dance at Height.** The pattern closes. Hundreds of candle-flames braid into a wheeling ribbon of fey-blue divine light that rises above the bowl in a single living column. This is the wonder. This is what the party was invited to witness. It runs its full ten minutes uninterrupted. The Howling Moon wedge arrives at the _climax_ of Phase 2 — drums thickening at the trees, a horn, then Gorrogh breaking the rim. The wedge's arrival ends Phase 2 and triggers Phase 3.  
    **Phase 3 — The Desecration.** Combat. The wedge drives for the bowl center to feed. The sprite-consumption clock runs across roughly ten combat rounds. Outcome is determined by how many sprites are consumed before the Dance completes its cycle or is broken in place.

### Plot Hooks — How Aelwynn Finds the Party

Choose the hook that fits where your party currently is in the campaign. All five deliver the same invitation; only the framing differs. In all cases, Aelwynn extends the invitation face-to-face and will not send the offer by messenger — she must see the party before she commits.

##### Hook A — The Hommlet Introduction (Default)

*   **Where:** [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), grove behind the Church of the Old Faith.  
    **Trigger:** Party is in or passing through Hommlet on Readying 7–10.  
    **How:** [Jaroo Ashstaff](jaroo-ashstaff-person.md) sends word to Aelwynn that strangers of interest have arrived in the village. She comes in on Readying 9 or 10, watches the party from the margins of the village for one or two days without approaching, and sends Jaroo to bring them to his grove on the morning of the invitation. She has already made her decision by the time they arrive — she simply wants to confirm the impression. The first-meeting flavor block (see below) runs here in its canonical form.  
    **Tone:** Observed, considered, deliberate. She has chosen them. The invitation is a bestowal, not a request.

##### Hook B — The Long Road Encounter

*   **Where:** The Long Road between [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) and Verbobonc City, near the final milestone before Hommlet's eastern fence.  
    **Trigger:** Party is traveling the Long Road on Readying 8–10.  
    **How:** The party comes upon a middle-aged woman in a bark-colored cloak tending a waist-high weathered stone at the roadside. She is pouring a small measure of water from a clay flask into a worn spiral cut in the stone's top. She does not look up as they approach. If the party passes without disturbing her, she watches their backs recede and follows them to Hommlet at her own pace, presenting the invitation there. If the party stops and offers help unbidden — moving a fallen branch, offering clean water, waiting respectfully — she accepts with a small nod and invites them on the spot, naming the night and the milestone where they should meet her.  
    **Tone:** Observed in passing. The invitation is provisional; she is still measuring them as the meeting unfolds.

##### Hook C — The Mounted Borderer Referral from Anshan

*   **Where:** The [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md) tower outpost in the thorp of **Anshan** — the easternmost Viscounty holding, perched above the western edge of the **Fens of Tor**, where the Viscounty's cultivated land gives out to marshland.  
    **Trigger:** Party is doing business at Anshan, patrolling the eastern frontier, or making the run between Verbobonc and the Fen's edge.  
    **How:** The Borderer station commander briefs the party on deteriorating sign from the Fens. Howling Moon orc tracks have been cut within a day's ride of Anshan for the past fortnight — far further west than the tribe has ever strayed. Scouts have confirmed the Howling Moon's main village lies within the Fens east of **Emryd's Run**, the river that cuts the Fens in half running south-to-north. Two weeks ago the tribe began crossing Emryd's Run in force and pushing east into the Gnarley verge — behavior the Borderers cannot account for. The commander tells the party the druids may know more than the Viscounty does and hands them a sealed note of introduction to a druid named Thornroot who watches the western Gnarley. _(The commander does not know Aelwynn by face and has only the name; the Borderers and the Druids of the Gnarley Forest cooperate through formal relay, not friendship.)_ The party rides west, delivers the note at Jaroo's grove in Hommlet, and Aelwynn meets them there within a day.  
    **Tone:** Professional, consequential, campaign-wide. The invitation is not a gift — it is an alignment of two separate investigations that happen to converge on the same ridge. The party arrives at the Hollow already knowing that Gorrogh's movements are part of a larger pattern they will have reason to pursue after this quest ends.

##### Hook D — The Rescue

*   **Where:** Any stretch of the Long Road or its tributaries within two days of Hommlet.  
    **Trigger:** Party intervenes in a roadside incident where two young druid novices — Aelwynn's wards **Brenna** (16) and **Calenwyr** (14) — are threatened by bandits, Pomarj humanoid scouts, or a wandering monster. (Brenna and Calenwyr are named in the Aelwynn article; for campaigns where they have not yet been established, any two Druid-sworn novices will serve.)  
    **How:** The novices were returning from a foraging task along the verge when they were set upon. If the party rescues them, Calenwyr recognizes their conduct as something Sister Aelwynn would want to hear about. The novices lead the party back to Aelwynn's grove-house within the Gnarley. Aelwynn receives them with the formality a debt requires and, after a night of quiet conversation and observation, extends the invitation to the Dance as partial payment of the debt.  
    **Tone:** Obligation-driven, intimate. Aelwynn is paying them for saving what she loves; the invitation feels earned by action rather than bestowed by judgment.

##### Hook E — Through Hrudek

*   **Where:** Anywhere, at the Archdruid's convenience.  
    **Trigger:** Party is already aligned with the [Druids of the Gnarley Forest](druids-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) — perhaps through prior service, a druidic PC, or standing earned in another adventure.  
    **How:** [Hrudek Oakheart](hrudek-oakheart-person.md) himself sends word that the party should present themselves to Sister Aelwynn Thornroot at the westernmost Old Faith grove by Readying 10. He offers no explanation. The instruction is a test as much as a deployment. Aelwynn receives the party with visible surprise — she had asked for permission to bring witnesses but had not expected the Archdruid to choose them for her. She may be briefly curt from the imposition, then warm once she recognizes Hrudek's judgment in the selection.  
    **Tone:** Institutional. The party arrives with authority behind them; Aelwynn must recalibrate her own relationship to her mentor through the encounter. Good for campaigns where the party has accumulated Druid standing and deserves an escalation in trust.

### What Aelwynn Offers and Withholds in the First Meeting

Aelwynn will answer, straight and without adornment:

*   **What the sight is:** The Banas-Nimoram, the Dance of the Sprites of the Old Faith. Once in a generation. The forest's own spirit made briefly visible.
*   **Whether it is dangerous:** She will say honestly that she has heard of killings along the verge to the south — small things, forest things, a fawn found wrong-torn — and that she cannot be certain the Hollow is safe. She will not lie by omission about this. (She does not yet know it is Gorrogh.)
*   **Whether she vouches for them:** Yes, plainly, and the forest vouches through her. She will say this without flinching.

Aelwynn will deflect with warmth but no answer:

*   **Payment:** "There is no coin in this. If you come for coin you have misheard me."
*   **Exact route or location of the Hollow:** "You will walk with me. If I told you the way, the way would change."
*   **Whether she will fight alongside the party if trouble comes:** She will say, honestly, that she is not a warrior — and then decline to elaborate further.
*   **How she was chosen to host:** "That is between Hrudek and the forest. It is not a story for tonight."

Aelwynn will refuse outright:

*   **Any request to bring the invitation to more than the present party** — friends, hirelings, contacts. The forest chose these. No others.
*   **Any request to scry, divine, or otherwise preview the site before the night** — "The forest does not show its face to strangers before it shows it to them."
*   **Any invocation of Cuthbertine or Heironean moral authority as grounds for her trust** — she will become cold and the invitation will be at risk.

##### The Two-Question Rule

Aelwynn caps the first meeting at two questions from the party. This is deliberate — it forces the players to prioritize and signals that she is not a quest-board NPC who will dump exposition. If a player asks a third, she simply says, _"That is three,"_ and waits. It tells them she meant what she said. A player who is rude or dismissive triggers the withdrawal response — she says, _"Then perhaps Jaroo was wrong,"_ turns, and walks back into the trees. The invitation is withdrawn without negotiation. This is the single most important lesson to deliver in the encounter: her trust is finite and her "no" is absolute.

### The First Meeting — Read-Aloud

The grove behind the Church of the Old Faith is quieter than it should be, given how close it stands to the road. Wind moves in the upper branches, but not in the grass. A woman is standing near the largest of the oaks — not leaning on it, not tending it, just standing, the way a second tree might stand.She is in her middle years, neither tall nor short. An auburn braid streaked with grey falls over her left shoulder. A cloak the color of wet bark. A pale staff in her right hand, bound with spiraling silver wire, a small cluster of dried red berries near the top. She does not turn when you enter the grove. She turns when you _stop_.Her eyes are the green of old ferns. They are quietly delighted.**"Jaroo sent you."**It is not a question. She lets the silence hold for a breath before she goes on.**"I am Aelwynn. Thornroot, if you need a second name — my mother's, and hers before her. I keep the western verge from the last milestone on the Long Road to the deep trees. I serve Hrudek Oakheart, who keeps the whole of the Gnarley west of the Kron Hills. He does not know I am here. He will, before the moon rises full."**She lets that settle. Then, more quietly:**"I have something to show you. Not a trial. Not a contract. A sight. Twice in a generation the forest opens its hand and shows what it is. I am permitted to bring witnesses. One. Or a small few. I choose carefully, because the forest watches the choosing."**Her gaze moves across each of you in turn, slow and unhurried, and she does not pretend otherwise.**"I have watched you walk the village for two days. I have listened to what the innkeeper says about you when you are not in the room. I have asked the ravens. I am not certain of you. I am certain enough."**She shifts the staff to her other hand. The berries click softly.**"Readying eleven. Four nights from now. I will meet you at the old milestone past the last fence, two hours before moonrise. Bring boots you can walk in, and the quiet you were born with. Leave your steel bright but sheathed. Leave your gods at the gate — not out of disrespect. Out of manners. The place we are going has older names than yours, and it does not like to be introduced."**A small, dry almost-smile, there and gone.**"You may ask me two questions now. More will come later, if the forest still likes you. Choose them well."**

_For hooks B–E, adjust the opening paragraph's location and Jaroo-reference but preserve the core self-introduction, the two-question cap, and the closing instruction about the milestone. Her voice does not change from hook to hook; only the frame around it does._

### Consequences Matrix

**Party accepts and the Dance survives (≤6 sprites consumed):** Full Witness's Mark reward for each PC. Aelwynn's lifelong regard. Hrudek grants an introduction. Party enters the Druid faction's western-Gnarley intelligence network. Earth Temple never learns the Hollow exists. Gorrogh, if slain, is a one-off antagonist. Gorrogh, if escaped, returns as a wounded recurring threat — any Fey-Blooded Rage stacks he accumulated bleed off within a week and he carries no permanent empowerment forward.

**Party accepts and the Dance is wounded (7–9 sprites consumed):** Partial Witness's Mark. Aelwynn is grateful but grieves privately. She does not break her invitation or her thanks — she loves them still. Hrudek's introduction is deferred but not denied. No major political fallout. A generation of druidic mourning. If Gorrogh survives, he retains 3–5 Fey-Blooded Rage stacks as a lingering condition (tier-one threshold) for several months before they fade.

**Party accepts and the Dance dies (10+ sprites consumed):** No Mark. Aelwynn's arc shifts from custodian to avenger. She will not blame the party aloud but her invitation will never extend again to any outsider. Hrudek closes all Druid groves to strangers for one year. Celene sends a Seelie emissary to Verbobonc demanding the party's names. If Gorrogh survives, his Fey-Blooded Rage stacks become permanent at all three thresholds (force-damage teeth, refreshed Terrifying Howl, magical natural attacks and immunity to the charmed condition), and he hunts druids across the verge for the campaign's remainder. The Earth Temple at the Sepulchre-Priory hears of the weakened Hollow within ten days and marks it for extraction.

**Party declines the invitation:** Aelwynn does not push. She simply withdraws — the same quiet refusal she uses for betrayal. No immediate cost, but the party loses the opportunity permanently; the Dance is not rescheduled. Offscreen, Gorrogh consumes the full Dance unopposed. Aelwynn survives (he came for sprites, not druids, and she was outside the bowl floor) but is never the same. All the fallout of the total-failure outcome unfolds around the party without their involvement, and they may encounter its ripples without knowing their origin.

**Party betrays the rite at the Hollow (theft, violence against the sprites, mockery, Aelwynn's murder):** The worst outcome. All fallout of total failure applies, plus the Druids declare the party hostile. Seelie displeasure escalates to active hostility from Celene-aligned fey for 1d6 months. If Aelwynn survived the betrayal, she reports personally to Hrudek; if she did not, the party's faces are known to every druid on the verge within the week. This outcome should be rare and should be telegraphed clearly before it becomes irrevocable.

### Adventure Hooks

1.  **The Invitation (this quest).** See Hooks A–E above. Triggers GNQ-01b and GNQ-01c.
2.  **The Howling Moon's Fen Village.** Follow-up to Hook C or any successful GNQ-01 outcome. The party has reason to strike at the Howling Moon's main settlement east of **Emryd's Run** in the **Fens of Tor**. The Mounted Borderers from **Anshan** will support the ride. This is the natural sequel quest for parties who want to finish what started at the Hollow.
3.  **The Earth Temple's Extraction.** Triggers only if GNQ-01 fails. Within a season, [Earth Temple Cultist](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) operatives from the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) attempt to claim the weakened Hollow as a geomantic node. Aelwynn, if alive, begs the party for aid. The window is narrow.

##### GM Notes — Running This Quest

Travel time: From [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), half a day on foot with Aelwynn as guide. From Verbobonc City, add a full day's ride to Hommlet first. From **Anshan** (Hook C), allow two to three days' ride west via the Long Road.

Pacing: The invitation and first meeting should run 20–30 minutes of table time. The journey (GNQ-01b) another 30–45 minutes. The climax (GNQ-01c) 90–120 minutes. Budget a full session.

Do not rush the invitation. The first meeting is where the tone of the entire quest is set. Let Aelwynn's deliberation land. Let the two-question cap do its work. Players who spend their questions on coin or tactics have learned something about her; players who spend them on "what is the sight?" and "what is the danger?" have learned something too. Both responses are good play.

Do not let the party bypass the journey. Even if they have flying mounts, teleport options, or guides of their own, the Hollow is warded and cannot be found without Aelwynn's feet on the ground. The journey is the quest as much as the climax is.

Do not soften Aelwynn's withdrawal. If the party is rude and she walks away, she walks away. The invitation is not rescheduled. This is the single strongest lesson the quest teaches about the Druids, and it will pay for itself ten times over in the main campaign.

##### DM Quick Reference — Outcome Matrix

**DANCE SURVIVES (≤6 sprites consumed)**

*   Full Witness's Mark for each PC
*   Aelwynn's lifelong regard
*   Hrudek's introduction granted
*   Access to western Gnarley druidic intelligence
*   Earth Temple never learns Hollow exists

* * *

**DANCE WOUNDED (7–9 sprites consumed)**

*   Partial Witness's Mark
*   Aelwynn grieves privately but does not blame
*   Hrudek's introduction deferred, not denied
*   Generation of druidic mourning
*   Gorrogh retains 3–5 Fey-Blooded Rage stacks for months, then fades

* * *

**DANCE DIES (10+ sprites consumed)**

*   No Mark
*   Aelwynn shifts custodian → avenger
*   Druid groves closed to strangers for 1 year
*   Celene emissary arrives demanding names
*   Gorrogh's Fey-Blooded Rage permanent at all three thresholds
*   Earth Temple marks Hollow for extraction within 10 days

*   No immediate cost, permanent loss of opportunity

*   Aelwynn survives but is never the same
*   Full-failure fallout unfolds without party's involvement
