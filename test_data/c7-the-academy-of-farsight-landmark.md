C7 The Academy of Farsight
==========================

The Academy of Farsight, nestled in the heart of the cobblestone civic center of Verbobonc, is a testament to the city’s dedication to education and enlightenment. Founded in 422 CY by gnomish investors, this public academy has grown from its humble beginnings into an institution resembling a university. Known for its specialized curriculum focused on business and administration, the Academy of Farsight offers a unique educational experience amidst the scholarly pursuits typical of Verbobonc.

##### Approaching the Academy

The street widens here into a paved square of deliberate design. Tall, interconnected stone buildings rise on all sides, their roofs tiled in muted reds and blues. Brass plaques and carved lintels mark lecture halls, counting rooms, and translation chambers. Students—mostly gnomes, but not exclusively—move with ledgers tucked under their arms, murmuring figures and trade terms as they pass. This is not a place of spellcraft or prayer. This is where numbers decide fates.

#### Purpose & Reputation

The Academy of Farsight exists to do what no temple or noble house truly wishes to manage:  
**the careful, tedious, indispensable work of running a city.**

Its focus is unapologetically practical:

*   Accounting and advanced bookkeeping
*   Trade law and civic contracts
*   Mathematics, tariffs, weights, and measures
*   Foreign and trade languages (Keoish, Baklunish, Elvish, Gnomish, Old Oeridian)

Among guilds and merchants, a Farsight certification is respected currency. Among nobles, it is quietly feared.

* * *

#### Campus & Facilities

**Interconnected Civic Buildings  
**Rather than a single grand hall, the Academy is a network of linked structures, each devoted to a discipline—accounting halls, language chambers, legal classrooms, and examination rooms.

**The Counting Library  
**A vast but sober archive of trade manuals, shipping logs, tax precedents, and mercantile histories. No grimoires here—only the slow magic of precedent.

**Lecture Courts  
**Tiered stone halls where instructors teach by example, often dissecting real disputes, failed ventures, or famous bankruptcies from Verbobonc’s past.

#### History

*   **Founding:** Established in 422 CY by visionary [Gnomes](gnomes-species-1.md), the Academy was intended to formalize and enhance the business acumen among locals.
*   **Expansion:** Over the years, as Verbobonc expanded, so did the Academy, growing in size and scope to meet the increasing demand for business education.

#### Politics & Influence

*   **Guild Integration:** Most Verbobonc guilds recruit directly from the Academy
*   **Civic Dependence:** City Hall relies on Farsight-trained clerks for audits and records
*   **Aristocratic Distance:** Many noble houses prefer tutors or foreign academies, creating a social divide that the Academy quietly resents
*   **Gnomish Leverage:** Though public, its heart remains gnomish—giving Prince Jimm long-term influence over Verbobonc’s economic spine

#### Motivation and Goals

*   **Educational Excellence:** Strives to provide the best business education in the region, focusing on practical skills that students can immediately apply.
*   **Community Integration:** Aims to integrate more fully with local businesses and guilds to ensure students have practical experience and job placement after graduation.

### Notable People

*   **Gnomish Influence:** Predominantly attended by gnomes due to the founders' heritage and the practical nature of its curriculum.
*   **Faculty and Alumni:** Includes a number of respected scholars in the fields of finance, law, and the liberal arts, many of whom are published authors or have developed innovative business practices.

#### Goods and Services

*   **Educational Programs:** Offers courses in accounting, bookkeeping, various languages, and math-related subjects.
*   **Certifications:** Provides certification in various business disciplines, enhancing graduates' employability in Verbobonc and beyond.

#### Use in the Campaign (DM Notes)

The Academy of Farsight is invaluable for:

*   Tracing **Temple of Elemental Evil funding routes**
*   Identifying falsified ledgers or shell merchants
*   Training or legitimizing PC backgrounds (clerks, merchants, guild agents)
*   Political intrigue where _numbers matter more than swords_

A single cooperative instructor can unravel months of cult logistics—or bury them deeper with “missing paperwork.”

* * *

### Closing Note

The Academy of Farsight does not teach heroes.  
It teaches the people who decide **who gets paid, who gets fined, and who gets forgotten**.

Ignore it at your peril.

First Meeting — **Sapply Welber**
---------------------------------

##### Boxed Narration (Read Aloud)

You are ushered into a tall counting chamber lined with shelves of ledgers and rolled parchments. Sunlight filters through high windows, falling across a long oak table etched with chalked figures that have not yet been wiped away. Brass weights and scales sit neatly arranged, every tool placed with deliberate care.

At the table stands a gnome in a scholar’s robe of muted blue-grey, hands clasped behind his back. His beard is meticulously trimmed, his spectacles perched low on his nose as he finishes a calculation without once looking up.

Only when the chalk clicks softly against the slate does he turn to face you.

His eyes are sharp. Assessing.  
As though you are already being counted.

* * *

##### Dialogue (Sapply Welber)

“Ah. You are early… or late. That depends entirely on what you believe you are here to accomplish.”

He gestures to the table, not inviting you to sit—merely allowing it.

“You stand within the Academy of Farsight. We do not deal in heroics, proclamations, or destiny. We deal in **records**, **contracts**, and **consequences**.”

His gaze moves from face to face, lingering just long enough to make each of you aware you’ve been measured.

“So. Speak plainly.”

A thin, polite smile touches his lips.

“Are you here to **learn**, to **prove**, or to **untangle someone else’s mistakes**?”

He folds his hands.

“And before you answer—know this: numbers do not lie.  
People do.”

* * *

##### DM Notes (Optional Use)

*   Sapply is cordial but **never deferential**
*   He will:
*   Offer help if the party presents **evidence, ledgers, names, or contracts**
*   Dismiss vague accusations or “gut feelings”
*   Mentioning **Prince Jimm**, **guilds**, or **trade routes** immediately sharpens his interest
*   He remembers the party permanently after this meeting

##### **The Academy of Farsight**

A gnomish-founded public academy devoted to commerce, administration, and civic literacy, the Academy of Farsight trains the clerks, factors, translators, and accountants who keep Verbobonc running. Though often dismissed by nobles as “practical schooling,” its graduates quietly control contracts, tariffs, and ledgers across the city—making the Academy one of Verbobonc’s most underestimated centers of power.

*   Type: College / Civic Academy
*   District: Civic Centre District (C1–16)
*   Founded: 422 CY
*   Ownership & Patronage: Public institution, endowed by gnomish merchant families and civic trusts
*   Primary Factions: Gnomish merchant houses of Verbobonc
*   Local guilds: (Brewers, Bakers, Bankers, Lapidaries, Advocates)
*   Indirect patronage from Prince Jimm through gnomish civic influence

##### Academy of Farsight — Certified Associate

_Wondrous non-magical credential (requires Academy approval)_

A formal civic certification issued by the Academy of Farsight and signed by **Sapply Welber**. While not magical, it carries institutional authority throughout Verbobonc and its allied trade networks.

* * *

#### Mechanical Benefits (5e)

##### **1\. Scholar of Ledgers**

You gain **advantage** on **Intelligence (Investigation)** checks made to:

*   Examine financial records, ledgers, contracts, or tax rolls
*   Identify forged, altered, or falsified documents
*   Trace money, supply routes, or mercantile ownership

This applies directly to uncovering **Temple funding, cult logistics, or shell merchants**.

* * *

##### **2\. Civic Credibility**

When presenting your certification to:

*   City clerks
*   Guild officials
*   Merchant houses
*   Academy-aligned institutions

You gain **advantage on one Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Deception)** check per social scene, as long as your request is _reasonable and procedural_.

This benefit **does not apply** to intimidation or overtly illegal requests.

* * *

##### **3\. Farsight Recall**

Once per **long rest**, you may treat an **Intelligence (History)**, **(Arcana)**, or **(Investigation)** check as if you rolled a **10 on the die**, before modifiers.

This represents trained recall, not inspiration.

* * *

##### **4\. Academic Safe Haven**

Within Verbobonc, you can usually:

*   Secure **basic lodging** at Academy-affiliated hostels
*   Access **restricted public archives** with supervision
*   Request **non-magical research assistance**

DMs may waive minor fees or reduce research time by **25–50%**.

* * *

##### **5\. Pattern Recognition**

When the DM presents **three or more related clues** involving:

*   Trade anomalies
*   Repeated names or symbols
*   Supply inconsistencies

The DM may (once per mystery arc) offer you:

“You notice a pattern forming…”

This is a _permission tool_, not an auto-solution.

* * *

##### Limits & Consequences (Important)

*   The certification can be **revoked** if the PC:
*   Publicly falsifies records
*   Abuses civic authority
*   Assists enemies of Verbobonc openly
*   Repeated misuse may cause:
*   Disadvantage on future civic checks
*   Summons from Sapply or the Academy council
*   Cult infiltration or corruption may place the PC under **quiet observation**

* * *

##### Roleplay Hooks for the DM

*   Clerks _trust you first_
*   Merchants _ask your opinion_
*   Cult agents may try to **bribe or mislead** you
*   Nobles may see you as _useful_… or _dangerous_

This certification turns Intelligence into **social leverage**, not combat power.

* * *

##### Optional Upgrade: Senior Certification (Mid-Campaign)

At DM discretion, after major contributions:

*   Add **+1d4** to one Investigation or History check per long rest
*   Gain access to **sealed civic records**
*   Be consulted quietly during **Noble Ambitions** political arcs
