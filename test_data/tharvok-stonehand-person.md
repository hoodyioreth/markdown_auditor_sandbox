Tharvok Stonehand
=================

### Dread Delver of the Black Earth

_Romag’s Voice in the Long Road Outpost_

Tharvok Stonehand is not a visionary like Romag, nor a political manipulator like [Lucius Graeme](lucius-graeme-person.md). He is something far more dangerous in the field — a patient, unyielding enforcer of doctrine.

Where [Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful seduced the Moathouse with charm and ambition, Tharvok governs through inevitability. His presence in the outpost is not flamboyant. It is oppressive. Heavy. Inescapable.

Romag did not send him to negotiate. He sent him to root the Earth Temple into the soil of southern Verbobonc and let it spread like bedrock beneath a crumbling foundation.

This outpost exists for one purpose:  
**Recruit. Raid. Supply. Crush.**

Tharvok is its warden.

* * *

#### Role Within the Earth Temple

Tharvok is one of Romag’s trusted second-tier priests — a **field commander-priest**, not a chamber politician. His authority is unquestioned among Black Earth cultists and backed by Black Earth Guard detachments.

Romag sees in him the ideal Dread Delver: disciplined, durable, and utterly loyal to the philosophy that civilization must be ground into dust

**Function at the Outpost**

*   Oversees recruitment of raiders from:
*   Nulb
*   Verbobonc City
*   Kron Hills outliers
*   Organizes Long Road ambushes between:
*   Nigb’s Rest (10 miles north)
*   Sheernobb (10 miles south)
*   Coordinates prisoner transfer (slaves, sacrifices, hostages)
*   Supervises interrogation of captured enemies
*   Secures supplies for transport to:
*   The Greater Temple
*   The Earth Temple specifically

All major orders pass through **Lucius Graeme**, who delivers Romag’s instructions and reports results back to the Temple hierarchy.

Tharvok does not question those orders. He executes them.

* * *

#### Philosophy & Doctrine

Tharvok embodies the Earth Temple doctrine as outlined in the Cult of the Black Earth

*   Strength is inevitable.
*   Civilization is temporary.
*   Stone outlasts all.

He frequently reminds his followers:

“Men build. The earth buries.”

He teaches recruits that they are not bandits — they are instruments of geological correction.

**Core Beliefs**

*   Massacre is acceptable if it fractures alliances.
*   Fear destabilizes governance.
*   Gnome–human distrust weakens Verbobonc.
*   The Elder Elemental Eye desires collapse, not conquest.

He despises the Air Temple, seeing them as mercurial and unreliable. He openly mocks their unpredictability and considers them spiritually hollow compared to the “honest weight of stone.”

* * *

#### Control of the Outpost

Unlike Lareth’s theatrical command of the Moathouse, Tharvok runs a fortified, methodical operation.

##### Structural Control

*   Central shrine chamber dedicated to the Black Earth
*   Guard barracks manned by Black Earth Guard
*   Holding cells for prisoners
*   Supply stores for weapons, chain, and sacrificial implements
*   A concealed stone chamber for ritual burials (initiation rite inspired by Black Earth practice)

##### Security Measures

*   Earth-shaped choke points
*   Loose stone floors for tremor sensing
*   Trapped approaches disguised as natural terrain
*   Earthbind and Earth Tremor frequently prepared

Tharvok has personally reshaped portions of the terrain around the outpost using Mold Earth and stone shaping techniques consistent with Earth Temple spell doctrine.

* * *

#### The Prisoner: Custodian of the Sepulchre-Priory

Among the most significant developments under Tharvok’s tenure is the capture of the **custodian priest from the Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath**. This priest was seized during a raid tied to the interception of the Veluna Rao delegation and the theft of the Gift of Beauty. The custodian remains alive.

Why?

Because Tharvok understands leverage.

##### Purpose of the Prisoner

The prisoner is kept in near-burial conditions — partially confined in stone-lined restraints as symbolic humiliation. Tharvok considers this poetic justice.

*   Extract knowledge of Veluna routes
*   Break morale of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) loyalists
*   Use as potential sacrifice if required
*   Trade asset if political conditions shift

### The Gift of Beauty

Romag’s raid successfully captured the **Gift of Beauty**, a sacred diplomatic artifact intended for [Celene, Faerie Kingdom of](../c/celene2C-faerie-kingdom-of-category.md). Tharvok’s outpost served as its first secured staging point. This changes everything.

##### Strategic Implications

*   Earth Temple prestige rises sharply
*   Romag gains leverage over rival temples
*   Greater Temple leadership becomes alert
*   Lareth the Beautiful begins maneuvering to seize it

The artifact is currently:

*   Secured in a reinforced stone reliquary
*   Guarded by Black Earth elite
*   Examined for hidden magical properties
*   Considered either:
*   A bargaining chip
*   A sacrifice to the Elder Elemental Eye
*   A symbol of Earth Temple dominance

Tharvok believes it should be crushed ceremonially. Lucius has advised patience. Romag has not yet decided.

* * *

#### Relationship with Lucius Graeme

Lucius functions as Romag’s courier and political nerve line. Tharvok does not like him. But he respects him.

Lucius:

*   Delivers sealed orders
*   Assesses recruitment efficiency
*   Reports discipline failures
*   Carries intelligence regarding rival temple movements
*   Monitors Tharvok’s loyalty

Their relationship is professional, not friendly. Lucius ensures the outpost serves the Greater Temple’s strategy — not merely Romag’s ambition.

* * *

#### Rivalry with Lareth the Beautiful

Once [Lareth](lareth-person.md) learned of the Gift of Beauty’s capture, he began maneuvering.

Tharvok sees Lareth as:

*   Ambitious
*   Dangerous
*   Politically favored
*   Not to be trusted

If Lareth attempts to claim the artifact, Tharvok will resist — though he understands that if the Greater Temple commands compliance, he must yield. The tension between Earth Temple dominance and Greater Temple authority is palpable.

* * *

#### Combat & Divine Power

Tharvok is a Black Earth Priest consistent with the Earth Temple specialty progression.

##### Abilities Reflecting Doctrine

*   Earth Tremor (battlefield disruption)
*   Maximilian’s Earthen Grasp (capture and restraint)
*   Earthbind (control of aerial threats)
*   Unyielding reaction ability (cannot be knocked prone)
*   Tremorsense within stone corridors

He fights methodically:

*   Cripple movement
*   Divide formations
*   Collapse terrain
*   Force surrender

He never wastes effort on spectacle.

### Detection & Escalation Protocol — Tharvok's Surveillance Doctrine

**Tharvok Stonehand runs the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) like a military garrison, not a dungeon.** He has implemented a layered detection system built on Earth Temple doctrine: the ground beneath your feet is always watching. Every approach to the priory is monitored. Every interior corridor has a detection mechanism. The party cannot simply walk up to this outpost, kick the door in, and fight room to room without triggering an escalating response.

This section provides the DM with specific DCs, patrol schedules, and alert stages so that player decisions about stealth, timing, and approach matter mechanically.

 

#### Layer 1: Outer Perimeter — Approach Detection

**Range:** 500 feet from the priory walls in all directions  
**Coverage:** The Long Road (north and south), the forest trails (east and west), and the overgrown garden approach

 

##### Gnoll Patrols

*   **Composition:** 2 gnolls per patrol, operating in pairs on a rotating circuit
*   **Schedule:** Patrols circle the perimeter every 90 minutes during daylight, every 60 minutes at night. Tharvok shortens night patrols because gnolls have darkvision and are more alert after dark
*   **Passive Perception:** 12 (gnolls). The gnolls are disciplined by Tharvok's standards — they do not hunt or scavenge during patrol
*   **Stealth DC to avoid gnoll patrols:** DC 12 (forest cover, 100+ feet from walls). DC 15 (open ground within 100 feet). DC 18 (approaching the wall breaches during a patrol pass)
*   **Gnoll alert behavior:** If a patrol spots intruders, one gnoll breaks off to raise the alarm at the nearest wall breach while the other tracks the intruders from a distance. They do not engage alone — Tharvok has drilled this discipline into them

 

##### Wall Breach Sentries

*   **Composition:** 1 Black Earth Guard at each of the two wall breaches, plus 1 guard at the main gate
*   **Passive Perception:** 14 (Black Earth Guards are better trained than gnolls)
*   **Stealth DC to pass a wall breach undetected:** DC 14 (night, no light sources). DC 17 (dusk/dawn, partial visibility). DC 20 (daylight — the breaches have clear sightlines for 60 feet)
*   **Sentry rotation:** Guards rotate every 4 hours. During the 5-minute handoff, both the outgoing and incoming guard are present — Passive Perception effectively becomes 16 (advantage from two observers)

 

##### The Garden Approach

*   **Special condition:** The gnoll bivouac is positioned outside the walls near the garden. This is not a formal sentry post — it is a camp of 4–6 gnolls who sleep, eat, and sharpen weapons in the open. But their presence means the garden approach is effectively covered 24 hours a day by at least 2 waking gnolls at any time
*   **Stealth DC to bypass the bivouac:** DC 14 (night, approaching from downwind). DC 11 (night, if the party creates a distraction elsewhere that draws gnoll attention). DC 18 (daylight — the bivouac has unobstructed sightlines to the garden wall)

 

#### Layer 2: Interior Detection — The Stone Listens

Once inside the priory walls, the party faces Tharvok's signature innovation: **tremor-sensing floors**. He has spent weeks using Mold Earth to loosen specific stone sections throughout the priory's corridors. These loose stones shift underfoot, creating faint vibrations that propagate through the building's stone structure.

 

##### Tremor-Sensing Floors

*   **Locations:** Main corridor (AO-01 to AO-06), the north wing approach (AO-15 to AO-18), and the crypt stairwell entrance
*   **Detection mechanism:** Any creature weighing more than 50 lbs walking at normal speed across a tremor floor generates vibrations detectable by anyone with tremorsense or by guards stationed in adjacent rooms (DC 10 Perception to hear the distinctive stone-on-stone grinding)
*   **Stealth DC to cross a tremor floor silently:** DC 16 (moving at half speed, testing each stone). DC 20 (moving at normal speed). Automatically detected if running or in combat
*   **Countermeasure:** DC 14 Investigation to identify the loose stones on visual inspection. Once identified, a character can map a path across solid stones — reducing the Stealth DC to 12. Fly, Levitate, or Spider Climb bypasses the tremor floor entirely
*   **Who monitors:** [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has tremorsense 30 ft when in contact with stone. If he is in AO-17, he automatically detects any creature crossing the north wing tremor floor within 30 feet — no check required

 

##### Ward Glyphs

*   **Locations:** AO-18 doorway (Tharvok's quarters / reliquary), AO-17 doorway (Varnor's quarters), crypt stairwell entrance
*   **Detection:** DC 15 Investigation or DC 14 Arcana to spot before triggering
*   **Effect:** Thunderwave variant — Constitution DC 14, 2d8 thunder damage, pushed 10 feet. The noise is audible throughout the entire wing. These glyphs serve as **alarms** as much as traps — their primary purpose is to announce intruders
*   **Countermeasure:** Dispel Magic DC 14. Thieves' Tools DC 16 to carefully deactivate the trigger plate without triggering the glyph. [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) maintains the ward glyphs and can reactivate them within 10 minutes if they are dispelled

 

##### Caidax's Arcane Eye

*   **Condition:** If [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) has reason to suspect intruders (a missed patrol, a triggered glyph, or a report from the gnolls), he casts Arcane Eye and sends it through the priory corridors
*   **Detection:** The Arcane Eye is invisible. DC 19 Perception or DC 15 Arcana (if the party is actively scanning for magical surveillance) to detect it
*   **Countermeasure:** Dispel Magic, Antimagic Field, or destroying the sensor (AC 10, 1 HP, immune to non-magical damage). If detected, the party knows they have been seen — Caidax reports their position, numbers, and equipment to Tharvok immediately

 

#### Layer 3: Surveillance Countermeasures — What the Party Can't Easily Do

Tharvok has anticipated common adventurer tactics and prepared specific countermeasures:

*   **Invisibility:** Tremor-sensing floors detect invisible creatures by weight. Gnolls with Passive Perception 12 may hear footsteps (contested Stealth vs. Perception). [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s tremorsense detects invisible creatures within 30 feet automatically
*   **Disguise / Deception:** Black Earth Guards know each other by name and face. An unfamiliar face in Earth Temple robes triggers suspicion immediately — DC 18 Deception to bluff past a guard who has time to study the disguise. DC 14 if the guard is distracted or in combat. **Sergeant Drogath "Stonejaw" Varn** knows every member of the garrison and cannot be fooled by visual disguise alone — only magical compulsion or a flawless cover story (DC 20 Deception) will pass his scrutiny
*   **Scrying / Divination (party targeting the priory):** [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has cast Nondetection on AO-17 (his quarters) and AO-18 (the reliquary). Scrying on Tharvok or Varnor while they are in these rooms fails automatically. Scrying elsewhere in the priory works normally
*   **Flying / Climbing the walls:** Wall breach sentries have clear sightlines to the rooftops of the three towers. DC 16 Stealth to reach a tower roof without being seen from below. The north tower (tallest) has a gnoll lookout posted on the parapet at night — Passive Perception 12, darkvision 60 feet
*   **Tunneling / Burrowing:** Varnor's tremorsense detects underground vibrations within 30 feet. Any tunneling within that range alerts him immediately. Beyond 30 feet, natural earth and stone conduct vibrations poorly — burrowing from 50+ feet away is undetected unless it breaks through a priory wall or floor

 

### Alert Stages — Escalation Chain

Tharvok's garrison responds to threats in escalating stages. Each stage triggers specific actions and notifications. The DM should track which alert stage the priory is in and apply the corresponding response.

#### Alert Stage 0: Normal Operations

**Trigger:** Default state. No threats detected  
**Garrison posture:** 3 wall/gate sentries on duty. 1 gnoll patrol circling. Gnoll bivouac at half-alert (2 awake). Off-duty guards in barracks (AO-06). Tharvok in AO-18 or the shrine. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) in AO-17 studying geomantic notes. [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) in AO-13 working on false-flag research

#### Alert Stage 1: Suspicious Activity

**Trigger:** A gnoll patrol reports something unusual (tracks, campfire smoke, distant figures). A sentry hears an unexplained noise. A tremor floor detects unidentified movement  
**Tharvok's response:** Doubles gnoll patrol frequency (every 45 minutes day, 30 minutes night). Posts an additional guard at each wall breach. Sends [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) to cast Arcane Eye for a corridor sweep. Does NOT alert the chain of command yet — suspicious activity may be wildlife, a lost traveler, or a false alarm  
**Duration:** Maintained for 4 hours after last suspicious report. If nothing further occurs, reverts to Stage 0

#### Alert Stage 2: Intruders Confirmed

**Trigger:** A sentry visually confirms intruders. A ward glyph detonates. Caidax's Arcane Eye spots armed individuals inside the priory. Combat is heard  
**Tharvok's response:**

*   All off-duty guards in AO-06 barracks are roused — available within 2 rounds
*   Gnoll bivouac fully mobilizes — 4–6 gnolls converge on the nearest wall breach within 3 rounds
*   Tharvok moves to AO-18 and positions himself between the reliquary (the Gift of Beauty) and the door. He fights defensively until he understands the threat
*   [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) casts Earth Tremor in the corridor nearest the intruders (difficult terrain, DC 14 DEX save, 1d6 bludgeoning) to slow their advance
*   [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) provides spell support from a defensible position — Lightning Bolt down corridors, Shield on himself, Counterspell on enemy casters
*   [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) (if present at the priory) leads the tactical counterattack — flanking maneuvers through side corridors to get behind the intruders
*   **Prisoners in AO-20 are ignored.** All resources go to the Gift of Beauty. **Halven Sere** and any other captives are left unguarded during a Stage 2 alert — this is the party's best window to rescue prisoners

#### Alert Stage 3: Breach — The Gift Is Threatened

**Trigger:** Combat reaches AO-18. The reliquary thunderwave trap detonates. Intruders are seen carrying the reliquary or the Gift  
**Tharvok's response:** Fights to the death. Does not retreat. Does not negotiate. See the Gift of Beauty Alarm Protocols for the full chain of command notification timeline (Tharvok → [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) → [Romag](romag-person.md) → [Smigmal Redhand](smigmal-redhand-person.md) → **Lucius Graeme** → **Hedrack**)

#### Alert Stage 4: Post-Attack Lockdown

**Trigger:** The party retreats or is repelled after a confirmed breach. Tharvok survives  
**Tharvok's response:**

*   Full lockdown. No one enters or leaves the priory without Tharvok's personal authorization
*   Gnoll patrols doubled and extended to 1-mile radius
*   [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) recasts all ward glyphs and adds Alarm spells to every exterior entrance
*   **Lucius Graeme** is notified via courier. His response: dispatch intelligence assets to identify the attackers — descriptions go out to agents in the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md), on the Long Road, and in Nulb
*   If the party took evidence (the Slikkina letter, scriptorium documents, Caidax's notes), Tharvok reports this to [Romag](romag-person.md) as a counterintelligence breach. Romag orders the false-flag operation suspended and [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s hidden valley camp evacuated
*   The priory remains at Stage 4 indefinitely until Romag sends new orders or reinforcements arrive

 

### DM Notes: Using the Detection System in Play

The detection and escalation system is designed to **reward player planning and punish brute force**. A party that scouts the priory, times the gnoll patrols, identifies the tremor floors, and enters through a wall breach at the right moment can reach AO-18 before the garrison fully mobilizes. A party that charges in through the front gate triggers Stage 2 within 1 round and faces the entire garrison in coordinated defense.

*   **Stealth approach (optimal):** The party spends 1–2 hours observing patrol patterns (DC 12 Perception to time the gnoll circuit). They enter through the east wall breach during a gap (DC 14 Stealth). They identify and avoid tremor floors (DC 14 Investigation + DC 12 Stealth). They reach AO-18 at Alert Stage 0 with the garrison unaware. The party has 2–3 rounds to work on the reliquary before any response mobilizes
*   **Distraction approach (viable):** The party creates a diversion at the garden bivouac (fire, noise, a summoned creature) to draw gnolls and sentries. This drops the Stealth DCs at the opposite wall breach by 3. However, it raises the priory to Alert Stage 1 — Caidax deploys Arcane Eye, and the party has a narrower window before Stage 2
*   **Frontal assault (punishing):** Immediate Stage 2. Full garrison response within 3 rounds. Tharvok fights from a prepared position. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) collapses corridors with Earth Tremor. [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) uses Lightning Bolt in chokepoints. The party faces every combatant in the priory simultaneously — this is a potential TPK scenario for a level 4 party unless they have overwhelming resources
*   **Infiltration by deception (high risk, high reward):** If the party has Earth Temple robes and a plausible cover story (DC 18 Deception at the gate, DC 20 to fool **Sergeant Drogath "Stonejaw" Varn**), they can enter at Stage 0 and move freely — but one failed check triggers Stage 2 with the party surrounded and inside the garrison
