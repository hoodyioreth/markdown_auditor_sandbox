Amarien of Ekbir
================

### Professor of Conjuration and Evocation

Amarien is a short, slender Baklunish woman with luminous green eyes that miss very little. Her skin bears the warm golden tones common to her people, and her straight, fine-textured hair—dark with a bluish cast—is usually bound in silk cords or braided with small charms. She favors bright patterns and boldly colored robes, often layered with flowing scarves or embroidered sashes that recall the courts of her homeland.

She dresses lightly, preferring reinforced cloth armor beneath her gowns, and is rarely seen without her wand and quarterstaff close at hand. Perched nearby, or basking in the warmth of a spell’s aftermath, is her familiar: **Nelothel**, a sleek green lizard with clever eyes and an unsettling habit of appearing exactly where one least expects.

> “Magic is not merely summoned—it is invited. And it remembers how you treat it.” — Professor Amarien, during her first lecture in Verbobonc

* * *

#### Origins and Arrival in Verbobonc

Amarien hails from the **Sultan’s Court of Ekbir**, far to the west beyond the Crystalmist Mountains. Trained among Baklunish scholars who view magic as both art and responsibility, she was accustomed to ceremonial learning, courtly intrigue, and spellcraft practiced in equal measure.

Her journey eastward was not one of exile, but of purpose.

The Wrinkle Academy’s reputation—chaotic, inquisitive, and unbound by rigid magical dogma—intrigued her. She came to Verbobonc to assist in what she describes as the Academy’s “wanderings”: its constant experimentation, its refusal to accept settled answers, and its willingness to explore magic beyond tradition or orthodoxy.

Her presence lends the Academy a rare bridge between Baklunish arcane philosophy and Flanaess scholastic practice.

* * *

#### Teaching Style and Reputation

Professor Amarien is **dramatic**, **florid**, and unapologetically theatrical.

Her lectures are as likely to begin with a story as a theorem, and many students recall her classes as unforgettable experiences rather than lessons. She believes magic must be _felt_ as well as understood.

Notable examples include:

*   Transforming an entire lecture hall into a **desert oasis**, complete with silk pavilions, conjured palms, and several tons of imported sand, to teach elemental balance.
*   Releasing **bound earth elementals** among students during an examination to test composure, adaptability, and teamwork under pressure.
*   Using conjured heat, wind, and illusion to recreate the climate and cadence of Baklunish spellcasting traditions.

Despite her flamboyance, Amarien adheres strictly to Academy rules when pressed. Her **celestial camel, Halamin**, is kept respectfully housed as a finely crafted **sandalwood stall** within the stables, rather than roaming the grounds as she would prefer.

Students may laugh, marvel, or panic—but they learn.

* * *

#### Personality and Beliefs

Amarien is wise, proud, and deeply disciplined beneath her expressive exterior. She believes magic carries cultural memory and that reckless spellcasting without context leads to spiritual and physical ruin.

She holds herself to high standards and expects no less from her students, though she is patient with honest curiosity. She has little tolerance for cruelty, laziness, or magical arrogance divorced from responsibility.

While she presents a noble demeanor, she is not politically naïve. Amarien is keenly aware of Verbobonc’s tensions, the growing shadow of cult activity, and the dangerous currents flowing beneath the city’s academic institutions.

* * *

#### Familiar: Nelothel

**Nelothel**, her green lizard familiar, is unusually intelligent and serves as both observer and silent messenger within the Academy. Some students swear it listens at doors. Others insist it understands multiple languages.

Amarien neither confirms nor denies these claims.

* * *

#### Role in the Campaign

Amarien can serve as:

*   A demanding but fair magical mentor
*   A cultural counterpoint to Oeridian or Suel spellcasters
*   A conduit for Baklunish lore, planar theory, or elemental philosophy
*   A stabilizing influence when magical experiments spiral out of control

She is respected within the Wrinkle Academy and regarded with a mixture of awe and caution by her peers.

* * *

#### **DM Guidance: Using Amarien at the Table**

Amarien works best when treated as a **force of atmosphere rather than authority**. She should not lecture the party directly unless they seek her out; instead, let them _overhear_, _observe_, or be invited to witness one of her demonstrations. Her presence raises tension through spectacle—sudden heat, shifting terrain, conjured phenomena—without turning hostile. Use her to test player composure, curiosity, and restraint rather than raw combat ability. Amarien respects clever solutions, calm thinking under pressure, and characters who recognize when _not_ to escalate. She is an excellent vector for magical rumors, planar foreshadowing, or experimental spells gone awry, but she should never feel reckless or cruel. When things go wrong in her space, it is because students or outsiders misunderstood the lesson—not because Amarien lost control.

##### Roleplaying Notes for the DM

Amarien is **not a battlefield commander**. She shines in controlled spaces—lecture halls, courtyards, demonstrations, and tests. In combat, she prioritizes **control, spectacle, and teaching moments**, often pausing to explain _why_ a spell works as it does. She becomes dangerous only if students, the academy, or planar stability are threatened.

##### Traits

**Professor of Controlled Power.** Amarien has advantage on Arcana checks related to **elementals, planar effects, and summoning magic**.

**Demonstrative Caster.** When Amarien casts a spell of 1st level or higher that creates visible terrain, energy, or creatures, she may add harmless environmental effects (heat shimmer, sand, wind, illusionary scenery). These effects are non-damaging but impose **advantage on Intimidation or Performance checks** related to the display.

**Academic Restraint.** Amarien avoids lethal force unless absolutely necessary. If she reduces a creature to 0 hit points with a spell, she may choose to render it **stable but unconscious** instead.

**Arcane Familiar — Nelothel.** Amarien has a familiar (green lizard). While Nelothel is within 30 feet, Amarien has advantage on Perception checks.

* * *

##### Spellcasting

Amarien is a **6th-level spellcaster**. Her spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 12, +4 to hit with spell attacks).

She has the following wizard spells prepared:

**Cantrips (at will):  
**_control flames, fire bolt, mold earth, prestidigitation_

**1st level (4 slots):  
**_mage armor, magic missile, fog cloud, grease_

**2nd level (3 slots):  
**_misty step, flaming sphere, web_

**3rd level (3 slots):  
**_summon lesser elementals_ (earth only), _fireball_ (used sparingly and theatrically), _wall of sand_†

† _Wall of sand_ may be reskinned as _wall of wind_ or _wall of dust_ for thematic use.
