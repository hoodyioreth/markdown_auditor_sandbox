C18 Manor House
===============

### The Thieves’ Guild Beneath the Vacant Manor

_Hidden Depths_

To the untrained eye, **C18 Manor House** is a condemned relic—an abandoned noble residence slowly rotting at the edge of respectability within the Civic Centre. To those who know better, it is the masked heart of **Verbobonc’s criminal order**, the quiet seat of [The Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md), and the city’s most carefully tolerated secret.

The manor is not a court, not a guildhall, and not a place of judgment. It is a _pressure valve_—a controlled shadow beneath the city’s lawful skin.

##### Arrival at the Manor

The manor slumps between better-kept civic buildings like a tooth long left to rot. Its shutters hang crooked, the stonework is cracked, and ivy crawls freely over the walls as if even the city has forgotten it. A single lantern burns behind a grimy window—too steady to be accidental. The front door bears no heraldry, no name, no lock you can see… yet you feel watched the moment you step onto the broken flagstones.

### The Civic Fiction

Officially, **C18 Manor House does not exist** in any active registry.

*   No taxes are collected
*   No repairs are ordered
*   No guards are stationed
*   No magistrate claims jurisdiction

This is not ignorance—it is _policy_.

The Viscount’s court at **Castle Greyfist** and the bureaucratic organs at **City Hall (C2)** are aware that removing the Battirovkas would not eliminate crime—it would **fragment it**, inviting rival syndicates, cult agents, and foreign gangs into the city.

A single, stable criminal power is preferable to chaos.

#### Lore and History

*   **Foundation:** Originally an esteemed manor house, the property fell into disrepair and was eventually abandoned due to undisclosed tragic events linked to its last owners.
*   **Transformation:** The Thieves’ Guild, seeking a discreet headquarters, capitalized on the manor’s neglected state and rumors to mask their activities.
*   **Evolution:** Over the years, the guild has expanded its reach, making the manor a central hub for operations across the region.

#### Facilities Description

*   **Basement and Sub-basement:** Refurbished and secured, these areas serve as the guild’s main quarters, equipped with training areas, meeting rooms, and storage for stolen goods.
*   **Secret Entrances/Exits:** Multiple hidden passages connect the manor to the city’s sewer system, allowing members to move undetected.
*   **Security Measures:** Advanced security, including traps and secret codes, protects the guild from unwelcome visitors or law enforcement raids.

### Relationships and Politics

*   **City Officials:** While most are unaware of the guild's true headquarters, some corrupt officials may be in league with the guild, providing protection or information for a price.
*   **Other Criminal Organizations:** The Thieves’ Guild maintains a delicate balance of cooperation and competition with other criminal factions in Verbobonc.
*   **Local Businesses and Citizens:** The guild often extorts or protects local businesses, influencing the economic dynamics of the civic center district.

#### Motivation and Goals

*   **Profit and Power:** Primary motivations include gaining wealth and influence within and beyond Verbobonc.
*   **Survival:** Ensuring the ongoing secrecy and security of their operations is paramount.
*   **Expansion:** Aiming to expand their reach and control over the city’s underworld activities.

#### Notable People

*   **Guild Master:** The elusive leader of the guild, known only by their alias, orchestrates the guild’s operations and strategies.
*   **Security Chief:** Manages the security measures and the training of new recruits.
*   **Liaison Officer:** Handles relations with other criminal factions and negotiates with corrupt officials.

#### The Truth Beneath

Beneath the manor lies a **well-maintained thieves’ guild complex**, accessed through:

*   A concealed cellar door
*   A spoken password
*   And the silent judgment of unseen watchers

The upper floors are intentionally unsafe—rotting stairs, collapsing beams, and empty rooms serve as a natural deterrent. Below ground, however, the stone is dry, the passages trapped, and the halls alive with quiet purpose.

This is where:

*   Heists are planned
*   Intelligence is traded
*   Contracts are weighed
*   And Verbobonc’s underworld is _regulated_

#### Organization & Authority

The Battirovkas operate on strict hierarchy and compartmentalization:

*   **Friends of the Family** – Runners, informants, and outsiders
*   **Cousins** – Trusted intermediaries
*   **Brothers & Sisters** – Senior operatives
*   **Aunts & Uncles** – Mythic leadership; never publicly seen

No member knows the whole structure. Loyalty is enforced not by cruelty, but by **consequence**.

* * *

#### Relationship to City Government

*   **Castle Greyfist:  
    **The Viscount does _not_ command the Family—but understands its usefulness.
*   **City Hall (C2):  
    **Advocates and magistrates quietly avoid cases that would destabilize the balance.
*   **Vigil Wardens:  
    **Actively opposed and perpetually frustrated; they catch thieves, never the guild.
*   **Churches of St. Cuthbert & Zilchus:  
    **Ideological enemies, particularly over theft, fraud, and smuggling.

This arrangement exists in tension—_not peace_.

#### Thief PCs & Campaign Integration (DM Tool)

This location is **the perfect bridge** between street-level play and high politics.

A rogue, bard, or criminal-aligned PC may:

*   Gain **Friend of the Family** status
*   Receive jobs aligned with party goals
*   Access black-market intelligence
*   Be shielded from petty prosecution

Most importantly, the Battirovkas are **actively hostile to Temple interference** in their territory.

#### The Lakash Connection (Critical Plot Lever)

Through the Family, PCs can gain access to [A12 Lakash Quallad's Manor](a12-lakash-quallad-s-manor-landmark.md)**'s Manor**—not as nobles, but as _useful shadows_.

The guild knows:

*   Lakash’s parties move money and favors
*   Human–gnome tensions threaten guild trade
*   Cult coin is circulating behind polite smiles

The Forgotten Manor becomes the briefing room for infiltration, espionage, and revelation—placing thief PCs at the **center of the Second Rising investigation**.  

#### Guild Activities

*   **Training Programs:** New recruits are trained in the arts of stealth, lock-picking, and other necessary skills for thievery. **Planned Heists:** Coordination of complex burglaries and heists is a regular activity.
*   **Community Engagements:** Despite their criminal nature, the guild occasionally engages in acts that could be seen as supporting the local community, whether for goodwill or strategic purposes.

  

#### Description of the Manor

_First Floor_

*   **Foyer:** Grand in its day, now the foyer is littered with debris from the collapsed ceiling, with faded murals peeling off the walls.
*   **Parlor:** Once a room of luxury, the parlor is now dominated by broken furniture and a fireplace choked with soot.
*   **Dining Hall:** The large dining hall features a rotting wooden table and scattered chairs, with a chandelier precariously dangling by a single chain.

_Second Floor_

*   **Bedrooms:** Multiple bedrooms, each in varying stages of decay, their once opulent furnishings now moth-eaten and dusty.
*   **Library:** Shelves stand mostly empty, the few remaining books swollen with moisture and mold.
*   **Gallery:** This room once displayed valuable art, now only the faded outlines on the wallpaper suggest their previous existence.

#### Basement and Secret Exits

*   **General Condition:** Dark and damp, the basement holds the remnants of what might have been a wine cellar and storage rooms.
*   **Secret Tunnel:** A hidden door leads to a narrow tunnel that connects to Verbobonc’s sewage system, a pathway rumored to be used by criminals.

### Relationships and Politics

_Impact on the Community_

*   **Local Lore:** The manor is the subject of many local ghost stories and myths, contributing to its eerie reputation.
*   **Criminal Element:** The secret tunnels are suspected to be in use by local thieves and smugglers, creating a reluctant tolerance among city officials due to the difficulty in policing these passages.
