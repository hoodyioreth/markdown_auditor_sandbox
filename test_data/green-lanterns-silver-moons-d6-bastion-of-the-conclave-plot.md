Green Lanterns, Silver Moons -D6 Bastion of the Conclave
========================================================

##### Ready-to-Run Scene: Green Lanterns, Silver Moons

(D6 Bastion of the Conclave)

##### Scene Purpose (DM)

Show the **living alliance** between the Elven Enclave and the **Knights of Luna**, and let the Conclave quietly judge whether the PCs are:

*   **useful allies** against rising evils, or
*   the sort of loud, grasping outsiders that drive Celene toward **isolation**.

This scene also seeds: **Temple-remnant ops**, **Viscount diplomacy**, and **Knights of the Hart coordination**.

##### Conclave Approach

The pebblestones underfoot are worn smooth, as if a thousand measured patrols have polished them. Ahead riseth the Bastion—pale stone and living bough, lanterns hooded in green glass so their light falleth like leaves upon the night. No gate standeth open; yet thou art certain thou hast been marked and measured ere thou camest within bowshot.  
  
Then the street hushes further. From the far lane, a small procession glideth into view—elves in silvered cloaks, their helms bearing crescent-work, their bridles chiming soft as distant bells. Over them the green lanterns burn; beneath them, the moon seemeth to ride nearer.  
  
A sentinel lifts a hand—neither welcome nor warding, but the gesture of one who would know thy name before thy shadow.

### Setup (DM)

**Location:** The Bastion gate-court and reception hall.  
**Time:** Nightfall, under bright moon (or use overcast to heighten tension).  
**Key NPCs:**

*   **Captain Lathlaeril Silverbough** (Conclave Captain)
*   **Sir Aelar Moonward** (Knight of Luna commander of the delegation)
*   **Envoy-Scribe Myrith** (records what is said _and what is not said_)
*   **Two Conclave Sentinels** (silent, watchful)
*   Optional cameo: **Hart Runner** (human messenger of the Knights of the Hart, waiting outside)

**What the delegation brings (pick one):**

1.  A sealed request for a **joint operation** against a Temple remnant.
2.  A warning: someone in Verbobonc advocates “closing roads” and “tightening quarters” (isolation pressure).
3.  A list of names—merchants, couriers, and “too-curious” folk—possibly tied to the Temple.

* * *

##### DM Notes: The Elven Test (Quiet, Not Cruel)

The Conclave and Luna Knights are not trying to humiliate the PCs. They are testing:

*   **Discretion** (do the PCs blurt names and secrets?)
*   **Temperance** (do they demand? bargain? posture?)
*   **Purpose** (do they serve a cause beyond coin?)
*   **Respect** (can they honor elven customs without groveling?)

* * *

##### Mechanics (Optional, 5e-friendly)

*   **Passive Insight 13+**: The “warmth” offered is a measurement, not kindness.
*   **History 12+**: The Luna crescent-work recalls Emridy Meadows and old vows.
*   **Persuasion 14+**: Earns a _measured courtesy_ (not trust yet).
*   **Deception 14+** (and any lie here): Sentinels exchange a glance; the scribe’s quill pauses—later consequences.
*   **If PCs mention the Temple with clarity and restraint:** grant **advantage** on one social check in this scene.

### Dialogue (Keep Separate for DM Use)

##### Gate Sentinel

**Sentinel:** “Hold, travelers. The Dawn is fair—yet fairness is not permission. Name thy business.”

**Sentinel (to Luna Knights):** “Silver Moons. Thou art expected.”

* * *

#### Captain Lathlaeril Silverbough (Conclave)

**Lathlaeril:** “Green lanterns burn for the peace of this quarter. We do not douse them for loud men with quick smiles.”

**Lathlaeril (to Sir Aelar):** “Sir Moonward. Thy road hath found our threshold again.”

**Lathlaeril (to PCs):** “Speak thy purpose in three breaths. If it needeth four, it is either vanity… or a lie.”

**Lathlaeril:** “What dost thou bring that cannot be bought?”

**Lathlaeril:** “We have learned, in long years, that ‘friend’ is a word most easily spoken by those who would be served.”

* * *

#### Sir Aelar Moonward (Knights of Luna)

**Sir Aelar:** “Captain. Thy lanterns are as steady as ever. It gladdens me—though gladness is not why I ride.”

**Sir Aelar (to PCs):** “Outsiders at our meeting—how rare. Yet the world groweth less courteous with each season.”

**Sir Aelar:** “If thou art here for coin, take thy leave. If thou art here for glory, take thy leave sooner.”

**Sir Aelar:** “Tell me, traveler—when thou findest evil, dost thou name it… or sell it?”

* * *

#### Envoy-Scribe Myrith (quiet, sharp)

**Myrith:** “I write for memory, not for mercy. Choose thy words as if they shall be read in ten years.”

**Myrith (to PCs):** “State thy names. State thy allegiances. Leave out thy boasts.”

* * *

#### A “Measured Courtesy” Offer (if PCs behave well)

**Lathlaeril:** “Thou hast not barked. That is a promising beginning.”

**Sir Aelar:** “Aye. Silence is oft the first sign of wisdom.”

**Lathlaeril:** “Then hear this much: a shadow that should have died at Emridy stirreth again. We would know who feeds it.”

* * *

#### If PCs bluster, demand entry, or speak rudely

**Lathlaeril:** “Thou mistakest this for a tavern door.”

**Sir Aelar:** “And thou mistakest patience for welcome.”

**Myrith:** “Say it plainly: thou wouldst be served. Very well. Go—be served elsewhere.”

* * *

#### If PCs lie (or are caught in contradiction)

**Myrith:** “Once more, if thou pleasest.”

**Lathlaeril:** “Nay. Once is enough. A crooked thread needeth no second knot.”

**Sir Aelar:** “The moon showeth many things. It showeth thee, now.”

* * *

#### The “Isolation” Pressure (drop this if you want Celene politics)

**Sir Aelar:** “There are voices at court that call every outsider a spark near dry leaves.”

**Lathlaeril:** “And there are voices here that would bolt every door and call it wisdom.”

**Sir Aelar (to PCs):** “Which art thou? The spark… or the hand that covereth it?”

* * *

#### The Sealed Request (Quest Hook)

**Sir Aelar:** “A sealed matter: a remnant of the old evil yet draweth breath. Not in the open—never in the open.”

**Lathlaeril:** “We will not loose our veterans upon rumor.”

**Sir Aelar:** “Nor will we wait until rumor hath become fire.”

**Lathlaeril (to PCs):** “If thou wouldst prove thy worth, take this test: fetch truth without making noise.”

* * *

#### The Test Offered (Choose One “Proof Task”)

##### Proof Task A — The Quiet Verification

A reported Temple courier route exists.  
**Task:** Confirm it within two nights. Bring back **one** undeniable token: a mark, a message fragment, a name tied to an object.

##### Proof Task B — The Hart Knot

A Hart messenger waits with partial intel but may be compromised.  
**Task:** Meet the Hart runner, test the tale, and report back without letting anyone follow.

##### Proof Task C — The False Friend

An “ally” has been petitioning for patrol times.  
**Task:** Identify the petitioner and learn for whom they truly work—without bloodshed if possible.

* * *

### Closing Lines (Pick Based on Outcome)

##### If PCs impress both factions

**Lathlaeril:** “Very well. Thou may’st be of use.”

**Sir Aelar:** “And if thou art of use, we shall not forget it.”

**Myrith:** “Nor shall I.”

##### If PCs are tolerated but not trusted

**Lathlaeril:** “Thou may’st walk our stones. Do not imagine they are thine.”

**Sir Aelar:** “Bring proof, not promises.”

##### If PCs fail the measure

**Lathlaeril:** “The quarter is not closed. Yet it is closed to _thee_.”

**Sir Aelar:** “Go, and learn the difference between welcome and permission.”

* * *

#### DM Aftermath Rewards

*   **Success:** A sealed letter of audience, a minor Conclave favor, and a Luna contact who will answer one message per season.
*   **Mixed:** Access to a single map-room consultation (supervised), but watched thereafter.
*   **Failure:** Rumors spread that the PCs are “unquiet,” giving **disadvantage** on social checks with enclave elves until they redeem themselves.

### Scene Beats

##### Beat 1 — The Lantern Line

The PCs arrive as the Luna delegation arrives. The Conclave does _not_ choose between them; it weighs how they behave when **others of consequence** are watching.

##### Beat 2 — The Two Lights

Green lanterns (Enclave) and silver moon (Luna) share the same threshold. The captain and the knight exchange ritual courtesies—brief, precise, laden.

##### Beat 3 — The Courtesy Question

Captain Silverbough addresses the PCs with a question that is also a warning:

*   “Why are you here?” is too blunt.
*   She asks: **“What dost thou bring that cannot be bought?”**

##### Beat 4 — The Luna Glance

Sir Aelar studies the PCs as one might study weather on a pass: not judging beauty, judging danger.

##### Beat 5 — The Hook Offered, Under Glass

A sealed matter is hinted at—Temple remnants, border security, or an internal political pressure. The PCs are not invited in outright; they are offered a _chance to prove they deserve invitation_.

##### Beat 6 — Close

The Conclave decides: **useful**, **tolerated**, or **kept at arm’s length**.
