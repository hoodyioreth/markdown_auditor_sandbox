F2 The Players Inn
==================

The Players Inn is Verbobonc’s beating heart of performance, rumor, and revelry—a loud, joyous refuge for travelers, entertainers, and anyone who would rather be heard than watched. Situated in the cobblestone core of the Verbobonc Foreign Quarter, it functions as both a cultural crossroads and a pressure valve for the city’s tensions.

In a campaign shadowed by the Temple of Elemental Evil, the Players Inn is where truths slip out between verses, secrets are shouted instead of whispered, and factions collide beneath laughter loud enough to drown caution.

##### Arrival: The Players Inn

Music reaches you before the door—laughter, stamping feet, and the sharp twang of strings rising through stained glass like heat through stone. Inside, the Players Inn explodes with life. Long tables crowd the floor, packed shoulder to shoulder with travelers and performers alike. A raised stage dominates the taproom, where a half-finished sonnet is interrupted by cheers, jeers, and a dancer spinning laughter into the air. The scent of spiced wine, sweat, and old wood clings to the rafters. Somewhere behind the bar, a broad-shouldered man with a tangled beard catches your eye and raises a mug in greeting—welcoming you not as guests, but as participants.

### A Stage for Every Soul

_Legacy of Performance_

*   **Inn of the Arts:** The inn has long been a sanctuary for bards, actors, musicians, and poets.
*   **Memorabilia Collection:** Hundreds of signed items donated by past patrons adorn the walls, showcasing the inn's rich history.

##### Entertainment for All

*   **Open Stage Policy:** Performers of all skill levels are welcome, leading to impromptu plays, concerts, and sonnet recitals.
*   **Audience Participation:** The crowd frequently joins in the middle of a sonnet or heckles actors during plays, adding to the lively atmosphere.

#### A Charming Inn Full of Life

##### Exterior

*   **Cheerful Facade:** Small stones and intricate carvings create a welcoming and homey outer structure.
*   **Stained Glass Windows:** Impossible to see through, but the music within can be felt outside.

##### Interior

_Taproom and Stage_

*   **Packed Taproom:** Filled with travelers and artists singing, dancing, and drinking.
*   **Stage for Performers:** A large stage dominates the central taproom, hosting plays, concerts, and recitals.
*   **Memorabilia-Laden Walls:** Signed items donated by patrons cover the walls.
*   **Private Rooms:** Small, non-descript rooms with a simple cot.
*   **Common Rooms:** Lined with bunk beds and thin blankets, often too noisy for restful sleep.

#### Entertainment

*   **Live Performances:** Bards, actors, and musicians entertain guests nightly.
*   **Amateur Performers Welcome:** Encourages impromptu acts from guests and audience participation.
*   **Dancing and Singing:** Common scenes of people dancing with waitresses and singing in groups.

* * *

#### **Food, Drink & Hospitality**

*   **Food:** Simple but plentiful, catered from nearby kitchens
*   **Drink:** Strong spirits, house wine, spiced ales
*   **Tradition:**
*   Any performer who takes the stage receives a free round
*   Exceptional performances may earn free lodging for the night

### A Storied Past of Song and Merriment

##### Founding Era

*   **Early Roots:** Established over a century ago as a meeting place for traveling bards and poets.
*   **Cultural Hub:** Became a cultural hub in the Foreign Quarter for artists and travelers.

##### Golden Age of Entertainment

*   **Height of Popularity:** The inn saw its golden age during the reign of Viscount Wilfrick.
*   **Memorabilia Collection Growth:** Collected hundreds of signed items during this period.

##### Modern Times

*   **Lively Refuge:** Remains a popular destination for travelers and artists alike.
*   **Artsy and Aloof Crowd:** The inn’s regular patrons maintain an artsy and aloof atmosphere.

### Relationships

_Connecting Artists and Travelers Across Verbobonc_

##### Foreign Quarter Community

*   **Travelers and Merchants:** Maintains a strong rapport with local merchants and travelers.
*   **Cultural Venues:** Collaborates with neighboring cultural venues to draw in diverse crowds.

##### City Watch

*   **Occasional Raids:** Sometimes raided by the [Vigil Wardens of Verbobonc](vigil-wardens-of-verbobonc-organization.md) due to noise complaints.
*   **Bribery Tactics:** Occasional bribes to the watch (unless there is a Billet of St Cuthbert) ensure the inn’s activities continue.

#### Motivations

*   **Promoting the Arts:** Supports bards, actors, and musicians by providing a welcoming stage.
*   **Traveler Refuge:** Offers a cozy refuge for travelers seeking camaraderie and entertainment.

### Cost and Quality

_Affordable Merriment for All_

*   **Private Rooms:** Average cost.
*   **Common Rooms:** Low-cost bunk beds in shared rooms.

##### Quality

*   **Rooms:** Basic and non-descript.
*   **Food:** Simple but plentiful, thanks to nearby catering.
*   **Entertainment:** High-quality, varied, and vibrant.

### **Running the Players Inn in a TOEE Campaign**

##### **Why This Location Matters**

The Players Inn is _not_ a place for covert meetings.  
It is where secrets surface because people forget to hide them.

Use it to:

*   Deliver rumors without DM monologues
*   Introduce foreign factions organically
*   Let players learn by listening instead of interrogating
*   Create social chaos that hides meaningful clues

##### **Temple of Elemental Evil Hooks**

*   A performer sings a verse that mirrors a recent cult action
*   A merchant boasts of profits tied to Temple supply lines
*   A heckler recognizes a PC from a prior encounter
*   A cult agent avoids the inn entirely—too many witnesses

* * *

##### **Tone & Themes**

*   Loud, joyful, unruly—but never cruel
*   Creative freedom as quiet resistance
*   Community over hierarchy
*   Truth slipping through art

* * *

#### **DM Notes**

*   The City Watch only intervenes after repeated complaints
*   Bribes work unless a Billet of St. Cuthbert is present
*   Violence here brings swift social consequences—even if legal ones lag

*   Primary Clientele: Entertainers, travelers, foreign merchants, free spirits

### Notable People

_Faces Behind the Bar and on the Stage_

##### **Bramwell "Brass" Merritt (**Innkeeper and Owner)

*   **Role:** Gruff yet charismatic owner of the Players Inn, serves as barkeeper.
*   **Background:** An [Oeridian Oerid](oeridian-oerid-ethnicity.md) ex-merchant with a love for the arts, he purchased the inn after retiring from trading.
*   **Personality:** Gruff, odd, and occasionally aloof, yet deeply cares about his guests.

##### **Calvin Windwhistle** (Human Bard Minstrel)

*   **Role:** Entertains guests with his panpipe music nightly.
*   **Background:** A [Oeridian Oerid](oeridian-oerid-ethnicity.md) / [Flannae Flan](flannae-flan-ethnicity.md)  mix minstrel who roams from inn to inn, Calvin finds solace in the Players Inn’s stage.
*   **Personality:** Energetic and warm, his panpipe melodies bring cheer to the taproom.

##### **Isolde the Enchantress** (Resident Singer and Performer)

*   **Role:** Performs enchanting ballads that captivate the audience.
*   **Background:** A [Half-Elves](half-elves-ethnicity.md)  bard who found her calling at the Players Inn.
*   **Personality:** Mysterious and charming, known for her mesmerizing performances.

##### **Rowan "Ro" Silverfoot** (Head Waitress and Dancer)

*   **Role:** Keeps the bar organized and dances with guests nightly.
*   **Background:** A [Harfoot](harfoot-ethnicity.md) halfling ex-traveler who settled down at the Players Inn
*   **Personality:** Cheerful and witty, ensures all guests feel welcome.

> _"Artists and minstrels, leave yer troubles at the door and share yer magic with us!"_\- A call for performers to take the stage and entertain the crowd.
