W4 Temple of the Velverdyva River
=================================

A Marble Shrine Between River and Coin

At the very edge of the Velverdyva, where the long stone piers of Verbobonc reach into the trade current like patient fingers, rises a gleaming structure of white marble. Tall fluted columns support a high pediment carved with river-crests, fish, dragon turtles, and curling waves. Barges moor just below its steps. Sailors remove their boots before entering. Merchants bow their heads. Hardheads pause, if only for a breath.

The Temple of the Velverdyva River is not merely a shrine—it is the spiritual heart of the docks. Here, water is not background. It is law, livelihood, blessing, and judgment.

Built in the old Aerdy style, the temple stands as both religious monument and political instrument. It is the quiet power beneath the harbor’s commerce.

##### Arrival at the Temple

The air smells of river mist and incense. The marble steps are wet beneath your boots, washed by a thin channel of flowing water that spills from within the temple’s threshold. Tall white pillars rise overhead, their carvings catching the late sun like pale gold. Beyond them, you glimpse a broad interior pool, clear and cold, where worshipers wade knee-deep toward three altars lit by steady blue flame. Coins gleam beneath the surface of the water. A priest in sea-blue robes speaks softly over the sound of the river.

#### Structure & Sacred Design

The temple interior is dominated by a large, shallow ritual pool. Worshipers must wade through living water before approaching the altars. This design is deliberate—purification before petition.

The architecture blends Suel maritime symbolism with Aerdy civic grandeur.

**Notable Features:**

*   High vaulted ceiling with wave-carved beams
*   Ornate marble pillars etched with maritime trade routes
*   Central purification pool (3 ft. deep) fed directly from the river
*   Three primary altars: Xerbo, Osprem, Geshtai
*   Subtle waterspout motif carved along the outer façade
*   Hidden drainage grates beneath the pool (connected to W3 Waterworks)

### The Three Deities of the River

This temple is a rare triune sanctuary devoted to three water-aligned Suel deities, whose overlapping domains reflect the layered reality of life on the Velverdyva.

* * *

#### Xerbo — The Sea Dragon

_(Sea, Sailing, Money, Business)_

![](./images/Xerbo%20holy%20symbol.png "Xerbo holy symbol.png")

by 

Xerbo’s presence dominates the mercantile side of the temple. His altar is marked by a dragon turtle relief and bronze scales embedded into the marble.

Xerbo, dogma emphasizes maritime respect and hard negotiation. Merchants cast minted coins into the pool before large ventures. Sailors whisper bargains beneath their breath.

**Temple Practices:**

*   Blessings for profitable trade
*   Maritime contracts sanctified in Xerbo’s name
*   Offerings of coin or foreign trade goods
*   Counsel on piracy avoidance and river toll disputes

* * *

#### Osprem — The Sea Princess

_(Sea Voyages, Ships, Sailors)_

![](./images/Osprem%20holy%20symbol.png "Osprem holy symbol.png")

Ophrem holy symbol by

Her altar bears a carved fish skull and a polished whale-tooth ring displayed upon velvet. Osprem protects sailors who honor the sea’s balance.

She is invoked before departures and after storms. Ship captains kneel here before every long voyage.

**Temple Practices:**

*   Bless seagoing vessels (ceremonial ship anointing)
*   Memorial rites for drowned sailors
*   Blue-robed clerics assigned to dockside prayer patrols
*   Mariners’ oath of safe conduct

* * *

#### Geshtai — Keeper of River Purity

_(Lakes, Rivers, Wells, Streams)_

![](./images/Geshtai%20%E2%80%94%20HolySymbol.png "Geshtai — HolySymbol.png")

Geshtai — HolySymbol by

**Geshtai** is the  goddess of Lakes, Rivers, Wells, and Streams. Her symbol is a waterspout. Geshtai’s altar is simpler—a sculpted waterspout carved in flowing lines. Her doctrine centers on respect for fresh water and its sanctity.

She is particularly revered by riverfolk, fishermen, and those wary of industrial pollution.

**Temple Practices:**

*   Ritual river cleansing ceremonies
*   Inspection blessings for wells and cisterns
*   Public admonishments against waste dumping
*   Quiet investigations into river contamination

#### History: A Testament to Maritime Faith

Built by the Aerdy forces, the temple has long been a place of reverence for those who depend on the waterways for their livelihood. Over time, it has become a central hub for the community.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Construction:** Erected by Aerdy forces with their unique architectural style.
*   **Evolution:** Evolved from a place of worship to a community center for merchants and sailors.

#### Political & Economic Influence

_A Center for Trade and Worship  
_The temple is a key player in the politics and economy of Verbobonc, influencing trade policies and harbor regulations. The priests often act as informal arbitrators during dockside disputes.

*   **Collaboration with Lord Mayor Haxx:** Works closely with [House Haxx](house-haxx-organization.md) on trade regulations.
*   **Support from Harbormaster:** Ensures the smooth operation of the harbor and safety of merchants with the [W2 Office of Harbormaster](w2-office-of-harbormaster-landmark.md).
*   **House Haxx:** Quiet negotiations over trade regulations
*   **Merchants’ Guilds:** Blessings for caravans headed upriver
*   **Blessings and Rituals:** The Harbormaster often coordinates with the temple priests to perform blessings and rituals for safe and prosperous voyages for the ships docking in the harbor.

If House Haxx pushes trade policies too aggressively, the temple can quietly shift public opinion by invoking river omens.

### Key Temple Figures

The temple’s leadership balances commerce and conscience.

* * *

##### Cedric Talus — Priest of Xerbo

_Human Clr4 / Rog2_

A former river pirate turned legitimate advisor. Cedric’s knowledge of pirate routes gives him influence with both merchants and the Harbormaster’s office.

*   Shrewd, charming, pragmatic
*   Advises on trade security
*   Knows which pirate threats are real… and which are political theater

* * *

##### Nadia M’karr — Priestess of Geshtai

Serene but quietly unyielding. She speaks softly yet commands respect from river workers.

*   Oversees purification rites
*   Opposes excessive dock expansion that harms riverbanks
*   Suspects certain industrial guilds of contamination

* * *

##### Leif Vand — Priest of Osprem

Energetic and strategic, Leif coordinates with captains before major voyages.

*   Conducts blessing rituals at W2 Harbormaster’s request
*   Advocates for safer ship construction standards
*   Maintains tension with House Haxx when cost-cutting threatens sailors.

_A Blend of Faith and Commerce  
_The temple offers a variety of rituals and services to support the maritime community, drawing on the teachings of Xerbo, Geshtai, and Osprem.

*   **Healing Services:** Free Cure Light Wounds for sailors in the pool.
