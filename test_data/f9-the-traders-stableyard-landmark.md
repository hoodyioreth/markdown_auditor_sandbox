F9 The Trader's Stableyard
==========================

##### _Barns, Stalls, and Secure Rest for Beasts of Every Road_

The Trader’s Stableyard is the primary stabling hub of the Foreign Quarter, serving caravans, mercenary companies, visiting dignitaries, and any traveler whose livelihood depends on the safety of their animals. Its reputation rests not on luxury, but on reliability, discretion, and flexibility—qualities that have made it indispensable to Verbobonc’s trade economy.

More than a place to leave horses, this yard is a crossroads of rumors, schedules, and movement, where who arrives and who departs often matters more than who stays the night.

##### Arrival at The Trader’s Stableyard

The smell of hay and damp earth reaches you before the sound of hooves. Broad wooden barns rise on either side of an open yard, their doors thrown wide as stablehands move with practiced ease among horses, mules, and stranger beasts besides.  
  
A pair of guards stand near the gate, watching the street with unhurried eyes. Inside, animals are being groomed, fed, or simply allowed to rest beneath open rafters. This is no showpiece—everything here speaks of long use, careful order, and the steady rhythm of commerce.

### **Description & Layout**

##### **Exterior**

*   **Two Large Barns:** One primarily for horses and large caravan animals; the other adaptable for unusual or oversized creatures.
*   **Open Courtyard:** Packed earth yard where animals can stretch, water, or be assessed.
*   **Guard Stations:** Discreetly positioned near entrances; presence is calm but constant.

#### **Interior**

*   **Variable Stall Sizes:** From hounds and pack animals to camels and even elephants.
*   **Secure Storage Lockers:** Lockable chests and cages for saddles, tack, and merchant gear.
*   **Feeding & Grooming Areas:** Separate zones kept clean and efficiently managed.

### History

_From Modest Beginnings to a Bustling Stable_

##### Humble Origins

*   **Old Stables Renovated:** Started as a single stableyard, expanded to meet the growing needs of the Foreign Quarter.
*   **Merchant Demand:** Rapid growth due to the increasing number of merchant caravans passing through the city.

##### Expansion and Reputation

*   **Additional Barns Built:** Added two new barns to accommodate the growing demand for stabling.
*   **Reputation for Flexibility:** Gained a reputation for accommodating all types of animals.

### Relationships

_A Network of Merchants, Caravans, and Guards_

##### Merchant Caravans

*   **Preferred Stabling Location:** Popular among merchant caravans for its flexible and secure stabling.
*   **Equipment Storage:** Offers merchants a secure place to store their equipment and supplies.

##### City Guards and Watch

*   **Collaborative Security Efforts:** Works closely with the [Vigil Wardens of Verbobonc](vigil-wardens-of-verbobonc-organization.md)  to ensure security.
*   **Patrols and Guard Stations:** Guards regularly patrol the stableyard and man the guard stations.

##### Local Businesses

*   **Feed Suppliers:** Collaborates with local feed suppliers for quality animal feed.
*   **Grooming Services:** Partners with grooming businesses to provide professional grooming.
*   **Merchant Convenience:** Strives to provide convenient and secure stabling for merchants.
*   **Animal Welfare:** Ensures all animals receive proper care during their stay.

#### **Role in the Sandbox**

The Trader’s Stableyard is a **logistical intelligence node**.

Use it to:

*   Track caravan arrivals and departures.
*   Introduce NPCs passing through Verbobonc.
*   Leak rumors about delays, disappearances, or unusual cargo.
*   Provide safe, neutral ground for meetings involving merchants or mercenaries.

Animals left here may become **hooks themselves**—a missing mount, a guarded wagon, or a beast no one wants to claim publicly.

#### **DM Notes**

*   Bram does **not** volunteer information—but will answer honest questions.
*   Lena notices animal neglect immediately and reacts harshly.
*   Gorik remembers faces; repeat visits don’t go unnoticed.

This location works best when treated not as a service stop, but as a **living artery of the city**, quietly pulsing with movement, money, and secrets.

#### Encounter: The Caravan That Won’t Meet Your Eye

The smell of damp hay and horse-sweat hangs thick in the Trader’s Stableyard. Hooves scrape stone as a caravan rolls in from the gate road—three wagons, canvas pulled low, iron rims muffled with leather wraps.  
  
The guards are wrong. They wear decent mail and carry serviceable spears, but they don’t talk to one another. Their eyes track movement too carefully, hands never straying far from weapon hafts. One wagon creaks as it settles, and you hear something shift inside that does not sound like grain.

##### **What the Party Immediately Notices**

*   The **caravan guards** avoid eye contact with stablehands.
*   No one dismounts to stretch or water horses—_unnatural discipline_.
*   The lead guard gives clipped instructions, never raising his voice.
*   One wagon bears **fresh mud** inconsistent with the road outside the city.

* * *

##### **Roleplay Beat — The Guards**

Use one or two of these lines as needed.

**Lead Guard (flat, controlled):**

“Paid for the night. No help required. Keep the horses fed and leave the wagons be.”

**Second Guard (too quick):**

“Just trade goods. Bulk cargo. Nothing worth the look.”

If pressed:

“Orders are orders. We don’t open wagons in the yard.”

* * *

##### **Tension Escalation Options (DM Choice)**

Pick **one** to keep the scene sharp.

1.  **A Horse Spooks  
    **One of the caravan horses shies hard, nearly snapping a tether—_something inside the wagon thumps in response_.
2.  **A Bribe Attempt  
    **A guard palms a few silver to a stablehand… then freezes when he realizes the party is watching.
3.  **Guard Reinforcements  
    **Two more guards drift closer—not threatening, but blocking angles of approach.

* * *

##### **If the Players Investigate**

*   **Casual Observation:** Confirms the guards are _trained fighters_, not common drovers.
*   **Conversation:** Guards know little beyond _delivery point and contact name_—both vague.
*   **Sneaking a Look:** Crates marked as grain… packed to muffle sound.
*   **Direct Confrontation:** Guards stall for time; they do **not** want a fight here.

* * *

##### **What This Caravan Might Be (Choose Later)**

You don’t need to decide immediately:

*   Temple supplies moved under civilian cover
*   Smuggled prisoners or cult recruits
*   Stolen goods meant for a river transfer
*   Something _alive_ that shouldn’t be in the city

Let player actions determine how much is revealed.

* * *

##### **Closing Beat**

As the caravan settles in, one guard finally looks your way—just once. His gaze lingers a heartbeat too long, measuring you, then slips away as if you were already counted… and found wanting.

If you want, next we can:

*   tie this caravan directly to **Temple logistics**,
*   turn it into a **midnight escape scene**, or
*   seed it as a **recurring threat** the party keeps crossing paths with.

### Notable People

_Faces Behind the Trader's Stableyard_

*   **Role:** Owner and manager of The Trader's Stableyard.
*   **Background:** Former merchant who opened the stableyard after retiring from the caravan trade.
*   **Personality:** Friendly and accommodating, values trust and reliability.

*   **Personality:** Stern but fair, maintains discipline among the stablehands.
*   **Motivation:** Seeks to ensure all animals receive proper care and attention.

*   **Role:** Oversees security at the Merchant's Haven.
*   **Background:** Veteran guard with years of experience in caravan protection.
*   **Personality:** Diligent and strategic, values loyalty and teamwork.
*   **Motivation:** Aims to maintain a safe environment for merchants and their animals.
