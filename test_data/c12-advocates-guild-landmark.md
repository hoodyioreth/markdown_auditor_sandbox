C12 Advocates Guild
===================

### The Bastion of Legal Expertise

The Advocates’ Guild is the beating heart of law, contracts, and public judgment in Verbobonc. Backed by the uncompromising doctrines of Pholtus, this austere guildhall provides legal counsel, arbitration, and prosecution under the Viscounty’s laws—where truth is sacred, ambiguity is suspect, and justice is meant to be seen as clearly as light at noon.

*   **Guild Description:** The building serves strictly as an office but is adorned with symbols and ties to the Temple of the Blinding Light of the [One True Way Church of Pholtus](church-of-pholtus-the-one-true-way-organization.md), reflecting a deep intertwining of legal and spiritual guidance.
*   **Vital Role:** It controls and organizes the services of lawyers and arbiters who play pivotal roles in the city's legal proceedings and disputes.

#### The House of Law and Light

The Advocates’ Guild stands as a severe yet immaculate stone structure along the Civic Centre square, its façade deliberately plain save for tall, narrow windows and the ever-present symbol of the Blinding Light worked into lintels, seals, and ironwork. The building shares architectural lineage with the nearby Temple of the Blinding Light, reinforcing its authority not merely as a civic institution, but a moral one.

Inside, the air is cool and hushed. Voices are kept low. Footsteps echo. Every design choice reinforces discipline, clarity, and order—values sacred to Pholtus and demanded of those who practice law within these walls.

This is where disputes between guilds are settled, accusations against nobles are cautiously weighed, and confessions—willing or compelled—are recorded with merciless precision.

* * *

Role in Verbobonc
-----------------

The Advocates’ Guild serves several critical civic functions:

*   **Legal Counsel:** Providing advocates for those able to afford representation—or for causes deemed “necessary to the Light.”
*   **Arbitration:** Settling commercial disputes between guilds, merchants, and noble houses.
*   **Prosecution:** Acting on behalf of the Viscounty in cases of treason, heresy, cult activity, and civic corruption.
*   **Doctrine Enforcement:** Ensuring all rulings align with the moral absolutism of Pholtus—truth is singular, deviation is error.

While not formally a temple, the Guild operates under the theological oversight of the Church of Pholtus, and senior advocates are often ordained clerics or lay theologians trained in doctrine as much as law.

* * *

#### The Influence of Pholtus

Pholtan doctrine shapes every aspect of the Guild’s operation:

*   **Truth Is Absolute:** Testimony is sacred; falsehood is sin.
*   **Order Above Mercy:** Justice is not concerned with comfort or sentiment.
*   **Light Reveals All:** Investigation, record-keeping, and witness scrutiny are exhaustive and relentless.

Advocates are trained to view compromise with suspicion. Grey areas are treated as shadows that must be burned away—not accommodated.

This makes the Guild both respected and feared.

* * *

#### Key Areas Within the Guild

*   **The Hall of Petitions:** Where grievances are filed and initial hearings conducted.
*   **The Record Vaults:** Sealed chambers holding contracts, verdicts, confessions, and censured testimonies dating back generations.
*   **The Chamber of Clarification:** Interrogation rooms used when “truth requires illumination.”
*   **Advocates’ Studies:** Private offices where cases are prepared and legal strategies debated under strict guild law.

#### Goods and Services

The Advocates Guild offers a range of services aimed at addressing the legal needs of Verbobonc’s citizens, from the wealthiest merchants to the common folk.

*   **Criminal Defense:** Representation in major criminal cases with a structured fee system that includes a significant retainer to secure the services of skilled advocates.
*   **Trade Disputes:** Specialized legal support for merchant houses involved in territorial and commercial disputes.

#### Pricing Structure

The cost of legal services provided by the Guild is structured to cater to the severity and complexity of the cases.

*   **Minor Legal Matters:** Priced at 12gp per day with a 10% retainer fee.
*   **Major Crimes:** Heavier legal accusations demand a fee of 60gp per day, with a 50% retainer to secure the services of the guild's most competent advocates.

#### Using the Advocates’ Guild in Play

The Advocates’ Guild is a powerful narrative lever for the DM:

*   A place where **Temple of Elemental Evil cult accusations** may surface—carefully, cautiously, and politically.
*   A dangerous forum where PCs might be **questioned under oath**, especially if their actions attract attention.
*   A venue for **noble intrigue**, sealed contracts, or suppressed truths.
*   A moral crucible where characters must decide whether truth, loyalty, or survival comes first.

Adventurers quickly learn: once your name is written in the Guild’s ledgers, it is never truly erased.

* * *

DM Notes
--------

*   Pholtan advocates are unlikely to tolerate deception—_Zone of Truth_ and similar magic are doctrinally endorsed.
*   The Guild may quietly obstruct investigations that threaten civic stability—even if truth suffers.
*   Characters aligned with Pholtus may find allies here; chaotic or morally flexible PCs will feel constant pressure.

##### **First Meeting at the Advocates’ Guild**

You are ushered from the glare of the Civic Centre square into a hall of pale stone and measured quiet. Sunlight pours through high, narrow windows of clear glass, falling in straight, disciplined bars upon the tiled floor. Every footstep echoes with unwelcome clarity.  
  
Clerks in white and silver robes move with deliberate purpose, their voices low, their expressions stern but not unkind. Upon the far wall hangs the symbol of the Blinding Light—sun and moons wrought in polished metal—catching the light so sharply it bids you blink.  
  
A long oaken desk stands before you like a threshold. Quills rest in perfect order. Ledgers lie open, their pages ruled with painstaking care. From behind the desk, a senior advocate lifts his gaze, pale eyes weighing you as one might weigh evidence.

* * *

**Dialogue — Senior Advocate of the One True Way**

“Hold.”  
The word is spoken softly, yet it admits no argument.

“You stand within the Advocates’ Guild of Verbobonc,” he says, folding his hands upon the desk. “Here, law is not bent for coin, nor truth bartered for convenience. Speak plainly, and speak truly.”

His gaze sharpens, the light at his back seeming almost to halo his figure.

“Do you come seeking counsel, judgment, or redress under the law of the Viscounty? Or do you stand accused, and pray the Light finds you… redeemable?”

A clerk nearby dips a quill, waiting.

“State your names,” the advocate continues, voice calm as cut stone, “and the matter you would place before the One True Way.”
