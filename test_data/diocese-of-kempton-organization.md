Diocese of Kempton
==================

The Diocese of Kempton lieth south of Veluna City, stretching in quiet breadth toward the shadowed rises of the Lortmil Mountains. Where other dioceses contend with trade, doctrine, or border tensions, Kempton endureth in stillness—unchanged across generations, rooted in soil, tradition, and the slow certainty of rural life.

 

It is a land not of ambition, but of continuity… though beneath that stillness, quiet tensions stir.

#### Notable Figures

*   **Baroness Ladawn Basalle** — Half-elven noble ruler of Kempton, practical and steady; ascended twelve years ago upon her mother’s death. Rumors whisper she may soon relinquish rule to travel the wider world
*   **Bishop Ansile Orish** — Raoan authority and cousin to the Baroness; a scarred former adventurer, quietly rumored to have played a role in the return of the Crook of Rao
*   **Peter Basalle** — Young heir and Celestial Circle representative, aged 22; burdened by legacy and prone to emotional outbursts when his family’s honor is challenged
*   **Aldun Kinninger** — Captain of the Knights of Kempton stationed in Shandalanar, reporting directly to House Basalle

### Geography & Character

Kempton is the most rural of all Veluna’s dioceses, its lands given almost wholly to farming, pasture, and quiet woodland. There are no great trade arteries here, no river hubs such as those that define Devarnish, nor the doctrinal dominance of Grayington.

*   Instead, the land is wide and patient.
    
*   To the north, the fields roll toward Veluna City. To the south, they break upon the Lortmil Mountains. Between these bounds lie scattered farmsteads, small villages, and the modest settlements of Kempton, Asnath, and Shandalanar.
    
*   The region’s stillness is not weakness—it is stability preserved through generations untouched by the urgency of greater politics.
    

### The Land & Its People

The folk of Kempton are known throughout Veluna as simple, honest, and unyielding in their customs. They do not welcome disruption, nor do they bend easily to outside influence.

*   A stranger may pass through untroubled—so long as he disturbeth not the order of things.
    
*   The rhythm of life is governed by season, harvest, and parish. Here, the teachings of Rao are lived quietly rather than debated loudly.
    

 

### Asnath Copse & Elven Autonomy

Along the northern edge of the great woodland known as Asnath Copse, the influence of elves is stronger than anywhere else in Veluna. Though portions of the Copse fall within neighboring dioceses, in truth it is governed by those who dwell within its depths.

 

*   These elves maintain their own traditions, their own authority, and their own quiet boundaries. Kempton tolerates this autonomy—not from weakness, but from long-standing necessity.
    
*   It is here that the authority of the Archclericy grows… uncertain.
    

### Settlements of Kempton

#### Kempton — The Quiet Seat

The capital of the diocese is less a city than a gathering place of landholders, farmers, and parish life. Markets form its lifeblood, and the Baroness’s estate rises upon a gentle hill overlooking the settlement.

 

It is a place of order, calm, and quiet prosperity—touched faintly by elven aesthetics brought from nearby Asnath.

#### Asnath — The Divided Town

Asnath is one of the most unusual settlements in Veluna, straddling both the Great Western Road and the edge of Asnath Copse.

 

The town is divided in nature:

*   **Northern Asnath:** Human-built inns, trade houses, and road traffic
*   **Southern Asnath:** Elven tree-set dwellings connected by bridges and platforms

This duality is governed through a rare triune council:

*   A representative of the Elven Elder
*   A representative of Bishop Orish
*   A representative of Baroness Basalle

Together, they maintain a delicate balance between Velunan authority and elven sovereignty.

#### Notable Locations of Asnath

*   **The Swaying Bough Inn** — A tree-top inn of fine elven craft, reached by spiral stair and rope bridge, where music and delicate meals fill the air
*   **The Library of Asnath** — A towering, winding structure of wood and knowledge, guarded against those deemed too rough for its halls
*   **Temple of Rao** — A white marble sanctuary adorned with intricate carvings, the only fully stone structure in the town
*   **The Cathedral of Stone** — A rumored ancient ruin deep within the Copse, unseen for over a century and untouched by modern hands

#### Shandalanar — The Southern Trade Rise

Situated near the Lortmil foothills, Shandalanar is a growing settlement where agriculture and mining converge. Though distant from major trade routes, its position has made it a rising center for processing goods from both field and mountain.

 

The town houses:

*   An outpost of Guardsmen and Knights of Kempton
*   Warehouses from regional trade interests
*   Temples to Rao and Heironeous, and a shrine to Zilchus

Its steady growth marketh it as the one place in Kempton where change may yet take root.

### The Lortmil Edge & Dwarven Clans

Along the southern frontier, the land riseth into the Lortmil Mountains, where isolated dwarven clans dwell in guarded halls. These dwarves trade sparingly and trust slowly, maintaining their independence from Velunan authority.

### Governance & Noble Authority

Kempton is ruled by Baroness Ladawn Basalle, whose family hath governed the region for centuries. The last five rulers have been women, owing to the family’s lineage.

 

Her rule is steady, her alliances strong, and her governance largely uncontested. Yet whispers of her desire to step down introduce uncertainty for the first time in generations.

 

##### The Church & Bishopric

Bishop Ansile Orish serveth not only as the Church’s voice, but as a bridge between doctrine and governance. His past as an adventurer—and the rumors surrounding his connection to the Crook of Rao—lend him an authority beyond simple rank.

 

In Kempton, Church and noble rule are not opposed—they are entwined.

 

##### Role within the Archclericy

Kempton’s voice in the Celestial Circle is carried by Peter Basalle. Though young, he is determined to prove himself worthy of both family and faith.

 

Yet Kempton itself remains a quiet power—its influence not loud, but enduring.

### Political Position & Tensions

Though outwardly peaceful, Kempton containeth subtle tensions:

*   Elven autonomy within Asnath Copse
*   Dwarven isolation in the Lortmils
*   Emerging trade growth in Shandalanar
*   Uncertain succession within House Basalle

It is a land balanced delicately between stillness and change.

### The Nature of Kempton

Kempton is not a place of great proclamations nor rising ambition. It is a place where things endure.

Fields are sown as they have always been. Faith is kept as it has always been kept. And yet—beneath that quiet certainty—new forces gather: elven independence, rising trade, and the uncertain future of House Basalle.

The land remaineth still… but not forever.
