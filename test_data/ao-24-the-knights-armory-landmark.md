AO-24. The Knight's Armory
==========================

> The Knight's Armory of the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) chapter — the room where the order's weapons were blessed before battle, where oath-blades awaited consecration in an iron-bound chest, and where knights armed themselves in the name of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) before riding out to defend the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) now stocks it with their own weapons, captured caravan equipment, and the most damning evidence in the entire priory: a locked trunk of false-flag gear — gnome-crafted weapons with clan markings, stolen [Mounted Borderer](mounted-borderers-organization.md) uniforms, and forged travel documents — staged here by [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) for the false-flag raids designed to shatter the peace between [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) and the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) gnomes. If the party finds this trunk and gets it to the right people, it blows the conspiracy wide open.

_The Knight's Armory of the Azure Oath — Now the Earth Temple Arsenal and False-Flag Staging Ground_

* * *

### The Knight's Armory

Every priory of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) kept an armory. The Arch-Paladin's knights were warrior-priests, and a warrior's weapons are sacred instruments — blessed before battle, maintained with devotion, stored with reverence. The armory of the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) was no exception.

The room sits in the west wing of the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), between the [Knights Access Corridor](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md) and [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s [chamber](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md), with the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) and **AO-22 (Hall of Final Vigil)** nearby. Two doors — one east to the corridor, one south to the outer courtyard **(AO-06)**. The courtyard door was the working entrance: knights coming off patrol walked straight from the stables to the armory, racked their weapons, and reported for debrief. The corridor door was for ceremony — the route a knight took when he was called to the [chapel](ao-14-chapel-of-heironeous-landmark.md) to have his blade blessed before a campaign.

The room is roughly 20 feet by 15 feet, with a 12-foot ceiling — high enough for lance racks, low enough to feel enclosed. Stone walls, flagstone floor, no windows. The original layout was disciplined: weapon racks along the east and west walls, a central armor stand displaying the chapter's ceremonial plate, shield pegs above the racks, and in the northeast corner, a heavy iron-bound chest where oath-blades awaited consecration — weapons pledged by knights-aspirant who had completed their trials but not yet received the blessing of the Arch-Paladin's rite.

**Halven Sere** polished these weapons for years after the order left. He oiled the blades that no one would swing. He dusted the armor that no one would wear. He kept the racks organized according to the old inventory — swords to the east, polearms to the west, shields above — because the order might come back. Until the day [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) broke the courtyard door in and told him the armory was no longer his concern.

* * *

### What the Earth Temple Made of It

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) under [Romag](romag-person.md) recognized the room for what it was — a secure, windowless, defensible space purpose-built for storing weapons — and kept it as an armory. The conversion was minimal. They cleared the old Azure Oath blades (most were sold through [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) or melted down), replaced the racks with their own weapons, and added a second function that makes this room one of the most dangerous finds in the entire priory.

Because [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) operates next door, the armory doubles as the staging area for his false-flag deception operations. A locked trunk against the south wall — separate from the garrison weapons, deliberately inconspicuous — contains the costumes, weapons, and documents that [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiders wear when they ride out to commit atrocities in someone else's name.

* * *

##### Entering the Knight's Armory

The door opens onto a windowless stone room that smells of oil, leather, and cold iron. Weapon racks line the east and west walls — mostly spears, hand-axes, and short swords, racked without ceremony. A few shields lean against the wall beneath empty pegs. In the center of the room, an iron armor stand holds a suit of chain mail on a wooden frame, and beside it, a crate of crossbow bolts sits open on the floor.  
  
The walls are bare stone except for a single feature: a long shelf above the east rack where a row of wooden weapon pegs still bears faint paint — blue and gold, the colors of an order that no longer exists here. One peg still holds something: a knotted blue cord, faded almost to grey, looped around the peg as though someone hung it there and forgot it.  
  
Against the south wall, half-hidden behind a stack of wooden shields, sits a heavy iron-bound trunk with a padlock.

* * *

### Features of the Knight's Armory

#### 1\. Garrison Weapon Racks

The east and west wall racks hold the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison's working arsenal — functional weapons, not ceremonial ones. The equipment is mixed, reflecting the garrison's diverse recruitment from [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) dockworkers, mercenaries, and Earth Temple converts:

*   **East rack:** 8 spears, 4 hand-axes, 3 short swords, 2 maces. Standard garrison issue — nothing remarkable
*   **West rack:** 6 javelins, 2 light crossbows, a heavy crossbow (cracked stock, -1 to attack), and a morningstar with dried blood on the flanges that no one has cleaned
*   **Shield stack (south wall):** 6 wooden shields, plain and undecorated. Three show repair work — split wood bound with iron strips. The garrison fights hard enough to break shields

**DC 13 Investigation (searching the racks carefully):** One of the short swords on the east rack doesn't match the others. It's better steel — lighter, with a fuller groove and a maker's mark stamped near the tang: a tiny anvil inside a circle. This is a [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) trade blade, mass-produced for caravan guards operating between [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) and [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md). Someone took it from a caravan guard who no longer needed it.

* * *

#### 2\. The Armor Stand

The central armor stand — once the proud display for the chapter's finest consecrated plate — now holds a plain suit of chain mail on a rough wooden frame. Functional, not ceremonial. Beside it, an open crate of crossbow bolts and a pile of bowstrings in various states of decay.

**DC 12 Investigation (examining the stand itself):** The wooden frame is newer than the iron stand. The original frame was shaped to hold full plate armor with articulated joints — the current chain mail hangs loosely on a frame that wasn't built for it. The iron stand base still has four bolt-holes where the original frame was secured with religious precision. Someone ripped the old frame out and replaced it with scrap wood. The original consecrated plate was likely the first thing sold or melted when the Earth Temple took over.

**DC 15 Religion (touching the iron stand):** A faint warmth pulses through the metal — brief, almost imperceptible. The stand was blessed. Specifically, the iron base was consecrated as part of the armory's dedication ritual, when the first weapons of the Azure Oath chapter were placed here. The consecration is nearly spent — decades of neglect and desecrated surroundings have eroded it — but a cleric of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) touching the base would feel the god's recognition: _this iron was mine_. No mechanical effect, but the emotional weight is real. The weapons that defended the faith stood on this iron.

* * *

#### 3\. The Blue Cord

On the shelf above the east rack, a knotted blue cord hangs from an old weapon peg. Faded almost to grey. The garrison has ignored it — too worthless to steal, too insignificant to notice.

**DC 14 Religion:** This is an **oath-cord** of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md). Knights-aspirant tied one knot for each year of service before consecration. This cord has eleven knots — eleven years of faithful waiting. The knight who tied it either received the Arch-Paladin's blessing and no longer needed it, or died before the twelfth knot.

**DC 16 History (with Religion success):** Eleven knots corresponds to a specific tradition within the Azure Oath chapter: a knight who served eleven years without consecration was called a _Brother of the Long Vigil_ — one who served faithfully despite the order never recognizing him with the full rite. It was both an honor and a sorrow. The cord was kept as proof of patience. Whoever owned this cord was still waiting when the order abandoned the priory.

_A character who takes the cord and later presents it to **Halven Sere** receives an immediate reaction: he recognizes it. It belonged to **Brother Fenwick Grael** — one of the last knights, who left with the order and never returned. Halven tried to send it to him but had no address. He will ask to hold it. If allowed, he ties a twelfth knot — completing the vigil — and returns it to the character, saying: "He earned it. Someone should carry it."_

The cord has no mechanical properties. But it weighs more than it should.

* * *

#### 4\. The False-Flag Trunk (CAMPAIGN-CRITICAL)

Against the south wall, half-hidden behind the shield stack, sits a heavy iron-bound trunk with a sturdy padlock. This is not garrison storage. This is [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s operational trunk — the physical evidence of the false-flag conspiracy that is tearing the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) apart.

**The padlock:** DC 14 Thieves' Tools to pick. DC 18 Strength to force. [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) carries the key. [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md) has a spare.

**Inside the trunk:**

*   **Three gnome-sized war-picks** — forged to look like [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) militia weapons. **DC 12 Investigation:** The picks are the right shape but the wrong weight — sized for human hands, not gnome proportions. The clan markings stamped into the hafts are accurate but machine-crisp, lacking the hand-finished irregularity of genuine gnomish smithwork. **DC 15 Investigation (or DC 12 if the character is a gnome or has proficiency with smith's tools):** The steel alloy is wrong. [Sheernobb](sheernobb-settlement.md) smiths use a distinctive iron-nickel blend sourced from [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) deposits. These weapons were forged with Furyondian merchant steel — available in bulk from any trading house. Someone with metalworking knowledge built these to pass a glance, not an inspection
*   **Two [Mounted Borderer](mounted-borderers-organization.md) patrol surcoats** — the green-and-gold tabards worn by [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md)'s frontier cavalry. **DC 10 Investigation:** Clearly stolen, not forged. The stitching is professional, the dye is correct, and one surcoat has a patrol number stitched inside the collar: _"3rd Ride, Gnarley Station."_ These were taken from dead or captured Borderers. **DC 14 History:** The 3rd Ride patrols the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) border east of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). That patrol was reported missing four months ago — the Borderer command assumed gnoll ambush
*   **Forged travel documents** — four sets. **DC 14 Investigation:** Each set identifies the bearer as a licensed merchant or surveyor operating under [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) charter between [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) and [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md). The seals are excellent — wax impressions taken from genuine Consortium documents, probably sourced through the [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) black market. A forger of real skill made these. **DC 16 Investigation:** One set of papers contains a name that doesn't exist in the Consortium's public registry: _"Marlen Devries, Licensed Factor, Western Circuit."_ The handwriting matches other documents in [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s [chamber](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md). Caidax wrote these himself
*   **A pouch of gnomish clan badges** — small copper-and-tin pins bearing the sigils of three [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) clans: Jillink, Brassbottom, and Hillwatch. **DC 12 Investigation:** The Jillink badges are genuine — supplied by **Lakash Quallad**, the clan's own agent, as part of the conspiracy. The Brassbottom and Hillwatch badges are forgeries. **DC 15 Investigation (or DC 12 for gnome characters):** The genuine Jillink badges are hand-cast with the clan's distinctive double-acorn hallmark on the reverse. The Brassbottom forgeries lack **Pilnik Brassbottom**'s maker's stamp — a detail any Kron Hills gnome would spot immediately
*   **A folded operational brief** — a single sheet of parchment in [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s handwriting. **DC 10 to read (no check needed if the party can read Common):** The document outlines the procedure for a false-flag raid: dress in gnomish gear, strike a [Silver Consortium](mages-guild-of-the-silver-consortium-organization.md) caravan on the [Ostverk](ostverk-settlement.md) road, leave one survivor to report "gnomish bandits," scatter forged clan badges at the scene, and withdraw to the priory via the [Gnarley Forest](gnarley-forest-location.md) trail. The brief is unsigned but references [Romag](romag-person.md) by initial only: _"R. confirms next target. Await signal from L."_ The _"L"_ is **Lucius Graeme** — the Greater Temple's agent in [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md), though the party would need additional context to make that connection

**Total evidence value:** If this trunk reaches [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) intact, it is enough to prove the false-flag campaign exists, implicate Clan Jillink, exonerate Clans Brassbottom and Hillwatch, connect the missing 3rd Ride patrol to the Earth Temple, and establish that a figure identified only as _"R"_ is directing the attacks from within the Greater Temple's network. Combined with the Slikkina Jillink letter from [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s [chamber](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md) and the journal fragments naming [Romag](romag-person.md), it is a political bombshell.

* * *

#### 5\. The Oath-Blade Chest (Empty)

In the northeast corner, the original iron-bound chest where oath-blades awaited consecration still sits against the wall. The Earth Temple broke the lock and emptied it months ago. The blades inside — weapons pledged by knights who never received the blessing — were sold through [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md).

The chest is empty now. But not quite.

**DC 14 Investigation (searching the chest interior):** The chest has a false bottom — a thin wooden panel fitted flush with the base, invisible unless the chest is lifted and the weight feels wrong. Beneath the panel: a folded piece of blue linen, very old, embroidered with the Azure Oath sigil in silver thread. It is the **consecration cloth** — the altar cloth that was draped over each oath-blade during the blessing ceremony. **Halven Sere** hid it here before the Earth Temple cleared the room, tucking it beneath the false bottom because he knew they would take everything they could see.

**DC 16 Religion (examining the cloth):** The cloth is still consecrated. Faintly, barely — like a candle flame in a draft — but the blessing of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) still clings to the silver threads. If a weapon is wrapped in this cloth and a prayer to the Arch-Paladin is spoken over it (any character proficient in Religion, or any paladin/cleric of a good deity), the weapon functions as **silvered for 24 hours**. This is not enchantment — it is residual divine favor, the last warmth of a blessing that was never formally ended because the last cleric left without performing the rite of closure. The blessing was simply abandoned, and it has been waiting.

_One use. After the first weapon is blessed, the cloth's remaining consecration is spent. The cloth remains, but it is just linen._

* * *

#### 6\. Caidax's Escape Route

The armory connects [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s [chamber](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md) to **AO-22 (Hall of Final Vigil)** via the [Knights Corridor](ao-11-knights-access-corridor-landmark.md). This is deliberate. [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) chose the room next to the armory specifically because it gives him a secondary exit route — through the armory, into the corridor, and down to the crypt level via **AO-22** if the priory is breached. He has rehearsed this route.

**DM Note:** If [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) flees through the armory during combat (see his tactics in [AO-13](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md)), he does **not** stop to grab the false-flag trunk. He prioritizes his own survival over the evidence. This means the trunk is left behind for the party to find — one of the few breaks in the Earth Temple's operational security. [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md) is smart enough to have planned for many contingencies, but not smart enough to have destroyed evidence he might need again.

### Encounter

**This room is usually empty.** Garrison fighters retrieve weapons as needed and leave. No one lingers. The armory is a supply point, not a post.

**During normal operations (75% chance):** The room is unoccupied. The door from the corridor may be ajar or closed (no lock — the garrison needs quick access during alerts). The courtyard door is barred from the inside.

**During alert (25% chance):** 1–2 Black Earth Guards are arming themselves — grabbing spears, shields, and crossbow bolts before moving to their posts. They are focused on equipment, not expecting intruders inside the armory. Surprise is possible (Stealth vs. Passive Perception 12).

**If Caidax is fleeing:** He passes through at speed, casting a cantrip or minor illusion to cover his retreat. He does not engage. If the party is already in the armory when he arrives, he is genuinely surprised — this was supposed to be his escape route, not an ambush. He attempts to Misty Step past them or talks his way through (Deception +7).

### DM Notes

#### The Evidence Room

AO-25 is one of the two most important intelligence rooms in the priory, paired with [AO-13 (Caidax's Chamber)](ao-13-caidax-nightshadeE28099s-chamber-landmark.md). Where AO-13 provides the _strategic_ picture — who is running the conspiracy, why, and how the geomantic operations connect to the Greater Temple — AO-25 provides the _physical evidence_. The false-flag trunk is the smoking gun.

The distinction matters. Intelligence gathered in AO-13 helps the party _understand_ what the Earth Temple is doing. Evidence gathered in AO-25 lets them _prove it_ to [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md)'s authorities. Both are valuable. Together, they are devastating.

#### The Sacred Layer

The armory's sacred elements are quieter than most rooms in the priory — no grand inscriptions, no processional architecture, no ritual chambers. The sacred layer here is personal and small: the blue cord on the peg, tied by a knight who waited eleven years. The consecration cloth hidden by **Halven Sere** in the one place he thought no one would look. The faint warmth in the iron stand that once held blessed armor.

These are the faith of individuals, not institutions. The order is gone. The grand chapel is desecrated. The Hall of Final Vigil serves tomb-robbers. But a cord still hangs on a peg, and a cloth still carries a blessing that no one remembered to end. Faith survives in the details the enemy thought too small to notice.

#### Running This Room

*   **If the party searches quickly:** They find the garrison weapons (obvious), the false-flag trunk (if they notice it behind the shields — Perception DC 12 to spot the trunk, then Investigation DC 14 to pick the lock), and nothing else. This is still a major find
*   **If the party searches thoroughly:** They find the Consortium blade on the rack, the blue cord, the consecration cloth in the oath-blade chest, the warmth in the armor stand, and the full contents of the false-flag trunk. Reward thorough searching with every detail
*   **If the party has already cleared AO-13:** They can cross-reference [Caidax](caidax-nightshade-person.md)'s handwriting on the operational brief with his journal fragments and the Slikkina Jillink letter. The case becomes airtight
*   **If the party tries to take the trunk:** It weighs approximately 40 lbs. It can be carried but not concealed. Extracting it from the priory requires either clearing the route to the courtyard or finding a way to smuggle it out. The trunk is too heavy for one person to move quietly

#### Campaign Connections

*   **The Kron Hills Destabilization:** The forged gnomish weapons and clan badges connect directly to the political crisis between [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) and the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) gnomes. **Conner Bridgeforth** and other Verbobonc politicians have been pushing for punitive action against gnomish clans based on false-flag attacks. This trunk proves those attacks were staged
*   **Clan Jillink complicity:** The genuine Jillink badges prove that **Lakash Quallad** is cooperating with the Earth Temple, not an innocent bystander. This is a betrayal from within the gnomish community
*   **The Missing 3rd Ride:** The [Mounted Borderer](mounted-borderers-organization.md) surcoat with the patrol number connects to a real missing patrol. Returning this evidence to Borderer command answers the question of what happened to their people — and redirects military anger from the gnomes to the Earth Temple
*   **Lucius Graeme connection:** The "L" in the operational brief is thin evidence alone, but combined with what the party may learn from [Romag](romag-person.md) himself (if captured) or from other Greater Temple sources, it points directly at **Lucius Graeme** as the handler coordinating the attacks from [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md)

#### Loot Summary

*   Garrison weapons: functional but common. ~20 gp total resale value
*   Silver Consortium trade blade: 15 gp (or evidence value if returned to the Consortium)
*   Consecration cloth: priceless to the right person, no market value. One-use silvering blessing
*   Blue oath-cord: no market value. Emotional weight only
*   False-flag trunk contents: no resale value — the value is political. Delivering this to [Verbobonc](verbobonc2C-city-settlement.md) is worth more than gold
*   **Total monetary loot:** ~35 gp. Total _campaign_ value: incalculable

##### Sacred Layer

The armory's faith is personal, not institutional. A blue cord still hangs from a peg — eleven years of waiting. A cloth still carries a blessing that no one remembered to end. The iron that held consecrated armor still recognizes the god who blessed it. The Earth Temple took the weapons, sold the blades, and filled the racks with their own steel. They did not notice the cord. They did not find the cloth. They did not feel the warmth in the iron. Faith survives in the things too small for the enemy to see.
