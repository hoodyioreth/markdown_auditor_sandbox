Lord Rufus
==========

Lord Rufus is the sword-arm of Hommlet — a veteran fighter of the [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md), slayer of a black dragon in the Suss Forest, and the man entrusted by Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) to build Doomwatch Keep and hold the western approach to the Temple of Elemental Evil. Blunt, dependable, and harder than the adamantine in his armor, Rufus is the reason the bandits haven't taken the Long Road. Where his companion [Lord Burne](lord-burne-person.md) theorizes and strategizes, Rufus acts. Where Burne speaks in paragraphs, Rufus speaks in sentences — and means every word.

> _"People around here call me Lord. I didn't ask for it. But I'll earn it — every morning, on the training ground, with the Badgers. That's what a lord does."_

#### Physical Description

Rufus is a broad-shouldered Oeridian human in his mid-thirties, carrying the unmistakable bearing of a man who has survived real battles rather than tavern brawls.

*   **Build and Stature:** Six feet and one inch of dense muscle, with a neck like a tree stump and forearms that make his gauntlets look tight. He moves with the deliberate economy of a man who has worn heavy armor most of his adult life.
*   **Face:** Square-jawed and weather-beaten, with a nose that has been broken at least twice and healed slightly wrong. A pale scar runs from his left cheekbone to the corner of his mouth — a souvenir from the Moathouse siege of 569 CY. His eyes are dark brown and direct; they tend to unsettle people who are lying to him.
*   **Hair:** Close-cropped dark brown, going silver at the temples. He keeps it short because he dislikes the feel of hair inside a helm.
*   **Armor and Arms:** Rufus wears intricately inlaid adamantine half-plate that he commissioned from a Verbobonc smith after the Emridy campaign — it is his single extravagance. He carries a heavy shield bearing the badger-and-sword device of @Burne's Badgers and a well-used longsword. A dirk rides his left hip for close work.
*   **Presence:** Off-duty, Rufus favors plain working clothes and boots thick with Keep construction dust. On duty, he is a wall of polished metal. Either way, he is not a man you mistake for a merchant.

_

> "Don't let the scar fool you. The other man looked worse."

_

### Personality

Rufus is the kind of man who says what he means, means what he says, and has very little patience for the distance between those two things.

*   **Blunt to a Fault:** Rufus has no talent for diplomacy and does not particularly want one. He will tell a merchant his prices are inflated, a noble his plan is stupid, and a bandit he has five seconds to reconsider — and he means all three with equal sincerity. Burne occasionally has to smooth over what Rufus has said; Rufus occasionally has to follow up what Burne has implied.
*   **Loyalty Without Limit:** His commitment to Burne, to the Badgers, and to the village of Hommlet is absolute. He does not break faith. He does not make promises he cannot keep. He has told people to their face that he dislikes them — and then stepped between them and danger anyway, because they were under his protection.
*   **Practical and Results-Driven:** Rufus has no interest in elegant solutions. He wants the problem fixed, the road clear, the enemy gone. If the elegant solution and the direct solution both work, he will take the direct one, because fewer things can go wrong in the middle.
*   **Quietly Courageous:** Rufus does not talk about fear. He is not unafraid — he has seen enough to know better — but he treats courage as a job requirement, not a virtue worth praising. He expects it of himself and of every Badger under his command.
*   **Gruff Warmth:** He is not cold. He remembers the names of soldiers who served under him years ago, asks after their families, and makes sure the Badgers are fed before he sits down to eat. He would never say any of this out loud.

_

> "A sword in hand and a clear goal — there's no problem we can't solve. Stop overthinking it."

_

### Background

#### Velunese Origins

Rufus was born in the Archclericy of Veluna to a family of minor freeholders — landowners of modest means, respected in their community but nowhere near the noble class. He was the second of three sons, which meant the farm was going to his elder brother and the temple novitiate to the younger. What was left for Rufus was the road. He left Veluna at seventeen with a secondhand sword, a month's worth of traveling coin, and a vague plan to find adventure in the east. He found Verbobonc instead, which amounts to the same thing.

#### Meeting Burne — A Fellowship Forged in Adventure

In the city of Verbobonc, a teenage Rufus fell in with a young wizard of similar age and complementary disposition: sharp-tongued, theoretically fearless, and equally broke. Lord Burne was then simply "Burne," a scholarship student of middling means with an extraordinary gift for evocation magic and a complete absence of physical courage. Rufus had the inverse problem. The arrangement that began as mutual necessity became something neither man would call friendship out loud — though everyone around them would. Over the next decade they worked their way through the adventuring circuits of the central Flanaess, taking contracts, clearing ruins, and generally surviving things that should have killed them.

##### The Black Dragon of the Suss Forest

The encounter that made their names in Verbobonc was a commission from a consortium of Kron Hills merchants whose trade caravans had been raided repeatedly by a black dragon denning in the northern Suss. The bounty was generous because the job was suicidal. Rufus and Burne took three weeks to scout the approach, study the dragon's patrol patterns, and prepare. On the day of the assault, Burne's magic blinded the creature at the precise moment Rufus committed to a mounted charge down a forest game trail. The dragon died. Both men nearly did. The scars on Rufus's left forearm that he never discusses are from that day. The Kron merchant consortium paid double. The story spread west to Verbobonc and east to Greyhawk City. The Viscount's chamberlain sent a letter three weeks later.

#### The Battle of Emridy Meadows — 569 CY

The great coalition battle against the forces of the Temple of Elemental Evil at [The Battle of Emridy Meadows](the-battle-of-emridy-meadows-myth.md) is the defining military event of a generation in Verbobonc. Rufus and Burne fought under the banner of the allied good forces — Rufus in the shield wall during the main engagement, Burne providing magical support from the second line. The battle was a decisive victory. The Temple's armies were broken, its humanoid allies scattered, its key leaders killed or fled. The Temple itself was sealed. Viscount [Wilfrick Revepaix](wilfrick-revepaix-person.md) personally recognized Rufus and Burne's contributions afterward. It was in the aftermath of Emridy Meadows that the two adventurers received their mandate: go to @Hommlet, hold the western approach, and build something that will last._

> "Emridy was the loudest day of my life. I spent most of it watching the man next to me die. We won. That's what I remember."

_

### Role in Hommlet — 576 CY

#### The Viscount's Mandate

Rufus and Burne arrived in Hommlet on behalf of Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix as agents of the Viscounty, authorized to administer the village and oversee the construction of Doomwatch Keep. Their authority is backed by the Viscount's seal. Their credibility is backed by their reputation. In practice, Rufus functions as the military commander of Hommlet's defense. He does not run the village council — that is Burne's territory — but no sword is drawn in Hommlet without Rufus knowing about it first.

#### Doomwatch Keep — A Fortress Against the Dark

The construction of [30\. Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md) is Rufus's primary project and his principal frustration. Stone deliveries from the western quarry run late or fail to arrive entirely. Skilled masons are expensive and occasionally poached by Verbobonc city projects. The ground at the eastern end of the village is softer than the initial survey suggested. Rufus manages the construction personally, reviewing work each morning before the Badger training rotation. He has learned more about foundation drainage than any fighter should know. The keep, when complete, will anchor Hommlet's defense and house a permanent garrison capable of holding the Long Road against organized assault. In 576 CY, completion is still three to five years away depending on funding — and the stone shipment that was due from the western quarry a week ago has not arrived. The merchant contractor is not responding to Burne's letters. The Badger outrider Rufus sent to scout the road has not returned either.

#### Burne's Badgers

[Burne's Badgers](burne-s-badgers-organization.md) are Rufus's instrument and his daily responsibility. In 576 CY the unit stands at ten soldiers and three scouts — thirteen men, every one of them a former brigand who chose service over the scaffold, and every one of them the better for it. Rufus is not a lord who gives orders from a distance. He drills the Badgers himself every morning at first light on the open ground outside the tower. He eats the same rations, wears the road dust they wear, and has been known to personally demonstrate why a shield angle matters by putting a sparring partner on the ground. The Badgers do not love him the way they might love a warm or demonstrative commander. They respect him the way soldiers respect someone who has never once asked them to do what he would not do himself.

*   **Markan of Narwell:** Lieutenant and senior veteran, Rufus's operational arm. He handles the patrol schedule, the duty rotations, the discipline matters Rufus does not need to see personally. He has been with the Badgers since before either of them was using the name.
*   **Ferkis:** Demi-elven sergeant — the NCO who knows every man's name, habit, and weakness and uses all three to keep them functional. His longevity of experience and non-human combat instinct make him invaluable on the scout side of the unit's work. He is fiercely loyal to Rufus and keeps the younger soldiers from doing anything memorable at the wrong moment.

In 576 CY, the Badgers' patrol territory runs from the village outward: the Long Road south toward [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), and the High Road and Low Road east of Nulb toward the Verbobonc approaches. They provide security escorts for stone convoys to Doomwatch Keep Construction Site, patrol the northern track toward the Etters range and the lands beyond, and respond to bandit activity reports on the trade roads. What thirteen men cannot do is replace the [Mounted Borderers](mounted-borderers-organization.md). The Viscount's withdrawal of Borderer patrols from [Greenway Valley](greenway-valley-settlement.md) and the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) has left a gap in coverage that the Badgers are being asked to partially fill without the manpower to fill it. Rufus has submitted a formal request for Borderer redeployment through Burne's correspondence channel. He does not expect a response. The missing outrider is a Badger named Derwin, two years' service, reliable. He was sent west on the stone road six days ago. Rufus has not said how long he will wait before riding that road himself. Burne suspects the answer is "two more days."_

> "The safety of Hommlet is our highest priority. Nothing will stand in our way — not bandits, not cults, not budget shortfalls, and not soft foundations."

### Relationships

##### Lord Burne

The friendship between Rufus and Burne is the load-bearing structure of both their lives. They have adventured together for nearly fifteen years, survived things no one should survive twice, and built a working partnership that functions precisely because they are opposite in almost every way. Rufus does not understand half of what Burne is doing in his laboratory. Burne cannot hold a sword without hurting himself. Neither has ever suggested the other is anything less than essential. They share the [31\. Two Story Tower - Rufus and Burne](31-two-story-tower---rufus-and-burne-landmark-1.md) as their residence, Burne's quarters above, Rufus's below. The arrangement suits both.

##### Viscount Wilfrick Revepaix

Rufus respects the Viscount as his lord and the source of his mandate. He is loyal to the office. He is somewhat less confident in the man who currently holds it — Wilfrick's indecisiveness in the face of rising threats is a recurring source of private frustration. The Mounted Borderer withdrawal in particular sits badly with Rufus; it is a military decision, and he was not consulted. Rufus does not say this to anyone except Burne, and then only once.

##### House Asbury and the Northern Lands

The Knight Banneret holdings of [House Asbury](house-asbury-organization.md) at Penwick lie to the north of Hommlet, beyond the Etters range. The Etters are a modest ridgeline rather than a true mountain barrier, but they define the northern limit of Hommlet's immediate operational zone. Rufus maintains a working relationship with the Asbury household — their lands and those of Hommlet share road networks, and their agricultural and wine shipments move through territory the Badgers patrol. The recent banditry affecting Asbury shipments on the Long Road is a problem Rufus takes seriously both as a security matter and as a relationship one; a Banneret whose income is bleeding tends eventually to make his frustrations felt in Verbobonc.

##### The People of Hommlet

The village knows what Rufus is and what he is for. He is not beloved the way a popular innkeeper or a healing cleric might be beloved. He is trusted the way a stone wall is trusted — you don't think about it much, but you'd notice immediately if it wasn't there. Most villagers find him taciturn and slightly intimidating. They also sleep soundly.

##### Temple Threats and Rising Dangers

Rufus has watched the bandit traffic on the roads increase over the past two years. He does not believe in coincidence. He has told Burne that someone is reorganizing the Temple's supply lines, and Burne has told him the arcane signs suggest the same. They are preparing accordingly — quietly, without alarming the village council, because panic helps no one and the Keep is not finished yet. Rufus watches the road south toward Nulb and the High Road and Low Road east of it with particular attention. The pattern of where the bandits operate and where they don't tells him something he hasn't fully named yet._

> "Something is moving out there. I can feel it the same way I feel weather changing — in the old scar on my shoulder, in the way the road goes quiet at night. We need that wall finished."

_

### DM Notes — Playing Lord Rufus

Rufus is the party's most direct point of access to official authority in Hommlet. He will not waste their time with pleasantries. He will tell them exactly what he needs and what he can offer in return.

#### When Players Seek an Audience

*   **He respects competence:** Adventurers who have already done something useful — cleared a road, dealt with a bandit scout, returned something that was lost — get his attention immediately. Adventurers who arrive with claims and no evidence get polite skepticism.
*   **He does not delegate the important things:** If players bring him intelligence about Temple activity, Rufus handles it personally. He may ask them to show him rather than tell him.
*   **He will not be deceived:** His Wisdom and his years make him difficult to fast-talk. He is not paranoid, but he is experienced. Lying to Rufus and being caught is a significant relationship setback.
*   **Tone:** Gruff, brief, fair. He does not praise easily but when he does, it counts. A "not bad" from Rufus is high commendation.

#### Adventure Hooks

*   **Badger Down — Derwin on the Western Road:** A Badger scout named Derwin was sent to check the stone road west toward the quarry and has not returned in six days. Rufus will ask trustworthy adventurers to ride that road before he rides it himself. He will tell them what Derwin was riding, what he was wearing, and where he was expected to stop. He will not tell them how worried he is.
*   **The Supply Problem:** A stone shipment for the Keep arrived short — and the merchant claims it was delivered in full. Someone is skimming, and Rufus wants to know if it is incompetence or Temple coin doing the hiring.
*   **Emridy Meadows Veteran:** An old soldier from the Battle has arrived in Hommlet, badly injured, bearing a message for Rufus that he won't let anyone else read. Rufus will ask trustworthy adventurers to backtrack the man's route and find out who attacked him on the road — and whether they were looking for the message or just for anyone carrying one.
*   **The Viscount's Letter:** Rufus has received orders from Verbobonc that conflict with what he believes is right for Hommlet. He will not disobey. But he needs someone to go to Verbobonc and make the case he cannot make himself, to someone in the Council who will listen.
*   **High Road Ambush Pattern:** Reports from traders coming off the High Road east of Nulb describe a consistent ambush point — same stretch of road, same time of day. Rufus needs someone to stake it out and find where the bandits are staging from. The Badgers are too visibly military to do it unnoticed. Adventurers are not.
