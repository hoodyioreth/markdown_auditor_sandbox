E2 Hamstid’s Horse Stables
==========================

_**Premier Stables of Ryemend  
**_Ryemend’s busiest stableyard—trusted by merchants, couriers, and wary travelers who need **fresh mounts, quiet tack-repairs, and discreet swaps**. Use it to pace overland travel, introduce **shadow riders**, and hide “ordinary” clues (brands, hoof-mud, saddle-stains) that point toward larger plots.

##### Approuch Hamstid’s Horse Stables

The lane widens into hard-packed earth and trampled straw. A long rail-fence ringeth a yard of stalls and wagon bays, and the air is thick with warm horse-breath, oiled leather, and sweet hay. A bell clinks once—low and practiced. Hooves strike stone in steady rhythm. Over the gate a painted board showeth a laughing horse-head with a single brass nail for an eye. A broad man in a stained apron turns from a hoof he’s trimming, and his gaze measures thee the way a drover measures a storm-cloud.

### What This Place Is

Hamstid’s is the **hinge-yard** of Ryemend: the place folk use when the bridge, the river road, and the northbound tracks must all be served at once. It is practical, bustling, and deceptively important—because **horses carry what boats cannot**: sealed letters, whispered bargains, fugitives, and fast trouble.

To the district, it is a necessity.  
To the DM, it is a lever: _every travel choice becomes a scene._

##### Exterior & Layout

**1) The Gate Yard  
**A fenced court with hitching rails, watering trough, and a “quiet post” (a sheltered corner where folk speak low while pretending to check girths).

**2) The Long Stable (Public Stalls)  
**Two rows of stalls, swept daily, with painted numbers and a chalk-board of arrivals. This board is a story engine: names, destinations, and last-seen times.

**3) The Farrier’s Shed  
**A lean-to with forge, anvil, and racks of shoes. The farrier is the district’s best rumor-sifter—folk talk when their horse is lame and their plans are delayed.

**4) The Wagon Bays  
**Covered bays for carts and coaches. One bay is always “reserved,” though no sign says so.

**5) The Hay Loft & Tack Room  
**Loft above the long stable—warm, dusty, and private. Perfect for clandestine meetings, stakeouts, or “sleeping rough indoors.”

#### History

_From Ranch to City Stables_

Harnstid, once a human rancher, established his stables in Verbobonc eight years ago. Since then, his stables have become a cornerstone of the city, known for their excellent treatment of animals.

##### Historical Highlights

*   **Transition:** From rancher to city stable owner
*   **Reputation:** Built a legacy of care and exotic mounts
*   **Growth:** Expanded selection to include rare and exotic animals

### DM Tools: How to Leverage the Stable

##### 1) Travel Becomes Plot

Need the party to head south, slow down, or choose a route? A lame horse, a stolen saddle, or a sudden “no mounts available” forces decisions.

##### 2) Clues That Feel Real

*   A fresh brand half-scraped away
*   Blue-gray clay in hooves (from a specific riverbank)
*   A tack strap cut clean (knife) instead of snapped (panic)
*   A saddle blanket stitched with a merchant mark

##### 3) Social Pressure Without Combat

Stableyards are public. Everyone sees everything. If the party threatens or lies here, word spreads fast.

* * *

### Player Interaction Hooks

**Why PCs come here**

*   Replace ruined mounts or disguise travel gear
*   Hire a rider-guide or messenger
*   Hide an item “in plain sight” (tack room / loft)
*   Track someone by their horse’s brand or shoe-work

**If they linger**

*   Hamstid offers a quiet job: “Ride this horse to that inn. Bring back the blanket.”
*   Vessa notices their boots: “That mud is not from the north bank. Where have you truly been?”

**What goes wrong**

*   A courier horse arrives riderless—foam-flecked, bleeding at the flank
*   A Furyondy agent tries to buy the party’s destination “for their safety”
*   Someone attempts a midnight tack-swap to frame the PCs for theft

### Owning and Riding a Griffon

_A Griffon's Majesty and Challenge_

Owning and riding a griffon requires significant skill, preparation, and understanding of the inherent risks involved. These majestic creatures, with the body of a lion and the wings and head of an eagle, are not for the faint-hearted.

##### Required Skills

*   **Advanced Riding Skills:** Mastery in handling powerful and unpredictable flying mounts.
*   **Bonding and Training:** Establishing trust and a strong bond with the griffon.
*   **Aerial Combat Trainin**g: Proficiency in combat while in flight.

##### Hazards

*   **Temperament:** Griffons are fierce and can be aggressive if not properly trained.
*   **Maintenance:** High upkeep with specialized live diet and habitat needs.
*   **Flight Risks:** Navigating the skies poses dangers from weather and potential aerial predators.

##### Hamstid Brindle (Stablemaster)

Broad-shouldered, sun-browned, with hands like mallets and a voice that can quiet a yard. He was once a caravan outrider; now he keeps other folk’s journeys from ending early.

**Wants**

*   Keep his yard reputable and paid
*   Avoid being pressed into politics he cannot win
*   Protect his hostlers (most are young and easily threatened)

**Secret  
**Hamstid maintains a **discreet exchange practice**: a horse arrives; a different horse leaves—same saddle blanket, different brand. He calls it “courtesy.” Others call it “logistics.”

**Dialogue**

*   **Hamstid:** “Hold thy reins gentle. A frightened beast telleth me more of thee than thy tongue ever will.”
*   **Hamstid:** “Coin buys oats. It doth not buy silence. That must be earned.”
*   **Hamstid:** “If thou’rt riding south… then mind thy straps. South-roads have a way of loosening what men thought tight.”

##### Vessa “Nailbright” (Farrier)

A sharp-eyed woman with soot-streaked cheeks, known for setting shoes that hold through marsh and rock alike.

**Dialogue**

*   **Vessa:** “Show me the hoof, and I’ll show thee the road. Mud speaks—if thou know’st how to read it.”
*   **Vessa:** “Bandits don’t fear steel half so much as they fear a fast horse.”

##### Joryn & Pell (Hostlers)

Two quick lads (or lasses, as you prefer) who know every stable sound: the honest snort, the nervous stamp, the horse that has run too hard.

**Dialogue**

*   **Joryn:** “We saw riders at dawn with their lanterns hooded—no singing, no jokes. That ain’t normal.”
*   **Pell:** “One horse came in trembling, like it had smelled fire. But there was no smoke on it.”

##### Rumors / Secrets / Plot Hooks (12)

1.  A mount came in with a **brand burned over**—beneath it, a sigil no drover will name.
2.  The “reserved bay” hosted a coach with **no heraldry** and servants who never spoke above a whisper.
3.  A hostler swears a horse in stall seven **shied from a holy symbol**—then calmed when it was hidden.
4.  Hamstid has a ledger page torn out and kept in his pocket—“for insurance.”
5.  Vessa can tell which riders have been in **Kron Hills scree** by tiny stone chips in the hoof.
6.  Someone is buying **oats in bulk** and paying extra for delivery at night.
7.  A lost child in Gnomeburg says, “The horses keep secrets in their teeth.”
8.  A saddle blanket with a foreign stitch-pattern is being passed hand to hand like a token.
9.  A Vigil Warden asks who crossed the yard at dawn—then pretends he did not ask.
10.  A horse returned from the south with its mane braided in **three knots**—a signal from someone who knows old codes.
11.  A stablehand heard Hamstid say, “Not here,” to a well-dressed stranger—and the stranger smiled like a knife.
12.  A rider from the road mutters: “They’re taking folk alive now,” before collapsing in the hay.
