AO-C02 — The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead
=========================================

> The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead — the central spine of the crypts beneath the [Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). A 120-foot barrel-vaulted corridor of dressed limestone, fiften feet wide and eight feet tall, with burial alcoves stacked three high on both walls — over sixty sealed recesses containing the rank-and-file dead of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md). The gallery is the hub from which every zone of the crypt level branches: the looted wing to the northeast, the sacred warded wing to the southwest, the ossuary and eastern exit to the southeast, the collapsed creature passages to the far southwest, and the duergar excavation tunnel descending through a hole in the gallery floor at its southern end. The gallery teaches its own story as you walk it. The north end is ransacked — alcoves smashed open, stone slabs shattered, grave goods stripped, boot-prints and rubble dust trampled into the flagstone. The middle section is contested — sealed alcoves glow with a flickering blue light that strengthens as you move south. The south end is untouched and luminous — the oldest tombs, the strongest wards, the faith of the founding era still burning in the stone after six centuries. Three divine ward-gates block access to the deeper zones, each with its own visual signature and its own key. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has mapped the gallery exhaustively. He can walk its length in darkness by tremorsense alone. But the wards will not let him pass where the dead do not want him to go.

_AO-C02. The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead — Central Burial Corridor and Hub of the Crypt Level — The Skyrim Corridor_

* * *

### The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead

The gallery begins at the entrance arch from **AO-C01** and runs south for one hundred and twenty feet in a straight line, terminating at a blank wall of dressed limestone that the duergar have since broken through to begin their excavation tunnel. It is fifteen feet wide — broad enough for pallbearers carrying a funeral bier to pass in single file but too narrow for two armored warriors to fight abreast. The ceiling is a continuous barrel vault, eight feet at the crown, constructed from interlocking limestone blocks that amplify sound in both directions. A whispered conversation at one end is audible as a murmur at the other. Combat is audible everywhere.

And on both sides, from floor to ceiling, the dead watch.

The burial alcoves define the gallery. Horizontal recesses cut into the dressed limestone walls, stacked three high — bottom row at knee height, middle row at chest height, top row at head height — each large enough to hold a single body lying in repose. The alcoves are eighteen inches tall, twenty-four inches wide, and six feet deep. Each was sealed with a carved stone slab fitted flush to the wall, bearing the occupant's name, rank, dates of service, and a short dedication inscription. Where the slabs bear portraits, the carved faces look outward — watching the corridor, watching anyone who passes. Where the slabs bear only sigils, the Azure Oath's lightning-bolt-and-fist motif repeats in variations unique to each knight.

Over sixty alcoves line the gallery. The [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) expected to bury its dead here for centuries, and for over five hundred years — from the founding in -12 CY to the withdrawal in 562 CY — they did. The gallery filled from south to north: the oldest burials at the far end, the most recent nearest the staircase. This means walking the gallery from north to south is walking backward through time. The north end holds the order's final decades. The south end holds its first century. The faith that sealed those tombs grows stronger with every step south.

### The Visual Language of the Wards

The ward system is visible. The players can see it. This is not hidden magic — it is consecrated architecture made manifest, and the gallery teaches its own visual language as the party moves through it.

##### North Section (First 40 Feet — Late Era, 450–562 CY)

The north end is **dark**. The alcove slabs here have been smashed, pried off, or removed entirely. Broken stone fragments litter the floor. Empty alcoves gape open — dark rectangular mouths in the limestone, some still holding the impression of a body in the dust, some completely stripped. Torn burial shrouds trail from the recesses like shed skin. The few slabs that remain are cracked, their inscriptions defaced by pry-bar gouges. Iron lantern hooks have been driven into the alcove edges by the duergar, but most are unlit. The boot-prints of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) work crews are trampled into stone dust on the flagstone floor.

There is no ward-glow here. The divine protection on these alcoves has been broken — either by force or by the natural decay of wards laid during the order's declining years, when fewer brothers attended the consecration rites and the prayers were thinner. The stone is just stone. The dead who rested here are gone — their bones disturbed, their grave goods taken, their names half-illegible under the damage.

**What this section tells the party:** The Earth Temple has been here. They looted what they could reach. The damage is methodical, not frantic — pry bars and hammers, not axes and rage. Someone directed this work. Someone chose which tombs to open first.

##### Middle Section (Next 40 Feet — Middle Era, 100–450 CY)

The mood changes. The boot-prints thin and then stop. The rubble on the floor gives way to undisturbed flagstone. And the alcove slabs begin to **glow**.

It is faint at first — a blue luminescence so dim it could be mistaken for reflected lantern-light, visible only because the surrounding gallery is dark. But as the party moves south, the glow strengthens. Each sealed slab pulses with a soft blue-white light that emanates from the carved inscriptions and sigils — the names of the dead glowing as though the letters were filled with phosphorescent ink. The glow is not steady. It flickers — slow, rhythmic, like breathing. Some slabs pulse brighter than others. Some have hairline cracks where the light leaks through in thin bright lines, as though the ward inside is pressing against the stone from behind.

The air temperature drops. The smell of mineral dust gives way to something cleaner — stone and cold air and a faint electric tang, like the air before a lightning strike. The barrel vault overhead catches the blue light and scatters it in dim reflections along the curved ceiling, creating a rippling luminous effect that makes the corridor feel like the interior of a glowing ribcage.

The sealed slabs in this section are warm to the touch. Not hot — warm, like stone that has been sitting in sunlight, though no sunlight has reached this place in six centuries. The warmth comes from the ward itself — divine energy radiating outward from the consecrated remains within. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has touched these slabs. He described the sensation in his journal as "compressed prayer." He is not wrong.

**What this section tells the party:** The wards are real. They are visible. They are _working_. The Earth Temple stopped here — not by choice, but because the stone would not let them continue. The dead in this section are protected. The glow is the proof.

##### South Section (Final 40 Feet — Founding Era, -12–100 CY)

The south end of the gallery burns.

Not with fire. With **faith**. The blue-white glow that flickered in the middle section becomes a steady, unwavering radiance here — bright enough to read by, bright enough to cast shadows, bright enough that the party does not need torches or darkvision to see. Every sealed slab in this section blazes with light. The inscriptions are not merely glowing — they are **incandescent**, the carved letters sharp and clear as the day they were cut, the names of the founding-era dead burning in the stone as though the masons carved them with lightning.

The air here is cold — not the damp cold of underground stone, but a clean, dry, electric cold that prickles the skin and raises the hair on the back of the neck. The blue-white light has a weight to it, a presence, like standing in a cathedral when the organ plays a chord low enough to feel in the chest. The stone hums. Not audibly — in the bones. The geomantic pulse from the Black Mouth below is present here too, but it is **opposed** — the ward-glow and the deep pulse push against each other, creating a standing vibration that makes loose objects rattle and teeth ache.

The flagstone floor inscription — the continuation of the processional prayer from **AO-22** above — is brightest here. The carved letters run the gallery's centerline in a continuous line of Old Oeridian text, and in the south section, the letters glow with the same blue-white radiance as the alcove slabs. The prayer is part of the ward. The floor is part of the seal. Everything in this section is connected — alcoves, floor, ceiling, the barrel vault itself — a single integrated system of consecrated architecture that has been holding for over five centuries because the faith that built it was absolute.

**What this section tells the party:** This is what the entire crypt was supposed to look like. The north end is what happens when the wards fail. The south end is what happens when they hold. **Halven Sere**'s fourteen years of prayer kept this light burning. The party is looking at the physical manifestation of one old man's refusal to stop believing.

### The Six Passages

The Gallery of the Vigilant Dead is the hub. Every zone of the crypt level branches from this corridor. Six passages lead outward — north, northeast, southwest, southeast, far southwest, and down through the floor at the south end. Each passage has its own character, its own access conditions, and its own visual signature. The party will return to this gallery again and again, choosing which branch to explore, which ward to challenge, which secret to pursue.

##### 1\. The Entrance Arch (North Wall → AO-C01)

The gallery's north end opens onto **AO-C01** through the same carved arch the party entered from — the gallery entrance with its inscribed lintel reading _"The faithful descend. The stone receives."_ The archway ward (see AO-C01) provides a minor defensive barrier — undead crossing north through the arch take 1d4 radiant damage. This makes the landing a fallback point if the party triggers hostile undead in the gallery.

The arch is always open. There is no gate or door. The gallery begins immediately beyond it — the first breached alcoves are visible from **AO-C01**, and the smell of old dust and broken stone drifts north.

##### 2\. The Looted Passage (East Wall, North Section → AO-C03)

Thirty feet south of the entrance arch, a corridor branches east from the gallery through a plain stone doorway — no arch, no inscription, no ward. This was a functional access corridor for the tomb chambers east of the gallery, built for convenience rather than ceremony. The doorway is six feet wide and seven feet tall, its stone lintel unmarked.

The passage beyond is visible from the gallery — ten feet of corridor leading to the Desecrated Tombs of the Last Watch (**AO-C03**). The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has used this passage constantly. The floor is trampled with boot-prints and rubble dust. Lantern light flickers from beyond. Voices carry — duergar Undercommon, occasionally human Common. The Duergar Work Camp (**AO-C04**) is audible from here.

There is no ward on this passage. The tombs in the looted wing were the most recent and weakest — their wards degraded naturally, and the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) broke through what remained. This is the open door. Anyone can walk through.

**Visual signature:** Dark stone, trampled floor, lantern-glow from beyond. The working entrance. Industrial.

##### 3\. The Arch of First Oath (West Wall, South Section → AO-C05)

Eighty feet south of the entrance — deep in the gallery's brightly warded south section — a ceremonial arch opens in the west wall. This is not a plain doorway. The arch is carved from a single block of pale limestone, eight feet tall and six feet wide, with fluted columns on either side and a keystone bearing the Azure Oath's sigil: a clenched fist gripping a bolt of lightning, rendered in meticulous relief. The columns are carved with spiraling vine-patterns that incorporate tiny lightning bolts — the order's decorative vocabulary, used only in their most sacred spaces.

The archway blazes with light.

The ward on the Arch of First Oath is the strongest divine barrier in the crypt complex — a curtain of blue-white radiance that fills the entire archway opening, visible from sixty feet away in the gallery, bright enough to illuminate the corridor in both directions. The light is not flat. It has depth — layers of luminous energy that shift and ripple like sunlight through deep water. Standing close, the party can see shapes in the light: faint outlines of armored figures, the suggestion of faces, the ghostly impression of knights standing shoulder to shoulder in the archway, barring passage. These are not illusions or undead — they are **ward-echoes**, the accumulated residue of every funeral procession that passed through this arch into the Founders' Sepulchre beyond. Five centuries of grief and honor and faith, compressed into light.

The temperature within five feet of the arch drops sharply. The air hums. The blue-white glow pulses with a slow rhythm that matches **Halven Sere**'s prayer cadence — a heartbeat of faith, still synchronized with the old man's devotions above even though he does not know this arch exists in its current state.

**Ward Mechanics — The Faith Gate:**

*   **Earth Temple bearers:** Anyone carrying or wearing the symbol of the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) or the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is **physically repelled** — hurled backward 10 feet from the archway, no save, taking 2d6 radiant damage. The ward-echoes flare bright white for an instant and a sound like a thunderclap rolls through the gallery. This has happened to [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s acolytes. Twice. They no longer approach
*   **Cleric or Paladin of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md):** Passes freely. The ward-echoes part like a curtain, the light dims momentarily, and the character steps through into the Founders' Sepulchre as though walking through warm water. The experience is profoundly moving — a sensation of welcome, of recognition, of being expected
*   **Character carrying Halven Sere's holy symbol (from AO-15):** Passes automatically. The ward recognizes the symbol — the light flares, then softens, and the ward-echo figures incline their heads as the character passes. **Halven**'s symbol is the custodian's key. The wards know it
*   **Any other character:** Must succeed on a **DC 16 Charisma check**. The ward judges intent — not alignment, not class, but the character's relationship to the dead. A character who has shown respect (restored the guardian in **AO-22**, lit the eternal flame sconces, spoken the Processional Ward prayer) makes this check with advantage. A character who has looted tombs or desecrated the priory makes it with disadvantage. On failure, the character is gently but firmly pushed back 5 feet — not thrown, not damaged, just stopped. The ward does not punish ignorance. It only punishes malice
*   **If Halven Sere is present and guiding the party:** He places his palm on the keystone sigil and speaks a word in Old Oeridian — _"Valegate"_ — the name of the first Knight-Commander. The ward drops entirely for one minute. All party members may pass. This is the canonical method — the custodian opens the way

[Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) has spent weeks studying this arch. His excavation journal contains detailed measurements, resonance readings, and increasingly frustrated notes. He knows the ward is tied to **Halven Sere**'s prayers. He knows the old man holds the key. This is why **Halven** is alive. This is why the interrogations in **AO-20** have focused not on treasure but on _how to open the doors that will not open_.

**Visual signature:** Blazing blue-white arch, ghostly knight figures in the light, cold air, audible hum. Unmissable. Sacred.

##### 4\. The Seal of the Champions (Accessed via AO-C05 → AO-C06)

This ward-gate is not visible from the gallery — it lies beyond the Founders' Sepulchre, sealing the passage deeper into the warded wing. It is described here because the party will encounter it after passing through the Arch of First Oath, and the gallery article serves as the master reference for the ward system.

The passage from **AO-C05** to **AO-C06** (the Champions' Rest) is sealed by a heavy stone door set into the south wall of the Founders' Sepulchre. The door is eight feet tall and four feet wide, carved in deep relief with a procession of armored knights — twelve figures, matching the twelve founders, walking in single file toward a stylized sunrise. At the center of the door, an iron lock shaped like a clenched fist gripping a lightning bolt protrudes from the stone. The fist is life-sized, exquisitely detailed — individual knuckles, veins on the back of the hand, the lightning bolt rendered as a jagged bar of iron emerging from between the fingers.

The lock has no keyhole.

**Ward Mechanics — The Knowledge Gate:**

*   **The Processional Ward:** The lock opens when a character recites the full six-line Processional Ward — the funeral prayer carved on the walls of **AO-22** above. The six lines must be spoken in order, without pause, in Old Oeridian or Common. As each line is spoken, one pair of knight figures on the door begins to glow — first line, first pair; second line, second pair; and so on until all twelve figures blaze with blue-white light. When the sixth line is completed, the iron fist opens — fingers unclenching one by one, the lightning bolt rotating ninety degrees with a grinding sound — and the door swings inward
*   **Learning the prayer:** The wall inscriptions in **AO-22** contain all six lines. Religion DC 12 identifies them as a functional prayer sequence (see AO-22 article). **Halven Sere** knows the prayer and can teach it. Knight-Commander Theran Valegate's prayer book (inside **AO-C05**, if the party has already opened his tomb) contains a written copy
*   **Partial knowledge:** Speaking fewer than six lines activates the corresponding pairs of knight figures but the lock does not open. The glow fades after one minute. This is the clue: the party sees the mechanism responding and understands they need the complete sequence. Partial activation is not punished — the door simply remains shut
*   **Wrong words or hostile intent:** Speaking the Elder Elemental God's invocations at the door triggers a defensive response — the iron fist clenches tighter, and a pulse of radiant energy fills the passage (2d6 radiant damage, WIS DC 14 to halve, 10-foot radius). The door remains sealed
*   **Brute force:** The door is consecrated stone — AC 20, HP 100, immunity to non-magical damage, resistance to all magical damage except radiant. The duergar could theoretically break through with adamantine tools in approximately 48 hours of continuous work. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) judged this impractical

**Visual signature:** Carved knight procession on stone door, iron fist-and-lightning lock, pairs of figures that light up in sequence. Puzzle door. Players will immediately recognize this as a "speak the password" mechanic.

##### 5\. The Warden's Threshold (East Wall, South Section → AO-C08)

Ninety feet south of the entrance arch — nearly at the gallery's far end, opposite the Arch of First Oath — a low doorway opens in the east wall. The doorway is smaller than the other passages: five feet wide, six feet tall, framed in stone that is visibly older than the surrounding gallery walls. The lintel is carved with a repeating geometric pattern of interlocked lightning bolts — not decorative, but **structural**, each lightning bolt linking to the next in an unbroken chain that runs the full width of the doorframe.

The doorway was originally an unobstructed passage to the Ossuary of the Faithful — the communal bone repository where common soldiers and lay brothers were interred. It was never intended to be warded, because the ossuary held no consecrated individual burials. But the doorframe itself is consecrated — the interlocked lightning bolts form a containment ward that prevents undead from crossing the threshold in either direction. This was a precaution, not a barrier: the masons built it to ensure that if anything stirred in the ossuary's mass remains, it could not reach the gallery.

The duergar sealed the doorway early in the occupation. Three skeletons rose and attacked a work crew that strayed too close. **Grimlok Ashpick** ordered a rubble wall built across the opening — rough-stacked limestone blocks and packed clay, five feet thick, filling the doorway completely. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) approved. The ossuary had no strategic value. The wall went up. The skeletons were forgotten.

The rubble wall has since been breached — not from the gallery side, but from behind. Ghouls that entered through the Collapsed Passage (**AO-C11**) and the natural caves beyond have pushed through the rubble, creating a gap large enough for a Medium creature to squeeze through (Athletics DC 10) or widen in five minutes of work. The gap is visible from the gallery if the party investigates — a dark hole in the rubble, roughly three feet across, with claw marks on the displaced stones and a smell of rot and ammonia drifting through.

**Ward Mechanics — The Barrier Gate:**

*   **The consecrated doorframe:** Still functional. The interlocked lightning bolts pulse faintly when undead are within 30 feet on either side — a visual warning system. Undead cannot cross the threshold in either direction. The restless skeletons in the ossuary are trapped beyond this doorframe. The ghouls squeezed through the rubble gap but cannot pass the doorframe stones above and below the gap — they are confined to the rubble breach itself. This is why the ghouls have not spread into the gallery
*   **The rubble wall:** Physical barrier, not divine. The party can widen the gap (5 minutes of work, or STR DC 14 to force a section quickly but loudly). Clearing the rubble entirely takes 30 minutes and creates significant noise (Perception DC 8 from anywhere in the gallery)
*   **Passing through:** The doorframe ward does not impede living creatures. The party can squeeze through the ghoul gap or clear the rubble and walk through freely. The ward is one-directional for undead — it prevents them from entering _or_ leaving. Characters carrying a holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) cause the lightning-bolt pattern to flare bright blue as they pass — a welcoming pulse, not a barrier
*   **Tactical value:** The doorframe is a chokepoint. If the party fights undead in the ossuary and retreats through the doorway, the undead cannot follow. If the party needs to prevent ghouls from reaching the gallery, re-sealing the rubble gap restores the containment. The original builders planned for this

**Visual signature:** Low doorway, interlocked lightning-bolt frame, rubble wall with ghoul-clawed gap, faint pulse when undead are near. Utilitarian. A warning, not a welcome.

##### 6\. The Collapsed Side-Passage (West Wall, Mid-Section → AO-C11)

Sixty feet south of the entrance — in the gallery's middle section where the ward-glow transitions from flickering to steady — a narrow side-passage branches southwest through the west wall. The passage is partially concealed: it was originally a storage access corridor, not a tomb entrance, and the doorway is set back in an alcove between two burial recesses. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has not explored it. The duergar have marked the entrance with an iron piton bearing a strip of hide inscribed with a single Dwarvish rune: _"Death."_

The passage beyond is partially collapsed. Rubble chokes the first thirty feet. The ceiling is cracked and unstable — loose stone has fallen, and the timber supports that the original builders installed have rotted to splinters. A draft of cool, damp air flows from the passage, carrying the smell of rot and ammonia — the carrion crawler nest in **AO-C12** beyond. Faint web-traces on the ceiling near the passage entrance (Perception DC 16) indicate the phase spider uses this route when hunting in the gallery.

There is no divine ward on this passage. It was a storage corridor — never consecrated, never part of the burial system. The only barrier is the physical collapse and whatever lives on the other side.

**Visual signature:** Half-hidden doorway, duergar death-rune, rubble, bad smell, web-traces on the ceiling. Every instinct says "do not enter." The passage is optional — the party can ignore it entirely, but the creature threats from Zone E will continue via the random encounter table until the nest is cleared.

##### 7\. The Excavation Descent (Gallery Floor, South End → AO-C13)

At the gallery's southern terminus — the last ten feet of corridor before the original blank wall — the flagstone floor has been broken through. A ragged hole, approximately five feet square, opens in the ground where the duergar cut through the gallery floor to begin their excavation tunnel. A wooden ladder descends ten feet to the tunnel's upper end. Stone debris from the cutting is piled beside the opening. The edges of the hole are rough — the duergar used copper chisels, not iron, because [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) insisted that iron tools near the geomantic boundary would interfere with his tremorsense readings.

The hole is obvious. No concealment was attempted. It is a work site, not a secret passage. The wooden ladder is sturdy — duergar construction, rated for heavy loads. A crude track system (wooden rails and a small rubble cart) is visible at the bottom of the ladder, extending south into the tunnel's darkness.

Looking down through the hole, the party sees the tunnel angling downward at approximately twenty degrees — five feet wide, six feet high, timber-shored, lit by iron-reinforced lanterns at every other support frame. The tunnel extends eighty feet before leveling out at the Flan Antechamber far below. The air rising from the hole is colder than the gallery — 45°F and dropping. It smells of iron and ozone and mineral dust. The geomantic vibration is stronger here — not just felt in the bones anymore but audible as a low, rhythmic hum. One beat per minute. The earth's heartbeat.

The excavation hole sits in the gallery's brightest section — the founding-era wards blaze on either side of it, and the blue-white glow falls into the hole like light pouring into a drain. The juxtaposition is jarring: the most sacred section of the gallery, the strongest wards, the purest light — and in the middle of it, a raw wound in the floor where the duergar punched through six centuries of consecrated stone to reach what lies beneath.

There is no ward on the hole itself. The wards protect the alcoves and the passage entrances — the floor between them was consecrated by the processional prayer inscription, but the duergar cut through the inscription when they made the hole. The prayer line is severed. The floor ward is broken at this point. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) knows this. It is one of the reasons the overall ward system is weakening — every broken element reduces the network's total integrity.

**Visual signature:** Ragged hole in glowing floor, ladder descending into darkness, cart tracks, cold air rising, the sound of something breathing below. The wound in the sacred ground. The reason the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is here.

### The Floor Inscription

A processional prayer is carved into the flagstone centerline, running the full 120-foot length of the gallery — a continuation of the liturgical text that begins on the walls of **AO-22** above and on the lintel of **AO-C01**. The inscription is carved in Old Oeridian, the letters shallow but precise, following the exact center of the corridor.

**Investigation DC 14** to notice the inscription in the dark north section (the letters are worn and filled with dust). The inscription is impossible to miss in the south section — the letters glow with the same blue-white radiance as the alcove slabs.

The floor inscription is part of the ward network. It serves as a **prayer conduit** — a physical channel that connects the Processional Ward spoken in **AO-22** to the ward-gates throughout the gallery. When the six-line Processional Ward is recited aloud in **AO-22**, the effect travels down the staircase, through the landing, and along this inscription, reinforcing every ward it passes. The floor inscription is the nervous system of the crypt level's divine architecture.

The duergar's excavation hole at the south end has severed the inscription. The prayer conduit is broken at that point — effects from the Processional Ward no longer reach the ward-gates beyond the hole. This is a recent development. It is accelerating the overall ward degradation. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is aware of this and considers it an acceptable cost of reaching the Black Mouth.

**Repairing the inscription:** A character proficient in mason's tools or Religion (DC 15) who spends 1 hour can re-carve the severed section of the prayer line, reconnecting the conduit across the excavation hole. This requires the character to know the Old Oeridian text — available from the inscriptions in **AO-22**, from **Halven Sere**, or from Theran Valegate's prayer book in **AO-C05**. Restoring the conduit allows the Processional Ward spoken above to reach the full ward network again, strengthening all wards by one tier for 24 hours (recent wards → middle strength, middle wards → founding strength).

### The Alcoves: What the Party Finds

##### Breached Alcoves (North Section — ~12 Total)

The opened alcoves tell the story of the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md)'s looting in forensic detail. Each breached alcove shows the same pattern: the stone slab pried off or smashed, the burial shroud disturbed or removed, the grave goods taken. What remains is evidence.

*   **Investigation DC 10:** Tool marks on the alcove edges — pry-bar gouges, hammer strikes. The work was methodical. Each alcove was opened in sequence, north to south, suggesting a systematic operation directed by someone who understood the burial chronology
*   **Investigation DC 13:** The bodies themselves were not destroyed. The shrouds were pulled aside, the grave goods removed, and the shrouds replaced — roughly, without care, but replaced. [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) ordered this. He wanted the relics, not the dead. Disturbing the bodies more than necessary risked triggering secondary wards that he could not predict
*   **Investigation DC 16:** One alcove near the boundary between breached and sealed sections shows a different pattern. The slab is cracked but not removed — half-pried, then abandoned. The pry bar is still wedged in the gap. Beyond the crack, the blue ward-glow is visible. This is where the looting stopped. This is where the wards held. A character who examines the wedged pry bar notices that the iron is discolored — heat-blued, as though the metal was briefly exposed to extreme radiant heat. The acolyte who held it almost certainly burned his hands

##### Sealed Alcoves (Middle and South Sections — ~50+ Remaining)

The sealed alcoves are intact — stone slabs flush to the wall, inscriptions legible, the blue ward-glow visible. Each slab bears the following information, readable with Investigation DC 10 and Religion DC 10 (Old Oeridian):

*   The occupant's name and rank
*   Dates of service (year of oath, year of death)
*   A short dedication inscription — typically one sentence, specific to the individual
*   The Azure Oath's sigil (fist-and-lightning) or, for officers, a personal heraldic device

The party can read these slabs as they walk. Each one is a miniature biography. The DM should prepare 4–6 sample inscriptions to read aloud as the party explores (see sidebar for examples). The cumulative effect should be a roster of lives — not legends, but working warriors who served, fought, and died on the Long Road over five centuries.

**Touching a sealed slab:** The ward responds. The middle-era slabs feel warm — distinctly warmer than the surrounding stone. Characters with divine magic or senses feel a pulse of recognition, like a sleeping guard stirring at a familiar footstep. The founding-era slabs are hot — uncomfortable to hold a bare hand against for more than a few seconds. The heat is divine, not thermal — it does not damage, but it communicates _strength_. Characters who touch a founding-era slab feel an echo of the oath that was spoken when the knight was interred: a wordless sensation of commitment, duty, and refusal to yield. The experience is visceral. Some players will find it moving.

### Sacred Layer

The gallery is the sacred core of the crypt level — the spine that connects every room, the conduit that carries the ward system, the corridor that held five centuries of funeral processions. Every knight who died in service to the Azure Oath was carried through this corridor. Every prayer spoken over the dead echoed from this barrel vault. Every tomb slab was sealed with a whispered oath in this stone hallway.

The desecration is precise and revealing. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) could not desecrate what they could not reach. The north section — the weakest wards, the most recent dead — was looted systematically. The middle and south sections resisted. The result is a gallery that is desecrated at one end and sacred at the other, with a visible gradient between them. The party walks from ruin to radiance in 120 feet. The contrast is the point. This is what the wards protect. This is what happens when they fail. And somewhere above, an old man is praying to keep the light burning, and the Earth Temple is trying to make him stop.

### DM Notes

##### Running the Gallery

The gallery is not a single encounter — it is a **base of operations**. The party will return here repeatedly as they explore the crypt level's zones. Each time they return, describe the gallery differently based on what has changed:

*   If the party has lit the eternal flame sconces in **AO-22**, the gallery's north section is faintly illuminated by reflected sacred light from the staircase
*   If the party has restored the guardian in **AO-22**, the ward-glow in the middle section brightens slightly
*   If the party has repaired the floor inscription, the south section blazes brighter and the ward-gates visibly strengthen
*   If **Halven Sere** has been killed or removed from the priory, describe the glow dimming throughout the gallery — a slow, visible decline that accelerates over 24 hours until the wards fail entirely
*   If the party has lit the brazier in **AO-C05**, the entire gallery is illuminated — every ward at maximum, every alcove blazing, the ceiling vault rippling with blue-white light like the inside of a star

##### The Ward System as Game Design

The ward-gates are designed as an Elder Scrolls-style progression system. The party learns the visual language (glow = ward = barrier), discovers the keys through exploration and NPC interaction (Halven's symbol, the Processional Ward prayer, brute force for the rubble wall), and earns access to increasingly sacred and rewarding zones. Each key rewards different playstyles:

*   **The Arch of First Oath (Faith Gate):** Rewards NPC investment — rescuing Halven, recovering his holy symbol, showing respect to the dead. Roleplay key
*   **The Seal of the Champions (Knowledge Gate):** Rewards exploration — reading the wall inscriptions in **AO-22**, studying the prayer sequence, understanding the liturgy. Puzzle key
*   **The Warden's Threshold (Barrier Gate):** Rewards combat readiness — clearing the rubble, fighting through the ossuary, dealing with the undead. Physical key
*   **The Excavation Descent (Open Wound):** Always accessible — but leads to the most dangerous zone. Risk key

The party does not need to open all gates to complete the crypt level. But each gate opened provides strategic advantages, sacred rewards, and campaign intelligence that makes the climactic encounter at the Black Mouth easier to handle.

##### Halven Sere in the Gallery

If **Halven Sere** accompanies the party into the gallery, his reactions provide essential guidance:

*   **North section (looted):** He goes silent. He touches the empty alcoves. He reads the defaced names aloud, quietly, as though reciting a roll call. He does not weep — he has no tears left for this. His voice is flat
*   **Middle section (flickering wards):** He relaxes slightly. He presses his palms against the sealed slabs and closes his eyes. _"They are still here. They are still sleeping."_ He explains the ward system if asked — the glow, the warmth, the prayer conduit. He knows every name on every slab
*   **South section (blazing wards):** He stops walking. He kneels. He places both hands flat on the glowing flagstone floor and prays — not a request, but a communion. The ward-glow brightens perceptibly while he prays. When he finishes, his eyes are shining. _"It still holds. After everything. It still holds."_
*   **The Arch of First Oath:** He places his palm on the keystone and speaks the first commander's name. The ward drops. He turns to the party: _"This is where we began. Be worthy of it."_
*   **The excavation hole:** He stares into it for a long time. The glow around him dims. His face changes — the first real anger the party has seen from him. _"He cut through the prayer. He cut through the prayer and he does not even know what he broke."_

### Encounter: The Gallery Patrol

The gallery has no permanent occupants, but it is not empty. The following creatures and NPCs use the gallery regularly:

*   **Duergar work crews (2–4 miners):** Transit between **AO-C01** and the excavation tunnel at the south end. They walk the gallery's center line, lanterns hooded, tools over their shoulders, speaking in low Undercommon. They do not stop in the middle or south sections — **Grimlok**'s orders. They ignore the sealed alcoves. The ward-glow makes them uncomfortable
*   **Earth acolyte runners (1–2):** Carry messages between the main level and the excavation. Less disciplined than the duergar — they sometimes pause to examine the sealed alcoves or touch the ward-glow out of curiosity. A lone acolyte caught in the gallery is a potential interrogation target
*   **Ghoul scouts (1–2):** Occasionally emerge from the rubble breach at the Ossuary doorway and venture into the south gallery. They are drawn by the vibration from below and repelled by the ward-glow. They skirt the edges of the corridor, staying in the shadows between alcove rows. Perception DC 15 to spot them before they spot the party
*   **Ward flickers:** Not a creature — an event. Periodically (DM's discretion or on a random encounter result), the ward-glow in a section of the gallery dims sharply, then surges back. During the dim, a sound is audible from inside the sealed alcoves — the scrape of stone on stone, a faint rattle of bones, the shift of a body in a shroud. The dead stir when the wards weaken. They do not rise. Not yet. But they are not entirely at rest
