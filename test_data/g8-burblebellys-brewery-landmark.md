G8 Burblebelly’s Brewery
========================

##### **A Gem of the Gnomesberg Gnome Quarter**

_“A Good Ale Keeps a City Honest… and a Quarter United.”_

Burblebelly’s is a generational brewery tunneled deep into a traditional gnomish mound: five chambers of heart-wood casks and four chambers of bottle vaults—some bottles laid down decades ago by Burblebelly’s forebears. Ingredients come from Kron Hills farmers and traders, binding the brewery to the region’s rural network and politics.

In play, this location is not a tavern—it’s a source, a supply chain, and a status marker. Where The Rusty Nail and Brass Rail serve the quarter’s social life, Burblebelly’s quietly controls what those taps can pour.

### Approach Burblebelly’s Brewery

The round door yieldeth to a breath of cool air and sweet grain. Beyond, the burrow widens into a ribbed hall of timber and stone, where massive heart-wood casks stand like sleeping giants, their hoops dark with age and their staves fragrant with ale.  

### What’s Here (Short, Play-Useful)

##### The Cask Chambers (5)

*   Huge **heart-wood casks** aging ales (cool, stable temperature)
*   A narrow tasting bench where Burblebelly samples with a thief’s caution
*   Stamped cask seals and chalk marks—batch, date, valley origin

##### The Bottle Vaults (4)

*   Long-term wine storage, bottles laid down by prior generations
*   A ledger shelf (meticulous records; Burblebelly’s pride)
*   A “quiet corner” where Alliance folk speak softly and leave quickly

### A Deeply Rooted Tradition

Burblebelly’s Brewery is more than just a place to purchase alcohol; it is a legacy. The brewery is tunneled deep into a gnomish burrow, featuring five chambers filled with large heart-wood casks and four more chambers storing countless bottles of wine, aged for decades by Burblebelly’s grandfather and father.

##### Key Features

*   **Location:** Deep within a traditional gnomish mound
*   **Casks and Storage:** Five chambers of heart-wood casks and four chambers of aged wine bottles
*   **Specialty:** Award-winning wines and ales, including a local favorite, the berry ale

* * *

#### Free Alliance of the Kron Hills Connection

Burblebelly is openly engaged in gnome quarter politics and supports policies that protect local farms and gnome autonomy. His supply relationships with Kron Hills farmers and traders make him a quiet pillar of the Alliance’s economic backbone.

**What that means at the table:**

*   Alliance couriers can use deliveries as cover.
*   A boycott by Burblebelly’s _hurts_ the wrong people fast—so it’s a last resort threat.
*   If the party earns Burblebelly’s respect, they earn **credible introductions** in Gnomesberg.

* * *

#### Politics of Verbobonc

_Navigating Gnome Politics_

Burblebelly is actively involved in the politics of the Gnome Quarter and the Free Assembly of Gnomes. He advocates for the autonomy of the gnomish community and supports policies that protect local businesses from external pressures.

##### Political Engagement

*   **Gnome Quarter:** Supports local autonomy
*   [Free Alliance of the Kron Hills](free-alliance-of-the-kron-hills-organization.md): Advocates for gnome interests
*   **Economic Policies:** Protects local farms

### Adventure Hooks

(Brewery-Scale, Not Tavern-Scale)

##### 1) The Missing Cask

A cask marked for a respectable Gnomesberg buyer vanishes between vault and wagon. Burblebelly refuses to accuse anyone publicly—yet. He asks the party to trace it quietly before rumors become a weapon.

##### 2) Sabotage in the Seal-Wax

A batch seal is altered—subtle enough that only a meticulous eye would catch it. Burblebelly suspects someone wants to discredit him (and, by extension, the Alliance’s credibility).

##### 3) The “Friendly” Buyer

A new buyer offers generous coin for repeat bulk orders and asks too many questions about delivery routes and storage. Burblebelly smells logistics, not thirst.

* * *

#### Rewards

*   A stamped **“Cask-Token”** (a wooden disk) that grants the party respect at gnome taps that carry Burblebelly product
*   Access to one **delivery ledger line** (a date, a wagon, a destination)
*   A **Free Alliance introduction**—if the party solves a problem without embarrassing the quarter

##### **Burblebelly** (Owner & Master Brewer)

Stout, rosy-cheeked, immaculately kept, and fiercely proud of the family craft. He is polite—but he will not be hurried, and he will not be cheated.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Sniffs a cork like a priest sniffing incense.
*   Speaks of ingredients the way knights speak of lineage.
*   Treats sloppy coin and sloppy stories the same: with quiet contempt.
*   **Motivation:** Upholding the family legacy of fine brewing
