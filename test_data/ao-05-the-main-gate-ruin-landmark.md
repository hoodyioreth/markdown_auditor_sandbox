AO-05. The Main Gate Ruin
=========================

_Sepulchre-Priory of the Azure Oath – Outer Wall Access_

* * *

The gate was once a symbol of sacred welcome — an arched threshold through which knights of Heironeous rode beneath banners of azure and white. It now stands as a scar upon the ridge.

The heavy stone arch remains intact, though scorched and blackened from the assault of 576 CY. The original oaken doors were burned away during the fall of the priory. In their place stands a brutal, functional replacement — thick timbers lashed together and reinforced with iron bands hammered in haste rather than craft. The new gate does not belong here. It feels wrong in the old stone.

Above the arch, chiseled into the weathered rock, is the triangular glyph of the Black Earth — not subtle, not hidden. A declaration. The sanctified threshold of Heironeous now bears the mark of his enemy.

This is no ruin left unattended. It is a fortified choke point.

#### Physical Description

The arch itself is ancient — cut stone blocks fitted with disciplined masonry from an earlier century. The keystone bears faint lightning motifs, now cracked and defaced.

The current gate:

*   Thick planked wood, roughly cut
*   Crude iron bands riveted across the exterior
*   Chain and crossbar reinforcement on the interior
*   Earth Temple triangular glyph carved above arch (deep gouged, recent work)

Two partial wall collapses flank the gate — jagged stone ribs exposed where outer facing stones have fallen away. The breaches are climbable but steep and unstable.

* * *

##### Guard Presence

The Earth Temple does not leave this entrance unwatched.

**At All Times:**

*   2 Black Earth Guards stationed at gate
*   1 Raider assigned messenger rotation
*   Gnolls roam intermittently (no fixed post, but present within courtyard or perimeter)

The guards stand just inside the gate during daylight, stepping into the archway shadows when necessary. At night, torches burn low in iron sconces.

The Raider rotates between:

*   Main gate
*   Upper chapel
*   Courtyard barracks
*   Tharvok’s chamber

He serves as runner and signal relay.

* * *

#### Defensive Features

This is not a decorative ruin — it is an engineered choke point.

**Structural Advantages:**

*   Narrow murder slit above gate (former monk watch position, now Black Earth sentry nest)
*   Black Earth sentry positioned above entry during high alert
*   Heavy stone block rigged just inside as drop barrier (requires two guards to release)
*   Loose gravel spill zones placed just inside entry to destabilize charge or mounted entry

If the stone block is dropped:

*   DC 15 Dex save or take 2d10 bludgeoning and knocked prone
*   Gate becomes fully sealed from interior side

The gravel zones:

*   Count as difficult terrain
*   DC 12 Dex save when dashing or charging or fall prone

The slit darkens as both figures withdraw. For half a heartbeat, there is silence. Then a sharp blast splits the air — the horn’s call rolling across the ridge and down into the valley below. Stone grinds against stone from within. The gate is no longer just a door. It is a barrier.

* * *

#### Signs of Occupation

A Ranger, Rogue, or attentive character may notice:

*   Boot patterns concentrated in inward-facing arcs (guards patrol inward)
*   Gnoll claw scrapes along lower stone blocks
*   Fresh iron rivets compared to ancient stonework
*   Soot and pitch residue from the 576 CY burn
*   Earth Temple glyph carving less than a year old

**Skill Checks**

*   DC 13 Survival: Identify multiple species traffic (human, gnoll, warhorse)
*   DC 15 Investigation: Determine drop barrier mechanism
*   DC 12 Perception: Spot murder slit shadow movement

* * *

#### If Combat Breaks Out

This location is designed to delay, not win.

**Reinforcement Timeline**

*   Round 1: Horn sounded
*   Round 2: 4 Black Earth Guards mobilize from courtyard
*   Round 3: Full courtyard alert (including gnolls if nearby)
*   Round 4+: Tharvok or Maris responds depending on presence

The signal horn echoes through the valley. Sound carries nearly a mile on still air.

If the horn sounds, stealth approaches to other sections of the priory are compromised.

* * *

#### Tactical Notes for the Dungeon Master

*   This is a pressure valve encounter.
*   If players attempt frontal assault, expect layered defense.
*   Earth Temple forces fight to delay, not to die.
*   If overwhelmed, one guard will attempt to flee inward to alert command.

This gate is meant to feel oppressive — a visible sign that the priory is no longer abandoned ruin, but an organized Earth Temple forward stronghold.

##### Forcing the Gate

You set your weight against the rough timber, iron bands biting into your palms. The gate groans beneath the pressure — thick wood strained against stone. Dust sifts from the archway above as the hinges protest.  
  
Then — A scrape of metal overhead.  
  
From the narrow slit above the arch, a shadow shifts. A pair of dark eyes stare down at you through the stone. A voice follows — low, hard, unhurried. _“Step back from the gate.”  
  
_Another shape moves beside the first. You hear the unmistakable sound of a spear shaft sliding into position. _“You’ve climbed high for your graves.”_

##### If the Party Continues Forcing the Gate

The wood shudders again under your effort. Above you, the second voice speaks — closer now, more force behind it.  
  
_“Horn?”_ A pause. _“Not yet.”  
  
_A dull thud sounds from within — something heavy dragged into place behind the gate. Gravel shifts just beyond the threshold. _“Last warning,”_ the first voice calls down. _“Turn and walk down the hill. Or we drop it.”_
