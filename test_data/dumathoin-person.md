Dumathoin
=========

**Dumathoin, god of Buried secrets, wealth, mining, gems, exploration  
**_Keeper of Secrets Under the Mountain, the Silent Keeper, the Mountain Shield_  

##### Guardian of the Mountain Dwarves and Hidden Riches

Dumathoin, known as the Silent Keeper, is a revered figure in the dwarven pantheon, serving as the patron deity of mountain dwarves, mining, and underground exploration. His divine mandate includes the protection of the dwarven dead and the hidden treasures of the earth. Characterized by his silence and secretive nature, Dumathoin embodies the principles of preservation, discovery, and the respectful utilization of natural resources.

##### Key Attributes

*   **Patronage:** Mountain dwarves, mining, underground exploration.
*   **Symbol:** Two-handed mattock made from solidified magma.
*   **Disposition:** Silent, communicates non-verbally, protective of the earth’s riches.

#### Description of Dumathoin

Dumathoin presents as a formidable dwarf with a robust build, his appearance echoing the elements of the underground world he protects. His hair and beard resemble sculpted gray stone, and his earth-brown skin and eyes of silvery flame symbolize his deep connection to the mineral wealth of the world.

##### Physical Characteristics

*   **Appearance:** Gigantic male dwarf with a barrel chest, stone-like hair and beard.
*   **Attire:** Typically depicted with a two-handed mattock, symbolizing his domain over mining and excavation.

#### Historical and Mythological Role

Dumathoin's historical significance among the dwarves is profound. Appointed by Moradin as the protector of mountain dwarves, he initially resented the mining of mountains but grew to appreciate the exquisite metalwork produced by his followers. His lore is filled with tales of concealing valuable mineral deposits across the world, intending them as rewards for the worthy.

##### Historical Contributions

*   **Creation of Paradise:** Crafted a paradise for mountain dwarves, which later became mining sites.
*   **Protector of Secrets:** Known for hiding precious metals and gems, revealing them only to those he deems deserving.

#### Relationships Within the Pantheon

Dumathoin's interactions with other deities range from brotherly rivalry to strategic alliances, shaping his role within the divine hierarchy.

##### Divine Relations

*   **Allies:** Allied with deities like Calladuran Smoothhands and Cyrrollalee who share aspects of his domain.
*   **Rivals:** His brother Abbathor, who envies Dumathoin's role and abilities.
*   **Adversaries:** Opposes deities such as Abbathor and Laduguer, as well as gods of destructive races like goblins and orcs.

#### Realm: Deepshaft Hall

Dumathoin's divine realm, Deepshaft Hall, is situated in the Dwarven Mountain on the Outlands, marked by its deep, icy, and maze-like mineshafts. This realm is both a sanctuary for his followers and a labyrinth guarding his sacred secrets.

##### Characteristics of Deepshaft Hall

*   **Environment:** Dark, icy, with a stale smell, reflective of the deep underground.
*   **Soul Gems:** Utilized by petitioners to connect with the deity and the realm.
*   **Interactions with Other Realms:** Overlaps with the realm of Ilsensine, creating complex divine and follower dynamics.

#### Dogma and Worship

Dumathoin’s dogma emphasizes the ethical extraction and appreciation of the earth’s riches. He promotes careful and considerate mining practices, the artistic enhancement of discovered minerals, and a staunch opposition to greed and the undead.

##### Core Tenets

*   **Respect for Nature:** Advocates for responsible mining and the beautification of extracted resources.
*   **Opposition to Greed:** Teaches that the true value of treasures lies in their discovery and craftsmanship, not in accumulation.
*   **Sanctity of Tombs:** Stresses the importance of maintaining the sanctity of dwarven tombs and opposing undead desecration.

### Worshipers of Dumathoin

Dumathoin, known as the Keeper of Secrets Under the Mountain, is predominantly worshiped by mountain dwarves, especially those residing in regions rich in mineral deposits like the Stark Mounds. His followers see him as the divine protector of both their livelihood and their legacy, guiding them to unearth the riches of the earth while maintaining the sanctity of the underground.

##### Characteristics of Worshipers

*   **Mountain Dwarves:** Primarily worshipers due to their close association with mining.
*   **Regions of Worship:** Most prevalent in mountainous areas rich in minerals, like the Stark Mounds.
*   **Values:** Emphasize ethical mining, respect for nature, and conservation of underground beauty.

### Clergy of Dumathoin

The clergy of Dumathoin play multiple roles within dwarven society, from supervising mining operations to ensuring the safety and sanctity of subterranean environments. They are easily recognized by their distinctive attire and their uncut hair and beards, symbolizing their commitment to natural preservation.

##### Role and Attire of the Clergy

*   **Dress Code:** Brown leather outfits with earth-brown cloaks and robes, untrimmed hair and beards.
*   **Weapons:** Favor the warhammer or military pick, reflecting their connection to mining.
*   **Duties:** Supervise mining, ensure subterranean safety, protect tombs, and facilitate subterranean agriculture.

#### Holy Days: The Deepstone Triad

Dumathoin’s followers observe holy days known as the Deepstone Triad, which coincide with the new moons. These days are considered particularly sacred as they symbolize the hidden treasures of the earth being closest to the surface, ready to be discovered by the worthy.

##### Observances During the Deepstone Triad

*   **Timing:** Occurs on the nights of the new moons and the days immediately before and after.
*   **Significance:** Represents the moons hiding beneath the earth, a metaphor for hidden treasures.

#### Rituals of Dumathoin

The rituals performed by Dumathoin’s followers reflect their reverence for the earth and its hidden wealth. These rituals are designed to honor Dumathoin without disturbing the tranquility he embodies.

##### Key Ritual Practices

**Gem Sacrifices:** During the Deepstone Triad, gems and jewels are offered, ground into powder mixed with herbs and fungi as fertilizer for subterranean plants. **Funerary Rites:** The dwarven dead are prepared with stone tokens in their beards and dressed in armor, with silent songs composed to honor their deeds.

#### Myths and Legends of Dumathoin

Dumathoin’s myths emphasize his role as the silent custodian of the earth’s riches. He is depicted as carefully placing veins of precious minerals where they can eventually benefit his followers, guiding the worthy to these hidden treasures.

##### Mythological Depictions

*   **Veins as Beards:** Some legends describe the mineral veins as the hairs of Dumathoin’s beard, symbolizing their precious and personal nature to him.
*   **Guidance to Treasures:** Believed to guide his followers to discover these veins when they are most needed and when the dwarves are deemed worthy.

  Dumathoin's worship centers on a profound respect for the natural world and a commitment to uncovering its secrets responsibly. His clergy and followers strive to balance the exploitation of these resources with the preservation of the underground landscapes they hold sacred.

##### Clergy Titles of Dumathoin

*   **Agate** - Represents the novices or initiates, new to the clerical path and still learning the foundational aspects of their faith.
*   **Onyx** - Clergy members who have demonstrated basic understanding and are entrusted with minor responsibilities.
*   **Amethyst** - Intermediate clerics who begin to take on more significant duties, often involved in the supervision of mining operations.
*   **Zircon** - Experienced members of the clergy who mentor younger clerics and oversee important ceremonial duties.
*   **Garnet** \- Senior clergy responsible for strategic planning and major decisions in temple operations.
*   **Topaz** - Higher echelon of the clergy, involved in the broader community engagement and regional activities.
*   **Opal** - Clerics who have achieved a deep connection with Dumathoin’s teachings, often serving as advisors to mining leaders.
*   **Sapphire** - Elite members of the clergy, directly involved in the most sacred rituals and preservation of holy sites.
*   **Diamond** - The highest clerical rank, leaders of the faith, custodians of the most profound secrets and treasures of Dumathoin.
