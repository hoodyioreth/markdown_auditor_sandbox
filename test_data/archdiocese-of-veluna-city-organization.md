Archdiocese of Veluna City
==========================

#### Overview

The Archdiocese of Veluna City is not large—but it is absolute.

Within its narrow bounds lieth the true heart of Veluna: not in field, nor mine, nor trade—but in authority.

Here dwelleth Canon Hazen, shepherd of the faithful and final voice in all matters of faith and state. From this city flow decrees that shape every diocese—from the far ridges of Lorrish to the fertile plains of Whitehale. Yet the Archdiocese ruleth not by force. It ruleth by recognition—by the quiet understanding that all power in Veluna must, in the end, pass through here.

#### Veluna City — The Seat of Power

Veluna City is a place of restraint and intention.

Unlike Devarnish’s noise or Valkurl’s ambition, this city is measured. Streets are clean. Voices are low. Movements are watched—not by guards alone, but by habit, expectation, and unseen authority.

Temples, administrative halls, and residences of ranking clergy dominate the city. Noble houses maintain estates here, though none may claim true dominion. This is not a city of commerce. It is a city of decision.

#### The Archclericy of Veluna

The Archdiocese existeth as the physical seat of the Archclericy—the supreme religious authority of Veluna. From here, doctrine is shaped, bishops are guided, and the unity of the realm is preserved. Though each diocese governeth its own affairs, all remain bound—formally or otherwise—to the will expressed here.

Canon Hazen himself rarely interveneth directly. But when he doth… the matter is ended.

#### The Celestial Circle

Within the Archdiocese conveneth the Celestial Circle—the great council wherein secular and ecclesiastical power meet. Representatives from each diocese, noble houses, and influential bodies gather here to debate matters of trade, defense, doctrine, and law. Yet debate is not equality.

 

All know that beneath the Circle’s deliberations lieth a deeper truth: the Archclericy permits discourse—but retaineth judgment.

Thus, the Circle is both forum and instrument—its outcomes shaped as much by quiet influence as by open speech.

#### Government & Authority

The Archdiocese standeth apart from all others. It answereth to no duke, no bishop, no council—save that which resideth within its own walls.

Its authority manifesteth in three forms:

*   **Spiritual Authority:** Through the Archclericy and Church of Rao
*   **Political Authority:** Through the Celestial Circle and diplomatic control
*   **Moral Authority:** Through Canon Hazen himself

It commandeth little… yet directeth much.

#### Military & Defense

The Archdiocese maintaineth no standing army.

Such a force would imply division—something Veluna cannot afford at its heart. Instead, defense is entrusted to the Diocese of Grayington, whose forces stand ready should the need arise. Within the city itself, order is maintained by disciplined watch units and temple guards—more ceremonial than martial, yet far from weak. For though swords are few here… consequences are not.

#### Economy & Function

The Archdiocese produceth little—and needeth little. Its wealth floweth not from fields or mines, but from influence, tribute, and the steady movement of officials, envoys, and pilgrims.

*   Administrative centers and archives
*   Religious institutions and pilgrimage support
*   Diplomatic residences and noble estates

What is required is provided—by the dioceses that depend upon its stability.

#### Notable Locations

**The Hall of the Celestial Circle**

A chamber of polished stone and quiet voices, where the fate of Veluna is debated beneath vaulted ceilings and watchful eyes.

**The Canon’s Residence**

A place of simplicity and gravity; those who enter do not do so lightly—and fewer leave unchanged.

**Tannenbaum’s Chalice**

A refined inn of calm atmosphere, its halls adorned with symbols of Rao and its air filled with soft music and quiet conversation. It serveth as a meeting place for minor officials, envoys, and travelers of means.
