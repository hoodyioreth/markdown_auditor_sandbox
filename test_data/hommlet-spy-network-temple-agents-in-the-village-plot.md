Hommlet Spy Network — Temple Agents in the Village
==================================================

> Four Temple of Elemental Evil agents are embedded in the Village of Hommlet — Rannos Davl, Gremag Gozlin, Zert, and Joseph Millar — all reporting to a single handler: Lucius Graeme, the Greater Temple's courier and intelligence handler. Originally built to support Lareth the Beautiful's Moathouse operation, the spy network persists under Lucius's direct control even if Lareth falls. This article consolidates the full command chain, agent profiles, evidence trails, confrontation reactions with DCs, and network disruption consequences for the DM.

##### Summary

**Four Temple of Elemental Evil agents are embedded in the [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), all reporting to a single handler: **Lucius Graeme**.** Lucius is the Greater Temple's courier and intelligence handler. He recruited these agents, provides their operational direction, and evaluates their intelligence before forwarding it to **Hedrack**. The agents do not know each other's handler by the same chain — Lucius keeps them compartmented. But every thread in Hommlet leads back to him.

This network was originally built to support [Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful's [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) operation. If Lareth is killed or captured, the Hommlet spy network continues to operate under Lucius Graeme's direct control — the agents' loyalty is to the Greater Temple, not to Lareth personally.

 

### Chain of Command — Who Reports to Whom in Hommlet

The Hommlet spy network's chain of command runs through the Greater Temple hierarchy. Every piece of intelligence gathered by the four village agents flows upward through a single pipeline:

*   **Strategic authority:** **Hedrack** — Mouth of Iuz, supreme commander of the Greater Temple. Issues strategic directives. The Hommlet agents do not know his name or role
*   **Intelligence handler:** **Lucius Graeme** — Greater Temple courier and intelligence handler. Travels the [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)–Hommlet corridor. Directly manages all four Hommlet agents through an encrypted courier network. Evaluates intelligence and forwards actionable items to Hedrack. Carries orders back down to the agents
*   **Field commander (former):** [Lareth](lareth-person.md) the Beautiful — originally commanded the Moathouse operation that the spy network supported. Lareth's diary references the agents but does NOT name Lucius. If Lareth falls, the network persists under Lucius
*   **Courier relay:** [Gattas Void](gattas-void-person.md) — Lareth's lieutenant at the Moathouse. Carried intelligence between Lareth and Lucius. If the Moathouse falls, this relay is severed but Lucius maintains direct courier contact with the Hommlet agents

 

**Key fact for the Lore Query tool:** [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) reports to **Lucius Graeme**. [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md) reports to Lucius Graeme. [Zert](zert-person.md) reports to Lucius Graeme. [Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) reports to Rannos Davl and through him to Lucius Graeme. All four Hommlet agents report to Lucius Graeme. Lucius Graeme reports to Hedrack.

 

### The Four Hommlet Spies — Who They Are and What They Do

##### Agent 1: Rannos Davl — D&G Dry Goods (Intelligence & Smuggling)

[Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) is co-proprietor of [D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile](13-d26g-dry-goods-26-general-mercantile-landmark.md) in Hommlet. His cover is a slow, complacent merchant who provides essential goods to villagers and adventurers. His true role is intelligence gathering, smuggling coordination, and civilian recruitment for the Temple.

*   **Cover:** Unremarkable merchant. Friendly, laid-back, content. Nobody suspects him
*   **Handler:** **Lucius Graeme** — recruited Rannos, provides operational direction
*   **Communication:** Passes written reports to a brigand courier who carries them to Lucius along the Nulb–Hommlet corridor
*   **Functions:** Monitors adventurer activity. Reports village events to the Temple. Manages the shop's legitimate inventory to mask smuggling movements. First-contact recruiter for civilian sympathizers. Employs [Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) as a sub-agent
*   **Operational partner:** [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md) — Lucius considers Rannos and Gremag a single intelligence cell
*   **Weakness:** Rannos is the weaker link. Under pressure he will reveal the existence of his handler. He panics without Gremag

##### Agent 2: Gremag Gozlin — D&G Dry Goods (Intelligence & Enforcement)

[Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md) is co-proprietor of [D&G Dry Goods & General Mercantile](13-d26g-dry-goods-26-general-mercantile-landmark.md). His cover is a fussy, sharp-featured merchant who dithers over customers. His true role is the sharper half of the D&G intelligence cell — he handles the more dangerous intelligence work while Rannos provides commercial cover.

*   **Cover:** Fussy merchant with a comical demeanor. Sharp business acumen. Meticulous about inventory
*   **Handler:** **Lucius Graeme** — same courier network as Rannos
*   **Functions:** Gathers intelligence from adventurers under the guise of friendly conversation. Passes information to evil adventurers for a cut of their profits. Coordinates smuggling operations with Rannos
*   **Secret:** Gremag is a sociopathic serial killer. Once a month he stalks and murders someone, ritually cutting out their brains and eating them. Some villagers speculate a mind flayer is loose. Lucius is aware of this "hobby" and considers it a security liability — if Gremag is caught for the killings, the entire Hommlet spy network is compromised
*   **Weakness:** His serial killing is exploitable leverage. A party that discovers the murders can pressure Gremag without even knowing about his Temple connection

##### Agent 3: Zert — Inn of the Welcome Wench (Enforcer & Adventurer Surveillance)

[Zert](zert-person.md) is staying at the [Inn of the Welcome Wench](7-the-inn-of-the-welcome-wench-landmark.md) in Hommlet, posing as a mercenary caravan guard seeking adventuring companions. He is Lucius Graeme's enforcer in Hommlet — the only agent tasked with violence if needed.

*   **Cover:** Charismatic mercenary from Blackmoor. Eager to join adventuring parties. Buys rounds of ale and maps adventurers' exploits on the tavern table
*   **Handler:** **Lucius Graeme** — Zert receives orders through midnight courier visits (cultist in black cloak with Elder Elemental Eye symbol). Quest: [5\. Catching Zert in the Act](5-catching-zert-in-the-act---hommlet-plot.md)
*   **Functions:** Monitors adventurers at the inn. Interviews them over ale, drawing detailed maps of their dungeon explorations. Assesses threats to the Temple. Reports adventurer movements and plans to Lucius. Will betray and attempt to murder party members if ordered
*   **Special ability:** Double drinking capacity — cannot become drunk, giving him advantage in social settings where alcohol loosens tongues
*   **Hidden wealth:** 265 gp, 100 pp, and 10 flawed black pearls (each worth 100 gp). Hidden in his room at the inn
*   **Midnight note:** If the party follows Zert at night, they witness him receiving a message from a hooded cultist. The note reads: "The new adventurers are becoming a problem, inform G.R. to be ready to eliminate them on the usual sign. Look into recruiting the foreigner and his warrior friend further. -L." The "G.R." is [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md) and [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md). The "-L." is [Lareth](lareth-person.md)
*   **Weakness:** Zert does not know Rannos and Gremag are also Temple agents — only that "G.R." exists. Capture him and he cannot expose the D&G cell directly

##### Agent 4: Joseph Millar — Doomwatch Keep Construction (Saboteur)

[Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) is a laborer at the [Doomwatch Keep Construction Site](30-doomwatch-keep-construction-site-landmark.md) in Hommlet. He presents himself as a humble worker from Dyvers. His true role is sabotaging the keep's construction to prevent Hommlet from building fortifications that would threaten the Temple. Quest: [6\. Finding the Saboteur](6-finding-the-saboteur---hommlet-plot.md)

*   **Cover:** Laborer with a pointed cropped beard and slick hair. Lives in [Tent Town](32-tent-town-landmark.md) among other transient workers
*   **Employers:** Recruited by [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) and [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md). Reports through them to **Lucius Graeme**. Also has a separate link to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) through [Romag](romag-person.md) — a rare connection between Greater Temple and Earth Temple intelligence operations
*   **Functions:** Sabotages blueprints by introducing subtle errors. Delays construction through equipment tampering. Makes copies of defensive plans for Temple analysis. Meets cultist contacts at night in secluded areas around Hommlet or within Tent Town
*   **Future plans:** After the keep is completed, Joseph is slated to transition into a security role under Rannos Davl, maintaining long-term intelligence access
*   **Weakness:** Joseph's nighttime meetings are observable. Workers in Tent Town rotate frequently with supply shipments from Verbobonc — a sharp party can track which arrivals coincide with Joseph's movements

 

### How the Party Discovers the Hommlet Spy Network

The spy network is designed to be uncovered in stages. The party should not learn everything at once — each thread pulls toward the next.

##### Evidence Trail — What Leads Where

*   **Lareth's diary (Moathouse):** If the party clears the [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md) and recovers Lareth's diary, it implicates [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md), [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md), and [Zert](zert-person.md) as Temple agents. The diary does NOT name **Lucius Graeme**. It only references a "courier" who delivers Hedrack's orders
*   **Zert's midnight note:** Following [Zert](zert-person.md) at night yields the note referencing "G.R." and signed "-L." This confirms a Hommlet agent network but does not directly name Rannos, Gremag, or Lucius
*   **Catching Zert in the Act:** Quest [5\. Catching Zert in the Act](5-catching-zert-in-the-act---hommlet-plot.md) — the party witnesses Zert's cultist meeting. The cultist, if captured, knows only that he carries messages between Zert and "the New Master" at the Moathouse
*   **Finding the Saboteur:** Quest [6\. Finding the Saboteur](6-finding-the-saboteur---hommlet-plot.md) — investigating the Doomwatch construction delays leads to [Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md). Joseph's connection to [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) is discoverable through surveillance of his nightly meetings or by searching his belongings in Tent Town
*   **Connecting the courier to Lucius:** DC 18 Investigation to cross-reference courier descriptions from multiple sources (Lareth's diary, Zert's cultist contact, Rannos's reports) and identify **Lucius Graeme** as the common handler. This requires evidence from at least two separate agents

 

### Confronting the Hommlet Spies — Accusation and Reaction

**If the party accuses the Temple agents, each reacts differently based on their personality, training, and what they know about the party's evidence.**

##### Rannos Davl — Denial, Then Panic

*   **Initial reaction:** Flat denial. Feigns confusion and outrage. "I'm just a merchant! How dare you!"
*   **Deception check:** DC 12 Insight to see through the performance — Rannos is not a good liar
*   **Under pressure (DC 14 Intimidation):** Admits to having a "handler" who travels the road between [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) and Hommlet. Will name **Lucius Graeme** if pressed further (DC 16 Intimidation or DC 18 Persuasion). Does NOT know Lucius's connection to **Hedrack** or the Greater Temple hierarchy
*   **If confronted with Lareth's diary:** Breaks immediately. Will cooperate to save his own life. Reveals Gremag, the courier schedule, and the location of smuggled goods in the shop's back room
*   **Escape attempt:** If given time, Rannos will attempt to flee Hommlet within 6 hours. He will head north toward the Moathouse road, not south — he does not know any safe routes except the one the courier uses

##### Gremag Gozlin — Deception, Then Violence

*   **Initial reaction:** Dismissive and fussy. Acts insulted. Tries to talk his way out with elaborate lies
*   **Deception check:** DC 18 Insight to see through Gremag's lies — he is a practiced sociopath and an excellent deceiver
*   **Under pressure (DC 16 Intimidation):** Reveals the existence of his handler. Names **Lucius Graeme** under sustained pressure. Will try to bargain: "I can give you names if you let me walk"
*   **If cornered with no escape:** Gremag turns violent. He would rather fight than be captured — his serial killing secret (monthly brain-eating murders) means imprisonment leads to execution regardless of the Temple connection
*   **Exploitable leverage:** If the party discovers the serial murders independently and confronts Gremag with that evidence, he will cooperate on Temple intelligence to avoid being exposed as a killer. His fear of the villagers' justice for the murders exceeds his fear of the Temple

##### Zert — Betrayal, Then Combat

*   **Initial reaction:** Friendly deflection. "Me? A spy? Come on, friend, have another drink." Tries to isolate the accuser from the party
*   **Deception check:** DC 14 Insight — Zert is charming but not deeply deceptive
*   **If the party has evidence:** [Zert](zert-person.md) does not negotiate. He attacks. He fights to kill or to escape — whichever seems more achievable. His amber-glowing battleaxe and shield are always within arm's reach
*   **If captured alive (DC 16 Grapple or Restraint):** Zert knows less than the other agents. He knows "the New Master" at the Moathouse (Lareth), knows he receives orders via midnight couriers, and knows that "G.R." in Hommlet is also working for the Temple. He does NOT know Rannos and Gremag are "G.R." — only that the initials exist. He does NOT know Lucius by name
*   **Wealth seizure:** Searching Zert's room at the inn yields 265 gp, 100 pp, and 10 flawed black pearls worth 100 gp each — a substantial reward that also serves as evidence of Temple funding

##### Joseph Millar — Flight

*   **Initial reaction:** Joseph panics. He is not a fighter. He will attempt to flee immediately — out of [Tent Town](32-tent-town-landmark.md) and into the surrounding countryside
*   **Deception check:** DC 10 Insight — Joseph is a terrible liar and his fear is obvious
*   **If captured (DC 12 Athletics to run him down):** Joseph will cooperate immediately. He reveals his employers: [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) and [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md) at D&G Dry Goods. He knows they report to someone outside Hommlet but cannot name **Lucius Graeme**. He also reveals his connection to the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) through [Romag](romag-person.md) — a separate reporting line
*   **Valuable intelligence:** Joseph has copies of the Doomwatch Keep blueprints with his sabotage marks. These are physical evidence linking the Temple to active operations against Hommlet's defenses

 

### Disrupting the Hommlet Spy Network — Consequences by Agent

Each agent's removal has specific consequences for the network's function and for **Lucius Graeme**'s response.

*   **Rannos killed or captured:** Lucius loses his primary intelligence source in Hommlet. He redirects intelligence tasking to [Zert](zert-person.md) and [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md), increasing their operational tempo and making them more likely to be detected
*   **Gremag killed or captured:** [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) panics and attempts to flee Hommlet within 48 hours unless Lucius sends a courier ordering him to hold position. The D&G intelligence cell is functionally destroyed
*   **Both Rannos and Gremag eliminated:** Lucius falls back on Zert as his sole Hommlet intelligence source. The shop front is blown. Smuggling through [D&G Dry Goods](13-d26g-dry-goods-26-general-mercantile-landmark.md) ceases. [Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) is stranded without his employers and becomes increasingly reckless
*   **Zert killed or captured:** Lucius loses his enforcer and his eyes at the inn. He has no one to monitor adventurers' movements directly. If Lucius learns of Zert's death, he sends a warning to Rannos and Gremag to increase their caution
*   **Joseph killed or captured:** The Doomwatch Keep sabotage stops. Construction accelerates. Rannos loses his sub-agent but the D&G cell is otherwise unaffected
*   **All four agents eliminated:** Lucius's Hommlet network is destroyed. He reports the loss to **Hedrack** as a counterintelligence catastrophe. Hedrack may order Lucius to rebuild — or may decide Hommlet is no longer worth the investment. Either way, the Temple loses real-time intelligence on Hommlet for 2–4 weeks while Lucius scrambles to recruit replacements
*   **Lucius himself identified and captured:** The entire Greater Temple intelligence apparatus in the Hommlet–Nulb corridor collapses. Lucius knows the names and locations of every Temple agent in both villages, the courier routes, the Moathouse supply pipeline, and Hedrack's strategic priorities. This is the single most valuable intelligence capture the party can make in the campaign's first tier

 

### DM Notes: Running the Hommlet Spy Network

##### Pacing the Reveals

Do not dump all four agents on the party at once. The spy network is designed to unravel over multiple sessions:

*   **Session 1–2 (Hommlet arrival):** Zert approaches the party at the inn. He is friendly, buys drinks, asks about their plans. No suspicion yet
*   **Session 2–3 (Moathouse investigation):** If the party returns to Hommlet after the Moathouse, they have Lareth's diary. The diary names Rannos, Gremag, and Zert. Now the party has to decide: confront openly or investigate quietly?
*   **Session 3–5 (Investigation):** Following the evidence threads. Catching Zert at night. Investigating Doomwatch delays. Staking out D&G Dry Goods. Each investigation risks alerting the network
*   **Session 5+ (Confrontation):** The party makes their move. The order they confront agents matters — if Rannos is taken first, Gremag may flee. If Zert is taken first, the D&G cell is not immediately alerted

##### The Lucius Graeme Thread

**Lucius Graeme** should not be encountered in Hommlet unless the party specifically stakes out the courier route or captures an agent who names him. He is a traveling operative, not a resident. The party might encounter him on the road between Nulb and Hommlet, at the [Moathouse](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md), or in Nulb — never casually in the village. Finding Lucius requires deliberate investigation, not luck.

##### What Lareth's Diary Actually Says

The diary is the single most important piece of evidence for the Hommlet spy network. It implicates three agents by name or description but does NOT reveal the full command chain:

*   Names [Rannos Davl](rannos-davl-person.md) and [Gremag Gozlin](gremag-gozlin-person.md) as Temple agents at D&G Dry Goods
*   References [Zert](zert-person.md) as an agent at the inn
*   References a "courier" who carries orders from the Greater Temple — this is **Lucius Graeme**, but the diary does not name him
*   Does NOT mention [Joseph Millar](joseph-millar-person.md) — his sabotage operation is a separate thread
