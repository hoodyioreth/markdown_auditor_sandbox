Where to Start: 3orcs Campaign Guide for TOEE Epic Sandbox campaign
===================================================================

Hey, welcome aboard, and glad you’re looking at Greyhawk for your first big campaign!

Short version:  
**Yes, you absolutely can start in Greyhawk using the 2024 DMG**, and the [Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576cy](../../verbobonc-campaign-guide-576cy-stylinlp38.md) is meant to be a giant, organized DM notebook you can dip into as needed—not something you have to memorize cover-to-cover.

Think of it like this:

**Official books = rules.  
Verbobonc site = setting + NPCs + adventures.**

You run the _rules_ with the 2024 PHB/DMG/MM. You use my stuff for **where** you are, **who** lives there, and **what’s going wrong.**

* * *

### 1\. Where should a new DM start?

Start **small**:

*   One **home base** (a village or city district)
*   One **clear problem**
*   One **first adventure site** (dungeon/mission)

In this project, your easiest on-ramps are:

1.  **The** [Village of Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md) → then Nulb → then the Temple (classic T1–4 flow).
2.  **Verbobonc City** as the home base → then out to Hommlet / Nulb / the Temple once the players care about the region.

Both are valid. If you’re very new, I’d:

*   Start in **Hommlet** (small cast, local trouble).
*   Use **Verbobonc** and all my city material as the “big city” they travel to once they need serious allies, supplies, and politics.

If you want a more political, story-driven start, you can begin in Verbobonc instead (at [Jylee’s Inn](c3-jyleeE28099s-inn-landmark.md) or the [Adventurers Guild](b26-adventures-guild-landmark.md) ) and send them toward Hommlet/Nulb via a contract or noble patron.

* * *

### 2\. How do the 2024 rules fit with all this old stuff?

You don’t need special conversions to “make Greyhawk work” with 2024:

*   Treat **Temple of Elemental Evil**, Hommlet, etc. as _story and maps_.
*   When something calls for “orcs, ogres, trolls, cultists,” just use the **2024 versions** of those monsters.
*   Treasure, DCs, magic items: feel free to **scale up or down** to match your party’s level and what feels fun.

The **Temple** itself is quite deadly if you run it straight. Use the 2024 DMG tools for:

*   Adjusting encounter difficulty
*   Adding safety valves (retreat points, friendly NPCs, factions who might parley instead of fight to the death)

* * *

### 3\. How to use the Verbobonc site as a beginner

On the **Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576cy** World Anvil:

1.  Start with the **main landing page** (the “Gateway to Adventure” intro).
2.  Pick **one area** to actually use at the table first:
3.  Village of Hommlet, _or_
4.  A single **Verbobonc district** (High Quarter, Business Quarter, Civic Center) as their starting neighborhood.
5.  Only read:
6.  The **district overview**
7.  2–3 **key locations** you’ll actually put in that first session (a tavern, a temple, maybe one guild or noble house).

Everything else can wait. The site is built so **you can gradually pull more articles in** as the players explore and ask questions.

* * *

### 4\. What about “beginner modules”?

For a first campaign in this Greyhawk/TOEE framework, I’d suggest:

*   **“The Village of Hommlet” (T1)** as your first proper adventure site.
*   Use my **Verbobonc content** as:
*   Where their patron lives,
*   Where they go to resupply and level up,
*   Where bigger plots start to appear (Temple logistics, politics, cult agents, etc.).

If you want something gentler even than Hommlet, you can:

*   Start with a **small side-quest** pulled from the Verbobonc area (bandits on the road, a missing merchant, a minor temple problem),
*   Then have the clues naturally point toward “some trouble brewing around Hommlet and Nulb.”

* * *

### 5\. If you feel overwhelmed

Totally normal. Here’s the “safety rail” version:

1.  Pick **level 1–3**.
2.  Choose **Hommlet** as the starting village.
3.  Decide **who sends them** (a Verbobonc noble, a church, the Adventurers Guild—there are articles for all of these on the site).
4.  Run **only the first little slice**: a few Hommlet encounters, maybe one small dungeon.
5.  Between sessions, look up **one or two** extra Verbobonc articles at a time to deepen the world.

You don’t have to use _everything_ I’ve written. It’s a buffet, not homework.

* * *

For Experienced DMs / TOEE Veterans
-----------------------------------

Running the Epic Sandbox with _Noble Ambitions_

If you’ve run **T1–4 Temple of Elemental Evil** before and you’re coming back to Greyhawk with the 2024 rules, the **Verbobonc Campaign Guide 576/579 CY** and **Noble Ambitions 579 CY** are built to let you turn the whole thing into a **big, political sandbox** instead of a straight-line dungeon bash.

Here’s a clean way to do it:

#### 1\. Use _Noble Ambitions_ as your Act I spine

Treat [Noble Ambitions](noble-ambitions-579cy-plot.md) as the _campaign frame_ for the Second Rising of the Temple:

*   Start in **Verbobonc City** at the Civic Centre and **Jylee’s Inn** (NA-01, NA-02), with Lady Elinor Asbury’s discreet summons and the public notice on the message pole / Adventurers Guild.
*   Run through the early NA scenes:
*   **NA-01–NA-03**: Civic Square, Jylee’s Inn, the Oath of Asbury.
*   **NA-04**: The shadow contact and first hints that _someone_ in Verbobonc’s nobility is working against the Viscounty.

These sessions ground the players in **politics, noble houses, and city factions** before they ever see a Temple hallway.

#### 2\. Let Verbobonc City be the “living overmap”

As veterans, you already know the Temple’s bones. Use Verbobonc as the **overworld control panel**:

*   **High Quarter & Civic Center** – nobles, bishops, councils, Lakash’s parties.
*   **Business Quarter** – caravans, markets, temples, guilds, and the **Adventurers Guild message pole**.
*   **Hidden layers** – the Battirovka Thieves’ Guild under B32, Lakash Quallad’s quiet collusion with assassins like Alber Guldenstern, and the human–gnome trade tensions feeding the Temple’s logistics.

Every time the players come back from the wilds, you can adjust **prices, rumors, street mood, and faction moves** based on what they did around Hommlet/Nulb/Anshan.

#### 3\. Slot the classic TOEE content in as “where the trail leads”

_Noble Ambitions_ already points outward to:

*   [Asbury Manor](asbury-manor-landmark.md) **and** [Penwick](penwick-settlement.md) ,
*   **Anshan and the Fens of Tor**,
*   and the buried [Ancient Suel Temple](na-12-E28094-ancient-suel-temple---plot-plot.md) (NA-12) tied to deeper elemental corruption.

As an experienced DM, you can decide where to weave in:

*   **The [THE RUINS OF THE MOATHOUSE](the-ruins-of-the-moathouse-settlement.md)** – as an early “Temple echo” between NA-06 and NA-09, or as a side job when clues mention old ruins and strange raiders on the roads.
*   **Nulb & the main Temple** – when the investigation into banditry, missing caravans, and cult coin points clearly beyond the Fens and Suel sites to the _true_ resurgence of Elemental Evil.

You essentially use NA as **Act I–II** of a long arc, and the classic Temple as **Act III+**, with Verbobonc as the home base between all of it.

#### 4\. Give thieves and intrigue PCs real teeth

For rogue / face-heavy groups, lean into:

*   The [Battirovka Family](the-battirovka-family-organization.md) thieves’ guild under B32 as the street-level info network.
*   [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md) (and his manor in the High Quarter) as the charming, Temple-aligned power-broker using parties, trade deals, and assassins to weaken Verbobonc from inside.

A thief PC who is “Family” can:

*   Get **invited** to Lakash’s parties as staff, “security,” or a forged noble retainer.
*   Spy in the **Ballroom of Thirteen Mirrors** and the **Green Salon**, where deals are made and cult logistics are quietly arranged.
*   Bring that intel back to the thieves’ guild or their patron temple, turning the Temple war into a **three-way game** between cult, city, and underworld.

That’s where Noble Ambitions really shines for veterans: you already know the dungeon, but now you get to **play the political campaign around it**—who funds it, who covers for it, who is quietly trying to stop it without collapsing the whole Viscounty.
