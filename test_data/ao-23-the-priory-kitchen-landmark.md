AO-23. The Priory Kitchen
=========================

> The monastery kitchen where the [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) chapter once cooked for its knights and for every traveler who reached the gate. A working room — a great stone hearth, a brick baking oven, iron pots, oak preparation tables, and a water cistern fed by a roof-collection system that still works when it rains. The charter of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) required hospitality: any pilgrim, any merchant, any lost shepherd who arrived at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) was to be fed. The kitchen made that possible. Now it feeds the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison under [Romag](romag-person.md) — thirty-eight fighters, a command staff, and a rotation of excavation crews. The man who runs it is called **Cookie** — a [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) riverfront cook named **Fennick Grole** who signed on with the reavers for steady pay and now finds himself cooking three meals a day in a desecrated holy house and trying not to think about it. He is not a cultist. He is not a fighter. He is a man who makes stew, and he knows things.

 

_Monastery Kitchen of the Azure Oath — Now the Garrison Mess_

* * *

### The Kitchen That Fed Strangers

The [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) was a martial order, not a monastic one — but the Oath of Hospitality was carved into their charter alongside the Oath of Vigilance. A knight of the Azure Oath would die for a stranger on the road. He could also cook for one. The kitchen was where that obligation became practical.

The room sits in the northeast wing of the priory, connected to **AO-08. The Service Hall** by a broad serving arch — a wide opening in the stone wall, framed by iron hooks where curtains once hung, through which food was passed to the dining area. The kitchen was built for volume: a great stone hearth dominates the south wall, wide enough for two cauldrons to hang side by side over the fire, its chimney rising through the building's roof. A brick baking oven is set into the east wall — a beehive-shaped chamber that retains heat for hours after firing. Oak preparation tables run along the north wall beneath a rack of iron hooks for hanging dried meats, herbs, and utensils. A stone water cistern in the northeast corner collects rainwater from a roof-drainage channel — a practical engineering solution that still functions, though the channel is clogged with debris and flows intermittently.

This kitchen fed twenty to thirty people daily during the order's active years — knights, servants, visiting clergy, and whatever travelers arrived at the gate. The meals were simple but generous: bread, stew, root vegetables from the herb patch at AO-04, salt pork stored in the larder, and a thin hill-country beer that the knights drank at supper and pilgrims accepted gratefully. On feast days — the Valorous Saints' Day, the Vigil of the Arch-Paladin, the anniversary of the priory's founding — the kitchen produced more ambitious fare, and the smell of roasting meat and baking bread carried across the ridge.

**Halven Sere** cooked alone in this kitchen for fourteen years. He was not skilled at it. The meals he made were functional — boiled grain, foraged greens, whatever his garden produced. But he kept the hearth clean, the cistern maintained, and the oven functional. He carved a small blessing into the hearthstone: _"Fed by faith, warmed by duty"_ — in the same liturgical shorthand he used on his herb jars in the larder. He hung his own dried herbs from the preparation rack — the same medicinal plants that still hang there, brittle and faded, among the garrison's cruder provisions.

### Cookie's Kitchen

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison eats here. Thirty-eight fighters, [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiding crews, [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s excavation acolytes, and the command staff all take their meals from this room, carried to the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) or eaten standing in the service hall. The kitchen operates three times daily — before dawn, at midday, and at dusk. The schedule is rigid. [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) insists on regularity because meal times are the only thing that keeps garrison discipline from unraveling entirely.

The man who makes this happen is **Fennick Grole** — called **Cookie** by every man in the garrison, including [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), who has never used his real name.

Cookie is a [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) riverfront cook. Forty-two years old, stocky, balding, with burn scars on both forearms and a permanent squint from years of working over open fires in dockside taverns. He signed on with [Romag](romag-person.md)'s operation six months ago because the pay was steady and the alternative was another season of cooking for river pirates who paid in threats. He is not a cultist. He has never prayed to the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). He does not care about geomancy, caravan raids, or the politics of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). He cares about the stew not burning, the bread rising properly, and the guards not complaining about the portions.

He is also deeply, quietly uncomfortable. He has heard the prisoners in **AO-20** calling out at night. He has seen the bloodied hoods brought through the courtyard. He has watched [Maris Blackshale](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s raiders return from the Long Road with cargo that belongs to other people. He does not talk about any of this, because he has also seen what happens to people who ask questions — **Sergeant Drogath "Stonejaw" Varn** handles that kind of problem, and Drogath is not subtle. So Cookie makes the stew and keeps his mouth shut and tries not to think about the old man they keep in the cells.

##### Entering the Kitchen

The serving arch from the service hall opens onto a warm, low-ceilinged room thick with the smell of woodsmoke, boiling grain, and something that might generously be called stew. A great stone hearth dominates the far wall, its fire banked low beneath a single iron cauldron. The chimney above it is stained black with decades of soot. To the right, a brick oven is set into the wall — beehive-shaped, radiating faint heat. Its iron door is crusted with old bread-ash.  
  
Oak tables run along the left wall, their surfaces scarred with knife-marks and stained with years of food preparation. Iron hooks hang from a wooden rack above the tables — some hold bundles of dried onions and garlic, others hold strings of salt pork, and a few hold small bundles of dried herbs that look older and more carefully tied than the rest.  
  
A stone basin in the far corner collects water from a ceiling-mounted copper pipe — a slow drip, filling the basin about halfway. Beside it, a stack of clay bowls and wooden spoons sits in rough order.  
  
A stocky, balding man in a grease-stained apron stands at the central table, chopping something with the mechanical efficiency of a person who has done this ten thousand times. He glances up at you, knife paused mid-stroke, and his expression is not fear exactly — it is the wary calculation of a man deciding whether you are going to make his day worse.

### Features of the Kitchen

#### The Great Hearth

The stone hearth is the room's anchor — five feet wide, three feet deep, with an iron bar spanning the opening from which cauldrons and kettles are suspended by chain hooks. The fire pit is lined with firebrick, cracked in places but still sound. The chimney draws cleanly. The hearth has been in continuous use for centuries — first by the monastery, then by **Halven Sere** alone, now by the garrison. It is one of the few things in the priory that has not changed function.

**DC 14 Investigation**  
Carved into the hearthstone at the base of the firebox — partially hidden by ash and soot — a line of text in the liturgical shorthand of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md): _"Fed by faith, warmed by duty."_ **Halven Sere**'s hand. The carving has survived because the hearth is always in use — the soot protects it from casual notice, and no one in the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) can read the script. A character who has found Halven's other carvings (the stables at AO-07, the herb jars in **AO-21**) recognizes the same hand immediately.

**DC 16 Religion (Cleric or Paladin of Heironeous)**  
The hearthstone carries a faint consecration — not a ward or a seal, but a **blessing of sustenance** laid down by the original monastery builders. Food cooked over this hearth provides slightly more nourishment than it should. Mechanically: a long rest taken after eating a meal prepared on this hearth restores one additional hit die. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has been eating blessed food for months without knowing it. The irony is structural.

* * *

#### The Brick Oven

A beehive-shaped brick chamber set into the east wall, designed for baking bread and roasting meat. The iron door is heavy, hinged, and crusted with decades of bread-ash. The oven retains heat for hours after firing — Cookie bakes bread before dawn and the oven stays warm through midday.

The oven is functional and unremarkable. Its primary value is domestic: the party can use it to bake bread if they hold the kitchen, which matters during a prolonged siege or if they are sheltering rescued prisoners who need to eat.

* * *

#### The Preparation Tables and Herb Rack

Two heavy oak tables run along the north wall, their surfaces deeply scarred by generations of knives. Above them, an iron-hook rack suspends the kitchen's stores: bundles of dried onions, garlic braids, strings of salt pork, and — on three hooks at the far end — small bundles of dried herbs tied with linen thread that is distinctly older and more carefully knotted than the rest.

**DC 10 Perception**  
The three older herb bundles are different from the garrison's provisions. They are smaller, more precisely tied, and hung with the care of someone who knew which herbs were which. Dried rosemary, thyme, and sage — kitchen herbs, not medicinal ones.

**DC 12 Nature or Medicine**  
These herbs were dried and tied by the same hand that labeled the jars in **AO-21** — **Halven Sere**'s work, years old but still fragrant when crushed. He grew them in AO-04 and hung them here alongside whatever stores the monastery had. The garrison hung their own provisions on the same rack without noticing the difference.

* * *

#### The Water Cistern

A stone basin in the northeast corner, roughly three feet square and two feet deep, fed by a copper pipe that descends from the ceiling. The pipe connects to a roof-mounted rain-collection system — a practical piece of priory engineering that channels rainwater into the kitchen. The system works intermittently; the roof channel is partially clogged with debris and leaf-fall, so the cistern fills slowly and never reaches more than half capacity.

The cistern is the kitchen's only water source. Cookie supplements it with buckets hauled from the courtyard well, but the cistern provides clean water for cooking and drinking.

**Tactical note:** Contaminating the cistern (with poison, purgative herbs, or even just a significant quantity of spoiled food) would affect the garrison's water supply. Cookie would notice within hours — the water tastes different — but if the party times it correctly, a single meal's worth of contaminated food could incapacitate a significant portion of the garrison. This is a morally grey tactic; DMs should consider whether the party's actions affect non-combatants like Cookie or the prisoners in **AO-20**, who also eat from this kitchen.

* * *

#### Cookie's Corner

In the southeast corner, behind the hearth's warmth, **Fennick Grole** has carved out a personal space. A straw-stuffed sleeping pallet on a raised stone platform (to keep it off the cold floor). A folded wool blanket. A wooden box containing his personal effects: a razor, a comb missing half its teeth, a pouch of tobacco, three copper coins, and a folded letter he has never sent — addressed to a woman named **Darva** in [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), written in a rough, unpracticed hand.

Cookie sleeps in the kitchen, not the barracks. This was his choice — he told [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) he needed to be near the hearth to start the fire before dawn, which is true. The deeper reason is that he does not like the men in the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md). He is not one of them. He makes their food. That is not the same thing.

**DC 12 Investigation (the letter)**  
The unsent letter is addressed to "Darva, at the Waterside Rooms, [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md)." The contents are brief and difficult — Cookie's handwriting is barely literate. He tells Darva he is "working steady, feeding men at a house on the ridge." He says the pay is fair. He says he will come back when the contract ends. He does not say what the men he feeds do for a living. He does not say when the contract ends. There is no date on the letter.

### Encounter — Fennick "Cookie" Grole

**Cookie is almost always here.** He sleeps in the kitchen, works in the kitchen, and leaves only to haul water from the courtyard well or to carry scraps to the midden behind the building. He is the most accessible NPC in the priory — and the least dangerous.

When the party enters, Cookie's reaction depends on context:

*   **If the party enters quietly (no alarm raised):** He freezes, knife in hand, and stares. He does not shout. He does not fight. He assesses. After a long moment: _"You're not with us."_ It is not a question. Then, carefully: _"Are you here to kill everyone, or just the important ones?"_
*   **If an alarm has been raised:** Cookie is crouched behind the hearth with a cleaver held defensively. He will not attack. If approached, he drops the cleaver immediately and puts his hands up. _"I'm just the cook. I'm just the cook."_
*   **If the party is disguised as Earth Temple:** He treats them as he treats everyone — with gruff, transactional efficiency. _"Stew's not ready for another hour. Take bread if you want it."_ He does not volunteer information but does not hide his discomfort if they ask about the prisoners or the excavation

**What Cookie knows (and will share if he believes the party can protect him):**

*   The garrison's daily schedule — meal times, guard rotations, who eats when, who skips meals (sick, injured, or on extended patrol)
*   [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s routine — he eats alone in his quarters (**AO-18**), same time every evening, one guard outside. The door is unlocked during meals because Cookie or a runner brings the food
*   [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s pattern — he forgets to eat for days at a time when he is working below. When he does eat, he comes to the kitchen directly and eats standing up, distracted, muttering about stone
*   **Halven Sere**'s condition — Cookie prepares a separate meal for the old prisoner twice daily. He has never spoken to Halven, but he has seen him through the cell bars. _"He prays. All the time. Even when he eats. I've never seen a man pray like that."_
*   The garrison's morale — it is fraying. **Sergeant Drogath** holds the fighters together through fear, not loyalty. The excavation acolytes are afraid of what they have found below. Two raiders deserted last month and were hunted down by [Maris](maris-blackshale-person.md)'s crew
*   The one thing he will not talk about unprompted: what happens to the prisoners who are shipped out. He knows. He has heard the screams at the waystation handoffs. He does not want to say it aloud

**What Cookie wants:** To leave. He does not care about the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md). He does not care about the priory. He wants to walk down the ridge, walk back to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md), find Darva, and never cook for men with swords again. If the party promises to get him out alive, he will tell them everything he knows and help them in any way a non-combatant cook can — including timing a distraction (burning food, creating smoke), preparing drugged food, leaving a door unbarred, or identifying which guards are on duty at any given time.

**If the party threatens him:** He cooperates immediately. He is not brave. He is a cook. But he will resent it, and resentful informants give incomplete information. A party that earns his trust gets more than one that scares him.

### DM Notes

#### The Domestic Room

AO-23 is the priory's most human space. It smells like food. It has warmth from the hearth. A man sleeps here and keeps personal letters. After rooms full of barricades, stolen goods, and fortified chokepoints, the kitchen is a deliberate change in tone — domestic, mundane, almost uncomfortably normal. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is an occupying military force, and military forces eat. Someone has to cook. That someone is Cookie, and he is the most ordinary man in the building.

This normalcy is the point. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) is not a collection of monsters in a dungeon. It is an organization staffed by people — some of them zealots, some of them mercenaries, and some of them cooks who signed on for steady pay and now can't leave. Cookie humanizes the occupation without excusing it. He is complicit — he feeds the men who raid the Long Road and imprison travelers. He knows what they do. He chooses not to act. But he is not evil, and a party that treats him with basic decency will find him more useful than any treasure chest.

#### Cookie as Intelligence Asset

Cookie knows the garrison's routine better than anyone in the building, including [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md). Meal times are the backbone of military discipline — Cookie knows who eats, when they eat, where they eat, and who hasn't shown up. He can tell the party:

*   When guard shifts change (they overlap with meals)
*   When [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s door is unlocked (during meal delivery)
*   When [Varnor](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) is below in the crypts (he misses meals for days)
*   How many fighters are currently in the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) at any given time
*   Which guards are posted where (he delivers meals to the crypt sentries and the prisoner jailer)
*   The garrison's current morale (low and getting lower)

This information is tactically invaluable for a party planning their approach to any room in the priory.

#### Sacred Layer

The kitchen's desecration is the most ironic in the priory. The [Azure Oath](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) charter required hospitality — feed the stranger, shelter the pilgrim. The kitchen was the practical expression of that oath. Now it feeds the men who rob and imprison the same travelers the priory once sheltered. The hearth that warmed pilgrims' meals warms raiders' stew. The oven that baked feast-day bread bakes garrison loaves. And the hearthstone still carries its blessing of sustenance — feeding the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) with consecrated warmth they do not deserve and cannot perceive.

**Halven Sere**'s carving on the hearthstone — _"Fed by faith, warmed by duty"_ — is the priory's epitaph. The faith is still there, buried under soot. The duty has been abandoned by everyone except a thin old man in a cell who still prays over every meal Cookie brings him.

#### Connections

*   **AO-08 (Service Hall):** Serving arch — the primary connection. Food passes through here to the dining area. The arch is wide and unbarred — easy movement between rooms
*   **AO-21 (Supply Store):** Provisions for the kitchen come from the supply store. Cookie or a runner retrieves ingredients from **AO-21** as needed. The supply chain is: **AO-21** (raw stores) → AO-23 (kitchen) → **AO-08** (serving) → garrison
*   **AO-10 (Barracks):** Most meals are carried to the [barracks](ao-10-barracks-of-the-fallen-oath-landmark.md) by runners. Cookie does not leave the kitchen during meal service
*   **AO-18 (Tharvok's Quarters):** Cookie or a runner delivers Tharvok's evening meal. The door is unlocked during delivery — a narrow window of vulnerability
*   **AO-20 (Guest Cells):** Cookie prepares prisoner meals separately. A guard carries them to the cells. Cookie has never entered the cells himself

##### Gaining Cookie's Cooperation

*   **Persuasion DC 10:** Basic information (meal times, headcount)
*   **Persuasion DC 13:** Tactical details (Tharvok's routine, Varnor's pattern, guard posts)
*   **Persuasion DC 16:** Sensitive information (prisoner conditions, shipment horror, who deserted)
*   **Promise of escape:** Full cooperation — timing distractions, drugged food, unbarred doors, real-time intelligence
*   **Intimidation:** Works immediately. But incomplete info — he tells you what he thinks you want to hear, not what you need

##### Common Player Actions

*   **Talk to Cookie:** Persuasion DC 10/13/16 — escalating intelligence. Promise of escape = full cooperation
*   **Threaten Cookie:** Intimidation (any DC) — instant compliance, incomplete info
*   **Search the kitchen:** Investigation DC 14 (hearthstone carving), DC 12 (Cookie's letter), DC 10/12 (Halven's herbs)
*   **Identify hearthstone blessing:** Religion DC 16 — meals restore +1 hit die on long rest
*   **Contaminate cistern:** Poison/purgative in water supply. Affects garrison meals within hours. Cookie notices taste change. Risk: also affects prisoners
*   **Drug the food:** Cookie can do this if cooperative. Requires suitable substance. Affects one meal service — up to 15 garrison members depending on timing
*   **Use kitchen as staging area:** Warm, defensible, single entry. Cookie present as informant. Good rally point for rescued prisoners who need food
*   **Time an assault using meal intelligence:** Cookie knows exactly who is where during each meal period. This turns a dungeon crawl into a tactical strike
