Iron Claw Clan
==============

The **Iron Claw Clan** is a brutal and well-organized warband of **bugbears**, known for their **guerrilla tactics and ruthless raids** across the Welkwood Forest. Their growing influence has drawn the attention of **Commander Feldrin**, one of the leaders of the **Temple of Elemental Evil**, making them a key ally in his network of **bandits, mercenaries, and raiders**. The clan's power is centered in [Varnifane](varnifane-settlement.md), a former **grugach (wild elf) settlement**, now repurposed into a fortified base where humanoid warriors gather to plan new strikes against the region.

* * *

### **The Fall of Varnifane**

Varnifane was once a **treehouse village** home to a small band of **forty Grugach elves** deep within the Welkwood. These reclusive elves might have defended their home successfully, but fate turned against them. A vicious attack by a horde of **marauding kech (arboreal ambushers)** weakened the elves. Before they could recover, the Iron Claw Clan and their humanoid allies launched a devastating **night assault**, catching them off guard and overwhelming them with sheer numbers.

The **bugbear vanguard**, an elite unit skilled in surprise attacks, led the charge and breached the elven defenses. Their initial victory cemented their status as the dominant force in the warband, earning them the **lion’s share of the loot** from the elven treasury. Their prize included **five ancient, enchanted dwarven battle axes**—legendary weapons of the dwarves, which the Grugach had hidden away for centuries.

Now, Varnifane has been transformed into a war camp, housing a brutal mix of **125 humanoid raiders**, led by the bugbears who seized control. While the settlement’s original **treehouses** still stand, they are used as watchposts and spy nests, while the main force lives within the newly unearthed **Dwarven Warren** beneath Varnifane.

* * *

### **The Dwarven Warren**

Long before the elves settled there, Varnifane stood atop the **ruins of an ancient dwarven stronghold**. The Grugach, against their better judgment, had once explored its dark tunnels, drawn by the **residual magic** of the place. Within its depths, they found **the remains of a fallen dwarven clan**, long dead, but leaving behind enchanted treasures. Among these were **five battleaxes +3 (which are +5 in the hands of dwarves)**, weapons of immense power, hidden away out of reverence and fear of divine retribution from the dwarven gods.

The Iron Claw Clan, **unburdened by such superstitions**, now wields these relics openly, their war-leaders brandishing them as symbols of dominance. With these weapons in hand, the bugbears have forced the **goblins, gnolls, orcs, and other humanoids in the warband** to defer to their rule. The **Dwarven Warren** itself has become their stronghold, providing shelter, storage, and defensive advantages against would-be attackers. The humanoids have also stationed **scouts in the treehouses**, ensuring that no intruders approach undetected.

* * *

### **The Iron Claw Clan’s Leadership and Organization**

##### **The Warlord: Braggok Iron-Claw**

Braggok, a **hulking and cunning bugbear chieftain**, leads the clan with an iron grip. He is the bearer of one of the stolen **dwarven axes**, using it as both a weapon and a badge of office. Unlike the average brute, Braggok is a strategist who respects organization and **calculated aggression**. He has forged a strong **military alliance with Commander Feldrin**, making his warband an extension of the Temple’s mercenary network.

##### **The Spiritual Guide: Urglash the Shadow-Tongue**

Urglash, a bugbear shaman and priest of **Hruggek (the bugbear god of war and ambush)**, ensures that the warriors remain **fierce and fearless** in battle. He has **blessed the dwarven axes with dark rites**, ensuring their continued **use against the enemies of the clan**. Urglash also commands a small band of **clerics of Iuz**, sent by the Temple to provide additional magical support.

##### **The Enforcer: Gorz Blacktooth**

Braggok’s **second-in-command**, Gorz Blacktooth, is the **tactical commander** of the clan’s raids. A brutal and intimidating warrior, he personally leads many **ambushes and night raids**, ensuring that enemies are struck down before they can react. Gorz relishes in the chaos of war but understands the importance of discipline among his troops.

* * *

### **Varnifane as a Military Stronghold**

##### **Defenses and Strategy**

Varnifane is a **heavily fortified** encampment, with multiple layers of defense:

1.  **Treehouse Lookouts** – Bugbear scouts keep watch from above, ensuring that no one approaches undetected.
2.  **Hidden Pitfalls & Traps** – The forest surrounding Varnifane is filled with **camouflaged pits, snares, and tripwires** to slow invaders.
3.  **Warren Entrances** – The **Dwarven Warren** below the settlement provides a defensive fallback, allowing the humanoids to **fortify their position underground** if attacked.
4.  **Elite Warbands** – Braggok deploys **roving warbands** to harass enemies, preventing large forces from gathering nearby.

* * *

### **The Role of the Temple of Elemental Evil**

##### **Commander Feldrin’s Influence**

The Iron Claw Clan has become an **instrument of destruction** for **Commander** [Feldrin](feldrin-person.md) , the Temple’s master of **bandits and mercenaries**. Braggok and his warbands **raid villages, ambush caravans, and harass elven patrols**, all in service to the Temple’s greater schemes. Feldrin supplies the bugbears with **gold, weapons, and intelligence**, ensuring their loyalty and continued service.

* * *

### **Adventure Hooks & Opportunities**

1.  **Recover the Dwarven Axes** – If the legendary dwarven weapons were reclaimed and returned to their rightful owners in **Ulek**, the rewards would be immense.
2.  **Sabotage the Iron Claw Clan** – Adventurers could be hired to **disrupt** the bugbears' operations and weaken their hold on the Welkwood.
3.  **Rescue Captured Elves** – A few surviving **Grugach elves** remain as prisoners in Varnifane. A daring raid could free them.
4.  **Strike Against the Temple’s Forces** – Disrupting the bugbear-Temple alliance would set back the **Temple of Elemental Evil’s** plans in the region.

* * *

**Conclusion**
--------------

The **Iron Claw Clan** has carved out a **fearsome stronghold** in **Varnifane**, transforming it from an elven haven into a **militarized war camp**. With their **dwarven relics, alliance with the Temple of Elemental Evil, and disciplined leadership**, they are a growing threat to the **Welkwood, Celene, and beyond**. If left unchecked, their power will only grow, spelling disaster for the forces of civilization in the region.

### **Forces of the Iron Claw Clan**

The warband numbers **125 humanoids**, a mix of different monstrous races:

*   **Bugbears** (40) – The elite core, well-trained in ambush tactics.
*   **Orcs** (30) – Heavy frontline troops and shock attackers.
*   **Goblins** (25) – Scouts, messengers, and archers.
*   **Gnolls** (20) – Savage fighters used for their ferocity in battle.
*   **Clerics of Iuz** (10) – Priests providing magical support and healing.

The **bugbears** hold the highest rank among the group, and their possession of the **dwarven axes** further cements their leadership over the others.
