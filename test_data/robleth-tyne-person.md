Robleth Tyne
============

Robleth Tyne is the **current mayor of** [Twilight Falls](twilight-falls-settlement.md) **(as of 576 CY)**, a **shrewd and miserly lumber baron** whose vision for the village is one of **economic expansion and growing political influence**. As the owner of **a highly profitable lumber and milling company called** [Tyne Timber & Trade](10-tyne-timber-lumberyard-landmark.md), Tyne has positioned himself as a key player in **Verbobonc’s fine wood trade**, selectively harvesting rare and valuable timber while maintaining tight control over the industry. A pragmatist to his core, he lacks the intellectual and political finesse of Verbobonc’s elite but understands the value of **alliances and wealth**. Rather than challenging greater minds, he has aligned himself with **Dantilus Trimber**, a powerful merchant lord, ensuring his continued prosperity.

#### **Personality**

*   **Miserly & Controlling** – Tyne hoards wealth and resources, ensuring his financial empire remains secure.
*   **Pragmatic & Cautious** – He does not overreach but aligns himself with **more powerful figures**, knowing his limitations.
*   **Charismatic & Persuasive** – While not the most brilliant strategist, he knows how to **secure favor through deals and negotiations**.
*   **Dismissive of Art & Beauty** – Views Mistress Anda Dove’s **artist colony and cultural influence as frivolous distractions**.
*   **Unapologetically Greedy** – He believes wealth **defines power** and sees Twilight Falls as his personal stepping stone to greater riches.

* * *

### **Background & Rise to Power**

Tyne’s ambitions were shaped by his father, **the elder Robleth Tyne**, a former **two-time mayor of Twilight Falls** and one of the **earliest backers of Dantilus Trimber** in Verbobonc’s merchant circles. Inheriting both his father’s wealth and political influence, the younger Tyne **expanded their selective logging enterprise company called** [Tyne Timber & Trade](tyne-timber-26-trade-organization.md), making himself indispensable to **Verbobonc’s noble families and shipbuilders** who prized fine timber from the Gnarley.

*   **From Merchant to Mayor** – Leveraging wealth and influence, he secured the mayoralty, promising **economic growth and prosperity**.
*   **Alliance with Dantilus Trimber** – Rather than vying for higher status, he aligned himself with **Trimber’s interests**, ensuring a steady flow of profits.
*   **Undermining the Old Faith** – He **clashed with druids**, seeing their **environmental concerns as an obstacle** to progress.
*   **Elven Hostility** – His **deforestation policies** have strained relations with the **elves of the Gnarley Forest**.
*   **Mistress Anda Dove’s Opposition** – While she no longer seeks political power, she remains **one of the few figures in Twilight Falls who openly challenges his vision**.

> "If the elves cared so much for these woods, they’d have done something useful with them."

* * *

### **Political Affiliations & Alliances**

##### **Verbobonc’s Merchant Companies & Nobility**

*   **Backed by several noble families**, especially those profiting from timber exports.
*   **Strong ties to the Merchant Guilds** of Verbobonc, who see him as a valuable business ally.
*   Some **suspect Tyne of bribery and backroom dealings**, though nothing has been proven.
*   **Loyal to Dantilus Trimber**, preferring to follow rather than lead in the greater political arena.

> "I built this fortune with my own hands. No druid, no elf, no backward-thinking fool is going to take it from me."

##### **Conflict with the Old Faith & Druids**

*   [Mahrli Wildbriar](mahrli-harefoot-person.md) **and her followers** openly oppose his **expansionist policies**.
*   Tyne has tried **to push druidic influence out of village affairs**, with mixed success.
*   He secretly **undermines druidic gatherings**, promoting **pro-trade factions** among locals.

##### **Tense Relations with the Elves of the Gnarley**

*   **Elven leaders refuse to meet with him**, seeing him as a threat to the forest.
*   Certain **half-elven merchants act as intermediaries**, but the **elves largely reject his trade offers**.
*   His **deforestation efforts** have **heightened elven hostility**, making them potential enemies.

##### **Mistress Anda Dove & the Artist’s Colony**

*   Arrived in Twilight Falls in **566 CY** with a troupe of **artists and visionaries**, aiming to turn the village into **an artistic haven**.
*   Served **twice as mayor**, working to preserve the village’s **beauty and culture**.
*   Now has **no interest in politics**, though she **quietly supports the druids** and works against Tyne’s **destructive expansionism**.
*   Tyne views her as **a relic of the past**, while she sees him as **a blight upon the village**.

> "Mistress Dove can paint all the flowers she likes, but a pretty picture won’t fill a man’s purse."

* * *

### **The Coming Assassination & Political Struggles**

##### **Years Before His Death (576 CY–578 CY)**

Though still alive in 576 CY, Tyne’s **downfall is looming**. His **enemies are growing bolder**, and his political maneuvers have placed him in a **dangerous web of alliances and betrayals**.

*   **Increased threats** – **Anonymous warnings**, dead animals left on his doorstep, and whispered rumors of a plot.
*   **Bandit involvement?** – Some suspect **disgruntled loggers, smugglers, or brigands** may have been hired to remove him.
*   **Druidic or Elven Retribution?** – Many believe his assassination will come from those **seeking to protect the forest**.
*   **Betrayal from Within?** – Some of his **own business partners** in Verbobonc **stand to profit from his death**.

##### **The Assassination (578 CY?)**

While the details of his **assassination remain unclear**, later years will see **his mysterious death shake the village’s leadership**, leading to:

*   **Mistress Anda Dove’s Rise** – A return to **Twilight Falls’ artistic and cultural roots**.
*   **A Shift in Power** – The village turning **away from aggressive expansion**.
*   **Ongoing Uncertainty** – The **truth behind his assassination** may never be fully uncovered, leading to **rumors, conspiracy, and lingering vendettas**.

* * *

### **Adventure Hooks**

*   **The Warning Signs** – The party is hired to **investigate threats against Mayor Tyne**, uncovering clues to a larger conspiracy.
*   **A Dangerous Meeting** – A secret gathering in Verbobonc could decide Tyne’s fate—can the players intervene before it’s too late?
*   **The Final Night** – Years later, adventurers must unravel **who truly ordered Tyne’s assassination**, uncovering a tangled web of power struggles.
*   **The Shadow Behind the Throne** – Was Tyne’s death orchestrated by **a greater force**—perhaps a merchant lord, a secret cult, or even something beyond mortal politics?

> "Artists dream, merchants deal in reality. That’s why I run this village, and she just hosts tea parties."

#### **Conclusion**

Robleth Tyne is a **man on borrowed time**. As the **mayor of Twilight Falls**, he has **clawed his way to power**, reshaping the village in his image. Yet his **relentless ambition has forged enemies in every shadow**—and soon, **one of them will strike the final blow**.
