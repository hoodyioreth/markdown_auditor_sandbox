NSQ-GN10: The Songbird & the Ledger
===================================

_A pub-crawl investigation across Gnomesberg (G) and the south-west Foreign Quarter (F), on the Nibg’s Run side of Verbobonc — 576 CY.  
_**Related Side Quest:** [NSQ-03: The Merchant Argument](nsq-033A-the-merchant-argument-plot.md) (the “paper-cut” that started the bleeding)

* * *

#### The Premise

Someone is **stirring the city with song**—not with blades. A disguised Temple-aligned field agent, **Gallin** (elf, bard 6), moves from tavern to tavern, laying **honeyed slanders** that turn human and gnome against one another. He is **hired by** [Lakash Quallad](lakash-quallad-person.md), who has already shown willingness to sabotage gnome–human relations through deceit and intermediaries (as seen in NSQ-03).

Gallin’s work is simple:

*   make gnomes look like **liars and traitors**
*   make humans look like **victims and righteous avengers**
*   make the Viscount look like a **puppet**
*   keep everyone shouting so nobody listens for the **Temple’s boots** in the dark.

The party’s job is to **find him**, **prove intent**, and **learn who is paying him**—before he slips away via the **Gnomesberg River-Piers (G10)** to a waiting contact.

### Where the Bard Is Found

#### **Gallin is performing at G6 — The Rusty Nail.**

It fits his method: the Rusty Nail’s illusion-laced ambience makes “truth” feel optional, and the crowd is primed for clever ridicule. It’s also perfectly placed for a fast flight toward the river route and the pier contact.

If you want a _second choice_ (if the party misses him), he turns up the next night at **F6 The Blind Beholder** to “play to the longshanks.” But the **main scene** is at **G6**.

* * *

### Objectives

##### Primary Objectives

1.  **Identify** Gallin as a deliberate agitator (not “just a rude minstrel”).
2.  **Learn** who hired him (Lakash Quallad) and why.
3.  **Prevent escape** (or chase him to G10 and seize proof there).

##### Secondary Objectives

*   Gain leverage with **Prince Jimm’s circle** (G1) or the Free Alliance’s traders.
*   Tie Gallin’s lies to **real disruptions**: caravan raids, tariffs, missing patrols, forged receipts—exactly the sort of fracture NSQ-03 exposed.

* * *

#### What Gallin Knows

Gallin does **not** know the full Temple hierarchy. He is a field instrument—paid, protected, and disposable.

He knows:

*   His patron is **Lakash Quallad** (never spoken aloud; only “my benefactor,” “a Dyvers man,” “a merchant of consequence”).
*   His job is to **worsen gnome/human trust** and keep pressure on Prince Jimm.
*   A **river contact** awaits at **G10** (a “factor’s runner” with a token and a skiff).
*   The next planned spark: a song blaming “gnome sabotage” for a **fresh patrol injury incident**, driving outrage and calling for “justice.”

He does **not** know:

*   the true endgame of the Temple’s regional logistics
*   names of deeper cult officers
*   where “the real goods” go after they vanish

##### Gallin’s Mental Defense (table-friendly)

If a PC tries mind-reading magic: Gallin has a **warded mind charm** (a hired trinket) that makes his thoughts feel like **banal stage-fright and vanity** unless the caster beats a hard contest. He _can_ be cracked with persistent pressure, clever questions, or catching him mid-flight.

#### F9 — The Trader’s Stableyard (the first whiff)

The yard stinketh of wet straw, horse-sweat, and river mud. Wagons crowd the fence like cattle at market, and men speak low when they reckon coin. A boy with a currycomb pauses mid-stroke to stare at thee—then looks away too quick, as though he’d seen a shadow where none should be.

**Rumor & Dialog:  
**A young driver mutters, “Long Road’s dead. Dead as last winter’s cabbage.”  
An older carter spits. “Dead ’cause o’ claws and wings—ain’t gnome-work, lad.”  
A stable-hand leans in: “But there’s a _songman_ been biddin’ folk blame the hills. Sings it sweet, like honey o’er rot.”

**Clue:** a scrap of lyric-sheet in the straw: _“…short kings, long lies…”_ with a smear of expensive ink.

#### F3 — The Bronze Unicorn Inn (polite knives in velvet)

Brass sconces glow warm upon polished boards, and the air is thick with perfume and roasted meats. Here, folk smile with their mouths and measure thee with their eyes. A unicorn sigil gleams above the hearth—bright, proud, and paid for.

**Rumor & Dialog:  
**A merchant’s wife: “Prince Jimm? A darling puppet. He dances when the Council claps.”  
A quiet factor replies: “A puppet may yet bite the hand that holds the strings.”  
A server whispers to the party: “The bard’s been here. He asked which tables hold guildmen… and which hold gnomes.”

**Clue:** someone paid for a private supper “for Gallin”—signed only with a curling **Q**.

* * *

#### F6 — The Blind Beholder (where uglier truths are said aloud)

Smoke hangs like old cobwebs beneath the beams. Tankards thud; dice clatter; laughter is harsh and honest. In the corner, a one-eyed carved signboard grins at thee, as though it knoweth every shame in this room.

**Rumor & Dialog (choose a table):**

*   “Wilfrick’s a puppet—nobles pull his beard like reins.”
*   “Milinous wants Borderers command—an’ he’ll get it, mark me.”
*   “A masked monk watched a caravan—golden face, stone stare—then gone.”

**Clue:** A drunk boasts, “I heard that bard say he’d ‘sing Gnomesberg into a war’ if the pay were right.” His mate laughs—then goes pale when asked who paid.

#### G5 — The Brass Rail (mischief with teeth)

Round doors and warm lanterns. The scent of goose-egg stew clings to thy cloak, and laughter rolls over thee like a friendly wave—yet beneath it lies the clink of coin and the quiet counting of debts.

**Rumor & Dialog:  
**Pamphila (or any serving gnome) murmurs, “We’ve had a sweet-voiced elf here, too clever by half. He laugheth at Jimm… but never laugheth at _Lakash_.”  
A patron adds, “He asked after the river-piers—where a man might depart unseen.”

**Clue:** “quiet passage” token mentioned in gossip (foreshadow G10).

#### **G6 — The Rusty Nail (Main Scene: the Satire Riot)**

##### Arrival & Set-Up

Thou descendest into warmth and trickery. Illusion-windows show gardens that aren’t there—pixies darting through painted boughs, a unicorn lifting its bright head as though it heard thy footfall. Yet the beer is real, and so are the stares. A small stage sits to one side. Upon it, an elf with a bright smile and a lute polished like a mirror sings as though he were born to be listened to. His voice is honey—yet there is vinegar in his eyes.

##### Gallin’s First Song (Love Ballad, “Keoish West” flavor)

_(This is_ _**original**__—use the rhythm you like at table.)_

**“The River Took My Sorrow”** (in the old Flan tongue / local flavor)

_By western heath where skylarks fare,  
I gave my oath to summer air;  
Her hair was gold, her hands were swift,  
And all my grief became a drift._

_O river, run—run gentle, run,  
Carry my tears from sun to sun;  
If love be lost, yet let it be  
Lost kindly, like a leaf at sea._

The room sighs. Even sour folk soften.

##### Gallin’s Second Song (The Satire: Prince Jimm)

He pivots with a grin.

The elf plucks a bright chord—too bright—and the laughter in the room turns expectant, like dogs scenting meat. His eyes flick toward a table where three gnomes sit stiff-backed, their mugs untouched.

**“The Prince of Pockets”**

_Oh, Jimm of the Hills with his crown made of tin,  
He signs with a flourish and calls it a win;  
He counts out a promise, then pockets the day,  
And smiles while the wagons are taken away._

_For when the Council thundereth, “What shall be done?”  
He nods like a child who hath swallowed the sun;  
Then whispers, “Be patient—be merry—be small,”  
While longshanks take scrip and the gnomes take the fall._

##### The Confrontation (your provided beats, tightened for table)

As the chorus returns, the three gnomes rise.

**Gnome spokesman:** _“Why sing’st thou that? Thou know’st it false. ’Twas the Council-men who fouled the matter.”_**Gallin (smiling):** _“Not so I heard, little masters. If the tune offendeth thee, the door standeth open. And—ah—had I spied thee sooner, I might have chosen gentler words… but thou being so short, I did not see thee. Accept my apologies.”_

Laughter. A chair scrapes. A gnome’s fingers twitch.

**Gnome caster:** _“Toad-tongued liar.”_ _(He casts a small glamour: Gallin appears as a toad in a fine doublet with a lute.)  
_The room roars.

**Gallin (snapping):** _“A pretty trick—yet poorly aimed! Wouldst thou paint me a fool because thy prince cannot hold a promise?”_

##### Player Intervention Options

If the party steps in, they can:

*   calm the room (Persuasion / Deception / Intimidation as fits)
*   side with gnomes, side with Gallin, or “arbitrate”
*   follow Gallin when he’s hustled toward the kitchen/back exit

##### What PCs Can Learn Here

**From Gallin (if engaged, not captured):**

*   “Gnomes are trouble—always late, always sly.” _(He offers no specific proof; he weaponizes vagueness.)_
*   “I sing what folk whisper. If the truth hurteth, blame the truth.”
*   If pressed on source: “Council talk. A friend of a friend. A man with coin and taste.”

**From the gnomes:**

*   “Humans have treated us like weeds these past weeks.”
*   “Our caravans are raided; patrols turn their eyes away; then humans blame us for missing goods.”
*   “This began with _paper_—receipts, signatures, lies.” (Point the party toward NSQ-03’s truth: sabotage via intermediaries.)

**Hard clue (if PCs succeed on pointed questions):  
**Gallin slips and mentions the name of his “patron’s man”—a **river runner** who “keeps a skiff ready by the piers.”

#### G8 — Burblebelly’s Brewery (where the workers hear everything)

The cask-hall is warm as bread. Yeast and wood-smoke fill thy lungs. Men and gnomes alike come here to forget their own names for an hour—and in forgetting, they speak too freely.

**Rumor & Dialog:

A dockhand replies, “Aye—and someone’s been buying schedules.”  
A fat-cheeked gnome adds, “He paid in bright coin, not common—like a lord’s purse dressing itself as a minstrel.”

#### G1 — Old House of Prince Jimm (the polite hook)

The old house keepeth its dignity like a coat worn thin at the elbows—well-brushed, well-mended, and hiding every patch. Lanterns glow soft; guards watch with eyes that smile not at all.

**Rumor & Dialog:

A staff gnome adds quietly: “If thou findest the singer’s purse-string… thou findest the hand behind it.”

**Clue:** Jimm’s people suspect the agitation is _paid_, not organic—exactly the pattern seen in NSQ-03 (manufactured mistrust).

#### The Escape: G10 River-Piers (Chase & Contact)

##### G10 — The River-Piers (the slip into water)

The river mouth yawns wide—dark water taking all things without complaint. Lanterns sway; ropes creak; a gull cries like a warning. Between warehouse and boathouse, a narrow plank-walk leadeth to a small skiff already nosing the pilings, oar in place, as if it were waiting for a name.

##### The Contact (TOEE runner)

A “gnome factor’s runner” (actually Temple-aligned) waits with a **token** and a sealed pouch. He doesn’t fight to win—he fights to **delay**.

**Runner (hissed):** “Quick now—songbird. The tide won’t bide for pride.”  
**Gallin (breathless, furious):** “They’ve got teeth, these heroes. Next time, pay me double.”

##### Proof Available Here (choose 1–3)

*   A note: _“Keep the Hills angry. Keep the City hungry.”_ signed with a **Q** flourish.
*   A purse with **unusual mint** coin (not common tavern change).
*   A torn ledger page listing taverns and “audience types” (humans/gnomes/merchants).
*   A waxed packet: “lyrics” that explicitly instruct blaming specific gnome clans.

##### Outcomes

*   **If Gallin escapes:** the party gains partial proof; Gallin becomes a recurring nuisance—until someone silences him.
*   **If Gallin is caught:** he clams up, but the **runner** can still be traced to a chain of agents.
*   **If both are caught:** the party can flip the runner with fear, greed, or a promise of protection.

##### Running Notes

_Making the Pub Crawl Feel Like an Investigation_

Use each stop to hand the party **one of three things**:

1.  a rumor that _sounds_ true
2.  a witness who saw Gallin or heard his patron’s “Q”
3.  a logistical detail pointing toward **G10** (skiff, token, runner, schedule)

By the time they reach G6, they should already _expect_ a staged social explosion—and choose whether to defuse it, exploit it, or pursue the bard.

### Gallin — Captured & Questioned

He does not look afraid at first.

He smooths his hair. Adjusts his cuffs. Even tied to a chair, he sits as though it were a stage stool.

* * *

##### Stage One: Charm & Dismissal

**Gallin (smiling faintly):  
**“My friends… heroes, surely? We’ve had a misunderstanding. I sing. They take offense. It is the oldest quarrel in any city that still has breath in it.”

**If accused of stirring unrest:**

“Unrest? Oh, no. I merely give voice to what folk already whisper. Would you silence every tavern table too?”

**If asked who hired him:**

“Hired? My dear, I am hired by applause and paid in tankards.”

He laughs softly.

“Must there be a conspiracy behind every sharp tongue?”

* * *

##### Stage Two: Subtle Counter-Accusation

If pressed harder:

“You’ve been listening to gnomes, haven’t you? Clever creatures. Very quick with numbers… slower with truth. Have you asked them why caravans go missing? Why patrols limp home bleeding? Or is it simpler to blame the bard?”

**If accused of lying:**

“Lies? No. Exaggerations? Perhaps. It is called satire.”

* * *

##### Stage Three: Fracture & Deflection

If the party produces real evidence: The smile tightens.

“You mistake coin for command. Very well. A merchant paid for songs. A man with interests. Merchants _always_ have interests.”

**If asked his name directly in relation to Lakash:**

“Lakash? I have heard the name. Dyvers, perhaps? Wealthy men love a tune that pleases them.”

He shrugs.

“But I sing for many.”

* * *

##### Stage Four: Fear Slips Through

If magic is used successfully or he feels escape is impossible:

His composure cracks for a breath.

“You don’t understand what you meddle in. There are men in this city who prefer _storms_ to sunshine. They grow rich when neighbors glare at one another.”

**If asked about the Temple directly:**

“The Temple? Pah. Old stones and older fools.”

But his eyes flicker. He looks toward the direction of the river instinctively.

* * *

##### Stage Five: Bargaining

If the party offers protection or threatens exposure:

“Listen carefully. my employer does not attend council meetings. He attends counting houses. He wishes the gnomes… unsettled. Angry. Ready to leave. Trade flows where coin commands it.”

He leans forward as far as bonds allow.

“I am a match tossed into dry straw. If you quench me, another spark will come.”

**If pressed for proof:**

“There is a runner at the piers. A token. A pouch. I do not meet the patron. I meet the purse.”

* * *

##### Stage Six: Last Attempt to Escape (Even Bound)

He shifts tone suddenly.

“Tell me—how many of you truly trust Prince Jimm? How many believe Wilfrick speaks his own mind?”

“War is coming. You cannot sing it back into silence.”

He tries to create doubt between party members.

If one PC shows hesitation, Gallin pounces verbally:

“You see? Even your own are not convinced.”

* * *

##### If the Party Threatens to Turn Him Over to the Constables

He smiles again, thin as paper.

“Oh, by all means. I shall persuade my guards. I always do.”

“They enjoy a good song.”

* * *

##### If They Break Him Completely

Very rare. Only if magical compulsion or overwhelming pressure is used.

“I was told to keep the hills angry and the city hungry.”
