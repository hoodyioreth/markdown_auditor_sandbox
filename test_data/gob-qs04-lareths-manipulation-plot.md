GOB-QS04-Lareths-Manipulation
=============================

Lareth's Manipulation is not a quest starter in the traditional sense — it is a trap disguised as one. Lareth the Beautiful approaches the party under a false identity, presents himself as a representative of the Viscounty or another legitimate authority, and manipulates them into raiding the Sepulchre-Priory to recover the Gift of Beauty on his behalf. His charm is devastating, his intelligence flawless, and his scheme elegant: every possible outcome benefits him. If the party succeeds, he takes the Gift. If they fail, the Earth Temple loses resources. If they grow suspicious, he has still weakened his rival's grip on the priory. And behind it all lies Lareth's true master — Lolth, the Demon Queen of Spiders — whose web extends through Falrinth and into the heart of the Temple of Elemental Evil itself. This article provides full dialogue for Lareth's approach, his charm offensive, the bugbear betrayal, and all DM decision points for running the most dangerous con in the Gift of Beauty questline.

 

### The Spider's Door — When the Villain Approaches First

_"The world is filled with filth and frailty, but I am its dark perfection."_  
— [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md)

 

This is **not a standard quest starter**. In GOB-QS01 through QS03, the good factions of Verbobonc — the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md), the elven clans, the Church of [Rao](rao-person.md) — approach the party and send them toward the Gift of Beauty. In GOB-QS04, the **enemy** approaches first. [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md), the most dangerous operative of the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md), finds the party before anyone else does — and offers them the quest himself.

 

The party does not know they are being played. Lareth's disguise is flawless, his intelligence is accurate, and his cause sounds righteous. He points them at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md), tells them the Gift is there, tells them the truth about the Earth Temple garrison — and sends them in like a surgeon's knife aimed at his rival's throat. Every outcome serves Lareth. And behind Lareth stands [Lolth](lolth-person.md), the Demon Queen of Spiders, whose web runs through [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) and into the heart of the Temple itself.

 

**DM Warning:** This starter should only be used with experienced players who can handle moral complexity and betrayal. It is devastating when it works — and it creates some of the best sandbox moments in the entire campaign when players realize they've been manipulated and decide what to do about it.

 

### Understanding Lareth

Before running this encounter, the DM must understand what [Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) is, because he is not a simple villain.

 

Lareth is young, handsome, and terrifyingly charismatic. Jet-black hair, piercing grey eyes, flawless skin — he looks like a minor noble or a poet, not a cult commander. His voice is warm, educated, and persuasive. He makes eye contact. He listens. He remembers names. He asks the right questions and waits for the answers. He is, by any measure, **the most socially dangerous person** the party has ever met.

 

He commands the Moathouse — a forward outpost of the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md) positioned south of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md). His forces include bandits, gnolls, goblins, and a company of bugbears under **Gorvash the Cruel** of the Iron Claw Clan. But Lareth's true weapon is not military force — it is **manipulation**. He turns enemies into tools. He turns allies into assets. And he turns the party into the perfect deniable strike team against his rival, [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md), the Earth Temple's garrison warden at the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md).

 

**Lareth's secret allegiance:** [Lolth](lolth-person.md), the Demon Queen of Spiders. Lareth is not loyal to [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) or the Greater Temple. He is [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s primary infiltrator within the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md), working in partnership with [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) — an 8th-level magic-user who possesses the Orb of Golden Death and operates from the Temple's third level. Together, they aim to collapse the Temple's alliances from within, seize the elemental nodes, and deliver everything of value to [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s designs. The Gift of Beauty is a means to that end.

### The Approach: How Lareth Finds the Party

 

##### Timing — DM Decision Point

The DM must decide **when** Lareth makes contact. Each timing creates a different dynamic:

 

*   **Before the party has any quest hook:** Lareth is the _first_ person to tell the party about the Gift. This makes his version of events the baseline — everything the party hears later (from Wyn, Glorandal, the Church of Rao) is measured against what Lareth told them. Maximum deception. Maximum impact when the truth emerges
*   **After the party has received another starter (QS01–QS03) but before they've traveled south:** Lareth appears as a "second source" confirming what the party already knows — and offering help. His intelligence is better than Wyn's or Aldric's because he actually knows where the Gift is. This makes him seem more competent and trustworthy than the legitimate contacts
*   **After the party has already found the priory:** Lareth appears as a potential ally who knows the priory's defenses from the inside. He offers tactical intelligence and reinforcements (the bugbears). This is the most operationally useful approach but the most suspicious — why does this stranger know so much about a secret cult fortress?

 

##### The Disguise

[Lareth](lareth-person.md) does not appear as himself. He has never shown his face to the party. He has never spoken in their presence. He is careful about that — always. When he approaches, he wears one of several possible covers:

 

*   **"Lord Calren" — a minor Viscounty official:** Well-dressed, articulate, carrying forged documents bearing the seal of the Viscount's office. Claims to be a special envoy tasked with recovering the Gift through unofficial channels. This is Lareth's preferred cover — it gives him authority without being verifiable in the field
*   **"Brother Maren" — a traveling priest of [Rao](rao-person.md):** Humble robes, a calm demeanor, and a convincing knowledge of Raoan theology. Claims to be searching for the missing Rao delegation on behalf of the Church. This cover works best if the party has already received GOB-QS03
*   **An intermediary — someone else entirely:** Lareth sends a trusted agent (not himself) to make first contact. The agent delivers the pitch, arranges a meeting, and Lareth appears only after the party has agreed in principle. This is the safest approach for Lareth but the weakest for his charm — the party hasn't felt his presence yet

 

### The Encounter: Lareth's Pitch

 

##### Setting the Scene

The encounter can occur at an inn, along a road, in a private room at [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), or at a crossroads camp. Lareth prefers indoor settings where he can control the conversation — a private dining room, a merchant's parlor, a quiet corner of an inn's second floor. He arrives alone or with a single attendant (a disguised bandit playing a servant). No visible weapons. No visible threat. He looks like exactly what he claims to be: a well-bred official on a sensitive errand.

 

The man who enters the room is not what you expected. He is young — perhaps thirty — with jet-black hair swept back from a face that could grace a court portrait. His eyes are the color of winter sky, sharp and warm at the same time. He wears a travelling cloak of deep blue over a fitted doublet, and he moves with the easy confidence of someone who has never been refused a seat at any table. He carries no visible weapon. His hands are clean, his nails trimmed, and when he smiles — and he smiles immediately upon seeing you — the expression reaches his eyes with practiced sincerity.   "Thank you for agreeing to meet. I know this is unusual — a stranger requesting a private audience. But the matter I bring to you is both urgent and, I must confess, beyond my own ability to resolve. I need people of courage. And by every account I have gathered, that is precisely what you are."

 

**DM Notes — Playing Lareth:**

*   Lareth is **Charisma incarnate**. He does not threaten, pressure, or rush. He listens. He asks the party about themselves — their travels, their deeds, their motivations. He flatters without being obvious. He makes each person in the group feel seen and valued. This is not an act. Lareth genuinely enjoys reading people. He finds it entertaining to identify what motivates each person and then give them exactly what they want to hear
*   His Deception modifier is exceptional. If a player calls for Insight, set the DC at **20 or higher**. Lareth does not "feel" like he is lying because, in many ways, he is telling the truth. The priory _is_ real. The Gift _is_ there. The Earth Temple _is_ dangerous. He simply omits the part where he is the other half of the danger
*   Do not play Lareth as a cartoon villain. He should feel like the **most helpful NPC the party has ever met**. Warm. Competent. Concerned. Slightly vulnerable — he admits he cannot do this alone. That vulnerability is calculated, but it should feel genuine

 

##### The Charm Offensive — Lareth Paints the Picture

Once introductions are made, Lareth lays out his case. He speaks with the measured urgency of a man who has seen something terrible and needs help stopping it. The DM should deliver this as a continuous read-aloud, pausing only where indicated to allow party questions.

 

"Let me be direct with you. What I am about to describe is not rumor — it is intelligence gathered at considerable personal risk, and I share it because I believe you are capable of acting on it where I cannot."   He leans forward. His voice drops — not to a whisper, but to the quiet register of a man sharing a secret that matters.   "South of [Hommlet](village-of-hommlet-settlement.md), in the foothills beyond the Long Road, there is a fortified priory — the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) of the Azure Oath. It was once a house of contemplation. Now it is something else entirely."   A pause. Lareth lets the weight build.   "A splinter cult has taken the priory. Insane, demented radicals — devotees of the old Earth Temple, broken off from whatever central authority once held them in check. They are not organized. They are not rational. They thrive on chaos and destruction, and they have been raiding the roads of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) with impunity. Merchant caravans. Gnome supply trains. Mounted Borderer patrols. Nothing is safe."   He reaches into his cloak and produces a folded parchment — a hand-drawn map. It is accurate. It shows the priory's location, its approaches, and the surrounding terrain. Lareth has no reason to give bad intelligence here. The truth serves him better.   "Three tendays ago, these radicals intercepted a diplomatic convoy carrying the Gift of Beauty — a masterwork of Ipp wood and [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) carvings, blessed by the Archdruid of the [Gnarley](gnarley-forest-location.md), meant for Her Fey Majesty [Yolande](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md) of [Celene](celene-organization.md). They slaughtered the Borderer escort. They took priests of [Rao](rao-person.md) captive. And now they sit in that priory with the Gift locked in a stone reliquary, arguing about whether to destroy it, sell it, or use it as a trophy of their madness."

 

**Insight DC 20:** Everything Lareth has said is _technically true_ — from a certain point of view. The Earth Temple garrison did take the priory. They did raid the convoy. They do have the Gift. But the word "splinter" is the lie. These are not rogue radicals — they are an organized outpost reporting to [Romag](romag-person.md), the Earth Temple Prophet, who answers to [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md), the Supreme Commander. Lareth knows this. He is painting them as unorganized fanatics so the party underestimates the threat — and so the party does not ask who _else_ in the Temple hierarchy might be interested in the Gift.

 

##### The Escalation — Why This Matters Now

Lareth builds urgency. He shifts from describing the problem to describing what happens if no one acts.

 

"If the Gift is not recovered, the consequences extend far beyond a missing box of wood. The Viscounty's relationship with [Celene](celene-organization.md) will collapse. The trade routes through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) will remain under siege. And these radicals — emboldened by their success — will grow bolder. They have already begun excavating something beneath the priory. I do not know what. But the reports suggest something ancient, something connected to the dark powers they worship."   He pauses again. Then, with what appears to be genuine emotion:   "And the priests. The priests of [Rao](rao-person.md) taken from the delegation — some may still be alive. Held in cells beneath the priory. Used as leverage, or worse. Every day we delay is a day closer to them being moved to [Nulb](nulb-settlement.md) or killed outright. I cannot — in good conscience — sit by while servants of peace rot in a cultist dungeon."   He lets that settle. Then the ask.   "I need you to go to the priory. I need you to recover the Gift of Beauty — before these fanatics destroy it or use it to do irreparable damage. I need you to free any prisoners you find. And I need you to bring the Gift to me, so that I can ensure it reaches the priests of [Rao](rao-person.md) in the Kingdom of Veluna, where it can be properly escorted to its rightful destination."

 

##### The Party Pushes Back — Lareth Responds

Experienced players will have questions. Lareth is prepared for all of them. The DM should use these responses as needed when the party challenges his story.

 

**"Why don't you go yourself?"**

 

A self-deprecating smile. "Because I am one man with a dispatch case and a sword I use poorly. I am not a warrior. I am not a mage. I am a functionary who has stumbled onto intelligence that demands action — and the only action I can take is to find people capable of doing what I cannot."

 

**Insight DC 22:** This is a masterful lie. Lareth is an accomplished fighter and cleric with significant combat ability. But his delivery is flawless — the self-deprecation, the slight embarrassment, the way he looks at his own hands as if acknowledging their inadequacy. He has rehearsed this.

 

**"Why not go to the Viscount? The Mounted Borderers?"**

 

"I wish I could. But the Viscount's office cannot act openly. If word spreads that the Gift was stolen by agents of the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md), it confirms that Verbobonc's roads are not safe — that the Temple's reach extends into the heartland. The Viscount would face panic, political crisis, and demands from every lord in the Viscounty. He needs this resolved quietly. Deniably. That is why people like me exist — and why I need people like you."

 

**Insight DC 18:** This is actually true. The Viscount _does_ need the Gift recovered quietly. Lareth has simply placed himself in the role of the Viscount's cutout — a role that actually belongs to Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md). The party has no way of knowing this unless they have already received a legitimate quest starter.

 

**"How do you know so much about this priory?"**

 

He hesitates — a calculated pause that suggests he is deciding how much to trust you. Then, quietly:   "Because I have been investigating these radicals for months. I have contacts along the Long Road — informants who watch the routes, who track movements. One of them managed to get close enough to the priory to map its approaches before he was... no longer able to report."   A moment of what appears to be genuine grief.   "I know the priory's layout. I know the garrison strength — roughly twenty to thirty cultists, with ogres and gnolls as shock troops. I know there is a war-priest who commands the garrison and an arcane advisor who runs their intelligence. And I know the Gift is locked in a reinforced reliquary in the war-priest's quarters, sealed with three locks — one physical, one divine, and one held by a key that is not even in the building."

 

**DM Notes:** This intelligence is **entirely accurate**. Lareth is describing [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md) (the war-priest), [Caidax Nightshade](caidax-nightshade-person.md) (the arcane advisor), and the reliquary in AO-18. The three-lock system is correct. Romag's key is not at the priory. Lareth knows all of this because he has his own intelligence network and because he sent an emissary to the priory three tendays ago (who was rejected by Tharvok). The accuracy of his information is what makes him so dangerous — the party cannot catch him in a factual error.

 

**"What do you get out of this?"**

 

A long look. Then, with quiet intensity:   "I get to sleep at night. I get to know that the Gift of Beauty — a symbol of everything the common folk of Verbobonc hoped for — was not ground to dust by fanatics in a basement. I get to know that priests of Rao are not dying in chains while men with the power to act did nothing."   A pause.   "And yes — I will not lie to you — if the Gift is recovered through my efforts, it advances my position within the Viscounty. I am an ambitious man. I do not pretend otherwise. But my ambition and your duty align perfectly here. The Gift recovered. The prisoners freed. The radicals broken. Everyone wins."

 

**Insight DC 22:** The admission of ambition is the most brilliant piece of the performance. By confessing to a small, understandable vice (career ambition), Lareth inoculates himself against suspicion of larger ones. The party thinks: _"At least he's honest about wanting the credit."_ They do not think: _"He is a cult commander who worships the Demon Queen of Spiders and wants to deliver the Gift to his dark goddess."_

 

##### The Confrontation — If the Party Is Skeptical

Some parties — especially experienced players — will not accept Lareth's story at face value. They will probe, challenge, and test. Lareth does not crumble under pressure. He **rises to meet it** — because confrontation is where his charisma shines brightest.

 

**"We don't know you. Why should we trust a stranger?"**

 

He does not flinch. He does not deflect. He meets your eyes.   "You should not. Trust is earned, not given. And I have done nothing yet to earn yours."   He places both hands flat on the table — open, visible, weaponless.   "But consider this: I have given you the location of a cult fortress, the composition of its garrison, the location of the Gift within it, and the knowledge that prisoners may be held there. If I were lying — if I were leading you into a trap — why would I arm you with intelligence that could save your lives?"   A beat.   "I am asking you to do something dangerous. The least I can do is make sure you go in with your eyes open."

 

**"Prove you work for the Viscount."**

 

He reaches into his cloak and produces a sealed document — heavy parchment, the Viscount's seal in red wax. It looks authentic. The language is formal and vague — the kind of letter that authorizes an agent to act "in the interests of the Viscounty" without specifying what those interests are.   "I cannot prove it to the standard you deserve. These letters are deliberately vague — that is the nature of deniable operations. What I can tell you is this: if you travel to [Deleveu Manor](2-deleveu-manor-landmark.md) and ask Lord [Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md), he will confirm that the Gift was lost under his protection. He may even know of my involvement — we operate through different channels, but toward the same goal."

 

**DM Notes:** The sealed document is a **forgery** — an excellent one. Investigation DC 20 to detect inconsistencies in the wax seal (slightly wrong shade of red, the impression is a hair too deep — it was pressed from a stolen copy, not the original signet). The mention of Lord Deleveu is a calculated risk: Lareth knows Deleveu is involved in the Gift recovery. If the party goes to Deleveu first, they will discover the truth and Lareth's cover is blown — but Lareth is gambling that the party will go to the priory _first_ and check with Deleveu _later_.

 

**"You're lying. You're part of this cult, aren't you?"**

 

For the first time, real emotion crosses his face — or what appears to be real emotion. His jaw tightens. His eyes harden. He stands.   "If I were part of this cult, you would be dead. Not threatened. Not warned. Dead. I would not be sitting in a room offering you intelligence and asking for your help. I would have arranged an ambush on the road south and taken what I wanted from your corpses."   He takes a breath. Controls himself. Sits back down.   "I understand your caution. I respect it. In your position, I would ask the same questions. But every moment we spend debating my credentials is a moment those priests spend in chains. So I will ask you plainly: will you act, or will you sit here questioning the one person who is trying to help?"

 

**Insight DC 24:** This is Lareth's emergency response — righteous anger deployed as a weapon. By reacting with controlled fury to the accusation, he forces the party into a defensive posture. They feel guilty for questioning him. They feel the urgency of the missing priests. And they feel the social pressure of a man who appears to have been genuinely insulted by a false accusation. It is **masterful emotional manipulation** — and at DC 24, almost impossible to see through.

 

### The Priory Intelligence — What Lareth Shares

If the party agrees (or even hesitates toward agreement), Lareth shares tactical intelligence about the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md). All of this information is **accurate** — because accurate intelligence gets the party into the priory and onto the Earth Temple's throat, which is exactly what Lareth wants.

 

Lareth unfolds the hand-drawn map and begins marking positions with a charcoal stick.   "The priory is built into a hillside. The main entrance is a fortified gatehouse — guarded, always, by at least two sentries with ogre backup inside. You will not walk through the front door without a fight."   He marks a second point.   "There is a secondary entrance — a drainage channel on the western slope. Narrow. Wet. Unpleasant. But it bypasses the gatehouse entirely and puts you inside the lower level, near the cell blocks where prisoners are held."   He taps the center of the map.   "The Gift is in the war-priest's quarters — the north wing, upper level. The reliquary is iron-banded stone, triple-locked. You will need to either take the war-priest's key from his body, find a way to defeat the divine ward, and accept that the third lock — [Romag](romag-person.md)'s key — is not in the building. Brute force is possible, but it will make noise. A great deal of noise."   He looks up.   "The garrison is approximately twenty-five combatants. Black Earth cultists in stone armor. Monks with golden gargoyle masks. Gnolls. Ogres. And one arcane advisor who has been running intelligence operations from a chamber on the upper level. He is the most dangerous mind in the building. If you can neutralize him early, the rest of the garrison loses coordination."

### Attempt 4: The Bandit "Assistance"

If the party agrees to the mission but expresses concern about the garrison's strength, Lareth plays his second card: reinforcements.

 

Lareth studies you for a moment — assessing your numbers, your equipment, your confidence.   "You are capable. I have no doubt of that. But twenty-five cultists with ogres and an arcane advisor — that is not a fight won with courage alone. You need numbers."   He hesitates — another calculated pause, as if debating whether to reveal a secret.   "I have access to... irregular forces. Not soldiers. Not men I would trust with a diplomatic mission. But fighters who can hold a corridor and break a shield wall. Mercenaries. A company of hard men — led by a captain called Gattas Void. They are brutal, but they follow orders. My orders."   He raises a hand to forestall objection.   "I know. Mercenaries are not the allies anyone would choose. But they serve a purpose. They absorb the garrison's attention while you move through the priory, secure the Gift, and free the prisoners. When the job is done, they withdraw. I handle them. You never deal with them again."

 

**What Lareth does not say:** The bandits answer to [Gattas Void](gattas-void-person.md), who answers to Lareth. Their orders are not "support the adventurers." Their orders are: **"When the party has the Gift in hand, take it from them. Kill them if necessary. Bring the Gift to me."** The bandits are not reinforcements. They are the ambush team that activates at the moment of the party's greatest vulnerability — when they have just fought through the priory and are carrying the prize.

 

**DM Notes — Running the Bandits:**

*   If the party accepts the bandits, play them as **competent but surly** allies during the priory assault. They fight well. They take hits. They hold corridors. They do everything Lareth promised — right up until the moment the Gift changes hands
*   **Perception DC 16 (during the assault):** A character who watches the bandits closely notices that Gattas Void keeps track of _the party_ as much as the enemy. His eyes follow whoever is carrying treasure. His warriors shift position to stay between the party and the exit
*   **Insight DC 18 (during the assault):** Gattas Void takes instructions from no one in the party. He nods, grunts, and does what he was already planning to do. If a party member gives an order that conflicts with his positioning, he ignores it
*   The betrayal can come at any point after the Gift is recovered — in the priory, on the road out, or at the rendezvous point where Lareth is waiting

### The Betrayal

#### Scenario A: The Bandit Ambush Inside the Priory

 

If the party recovers the Gift with the bandits still present, Gattas Void makes his move.

 

The reliquary lies open. The Gift of Beauty — a box of luminous Ipp wood carved with the likeness of [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) — rests in your hands. For a moment, the priory is quiet. The Earth Temple garrison is broken. The prisoners are free. You have won.   Then Gattas Void steps forward. His hand rests on the pommel of his sword. His cold eyes fix on whoever holds the Gift.   "That's far enough. The boss wants the box. Hand it over and walk away — or don't."   The other bandits shift. Suddenly, they are no longer standing beside you. They are standing _around_ you. Blocking the doors. Filling the corridor. And their weapons — which moments ago were pointed at the Earth Temple — are now pointed at you.  

 

#### Scenario B: The Road Ambush — Lareth Waiting

 

If the party exits the priory with the Gift and travels to the agreed rendezvous point, Lareth is waiting — but he is not alone. His bandits under Gattas Void are positioned in the trees. The moment is designed to look like a friendly handoff — until it isn't.

 

You find him where he said he would be — at the crossroads south of the [Laughing Dog Inn](the-laughing-dog-inn-landmark.md), standing beside a saddled horse with the easy composure of a man who has been waiting patiently. He smiles when he sees you. That same warm, genuine smile.   "You did it. I knew you would."   He steps forward, hand extended.   "May I see it? The Gift — just to confirm it's intact before we arrange transport."   If you hand it over — or even hold it out for inspection — the treeline erupts. Arrows from three positions. Bandits charging from the brush on both flanks. And Lareth — the mask finally dropping — steps back, the Gift in his hands, and his smile turns cold.   "I appreciate your service. Truly. You have done what I could not do alone — broken the Earth Temple's grip on the priory and delivered me the most politically valuable object in the Viscounty."   He tucks the Gift under his arm.   "I would offer an apology, but I suspect it would lack sincerity. You understand — this was never about the Viscount, or the priests, or the common folk. This was about power. And you have just given me a great deal of it."

 

**Combat encounter:** [Lareth](lareth-person.md) (cleric/fighter), Gattas Void, 8–10 bandits, positioned for a prepared ambush. Lareth oversees from the rear, lending cleric spells to control the field while his forces press the party. If the tide turns against him, he flees — with or without the Gift. He is pragmatic. A dead Lareth serves no one. A living Lareth with the Gift serves [Lolth](lolth-person.md).

### The Endgame: Lolth's Web

What does [Lareth](lareth-person.md) actually want with the Gift of Beauty? He does not want it for himself. He does not want it for the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). He wants it for [Lolth](lolth-person.md).

 

#### The Lolth Connection

 

[Lareth the Beautiful](lareth-person.md) is [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s primary infiltrator within the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). His partner, [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md), operates from within the Temple's third level — an 8th-level magic-user who possesses the Orb of Golden Death and holds secret loyalty to the Demon Queen of Spiders. Together, they form the two prongs of [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s infiltration: Lareth from the outside (the Moathouse, the road, the field operations), [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) from the inside (the Temple's power structure, the elemental nodes, the Orb).

 

[Falrinth](falrinth-person.md)'s loyalty to [Lolth](lolth-person.md), hidden behind [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md)'s trust, further destabilizes internal Temple politics. As Falrinth himself has written: _"The fools of this Temple squabble for power like ants. Soon, they will be bound in Lolth's web."_

 

##### Why Lolth Wants the Gift

The Gift of Beauty is not a magical artifact. It has no inherent power. But it has **political value** beyond measure — and [Lolth](lolth-person.md) does not think in terms of magic. She thinks in terms of **leverage**.

 

*   **Humiliate the surface elves:** The Gift bears carvings of [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) — [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s eternal enemy, the Creator who cast the drow into the Underdark. A gift bearing Corellon's image, stolen on its way to the elven queen, and delivered to the Demon Queen of Spiders? The symbolism is devastating. It is a declaration that Lolth's reach extends to the surface, that even the offerings meant for the Creator can be taken
*   **Destabilize the Celene alliance:** If the Gift disappears permanently — not destroyed but _taken_ — [Celene](celene-organization.md)'s isolationist faction gains power. Queen [Yolande](yolande2C-her-fey-majesty-person.md)'s moderates lose their argument for cooperation with human nations. The alliance between Verbobonc and Celene weakens. The southern frontier becomes vulnerable. And [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s Underdark operations in the region face less surface-world opposition
*   **Collapse the Temple from within:** If Lareth steals the Gift from under the Earth Temple's nose — using adventurers as his weapon — it proves that [Romag](romag-person.md)'s people cannot protect what they steal. [Hedrack](hedrack-person.md) loses confidence in the Earth Temple. The inter-faction rivalry between Earth, Air, Fire, and Water intensifies. The Temple's command structure fragments. And in the chaos, [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) moves to seize the elemental nodes and the Orb of Golden Death for [Lolth](lolth-person.md)
*   **Bind the Gift into Lolth's web of relics:** Through [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md)'s ritual knowledge and access to the elemental nodes, the Gift could be corrupted, consecrated to [Lolth](lolth-person.md), and transformed from a symbol of cooperation into a trophy of domination — displayed in the Underdark as proof that the Spider Queen's reach is absolute

### All Possible Outcomes — The Game Theory

The beauty of Lareth's scheme is that **every outcome works for him**. The DM should understand all possibilities:

##### Outcome 1: The Party Recovers the Gift and Delivers It to Lareth

Best case for Lareth. He takes the Gift, disappears, and delivers it to [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) at the [Temple of Elemental Evil](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md). [Lolth](lolth-person.md) gains her trophy. The party eventually learns they were used. The quest to recover the Gift begins again — but now they must track Lareth to the Moathouse or deeper into the Temple's territory.

##### Outcome 2: The Party Recovers the Gift but Grows Suspicious

The party keeps the Gift instead of handing it over. Lareth is displeased but adapts. He has still **weakened the Earth Temple** — the priory's garrison is broken, [Tharvok](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s grip is shattered, and the Gift is no longer in Earth Temple hands. Lareth pursues the Gift through other channels — perhaps sending agents after the party, or arranging a second ambush on the road north.

##### Outcome 3: The Earth Temple Kills the Party

The party fails inside the priory. Lareth loses nothing — he risked no resources of his own. The Earth Temple has expended fighters, attention, and ammunition on a threat that Lareth engineered. The priory is weakened. Lareth sends another team — or waits for the next group of adventurers to pass through.

##### Outcome 4: The Bandit Betrayal Succeeds

Gattas Void's bandits take the Gift from the party by force. They bring it to Lareth. The party survives (injured, furious) and now knows they were betrayed. The hunt for Lareth becomes personal — and the Gift quest interweaves with a revenge arc against the most dangerous man in the region.

##### Outcome 5: The Bandit Betrayal Fails

The party defeats the bandits, keeps the Gift, and now has proof that their employer was lying. They know Lareth's face, his voice, his methods. They can bring this intelligence to Lord [Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md), to the [Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md), or to the Church of [Rao](rao-person.md). The Gift is safe, but the party now knows there is a second faction — beyond the Earth Temple — operating in the region.

#### Outcome 6: The Party Pretends to Work for Lareth

The boldest path. The party suspects Lareth from the beginning (or figures it out after the priory) and decides to **play along** — feeding Lareth intelligence while actually working against him. They accept Gattas Void's bandits, use them in the priory, and then turn on them when the betrayal comes. Or they pretend to deliver the Gift and use the handoff meeting to capture Lareth.

 

**This is a viable and encouraged sandbox path.** One play-tested campaign scenario had a party **pretend to befriend Lareth** and work for him, hoping to discover the TOEE's plans and leverage the factions against each other — exactly the kind of player-driven scheming that the Temple of Elemental Evil's fractured command structure makes possible. The party becomes a double agent operating inside the Temple's own faction war.

 

### DM Decision Points

*   **Does Lareth approach the party before or after they've already found the priory?** Before = maximum deception. After = Lareth appears as a tactical asset offering help the party needs
*   **Does the party realize they're being used?** If so, _when_? During Lareth's pitch? During the priory assault? After the bugbear betrayal? Each moment of revelation creates a different story
*   **Does the party play along to learn Lareth's secrets?** If yes, reward this with intelligence about the Temple's faction structure, the Moathouse's location, and hints of a deeper conspiracy (Lolth's infiltration)
*   **Does **Cookie**'s intelligence at the [Laughing Dog Inn](the-laughing-dog-inn-landmark.md) reach [Alrrem](alrrem-person.md) before or during the party's recovery attempt?** If yes, the Fire Temple may send its own team — creating a three-way collision at the priory: the party (working for Lareth), the Earth Temple garrison, and a Fire Temple strike team. Maximum chaos. Maximum sandbox
*   **Does Lareth send Gattas Void's bandits?** If the party seems too strong or too suspicious, Lareth may hold the bandits back and attempt the road ambush instead. If the party seems overwhelmed, the bandits may be genuine help — until the Gift is in hand
*   **What if the party has already met Wyn, Glorandal, or Canon Aldric?** If the party has received a legitimate quest starter, Lareth's story has competition. The party may compare notes and realize the discrepancies. This is healthy — it rewards players who investigate rather than accept the first quest-giver they meet

 

### Using This Starter with Others

GOB-QS04 is designed to **complicate** the other starters, not replace them. The richest campaigns use Lareth's approach alongside one or more legitimate quest hooks:

 

*   **QS01 (Wyn) + QS04 (Lareth):** The party receives Wyn's honest hook first. Then Lareth appears offering better intelligence and tactical support. The party must decide: trust the ranger who gave them a wooden coin and walked away, or trust the charming official who gives them a map and an army?
*   **QS03 (Aldric) + QS04 (Lareth):** Canon Aldric and Lareth are both pointing at the same priory. But Aldric wants to _investigate_ while Lareth wants the party to _assault_. The party may bring Aldric along on Lareth's mission — creating a devastating moment when the Velunan investigator realizes his allies are working for a cult operative
*   **QS04 alone:** Lareth is the only quest-giver. The party has no legitimate comparison point. This is the darkest version — the party does not learn the truth until the betrayal, and the entire Gift recovery was orchestrated by the enemy from start to finish

##### Lolth's Endgame

_"The fools of this Temple squabble for power like ants. Soon, they will be bound in Lolth's web."_  
— [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md)

The Gift of Beauty bears carvings of [Corellon Larethian](corellon-larethian-person.md) — [Lolth](lolth-person.md)'s eternal enemy. Delivering it to the Demon Queen is an act of symbolic domination: the Creator's image, stolen from surface elves, claimed by the Spider Queen. Beyond symbolism, the Gift destabilizes the [Celene](celene-organization.md) alliance, collapses the Earth Temple's credibility, and feeds the inter-faction war that allows [Falrinth](falrinth-person.md) to seize the elemental nodes.
