3\. Guest Noble House
=====================

The Noble’s Guest House in Ostverk is a distinguished residence located in the heart of the human side of the village, close to the central marketplace and other essential amenities. Built in a style reminiscent of Deleveu Manor but slightly smaller, the guest house is reserved for visiting nobles, relatives of the magistrate, and other important figures who pass through the village. Though currently unoccupied, it stands as a symbol of hospitality and prestige, reflecting the status and importance of those who stay within its walls.

### Architecture and Design

The Noble’s Guest House is a fine example of the architectural style favored by the human nobility in Ostverk. Constructed from sturdy local stone and timber, the house features elegant design elements that convey both comfort and grandeur. The interiors are tastefully decorated, with rich tapestries, finely crafted furniture, and intricate woodwork that speaks to the craftsmanship of the village's artisans.

##### Key Architectural Features

*   **Elegant Facade:** The front of the house is adorned with finely carved wooden accents and stone pillars, creating a welcoming yet imposing entrance.
*   **Spacious Interiors:** The interior rooms are large and well-lit, with high ceilings and ample space for entertaining guests or conducting private meetings.
*   **Comfortable Quarters:** The guest rooms are furnished with plush beds, soft linens, and tasteful decor, providing a luxurious stay for any visitor.
*   **Private Courtyard:** A small, enclosed courtyard at the back of the house offers a serene space for relaxation, complete with a garden tended by the house staff.

### Amenities and Services

Guests at the Noble’s Guest House can expect a range of amenities and services designed to provide comfort and convenience during their stay. The house is equipped with everything needed to cater to the needs of noble visitors, whether they are staying for a short visit or an extended period.

##### Available Amenities

*   **Fine Dining:** Earns personally prepares meals for the guests, using the best ingredients available in Ostverk. The dining room is elegantly set, and meals are served with the utmost care and attention to detail.
*   **Private Study:** The guest house includes a private study, perfect for guests who need a quiet place to work or engage in private discussions. The study is lined with bookshelves filled with a selection of literature, historical texts, and records relevant to the region.
*   **Secure Storage:** For guests who travel with valuable items, the guest house offers secure storage facilities. These are located in a well-guarded room within the house, ensuring that personal belongings are kept safe.
*   **Transport Arrangements:** Guests requiring transportation to other parts of the Viscounty or even into the Kingdom of Celene can request arrangements through Earns, who will ensure that reliable and secure transport is provided.

### Historical and Political Significance

The Noble’s Guest House plays a subtle but important role in the political landscape of Ostverk and the wider Viscounty of Verbobonc. While the house is primarily a place of hospitality, it is also a venue where significant decisions can be made, alliances forged, and discussions held in privacy.

##### Political Connections

*   [Talarien Carewlein](talarien-carewlein-person.md) and Celene: As the Borderlord of Caer Ostverk, Talarien Carewlein maintains a cautious but respectful relationship with the nobility of Verbobonc. The Noble’s Guest House often serves as a neutral ground for discussions between the elven representatives and visiting nobles from the Viscounty.
*   Lord [Corbin Deleveu](corbin-deleveu-person.md): The magistrate of Ostverk, Lord Corbin Deleveu, holds the key to the Noble’s Guest House and decides who may stay there. His approval is a sign of trust and respect, indicating the guest’s importance in the local political sphere.
*   **Verbobonc Nobility:** The guest house has hosted various members of the Verbobonc nobility, who come to Ostverk for matters of trade, diplomacy, or simply to enjoy the tranquility of the village. Their presence often brings with it discussions of regional politics, trade agreements, and other matters of importance to the Viscounty.

#### Potential Adventures and Plot Hooks

For adventurers, the Noble’s Guest House offers more than just a comfortable place to stay—it can be the setting for intrigue, mystery, and political maneuvering. Whether players are visiting Ostverk for the first time or returning after a successful adventure, the guest house can serve as a central location for various quests and storylines.

#### Conclusion

The Noble’s Guest House in Ostverk is more than just a place to rest—it is a symbol of the village’s connection to the wider world of Greyhawk, a venue for political intrigue, and a potential site for adventure. Whether hosting nobles, diplomats, or adventurers, the guest house remains a key fixture in the life of Ostverk, reflecting the village's unique blend of hospitality, tradition, and power.

#### Services and Staff

The Noble’s Guest House is maintained by a dedicated staff who ensure that the property is always in pristine condition and that guests are well cared for. The small team, led by an elder gnome named Earns, provides all the necessary services to make a noble's stay as comfortable and pleasant as possible.

##### Key Staff Members

*   **Earns, the Butler:** Earns is an elder gnome with a wealth of experience in managing noble households. He is responsible for overseeing the daily operations of the guest house, including meal preparation, maintenance, and ensuring that all guests' needs are met. Earns is known for his meticulous attention to detail and his ability to anticipate the needs of his guests.
*   **Eimma, the Maid:** Eimma, Earns' wife, serves as the maid of the guest house. She is diligent and efficient, with a warm demeanor that makes guests feel at home. Eimma is assisted by her three children, who help keep the house spotless.
*   **The Children:** Ebba, Dara, and Kaka: Eimma’s nine-year-old triplets are a curious sight to behold. The three children, always dressed in long floor-gliding skirts, move in unison as they help their mother clean the house. Their synchronized movements and tireless work ensure that every corner of the house is immaculate, with the floors and low furniture gleaming wherever they pass.
