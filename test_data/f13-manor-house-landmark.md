F13 Manor House
===============

##### The Forgotten Manor of Verbobonc

Manor House is nothing more than another failed noble residence—one of many half-forgotten buildings that litter the older streets of Verbobonc’s Foreign Quarter. Its roof sags, its shutters hang crooked, and the iron gate remains perpetually chained, bearing the weathered seal of municipal seizure.

To the casual passerby, it is a ruin awaiting sale.

To those who know how to knock—and what words to speak—it is the beating heart of the Battirovka Family, one of the most entrenched and disciplined criminal organizations operating within the city.

This manor does not advertise danger. It absorbs it.

* * *

#### **Public History: The Forgotten Manor**

Five years ago, the manor belonged to a middling mercantile family whose fortunes collapsed with suspicious speed. Records show unpaid tariffs, failed investments, and sudden debt to unnamed lenders. Then, without ceremony or alarm, the family vanished.

*   No bodies were found.
*   No accusations were proven.
*   No heirs stepped forward. With no claimants, the city seized the property and quietly listed it for sale. Repeated inspections were thwarted by structural decay, rumored hauntings, and persistent vandalism. Eventually, patrols became irregular. Then symbolic. The manor slipped from memory. Which was precisely the point.

#### **The Truth Beneath the Floorboards**

The upper floors of F13 are intentionally neglected. Rain leaks through cracked slate. Plaster crumbles. Furniture remains broken and useless. Everything aboveground is curated decay—enough to deter curiosity, but not enough to prompt demolition.

**Belowground is another matter entirely.**

Hidden behind a false wine rack in the cellar lies a reinforced stone door, expertly disguised and warded against casual discovery. Beyond it, a staircase descends into a maintained sub-basement, dry, torch-lit, and patrolled.

From here, narrow tunnels connect into abandoned sewer arteries, long severed from the active city system. These passages form a concealed network of meeting chambers, training rooms, vaults, and escape routes—collectively known among the guild as The Vaulted Hall.

This is the true seat of the Battirovka Family.

#### The Battirovka Family Presence

The Battirovka Family does not rule Verbobonc openly. They do not issue threats in the streets or leave corpses as warnings. Their power lies in **memory, obligation, and access**.

F13 Manor House serves as:

*   A **neutral council site** for senior guild members
*   A **training ground** for recruits and specialists
*   A **secure holding location** for sensitive goods and people
*   A **clearinghouse** for information moving through the Foreign Quarter

* * *

#### Description of the Manor

_First Floor_

*   **Foyer:** Grand in its day, now the foyer is littered with debris from the collapsed ceiling, with faded murals peeling off the walls.
*   **Parlor:** Once a room of luxury, the parlor is now dominated by broken furniture and a fireplace choked with soot.
*   **The Vaulted Hall:** A long stone chamber supported by repurposed foundation arches. Here, disputes are settled, contracts negotiated, and sentences delivered. No blood is spilled here unless absolutely necessary.
*   **The Counting Room:** Ledger tables, coded account books, and coin scales dominate this room. The Battirovkas keep meticulous records—debts remembered longer than faces.
*   **Dining Hall:** The large dining hall features a rotting wooden table and scattered chairs, with a chandelier precariously dangling by a single chain.

_Second Floor_

*   **Bedrooms:** Multiple bedrooms, each in varying stages of decay, their once opulent furnishings now moth-eaten and dusty.
*   **Library:** Shelves stand mostly empty, the few remaining books swollen with moisture and mold.
*   **Gallery:** This room once displayed valuable art, now only the faded outlines on the wallpaper suggest their previous existence.

#### Basement and Secret Exits

*   **General Condition:** Dark and damp, the basement holds the remnants of what might have been a wine cellar and storage rooms.
*   **Secret Tunnel:** A hidden door leads to a narrow tunnel that connects to Verbobonc’s sewage system, a pathway rumored to be used by criminals.
*   **Training Cell:** A low-ceilinged chamber fitted with balance beams, lock rigs, practice doors, and throwing targets. Silence is enforced here. Instruction is brutal and precise.
*   **The Quiet Rooms:** Sparse chambers used for rest, interrogation, or protection. Prisoners are rare—but when held, they are kept unseen and unheard.
*   **The Old Sewer Gate:** A concealed iron hatch leading into forgotten tunnels. Multiple exits branch toward docks, alleys, and foreign inns—including routes that pass dangerously close to The Pampered Polecat.

### Relationships and Politics

_Impact on the Community_

*   **Local Lore:** The manor is the subject of many local ghost stories and myths, contributing to its eerie reputation.
*   **Criminal Element:** The secret tunnels are suspected to be in use by local thieves and smugglers, creating a reluctant tolerance among city officials due to the difficulty in policing these passages.

##### Relationship to the City

City officials unofficially tolerate the manor’s continued “vacancy.” Everyone understands that attempting a full inspection would require resources, political will, and casualties—none of which the city is eager to spend.

More importantly, the Battirovkas keep **order** among thieves. Petty crime is regulated. Violence is discouraged. Targets are chosen carefully.

As long as chaos remains contained, the city looks away.

#### Motivation for Sale

_City’s Stance_

*   **Economic Burden:** The property is a drain on city resources, requiring frequent patrols to deter trespassers and vandals.
*   **Development Potential:** There is potential for redevelopment, but the cost of renovation or demolition is prohibitive without significant investment.

#### Notable Features and Future Potential

_Architectural Interest_

*   **Restoration Appeal:** For the right investor, the manor could be restored to its former glory, capitalizing on its historical architecture and prime location.
*   **Commercial Opportunity:** The space could be transformed into a boutique hotel or offices, integrating modern amenities with historical charm.

The Forgotten Manor of Verbobonc, with its layers of history and decay, awaits a new chapter that could either preserve its legacy or usher in a completely new era for the property.

##### Security & Operations

The manor is guarded subtly:

*   No permanent watch aboveground
*   Rotating sentries below
*   Traps designed to delay, not kill
*   Silent signals rather than alarms

The Battirovkas prefer escape over confrontation. If exposed, they vanish, abandoning rooms that can be reclaimed later.

They are patient.

### A Scrap from the Foreign Quarter

_(circulated among caravan guards, dockhands, and long-term lodgers)_

_“There’s a house down the foreign road what no one owns and no one breaks into.”_

They say it once belonged to merchants, though no one remembers what they sold.  
They say the family left in the night, though no one saw wagons.  
They say the city seized it, though no keys were ever taken.

The shutters hang crooked, yet never fall.  
The gate rusts, yet the chain is always sound.  
Rain goes in, but rats come out fat and clean.

No fires ever catch there.  
No squatters ever last.  
And when drunks piss on the wall, they don’t come back the next night.

Some swear they’ve heard voices beneath the cellar stones—  
not ghosts, mind you, but **men speaking low and counting slow**.

Others say it’s cursed.  
Others say it’s watched.

Most folk simply cross the street and mind their business.

_“Empty houses don’t stay empty in Verbobonc unless someone wants them so.”_
