AO-C12. The Vermin Nest
=======================

> The Vermin Nest — a natural limestone cave system beyond the breach in **AO-C11**, deep inside the western rock face of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). Three connected chambers formed by millennia of water erosion, never touched by the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) builders, never mapped, never blessed. The first chamber is the nest proper — a carrion crawler breeding ground reeking of ammonia and acid, the floor a slick depression of half-digested remains and leathery egg clusters. The second is a narrow gallery of stalactites and flowstone where a tight crevice in the south wall opens onto a natural passage leading to the Kron Hills hillside — too narrow for a human but passable for a halfling or gnome, a hidden back door that nothing in the crypt level knows about. The third is the deepest and the worst — a vaulted chamber where the cave floor drops away into a vertical fissure that descends sixty feet to a subterranean stream in the living rock. Something lives down there. Something old and patient that climbs the fissure walls when it is hungry and drags whatever it catches back into the dark water below. The duergar lost a miner named Durnik to the phase spider in this cave. His wrapped body hangs in a web above the nest chamber. His clan-token is still in his belt pouch. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has never set foot here. The cave belongs to the things that found it first.

_AO-C12. The Vermin Nest — Natural Cave System — Creature Territory — Zone E_

* * *

### The Vermin Nest

The cave system is natural limestone — formed by water erosion over geological time, long before the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) built the priory above. The caves sit inside the western face of the hill, separated from the crypt level's dressed stone by a thin wall of natural rock that the priory's builders never broke through. The duergar excavation vibrations did what centuries of weather could not — shattered the boundary and connected the crypt to the cave.

The caves are divided into three connected chambers, each progressively deeper and more dangerous. The party enters from the northeast through the breach in **AO-C11** and moves southwest and downward through increasingly raw, unworked stone. There is no light. There are no wards. There is no architecture. Everything here is natural — including the things that live in it.

The breach opens into raw stone. The dressed limestone of the crypt ends in a jagged edge, and beyond it the walls are natural — unworked, irregular, glistening with moisture. The ceiling rises sharply to fifteen feet, lost in shadow and stalactite formations. The floor slopes downward to the southwest, slick with condensation and something else — a glistening organic residue that catches your light and reflects it back in streaks of pale yellow. The stench is immediate and overpowering: ammonia, rot, and a sweet acidic undertone that burns the back of the throat. Something is nesting here. You can hear it — a wet, rhythmic scraping, like flesh dragging across stone, somewhere in the dark ahead.

* * *

### Chamber 1 — The Nest

The first and largest chamber opens directly beyond the breach from **AO-C11**. The cave is roughly thirty feet across and forty feet long, with an irregular ceiling that rises from eight feet near the breach to fifteen feet at its highest point above the center of the room. The floor is uneven natural limestone, sloping downward to the southwest. Stalagmites rise from the floor in clusters — waist-high formations that create natural cover but restrict sightlines.

The air is thick. The ammonia smell is strongest here. The temperature is 40°F — significantly colder than the crypt level — and the humidity is high enough that moisture beads on exposed metal within minutes.

##### The Carrion Crawler Nest

A shallow depression in the cave floor, roughly ten feet across and two feet deep, sits at the chamber's center. This is the nest — a slick bowl of organic debris, partially digested remains, and leathery egg clusters. The depression is coated with a layer of carrion crawler digestive mucus that glistens in any light source, reflecting pale yellow-green. The stench within five feet of the nest is overwhelming.

*   **CON DC 12** upon first entering the chamber or be **poisoned for 1 minute** from the concentrated ammonia and acid fumes. Characters who cover their mouth and nose with cloth make the save with advantage
*   **The nest contents:** Bones (animal and humanoid — ghoul remains, a few rat skeletons, insect chitin), matted fur, and 12–15 carrion crawler eggs. The eggs are leathery, translucent, each the size of a fist, clustered in groups of three to four. They pulse faintly with internal movement
*   **The eggs:** If not destroyed, a new brood hatches in 1d6+1 days — 2d6 larval crawlers that will spread through the breach into the crypt level, infesting **AO-C11** and eventually reaching the gallery. Destroying the eggs requires fire, acid, or 1 minute of deliberate effort with weapons. The adult crawlers become hostile the moment the eggs are threatened

##### The Carrion Crawlers (2)

Two adult carrion crawlers occupy this chamber — a mated pair that established the nest after entering through the natural caves from the hillside. They are ten feet long, segmented, with eight tentacles surrounding a lamprey-like mouth. They cling to the walls or ceiling when not feeding and drop onto prey from above.

*   **Behavior:** The crawlers are not aggressive by default — they are scavengers first, predators second. They attack if the party approaches the nest, threatens the eggs, or if a character is wounded and bleeding (the smell of fresh blood overrides their caution). Otherwise, they watch from the walls, tentacles twitching, gauging whether the intruders are food or threat
*   **Combat:** The crawlers fight to defend the nest. They use the ceiling and stalagmites for cover, dropping onto characters from above (Stealth +2, contested vs. Perception). Their primary attack is the tentacle cluster — **Tentacles: +8 to hit, reach 10 ft, 1d6+2 poison damage, CON DC 13 or paralyzed for 1 minute (repeat save at end of each turn)**. A paralyzed character is dragged toward the nest at 15 ft per round
*   **If both crawlers are killed:** The eggs remain viable. The DM should remind the party about the eggs — players often kill the adults and forget the clutch. If a character asks whether there are eggs, the answer is immediately obvious. If no one asks, the eggs hatch on schedule

##### The Phase Spider's Web

Fifteen feet above the nest, anchored between stalactites and the cave ceiling, a thick web structure spans a ten-foot area. The web is dense enough to support a Large creature's weight and is strung with wrapped bundles — prey the phase spider has cached for later feeding.

*   **Climbing to the web:** Athletics DC 12 (cave wall, wet limestone, handholds available via stalagmite formations). The web itself is sticky — a character who touches it must succeed on a STR DC 12 check or be restrained. The web has AC 10, HP 15, vulnerability to fire, and immunity to bludgeoning, poison, and psychic damage
*   **Durnik's body:** One of the wrapped bundles is a duergar miner — Durnik, the crew member **Grimlok Ashpick** lost to the spider. His body is desiccated but intact, wrapped in spider silk from the chest down. His face is visible — gray skin, white eyes open, expression frozen in surprise. His tool belt is still buckled: a war pick, copper chisels, 8 gp in a belt pouch, and a **flat stone token bearing Grimlok's clan-mark** — a circle bisected by a vertical line with three horizontal hash marks. Returning this token to **Grimlok** shifts his disposition from transactional to cooperative (see **AO-C04**)
*   **Other bundles:** Two wrapped giant rat carcasses (drained, worthless) and one wrapped ghoul (partially dissolved by its own acid — Investigation DC 14 reveals a tarnished copper ring on its finger worth 5 gp)

##### The Phase Spider

If the phase spider was not encountered or killed in **AO-C11**, it is here — lurking in the Ethereal Plane near its web, watching the material world through the planar boundary. It phases into the Material Plane to defend its web if the party approaches the ceiling or disturbs the wrapped bundles.

*   **HP:** 32. **AC:** 13. **Bite:** +4 to hit, 1d10+3 piercing plus 4d8 poison (CON DC 11 half). **Ethereal Jaunt:** Bonus action, shift between Material and Ethereal Plane
*   **Tactics:** Phases in, bites the most isolated character, phases out. Repeats every 1d4 rounds. If the party threatens the web, it phases in and fights to defend — this is its lair, and it will not retreat from here. If reduced to 10 HP or fewer, it fights desperately rather than fleeing
*   **Lair advantage:** In this chamber, the spider knows every surface. It does not need to cling to the ceiling — it can phase through the cave walls themselves, appearing from any direction. The party cannot predict which wall it will emerge from

### Chamber 2 — The Narrow Gallery

A low passage at the southwest end of the nest chamber leads downward and narrows to four feet wide before opening into the second cave. This chamber is smaller — twenty feet long and fifteen feet wide, with a ceiling of eight to ten feet hung with dense stalactite formations and curtains of pale flowstone. The floor is smoother than the nest chamber, worn by water flow that has long since diverted deeper underground. Shallow pools of standing water collect in depressions, clear and cold.

The Gallery is quieter than the nest. The ammonia smell fades here, replaced by the clean mineral scent of wet limestone and a faint sulfurous undertone from deeper below. The stalactite formations are dense enough to create a forest of hanging stone that restricts vertical sightlines and creates acoustic distortion — sounds bounce unpredictably, making it difficult to judge direction or distance.

##### The Kron Hills Crevice

In the south wall of the gallery, at floor level, a natural crevice opens into a narrow passage that angles upward and southeast through the hillside's rock. The passage is natural — water-eroded limestone, smooth-walled, barely eighteen inches wide at its narrowest point and no more than three feet tall throughout. It extends roughly one hundred and twenty feet through the hill before emerging at a concealed opening on the Kron Hills' western slope, screened by thornbrush and loose scree.

*   **Size restriction:** Only a **Small creature** can fit through the passage. A halfling or gnome can crawl the full distance in approximately 10 minutes, moving on hands and knees. A Medium creature cannot enter — the narrowest section is physically impassable for anyone with human-sized shoulders. Squeezing rules do not apply — the passage is simply too small
*   **Perception DC 14** to notice the crevice opening. It is partially concealed behind a flowstone curtain at floor level. A faint draft of fresh outside air flows from it — the first outdoor air the party has felt since entering the crypt. Survival DC 12 confirms the draft carries surface smells: pine, grass, and rain-washed stone
*   **The exit:** The hillside opening is on the western slope of the Kron Hills, approximately 200 feet below and half a mile southwest of the priory. The opening is natural, unmarked, and overgrown — no one knows it exists. It is not on any map. A Small character who exits here is standing on a steep hillside overlooking the Long Road valley, with a clear line of sight to the Kron Hills ridgeline to the south and the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) foothills to the west
*   **Strategic value:** This is an emergency exit for Small party members, a potential route for sending a halfling or gnome scout to fetch help, or a supply line that bypasses the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) garrison entirely. It could also serve as an entrance — a Small character could crawl in from the hillside and reach the crypt level without passing through any occupied rooms. The DM should note this passage exists but not telegraph it — let the party discover it through exploration

##### Piercers

The dense stalactite formations in the gallery conceal four **piercers** (CR 1/2 each) — living stalactite mimics that cling to the ceiling and drop onto prey passing beneath them. They are nearly indistinguishable from the natural formations around them.

*   **Perception DC 16** to spot the piercers among the natural stalactites. They are motionless until triggered. A character who specifically examines the ceiling with Investigation DC 13 notices subtle differences — the piercers are slightly darker, slightly wetter, and hang at angles inconsistent with natural mineral growth
*   **Drop attack:** When a creature passes directly beneath, the piercer drops — **+3 to hit, damage based on fall distance** (2d6 piercing from 10 ft ceiling). On a miss, the piercer lands on the ground and is helpless until it can spend 1 minute climbing back to the ceiling. They are slow, stupid, and one-shot — but four of them dropping on a party already weakened by the crawler fight can turn a controlled situation dangerous
*   **Attracted by vibration:** The piercers were drawn to this gallery by the carrion crawler activity in the adjacent chamber. They feed on the crawlers' leavings and on anything small that wanders through — bats, rats, cave insects. The party is the largest prey they have ever encountered

##### Cave Life

The gallery hosts a small ecosystem of lesser creatures drawn to the cave system from the Kron Hills hillside through the crevice and from the deeper fissure below:

*   **Giant rats (4–6):** Nesting in a crevice near the flowstone curtains. They scatter when the party enters and are not a combat threat unless cornered. They entered through the Kron Hills crevice
*   **Cave bats:** A small colony roosting in the stalactite formations. They swarm and flee if disturbed — not dangerous, but the noise and movement can trigger the piercers and alert creatures in Chambers 1 and 3
*   **Fire beetles (3):** Crawling along the gallery floor, their bioluminescent glands providing faint reddish-orange spots of light in the otherwise total darkness. Each beetle's gland provides dim light in a 10-foot radius for 1d6 days after the beetle dies. Useful if the party's light sources are failing

### Chamber 3 — The Fissure

The gallery's western end narrows to a three-foot-wide passage that descends steeply for fifteen feet before opening into the third and final chamber — a vaulted natural cavern roughly twenty-five feet across with a ceiling that rises to twenty feet at the dome's peak. The walls are slick with mineral deposits — pale yellow calcite and dark veins of iron oxide that streak the stone like old blood. The air here is colder than anywhere else in the cave system — 35°F, cold enough to see breath, carrying a deep mineral smell laced with something organic and ancient. The sound of running water is audible — distant, echoing, rising from below.

The chamber floor is not flat. It slopes inward from all sides toward a ragged fissure that splits the center of the room — a natural crack in the limestone, roughly fifteen feet long and three to five feet wide, that drops vertically into darkness. The fissure is the cave system's defining feature. Everything else — the nest, the gallery, the creatures — exists because of this crack in the earth and what flows through it.

##### The Fissure

The vertical drop is sixty feet — a sheer descent through natural limestone, the walls slick with moisture and mineral deposits, narrowing to approximately two feet wide at the bottom before opening into a subterranean stream channel carved through the bedrock of the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md). The stream is audible from the top of the fissure — a steady rush of cold water moving northeast to southwest through the deep rock.

*   **Looking down:** Darkvision or a dropped light source reveals the fissure walls — wet limestone, narrow, with horizontal ledges and crevices at various depths where creatures can rest or cling. At the bottom, the glint of moving water is visible — dark, fast-flowing, and cold. The stream channel is approximately eight feet wide and five feet tall at the waterline
*   **Climbing down:** Athletics DC 15 (wet, slick walls, narrow). The descent takes 5 minutes carefully or 2 rounds at speed with climbing gear. A fall deals 6d6 bludgeoning damage and deposits the character in the subterranean stream (see below)
*   **Dropping a light source:** A torch or lantern dropped into the fissure falls for three seconds before hitting water. During the fall, the light briefly illuminates the fissure walls — and the claw marks. Deep parallel gouges scored into the limestone at regular intervals, running vertically from the water level to the chamber floor. Something climbs this fissure regularly. The gouges are old — the oldest are smoothed by mineral deposits. The newest are sharp-edged and fresh
*   **The sound:** Beyond the water, a second sound is intermittently audible — a low, wet clicking, like mandibles or claws tapping stone in a deliberate rhythm. Perception DC 16 to distinguish it from the water noise. The sound comes from below and to the southwest, deeper in the stream channel. It stops when noise is made at the fissure's edge, then resumes after 1d4 minutes of silence

##### The Subterranean Stream

The stream at the fissure's base is a branch of the underground water system that runs through the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) — the same aquifer that feeds the spring in **AO-C04**, though the connection is indirect and separated by hundreds of feet of rock. The water is cold (38°F), fast-moving (10 ft per round), and between two and four feet deep. The stream channel extends in both directions — northeast toward the hill's interior and southwest toward deeper subterranean systems. This is a future expansion point for the DM (see DM Notes).

##### The Fissure Dweller — The Galeb Dur Hatchling

Something lives in the fissure. It is not a carrion crawler or a ghoul or anything the party has encountered before. It is older than the nest, older than the crypt, possibly older than the priory itself — a creature of the deep earth that has inhabited the subterranean stream channel for decades, feeding on whatever the water brings and whatever falls from above.

The creature is a **cave fisher** — a lobster-like ambush predator, eight feet long, with a hard chitinous shell the color of wet limestone and two massive claws adapted for clinging to vertical stone. It anchors itself to the fissure wall twenty feet below the chamber floor and extends a single filament — a thin, sticky, nearly invisible line of organic adhesive — up through the fissure opening and across the chamber floor. The filament is laid across the approach to the fissure's edge, where any creature investigating the drop will walk through it.

*   **The filament:** Perception DC 18 to spot the filament before touching it. It is gossamer-thin, translucent, and adheres to the first creature that contacts it. A creature that touches the filament must succeed on **STR DC 13** or be **restrained** and dragged toward the fissure edge at 20 feet per round. The filament has AC 17, HP 10, and is immune to all damage except slashing and fire. If the filament is cut, the cave fisher reels it in and waits 1d4 hours before laying a new one
*   **If a creature is dragged to the fissure:** The cave fisher pulls the restrained creature over the edge and down the fissure wall to its anchoring ledge (20 ft fall — 2d6 bludgeoning). It then attacks with its claws: **+5 to hit, 2d6+3 slashing damage**. It fights on its ledge where it has advantage — the ledge is three feet wide, the walls are slick, and melee attackers must be climbing or secured by rope
*   **Climbing behavior:** Once every 2d6 days, the cave fisher climbs the fissure to the chamber above and hunts. It moves slowly — one foot per round on vertical surfaces — and relies on ambush, not pursuit. It feeds on carrion crawlers (immune to their paralysis), giant rats, bats, and anything else organic. It has taken at least one ghoul. The partially dissolved remains on the ledge around its anchor point include humanoid bones — not Durnik (he is in the spider's web) but older remains. Someone or something fell down here long before the priory was built
*   **Stats — Cave Fisher:** CR 3. HP 58. AC 16 (natural armor). Speed 20 ft, climb 20 ft. **STR** 16 **DEX** 13 **CON** 14 **INT** 3 **WIS** 10 **CHA** 3. Senses: blindsight 60 ft. Adhesive filament (60 ft range). Claws: +5, 2d6+3 slashing. The cave fisher is not evil or malicious — it is a predator. It does not pursue fleeing prey beyond the fissure chamber. It anchors, fishes, feeds, and waits

**DM Note:** The cave fisher is the fissure's guardian by default, not by intent. It does not protect anything — it simply lives here and eats what comes close. The party may never encounter it if they do not approach the fissure edge. But the filament is laid across the obvious approach path, and curiosity about the sound of water and the claw marks on the fissure walls will draw characters forward. The first character to step into the filament learns that Zone E has one more predator than they expected.

### Sacred Layer

There is none. This cave system has never been touched by the divine architecture of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md). No prayers were spoken here. No wards were laid. No inscriptions were carved. The stone is natural — formed by water and time, inhabited by creatures that were here before the priory and will be here after it falls. The cave is indifferent to the conflict above. It does not care about the Azure Oath or the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) or the geomantic node beneath the Black Mouth. It is a hole in the earth where things live and feed and die, and it has been that for longer than anyone remembers.

The absence of consecration is what drew the creatures here. The ghouls, the crawlers, the spider, the cave fisher — they are all species that avoid divine ground. The ward network in the crypt pushes them away from the gallery and the tomb chambers. This cave is the one place within the priory's footprint where the earth is just earth, and the creatures have filled the gap like water filling a crack.

### DM Notes

##### Running This Room

The Vermin Nest is a three-stage combat dungeon with escalating threat. Chamber 1 is the main fight — crawlers and the phase spider, with the emotional beat of finding Durnik. Chamber 2 is a breather with minor threats (piercers, rats) and the discovery payoff of the Kron Hills crevice. Chamber 3 is the fissure — a set-piece encounter that punishes carelessness and rewards caution. Run the three chambers as a progression, not a single encounter. Let the party rest between chambers if they choose — the creatures do not coordinate across rooms.

##### The Three Discoveries

*   **Durnik's clan-token (Chamber 1):** The intelligence reward. Returning it to **Grimlok** is worth more than every piece of loot in Zone E combined. It unlocks the leather map, the Black Mouth intelligence, and shifts **Grimlok** from contractor to reluctant ally
*   **The Kron Hills crevice (Chamber 2):** The strategic reward. A hidden exit that the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) does not know exists. Small characters can use it for scouting, resupply, or escape. It also explains how the creatures entered — the ghouls came through the breach, but the giant rats and bats came through the crevice from the hillside
*   **The subterranean stream (Chamber 3):** The expansion hook. The stream connects to the deeper Kron Hills underground. The DM can use this as a future adventure entrance — Underdark connections, deeper cave systems, or a secondary route to the geomantic node that bypasses the Black Mouth entirely. For this adventure, it is simply a dangerous place with a dangerous creature

##### Encounter Budget

Zone E as a whole is designed for a party of level 4–5 that has already been through at least one combat encounter on the crypt level. The full zone, fought sequentially:

*   **Chamber 1:** 2 carrion crawlers (CR 2 each) + 1 phase spider (CR 3) = approximately 1,400 XP adjusted. Hard encounter
*   **Chamber 2:** 4 piercers (CR 1/2 each) = approximately 400 XP adjusted. Easy encounter. Intended as a palate cleanser between hard fights
*   **Chamber 3:** 1 cave fisher (CR 3) = approximately 700 XP. Medium encounter — but the terrain (fissure, wet walls, filament) makes it functionally hard
*   **Total zone XP:** approximately 2,500 XP adjusted. This is a full adventuring day's worth of combat for a 4-person party at level 4. Encourage the party to rest before attempting the fissure chamber if they cleared the nest in one push

##### Clearing the Zone

If the party kills all creatures in Chambers 1–3 and destroys the carrion crawler eggs, Zone E is effectively cleared. The cave fisher in the fissure is the exception — it does not leave the fissure chamber and poses no threat to the crypt level unless someone approaches its filament. The party can leave it alive without consequence. However, if the party wants to use the subterranean stream as a future route, the cave fisher must be dealt with first.

After clearing, seal the breach in **AO-C11** with the salvaged stone blocks and morite mortar. This permanently removes Zone E creatures from the **AO-C02** random encounter table and earns **Grimlok**'s respect.

##### Connections

*   **AO-C11 (northeast, through breach):** The Collapsed Passage. The party's entry and exit route. The breach is the bottleneck — seal it and Zone E is contained
*   **Kron Hills crevice (south wall, Chamber 2):** Natural passage to the western hillside. Small creatures only. 120 ft crawl to concealed exit. Potential scout route, escape route, or supply line
*   **Subterranean stream (Chamber 3 fissure, 60 ft down):** Underground water channel running NE–SW through the Kron Hills bedrock. Future expansion point. Connects to deeper underground systems at DM discretion

##### Carrion Crawler (×2) — CR 2

*   **AC:** 13 (natural armor). **HP:** 51 (6d10+18). **Speed:** 30 ft, climb 30 ft
*   **STR** 14 **DEX** 13 **CON** 16 **INT** 1 **WIS** 12 **CHA** 5
*   **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft, PP 11
*   **Tentacles:** +8 to hit, reach 10 ft, 1d6+2 poison. CON DC 13 or paralyzed 1 min (repeat save each turn)
