G4 Clotho’s Cheese House
========================

_A Fragrance of Festivity and Flavor  
  
_Clotho’s Cheese House is the tastiest trap in Gnomesberg: a low-ceilinged underhill parlor where waxed rounds of cheese sit like treasure, and every table is positioned so conversations overlap without seeming to.

Adventurers come for food, but leave having told Clotho far more than they meant to—where they’re staying, who they’re chasing, what they fear, and what coin they carry. Not because Clotho threatens them… but because he flatters them, feeds them, and makes them feel clever.

It’s an ideal location for:

*   social encounters that _feel_ safe
*   overheard clues and coded messages
*   faction meetings disguised as tastings
*   quiet recruitment into gnomish politics (or rival schemes)

##### Approaching Clotho’s Cheese House

The round door openeth upon warmth and scent—sharp and sweet together—like milk turned to gold. Shelves line the walls in neat ranks, stacked with waxed wheels, wrapped bundles, and jars of preserves that gleam ruby and amber in the lamplight. A slate board lists today’s tastings in looping script, though the words seem half poetry and half warning.  
  
Tiny tables dot the room, each with a candle and a little dish of apple slices. In the corner, a small barrel is tapped, and the air is thick with cheerful murmurs—gnomes speaking softly, laughing gently, and listening as though listening were a sport.  
  
Behind the counter, Clotho flashes a grin bright as a polished gem. “Ah, my darlings,” he sings, “come! Sit! Eat! Tell me everything thou hast been too proud to confess!”

### What’s Here (Layout & Atmosphere)

##### Front Shop — The Counter of Wonders

A clean, well-lit sales floor with:

*   waxed cheese wheels in crates
*   hanging bundles in cloth and twine
*   jars of pickles, honey, pepper relishes, berry preserves
*   carved wooden “sample boards” and tasting knives

##### The Tasting Parlor — Where Business Becomes Friendship

A cozy underhill lounge:

*   low ceiling with carved beams
*   tiny hearth-stove for warming bread
*   small tables carefully spaced (for “privacy” that isn’t)
*   a chalkboard menu of pairings: cheese + ale + fruit + honey

##### The Back Room — “Storage”

A curtained back area that can be:

*   simple pantry and cool cellar access (the honest version), **or**
*   a private booth for whispered meetings, **or**
*   a small hidden office with receipts and coded order lists (the useful version)

##### Prices (Simple Table Use)

Keep it flexible; it’s a narrative shop:

*   A tasting board is cheap enough to lure anyone.
*   A full “gift wheel” is expensive enough to matter.
*   The rarest cheeses are priced like luxury goods—often bought as bribes.

#### Why This Place Matters in Gnomesberg

Cheese is **culture** here: it is gift, apology, introduction, and leverage.

Clotho’s Cheese House becomes the quarter’s “soft door,” because:

*   strangers will enter for food when they won’t enter for politics
*   wealthy folk will loosen tongues after a second cup
*   gnomes will talk here because it feels like home

This makes it a perfect **information funnel** for your campaign.

### Temple of Elemental Evil & Iuz Cult Hooks

##### 1) The “Salted Wheel” Drop

A patron buys a particular cheese wheel that Clotho knows is used as a code phrase—“salted wheel for the road.” It’s not about cheese. It’s a message.

**What it means:** a meeting, a transfer, or a warning.  
**What the party can do:** tail, intercept, replace the delivery, or turn the courier.

##### 2) A Taste of Fear

A gnome caravan master drinks too much, then admits someone has been threatening his family—“pay us, or we tell the city you’re a traitor.” He thinks it’s a guild racket… but the language reeks of Iuzite intimidation.

**Non-combat win:** protect the family, expose the blackmailer, leverage the threat back.

##### 3) The Strange Purchase

A human clerk buys large quantities of wax, cloth wrap, and “long-lasting hard cheese.” That’s logistics—packaging for travel and storage. Too much for a household.

**Temple angle:** provisioning for hidden movement.  
**Clotho’s angle:** he wants proof before he accuses.

##### 4) The Cheese Knife That Doesn’t Fit

A “patron” leaves behind a blade with a maker’s mark tied to known troublemakers. Auntie Nibbet spots it instantly and demands it be dealt with—quietly.

* * *

#### Non-Combat Leverage & Social Play

This location rewards parties who talk instead of fight:

*   **Make friends with workers:** Auntie Nibbet becomes a powerful ally if treated with respect.
*   **Buy gifts strategically:** a fine cheese wheel can open doors in Gnomesberg faster than gold.
*   **Listen for coded talk:** “pairings” and “orders” can double as signals.
*   **Use the parlor as neutral ground:** set meetings here; hide allies in plain sight.

### Scenes You Can Run Immediately

##### Scene 1: The Tasting Test

Clotho offers the party a “house board” and asks a question between bites:  
“So… what bringeth thee to Gnomesberg, darling? Adventure? Profit? Or trouble with a fine coat?”

If they answer well, he opens up. If they answer poorly, he still opens up—just not in the way they expect.

##### Scene 2: The Loud Laugh

A patron laughs too loudly at the wrong name (a noble house, a guildmaster, a temple). The room goes quiet for one heartbeat, then resumes. That heartbeat matters.

The party can:

*   notice it (Insight/Perception)
*   ask who the name belongs to
*   learn they’ve stepped on a real political wire

##### Scene 3: Back Room Courtesy

A servant slips the party a napkin with a tiny note:  
“Rear curtain. Two minutes. Come alone.”

Behind it is either:

*   a Free Alliance courier
*   a frightened witness
*   or a Temple agent trying to recruit them with honeyed lies

### Secrets & DM Tools

##### The “Comfort Trap” Effect

If the party sits, eats, and relaxes:

*   they become easier to read
*   more willing to share
*   and more likely to be overheard

Clotho doesn’t need to spy. He just needs them comfortable.

##### Receipts as Evidence

Clotho keeps immaculate receipts. If the party earns his trust, he can provide:

*   a purchase date
*   a quantity
*   a name (or false name)
*   a pattern repeated

It’s not “proof,” but it’s **the start of a case**.

#### Rewards

*   A **gift wheel** sealed with Clotho’s mark (social advantage with gnomes, merchants, and riverfolk)
*   A **receipt copy** pointing to suspicious logistics purchases
*   A **soft introduction** to a Free Alliance contact
*   A single-use **“private tasting” invitation**—a covert meeting in plain sight

* * *

##### Clotho (Owner; Social Alchemist)

Clotho is at his most dangerous here—because everyone is disarmed by comfort. He moves between tables like a friendly song, topping cups, adjusting plates, making compliments, and catching rumors as if they were falling coins.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Offers food the way a priest offers blessing.
*   Asks questions that sound like jokes.
*   Remembers everyone’s preferred flavor… and their last lie.

##### “Auntie Nibbet”

(Head Cheesemaker / House Matron)

A stout older gnome with flour on her sleeves and a stare that can curdle milk. She loves Clotho like family and distrusts outsiders by default.

**Roleplay cues**

*   Short sentences. Heavy meaning.
*   Calls adventurers “knife-folk” when she thinks they can’t hear.
*   Will soften only if the party treats workers kindly.
