AO-C05. The Founders' Sepulchre
===============================

> The Founders' Sepulchre — the most sacred chamber in the Crypts of the Azure Oath, sealed behind the Arch of First Oath in the warded south section of the Gallery of the Vigilant Dead (**AO-C02**). A circular room thirty feet in diameter with a domed ceiling twelve feet at the apex, housing the twelve founding knights of the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md)'s Azure Oath chapter — the warriors who established the [Sepulchre-Priory](the-sepulchre-priory-of-the-azure-oath-settlement.md) when it was dedicated in -12 CY. Twelve burial alcoves ring the walls at equal intervals, each sealed with carved stone slabs bearing the knight's name, rank, and the inscription \*"First Oath."\* The wards here are at absolute maximum strength — founding-era consecrations immune to all conventional breach attempts. At the center of the chamber, a cold bronze brazier sits on a stone pedestal, once used for commemorative vigils. The Azure Oath's sigil — a clenched fist gripping a bolt of lightning — is carved into the dome's keystone. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has never set foot in this room. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md)'s acolytes were repelled by the Arch ward. Twice. They no longer approach. The south wall holds the sealed door to **AO-C06** — the Champions' Rest — protected by a physical riddle-lock carved with lightning motifs that demands knowledge of a warrior's tools before it will open.

_AO-C05. The Founders' Sepulchre — The Holiest Ground Beneath the Priory — Zone C, Warded Wing_

* * *

### The Founders' Sepulchre

The Arch of First Oath opens into silence. Not the absence of sound — the presence of it. The kind of silence that five centuries of unbroken faith leaves behind in stone. The air beyond the blazing ward-curtain is cold and still and tastes of mineral dust and old bronze. The gallery's blue-white glow spills through the archway and falls across a circular chamber that has not been entered by a living soul since **Halven Sere** last descended to pray — years before the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) came.

This is the heart of the crypts. The room where the Azure Oath began and where its founders sleep. Everything else in the crypt complex — the gallery, the looted wing, the ossuary, the excavation tunnel — exists in relation to this chamber. The wards radiate outward from here. The consecration originates here. The faith that still holds the line against the [Elder Elemental God](cult-of-the-elder-elemental-god-organization.md)'s influence was laid down in this room, by these twelve knights, when they sealed their first brothers into the wall and swore the oath that named the chapter.

The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has never entered this room. The ward on the Arch of First Oath has held against every attempt. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) documented his failures in his excavation journal — geomantic resonance, brute force, ritual dispelling. Nothing worked. This is why **Halven Sere** is alive. This is why the interrogations in **AO-20** focus not on treasure but on how to open the doors that will not open.

The ward-curtain parts around you like water — cold, bright, and heavy with the weight of something older than the stone. Beyond it, the air changes. Colder. Stiller. The smell of mineral dust and bronze. You stand in a circular chamber walled in pale limestone, twelve alcoves cut into the walls at even intervals, each sealed with a carved stone slab. The ceiling is domed, rising to a keystone twelve feet above where a clenched fist gripping a bolt of lightning is carved in deep relief. At the center of the room, a stone pedestal holds a bronze brazier — cold, dark, coated in centuries of verdigris. The blue-white glow from the archway behind you is the only light. It falls across the floor in a bright wedge and stops at the pedestal's base, as if the room's own darkness has drawn a boundary. Nothing has been disturbed. Nothing has been touched. The dust on the floor is undisturbed except for one set of old footprints — bare feet, walking slowly from alcove to alcove, pausing at each one. **Halven Sere**'s last visit.

* * *

### Chamber Layout

The Founders' Sepulchre is circular — thirty feet in diameter, with walls of dressed pale limestone laid by the original masons in -12 CY. The floor is flagstone, each stone carved with a single line of Old Oeridian script running in a continuous spiral from the entrance to the center pedestal — the First Oath itself, the founding prayer that named the chapter and dedicated the priory to [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md). The text is legible with a Religion DC 10 check (Old Oeridian, archaic liturgical dialect). The ceiling is a smooth dome rising to twelve feet at the apex, with the Azure Oath's sigil — the clenched fist and lightning bolt — carved into the keystone in meticulous relief.

*   **Entrance:** North — the Arch of First Oath, opening to **AO-C02** (the Gallery of the Vigilant Dead). The arch is the only way in from the gallery. The ward mechanics are detailed in the **AO-C02** article
*   **Exit:** South — a sealed stone door leading to **AO-C06** (the Champions' Rest). Protected by the Seal of the Champions, a physical riddle-lock (see below)
*   **Lighting:** None inherent. The ward-glow from the Arch of First Oath provides dim blue-white illumination in the north half of the chamber. The south half is in darkness unless the party brings light. If the bronze brazier is lit, the entire chamber blazes with blue-white consecrated flame
*   **Temperature:** 48°F — noticeably colder than the gallery. The consecrated stone radiates a chill that is not natural cold but ward-pressure — the accumulated weight of divine energy compressed into a small space
*   **Smell:** Mineral dust, old bronze, cold limestone. No decay. The founding-era wards have preserved the chamber perfectly — no rot, no mold, no organic deterioration of any kind
*   **Sound:** Absolute stillness. The ward-curtain on the arch filters sound in both directions — voices from the gallery do not carry here, and sounds made in the sepulchre do not reach the gallery. The only audible sound is a low, subsonic hum — the ward network's resonance, felt more than heard, pulsing at the same rhythm as **Halven Sere**'s prayer cadence above

* * *

### The Twelve Founder Alcoves

Twelve burial alcoves ring the chamber walls at equal intervals — one for each founding knight of the Azure Oath chapter. The alcoves are cut in the Nordic horizontal style: three feet deep, two feet tall, six feet long, sealed with carved stone slabs set flush with the wall. Each slab bears the knight's name, their rank at death, and the inscription _"First Oath"_ in Old Oeridian — the same two words, repeated twelve times around the room, a circle of commitment that has held for five hundred and eighty-eight years.

The wards on these alcoves are founding-era consecrations — the strongest tier in the crypt complex. They cannot be removed, dispelled, or bypassed by any physical or magical means short of a _Wish_ spell. They can only be opened by speaking the Final Seal prayer, known to **Halven Sere**. The slabs glow with a faint blue-white light — constant, steady, unwavering. This is the original light of the founding consecration, still burning after nearly six centuries. Detect Magic reveals overwhelming abjuration — strong enough to cause a momentary headache in the caster (no mechanical effect, purely atmospheric).

##### Knight-Commander Theran Valegate

The largest alcove, directly opposite the entrance on the south wall beside the sealed door. Valegate's slab bears a carved portrait in shallow relief — a stern, bearded face with calm eyes and a jaw set in quiet determination. He does not look heroic. He looks tired and certain. The inscription reads: _"He drew the line. The line held."_

*   **Opening the alcove:** Only the Final Seal prayer (known to **Halven Sere**) can unseal this slab. **Halven** will not speak the prayer lightly — he requires the party to demonstrate genuine respect for the dead and a clear reason to disturb the Knight-Commander's rest. Persuasion DC 14, with advantage if the party has already lit the bronze brazier or restored any desecrated tombs in **AO-C03**
*   **Contents:** Valegate's remains in full plate armor (ceremonial, non-functional — the metal has bonded with the consecrated stone and cannot be removed). His personal effects are intact: a **consecrated longsword (+1, extra 1d6 radiant vs. undead)**, his **Knight-Commander's signet ring** (worth 100 gp, grants advantage on Persuasion checks when dealing with [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) clergy), and his **personal prayer book** (Old Oeridian, contains the Final Seal prayer, the Dawn Invocation, and a handwritten copy of the First Oath with personal annotations in the margins). The prayer book also contains a written record of the riddle sequence for the Seal of the Champions — Valegate's own notes, as he designed the lock
*   **The signet ring — campaign intelligence:** The Valegate family name is still known in Verbobonc. A History DC 14 check identifies the crest as belonging to a minor noble house that held lands near the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) border. The family still exists — reduced to merchants, but proud. Returning the ring to the Valegate descendants in Verbobonc opens a political subplot and grants the party a noble contact (see [Religion in Verbobonc](religion-in-verbobonc-article.md))

##### Dame Seripha Dawnshield

Third alcove clockwise from the entrance. Her slab bears a shield motif in relief — a round shield with a sunburst pattern, the rays carved so finely they catch the ward-light and seem to glow independently. Inscription: _"She carried the dawn into the dark."_

*   **Opening the alcove:** Same requirements as Valegate's — Final Seal prayer from **Halven Sere**, with the same Persuasion DC 14
*   **Contents:** Seripha's remains in plate armor (ceremonial, bonded to stone as with Valegate). The **Dawnshield** still rests on her arm — a **+1 shield that sheds bright light in a 10-foot radius and dim light for an additional 10 feet when the wielder takes the Dodge action**. The light lasts until the start of the wielder's next turn. The shield is consecrated and cannot be removed from the alcove by a character who is not good-aligned — the hand simply cannot grip it, as if the metal were coated in oil. A good-aligned character lifts it easily
*   **DM note — weight of the choice:** Seripha's body shifts when the shield is removed. Her arm falls to her side. The gap where the shield rested is visible — a pale outline in the stone where the consecration held the metal in place for centuries. This is not a trap. It is a consequence. The party has taken a weapon from a dead woman's hand. **Halven Sere**, if present, says nothing. He looks away

##### The Remaining Ten Founders

The other ten alcoves contain the founding knights of the Azure Oath — warriors whose names appear on the slabs but whose personal effects are less individually significant. Each alcove contains: consecrated plate armor (bonded, non-removable), a longsword or mace (functional but not magical — standard steel, worth 15 gp each if removed), a holy symbol of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) (silver, worth 15 gp each), and a personal memento (a letter, a carved figure, a lock of hair sealed in resin).

*   **Named founders (slab inscriptions):** Sir Garent Oathforge (_"First to kneel"_), Dame Korrath Shieldborn (_"Last to leave"_), Sir Aldren Brightmail (_"He built the wall"_), Sir Dravos Ironhearth (_"Kept the fire"_), Dame Illara Stonewatch (_"Eyes open, always"_), Sir Bennick Truemark (_"His word was law"_), Dame Osra Lightmantle (_"She sang them home"_), Sir Haldric Greyhelm (_"Blood of the line"_), Sir Morath Duskward (_"Guard until dawn"_), Dame Elain Oathkeeper (_"Named the name"_)
*   **Sir Haldric Greyhelm — note:** The surname Greyhelm connects to Knight-Commander Aldara Grayhelm, sealed in **AO-C07**. History DC 16 identifies Haldric as an ancestor — the Grayhelm/Greyhelm lineage served the Azure Oath from founding to withdrawal. This is the longest unbroken service record in the chapter's history
*   **Looting the founders:** Any character who removes items from the founder alcoves without **Halven Sere**'s blessing must make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw. On failure, the character suffers a lingering sense of guilt and reproach — disadvantage on Charisma checks made in the presence of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) clergy or devout followers for 7 days. This is not a curse — it is conscience. A _Remove Curse_ spell does nothing. The effect fades naturally or can be absolved by performing a significant act of restitution (restoring desecrated tombs, returning stolen grave goods, defeating the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) presence in the priory)

### The Bronze Brazier

At the center of the chamber, a stone pedestal three feet tall supports a bronze brazier two feet in diameter. The brazier is dark, cold, and coated in a patina of green verdigris — it has not been lit since the order's withdrawal in 562 CY. The pedestal is carved with the twelve founders' names in a ring, mirroring the alcove arrangement. The brazier itself is a simple bowl with no ornamentation — the masons valued function over decoration in this room.

*   **Lighting the brazier:** Requires consecrated oil. A flask of consecrated oil can be found in **AO-15** (the Custodian's Quarters above). Standard lamp oil produces ordinary fire with no special effect. Holy water poured into the brazier produces a brief flash of blue light (1 round) but does not sustain the flame
*   **Effect — consecrated flame:** When lit with consecrated oil, the brazier erupts with **blue-white fire** that burns without heat and illuminates the entire chamber in bright light. The flame does not consume the oil — it burns indefinitely once ignited. Effects:
*   **Ward flare:** All divine wards in the crypt complex surge to maximum strength for 24 hours. Any degraded wards in **AO-C02** and **AO-C03** temporarily restore to full power. The restless skeletons in **AO-C08** collapse immediately and do not reanimate for the duration
*   **Sigil beacon:** The Azure Oath's sigil in the dome keystone glows with intense blue-white light — visible from the gallery, visible from the staircase in **AO-C01**, visible as a faint blue pulse in the earth from the chapel above (**AO-14**). Any [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) cleric or paladin within one mile senses the activation as a warm pressure behind the sternum
*   **Preservation surge:** The founding-era wards, already immune to breach, intensify further — the slab-glow brightens from faint to steady, and the ward-echo figures in the Arch of First Oath become sharply defined, almost solid. The entire warded wing radiates consecrated energy
*   **DM note:** Lighting the brazier is the single most powerful restorative action the party can take in the crypt complex. It should feel significant. Describe it with weight — the flame catching, the light spreading, the twelve names on the pedestal illuminating one by one, the dome sigil blazing to life above. The founders respond to this. Not as undead, not as spirits — but as a wave of warmth and presence that passes through the room and is gone. The party feels welcomed. Then the room is still again, and the flame burns alone

##### The Gift of Beauty Interaction

The Gift of Beauty is a recent diplomatic treasure seized by [Romag](romag-person.md)'s raiders — it has no historical connection to the priory. However, if the party recovers the Gift from **AO-18** and places it on the bronze brazier (whether the brazier is lit or not), the Gift's concentrated arcane significance interacts with the consecrated wards in an unintended resonance.

*   **Immediate effect:** The twelve founder alcoves glow simultaneously — all twelve slabs flare bright blue-white for six seconds, then settle to a steady radiance twice their normal intensity. The ward on the Arch of First Oath intensifies. The sealed door to **AO-C06** hums with energy
*   **Geomantic suppression:** The geomantic pulse from the Black Mouth slows from its normal rhythm (once per minute) to once per ten minutes. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md), if still alive, senses this immediately through tremorsense and is alarmed — his excavation timetable is disrupted. The Flan ward-stones strengthened
*   **Duration:** The suppression effect lasts as long as the Gift remains on the brazier. Removing the Gift ends the effect in 1d4 rounds
*   **DM note:** This is not a destined return. The Gift was never meant to be here. But concentrated divine energy and concentrated arcane significance in close proximity produce interference — the same principle that makes temples hard to scry into and ley-line crossings unpredictable. The party has stumbled onto a powerful tool. Whether they use it strategically or recover the Gift for its political value is their choice

### The Seal of the Champions — Lightning Riddle Lock

The south wall of the Founders' Sepulchre holds the passage to **AO-C06** — the Champions' Rest. The passage is sealed by a heavy stone door eight feet tall and eight feet wide, carved in deep relief with a procession of twelve armored knights walking in single file toward a stylized sunrise. At the center of the door, an iron lock shaped like a clenched fist gripping a lightning bolt protrudes from the stone — life-sized, exquisitely detailed, individual knuckles and veins rendered in dark iron. The fist is clenched shut. The lightning bolt is vertical, pointing upward, rigid between the iron fingers.

The lock has no keyhole. There is no spoken passphrase. The Seal of the Champions is a physical puzzle — a test of knowledge, not faith. Where the Arch of First Oath judged the heart, the Seal of the Champions judges the mind.

##### The Riddle Columns

Flanking the sealed door, two stone pillars rise from floor to ceiling — each carved from a single block of pale limestone, three feet in diameter, covered in spiraling lightning-bolt motifs that mirror the decorative vocabulary of the Arch of First Oath. The pillars are dark and inert when the party first enters. Carved into the base of each pillar, at waist height, is a bronze plaque bearing an inscription in Old Oeridian. Below each plaque, the pillar's surface is carved with three **stone relief protrusions** — raised carvings of martial objects, each the size of a clenched fist, protruding two inches from the pillar's surface. The protrusions are mounted on internal stone mechanisms and can be pushed inward like buttons — they depress with a grinding click and hold in position.

**Left Pillar — six protrusions, three per side, arranged vertically:**

*   **Top:** A carved **helm** — a great helm with a T-shaped visor and a crest of stylized lightning bolts
*   **Middle:** A carved **shield** — a heater shield bearing the Azure Oath's fist-and-lightning sigil
*   **Bottom:** A carved **gauntlet** — a mailed fist, fingers slightly curled, palm facing outward

**Right Pillar — six protrusions, three per side, arranged vertically:**

*   **Top:** A carved **lance** — a jousting lance with a spiraling grip and a lightning-bolt pennant
*   **Middle:** A carved **sword** — a longsword with a crossguard shaped like two lightning bolts, point downward
*   **Bottom:** A carved **banner** — a rectangular standard bearing the Azure Oath's heraldic device

##### The Riddle Inscriptions

Each bronze plaque bears a riddle in Old Oeridian. A character who reads Old Oeridian can read them directly. A Religion DC 10 or History DC 12 check identifies the script as liturgical Old Oeridian — the formal language of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) military orders. **Halven Sere** can translate both riddles instantly if present. A _Comprehend Languages_ spell also works.

**Left Pillar Plaque (Riddle One):**

> Friend to soldiers, guardsmen, and mercs.  
> Scarred from battle, unbowed by hurt.  
> I always face forward when battle is joined.  
> When on the march, I watch from behind.  
> What am I?

**Answer: Shield.**

**Right Pillar Plaque (Riddle Two):**

> I attend every battle. I attend every duel.  
> My tooth is sharp. My swing is cruel.  
> What am I?

**Answer: Sword.**

##### Puzzle Mechanics — Step by Step

The puzzle requires the party to read each riddle, determine the answer, and press the correct stone protrusion on the corresponding pillar. The sequence matters — the left pillar riddle must be solved first.

*   **Step 1 — Solve the left riddle (Shield):** Press the **shield protrusion** on the left pillar. The protrusion depresses with a grinding click. The lightning-bolt carvings on the left pillar begin to glow — blue-white energy climbs the spiraling motifs from base to ceiling, crackling with visible arcs of static. The left pillar hums with resonant vibration. Three of the six knight figures on the left half of the door begin to glow, their carved armor limned in blue-white light
*   **Step 2 — Solve the right riddle (Sword):** Press the **sword protrusion** on the right pillar. Same grinding click. The right pillar's lightning carvings ignite — blue-white energy races up the column. The remaining three knight figures on the door's right half blaze to life. Both pillars are now fully active, crackling with arcs of energy that leap between the lightning motifs. The chamber fills with the smell of ozone and the hum rises to an audible pitch
*   **Step 3 — The lock opens:** With both pillars active, the lightning energy arcs from the top of each pillar across the doorway, converging on the iron fist-and-lightning lock at the door's center. The arcs strike the iron lightning bolt held in the fist. The bolt glows white-hot. The iron fingers unclench — one by one, with grinding mechanical precision, each knuckle releasing. The lightning bolt rotates ninety degrees with a deep resonant _clang_ that reverberates through the chamber. The fist opens. The door swings inward on ancient stone hinges, revealing the passage to **AO-C06** beyond
*   **Reset:** The door remains open for ten minutes after activation, then slowly swings shut. The pillars deactivate. The protrusions pop outward to their original position. The puzzle must be solved again to reopen the door — but once solved, the party knows the answers

##### Wrong Answers

*   **Pressing the wrong protrusion:** The pressed protrusion depresses with the same grinding click, then immediately rejects — it snaps back outward with a sharp _crack_ and a pulse of blue-white energy erupts from the pillar. Every creature within 10 feet of the pillar takes **2d6 radiant damage** (WIS DC 14 to halve). The knight figures on the door that had been glowing (if any) flicker and go dark. Both pillars reset to their inactive state. The puzzle must be started from Step 1
*   **Pressing both pillars simultaneously or out of order:** If the right pillar is pressed before the left, the same radiant pulse occurs. The left riddle must be answered first — the shield before the sword. This reflects the martial doctrine of [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md): defend first, then strike
*   **Multiple wrong attempts:** The damage does not escalate. The radiant pulse is a deterrent, not a death trap. The founders designed this lock to test knowledge, not to kill the ignorant. However, each failed attempt is audible from **AO-C02** — the crack of the radiant pulse echoes through the arch and into the gallery (Perception DC 10 from anywhere in the gallery)

##### Alternative Solutions

*   **Halven Sere present:** **Halven** knows the riddle answers — he learned them from the previous custodian. He solves the puzzle without hesitation, pressing each protrusion with the practiced confidence of someone who has done this before. He does not explain the riddles. He assumes the answers are obvious to anyone worthy of entering
*   **Valegate's prayer book:** If the party has opened Knight-Commander Valegate's alcove and recovered his prayer book, the riddle answers are written inside — Valegate designed the lock and recorded the solutions in his personal notes. The relevant page is titled _"The Proving of the Gate"_ and lists both riddles with their answers in his handwriting
*   **Intelligence check:** A character proficient in Religion or History can attempt to reason through the riddles without reading Old Oeridian, by studying the protrusion carvings and the martial iconography. Intelligence (Religion) DC 14 or Intelligence (History) DC 16 to determine the correct protrusions by logic alone — the shield-and-sword pairing is a standard [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) catechism motif: the shield represents defense and duty, the sword represents justice and action. Together they form the core martial doctrine
*   **Brute force:** The door is consecrated stone — AC 20, HP 100, immunity to non-magical damage, resistance to all magical damage except radiant. The pillars are equally durable. Adamantine tools could breach the door in approximately 48 hours of continuous work. [Varnor Earthcaller](varnor-earthcaller-person.md) judged this impractical even if his crew could reach the room

### The Sacred Layer

The Founders' Sepulchre is the Azure Oath at its origin point. This is where twelve knights knelt in -12 CY and swore the oath that created the chapter. This is where the first consecration was spoken. This is where the ward network begins — every seal on every alcove in every corridor of the crypt complex traces its power back to this room, to the faith of twelve people who believed in something strongly enough to carve it into stone and then die inside it.

*   **Founding date:** -12 CY — twelve years before the Common Year calendar began. The Azure Oath chapter predates the modern political structure of the Flanaess. The priory was established during the Oeridian migration period, when the [Kron Hills](kron-hills-location.md) were still contested borderland and [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) military orders served as both garrison and missionary force
*   **The First Oath (floor inscription):** The continuous spiral of Old Oeridian text on the floor is the founding prayer — a formal dedication of the chapter to [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md)'s service, naming each of the twelve founders, committing their lives and their deaths to the defence of the road and the hills. Religion DC 10 to read; DC 16 to understand the theological significance — the oath is structured as a _covenant_, a two-way binding that commits the god as well as the knights. This is why the wards still hold. [Heironeous](heironeous-person.md) is bound by the oath as much as the knights were
*   **Ward origin:** The consecration that powers every ward in the crypt complex was first spoken here. The brazier was the focal point — the consecrated flame carried the prayer's power into the stone, and the stone carried it outward through the walls and floors of the crypts as they were built. The later wards (middle-era, late-era) are extensions of this original consecration, growing weaker with distance and time. The founding-era wards in this room are at the source. They will not weaken until the oath itself is broken — and the oath has no expiration clause

### The Desecration — What Has Not Happened

There is no desecration in the Founders' Sepulchre. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has never entered. Nothing has been looted, defaced, broken, or moved. The dust is undisturbed. The alcoves are sealed. The brazier is cold but intact. This is the only room in the crypt complex that remains exactly as the [Holy Order of Heironeous](the-holy-order-of-heironeous-organization.md) left it.

*   **The contrast:** The party has walked through **AO-C03** — the Desecrated Tombs — where [Tharvok Stonehand](tharvok-stonehand-person.md)'s men smashed open alcoves, scattered bones, and stripped grave goods. They have seen what the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) does to the dead. Now they stand in the room the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) could not reach. The contrast should be stark
*   **The implication:** The wards work. Faith held the line. Twelve knights who have been dead for five centuries are still protecting this room. The [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) has geomancers, duergar sappers, and a dark god behind them — and they could not break through a closed door. This is what the party is fighting to preserve

### DM Guidance — Running the Room

The Founders' Sepulchre is a reward, not a fight. There are no enemies here, no traps (the radiant pulse on the riddle lock is a deterrent, not a lethal hazard), and no time pressure unless the party creates it. The room earns its impact through contrast — after the desecrated tombs, the duergar camp, the gallery's damaged wards and undead threats, the party enters a room that is _perfect_. Untouched. Still sacred. Still working.

*   **Pacing:** Slow this room down. Let the party explore. Read the aloud text carefully. Describe the silence, the cold, the dust, the footprints. Let them read the inscriptions. Let them study the alcoves. Do not rush to mechanics
*   **The riddle puzzle:** Give the players time with the riddles. The answers (Shield and Sword) are fair — experienced D&D players will likely solve them quickly, but the physical interaction (pressing protrusions, watching lightning climb the pillars, hearing the mechanical grinding) makes it feel earned. If the party is stuck, **Halven Sere** can hint: _"The founders tested knowledge of the warrior's way. Every soldier knows the two things they carry."_
*   **Looting:** The items in the founder alcoves are genuinely useful — a +1 radiant longsword and a +1 light shield are appropriate for a level 4–5 party. But taking them should feel like a choice with weight, not routine treasure collection. The guilt mechanic (WIS DC 14 or disadvantage on CHA checks with Heironeous clergy) reinforces this without punishing the players mechanically in combat. Let the party decide what kind of heroes they are
*   **Halven Sere:** If **Halven** is with the party, this room hits him hardest. He was the custodian of this place. He walked these floors. He prayed at these alcoves. He lit the brazier. His reaction depends on what the party does — if they are respectful, he weeps quietly and thanks them for bringing him home. If they loot immediately, he says nothing but his trust drops permanently. Play **Halven**'s silence as loudly as his words
*   **The brazier decision:** Lighting the brazier is a strategic choice — the 24-hour ward surge is powerful, but the sigil beacon is visible from the chapel above, which means the [Earth Temple](specialty-cleric3A-earth-temple-cultist-of-the-black-earth-organization.md) will know something has changed in the crypts.
