Tyne Timber & Trade
===================

Tyne Timber & Trade is the **largest and most influential merchant enterprise** in Twilight Falls, controlling much of the village’s **lumber industry and forest trade**. Founded by **Elder Romarl Tyne**, a former mayor and master merchant, the company has been passed down to his son, **Robleth Tyne**, the **current mayor of Twilight Falls**. The business deals primarily in **selective logging, milling, and the sale of high-quality timber**, but it also acts as a **merchant hub** for pelts, medicinal plants, and other **resources from the Gnarley Forest**.

As a business empire, Tyne Timber & Trade has **brought both wealth and conflict** to Twilight Falls. The company’s expansion has led to **increasing tensions with druids and elves** who seek to preserve the forest. Meanwhile, its dominance over local commerce has made [Robleth Tyne](robleth-tyne-person.md) **a powerful and controversial figure**, both in the village and among the merchant circles of Verbobonc.

**The Lumberyard & Headquarters**
---------------------------------

##### **Location & Layout**

Tyne Timber & Trade is headquartered on the **southern edge of** [Twilight Falls](twilight-falls-settlement.md), where the **Twilight Run River** provides a convenient **water route for transporting logs**. The headquarters consists of **several large structures**, including **a sawmill, storage yards, and merchant offices**.

*   **The Sawmill** – A **massive wooden structure** powered by the flow of the river, where logs are milled into **planks and beams** for sale and export.
*   **The Log Yards** – Stacks of **freshly harvested lumber**, organized by **size, type, and quality**.
*   **Merchant Offices** – A stone-and-wood building that houses **trade records, ledgers, and Robleth Tyne’s personal office**.
*   **Guardhouse & Watchtower** – A small **stone guardhouse** where **Tyne’s hired enforcers** oversee operations and protect against theft.

##### **Logging Territories**

Tyne Timber & Trade controls **several selective cutting zones** in the **Gnarley Forest**, marked by **logging camps** and **patrolled trade routes**.

*   **Green Hollow Logging Camp** – A major site **deep in the Gnarley**, where workers **fell and transport prime hardwoods**.
*   **Raven’s Hollow Outpost** – A remote camp **near elven lands**, where tension between loggers and **forest guardians often flares**.
*   **The Twilight Run Trade Route** – A **river-based transport network**, using **rafts and barges to send timber to Verbobonc**.

##### **Trade & Export Partners**

Tyne Timber & Trade supplies **some of the most powerful merchant houses and guilds** in Verbobonc, ensuring its continued wealth and influence.

*   **Dantilus Trimber’s Merchant Consortium** – A close ally of **Robleth Tyne**, benefiting from exclusive timber trade deals.
*   **Verbobonc’s Shipbuilders & Nobility** – The company supplies **fine wood for ships, manor construction, and high-end furniture**.
*   **Independent Traders & Caravans** – While the company deals mostly in bulk contracts, it also **sells to traveling merchants**.

### **Political Influence & Conflicts**

##### **Robleth Tyne’s Grip on Twilight Falls**

As the owner of Tyne Timber & Trade and **mayor of Twilight Falls**, **Robleth Tyne holds immense power** over the village’s economy and governance. His influence extends to **local merchants, town guards, and even certain noble allies in Verbobonc**.

*   **Control Over Trade Permits** – All major **lumber and forest trade in Twilight Falls passes through Tyne’s company**, forcing merchants to work with him.
*   **Influence Over Town Policy** – His **economic policies prioritize profit**, often at the expense of nature and tradition.
*   **Enforcers & Hired Muscle** – The company employs **private guards and enforcers**, ensuring **no rival businesses rise in opposition**.

##### **Conflicts with the Old Faith & Druids**

The expansion of Tyne Timber & Trade has brought **open conflict with the druids of the Old Faith**, led by **Mahrli Wildbriar**. The company’s aggressive logging efforts have also strained **relations with the elves of the Gnarley**.

*   [Mahrli Wildbriar](mahrli-harefoot-person.md) **&** [4\. The Circle of the Old Faith](4-the-circle-of-the-old-faith-landmark.md) – The druids **oppose unchecked deforestation** and have **clashed with Tyne’s loggers** in the past.
*   **Elven Hostility** – The [Elves of the Gnarley Forest](elves-of-the-gnarley-forest-organization.md) clans see Tyne’s company as a **threat to the balance of the woods**.
*   [The Gnarley Rangers](the-gnarley-rangers-organization.md) – While neutral, **some rangers keep a close watch** on Tyne’s operations, ensuring he does not overstep.

##### **Merchant Rivalries & Internal Dissent**

Not all **merchant factions support Robleth Tyne**. Some believe he is **too focused on short-term profit**, while others **seek to undermine his control**.

*   **Rival Logging Companies** – Small-scale **independent loggers** have been **pushed out or bought out** by Tyne.
*   **Mistress Anda Dove & The Artist’s Colony** – Anda believes **Twilight Falls should remain a cultural sanctuary**, not a **hub for industrial trade**.
*   **Discontented Workers** – Some **lumberjacks and traders** grumble about **Tyne’s tight-fisted control**, and rumors suggest some **may be working against him**.

### **Secrets & Adventure Hooks**

##### **Mysteries & Shady Deals**

*   **Illegal Logging?** – Some whisper that **Tyne Timber & Trade has been harvesting beyond its legal borders**, even **into sacred druidic groves**.
*   **Hidden Smuggling Ring** – Rumors suggest that Tyne’s trade network may be **moving more than just timber**, perhaps even **contraband or stolen artifacts**.
*   **The Assassination Plot** – Unknown forces **seek to remove Robleth Tyne from power**. Is it the druids, a rival merchant, or someone within his own ranks?

##### **Adventure Hooks**

*   **Sabotage in the Sawmill** – The adventurers are hired to **investigate strange incidents** at the sawmill, where workers report **mysterious accidents and disappearances**.
*   **Escort a Timber Shipment** – A valuable load of **rare wood** must be transported to Verbobonc, but **bandits, elves, or political enemies** may seek to destroy it.
*   **A Disgruntled Merchant’s Plea** – A former **business partner of Tyne seeks revenge**, offering the party **evidence of corruption** in the company.
*   **Druids on the Warpath** – The Old Faith demands an end to **Tyne’s expansion**, and the adventurers must **negotiate—or take sides in an escalating conflict**.

* * *

#### **Conclusion**

Tyne Timber & Trade is **both the beating heart of Twilight Falls’ economy and the source of its greatest tensions**. While it brings **wealth, jobs, and influence**, it also threatens **the balance of the forest and the town’s future**. Whether it \*\*thrives, falls, or changes hands depends on those who dare to challenge—or uphold—Robleth Tyne’s rule.
